Diplacus longiflorus var. linearis

Diplacus longiflorus var. linearis, commonly known as the linear leaf twinflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are often pink or red in color, with a tubular throat that is usually a contrasting hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
