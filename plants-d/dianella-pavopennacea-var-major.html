Dianella pavopennacea var. major

Dianella pavopennacea var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved flax lily, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette at the base. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
