Dianthus pungens subsp. catalaunicus

Dianthus pungens subsp.

catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, double or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
