Dipteracanthus domingensis

Dipteracanthus domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the forest understory. Its leaves are ovate and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
