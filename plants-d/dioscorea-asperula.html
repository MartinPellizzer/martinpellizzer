Dioscorea asperula

Dioscorea asperula, commonly known as rough yam, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cordate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces tubers that are typically rough and irregular in shape. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

While not widely cultivated for food, it has been used traditionally in some indigenous practices.
