Daphne baksanica

Daphne baksanica, commonly known as the Baksan daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture.

Daphne baksanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
