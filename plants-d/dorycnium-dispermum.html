Dorycnium dispermum

Dorycnium dispermum, commonly known as the two-seeded burclover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain two seeds, giving it its specific epithet "dispermum.". It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Dorycnium dispermum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for certain wildlife species.
