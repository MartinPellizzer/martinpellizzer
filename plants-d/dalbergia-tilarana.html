Dalbergia tilarana

Dalbergia tilarana, commonly known as the Tilarana rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical conditions and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a distinctive, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

Due to its ornamental and commercial value, Dalbergia tilarana is also of interest for conservation efforts.
