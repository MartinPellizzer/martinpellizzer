Deschampsia argentea f. versicolor

Deschampsia argentea f. versicolor, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a variant of the silver hairgrass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, especially when viewed in direct light. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This grass is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in natural environments.
