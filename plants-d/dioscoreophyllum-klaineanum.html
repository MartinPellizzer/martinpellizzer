Dioscoreophyllum klaineanum

Dioscoreophyllum klaineanum, commonly known as the African yam vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are sometimes used as a food source, though they are not as widely cultivated as traditional yams.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential in horticulture.
