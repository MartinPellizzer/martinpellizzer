Deyeuxia anthoxanthum

Deyeuxia anthoxanthum, commonly known as the yellow false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with yellow or reddish hues. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is adapted to thrive in environments with high humidity.

Deyeuxia anthoxanthum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
