Doliocarpus dentatus subsp. obovatus

Doliocarpus dentatus subsp.

obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed doliocarpus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its obovate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a drupe, which contains a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody shell.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
