Desmodium boottii

Desmodium boottii, commonly known as the yellow burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small yellow flowers bloom in late spring, and the plant is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Desmodium boottii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which enhance soil fertility.
