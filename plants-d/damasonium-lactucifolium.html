Damasonium lactucifolium

Damasonium lactucifolium, commonly known as the milk thistle.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its milky sap, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while the flowers are arranged in clusters.

Damasonium lactucifolium is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
