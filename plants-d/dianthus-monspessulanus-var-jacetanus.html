Dianthus monspessulanus var. jacetanus

Dianthus monspessulanus var. jacetanus, commonly known as the Corsican campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
