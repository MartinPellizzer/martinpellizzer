Duroia genipifolia

Duroia genipifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or genipapo, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

The plant belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. Its berries contain a compound called genipin, which is used in the production of synthetic flavors and dyes.
