Dipsacus gmelinii

Dipsacus gmelinii, commonly known as the common teasel, is a biennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, which give it a unique appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Dipsacus gmelinii is known for its historical use in the textile industry for raising wool on fabric.
