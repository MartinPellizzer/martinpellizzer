Dioscorea rockii

Dioscorea rockii, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and climbing.

The tubers of Dioscorea rockii are valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal properties.
