Diplacus glutinosus var. puniceus

Diplacus glutinosus var. puniceus, commonly known as the crimson monkeyflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a sticky, glandular coating, which gives it the species name "glutinosus.".

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and adequate sunlight.
