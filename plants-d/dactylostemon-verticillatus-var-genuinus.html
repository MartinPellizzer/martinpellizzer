Dactylostemon verticillatus var. genuinus

Dactylostemon verticillatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in southern Australia.

It has small, ovate, glossy leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces white, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. It is characterized by its lack of roots and its ability to derive water and nutrients from its host.

This species is often found in coastal and heathland environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions.
