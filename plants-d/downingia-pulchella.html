Downingia pulchella

Downingia pulchella, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in open meadows and forested areas at high elevations.
