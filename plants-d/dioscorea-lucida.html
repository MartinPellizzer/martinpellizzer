Dioscorea lucida

Dioscorea lucida, commonly known as the shining yam, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by tuberous roots, which are its primary economic and medicinal value. Dioscorea lucida is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its potential use in traditional medicine.

Its tubers contain compounds that have been studied for their pharmacological properties.
