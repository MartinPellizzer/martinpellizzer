Dontostemon dentatus var. pilosus

Dontostemon dentatus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy toothed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, ovate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
