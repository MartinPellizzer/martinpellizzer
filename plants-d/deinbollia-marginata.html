Deinbollia marginata

Deinbollia marginata, commonly known as the white salsify, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with stout, hairy stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender, cylindrical fruits. Its root system is fibrous, and the plant is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Deinbollia marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible roots.
