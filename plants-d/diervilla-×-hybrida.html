Diervilla × hybrida

Diervilla × hybrida, commonly known as crossberry or hybrid azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Diervilla × hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
