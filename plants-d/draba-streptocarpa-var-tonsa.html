Draba streptocarpa var. tonsa

Draba streptocarpa var. tonsa, commonly known as the alpine mouse-eared cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and small, yellow flowers. Native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in rocky and alpine habitats. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
