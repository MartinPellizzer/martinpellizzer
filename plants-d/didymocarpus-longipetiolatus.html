Didymocarpus longipetiolatus

Didymocarpus longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
