Deyeuxia setiflora

Deyeuxia setiflora, commonly known as the silky false brome, is a grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, silky-haired leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained to wet soils. Deyeuxia setiflora produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
