Disperis cardiophora

Disperis cardiophora, commonly known as the heart-shaped disperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
