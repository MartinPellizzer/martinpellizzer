Dalea inconspicua

Dalea inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Dalea inconspicua plays a role in soil stabilization and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
