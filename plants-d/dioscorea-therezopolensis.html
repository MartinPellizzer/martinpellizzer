Dioscorea therezopolensis

Dioscorea therezopolensis, commonly known as the Zambian yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellowish-white flowers with prominent stamens. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source by local communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
