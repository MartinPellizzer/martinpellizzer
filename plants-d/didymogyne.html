Didymogyne

Didymogyne, commonly known as the "false sandalwood," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions of Australia and New Caledonia. The plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers with showy petals. Didymogyne species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, often with rough, bark-like stems.

They play a role in local ecosystems and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
