Dendrocolla merguensis

Dendrocolla merguensis, commonly known as the Merguensis tree, is a rare and unique plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and dense, evergreen foliage that provides shade under the forest canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in the humid environment.

The tree's root system is extensive, helping to stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in its natural habitat.
