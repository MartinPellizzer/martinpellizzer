Diospyros abyssinica subsp. abyssinica

Diospyros abyssinica subsp.

abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red, fleshy berries. Its bark is thick and rugged, often marked with longitudinal fissures, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
