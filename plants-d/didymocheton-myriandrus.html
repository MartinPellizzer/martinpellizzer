Didymocheton myriandrus

Didymocheton myriandrus, commonly known as the many-flowered rain tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading canopy and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support local wildlife.
