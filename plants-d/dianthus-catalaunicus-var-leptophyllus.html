Dianthus catalaunicus var. leptophyllus

Dianthus catalaunicus var. leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
