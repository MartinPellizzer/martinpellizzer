Dennstaedtia kalbreyeri

Dennstaedtia kalbreyeri, commonly known as the Kalbreyer's water fern, is a species of fern native to the wet tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or drooping, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
