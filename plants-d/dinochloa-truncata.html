Dinochloa truncata

Dinochloa truncata, commonly known as the dwarf grass or narrow-leaf grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the tips. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it reproduces through wind-pollinated seeds.

Dinochloa truncata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
