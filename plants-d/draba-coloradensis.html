Draba coloradensis

Draba coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado draba, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. This species typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often in rocky soils and open habitats. Draba coloradensis produces small, white or pale yellow flowers and has a compact, mat-forming growth habit.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high elevations.
