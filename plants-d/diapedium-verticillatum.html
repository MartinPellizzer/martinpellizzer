Diapedium verticillatum

Diapedium verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively through underground stems. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Diapedium verticillatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
