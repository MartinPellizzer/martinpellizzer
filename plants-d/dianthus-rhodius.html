Dianthus rhodius

Dianthus rhodius, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Dianthus rhodius is often found in alpine and coastal regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
