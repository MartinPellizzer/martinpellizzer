Dactylis polygama f. tenuifolia

Dactylis polygama f. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender goatgrass, is a variant of the common goatgrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is adapted to a variety of soil types. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like flower clusters.

This subspecies is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
