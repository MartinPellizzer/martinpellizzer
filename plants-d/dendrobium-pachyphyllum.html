Dendrobium pachyphyllum

Dendrobium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in humid tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. It produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, often in clusters.

Dendrobium pachyphyllum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
