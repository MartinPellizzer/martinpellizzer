Dactylis glomerata subsp. judaica

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

judaica, commonly known as Jerusalem grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, two-lobed flowers. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in poor agricultural conditions.

This subspecies is often used in erosion control and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and resilience.
