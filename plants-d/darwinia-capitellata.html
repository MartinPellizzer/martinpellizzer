Darwinia capitellata

Darwinia capitellata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed devil's claw, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Darwinia capitellata is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees.
