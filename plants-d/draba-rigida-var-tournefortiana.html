Draba rigida var. tournefortiana

Draba rigida var. tournefortiana, commonly known as the rigid whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing habit and basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster, typically blooming in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to alpine gardens and a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
