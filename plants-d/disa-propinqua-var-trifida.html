Disa propinqua var. trifida

Disa propinqua var. trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed disa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that sets it apart from other disa species.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
