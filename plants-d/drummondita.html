Drummondita

Drummondita, commonly known as the Texas bluebonnet, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in open fields and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Drummondita has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is the state flower of Texas and holds cultural significance in the region.
