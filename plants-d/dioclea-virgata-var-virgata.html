Dioclea virgata var. virgata

Dioclea virgata var. virgata, commonly known as the scarlet bignay, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
