Dalbergia pendula

Dalbergia pendula, commonly known as the hanging or weeping ebony, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its wood is dense and dark, valued for its use in traditional crafts and furniture.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
