Dalea wrightii

Dalea wrightii, commonly known as Wright's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of pink to reddish-purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Dalea wrightii is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
