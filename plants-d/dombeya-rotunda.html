Dombeya rotunda

Dombeya rotunda, commonly known as the roundleaf dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Dombeya rotunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
