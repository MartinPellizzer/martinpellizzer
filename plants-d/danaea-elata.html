Danaea elata

Danaea elata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Danaea elata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
