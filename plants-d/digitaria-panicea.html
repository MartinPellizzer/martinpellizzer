Digitaria panicea

Digitaria panicea, commonly known as Panicum digitatum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Digitaria panicea is used in some regions for forage and has ornamental value due to its delicate appearance.
