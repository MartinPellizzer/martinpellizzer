Dioscorea pentaphylla var. javanica

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. javanica, commonly known as the Javan yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are typically cylindrical and have a starchy, edible texture when cooked.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and medicinal properties in various cultures.
