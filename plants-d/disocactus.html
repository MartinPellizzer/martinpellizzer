Disocactus

Disocactus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to Central and South America.

These plants are characterized by their tall, slender stems and are typically spiny, with some species having white or pale green stems. Disocactus species are known for their showy, night-blooming flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. They thrive in well-draining soil and require full sun to partial shade.

Due to their unique flowering habit, they are popular among cactus enthusiasts and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.
