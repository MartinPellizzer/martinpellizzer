Delonix leucantha

Delonix leucantha, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, umbrella-shaped canopy and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Delonix leucantha thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
