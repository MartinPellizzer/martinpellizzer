Dianthus prolifer var. atapuercae

Dianthus prolifer var. atapuercae, commonly known as the Atapuerca carnation, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features showy, double or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming habit with dense foliage and is well-suited for rock gardens or borders. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
