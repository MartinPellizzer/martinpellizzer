Digitaria ciliaris f. intercedens

Digitaria ciliaris f. intercedens, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with hairy margins. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
