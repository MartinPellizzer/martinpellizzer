Dianthus broteri

Dianthus broteri, commonly known as the African carnation, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers with prominent white markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus broteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
