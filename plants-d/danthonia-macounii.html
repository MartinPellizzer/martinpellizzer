Danthonia macounii

Danthonia macounii, commonly known as Macoun's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Danthonia macounii is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.
