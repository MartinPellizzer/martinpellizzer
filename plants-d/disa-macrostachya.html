Disa macrostachya

Disa macrostachya, commonly known as the giant disa, is a striking member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing clusters of bright pink or red flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Disa macrostachya is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
