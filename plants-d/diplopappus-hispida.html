Diplopappus hispida

Diplopappus hispida, commonly known as the hairy burr bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
