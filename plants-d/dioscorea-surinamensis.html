Dioscorea surinamensis

Dioscorea surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces tubers that are used as a food source in some indigenous communities. These tubers are typically harvested after the plant has flowered and set fruit.

Dioscorea surinamensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
