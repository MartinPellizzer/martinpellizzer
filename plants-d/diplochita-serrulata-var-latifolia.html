Diplochita serrulata var. latifolia

Diplochita serrulata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved diplochita, is a variant of the species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves that provide a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a subtle fragrance.

The varietal form, latifolia, is distinguished by its broader leaf morphology compared to the typical species.
