Damnacanthus major var. submitis

Damnacanthus major var. submitis, commonly known as the narrowleaf false mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical structure includes opposite leaf arrangement and a fibrous root system.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
