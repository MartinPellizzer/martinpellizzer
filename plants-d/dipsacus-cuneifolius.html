Dipsacus cuneifolius

Dipsacus cuneifolius, commonly known as the spear thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Dipsacus cuneifolius is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
