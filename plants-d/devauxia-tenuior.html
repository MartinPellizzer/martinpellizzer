Devauxia tenuior

Devauxia tenuior, commonly known as the slender devauxia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
