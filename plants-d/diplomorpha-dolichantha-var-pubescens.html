Diplomorpha dolichantha var. pubescens

Diplomorpha dolichantha var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy long-stalked orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, hairy stems and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which can be white, yellow, or orange, are arranged in clusters and attract various pollinators.
