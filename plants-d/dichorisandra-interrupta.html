Dichorisandra interrupta

Dichorisandra interrupta, commonly known as the blue star lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Dichorisandra interrupta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
