Deutzia pauciflora

Deutzia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparseflower deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Deutzia pauciflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
