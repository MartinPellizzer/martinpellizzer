Dendrobium epidendropsis

Dendrobium epidendropsis, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Dendrobium, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a caudex and produces numerous racemes of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Dendrobium epidendropsis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
