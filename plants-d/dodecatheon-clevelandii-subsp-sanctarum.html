Dodecatheon clevelandii subsp. sanctarum

Dodecatheon clevelandii subsp.

sanctarum, commonly known as the Santa Rosa Mountains glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, mountainous environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique floral morphology.
