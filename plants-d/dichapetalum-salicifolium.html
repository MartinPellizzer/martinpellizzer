Dichapetalum salicifolium

Dichapetalum salicifolium, commonly known as the waterleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used traditionally in treating various ailments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking foliage.
