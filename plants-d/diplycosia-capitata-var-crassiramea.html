Diplycosia capitata var. crassiramea

Diplycosia capitata var. crassiramea, commonly known as the thick-stemmed diplycosia, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences and lanceolate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in compact, terminal clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
