Digitalis canariensis var. isabelliana

Digitalis canariensis var. isabelliana, commonly known as the Canary Island foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
