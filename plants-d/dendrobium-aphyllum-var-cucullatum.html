Dendrobium aphyllum var. cucullatum

Dendrobium aphyllum var. cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded phallic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that thrives in humid tropical environments, often growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant features a compact growth habit with clustered pseudobulbs and elongated, narrow leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its unusual, elongated flower spike that resembles a phallic shape, which gives it its common name.

The flowers are typically yellow or green with a contrasting red or orange lip, making it a striking ornamental plant.
