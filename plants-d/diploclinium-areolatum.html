Diploclinium areolatum

Diploclinium areolatum, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often with yellow or greenish petals.

The species is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
