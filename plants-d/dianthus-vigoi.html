Dianthus vigoi

Dianthus vigoi, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known genera like Dianthus and Silene. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with upright stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus vigoi thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
