Dendropanax langeanus

Dendropanax langeanus, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant is native to New Zealand and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system allows it to store nutrients and water, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

The fronds are typically evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in cultivation.
