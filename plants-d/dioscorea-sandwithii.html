Dioscorea sandwithii

Dioscorea sandwithii, commonly known as the Sandwith yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend support structures, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The tubers of Dioscorea sandwithii are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
