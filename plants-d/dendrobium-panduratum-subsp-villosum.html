Dendrobium panduratum subsp. villosum

Dendrobium panduratum subsp.

villosum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments.

The plant features long, cane-like stems covered with fine, hairy structures, which give it its subspecies name. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with purple or red markings, and it blooms during the warmer months.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
