Davilla sagrana

Davilla sagrana, commonly known as the sacred bignonia, is a woody vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Davilla sagrana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
