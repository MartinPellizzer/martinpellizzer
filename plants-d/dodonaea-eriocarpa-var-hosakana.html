Dodonaea eriocarpa var. hosakana

Dodonaea eriocarpa var. hosakana, commonly known as the silverleaf glorybower, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
