Disaster novoguinensis

Disaster novoguinensis, commonly known as the New Guinea disaster plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored foliage, which can range from deep reds to vibrant yellows. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for indoor cultivation. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and unusual appearance.
