Dianthus pogonopetalus

Dianthus pogonopetalus, commonly known as the beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like carnations and pinks.

Dianthus pogonopetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
