Dryadella odontostele

Dryadella odontostele, commonly known as the toothed-stemmed fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and delicate, pinnately divided fronds. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as streams and ponds. Its leaves are typically green and have a serrated or toothed margin, which gives it its common name.

Dryadella odontostele plays a role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
