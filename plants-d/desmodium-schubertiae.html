Desmodium schubertiae

Desmodium schubertiae, commonly known as the slender desmodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and contributes to nitrogen fixation through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
