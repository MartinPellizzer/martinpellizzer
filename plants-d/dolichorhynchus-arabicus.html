Dolichorhynchus arabicus

Dolichorhynchus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with minimal rainfall.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.
