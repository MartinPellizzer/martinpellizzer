Dioscorea tabatae

Dioscorea tabatae, commonly known as the Tabata yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, tendril-bearing stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their medicinal and nutritional properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.
