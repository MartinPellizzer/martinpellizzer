Dichaea filiarum

Dichaea filiarum, commonly known as the small-flowered dichaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically short and strap-like, forming a rosette that helps retain moisture.

Dichaea filiarum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in humid environments.
