Diaphananthe trigonopetala

Diaphananthe trigonopetala, commonly known as the three-lobed flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or near water sources.
