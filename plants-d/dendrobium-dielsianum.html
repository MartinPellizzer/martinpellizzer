Dendrobium dielsianum

Dendrobium dielsianum, commonly known as the Diels' orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing white or pale yellow petals with a distinctive greenish hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dendrobium dielsianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in both tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
