Drymaria macrantha

Drymaria macrantha, commonly known as the common sand-plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.

Drymaria macrantha is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
