Diplotaxis crassifolia var. pitardiana

Diplotaxis crassifolia var. pitardiana, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically succulent, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
