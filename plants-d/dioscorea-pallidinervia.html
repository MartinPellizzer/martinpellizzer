Dioscorea pallidinervia

Dioscorea pallidinervia, commonly known as the pale-veined yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent, pale veins, and it bears small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
