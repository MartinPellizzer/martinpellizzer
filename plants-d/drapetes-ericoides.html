Drapetes ericoides

Drapetes ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland landscapes.

Drapetes ericoides is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
