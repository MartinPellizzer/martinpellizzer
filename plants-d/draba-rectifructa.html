Draba rectifructa

Draba rectifructa, commonly known as the straight-fruited whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its fruits are short and cylindrical, giving it the common name "straight-fruited," and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Draba rectifructa is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
