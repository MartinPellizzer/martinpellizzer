Dissotis speciosa

Dissotis speciosa, commonly known as the showy spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often striking floral structures. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal.
