Draba affghanica

Draba affghanica, commonly known as Afghan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces delicate white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, and its leaves are narrow and basal, often covered in fine hairs. Draba affghanica is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

It is adapted to harsh, cold climates and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
