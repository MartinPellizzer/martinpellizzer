Dalea kingii

Dalea kingii, commonly known as the King's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub or subshrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea kingii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
