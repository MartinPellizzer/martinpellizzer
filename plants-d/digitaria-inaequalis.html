Digitaria inaequalis

Digitaria inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal digitary, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Digitaria inaequalis is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
