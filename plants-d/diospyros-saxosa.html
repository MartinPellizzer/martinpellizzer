Diospyros saxosa

Diospyros saxosa, commonly known as the rock persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit, which is usually small and fleshy, is edible and has a sweet, sometimes astringent taste.

Diospyros saxosa is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.
