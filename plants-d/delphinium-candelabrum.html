Delphinium candelabrum

Delphinium candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that resemble a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Delphinium candelabrum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking dramatic floral displays.
