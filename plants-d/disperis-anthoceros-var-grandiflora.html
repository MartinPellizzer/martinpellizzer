Disperis anthoceros var. grandiflora

Disperis anthoceros var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered disperis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow and violet coloration. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
