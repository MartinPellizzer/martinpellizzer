Dittrichia revoluta

Dittrichia revoluta, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
