Dianella longifolia var. stupata

Dianella longifolia var. stupata, commonly known as the Stupated Flax Lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late winter to early spring, which are borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
