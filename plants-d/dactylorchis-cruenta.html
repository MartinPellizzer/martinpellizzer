Dactylorchis cruenta

Dactylorchis cruenta, commonly known as the bloodroot orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its solitary, striking red or orange flower that emerges from a basal rosette of waxy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Dactylorchis cruenta is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native range.
