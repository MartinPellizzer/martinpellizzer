Dombeya hirsuta

Dombeya hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows in woodland and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
