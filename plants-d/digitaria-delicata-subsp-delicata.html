Digitaria delicata subsp. delicata

Digitaria delicata subsp.

delicata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecosystems and a potential forage plant for livestock.
