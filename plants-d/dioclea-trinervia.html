Dioclea trinervia

Dioclea trinervia, commonly known as the Amazonian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by local communities. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with three or five veins, giving it the species name *trinervia*.

Dioclea trinervia thrives in tropical rainforests and is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various wildlife species.
