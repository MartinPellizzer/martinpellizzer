Deyeuxia sylvatica var. laxiflora

Deyeuxia sylvatica var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender false barley, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
