Dryopteris subincisa var. canescens

Dryopteris subincisa var. canescens, commonly known as the silver fern, is a variant of the brittle shield fern.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a delicate, feathery texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
