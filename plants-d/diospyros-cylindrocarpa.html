Diospyros cylindrocarpa

Diospyros cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the round-fruited persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a small, globose berry that turns from green to orange when mature.

The tree is often grown for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, astringent flavor when ripe.
