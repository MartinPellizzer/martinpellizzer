Diuris subalpina

Diuris subalpina, commonly known as the Alpine Rocket Orchid, is an herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short flowering stem that bears a single or few flowers.

Diuris subalpina is often found in open heathlands and is a rare species due to its specific habitat requirements.
