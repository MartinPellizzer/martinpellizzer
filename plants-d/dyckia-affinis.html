Dyckia affinis

Dyckia affinis, commonly known as the silver beard grass, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with narrow, silver-tomentose leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, white flowers, which are often hidden among the foliage. Dyckia affinis is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
