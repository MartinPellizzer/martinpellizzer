Draba gilliesii var. rosulada

Draba gilliesii var. rosulada, commonly known as the Rosulate Draba, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms a dense rosette of basal leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, appearing in early spring.

The species is noted for its compact growth habit and is often found in meadows and slopes above the tree line.
