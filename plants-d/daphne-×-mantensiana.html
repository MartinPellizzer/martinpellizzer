Daphne × mantensiana

Daphne × mantensiana, commonly known as the hybrid daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Daphne × mantensiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its winter interest.
