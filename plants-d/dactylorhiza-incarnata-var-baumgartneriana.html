Dactylorhiza incarnata var. baumgartneriana

Dactylorhiza incarnata var. baumgartneriana, commonly known as the Baumann's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new growth from a lateral bud. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker pigments.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
