Dalea vulneraria f. barbata

Dalea vulneraria f. barbata, commonly known as the barbed prairie clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, barbed leaflets, which give it its specific epithet "barbata.". It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Dalea vulneraria f. barbata plays a role in nitrogen fixation and provides nectar for pollinators.
