Disa ovalifolia

Disa ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are oval-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Disa ovalifolia is admired for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
