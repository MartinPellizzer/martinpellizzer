Diospyros beankensis

Diospyros beankensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lanceolate leaves with prominent veins. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits.

Its fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed raw or cooked, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
