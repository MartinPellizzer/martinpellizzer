Desmodium weberbaueri

Desmodium weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seed pods are hairy and contain several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
