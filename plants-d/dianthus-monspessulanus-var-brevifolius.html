Dianthus monspessulanus var. brevifolius

Dianthus monspessulanus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
