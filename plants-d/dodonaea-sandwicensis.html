Dodonaea sandwicensis

Dodonaea sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian Dodonaea, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii, with a preference for well-drained soils and full sun.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it a characteristic component of coastal vegetation.
