Dianthus carthusianorum var. fasciculatus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the Carthusian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of pink or red flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
