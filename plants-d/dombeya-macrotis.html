Dombeya macrotis

Dombeya macrotis, commonly known as the large-flowered dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

Dombeya macrotis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
