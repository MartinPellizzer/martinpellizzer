Draba cacuminum subsp. cacuminum

Draba cacuminum subsp.

cacuminum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a small, flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
