Dimorphochloa rigida

Dimorphochloa rigida, commonly known as the rigid dimorphochloa, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, stiff stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is a defining botanical feature.

Dimorphochloa rigida is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
