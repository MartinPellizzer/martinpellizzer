Delphinium patens subsp. montanum

Delphinium patens subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. Delphinium patens subsp.

montanum is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
