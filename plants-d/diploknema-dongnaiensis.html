Diploknema dongnaiensis

Diploknema dongnaiensis, commonly known as the Dong Nai ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage, which contribute to its ecological significance in forest ecosystems. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forests, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its durability and is often used in construction and furniture-making.
