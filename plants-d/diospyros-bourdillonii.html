Diospyros bourdillonii

Diospyros bourdillonii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony trees, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a thick, waxy coating.

Its wood is dense and highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and furniture.
