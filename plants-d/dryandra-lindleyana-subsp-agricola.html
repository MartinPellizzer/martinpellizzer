Dryandra lindleyana subsp. agricola

Dryandra lindleyana subsp.

agricola, commonly known as the agricultural dryandra, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in summer.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry landscapes. It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is part of the Dryandra genus, which is known for its ecological significance in the region.

This subspecies is often used in rehabilitation projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
