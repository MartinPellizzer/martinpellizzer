Desmodium punctatum

Desmodium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Desmodium punctatum is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
