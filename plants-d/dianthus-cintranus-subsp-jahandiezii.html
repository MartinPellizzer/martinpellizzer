Dianthus cintranus subsp. jahandiezii

Dianthus cintranus subsp.

jahandiezii, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy pink to red flowers with frilled petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
