Dactylorchis maculata var. grandiflora

Dactylorchis maculata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of North America.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with distinctive purple spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in association with other orchid species.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.
