Diospyros dasyphylla

Diospyros dasyphylla, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairless. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, astringent flavor, making it a valuable food source for both wildlife and humans.
