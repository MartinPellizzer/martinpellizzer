Dendrophorbium amplexicaule

Dendrophorbium amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping stem orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, where the leaves tightly clasp the stem, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
