Disa racemosa var. venosa

Disa racemosa var. venosa, commonly known as the spotted disa, is a striking South African orchid species characterized by its vivid purple and white flowers with distinctive markings.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the grassland regions of South Africa. The plant typically grows from a rhizome and produces tall, slender stems with clustered flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

The variety 'venosa' is distinguished by its speckled or spotted appearance on the petals and sepals, making it highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
