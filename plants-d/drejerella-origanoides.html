Drejerella origanoides

Drejerella origanoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has slender, aromatic leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length, and it produces small, white or pale blue flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
