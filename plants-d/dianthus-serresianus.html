Dianthus serresianus

Dianthus serresianus, commonly known as the Sierra pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, pink or red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Dianthus serresianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
