Daviesia pachyphylla

Daviesia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved daviesia, is a flowering plant native to south-western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers, which attract a variety of pollinators. It is typically found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
