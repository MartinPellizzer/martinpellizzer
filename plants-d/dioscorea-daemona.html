Dioscorea daemona

Dioscorea daemona, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a climbing plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and delicate, fern-like leaves that give it a ghostly appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible tubers, which are sometimes used in traditional cuisines.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
