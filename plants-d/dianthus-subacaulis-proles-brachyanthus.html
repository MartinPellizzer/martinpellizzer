Dianthus subacaulis proles brachyanthus

Dianthus subacaulis proles brachyanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological habitats.
