Dendrocalamus ovatus

Dendrocalamus ovatus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Dendrocalamus ovatus is valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and traditional crafts.
