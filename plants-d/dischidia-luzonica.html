Dischidia luzonica

Dischidia luzonica, commonly known as the Luzon spider plant, is a climbing or trailing plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Ancistrocladaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, bead-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
