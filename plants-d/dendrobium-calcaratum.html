Dendrobium calcaratum

Dendrobium calcaratum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or clay soils and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Dendrobium calcaratum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
