Dalbergia teixeirae

Dalbergia teixeirae, commonly known as the Teixeira rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its rarity and demand, it is also considered a threatened species in its native habitat.
