Dianthus kapinaensis

Dianthus kapinaensis, commonly known as the Kapina carnation, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cooler, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits known as achenes.

Dianthus kapinaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
