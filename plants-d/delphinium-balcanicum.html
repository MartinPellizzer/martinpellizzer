Delphinium balcanicum

Delphinium balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are arranged in racemes, adding to its ornamental value.

Delphinium balcanicum is also noted for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
