Diospyros elliptifolia

Diospyros elliptifolia, commonly known as the elliptifoliate persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the shoots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are typically small, round, and initially green, maturing to a red or orange color.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
