Desmos praecox

Desmos praecox, commonly known as the early desmodium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Desmos praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
