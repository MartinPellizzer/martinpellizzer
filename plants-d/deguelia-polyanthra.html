Deguelia polyanthra

Deguelia polyanthra, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth surface with a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
