Disa tenuifolia

Disa tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas with well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Disa tenuifolia is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for various pollinators.
