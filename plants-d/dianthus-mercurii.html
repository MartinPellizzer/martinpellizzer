Dianthus mercurii

Dianthus mercurii, commonly known as Mercury's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Dianthus mercurii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
