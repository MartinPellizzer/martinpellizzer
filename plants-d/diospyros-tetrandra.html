Diospyros tetrandra

Diospyros tetrandra, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in crafts and construction.
