Draba rosularis var. leiocarpa

Draba rosularis var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth form with dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically oblanceolate, with a soft, silvery underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
