Dactyloctenium henrardianum

Dactyloctenium henrardianum, commonly known as the henrardian grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential forage for livestock.
