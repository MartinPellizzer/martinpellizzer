Diasperus montanus

Diasperus montanus, commonly known as the mountain diaspore, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features a compact shrub form with small, elliptical leaves and produces pea-like flowers in shades of white and purple. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
