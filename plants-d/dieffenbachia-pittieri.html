Dieffenbachia pittieri

Dieffenbachia pittieri, commonly known as the Leopard Plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with deep green veins and cream or white spots. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a spathe. Dieffenbachia pittieri typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
