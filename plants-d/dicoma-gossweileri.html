Dicoma gossweileri

Dicoma gossweileri, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dicoma gossweileri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its winter flowering.
