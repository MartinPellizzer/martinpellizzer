Deguelia arborescens

Deguelia arborescens, commonly known as the mountain ebony, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and it is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
