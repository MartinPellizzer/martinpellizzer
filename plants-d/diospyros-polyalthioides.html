Diospyros polyalthioides

Diospyros polyalthioides, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its fruit is a small, orange to reddish berry that ripens in the dry season.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
