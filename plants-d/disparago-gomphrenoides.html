Disparago gomphrenoides

Disparago gomphrenoides, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and grows in dense clusters near water sources. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

Disparago gomphrenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
