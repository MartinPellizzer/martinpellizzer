Dioscorea pumilio

Dioscorea pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial vine that is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, tuberous roots that are often used as a food source in some cultures. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 20 centimeters in diameter.

The flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters along the stems.
