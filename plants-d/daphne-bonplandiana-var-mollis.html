Daphne bonplandiana var. mollis

Daphne bonplandiana var. mollis, commonly known as the soft daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.

The variety mollis is distinguished by its softer, more velvety texture compared to other daphne species.
