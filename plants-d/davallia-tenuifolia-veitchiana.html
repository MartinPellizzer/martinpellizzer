Davallia tenuifolia veitchiana

Davallia tenuifolia veitchiana, commonly known as the Veitch's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with small, clustered fronds that are typically dark green and slightly curled. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental fern is valued for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
