Draba caroliniana f. andrenae

Draba caroliniana f. andrenae, commonly known as the Carolina rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically forms dense mats of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to its specific ecological niche.
