Diporidium zanguebaricum

Diporidium zanguebaricum, commonly known as the Zanzibar flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange floral bracts that resemble flames, which are highly visible and attract pollinators. The plant has a dense canopy with compound leaves that provide shade, making it suitable for ornamental and shade-giving purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
