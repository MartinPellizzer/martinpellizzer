Dalbergia boniana

Dalbergia boniana, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its density and fine grain. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental and timber value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture and forestry.
