Dendrophthora tenuifolia

Dendrophthora tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain toxic seeds.

Dendrophthora tenuifolia is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments.
