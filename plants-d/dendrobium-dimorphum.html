Dendrobium dimorphum

Dendrobium dimorphum, commonly known as the two-form orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal climates. Its flowers typically bloom in summer and are known for their vibrant colors, ranging from white to pink and yellow.

Dendrobium dimorphum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
