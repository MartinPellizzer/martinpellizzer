Disocactus heterodoxus

Disocactus heterodoxus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented stems that resemble leaves. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Disocactus heterodoxus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
