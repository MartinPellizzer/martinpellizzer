Delphinium hesperium subsp. hesperium

Delphinium hesperium subsp.

hesperium, commonly known as the western larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with several leaflets arranged around a central axis.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
