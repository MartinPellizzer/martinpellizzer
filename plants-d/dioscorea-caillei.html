Dioscorea caillei

Dioscorea caillei, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible tubers, which are a staple food in some regions. Dioscorea caillei is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its traditional medicinal uses.

Its growth habit and morphology make it well adapted to the humid, tropical environments where it thrives.
