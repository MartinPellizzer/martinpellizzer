Dianthus pyrenaicus subsp. pyrenaicus

Dianthus pyrenaicus subsp.

pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers with five petals, arranged in a showy inflorescence. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
