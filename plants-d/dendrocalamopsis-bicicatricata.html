Dendrocalamopsis bicicatricata

Dendrocalamopsis bicicatricata, commonly known as the double-scar bamboo, is a unique species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive double-scar nodes on its stems, which are formed by the fusion of two internodes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
