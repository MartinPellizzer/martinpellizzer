Dryopteris consanguinea

Dryopteris consanguinea, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that are covered in a dense, rusty-brown to cinnamon-colored fiddlehead-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

The cinnamon fern is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.
