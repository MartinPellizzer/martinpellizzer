Dianthus epirotus

Dianthus epirotus, commonly known as the Epirus carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Dianthus epirotus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
