Diospyros micrantha

Diospyros micrantha, commonly known as the small persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when ripe, often containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
