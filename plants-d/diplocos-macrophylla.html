Diplocos macrophylla

Diplocos macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf diplocos, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf structure and its role in local ecosystems.
