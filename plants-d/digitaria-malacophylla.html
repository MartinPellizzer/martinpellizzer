Digitaria malacophylla

Digitaria malacophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, pale-green spikelets that are clustered along the stem.

Digitaria malacophylla is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in poor conditions.
