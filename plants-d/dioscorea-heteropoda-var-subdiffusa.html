Dioscorea heteropoda var. subdiffusa

Dioscorea heteropoda var. subdiffusa, commonly known as the rough yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The tubers of this species are valued for their nutritional content and potential medicinal properties.
