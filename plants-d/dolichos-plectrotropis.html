Dolichos plectrotropis

Dolichos plectrotropis, commonly known as the climbing hyacinth bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, purple or violet flowers, followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Dolichos plectrotropis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds.
