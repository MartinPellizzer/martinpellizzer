Dactylorhiza fuchsii var. sooana

Dactylorhiza fuchsii var. sooana, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet, peaty or boggy habitats, preferring shaded environments. The plant has a slender, erect stem with several elongated, narrow leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish, often with a slight purple tinge, and are arranged in a loose spike.

The species is characterized by its unique morphology and is often found in association with other orchid species in moist, nutrient-poor soils.
