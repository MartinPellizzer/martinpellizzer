Delphinium gypsophilum subsp. parviflorum

Delphinium gypsophilum subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a notable species in alpine flora.
