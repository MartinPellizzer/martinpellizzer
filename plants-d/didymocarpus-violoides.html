Didymocarpus violoides

Didymocarpus violoides, commonly known as the violet-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive violet hue on the underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. It blooms during the summer months and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
