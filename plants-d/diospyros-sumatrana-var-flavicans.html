Diospyros sumatrana var. flavicans

Diospyros sumatrana var. flavicans, commonly known as the yellow persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish-orange fruits. The fruits are typically edible, though they may have astringent qualities when unripe.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
