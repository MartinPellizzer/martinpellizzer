Delphinium grandiflorum var. gmelinii

Delphinium grandiflorum var. gmelinii, commonly known as the Siberian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically spurred at the base. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
