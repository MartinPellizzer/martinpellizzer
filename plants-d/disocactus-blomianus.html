Disocactus blomianus

Disocactus blomianus, commonly known as the Blomianus cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and smooth, greenish-gray stem that often has a faintly ribbed texture. The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
