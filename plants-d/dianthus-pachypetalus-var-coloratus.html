Dianthus pachypetalus var. coloratus

Dianthus pachypetalus var. coloratus, commonly known as the showy pinks, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white or pale pink center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
