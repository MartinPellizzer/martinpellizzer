Dianthus ferrugineus subsp. liburnicus

Dianthus ferrugineus subsp.

liburnicus, commonly known as the Balkan campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems and bright red or pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
