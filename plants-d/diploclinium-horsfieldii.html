Diploclinium horsfieldii

Diploclinium horsfieldii, commonly known as the Horsfield's climbing bignonia, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warm season. Its stems are often hairy and can climb by means of tendrils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and vibrant floral display.
