Daucus gingidium

Daucus gingidium, commonly known as the Persian carrot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features finely divided leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels. Its roots are typically small and not as commonly cultivated as other carrots.
