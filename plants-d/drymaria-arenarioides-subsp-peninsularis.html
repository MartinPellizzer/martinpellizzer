Drymaria arenarioides subsp. peninsularis

Drymaria arenarioides subsp.

peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsula sandplant, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fleshy, greenish-gray leaves that are adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an attractive and hardy addition to Mediterranean-style landscapes.
