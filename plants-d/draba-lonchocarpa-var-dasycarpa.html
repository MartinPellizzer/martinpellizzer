Draba lonchocarpa var. dasycarpa

Draba lonchocarpa var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy clubbed whitlow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine and subalpine regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features basal rosettes of hairy, linear leaves and produces yellow or greenish flowers in early spring. Its fruits are distinctive, being elongated and often covered with a dense layer of hairs.

This variety is adapted to harsh, rocky environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems.
