Dianthus silenoides

Dianthus silenoides, commonly known as the silvery campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are pink to red in color, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Dianthus silenoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and meadows.
