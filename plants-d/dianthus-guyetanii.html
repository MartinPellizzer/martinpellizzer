Dianthus guyetanii

Dianthus guyetanii, commonly known as the Guyetan carnation, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus guyetanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
