Dianthus catalaunicus var. brachyphyllus

Dianthus catalaunicus var. brachyphyllus, commonly known as the short-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red, showy flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
