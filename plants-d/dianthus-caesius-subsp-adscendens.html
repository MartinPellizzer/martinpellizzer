Dianthus caesius subsp. adscendens

Dianthus caesius subsp.

adscendens, commonly known as the ascending carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink and red.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in rocky or calcareous soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
