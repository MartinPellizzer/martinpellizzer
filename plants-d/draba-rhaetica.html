Draba rhaetica

Draba rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard plants. This species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous environments.

Draba rhaetica is adapted to cold climates and is typically found in meadows and slopes above the tree line.
