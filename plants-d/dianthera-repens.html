Dianthera repens

Dianthera repens, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Dianthera repens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
