Delphinium patens subsp. patens

Delphinium patens subsp.

patens, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the flowers are arranged in dense spikes. Delphinium patens subsp.

patens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
