Dioscorea putisensis

Dioscorea putisensis, commonly known as the Putisensi yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend support structures. The plant produces oval-shaped tubers that are often used as a food source in local communities. Its botanical features include compound leaves with palmate venation and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The tubers are typically harvested after a growing season of several months, making it an important crop in certain agricultural systems.
