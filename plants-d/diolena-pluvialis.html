Diolena pluvialis

Diolena pluvialis, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its simple, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant is known for its ability to float on the water's surface, supported by air-filled cavities in its stem. It reproduces through seeds and can also propagate vegetatively by fragmentation.

Diolena pluvialis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
