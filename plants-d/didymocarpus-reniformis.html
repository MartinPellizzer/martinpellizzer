Didymocarpus reniformis

Didymocarpus reniformis, commonly known as the kidney bean tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bean-like pods. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season. Its fruit contains edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional medicinal uses.
