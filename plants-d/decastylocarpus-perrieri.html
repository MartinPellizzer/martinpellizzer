Decastylocarpus perrieri

Decastylocarpus perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's starfruit, is a unique member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces multiple small, fleshy fruits clustered together on the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

This plant is native to specific regions of Southeast Asia and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
