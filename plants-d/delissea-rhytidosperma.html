Delissea rhytidosperma

Delissea rhytidosperma, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, silver-green foliage that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Delissea rhytidosperma is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
