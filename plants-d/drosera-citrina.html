Drosera citrina

Drosera citrina, commonly known as the yellow sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, sticky secretions on its leaves, which trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are covered in glandular hairs that secrete digestive enzymes.

Drosera citrina is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
