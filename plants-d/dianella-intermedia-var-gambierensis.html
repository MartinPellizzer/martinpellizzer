Dianella intermedia var. gambierensis

Dianella intermedia var. gambierensis, commonly known as the Gambier sedge, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, growing in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear white or pale blue flowers, which appear in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
