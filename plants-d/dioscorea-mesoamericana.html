Dioscorea mesoamericana

Dioscorea mesoamericana, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a perennial vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are edible and have been used traditionally in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, humid environments.
