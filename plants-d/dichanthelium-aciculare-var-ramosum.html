Dichanthelium aciculare var. ramosum

Dichanthelium aciculare var. ramosum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle of spikelets, which are small and often tinged with purple.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
