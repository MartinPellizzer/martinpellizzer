Dalea macrostachya

Dalea macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.

Dalea macrostachya is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a habitat for various wildlife species.
