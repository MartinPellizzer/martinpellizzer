Dovyalis salicifolia

Dovyalis salicifolia, commonly known as the sour plum or wild mango, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, lanceolate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to oval, sour-tasting berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

Dovyalis salicifolia is valued for its edible fruits and has traditional uses in local medicine.
