Dryopteris Longifolia

Long-leaf fern, scientifically known as Dryopteris longifolia, is a striking member of the fern family that thrives in moist, shaded environments. With its elongated, lance-shaped fronds, this plant adds a dramatic touch to woodland gardens and natural landscapes. Native to regions across Asia, it is often found in forest understories where it benefits from the dappled sunlight and rich soil. Its delicate, feathery appearance makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological purposes. Despite its beauty, the Long-leaf fern requires careful attention to moisture and light conditions to flourish.
