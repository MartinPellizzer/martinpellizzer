Dianthus cintranus subsp. charidemi

Dianthus cintranus subsp.

charidemi, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are upright with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
