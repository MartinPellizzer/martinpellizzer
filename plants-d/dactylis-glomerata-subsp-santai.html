Dactylis glomerata subsp. santai

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

santai, commonly known as the Santai grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
