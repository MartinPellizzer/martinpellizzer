Dioclea virgata var. crenata

Dioclea virgata var. crenata, commonly known as the serrated dioclea, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred petal.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, often used in traditional medicine.
