Draba thylacocarpa

Draba thylacocarpa, commonly known as the tiger's claw draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in dense, woolly hairs.

Draba thylacocarpa is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
