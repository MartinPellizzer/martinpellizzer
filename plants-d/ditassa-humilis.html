Ditassa humilis

Ditassa humilis, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
