Dendrobium wallichianum

Dendrobium wallichianum, commonly known as the Wallich's orchid, is a perennial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a tall, branched stem that often produces numerous flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a greenish lip, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Dendrobium wallichianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
