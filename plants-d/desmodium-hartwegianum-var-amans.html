Desmodium hartwegianum var. amans

Desmodium hartwegianum var. amans, commonly known as the hairy desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
