Dipteracanthus rubiiformis

Dipteracanthus rubiiformis, commonly known as the red-leaf dipteracanthus, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has a unique root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an important species in its ecosystem. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
