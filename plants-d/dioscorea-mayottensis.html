Dioscorea mayottensis

Dioscorea mayottensis, commonly known as Mayotte yam, is a climbing vine native to the Comoros Islands.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine due to its bioactive compounds.
