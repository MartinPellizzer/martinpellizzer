Dendrobium speciosum var. pedunculatum

Dendrobium speciosum var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a thick, fleshy rhizome that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive pouch-like lip, which attracts pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
