Daphne gnidium

Daphne gnidium, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky or dry slopes.

Daphne gnidium is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid climates.
