Dissotis linearis

Dissotis linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
