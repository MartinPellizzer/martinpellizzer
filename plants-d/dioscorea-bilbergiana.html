Dioscorea bilbergiana

Dioscorea bilbergiana, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as food in some regions. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

The tubers are an important traditional food source and have also been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
