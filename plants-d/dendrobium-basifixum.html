Dendrobium basifixum

Dendrobium basifixum, commonly known as the black root dendrobium, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black roots and its ability to grow in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
