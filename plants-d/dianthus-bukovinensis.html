Dianthus bukovinensis

Dianthus bukovinensis, commonly known as the Bukovina carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is found in mountainous areas. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dianthus bukovinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
