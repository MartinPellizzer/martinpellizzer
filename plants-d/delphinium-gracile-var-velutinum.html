Delphinium gracile var. velutinum

Delphinium gracile var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvet larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and meadows.
