Dianthus cruentus

Dianthus cruentus, commonly known as bloody cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers consist of five petals that are often tinged with purple or crimson, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Dianthus cruentus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
