Dalbergia neoperrieri

Dalbergia neoperrieri, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its dense, dark wood and intricate grain patterns. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several flattened, dark seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and valuable timber, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
