Dracocephalum fruticulosum

Dracocephalum fruticulosum, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
