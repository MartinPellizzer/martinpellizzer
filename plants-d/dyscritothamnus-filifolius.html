Dyscritothamnus filifolius

Dyscritothamnus filifolius, commonly known as the silky feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.

Dyscritothamnus filifolius is often used in coastal restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.
