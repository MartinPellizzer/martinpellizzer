Dolichos gracilis

Dolichos gracilis, commonly known as slender cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its thin, trailing stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
