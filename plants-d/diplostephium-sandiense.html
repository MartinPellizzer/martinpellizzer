Diplostephium sandiense

Diplostephium sandiense, commonly known as the sandhill milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is a common feature in the milkweed family. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Diplostephium sandiense thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.
