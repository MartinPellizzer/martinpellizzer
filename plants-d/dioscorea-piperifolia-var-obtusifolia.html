Dioscorea piperifolia var. obtusifolia

Dioscorea piperifolia var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved yam, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is known for its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend along supports, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The variety obtusifolia is distinguished by its rounded leaf lobes, setting it apart from other varieties of the species.
