Diospyros austroafricana subsp. microphylla

Diospyros austroafricana subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved ebony, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae. It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in countries like South Africa, Namibia, and Botswana.

The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from yellow to reddish when mature.

This subspecies is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of dye.
