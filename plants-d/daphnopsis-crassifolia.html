Daphnopsis crassifolia

Daphnopsis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved daphnopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually clustered in inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is characterized by a low, spreading form, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded environments.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role within its native habitat.
