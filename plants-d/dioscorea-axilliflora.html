Dioscorea axilliflora

Dioscorea axilliflora, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its tubers are starchy and can be processed into a flour-like substance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.
