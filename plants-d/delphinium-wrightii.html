Delphinium wrightii

Delphinium wrightii, commonly known as Wright's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided, fern-like lobes that are often hairy.

Delphinium wrightii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.
