Dimeiostemon vaginatus

Dimeiostemon vaginatus, commonly known as the slender-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves that form a protective layer around the stem. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that are bell-shaped and often have a distinctive white or yellow throat. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
