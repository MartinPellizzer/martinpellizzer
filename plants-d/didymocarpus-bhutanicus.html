Didymocarpus bhutanicus

Didymocarpus bhutanicus, commonly known as the Bhutanese flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly Bhutan.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its mountainous environment.
