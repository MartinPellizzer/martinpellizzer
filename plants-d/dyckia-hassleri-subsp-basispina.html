Dyckia hassleri subsp. basispina

Dyckia hassleri subsp.

basispina, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow and sword-like, with a distinctive grayish-green coloration.

The plant produces striking orange to red flowers that bloom in summer, emerging from the center of the rosette. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
