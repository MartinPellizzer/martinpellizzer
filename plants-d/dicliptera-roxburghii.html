Dicliptera roxburghii

Dicliptera roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot or arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
