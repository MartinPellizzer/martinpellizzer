Daphne occidentalis

Daphne occidentalis, commonly known as Western daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne in clusters. The plant has glossy, ovate leaves that are dark green and provide a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens despite its toxic properties.
