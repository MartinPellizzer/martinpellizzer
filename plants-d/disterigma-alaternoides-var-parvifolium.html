Disterigma alaternoides var. parvifolium

Disterigma alaternoides var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf disterigma, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
