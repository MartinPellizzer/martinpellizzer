Desmodium suembuense

Desmodium suembuense, commonly known as the East African hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in grasslands and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

The seed pods are elongated and dehiscent, releasing seeds when mature.
