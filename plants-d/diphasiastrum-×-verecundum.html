Diphasiastrum × verecundum

Diphasiastrum × verecundum, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Diphasiastrum complanatum and Diphasiastrum sphondylium, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.
