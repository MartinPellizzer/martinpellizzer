Delphinium pentagynum f. gautieri

Delphinium pentagynum f. gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's larkspur, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
