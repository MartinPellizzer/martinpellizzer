Dodonaea peduncularis var. coriacea

Dodonaea peduncularis var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved Dodonaea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough, leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
