Dioscorea ulugurensis

Dioscorea ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers that are an important food source in local communities. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and borne in clusters.

Dioscorea ulugurensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is a subject of botanical research for its genetic diversity and adaptability.
