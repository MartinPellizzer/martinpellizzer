Dalbergia medicinalis

Dalbergia medicinalis, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy hardwood and distinctive bark that exudes a reddish hue. The plant has small, white or pale yellow flowers and produces legume pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its unique properties, it has been used traditionally in various medicinal applications.
