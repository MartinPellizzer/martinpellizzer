Dianthus attenuatus subsp. pyrenaicus

Dianthus attenuatus subsp.

pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean campion, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It is a low-growing perennial that produces pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is adapted to rocky and alpine environments. It is often found in open, sunny habitats at high altitudes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated flower stems compared to other Dianthus species.
