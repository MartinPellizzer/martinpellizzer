Daphne micrantha subsp. paniculata

Daphne micrantha subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as the clustered spurge laurel, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in spring. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
