Dendrobium williamsianum

Dendrobium williamsianum, commonly known as the Williams' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing white or pale yellow petals with a contrasting lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers shaded, moist environments.

Dendrobium williamsianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
