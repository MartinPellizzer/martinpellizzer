Didymocarpus mollifolius

Didymocarpus mollifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly mallow, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically yellow or pink with five petals. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain small, round seeds. It is native to regions in Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
