Durio spontaneus

Durio spontaneus, commonly known as the wild durian, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, spiky fruit with a strong aroma. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a unique flavor profile.
