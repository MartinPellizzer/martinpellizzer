Deschampsia nitida

Deschampsia nitida, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Deschampsia nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
