Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana nothosubsp. templinensis

Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana nothosubsp.

templinensis, commonly known as the Temple's yellow marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its yellowish-green to yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, preferring moist, calcareous soils. It has a pseudobulbous structure and linear leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a raceme.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations.
