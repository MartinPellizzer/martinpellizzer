Dioscorea quartiniana var. subpedata

Dioscorea quartiniana var. subpedata, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are used for their nutritional value and have been traditionally used in various medicinal practices.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to different soil types and its role in local agriculture.
