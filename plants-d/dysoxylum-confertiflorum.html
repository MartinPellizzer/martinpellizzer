Dysoxylum confertiflorum

Dysoxylum confertiflorum, commonly known as the thick-flowered dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and large, simple leaves that are alternate and elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
