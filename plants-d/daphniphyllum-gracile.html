Daphniphyllum gracile

Daphniphyllum gracile, commonly known as the silver leaf holly, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Daphniphyllum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
