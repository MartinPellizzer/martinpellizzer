Diasperus beckleri

Diasperus beckleri, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Diasperus beckleri is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
