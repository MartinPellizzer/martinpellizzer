Dianthus deltoides proles nuriae

Dianthus deltoides proles nuriae, commonly known as the Norway carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.

The flowers have a pleasant, spicy scent and are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
