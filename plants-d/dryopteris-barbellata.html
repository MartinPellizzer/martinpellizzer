Dryopteris barbellata

Dryopteris barbellata, commonly known as the velvet shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that have a velvety texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically divided into lance-shaped segments with a prominent midrib. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It reproduces via spores and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
