Dysoxylum pachyphyllum

Dysoxylum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved sandarac, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used historically for various practical and medicinal purposes.
