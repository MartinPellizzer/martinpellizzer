Dissotis pygmaea

Dissotis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in a spike. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant's rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.
