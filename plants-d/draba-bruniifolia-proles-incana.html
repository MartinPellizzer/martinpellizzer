Draba bruniifolia proles incana

Draba bruniifolia proles incana, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the European Alps.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in harsh, cold environments. It has basal leaves that are hairy and often covered in a white wax layer, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is noted for its resilience to extreme conditions, making it a common sight in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.
