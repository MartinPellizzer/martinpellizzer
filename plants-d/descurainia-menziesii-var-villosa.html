Descurainia menziesii var. villosa

Descurainia menziesii var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
