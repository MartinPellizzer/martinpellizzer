Dinochloa diffusa

Dinochloa diffusa, commonly known as the common marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in temperate regions. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil.

Dinochloa diffusa plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
