Diospyros gonoclada

Diospyros gonoclada, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
