Dioscorea crispata

Dioscorea crispata, commonly known as the curly yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, yellowish fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin, and the stems are often covered in stiff, curly hairs.

The tubers of Dioscorea crispata are sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
