Delphinium jacobsii

Delphinium jacobsii, commonly known as the Jacob's larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, typical of the Delphinium genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
