Diospyros galpinii

Diospyros galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, ovate leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in greenish-white clusters, and it produces small, edible berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
