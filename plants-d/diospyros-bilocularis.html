Diospyros bilocularis

Diospyros bilocularis, commonly known as the two-chambered ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known ebony species. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow when mature.
