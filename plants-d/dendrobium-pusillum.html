Dendrobium pusillum

Dendrobium pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf orchid or miniature orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with short, fleshy stems that often appear in clusters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to absorb moisture from the air.

Dendrobium pusillum is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
