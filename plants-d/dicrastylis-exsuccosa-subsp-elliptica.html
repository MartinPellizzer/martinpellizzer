Dicrastylis exsuccosa subsp. elliptica

Dicrastylis exsuccosa subsp.

elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia. It typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, often forming dense tussocks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow or greenish flowers. Its botanical features are adapted to waterlogged soils, with rhizomes that help in nutrient absorption and stabilization.

It plays an important ecological role in maintaining wetland ecosystems.
