Dalbergia papuana

Dalbergia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Indonesia and Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its straight, dark-colored wood with a fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
