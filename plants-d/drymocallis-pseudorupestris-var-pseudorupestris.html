Drymocallis pseudorupestris var. pseudorupestris

Drymocallis pseudorupestris var. pseudorupestris, commonly known as the rock goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in woodland or open areas. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
