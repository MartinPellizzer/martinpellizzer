Disporum trachycarpum

Disporum trachycarpum, commonly known as the roughfruit disporum, is a member of the Liliaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States, particularly found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
