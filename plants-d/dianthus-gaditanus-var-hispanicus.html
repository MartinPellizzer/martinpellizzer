Dianthus gaditanus var. hispanicus

Dianthus gaditanus var. hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in Mediterranean climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
