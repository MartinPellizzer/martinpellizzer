Disa ochrostachya var. major

Disa ochrostachya var. major, commonly known as the white-flowered disa, is a South African orchid species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
