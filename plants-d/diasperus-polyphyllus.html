Diasperus polyphyllus

Diasperus polyphyllus, commonly known as the many-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its numerous, small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces a slender, branching stem that bears the flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Diasperus polyphyllus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
