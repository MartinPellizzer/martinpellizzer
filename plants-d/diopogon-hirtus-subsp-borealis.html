Diopogon hirtus subsp. borealis

Diopogon hirtus subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to arctic and alpine regions of North America. It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted form and dense, hairy leaves that help it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude and polar ecosystems.
