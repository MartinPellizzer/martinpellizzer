Dianella longifolia var. aspera

Dianella longifolia var. aspera, commonly known as the rough-leaved flax lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in summer, which are followed by blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a favored plant in horticultural settings.
