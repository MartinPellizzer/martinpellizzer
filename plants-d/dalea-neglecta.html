Dalea neglecta

Dalea neglecta, commonly known as the plains false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and produces small, bean-like seed pods.

Dalea neglecta is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
