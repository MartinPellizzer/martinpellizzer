Draba longirostra subsp. turgida

Draba longirostra subsp.

turgida, commonly known as the long-beaked whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in racemes. It thrives in alpine and rocky habitats, often found in mountainous regions of Europe.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
