Draba verna var. campestris

Draba verna var. campestris, commonly known as the spring whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, typically from March to May, depending on the climate. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and fields.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for early pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.
