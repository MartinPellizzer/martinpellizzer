Dactylorchis maculata subsp. ericetorum

Dactylorchis maculata subsp.

ericetorum, commonly known as the Ericaceous Dactylorchis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and often have a slight purple or brownish tinge.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to thrive in acidic soils.
