Digitaria corymbosa subsp. roxburghiana

Digitaria corymbosa subsp.

roxburghiana, commonly known as Roxburgh's Digitaria, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
