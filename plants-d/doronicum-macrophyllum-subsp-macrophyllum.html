Doronicum macrophyllum subsp. macrophyllum

Doronicum macrophyllum subsp.

macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with distinctive black markings.

The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
