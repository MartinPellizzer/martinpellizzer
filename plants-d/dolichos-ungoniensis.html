Dolichos ungoniensis

Dolichos ungoniensis, commonly known as the African horsebean, is a leguminous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Dolichos ungoniensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
