Dontostemon crassifolius

Dontostemon crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees.

It possesses broad, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are usually red or yellow and serve as a food source for birds and other animals.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
