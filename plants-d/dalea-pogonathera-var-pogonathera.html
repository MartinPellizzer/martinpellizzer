Dalea pogonathera var. pogonathera

Dalea pogonathera var. pogonathera, commonly known as the purple prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet to purple flower heads. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites, and is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils.
