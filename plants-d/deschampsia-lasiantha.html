Deschampsia lasiantha

Deschampsia lasiantha, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silvery-blue in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in panicles, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deschampsia lasiantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
