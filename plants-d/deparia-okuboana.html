Deparia okuboana

Deparia okuboana, commonly known as the Okubo's shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive pattern of sori on the undersides.
