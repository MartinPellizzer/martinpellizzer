Demorchis takeshimensis

Demorchis takeshimensis, commonly known as the Takeshimensis orchid, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers with a complex structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, adapted to maximize light absorption in low-light conditions.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
