Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. schurii

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

schurii, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands.

The plant features a short, stocky rhizome and produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive yellow or greenish marking on the lip.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
