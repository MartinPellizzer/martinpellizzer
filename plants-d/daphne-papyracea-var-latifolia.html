Daphne papyracea var. latifolia

Daphne papyracea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
