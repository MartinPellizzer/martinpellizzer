Dianthus gracilis var. armerioides

Dianthus gracilis var. armerioides, commonly known as the slender campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or white flowers with a distinctive, slightly fragrant scent. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
