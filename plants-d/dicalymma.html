Dicalymma

Dicalymma, commonly known as the "false bluebells," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species native to South America, particularly Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay. The plants are characterized by their delicate, often blue or purple flowers that resemble those of true bluebells. Dicalymma species typically grow as herbaceous perennials, with trifoliate leaves and pods that contain seeds.

These plants are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their aesthetic value.
