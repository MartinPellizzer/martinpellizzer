Diospyros beccarii

Diospyros beccarii, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and tool-making.
