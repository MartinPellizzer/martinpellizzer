Delphinium pulchrum

Delphinium pulchrum, commonly known as the beautiful larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are arranged in racemes, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Delphinium pulchrum is also known for its toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
