Daucus hispanicus var. halophilus

Daucus hispanicus var. halophilus, commonly known as the salt marsh carrot, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It thrives in saline environments, showcasing its adaptation to high salt concentrations in the soil. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a bluish or grayish hue due to a waxy coating.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
