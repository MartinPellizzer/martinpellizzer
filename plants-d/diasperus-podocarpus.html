Diasperus podocarpus

Diasperus podocarpus, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, woody cones. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and pests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
