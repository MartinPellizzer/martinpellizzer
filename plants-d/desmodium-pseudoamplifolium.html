Desmodium pseudoamplifolium

Desmodium pseudoamplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, clustered pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments.

Desmodium pseudoamplifolium is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
