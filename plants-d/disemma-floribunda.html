Disemma floribunda

Disemma floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it grows in clusters along the stems. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
