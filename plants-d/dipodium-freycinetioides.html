Dipodium freycinetioides

Dipodium freycinetioides, commonly known as the Freycinet's dipodium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features a short rhizome with a single or few pseudobulbs, supporting its foliage and inflorescence. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a complex structure typical of orchids, and can exhibit a range of colors including white, yellow, and green.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
