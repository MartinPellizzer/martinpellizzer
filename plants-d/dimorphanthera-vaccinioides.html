Dimorphanthera vaccinioides

Dimorphanthera vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats. Its botanical features include dimorphic leaves, with some leaves being leathery and others more succulent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
