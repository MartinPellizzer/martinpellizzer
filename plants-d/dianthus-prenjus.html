Dianthus prenjus

Dianthus prenjus, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Dianthus prenjus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate blooms.
