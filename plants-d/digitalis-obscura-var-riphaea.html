Digitalis obscura var. riphaea

Digitalis obscura var. riphaea, commonly known as the Alpine foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear tubular flowers. The flowers are typically purple or pink, with a distinctive two-lipped structure, and bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.
