Draba magellanica

Draba magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan's whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs.

Draba magellanica is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.
