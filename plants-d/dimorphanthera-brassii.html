Dimorphanthera brassii

Dimorphanthera brassii, commonly known as the Western Australian waratah, is a striking shrub native to the southwestern region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal and heathland environments, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
