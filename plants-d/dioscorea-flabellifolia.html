Dioscorea flabellifolia

Dioscorea flabellifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellowish or orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is known for its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
