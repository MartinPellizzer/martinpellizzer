Digitalis mariana subsp. mariana

Digitalis mariana subsp.

mariana, commonly known as the Mexican foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces helmet-shaped flowers with spurred petals, a typical feature of the Digitalis genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
