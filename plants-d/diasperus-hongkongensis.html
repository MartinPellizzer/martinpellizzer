Diasperus hongkongensis

Diasperus hongkongensis, commonly known as Hong Kong rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Hong Kong and southern China.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
