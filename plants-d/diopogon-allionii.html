Diopogon allionii

Diopogon allionii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Diopogon allionii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
