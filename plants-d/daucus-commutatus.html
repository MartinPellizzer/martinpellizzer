Daucus commutatus

Daucus commutatus, commonly known as the common carrot, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and a long, taproot that is the primary edible part. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, often found in temperate regions.

Its cultivation has a long history, dating back thousands of years, and it is widely grown for its nutritious and flavorful root.
