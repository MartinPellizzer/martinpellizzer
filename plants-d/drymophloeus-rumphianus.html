Drymophloeus rumphianus

Drymophloeus rumphianus, commonly known as the Java ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its hard, dense wood and thick, waxy leaves. The tree is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in local communities.
