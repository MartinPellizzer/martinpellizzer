Delphinium decorum subsp. decorum

Delphinium decorum subsp.

decorum, commonly known as the garden delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features tall, spindly stems that bear dense clusters of blue to violet spurred flowers, which are characteristic of the Delphinium genus.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its striking floral display and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
