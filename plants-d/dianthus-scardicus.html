Dianthus scardicus

Dianthus scardicus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its pink or white flowers, which bloom in clusters at the top of slender stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Dianthus scardicus is a hardy species that is adapted to harsh climatic conditions.
