Drypetes polyneura

Drypetes polyneura, commonly known as the white-flowered drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by yellow, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
