Dentaria ochroleuca

Dentaria ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellowroot sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Dentaria ochroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
