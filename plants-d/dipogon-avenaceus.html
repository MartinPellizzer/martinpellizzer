Dipogon avenaceus

Dipogon avenaceus, commonly known as the slender grass or slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
