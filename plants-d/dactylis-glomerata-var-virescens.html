Dactylis glomerata var. virescens

Dactylis glomerata var. virescens, commonly known as the green oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in landscaping and erosion control.
