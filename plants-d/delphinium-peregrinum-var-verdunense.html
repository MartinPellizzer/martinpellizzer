Delphinium peregrinum var. verdunense

Delphinium peregrinum var. verdunense, commonly known as the Verdun larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
