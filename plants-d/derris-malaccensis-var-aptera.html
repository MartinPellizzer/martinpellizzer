Derris malaccensis var. aptera

Derris malaccensis var. aptera, commonly known as the smooth derris, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its alkaloid content.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other varieties of Derris malaccensis.
