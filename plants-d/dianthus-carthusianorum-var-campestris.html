Dianthus carthusianorum var. campestris

Dianthus carthusianorum var. campestris, commonly known as the Campestris carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink or white petals that resemble small carnations. The plant typically produces flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, with prominent central stamens that add to its ornamental appeal. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
