Draba pamplonensis

Draba pamplonensis, commonly known as the Pamplona whitlow, is a small flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with characteristic four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
