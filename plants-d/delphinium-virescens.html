Delphinium virescens

Delphinium virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Delphinium virescens is adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
