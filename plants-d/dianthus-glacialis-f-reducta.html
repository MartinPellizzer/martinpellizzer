Dianthus glacialis f. reducta

Dianthus glacialis f. reducta, commonly known as the alpine carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features compact, cushion-like foliage that is densely packed and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
