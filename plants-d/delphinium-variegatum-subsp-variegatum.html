Delphinium variegatum subsp. variegatum

Delphinium variegatum subsp.

variegatum, commonly known as the variegated larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive variegation pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and alpine landscapes.
