Dianthus velutinus

Dianthus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or red flowers. The plant features hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. Dianthus velutinus is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural environment.
