Desmodium subsecundum

Desmodium subsecundum, commonly known as the creeping desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in a prostrate or creeping habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice in landscaping and restoration projects.
