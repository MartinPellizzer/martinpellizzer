Dioscorea pentaphylla var. malaica

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. malaica, commonly known as the Malaica yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various regional cuisines.
