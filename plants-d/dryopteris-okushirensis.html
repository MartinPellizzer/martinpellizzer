Dryopteris okushirensis

Dryopteris okushirensis, commonly known as the Okushiri shield fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its sori are round and closely spaced, typically located on the lower part of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
