Dioscorea floribunda

Dioscorea floribunda, commonly known as the flowering yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces clusters of small, white or greenish-yellow flowers, which are often borne in terminal spikes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and woody.

The tubers of D. floribunda are not typically cultivated for food but are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
