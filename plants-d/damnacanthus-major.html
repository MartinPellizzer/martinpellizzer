Damnacanthus major

Damnacanthus major, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to support local wildlife and promote biodiversity.
