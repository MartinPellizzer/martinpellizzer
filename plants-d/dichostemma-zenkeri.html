Dichostemma zenkeri

Dichostemma zenkeri, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Dichostemma zenkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
