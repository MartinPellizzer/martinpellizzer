Draba henrici

Draba henrici, commonly known as the Henry's rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant features basal leaves that are rosette-shaped and are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-white, and arranged in a single cluster at the top of the stem.

Draba henrici is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
