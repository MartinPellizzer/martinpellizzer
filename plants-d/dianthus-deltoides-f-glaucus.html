Dianthus deltoides f. glaucus

Dianthus deltoides f. glaucus, commonly known as the glaucous campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers and fuzzy, gray-green foliage.

It is a compact, low-growing perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to southern Europe and western Asia. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs that give it a silvery appearance, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
