Draba rosulata

Draba rosulata, commonly known as the rosette saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of basal leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in harsh conditions.

Draba rosulata is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
