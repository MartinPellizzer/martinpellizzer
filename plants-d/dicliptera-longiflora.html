Dicliptera longiflora

Dicliptera longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered dicliptera, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to yellowish flowers, which are arranged in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Dicliptera longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its elegant appearance.
