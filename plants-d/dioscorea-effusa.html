Dioscorea effusa

Dioscorea effusa, commonly known as the slender yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its tubers are often cultivated for their nutritional value, containing starch and various bioactive compounds.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
