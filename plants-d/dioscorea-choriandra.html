Dioscorea choriandra

Dioscorea choriandra, commonly known as the purple yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, white or green flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its tubers are often used in traditional cuisine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
