Diasperus virens

Diasperus virens, commonly known as the green-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Diasperus virens is typically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
