Dioscorea compacta

Dioscorea compacta, commonly known as the dwarf yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has slender, hairy stems that twine around supporting structures, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its medicinal properties and adaptability, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for both agricultural and therapeutic purposes.
