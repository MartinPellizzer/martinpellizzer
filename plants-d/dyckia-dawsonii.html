Dyckia dawsonii

Dyckia dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Dyckia dawsonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
