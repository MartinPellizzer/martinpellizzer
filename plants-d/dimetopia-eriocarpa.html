Dimetopia eriocarpa

Dimetopia eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly dimetopia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
