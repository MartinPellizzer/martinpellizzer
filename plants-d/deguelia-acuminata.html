Deguelia acuminata

Deguelia acuminata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Deguelia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for shade in tropical landscapes.
