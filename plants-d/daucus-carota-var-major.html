Daucus carota var. major

Daucus carota var. major, commonly known as the giant parsnip, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant can grow up to 2 meters tall, with a thick, swollen root that is typically white or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The leaves and roots are edible but can be toxic if not prepared properly.
