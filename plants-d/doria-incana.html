Doria incana

Doria incana, commonly known as the white doria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and relatively rare species within its native habitat.
