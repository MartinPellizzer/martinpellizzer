Dianthus poiretianus

Dianthus poiretianus, commonly known as the pink rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Dianthus poiretianus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
