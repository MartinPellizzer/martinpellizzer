Dioscorea persimilis var. wukangensis

Dioscorea persimilis var. wukangensis, commonly known as the Wukang yam, is a variant of the Chinese yam species native to southern China.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is known for its starchy, edible tubers that have been used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its growth habit involves climbing stems with palmate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The tubers of this variety are valued for their nutritional content and are often cultivated for both food and traditional remedies.
