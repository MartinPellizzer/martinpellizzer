Dioscorea cirrhosa

Dioscorea cirrhosa, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The tubers of Dioscorea cirrhosa are valued for their nutritional content and potential medicinal properties.
