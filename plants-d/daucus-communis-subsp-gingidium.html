Daucus communis subsp. gingidium

Daucus communis subsp.

gingidium, commonly known as the Indian cowpea or wild carrot, is a member of the Apiaceae family. It is a biennial or annual herb characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and umbel-like inflorescences.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that give way to round, orange to reddish-brown fruits. It is native to regions of Asia, Africa, and the Middle East, and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
