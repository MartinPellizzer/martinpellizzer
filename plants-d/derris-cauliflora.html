Derris cauliflora

Derris cauliflora, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems, a feature known as cauliflory. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, fibrous coating. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with three to five leaflets that are often hairy.

Derris cauliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures for its medicinal properties.
