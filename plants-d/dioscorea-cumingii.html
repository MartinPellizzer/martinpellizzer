Dioscorea cumingii

Dioscorea cumingii, commonly known as Chinese yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal properties.

Dioscorea cumingii is often cultivated for its high starch content and adaptability to various soil conditions.
