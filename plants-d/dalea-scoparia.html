Dalea scoparia

Dalea scoparia, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish-purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Dalea scoparia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
