Dalbergia densa

Dalbergia densa, commonly known as the dense legume or African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruits are legumes that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
