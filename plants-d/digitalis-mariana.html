Digitalis mariana

Digitalis mariana, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its showy, ornamental appearance. Digitalis mariana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
