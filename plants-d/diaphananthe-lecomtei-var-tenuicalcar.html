Diaphananthe lecomtei var. tenuicalcar

Diaphananthe lecomtei var. tenuicalcar, commonly known as the slender-spurred diaphananthe, is a species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, elongated stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blooms with a distinctive spurred structure, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
