Dipterocarpus punctulatus

Dipterocarpus punctulatus, commonly known as the punctulate dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

This species plays an important ecological role in rainforest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
