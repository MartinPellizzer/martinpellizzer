Drosera platypoda

Drosera platypoda, commonly known as the broad-leaf sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, sticky leaves that secrete a mucilage to trap insects. The plant's leaves are typically green with red pigmentation, and they curl around captured prey to aid in digestion. It thrives in wet, acidic environments and is often found in heathlands and swampy areas.

Drosera platypoda is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
