Dypsis ramentacea

Dypsis ramentacea, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its short, thick trunk and dense, feathery foliage. The plant features a rosette of dark green, leathery leaves that give it a unique appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, coconut-like fruits.

Dypsis ramentacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
