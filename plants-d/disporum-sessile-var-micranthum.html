Disporum sessile var. micranthum

Disporum sessile var. micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered disporum, is a variant of the disporum plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, providing a delicate texture to the plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
