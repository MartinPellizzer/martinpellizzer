Dianthus marianii

Dianthus marianii, commonly known as the Marian's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composed of five petals, with the central part forming a distinctive frilled appearance.

Dianthus marianii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
