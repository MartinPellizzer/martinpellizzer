Deschampsia cespitosa var. exaristata

Deschampsia cespitosa var. exaristata, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and forms dense, tufted clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are slightly bent at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
