Diplacus aurantiacus var. lompocensis

Diplacus aurantiacus var. lompocensis, commonly known as the Lompoc monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal bluffs and dunes. It has slender, trailing stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.
