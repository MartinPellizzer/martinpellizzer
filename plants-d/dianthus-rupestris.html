Dianthus rupestris

Dianthus rupestris, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
