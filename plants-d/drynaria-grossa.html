Drynaria grossa

Drynaria grossa, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Drynariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Australia, and the Pacific Islands. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, supporting the plant's growth and storing nutrients.

Drynaria grossa is often found in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests and along stream banks.
