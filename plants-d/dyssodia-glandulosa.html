Dyssodia glandulosa

Dyssodia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, often with glandular hairs on its stems and leaves. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, sticky secretions.

Dyssodia glandulosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
