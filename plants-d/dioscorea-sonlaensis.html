Dioscorea sonlaensis

Dioscorea sonlaensis, commonly known as Son La yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears small, greenish-white flowers. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their medicinal and nutritional properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
