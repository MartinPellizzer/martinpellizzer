Dasiphora reptans

Dasiphora reptans, commonly known as the creeping goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and it produces showy yellow flowers in the summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
