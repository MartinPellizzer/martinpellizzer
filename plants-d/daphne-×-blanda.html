Daphne × blanda

Daphne × blanda, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, evergreen foliage that remains vibrant throughout the winter. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value year-round.

Daphne × blanda prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.
