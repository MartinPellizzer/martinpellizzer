Dalechampia sidifolia

Dalechampia sidifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf dalechampia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and native plant collections.
