Dicliptera paniculata var. subaequibracteata

Dicliptera paniculata var. subaequibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bract muhly, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with bracts that are nearly equal in length to the flowers, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in wetland or marshy habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
