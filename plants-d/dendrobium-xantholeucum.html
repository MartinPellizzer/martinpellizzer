Dendrobium xantholeucum

Dendrobium xantholeucum, commonly known as the yellow-leaf dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with long, cane-like stems that produce numerous lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with white petals and a yellow lip, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
