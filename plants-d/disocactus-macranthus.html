Disocactus macranthus

Disocactus macranthus, commonly known as the giant night-blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night and emit a strong fragrance. Its stems are covered in spines, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

The botanical features of Disocactus macranthus make it a striking and unique addition to desert gardens.
