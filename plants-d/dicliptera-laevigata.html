Dicliptera laevigata

Dicliptera laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf dicliptera, is a tropical perennial grass native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically smooth to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Dicliptera laevigata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
