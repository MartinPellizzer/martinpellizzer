Dissotis buraeavii var. pauciramosa

Dissotis buraeavii var. pauciramosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dissotis, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
