Dendrobium biflorum

Dendrobium biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a stem that produces two to three pairs of opposite leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium biflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
