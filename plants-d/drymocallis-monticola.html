Drymocallis monticola

Drymocallis monticola, commonly known as mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Drymocallis monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
