Dianthus elegans var. cous

Dianthus elegans var. cous, commonly known as the Corsican carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-flowered blooms in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features serrated, grass-like leaves and produces fragrant, clustered flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus elegans var. cous is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
