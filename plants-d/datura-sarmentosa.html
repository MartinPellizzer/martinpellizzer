Datura sarmentosa

Datura sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces spiny capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
