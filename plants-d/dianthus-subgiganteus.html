Dianthus subgiganteus

Dianthus subgiganteus, commonly known as the giant carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces dense clusters of petals that resemble carnations, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Dianthus subgiganteus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
