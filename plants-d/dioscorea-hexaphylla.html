Dioscorea hexaphylla

Dioscorea hexaphylla, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by cylindrical, winged fruits. Its tubers are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
