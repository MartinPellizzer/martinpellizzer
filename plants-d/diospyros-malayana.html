Diospyros malayana

Diospyros malayana, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, though some varieties may have astringent properties when unripe.

The tree is valued for its timber, which is hard and durable, and for its ornamental potential.
