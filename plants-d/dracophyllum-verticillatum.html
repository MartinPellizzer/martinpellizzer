Dracophyllum verticillatum

Dracophyllum verticillatum, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Dracophyllum verticillatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
