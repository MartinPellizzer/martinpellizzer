Danbya

Danbya, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Danbya species are typically perennial and thrive in well-drained soil and indirect sunlight. They are popular houseplants due to their compact size and ability to bloom throughout the year.

The genus includes several species, such as Danbya nobilis and Danbya thwaitesii, which are often cultivated for their ornamental value.
