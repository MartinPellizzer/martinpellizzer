Darlingtonia brachyloba

Darlingtonia brachyloba, commonly known as the short-stalked corkscrew plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its spiral-shaped, tubular leaves that trap prey, and its striking red and green coloration. The plant thrives in moist, acidic, and nutrient-poor environments, often found in bogs and wetlands. Its specialized structure allows it to capture insects, supplementing its nutrient intake in poor soil conditions.

The species is notable for its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptation to its specific habitat.
