Dracophyllum brachycladum

Dracophyllum brachycladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are often visited by insect pollinators. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Dracophyllum brachycladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
