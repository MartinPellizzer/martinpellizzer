Danthonia petriei var. mucronulata

Danthonia petriei var. mucronulata, commonly known as the mucronulate danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open woodlands or grasslands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native habitats.
