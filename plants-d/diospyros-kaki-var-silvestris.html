Diospyros kaki var. silvestris

Diospyros kaki var. silvestris, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

Its fruit is valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
