Dianthus kotschyanus

Dianthus kotschyanus, commonly known as the Kotschyan's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small, showy cups. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Dianthus kotschyanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
