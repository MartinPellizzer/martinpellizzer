Dactylorhiza × grandis

Dactylorhiza × grandis, commonly known as the great marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing numerous showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in wet, boggy habitats and requires consistently moist conditions for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose raceme and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Dactylorhiza × grandis is a perennial herb that plays an important ecological role in its native wetland ecosystems.
