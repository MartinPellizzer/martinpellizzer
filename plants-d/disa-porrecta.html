Disa porrecta

Disa porrecta, commonly known as the straight disa, is a striking member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing clusters of flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps it thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Disa porrecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
