Delphinium bicolor f. devriesii

Delphinium bicolor f. devriesii, commonly known as the Blue and Yellow Larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of showy, two-toned flowers, with blue upper petals and yellow lower ones. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its role in supporting local pollinators.
