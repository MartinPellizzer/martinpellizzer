Drimia sigmoidea

Drimia sigmoidea, commonly known as the African lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, often arranged in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Drimia sigmoidea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
