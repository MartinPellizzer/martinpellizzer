Dipterocarpus blancoi

Dipterocarpus blancoi, commonly known as the white dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Its seeds are enclosed in woody capsules and are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of tropical rainforests.
