Diospyros perplexa

Diospyros perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing ebony, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptic in shape, often with a glossy surface.

The flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are usually greenish-white in color.
