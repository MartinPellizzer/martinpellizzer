Dioscorea oligophylla

Dioscorea oligophylla, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are edible and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

Dioscorea oligophylla is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its adaptability to poor soils.
