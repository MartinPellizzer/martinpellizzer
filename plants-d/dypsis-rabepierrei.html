Dypsis rabepierrei

Dypsis rabepierrei, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery foliage. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers, followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an indoor plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both container gardening and landscape design in tropical and subtropical regions.
