Dupineta brazzae

Dupineta brazzae, commonly known as the Brazza's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to arid environments.

Dupineta brazzae is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in regions with limited rainfall.
