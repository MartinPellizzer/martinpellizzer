Dorstenia rosenii var. multibracteata

Dorstenia rosenii var. multibracteata, commonly known as the multibracteate woolly fig, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, woolly appearance with dense, soft hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden beneath the foliage. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and may have a slightly waxy texture.

Dorstenia rosenii var. multibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
