Digitaria pilosa

Digitaria pilosa, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its wiry, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
