Dracaena serrulata subsp. mccoyorum

Dracaena serrulata subsp.

mccoyorum, commonly known as the golden dracaena, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar. It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright yellow-green color.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is often used in ornamental horticulture.
