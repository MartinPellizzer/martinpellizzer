Dianthus seguieri subsp. requienii

Dianthus seguieri subsp.

requienii, commonly known as the rock campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers with five petals, often arranged in a showy display. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
