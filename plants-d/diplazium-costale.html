Diplazium costale

Diplazium costale, commonly known as the veined brake, is a fern belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its bipinnate fronds, which are divided into two rows of pinnae, giving it a distinctive, lacy appearance. Diplazium costale is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in various regions across Asia and Africa.
