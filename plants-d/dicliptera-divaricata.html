Dicliptera divaricata

Dicliptera divaricata, commonly known as the variable blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive bluish-green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
