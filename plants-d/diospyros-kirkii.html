Diospyros kirkii

Diospyros kirkii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and long, pinnate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its fruits are an important food source for both wildlife and humans in its native range.

Diospyros kirkii is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture making.
