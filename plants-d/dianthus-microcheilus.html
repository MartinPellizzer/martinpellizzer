Dianthus microcheilus

Dianthus microcheilus, commonly known as the narrow-lipped campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
