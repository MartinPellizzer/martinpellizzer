Dalea rupertii

Dalea rupertii, commonly known as the Rupert's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

Its flowers are often pale pink to white, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.
