Dorstenia holstii var. grandifolia

Dorstenia holstii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant false arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces unique, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
