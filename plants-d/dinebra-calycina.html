Dinebra calycina

Dinebra calycina, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom on long, slender stems above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Dinebra calycina is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in Andean traditions.
