Desmanthus salinarum

Desmanthus salinarum, commonly known as the saltbush desmanthus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to saline and drought-prone environments, making it a hardy species for xerophytic conditions. Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in harsh landscapes.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
