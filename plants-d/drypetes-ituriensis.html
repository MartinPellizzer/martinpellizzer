Drypetes ituriensis

Drypetes ituriensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
