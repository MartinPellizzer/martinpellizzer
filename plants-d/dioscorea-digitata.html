Dioscorea digitata

Dioscorea digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf yam, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with deeply lobed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The tubers of Dioscorea digitata are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cuisines across its native range.
