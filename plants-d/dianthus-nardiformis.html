Dianthus nardiformis

Dianthus nardiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged opposite along the stems.

Dianthus nardiformis is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
