Diospyros obtusifolia

Diospyros obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its fruit is a small, orange to reddish berry that contains a single seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
