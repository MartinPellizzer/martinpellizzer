Dioscorea brachycarpa

Dioscorea brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments.

Dioscorea brachycarpa is valued for its medicinal properties and has been studied for its potential health benefits.
