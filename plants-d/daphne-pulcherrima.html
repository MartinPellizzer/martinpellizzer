Daphne pulcherrima

Daphne pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Daphne pulcherrima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
