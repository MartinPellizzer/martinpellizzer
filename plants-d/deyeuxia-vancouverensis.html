Deyeuxia vancouverensis

Deyeuxia vancouverensis, commonly known as Vancouver grass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet or semi-aquatic environments.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and stability in wetland ecosystems.
