Diasperus fagifolius

Diasperus fagifolius, commonly known as the fagifolius acanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
