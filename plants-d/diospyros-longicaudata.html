Diospyros longicaudata

Diospyros longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe. Its fruits are highly valued for their sweet, aromatic pulp and are often used in traditional cuisines.

The tree is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
