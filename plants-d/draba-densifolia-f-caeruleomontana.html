Draba densifolia f. caeruleomontana

Draba densifolia f. caeruleomontana, commonly known as the blue mountain rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial with compact, mat-forming growth habit, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features small, bright blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its vivid blue flower color, which sets it apart from other forms of Draba densifolia.
