Daucalis

Daucalis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and silvery, velvety leaves. The plant is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils. Daucalis species typically have a compact growth habit and are valued for their ornamental appeal.

They are also known for their ability to thrive in saline environments, making them resilient to coastal conditions.
