Dahlia

Dahlia, commonly known as the "flower of the Andes," is a tuberous-rooted herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that come in a wide range of hues. The plant produces large, single or double flowers with a central disk surrounded by petal-like rays. Dahlia's tuberous roots are used as propagative material, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Its versatility in garden design and ornamental value make it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
