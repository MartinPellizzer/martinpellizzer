Dendropemon gonavensis

Dendropemon gonavensis, commonly known as the Gonáves Island orchid, is a flowering plant native to the island of Gonáves in the Dominican Republic.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid tropical forests. Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
