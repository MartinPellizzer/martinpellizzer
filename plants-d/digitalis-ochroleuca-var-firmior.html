Digitalis ochroleuca var. firmior

Digitalis ochroleuca var. firmior, commonly known as the white foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear tubular flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The flowers are typically arranged in whorls along the stem, a characteristic feature of the Digitalis genus. The leaves are broadly ovate with a velvety texture and are arranged alternately along the stem.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its cardiac glycosides.
