Dichelachne robusta

Dichelachne robusta, commonly known as robust false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed sites, showing a degree of adaptability to different soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue when young.

Dichelachne robusta is often found in association with other grasses and is an important component of the vegetation in its native habitat.
