Dendrobium mussauense

Dendrobium mussauense, commonly known as the Mussau orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the Pacific islands, particularly the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding substrate, making it well-suited for climbing.

Dendrobium mussauense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
