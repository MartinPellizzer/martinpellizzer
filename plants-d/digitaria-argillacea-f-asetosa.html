Digitaria argillacea f. asetosa

Digitaria argillacea f. asetosa, commonly known as the silky fingergrass, is a variant of the grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and slightly hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its neat growth habit and adaptability.
