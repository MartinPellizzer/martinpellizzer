Diospyros miroma

Diospyros miroma, commonly known as the Miroma persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are edible when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a distinctive scent.

The bark is thick and dark, often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
