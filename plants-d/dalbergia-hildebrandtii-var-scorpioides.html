Dalbergia hildebrandtii var. scorpioides

Dalbergia hildebrandtii var. scorpioides, commonly known as the yellow African rosewood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its density and rich coloration. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.
