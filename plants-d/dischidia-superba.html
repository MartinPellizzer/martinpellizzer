Dischidia superba

Dischidia superba, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its unique appearance and ability to climb make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
