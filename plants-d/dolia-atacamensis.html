Dolia atacamensis

Dolia atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama dolia, is a rare and unique plant native to the hyperarid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a succulent species characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and minimal leaf structure, which helps it conserve water in extreme conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the brief rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its ability to survive in such an arid environment makes it an interesting subject for botanical research.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Dolia atacamensis is considered a vulnerable species.
