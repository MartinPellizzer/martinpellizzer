Dalea austrotexana

Dalea austrotexana, commonly known as Texas milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Dalea austrotexana is often found in open grasslands and shrublands, where it thrives in full sun.
