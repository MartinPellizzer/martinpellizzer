Dianthus cibrarius

Dianthus cibrarius, commonly known as the carnation flower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Dianthus cibrarius is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of love and devotion.
