Dianthus integerrimus

Dianthus integerrimus, commonly known as the mountain campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive red or purple central stripe. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to withstand cold temperatures.

Dianthus integerrimus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
