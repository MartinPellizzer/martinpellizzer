Dianthus giganteus subsp. giganteus

Dianthus giganteus subsp.

giganteus, commonly known as the giant carnation, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive pinkish hue.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are sturdy and upright. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
