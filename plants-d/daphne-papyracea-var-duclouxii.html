Daphne papyracea var. duclouxii

Daphne papyracea var. duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's spurge laurel, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and olfactory characteristics.
