Delphinium ruspolianum var. albiflorum

Delphinium ruspolianum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white center, which are arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough, hairy texture, typical of many members of the delphinium genus.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
