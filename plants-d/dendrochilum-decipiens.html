Dendrochilum decipiens

Dendrochilum decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are often pink or purple with intricate patterns that may mimic other species, a trait that may contribute to its common name.

Dendrochilum decipiens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
