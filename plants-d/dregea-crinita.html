Dregea crinita

Dregea crinita, commonly known as the hairy dregea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palm-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Dregea crinita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowers.
