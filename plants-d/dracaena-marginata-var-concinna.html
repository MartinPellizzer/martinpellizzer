Dracaena marginata var. concinna

Dracaena marginata var. concinna, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a slender, upright stem with a distinctive red or purple hue at the base, often with a whitish or yellowish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a waxy texture with a pale green to yellowish margin.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
