Doliocarpus schottianus

Doliocarpus schottianus, commonly known as the Schottian waxtree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, waxy, orange-red fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

Doliocarpus schottianus is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and coastal areas.
