Draba argentina var. latifolia

Draba argentina var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Argentine draba, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are broad and basal, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
