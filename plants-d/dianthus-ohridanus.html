Dianthus ohridanus

Dianthus ohridanus, commonly known as the Ohrid carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

Dianthus ohridanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
