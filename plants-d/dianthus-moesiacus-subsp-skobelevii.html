Dianthus moesiacus subsp. skobelevii

Dianthus moesiacus subsp.

skobelevii, commonly known as the Skobelev's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers, which bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually upright.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
