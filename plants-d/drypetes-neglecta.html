Drypetes neglecta

Drypetes neglecta, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.
