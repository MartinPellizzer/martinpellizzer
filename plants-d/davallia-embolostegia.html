Davallia embolostegia

Davallia embolostegia, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically dark green with a golden hue on the underside. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
