Dendrochilum cobbianum

Dendrochilum cobbianum, commonly known as the cobbian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with purple or brown markings, and it produces elongated, segmented pseudobulbs.

Dendrochilum cobbianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
