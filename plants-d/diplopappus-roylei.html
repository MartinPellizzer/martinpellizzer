Diplopappus roylei

Diplopappus roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant features a robust stem with soft, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense heads, with pink to purple hues, and are adapted for wind pollination.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it well-suited to the harsh, cold climates of its native habitat.
