Dendrobium furcatum

Dendrobium furcatum, commonly known as the forked orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a contrasting red or purple lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium furcatum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
