Desmodium densum

Desmodium densum, commonly known as hairy burr senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, often with a rough or hairy texture. Desmodium densum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
