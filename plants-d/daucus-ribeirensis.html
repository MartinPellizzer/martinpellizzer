Daucus ribeirensis

Daucus ribeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian carrot, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a biennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces umbels of small, white or yellowish flowers. Its root system is less developed compared to other carrots, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
