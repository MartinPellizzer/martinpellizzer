Deguelia duckeana

Deguelia duckeana, commonly known as the Amazonian senna, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
