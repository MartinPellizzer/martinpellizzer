Disa filicornis

Disa filicornis, commonly known as the horned disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of vivid purple or pink flowers that resemble small horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or forested areas.

Disa filicornis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique and vibrant blooms.
