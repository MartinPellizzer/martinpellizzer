Dianthus agrostolepis

Dianthus agrostolepis, commonly known as the sand dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense, mat-forming foliage that helps it withstand harsh coastal conditions. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Dianthus agrostolepis is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
