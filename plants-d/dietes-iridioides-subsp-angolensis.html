Dietes iridioides subsp. angolensis

Dietes iridioides subsp.

angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped, dark green leaves and striking blue-violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
