Desmanthus brachylobus

Desmanthus brachylobus, commonly known as the short-lobed burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Desmanthus brachylobus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
