Dianthus caryophyllus f. mogadorensis

Dianthus caryophyllus f. mogadorensis, commonly known as the Mogador carnation, is a botanical variant of the carnation species characterized by its distinct floral morphology.

It features compact, double blooms with a rich, velvety texture and a deep red to crimson hue, often with a subtle white or pink margin. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been selectively bred for its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
