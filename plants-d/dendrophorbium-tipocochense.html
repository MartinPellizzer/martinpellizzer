Dendrophorbium tipocochense

Dendrophorbium tipocochense, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender pedicels, creating a dramatic visual effect.

Dendrophorbium tipocochense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
