Dianthus monspessulanus

Dianthus monspessulanus, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus monspessulanus is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in floral arrangements.
