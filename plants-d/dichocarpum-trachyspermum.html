Dichocarpum trachyspermum

Dichocarpum trachyspermum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southeast Asia.

It features a thick, aromatic rhizome and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
