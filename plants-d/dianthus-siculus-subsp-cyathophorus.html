Dianthus siculus subsp. cyathophorus

Dianthus siculus subsp.

cyathophorus, commonly known as the Sicilian carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny locations. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, cup-shaped inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
