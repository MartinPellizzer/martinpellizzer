Disa lacera var. multifida

Disa lacera var. multifida, commonly known as the multifid disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright pink to purple flowers with intricate, fringed petals. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
