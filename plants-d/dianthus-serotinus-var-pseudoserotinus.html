Dianthus serotinus var. pseudoserotinus

Dianthus serotinus var. pseudoserotinus, commonly known as the late-flowering campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This variety is closely related to other Dianthus species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
