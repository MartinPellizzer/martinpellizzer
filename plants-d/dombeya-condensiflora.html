Dombeya condensiflora

Dombeya condensiflora, commonly known as the clustered dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that produce abundant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with smooth, often grayish bark. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dombeya condensiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
