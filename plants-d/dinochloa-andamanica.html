Dinochloa andamanica

Dinochloa andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman dwarf sedge, is a small aquatic plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact rosette. Its flowering structures are small and inconspicuous, producing tiny seeds that are dispersed by water.

Dinochloa andamanica plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing microhabitats for small aquatic organisms.
