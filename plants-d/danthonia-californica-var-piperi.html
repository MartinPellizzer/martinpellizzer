Danthonia californica var. piperi

Danthonia californica var. piperi, commonly known as California needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
