Dichopsis canaliculata

Dichopsis canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise spiky form.
