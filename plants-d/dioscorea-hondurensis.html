Dioscorea hondurensis

Dioscorea hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras yam, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used as food in some regions. Its tubers are starchy and have a mild flavor, making them a valuable crop in local agriculture.

Dioscorea hondurensis is also of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
