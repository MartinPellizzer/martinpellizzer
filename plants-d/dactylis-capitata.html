Dactylis capitata

Dactylis capitata, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Dactylis capitata is valued for its forage quality and ability to stabilize soil in agricultural and ecological settings.
