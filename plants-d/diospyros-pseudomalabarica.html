Diospyros pseudomalabarica

Diospyros pseudomalabarica, commonly known as the Malabar persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and other hard-wooded trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
