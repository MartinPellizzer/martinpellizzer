Dregea arabica

Dregea arabica, commonly known as the Arabian trumpet-vine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has woody stems and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
