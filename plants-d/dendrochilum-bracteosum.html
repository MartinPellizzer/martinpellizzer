Dendrochilum bracteosum

Dendrochilum bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteose tree orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and is typically found growing in tropical rainforests. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and help store water, adapting it to its humid, shaded environment.

Dendrochilum bracteosum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
