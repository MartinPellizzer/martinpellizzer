Dieffenbachia seguine subvar. nobilis

Dieffenbachia seguine subvar. nobilis, commonly known as the African mask plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves with striking white and green variegation. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are toxic if ingested, containing calcium oxalate crystals that can cause irritation.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.
