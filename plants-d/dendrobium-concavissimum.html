Dendrobium concavissimum

Dendrobium concavissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Dendrobium concavissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
