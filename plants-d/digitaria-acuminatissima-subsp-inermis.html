Digitaria acuminatissima subsp. inermis

Digitaria acuminatissima subsp.

inermis, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
