Dissochaeta ligulata

Dissochaeta ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces white or pale green flowers that are clustered in small inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological interactions of its host plants.
