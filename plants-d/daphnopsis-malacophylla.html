Daphnopsis malacophylla

Daphnopsis malacophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf daphnopsis, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Daphnopsis malacophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
