Desmodium birmannicum

Desmodium birmannicum, commonly known as the Birmann's ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Desmodium birmannicum is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
