Dalbergia pubescens

Dalbergia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and deciduous leaves. The plant is notable for its dense, dark reddish-brown timber, which is highly valued for carving and furniture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Dalbergia pubescens plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
