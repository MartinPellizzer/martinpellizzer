Dianthus superbus f. chionanthus

Dianthus superbus f. chionanthus, commonly known as the snow-white campion, is a botanical variant of the common campion.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a soft, ethereal appearance. The plant typically grows in alpine or rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
