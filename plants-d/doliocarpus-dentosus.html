Doliocarpus dentosus

Doliocarpus dentosus, commonly known as the toothed evergreen, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Anacardiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Doliocarpus dentosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
