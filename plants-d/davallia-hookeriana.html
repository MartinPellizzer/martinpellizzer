Davallia hookeriana

Davallia hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's spleenwort, is a fern species native to various regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically light green and have a soft, velvety texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its rhizomes are short and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing sorus.

Davallia hookeriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper care.
