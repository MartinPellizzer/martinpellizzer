Dendrobium lycopodioides

Dendrobium lycopodioides, commonly known as the club-moss orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces compact, greenish to brownish inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a distinctive species within the orchid family.
