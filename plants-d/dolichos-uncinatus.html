Dolichos uncinatus

Dolichos uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked dolichos, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Dolichos uncinatus is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various traditional cuisines.
