Dipholis parvifolia

Dipholis parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved flaxviolet, is a member of the Violaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Its botanical features make it a desirable plant for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
