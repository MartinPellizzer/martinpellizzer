Deyeuxia atjehensis

Deyeuxia atjehensis, commonly known as the Atjeh sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Deyeuxia atjehensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
