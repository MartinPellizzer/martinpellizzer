Derris pseudomarginata

Derris pseudomarginata, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to thrive in humid climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically used in traditional medicine.
