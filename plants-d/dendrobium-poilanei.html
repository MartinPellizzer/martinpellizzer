Dendrobium poilanei

Dendrobium poilanei, commonly known as the Poilane's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Dendrobium poilanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
