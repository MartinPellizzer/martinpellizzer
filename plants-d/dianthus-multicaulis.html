Dianthus multicaulis

Dianthus multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Dianthus multicaulis is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
