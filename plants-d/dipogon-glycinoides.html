Dipogon glycinoides

Dipogon glycinoides, commonly known as the slender legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its characteristic pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pale green or yellow, and it produces slender, curved pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
