Didymococcus danura

Didymococcus danura, commonly known as the red mangrove seedling, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, reddish leaves and its ability to tolerate high levels of salt in the soil. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and preventing erosion.

Its unique physiological adaptations allow it to thrive in intertidal zones where few other plants can survive.
