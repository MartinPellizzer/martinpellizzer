Dupatya ochrocephala

Dupatya ochrocephala, commonly known as the yellow-headed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structure, which is adapted for specific pollination strategies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ability to capture light in low-light conditions.

The orchid's flowers are often clustered in inflorescences, showcasing its ornamental value and ecological significance.
