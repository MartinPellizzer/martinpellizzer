Dianthus cintranus subsp. barbatus

Dianthus cintranus subsp.

barbatus, commonly known as the Barbatus Campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
