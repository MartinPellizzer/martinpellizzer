Dolichorrhiza caucasica

Dolichorrhiza caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and two opposite, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spike, typical of the orchid family. It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Dolichorrhiza caucasica is often found in forest understories and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
