Dryades cyrtantha

Dryades cyrtantha, commonly known as the slender rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with a distinctive lip structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
