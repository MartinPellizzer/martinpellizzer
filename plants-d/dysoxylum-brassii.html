Dysoxylum brassii

Dysoxylum brassii, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and durable wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits.

Dysoxylum brassii is often found in moist, lowland forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
