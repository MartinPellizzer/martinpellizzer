Dactylorhiza andoeyana

Dactylorhiza andoeyana, commonly known as the Andaman marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andaman Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in wet, marshy habitats and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
