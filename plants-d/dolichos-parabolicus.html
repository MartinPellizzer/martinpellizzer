Dolichos parabolicus

Dolichos parabolicus, commonly known as the parabolic bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive parabolic-shaped pods, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom along the stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Dolichos parabolicus is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines and can be dried and ground into a flour.
