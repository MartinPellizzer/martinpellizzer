Dendrobium parishii var. dives

Dendrobium parishii var. dives, commonly known as the diving dendrobium, is a sympodial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with red veins during the dry season. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to arid conditions.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
