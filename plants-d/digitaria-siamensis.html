Digitaria siamensis

Digitaria siamensis, commonly known as the Asian Digitaria, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open areas and along roadsides.

Digitaria siamensis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
