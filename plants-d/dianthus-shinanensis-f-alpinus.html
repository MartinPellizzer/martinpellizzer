Dianthus shinanensis f. alpinus

Dianthus shinanensis f. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant in alpine gardens.
