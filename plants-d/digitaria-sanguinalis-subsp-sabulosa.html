Digitaria sanguinalis subsp. sabulosa

Digitaria sanguinalis subsp.

sabulosa, commonly known as sand bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
