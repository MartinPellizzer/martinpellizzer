Diospyros phlebodes

Diospyros phlebodes, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood and has cultural and medicinal significance in various African communities.
