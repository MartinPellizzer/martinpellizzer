Diastella serpyllifolia

Diastella serpyllifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Diastella serpyllifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
