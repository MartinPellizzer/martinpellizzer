Dischidia chinghungensis

Dischidia chinghungensis, commonly known as the Guangxi Dischidia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is a climbing or trailing vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to southern China, particularly Guangxi, it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant's stems can develop aerial roots, allowing it to attach to trees or other supports.

It is often found in shaded, moist environments within forest understories.
