Dendrochilum cagayanense

Dendrochilum cagayanense, commonly known as the Cagayan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces elongated, lance-shaped leaves and bears inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
