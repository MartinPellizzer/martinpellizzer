Daucus setifolius

Daucus setifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf parsley.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer.

Daucus setifolius is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and aromatic foliage.
