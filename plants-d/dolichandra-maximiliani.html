Dolichandra maximiliani

Dolichandra maximiliani, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Dolichandra maximiliani is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
