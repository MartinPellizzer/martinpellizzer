Delphinium consolida subsp. paniculatum

Delphinium consolida subsp.

paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in panicles, giving it its common name.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are characterized by their spurred petals, a feature typical of the Delphinium genus.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
