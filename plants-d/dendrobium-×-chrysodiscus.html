Dendrobium × chrysodiscus

Dendrobium × chrysodiscus, commonly known as the golden disc orchid, is a hybrid orchid species originating from crosses between Dendrobium nobile and Dendrobium phalaenopsis.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features long, cane-like stems that bear numerous clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The botanical characteristics of Dendrobium × chrysodiscus make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
