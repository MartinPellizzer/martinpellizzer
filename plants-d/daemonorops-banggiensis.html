Daemonorops banggiensis

Daemonorops banggiensis, commonly known as the Banggi palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its reproductive structures include small, clustered inflorescences that produce both male and female flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological and economic significance.
