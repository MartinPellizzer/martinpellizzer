Distimake dissectus var. chacoensis

Distimake dissectus var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco dissected fern, is a unique species native to the Chaco region of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often along riverbanks or in forest understories. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This rare variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
