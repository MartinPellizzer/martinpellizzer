Diplopappus lavandulifolius

Diplopappus lavandulifolius, commonly known as silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that resemble dandelion fluff. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, also known as the sunflower family.
