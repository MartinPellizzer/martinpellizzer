Digitaria nuda subsp. schumacheriana

Digitaria nuda subsp.

schumacheriana, commonly known as Schumacher's nakedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairless. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is usually bare or nearly so.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and resilience in diverse environmental conditions.
