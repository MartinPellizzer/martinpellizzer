Doronicum wendelboi

Doronicum wendelboi, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and meadow habitats at high altitudes. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. Its leaves are basal, arranged in a rosette, and are deeply lobed.

Doronicum wendelboi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
