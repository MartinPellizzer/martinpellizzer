Diospyros scabrida

Diospyros scabrida, commonly known as the rough persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its rough, bark-like texture and small, clustered leaves. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange to reddish berry that ripens in the fall.

Diospyros scabrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
