Dipladenia pulchella

Dipladenia pulchella, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight for optimal growth.

Dipladenia pulchella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.
