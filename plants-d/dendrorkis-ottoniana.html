Dendrorkis ottoniana

Dendrorkis ottoniana, commonly known as the Otto's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Dendrorkis ottoniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
