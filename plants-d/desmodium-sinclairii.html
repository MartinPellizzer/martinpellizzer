Desmodium sinclairii

Desmodium sinclairii, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Desmodium sinclairii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration.
