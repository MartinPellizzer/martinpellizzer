Dendrobium oscarii

Dendrobium oscarii, commonly known as the Orange-veined Dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that often branch and bear numerous leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and rain, making it well-suited for humid environments.

Dendrobium oscarii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
