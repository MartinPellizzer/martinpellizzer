Diospyros srilankana

Diospyros srilankana, commonly known as the Sri Lankan persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the wet tropical regions of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit, which is typically pear-shaped, is edible and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
