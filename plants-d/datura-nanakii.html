Datura nanakii

Datura nanakii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jimsonweed, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Datura nanakii is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in dry, arid environments.

It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
