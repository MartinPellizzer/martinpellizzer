Diospyros coccolobifolia var. pubescens

Diospyros coccolobifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits. Its botanical features include a sturdy trunk, compound leaves, and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which can be consumed raw or cooked.
