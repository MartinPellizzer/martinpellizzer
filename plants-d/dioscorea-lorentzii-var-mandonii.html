Dioscorea lorentzii var. mandonii

Dioscorea lorentzii var. mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers, which are the primary economic product, are rich in starch and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitats.
