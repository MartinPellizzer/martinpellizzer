Dioscorea intempestiva

Dioscorea intempestiva, commonly known as the wild yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by cylindrical, tuberous roots that are its primary economic value. These roots contain compounds such as diosgenin, which is used in the synthesis of hormonal medications.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is widely studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
