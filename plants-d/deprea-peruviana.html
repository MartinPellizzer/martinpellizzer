Deprea peruviana

Deprea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature common in many desert plants.

Deprea peruviana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique shape and resilience.
