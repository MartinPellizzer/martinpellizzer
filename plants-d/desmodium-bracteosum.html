Desmodium bracteosum

Desmodium bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteated desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant is native to various regions in Africa and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats. It has a climbing habit and produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

Desmodium bracteosum is also noted for its potential use in soil nitrogen fixation due to its association with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
