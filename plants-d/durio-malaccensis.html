Durio malaccensis

Durio malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca durian, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, spiky fruit, which is a feature of the Durio genus. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture. The fruit develops from the flower and is known for its strong aroma and complex flavor.

It is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit, which is highly valued in local markets.
