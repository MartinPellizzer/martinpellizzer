Dianthus anticarius subsp. subbaeticus

Dianthus anticarius subsp.

subbaeticus, commonly known as the Baetican campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with stems that are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
