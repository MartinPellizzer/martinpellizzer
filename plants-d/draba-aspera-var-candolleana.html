Draba aspera var. candolleana

Draba aspera var. candolleana, commonly known as the rough rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh mountain climates.
