Dactylis glomerata subsp. altaica

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

altaica, commonly known as Altai grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and rough, with a bluish-green color, and the stems are sturdy and often branched.

It is valued for its forage quality and is used in agricultural and ecological restoration projects.
