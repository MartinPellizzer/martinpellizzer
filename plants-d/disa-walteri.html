Disa walteri

Disa walteri, commonly known as Walter's Disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in areas with partial shade.

Disa walteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
