Diospyros major var. andersonii

Diospyros major var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often pubescent leaves that turn reddish in autumn. Its small, yellowish flowers are typically followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
