Didymocarpus rodgeri var. siamensis

Didymocarpus rodgeri var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam dwarf mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, segmented fruits. The fruit's pericarp is typically divided into segments, each containing a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
