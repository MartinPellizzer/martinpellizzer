Dalbergaria purpurimarginata

Dalbergaria purpurimarginata, commonly known as the purple-margin tree, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged margins on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands and savannas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
