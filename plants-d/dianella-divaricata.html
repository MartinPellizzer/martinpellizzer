Dianella divaricata

Dianella divaricata, commonly known as the variable flax lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive white midrib. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Dianella divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
