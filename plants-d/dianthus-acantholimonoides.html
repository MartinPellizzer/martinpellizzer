Dianthus acantholimonoides

Dianthus acantholimonoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many showy flowering plants. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typical of the genus Dianthus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
