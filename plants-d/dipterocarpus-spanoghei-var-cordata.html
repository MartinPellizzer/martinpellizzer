Dipterocarpus spanoghei var. cordata

Dipterocarpus spanoghei var. cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its straight, cylindrical trunk and broad, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is adapted to high humidity and warm climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces woody fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
