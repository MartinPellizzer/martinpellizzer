Draba laxa var. hebecarpa

Draba laxa var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow or white flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
