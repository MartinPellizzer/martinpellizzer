Disa basutorum

Disa basutorum, commonly known as the Basotho orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting purple or black markings. The plant features a compact, upright growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, stocky stem. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters, making it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and floral arrangements.

Disa basutorum thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
