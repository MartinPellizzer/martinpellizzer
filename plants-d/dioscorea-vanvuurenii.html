Dioscorea vanvuurenii

Dioscorea vanvuurenii, commonly known as the Van Vuuren yam, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish male flowers that are typically borne on separate plants. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in local communities.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in both ecological and cultural contexts.
