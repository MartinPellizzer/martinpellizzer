Dichroa cyanitis

Dichroa cyanitis, commonly known as the blue dichroa, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a unique visual appeal to its habitat. The plant belongs to the family Dichroanthemaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dichroa cyanitis thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for shaded garden areas.
