Delphinium burkei var. distichiflorum

Delphinium burkei var. distichiflorum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, with a rough, hairy texture. This variety is known for its striking floral display and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other showy flowering plants.
