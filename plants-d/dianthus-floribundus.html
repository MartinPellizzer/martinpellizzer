Dianthus floribundus

Dianthus floribundus, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus floribundus is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
