Dalea orcuttii

Dalea orcuttii, commonly known as Orcutt's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to reddish pink flowers in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dalea orcuttii is adapted to arid environments and is considered a species of conservation concern due to habitat loss.
