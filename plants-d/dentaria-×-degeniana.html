Dentaria × degeniana

Dentaria × degeniana, commonly known as the hybrid toothache plant, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of oval, tooth-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal historical use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
