Dracocephalum adylovii

Dracocephalum adylovii, commonly known as the Persian bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Dracocephalum adylovii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
