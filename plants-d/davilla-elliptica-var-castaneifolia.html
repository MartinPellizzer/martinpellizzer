Davilla elliptica var. castaneifolia

Davilla elliptica var. castaneifolia, commonly known as the red maple or Chinese red maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically reddish-brown on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
