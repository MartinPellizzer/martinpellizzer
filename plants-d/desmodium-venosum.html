Desmodium venosum

Desmodium venosum, commonly known as purple prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, often forming dense stands. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in its native range.
