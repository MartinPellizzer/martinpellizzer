Draba parnassica var. microcarpa

Draba parnassica var. microcarpa, commonly known as the alpine wintercress, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to other varieties of Draba parnassica.
