Dalea terminalis

Dalea terminalis, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea terminalis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
