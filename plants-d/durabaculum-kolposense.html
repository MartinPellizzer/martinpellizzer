Durabaculum kolposense

Durabaculum kolposense, commonly known as the Kolposian rock cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. Its roots are adapted to anchor firmly in rocky soil, providing stability in its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
