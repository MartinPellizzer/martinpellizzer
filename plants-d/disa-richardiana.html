Disa richardiana

Disa richardiana, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Disa richardiana is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
