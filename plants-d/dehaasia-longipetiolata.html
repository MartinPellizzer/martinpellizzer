Dehaasia longipetiolata

Dehaasia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled karanj, is a perennial shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the leaf stalks that connect the leaves to the stem. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several small, lance-shaped leaflets.

The flowers are small and yellowish-green, often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
