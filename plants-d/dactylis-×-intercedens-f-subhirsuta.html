Dactylis × intercedens f. subhirsuta

Dactylis × intercedens f. subhirsuta, commonly known as hairy cocksfoot, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture on both sides, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and ecological resilience.
