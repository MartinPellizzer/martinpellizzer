Dalea zimapanica

Dalea zimapanica, commonly known as the Zimapan wild pea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Dalea zimapanica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.
