Drypetes gerrardii var. gerrardii

Drypetes gerrardii var. gerrardii, commonly known as the white-leafed drypetes, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptic leaves that are usually pale green and glossy. Its flowers are small and pale yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round, woody fruits.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in secondary tropical forests.
