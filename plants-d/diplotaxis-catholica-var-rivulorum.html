Diplotaxis catholica var. rivulorum

Diplotaxis catholica var. rivulorum, commonly known as the river dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in sandy soils.

Diplotaxis catholica var. rivulorum is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
