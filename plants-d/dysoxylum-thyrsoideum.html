Dysoxylum thyrsoideum

Dysoxylum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the slender tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has been used traditionally for its timber and seeds, which have various culinary and medicinal applications.
