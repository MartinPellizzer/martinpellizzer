Dryopteris dilatata f. oblonga

Dryopteris dilatata f. oblonga, commonly known as the oblong fern, is a variant of the male fern characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds.

This fern typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream sides, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its fronds are usually longer and more slender than those of the typical form, with a finely divided, feather-like structure. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which release spores for reproduction.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
