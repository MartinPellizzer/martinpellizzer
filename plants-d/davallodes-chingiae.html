Davallodes chingiae

Davallodes chingiae, commonly known as the Chinese climbing fern, is a rare and lesser-known fern species native to China.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that often form a dense, climbing growth habit. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments, such as along forest floors or in rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, a common reproductive feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
