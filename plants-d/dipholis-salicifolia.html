Dipholis salicifolia

Dipholis salicifolia, commonly known as the willow leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, willow-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil, while its aerial roots help in climbing or anchoring to trees.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are often pink or white and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
