Dioscorea duchassaingii

Dioscorea duchassaingii, commonly known as the giant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential pharmacological properties.
