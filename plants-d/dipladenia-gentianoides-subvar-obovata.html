Dipladenia gentianoides subvar. obovata

Dipladenia gentianoides subvar. obovata, commonly known as the waxplant, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The leaves are typically ovate to obovate in shape, with a waxy, glossy surface. This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
