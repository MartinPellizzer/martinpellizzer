Dischidia oiantha

Dischidia oiantha, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, hair-like coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Dischidia oiantha is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its preference for well-draining soil.
