Dalechampia chevalieri

Dalechampia chevalieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a red throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Dalechampia chevalieri is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
