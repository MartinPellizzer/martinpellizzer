Derris elliptica var. chittagongensis

Derris elliptica var. chittagongensis, commonly known as Chittagong derris, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive seed pods. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a natural toxin used in traditional fishing and pest control. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
