Dianthus virgineus var. brevistylus

Dianthus virgineus var. brevistylus, commonly known as the short-styled carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with prominent, short styles, which distinguish it from other varieties of Dianthus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and dye production.
