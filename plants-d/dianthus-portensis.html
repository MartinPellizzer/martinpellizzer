Dianthus portensis

Dianthus portensis, commonly known as the Portensian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus portensis is also valued for its fragrance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
