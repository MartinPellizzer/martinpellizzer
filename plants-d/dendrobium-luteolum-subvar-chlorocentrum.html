Dendrobium luteolum subvar. chlorocentrum

Dendrobium luteolum subvar. chlorocentrum, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant features a cane-like stem with fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a yellowish tinge. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
