Dionysia bachtiarica

Dionysia bachtiarica, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
