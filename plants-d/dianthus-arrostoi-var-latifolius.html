Dianthus arrostoi var. latifolius

Dianthus arrostoi var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf campfire flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink. The leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to withstand the dry, rocky conditions of its natural habitat. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to coastal and Mediterranean landscapes.
