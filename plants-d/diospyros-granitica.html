Diospyros granitica

Diospyros granitica, commonly known as the granite persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and small, orange-red fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Diospyros granitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
