Dioclea reflexa

Dioclea reflexa, commonly known as the climbing passionflower, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals and five petals. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns and nectar-rich structures. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Dioclea reflexa is also known for its edible fruits, which are small, yellow, and contain a sweet, fleshy pulp.
