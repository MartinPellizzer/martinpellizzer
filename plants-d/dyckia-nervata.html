Dyckia nervata

Dyckia nervata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, white flowers, which are typically clustered in a spike. Dyckia nervata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
