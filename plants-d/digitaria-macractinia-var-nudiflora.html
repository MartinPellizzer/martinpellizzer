Digitaria macractinia var. nudiflora

Digitaria macractinia var. nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often glabrous, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
