Decaneurum silhetense

Decaneurum silhetense, commonly known as Silhet grass, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is classified under the family Poaceae, which includes other grasses and cereal crops.
