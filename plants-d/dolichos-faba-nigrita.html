Dolichos faba-nigrita

Dolichos faba-nigrita, commonly known as black field bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods that contain dark brown, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. Its root system has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and can be processed into flour or porridge.
