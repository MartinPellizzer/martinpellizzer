Danthonia porosa var. subinermis

Danthonia porosa var. subinermis, commonly known as the slender danthonia, is a grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a common component of native grasslands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
