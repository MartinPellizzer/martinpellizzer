Disynstemon

Disynstemon, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a thick, upright stem with a hollow, cup-like structure at the top, which holds dew or rainwater. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Disynstemon is often found in moist, open areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
