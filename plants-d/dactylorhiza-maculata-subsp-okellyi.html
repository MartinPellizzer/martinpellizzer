Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. okellyi

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

okellyi, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

The plant features a tall, slender stem with showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and is often found in association with other orchid species in its native range.
