Discocactus zehntneri subsp. albispinus

Discocactus zehntneri subsp.

albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined discocactus, is a cactus species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white spines, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and appear in clusters at the base of the stems.

It is a slow-growing species that requires minimal water and is often cultivated for its unique appearance.
