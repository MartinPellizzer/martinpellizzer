Dalbergaria albovinosa

Dalbergaria albovinosa, commonly known as white vining dalbergia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
