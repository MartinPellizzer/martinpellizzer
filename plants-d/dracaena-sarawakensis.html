Dracaena sarawakensis

Dracaena sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak dragon tree, is a tropical plant native to the island of Borneo.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red or orange berries, which are edible for some bird species. Dracaena sarawakensis thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in Borneo's flora.
