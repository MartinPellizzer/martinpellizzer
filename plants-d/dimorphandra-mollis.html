Dimorphandra mollis

Dimorphandra mollis, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical regions.
