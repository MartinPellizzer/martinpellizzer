Dryandra teretifolia

Dryandra teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dryandra, is a member of the Proteaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that bear small, pink or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in native gardens.
