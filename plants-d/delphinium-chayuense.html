Delphinium chayuense

Delphinium chayuense, commonly known as the Chayu delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear showy, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped lobes, and the flowers are arranged in a raceme, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Delphinium chayuense is valued for its striking blooms and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
