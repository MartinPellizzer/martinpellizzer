Dendrorkis rosellata

Dendrorkis rosellata, commonly known as the Rosell's orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often with a waxy coating to retain moisture.

Dendrorkis rosellata is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
