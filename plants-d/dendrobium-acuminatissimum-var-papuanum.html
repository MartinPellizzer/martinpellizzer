Dendrobium acuminatissimum var. papuanum

Dendrobium acuminatissimum var. papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with prominent, tooth-like lobes on the petals. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

This variety is valued for its unique floral morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
