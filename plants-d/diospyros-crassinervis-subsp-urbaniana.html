Diospyros crassinervis subsp. urbaniana

Diospyros crassinervis subsp.

urbaniana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent veins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a globose, fleshy drupe that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.

The bark and leaves have been used traditionally in folk medicine for their potential health benefits.
