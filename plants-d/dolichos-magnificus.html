Dolichos magnificus

Dolichos magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent bean, is a climbing legume native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Dolichos magnificus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
