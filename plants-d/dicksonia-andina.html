Dicksonia andina

Dicksonia andina, commonly known as the Andean tree fern, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Its rhizome produces roots that anchor it firmly in the soil, while the fronds are responsible for photosynthesis.

Dicksonia andina is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
