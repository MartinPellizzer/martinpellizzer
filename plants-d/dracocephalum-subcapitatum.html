Dracocephalum subcapitatum

Dracocephalum subcapitatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features compact, spike-like inflorescences with violet to blue flowers, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
