Dombeya laxiflora

Dombeya laxiflora, commonly known as the loose-flowered dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are borne in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Dombeya laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
