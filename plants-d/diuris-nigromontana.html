Diuris nigromontana

Diuris nigromontana, commonly known as the Black Mountain spider orchid, is an orchid native to the wet tropical rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple and black flower spikes, which are adorned with intricate, spider-like patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the late winter to early spring season. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, typically arranged in a rosette formation.

Diuris nigromontana is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions to thrive.
