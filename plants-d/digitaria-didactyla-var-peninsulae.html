Digitaria didactyla var. peninsulae

Digitaria didactyla var. peninsulae, commonly known as the Peninsula Digitaria, is a grass species native to specific regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.
