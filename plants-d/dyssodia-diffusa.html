Dyssodia diffusa

Dyssodia diffusa, commonly known as the hairy dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually opposite and covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dyssodia diffusa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
