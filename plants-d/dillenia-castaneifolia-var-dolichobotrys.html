Dillenia castaneifolia var. dolichobotrys

Dillenia castaneifolia var. dolichobotrys, commonly known as the long-stemmed dillenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble those of the horse chestnut. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are arranged in umbels, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
