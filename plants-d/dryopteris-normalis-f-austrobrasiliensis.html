Dryopteris normalis f. austrobrasiliensis

Dryopteris normalis f. austrobrasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian shield fern, is a fern species native to South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive shield-shaped structure at the base.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
