Disa micropetala

Disa micropetala, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers with a distinctive fragrance.

Disa micropetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
