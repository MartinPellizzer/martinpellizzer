Dichaea latifolia

Dichaea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
