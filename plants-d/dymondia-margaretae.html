Dymondia margaretae

Dymondia margaretae, commonly known as the silver carpet grass, is a low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with narrow, bluish-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid climates.
