Duchesnea indica f. japonica

Duchesnea indica f. japonica, commonly known as Japanese woodsorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine, stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
