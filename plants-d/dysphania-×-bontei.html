Dysphania × bontei

Dysphania × bontei, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a hybrid species native to New Zealand.

It is a leafy vegetable characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are similar in taste to spinach. The plant thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in cooking. It has a sprawling growth habit and can be grown as an annual in temperate regions.

Dysphania × bontei is valued for its ability to tolerate light frosts and its high content of vitamins and minerals.
