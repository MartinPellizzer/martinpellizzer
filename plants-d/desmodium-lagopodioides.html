Desmodium lagopodioides

Desmodium lagopodioides, commonly known as the horsebean clover, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and cover cropping.

Its growth habit makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
