Dichapetalum coronadoae

Dichapetalum coronadoae, commonly known as the Coronado's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico and the southwestern United States.

The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability. Its leaves are opposite and usually covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dichapetalum coronadoae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
