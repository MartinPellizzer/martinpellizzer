Dyckia digitata

Dyckia digitata, commonly known as the finger dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, showy white or pink flowers. Dyckia digitata is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in many regions.
