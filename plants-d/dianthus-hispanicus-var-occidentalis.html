Dianthus hispanicus var. occidentalis

Dianthus hispanicus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
