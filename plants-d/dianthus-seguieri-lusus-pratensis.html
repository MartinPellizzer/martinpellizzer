Dianthus seguieri lusus pratensis

Dianthus seguieri lusus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and leaves.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
