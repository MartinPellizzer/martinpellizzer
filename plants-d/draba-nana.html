Draba nana

Draba nana, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cold climates with well-drained soil. Draba nana produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
