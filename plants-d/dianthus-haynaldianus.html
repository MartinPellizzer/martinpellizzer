Dianthus haynaldianus

Dianthus haynaldianus, commonly known as the Haynald's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits called capsules.

Dianthus haynaldianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
