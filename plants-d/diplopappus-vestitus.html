Diplopappus vestitus

Diplopappus vestitus, commonly known as the velvet pappus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, often covered in a fine, hairy coating. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, wind-dispersed achene, adapted for aerial propagation.
