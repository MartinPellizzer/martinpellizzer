Dendrochilum hampelii

Dendrochilum hampelii, commonly known as the Hampel's tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces strikingly large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dendrochilum hampelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
