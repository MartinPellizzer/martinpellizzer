Dalea carthagenensis var. capitulata

Dalea carthagenensis var. capitulata, commonly known as the clustered prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

This variant is distinguished by its more compact growth form compared to other varieties of Dalea carthagenensis.
