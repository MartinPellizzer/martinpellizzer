Disperis hamadryas

Disperis hamadryas, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of East Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Disperis hamadryas is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native environment.
