Dioclea dichrona

Dioclea dichrona, commonly known as the red-flowered spider plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling the appearance of spiders, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires support for its vine-like growth habit.

Dioclea dichrona is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
