Dendrobium vestitum

Dendrobium vestitum, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, wiry stem covered in dense, fibrous roots and numerous small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent yellow lips. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires good air circulation.

Dendrobium vestitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
