Dianthus superbus f. tricolor

Dianthus superbus f. tricolor, commonly known as the tricolor carnation, is a flowering plant characterized by its strikingly colorful petals.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to Europe, particularly the British Isles. The plant features dense, showy blooms with three distinct colors—typically red, white, and pink—arranged in a radial pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, adding texture to its ornamental appearance.
