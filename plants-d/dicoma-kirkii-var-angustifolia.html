Dicoma kirkii var. angustifolia

Dicoma kirkii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dicoma, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in full sun and its use in traditional medicine.
