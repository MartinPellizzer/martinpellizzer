Didymocarpus atrosanguineus

Didymocarpus atrosanguineus, commonly known as the black blood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by dark red to black fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
