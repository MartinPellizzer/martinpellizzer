Davallia strigosa var. rhomboidea

Davallia strigosa var. rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid shield fern, is a compact evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features rhomboid-shaped, leathery fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of brown, woolly hairs. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds, which are typically grouped in clusters. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
