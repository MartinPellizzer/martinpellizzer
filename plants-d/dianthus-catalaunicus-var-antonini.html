Dianthus catalaunicus var. antonini

Dianthus catalaunicus var. antonini, commonly known as the Catalan pink, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
