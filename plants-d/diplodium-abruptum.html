Diplodium abruptum

Diplodium abruptum, commonly known as the sharpleaf diplodium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often sharply toothed along the margins. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to partial shade.

Diplodium abruptum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
