Daphne pycnophylla

Daphne pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, fleshy fruits containing seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
