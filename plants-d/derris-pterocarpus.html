Derris pterocarpus

Derris pterocarpus, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, dark seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its density and aesthetic qualities, making it a sought-after material in craftsmanship.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
