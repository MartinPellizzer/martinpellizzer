Dracocephalum origanoides subsp. origanoides

Dracocephalum origanoides subsp.

origanoides, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties.

The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its small, pink to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

It is valued in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications for its pungent, aromatic leaves.
