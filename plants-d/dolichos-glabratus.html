Dolichos glabratus

Dolichos glabratus, commonly known as the smooth dolichos, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Dolichos glabratus is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various culinary traditions.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
