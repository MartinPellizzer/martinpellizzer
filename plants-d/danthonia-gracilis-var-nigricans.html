Danthonia gracilis var. nigricans

Danthonia gracilis var. nigricans, commonly known as black-stemmed beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, dark-colored stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
