Digitalis dubia var. micrantha

Digitalis dubia var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
