Dryopteris ctenitis

Dryopteris ctenitis, commonly known as the Hart's tongue fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, strap-like fronds.

It is native to temperate regions of Asia, Europe, and North America, often growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile fronds producing spores on the underside. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
