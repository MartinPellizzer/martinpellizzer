Dianthus arenarius

Dianthus arenarius, commonly known as the sand pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It blooms from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus arenarius is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
