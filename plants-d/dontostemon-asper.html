Dontostemon asper

Dontostemon asper, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often glandular and slightly serrated, contributing to its rough texture. The plant produces showy, pentagonal flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable addition to garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
