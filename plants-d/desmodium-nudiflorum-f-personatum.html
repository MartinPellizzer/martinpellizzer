Desmodium nudiflorum f. personatum

Desmodium nudiflorum f. personatum, commonly known as the naked-flowered desmodium, is a variant of the Desmodium genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
