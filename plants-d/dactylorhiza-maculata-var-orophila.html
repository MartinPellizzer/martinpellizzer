Dactylorhiza maculata var. orophila

Dactylorhiza maculata var. orophila, commonly known as the alpine marsh orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the latter being showy and often marked with dark spots.

This orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in open, sunny areas at high altitudes.
