Digitalis obtusa

Digitalis obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or pink. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
