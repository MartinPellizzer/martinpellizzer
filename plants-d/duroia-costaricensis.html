Duroia costaricensis

Duroia costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican durio, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, spade-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
