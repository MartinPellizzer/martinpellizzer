Dombeya pervillei

Dombeya pervillei, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
