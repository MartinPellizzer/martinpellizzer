Dianthus cintranus subsp. anticarius

Dianthus cintranus subsp.

anticarius, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the stems are often prostrate or ascending.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
