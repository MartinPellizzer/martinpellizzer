Douglasia laevigata subsp. ciliolata

Douglasia laevigata subsp.

ciliolata, commonly known as the hairy Douglasia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with ciliate margins, which give it its subspecies name.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
