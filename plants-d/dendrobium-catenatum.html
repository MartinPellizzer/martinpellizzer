Dendrobium catenatum

Dendrobium catenatum, commonly known as the chain orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, often in humid tropical environments. The plant produces long, pendant-like inflorescences with clusters of fragrant, white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it has a unique growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Dendrobium catenatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
