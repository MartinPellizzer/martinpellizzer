Dipcadi madrasicum

Dipcadi madrasicum, commonly known as the Persian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and floral arrangements.
