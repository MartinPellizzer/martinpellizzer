Dioscorea morelosana

Dioscorea morelosana, commonly known as the Morelos yam, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are often used in traditional cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The species is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
