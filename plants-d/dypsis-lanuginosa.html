Dypsis lanuginosa

Dypsis lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky cycad, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are typically a deep green color and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

Dypsis lanuginosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and air-purifying qualities.
