Danthonia annableae

Danthonia annableae, commonly known as the Annable's grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid to arid environments. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Danthonia annableae is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
