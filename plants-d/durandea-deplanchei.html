Durandea deplanchei

Durandea deplanchei, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions. Its seeds are encased in a hairy, velvety capsule, which aids in seed dispersal.

Durandea deplanchei is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
