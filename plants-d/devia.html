Devia

Devia, commonly known as the common devil's bit, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Devia has a taproot system and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
