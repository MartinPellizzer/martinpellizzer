Dasystephana glauca

Dasystephana glauca, commonly known as the blue-flowered dasystephana, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
