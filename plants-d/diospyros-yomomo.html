Diospyros yomomo

Diospyros yomomo, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and forest edges.

The tree is valued for its fruit, which is consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.
