Drimia lilacina

Drimia lilacina, commonly known as the purple arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a stout, tuberous rhizome and produces striking, violet-colored spatulate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant's inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a showy, purple-pink spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Drimia lilacina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
