Decaschistia ficifolia

Decaschistia ficifolia, commonly known as the fig tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its unique structure, with a thick, woody trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are enclosed within a syconium, a specialized structure that houses the true flowers. These fruits are a vital food source for various animals and birds.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
