Diplazium medogense

Diplazium medogense, commonly known as the Medog spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori surrounded by a protective indusium. Diplazium medogense is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
