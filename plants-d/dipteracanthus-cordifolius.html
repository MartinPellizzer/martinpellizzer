Dipteracanthus cordifolius

Dipteracanthus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically thick and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
