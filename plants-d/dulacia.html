Dulacia

Dulacia, commonly known as the redroot goldenweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Dulacia has slender, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Its roots are often reddish in color, which contributes to its common name.
