Dioscorea birmanica

Dioscorea birmanica, commonly known as Burmese yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local agriculture.
