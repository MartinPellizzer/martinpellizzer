Dracaena taylorii

Dracaena taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's dracaena, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or green, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Dracaena taylorii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
