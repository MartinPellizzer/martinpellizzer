Dioscorea tamoidea subsp. tamoidea

Dioscorea tamoidea subsp.

tamoidea, commonly known as the tamoidea yam, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are the primary economically valuable part, used in traditional cuisines and sometimes for medicinal purposes.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local agricultural systems.
