Diploclinium wallichianum

Diploclinium wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Diploclinium. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
