Dianthus bohemicus

Dianthus bohemicus, commonly known as the Bohemian carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters on tall, slender stems. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits called achenes.

Dianthus bohemicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
