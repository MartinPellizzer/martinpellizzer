Dendrobium dalhousieanum

Dendrobium dalhousieanum, commonly known as the Dalhousie orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a thick, fleshy rhizome that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
