Dalbergia hupeana var. bauhiniifolia

Dalbergia hupeana var. bauhiniifolia, commonly known as the Chinese rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued in traditional craftsmanship. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, and it has compound leaves with a feathery appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is both cultivated and harvested in various parts of Asia.
