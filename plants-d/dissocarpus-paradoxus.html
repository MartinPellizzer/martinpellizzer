Dissocarpus paradoxus

Dissocarpus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical dissociated fruit plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unusual botanical structure, featuring fruits that split into two distinct parts at maturity. The plant has elliptical leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, white flowers during the rainy season. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and dense foliage.

The botanical interest of Dissocarpus paradoxus lies in its peculiar fruit development and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
