Diplotaxis virgata var. multiflora

Diplotaxis virgata var. multiflora, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
