Dimorphanthera amplifolia var. stabilipes

Dimorphanthera amplifolia var. stabilipes, commonly known as the stable-footed broad-leaved orchid, is a unique orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
