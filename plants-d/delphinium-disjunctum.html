Delphinium disjunctum

Delphinium disjunctum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium disjunctum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
