Danthonia villosa var. communis

Danthonia villosa var. communis, commonly known as the hairy danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant features slender, leafy stems with hairy foliage, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence is a panicle, composed of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This grass plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
