Dianthus nicaeensis

Dianthus nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant produces clusters of small, rounded petals that resemble tiny carnations, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus nicaeensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
