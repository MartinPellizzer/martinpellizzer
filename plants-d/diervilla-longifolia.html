Diervilla longifolia

Diervilla longifolia, commonly known as creeping firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Diervilla longifolia is also valued for its ability to attract birds and its hardy nature in various soil conditions.
