Dennstaedtia auriculata

Dennstaedtia auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, silky coating. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its spores are released from small, clustered structures called sorangia, which are embedded in the fronds.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
