Diospyros ferrea var. villosa

Diospyros ferrea var. villosa, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense canopy and rough, bark-like trunk. The leaves are typically elliptical, dark green, and have a leathery texture. Its fruit is a small, yellowish-orange berry that ripens in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
