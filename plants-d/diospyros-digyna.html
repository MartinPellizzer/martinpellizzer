Diospyros digyna

Diospyros digyna, commonly known as the Chinese date or persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine for its high nutritional value.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
