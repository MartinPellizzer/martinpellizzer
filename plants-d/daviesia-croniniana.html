Daviesia croniniana

Daviesia croniniana, commonly known as the Cronin's daviesia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting native pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a sought-after species for native gardens and conservation efforts.
