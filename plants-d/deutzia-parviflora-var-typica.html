Deutzia parviflora var. typica

Deutzia parviflora var. typica, commonly known as the dwarf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and dark green foliage that turns yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers partial to full sun.

This ornamental plant is well-suited for gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
