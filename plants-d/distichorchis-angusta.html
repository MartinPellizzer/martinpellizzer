Distichorchis angusta

Distichorchis angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with two opposite leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Distichorchis angusta is known for its specialized mycorrhizal relationships, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.
