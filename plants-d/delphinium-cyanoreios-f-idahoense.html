Delphinium cyanoreios f. idahoense

Delphinium cyanoreios f. idahoense, commonly known as the Idaho blue larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of blue to violet flowers with prominent white markings. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
