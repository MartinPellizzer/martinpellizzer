Dianthus reuteri

Dianthus reuteri, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, and its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals that are often marked with darker veins. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or on limestone outcrops.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
