Diperium

Diperium, commonly known as the wild daisy or mountain daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It typically features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow central disk. The plants are perennial herbs that often grow in rocky or alpine environments. Diperium species are characterized by their hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

They are native to regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa, where they thrive in sunny, well-drained soils.
