Diasperus mimosoides

Diasperus mimosoides, commonly known as the African mimosa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system for water absorption. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment.
