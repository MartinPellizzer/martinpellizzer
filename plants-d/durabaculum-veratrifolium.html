Durabaculum veratrifolium

Durabaculum veratrifolium, commonly known as the everlasting leaf, is a unique plant characterized by its persistent, waxy leaves that remain green throughout the year.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant's foliage is typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. It produces small, pale yellow flowers during the late spring and early summer months.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens and rockeries.
