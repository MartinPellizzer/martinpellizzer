Dichromena pilosa

Dichromena pilosa, commonly known as the hairy dichromena, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Dichromena pilosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
