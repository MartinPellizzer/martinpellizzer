Dalea versicolor var. argyrostachya

Dalea versicolor var. argyrostachya, commonly known as the silver-stem milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, silver-hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, often in clusters along the stem. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
