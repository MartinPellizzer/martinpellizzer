Dioclea leiophylla

Dioclea leiophylla, commonly known as the smooth-leafed dioclea, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for its unique floral display.
