Dianthus moravicus

Dianthus moravicus, commonly known as the Moravian campion, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, preferring full sun. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are often surrounded by narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Dianthus moravicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
