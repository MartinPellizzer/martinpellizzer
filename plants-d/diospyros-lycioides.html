Diospyros lycioides

Diospyros lycioides, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that ripen in the fall.

Diospyros lycioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
