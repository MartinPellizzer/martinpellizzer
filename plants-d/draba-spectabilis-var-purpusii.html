Draba spectabilis var. purpusii

Draba spectabilis var. purpusii, commonly known as the showy whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, rounded seed pods.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
