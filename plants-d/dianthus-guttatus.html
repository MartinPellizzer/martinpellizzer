Dianthus guttatus

Dianthus guttatus, commonly known as the Sierra pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and its preference for rocky, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Dianthus guttatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
