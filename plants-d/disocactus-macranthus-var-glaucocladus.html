Disocactus macranthus var. glaucocladus

Disocactus macranthus var. glaucocladus, commonly known as the Blue-leafed Easter Cactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is a hybrid between Disocactus macranthus and Disocactus phyllochilus, resulting in unique growth patterns and coloration. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
