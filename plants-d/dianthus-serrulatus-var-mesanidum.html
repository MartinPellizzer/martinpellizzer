Dianthus serrulatus var. mesanidum

Dianthus serrulatus var. mesanidum, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, rocky environments.
