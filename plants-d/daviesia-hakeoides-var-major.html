Daviesia hakeoides var. major

Daviesia hakeoides var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered daviesia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, erect growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a hairy texture, and the plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has adapted to thrive in Mediterranean climate conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
