Dendrobium corniculatum

Dendrobium corniculatum, commonly known as the horned orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments on trees or rocks. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often have a waxy texture to reduce water loss.

Dendrobium corniculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
