Dioscorea articulata

Dioscorea articulata, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, yellow berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Dioscorea articulata is also valued for its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
