Diospyros christophersenii

Diospyros christophersenii, commonly known as the Christensen's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to reddish fruits that ripen in late summer. Its fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed in their unripe state due to their astringent taste.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
