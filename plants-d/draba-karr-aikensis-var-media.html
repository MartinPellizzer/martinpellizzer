Draba karr-aikensis var. media

Draba karr-aikensis var. media, commonly known as the Karr-aik medium winter cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, contributing to biodiversity in high-altitude regions.
