Dorstenia poinsettiifolia var. longicauda

Dorstenia poinsettiifolia var. longicauda, commonly known as the long-tailed poinsettia, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, red bracts that surround small, greenish-yellow flowers, which are typical of the genus Dorstenia. The plant has a distinctive, fleshy, creeping stem that can form a dense, low-growing mat. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season due to its association with festive displays.
