Diasperus velutinus

Diasperus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Diasperus velutinus is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
