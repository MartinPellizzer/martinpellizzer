Disa bivalvata

Disa bivalvata, commonly known as the yellow disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky rhizome and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are characterized by their two-lipped structure and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Disa bivalvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
