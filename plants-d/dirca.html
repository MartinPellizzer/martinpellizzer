Dirca

Dirca, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veined pattern and produces delicate white or yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Dirca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
