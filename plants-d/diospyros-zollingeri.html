Diospyros zollingeri

Diospyros zollingeri, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local communities.
