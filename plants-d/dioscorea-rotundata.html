Dioscorea rotundata

Dioscorea rotundata, commonly known as yam, is a tropical vine native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for food and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been cultivated for thousands of years.

Dioscorea rotundata is also valued for its nutritional content and is a staple in many cuisines.
