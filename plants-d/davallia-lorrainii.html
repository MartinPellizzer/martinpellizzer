Davallia lorrainii

Davallia lorrainii, commonly known as the Lorrain's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, brownish coating, which helps in moisture retention.

Davallia lorrainii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
