Dichanthelium boreale

Dichanthelium boreale, commonly known as the northern dichanthelium, is a grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and tundra. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow leaves, with small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
