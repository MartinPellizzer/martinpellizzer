Didymocarpus uniflorus

Didymocarpus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flowered waterapple, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
