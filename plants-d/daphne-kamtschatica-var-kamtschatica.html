Daphne kamtschatica var. kamtschatica

Daphne kamtschatica var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatake Daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, often appearing in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming period and unique, spicy scent.
