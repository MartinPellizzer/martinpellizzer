Dendrobium tetragonum subsp. cataractarum

Dendrobium tetragonum subsp.

cataractarum, commonly known as the waterfall dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments.

The plant features long, cane-like stems with clustered, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinct, fragrant scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
