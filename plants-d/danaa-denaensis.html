Danaa denaensis

Danaa denaensis, commonly known as the Denas Island fern, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively on the island of Denas in the South Pacific.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant with delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble those of a traditional fern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its leaves are bioluminescent at night, a unique adaptation that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, Danaa denaensis is classified as critically endangered.
