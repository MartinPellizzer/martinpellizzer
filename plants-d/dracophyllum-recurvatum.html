Dracophyllum recurvatum

Dracophyllum recurvatum, commonly known as the zigzag heath, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with leaves that are often twisted or zigzagged in appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates.

Dracophyllum recurvatum is an important component of alpine ecosystems and is often found in heathlands and rocky outcrops.
