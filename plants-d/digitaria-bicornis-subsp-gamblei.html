Digitaria bicornis subsp. gamblei

Digitaria bicornis subsp.

gamblei, commonly known as Gambler's fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and often invasive species in disturbed environments.
