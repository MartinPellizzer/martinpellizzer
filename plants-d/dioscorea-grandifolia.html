Dioscorea grandifolia

Dioscorea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, tuberous roots. Its tubers are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for centuries.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in various indigenous cultures.
