Dischidia subulata subsp. angustata

Dischidia subulata subsp.

angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fig vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, narrow, and pointed leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ability to climb make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.
