Dichelachne crinita var. intermedia

Dichelachne crinita var. intermedia, commonly known as the hairy flat sedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the lower surface. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of wetland habitats.
