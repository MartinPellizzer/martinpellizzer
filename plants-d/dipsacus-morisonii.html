Dipsacus morisonii

Dipsacus morisonii, commonly known as the Morison's teasel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-like structures that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dipsacus morisonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in the production of teasel combs.
