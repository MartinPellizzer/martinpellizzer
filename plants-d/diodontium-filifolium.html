Diodontium filifolium

Diodontium filifolium, commonly known as the threadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has a taproot system and produces a capsule-like fruit that releases tiny seeds with delicate parachutes.

Diodontium filifolium is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is a common sight in early spring gardens.
