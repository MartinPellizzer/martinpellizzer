Dendrobium vexillarius var. elworthyi

Dendrobium vexillarius var. elworthyi, commonly known as the Elworth's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with contrasting red or purple markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for horticultural purposes in tropical and subtropical regions.
