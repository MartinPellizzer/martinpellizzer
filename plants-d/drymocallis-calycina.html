Drymocallis calycina

Drymocallis calycina, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its showy, yellow inflorescences that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands, meadows, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are sturdy and often hairy.

Drymocallis calycina plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late growing season.
