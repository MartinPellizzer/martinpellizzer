Dendrobium orientale

Dendrobium orientale, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often adorned with clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker and more intricate. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium orientale is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
