Dioscorea megaphylla

Dioscorea megaphylla, commonly known as the giant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are often used as a food source in some cultures, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful preparation.

The botanical features of D. megaphylla make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
