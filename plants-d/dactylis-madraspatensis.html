Dactylis madraspatensis

Dactylis madraspatensis, commonly known as the Madras grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Dactylis madraspatensis is valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
