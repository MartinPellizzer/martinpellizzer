Drosera hirtella

Drosera hirtella, commonly known as the hairy sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a sweet-smelling mucilage to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments and has a rosette of narrow, upright leaves. Its flowers are small and usually pink or red, appearing on a single stem.

Drosera hirtella is a hardy species that can survive in various conditions, making it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
