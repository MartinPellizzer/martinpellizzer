Drimia cochlearis

Drimia cochlearis, commonly known as the African lily or narrow-leaved dragon's blood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Drimia cochlearis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in various cultural practices.
