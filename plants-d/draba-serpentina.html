Draba serpentina

Draba serpentina, commonly known as the serpent's root, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, with a bluish-green hue. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in a raceme and bloom in early spring.

Draba serpentina is native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils.
