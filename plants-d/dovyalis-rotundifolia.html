Dovyalis rotundifolia

Dovyalis rotundifolia, commonly known as the sour plum or yellow star apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when ripe, often with a tangy, acidic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Dovyalis rotundifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
