Dioscorea grandis

Dioscorea grandis, commonly known as the giant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, tuberous roots that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are edible when properly prepared and have been used in traditional cuisines for centuries.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in some cultures.
