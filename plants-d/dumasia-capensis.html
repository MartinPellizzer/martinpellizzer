Dumasia capensis

Dumasia capensis, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Dumasia capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
