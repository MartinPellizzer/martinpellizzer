Desmodium caudatum var. latifolium

Desmodium caudatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
