Digitalis purpurea var. rosea

Digitalis purpurea var. rosea, commonly known as the pink foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant belongs to the genus Digitalis, which is known for its cardiac glycosides, though this variety is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and bumblebees.
