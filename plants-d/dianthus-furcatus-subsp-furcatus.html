Dianthus furcatus subsp. furcatus

Dianthus furcatus subsp.

furcatus, commonly known as the forked campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive forked shape at the base.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
