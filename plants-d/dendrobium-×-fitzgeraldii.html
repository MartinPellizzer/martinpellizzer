Dendrobium × fitzgeraldii

Dendrobium × fitzgeraldii, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features long, cane-like stems that can store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a wide range of hues including yellow, orange, and red.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
