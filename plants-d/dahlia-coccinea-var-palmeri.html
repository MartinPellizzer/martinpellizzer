Dahlia coccinea var. palmeri

Dahlia coccinea var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's scarlet dahlia, is a strikingly colorful tuberous-rooted perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features showy, single or semi-double blooms with a prominent central disc, and its foliage consists of heart-shaped, medium-green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

This variety is prized for its bold color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
