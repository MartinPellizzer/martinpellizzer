Diplachne guatemalensis

Diplachne guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Diplachne guatemalensis thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
