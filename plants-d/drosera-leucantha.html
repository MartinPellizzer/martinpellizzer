Drosera leucantha

Drosera leucantha, commonly known as the white sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Drosera leucantha is a low-growing species, typically reaching heights of less than 10 centimeters.
