Dracaena marachiensis

Dracaena marachiensis, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Dracaena marachiensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
