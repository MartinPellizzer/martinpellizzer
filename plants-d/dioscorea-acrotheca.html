Dioscorea acrotheca

Dioscorea acrotheca, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are edible and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
