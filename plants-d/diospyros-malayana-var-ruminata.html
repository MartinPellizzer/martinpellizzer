Diospyros malayana var. ruminata

Diospyros malayana var. ruminata, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into clustered, orange-red fruits. The fruits are typically edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, astringent flavor.
