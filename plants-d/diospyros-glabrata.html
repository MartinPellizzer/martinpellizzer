Diospyros glabrata

Diospyros glabrata, commonly known as the African date palm, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Diospyros glabrata is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional craftsmanship.
