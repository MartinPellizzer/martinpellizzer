Diplotaxis saxatilis var. brevifolia

Diplotaxis saxatilis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and gray-green, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
