Diospyros gracilipes var. parvifolia

Diospyros gracilipes var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves that are usually smaller than those of the standard species. Its flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit is a small, sweet, edible berry that ripens in the fall.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit in its native habitat.
