Dodonaea hispidula var. arida

Dodonaea hispidula var. arida, commonly known as the desert Dodonaea, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. Its woody stems are often branched and can become twisted with age, providing structural support in harsh environments.

This variety is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in semi-arid ecosystems.
