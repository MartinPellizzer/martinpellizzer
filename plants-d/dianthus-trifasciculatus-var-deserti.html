Dianthus trifasciculatus var. deserti

Dianthus trifasciculatus var. deserti, commonly known as the desert pinks, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and early summer. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and full sun, making it a resilient choice for desert landscapes.
