Dyschoriste schiedeana var. decumbens

Dyschoriste schiedeana var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping dyschoriste, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in loose inflorescences, and are typically greenish or yellowish in color.
