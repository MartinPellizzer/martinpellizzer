Dombeya stuhlmannii

Dombeya stuhlmannii, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. These flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Dombeya stuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
