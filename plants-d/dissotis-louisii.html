Dissotis louisii

Dissotis louisii, commonly known as the Louisiana spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
