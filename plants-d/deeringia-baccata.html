Deeringia baccata

Deeringia baccata, commonly known as the red-stemmed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsules as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Deeringia baccata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
