Dipterocarpus woodii

Dipterocarpus woodii, commonly known as the Borneo ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family, which includes many valuable timber species. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
