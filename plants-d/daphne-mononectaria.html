Daphne mononectaria

Daphne mononectaria, commonly known as the Christmas daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is known for its winter blooming habit.

Daphne mononectaria is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique flowering period.
