Dyckia jonesiana

Dyckia jonesiana, commonly known as the Jones' dyckia, is a species of grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fibrous leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant orange or red flowers that attract pollinators. Dyckia jonesiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
