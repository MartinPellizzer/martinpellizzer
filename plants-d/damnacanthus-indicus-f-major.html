Damnacanthus indicus f. major

Damnacanthus indicus f. major, commonly known as the Indian flame flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
