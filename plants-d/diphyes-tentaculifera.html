Diphyes tentaculifera

Diphyes tentaculifera, commonly known as the sea holly, is a marine algae belonging to the order Ectocarpales.

It is characterized by its branching thallus with small, cup-like structures called conceptacles. The plant exhibits a complex life cycle involving both sexual and asexual reproduction. It thrives in shallow coastal waters and is often found attached to rocky substrates.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in marine ecosystems.
