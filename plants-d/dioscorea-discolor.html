Dioscorea discolor

Dioscorea discolor, commonly known as the purple yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, tuberous roots that store nutrients. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its tubers are used as a food source and have a starchy, slightly sweet flavor.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
