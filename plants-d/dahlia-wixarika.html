Dahlia wixarika

Dahlia wixarika, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. Its tuberous roots are used as a food source by indigenous peoples, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.
