Diospyros matheriana

Diospyros matheriana, commonly known as the Mather's persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to red berries that contain one to three seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
