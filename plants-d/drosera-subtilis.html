Drosera subtilis

Drosera subtilis, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Drosera subtilis is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist conditions.
