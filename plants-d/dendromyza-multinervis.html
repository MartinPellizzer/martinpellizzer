Dendromyza multinervis

Dendromyza multinervis, commonly known as the fine-veined mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses a distinctive appearance with small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant's complex network of vascular bundles allows it to efficiently absorb nutrients and water from its host. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a fragrant nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and birds.

The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds, facilitating seed dispersal by animals.
