Digitalis purpurea f. tomentosa

Digitalis purpurea f. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose foxglove, is a cultivated form of the common foxglove.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces tall spikes of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of purple or pink. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
