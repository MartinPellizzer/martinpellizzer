Diplotaxis virgata subsp. vicentina

Diplotaxis virgata subsp.

vicentina, commonly known as the Vicentine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
