Dalbergia lanceolaria var. hemsleyi

Dalbergia lanceolaria var. hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley's rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a delicate pink or white color.

The wood of this species is highly valued for its density and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.
