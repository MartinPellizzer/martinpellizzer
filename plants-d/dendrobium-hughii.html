Dendrobium hughii

Dendrobium hughii, commonly known as the Hargreaves' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that bear numerous lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with pale yellow lips, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium hughii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
