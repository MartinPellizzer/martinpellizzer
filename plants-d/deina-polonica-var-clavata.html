Deina polonica var. clavata

Deina polonica var. clavata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges or stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
