Draconopteris grandidentata

Draconopteris grandidentata, commonly known as the giant huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, broad leaves that are often clustered at the tips. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus, which contains the sporangia for spore production. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is notable for its primitive features and ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.
