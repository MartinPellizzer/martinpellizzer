Digitaria verrucosa

Digitaria verrucosa, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and agricultural areas. The plant has slender, erect stems with a hairy texture, and its leaves are narrow with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Digitaria verrucosa is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
