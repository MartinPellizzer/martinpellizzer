Dendroglossa latifolia

Dendroglossa latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used in traditional remedies.

Dendroglossa latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
