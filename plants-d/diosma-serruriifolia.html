Diosma serruriifolia

Diosma serruriifolia, commonly known as the mountain diosma, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
