Darea inaequalis

Darea inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellow-orange flower head that develops into a crown of seeds. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, while the stems are upright and hairy.

Darea inaequalis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
