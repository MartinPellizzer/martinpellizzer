Dissotis petiolata

Dissotis petiolata, commonly known as the slender mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly wavy margin.

Dissotis petiolata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
