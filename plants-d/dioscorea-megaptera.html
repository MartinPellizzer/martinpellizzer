Dioscorea megaptera

Dioscorea megaptera, commonly known as the giant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, tuberous roots that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
