Dendrolirium erectum

Dendrolirium erectum, commonly known as the upright lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually white or pale pink and emit a subtle fragrance.

Dendrolirium erectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
