Diosma perforata

Diosma perforata, commonly known as the perforate diosma, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, dry habitats such as slopes and cliffs. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive perforated base. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and range in color from white to pink.

Diosma perforata is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
