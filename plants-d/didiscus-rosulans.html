Didiscus rosulans

Didiscus rosulans, commonly known as the rosette daisy, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a waxy coating, and it prefers full sun and well-draining soil.

Didiscus rosulans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
