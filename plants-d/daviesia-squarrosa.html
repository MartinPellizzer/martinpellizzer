Daviesia squarrosa

Daviesia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Daviesia squarrosa is often used in ecological restoration due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
