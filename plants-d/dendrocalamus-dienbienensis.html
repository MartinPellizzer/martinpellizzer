Dendrocalamus dienbienensis

Dendrocalamus dienbienensis, commonly known as the Dien Bien bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its culms are widely used in construction, handicrafts, and as a source of fiber.

Dendrocalamus dienbienensis also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
