Desmodium varians var. typicum

Desmodium varians var. typicum, commonly known as the purple crownbeard, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple hue. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Desmodium varians var. typicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
