Dactylorhiza × viridella

Dactylorhiza × viridella, commonly known as the green orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces tall spikes of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Dactylorhiza × viridella is a perennial plant that requires careful cultivation due to its specific habitat and ecological needs.
