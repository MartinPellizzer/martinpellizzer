Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp. spretum

Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp.

spretum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is often densely packed, forming a dense turf that can stabilize soil in wetland environments.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to waterlogged conditions.
