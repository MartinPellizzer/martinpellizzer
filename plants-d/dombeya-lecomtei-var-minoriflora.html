Dombeya lecomtei var. minoriflora

Dombeya lecomtei var. minoriflora, commonly known as the dwarf flowering dombeya, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Africa.

It features dense, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white or grayish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
