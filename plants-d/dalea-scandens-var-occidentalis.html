Dalea scandens var. occidentalis

Dalea scandens var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea scandens var. occidentalis plays an important role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in native ecosystems.
