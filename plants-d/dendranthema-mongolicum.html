Dendranthema mongolicum

Dendranthema mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces white to pale yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Dendranthema mongolicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and calming properties.
