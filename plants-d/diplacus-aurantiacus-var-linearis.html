Diplacus aurantiacus var. linearis

Diplacus aurantiacus var. linearis, commonly known as the linear honeysuckle, is a variant of the showy honeysuckle native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of vibrant orange to red tubular flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees, due to their nectar-rich structure.

The species is valued in xeriscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
