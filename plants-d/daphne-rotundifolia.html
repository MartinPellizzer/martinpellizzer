Daphne rotundifolia

Daphne rotundifolia, commonly known as roseleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Daphne rotundifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and early spring bloom.
