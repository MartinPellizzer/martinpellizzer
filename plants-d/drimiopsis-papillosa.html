Drimiopsis papillosa

Drimiopsis papillosa, commonly known as the velvet drimiopsis, is a striking evergreen perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, velvety hairs, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Drimiopsis papillosa is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
