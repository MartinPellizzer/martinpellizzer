Diospyros hassleri

Diospyros hassleri, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that ripens to a deep red or orange color, often containing a single seed.
