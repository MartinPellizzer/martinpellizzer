Dalbergia lagosana

Dalbergia lagosana, commonly known as Lagos rosewood, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant typically grows in humid lowland areas and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also used in traditional medicine.
