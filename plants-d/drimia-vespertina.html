Drimia vespertina

Drimia vespertina, commonly known as the evening glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. Drimia vespertina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
