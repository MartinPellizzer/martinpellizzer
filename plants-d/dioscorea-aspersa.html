Dioscorea aspersa

Dioscorea aspersa, commonly known as the rough yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish or greenish flowers and develops tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also valued for their nutritional content and have been utilized in various culinary applications.

Dioscorea aspersa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
