Diervilla japonica var. nivea

Diervilla japonica var. nivea, commonly known as Japanese beauty bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
