Draba gilliesii var. davilae

Draba gilliesii var. davilae, commonly known as the Stewart's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows.

This variety is characterized by its distinct floral structure and is often found in areas with cool, temperate climates.
