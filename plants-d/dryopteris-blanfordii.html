Dryopteris blanfordii

Dryopteris blanfordii, commonly known as Blanford's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Dryopteris genus, which includes various species of wood ferns characterized by their feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky slopes, where it can thrive in cool, humid conditions. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive arrangement of pinnae that give it a unique appearance.

Dryopteris blanfordii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be cultivated in gardens with appropriate care.
