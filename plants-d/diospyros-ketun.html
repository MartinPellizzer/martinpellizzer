Diospyros ketun

Diospyros ketun, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and other hard-wooded trees. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its bark is dark and deeply fissured.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
