Dupatya atrovirens

Dupatya atrovirens, commonly known as the black-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with dark green veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dupatya atrovirens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
