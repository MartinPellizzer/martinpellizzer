Dianthus zonatus var. zonatus

Dianthus zonatus var. zonatus, commonly known as the zoned carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces flowers with five to six petals that are often marked with distinctive color zones. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus zonatus var. zonatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
