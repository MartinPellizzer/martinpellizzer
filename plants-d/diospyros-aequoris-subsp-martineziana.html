Diospyros aequoris subsp. martineziana

Diospyros aequoris subsp.

martineziana, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees.

The plant produces small, orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
