Dendrolobium australe

Dendrolobium australe, commonly known as the Australian sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that are curved and resemble a sickle, giving it its common name. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Dendrolobium australe is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.
