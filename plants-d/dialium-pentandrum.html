Dialium pentandrum

Dialium pentandrum, commonly known as the African plum or African cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by edible drupes with a sweet, acidic pulp.

Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife and is also used in traditional medicine.
