Drupifera oleosa

Drupifera oleosa, commonly known as the oil drupes plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique fruit structure, which consists of a fleshy outer layer surrounding a hard, stony inner core. The plant produces small, oily seeds that are rich in essential oils, making it valuable for both ornamental and industrial purposes. Its growth habit includes shrub-like forms with alternate, simple leaves.

Drupifera oleosa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
