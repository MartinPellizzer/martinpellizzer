Dianthus seguieri subsp. vigoi

Dianthus seguieri subsp.

vigoi, commonly known as the Alaskan pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It features compact, mat-forming growth with dense clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically blue-green, fuzzy, and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cold climates.
