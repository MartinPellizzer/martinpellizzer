Dactylorhiza maculata var. arduennensis

Dactylorhiza maculata var. arduennensis, commonly known as the Arduennian spotted orchid, is a rare orchid species native to specific regions in Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks. It has a complex root system and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
