Diplycosia punctulata

Diplycosia punctulata, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.
