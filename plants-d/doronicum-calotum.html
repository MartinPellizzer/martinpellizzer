Doronicum calotum

Doronicum calotum, commonly known as the Alpine goldcup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Doronicum calotum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
