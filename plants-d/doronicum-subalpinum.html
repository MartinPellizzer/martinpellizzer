Doronicum subalpinum

Doronicum subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Doronicum subalpinum is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to add color to high-altitude gardens.
