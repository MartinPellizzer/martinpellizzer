Diplacus tricolor

Diplacus tricolor, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the mallow family (Malvaceae) and is characterized by its showy, nectar-rich blooms that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Diplacus tricolor typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
