Dicoma nyikensis

Dicoma nyikensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and hairy leaves adapted to conserve water in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, followed by the formation of fluffy seed heads. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources in the dry soil.

Dicoma nyikensis plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing food for local wildlife.
