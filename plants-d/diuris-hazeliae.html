Diuris hazeliae

Diuris hazeliae, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid species belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that are typically golden or yellow in color, with intricate petal structures. The plant is endemic to the southeastern regions of Australia, particularly in New South Wales and Victoria. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys, often growing on rocks or in soil.

Diuris hazeliae is a perennial herb with a short, tuberous rhizome and is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.
