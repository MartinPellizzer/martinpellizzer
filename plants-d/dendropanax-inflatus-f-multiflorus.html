Dendropanax inflatus f. multiflorus

Dendropanax inflatus f. multiflorus, commonly known as the multibloom arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is mostly herbaceous, with a tendency to spread through rhizomes, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

The foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
