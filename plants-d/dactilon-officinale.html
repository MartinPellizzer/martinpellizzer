Dactilon officinale

Dactilon officinale, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Sweet woodruff is known for its strong, pleasant fragrance, which comes from compounds like coumarin.

It is often used in herbal teas and as a decorative plant in gardens.
