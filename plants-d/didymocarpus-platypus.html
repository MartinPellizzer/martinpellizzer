Didymocarpus platypus

Didymocarpus platypus, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use in tropical and subtropical landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
