Dorstenia maoungouensis

Dorstenia maoungouensis, commonly known as the Maoungou mountain fig, is a unique member of the Moraceae family native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
