Danthonia glabra

Danthonia glabra, commonly known as smooth danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and tufted growth habit, often forming dense, low-growing clumps. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Danthonia glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
