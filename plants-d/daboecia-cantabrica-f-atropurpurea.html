Daboecia cantabrica f. atropurpurea

Daboecia cantabrica f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered heath, is a variant of the coastal heath plant native to the Atlantic coast of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-pink flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky, acidic soils along cliff edges and coastal dunes, thriving in harsh, maritime conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are evergreen, providing year-round structure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
