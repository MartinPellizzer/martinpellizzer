Draba crassifolia

Draba crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Draba crassifolia has basal leaves that are broad and fleshy, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers in early spring.

Its short stature and hardy nature make it a resilient species adapted to harsh climatic conditions.
