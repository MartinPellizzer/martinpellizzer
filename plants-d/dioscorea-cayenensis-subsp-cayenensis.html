Dioscorea cayenensis subsp. cayenensis

Dioscorea cayenensis subsp.

cayenensis, commonly known as yam, is a tropical vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae. It is characterized by its climbing habit, with elongated stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces tuberous roots that are the primary edible part, often used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally cultivated for both food and medicinal purposes.
