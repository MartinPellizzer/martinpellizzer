Dendrobium suavissimum

Dendrobium suavissimum, commonly known as the orchid of the moon, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, making it well-suited for tropical climates.

Dendrobium suavissimum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beautiful, long-lasting blooms.
