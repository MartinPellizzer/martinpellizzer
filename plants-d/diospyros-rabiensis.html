Diospyros rabiensis

Diospyros rabiensis, commonly known as the Rabiensian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its fruit is a small, orange to red berry that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its hard, dark wood, which is used in various crafts and construction.
