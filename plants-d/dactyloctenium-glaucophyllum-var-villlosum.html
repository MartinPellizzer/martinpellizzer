Dactyloctenium glaucophyllum var. villlosum

Dactyloctenium glaucophyllum var. villlosum, commonly known as the silky grass or silky dactyloctenium, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, while its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters.

It is often used for forage in livestock feeding due to its high nutritional value.
