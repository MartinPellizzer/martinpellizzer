Digera frutescens

Digera frutescens, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.
