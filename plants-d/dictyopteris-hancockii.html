Dictyopteris hancockii

Dictyopteris hancockii, commonly known as Hancock's feather wort, is a marine brown alga found in coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It has a distinctive morphology with long, branching fronds that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in intertidal zones, where it attaches to rocky substrates using a holdfast. Its reproductive structures, called conceptacles, are located along the fronds and facilitate sexual reproduction.

The species plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
