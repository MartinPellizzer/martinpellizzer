Delphinium korshinskyanum

Delphinium korshinskyanum, commonly known as the Korshinsky's larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
