Disa deckenii

Disa deckenii, commonly known as the Decken's orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, tubular flowers that are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Disa deckenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
