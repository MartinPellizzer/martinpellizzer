Digitalis lutea var. hirsuta

Digitalis lutea var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy yellow foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and contains cardiac glycosides, making it both poisonous and historically significant in medicine.
