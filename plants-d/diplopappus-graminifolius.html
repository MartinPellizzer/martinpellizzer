Diplopappus graminifolius

Diplopappus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaved sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its flowers are typically arranged in a composite head, with ray florets surrounding disk florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow blooms.
