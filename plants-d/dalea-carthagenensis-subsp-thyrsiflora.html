Dalea carthagenensis subsp. thyrsiflora

Dalea carthagenensis subsp.

thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thrysoflora milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets that help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
