Dioscorea hoffa

Dioscorea hoffa, commonly known as Hoffa yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. These tubers are often used in traditional cuisine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
