Daemonorops hygrophila

Daemonorops hygrophila, commonly known as the water fern or swamp palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and robust, clustered growth habit. The plant thrives in wet, shaded environments, often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a leathery texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is cultivated in humid gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
