Delima frangulifolia

Delima frangulifolia, commonly known as the fragrant acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically divided into several narrow segments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters, which bloom in the spring. Delima frangulifolia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
