Diospyros octandra

Diospyros octandra, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when ripe.

The tree is valued for its timber, which is hard and durable, and for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
