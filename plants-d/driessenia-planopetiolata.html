Driessenia planopetiolata

Driessenia planopetiolata, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are upright and branching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar composite flower structures.
