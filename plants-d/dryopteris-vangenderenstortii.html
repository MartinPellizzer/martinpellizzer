Dryopteris vangenderenstortii

Dryopteris vangenderenstortii, commonly known as the Vangenderen's wood fern, is a rare and distinctive species of fern native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often exhibit a pale green or silvery hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in the understory of dense forests where it relies on high humidity and nutrient-rich soil. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, and it reproduces primarily through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this species is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
