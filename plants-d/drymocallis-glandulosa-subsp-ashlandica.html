Drymocallis glandulosa subsp. ashlandica

Drymocallis glandulosa subsp.

ashlandica, commonly known as the Ashland goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States. It is characterized by its yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting native wildlife.
