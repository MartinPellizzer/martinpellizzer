Diplolepis apiculata

Diplolepis apiculata, commonly known as the hooked bramble, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while the stems are thorny and can be invasive in certain regions.

The plant has a complex root system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
