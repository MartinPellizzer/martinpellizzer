Downingia elegans var. brachypetala

Downingia elegans var. brachypetala, commonly known as the dwarf rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

It is known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
