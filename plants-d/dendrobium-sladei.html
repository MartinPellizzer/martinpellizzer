Dendrobium sladei

Dendrobium sladei, commonly known as the Slade's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that produce leaves and flowers sequentially. The plant typically blooms with fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dendrobium sladei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
