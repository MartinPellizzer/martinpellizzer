Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana nothovar. uliginosa

Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana nothovar. uliginosa, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, moist soils and requires a cool, temperate climate for optimal growth. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, dark brown seed pods after flowering.

This nothovar is often found in peat bogs and marshy areas, where it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of these habitats.
