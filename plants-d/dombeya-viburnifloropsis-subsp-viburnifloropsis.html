Dombeya viburnifloropsis subsp.

viburnifloropsis, commonly known as the shrub dombeya, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically oval to elliptical, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
