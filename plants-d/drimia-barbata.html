Drimia barbata

Drimia barbata, commonly known as the hairy spear or African lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a stout, rhizomatous stem that produces several narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant blooms with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that are typically tubular in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Drimia barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
