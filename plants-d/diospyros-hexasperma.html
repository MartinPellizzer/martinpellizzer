Diospyros hexasperma

Diospyros hexasperma, commonly known as the six-ribbed persimmon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. Its fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
