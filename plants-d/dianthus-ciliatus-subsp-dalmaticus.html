Dianthus ciliatus subsp. dalmaticus

Dianthus ciliatus subsp.

dalmaticus, commonly known as the Dalmatian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to white.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional horticulture.
