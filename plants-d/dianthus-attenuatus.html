Dianthus attenuatus

Dianthus attenuatus, commonly known as the slender carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers consist of five petals that are often notched at the edges, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Dianthus attenuatus is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
