Disa equestris var. concinna

Disa equestris var. concinna, commonly known as the Purple Firecracker, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

This variant is distinguished by its vibrant purple and red flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces compact, upright stems with showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer.

The botanical features of Disa equestris var. concinna make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
