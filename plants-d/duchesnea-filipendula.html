Duchesnea filipendula

Duchesnea filipendula, commonly known as false strawberry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with reddish stems and produces small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant is characterized by its edible, red, raspberry-like fruits that appear in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Duchesnea filipendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
