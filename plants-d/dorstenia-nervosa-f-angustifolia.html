Dorstenia nervosa f. angustifolia

Dorstenia nervosa f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed woolly custardapple, is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that are elongated and narrow. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a syncarp, which develops into a fleshy, yellowish-orange structure when mature.

This species is native to tropical regions of Central and South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
