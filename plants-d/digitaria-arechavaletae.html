Digitaria arechavaletae

Digitaria arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Digitaria arechavaletae is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential for use in restoration projects.
