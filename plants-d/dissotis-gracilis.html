Dissotis gracilis

Dissotis gracilis, commonly known as the slender dissotis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
