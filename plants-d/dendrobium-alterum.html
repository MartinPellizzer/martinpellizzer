Dendrobium alterum

Dendrobium alterum, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are linear and arranged spirally along the stem, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium alterum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
