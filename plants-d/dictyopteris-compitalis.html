Dictyopteris compitalis

Dictyopteris compitalis, commonly known as the common sea holly, is a species of brown algae that thrives in coastal environments.

It is characterized by its intricate, branching thallus with a leathery texture and a distinctive, star-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow waters, often forming dense mats on rocky substrates. Its reproductive structures include both sporangia and gametangia, which are essential for its life cycle.

The alga plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
