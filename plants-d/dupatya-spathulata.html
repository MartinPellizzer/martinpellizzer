Dupatya spathulata

Dupatya spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with a central disk. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
