Diplotaxis siifolia subsp. vicentina

Diplotaxis siifolia subsp.

vicentina, commonly known as the Sicilian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing annual herb with erect, branched stems and bright yellow flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
