Drymonia pudica

Drymonia pudica, commonly known as the dwarf pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its small, spiny leaves and clustered yellow flowers make it a distinctive and ornamental species.

Drymonia pudica is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
