Diasperus rotundifolius

Diasperus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved desert bean, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its rounded, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Diasperus rotundifolius is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in regions where it is found.
