Dombeya linearifolia

Dombeya linearifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, adding to its ornamental value.

Dombeya linearifolia is well-suited for cultivation in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
