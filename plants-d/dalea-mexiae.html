Dalea mexiae

Dalea mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Dalea mexiae is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, where it thrives in full sun.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
