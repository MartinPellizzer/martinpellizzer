Dianthus rehmannii

Dianthus rehmannii, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and upright.

Dianthus rehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
