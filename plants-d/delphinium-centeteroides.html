Delphinium centeteroides

Delphinium centeteroides, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
