Dipteracanthus geminiflorus var. procumbens

Dipteracanthus geminiflorus var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate twin-flowered dipteracanthus, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in pairs along the stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
