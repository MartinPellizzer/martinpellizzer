Diospyros greveana subsp. boinensis

Diospyros greveana subsp.

boinensis, commonly known as the Boinen mango, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other notable genera like Diospyros and Spondias.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in summer and is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp.

The species is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruit.
