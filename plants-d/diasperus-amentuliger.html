Diasperus amentuliger

Diasperus amentuliger, commonly known as the woolly burr, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated, spiny fruits called achenes, which are often used as dispersal mechanisms. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, typically found in sandy or rocky soils.

Diasperus amentuliger is native to regions of North America and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
