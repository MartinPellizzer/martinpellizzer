Damasonium alisma

Damasonium alisma, commonly known as narrow-leafed water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, white flowers appear in spikes above the water surface, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.
