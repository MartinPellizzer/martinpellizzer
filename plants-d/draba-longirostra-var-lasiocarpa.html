Draba longirostra var. lasiocarpa

Draba longirostra var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy-flowered whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, basal rosette of leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The variety lasiocarpa is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive texture. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This plant is adapted to cold climates and has a long flowering period, typically in late spring to early summer.
