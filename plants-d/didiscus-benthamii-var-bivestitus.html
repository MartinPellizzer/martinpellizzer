Didiscus benthamii var. bivestitus

Didiscus benthamii var. bivestitus, commonly known as the two-vested didiscus, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a hand. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
