Dodonaea ceratocarpa

Dodonaea ceratocarpa, commonly known as the hairy devil's claw, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, claw-like fruits that are covered in fine hairs. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Dodonaea ceratocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
