Dipteracanthus dejectus

Dipteracanthus dejectus, commonly known as the weeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
