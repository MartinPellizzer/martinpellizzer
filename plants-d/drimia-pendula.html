Drimia pendula

Drimia pendula, commonly known as the hanging lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale purple flowers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Drimia pendula thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
