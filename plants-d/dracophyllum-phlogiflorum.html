Dracophyllum phlogiflorum

Dracophyllum phlogiflorum, commonly known as the flame flower, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
