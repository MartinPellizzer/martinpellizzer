Diospyros leucomelas

Diospyros leucomelas, commonly known as the white-leafed persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
