Danaea trifoliata

Danaea trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Danaea trifoliata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
