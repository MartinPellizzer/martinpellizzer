Dianthus elegans var. actinopetalus

Dianthus elegans var. actinopetalus, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters, with five petals that are often reflexed at the tips.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
