Dianthus ramiger

Dianthus ramiger, commonly known as the everlasting or sweet William, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds.

Dianthus ramiger thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
