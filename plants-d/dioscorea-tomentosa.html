Dioscorea tomentosa

Dioscorea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and develops tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with high humidity.

Dioscorea tomentosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used as a food source in some cultures.
