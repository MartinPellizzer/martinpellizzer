Dioscorea caucensis

Dioscorea caucensis, commonly known as the Caucasian yam, is a perennial vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia. The plant produces tuberous roots that are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. These tubers are typically elongated and have a rough, bark-like exterior.

Dioscorea caucensis is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in herbal medicine.
