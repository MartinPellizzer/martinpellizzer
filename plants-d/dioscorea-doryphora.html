Dioscorea doryphora

Dioscorea doryphora, commonly known as the climbing yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
