Draba dovrensis

Draba dovrensis, commonly known as the Dovren's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic environments. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates with poor soil conditions.

Draba dovrensis is native to regions in Central Asia and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
