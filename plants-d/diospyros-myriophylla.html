Diospyros myriophylla

Diospyros myriophylla, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or serrated, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. Diospyros myriophylla thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
