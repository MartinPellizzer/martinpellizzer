Damburneya mirafloris

Damburneya mirafloris, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has palm-like leaves that are long and feather-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Due to its unique appearance and rapid growth, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
