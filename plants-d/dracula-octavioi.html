Dracula octavioi

Dracula octavioi, commonly known as the Octavio's vampire orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, spurred petals that resemble the fangs of a vampire, giving it its dramatic common name. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Dracula octavioi is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and challenging cultivation requirements.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a flagship species for the conservation of Ecuador's biodiversity.
