Dendrobium vexillarius var. microblepharum

Dendrobium vexillarius var. microblepharum, commonly known as the miniature lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in late winter or early spring, are prized for their ornamental value in horticulture.
