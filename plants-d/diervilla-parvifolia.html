Diervilla parvifolia

Diervilla parvifolia, commonly known as parvifoliate hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with finely toothed margins and turn yellow in the fall.

Diervilla parvifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats.
