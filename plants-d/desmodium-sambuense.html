Desmodium sambuense

Desmodium sambuense, commonly known as African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various traditional cuisines. It also has a fibrous root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Desmodium sambuense is often grown as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value.
