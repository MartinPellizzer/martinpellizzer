Diospyros acapulcensis subsp. mejocotensis

Diospyros acapulcensis subsp.

mejocotensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits.

The fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed when fully ripe, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
