Dalbergia carringtoniana

Dalbergia carringtoniana, commonly known as the Carrington's rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
