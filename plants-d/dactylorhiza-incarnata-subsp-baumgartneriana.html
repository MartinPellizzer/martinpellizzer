Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. baumgartneriana

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

baumgartneriana, commonly known as the Baumann's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in shaded areas with moist, acidic soils.

The plant has a rhizome and produces a single upright stem bearing several linear to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a loose spike, and are characterized by their slender, erect sepals and petals.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
