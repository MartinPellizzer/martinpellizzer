Dahlia yuarezii

Dahlia yuarezii, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its tuberous roots store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in various soil conditions.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.
