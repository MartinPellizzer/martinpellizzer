Delphinium midzorense

Delphinium midzorense, commonly known as the Midzore delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several lance-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

Delphinium midzorense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
