Daedalacanthus ciliatus

Daedalacanthus ciliatus, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The silky tamarind is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
