Digitaria macroblephara

Digitaria macroblephara, commonly known as the large-leaved fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as pastures and disturbed areas. It has fibrous roots and is known for its rapid growth, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
