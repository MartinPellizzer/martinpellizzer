Dontostemon pinnatifidus subsp. pinnatifidus

Dontostemon pinnatifidus subsp.

pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Australia. It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with slender, ribbed stems that often root at the nodes.

The leaves are lance-shaped, pinnately divided, and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy plant for dry landscapes.
