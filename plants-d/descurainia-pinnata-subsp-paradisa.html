Descurainia pinnata subsp. paradisa

Descurainia pinnata subsp.

paradisa, commonly known as paradise flax, is a herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for various garden environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
