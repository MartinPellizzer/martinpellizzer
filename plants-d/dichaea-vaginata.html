Dichaea vaginata

Dichaea vaginata, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of white, yellow, or green.

Dichaea vaginata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
