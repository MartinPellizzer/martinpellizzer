Dryopteris taitoensis

Dryopteris taitoensis, commonly known as the Taito fern, is a species of polypody fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a leathery texture. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Dryopteris genus.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
