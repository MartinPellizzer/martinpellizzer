Dicentra pusilla

Dicentra pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bleeding heart, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that add a soft texture to its appearance. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland settings.

Dicentra pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
