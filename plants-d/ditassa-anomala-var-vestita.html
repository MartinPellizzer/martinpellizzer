Ditassa anomala var. vestita

Ditassa anomala var. vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a tropical vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils make it an invasive species in many parts of the world.
