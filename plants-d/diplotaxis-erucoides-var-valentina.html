Diplotaxis erucoides var. valentina

Diplotaxis erucoides var. valentina, commonly known as the Valentina cabbages, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It produces long, slender pods that contain edible seeds, often used in traditional cuisine.
