Deschampsia congestiformis

Deschampsia congestiformis, commonly known as tufted hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, wiry stems and fine, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
