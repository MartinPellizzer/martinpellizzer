Deschampsia flexuosa var. parviflora

Deschampsia flexuosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple spikelets, which are typically clustered in groups of three along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
