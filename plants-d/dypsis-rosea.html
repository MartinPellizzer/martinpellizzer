Dypsis rosea

Dypsis rosea, commonly known as the pink bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems that resemble bamboo. The plant features bright green, lance-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern around the stem. Dypsis rosea produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by bright red fruits.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
