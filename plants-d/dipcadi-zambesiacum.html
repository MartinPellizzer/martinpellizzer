Dipcadi zambesiacum

Dipcadi zambesiacum, commonly known as the Zambesi lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils near rivers and wetlands. Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
