Dioscorea obtusifolia var. philippii

Dioscorea obtusifolia var. philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers and develops tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it distinct from other yam species, and it is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
