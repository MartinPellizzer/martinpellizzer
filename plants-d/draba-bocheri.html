Draba bocheri

Draba bocheri, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in harsh, cold environments. Draba bocheri has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

It is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
