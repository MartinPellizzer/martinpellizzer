Dianthus × varians

Dianthus × varians, commonly known as the double carnation, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Dianthus caryophyllus and Dianthus chinensis.

It is characterized by its showy, double flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and has a compact, bushy habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Dianthus × varians is known for its long blooming period, often from spring through early autumn.
