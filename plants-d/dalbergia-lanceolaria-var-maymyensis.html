Dalbergia lanceolaria var. maymyensis

Dalbergia lanceolaria var. maymyensis, commonly known as the Maymyen rosewood, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a deep green color. The flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators, while the fruit is a pod containing several seeds.

This species is also noted for its slow growth and is often used in traditional woodworking and furniture crafting.
