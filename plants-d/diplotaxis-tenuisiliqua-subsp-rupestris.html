Diplotaxis tenuisiliqua subsp. rupestris

Diplotaxis tenuisiliqua subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock wall gooseberry, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to red berries. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and vibrant fruit, which also attract birds and other wildlife.
