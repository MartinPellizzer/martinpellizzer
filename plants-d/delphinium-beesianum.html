Delphinium beesianum

Delphinium beesianum, commonly known as the Tibetan larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom in late summer.

Delphinium beesianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its toxic properties.
