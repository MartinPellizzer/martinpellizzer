Diospyros udaiyanii

Diospyros udaiyanii, commonly known as the Nilgiri persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and pale underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully mature.

Diospyros udaiyanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of fruit in its native habitat.
