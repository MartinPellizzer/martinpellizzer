Dipterocarpus geniculatus

Dipterocarpus geniculatus, commonly known as the hill dipterocarp, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its buttressed roots and thick, flaky bark. The tree produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

This species plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
