Doliocarpus grandiflorus var. pubens

Doliocarpus grandiflorus var. pubens, commonly known as the hairy large-flowered doliocarpus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
