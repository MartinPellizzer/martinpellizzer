Dianthus carthusianorum f. pumilus

Dianthus carthusianorum f. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a compact, low-growing plant with dense clusters of small, showy flowers.

It typically features pink to red petals with a white or pale pink center, and its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It is a perennial that can spread through stolons, forming a mat-like growth.
