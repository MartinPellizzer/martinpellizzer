Dendrophthora brachyclada

Dendrophthora brachyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

Its leaves are small, leathery, and often clustered near the base of the stem.

The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are important for local wildlife.
