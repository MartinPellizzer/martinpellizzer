Delphinium tomentellum var. micranthum

Delphinium tomentellum var. micranthum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
