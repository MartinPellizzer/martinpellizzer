Dubyaea chimiliensis

Dubyaea chimiliensis, commonly known as the Chimilis plant, is a rare and unique species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Dubyaea chimiliensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
