Diplachne nana

Diplachne nana, commonly known as dwarf flatgrass, is a small perennial grass native to Australia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of loose, spike-like clusters of tiny flowers.

Diplachne nana is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low rainfall.
