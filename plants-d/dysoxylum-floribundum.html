Dysoxylum floribundum

Dysoxylum floribundum, commonly known as the tropical flowering tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth surface with a waxy texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
