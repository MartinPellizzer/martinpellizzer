Dichanthelium annulum

Dichanthelium annulum, commonly known as the ringhopper, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive ring-like nodes on its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
