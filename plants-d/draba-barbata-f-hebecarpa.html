Draba barbata f. hebecarpa

Draba barbata f. hebecarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh climates and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
