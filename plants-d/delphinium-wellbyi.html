Delphinium wellbyi

Delphinium wellbyi, commonly known as the wellby's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium wellbyi is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
