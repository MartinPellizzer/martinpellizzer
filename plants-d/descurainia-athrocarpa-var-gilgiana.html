Descurainia athrocarpa var. gilgiana

Descurainia athrocarpa var. gilgiana, commonly known as Gilgian stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and the plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is a common trait among plants in the Brassicaceae family.
