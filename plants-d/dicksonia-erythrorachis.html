Dicksonia erythrorachis

Dicksonia erythrorachis, commonly known as the red-stemmed tree fern, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk that becomes reddish in color with age. The plant has a crown of feathery, compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fronds are arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dicksonia erythrorachis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
