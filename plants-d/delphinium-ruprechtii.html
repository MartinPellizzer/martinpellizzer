Delphinium ruprechtii

Delphinium ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are showy and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Delphinium ruprechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
