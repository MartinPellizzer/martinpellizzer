Danaea leussinkiana

Danaea leussinkiana, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with bright red veins against a deep green background. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Danaea leussinkiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
