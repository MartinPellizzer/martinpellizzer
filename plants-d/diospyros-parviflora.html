Diospyros parviflora

Diospyros parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ebony, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture.
