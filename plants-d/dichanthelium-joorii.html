Dichanthelium joorii

Dichanthelium joorii, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive growth habit that allows it to colonize disturbed or waterlogged areas.

It plays an important ecological role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
