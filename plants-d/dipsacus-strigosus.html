Dipsacus strigosus

Dipsacus strigosus, commonly known as the bristly teasel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, purple to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Dipsacus strigosus is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
