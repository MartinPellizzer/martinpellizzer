Damironia

Damironia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of fleshy, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Damironia species are typically evergreen and thrive in humid, well-drained soil conditions. They are popular as houseplants due to their compact size and ability to bloom year-round.

The genus includes several species, such as Damironia glabra and Damironia macrophylla, which are valued for their ornamental appeal.
