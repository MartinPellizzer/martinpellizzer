Dendrocalamus farinosus f. flavostriatus

Dendrocalamus farinosus f. flavostriatus, commonly known as the golden-striped bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow stripes on the culms, which distinguish it from other members of the Dendrocalamus genus. The plant grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its culms are tall and slender, reaching heights of up to 20 meters, and are used in construction and handicrafts.

The botanical features of this bamboo make it both ornamental and economically valuable in its native habitat.
