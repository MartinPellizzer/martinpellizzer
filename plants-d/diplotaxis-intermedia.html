Diplotaxis intermedia

Diplotaxis intermedia, commonly known as the African bishop's weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and produces small, oblong seeds enclosed in a hairy capsule. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptability to harsh climates.
