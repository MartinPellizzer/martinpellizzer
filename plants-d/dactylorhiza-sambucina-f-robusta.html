Dactylorhiza sambucina f. robusta

Dactylorhiza sambucina f. robusta, commonly known as the robust autumn orchid, is a rhizomatous orchid native to parts of Europe and northwest Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heaths. The plant features tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale pink to purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a complex root system and a distinctive floral structure typical of the Orchidaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
