Draba viperensis

Draba viperensis, commonly known as the Viper's Bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Draba viperensis is known for its delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
