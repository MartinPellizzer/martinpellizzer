Digitalis purpurea var. mauretanica

Digitalis purpurea var. mauretanica, commonly known as the Mauretanian foxglove, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
