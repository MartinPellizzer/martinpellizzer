Didymocarpus robinsonii

Didymocarpus robinsonii, commonly known as the Solomon Islands fig, is a small evergreen tree native to the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, green fruits that mature to a yellowish color. The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
