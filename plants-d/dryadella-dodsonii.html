Dryadella dodsonii

Dryadella dodsonii, commonly known as the dwarf dryad, is a rare and unique plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and its role in supporting local ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
