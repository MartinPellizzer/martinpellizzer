Dendrobium scariosum

Dendrobium scariosum, commonly known as the scaly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are covered in a dense, scaly cuticle, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinct three-lobed lip.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
