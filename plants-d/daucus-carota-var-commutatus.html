Daucus carota var. commutatus

Daucus carota var. commutatus, commonly known as the bird's nest carrot, is a variant of the wild carrot.

It is characterized by its unique, clustered inflorescence that resembles a bird's nest, with small white flowers arranged in a tight, umbel-like structure. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild carrot-like aroma.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
