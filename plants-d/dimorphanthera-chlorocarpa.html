Dimorphanthera chlorocarpa

Dimorphanthera chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-fruit orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
