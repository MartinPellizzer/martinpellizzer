Dulacia grandiflora

Dulacia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered dulacia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Dulacia grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
