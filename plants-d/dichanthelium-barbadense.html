Dichanthelium barbadense

Dichanthelium barbadense, commonly known as the Bahama grass, is a perennial grass native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
