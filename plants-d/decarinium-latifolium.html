Decarinium latifolium

Decarinium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf decarinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its upright stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the late spring to early summer season. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.

Decarinium latifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
