Dalea viridiflora

Dalea viridiflora, commonly known as greenflower milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. Dalea viridiflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
