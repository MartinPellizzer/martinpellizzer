Delphinium pauperculum

Delphinium pauperculum, commonly known as the poor larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium pauperculum is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
