Digitaria divulsa

Digitaria divulsa, commonly known as the slender digitgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Africa and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in open grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, showing a preference for well-drained environments. Its slender, erect stems are often branched, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
