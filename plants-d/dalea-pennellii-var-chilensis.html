Dalea pennellii var. chilensis

Dalea pennellii var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean burro's tail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its foliage consists of small, fleshy, succulent leaves that help conserve water.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
