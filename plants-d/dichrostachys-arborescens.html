Dichrostachys arborescens

Dichrostachys arborescens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
