Delphinium obcordatilimbum var. minus

Delphinium obcordatilimbum var. minus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved larkspur, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow, ovate, and often have a distinctive obcordate shape at the base. This variety is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
