Dalbergaria medicinalis

Dalbergaria medicinalis, commonly known as the medicinal dalbergia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical structure of Dalbergaria medicinalis supports its role in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
