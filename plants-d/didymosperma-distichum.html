Didymosperma distichum

Didymosperma distichum, commonly known as the two-ranked grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, two-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically arranged in two vertical rows, giving it its common name.

Didymosperma distichum is often found in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
