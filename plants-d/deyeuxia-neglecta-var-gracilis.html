Deyeuxia neglecta var. gracilis

Deyeuxia neglecta var. gracilis, commonly known as slender false rice, is a grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland and riparian ecosystems.
