Dianthera candicans

Dianthera candicans, commonly known as the candleflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Dianthera candicans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
