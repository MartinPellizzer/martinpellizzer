Ditaxis polymorpha var. buttnerioides

Ditaxis polymorpha var. buttnerioides, commonly known as the Butterner's Ditaxis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers, which are borne on short pedicels. Its botanical characteristics include a distinctive branching pattern and the presence of glandular hairs on the stems and leaves.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
