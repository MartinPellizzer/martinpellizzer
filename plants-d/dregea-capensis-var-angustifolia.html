Dregea capensis var. angustifolia

Dregea capensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dregea, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
