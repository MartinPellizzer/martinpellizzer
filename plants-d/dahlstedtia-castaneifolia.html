Dahlstedtia castaneifolia

Dahlstedtia castaneifolia, commonly known as the red-twigged dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that become prominent in winter, contrasting with its dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Its foliage turns a vibrant yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant landscapes.
