Diedropetala

Diedropetala, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows.

Its unique floral structure and delicate beauty make it a sought-after species among botanists and gardeners alike.
