Dahlia dissecta var. sublignosa

Dahlia dissecta var. sublignosa, commonly known as the silverleaf dahlia, is a striking perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant has a bushy growth habit with deeply cut, fern-like foliage that adds texture to the garden. Its leaves are typically silver or gray-green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental value and hardiness.
