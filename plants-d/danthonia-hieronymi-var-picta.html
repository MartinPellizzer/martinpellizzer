Danthonia hieronymi var. picta

Danthonia hieronymi var. picta, commonly known as the Painted Danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful inflorescence that displays a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
