Dicranolepis laciniata

Dicranolepis laciniata, commonly known as the fringed rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocky or wooded substrates. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Dicranolepis laciniata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
