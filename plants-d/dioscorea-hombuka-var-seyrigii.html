Dioscorea hombuka var. seyrigii

Dioscorea hombuka var. seyrigii, commonly known as the Hombuka yam, is a tuberous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are used as a food source and have been traditionally utilized in various regions for their nutritional value.

The species is notable for its adaptability to different soil types and its role in local agriculture.
