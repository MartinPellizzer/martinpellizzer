Dendrobium curvicaule

Dendrobium curvicaule, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of long, narrow leaves and is famous for its vibrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
