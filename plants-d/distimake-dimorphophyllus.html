Distimake dimorphophyllus

Distimake dimorphophyllus, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant's leaves are divided into numerous small, needle-like segments, which help increase its surface area for photosynthesis. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, a trait common among many ferns.

Its distinctive morphology and ecological role make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
