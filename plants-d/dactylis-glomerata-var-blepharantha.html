Dactylis glomerata var. blepharantha

Dactylis glomerata var. blepharantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet vernal grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, often blue-green foliage.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple, adding ornamental value. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

This variety is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
