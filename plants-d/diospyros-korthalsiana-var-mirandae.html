Diospyros korthalsiana var. mirandae

Diospyros korthalsiana var. mirandae, commonly known as the Mirandana or Philippine persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
