Diastema caracasanum

Diastema caracasanum, commonly known as the Caracasa diastema, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known to attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Diastema caracasanum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and vibrant floral display.
