Diospyros gomeziorum

Diospyros gomeziorum, commonly known as the Gomez's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common persimmon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a small, yellow-orange berry that ripens in the dry season and is edible when fully mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
