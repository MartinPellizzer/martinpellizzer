Dipterocarpus angustifolius

Dipterocarpus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and produces heavy, woody fruits that contain seeds. The plant is part of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is often found in lowland rainforests. It plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its habitat, providing habitat and food for various species.

The tree is also valued for its durable timber, which has been historically used in construction and furniture.
