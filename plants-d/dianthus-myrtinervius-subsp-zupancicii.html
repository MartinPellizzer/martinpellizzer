Dianthus myrtinervius subsp. zupancicii

Dianthus myrtinervius subsp.

zupancicii, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to white.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green hue, and the stems are upright and branching. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
