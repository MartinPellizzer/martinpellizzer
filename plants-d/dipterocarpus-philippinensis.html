Dipterocarpus philippinensis

Dipterocarpus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine dipterocarp, is a large evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its buttressed roots and tall, straight trunk. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange-red aril. It thrives in lowland tropical rainforests and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of these ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species.
