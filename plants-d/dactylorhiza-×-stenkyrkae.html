Dactylorhiza × stenkyrkae

Dactylorhiza × stenkyrkae, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous root system and typically grows in wet, peaty or boggy habitats. The plant produces tall, erect stems with linear leaves and bears clusters of pale green to brownish flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a complex floral structure with three sepals and three petals, with the lip being distinctive in shape.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in moist, nutrient-poor environments.
