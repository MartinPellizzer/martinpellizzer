Delphinium taliense var. pubipes

Delphinium taliense var. pubipes, commonly known as the hairy delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in racemes, which are typically arranged in a loose, elongated cluster. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that have a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name.

Delphinium taliense var. pubipes prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.
