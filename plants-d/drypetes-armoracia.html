Drypetes armoracia

Drypetes armoracia, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.
