Dupontia fisheri var. aristata

Dupontia fisheri var. aristata, commonly known as the desert sandburs, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like structures that encase its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
