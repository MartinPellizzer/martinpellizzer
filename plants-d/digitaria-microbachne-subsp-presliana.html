Digitaria microbachne subsp. presliana

Digitaria microbachne subsp.

presliana, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil.
