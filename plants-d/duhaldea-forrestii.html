Duhaldea forrestii

Duhaldea forrestii, commonly known as the Tibetan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Tibet and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Duhaldea forrestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
