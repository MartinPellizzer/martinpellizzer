Daphne micrantha

Daphne micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Daphne micrantha is also noted for its toxicity, with all parts of the plant containing irritants that can be harmful to humans and animals.
