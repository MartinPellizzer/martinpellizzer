Delphinium zhangii

Delphinium zhangii, commonly known as the Zhang's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided foliage that gives it a delicate appearance.

Delphinium zhangii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic alkaloids that require careful handling.
