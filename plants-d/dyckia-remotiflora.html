Dyckia remotiflora

Dyckia remotiflora, commonly known as the remote-flowered dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces inflorescences of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Dyckia remotiflora is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive in full sun with minimal water.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
