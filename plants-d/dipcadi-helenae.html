Dipcadi helenae

Dipcadi helenae, commonly known as the Helen's squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces a bulb that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. It typically blooms in spring and is adapted to well-drained soils.
