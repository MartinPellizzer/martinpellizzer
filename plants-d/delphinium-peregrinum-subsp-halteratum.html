Delphinium peregrinum subsp. halteratum

Delphinium peregrinum subsp.

halteratum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, typical of many delphinium species. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine plant collections.
