Discocactus bahiensis subsp. gracilis

Discocactus bahiensis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender discocactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil. It features cylindrical stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color, covered with small, white spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This subspecies is appreciated for its unique appearance and ease of care in desert gardens.
