Darwinia laxifolia

Darwinia laxifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Darwinia laxifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
