Dioscorea riedelii

Dioscorea riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary vegetative structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some indigenous communities.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.
