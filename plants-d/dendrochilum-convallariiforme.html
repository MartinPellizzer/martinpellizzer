Dendrochilum convallariiforme

Dendrochilum convallariiforme, commonly known as the grape-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces aerial pseudobulbs covered with a thick, waxy layer. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often clustered at the base, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. The orchid's flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often yellow or greenish.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
