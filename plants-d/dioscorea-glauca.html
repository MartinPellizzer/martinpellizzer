Dioscorea glauca

Dioscorea glauca, commonly known as the blue yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its tubers, which are the primary edible part, are often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in various indigenous cultures.
