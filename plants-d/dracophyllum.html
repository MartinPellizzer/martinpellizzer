Dracophyllum

Dracophyllum, commonly known as the mistletoe shrub, is a genus of evergreen shrubs belonging to the heath family (Ericaceae).

These plants are typically found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Dracophyllum species are characterized by their small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The genus includes several species, such as Dracophyllum arboreum and Dracophyllum laetum, which are native to Australia and New Zealand.

These plants are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their ornamental appeal.
