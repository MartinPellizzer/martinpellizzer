Diceratotheca bracteolata

Diceratotheca bracteolata, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Diceratotheca bracteolata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
