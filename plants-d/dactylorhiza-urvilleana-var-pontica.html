Dactylorhiza urvilleana var. pontica

Dactylorhiza urvilleana var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and threatened species in many regions.
