Disperis anthoceros var. anthoceros

Disperis anthoceros var. anthoceros, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its short, horizontal rhizomes. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or forest margins.
