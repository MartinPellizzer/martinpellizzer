Diasperus odontadenius

Diasperus odontadenius, commonly known as the African sand dollar, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to sandy soils and drought conditions.
