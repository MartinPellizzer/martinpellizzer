Dubouzetia dentata

Dubouzetia dentata, commonly known as the serrated leaf plant, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded areas.

Dubouzetia dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
