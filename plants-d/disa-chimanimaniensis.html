Disa chimanimaniensis

Disa chimanimaniensis, commonly known as the Chimanimani Disa, is a striking orchid native to the Eastern Cape province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
