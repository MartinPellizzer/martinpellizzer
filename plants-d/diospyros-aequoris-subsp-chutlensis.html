Diospyros aequoris subsp. chutlensis

Diospyros aequoris subsp.

chutlensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
