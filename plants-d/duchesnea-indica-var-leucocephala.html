Duchesnea indica var. leucocephala

Duchesnea indica var. leucocephala, commonly known as white wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped leaflet and produces small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are prostrate or ascending, often rooting at the nodes.

The species is noted for its edible leaves, which have a mild, tangy flavor similar to sorrel.
