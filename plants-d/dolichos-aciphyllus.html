Dolichos aciphyllus

Dolichos aciphyllus, commonly known as the leafless dolichos, is a unique leguminous plant characterized by its reduced foliage.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly in countries like South Africa and Namibia. The plant features slender, climbing stems with small, often inconspicuous leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

Due to its sparse leaf structure, it is well adapted to arid environments where water conservation is crucial.
