Dischidia punctata

Dischidia punctata, commonly known as the spotted vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, raised spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it well adapted to its natural habitat.

Dischidia punctata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
