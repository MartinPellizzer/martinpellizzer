Dendrobium rhododiodes

Dendrobium rhododiodes, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant features long, cane-like stems with numerous fleshy leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with red veins. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a deep red or purple pattern, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Dendrobium rhododiodes requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to flourish.
