Dipsacus bulgaricus

Dipsacus bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian teasel, is a biennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, velvety leaves and cone-shaped flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems that are often branched. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are yellow to orange in color and are surrounded by bracts.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
