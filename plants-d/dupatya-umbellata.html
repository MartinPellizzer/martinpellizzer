Dupatya umbellata

Dupatya umbellata, commonly known as the white sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in a loose raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi.

Dupatya umbellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
