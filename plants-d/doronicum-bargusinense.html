Doronicum bargusinense

Doronicum bargusinense, commonly known as the Bargusin primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
