Didymostigma trichanthera

Didymostigma trichanthera, commonly known as the yellow star sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Didymostigma trichanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
