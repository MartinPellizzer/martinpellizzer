Dicliptera australis

Dicliptera australis, commonly known as the Australian flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among the foliage. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can be beneficial for soil stabilization.

Dicliptera australis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to colonize disturbed wetland sites.
