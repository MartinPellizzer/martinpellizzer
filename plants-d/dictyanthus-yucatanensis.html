Dictyanthus yucatanensis

Dictyanthus yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or limestone soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
