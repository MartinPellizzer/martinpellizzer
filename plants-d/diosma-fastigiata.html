Diosma fastigiata

Diosma fastigiata, commonly known as the upright diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Diosma fastigiata is typically found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
