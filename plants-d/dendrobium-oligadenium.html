Dendrobium oligadenium

Dendrobium oligadenium, commonly known as the orchid with few flowers, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with multiple stems that grow horizontally and produce new shoots at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are long and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Dendrobium oligadenium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
