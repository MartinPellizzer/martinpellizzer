Datura stramonium var. tatula

Datura stramonium var. tatula, commonly known as the Turkish angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. The plant produces large, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in disturbed habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and requires caution when handling.
