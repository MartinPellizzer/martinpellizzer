Draba × mixta

Draba × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Draba × mixta is a naturally occurring hybrid between Draba incana and Draba nemorosa, showcasing genetic diversity within the genus.

Its morphology and ecological niche reflect its evolutionary adaptations to harsh mountainous habitats.
