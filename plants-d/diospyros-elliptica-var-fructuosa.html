Diospyros elliptica var. fructuosa

Diospyros elliptica var. fructuosa, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to red fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The fruit is valued for its sweet taste and is used in traditional cuisines across its native range.
