Draba glabrata

Draba glabrata, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia, including parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, and its leaves are basal, simple, and hairy.

Draba glabrata is often found in mountainous areas and is known for its resilience in extreme environments.
