Diplostephium rhomboidale var. pauciflorum

Diplostephium rhomboidale var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed diplostephium, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called corymbs. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
