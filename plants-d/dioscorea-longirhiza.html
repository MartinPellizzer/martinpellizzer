Dioscorea longirhiza

Dioscorea longirhiza, commonly known as Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used medicinally. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal value.
