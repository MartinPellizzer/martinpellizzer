Diplazium arborescens

Diplazium arborescens, commonly known as the tree fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a central trunk and a crown of fronds. The plant has a rhizome that grows underground, supporting the upright stem. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive pattern of sori on the underside.

Diplazium arborescens thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
