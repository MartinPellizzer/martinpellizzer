Dizonium

Dizonium, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant yellow fruits that resemble small tomatoes. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical name, *Physalis peruviana*, reflects its origin and the distinctive papery calyx that encases its fruit.

Dizonium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which have a sweet, tangy flavor.
