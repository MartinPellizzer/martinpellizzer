Dendrobium trifolium

Dendrobium trifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white during the summer months. Its leaves are typically arranged in three at each node, giving it the species name "trifolium.".

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
