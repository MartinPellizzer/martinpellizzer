Doria laureola

Doria laureola, commonly known as the golden laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
