Draba petraea

Draba petraea, commonly known as the rock chickweed, is a small flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant has a low-growing habit with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Draba petraea is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
