Dendropanax inflatus f. paniculatus

Dendropanax inflatus f. paniculatus, commonly known as the paniculate sycamore, is a variant of the inflated sycamore, native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its alternate, simple, and often toothed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color, attracting various bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
