Draba verna var. major

Draba verna var. major, commonly known as the spring whitlowgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features a basal rosette of linear to lanceolate leaves and produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and along streambanks. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.

Draba verna var. major is noted for its early blooming habit, making it an important early indicator species in ecological studies.
