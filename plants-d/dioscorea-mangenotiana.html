Dioscorea mangenotiana

Dioscorea mangenotiana, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are used as a food source and have a starchy, sweet flavor.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
