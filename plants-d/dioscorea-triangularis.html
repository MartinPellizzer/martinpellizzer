Dioscorea triangularis

Dioscorea triangularis, commonly known as the triangular yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped stems. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used as a food source in various cultures. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are unisexual and typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
