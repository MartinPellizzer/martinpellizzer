Delphinium camporum

Delphinium camporum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in racemes, and are characterized by their spurred petals.

Delphinium camporum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
