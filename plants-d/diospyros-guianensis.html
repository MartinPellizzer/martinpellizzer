Diospyros guianensis

Diospyros guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese persimmon, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a globose, fleshy drupe with a sweet, aromatic pulp when ripe.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
