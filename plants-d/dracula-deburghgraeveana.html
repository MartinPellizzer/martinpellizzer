Dracula deburghgraeveana

Dracula deburghgraeveana, commonly known as the Dragon's Blood Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dark red to purple petals and a unique, elongated lip that resembles a dragon's tongue, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, showcasing its epiphytic nature. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic visual element to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
