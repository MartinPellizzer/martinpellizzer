Diesingia scandens

Diesingia scandens, commonly known as the climbing false indigo, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
