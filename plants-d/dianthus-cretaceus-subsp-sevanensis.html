Dianthus cretaceus subsp. sevanensis

Dianthus cretaceus subsp.

sevanensis, commonly known as the Caucasian carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and features fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
