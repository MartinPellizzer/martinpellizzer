Drabella muralis

Drabella muralis, commonly known as the wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found along walls and in dry, sunny locations. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, pink, and purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are linear and hairy, contributing to its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Drabella muralis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to colonize difficult sites.
