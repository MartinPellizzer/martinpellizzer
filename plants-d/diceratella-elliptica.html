Diceratella elliptica

Diceratella elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic horned grass, is a small, perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, typically during the rainy season. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
