Didymocarpus robustus

Didymocarpus robustus, commonly known as the rock apple or wild mango, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in local cuisines.
