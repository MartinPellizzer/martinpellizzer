Draba gilliesii f. araucana

Draba gilliesii f. araucana, commonly known as the Araucanian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its compact rosette of basal leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to the Andes mountains of South America, it thrives in rocky, alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its foliage is typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps protect it from harsh environmental conditions.
