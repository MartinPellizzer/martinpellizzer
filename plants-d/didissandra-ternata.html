Didissandra ternata

Didissandra ternata, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but have a distinctive, sometimes bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Didissandra ternata has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
