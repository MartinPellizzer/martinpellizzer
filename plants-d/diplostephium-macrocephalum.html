Diplostephium macrocephalum

Diplostephium macrocephalum, commonly known as the giant cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 6 feet tall, making it a striking feature in wetland environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
