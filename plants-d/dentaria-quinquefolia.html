Dentaria quinquefolia

Dentaria quinquefolia, commonly known as five-leafed toothplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, toothed leaves and produces small white or purple flowers in early spring. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and are visited by various pollinators.

The plant is adapted to moist, open habitats and is often found in woodlands and wet meadows.
