Dianthus subacaulis var. brachyanthus

Dianthus subacaulis var. brachyanthus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinctive fringed appearance. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
