Diospyros letestui

Diospyros letestui, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, orange-red berry that is edible and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
