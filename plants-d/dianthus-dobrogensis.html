Dianthus dobrogensis

Dianthus dobrogensis, commonly known as the Dobrogan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Dobruja region of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Dianthus dobrogensis is a rare and endemic species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
