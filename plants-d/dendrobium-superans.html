Dendrobium superans

Dendrobium superans, commonly known as the orchid of the sky, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Dendrobium superans thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
