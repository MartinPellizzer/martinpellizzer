Didymocarpus glandulosus

Didymocarpus glandulosus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with glandular hairs, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
