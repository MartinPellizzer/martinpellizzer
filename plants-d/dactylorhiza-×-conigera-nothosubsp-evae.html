Dactylorhiza × conigera nothosubsp. evae

Dactylorhiza × conigera nothosubsp.

evae, commonly known as the Eave's marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.

Botanically, it exhibits features typical of the Dactylorhiza genus, including a rhizomatous root system and a complex floral structure.
