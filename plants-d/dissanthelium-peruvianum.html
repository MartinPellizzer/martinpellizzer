Dissanthelium peruvianum

Dissanthelium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
