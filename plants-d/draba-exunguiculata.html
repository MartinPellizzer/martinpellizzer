Draba exunguiculata

Draba exunguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitlow grass, is a small perennial herb belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Draba exunguiculata is known for its resilience to cold temperatures and is a common component of high-altitude vegetation.
