Deutzia fauriei

Deutzia fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features slender, arching branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Deutzia fauriei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its showy floral display.
