Dysoxylum alliaceum var. laxiflorum

Dysoxylum alliaceum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered dysoxylum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
