Diospyros johnstoniana

Diospyros johnstoniana, commonly known as the Johnston's persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony trees. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating, while its bark is typically smooth with a dark brown to blackish color.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
