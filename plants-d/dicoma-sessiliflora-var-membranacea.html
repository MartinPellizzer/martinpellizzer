Dicoma sessiliflora var. membranacea

Dicoma sessiliflora var. membranacea, commonly known as the Cape fynbos.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. Its leaves are often fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
