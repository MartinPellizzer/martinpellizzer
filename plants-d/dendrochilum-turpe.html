Dendrochilum turpe

Dendrochilum turpe, commonly known as the Turpentine Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom during the wet season, often in the summer months.

Dendrochilum turpe is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
