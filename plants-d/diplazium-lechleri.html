Diplazium lechleri

Diplazium lechleri, commonly known as the "sacha inchi" or "omega-3 plant," is a climbing fern native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and pinnate leaves. The plant is notable for its seeds, which are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, making it valuable for nutritional purposes. Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various rainforest organisms.

Due to its unique properties, it is also studied for potential medicinal and agricultural applications.
