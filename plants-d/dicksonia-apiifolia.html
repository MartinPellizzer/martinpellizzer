Dicksonia apiifolia

Dicksonia apiifolia, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a unique reproductive structure called a sporangium, which produces and releases spores for propagation. Its fronds are bioluminescent in the dark, a trait that aids in attracting pollinators.

Dicksonia apiifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.
