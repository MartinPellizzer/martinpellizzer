Desmodium hamulatum

Desmodium hamulatum, commonly known as the hookleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, round legumes that contain the seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Desmodium hamulatum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
