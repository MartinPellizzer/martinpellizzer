Dentaria sinuata

Dentaria sinuata, commonly known as the zigzag gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the edges, giving it its common name.

Dentaria sinuata is often found in rich, loamy soils and is a valuable component of native plant communities.
