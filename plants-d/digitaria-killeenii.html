Digitaria killeenii

Digitaria killeenii, commonly known as Killeen's digitgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, bracted flowering structures.

Digitaria killeenii plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
