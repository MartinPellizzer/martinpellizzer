Delphinium saniculifolium subsp. centeteroides

Delphinium saniculifolium subsp.

centeteroides, commonly known as the western bluebells, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and shorter stems compared to other delphinium species.
