Diplotropis purpurea var. leptophylla

Diplotropis purpurea var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or prairie habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
