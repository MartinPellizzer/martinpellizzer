Deeringia holostachya

Deeringia holostachya, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet that is often mistaken for a single flower, though it is actually a cluster of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deeringia holostachya plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small aquatic organisms.
