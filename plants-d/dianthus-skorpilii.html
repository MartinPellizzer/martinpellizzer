Dianthus skorpilii

Dianthus skorpilii, commonly known as the Skorpion's fire or Skorpil's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their showy blooms. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers with prominent white or pink centers, often resembling those of carnations. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Dianthus skorpilii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
