Dissotis amplexicaulis

Dissotis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping leaf grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrowly elliptic, clasping leaves that encircle the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its fibrous roots make it a valuable plant for erosion control in wetland environments.

The species is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and resilience in moist conditions.
