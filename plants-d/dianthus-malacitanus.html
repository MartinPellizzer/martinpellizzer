Dianthus malacitanus

Dianthus malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces numerous small, fragrant flowers throughout the summer.

Dianthus malacitanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
