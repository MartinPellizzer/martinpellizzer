Deiregyne pandurata

Deiregyne pandurata, commonly known as the pandurata cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
