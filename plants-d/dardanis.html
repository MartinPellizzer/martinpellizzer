Dardanis

Dardanis, commonly known as the "Turkish rose" or *Rosa damascena*, is a fragrant shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green foliage, and clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is renowned for its essential oil, extracted from its flowers, which is widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy. Dardanis thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for both its beauty and its medicinal properties.
