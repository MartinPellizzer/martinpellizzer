Darwinia megalopetala

Darwinia megalopetala, commonly known as the giant petal darwinia, is a striking shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces large, showy bracts that resemble petals, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry inland areas.

Darwinia megalopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a key species in the Proteaceae family.
