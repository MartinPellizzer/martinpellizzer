Daucus carota f. fischeri

Daucus carota f. fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's carrot, is a botanical variant of the wild carrot.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its roots are smaller and less developed compared to the common carrot, making it more of a wild plant than a cultivated one.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and genetic diversity.
