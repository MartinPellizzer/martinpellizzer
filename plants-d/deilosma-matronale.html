Deilosma matronale

Deilosma matronale, commonly known as the red-flowered spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling spider webs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a fragrant scent that attracts specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Deilosma matronale is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
