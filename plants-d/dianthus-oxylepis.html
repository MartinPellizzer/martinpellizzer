Dianthus oxylepis

Dianthus oxylepis, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters at the tips of its stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dianthus oxylepis is a perennial herb that produces small, oval seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
