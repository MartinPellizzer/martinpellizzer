Dipterocarpus trinervis var. canescens

Dipterocarpus trinervis var. canescens, commonly known as the white-flowered dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, whitish bark and lanceolate leaves with three nerves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and ecological importance.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting local biodiversity.
