Dryadella acuminata

Dryadella acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped dryad, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Dryadella acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
