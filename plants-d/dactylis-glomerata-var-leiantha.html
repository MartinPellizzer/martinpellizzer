Dactylis glomerata var. leiantha

Dactylis glomerata var. leiantha, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and moderate grazing.
