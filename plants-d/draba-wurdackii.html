Draba wurdackii

Draba wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold, arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba wurdackii is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
