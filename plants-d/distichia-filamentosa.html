Distichia filamentosa

Distichia filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf daisy, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to white, often appearing in clusters.

Distichia filamentosa is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
