Dipodium venosum

Dipodium venosum, commonly known as the veiny dipody, is a member of the fern family, characterized by its intricate, branching fronds.

Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that enhances its ornamental value. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in specialized areas called sori.

The botanical features of Dipodium venosum make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
