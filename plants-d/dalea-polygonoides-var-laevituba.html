Dalea polygonoides var. laevituba

Dalea polygonoides var. laevituba, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed prairie clover, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant features slender, often branching stems covered in fine, smooth hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, pink to reddish-purple, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family.
