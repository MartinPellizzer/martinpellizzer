Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. scardica

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

scardica, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, fluffy flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
