Diospyros aifatensis

Diospyros aifatensis, commonly known as the Aifat date palm, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Aifat in the Federated States of Micronesia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and clustered, small, glossy leaves. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow when mature.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
