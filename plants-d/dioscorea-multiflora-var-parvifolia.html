Dioscorea multiflora var. parvifolia

Dioscorea multiflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved yam, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
