Diplachne brandegeei

Diplachne brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Diplachne brandegeei plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and is often found in association with other aquatic and semi-aquatic vegetation.
