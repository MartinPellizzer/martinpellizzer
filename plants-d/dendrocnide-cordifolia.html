Dendrocnide cordifolia

Dendrocnide cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped stinging nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fine, hair-like structures that release a potent irritant when touched. The plant belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes other stinging plants. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans.
