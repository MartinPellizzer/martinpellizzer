Delphinium koelzii

Delphinium koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz's larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium koelzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
