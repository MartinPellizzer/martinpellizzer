Deschampsia pulchella

Deschampsia pulchella, commonly known as the showy bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its striking blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple florets, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Deschampsia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
