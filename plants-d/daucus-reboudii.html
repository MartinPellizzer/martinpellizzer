Daucus reboudii

Daucus reboudii, commonly known as the Reboud's carrot, is a species of flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and umbel-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp, typical of the carrot family.

Daucus reboudii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ecological value.
