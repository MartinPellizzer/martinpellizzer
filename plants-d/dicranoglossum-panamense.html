Dicranoglossum panamense

Dicranoglossum panamense, commonly known as the Panamanian fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest floors in tropical regions. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Dicranoglossum panamense is known for its intricate spore-producing structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.
