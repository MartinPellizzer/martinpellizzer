Dypsis betamponensis

Dypsis betamponensis, commonly known as the Betampona palm, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in forested areas with well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Dypsis betamponensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
