Datura aurea

Datura aurea, commonly known as the golden jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant produces large, spiny capsules that contain numerous elliptical seeds. Datura aurea belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid environments.

It is also noted for its psychoactive properties due to the presence of tropane alkaloids.
