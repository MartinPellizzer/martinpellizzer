Dahlia platylepis

Dahlia platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, yellow, and white. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive in dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

Dahlia platylepis is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to different climates.
