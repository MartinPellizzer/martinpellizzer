Digitalis × media

Digitalis × media, commonly known as the common foxglove, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of tubular, hooded flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which have medicinal uses but are also toxic. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Digitalis × media thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
