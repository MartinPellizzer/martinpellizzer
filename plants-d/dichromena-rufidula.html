Dichromena rufidula

Dichromena rufidula, commonly known as the red-stemmed dichromena, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Dichromena rufidula is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

It is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
