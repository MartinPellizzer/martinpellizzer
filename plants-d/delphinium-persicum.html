Delphinium persicum

Delphinium persicum, commonly known as the Persian larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Delphinium persicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
