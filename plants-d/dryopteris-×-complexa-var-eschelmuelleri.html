Dryopteris × complexa var. eschelmuelleri

Dryopteris × complexa var. eschelmuelleri, commonly known as the Eschelmueller's shield fern, is a hybrid fern species characterized by its robust and hardy nature.

It features large, leathery, deeply lobed fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in groups known as sorus.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
