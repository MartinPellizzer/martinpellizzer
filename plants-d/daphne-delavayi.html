Daphne delavayi

Daphne delavayi, commonly known as the Chinese daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often hairy stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Daphne delavayi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
