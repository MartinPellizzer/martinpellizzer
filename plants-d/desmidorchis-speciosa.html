Desmidorchis speciosa

Desmidorchis speciosa, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in photosynthesis.

The flowers are borne on tall, erect stems and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and flies.
