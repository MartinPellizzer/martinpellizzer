Dendrobium obtusum

Dendrobium obtusum, commonly known as the blunt-leaf orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with red or purple markings. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Dendrobium obtusum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
