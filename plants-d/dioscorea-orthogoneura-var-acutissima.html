Dioscorea orthogoneura var. acutissima

Dioscorea orthogoneura var. acutissima, commonly known as the acute-leaf yam, is a variant of the yam genus native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local agricultural systems.
