Diphyes ciliolata

Diphyes ciliolata, commonly known as the silky flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
