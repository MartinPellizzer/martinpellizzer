Drypetes stylosa

Drypetes stylosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental and timber value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
