Diplostephium incanum

Diplostephium incanum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its fruits are berries that start green and mature to a dark purple or black color.

Diplostephium incanum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
