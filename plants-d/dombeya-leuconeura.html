Dombeya leuconeura

Dombeya leuconeura, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
