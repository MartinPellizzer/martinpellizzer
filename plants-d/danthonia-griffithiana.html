Danthonia griffithiana

Danthonia griffithiana, commonly known as Griffith's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
