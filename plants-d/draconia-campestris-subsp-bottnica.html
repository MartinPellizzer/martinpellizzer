Draconia campestris subsp. bottnica

Draconia campestris subsp.

bottnica, commonly known as the Baltic dragonhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Baltic Sea. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shorelines, thriving in saline and well-drained environments.

The plant features erect, branching stems with opposite leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne in whorled clusters, displaying blue to violet petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
