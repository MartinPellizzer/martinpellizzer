Dianthus schischkinii

Dianthus schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20 to 40 centimeters, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance.

The species is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience and unique botanical features.
