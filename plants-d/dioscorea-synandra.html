Dioscorea synandra

Dioscorea synandra, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The tubers are sometimes cultivated for their medicinal properties and have been used to treat various ailments in traditional practices.
