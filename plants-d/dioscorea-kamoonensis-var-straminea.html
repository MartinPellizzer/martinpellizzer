Dioscorea kamoonensis var. straminea

Dioscorea kamoonensis var. straminea, commonly known as the Kashmir yam, is a tuberous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical tubers that are often yellowish or pale brown in color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has compound leaves with palmate venation. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The tubers are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
