Dicrosperma

Dicrosperma, commonly known as the "honeycomb plant," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences that resemble a honeycomb, giving it its common name. The plants typically have small, green leaves and produce tiny, inconspicuous flowers. Dicrosperma species are often found in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

They are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their unique appearance.
