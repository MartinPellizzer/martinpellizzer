Diuris conspicillata

Diuris conspicillata, commonly known as the yellow-tongued orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a slender, hairy rhizome and produces a single or few erect, hairy stems. The flowers are pale yellow with a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a tongue, giving it its common name. It blooms in spring and is often found in open forests and heathlands.

The plant is characterized by its delicate appearance and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
