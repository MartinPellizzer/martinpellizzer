Dienia

Dienia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-like foliage and showy, tubular flowers that often come in shades of purple, pink, and white. Dienia species are typically evergreen and thrive in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight. They have fibrous roots and are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are usually thick and waxy, which helps the plant retain moisture in arid conditions.
