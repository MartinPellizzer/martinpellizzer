Dalbergia gardneriana

Dalbergia gardneriana, commonly known as Gardner's ebony, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and produces small, pea-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environmental conditions.
