Dianthus prolifer

Dianthus prolifer, commonly known as the spreading campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with spreading stems and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals with a distinctive notched or fringed appearance.

Dianthus prolifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscaping.
