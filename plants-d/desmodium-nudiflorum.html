Desmodium nudiflorum

Desmodium nudiflorum, commonly known as the naked-flowered velvetleaf, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Desmodium nudiflorum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
