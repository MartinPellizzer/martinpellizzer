Didymocarpus venosus

Didymocarpus venosus, commonly known as the veiny wax apple, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. The fruit turns red when mature and contains a single, hard seed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
