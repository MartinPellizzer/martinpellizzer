Dioscorea cephalocarpa

Dioscorea cephalocarpa, commonly known as the head-capped yam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary vegetative structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has compound leaves with alternate arrangement. Its flowers are usually small and unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers of Dioscorea cephalocarpa are not commonly cultivated for food but are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
