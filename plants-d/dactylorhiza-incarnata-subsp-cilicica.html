Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. cilicica

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a complex root system and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
