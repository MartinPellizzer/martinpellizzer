Dracontia powellii

Dracontia powellii, commonly known as Powell's dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
