Dolichos minimus

Dolichos minimus, commonly known as the dwarf hyacinth bean, is a small annual legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, climbing stems that produce small, pea-like flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant's compound leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three ovate leaflets. Its pods are narrow and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Dolichos minimus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.
