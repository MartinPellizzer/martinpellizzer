Diplotaxis humilis var. delphinensis

Diplotaxis humilis var. delphinensis, commonly known as the blueweed or Delphin's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces slender, branched stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
