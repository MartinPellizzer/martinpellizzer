Dichopogon undulatus

Dichopogon undulatus, commonly known as the wavy-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, often wavy leaves that give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
