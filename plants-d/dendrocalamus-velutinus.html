Dendrocalamus velutinus

Dendrocalamus velutinus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, golden-brown culms that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy surface, which gives it its scientific name "velutinus," meaning velvety. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus velutinus plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its versatility and aesthetic appeal.
