Dianthus serratus proles sallentii

Dianthus serratus proles sallentii, commonly known as the Sallent's fringed campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers with frayed edges. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
