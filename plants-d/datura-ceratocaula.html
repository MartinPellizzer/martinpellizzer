Datura ceratocaula

Datura ceratocaula, commonly known as the horned nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and it produces toxic, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds.

Datura ceratocaula is known for its hallucinogenic properties due to the presence of tropane alkaloids.
