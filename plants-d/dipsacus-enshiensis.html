Dipsacus enshiensis

Dipsacus enshiensis, commonly known as the Chinese teasel, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like structures that surround its flowering heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in a cluster at the top of the plant.

The botanical features of Dipsacus enshiensis make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
