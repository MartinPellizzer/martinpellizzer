Drimia filifolia

Drimia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf African lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and covered in fine, thread-like hairs. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear umbellate inflorescences of white or pale blue flowers. Its foliage and floral structure are adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and full sun exposure.

Drimia filifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
