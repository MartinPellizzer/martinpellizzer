Dimorphanthera latifolia

Dimorphanthera latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, colorful flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its striking, often bi-colored blooms. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant appearance make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
