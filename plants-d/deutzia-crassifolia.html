Deutzia crassifolia

Deutzia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features showy clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Deutzia crassifolia is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
