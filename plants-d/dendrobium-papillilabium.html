Dendrobium papillilabium

Dendrobium papillilabium, commonly known as the papillate-lipped dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that bear numerous leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
