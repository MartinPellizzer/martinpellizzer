Delphinium leiostachyum

Delphinium leiostachyum, commonly known as the slender larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments and prefers well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators.

However, like many members of the delphinium genus, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
