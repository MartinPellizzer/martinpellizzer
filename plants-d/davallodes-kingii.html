Davallodes kingii

Davallodes kingii, commonly known as the King's davallodes, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
