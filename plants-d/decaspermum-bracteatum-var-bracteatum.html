Decaspermum bracteatum var. bracteatum

Decaspermum bracteatum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
