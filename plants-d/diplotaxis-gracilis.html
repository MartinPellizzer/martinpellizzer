Diplotaxis gracilis

Diplotaxis gracilis, commonly known as slender desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, green, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its seeds are small and contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal uses.

Diplotaxis gracilis plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing forage for wildlife.
