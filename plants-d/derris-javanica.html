Derris javanica

Derris javanica, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a potent natural insecticide and piscicide. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal and pesticidal properties.

Derris javanica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.
