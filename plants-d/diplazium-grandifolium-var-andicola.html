Diplazium grandifolium var. andicola

Diplazium grandifolium var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean sword fern, is a perennial evergreen fern native to the Andes region of South America.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with arching, slender fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are bipinnate, with closely spaced, ovate pinnae that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests and rocky slopes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
