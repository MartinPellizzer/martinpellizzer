Dioscorea litoralis

Dioscorea litoralis, commonly known as the coastal yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
