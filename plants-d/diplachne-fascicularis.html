Diplachne fascicularis

Diplachne fascicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered in dense, finger-like arrangements, giving it its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Diplachne fascicularis is known for its ability to form dense mats, which can compete aggressively with other vegetation.
