Draba aizoides subsp. bertolonii

Draba aizoides subsp.

bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar structural and chemical characteristics.

This subspecies is noted for its horticultural value and is often cultivated in rock gardens for its ornamental appeal.
