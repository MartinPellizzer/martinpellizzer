Dudleya purpusii

Dudleya purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
