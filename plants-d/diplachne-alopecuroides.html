Diplachne alopecuroides

Diplachne alopecuroides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Diplachne alopecuroides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native vegetation.
