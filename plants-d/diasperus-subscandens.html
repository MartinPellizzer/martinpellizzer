Diasperus subscandens

Diasperus subscandens, commonly known as the climbing cypress, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
