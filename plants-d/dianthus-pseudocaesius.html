Dianthus pseudocaesius

Dianthus pseudocaesius, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, thriving in alpine and rocky habitats. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, with five petals arranged in a star-like pattern. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Dianthus pseudocaesius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
