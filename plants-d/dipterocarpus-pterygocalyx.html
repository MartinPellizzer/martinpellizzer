Dipterocarpus pterygocalyx

Dipterocarpus pterygocalyx, commonly known as the winged dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and long, spreading branches. The tree produces heavy, woody fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange-yellow aril. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
