Dactylorhiza × conigera nothosubsp. conigera

Dactylorhiza × conigera nothosubsp.

conigera, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, slender flower spike.

Botanically, it is notable for its hybrid origin and its role in orchid hybridization studies.
