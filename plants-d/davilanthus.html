Davilanthus

Davilanthus, commonly known as the Hawaiian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, pendulous seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Davilanthus is also valued for its role in traditional Hawaiian culture and ecological systems.
