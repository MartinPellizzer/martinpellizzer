Ditrichospermum

Ditrichospermum, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
