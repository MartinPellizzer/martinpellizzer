Dalea decora

Dalea decora, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea decora is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
