Diasperus macrophyllus

Diasperus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf diasperus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
