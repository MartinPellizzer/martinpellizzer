Drynaria cornucopia

Drynaria cornucopia, commonly known as the horned fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered fronds that resemble a horn, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with numerous upright, sword-shaped leaves. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a prominent midrib.

Drynaria cornucopia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to shaded, humid environments.
