Dicksonia punctulobula

Dicksonia punctulobula, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The fronds are typically dark green with a distinctive serrated margin, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
