Dactylorhiza fistulosa subsp. alpestris

Dactylorhiza fistulosa subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems covered in lance-shaped leaves.

The plant bears spikes of pale to dark purple flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive lip. It thrives in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and bogs.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.
