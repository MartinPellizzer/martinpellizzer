Dianthus algetanus

Dianthus algetanus, commonly known as the Algetan pink, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or red flowers with a distinctive, slightly fragrant scent. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
