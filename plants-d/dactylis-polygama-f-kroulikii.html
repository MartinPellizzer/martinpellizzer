Dactylis polygama f. kroulikii

Dactylis polygama f. kroulikii, commonly known as the Kroulik's hairy vetch, is a variant of the hairy vetch species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
