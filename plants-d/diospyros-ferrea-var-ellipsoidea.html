Diospyros ferrea var. ellipsoidea

Diospyros ferrea var. ellipsoidea, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a species native to Brazil and belongs to the Ebenaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its rough, dark bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish-brown fruits, which are edible when fully ripe.

Its leaves and fruits contain high levels of tannins, making it valuable for traditional medicine and tannin extraction.
