Dissotis verdickii

Dissotis verdickii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green inflorescences that give it a delicate, mist-like appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.
