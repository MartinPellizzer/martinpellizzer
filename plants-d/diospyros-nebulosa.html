Diospyros nebulosa

Diospyros nebulosa, commonly known as the misty persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
