Diphysa microphylla

Diphysa microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf diphysa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Diphysa microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
