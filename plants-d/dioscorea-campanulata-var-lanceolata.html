Dioscorea campanulata var. lanceolata

Dioscorea campanulata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the trumpet yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
