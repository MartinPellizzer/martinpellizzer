Dypsis ankirindro

Dypsis ankirindro, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped stems and clusters of spiky, paddle-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by yellow-orange fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
