Digitaria bosseri

Digitaria bosseri, commonly known as the African grass or dwarf bluestem, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Digitaria bosseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
