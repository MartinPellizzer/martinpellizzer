Draba sauteri

Draba sauteri, commonly known as the Sauter's whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in high-altitude meadows.

Draba sauteri is adapted to harsh conditions, showcasing its resilience in cold and arid climates.
