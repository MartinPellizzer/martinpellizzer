Desmodium perrottetii

Desmodium perrottetii, commonly known as the purple prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy violet to purple flowers. The plant is often found in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value has also made it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
