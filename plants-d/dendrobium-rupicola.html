Dendrobium rupicola

Dendrobium rupicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its blooms, which range in color from white to pale yellow, are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Dendrobium rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
