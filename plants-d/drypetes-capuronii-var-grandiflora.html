Drypetes capuronii var. grandiflora

Drypetes capuronii var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered drypetes, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local communities.
