Disparago ericoides

Disparago ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Disparago ericoides plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
