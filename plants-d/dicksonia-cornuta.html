Dicksonia cornuta

Dicksonia cornuta, commonly known as the serrate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, serrated appearance on its fronds, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Dicksonia cornuta plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
