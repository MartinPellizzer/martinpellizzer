Darcyanthus spruceanus

Darcyanthus spruceanus, commonly known as the mountain flame bush, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils. Its botanical features include small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a compact growth habit.

Darcyanthus spruceanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
