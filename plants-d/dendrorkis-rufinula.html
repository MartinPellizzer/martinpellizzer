Dendrorkis rufinula

Dendrorkis rufinula, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming during the rainy season.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
