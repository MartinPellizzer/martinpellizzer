Diospyros bangueyensis

Diospyros bangueyensis, commonly known as the Banguey ebony, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by small, round fruits.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its role in traditional medicine.
