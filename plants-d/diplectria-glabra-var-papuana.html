Diplectria glabra var. papuana

Diplectria glabra var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan flatleaf, is a species native to the tropical regions of Papua.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. The plant features smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it has a fibrous root system.

The plant is characterized by its lack of pubescence on the stem and leaves, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
