Dryandra ferruginea subsp. tutanningensis

Dryandra ferruginea subsp.

tutanningensis, commonly known as the Tutanning dryandra, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
