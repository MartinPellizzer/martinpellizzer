Diospyros maclurei

Diospyros maclurei, commonly known as the Maclure's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red, fleshy fruits, which are edible and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Diospyros maclurei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems, providing food for various wildlife species.
