Davallia corcovadensis

Davallia corcovadensis, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green, with a slight golden hue on the underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
