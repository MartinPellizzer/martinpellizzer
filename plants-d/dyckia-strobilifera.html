Dyckia strobilifera

Dyckia strobilifera, commonly known as the pineapple grass, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rosette of grass-like leaves that form a dense, compact structure. The plant produces inflorescences that resemble pineapples, giving it its common name. Dyckia strobilifera is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and container planting.
