Daucus carota subsp. siculus

Daucus carota subsp.

siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian carrot, is a botanical variant of the common carrot. It is characterized by its smaller, more compact root structure compared to the standard carrot.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and is often cultivated for its unique flavor and ornamental value.
