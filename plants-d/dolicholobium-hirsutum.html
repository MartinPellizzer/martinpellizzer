Dolicholobium hirsutum

Dolicholobium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with hairy surfaces, contributing to its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive pea-like shape.

Dolicholobium hirsutum is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ecological role in soil enrichment.
