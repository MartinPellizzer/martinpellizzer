Deschampsia cespitosa var. glauca

Deschampsia cespitosa var. glauca, commonly known as bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features fine, blue-green foliage that gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wetlands and stream banks. It has a clumping growth habit with slender, upright stems.

Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that mature into small, fluffy seeds.
