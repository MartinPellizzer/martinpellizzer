Daucus platycarpos f. foliosus

Daucus platycarpos f. foliosus, commonly known as the leafy wild carrot, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp, which releases small, curved seeds upon maturity.
