Doliocarpus spatulifolius

Doliocarpus spatulifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf doliocarpus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its simple, broad, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits that are edible when ripe. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a dense canopy that provides shade in its natural habitat.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
