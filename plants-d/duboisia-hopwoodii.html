Duboisia hopwoodii

Duboisia hopwoodii, commonly known as the Hopwood's ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually blue-green in color and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive cylindrical seed pods. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Duboisia hopwoodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its unique appearance.
