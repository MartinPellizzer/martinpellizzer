Digitaria elevatovenulosa

Digitaria elevatovenulosa, commonly known as the elevated vein grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with distinctive vein patterns. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often arranged in a panicle structure.

Digitaria elevatovenulosa is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often found in areas with moderate to high rainfall.
