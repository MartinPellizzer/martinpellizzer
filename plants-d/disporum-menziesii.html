Disporum menziesii

Disporum menziesii, commonly known as the western starflower, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. It is typically found in regions with cool, humid climates and well-drained soil.

Disporum menziesii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
