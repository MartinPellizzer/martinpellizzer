Discocalyx pachyphylla

Discocalyx pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved discocalyx, is a unique plant species characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, which includes cacti, though it is not a true cactus in the traditional sense. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its woody, succulent stems. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, and the plant can grow up to several feet in height under optimal conditions.

Discocalyx pachyphylla is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
