Drypetes viridis

Drypetes viridis, commonly known as green flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are usually bright green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical regions.
