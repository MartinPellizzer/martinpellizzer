Dysoxylum reticulatum

Dysoxylum reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined wood or African ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and complex, reticulate venation on its leaves. The plant belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic and often medicinally valuable properties. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and is valued for its hard, dark timber used in fine woodworking and traditional medicine.
