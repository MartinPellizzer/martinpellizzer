Disocactus salvadorensis

Disocactus salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvin's cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and areoles. The plant typically produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
