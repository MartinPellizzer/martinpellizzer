Dasiphora × friederichsenii

Dasiphora × friederichsenii, commonly known as the hybrid peavine, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the eastern United States. It produces small, edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
