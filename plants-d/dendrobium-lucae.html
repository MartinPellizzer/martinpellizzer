Dendrobium lucae

Dendrobium lucae, commonly known as the Luca's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Dendrobium lucae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
