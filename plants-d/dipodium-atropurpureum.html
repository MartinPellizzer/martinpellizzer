Dipodium atropurpureum

Dipodium atropurpureum, commonly known as purple-stemmed dipodium, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae, commonly known as the orchid family, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically features grass-like leaves and produces small, intricate flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its purple-tinged stems and foliage make it a visually striking plant, often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Dipodium atropurpureum is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
