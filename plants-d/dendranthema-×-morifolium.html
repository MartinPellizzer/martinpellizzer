Dendranthema × morifolium

Dendranthema × morifolium, commonly known as chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including yellow, red, white, and purple. The plant is native to East Asia but has been widely cultivated around the world for ornamental purposes. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 to 3 meters.

Its leaves are usually pinnately lobed, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
