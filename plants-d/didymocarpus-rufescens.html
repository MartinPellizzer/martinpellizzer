Didymocarpus rufescens

Didymocarpus rufescens, commonly known as the red-fruited sandarac tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, red, and edible, often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
