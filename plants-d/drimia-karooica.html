Drimia karooica

Drimia karooica, commonly known as the Karoo rain lily, is a perennial herb native to the Karoo region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
