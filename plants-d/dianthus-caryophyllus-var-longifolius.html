Dianthus caryophyllus var. longifolius

Dianthus caryophyllus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved carnation, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are often used in ornamental gardening for their visual appeal.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil conditions.
