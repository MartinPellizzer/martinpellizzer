Drymocallis × pumila

Drymocallis × pumila, commonly known as the dwarf goldenrod, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit, typically reaching heights of 10–30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
