Dianthus lumnitzeri subsp. pseudopraecox

Dianthus lumnitzeri subsp.

pseudopraecox, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that blooms in the summer months, adding vibrant color to high-altitude landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for studies on alpine flora adaptation and ecological resilience.
