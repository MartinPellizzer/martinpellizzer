Dendrocalamus strictus var. sericeus

Dendrocalamus strictus var. sericeus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated culms that are covered in a silvery, silky sheath, giving it its common name. The plant is a perennial grass that grows in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its tropical habitat.

This bamboo is valued for its lightweight yet strong material, making it useful in construction and crafts.
