Diasperus piscatorum

Diasperus piscatorum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in salty, sandy soils. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a star or a cluster of beads.

Diasperus piscatorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
