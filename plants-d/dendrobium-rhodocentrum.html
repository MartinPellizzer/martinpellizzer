Dendrobium rhodocentrum

Dendrobium rhodocentrum, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that often turn reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, arranged along the stem.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining potting mix.
