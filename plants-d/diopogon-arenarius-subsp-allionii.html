Diopogon arenarius subsp. allionii

Diopogon arenarius subsp.

allionii, commonly known as the Cape dune lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is often found in coastal dune ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.
