Dumoria heckelii

Dumoria heckelii, commonly known as the California goldenbush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Dumoria heckelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
