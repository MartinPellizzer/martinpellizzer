Diasperus emblica

Diasperus emblica, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn a bright orange when mature, containing a single seed. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its antioxidant properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
