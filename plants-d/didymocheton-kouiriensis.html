Didymocheton kouiriensis

Didymocheton kouiriensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant has pinnate leaves with multiple leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Didymocheton kouiriensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
