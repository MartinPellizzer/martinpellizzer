Diasperus cinerascens

Diasperus cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its pale gray-green foliage and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

Diasperus cinerascens is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
