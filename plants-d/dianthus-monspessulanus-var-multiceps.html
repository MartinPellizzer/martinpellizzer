Dianthus monspessulanus var. multiceps

Dianthus monspessulanus var. multiceps, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are usually upright.

It is a perennial species valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its historical use in herbal medicine.
