Diospyros neurosepala

Diospyros neurosepala, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe with a sweet, edible pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
