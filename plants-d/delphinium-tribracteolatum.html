Delphinium tribracteolatum

Delphinium tribracteolatum, commonly known as the three-bract delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple spires of flowers in late summer, which are composed of numerous small, tubular blooms. Its leaves are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Delphinium tribracteolatum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
