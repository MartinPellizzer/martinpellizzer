Digitaria bredoensis

Digitaria bredoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bristlegrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Digitaria bredoensis is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native range.
