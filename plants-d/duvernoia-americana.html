Duvernoia americana

Duvernoia americana, commonly known as American duvernoia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are white and clustered in inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a small, leathery capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
