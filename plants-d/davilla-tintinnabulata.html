Davilla tintinnabulata

Davilla tintinnabulata, commonly known as the bellflower tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that hang in clusters, typically in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
