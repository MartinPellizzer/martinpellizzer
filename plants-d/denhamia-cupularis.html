Denhamia cupularis

Denhamia cupularis, commonly known as the red-stemmed flax, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight sheen.

Denhamia cupularis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
