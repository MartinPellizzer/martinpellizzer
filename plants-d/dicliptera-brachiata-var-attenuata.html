Dicliptera brachiata var. attenuata

Dicliptera brachiata var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed dicliptera, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features erect, slender stems that are often hairy, with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences bearing tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
