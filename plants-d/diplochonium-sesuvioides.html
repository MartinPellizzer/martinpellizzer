Diplochonium sesuvioides

Diplochonium sesuvioides, commonly known as the coastal bladderwrack, is a perennial seaweed found in intertidal zones along the Pacific coast of North America.

It has a distinctive appearance with a dark brown, leathery thallus that is often segmented into blade-like structures. The plant grows in dense mats, forming a complex structure that provides habitat for various marine organisms. It is a member of the Fucaceae family and is characterized by its ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.

Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are embedded in the thallus and release spores into the water for dispersal.
