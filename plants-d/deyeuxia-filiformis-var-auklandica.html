Deyeuxia filiformis var. auklandica

Deyeuxia filiformis var. auklandica, commonly known as the Auckland grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that grow in dense tufts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
