Diospyros ferruginescens

Diospyros ferruginescens, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that are usually red or yellow when mature.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
