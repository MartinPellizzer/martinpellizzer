Drimia pellabergensis

Drimia pellabergensis, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, typically from September to November. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Drimia pellabergensis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
