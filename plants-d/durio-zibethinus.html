Durio zibethinus

Durio zibethinus, commonly known as the durian, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiky fruit, which can weigh up to 10 kilograms. The plant has a complex root system and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and evergreen, providing shade and contributing to the ecosystem.

The durian is also valued for its unique, aromatic pulp, which is highly sought after in global markets.
