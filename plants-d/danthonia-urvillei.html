Danthonia urvillei

Danthonia urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often obscured by the foliage.

Danthonia urvillei is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
