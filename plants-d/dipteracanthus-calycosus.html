Dipteracanthus calycosus

Dipteracanthus calycosus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
