Dendrobium araneum

Dendrobium araneum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, wiry stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that often resemble spiders, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often attached to trees or rocks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the winter months.

Dendrobium araneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
