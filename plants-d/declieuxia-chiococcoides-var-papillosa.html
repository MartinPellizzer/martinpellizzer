Declieuxia chiococcoides var. papillosa

Declieuxia chiococcoides var. papillosa, commonly known as the silky spikerush, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender branches. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important species in the study of wetland flora.
