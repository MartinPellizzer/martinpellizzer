Desmanthus virgatus var. santaniensis

Desmanthus virgatus var. santaniensis, commonly known as the Santanensis burr legume, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in forage and erosion control.
