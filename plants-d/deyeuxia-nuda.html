Deyeuxia nuda

Deyeuxia nuda, commonly known as the naked sedge, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Deyeuxia nuda plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
