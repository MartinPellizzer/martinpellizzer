Dalechampia convolvuloides

Dalechampia convolvuloides, commonly known as the Mexican hogweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

Dalechampia convolvuloides is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.
