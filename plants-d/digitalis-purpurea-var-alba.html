Digitalis purpurea var. alba

Digitalis purpurea var. alba, commonly known as the white foxglove, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like clusters of tubular, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
