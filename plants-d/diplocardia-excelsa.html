Diplocardia excelsa

Diplocardia excelsa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive greenish-brown center, blooming at night. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in tropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
