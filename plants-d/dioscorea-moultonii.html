Dioscorea moultonii

Dioscorea moultonii, commonly known as Moulton's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Its tubers are often harvested for their starchy content and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
