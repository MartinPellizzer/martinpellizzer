Dryopteris thelypteris subsp. squamigera

Dryopteris thelypteris subsp.

squamigera, commonly known as the silver shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds with a silvery, scaly appearance on the underside. It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like China, Japan, and Korea.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually pinnately divided, with a broad, leathery texture and a dark green upper surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
