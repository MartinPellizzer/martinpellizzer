Dombeya scabrida var. repanda

Dombeya scabrida var. repanda, commonly known as the rough dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish, rough-textured bark and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a hairy underside and a serrated margin. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
