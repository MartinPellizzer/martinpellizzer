Dipteracanthus strepens var. calycinus

Dipteracanthus strepens var. calycinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
