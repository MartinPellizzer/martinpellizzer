Dianthus serrulatus subsp. mauritanicus

Dianthus serrulatus subsp.

mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritanian campion, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
