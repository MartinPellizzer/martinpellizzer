Deyeuxia pulvinata

Deyeuxia pulvinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved couch grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairless and have a pointed tip. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

Deyeuxia pulvinata is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
