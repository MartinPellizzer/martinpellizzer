Dicrastylis exsuccosa f. lachnophylla

Dicrastylis exsuccosa f. lachnophylla, commonly known as the woolly flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a distinctive woolly texture on the leaf blades. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

This subspecies is noted for its unique morphological features and is often found in the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.
