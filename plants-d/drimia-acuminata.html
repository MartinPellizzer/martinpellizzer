Drimia acuminata

Drimia acuminata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is characterized by its robust bulbs, which store nutrients for growth and flowering. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Drimia acuminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
