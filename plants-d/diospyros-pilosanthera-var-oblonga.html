Diospyros pilosanthera var. oblonga

Diospyros pilosanthera var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is typically a small, sweet, orange-red berry that ripens in the fall. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common persimmon.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
