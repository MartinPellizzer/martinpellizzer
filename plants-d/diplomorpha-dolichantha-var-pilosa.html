Diplomorpha dolichantha var. pilosa

Diplomorpha dolichantha var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy long-stemmed orchid, is a unique orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and hairy foliage, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". The plant produces delicate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare and exotic plants.
