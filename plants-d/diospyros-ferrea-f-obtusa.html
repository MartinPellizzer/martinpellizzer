Diospyros ferrea f. obtusa

Diospyros ferrea f. obtusa, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
