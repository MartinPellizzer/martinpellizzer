Devillea speciosa

Devillea speciosa, commonly known as the showy devil's ivy, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers clustered in dense, pendulous clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Devillea speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
