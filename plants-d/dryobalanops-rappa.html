Dryobalanops rappa

Dryobalanops rappa, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is typically found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
