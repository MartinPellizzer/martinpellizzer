Dianthus serratifolius subsp. serratifolius

Dianthus serratifolius subsp.

serratifolius, commonly known as the serrated leaf carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features upright stems covered with narrow, serrated leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its flowers have five petals with a distinctive frilled edge, and the plant is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
