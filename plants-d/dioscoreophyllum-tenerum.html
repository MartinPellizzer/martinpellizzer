Dioscoreophyllum tenerum

Dioscoreophyllum tenerum, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tubers that are used as a starch source. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates and have been traditionally used for food in various African communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological roles in its native habitats.
