Diasperus flexuosus

Diasperus flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible diasperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and shrublands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a woolly texture.

Diasperus flexuosus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
