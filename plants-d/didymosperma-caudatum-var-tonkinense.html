Didymosperma caudatum var. tonkinense

Didymosperma caudatum var. tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin beardroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, hairy stem that is often branched and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
