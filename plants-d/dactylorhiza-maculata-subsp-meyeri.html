Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. meyeri

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

meyeri, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with each pseudobulb producing a single leaf and a flower spike. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with distinctive dark markings, and it blooms from May to July.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
