Dichromanthus cinnabarinus f. aureus

Dichromanthus cinnabarinus f. aureus, commonly known as the golden-flowered cinnabarin, is a rare and striking hybrid of the genus Dichromanthus.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to reddish-orange flowers, which are typically more intense in color compared to the typical species. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
