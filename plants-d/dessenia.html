Dessenia

Dessenia, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit plant. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a sweet, intoxicating fragrance. Its flowers are typically purple or violet in color, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Dessenia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
