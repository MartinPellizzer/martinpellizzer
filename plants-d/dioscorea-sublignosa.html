Dioscorea sublignosa

Dioscorea sublignosa, commonly known as the woody yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Dioscorea sublignosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated for its edible tubers.
