Daphne odora var. fasciata

Daphne odora var. fasciata, commonly known as the variegated daphne, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or cream variegation. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, white, and lilac, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring protection from strong winds.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
