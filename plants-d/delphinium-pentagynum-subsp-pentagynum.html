Delphinium pentagynum subsp. pentagynum

Delphinium pentagynum subsp.

pentagynum, commonly known as the five-lobed larkspur, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the flowers are arranged in racemes.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
