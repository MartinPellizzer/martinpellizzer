Dendrolobium triangulare subsp. cephalotoides

Dendrolobium triangulare subsp.

cephalotoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that often contain a single, hard seed.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow to orange flowers, which are clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

This subspecies is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
