Daemonorops beguinii

Daemonorops beguinii, commonly known as the Bornean rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems used in rattan crafts. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, where it relies on other plants for support. Its leaves are pinnate and can reach several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, fibrous capsule that contains seeds, aiding in its propagation in natural habitats.
