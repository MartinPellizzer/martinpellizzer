Diplachne vulpiastrum

Diplachne vulpiastrum, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Diplachne vulpiastrum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
