Dicellostyles zizyphifolia

Dicellostyles zizyphifolia, commonly known as the hairy leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Dicellostyles zizyphifolia is adapted to harsh conditions and is often found in saline or alkaline soils.
