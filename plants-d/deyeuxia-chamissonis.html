Deyeuxia chamissonis

Deyeuxia chamissonis, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to the wetlands and marshes of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Deyeuxia chamissonis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
