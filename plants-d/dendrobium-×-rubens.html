Dendrobium × rubens

Dendrobium × rubens, commonly known as the red-bellied orchid, is a hybrid orchid species originating from the cross between Dendrobium nobile and Dendrobium phalaenopsis.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that produce clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers are often brightly colored, with red or orange petals and a distinctive yellow lip, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
