Distylium racemosum f. pendulum

Distylium racemosum f. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping silverleaf maple, is a unique variant of the silverleaf maple characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

This form exhibits a cascading canopy of slender, drooping branches that create a distinctive, flowing appearance. Botanically, it features opposite, ovate leaves with a silvery underside, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow catkins. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade in gardens and landscapes.
