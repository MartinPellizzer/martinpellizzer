Dianthus medunensis

Dianthus medunensis, commonly known as the Balkan campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Dianthus medunensis is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
