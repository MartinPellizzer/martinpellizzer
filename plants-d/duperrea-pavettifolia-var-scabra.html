Duperrea pavettifolia var. scabra

Duperrea pavettifolia var. scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf duperrea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptic leaves that are covered in a dense, rough texture, giving it its varietal name "scabra.". The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
