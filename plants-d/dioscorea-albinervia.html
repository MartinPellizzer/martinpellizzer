Dioscorea albinervia

Dioscorea albinervia, commonly known as the white-veined yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers are valued for their starchy content and have been traditionally used in various cultures for food and herbal remedies.
