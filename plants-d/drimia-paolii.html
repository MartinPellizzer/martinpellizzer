Drimia paolii

Drimia paolii, commonly known as the Malabar lily, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has strap-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky layer of hair. Drimia paolii is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
