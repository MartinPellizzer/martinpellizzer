Dracophyllum rubrum

Dracophyllum rubrum, commonly known as the red-leaved dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with bright red or reddish-brown leaves that provide striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
