Dracontium amazonense

Dracontium amazonense, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are valued for their starchy content, similar to taro.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is increasingly studied for conservation and sustainable use.
