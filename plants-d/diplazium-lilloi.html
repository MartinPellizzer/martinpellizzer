Diplazium lilloi

Diplazium lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually divided into segments with a serrated edge, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Diplazium lilloi is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
