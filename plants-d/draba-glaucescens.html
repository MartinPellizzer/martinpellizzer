Draba glaucescens

Draba glaucescens, commonly known as the blue draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a bluish-gray appearance. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

Draba glaucescens is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
