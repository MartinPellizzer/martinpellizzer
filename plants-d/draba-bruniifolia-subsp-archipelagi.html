Draba bruniifolia subsp. archipelagi

Draba bruniifolia subsp.

archipelagi, commonly known as the island mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or alpine environments. It produces numerous small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh, maritime climates and its role in stabilizing coastal vegetation.
