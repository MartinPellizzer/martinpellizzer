Draba nuda

Draba nuda, commonly known as the naked draba, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Native to alpine and arctic regions, it thrives in cold, rocky environments. The plant is typically found in high-altitude areas across Europe, Asia, and North America.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a rare and sensitive species.
