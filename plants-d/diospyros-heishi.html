Diospyros heishi

Diospyros heishi, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to reddish-brown fruits. Its fruits are typically harvested when fully ripe, and they are valued for their sweet, non-astringent flavor.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its wood.
