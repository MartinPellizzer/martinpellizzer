Dolicholus cuernavacanus

Dolicholus cuernavacanus, commonly known as the Cuernavaca dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its yellow disk flowers bloom in the spring, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Dolicholus cuernavacanus is adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.
