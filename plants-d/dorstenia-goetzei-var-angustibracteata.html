Dorstenia goetzei var. angustibracteata

Dorstenia goetzei var. angustibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bracted mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, bract-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

This species is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.
