Ditremexa fetida

Ditremexa fetida, commonly known as the stinking nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and glandular.

The fruit is a green berry that turns red when mature, containing toxic seeds.
