Dactylorchis incarnata var. haematodes

Dactylorchis incarnata var. haematodes, commonly known as the blood-red orchid, is a striking orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive scent. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in rich, humus-filled soil. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
