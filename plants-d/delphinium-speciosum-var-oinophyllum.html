Delphinium speciosum var. oinophyllum

Delphinium speciosum var. oinophyllum, commonly known as the golden delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of showy, blue to violet flowers with distinctive golden-orange markings. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological habitats.
