Doronicum wightii var. laxus

Doronicum wightii var. laxus, commonly known as the lax flowered leopard's bane, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and tubular florets. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow with dark purple centers, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
