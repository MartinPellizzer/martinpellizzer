Drypetes perreticulata

Drypetes perreticulata, commonly known as the tropical tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
