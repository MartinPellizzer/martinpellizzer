Dinebra caudata

Dinebra caudata, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, feathery bracts, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental grass in gardens.

Dinebra caudata is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
