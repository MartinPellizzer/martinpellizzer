Drimys andina

Drimys andina, commonly known as the Andean drimys, is a small evergreen tree native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters and has glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Drimys andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
