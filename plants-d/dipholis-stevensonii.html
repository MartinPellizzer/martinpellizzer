Dipholis stevensonii

Dipholis stevensonii, commonly known as Stevenson's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the wetlands of South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with pale greenish-white flowers that resemble translucent petals. The plant typically grows in swampy or marshy habitats, where it thrives in the moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the waterlogged environment, and it relies on specific fungal associations for survival.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered critically endangered and is protected in many regions.
