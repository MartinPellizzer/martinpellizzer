Digitaria induta

Digitaria induta, commonly known as the velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Digitaria induta is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
