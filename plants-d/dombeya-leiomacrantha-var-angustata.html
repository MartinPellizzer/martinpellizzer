Dombeya leiomacrantha var. angustata

Dombeya leiomacrantha var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink. These flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
