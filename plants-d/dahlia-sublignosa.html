Dahlia sublignosa

Dahlia sublignosa, commonly known as the silverleaf dahlia, is a perennial tuberous-rooted plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Dahlia sublignosa is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
