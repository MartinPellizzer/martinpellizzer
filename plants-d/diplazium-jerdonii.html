Diplazium jerdonii

Diplazium jerdonii, commonly known as the Jerdon's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Diplazium jerdonii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
