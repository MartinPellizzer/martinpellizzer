Dalea quercetorum

Dalea quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf dalea, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that resemble oak leaves. The plant produces small, clustered pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Dalea quercetorum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
