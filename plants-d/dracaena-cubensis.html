Dracaena cubensis

Dracaena cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban dragon tree, is a species native to the island of Cuba.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and rosette of dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Dracaena cubensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
