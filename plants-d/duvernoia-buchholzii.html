Duvernoia buchholzii

Duvernoia buchholzii, commonly known as the Chinese cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant is native to China and is adapted to subtropical and temperate climates. Its reproductive structures include a large, cone-like male inflorescence and a female cone that produces seeds.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
