Digitalis macrostachya

Digitalis macrostachya, commonly known as the giant foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Digitalis macrostachya is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
