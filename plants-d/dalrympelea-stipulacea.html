Dalrympelea stipulacea

Dalrympelea stipulacea, commonly known as the red-flowered rock heath.

It is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
