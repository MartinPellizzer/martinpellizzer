Didymaea crassifolia

Didymaea crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf didymaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and has a compact rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, with succulent leaves that help store water.

Didymaea crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
