Diplachne viscida

Diplachne viscida, commonly known as silky grass or silky flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It has slender, grass-like blades that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which are often borne on long, slender stalks.

Diplachne viscida plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
