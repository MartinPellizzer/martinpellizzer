Disperis × duckittiae

Disperis × duckittiae, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Disperis corymbosa and Disperis tenuifolia.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to South Africa, characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.

The hybrid is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
