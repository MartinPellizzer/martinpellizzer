Diospyros commersonii

Diospyros commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, though it is often covered in a tough, inedible outer layer.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and moist forests.
