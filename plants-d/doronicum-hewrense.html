Doronicum hewrense

Doronicum hewrense, commonly known as the Hewren's leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers with a distinctive purple throat. The leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
