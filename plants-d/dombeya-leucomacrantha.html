Dombeya leucomacrantha

Dombeya leucomacrantha, commonly known as the white-flowered dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Dombeya leucomacrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
