Didiscus benthamii f. muricatus

Didiscus benthamii f. muricatus, commonly known as the rough didiscus, is a unique variant of the didiscus plant characterized by its distinctive, rugged surface texture.

This plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, arid environments. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of pink or white, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and hardy species, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water to flourish.
