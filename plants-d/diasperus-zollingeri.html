Diasperus zollingeri

Diasperus zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Diasperus zollingeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
