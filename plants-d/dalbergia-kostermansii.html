Dalbergia kostermansii

Dalbergia kostermansii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a species of legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits.

The species is also notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
