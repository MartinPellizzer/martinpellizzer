Dipterocarpus perakensis

Dipterocarpus perakensis, commonly known as the Pahang ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its tall stature and broad, lanceolate leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing winged seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat and is also economically significant due to its timber value.
