Doronicum grandiflorum var. pyrenaicum

Doronicum grandiflorum var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean leopard's bane, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It produces small, cylindrical flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
