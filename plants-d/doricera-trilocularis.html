Doricera trilocularis

Doricera trilocularis, commonly known as the three-chambered orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique three-chambered structure in its seed pods, which is an adaptation for dispersal in its specific ecological niche. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often blending with the surrounding vegetation.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Doricera trilocularis is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
