Doria mollis

Doria mollis, commonly known as the slender doria, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
