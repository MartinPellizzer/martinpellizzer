Doronicum walkeri

Doronicum walkeri, commonly known as the alpine goldcup, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Doronicum walkeri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in challenging environments.
