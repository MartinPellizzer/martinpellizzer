Digitaria heterantha var. holosericea

Digitaria heterantha var. holosericea, commonly known as the silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silky texture on the underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and resilience in various environmental conditions.
