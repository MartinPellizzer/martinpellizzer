Dyssodia speciosa

Dyssodia speciosa, commonly known as the showy dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that are surrounded by pale green bracts, making it visually striking. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dyssodia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
