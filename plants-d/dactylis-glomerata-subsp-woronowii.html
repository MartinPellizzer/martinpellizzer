Dactylis glomerata subsp. woronowii

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

woronowii, commonly known as the Woronow's goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and ecological resilience.
