Delphinium tricorne var. depauperatum

Delphinium tricorne var. depauperatum, commonly known as the poorflower delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely leafed stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
