Dipterocarpus affinis

Dipterocarpus affinis, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is known for its large, showy flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
