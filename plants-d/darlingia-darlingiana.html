Darlingia darlingiana

Darlingia darlingiana, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with upright stems and produces flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular houseplant worldwide.
