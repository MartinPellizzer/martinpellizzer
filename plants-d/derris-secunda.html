Derris secunda

Derris secunda, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its roots contain rotenone, a natural insecticide and piscicide.
