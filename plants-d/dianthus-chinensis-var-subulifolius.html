Dianthus chinensis var. subulifolius

Dianthus chinensis var. subulifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red, five-petaled flowers. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that give it its common name, and it typically blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
