Deinanthe caerulea

Deinanthe caerulea, commonly known as blue cohosh, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its attractive blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces red berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in shady, moist woodlands and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Deinanthe caerulea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
