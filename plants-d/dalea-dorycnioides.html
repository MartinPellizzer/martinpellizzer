Dalea dorycnioides

Dalea dorycnioides, commonly known as the Texas needlepod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, narrow pods that contain seeds with a hooked appendage, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Dalea dorycnioides is adapted to arid environments and is an important species for native pollinators.
