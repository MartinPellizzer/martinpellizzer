Dactylorhiza olocheilos

Dactylorhiza olocheilos, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and tall spikes of fragrant, purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring calcareous soils. It has a complex root system with a prominent rhizome, which helps it store nutrients and water.

Dactylorhiza olocheilos is often found in association with other orchid species in moist, temperate environments.
