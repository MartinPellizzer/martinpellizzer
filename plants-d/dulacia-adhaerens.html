Dulacia adhaerens

Dulacia adhaerens, commonly known as the sticky vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of a sticky substance on its stems and leaves. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters.

Dulacia adhaerens is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.
