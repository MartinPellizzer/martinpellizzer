Dianthus hypoleucus var. karami

Dianthus hypoleucus var. karami, commonly known as the Karami pinks, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink to reddish-pink flowers with a distinctive frilly appearance. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
