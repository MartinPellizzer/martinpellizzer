Delphinium ferganicum

Delphinium ferganicum, commonly known as the Fergana delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
