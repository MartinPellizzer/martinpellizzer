Dalechampia passiflora

Dalechampia passiflora, commonly known as the passion vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that resemble a heart, often with five petals and a central reproductive structure. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and contain astringent seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical studies.
