Dioscorea sororopana

Dioscorea sororopana, commonly known as the red veined yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a food source in some cultures. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent reddish veins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers of Dioscorea sororopana are valued for their nutritional content and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.
