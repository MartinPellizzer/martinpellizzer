Deschampsia littoralis var. beringensis

Deschampsia littoralis var. beringensis, commonly known as the Bering Sea grass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of the Arctic and subarctic.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in tundra and coastal dunes. The plant has slender, creeping stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to silver-blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various arctic flora and fauna.
