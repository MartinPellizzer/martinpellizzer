Diptychocarpus hispidus

Diptychocarpus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
