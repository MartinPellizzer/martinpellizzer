Dasylepis blackii

Dasylepis blackii, commonly known as the black mountain pine, is a species of conifer native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a cool, moist climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
