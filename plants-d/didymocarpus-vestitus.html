Didymocarpus vestitus

Didymocarpus vestitus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits. The plant is noted for its hairy, glandular leaves and yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
