Dianthus albaceteanus

Dianthus albaceteanus, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers, which range from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dianthus albaceteanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
