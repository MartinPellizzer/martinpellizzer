Dianthus hybridus

Dianthus hybridus, commonly known as the carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters and features linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of two rows of petals, with the outer petals being larger and more prominent.

Dianthus hybridus is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
