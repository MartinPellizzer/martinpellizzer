Dichapetalum subauriculatum

Dichapetalum subauriculatum, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in woodland or savanna habitats. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
