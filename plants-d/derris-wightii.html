Derris wightii

Derris wightii, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a potent natural insecticide and piscicide. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their antifungal and antiparasitic properties.

Derris wightii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
