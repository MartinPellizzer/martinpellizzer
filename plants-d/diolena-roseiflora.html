Diolena roseiflora

Diolena roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered diolena, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and rocky habitats.

Diolena roseiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
