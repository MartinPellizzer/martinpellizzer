Diospyros boivinii

Diospyros boivinii, commonly known as the Madagascar date palm, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to red when mature.

Diospyros boivinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
