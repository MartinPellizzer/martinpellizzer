Dichanthium bladhii

Dichanthium bladhii, commonly known as the bladh's grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in poor soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Due to its hardy nature, Dichanthium bladhii is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
