Dianthus muschianus

Dianthus muschianus, commonly known as the musk carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical petals that are often marked with distinctive spots or streaks. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in well-drained soils in sunny locations.

Dianthus muschianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
