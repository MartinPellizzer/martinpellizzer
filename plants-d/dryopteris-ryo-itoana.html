Dryopteris ryo-itoana

Dryopteris ryo-itoana, commonly known as the Ryoyu fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bright green and finely divided. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
