Drymophloeus leprosus

Drymophloeus leprosus, commonly known as the leprosy fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a vital role in supporting local biodiversity.
