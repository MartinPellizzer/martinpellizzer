Diphyes blepharoglossa

Diphyes blepharoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new growth from a lateral bud.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
