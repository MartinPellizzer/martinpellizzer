Dupatya aequalis

Dupatya aequalis, commonly known as the slender bamboo orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to thrive. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Dupatya aequalis is considered endangered and is often found in protected botanical gardens.
