Dianthus supinus

Dianthus supinus, commonly known as the sand pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive frilly appearance. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dianthus supinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming, early spring blooms.
