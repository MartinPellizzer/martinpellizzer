Dioscorea aculeata

Dioscorea aculeata, commonly known as the burr yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it bears spiny structures along its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Dioscorea aculeata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
