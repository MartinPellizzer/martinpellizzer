Diospyros chitoniophora

Diospyros chitoniophora, commonly known as the mangrove persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits. It thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in mangrove forests and tidal areas.

The fruit is edible and has been traditionally used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
