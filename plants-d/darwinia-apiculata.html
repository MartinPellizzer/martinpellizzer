Darwinia apiculata

Darwinia apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Darwinia apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
