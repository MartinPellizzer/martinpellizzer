Didymoplexis neocaledonica

Didymoplexis neocaledonica, commonly known as the Kermadec flame vine, is a climbing plant native to the Kermadec Islands in the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in coastal areas with well-drained soil.

Didymoplexis neocaledonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in its native range.
