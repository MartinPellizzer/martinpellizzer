Diglosselis latifolia

Diglosselis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf diglosselis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
