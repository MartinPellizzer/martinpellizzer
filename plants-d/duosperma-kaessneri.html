Duosperma kaessneri

Duosperma kaessneri, commonly known as the mountain pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and dense clusters of small, spiky leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular specimen in both botanical gardens and private collections.
