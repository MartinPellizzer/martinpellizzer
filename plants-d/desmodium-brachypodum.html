Desmodium brachypodum

Desmodium brachypodum, commonly known as smallpod desmodium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in agroforestry and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
