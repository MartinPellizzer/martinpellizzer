Diospyros sandwicensis var. elliptica

Diospyros sandwicensis var. elliptica, commonly known as the ʻōhiʻa lehua, is a native Hawaiian plant with distinctive botanical features.

It is a small tree or shrub characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, reddish-purple fruits. It thrives in volcanic soils and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii.

This species plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian forests and is also culturally significant to Native Hawaiians.
