Dipterocarpus nudus

Dipterocarpus nudus, commonly known as the naked dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often fissured bark. The plant is a deciduous species, shedding its leaves during the dry season. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody capsules containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its timber and plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
