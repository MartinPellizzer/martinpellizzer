Dendrochilum fuscum

Dendrochilum fuscum, commonly known as the dark orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest undergrowth. The plant produces striking, dark purple to brown flowers with intricate patterns. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Dendrochilum fuscum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
