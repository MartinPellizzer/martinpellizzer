Diasperus pallidifolius

Diasperus pallidifolius, commonly known as the pale-leaf candelabra, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its branching stems that resemble a candelabra, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The leaves are typically pale green, often with a bluish tint, and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, which are followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
