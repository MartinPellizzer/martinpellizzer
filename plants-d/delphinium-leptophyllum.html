Delphinium leptophyllum

Delphinium leptophyllum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium leptophyllum is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
