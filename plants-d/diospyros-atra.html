Diospyros atra

Diospyros atra, commonly known as the black ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round, black fruits.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of high-quality timber.
