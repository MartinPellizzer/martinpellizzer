Diploclinium longifolium var. luxurians

Diploclinium longifolium var. luxurians, commonly known as the long-leaved diploclinium, is a variant of the diploclinium genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its foliage is often densely packed, giving it a lush, bushy appearance.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
