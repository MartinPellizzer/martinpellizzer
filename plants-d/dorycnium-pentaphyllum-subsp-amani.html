Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp. amani

Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp.

amani, commonly known as the Amani burclover, is a perennial herb native to East Africa. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats. Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp.

amani is adapted to temperate climates and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
