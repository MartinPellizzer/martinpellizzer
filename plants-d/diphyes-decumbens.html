Diphyes decumbens

Diphyes decumbens, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features a sprawling, decumbent stem that lies close to the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or along stream banks.
