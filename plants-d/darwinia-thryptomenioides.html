Darwinia thryptomenioides

Darwinia thryptomenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy inflorescences. The plant produces spikes of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Darwinia thryptomenioides is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.
