Disparago pilosa

Disparago pilosa, commonly known as hairy false foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Disparago pilosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to support pollinators and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
