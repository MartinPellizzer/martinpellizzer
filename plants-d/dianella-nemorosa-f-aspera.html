Dianella nemorosa f. aspera

Dianella nemorosa f. aspera, commonly known as the rough-leaved flax lily, is a variant of the flax lily species native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its grass-like foliage, which is typically narrow and slightly rough to the touch. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
