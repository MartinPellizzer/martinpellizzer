Dianthus caesius var. flaccidus

Dianthus caesius var. flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid carnation, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is known for its delicate appearance and is relatively easy to care for.
