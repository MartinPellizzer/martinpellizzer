Dianthus gremblichii

Dianthus gremblichii, commonly known as the Gremblich's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

Dianthus gremblichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
