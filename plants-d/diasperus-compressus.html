Diasperus compressus

Diasperus compressus, commonly known as the compressed rockweed, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in umbels, a common feature among plants in its family.

Diasperus compressus is adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
