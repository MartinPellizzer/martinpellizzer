Digitaria myurus

Digitaria myurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Digitaria myurus is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
