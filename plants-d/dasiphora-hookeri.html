Dasiphora hookeri

Dasiphora hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Dasiphora hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
