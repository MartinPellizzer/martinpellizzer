Deschampsia ivanovae

Deschampsia ivanovae, commonly known as Ivanov's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, airy plumes of flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
