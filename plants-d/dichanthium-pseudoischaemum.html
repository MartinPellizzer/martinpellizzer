Dichanthium pseudoischaemum

Dichanthium pseudoischaemum, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system.

Dichanthium pseudoischaemum is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites, contributing to soil stabilization and erosion control.
