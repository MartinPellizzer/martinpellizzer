Dracocephalum stewartianum

Dracocephalum stewartianum, commonly known as Stewart's dragonhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of purple or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties.

Dracocephalum stewartianum is typically found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
