Disa cooperi

Disa cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's disa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its blooms, which are usually pink or purple, appear in late summer and can last for several weeks.

Disa cooperi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
