Drimopogon rosea

Drimopogon rosea, commonly known as the rose spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Drimopogon rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
