Diplospora buisanensis

Diplospora buisanensis, commonly known as the Buisan dwarf lichen, is a foliose lichen characterized by its dense, tightly packed thallus.

It typically grows on bark and wood surfaces in humid, shaded environments, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's morphology includes a dark green to blackish upper surface and a lighter, more leathery lower surface. It lacks distinct lobes but has a compact, crust-like structure that adheres closely to its substrate.

Botanically, it is notable for its chemical composition, which includes various secondary metabolites that contribute to its ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization.
