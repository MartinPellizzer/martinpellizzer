Dicranopteris splendida

Dicranopteris splendida, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bright green fronds that are often adorned with a golden hue on the underside. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically pinnately divided, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Dicranopteris splendida is also valued for its ability to purify air and its role in traditional medicine.
