Digitalis × purpurascens subsp. lutescens

Digitalis × purpurascens subsp.

lutescens, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a hybrid species within the genus Digitalis. It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers, which are tubular in shape and typically borne in clusters.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in woodland and meadow habitats. It produces a tall, erect stem with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly serrated.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
