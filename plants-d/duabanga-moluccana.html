Duabanga moluccana

Duabanga moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan duabanga, is a tropical tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional uses.
