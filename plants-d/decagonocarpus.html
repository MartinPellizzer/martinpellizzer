Decagonocarpus

Decagonocarpus, commonly known as the mountain pepper or *Decagonocarpus saxatilis*, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a fragrant, peppery aroma.

Decagonocarpus thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and mountainous regions.
