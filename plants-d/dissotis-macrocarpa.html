Dissotis macrocarpa

Dissotis macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are often pink or white, and it is known for its ability to form dense stands.

Dissotis macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
