Digitaria sanguinalis var. parvispicula

Digitaria sanguinalis var. parvispicula, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and compact inflorescences that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This grass is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
