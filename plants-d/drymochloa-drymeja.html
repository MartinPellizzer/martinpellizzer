Drymochloa drymeja

Drymochloa drymeja, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, clumping growth habit and slender, greenish-yellow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are a defining feature of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in landscaping.

Drymochloa drymeja is often used in garden settings for its ability to thrive in shaded areas and its low maintenance requirements.
