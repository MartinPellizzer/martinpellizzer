Desmoncus macrocarpus

Desmoncus macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant cup fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with thick, fleshy roots that help it store moisture. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The botanical features of Desmoncus macrocarpus make it a striking addition to tropical gardens and greenhouses.
