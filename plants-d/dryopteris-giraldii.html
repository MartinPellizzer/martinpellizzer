Dryopteris giraldii

Dryopteris giraldii, commonly known as the Giraldii wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, elegant appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This fern is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
