Dioscorea listeri

Dioscorea listeri, commonly known as the Lister yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source in various African communities.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some traditional systems, though its botanical properties are still under study.
