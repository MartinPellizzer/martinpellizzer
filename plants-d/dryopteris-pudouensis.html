Dryopteris pudouensis

Dryopteris pudouensis, commonly known as the Pudou wood fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its intricate fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are released from the underside of the fronds, which are covered in sori.

This fern is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
