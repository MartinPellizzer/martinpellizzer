Dendrobium tetragonum subsp. tetragonum

Dendrobium tetragonum subsp.

tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments.

The plant features a cane-like stem with four angular ridges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a strong fragrance, and bloom during the summer months.

It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.
