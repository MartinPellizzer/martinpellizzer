Diplodium truncatum

Diplodium truncatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed diplodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers during the warmer months. Diplodium truncatum is often found in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
