Dillwynia oreodoxa

Dillwynia oreodoxa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dillwynia, is a small, erect shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. It is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Dillwynia oreodoxa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
