Dioscorea herzogii

Dioscorea herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog yam, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by yellowish berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
