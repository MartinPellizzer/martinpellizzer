Digitalis purpurea f. palaui

Digitalis purpurea f. palaui, commonly known as the pale-flowered foxglove, is a variant of the common foxglove characterized by its lighter, more pastel-colored flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, particularly the British Isles. The plant features tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme and typically have a spurred shape, typical of the Digitalis genus.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often used in cottage gardens and woodland settings.
