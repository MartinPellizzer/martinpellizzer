Dactylorhiza × latirella

Dactylorhiza × latirella, commonly known as the broad-leaf marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. It thrives in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in peat bogs and damp woodlands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
