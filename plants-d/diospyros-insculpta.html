Diospyros insculpta

Diospyros insculpta, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Diospyros insculpta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
