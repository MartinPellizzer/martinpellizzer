Dianthus monspessulanus var. albidus

Dianthus monspessulanus var. albidus, commonly known as the white campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate appearance and long blooming period add ornamental value to gardens.
