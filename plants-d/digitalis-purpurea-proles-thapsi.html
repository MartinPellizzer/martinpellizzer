Digitalis purpurea proles thapsi

Digitalis purpurea proles thapsi, commonly known as the Greek foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
