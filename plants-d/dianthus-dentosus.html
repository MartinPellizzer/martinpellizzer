Dianthus dentosus

Dianthus dentosus, commonly known as the toothed carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically narrow, toothed, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in floral arrangements.
