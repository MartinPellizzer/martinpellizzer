Dioscorea maximowiczii

Dioscorea maximowiczii, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and woody.
