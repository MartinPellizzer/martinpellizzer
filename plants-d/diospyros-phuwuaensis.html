Diospyros phuwuaensis

Diospyros phuwuaensis, commonly known as the Phu Wu ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony species known for their dense, dark wood. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers.

Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow when mature, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
