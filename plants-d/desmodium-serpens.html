Desmodium serpens

Desmodium serpens, commonly known as the creeping sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Desmodium serpens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
