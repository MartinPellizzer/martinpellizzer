Davallia mitis

Davallia mitis, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that resemble lace, with a pale, silvery underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance.

Davallia mitis reproduces via spores and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
