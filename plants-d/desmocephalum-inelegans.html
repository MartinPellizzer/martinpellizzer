Desmocephalum inelegans

Desmocephalum inelegans, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its adaptability in various environments.
