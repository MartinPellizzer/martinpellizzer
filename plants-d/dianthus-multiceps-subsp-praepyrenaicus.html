Dianthus multiceps subsp. praepyrenaicus

Dianthus multiceps subsp.

praepyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean campion, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of pink to red flowers with five petals.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in high-altitude habitats.
