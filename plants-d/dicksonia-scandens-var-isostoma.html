Dicksonia scandens var. isostoma

Dicksonia scandens var. isostoma, commonly known as the silver hakea, is a fern that belongs to the family Dicksoniaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in height, with a distinctive silver-gray coloration on the underside of the leaves. The plant is native to southeastern Australia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are composed of a series of pinnae, which are narrow, lance-shaped segments arranged along the rachis.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for use in shaded garden areas.
