Dendrotrophe frutescens

Dendrotrophe frutescens, commonly known as the redroot false nettle, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in umbels. Its roots are reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in forest understories.
