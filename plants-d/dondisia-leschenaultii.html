Dondisia leschenaultii

Dondisia leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers produce small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Dondisia leschenaultii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
