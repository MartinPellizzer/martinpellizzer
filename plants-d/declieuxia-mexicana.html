Declieuxia mexicana

Declieuxia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican grass or Mexican reed, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

Declieuxia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
