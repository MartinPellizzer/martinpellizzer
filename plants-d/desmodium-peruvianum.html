Desmodium peruvianum

Desmodium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in traditional cuisines. It also has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a valuable crop in agricultural systems.
