Dolichos smilacinus

Dolichos smilacinus, commonly known as the pink bean or Indian bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces small, pink or white flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and is typically found in open woodland or disturbed areas. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The seeds are used in traditional cuisines and can be dried and ground into a flour-like substance.
