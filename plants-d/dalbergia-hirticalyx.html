Dalbergia hirticalyx

Dalbergia hirticalyx, commonly known as the Java rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
