Doryopteris duclouxii

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules.
