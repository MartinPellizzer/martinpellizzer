Discocalyx sarcophylla

Discocalyx sarcophylla, commonly known as the stonecrop saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Discocalyx sarcophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
