Dorema songoricum

Dorema songoricum, commonly known as the Songar Desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the arid regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or stony soils, adapted to the harsh conditions of its environment. The plant features a low-growing, branched stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals, and bloom during the summer months.

Dorema songoricum plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
