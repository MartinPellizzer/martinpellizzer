Diasperus ferdinandi

Diasperus ferdinandi, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 cm and prefers bright, indirect light. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white, and it is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Diasperus ferdinandi is relatively easy to care for, requiring moderate watering and well-draining soil.
