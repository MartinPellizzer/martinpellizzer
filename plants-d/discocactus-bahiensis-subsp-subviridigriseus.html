Discocactus bahiensis subsp. subviridigriseus

Discocactus bahiensis subsp.

subviridigriseus, commonly known as the greenish-gray discocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Brazil. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to pale green spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a desirable addition to cactus collections.
