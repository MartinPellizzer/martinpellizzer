Dioscorea tarmensis

Dioscorea tarmensis, commonly known as the Tarmes yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has heart-shaped leaves that are typically alternate along the stem. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The tubers of D. tarmensis are rich in starch and have been traditionally utilized in local cuisines and medicinal practices.
