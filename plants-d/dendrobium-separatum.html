Dendrobium separatum

Dendrobium separatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, often with a red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium separatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both horticultural and traditional medicinal purposes.
