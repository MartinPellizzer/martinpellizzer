Desmodium concinnum var. amoenum

Desmodium concinnum var. amoenum, commonly known as the small-flowered desmodium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

It produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
