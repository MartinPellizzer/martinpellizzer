Draba tomentosa subsp. frigida

Draba tomentosa subsp.

frigida, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is typically found in alpine and arctic regions, where it thrives in rocky and gravelly soils. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is often used in ecological studies due to its resilience and role in alpine ecosystems.
