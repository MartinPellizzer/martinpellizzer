Donax arundinaceus

Donax arundinaceus, commonly known as bamboo reed or reed bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a tall, perennial plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. Its growth habit and structure make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.

Donax arundinaceus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its potential in erosion control.
