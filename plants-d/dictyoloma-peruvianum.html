Dictyoloma peruvianum

Dictyoloma peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Andean regions.

It has small, elliptical leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and contributing to nutrient cycling.

Its parasitic nature means it draws water and nutrients from its host, affecting the host's growth and health.
