Dyckia montevidensis var. tandilensis

Dyckia montevidensis var. tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil Dyckia, is a bromeliad native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically grayish-green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that contrast with its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
