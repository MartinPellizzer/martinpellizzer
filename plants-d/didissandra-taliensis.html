Didissandra taliensis

Didissandra taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis sandstone plant, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
