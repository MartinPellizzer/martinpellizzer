Dargeria

Dargeria, commonly known as the rock rose, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Geraniaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their succulent, often hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. Dargeria species typically grow in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant flower colors and compact growth habit.

The genus includes several species, such as Dargeria coccinea, which is known for its striking red blooms.
