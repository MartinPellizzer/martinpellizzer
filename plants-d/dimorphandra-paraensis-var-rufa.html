Dimorphandra paraensis var. rufa

Dimorphandra paraensis var. rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant is notable for its striking reddish-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
