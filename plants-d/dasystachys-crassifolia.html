Dasystachys crassifolia

Dasystachys crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved stachys, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.
