Dimorphanthera dryophila var. trichoclada

Dimorphanthera dryophila var. trichoclada, commonly known as the Trichoclada orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a prominent lip and elongated petals that contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to low-light conditions, while its flowers bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
