Dianthus parviflorus

Dianthus parviflorus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, rosette-forming foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers with five petals, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus parviflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
