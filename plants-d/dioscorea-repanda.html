Dioscorea repanda

Dioscorea repanda, commonly known as the red veined arrowhead, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves with distinctive red veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking foliage.
