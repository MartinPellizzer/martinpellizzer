Dendrobium tortile var. roseum

Dendrobium tortile var. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered phalaenopsis, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, branching growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often resembling orchids of the Phalaenopsis genus. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for indoor gardening and floral arrangements.
