Damrongia

Damrongia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a bright and showy appearance. Damrongia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread in forested areas.
