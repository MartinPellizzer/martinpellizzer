Draba linearis var. macrocarpa

Draba linearis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its linear leaves are usually arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

The variety macrocarpa is distinguished by its larger, more robust seeds compared to other varieties of Draba linearis.
