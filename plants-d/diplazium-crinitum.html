Diplazium crinitum

Diplazium crinitum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, serrated pinnae that contribute to its ornamental appearance.

Diplazium crinitum is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.
