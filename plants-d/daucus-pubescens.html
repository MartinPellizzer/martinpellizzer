Daucus pubescens

Daucus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy parsley.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble those of other members of the Apiaceae family.
