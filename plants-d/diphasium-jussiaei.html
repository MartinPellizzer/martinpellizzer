Diphasium jussiaei

Diphasium jussiaei, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence and produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Diphasium jussiaei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
