Deschampsia andraei var. montana

Deschampsia andraei var. montana, commonly known as mountain bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and delicate, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the ecological stability of these environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
