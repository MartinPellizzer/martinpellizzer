Dichanthelium sabulorum var. thinium

Dichanthelium sabulorum var. thinium, commonly known as the sandblown panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually gray-green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is adapted to coastal and disturbed environments, exhibiting resilience to salt spray and sandy conditions.
