Daphniphyllum sumatraense

Daphniphyllum sumatraense, commonly known as the Sumatran holly, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive waxy texture.

Daphniphyllum sumatraense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to shaded environments.
