Dysophylla rupestris

Dysophylla rupestris, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid or rocky environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Dysophylla rupestris is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
