Davallia lobbiana

Davallia lobbiana, commonly known as the lobbian holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds that remain throughout the year. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

The fronds are divided into lance-shaped segments, giving the plant a distinctive and elegant appearance.
