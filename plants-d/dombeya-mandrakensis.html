Dombeya mandrakensis

Dombeya mandrakensis, commonly known as the Cape dombeya, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a woody stem with lance-shaped leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.

The flowers are tubular and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and birds.
