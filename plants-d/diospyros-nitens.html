Diospyros nitens

Diospyros nitens, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. Its fruits are valued for their sweet, astringent flavor and are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
