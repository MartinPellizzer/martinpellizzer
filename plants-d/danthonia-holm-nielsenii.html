Danthonia holm-nielsenii

Danthonia holm-nielsenii, commonly known as the Holm-Nielsen's danthonia, is a rare and specialized grass species native to the island of St. Helena in the South Atlantic Ocean.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique botanical relic from extinction.
