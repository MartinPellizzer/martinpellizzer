Didactyle meridensis

Didactyle meridensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
