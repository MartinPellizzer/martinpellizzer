Diasperus ruscifolius

Diasperus ruscifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a dark green to blackish upper surface with a distinctive, rough texture. The lower surface is white and covered with small, rounded lobes. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests.

This lichen plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator of air quality.
