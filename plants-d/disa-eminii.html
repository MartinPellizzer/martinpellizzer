Disa eminii

Disa eminii, commonly known as the Orange River Disa, is a striking member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along riverbanks and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Disa eminii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
