Dodonaea candolleana

Dodonaea candolleana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought. It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also a symbol of Australian national identity.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
