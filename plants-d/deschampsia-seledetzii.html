Deschampsia seledetzii

Deschampsia seledetzii, commonly known as Seladetz's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage that gives it a delicate appearance. The plant produces slender spikelets that are often tinged with brown or purple at the tips, adding visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
