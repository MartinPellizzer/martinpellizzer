Dudleya calcicola

Dudleya calcicola, commonly known as the limestone Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green with a powdery coating. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces vibrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
