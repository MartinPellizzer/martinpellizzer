Dictamnus albus var. himalayensis

Dictamnus albus var. himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan white dictamnus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, peppery scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
