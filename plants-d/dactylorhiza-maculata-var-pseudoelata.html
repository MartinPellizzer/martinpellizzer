Dactylorhiza maculata var. pseudoelata

Dactylorhiza maculata var. pseudoelata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale pink or white flowers with distinctive purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands, bogs, and heathlands. It has a complex root system with mycorrhizal associations that aid in nutrient uptake.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
