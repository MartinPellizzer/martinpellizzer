Daphne macrophylla

Daphne macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are thick and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flowering period, it is highly sought after by horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
