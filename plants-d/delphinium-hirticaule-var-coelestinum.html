Delphinium hirticaule var. coelestinum

Delphinium hirticaule var. coelestinum, commonly known as the Blue Milkweed Delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
