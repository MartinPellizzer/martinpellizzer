Dioscorea obcuneata

Dioscorea obcuneata, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, tuberous roots that are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

The plant's rapid growth and climbing habit make it both a valuable food source and a potential invasive species in non-native habitats.
