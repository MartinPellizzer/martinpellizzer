Diosma obtusifolia

Diosma obtusifolia, commonly known as the woolly diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a sprawling growth habit with woody stems that become covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, also adorned with the same soft, pubescent coating. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often displaying shades of pink or purple.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
