Ditassa caucana

Ditassa caucana, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its most striking feature is its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
