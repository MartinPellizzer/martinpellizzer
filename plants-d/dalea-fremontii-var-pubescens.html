Dalea fremontii var. pubescens

Dalea fremontii var. pubescens, commonly known as the Fremont's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Dalea fremontii var. pubescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
