Draba bracteata

Draba bracteata, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to alpine and arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. It produces delicate white or pale purple flowers with four petals, often resembling a bell shape.

Draba bracteata is known for its resilience, adapting to extreme conditions with a short growing season and compact growth form.
