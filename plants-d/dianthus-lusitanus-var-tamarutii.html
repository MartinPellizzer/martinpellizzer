Dianthus lusitanus var. tamarutii

Dianthus lusitanus var. tamarutii, commonly known as the Tamarutti campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic plant.
