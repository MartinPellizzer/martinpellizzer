Danthonia brachyathera

Danthonia brachyathera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Danthonia brachyathera plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
