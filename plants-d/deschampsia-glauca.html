Deschampsia glauca

Deschampsia glauca, commonly known as bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue-green sheaths and slender, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct blue-green hue, which gives it its common name.

Deschampsia glauca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
