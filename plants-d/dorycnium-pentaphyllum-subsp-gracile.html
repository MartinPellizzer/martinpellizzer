Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp. gracile

Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the slender goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features a creeping rhizome and produces slender, branching stems with palmately divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
