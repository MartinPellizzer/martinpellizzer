Dactylorhiza elata var. sesquipedalis

Dactylorhiza elata var. sesquipedalis, commonly known as the long-stemmed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, bogs, and other moist habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of linear leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.
