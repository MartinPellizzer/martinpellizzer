Diplachne maeotica

Diplachne maeotica, commonly known as the Crimean flat grass, is a perennial grass species native to the steppes of Eastern Europe and parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, creeping stems that form dense mats, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of loose, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Diplachne maeotica is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
