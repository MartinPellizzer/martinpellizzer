Delphinium iliense

Delphinium iliense, commonly known as the Ili Valley larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with lobes that are often hairy and serrated.

Delphinium iliense is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
