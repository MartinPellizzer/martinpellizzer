Dracontocephalium

Dracontocephalium, commonly known as the "serpent's head," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its unique, spiny, and often hairy leaves that resemble a serpent's head, giving it its common name. The flowers are typically small and clustered in composite heads, with both ray and disk florets. Native to regions of southern Africa, these plants often thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

They are known for their resilience and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
