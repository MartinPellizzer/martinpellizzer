Dahlia mixtecana

Dahlia mixtecana, commonly known as the Mixtec dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that come in a variety of hues. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually borne in compact inflorescences, and the species is noted for its adaptability to different soil types.

Dahlia mixtecana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
