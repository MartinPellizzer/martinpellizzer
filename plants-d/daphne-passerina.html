Daphne passerina

Daphne passerina, commonly known as the snowdrop daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens despite its toxic properties.
