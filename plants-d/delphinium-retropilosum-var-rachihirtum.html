Delphinium retropilosum var. rachihirtum

Delphinium retropilosum var. rachihirtum, commonly known as the hairy leaf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. This variety is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.
