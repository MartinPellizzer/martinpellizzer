Daucus microsciadius

Daucus microsciadius, commonly known as the small-flowered carrot, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a biennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp, typical of the carrot family.

It is often found in Europe and parts of Asia, and it has been historically used in herbal medicine.
