Deschampsia conferta

Deschampsia conferta, commonly known as hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often adorned with short, bristly hairs.

Deschampsia conferta plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small mammals.
