Dioscorea amaranthoides var. ulei

Dioscorea amaranthoides var. ulei, commonly known as the Ulei yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible tuberous roots that are used in local cuisines.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it a distinctive species within the genus Dioscorea.
