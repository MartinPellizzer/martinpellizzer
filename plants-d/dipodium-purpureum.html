Dipodium purpureum

Dipodium purpureum, commonly known as the purple dipodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands or marshy environments. It produces small, three-angled berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by water or animals.

Dipodium purpureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
