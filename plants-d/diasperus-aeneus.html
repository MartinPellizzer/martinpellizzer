Diasperus aeneus

Diasperus aeneus, commonly known as the coppery sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its stems are often creeping or prostrate, forming dense mats that help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
