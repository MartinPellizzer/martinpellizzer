Dolicholobium rubrum

Dolicholobium rubrum, commonly known as the red globular cactus, is a unique succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the warmer months, adding visual interest to its landscape. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss and deter herbivores.

Dolicholobium rubrum is also noted for its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
