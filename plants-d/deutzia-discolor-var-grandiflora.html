Deutzia discolor var. grandiflora

Deutzia discolor var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Chinese deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its showy blooms and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
