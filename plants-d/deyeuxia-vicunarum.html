Deyeuxia vicunarum

Deyeuxia vicunarum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that form dense tussocks, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Deyeuxia vicunarum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
