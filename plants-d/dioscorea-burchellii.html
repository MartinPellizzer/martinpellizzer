Dioscorea burchellii

Dioscorea burchellii, commonly known as the African yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used for both culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, green berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and can grow quite long.

The tubers of Dioscorea burchellii are a staple food in many African communities and are valued for their nutritional content.
