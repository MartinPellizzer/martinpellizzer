Distephanus grevei

Distephanus grevei, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Distephanus grevei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
