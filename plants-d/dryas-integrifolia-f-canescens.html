Dryas integrifolia f. canescens

Dryas integrifolia f. canescens, commonly known as the white mountain avens, is a variant of the white avens species native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are typically grayish-white in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in a short inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas of North America and parts of Europe.
