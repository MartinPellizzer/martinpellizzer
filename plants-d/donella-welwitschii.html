Donella welwitschii

Donella welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia mirabilis, is a unique and ancient plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is one of the oldest living seed plants, with a lineage dating back over 140 million years. The plant is characterized by its two large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 4 meters in length. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions by accessing underground water.

Donella welwitschii is also notable for its ability to produce a distinctive, fragrant resin that has been used by indigenous communities for various purposes.
