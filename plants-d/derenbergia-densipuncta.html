Derenbergia densipuncta

Derenbergia densipuncta, commonly known as the dense-punctured daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in fine, raised dots, giving it its scientific name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
