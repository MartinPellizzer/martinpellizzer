Delphinium intermedium var. divergens

Delphinium intermedium var. divergens, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, each with five petals and prominent spurred tips. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is noted for its preference for well-drained, alkaline soils and full sun exposure.
