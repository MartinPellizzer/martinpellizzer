Dodecatheon integrifolium var. minus

Dodecatheon integrifolium var. minus, commonly known as the small-flowered glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
