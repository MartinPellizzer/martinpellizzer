Dioscorea villosa subsp. quaternata

Dioscorea villosa subsp.

quaternata, commonly known as the Eastern arrowhead, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to the eastern United States. It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive tubers.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and are also a food source for some animals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and the shape of its leaves compared to other Dioscorea species.
