Dalea wilsonii

Dalea wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. It has small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Dalea wilsonii is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
