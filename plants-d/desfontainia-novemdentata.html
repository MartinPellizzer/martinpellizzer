Desfontainia novemdentata

Desfontainia novemdentata, commonly known as the nine-toothed fern, is a distinctive fern species characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

Its fronds are lanceolate and bear nine prominent teeth along the margins, giving it its common name. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The botanical features of Desfontainia novemdentata make it a notable species in the study of fern diversity and evolution.
