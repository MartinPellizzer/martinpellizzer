Drupina cristata

Drupina cristata, commonly known as the cristate drupina, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits known as drupes, which contain a single seed. Drupina cristata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
