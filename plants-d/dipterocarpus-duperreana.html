Dipterocarpus duperreana

Dipterocarpus duperreana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark brown to blackish bark and long, pendulous fruits that contain winged seeds. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family, which is known for its economically important timber species. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
