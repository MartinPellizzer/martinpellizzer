Desmoncus ferox

Desmoncus ferox, commonly known as the fierce desmoncus, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions through its thick, fleshy leaves that store water. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

However, due to its invasive potential in some areas, it requires careful management.
