Diastema bracteosum

Diastema bracteosum, commonly known as the bracted diastema, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescence, where modified leaves (bracts) enclose the flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are often small and clustered, with a variety of colors that attract pollinators.

Diastema bracteosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
