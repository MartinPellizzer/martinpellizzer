Dianthera secundiflora

Dianthera secundiflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters along the stem. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Dianthera secundiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
