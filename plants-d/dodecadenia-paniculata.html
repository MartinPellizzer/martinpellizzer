Dodecadenia paniculata

Dodecadenia paniculata, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
