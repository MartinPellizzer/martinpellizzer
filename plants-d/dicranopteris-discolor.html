Dicranopteris discolor

Dicranopteris discolor, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that can form dense mats. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Dicranopteris discolor plays an important ecological role in aquatic and wetland habitats.
