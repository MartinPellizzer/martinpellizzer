Diospyros mairei

Diospyros mairei, commonly known as the Maire's persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
