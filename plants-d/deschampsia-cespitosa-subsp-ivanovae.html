Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. ivanovae

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

ivanovae, commonly known as Ivanov's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage that turns golden in the fall.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its ecological importance in stabilizing rocky and arid landscapes.
