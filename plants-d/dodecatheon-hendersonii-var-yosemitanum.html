Dodecatheon hendersonii var. yosemitanum

Dodecatheon hendersonii var. yosemitanum, commonly known as the Yosemite bead lily, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinct red or purple spot at the base. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streamside habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is a member of the Primulaceae family.
