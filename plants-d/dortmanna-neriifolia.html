Dortmanna neriifolia

Dortmanna neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, often velvety leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
