Dioscorea sapinii

Dioscorea sapinii, commonly known as the Alpine yam, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by oval, winged fruits. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and have a starchy, edible quality.

Dioscorea sapinii thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude habitats.
