Dryopteris reptans var. angusta

Dryopteris reptans var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood fern, is a variant of the common wood fern characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped fronds.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a short, stout rhizome with dark brown, scaly stems. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to woodland gardens.
