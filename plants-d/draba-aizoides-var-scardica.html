Draba aizoides var. scardica

Draba aizoides var. scardica, commonly known as the Scardic Draba, is a low-growing, perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, where it forms dense mats on rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
