Darwinia masonii

Darwinia masonii, commonly known as the Mason's Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Darwinia masonii is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with narrow, leathery leaves.
