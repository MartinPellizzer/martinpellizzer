Dipodium fragrans

Dipodium fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant is noted for its fragrant spores, which release a mild, pleasant aroma. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental plant collections.
