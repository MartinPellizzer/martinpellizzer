Dicrastylis georgei var. cuneata

Dicrastylis georgei var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in loose spikes. Its flowering period usually occurs during the cooler months, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
