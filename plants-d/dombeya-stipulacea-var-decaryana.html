Dombeya stipulacea var. decaryana

Dombeya stipulacea var. decaryana, commonly known as the silverleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely toothed, gray-green leaves that provide ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
