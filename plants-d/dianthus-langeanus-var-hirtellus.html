Dianthus langeanus var. hirtellus

Dianthus langeanus var. hirtellus, commonly known as the rough-fruited campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
