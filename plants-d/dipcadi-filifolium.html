Dipcadi filifolium

Dipcadi filifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaved bulbous plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that store nutrients for the next growing season.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
