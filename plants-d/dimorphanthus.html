Dimorphanthus

Dimorphanthus, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
