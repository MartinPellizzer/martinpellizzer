Dimorphandra cuprea subsp. cuprea

Dimorphandra cuprea subsp.

cuprea, commonly known as the red ironwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown bark and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by small, woody fruits.

This species is often found in dry forests and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
