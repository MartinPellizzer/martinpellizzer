Dipladenia illustris var. tomentosa

Dipladenia illustris var. tomentosa, commonly known as the velvety dipladenia, is a trailing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Due to its attractive foliage and flowers, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
