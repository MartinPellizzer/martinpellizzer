Dracocephalum stachydifolium

Dracocephalum stachydifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Dracocephalum stachydifolium is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic effects.
