Diospyros camposii

Diospyros camposii, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a small, orange-red drupe that is edible and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Diospyros camposii is often found in humid, tropical lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
