Diospyros hemicycloides

Diospyros hemicycloides, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, elliptic leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
