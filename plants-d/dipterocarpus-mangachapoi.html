Dipterocarpus mangachapoi

Dipterocarpus mangachapoi, commonly known as the Malayan dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, winged seeds and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The tree produces flowers in panicles and has thick, flaky bark. It thrives in lowland rainforests and is an important component of the region's biodiversity.

Its wood is valuable but is also threatened by deforestation.
