Dichapetalum acuminatum

Dichapetalum acuminatum, commonly known as the African starwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
