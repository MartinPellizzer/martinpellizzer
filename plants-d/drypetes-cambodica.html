Drypetes cambodica

Drypetes cambodica, commonly known as the Cambodian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
