Danthonia raoulii var. rubra

Danthonia raoulii var. rubra, commonly known as the red-haired grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as coastal dunes and sandy soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal environments.
