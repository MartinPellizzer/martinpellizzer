Dorycnium jordanii subsp. germanicum

Dorycnium jordanii subsp.

germanicum, commonly known as the German spurred gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, and it produces small, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
