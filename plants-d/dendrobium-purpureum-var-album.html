Dendrobium purpureum var. album

Dendrobium purpureum var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem with numerous nodes and is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that can last several weeks, often in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
