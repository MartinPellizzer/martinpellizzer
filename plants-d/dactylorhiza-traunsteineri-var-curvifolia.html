Dactylorhiza traunsteineri var. curvifolia

Dactylorhiza traunsteineri var. curvifolia, commonly known as the bent-leaf marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies such as ponds and streams. The plant features long, narrow leaves that curve downward, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with three petals and three sepals, forming a showy cluster.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
