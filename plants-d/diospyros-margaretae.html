Diospyros margaretae

Diospyros margaretae, commonly known as the Margaretae persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
