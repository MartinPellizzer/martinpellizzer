Dodecatheon parviflorum

Dodecatheon parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Dodecatheon parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
