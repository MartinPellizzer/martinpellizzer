Diospyros leonardii

Diospyros leonardii, commonly known as the Leonard's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that ripens in late summer to early autumn.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
