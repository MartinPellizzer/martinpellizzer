Dendrobenthamia japonica var. leucotricha

Dendrobenthamia japonica var. leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers with a faintly fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its unique, hairy appearance on both stems and leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
