Dalea anthonyi

Dalea anthonyi, commonly known as Anthony's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Dalea anthonyi is often found in grasslands, rocky slopes, and along stream banks.
