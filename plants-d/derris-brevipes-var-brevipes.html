Derris brevipes var. brevipes

Derris brevipes var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed derris, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing flat, oblong seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of rotenone in its roots.
