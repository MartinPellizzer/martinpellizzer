Diplotropis purpurea f. leptophylla

Diplotropis purpurea f. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf purple coneflower, is a cultivar of the purple coneflower species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
