Dyckia horridula

Dyckia horridula, commonly known as the spiny dyckia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage. The plant features stiff, spiny leaves that are often covered in a layer of white powdery bloom. Dyckia horridula produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
