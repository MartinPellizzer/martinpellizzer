Dypsis hirtula

Dypsis hirtula, commonly known as the hairy areca palm, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife.

Dypsis hirtula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to indoor conditions.
