Didymotheca thesioides var. stricta

Didymotheca thesioides var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces conical flower heads composed of small yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat support.
