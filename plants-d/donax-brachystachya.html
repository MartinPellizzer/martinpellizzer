Donax brachystachya

Donax brachystachya, commonly known as the dwarf glassworts, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens for its ornamental value.
