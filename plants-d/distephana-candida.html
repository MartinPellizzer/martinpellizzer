Distephana candida

Distephana candida, commonly known as the white-flowered distephana, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Distephana candida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
