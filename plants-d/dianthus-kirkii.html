Dianthus kirkii

Dianthus kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's campion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the stems are erect and branching.

Dianthus kirkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
