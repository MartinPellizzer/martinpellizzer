Desmodium austrojaponense

Desmodium austrojaponense, commonly known as the Japanese burr legume, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces hairy, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by attachment to animals or clothing. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
