Daphne wilsonii

Daphne wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
