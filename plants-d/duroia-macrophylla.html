Duroia macrophylla

Duroia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The plant is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisine and as a source of carbohydrates.
