Dryopsila verrucosa

Dryopsila verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in rough, hairy textures. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
