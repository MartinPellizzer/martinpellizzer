Dissotis carrissoi

Dissotis carrissoi, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warm seasons. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite on the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Dissotis carrissoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and horticultural displays.
