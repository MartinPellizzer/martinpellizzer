Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp. lancifolium

Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp.

lancifolium, commonly known as the lanceleaf daphniphyllum, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
