Dysoxylum arborescens var. timorense

Dysoxylum arborescens var. timorense, commonly known as the Timor white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Maluku Islands and parts of Indonesia.

It is a deciduous species that can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
