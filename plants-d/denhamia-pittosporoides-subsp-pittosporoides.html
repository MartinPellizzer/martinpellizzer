Denhamia pittosporoides subsp. pittosporoides

Denhamia pittosporoides subsp.

pittosporoides, commonly known as the coastal pittosporum, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
