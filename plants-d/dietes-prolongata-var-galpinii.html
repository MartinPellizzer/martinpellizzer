Dietes prolongata var. galpinii

Dietes prolongata var. galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's reed, is a striking evergreen perennial that features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue.

Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces tall, upright flower spikes that bloom in shades of purple or pink, adding visual interest to the landscape. It is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense clumps over time.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
