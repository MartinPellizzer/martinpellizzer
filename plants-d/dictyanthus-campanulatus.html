Dictyanthus campanulatus

Dictyanthus campanulatus, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Dictyanthus campanulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
