Discocactus hartmannii var. mamillosus

Discocactus hartmannii var. mamillosus, commonly known as the Mamillosus Discocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, disc-like stems that are covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and often endangered species in the wild.
