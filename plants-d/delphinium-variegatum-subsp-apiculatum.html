Delphinium variegatum subsp. apiculatum

Delphinium variegatum subsp.

apiculatum, commonly known as the variable larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically spurred at the base.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
