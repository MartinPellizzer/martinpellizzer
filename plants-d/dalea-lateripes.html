Dalea lateripes

Dalea lateripes, commonly known as the prairie milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and prairies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open to release the seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Dalea lateripes plays a role in soil stabilization and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
