Daucus maximus

Daucus maximus, commonly known as the giant parsley or wild carrot, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like carrots and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces umbrella-shaped clusters of small white or pink flowers. Its fruit is a dry, ridged schizocarp that splits into two halves when mature.

Daucus maximus is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
