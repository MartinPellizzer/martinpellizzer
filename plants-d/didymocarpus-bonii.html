Didymocarpus bonii

Didymocarpus bonii, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into distinctive, round, golden fruits. These fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

Didymocarpus bonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
