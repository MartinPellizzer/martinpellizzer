Desmanthodium trianae

Desmanthodium trianae, commonly known as the Trianas' desmanthodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in grasslands and forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
