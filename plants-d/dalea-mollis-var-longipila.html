Dalea mollis var. longipila

Dalea mollis var. longipila, commonly known as the longpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces cylindrical, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being narrow and hairy.
