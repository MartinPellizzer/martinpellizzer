Diplazium virescens var. okinawaense

Diplazium virescens var. okinawaense, commonly known as the Okinawan water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to the Ryukyu Islands.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant has fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to humid, tropical climates and its ecological role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
