Draba borealis var. ochotensis

Draba borealis var. ochotensis, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a small perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine and tundra environments. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, which bloom in early summer. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is capable of surviving in nutrient-poor soils.
