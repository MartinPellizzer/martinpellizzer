Dioscorea yokusae

Dioscorea yokusae, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their nutritional content and medicinal properties.

Dioscorea yokusae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its culinary and therapeutic benefits.
