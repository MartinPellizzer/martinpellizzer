Draba mariae-aliceae

Draba mariae-aliceae, commonly known as the Alice Marie's rockcress, is a small flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and mustard-like scent. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
