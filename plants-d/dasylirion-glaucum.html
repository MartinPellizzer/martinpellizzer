Dasylirion glaucum

Dasylirion glaucum, commonly known as the blue agave, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, bluish-gray leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, typically blooming after many years. It is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
