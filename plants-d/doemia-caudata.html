Doemia caudata

Doemia caudata, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Doemia caudata is known for its ability to produce copious amounts of nectar, attracting bees and other pollinators.
