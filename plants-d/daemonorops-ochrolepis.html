Daemonorops ochrolepis

Daemonorops ochrolepis, commonly known as the yellow-veined cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically a deep green with prominent yellow veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its conservation status, it is sometimes threatened by habitat loss and over-collection.
