Distictis gnaphalantha subsp. rhynchocarpa

Distictis gnaphalantha subsp.

rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit sandmat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces distinctive, elongated, spiny fruits that contain minute seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
