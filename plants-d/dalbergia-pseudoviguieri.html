Dalbergia pseudoviguieri

Dalbergia pseudoviguieri, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a species of legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several flattened, winged seeds.
