Diervilla floribunda var. versicolor

Diervilla floribunda var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and purple, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features dark green, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the garden.
