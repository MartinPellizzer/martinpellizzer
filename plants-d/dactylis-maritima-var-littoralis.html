Dactylis maritima var. littoralis

Dactylis maritima var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal dactylis, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and maritime environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is well adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a characteristic species of coastal vegetation.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
