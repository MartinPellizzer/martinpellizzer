Daviesia asperula subsp. obliqua

Daviesia asperula subsp.

obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native gardens.
