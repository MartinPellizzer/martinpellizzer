Draba sambukii

Draba sambukii, commonly known as the Sambuk alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Draba sambukii is known for its delicate appearance, with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

It plays an important ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.
