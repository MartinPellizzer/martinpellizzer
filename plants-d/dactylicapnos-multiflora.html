Dactylicapnos multiflora

Dactylicapnos multiflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Dactylicapnos multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
