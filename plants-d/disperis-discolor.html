Disperis discolor

Disperis discolor, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically produces small, clustered inflorescences with petals that are often speckled or streaked with contrasting colors. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
