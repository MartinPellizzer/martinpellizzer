Dicoma foliosa

Dicoma foliosa, commonly known as the leafy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with a rosette growth habit. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Dicoma foliosa is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in dry and sunny conditions.
