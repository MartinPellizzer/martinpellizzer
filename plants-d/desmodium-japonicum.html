Desmodium japonicum

Desmodium japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese velvetleaf, is a tropical and subtropical plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seed pods are cylindrical and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Desmodium japonicum is often found in disturbed soils and can be an invasive species in certain regions.
