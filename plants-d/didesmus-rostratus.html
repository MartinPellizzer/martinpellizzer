Didesmus rostratus

Didesmus rostratus, commonly known as the rostrated dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
