Dracaena medeoloides

Dracaena medeoloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dracaena, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Dracaena medeoloides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
