Dianthus haematocalyx subsp. sibthorpii

Dianthus haematocalyx subsp.

sibthorpii, commonly known as the Kashmir pink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its showy, pink to crimson flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
