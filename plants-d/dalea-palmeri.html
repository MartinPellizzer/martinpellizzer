Dalea palmeri

Dalea palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with thread-like leaflets and produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Dalea palmeri is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
