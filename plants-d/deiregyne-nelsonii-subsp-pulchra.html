Deiregyne nelsonii subsp. pulchra

Deiregyne nelsonii subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the purple-flowered deiregyne, is a member of the Acanthaceae family. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often borne in dense clusters.

The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to parts of Central and South America.
