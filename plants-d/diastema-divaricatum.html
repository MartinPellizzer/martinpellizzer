Diastema divaricatum

Diastema divaricatum, commonly known as the variable diastema, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
