Deverra denudata subsp. aphylla

Deverra denudata subsp.

aphylla, commonly known as the leafless devil's claw, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its absence of leaves, with the stems bearing the primary photosynthetic structures.

The plant produces striking, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a distinctive, elongated capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
