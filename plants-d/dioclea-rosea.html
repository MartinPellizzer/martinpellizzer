Dioclea rosea

Dioclea rosea, commonly known as the pink passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are adorned with five sepals and five petals. Dioclea rosea is part of the Passifloraceae family, which includes other passionflower species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
