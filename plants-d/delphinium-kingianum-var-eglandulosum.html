Delphinium kingianum var. eglandulosum

Delphinium kingianum var. eglandulosum, commonly known as the King's Delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
