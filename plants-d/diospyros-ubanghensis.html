Diospyros ubanghensis

Diospyros ubanghensis, commonly known as the Ubanghensis persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies, and the tree is valued for its hard, dense wood.
