Delphinium lalesaricum

Delphinium lalesaricum, commonly known as the Lalesar larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are showy, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
