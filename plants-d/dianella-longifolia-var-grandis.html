Dianella longifolia var. grandis

Dianella longifolia var. grandis, commonly known as the broadleaf flax lily, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are broader and more robust than those of the typical species, often reaching lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant produces striking blue or purple inflorescences that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its status as a valued horticultural plant.
