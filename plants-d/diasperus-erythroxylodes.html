Diasperus erythroxylodes

Diasperus erythroxylodes, commonly known as the redwood fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate frond structure and vibrant reddish hues.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant exhibits a creeping growth habit, with rhizomes that form dense mats, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The species is notable for its ability to retain moisture, making it well-suited for humid climates and boggy soils.
