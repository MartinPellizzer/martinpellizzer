Dolichos malosanus var. trifidus

Dolichos malosanus var. trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifid leaves, meaning they are divided into three distinct lobes, which is a key identifying trait. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing edible seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
