Diporidium cinnabarinum

Diporidium cinnabarinum, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing and sought-after ornamental plant.
