Draba cinerea var. dovrensis

Draba cinerea var. dovrensis, commonly known as the grey draba, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are densely hairy and often covered in a whitish bloom. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters during the spring.

Draba cinerea var. dovrensis is noted for its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
