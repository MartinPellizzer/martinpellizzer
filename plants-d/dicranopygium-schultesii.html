Dicranopygium schultesii

Dicranopygium schultesii, commonly known as the Schultesi's feather moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems covered with leaf-like structures. The leaves are typically elongated and gradually tapering, with a distinct midrib and a smooth or slightly toothed margin. This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors and on tree trunks.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
