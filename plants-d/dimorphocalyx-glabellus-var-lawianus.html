Dimorphocalyx glabellus var. lawianus

Dimorphocalyx glabellus var. lawianus, commonly known as the Lawianus hibiscus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
