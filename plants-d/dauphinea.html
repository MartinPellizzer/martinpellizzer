Dauphinea

Dauphinea, commonly known as the "pink bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Dauphinea grandiflora, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep violet. Its foliage consists of ovate, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Dauphinea is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive blooms and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
