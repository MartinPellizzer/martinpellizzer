Delphinium fissum subsp. sordidum

Delphinium fissum subsp.

sordidum, commonly known as the sordid larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers with prominent, spurred petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive appearance and is often found in rocky or meadow-like environments at high elevations.
