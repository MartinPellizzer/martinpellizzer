Diuris punctata var. daltonii

Diuris punctata var. daltonii, commonly known as the Dalton's Spur Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with a single, fleshy, tuberous rootstock. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a few narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the showy, spurred flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream with reddish or purplish markings.

The species is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils in open, sunny habitats.
