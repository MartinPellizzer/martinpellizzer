Dichanthelium sphaerocarpon

Dichanthelium sphaerocarpon, commonly known as the globehead panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Dichanthelium sphaerocarpon plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
