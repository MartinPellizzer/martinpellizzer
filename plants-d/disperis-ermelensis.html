Disperis ermelensis

Disperis ermelensis, commonly known as the Ermelense dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of disk flowers, which are pollinated by insects.

Disperis ermelensis is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
