Didymocarpus cordatus

Didymocarpus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf soursop, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a smooth, green, spiny fruit that turns yellow when ripe. Its fruit is edible, with a creamy, sweet pulp that is often used in local cuisines.
