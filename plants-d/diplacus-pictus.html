Diplacus pictus

Diplacus pictus, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a flowering species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Diplacus pictus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
