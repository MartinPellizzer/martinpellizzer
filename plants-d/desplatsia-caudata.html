Desplatsia caudata

Desplatsia caudata, commonly known as the featherleaf desplatsia, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Desplatsia caudata thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
