Digitaria gymnotheca

Digitaria gymnotheca, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and grassland ecosystems. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. It is drought-tolerant and plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities.
