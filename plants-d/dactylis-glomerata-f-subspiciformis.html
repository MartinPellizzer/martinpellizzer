Dactylis glomerata f. subspiciformis

Dactylis glomerata f. subspiciformis, commonly known as the spike-form bentgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and compact inflorescence. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are tightly arranged along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
