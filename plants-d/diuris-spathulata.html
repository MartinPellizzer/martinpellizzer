Diuris spathulata

Diuris spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, often with a distinctive spur. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is typically found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Diuris spathulata is a perennial species that blooms during the cooler months, usually from late autumn to early spring.
