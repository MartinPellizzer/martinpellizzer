Dalea lachnostachys

Dalea lachnostachys, commonly known as the prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, fluffy flower clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea lachnostachys is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
