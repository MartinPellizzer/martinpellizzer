Diospyros elliptica var. fijiensis

Diospyros elliptica var. fijiensis, commonly known as the Fiji persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and smooth, glossy bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary practices.
