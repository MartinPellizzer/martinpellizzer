Dolia tomentosa

Dolia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly dolia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. Dolia tomentosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
