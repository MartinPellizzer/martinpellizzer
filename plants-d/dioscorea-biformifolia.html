Dioscorea biformifolia

Dioscorea biformifolia, commonly known as the two-lobed yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves twining stems that can reach several meters in length.

The tubers of D. biformifolia are sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value and potential medicinal properties.
