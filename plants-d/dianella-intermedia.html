Dianella intermedia

Dianella intermedia, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and strap-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are fibrous and have been historically used for making baskets and other woven items.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.
