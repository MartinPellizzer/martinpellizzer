Dendrocalamus poilanei

Dendrocalamus poilanei, commonly known as the Poilane bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. This tall bamboo can grow up to 20 meters in height, with slender, hollow stalks that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a pale green color.

The plant is valued for its rapid growth and versatility, making it an important resource in local economies.
