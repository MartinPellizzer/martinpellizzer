Drosera eremaea

Drosera eremaea, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a carnivorous plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a small, mat-forming species that thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant features delicate, hairy leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water.

Its unique appearance and specialized feeding mechanism make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
