Distictis cinerea

Distictis cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for various health purposes.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
