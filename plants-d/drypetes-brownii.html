Drypetes brownii

Drypetes brownii, commonly known as the African cherry or red cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various animals. Its ecological role includes supporting biodiversity and contributing to soil stability in its native habitat.
