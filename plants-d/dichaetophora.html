Dichaetophora

Dichaetophora, commonly known as the featherleaf lichen, is a genus of lichens belonging to the family Pilocarpaceae.

These organisms are characterized by their unique thallus structure, which often features a foliose or fruticose growth form. Dichaetophora species typically thrive in moist, shaded environments, such as on tree bark or rocks in temperate regions. They are known for their intricate symbiotic relationship between the fungal and algal components.

The genus is of particular interest to mycologists due to its ecological significance and morphological diversity.
