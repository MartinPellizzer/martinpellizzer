Diplotaxis muralis subsp. ceratophylla

Diplotaxis muralis subsp.

ceratophylla, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single umbel, which bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its unique morphology and adaptations make it well-suited for arid environments.
