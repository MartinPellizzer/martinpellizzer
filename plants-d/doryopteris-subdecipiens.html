Doryopteris subdecipiens

Doryopteris subdecipiens, commonly known as the false sword fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are often used in horticultural settings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clustered, spore-bearing leaves.

The botanical features of Doryopteris subdecipiens make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
