Desmodium canadense

Desmodium canadense, commonly known as Canada burr or wild indigo, is a leguminous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that encase its small, rounded seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Desmodium canadense plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
