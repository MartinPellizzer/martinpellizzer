Dyschoriste vinacea

Dyschoriste vinacea, commonly known as the red vine or red-stemmed dyschoriste, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that often display a reddish hue, especially when mature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Dyschoriste vinacea is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits and is also used as a source of natural dyes.
