Dioscorea metallica

Dioscorea metallica, commonly known as the metallic yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been historically used as a food source.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and requires careful preparation before consumption.
