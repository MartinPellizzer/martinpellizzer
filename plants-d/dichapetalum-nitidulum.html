Dichapetalum nitidulum

Dichapetalum nitidulum, commonly known as the velvetleaf stinkwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is notable for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
