Dudleya cultrata

Dudleya cultrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to coastal areas of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically narrow and fleshy, often covered in a layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Dudleya cultrata is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care once established.
