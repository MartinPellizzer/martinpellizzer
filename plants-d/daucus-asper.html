Daucus asper

Daucus asper, commonly known as rough carrot, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a taproot that is typically not edible, though it resembles that of the common carrot. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive rough texture, giving it its common name.

The small, yellowish flowers appear in umbels and are followed by small, dry fruits.
