Dolichos pseudopachyrhizus var. kilimandschari

Dolichos pseudopachyrhizus var. kilimandschari, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable crop in regions with limited water resources.

The seeds are rich in protein and are used as a traditional food source in local communities.
