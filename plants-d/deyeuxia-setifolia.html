Deyeuxia setifolia

Deyeuxia setifolia, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments in Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Deyeuxia setifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
