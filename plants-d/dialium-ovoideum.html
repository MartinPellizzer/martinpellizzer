Dialium ovoideum

Dialium ovoideum, commonly known as the oval flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, oval-shaped leaflets, giving it its specific epithet "ovoideum.". Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Dialium ovoideum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
