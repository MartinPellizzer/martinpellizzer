Dolichos bracteatus

Dolichos bracteatus, commonly known as the cluster bean or sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, curved pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional farming and ecological contexts.
