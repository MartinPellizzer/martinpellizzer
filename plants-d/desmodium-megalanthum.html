Desmodium megalanthum

Desmodium megalanthum, commonly known as the large-leaved burr legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically large and have a heart-shaped leaflet arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by the development of distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Desmodium megalanthum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
