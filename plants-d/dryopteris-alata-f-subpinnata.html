Dryopteris alata f. subpinnata

Dryopteris alata f. subpinnata, commonly known as the fragrant maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. The fronds are typically arching and have a distinctive, feathery appearance with a pale underside. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
