Diporidium wallichii var. normale

Diporidium wallichii var. normale, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers, which are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers are often adorned with a distinctive purple or reddish hue at the base, enhancing its ornamental value.

This species is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
