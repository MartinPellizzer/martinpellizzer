Dialium angolense

Dialium angolense, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are encased in a thick, woody pulp, which is used in traditional cuisine. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their antiseptic and astringent properties.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
