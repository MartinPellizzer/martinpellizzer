Dioscorea japonica var. oldhamii

Dioscorea japonica var. oldhamii, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a perennial vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are usually climbing and covered in small spines. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to East Asia, particularly Japan and China.

It is valued both for its culinary uses and traditional medicinal properties.
