Derris amoena

Derris amoena, commonly known as the pretty senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound and often used in traditional medicine for their antifungal and antiseptic properties.

The seeds contain toxic alkaloids, making the plant both valuable and potentially dangerous.
