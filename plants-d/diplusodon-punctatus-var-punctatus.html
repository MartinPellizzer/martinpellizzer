Diplusodon punctatus var. punctatus

Diplusodon punctatus var. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in forest understories. Its botanical structure includes a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs and linear leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
