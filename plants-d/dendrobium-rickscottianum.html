Dendrobium rickscottianum

Dendrobium rickscottianum, commonly known as the Rickscott's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts.
