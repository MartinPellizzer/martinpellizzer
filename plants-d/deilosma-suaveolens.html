Deilosma suaveolens

Deilosma suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Deilosma suaveolens is also noted for its fragrant foliage, which emits a subtle, sweet aroma.
