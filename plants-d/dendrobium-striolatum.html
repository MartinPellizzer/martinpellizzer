Dendrobium striolatum

Dendrobium striolatum, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and multiple stems that bear lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive red or purple stripe along the dorsal sepal and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium striolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
