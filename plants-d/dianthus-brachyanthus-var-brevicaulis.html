Dianthus brachyanthus var. brevicaulis

Dianthus brachyanthus var. brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or white, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are typically hairy as well. This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
