Diospyros ferrea var. humilis

Diospyros ferrea var. humilis, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon or small ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The plant has a dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in its native habitat. Its wood is hard and durable, historically used for tool handles and construction.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
