Dactylicapnos cordata

Dactylicapnos cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy, with a distinctive, bell-shaped flower that blooms in shades of blue or purple. The plant grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.

Dactylicapnos cordata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
