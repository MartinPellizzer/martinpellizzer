Dugandiodendron lenticellata

Dugandiodendron lenticellata, commonly known as the lenticellate woodbine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a woody vine characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant features distinctive lenticellate markings on its bark, which are small, cork-like pores that aid in gas exchange. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Dugandiodendron lenticellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
