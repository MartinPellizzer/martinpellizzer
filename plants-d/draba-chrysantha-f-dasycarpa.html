Draba chrysantha f. dasycarpa

Draba chrysantha f. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
