Dendrocolla compressa

Dendrocolla compressa, commonly known as the compressed tree fern, is a unique member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, compressed trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its growth is slow, and it can reach heights of up to 5 meters over several decades.

The plant's structure is adapted for efficient water retention, making it well-suited to its moist, tropical habitat.
