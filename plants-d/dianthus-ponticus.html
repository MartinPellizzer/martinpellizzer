Dianthus ponticus

Dianthus ponticus, commonly known as the Crimean carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers with prominent central markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branching.

Dianthus ponticus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
