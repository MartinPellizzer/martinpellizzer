Dacrydium beccarii var. subelatum

Dacrydium beccarii var. subelatum, commonly known as the mountain pine or subelated pine, is a variant of the Dacrydium genus native to New Zealand.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and compact, often pyramidal growth habit. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, typically found in mountainous regions with high altitudes. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
