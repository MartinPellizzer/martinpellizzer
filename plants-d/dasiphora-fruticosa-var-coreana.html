Dasiphora fruticosa var. coreana

Dasiphora fruticosa var. coreana, commonly known as Corean poppy, is a perennial shrub native to Korea.

It features upright, woody stems covered in dense, soft hairs, and it produces showy, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with 5-7 lobes, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
