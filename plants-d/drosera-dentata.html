Drosera dentata

Drosera dentata, commonly known as the serrated sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant's leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in wet, acidic environments and is often found in swamps or bogs.

Drosera dentata is a perennial plant that reproduces through seeds and can also propagate vegetatively.
