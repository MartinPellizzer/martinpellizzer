Dianthus mussini

Dianthus mussini, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Dianthus mussini is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
