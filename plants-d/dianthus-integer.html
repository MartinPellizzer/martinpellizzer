Dianthus integer

Dianthus integer, commonly known as the upright carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dense clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant features linear, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Dianthus integer blooms in late spring to early summer, producing flowers that are often used in floral arrangements.

It is a hardy perennial that prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
