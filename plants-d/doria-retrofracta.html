Doria retrofracta

Doria retrofracta, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
