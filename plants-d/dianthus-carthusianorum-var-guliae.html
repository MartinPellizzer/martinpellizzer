Dianthus carthusianorum var. guliae

Dianthus carthusianorum var. guliae, commonly known as the Gulia's carnation, is a compact, flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with white centers, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with broad, slightly hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
