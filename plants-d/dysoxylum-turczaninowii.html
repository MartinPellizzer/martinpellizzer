Dysoxylum turczaninowii

Dysoxylum turczaninowii, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The tree is often found in moist evergreen forests and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
