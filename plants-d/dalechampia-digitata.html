Dalechampia digitata

Dalechampia digitata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically followed by spiny, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Dalechampia digitata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
