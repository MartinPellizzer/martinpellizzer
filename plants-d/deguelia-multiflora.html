Deguelia multiflora

Deguelia multiflora, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in hanging clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
