Draba ludingensis

Draba ludingensis, commonly known as the Luding sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau in China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. This species is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and low-growing, mat-forming habit. It thrives in arid and alpine environments, often found in rocky soils and high-altitude meadows.

Draba ludingensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
