Diosphaera jacquinii subf. denudata

Diosphaera jacquinii subf.

denudata, commonly known as the naked subshrub, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is characterized by its small, simple leaves and unique floral structures.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent stems. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, a common feature in the Euphorbiaceae family.

The subshrub is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xerophytic landscapes.
