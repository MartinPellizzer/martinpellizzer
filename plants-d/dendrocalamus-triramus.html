Dendrocalamus triramus

Dendrocalamus triramus, commonly known as the three-branched bamboo, is a tall, slender grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, segmented stems and smooth, narrow leaves. This bamboo is highly valued for its strong and durable culms, which are widely used in construction and handicrafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense stands in lowland areas.

The plant's rapid growth rate makes it an important resource for sustainable materials and ecological restoration projects.
