Dendrochilum vanoverberghii

Dendrochilum vanoverberghii, commonly known as the Vanoverbergh's tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and linear, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats and is often found in forest understories.

Dendrochilum vanoverberghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
