Desmanthus palmeri

Desmanthus palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's burr, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive seed pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in spiny, burr-like structures that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.

Desmanthus palmeri is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
