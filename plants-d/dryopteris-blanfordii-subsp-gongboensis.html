Dryopteris blanfordii subsp. gongboensis

Dryopteris blanfordii subsp.

gongboensis, commonly known as the Gongbo brake, is a fern species native to the Himalayan regions of China and India. It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous forests. Its rhizomes are stout and covered with a thick, fibrous sheath.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its striking foliage.
