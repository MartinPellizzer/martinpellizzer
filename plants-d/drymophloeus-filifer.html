Drymophloeus filifer

Drymophloeus filifer, commonly known as the golden fig or wild fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
