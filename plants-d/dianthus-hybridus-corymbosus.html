Dianthus hybridus corymbosus

Dianthus hybridus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant has hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a distinctive appearance with a central cluster of stamens.

The clustered campion is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
