Diospyros tarim

Diospyros tarim, commonly known as the Tarim persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. Its fruit, which is typically a small, fleshy drupe, is edible and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Diospyros tarim is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
