Dalbergia glabra var. paucifoliolata

Dalbergia glabra var. paucifoliolata, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with few leaflets, which contribute to its varietal name "paucifoliolata.". The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
