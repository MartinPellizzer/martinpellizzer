Dianthus rhodopeus

Dianthus rhodopeus, commonly known as the Rhodope campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves and stems, which help it survive in dry, rocky environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Dianthus rhodopeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
