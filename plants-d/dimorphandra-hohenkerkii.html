Dimorphandra hohenkerkii

Dimorphandra hohenkerkii, commonly known as the Hohenkerkii candlewood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like, clustered inflorescences. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are alternate and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is adapted to the dry, tropical climate of its native habitat and plays a role in local ecosystems.
