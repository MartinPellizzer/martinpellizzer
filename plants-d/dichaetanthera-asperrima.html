Dichaetanthera asperrima

Dichaetanthera asperrima, commonly known as the rough anther grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has tough, fibrous leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for stabilizing soil in arid environments.
