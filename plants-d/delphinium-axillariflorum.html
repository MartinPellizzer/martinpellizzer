Delphinium axillariflorum

Delphinium axillariflorum, commonly known as the alpine delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in raceme-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium axillariflorum is adapted to cold, alpine environments and prefers well-drained, rocky soils.
