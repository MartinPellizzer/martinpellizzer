Dasylirion lindheimerianum

Dasylirion lindheimerianum, commonly known as the Lindheimer's sotol, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white to pale purple flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

The plant has fibrous roots and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
