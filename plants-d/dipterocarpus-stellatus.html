Dipterocarpus stellatus

Dipterocarpus stellatus, commonly known as the star-flowered dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy, star-shaped flowers. The plant is typically found in lowland evergreen rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its seeds are enclosed in hard, woody capsules, which are dispersed by animals.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and forest structure.
