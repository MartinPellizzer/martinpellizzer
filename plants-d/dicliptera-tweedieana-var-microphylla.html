Dicliptera tweedieana var. microphylla

Dicliptera tweedieana var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leafed dicliptera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
