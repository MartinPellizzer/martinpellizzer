Dendromyza uncinata

Dendromyza uncinata, commonly known as the hooked leaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, hook-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. Native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The plant's ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
