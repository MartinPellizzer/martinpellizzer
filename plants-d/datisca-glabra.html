Datisca glabra

Datisca glabra, commonly known as goat's beard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Datisca glabra is also known for its medicinal properties, having been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
