Drypetes ituriensis var. genuina

Drypetes ituriensis var. genuina, commonly known as the African ebony or blackwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by black, leathery fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
