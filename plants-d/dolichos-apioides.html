Dolichos apioides

Dolichos apioides, commonly known as the Indian bean or wild cowpea, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Dolichos apioides is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
