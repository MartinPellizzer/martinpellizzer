Dypsis scottiana

Dypsis scottiana, commonly known as the Scott's areca palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Dypsis scottiana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its slow growth rate makes it suitable for indoor or outdoor cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
