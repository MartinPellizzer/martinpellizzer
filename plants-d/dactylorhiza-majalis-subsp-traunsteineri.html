Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. traunsteineri

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

traunsteineri, commonly known as the Traunsteiner's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe. It typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, preferring damp, nutrient-poor soils.

The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing several pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and ecological preferences.
