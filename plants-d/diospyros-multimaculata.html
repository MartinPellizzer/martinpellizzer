Diospyros multimaculata

Diospyros multimaculata, commonly known as the spotted persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
