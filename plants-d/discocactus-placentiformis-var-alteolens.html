Discocactus placentiformis var. alteolens

Discocactus placentiformis var. alteolens, commonly known as the high-ribbed discocactus, is a unique cactus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its flattened, disc-like stems that are ribbed and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its growth form is adapted to arid environments, with succulent tissues that store water.

This variety is prized for its distinctive appearance and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
