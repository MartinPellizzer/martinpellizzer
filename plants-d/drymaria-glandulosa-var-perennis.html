Drymaria glandulosa var. perennis

Drymaria glandulosa var. perennis, commonly known as the perennial sandwort, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to arid and sandy environments.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky habitats.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
