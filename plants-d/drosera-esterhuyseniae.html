Drosera esterhuyseniae

Drosera esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysen's Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to South Africa.

It is a small, mat-forming species with hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and fens. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a dense spike, and it is known for its resilience in wet conditions.

Drosera esterhuyseniae is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ecological interest.
