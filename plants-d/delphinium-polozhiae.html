Delphinium polozhiae

Delphinium polozhiae, commonly known as the Polozhia larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium polozhiae is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
