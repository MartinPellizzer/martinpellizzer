Disa oliveriana

Disa oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's Disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Disa oliveriana prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
