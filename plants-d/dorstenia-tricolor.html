Dorstenia tricolor

Dorstenia tricolor, commonly known as the three-color false arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tri-colored bracts that range from deep red to yellow and green, creating a visually stunning display. The plant belongs to the family Araceae, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. Its unique inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the genus Dorstenia.

Dorstenia tricolor thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
