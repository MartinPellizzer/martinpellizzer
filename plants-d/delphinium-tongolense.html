Delphinium tongolense

Delphinium tongolense, commonly known as the Tongol larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Delphinium tongolense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
