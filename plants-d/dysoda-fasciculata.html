Dysoda fasciculata

Dysoda fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered dysoda, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom during the rainy season. Dysoda fasciculata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
