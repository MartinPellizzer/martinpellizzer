Daphne laciniata var. duclouxii

Daphne laciniata var. duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux daphne, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, leathery foliage with deep green, serrated leaves that provide a lush, textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by showy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical characteristics, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
