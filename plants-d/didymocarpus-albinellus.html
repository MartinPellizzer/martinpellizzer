Didymocarpus albinellus

Didymocarpus albinellus, commonly known as the white-fruited gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a mild, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
