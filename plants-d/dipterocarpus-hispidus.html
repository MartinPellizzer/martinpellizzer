Dipterocarpus hispidus

Dipterocarpus hispidus, commonly known as the rough-leaved dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and produces heavy, woody fruits that contain seeds. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is typically found in lowland rainforests. It plays a significant role in the ecological structure of these forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its timber is valued for its strength and durability, making it an economically important species in some regions.
