Diplazium megaphyllum

Diplazium megaphyllum, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a complex rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, forested areas.
