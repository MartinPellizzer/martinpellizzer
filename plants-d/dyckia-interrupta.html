Dyckia interrupta

Dyckia interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a rosette, often with a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Dyckia interrupta is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
