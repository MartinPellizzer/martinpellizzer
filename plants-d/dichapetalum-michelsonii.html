Dichapetalum michelsonii

Dichapetalum michelsonii, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a striking shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with contrasting red or purple markings. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections around the world.
