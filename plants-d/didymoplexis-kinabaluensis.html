Didymoplexis kinabaluensis

Didymoplexis kinabaluensis, commonly known as the Kinabalu waxplant, is a flowering plant native to Borneo, specifically found in the high-altitude regions of Mount Kinabalu.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, pendulous flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically found growing on rocky substrates or in forested areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its status as a rare species endemic to the region.
