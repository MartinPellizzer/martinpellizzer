Dichrostachys cinerea var. indica

Dichrostachys cinerea var. indica, commonly known as Indian velvet bean, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. It is often used in agroforestry systems for soil improvement and as a forage crop.

Additionally, it has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
