Ditaxis polymorpha var. brevifolia

Ditaxis polymorpha var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved ditaxis, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
