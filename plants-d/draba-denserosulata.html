Draba denserosulata

Draba denserosulata, commonly known as the dense-sulcate whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Draba denserosulata is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its hardy nature and ability to survive in harsh conditions.
