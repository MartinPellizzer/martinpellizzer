Dipterosperma personatum

Dipterosperma personatum, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with alternating leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in agroforestry systems.
