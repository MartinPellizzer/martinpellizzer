Dendrobium fuerstenbergianum

Dendrobium fuerstenbergianum, commonly known as the Fuerstenberg's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, cane-like pseudobulbs and long, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium fuerstenbergianum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
