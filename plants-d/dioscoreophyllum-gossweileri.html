Dioscoreophyllum gossweileri

Dioscoreophyllum gossweileri, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source in some regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential due to its attractive foliage.
