Dypsis gracilis var. sambiranensis

Dypsis gracilis var. sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, coconut-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
