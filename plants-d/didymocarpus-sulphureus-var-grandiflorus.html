Didymocarpus sulphureus var. grandiflorus

Didymocarpus sulphureus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.
