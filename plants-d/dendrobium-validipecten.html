Dendrobium validipecten

Dendrobium validipecten, commonly known as the golden lip dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive, wavy lips. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. It requires well-draining potting media and regular watering during its growing season.

Dendrobium validipecten is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
