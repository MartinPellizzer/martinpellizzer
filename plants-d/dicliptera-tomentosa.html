Dicliptera tomentosa

Dicliptera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly dicliptera, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a rough texture.

Dicliptera tomentosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
