Dupatya intermedia

Dupatya intermedia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

Dupatya intermedia is often used in native plant gardens to enhance biodiversity and support local ecosystems.
