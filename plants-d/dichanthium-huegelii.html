Dichanthium huegelii

Dichanthium huegelii, commonly known as the desert panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous minute florets, which are wind-pollinated. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Dichanthium huegelii plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
