Dryopteris ramosa

Dryopteris ramosa, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping fronds that are covered in a rusty-brown, cinnamon-colored fiddlehead-like structures at the base. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The fronds are bi-directionally divided, giving the plant a unique and elegant appearance.
