Dombeya aquifoliopsis

Dombeya aquifoliopsis, commonly known as the water-leaf dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically evergreen, providing year-round interest in gardens.

Dombeya aquifoliopsis is well-suited to moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
