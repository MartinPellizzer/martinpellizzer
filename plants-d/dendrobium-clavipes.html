Dendrobium clavipes

Dendrobium clavipes, commonly known as the club-club orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, cane-like pseudobulbs and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium clavipes is popular among orchid growers for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
