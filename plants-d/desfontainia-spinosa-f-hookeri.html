Desfontainia spinosa f. hookeri

Desfontainia spinosa f. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's spiny desfontainia, is a variant of the desfontainia genus native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
