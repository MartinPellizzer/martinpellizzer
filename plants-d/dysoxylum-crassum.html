Dysoxylum crassum

Dysoxylum crassum, commonly known as the broad-leaved dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
