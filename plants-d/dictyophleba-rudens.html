Dictyophleba rudens

Dictyophleba rudens, commonly known as the rough-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It is native to various regions in South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
