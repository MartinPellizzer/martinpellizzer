Daphne mezereum f. plena

Daphne mezereum f. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered mezereum, is a cultivar of the Daphne mezereum species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, double flowers that are typically pink or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
