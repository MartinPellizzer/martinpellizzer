Dryobalanops lanceolata

Dryobalanops lanceolata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and ecological stability.
