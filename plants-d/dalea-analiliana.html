Dalea analiliana

Dalea analiliana, commonly known as the Mexican prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has trifoliate leaves and produces small, usually yellow or reddish blooms.

Dalea analiliana is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
