Drimiopsis atropurpurea

Drimiopsis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple drimiopsis, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Drimiopsis atropurpurea is also known for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
