Desmoncus multijugus

Desmoncus multijugus, commonly known as the two-jug plant, is a unique member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, compound umbels that resemble a cluster of small, clustered fruits. The plant has a taproot system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a delicate, fern-like appearance.
