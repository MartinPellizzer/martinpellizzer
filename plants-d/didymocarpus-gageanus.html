Didymocarpus gageanus

Didymocarpus gageanus, commonly known as the Gage's flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with lanceolate to ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale yellow to orange in color, arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Didymocarpus gageanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
