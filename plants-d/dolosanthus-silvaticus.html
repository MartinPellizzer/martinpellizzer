Dolosanthus silvaticus

Dolosanthus silvaticus, commonly known as the woodland dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has basal leaves that are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy white seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Dolosanthus silvaticus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
