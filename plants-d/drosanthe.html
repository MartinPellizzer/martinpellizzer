Drosanthe

Drosanthe, commonly known as the "pink rock orchid," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette shape.

Drosanthe is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
