Diospyros horsfieldii

Diospyros horsfieldii, commonly known as the Horsfield's persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often marked with shallow fissures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
