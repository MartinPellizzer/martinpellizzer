Dichanthium serrafalcoides

Dichanthium serrafalcoides, commonly known as the serrated speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
