Desmodium bambusetorum

Desmodium bambusetorum, commonly known as the bamboo clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Desmodium bambusetorum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
