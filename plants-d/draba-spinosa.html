Draba spinosa

Draba spinosa, commonly known as the prickly rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, typically growing in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, and its hairy, often spiny stems and leaves. Draba spinosa is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species often found in high-altitude environments.
