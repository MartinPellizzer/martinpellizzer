Dolichos candicans

Dolichos candicans, commonly known as the climbing bean or red bean plant, is a leguminous species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain numerous small, edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible seeds.

Dolichos candicans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
