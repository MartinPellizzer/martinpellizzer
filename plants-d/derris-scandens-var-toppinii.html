Derris scandens var. toppinii

Derris scandens var. toppinii, commonly known as the climbing derris, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
