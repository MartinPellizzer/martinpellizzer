Drimia intricata var. intricata

Drimia intricata var. intricata, commonly known as the spider lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
