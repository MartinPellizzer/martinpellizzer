Dicksonia guatemalensis

Dicksonia guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered with persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Dicksonia guatemalensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
