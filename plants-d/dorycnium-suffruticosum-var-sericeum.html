Dorycnium suffruticosum var. sericeum

Dorycnium suffruticosum var. sericeum, commonly known as silky goat's rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and branched stems covered in soft, silky hairs. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in spring.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
