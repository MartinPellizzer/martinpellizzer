Draba hirta subsp. barguzinensis

Draba hirta subsp.

barguzinensis, commonly known as the Barguzin Draba, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and linear, with small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a loose cluster. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

This subspecies is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological niche and morphological traits.
