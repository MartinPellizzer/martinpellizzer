Digitalis purpurea f. alba

Digitalis purpurea f. alba, commonly known as the white foxglove, is a cultivar of the foxglove plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white in color. The plant produces pinkish-purple bracts beneath the flowers, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The white foxglove is a biennial plant, typically growing to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
