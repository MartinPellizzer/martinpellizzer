Dalechampia villosa

Dalechampia villosa, commonly known as the silky dalechampia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Dalechampia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
