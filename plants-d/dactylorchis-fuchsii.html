Dactylorchis fuchsii

Dactylorchis fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs' dactyl orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Dactylorchis fuchsii is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
