Dalbergia poolii

Dalbergia poolii, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic qualities. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also a subject of conservation concern.
