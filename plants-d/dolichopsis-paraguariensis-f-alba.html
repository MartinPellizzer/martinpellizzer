Dolichopsis paraguariensis f. alba

Dolichopsis paraguariensis f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered paraguariensis, is a cultivar of the paraguariensis species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant is a climbing vine that produces small, elongated fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
