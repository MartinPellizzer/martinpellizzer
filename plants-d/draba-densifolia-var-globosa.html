Draba densifolia var. globosa

Draba densifolia var. globosa, commonly known as the globular rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in rocky soils and harsh climates, showcasing adaptations to cold and nutrient-poor environments. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.
