Dianthus plumarius var. parviflorus

Dianthus plumarius var. parviflorus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
