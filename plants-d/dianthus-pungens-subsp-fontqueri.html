Dianthus pungens subsp. fontqueri

Dianthus pungens subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Sierra foothill campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, rounded seed pods that release tiny seeds upon maturity.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
