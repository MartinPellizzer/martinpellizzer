Delphinium spirocentrum

Delphinium spirocentrum, commonly known as the spiral delphinium, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its foliage consists of large, palmately compound leaves with a rough texture and a strong, somewhat peppery scent.

Delphinium spirocentrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
