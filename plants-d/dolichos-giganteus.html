Dolichos giganteus

Dolichos giganteus, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, producing long, slender pods that contain edible seeds. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food.

The seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.
