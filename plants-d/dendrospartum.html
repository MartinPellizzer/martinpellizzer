Dendrospartum

Dendrospartum, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Dendrospartum is native to North America and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
