Delphinium tricorne var. menziesii

Delphinium tricorne var. menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy and serrated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
