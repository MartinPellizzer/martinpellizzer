Dactylis distichophylla

Dactylis distichophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender stems. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry conditions.

Dactylis distichophylla is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate poor soil conditions.
