Dipterocarpus glandulosus

Dipterocarpus glandulosus, commonly known as the hairy dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it is known for its fragrant wood, which is highly valued in the timber industry.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a keystone species in lowland rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
