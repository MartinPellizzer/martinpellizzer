Dendranthema zawadzkii var. latilobum

Dendranthema zawadzkii var. latilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal remedies.

Its blooming period occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
