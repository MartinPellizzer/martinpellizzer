Dactylorchis maculata subsp. arduennensis

Dactylorchis maculata subsp.

arduennensis, commonly known as the Arduennian spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, which help in moisture retention.

The species is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare orchid with specific ecological requirements.
