Daviesia nudiflora subsp. amplectens

Daviesia nudiflora subsp.

amplectens, commonly known as the silky Daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, silver-grey foliage and its unique, fluffy flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more prostrate growth habit compared to other Daviesia species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
