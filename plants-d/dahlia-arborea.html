Dahlia arborea

Dahlia arborea, commonly known as the tree dahlia, is a striking perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stem, which gives it a tree-like appearance, and its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing intricate patterns of petals that are highly prized in gardens.

The botanical features of Dahlia arborea make it a unique and ornamental addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
