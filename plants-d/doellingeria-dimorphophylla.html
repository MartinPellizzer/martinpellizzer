Doellingeria dimorphophylla

Doellingeria dimorphophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf water shield, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are divided into two distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures include small, green flowers that bloom in the summer.

Doellingeria dimorphophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
