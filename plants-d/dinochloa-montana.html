Dinochloa montana

Dinochloa montana, commonly known as the mountain clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh alpine environments, often growing in rocky soils and at high elevations. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Dinochloa montana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
