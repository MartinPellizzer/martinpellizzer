Didymocarpus doryphyllus

Didymocarpus doryphyllus, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Didymocarpus doryphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
