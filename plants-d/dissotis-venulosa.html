Dissotis venulosa

Dissotis venulosa, commonly known as the veined mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale blue or white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fibrous roots make it suitable for erosion control in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.
