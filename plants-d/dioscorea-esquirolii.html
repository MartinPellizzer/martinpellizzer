Dioscorea esquirolii

Dioscorea esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are edible when properly prepared. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its medicinal and nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated for both culinary and traditional uses.
