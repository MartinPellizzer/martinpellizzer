Delphinium cucullatum

Delphinium cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant's leaves are palmately compound, with elongated leaflets that are often hairy and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its striking floral display makes it a notable species in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.
