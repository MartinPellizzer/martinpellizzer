Digitaria bangweolensis

Digitaria bangweolensis, commonly known as the Bangweolensis Digitaria, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
