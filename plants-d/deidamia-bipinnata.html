Deidamia bipinnata

Deidamia bipinnata, commonly known as the two-feathered grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Deidamia bipinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
