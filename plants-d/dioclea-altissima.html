Dioclea altissima

Dioclea altissima, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant has pinnate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

The fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.
