Desmogyne minor

Desmogyne minor, commonly known as the lesser desmogyne, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, branching stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Desmogyne minor is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local insect populations.
