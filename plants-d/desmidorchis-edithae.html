Desmidorchis edithae

Desmidorchis edithae, commonly known as the Edith's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces a single spike of flowers.

Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and unique floral structure, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
