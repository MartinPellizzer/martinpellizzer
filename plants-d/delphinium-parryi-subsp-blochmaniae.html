Delphinium parryi subsp. blochmaniae

Delphinium parryi subsp.

blochmaniae, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
