Dianthus seguieri

Dianthus seguieri, commonly known as the rock campion, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of pink or white flowers with prominent, showy sepals that resemble petals. The plant typically thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually evergreen.

Dianthus seguieri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
