Daphniphyllum himalense var. divaricatum

Daphniphyllum himalense var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Himalayan silverleaf, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery wax. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
