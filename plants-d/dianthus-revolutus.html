Dianthus revolutus

Dianthus revolutus, commonly known as the revolute campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
