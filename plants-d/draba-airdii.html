Draba airdii

Draba airdii, commonly known as the Aird's whitlow grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Draba airdii is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and hardy species, well-suited to harsh mountain climates.
