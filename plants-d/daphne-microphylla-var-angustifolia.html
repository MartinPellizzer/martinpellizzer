Daphne microphylla var. angustifolia

Daphne microphylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved daphne, is a compact evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
