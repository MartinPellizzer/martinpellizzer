Dianthus bolivaris

Dianthus bolivaris, commonly known as the Bolivian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Dianthus bolivaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
