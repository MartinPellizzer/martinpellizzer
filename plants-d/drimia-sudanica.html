Drimia sudanica

Drimia sudanica, commonly known as the Sudan lily, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long blooming period during the summer months.

Drimia sudanica is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
