Desmidorchis acutangula

Desmidorchis acutangula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and rhizomatous growth habit.
