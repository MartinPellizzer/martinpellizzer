Dendrobium dactyliferum

Dendrobium dactyliferum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and collections.

Dendrobium dactyliferum is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
