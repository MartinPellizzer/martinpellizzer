Dyckia missionum

Dyckia missionum, commonly known as the Mission Dyckia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Dyckia missionum is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
