Doryopteris scalaris

Doryopteris scalaris, commonly known as the scalloped shield fern, is a species of fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are deeply lobed and have a scalloped appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its fronds are evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in the landscape.

Doryopteris scalaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and soil conditions.
