Dianthus subscabridus

Dianthus subscabridus, commonly known as the rough pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or reddish flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
