Diospyros penangiana

Diospyros penangiana, commonly known as the Penang persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, edible pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its edible fruits and potential medicinal properties.
