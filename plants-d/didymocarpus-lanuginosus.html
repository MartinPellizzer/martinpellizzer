Didymocarpus lanuginosus

Didymocarpus lanuginosus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The hairy tamarind is valued for its ornamental features and has traditional uses in some regions.
