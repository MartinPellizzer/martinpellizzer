Dichanthelium thermale subsp. sericeum

Dichanthelium thermale subsp.

sericeum, commonly known as the silky threadgrass, is a grass species native to certain regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is distinguished by its soft, silky texture and is often found in association with other grasses in its native environment.
