Dianthus saetabensis var. medius

Dianthus saetabensis var. medius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking pink or red flowers with prominent white veins, which bloom in summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
