Dendrocnide mirabilis

Dendrocnide mirabilis, commonly known as the stinging tree, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are alternate and elliptical, often covered with fine, stinging hairs that can cause severe dermatitis in humans.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
