Dianthus carthusianorum var. puberulus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. puberulus, commonly known as the rough carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of pink to red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The leaves are linear, hairy, and often covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
