Dortmanna coronopifolia var. multiflora

Dortmanna coronopifolia var. multiflora, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
