Drymocallis inquinans

Drymocallis inquinans, commonly known as the smoky goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually upright and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
