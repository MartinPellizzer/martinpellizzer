Daphne alba

Daphne alba, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Daphne alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
