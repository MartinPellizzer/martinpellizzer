Diospyros pseudebenus

Diospyros pseudebenus, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony trees, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
