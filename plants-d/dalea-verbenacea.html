Dalea verbenacea

Dalea verbenacea, commonly known as silverleaf false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring deep roots and drought-resistant foliage. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Dalea verbenacea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
