Dalbergia rimosa

Dalbergia rimosa, commonly known as the white cedar or red cedar, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and carbon sequestration in its native ecosystems.
