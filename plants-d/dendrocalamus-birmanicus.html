Dendrocalamus birmanicus

Dendrocalamus birmanicus, commonly known as the Burmese bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles. It is often cultivated for its strong, durable bamboo poles used in construction and handicrafts.

Its rapid growth makes it an important resource for sustainable forestry and reforestation efforts.
