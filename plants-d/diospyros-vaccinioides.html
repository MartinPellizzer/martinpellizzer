Diospyros vaccinioides

Diospyros vaccinioides, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange to red in color when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The wood of Diospyros vaccinioides is hard and durable, making it valuable for crafting and traditional uses.
