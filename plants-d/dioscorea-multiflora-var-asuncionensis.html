Dioscorea multiflora var. asuncionensis

Dioscorea multiflora var. asuncionensis, commonly known as the Asuncionensis yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and twining.

This variety is native to parts of South America and is cultivated for its starchy tubers used in traditional cuisines.
