Desmodium laxum

Desmodium laxum, commonly known as slender ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a creeping growth habit with slender stems and is often used in traditional medicine.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable environments.
