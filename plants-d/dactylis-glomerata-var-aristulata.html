Dactylis glomerata var. aristulata

Dactylis glomerata var. aristulata, commonly known as the tufted grass or sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
