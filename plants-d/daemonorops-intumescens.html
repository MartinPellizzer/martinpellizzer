Daemonorops intumescens

Daemonorops intumescens, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a species of cycad native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fern-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces distinctive, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its slow growth and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
