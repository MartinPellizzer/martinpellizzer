Dictyopsis thunbergii

Dictyopsis thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Dictyopsis thunbergii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
