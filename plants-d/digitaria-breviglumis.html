Digitaria breviglumis

Digitaria breviglumis, commonly known as the shortgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Digitaria breviglumis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
