Ditepalanthus malagasicus

Ditepalanthus malagasicus, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a thick, columnar stem with a crown of soft, fleshy leaves that resemble a palm. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for humans and animals. It is adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its stem and leaves.

Its distinctive appearance and resilience make it a popular ornamental plant.
