Dendrosenecio johnstonii subsp. adnivalis

Dendrosenecio johnstonii subsp.

adnivalis, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and compact growth habit, which help it survive in harsh, cold environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and alpine collections.
