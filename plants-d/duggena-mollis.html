Duggena mollis

Duggena mollis, commonly known as the soft duggena, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and a soft, velvety texture.

Duggena mollis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
