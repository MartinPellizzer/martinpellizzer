Draba radicata var. hartianum

Draba radicata var. hartianum, commonly known as Hart's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, bright yellow flowers appearing in early spring. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
