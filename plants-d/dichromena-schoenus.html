Dichromena schoenus

Dichromena schoenus, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It possesses specialized root systems, including pneumatophores, which aid in gas exchange in waterlogged soils. The plant's leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a waxy surface to reduce water loss. It produces small, greenish flowers that are pollinated by wind, and its fruits are buoyant, facilitating seed dispersal by water.

Dichromena schoenus plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
