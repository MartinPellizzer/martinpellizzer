Dimorphanthera pulchra

Dimorphanthera pulchra, commonly known as the purple-leafed orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its unique morphology includes a sympodial growth habit and specialized orchid roots adapted for nutrient absorption.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
