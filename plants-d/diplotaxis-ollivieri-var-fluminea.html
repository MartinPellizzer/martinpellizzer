Diplotaxis ollivieri var. fluminea

Diplotaxis ollivieri var. fluminea, commonly known as the river dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces slender, hairy stems and has a taproot system that helps it survive in its native habitat.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its delicate appearance.
