Dendrobium friedericksianum var. oculatum

Dendrobium friedericksianum var. oculatum, commonly known as the "Ocellated Dendrobium," is a flowering orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant features long, cane-like stems with clustered, lance-shaped leaves. Its most striking characteristic is the presence of ocelli, or eye-like spots, on the petals and sepals.

It produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
