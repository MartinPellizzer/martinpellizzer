Dichapetalum floribundum

Dichapetalum floribundum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its bark is smooth and pale grey, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
