Dendrobium lyonii

Dendrobium lyonii, commonly known as the Lyon's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its leaves are long and narrow, often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Dendrobium lyonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
