Dioscorea carrii

Dioscorea carrii, commonly known as the Carri's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend support structures, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil.

The tubers of Dioscorea carrii are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their nutritional content.
