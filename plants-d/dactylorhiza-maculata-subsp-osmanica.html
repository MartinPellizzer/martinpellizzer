Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. osmanica

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

osmanica, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

The plant features a single, erect stem with lance-shaped leaves and a single, showy flower that is usually pink or purple. It has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient uptake.

The species is known for its distinctive spotted leaves and is considered a valuable indicator of nutrient-poor soils.
