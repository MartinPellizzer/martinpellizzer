Diplopappus chrysophyllus

Diplopappus chrysophyllus, commonly known as the golden-leaf pappus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant qualities.
