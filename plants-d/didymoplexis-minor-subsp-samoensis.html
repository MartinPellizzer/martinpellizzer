Didymoplexis minor subsp. samoensis

Didymoplexis minor subsp.

samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan climbing orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the South Pacific islands. It is a herbaceous perennial vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically arranged in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests or on rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
