Dioscorea velutina

Dioscorea velutina, commonly known as the velvet tuber or yam, is a perennial herbaceous vine native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, tuberous roots. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are used as a food source in many cultures and are also valued for their medicinal properties.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
