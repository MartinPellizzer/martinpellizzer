Danthonia raoulii

Danthonia raoulii, commonly known as the Raouli's grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Danthonia raoulii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
