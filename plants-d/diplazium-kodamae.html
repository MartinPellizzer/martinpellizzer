Diplazium kodamae

Diplazium kodamae, commonly known as Kodama's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are pinnately divided, with a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification.
