Deparia vegetior var. turgida

Deparia vegetior var. turgida, commonly known as the turgid shield fern, is a variant of the shield fern genus Deparia, characterized by its robust and sturdy fronds.

This plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. The fronds are usually arranged in a compact rosette, providing a dense, shield-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in woodland gardens and shaded rockeries.

The plant is noted for its resilience and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions, though it prefers well-drained, fertile soils.
