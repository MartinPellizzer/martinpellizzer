Drosera albonotata

Drosera albonotata, commonly known as the white-veined sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap insects. The leaves are typically reddish or pinkish in color with white veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and swamps.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like flies and beetles.
