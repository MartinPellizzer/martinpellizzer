Drypetes coriifolia

Drypetes coriifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries.

Drypetes coriifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
