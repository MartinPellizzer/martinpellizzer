Delphinium variegatum f. superbum

Delphinium variegatum f. superbum, commonly known as the Giant Larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue with a rough texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
