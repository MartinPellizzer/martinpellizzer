Dicliptera sebastinei var. sebastinei

Dicliptera sebastinei var. sebastinei, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.

The variety var. sebastinei is notable for its unique growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
