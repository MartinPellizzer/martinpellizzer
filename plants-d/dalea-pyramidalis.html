Dalea pyramidalis

Dalea pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, pyramidal growth habit. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea pyramidalis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
