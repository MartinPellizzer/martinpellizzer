Dalbergia masoalensis

Dalbergia masoalensis, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark wood with a distinctive grain. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its unique appearance and valuable timber, it is a species of interest for both ecological and economic reasons.
