Drosera dichrosepala subsp. enodes

Drosera dichrosepala subsp.

enodes, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects.

The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and swamps. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and arranged in a dense inflorescence.

It is a perennial species that thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils.
