Digitaria biformis subsp. willdenowii

Digitaria biformis subsp.

willdenowii, commonly known as willdenow's digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
