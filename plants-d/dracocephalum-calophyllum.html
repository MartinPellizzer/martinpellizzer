Dracocephalum calophyllum

Dracocephalum calophyllum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in dense spikes, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.

Dracocephalum calophyllum is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
