Drimia globuligera

Drimia globuligera, commonly known as the globular lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its globe-shaped, fleshy tubers and its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Drimia globuligera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
