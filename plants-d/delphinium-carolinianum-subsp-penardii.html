Delphinium carolinianum subsp. penardii

Delphinium carolinianum subsp.

penardii, commonly known as Penard's larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, often hairy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in wetland and woodland habitats.
