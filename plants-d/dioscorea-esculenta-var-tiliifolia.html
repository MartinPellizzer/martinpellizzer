Dioscorea esculenta var. tiliifolia

Dioscorea esculenta var. tiliifolia, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for various purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and oval-shaped berries that contain seeds. Its tubers are rich in compounds like diosgenin, which has been studied for potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
