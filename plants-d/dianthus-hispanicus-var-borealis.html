Dianthus hispanicus var. borealis

Dianthus hispanicus var. borealis, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Dianthus hispanicus var. borealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
