Diodonta mitis

Diodonta mitis, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers with a central disk. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Diodonta mitis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
