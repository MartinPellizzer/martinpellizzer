Drepanostachyum fimbriatum

Drepanostachyum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and panicle inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its flowering period usually occurs during the rainy season, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant's adaptability to humid environments makes it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
