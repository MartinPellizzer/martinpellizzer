Dioon argenteum

Dioon argenteum, commonly known as the silver shield fern, is a rare and ancient cycad species native to the Sierra Madre mountains in Mexico.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a rosette of large, waxy, silver-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces distinctive, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Dioon argenteum is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
