Delphinium glareosum subsp. glareosum

Delphinium glareosum subsp.

glareosum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes and bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh alpine environment.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
