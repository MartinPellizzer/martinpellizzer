Duhaldea nervosa

Duhaldea nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
