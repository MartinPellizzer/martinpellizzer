Dianthus requienii var. cognobilis

Dianthus requienii var. cognobilis, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, five-petaled blooms that appear in spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Dianthus requienii var. cognobilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
