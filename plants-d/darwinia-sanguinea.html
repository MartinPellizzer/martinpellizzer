Darwinia sanguinea

Darwinia sanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking red stems and showy, pinkish-red flower spikes. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and heathlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which appear in spring, attract a variety of pollinators such as birds and insects.

Darwinia sanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
