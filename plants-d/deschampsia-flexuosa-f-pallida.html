Deschampsia flexuosa f. pallida

Deschampsia flexuosa f. pallida, commonly known as the pale flowered bentgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading stems and pale blue to silver-green inflorescences, which distinguish it from other members of the Deschampsia genus. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and along stream banks. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a soft, tufted appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
