Diasperus ditassodes

Diasperus ditassodes, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are yellow with a prominent central disc.

Diasperus ditassodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
