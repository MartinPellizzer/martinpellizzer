Durio

Durio, commonly known as the durian, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiky husk and strong, often pungent odor. The fruit contains a creamy, custard-like pulp that is rich in nutrients and highly prized in many cultures. Durio species belong to the Malvaceae family and are known for their unique morphology and flavor profile.

The plant's botanical features make it both an ecologically significant and economically valuable species.
