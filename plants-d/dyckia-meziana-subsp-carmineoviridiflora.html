Dyckia meziana subsp. carmineoviridiflora

Dyckia meziana subsp.

carmineoviridiflora, commonly known as the Crimson-veined Dyckia, is a bromeliad native to Brazil. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with reddish or pinkish veins.

The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
