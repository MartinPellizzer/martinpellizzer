Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. sanguineus

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

sanguineus, commonly known as the blood red carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces showy, double-petaled flowers with a distinctive white or pink central tuft, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in floral arrangements.
