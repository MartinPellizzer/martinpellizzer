Dioscoreophyllum volkensii

Dioscoreophyllum volkensii, commonly known as the African yam vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible tubers that are similar in appearance to yams. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its tubers are not only a food source but also have traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
