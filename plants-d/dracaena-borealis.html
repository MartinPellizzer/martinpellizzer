Dracaena borealis

Dracaena borealis, commonly known as the Arctic dracaena, is a rare and cold-tolerant plant native to the Arctic regions.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early summer, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for alpine gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species.
