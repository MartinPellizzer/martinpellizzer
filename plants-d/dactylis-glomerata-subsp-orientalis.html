Dactylis glomerata subsp. orientalis

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as oriental cocksfoot, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It features robust, upright stems and dense, tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers.

The plant is characterized by its coarse, blue-green leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in forage agriculture due to its high nutritional value and drought tolerance.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and agricultural systems.
