Digitaria perrottetii var. angustifolia

Digitaria perrottetii var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf digitaria, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a prominent midrib, while the stems are erect and often branching.

This variety is often found in regions with a warm, temperate climate and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
