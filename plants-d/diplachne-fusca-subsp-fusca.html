Diplachne fusca subsp. fusca

Diplachne fusca subsp.

fusca, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense mats.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
