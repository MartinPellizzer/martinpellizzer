Delissea kohalaensis

Delissea kohalaensis, commonly known as Kohala dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian island of Kohala.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native range. Delissea kohalaensis is known for its yellow flowers and is often found in open areas with minimal vegetation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its native ecosystem.
