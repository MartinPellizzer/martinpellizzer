Dioscorea tepinapensis var. aggregata

Dioscorea tepinapensis var. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered yam, is a variant of the yam genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine with tuberous roots that are often harvested for their edible qualities. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are typically white or yellowish in color and have a starchy texture.

This variety is valued both for its culinary use and its role in traditional medicine.
