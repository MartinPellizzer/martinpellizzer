Dendrochilum hamatum

Dendrochilum hamatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and is often found growing in forested areas. The plant typically has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dendrochilum hamatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
