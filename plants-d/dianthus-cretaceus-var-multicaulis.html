Dianthus cretaceus var. multicaulis

Dianthus cretaceus var. multicaulis, commonly known as the rock campion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers with five petals. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing succulent characteristics that aid in water retention. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
