Duschekia maximowiczii

Duschekia maximowiczii, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It produces a capsule-like fruit that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Duschekia maximowiczii is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
