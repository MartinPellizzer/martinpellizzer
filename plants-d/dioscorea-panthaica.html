Dioscorea panthaica

Dioscorea panthaica, commonly known as the Panthai yam, is a tuberous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical tubers that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers of Dioscorea panthaica are valued for their nutritional content and potential medicinal properties.
