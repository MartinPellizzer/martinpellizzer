Dissotis loandensis

Dissotis loandensis, commonly known as the Loandens grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Dissotis loandensis is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.
