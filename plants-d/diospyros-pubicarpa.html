Diospyros pubicarpa

Diospyros pubicarpa, commonly known as the rough persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are often pubescent. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains a single large seed, and it is valued for its edible fruit and ornamental qualities.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
