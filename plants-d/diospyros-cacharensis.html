Diospyros cacharensis

Diospyros cacharensis, commonly known as the Assam persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and lanceolate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in crafts and furniture.
