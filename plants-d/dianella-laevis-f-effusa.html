Dianella laevis f. effusa

Dianella laevis f. effusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved flax lily, is a variant of the flax lily species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its attractive foliage and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
