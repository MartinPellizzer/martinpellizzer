Dorycnium suffruticosum proles germanicum

Dorycnium suffruticosum proles germanicum, commonly known as the German broom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

This plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
