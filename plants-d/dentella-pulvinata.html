Dentella pulvinata

Dentella pulvinata, commonly known as the pulvinate dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features basal leaves that are toothed and covered in fine hairs, giving it its scientific name. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, aiding in wind dispersal.

Dentella pulvinata is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
