Dipterocarpus applanatus

Dipterocarpus applanatus, commonly known as the flat-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, flat leaves and heavy, aromatic wood. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in primary and secondary forest habitats. It plays a significant role in the ecological structure of these forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable timber used in construction and furniture making.
