Drymophloeus saxatilis

Drymophloeus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows on rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, hairy texture.

Drymophloeus saxatilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
