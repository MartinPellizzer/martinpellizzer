Dombeya subviscosa subsp. subviscosa

Dombeya subviscosa subsp.

subviscosa, commonly known as silky dombeya, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, cylindrical flower clusters that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Dombeya subviscosa subsp.

subviscosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
