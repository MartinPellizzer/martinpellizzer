Dianthus brachyanthus var. australis

Dianthus brachyanthus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to South Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features dense, mat-forming foliage with a woolly texture, and it is well adapted to arid and coastal environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
