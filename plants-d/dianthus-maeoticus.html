Dianthus maeoticus

Dianthus maeoticus, commonly known as the Crimean campion, is a flowering plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and steppe regions. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Dianthus maeoticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
