Deschampsia parodiana

Deschampsia parodiana, commonly known as Parod's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. The plant has fine, blue-green leaves that are slightly rolled and covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

Deschampsia parodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.
