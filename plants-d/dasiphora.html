Dasiphora

Dasiphora, commonly known as fireweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It consists of several species, including Dasiphora frigida and Dasiphora spectabilis, which are native to North America. These plants are typically herbaceous perennials, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. They produce showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture, while the stems are usually hairy.
