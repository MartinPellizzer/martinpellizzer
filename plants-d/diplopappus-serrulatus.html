Diplopappus serrulatus

Diplopappus serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated burrweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its distinctive, rough, spiny fruits that persist on the plant well into the dry season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
