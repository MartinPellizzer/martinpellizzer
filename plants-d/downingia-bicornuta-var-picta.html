Downingia bicornuta var. picta

Downingia bicornuta var. picta, commonly known as the painted rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The varietal name "picta" refers to the distinctive, often colorful markings on its leaves, which can vary in hue depending on environmental conditions.
