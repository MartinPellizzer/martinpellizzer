Daphne laureola var. punctulata

Daphne laureola var. punctulata, commonly known as the dotted laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, with a distinctive dotted pattern on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its winter interest and early spring blooms.
