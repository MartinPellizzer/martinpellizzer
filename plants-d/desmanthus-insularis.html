Desmanthus insularis

Desmanthus insularis, commonly known as the Bahama dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by fluffy, seed-filled pods. It has adapted to thrive in sandy, coastal soils and is often found in dune environments.

Desmanthus insularis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
