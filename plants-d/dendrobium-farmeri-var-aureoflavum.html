Dendrobium farmeri var. aureoflavum

Dendrobium farmeri var. aureoflavum, commonly known as the golden yellow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often branching and bearing clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, arranged along the stem in a spiral pattern. This variety is prized for its striking color and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.
