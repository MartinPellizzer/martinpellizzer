Dectis arborescens

Dectis arborescens, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, with a dense, branching structure. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture with a serrated edge.

Dectis arborescens is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
