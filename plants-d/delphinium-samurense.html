Delphinium samurense

Delphinium samurense, commonly known as the Japanese larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets that are lanceolate in shape.

Delphinium samurense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
