Diospyros bejaudii

Diospyros bejaudii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits.

The fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed when fully ripe, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
