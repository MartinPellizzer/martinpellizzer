Draba stenophylla

Draba stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It is typically found in alpine and arctic regions, thriving in rocky and gravelly soils.

Draba stenophylla is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
