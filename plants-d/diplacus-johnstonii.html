Diplacus johnstonii

Diplacus johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to local biodiversity.

Diplacus johnstonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
