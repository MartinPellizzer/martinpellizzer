Dioclea macrantha

Dioclea macrantha, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. Its floral structure includes a distinctive crown of filaments and a central column of stamens.

Dioclea macrantha thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.
