Disa apetala

Disa apetala, commonly known as the naked-leaved disa, is a striking member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring vibrant purple and pink flowers that bloom on slender, erect stems. The plant lacks true leaves, with only modified stems and bracts that serve photosynthetic functions. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Disa apetala is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for rock gardens and collections of rare orchids.
