Dortmanna fervens

Dortmanna fervens, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique aesthetic.
