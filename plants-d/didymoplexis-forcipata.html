Didymoplexis forcipata

Didymoplexis forcipata, commonly known as the clawed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive claw-like appendages on the sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a prominent lip, and it blooms during the wet season.

Didymoplexis forcipata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
