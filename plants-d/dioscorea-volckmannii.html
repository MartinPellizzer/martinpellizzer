Dioscorea volckmannii

Dioscorea volckmannii, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a tuberous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its elongated, starchy tubers that are often used in traditional cuisine. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often green or yellowish in color.

The tubers are rich in carbohydrates and have been used for both culinary and medicinal purposes by indigenous communities.
