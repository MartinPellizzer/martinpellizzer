Desmodium plicatum

Desmodium plicatum, commonly known as the pleated burr legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, pleated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Desmodium plicatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
