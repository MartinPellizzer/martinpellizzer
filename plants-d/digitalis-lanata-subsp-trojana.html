Digitalis lanata subsp. trojana

Digitalis lanata subsp.

trojana, commonly known as the Trojan foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs, which give the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
