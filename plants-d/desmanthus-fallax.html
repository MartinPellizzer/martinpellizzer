Desmanthus fallax

Desmanthus fallax, commonly known as the fallax burr medic, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for forage and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
