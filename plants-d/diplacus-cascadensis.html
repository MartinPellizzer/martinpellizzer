Diplacus cascadensis

Diplacus cascadensis, commonly known as the Cascade monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments like forest floors and stream banks. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Diplacus cascadensis is a perennial herb that plays an important role in local ecosystems by supporting biodiversity.
