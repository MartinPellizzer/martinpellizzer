Dendrophthora cupressoides

Dendrophthora cupressoides, commonly known as the creeping cypress, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Dendrophthora cupressoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground covers.
