Drepanocarpus senegalensis

Drepanocarpus senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese acacia, is a deciduous tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
