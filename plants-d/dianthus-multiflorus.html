Dianthus multiflorus

Dianthus multiflorus, commonly known as the common campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces numerous small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a reddish hue.

Dianthus multiflorus is typically found in open areas such as roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils.
