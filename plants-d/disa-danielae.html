Disa danielae

Disa danielae, commonly known as the Daniel's orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

This perennial herbaceous plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Disa danielae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
