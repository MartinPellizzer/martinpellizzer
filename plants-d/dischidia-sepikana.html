Dischidia sepikana

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Papua New Guinea and parts of Indonesia. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Dischidia sepikana is often grown as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its trailing growth habit.
