Dysophylla martini

Dysophylla martini, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny, bract-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dysophylla martini is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that is well adapted to coastal environments.
