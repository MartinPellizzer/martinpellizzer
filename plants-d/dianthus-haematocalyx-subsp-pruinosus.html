Dianthus haematocalyx subsp. pruinosus

Dianthus haematocalyx subsp.

pruinosus, commonly known as the purple campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
