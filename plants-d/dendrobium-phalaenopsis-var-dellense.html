Dendrobium phalaenopsis var. dellense

Dendrobium phalaenopsis var. dellense, commonly known as the orchid with the distinctive white and green variegated leaves, is a hybrid orchid prized for its ornamental value.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is an epiphyte, often growing on trees or rocks, and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its flowers, which are typically white or green, bloom in clusters and can last for several weeks.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts due to its resilience and ability to produce numerous blooms under proper care.
