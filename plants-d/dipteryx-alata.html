Dipteryx alata

Dipteryx alata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces woody pods containing edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a long lifespan, often living over 100 years. Its flowers are pollinated by specific bees, and the tree plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

The Brazil nut is also valued for its high nutritional content and economic importance.
