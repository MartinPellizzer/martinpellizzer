Dactyladenia dichotoma

Dactyladenia dichotoma, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, woody structure. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the dry season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, often with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
