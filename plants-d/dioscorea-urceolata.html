Dioscorea urceolata

Dioscorea urceolata, commonly known as the water yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into cylindrical fruits. Its growth habit involves twining stems that can reach several meters in length, making it a notable species in its native habitats.

The tubers of D. urceolata are often harvested for their starchy content and have cultural significance in various communities.
