Dicliptera assurgens var. vahliana

Dicliptera assurgens var. vahliana, commonly known as Vahli's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential for wetland restoration.
