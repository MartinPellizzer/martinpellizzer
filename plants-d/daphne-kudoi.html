Daphne kudoi

Daphne kudoi, commonly known as the Chinese daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow, which bloom in early spring. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it has a compact growth habit.

Daphne kudoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and olfactory characteristics.
