Delphinium ambiguum

Delphinium ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its flowers are arranged in dense spikes.

Delphinium ambiguum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic members.
