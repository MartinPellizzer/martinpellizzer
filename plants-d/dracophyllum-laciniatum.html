Dracophyllum laciniatum

Dracophyllum laciniatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for rocky or sandy soils.

Dracophyllum laciniatum is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
