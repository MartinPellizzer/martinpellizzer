Drosanthemum chrysum

Drosanthemum chrysum, commonly known as the golden daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller flowers. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with hairy, grayish-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Drosanthemum chrysum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
