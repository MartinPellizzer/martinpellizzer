Davallia solida

Davallia solida, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a tropical evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shield-shaped, leathery fronds that form a dense rosette, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
