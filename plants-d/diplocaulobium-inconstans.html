Diplocaulobium inconstans

Diplocaulobium inconstans, commonly known as the variable leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or saturated soil, thriving in environments with high humidity and moderate temperatures.

Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
