Delphinium altissimum var. siamense

Delphinium altissimum var. siamense, commonly known as the Siam larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil with full sun.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
