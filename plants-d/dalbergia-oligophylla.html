Dalbergia oligophylla

Dalbergia oligophylla, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a species of legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bean-like pods.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
