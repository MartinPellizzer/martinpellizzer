Diospyros argyi

Diospyros argyi, commonly known as Chinese date or wolfberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright orange-red berries, which are edible and rich in nutrients. Diospyros argyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the medicinal properties of its fruit.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.
