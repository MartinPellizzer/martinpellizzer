Dudleya cymosa subsp. gigantea

Dudleya cymosa subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant desert holly, is a succulent plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are fleshy, spiky, and covered in a powdery white coating called farina.

The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers in late spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
