Didymoplexis cornuta var. cornuta

Didymoplexis cornuta var. cornuta, commonly known as the horned piper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like tubers that are used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and usually have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its medicinal properties in local communities.
