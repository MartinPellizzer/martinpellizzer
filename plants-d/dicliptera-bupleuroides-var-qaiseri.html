Dicliptera bupleuroides var. qaiseri

Dicliptera bupleuroides var. qaiseri, commonly known as the Kashmir blue oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add to its visual appeal, especially during the flowering season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring cool climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in alpine settings.
