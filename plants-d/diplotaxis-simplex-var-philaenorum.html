Diplotaxis simplex var. philaenorum

Diplotaxis simplex var. philaenorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
