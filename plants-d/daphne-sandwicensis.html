Daphne sandwicensis

Daphne sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian daphne, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, narrow leaves and is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of its native habitat. It plays an important role in the ecological balance of Hawaii's native flora.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
