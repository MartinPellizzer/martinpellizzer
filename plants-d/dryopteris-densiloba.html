Dryopteris densiloba

Dryopteris densiloba, commonly known as the dense-lobed wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or upright, depending on the growing conditions. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, organic soil. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters, a typical feature of ferns.

The dense-lobed wood fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
