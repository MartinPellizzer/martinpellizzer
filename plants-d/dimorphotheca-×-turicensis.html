Dimorphotheca × turicensis

Dimorphotheca × turicensis, commonly known as the African daisy or turk's cap, is a hybrid perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and coastal conditions. It produces two types of flowers—ray florets and disk florets—hence its scientific name.

Dimorphotheca × turicensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
