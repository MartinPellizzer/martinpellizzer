Draba compacta

Draba compacta, commonly known as the dwarf whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the alpine and arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. Draba compacta has a compact growth habit, with dense clusters of leaves and flowers that help it conserve heat and moisture.

It is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.
