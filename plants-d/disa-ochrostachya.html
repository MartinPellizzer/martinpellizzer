Disa ochrostachya

Disa ochrostachya, commonly known as the white-stemmed orchid, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late summer.

Disa ochrostachya is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
