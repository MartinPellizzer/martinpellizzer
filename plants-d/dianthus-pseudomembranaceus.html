Dianthus pseudomembranaceus

Dianthus pseudomembranaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers consist of five petals and numerous stamens, which give it a fluffy appearance.

Dianthus pseudomembranaceus is often found in open areas such as grasslands and along stream banks.
