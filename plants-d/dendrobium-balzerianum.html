Dendrobium balzerianum

Dendrobium balzerianum, commonly known as the Balzer's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium balzerianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
