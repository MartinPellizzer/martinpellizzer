Dacryodes longifolia var. penangensis

Dacryodes longifolia var. penangensis, commonly known as the Penang ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its hard, durable wood, it is valued for timber and traditional uses.
