Dryopteris baileyana

Dryopteris baileyana, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern North America.

It features fronds that are typically leathery and dark green, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky crevices. Its spores are released from sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Dryopteris baileyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
