Daucus carota subsp. capillifolius

Daucus carota subsp.

capillifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved parsley, is a subspecies of the carrot plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in disturbed or open habitats.
