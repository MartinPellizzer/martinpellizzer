Driessenia phasmolacuna

Driessenia phasmolacuna, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, forming cup-like clusters. Its foliage and flowers are often used in native plant gardens for their ornamental value.

Driessenia phasmolacuna thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
