Dracocephalum cordatum

Dracocephalum cordatum, commonly known as heart-leaved dragonhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often heart-shaped at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces spikes of purple to blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Dracocephalum cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
