Dactylis lobata

Dactylis lobata, commonly known as purple grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinged inflorescences. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its hardy nature.

Dactylis lobata is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its resilience and adaptability to various soil conditions.
