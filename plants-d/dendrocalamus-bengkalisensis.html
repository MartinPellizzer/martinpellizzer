Dendrocalamus bengkalisensis

Dendrocalamus bengkalisensis, commonly known as the Bengkalis bamboo, is a tall, robust grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in humid tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

This bamboo is valued for its strong and durable culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
