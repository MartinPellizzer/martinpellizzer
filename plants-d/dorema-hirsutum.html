Dorema hirsutum

Dorema hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy dorema, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low precipitation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dorema hirsutum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
