Dendrobium bifarium

Dendrobium bifarium, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its terrestrial or epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces two-flowered inflorescences, giving it its scientific name, and features long, slender pseudobulbs. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a greenish throat, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium bifarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
