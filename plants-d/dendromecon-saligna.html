Dendromecon saligna

Dendromecon saligna, commonly known as the tree dandelion, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with gray-green foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
