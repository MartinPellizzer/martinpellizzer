Deguelia paniculata

Deguelia paniculata, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its striking yellow flowers that bloom in panicles. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Deguelia paniculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
