Dasysphaera hyposericea

Dasysphaera hyposericea, commonly known as the white-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, white, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its root system includes haustoria, specialized structures that attach to the host plant's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
