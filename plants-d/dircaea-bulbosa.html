Dircaea bulbosa

Dircaea bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, bulb-like structures that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is primarily found in its natural habitat.
