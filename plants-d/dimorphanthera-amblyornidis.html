Dimorphanthera amblyornidis

Dimorphanthera amblyornidis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a faintly colored lip, and it produces a unique type of orchid seed.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
