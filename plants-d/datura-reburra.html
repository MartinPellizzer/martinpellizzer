Datura reburra

Datura reburra, commonly known as the desert datura, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Datura reburra contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
