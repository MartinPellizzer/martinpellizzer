Diospyros austroafricana var. rugosa

Diospyros austroafricana var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-skinned ebony, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its hard, dense wood and has traditional uses in medicine and crafts.
