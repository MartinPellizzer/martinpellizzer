Drypetes madagascariensis var. perrieri

Drypetes madagascariensis var. perrieri, commonly known as the Madagascar drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.
