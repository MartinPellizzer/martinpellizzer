Dasylirion tenuifolium

Dasylirion tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrowleaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing white or pale green flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its foliage and floral structure are well-suited for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
