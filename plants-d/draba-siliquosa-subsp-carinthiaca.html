Draba siliquosa subsp. carinthiaca

Draba siliquosa subsp.

carinthiaca, commonly known as the Carinthian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Alps and surrounding regions. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often in moist, calcareous soils. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.
