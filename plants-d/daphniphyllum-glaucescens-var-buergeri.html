Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. buergeri

Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. buergeri, commonly known as the Buerger's daphniphyllum, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
