Dodecatheon meadia var. hendersonii

Dodecatheon meadia var. hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's five-petaled glory, is a rare variant of the eastern glacier lily.

It is native to the Appalachian Mountains and thrives in moist, acidic soils in shaded woodlands. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
