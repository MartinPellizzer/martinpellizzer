Diplectria annulata var. seticarpa

Diplectria annulata var. seticarpa, commonly known as the ringed lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ringed stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
