Dieffenbachia irrorata

Dieffenbachia irrorata, commonly known as the "White Star Dieffenbachia," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves with striking white veins and a central white stripe. The plant produces tall, upright inflorescences with white bracts that resemble flowers, though it is not actually flowering. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dieffenbachia irrorata is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
