Dalea amoena var. pubescens

Dalea amoena var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy prairieclover, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it the "hairy" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

This plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
