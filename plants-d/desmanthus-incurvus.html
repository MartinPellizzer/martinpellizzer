Desmanthus incurvus

Desmanthus incurvus, commonly known as the curly desmanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Desmanthus incurvus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
