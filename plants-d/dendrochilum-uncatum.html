Dendrochilum uncatum

Dendrochilum uncatum, commonly known as the cat-tailed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

The species belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
