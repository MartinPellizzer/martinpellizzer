Dolichos tetragonolobus

Dolichos tetragonolobus, commonly known as the four-angled cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein.

Dolichos tetragonolobus is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
