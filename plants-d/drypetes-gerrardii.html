Drypetes gerrardii

Drypetes gerrardii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries containing three seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
