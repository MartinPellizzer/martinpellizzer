Dianthus cephalotes

Dianthus cephalotes, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dianthus cephalotes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
