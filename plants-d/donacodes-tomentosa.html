Donacodes tomentosa

Donacodes tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly reed, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
