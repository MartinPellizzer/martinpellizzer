Digitaria arushae

Digitaria arushae, commonly known as the Arusha grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
