Dracocephalum moldavicum var. foetidum

Dracocephalum moldavicum var. foetidum, commonly known as stinking skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its strong, pungent odor, it is sometimes referred to as the "stinking" variety of its species.
