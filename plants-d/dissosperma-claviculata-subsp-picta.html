Dissosperma claviculata subsp. picta

Dissosperma claviculata subsp.

picta, commonly known as the painted star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves and bright orange to red berries that appear in late summer.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils in coastal and inland regions. Its berries are edible and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
