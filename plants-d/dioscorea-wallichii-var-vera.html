Dioscorea wallichii var. vera

Dioscorea wallichii var. vera, commonly known as the Indian yam, is a tuberous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, swollen underground stems called tubers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
