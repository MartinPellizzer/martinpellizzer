Dioscorea nervosa

Dioscorea nervosa, commonly known as the veined yam, is a tropical vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal use. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and agriculture.
