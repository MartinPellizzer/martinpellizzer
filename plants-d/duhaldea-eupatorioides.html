Duhaldea eupatorioides

Duhaldea eupatorioides, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with slender, hairy stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.
