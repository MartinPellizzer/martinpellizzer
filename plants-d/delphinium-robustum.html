Delphinium robustum

Delphinium robustum, commonly known as the tall larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Delphinium robustum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.

It is also noted for its toxicity, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to both humans and animals.
