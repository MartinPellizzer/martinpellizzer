Dendrochilum rufum

Dendrochilum rufum, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, red-pink flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Dendrochilum rufum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
