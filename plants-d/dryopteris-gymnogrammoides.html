Dryopteris gymnogrammoides

Dryopteris gymnogrammoides, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a large, terrestrial fern native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The fronds are divided into a series of lance-shaped segments, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers rich, well-drained soil.

This species is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking foliage and hardy nature.
