Didymoplexis minor var. salmonea

Didymoplexis minor var. salmonea, commonly known as the salmon-flowered climbing orchid, is a tropical vine native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that range in color from pale yellow to salmon pink, resembling the color of salmon. The plant produces small, greenish-white leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
