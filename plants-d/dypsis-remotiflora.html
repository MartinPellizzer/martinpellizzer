Dypsis remotiflora

Dypsis remotiflora, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and crown of pinnate leaves that resemble a palm. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. These flowers are followed by bright orange or red fruits containing edible seeds.

Dypsis remotiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
