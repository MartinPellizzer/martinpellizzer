Daucus aleppicus

Daucus aleppicus, commonly known as the Aleppo carrot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its roots are typically small and not as commercially valuable as those of the common carrot.
