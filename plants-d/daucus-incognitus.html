Daucus incognitus

Daucus incognitus, commonly known as the unknown carrot, is a wild species within the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in parts of Europe and western Asia. Due to its rarity and limited distribution, it is considered a lesser-known but ecologically significant species.
