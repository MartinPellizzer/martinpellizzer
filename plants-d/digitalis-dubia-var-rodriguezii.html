Digitalis dubia var. rodriguezii

Digitalis dubia var. rodriguezii, commonly known as the Rodriguez's foxglove, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic plants.
