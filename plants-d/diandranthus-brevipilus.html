Diandranthus brevipilus

Diandranthus brevipilus, commonly known as the short-hair dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its spread.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
