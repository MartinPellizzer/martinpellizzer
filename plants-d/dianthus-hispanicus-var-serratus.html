Dianthus hispanicus var. serratus

Dianthus hispanicus var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with bright pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves that give it its scientific name, and it typically grows in rocky or dry soils. Its flowers are arranged in dense clusters and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its drought tolerance.
