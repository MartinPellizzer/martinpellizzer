Ditassa ramosa

Ditassa ramosa, commonly known as the branched sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its branched stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy and nutrient-poor soils.

Ditassa ramosa is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
