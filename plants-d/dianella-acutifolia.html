Dianella acutifolia

Dianella acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved flax lily, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Dianella acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
