Dysoxylum macrophyllum

Dysoxylum macrophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit, which resembles a large, spiky melon, is edible and has a sweet, tropical flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
