Daviesia corymbosa

Daviesia corymbosa, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in heathlands and open forests. It is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate dry conditions.

The yellow wattle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
