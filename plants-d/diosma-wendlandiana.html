Diosma wendlandiana

Diosma wendlandiana, commonly known as the Wendland's diosma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Diosma wendlandiana is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
