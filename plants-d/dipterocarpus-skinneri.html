Dipterocarpus skinneri

Dipterocarpus skinneri, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
