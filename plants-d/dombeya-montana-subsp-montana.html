Dombeya montana subsp. montana

Dombeya montana subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer seasons.

The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens with suitable climate conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural landscapes.
