Dudleya rigida

Dudleya rigida, commonly known as the rigid leaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as a bloom. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Dudleya rigida is well adapted to rocky habitats and can tolerate periods of drought due to its ability to store water in its leaves.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
