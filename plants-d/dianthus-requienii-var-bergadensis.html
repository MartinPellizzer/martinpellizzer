Dianthus requienii var. bergadensis

Dianthus requienii var. bergadensis, commonly known as the Bergaden's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
