Dendrobium luteolum var. superbum

Dendrobium luteolum var. superbum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, tropical climates, often growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant features long, cane-like stems covered in fleshy, elliptical leaves. Its most striking characteristic is its showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to flourish.
