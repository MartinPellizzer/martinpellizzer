Dyckia grandidentata

Dyckia grandidentata, commonly known as the giant dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, upright rosette of long, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking red and orange inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding a dramatic visual element. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable addition to both cultivated and natural settings.
