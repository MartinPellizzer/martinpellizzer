Dianthus imereticus

Dianthus imereticus, commonly known as the Corsican pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its flowers are often clustered in racemes.

Dianthus imereticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
