Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana

Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late autumn to early winter. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure, which helps it store water and nutrients during periods of dormancy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming season.
