Dianthus serbanii

Dianthus serbanii, commonly known as the Serbanii carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Dianthus serbanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
