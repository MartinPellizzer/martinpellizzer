Derris campestris

Derris campestris, commonly known as the field derris, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of rotenone in its roots.
