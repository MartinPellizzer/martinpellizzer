Dyschoriste mauritiana

Dyschoriste mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius starfruit, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is a shrub that grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Dyschoriste mauritiana is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, showing resilience to salt spray and drought.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
