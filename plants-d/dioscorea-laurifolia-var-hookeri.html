Dioscorea laurifolia var. hookeri

Dioscorea laurifolia var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellow berries. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though they are not as widely cultivated as other yam species.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
