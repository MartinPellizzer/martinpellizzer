Decostea scandens

Decostea scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its twining stems. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Decostea scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
