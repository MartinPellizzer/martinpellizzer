Dendrolobium cephalotes

Dendrolobium cephalotes, commonly known as the helmet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation.

The species is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
