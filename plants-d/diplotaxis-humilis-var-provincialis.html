Diplotaxis humilis var. provincialis

Diplotaxis humilis var. provincialis, commonly known as the provincial dandelion, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are oblanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or medicinal plant.
