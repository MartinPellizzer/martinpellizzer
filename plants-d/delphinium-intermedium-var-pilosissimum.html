Delphinium intermedium var. pilosissimum

Delphinium intermedium var. pilosissimum, commonly known as the woolly larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in raceme inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a dense, woolly down.

The species is characterized by its robust growth habit and is adapted to grow in well-drained, alkaline soils.
