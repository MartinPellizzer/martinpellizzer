Dinocanthium bequaertii

Dinocanthium bequaertii, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a taproot system and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a valuable plant in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
