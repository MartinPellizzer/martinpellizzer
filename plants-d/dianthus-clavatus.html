Dianthus clavatus

Dianthus clavatus, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often accompanied by five stamens, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family.

Dianthus clavatus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
