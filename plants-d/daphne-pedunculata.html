Daphne pedunculata

Daphne pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has smooth, gray-green bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Daphne pedunculata is also valued for its winter interest and can be propagated through cuttings or seeds.
