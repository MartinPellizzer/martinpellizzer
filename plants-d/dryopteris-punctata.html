Dryopteris punctata

Dryopteris punctata, commonly known as the spotted wood fern, is a perennial fern native to North America.

It features a tall, upright frond structure with a distinctive, deeply divided shape. The fronds are typically gray-green with a whitish or rust-colored margin, giving them their characteristic "spotted" appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant reproduces via spores and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
