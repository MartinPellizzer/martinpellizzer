Drypetes krukovii

Drypetes krukovii, commonly known as the Krukovii flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody pods containing flat, seed-like structures.

Drypetes krukovii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
