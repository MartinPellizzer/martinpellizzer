Dioscorea modesta

Dioscorea modesta, commonly known as the modest yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The tubers of Dioscorea modesta are valued for their nutritional content and traditional use in herbal medicine.
