Didonica

Didonica, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

The silverleaf tree is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage.
