Daphne oleoides var. atlantica

Daphne oleoides var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine white wax. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
