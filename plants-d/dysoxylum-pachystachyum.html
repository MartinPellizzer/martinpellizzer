Dysoxylum pachystachyum

Dysoxylum pachystachyum, commonly known as the broad-stemmed dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, rough bark and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
