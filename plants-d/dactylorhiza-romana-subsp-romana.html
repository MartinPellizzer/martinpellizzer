Dactylorhiza romana subsp. romana

Dactylorhiza romana subsp.

romana, commonly known as the Roman orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. It has a complex life cycle involving a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is considered rare and is protected in several countries due to habitat loss and over-collection.
