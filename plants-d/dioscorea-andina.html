Dioscorea andina

Dioscorea andina, commonly known as Andean yam, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its stolons and tubers that are used as food. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally cultivated by Andean communities for centuries.

Dioscorea andina is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
