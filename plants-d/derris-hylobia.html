Derris hylobia

Derris hylobia, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, particularly derrisine, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural insecticide. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its root system is fibrous, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
