Doryopteris lorentzii

Doryopteris lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Doryopteris lorentzii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
