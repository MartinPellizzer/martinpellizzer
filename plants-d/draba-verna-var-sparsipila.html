Draba verna var. sparsipila

Draba verna var. sparsipila, commonly known as the slender spring whitlow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically has a rosette of basal leaves and stems that are often hairy. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its ecological role includes providing early-season nectar for pollinators in its native habitats.
