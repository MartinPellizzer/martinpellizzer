Dianthus chinensis var. macrosepalus

Dianthus chinensis var. macrosepalus, commonly known as the Chinese carnation, is a perennial plant native to China.

It features showy, clustered flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are erect and branching. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
