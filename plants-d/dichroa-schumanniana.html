Dichroa schumanniana

Dichroa schumanniana, commonly known as the Schumann's dichroa, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, two-toned leaves that change color with the seasons, often displaying a deep red or purple on one side and a greenish-yellow on the other. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture.

Dichroa schumanniana is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage rather than its flowers.
