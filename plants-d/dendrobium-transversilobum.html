Dendrobium transversilobum

Dendrobium transversilobum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
