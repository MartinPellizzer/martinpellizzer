Dioscorea monadelphoides var. longirachis

Dioscorea monadelphoides var. longirachis, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, bean-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates and have been traditionally used as a food source in various African cultures.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for both culinary and horticultural purposes.
