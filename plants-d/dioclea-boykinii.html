Dioclea boykinii

Dioclea boykinii, commonly known as the Brazilian apple or boykinii vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Dioclea boykinii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
