Dactylorhynchus

Dactylorhynchus, commonly known as the Christmas Orchid, is a genus of orchids native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often brightly colored flowers that bloom during the Christmas season. They are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Dactylorhynchus species typically have thick, fleshy roots and are adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.

Their flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly honeyeaters, making them an important part of the local ecosystem.
