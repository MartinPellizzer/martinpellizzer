Dactylis glomerata subsp. merinoana

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

merinoana, commonly known as Merino grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and the presence of small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. It is well adapted to temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Merino grass is valued for its forage quality and is used in agricultural settings for livestock grazing.
