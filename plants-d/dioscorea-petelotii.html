Dioscorea petelotii

Dioscorea petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by yellowish or reddish fruits containing winged seeds. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

The tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic properties.
