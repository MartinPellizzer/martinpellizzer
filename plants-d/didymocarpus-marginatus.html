Didymocarpus marginatus

Didymocarpus marginatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy coating, and its flowers are showy, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Didymocarpus marginatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
