Diacranthera crenata

Diacranthera crenata, commonly known as the serrated leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its roots are often covered in a dense, white mycorrhizal coating, which aids in nutrient absorption.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
