Dichapetalum rhodesicum

Dichapetalum rhodesicum, commonly known as the Rhodes' mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Dichapetalum rhodesicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
