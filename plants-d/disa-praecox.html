Disa praecox

Disa praecox, commonly known as the early disa, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands, where it thrives in the cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and the flowers are borne on tall, slender stems.

Disa praecox is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
