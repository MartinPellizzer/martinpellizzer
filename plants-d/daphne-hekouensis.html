Daphne hekouensis

Daphne hekouensis, commonly known as the Hekou daphne, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Thymelaeaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
