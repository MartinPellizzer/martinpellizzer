Diplolepis myrtifolia

Diplolepis myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to black, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both landscaping and foraging.
