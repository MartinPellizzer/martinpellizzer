Daturicarpa

Daturicarpa, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit when sliced. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, followed by the unique, five-lobed fruit.
