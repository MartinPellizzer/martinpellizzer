Donax azurea

Donax azurea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery-blue underside.

Donax azurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
