Doronicum macrophyllum f. hyrcanum

Doronicum macrophyllum f. hyrcanum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian giant leaved leopard's bane, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features large, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinct serrated margin. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a height that can reach up to 1.5 meters.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
