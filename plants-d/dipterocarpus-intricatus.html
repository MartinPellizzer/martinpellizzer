Dipterocarpus intricatus

Dipterocarpus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and dense, heavy wood. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, followed by the development of woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

This species plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
