Dipetalia burchellii

Dipetalia burchellii, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry savannas and rocky areas.

The yellow bell is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
