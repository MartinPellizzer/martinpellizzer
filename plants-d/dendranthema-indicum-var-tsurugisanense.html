Dendranthema indicum var. tsurugisanense

Dendranthema indicum var. tsurugisanense, commonly known as the Tsurugisanense chrysanthemum, is a variant of the common chrysanthemum native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pompon-like flowers that range in color from white to deep yellow. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, showcasing dense inflorescences that add ornamental value to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is often prized for its decorative appeal and is used in traditional Japanese horticulture.
