Datura sanguinea var. flava

Datura sanguinea var. flava, commonly known as the yellow angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of tropane alkaloids. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it should be handled with care due to its poisonous nature.
