Derris glauca

Derris glauca, commonly known as the blue derris, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain elliptical, black seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Derris glauca is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
