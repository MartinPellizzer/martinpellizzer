Diospyros beccarioides

Diospyros beccarioides, commonly known as the Bornean persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, leathery leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits that are highly valued for their sweet flavor. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in primary and secondary rainforests.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
