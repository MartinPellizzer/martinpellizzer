Dendrobium khasianum

Dendrobium khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the northeastern regions of India.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces pendant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the winter months. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water and nutrients.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
