Diplopappus andryaloides

Diplopappus andryaloides, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds with pappus structures that aid in their spread. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
