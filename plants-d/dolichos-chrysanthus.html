Dolichos chrysanthus

Dolichos chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden bean or yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds and ornamental value.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
