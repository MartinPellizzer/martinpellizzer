Dryopteris tenuissima f. serrata

Dryopteris tenuissima f. serrata, commonly known as the serrated maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to Asia, particularly regions of China and Japan. The fronds are typically arching and have a light green color, with serrated edges that give it its distinguishing name. This fern prefers shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained, acidic soil.
