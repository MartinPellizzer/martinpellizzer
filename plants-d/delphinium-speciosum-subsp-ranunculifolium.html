Delphinium speciosum subsp. ranunculifolium

Delphinium speciosum subsp.

ranunculifolium, commonly known as the leopard's bane, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear large, showy blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are palmately compound, with a distinctive lobed structure, and often have a basal rosette of foliage. This subspecies is known for its striking appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for alpine gardens.
