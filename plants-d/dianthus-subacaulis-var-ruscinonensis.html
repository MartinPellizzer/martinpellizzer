Dianthus subacaulis var. ruscinonensis

Dianthus subacaulis var. ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the stems are short and sturdy. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
