Diospyros glaucifolia

Diospyros glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-leafed persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that often have a bluish tinge, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red fruits, which are edible when fully mature. Its wood is dense and hard, making it valuable for carving and construction.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climatic conditions.
