Dichorisandra vittata

Dichorisandra vittata, commonly known as the variegated dichorisandra, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, which adds visual interest to any garden. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
