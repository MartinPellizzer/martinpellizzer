Digitaria hubbardii

Digitaria hubbardii, commonly known as Hubbard's Digitaria, is a species of grass native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to wetland environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
