Distephanus madagascariensis

Distephanus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that display a vibrant array of colors, often with contrasting patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid rainforests of its native habitat. Its botanical features include elongated, lance-shaped leaves and a compact, pseudobulbous structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its distinctive floral display.
