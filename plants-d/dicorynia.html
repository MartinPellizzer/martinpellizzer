Dicorynia

Dicorynia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Dicorynia guianensis*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, woody pods that contain edible seeds. Dicorynia is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local communities.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
