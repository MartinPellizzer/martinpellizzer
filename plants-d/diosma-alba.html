Diosma alba

Diosma alba, commonly known as the white diosma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its delicate, white flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest understory habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, fleshy berries that contain the seeds.

Diosma alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
