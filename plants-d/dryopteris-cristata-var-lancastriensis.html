Dryopteris cristata var. lancastriensis

Dryopteris cristata var. lancastriensis, commonly known as the Lancashire Hart's-tongue fern, is a variant of the Hart's-tongue fern characterized by its distinctive, narrow, strap-like fronds.

It is a perennial fern native to parts of Europe, particularly the UK, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a compact growth habit with a rosette of leaves that form a dense, upright structure. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a slightly curled appearance, giving it a unique, ornamental look.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
