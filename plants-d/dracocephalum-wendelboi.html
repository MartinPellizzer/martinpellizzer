Dracocephalum wendelboi

Dracocephalum wendelboi, commonly known as Kashmiri basil, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its foliage has a strong, spicy scent similar to oregano and thyme.
