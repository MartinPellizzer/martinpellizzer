Dipterocarpus caudatus subsp. penangianus

Dipterocarpus caudatus subsp.

penangianus, commonly known as Penang ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength.

The plant typically grows in lowland dipterocarp forests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, with yellowish-green petals, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits.
