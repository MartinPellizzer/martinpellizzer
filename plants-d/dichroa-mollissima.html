Dichroa mollissima

Dichroa mollissima, commonly known as the silky dichroa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces striking clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Dichroa mollissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
