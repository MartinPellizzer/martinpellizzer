Dicoma plantaginifolia

Dicoma plantaginifolia, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, woolly coating.

Dicoma plantaginifolia is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
