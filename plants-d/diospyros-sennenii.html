Diospyros sennenii

Diospyros sennenii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed by animals rather than humans due to their astringent taste.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional crafts and construction.
