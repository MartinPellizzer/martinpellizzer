Dryandra cirsioides

Dryandra cirsioides, commonly known as the Kangaroo Island dryandra, is a shrub native to South Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to its arid and semi-arid habitat. Its floral structure is typical of the genus, featuring long, narrow petals and prominent stamens.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
