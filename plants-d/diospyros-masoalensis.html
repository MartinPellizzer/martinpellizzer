Diospyros masoalensis

Diospyros masoalensis, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible, though they are not widely cultivated outside of its native habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in Madagascar's ecosystems.
