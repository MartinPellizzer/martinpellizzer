Desmotrichum laciniosum

Desmotrichum laciniosum, commonly known as the silky desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native environment.
