Delphinium bracteosum var. macranthum

Delphinium bracteosum var. macranthum, commonly known as the giant delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its impressive size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
