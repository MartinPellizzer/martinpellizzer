Drimia porphyrantha

Drimia porphyrantha, commonly known as the purple-flowered dragon's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem base with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Drimia porphyrantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
