Dolichos trilobus subsp. occidentalis

Dolichos trilobus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, erect stems that often grow in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing small, edible seeds. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and use in traditional agriculture.
