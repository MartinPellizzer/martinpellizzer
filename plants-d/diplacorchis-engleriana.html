Diplacorchis engleriana

Diplacorchis engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
