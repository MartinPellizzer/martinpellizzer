Dactylorhiza umbrosa var. longibracteata

Dactylorhiza umbrosa var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, with showy, spurred sepals and petals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its distinctive long bracts and unique floral structure make it a notable species within its genus.
