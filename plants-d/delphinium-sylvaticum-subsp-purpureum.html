Delphinium sylvaticum subsp. purpureum

Delphinium sylvaticum subsp.

purpureum, commonly known as the purple squirreltail, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
