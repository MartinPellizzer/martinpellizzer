Dioscorea guanaiensis

Dioscorea guanaiensis, commonly known as the Guanaiensis yam, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The tubers of Dioscorea guanaiensis are valued for their potential health benefits and are often used in traditional medicine.
