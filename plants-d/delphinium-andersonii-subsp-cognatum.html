Delphinium andersonii subsp. cognatum

Delphinium andersonii subsp.

cognatum, commonly known as the Colorado larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Colorado and New Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is also an important plant for pollinators and local biodiversity.
