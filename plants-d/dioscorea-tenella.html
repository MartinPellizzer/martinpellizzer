Dioscorea tenella

Dioscorea tenella, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated tubers that resemble a spine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful preparation.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its unique foliage and growth habit.
