Duranta rupestris

Duranta rupestris, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has gray-green, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Duranta rupestris thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
