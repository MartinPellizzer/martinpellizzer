Diospyros tristis

Diospyros tristis, commonly known as the bitter persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellowish-green in color. Its fruit is a small, globose berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature, and it is known for its astringent taste when unripe.

Diospyros tristis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a food source in certain regions.
