Dioscorea novemloba

Dioscorea novemloba, commonly known as the nine-lobed yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive nine-lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, though they are not commonly cultivated for food.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
