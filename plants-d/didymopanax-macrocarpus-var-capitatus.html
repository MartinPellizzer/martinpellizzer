Didymopanax macrocarpus var. capitatus

Didymopanax macrocarpus var. capitatus, commonly known as the giant sycamore, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree characterized by its thick, buttressed roots and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are large, simple, and alternate, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib. The tree produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
