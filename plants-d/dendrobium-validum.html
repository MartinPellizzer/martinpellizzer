Dendrobium validum

Dendrobium validum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent purple markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium validum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
