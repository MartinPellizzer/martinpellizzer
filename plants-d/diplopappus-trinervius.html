Diplopappus trinervius

Diplopappus trinervius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. Its flowers are followed by wind-dispersed seeds, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
