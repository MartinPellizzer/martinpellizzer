Drosera villosa

Drosera villosa, commonly known as the hairy sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a sweet-smelling mucilage to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and swampy areas. Its leaves are usually clustered at the base, forming a rosette shape.

Drosera villosa produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal.
