Dipteracanthus lamiiformis

Dipteracanthus lamiiformis, commonly known as the lamiiform leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its stems are woody and have a fibrous texture, while its roots are extensively branched and help anchor it to trees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
