Diospyros ferruginea

Diospyros ferruginea, commonly known as the red-ironwood or rusty ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe.

The species is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
