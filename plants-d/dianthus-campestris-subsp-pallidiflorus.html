Dianthus campestris subsp. pallidiflorus

Dianthus campestris subsp.

pallidiflorus, commonly known as the paleflower carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces pinkish-red stems and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
