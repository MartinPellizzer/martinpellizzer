Didymocarpus rottlerianus var. wightii

Didymocarpus rottlerianus var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's star fruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruits. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped berries that are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
