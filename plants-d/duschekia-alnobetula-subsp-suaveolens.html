Duschekia alnobetula subsp. suaveolens

Duschekia alnobetula subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
