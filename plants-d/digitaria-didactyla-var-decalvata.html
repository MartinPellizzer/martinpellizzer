Digitaria didactyla var. decalvata

Digitaria didactyla var. decalvata, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and degraded soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its adaptability to various climates.
