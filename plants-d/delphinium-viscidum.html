Delphinium viscidum

Delphinium viscidum, commonly known as sticky larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has sticky, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Delphinium viscidum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
