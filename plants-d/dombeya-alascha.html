Dombeya alascha

Dombeya alascha, commonly known as Alaska dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the subalpine regions of western North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, gray-green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Dombeya alascha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
