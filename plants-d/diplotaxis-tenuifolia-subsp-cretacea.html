Diplotaxis tenuifolia subsp. cretacea

Diplotaxis tenuifolia subsp.

cretacea, commonly known as the Cretan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with basal rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy species within the Brassicaceae family.
