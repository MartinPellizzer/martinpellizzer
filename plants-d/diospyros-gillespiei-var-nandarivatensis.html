Diospyros gillespiei var. nandarivatensis

Diospyros gillespiei var. nandarivatensis, commonly known as the Nandarivatensis persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible when fully mature, though it may have a tart flavor when harvested too early.

This variant is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphological traits and potential for cultivation.
