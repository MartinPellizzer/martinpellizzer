Digitaria henryi

Digitaria henryi, commonly known as Henry's digitaria, is a species of grass native to parts of China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Digitaria henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
