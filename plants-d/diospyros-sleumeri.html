Diospyros sleumeri

Diospyros sleumeri, commonly known as the Bornean persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature, often covered in a whitish bloom.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood and is also cultivated for its edible fruit.
