Dentaria enneaphyllos var. angustisecta

Dentaria enneaphyllos var. angustisecta, commonly known as narrow-leaf toothwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, toothed leaves that are narrow and sharply divided. The plant produces a single, slender flowering stem that bears small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and its role in supporting local pollinators.
