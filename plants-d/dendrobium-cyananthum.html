Dendrobium cyananthum

Dendrobium cyananthum, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant features long, cane-like stems covered in fleshy, elliptical leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

Dendrobium cyananthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
