Dryopteris rubiginosa

Dryopteris rubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty wood fern, is a perennial fern native to the northern hemisphere.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, often rusty-colored underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are arranged in clusters on the undersides of the fronds.

Dryopteris rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
