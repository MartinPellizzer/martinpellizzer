Damasonium brasiliense

Damasonium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian water starwort, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are typically oval or rounded with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Damasonium brasiliense plays a role in stabilizing water ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
