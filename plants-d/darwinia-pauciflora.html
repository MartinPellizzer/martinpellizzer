Darwinia pauciflora

Darwinia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to south-western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and dense clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces showy inflorescences that are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in suitable climates.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
