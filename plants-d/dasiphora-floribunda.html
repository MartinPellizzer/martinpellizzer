Dasiphora floribunda

Dasiphora floribunda, commonly known as the showy lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its flowering stems are usually upright and covered with soft, fuzzy hairs.

Dasiphora floribunda is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
