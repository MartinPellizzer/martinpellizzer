Deina polonica var. semiaristata

Deina polonica var. semiaristata, commonly known as the curly-leaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in dense spikes, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
