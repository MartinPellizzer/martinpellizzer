Dactylorhiza atlantica

Dactylorhiza atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Atlantic coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces upright, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

The plant is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental value in horticulture.
