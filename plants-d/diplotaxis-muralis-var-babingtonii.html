Diplotaxis muralis var. babingtonii

Diplotaxis muralis var. babingtonii, commonly known as the Babington's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are narrow and linear.
