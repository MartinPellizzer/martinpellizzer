Dendropanax macrophyllus

Dendropanax macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowleaf, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically leathery and have a distinctive midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and rainforest margins.

Dendropanax macrophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
