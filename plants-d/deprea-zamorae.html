Deprea zamorae

Deprea zamorae, commonly known as the Zamora's dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Deprea zamorae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
