Dactylodes dactyloides

Dactylodes dactyloides, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
