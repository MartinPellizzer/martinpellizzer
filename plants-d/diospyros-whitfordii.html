Diospyros whitfordii

Diospyros whitfordii, commonly known as the whiteford persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, orange-red drupe that matures in late summer.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use due to its small size and specific habitat requirements.
