Dasyochloa argentina

Dasyochloa argentina, commonly known as Argentine dasyochloa, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to withstand seasonal droughts.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for livestock and supporting local biodiversity.
