Danthonia circinnata

Danthonia circinnata, commonly known as the golden tuft grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bright golden-colored inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Danthonia circinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
