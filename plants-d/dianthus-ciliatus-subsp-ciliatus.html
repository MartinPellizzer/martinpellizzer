Dianthus ciliatus subsp. ciliatus

Dianthus ciliatus subsp.

ciliatus, commonly known as the fringed campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers with fringed petals and a compact growth habit.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Dianthus ciliatus subsp.

ciliatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and borders.
