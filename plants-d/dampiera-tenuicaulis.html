Dampiera tenuicaulis

Dampiera tenuicaulis, commonly known as narrow-leaved dampiera, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats. It has a creeping root system that allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Dampiera tenuicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native insect populations.
