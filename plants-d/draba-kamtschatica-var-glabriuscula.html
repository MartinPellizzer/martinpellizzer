Draba kamtschatica var. glabriuscula

Draba kamtschatica var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the Kamchatka whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold climates of the Russian Far East and Alaska.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features dense, basal rosettes of linear leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky, alpine, or arctic environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
