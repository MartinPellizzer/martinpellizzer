Dahlia rosea

Dahlia rosea, commonly known as the rose dahlia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white center. The plant has tuberous roots and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Dahlia rosea is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
