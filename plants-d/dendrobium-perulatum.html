Dendrobium perulatum

Dendrobium perulatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizomatous stem that produces numerous pseudobulbs. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium perulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
