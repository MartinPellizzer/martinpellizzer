Dryopteris indusiata var. purpurascens

Dryopteris indusiata var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features fronds that are typically bright green with a striking purple hue on the underside, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces sori, which are clusters of sporangia, on the undersides of its mature fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
