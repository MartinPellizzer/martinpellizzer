Drimia depressa

Drimia depressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf rain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, underground rhizome and produces erect, leafy stems that bear showy white or pale purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette. It typically blooms during the rainy season, showcasing its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
