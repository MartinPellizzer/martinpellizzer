Diplazium caryifolium

Diplazium caryifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed chain fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into broad, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical structure includes both sterile and fertile fronds, with spores produced on the undersides of the leaves.
