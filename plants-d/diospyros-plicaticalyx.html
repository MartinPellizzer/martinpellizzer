Diospyros plicaticalyx

Diospyros plicaticalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leafed persimmon, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in panicles, and the fruit is a fleshy, orange to reddish drupe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
