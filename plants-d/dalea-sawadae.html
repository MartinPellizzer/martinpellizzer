Dalea sawadae

Dalea sawadae, commonly known as the Sawada's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea sawadae is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
