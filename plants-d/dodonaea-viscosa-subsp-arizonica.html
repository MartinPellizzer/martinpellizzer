Dodonaea viscosa subsp. arizonica

Dodonaea viscosa subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as Arizona soapbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several winged seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.
