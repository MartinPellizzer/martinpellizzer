Draba magellanica var. falklandica

Draba magellanica var. falklandica, commonly known as Falkland's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Falkland Islands.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in the cool, temperate climate of the Southern Hemisphere. It has small, basal leaves and produces numerous white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months.

This variety is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat, exhibiting resilience to strong winds and limited soil nutrients.
