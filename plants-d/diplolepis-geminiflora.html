Diplolepis geminiflora

Diplolepis geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystem.

The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
