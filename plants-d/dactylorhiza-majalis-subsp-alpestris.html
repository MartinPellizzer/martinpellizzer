Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. alpestris

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is a terrestrial orchid with a tall, erect stem and linear, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikes of pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in moist, acidic soils in mountainous and alpine regions.

This subspecies is adapted to cool, temperate climates and is often found in peat bogs and wet meadows.
