Diphyes xanthotes

Diphyes xanthotes, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
