Dianthus musalae

Dianthus musalae, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus musalae is often found in high-altitude environments, where it adapts to cold and harsh climatic conditions.
