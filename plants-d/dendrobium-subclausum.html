Dendrobium subclausum

Dendrobium subclausum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dendrobium, is a member of the orchid family Orchidaceae.

It is an epiphytic or lithophytic plant native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, and its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and often covered in a layer of white or brownish fur. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually appear in clusters, with colors ranging from white to pale yellow.

Dendrobium subclausum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
