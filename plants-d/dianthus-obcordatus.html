Dianthus obcordatus

Dianthus obcordatus, commonly known as the heart's-ease, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pink or red petals that resemble tiny hearts. The plant typically produces clusters of five-petaled flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Dianthus obcordatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
