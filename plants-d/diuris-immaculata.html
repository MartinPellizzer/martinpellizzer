Diuris immaculata

Diuris immaculata, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its delicate appearance.

The species is known for its ability to form dense colonies and is a popular subject in botanical studies due to its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
