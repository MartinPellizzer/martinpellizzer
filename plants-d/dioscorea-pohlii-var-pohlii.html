Dioscorea pohlii var. pohlii

Dioscorea pohlii var. pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

The tubers of this species are edible when properly prepared and have been used traditionally in various cuisines.
