Dasyglossum gracile

Dasyglossum gracile, commonly known as the slender tongue grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dasyglossum gracile is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
