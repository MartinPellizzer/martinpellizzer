Darwinia divisa

Darwinia divisa, commonly known as the divided Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native landscapes.

Darwinia divisa is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators.
