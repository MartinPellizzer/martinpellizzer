Drymocallis laxiflora

Drymocallis laxiflora, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Drymocallis laxiflora plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting various wildlife species.
