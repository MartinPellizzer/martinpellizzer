Dianthus × oenipontanus

Dianthus × oenipontanus, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dianthus chinensis and Dianthus deltoides, combining traits from both parent species. The plant typically produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, with prominent central stamens. It is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is often used in floral arrangements.

The foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall appearance.
