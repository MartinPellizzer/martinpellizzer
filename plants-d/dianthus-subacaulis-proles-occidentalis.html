Dianthus subacaulis proles occidentalis

Dianthus subacaulis proles occidentalis, commonly known as the western rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent stems and clustered pink or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, showy flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping.
