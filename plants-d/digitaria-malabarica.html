Digitaria malabarica

Digitaria malabarica, commonly known as Malabar grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland and disturbed areas.

Digitaria malabarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
