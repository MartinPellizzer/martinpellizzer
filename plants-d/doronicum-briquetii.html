Doronicum briquetii

Doronicum briquetii, commonly known as the alpine leopard's ear, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the shape of a dandelion, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Doronicum briquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
