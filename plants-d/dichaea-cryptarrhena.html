Dichaea cryptarrhena

Dichaea cryptarrhena, commonly known as the cryptarrhena bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dichaea cryptarrhena is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
