Diplazium fimbriatum

Diplazium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are often submerged in water. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help anchor it in muddy substrates.

Diplazium fimbriatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
