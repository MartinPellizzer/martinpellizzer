Dianthus burdurensis

Dianthus burdurensis, commonly known as the Burdurean carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Dianthus burdurensis is a perennial species that prefers full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
