Diplacus longiflorus var. calycinus

Diplacus longiflorus var. calycinus, commonly known as the California honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees.
