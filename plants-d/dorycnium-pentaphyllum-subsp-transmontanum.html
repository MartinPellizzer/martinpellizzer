Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp. transmontanum

Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp.

transmontanum, commonly known as the Transmontane cowvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant produces small, elongated legumes that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
