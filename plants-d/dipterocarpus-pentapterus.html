Dipterocarpus pentapterus

Dipterocarpus pentapterus, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its habitat.
