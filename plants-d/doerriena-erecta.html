Doerriena erecta

Doerriena erecta, commonly known as the upright doerriena, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Doerriena erecta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.
