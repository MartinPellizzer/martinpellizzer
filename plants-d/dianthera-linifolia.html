Dianthera linifolia

Dianthera linifolia, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
