Dianthus seguieri lusus humilis

Dianthus seguieri lusus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and long flowering period add ornamental value to garden landscapes.
