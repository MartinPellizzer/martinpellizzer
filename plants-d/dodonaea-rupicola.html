Dodonaea rupicola

Dodonaea rupicola, commonly known as the rock dodonaea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments.

Dodonaea rupicola is also valued for its ability to stabilize soils and is sometimes used in landscaping.
