Dactylorhiza × naris

Dactylorhiza × naris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, violet or purple flowers during late summer. It thrives in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and marshes.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Dactylorhiza genus.
