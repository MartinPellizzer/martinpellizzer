Dianthus serulis

Dianthus serulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many showy flowering plants. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are pink to red, with five petals and prominent white veins, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dianthus serulis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
