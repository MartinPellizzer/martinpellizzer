Dupatya regeliana

Dupatya regeliana, commonly known as the Regel's daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Dupatya regeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
