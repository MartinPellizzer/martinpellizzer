Digitalis purpurascens

Digitalis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes several species known for their medicinal properties. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
