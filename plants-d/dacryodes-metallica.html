Dacryodes metallica

Dacryodes metallica, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
