Dampiera brownii var. ovalifolia

Dampiera brownii var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved dampiera, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
