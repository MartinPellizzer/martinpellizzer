Dianthus plumarius f. palaviensis

Dianthus plumarius f. palaviensis, commonly known as the Palavien carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a compact, herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of showy, double-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
