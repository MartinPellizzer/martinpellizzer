Dendranthema indicum var. procumbens

Dendranthema indicum var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping chrysanthemum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in ornamental gardening for its compact form and prolonged blooming period.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to spread easily in suitable conditions.
