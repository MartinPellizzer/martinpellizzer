Dyckia goehringii

Dyckia goehringii, commonly known as the Goehring's Dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a distinctive, fibrous texture. It produces small, white flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, often in late summer or early fall.

This plant is well adapted to dry environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant due to its hardy nature.
