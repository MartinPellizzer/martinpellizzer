Dianthus superbus f. longicalycinus

Dianthus superbus f. longicalycinus, commonly known as the long-stemmed carnation, is a flowering plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. Its flowers typically display vibrant red, pink, or white hues, with five petals arranged in a distinctive, ruffled pattern. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, blooming in late spring to early summer.
