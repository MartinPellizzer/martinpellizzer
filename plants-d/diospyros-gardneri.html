Diospyros gardneri

Diospyros gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe.

The fruit has a sweet, astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
