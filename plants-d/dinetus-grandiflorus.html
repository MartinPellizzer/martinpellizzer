Dinetus grandiflorus

Dinetus grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Dinetus grandiflorus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
