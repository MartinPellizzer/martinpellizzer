Dioscorea scortechinii var. parviflora

Dioscorea scortechinii var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous elliptical seeds.

Due to its medicinal properties and adaptability, it is of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
