Dianthus Pungens Subsp. Tarraconensis

Tarraconensis campion, scientifically known as Dianthus pungens subsp. tarraconensis, is a rare and striking plant native to the coastal regions of southern Spain. This member of the Caryophyllaceae family is characterized by its vibrant pink flowers and succulent leaves that help it thrive in arid conditions. It is often found growing in rocky soils along cliffs and coastal dunes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Due to habitat loss and climate change, the species faces increasing threats to its survival. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique and beautiful flora for future generations.
