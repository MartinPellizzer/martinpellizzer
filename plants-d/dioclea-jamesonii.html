Dioclea jamesonii

Dioclea jamesonii, commonly known as the pink spider plant, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and its striking, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Dioclea jamesonii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
