Diospyros latisepala

Diospyros latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with five to seven sepals that are broader than typical for the genus.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that ripens to a yellow or orange color, often used in local cuisines.
