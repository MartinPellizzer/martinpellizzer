Dorotheanthus maughanii

Dorotheanthus maughanii, commonly known as the Maughan's daisy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Dorotheanthus maughanii is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
