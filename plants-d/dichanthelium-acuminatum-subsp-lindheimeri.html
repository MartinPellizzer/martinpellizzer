Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp. lindheimeri

Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp.

lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's threeawn, is a grass species native to the southern United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and spikelets that are often bristled with awns. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to warm climates and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
