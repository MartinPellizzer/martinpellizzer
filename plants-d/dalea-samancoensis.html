Dalea samancoensis

Dalea samancoensis, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Dalea samancoensis is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it an important part of its native ecosystem.
