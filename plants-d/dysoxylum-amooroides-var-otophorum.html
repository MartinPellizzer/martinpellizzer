Dysoxylum amooroides var. otophorum

Dysoxylum amooroides var. otophorum, commonly known as the Otophorum or a type of mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests along coastal areas. Its roots form a complex network that helps stabilize shorelines and filter water.

The species is valued for its timber and plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems.
