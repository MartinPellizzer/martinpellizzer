Deinbollia pervillei f. suarezensis

Deinbollia pervillei f. suarezensis, commonly known as the Suarez's false arum, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, spadix-like inflorescences.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is native to the humid lowland forests of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant exhibits a climbing habit, often using its aerial roots to attach to trees or other supports. Its botanical features include a thick, fleshy rhizome and waxy, glossy foliage that helps it thrive in shaded, moist environments.

The flowers, though small and inconspicuous, are surrounded by a colorful spathe, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
