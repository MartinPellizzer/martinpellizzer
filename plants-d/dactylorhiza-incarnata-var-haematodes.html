Dactylorhiza incarnata var. haematodes

Dactylorhiza incarnata var. haematodes, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive reddish or purplish coloration in its flowers and stems, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heaths. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or red, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking appearance.
