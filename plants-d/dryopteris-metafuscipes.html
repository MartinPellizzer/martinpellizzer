Dryopteris metafuscipes

Dryopteris metafuscipes, commonly known as the blackfoot fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive blackish or dark brown coloration on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

The blackfoot fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
