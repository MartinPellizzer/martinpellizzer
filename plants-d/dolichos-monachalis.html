Dolichos monachalis

Dolichos monachalis, commonly known as the monk's bean, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and greenhouses.

The seeds of Dolichos monachalis can be used in traditional cuisines, though they are generally less common than other legumes.
