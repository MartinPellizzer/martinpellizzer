Daviesia scabrella

Daviesia scabrella, commonly known as the rough-hairy Daviesia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, pink or pale red flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native gardens.
