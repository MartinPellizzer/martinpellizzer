Daustinia montana

Daustinia montana, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Daustinia montana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, low-maintenance plants.
