Dianella carolinensis

Dianella carolinensis, commonly known as the common flax lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Australia.

It features strap-shaped, dark green leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Dianella carolinensis is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
