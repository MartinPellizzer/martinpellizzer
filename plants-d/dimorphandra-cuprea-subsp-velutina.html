Dimorphandra cuprea subsp. velutina

Dimorphandra cuprea subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvet flame tree, is a species native to Madagascar. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and distinctive, palmate leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its velvety, hairy texture on young shoots and leaves, contributing to its unique appearance.
