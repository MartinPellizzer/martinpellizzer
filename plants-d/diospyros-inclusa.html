Diospyros inclusa

Diospyros inclusa, commonly known as the Hawaiian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, though often considered astringent when unripe, and is an important food source for native Hawaiian birds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
