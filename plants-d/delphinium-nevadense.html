Delphinium nevadense

Delphinium nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Delphinium nevadense is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
