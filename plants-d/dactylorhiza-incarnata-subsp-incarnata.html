Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. incarnata

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

incarnata, commonly known as the Common Spotted Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy, pinkish-purple flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a complex root system with a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aids in nutrient absorption.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
