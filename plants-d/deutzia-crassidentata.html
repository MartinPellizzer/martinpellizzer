Deutzia crassidentata

Deutzia crassidentata, commonly known as the broad-toothed deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in small, glandular dots. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Deutzia crassidentata is also valued for its moderate growth habit and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
