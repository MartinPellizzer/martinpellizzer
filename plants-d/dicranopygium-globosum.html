Dicranopygium globosum

Dicranopygium globosum, commonly known as the globe moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and compact, rounded shoots. The leaves are typically short and stout, with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky substrates or in forest floors.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
