Draba spectabilis var. glabrescens

Draba spectabilis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the showy whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which give the variety its name "glabrescens.".

The showy whitlow grass is known for its early spring blooming period, adding a splash of color to high-altitude environments.
