Derris goetzei

Derris goetzei, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Derris goetzei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
