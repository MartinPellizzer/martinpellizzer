Dilkea parviflora

Dilkea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered dilkea, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It typically grows in open woodland and heathland environments, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale blue to white, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Dilkea parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
