Dianthus hungaricus subsp. regis-stephani

Dianthus hungaricus subsp.

regis-stephani, commonly known as the Hungarian carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with prominent white or pink central markings.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the stems are upright and branched. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
