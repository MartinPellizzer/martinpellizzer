Daphne candida

Daphne candida, commonly known as white daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are white or pale pink, appearing in early spring. The plant has smooth, gray-green leaves and produces small, fleshy berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
