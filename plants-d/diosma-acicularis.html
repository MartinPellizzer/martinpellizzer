Diosma acicularis

Diosma acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf diosma, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Diosma acicularis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
