Disocactus nelsonii subsp. hondurensis

Disocactus nelsonii subsp.

hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran poinsettia, is a succulent plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers.

The plant typically grows in a compact, shrub-like form, with clusters of small, greenish-white flowers emerging from the base of the bracts. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home environment.
