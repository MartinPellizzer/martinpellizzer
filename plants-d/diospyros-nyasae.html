Diospyros nyasae

Diospyros nyasae, commonly known as the Malawi date palm, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lanceolate, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible drupes with a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
