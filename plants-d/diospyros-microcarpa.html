Diospyros microcarpa

Diospyros microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature.

The wood of Diospyros microcarpa is valued for its density and durability, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.
