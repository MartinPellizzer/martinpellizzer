Decaisnea angulosa

Decaisnea angulosa, commonly known as the angular decaisnea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive angular shape, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically appearing in clusters.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
