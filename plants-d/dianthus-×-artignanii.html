Dianthus × artignanii

Dianthus × artignanii, commonly known as the artichoke carnation, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are velvety to the touch, and its flowers consist of double layers of petals that bloom in clusters. It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its ornamental value and resilience in various soil conditions.

Dianthus × artignanii thrives in full sun and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
