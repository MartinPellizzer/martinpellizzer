Dipladenia alexicaca

Dipladenia alexicaca, commonly known as the red-flowered morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Dipladenia alexicaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
