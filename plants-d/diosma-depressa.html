Diosma depressa

Diosma depressa, commonly known as the creeping diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Diosma depressa is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.
