Desmazeria loliacea var. syrtica

Desmazeria loliacea var. syrtica, commonly known as the Syrtic grass, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale blue or white flowers, which bloom during the summer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
