Delphinium carolinianum var. crispum

Delphinium carolinianum var. crispum, commonly known as the curlyleaf larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically spurred at the base. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a wavy, curled edge, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
