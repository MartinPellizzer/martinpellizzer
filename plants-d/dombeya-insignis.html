Dombeya insignis

Dombeya insignis, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Dombeya insignis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and compact growth habit.
