Dioscorea hombuka

Dioscorea hombuka, commonly known as Hombuka yam, is a tuberous plant belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source. Its growth habit involves climbing vines that can reach several meters in length.

The tubers are often harvested after a growing season of several months, depending on the climate and soil conditions.
