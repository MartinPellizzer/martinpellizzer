Deparia pycnosora var. mucilagina

Deparia pycnosora var. mucilagina, commonly known as the sticky fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed woodfern.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery fronds that often have a waxy, sticky surface. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

This variant is notable for its unique texture and adaptability to humid conditions.
