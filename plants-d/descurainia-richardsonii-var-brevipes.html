Descurainia richardsonii var. brevipes

Descurainia richardsonii var. brevipes, commonly known as Richardson's tansy, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
