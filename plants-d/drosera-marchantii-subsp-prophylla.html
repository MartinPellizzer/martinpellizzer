Drosera marchantii subsp. prophylla

Drosera marchantii subsp.

prophylla, commonly known as the purple-stemmed sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects.

The subspecies prophylla is distinguished by its purple-tinged stems and reddish-pink flowers. It thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes.

Its botanical features make it a unique and fascinating subject for study in the realm of carnivorous flora.
