Deyeuxia emirnensis

Deyeuxia emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges. Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small mammals.

Deyeuxia emirnensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential for conservation significance.
