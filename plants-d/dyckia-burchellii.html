Dyckia burchellii

Dyckia burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's dyckia, is a hardy, evergreen bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of narrow, leathery leaves that form a water-holding cup at the center. The plant produces striking, branched inflorescences with showy, colorful bracts that range from red to orange. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dyckia burchellii is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal water and full to partial sunlight.
