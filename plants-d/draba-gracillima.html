Draba gracillima

Draba gracillima, commonly known as the slender whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, thriving in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant is characterized by its delicate, basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, making it a low-growing, herbaceous perennial.

Draba gracillima is well adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in open, sunny areas at high elevations.
