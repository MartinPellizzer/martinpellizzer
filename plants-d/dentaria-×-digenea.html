Dentaria × digenea

Dentaria × digenea, commonly known as the hybrid toothworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, tooth-like leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
