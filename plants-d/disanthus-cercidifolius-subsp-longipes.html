Disanthus cercidifolius subsp. longipes

Disanthus cercidifolius subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed maple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, typically in shades of pink or white. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
