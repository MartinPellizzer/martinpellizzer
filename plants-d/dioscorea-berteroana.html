Dioscorea berteroana

Dioscorea berteroana, commonly known as the Andean yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used as a food source, similar to other yam species. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are typically greenish-white with a yellow center.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.
