Delphinium wangii

Delphinium wangii, commonly known as the Wang's larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are composed of five petals and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Delphinium wangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
