Dioclea mollis

Dioclea mollis, commonly known as the pink passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five pink sepals and five white petals, often with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has a strong, woody stem.

Dioclea mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
