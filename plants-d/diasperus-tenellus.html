Diasperus tenellus

Diasperus tenellus, commonly known as the slender candelabra cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, slender stems that often branch out into a candelabra-like shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Diasperus tenellus is well-suited for cultivation in dry, well-drained soils and full sun.
