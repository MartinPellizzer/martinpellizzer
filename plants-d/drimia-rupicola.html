Drimia rupicola

Drimia rupicola, commonly known as the rock drimia, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Drimia rupicola is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
