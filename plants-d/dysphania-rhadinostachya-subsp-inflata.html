Dysphania rhadinostachya subsp. inflata

Dysphania rhadinostachya subsp.

inflata, commonly known as the inflated leaf goosefoot, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated leaves that are typically lobed or deeply divided.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is often found in moist, disturbed soils. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with an upright stem that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

This species is native to regions of South America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
