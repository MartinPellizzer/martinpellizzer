Drepanocarpus crista-castrensis var. latifolius

Drepanocarpus crista-castrensis var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf holly, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense, rounded crown and leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
