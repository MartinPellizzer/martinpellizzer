Dendrobium hamiferum

Dendrobium hamiferum, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium hamiferum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
