Dianthus × notabilis

Dianthus × notabilis, commonly known as the double carnation, is a hybrid flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, double blooms that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and produces vibrant, velvety petals that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Dianthus × notabilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
