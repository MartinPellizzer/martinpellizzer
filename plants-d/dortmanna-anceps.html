Dortmanna anceps

Dortmanna anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, often succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. The plant produces clusters of tiny white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Dortmanna anceps thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
