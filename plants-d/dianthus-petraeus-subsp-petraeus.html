Dianthus petraeus subsp. petraeus

Dianthus petraeus subsp.

petraeus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, blue-gray leaves.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Dianthus petraeus subsp.

petraeus is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes at high elevations.
