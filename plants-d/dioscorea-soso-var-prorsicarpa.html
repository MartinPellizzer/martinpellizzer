Dioscorea soso var. prorsicarpa

Dioscorea soso var. prorsicarpa, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically alternate along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers, which are the primary economically important part, are used as a food source in various regions of Africa.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
