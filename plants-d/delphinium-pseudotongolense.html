Delphinium pseudotongolense

Delphinium pseudotongolense, commonly known as the Tibetan larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and Nepal.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
