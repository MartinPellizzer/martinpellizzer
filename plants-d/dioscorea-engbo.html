Dioscorea engbo

Dioscorea engbo, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used as a food source in some regions. Its growth habit involves twining stems that climb supports, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The botanical features of Dioscorea engbo make it an important species in both traditional agriculture and botanical research.
