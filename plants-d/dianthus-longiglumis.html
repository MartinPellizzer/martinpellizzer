Dianthus longiglumis

Dianthus longiglumis, commonly known as the longbeak campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
