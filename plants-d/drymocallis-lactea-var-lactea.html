Drymocallis lactea var. lactea

Drymocallis lactea var. lactea, commonly known as white oak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in spikes during the summer months.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
