Dendropanax bilocularis

Dendropanax bilocularis, commonly known as the Solomon's seal, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its arching stems and bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinct veining pattern.

Dendropanax bilocularis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
