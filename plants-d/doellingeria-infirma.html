Doellingeria infirma

Doellingeria infirma, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often emerging from the base of the plant.

Doellingeria infirma plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
