Disa biflora

Disa biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.

Disa biflora is also noted for its delicate, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
