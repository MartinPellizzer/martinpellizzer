Doritis pulcherrima f. caerulea

Doritis pulcherrima f. caerulea, commonly known as the Blue Doritis, is a striking hybrid of the Doritis pulcherrima species.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet floral bracts, which are often more intense than those of the typical red or pink varieties. The plant is a monocot, belonging to the Asphodelaceae family, and is native to South Africa. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its color intensity.

This cultivar is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
