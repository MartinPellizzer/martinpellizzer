Dalea cylindrica var. sulfurea

Dalea cylindrica var. sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur curlleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, curling leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native range.
