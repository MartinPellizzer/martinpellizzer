Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. polonicus

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

polonicus, commonly known as the Polish carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or red, five-petaled flowers.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical D. carthusianorum.
