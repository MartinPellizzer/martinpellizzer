Dicksonia gracilis

Dicksonia gracilis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense, fibrous mat, helping to stabilize the soil. Its fronds are typically light green and have a soft, leathery texture.

Dicksonia gracilis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
