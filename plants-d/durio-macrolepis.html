Durio macrolepis

Durio macrolepis, commonly known as the large-leaf durian, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit. The plant belongs to the Durian genus, which is well-known for its aromatic and often pungent fruit. Durio macrolepis typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, warm climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
