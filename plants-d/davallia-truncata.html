Davallia truncata

Davallia truncata, commonly known as the serrate holly fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features fronds that are pinnately compound, with a distinctive serrated margin that gives it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture.

Davallia truncata thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.
