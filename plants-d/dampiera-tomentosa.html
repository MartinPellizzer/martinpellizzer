Dampiera tomentosa

Dampiera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly dampiera, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dampiera tomentosa is often found in the southeastern regions of Australia, including New South Wales and Victoria.
