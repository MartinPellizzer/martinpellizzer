Dalbergia chapelieri

Dalbergia chapelieri, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and hard, dark wood. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical forests and is known for its slow growth rate. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and wood quality, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
