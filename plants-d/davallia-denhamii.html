Davallia denhamii

Davallia denhamii, commonly known as the Denham's spleenwort, is a fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocks or tree trunks. Its fronds are usually dark green with a slightly curled appearance, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Davallia denhamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in indoor gardens.
