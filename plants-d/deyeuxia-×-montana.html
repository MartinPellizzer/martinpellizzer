Deyeuxia × montana

Deyeuxia × montana, commonly known as the mountain couchgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Deyeuxia × montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
