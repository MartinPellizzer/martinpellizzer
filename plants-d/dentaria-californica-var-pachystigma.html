Dentaria californica var. pachystigma

Dentaria californica var. pachystigma, commonly known as the California toothplant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, tooth-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to conserve moisture in arid environments.

The species is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
