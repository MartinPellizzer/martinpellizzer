Dianthus × arvernensis

Dianthus × arvernensis, commonly known as the French carnation, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of five petals that often have a frilled edge, giving them a delicate appearance.

Dianthus × arvernensis is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
