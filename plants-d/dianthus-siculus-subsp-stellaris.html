Dianthus siculus subsp. stellaris

Dianthus siculus subsp.

stellaris, commonly known as the star pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of petals that resemble small stars. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
