Diasperus villicaulis

Diasperus villicaulis, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a dense mat of silver-gray, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Diasperus villicaulis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
