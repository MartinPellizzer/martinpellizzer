Dolichos lablab var. rhomboideus

Dolichos lablab var. rhomboideus, commonly known as the broad bean or lablab bean, is an annual legume native to Africa and cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped, and its elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several edible seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a versatile crop in warm climates.

The seeds are rich in protein and are used in various culinary traditions around the world.
