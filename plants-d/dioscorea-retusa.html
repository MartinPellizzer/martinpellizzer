Dioscorea retusa

Dioscorea retusa, commonly known as the smooth yam, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic properties.

Dioscorea retusa is also valued as a food crop in many parts of the world due to its nutritional content and adaptability to various growing conditions.
