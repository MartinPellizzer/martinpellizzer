Dioscorea angulata

Dioscorea angulata, commonly known as the angular yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are used as a food source in some regions, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful processing.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
