Diasperus axillaris

Diasperus axillaris, commonly known as the axillary daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its stems are often branched and hairy.

Diasperus axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
