Dendrochilum merrillii

Dendrochilum merrillii, commonly known as the Philippine tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered with a thick, waxy cuticle.

Dendrochilum merrillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
