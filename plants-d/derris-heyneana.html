Derris heyneana

Derris heyneana, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a natural insecticide used in traditional agriculture. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal and pesticidal properties.

Derris heyneana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
