Desmaria

Desmaria, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is typically grayish-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Desmaria is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
