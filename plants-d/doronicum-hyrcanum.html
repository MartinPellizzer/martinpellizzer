Doronicum hyrcanum

Doronicum hyrcanum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white down.

Doronicum hyrcanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
