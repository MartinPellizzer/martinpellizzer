Diospyros panguana

Diospyros panguana, commonly known as the Panguana persimmon, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by small, round fruits resembling persimmons. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
