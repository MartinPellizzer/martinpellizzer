Delphinium virescens subsp. penardii

Delphinium virescens subsp.

penardii, commonly known as the Penard's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
