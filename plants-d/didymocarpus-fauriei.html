Didymocarpus fauriei

Didymocarpus fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
