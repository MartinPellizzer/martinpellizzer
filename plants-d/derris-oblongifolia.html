Derris oblongifolia

Derris oblongifolia, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal effects.

Derris oblongifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
