Dioscorea megacarpa

Dioscorea megacarpa, commonly known as the giant yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, swollen tubers as its primary storage organ. The plant's tubers are characterized by their thick, fleshy structure and can reach significant sizes, sometimes exceeding 30 centimeters in diameter. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

This species is valued for its edible tubers, which are a traditional food source in many parts of its native range.
