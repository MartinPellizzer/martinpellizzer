Dendrobium cyrilianum

Dendrobium cyrilianum, commonly known as the Cyrilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and often have a distinctive ridged appearance.

Dendrobium cyrilianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
