Dendrobium kauldorumii

Dendrobium kauldorumii, commonly known as the Kauld's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features long, cane-like stems that are often covered in dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its blooms are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dendrobium kauldorumii requires bright, indirect light and consistent moisture to thrive.
