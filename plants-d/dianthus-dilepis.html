Dianthus dilepis

Dianthus dilepis, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Dianthus dilepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
