Dalechampia hildebrandtii

Dalechampia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's euphorbia, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
