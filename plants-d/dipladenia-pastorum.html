Dipladenia pastorum

Dipladenia pastorum, commonly known as the orange-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Dipladenia pastorum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
