Diplostephium pachyphyllum

Diplostephium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved diplostephium, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in regions with low rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
