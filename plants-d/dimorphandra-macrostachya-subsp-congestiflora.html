Dimorphandra macrostachya subsp. congestiflora

Dimorphandra macrostachya subsp.

congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered-flowered dimorphandra, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant features a woody stem with a distinctive, often twisted trunk and dense, bipinnate foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in compact clusters, and it produces legume-like pods containing seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
