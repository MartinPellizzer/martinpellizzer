Dryopteris trichorhachis

Dryopteris trichorhachis, commonly known as the hairy wood fern, is a species of fern native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it easily identifiable.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
