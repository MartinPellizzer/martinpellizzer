Dianthus toletanus var. bolivaris

Dianthus toletanus var. bolivaris, commonly known as the Bolivian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits called capsules.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
