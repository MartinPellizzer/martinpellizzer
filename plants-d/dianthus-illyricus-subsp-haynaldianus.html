Dianthus illyricus subsp. haynaldianus

Dianthus illyricus subsp.

haynaldianus, commonly known as the Haynald's campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces striking pink to red flowers with five petals. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
