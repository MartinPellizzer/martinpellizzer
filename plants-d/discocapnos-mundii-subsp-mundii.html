Discocapnos mundii subsp. mundii

Discocapnos mundii subsp.

mundii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
