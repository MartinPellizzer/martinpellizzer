Diospyros longshengensis

Diospyros longshengensis, commonly known as the Longsheng persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit is a small, round persimmon that ripens in the fall and is edible when fully mature.

It is valued for its ornamental features and has limited use in traditional medicine.
