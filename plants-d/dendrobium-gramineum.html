Dendrobium gramineum

Dendrobium gramineum, commonly known as the grass orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, grass-like stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium gramineum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
