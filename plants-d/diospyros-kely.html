Diospyros kely

Diospyros kely, commonly known as the Kely date palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that resemble dates. The fruit has a thick, fibrous outer layer and a sweet, edible pulp.

Diospyros kely is valued for its ornamental value and its edible fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
