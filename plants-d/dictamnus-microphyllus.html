Dictamnus microphyllus

Dictamnus microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically less than an inch in length, giving it its species name "microphyllus.". The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its stems are often covered in a distinctive, aromatic resin that can emit a strong, spicy scent when broken.

This species is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in open woodlands or alpine meadows.
