Dodecatheon clevelandii var. alba

Dodecatheon clevelandii var. alba, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, globular flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-season blooms.
