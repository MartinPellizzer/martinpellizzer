Dianthus curticeps

Dianthus curticeps, commonly known as the hairy pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant produces small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus curticeps is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, delicate blooms.
