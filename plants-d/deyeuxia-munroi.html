Deyeuxia munroi

Deyeuxia munroi, commonly known as the narrowleaf false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, poorly drained soils. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deyeuxia munroi is often found in association with other wetland grasses and plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
