Dyssodia tephroleuca

Dyssodia tephroleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds.

Dyssodia tephroleuca is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
