Dendrobium undulatum

Dendrobium undulatum, commonly known as the wavy-leaf orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium undulatum thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
