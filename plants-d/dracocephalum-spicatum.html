Dracocephalum spicatum

Dracocephalum spicatum, commonly known as spiked skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Dracocephalum spicatum is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional use in herbal medicine.
