Dichanthium saccharoides subvar. laguroides

Dichanthium saccharoides subvar. laguroides, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable plant for erosion control in dry landscapes.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
