Demorchis appendiculata

Demorchis appendiculata, commonly known as the clustered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.
