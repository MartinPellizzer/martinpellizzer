Dactylis glomerata var. macrocephala

Dactylis glomerata var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed cocksfoot, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, large, spherical seed heads that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in grasslands, pastures, and disturbed sites. Its leaves are typically narrow and bluish-green, with a prominent midrib.

This variety is valued for its forage quality and its ability to withstand grazing and environmental stress.
