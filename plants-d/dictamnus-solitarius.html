Dictamnus solitarius

Dictamnus solitarius, commonly known as the solitary dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a distinctive, pungent scent when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Dictamnus solitarius thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
