Doronicum cavillieri

Doronicum cavillieri, commonly known as the alpine leopard's ear, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble a leopard's paw. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Doronicum cavillieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in harsh, high-altitude environments.
