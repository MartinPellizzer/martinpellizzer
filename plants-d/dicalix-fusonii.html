Dicalix fusonii

Dicalix fusonii, commonly known as the Malabar rock fig, is a rare and unique plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and a distinctive, swollen stem that stores water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically hidden within its dense foliage. Its fruit is a small, round fig-like structure that ripens to a yellowish color.
