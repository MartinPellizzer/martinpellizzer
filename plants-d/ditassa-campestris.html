Ditassa campestris

Ditassa campestris, commonly known as the field sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Ditassa campestris is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
