Dactylorhiza × paridaeniana

Dactylorhiza × paridaeniana, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, erect stems bearing clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is showy and erect.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained conditions.
