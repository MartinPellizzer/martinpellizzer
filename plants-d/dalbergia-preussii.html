Dalbergia preussii

Dalbergia preussii, commonly known as African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland ecosystems.

Due to its economic value, it faces threats from overharvesting and habitat loss.
