Dianthus serotinus f. dolomiticus

Dianthus serotinus f. dolomiticus, commonly known as the Dolomitic pinks, is a botanical variant of the common campion.

It is characterized by its pink to reddish flowers and is typically found in calcareous soils. This subspecies is native to regions with limestone-rich environments, such as the Dolomites in Italy. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms in late summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with well-drained soil.
