Dalbergia striata

Dalbergia striata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich, reddish-brown color and a distinctive grain pattern. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing flat, lens-shaped seeds.

Due to its high value for musical instruments and furniture, it is also considered an endangered species.
