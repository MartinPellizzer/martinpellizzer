Dichelachne hookeriana

Dichelachne hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's dichelachne, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both bisexual and stamenless florets. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas.

Dichelachne hookeriana is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to periodic waterlogging.
