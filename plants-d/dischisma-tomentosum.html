Dischisma tomentosum

Dischisma tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly dischisma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique texture and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among succulent enthusiasts.
