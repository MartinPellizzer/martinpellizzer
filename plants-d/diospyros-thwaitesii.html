Diospyros thwaitesii

Diospyros thwaitesii, commonly known as the Burmese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits.

Its fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed when fully ripe, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
