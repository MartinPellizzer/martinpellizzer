Diclidium odoratum

Diclidium odoratum, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers with a sweet fragrance.

Diclidium odoratum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and distinctive aromatic properties.
