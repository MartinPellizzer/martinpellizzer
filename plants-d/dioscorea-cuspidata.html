Dioscorea cuspidata

Dioscorea cuspidata, commonly known as the spurred yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cuisines and traditional medicine systems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
