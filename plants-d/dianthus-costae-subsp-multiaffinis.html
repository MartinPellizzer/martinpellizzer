Dianthus costae subsp. multiaffinis

Dianthus costae subsp.

multiaffinis, commonly known as the Sierra foothill campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The species belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

The plant is relatively low-growing, with flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
