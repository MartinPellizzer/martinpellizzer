Drepanostachyum kurzii

Drepanostachyum kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that form dense clumps, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
