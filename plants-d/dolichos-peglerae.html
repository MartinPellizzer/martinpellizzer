Dolichos peglerae

Dolichos peglerae, commonly known as the African horsebean, is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Dolichos peglerae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.

It is also valued for its nutritional content, as the seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.
