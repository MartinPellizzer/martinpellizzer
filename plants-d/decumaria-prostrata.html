Decumaria prostrata

Decumaria prostrata, commonly known as creeping beautyberry, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features attractive, showy clusters of violet-blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in shaded garden settings.
