Drypetes chevalieri

Drypetes chevalieri, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the dry season. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for reforestation and landscaping in tropical areas.
