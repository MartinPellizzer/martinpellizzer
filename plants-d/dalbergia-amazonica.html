Dalbergia amazonica

Dalbergia amazonica, commonly known as rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its rich color and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Dalbergia amazonica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
