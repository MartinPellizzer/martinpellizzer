Dinebra retroflexa var. brevifolia

Dinebra retroflexa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its woody stems and dense foliage make it well adapted to coastal and sandy soils.

It is often found in dunes and maritime hammocks, showcasing its resilience to salt spray and wind.
