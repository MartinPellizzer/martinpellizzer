Dorotheanthus criniflorus

Dorotheanthus criniflorus, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive crown-like appearance. The plant features bright yellow petals that surround a central cluster of stamens, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Dorotheanthus criniflorus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
