Draba ecuadoriana

Draba ecuadoriana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to high-altitude conditions. It has basal leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, aiding in water retention.

Draba ecuadoriana is an example of a species that thrives in nutrient-poor soils, demonstrating its resilience in harsh mountainous climates.
