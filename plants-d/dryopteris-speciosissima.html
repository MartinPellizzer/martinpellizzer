Dryopteris speciosissima

Dryopteris speciosissima, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bluish-green with a contrasting white or silver underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies.

The fronds are biennial, with the fiddleheads emerging in early spring and dying back in winter.
