Dicksonia baudouinii

Dicksonia baudouinii, commonly known as the African tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of spirally arranged fronds that give it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The fronds are used in traditional crafts and have cultural significance in some regions.
