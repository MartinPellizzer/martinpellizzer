Ditaxis diversiflora

Ditaxis diversiflora, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually white or pale pink in color.

The species is notable for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
