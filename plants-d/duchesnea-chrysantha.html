Duchesnea chrysantha

Duchesnea chrysantha, commonly known as golden groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features creeping stems that produce basal leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
