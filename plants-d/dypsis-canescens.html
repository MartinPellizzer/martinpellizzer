Dypsis canescens

Dypsis canescens, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its thick, ribbed trunk and sparse, feather-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
