Dioscorea sericea

Dioscorea sericea, commonly known as silky yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, sericeous stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also edible when properly prepared and have a starchy texture.

Dioscorea sericea is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its medicinal and nutritional properties.
