Dolichos karaviaensis

Dolichos karaviaensis, commonly known as the Karavia bean, is a climbing legume native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several oval seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated for both food and forage purposes.
