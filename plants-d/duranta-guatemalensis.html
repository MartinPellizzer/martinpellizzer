Duranta guatemalensis

Duranta guatemalensis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Duranta guatemalensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
