Dichorisandra musaica var. undata

Dichorisandra musaica var. undata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
