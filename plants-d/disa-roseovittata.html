Disa roseovittata

Disa roseovittata, commonly known as the rose-streaked orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing intricate flowers with a distinctive rose-colored stripe along the lip. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Disa roseovittata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
