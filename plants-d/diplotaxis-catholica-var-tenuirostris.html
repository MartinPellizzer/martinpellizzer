Diplotaxis catholica var. tenuirostris

Diplotaxis catholica var. tenuirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beaked wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in its natural wild environment.
