Deschampsia media var. iberica

Deschampsia media var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian bluegrass, is a grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
