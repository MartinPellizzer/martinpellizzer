Dais

Dais, commonly known as the daisy, are flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

They are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of a central disc surrounded by ray florets that resemble petals. Dais are typically herbaceous, with upright stems and alternate leaves that are often hairy or rough in texture. They thrive in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making them common in meadows and gardens.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, producing a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.
