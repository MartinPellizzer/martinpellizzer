Delphinium maackianum f. album

Delphinium maackianum f. album, commonly known as the white delphinium, is a hybrid cultivar of the delphinium genus.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers, which are typically arranged in tall, spires. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.
