Dodecatheon clevelandii var. insulare

Dodecatheon clevelandii var. insulare, commonly known as the Santa Cruz larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to coastal climates. It has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is considered rare and is protected due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
