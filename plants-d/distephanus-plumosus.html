Distephanus plumosus

Distephanus plumosus, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and shade-providing capabilities.
