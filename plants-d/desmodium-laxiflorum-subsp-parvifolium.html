Desmodium laxiflorum subsp. parvifolium

Desmodium laxiflorum subsp.

parvifolium, commonly known as small-leaved cowpea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to soil nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
