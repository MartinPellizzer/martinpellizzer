Dianthus tunicoides

Dianthus tunicoides, commonly known as the woolly campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of pink or red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus tunicoides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
