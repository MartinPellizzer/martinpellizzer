Dioscorea sylvatica var. paniculata

Dioscorea sylvatica var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled yam, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal use.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its nutritional content and traditional applications.
