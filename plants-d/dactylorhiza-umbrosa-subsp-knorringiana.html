Dactylorhiza umbrosa subsp. knorringiana

Dactylorhiza umbrosa subsp.

knorringiana, commonly known as the knorring's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a single spike of small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as bogs and wet meadows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized mycorrhizal associations that aid in nutrient uptake.
