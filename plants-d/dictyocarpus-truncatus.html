Dictyocarpus truncatus

Dictyocarpus truncatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant produces spores in small, clustered sporangia located on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and horticultural displays.
