Dioscorea proteiformis

Dioscorea proteiformis, commonly known as the variable yam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and the fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds.
