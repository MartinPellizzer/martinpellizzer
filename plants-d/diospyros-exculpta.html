Diospyros exculpta

Diospyros exculpta, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red, fleshy berries. Its bark is smooth and pale, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
