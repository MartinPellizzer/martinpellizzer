Daphne indica subsp. novae-caledoniae

Daphne indica subsp.

novae-caledoniae, commonly known as the New Caledonia daphne, is a species native to the island of New Caledonia. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange-red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
