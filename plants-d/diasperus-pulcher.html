Diasperus pulcher

Diasperus pulcher, commonly known as the handsome rockrose, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent, evergreen leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand drought conditions.
