Damrongia integra

Damrongia integra, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, white petals and a compact, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often epiphytic on trees. Its flowers bloom in clusters, emitting a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
