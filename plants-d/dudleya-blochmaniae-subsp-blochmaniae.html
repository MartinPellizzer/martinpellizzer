Dudleya blochmaniae subsp. blochmaniae

Dudleya blochmaniae subsp.

blochmaniae, commonly known as the Santa Barbara Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of California. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, adapted for water retention in arid environments.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
