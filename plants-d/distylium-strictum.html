Distylium strictum

Distylium strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces two types of flowers—short-styled and long-styled—facilitating self-incompatibility and cross-pollination. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of North America.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can impact the growth of its host plants, making it both ecologically significant and ecologically challenging.
