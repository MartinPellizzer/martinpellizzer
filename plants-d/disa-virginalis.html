Disa virginalis

Disa virginalis, commonly known as the white disa, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with a pinkish throat and greenish sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky areas and has a compact, cushion-like growth habit. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Disa virginalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
