Drosera spatulata

Drosera spatulata, commonly known as the spoon-leaf sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in sticky glands used to trap insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, typically from December to February. It thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands.

Drosera spatulata is a hardy species that can survive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
