Dypsis beentjei

Dypsis beentjei, commonly known as the Beentje's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive greenish-yellow color.

Dypsis beentjei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
