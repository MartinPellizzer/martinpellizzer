Deschampsia juncea

Deschampsia juncea, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
