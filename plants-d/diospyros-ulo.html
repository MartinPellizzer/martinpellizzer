Diospyros ulo

Diospyros ulo, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible drupes that range in color from yellow to reddish-brown. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or processed into various food products.

The plant is also notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for a variety of wildlife.
