Digitaria iburua

Digitaria iburua, commonly known as African ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Digitaria iburua is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
