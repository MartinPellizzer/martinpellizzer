Delphinium altissimum var. drepanocentrum

Delphinium altissimum var. drepanocentrum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its stem is often branched and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
