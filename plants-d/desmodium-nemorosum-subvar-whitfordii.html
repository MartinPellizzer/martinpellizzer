Desmodium nemorosum subvar. whitfordii

Desmodium nemorosum subvar. whitfordii, commonly known as the white-flowered burr vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
