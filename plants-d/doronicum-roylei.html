Doronicum roylei

Doronicum roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems.

Doronicum roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
