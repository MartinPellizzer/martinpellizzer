Drimys granadensis var. peruviana

Drimys granadensis var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian Chile tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates with high humidity and is often found in cloud forests.
