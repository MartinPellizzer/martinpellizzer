Diplopappus occidentalis

Diplopappus occidentalis, commonly known as the western burrweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that cling to animals and clothing, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are typically hairy and alternate, with a rough texture.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
