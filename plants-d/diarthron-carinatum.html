Diarthron carinatum

Diarthron carinatum, commonly known as the ridge grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often ribbed or ridged. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

Diarthron carinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
