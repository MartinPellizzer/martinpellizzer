Dracocephalum schischkinii

Dracocephalum schischkinii, commonly known as the Kashmir safflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
