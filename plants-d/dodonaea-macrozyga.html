Dodonaea macrozyga

Dodonaea macrozyga, commonly known as the broad-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Dodonaea macrozyga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
