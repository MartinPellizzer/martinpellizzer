Dioscorea bartlettii

Dioscorea bartlettii, commonly known as Bartlett's yam, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers, which are the edible part, are similar to other yam species and are used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
