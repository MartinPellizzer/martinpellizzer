Dracocephalum multicolor

Dracocephalum multicolor, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its aromatic foliage. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dracocephalum multicolor is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
