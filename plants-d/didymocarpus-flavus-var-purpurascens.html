Didymocarpus flavus var. purpurascens

Didymocarpus flavus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain several seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
