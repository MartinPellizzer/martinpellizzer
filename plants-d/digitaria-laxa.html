Digitaria laxa

Digitaria laxa, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a warm-season grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as fields and roadsides. It has a shallow root system and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various soil types.

Digitaria laxa is often found in disturbed sites and can be a common weed in agricultural settings.
