Dodonaea viscosa var. arborescens

Dodonaea viscosa var. arborescens, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and pairs of opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to sandy or coastal soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a common sight in coastal landscapes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
