Dolichos dissectus

Dolichos dissectus, commonly known as the dissected hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with finely dissected leaflets, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
