Disa gregoriana

Disa gregoriana, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are tubular and have a unique, intricate structure that facilitates pollination by specific insects.

Disa gregoriana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
