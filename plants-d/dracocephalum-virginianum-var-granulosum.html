Dracocephalum virginianum var. granulosum

Dracocephalum virginianum var. granulosum, commonly known as the roughhead germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is distinguished by its finely granular leaves, which give it its specific epithet "granulosum.".
