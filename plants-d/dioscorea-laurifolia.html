Dioscorea laurifolia

Dioscorea laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Dioscorea laurifolia thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
