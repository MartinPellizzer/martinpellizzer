Dolichos sesquipedalis

Dolichos sesquipedalis, commonly known as the longpod bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers and has compound leaves with several leaflets. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and agricultural studies.
