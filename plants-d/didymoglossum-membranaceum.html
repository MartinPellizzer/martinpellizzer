Didymoglossum membranaceum

Didymoglossum membranaceum, commonly known as the slender bog moss, is a small, mat-forming evergreen moss that thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and fens.

It has delicate, thread-like stems and leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The leaves are usually elongated and have a narrow, membranous appearance, which gives the plant its scientific name. It produces small, spore-bearing capsules that are clustered in dense groups near the tips of the stems.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and maintaining the hydrology of wetland ecosystems.
