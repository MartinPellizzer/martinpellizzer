Delphinium staphisagria subsp. pictum

Delphinium staphisagria subsp.

pictum, commonly known as the painted larkspur, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it a unique visual appeal.

The species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.
