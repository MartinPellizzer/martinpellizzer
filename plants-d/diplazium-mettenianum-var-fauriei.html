Diplazium mettenianum var. fauriei

Diplazium mettenianum var. fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's water fern, is a variant of the water fern family, which is part of the Osmundaceae family.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has a creeping rhizome with erect, compound fronds that are pinnately divided. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
