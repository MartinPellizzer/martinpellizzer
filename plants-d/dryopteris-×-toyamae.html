Dryopteris × toyamae

Dryopteris × toyamae, commonly known as the Toyama Shield Fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dryopteris affinis and Dryopteris japonica.

It is a medium-sized fern with fronds that are typically bipinnate, featuring a distinctive shield-shaped base. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with a robust and upright growth habit.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
