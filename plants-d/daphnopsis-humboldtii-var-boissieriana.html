Daphnopsis humboldtii var. boissieriana

Daphnopsis humboldtii var. boissieriana, commonly known as the Humboldt's daphnopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, with a distinctive aromatic scent. This variety is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
