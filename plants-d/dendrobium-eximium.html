Dendrobium eximium

Dendrobium eximium, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with erect, branching stems that often bear a dense covering of white or yellow flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of yellow and white, typically during the summer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding environment.

Dendrobium eximium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
