Dicranopteris peruviana

Dicranopteris peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its floating fronds that are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Dicranopteris peruviana plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
