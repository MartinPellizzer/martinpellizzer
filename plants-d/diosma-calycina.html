Diosma calycina

Diosma calycina, commonly known as the purple-flowered diosma, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Diosma calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
