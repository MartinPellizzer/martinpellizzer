Desmodium sericophyllum

Desmodium sericophyllum, commonly known as silky desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with silky, hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in racemes. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Desmodium sericophyllum is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in ecological succession.
