Dichopogon setosus

Dichopogon setosus, commonly known as the hairy dichopogon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the stems and nodes. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in spikelets, a common feature among grasses.

Dichopogon setosus is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.
