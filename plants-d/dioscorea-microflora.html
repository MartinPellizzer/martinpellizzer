Dioscorea microflora

Dioscorea microflora, commonly known as the dwarf yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

Dioscorea microflora is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
