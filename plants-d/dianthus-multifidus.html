Dianthus multifidus

Dianthus multifidus, commonly known as the fringed pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fringed petals that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Dianthus multifidus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
