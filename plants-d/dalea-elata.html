Dalea elata

Dalea elata, commonly known as the tall prairie clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

Dalea elata thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes, and it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
