Dactylorhiza × transiens subsp. corylensis

Dactylorhiza × transiens subsp.

corylensis, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and prefers acidic, nutrient-poor soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and a short, horizontal rhizome system.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable moisture conditions.
