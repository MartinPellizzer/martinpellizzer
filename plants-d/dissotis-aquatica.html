Dissotis aquatica

Dissotis aquatica, commonly known as the water spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged oppositely along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Dissotis aquatica plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
