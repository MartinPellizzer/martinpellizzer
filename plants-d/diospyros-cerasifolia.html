Diospyros cerasifolia

Diospyros cerasifolia, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with shallow fissures, and it is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
