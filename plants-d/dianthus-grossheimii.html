Dianthus grossheimii

Dianthus grossheimii, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with distinctive white or pink markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Dianthus grossheimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
