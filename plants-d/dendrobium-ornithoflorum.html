Dendrobium ornithoflorum

Dendrobium ornithoflorum, commonly known as the bird-flower orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often branching into clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, brightly colored blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium ornithoflorum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
