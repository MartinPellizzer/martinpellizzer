Dahlia variabilis

Dahlia variabilis, commonly known as the garden dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that come in a wide range of hues, including red, pink, orange, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with sturdy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically double or semi-double, with petals that are often ruffled or frilled.

Dahlia variabilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
