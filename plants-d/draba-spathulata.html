Draba spathulata

Draba spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically in clusters at the base of the leaves. It is native to rocky and alpine habitats in Europe and Asia, preferring well-drained soils.

Draba spathulata is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
