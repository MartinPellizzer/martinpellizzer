Drosera hookeri

Drosera hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a digestive enzyme to trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

Drosera hookeri is a perennial species that produces small, red or pink flowers during the summer months.
