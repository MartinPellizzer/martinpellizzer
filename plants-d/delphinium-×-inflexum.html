Delphinium × inflexum

Delphinium × inflexum, commonly known as the hybrid larkspur, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, adorned with spikes of blue, purple, or white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Delphinium × inflexum is valued in gardens for its showy blooms and is often used in perennial borders and cut flower arrangements.
