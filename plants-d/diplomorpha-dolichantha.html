Diplomorpha dolichantha

Diplomorpha dolichantha, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Diplomorpha dolichantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
