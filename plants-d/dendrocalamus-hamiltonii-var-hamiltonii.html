Dendrocalamus hamiltonii var. hamiltonii

Dendrocalamus hamiltonii var. hamiltonii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with rich, well-drained soil.

This bamboo species is valued for its strong, lightweight material, which is used in construction and traditional crafts.
