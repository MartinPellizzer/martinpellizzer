Dichaea pumila

Dichaea pumila, commonly known as the small-leaf dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the wet tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in small inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in greenhouse settings to ensure optimal growth conditions.
