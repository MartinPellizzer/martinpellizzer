Desmodium laevigatum

Desmodium laevigatum, commonly known as hairy burr legume, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods that are typically hairy and contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it a useful plant for soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
