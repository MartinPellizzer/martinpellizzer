Dolichos benadirianus

Dolichos benadirianus, commonly known as the Ethiopian bean, is a leguminous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in regions with limited water resources.

Its seeds are rich in protein and have been traditionally used as a food source in local communities.
