Dactylorhiza saccifera subsp. cartaliniae

Dactylorhiza saccifera subsp.

cartaliniae, commonly known as the alpine marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to alpine regions of Europe. It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower.

The plant has a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

It is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
