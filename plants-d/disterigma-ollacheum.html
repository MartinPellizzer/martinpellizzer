Disterigma ollacheum

Disterigma ollacheum, commonly known as the Ollacheum cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiky body.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
