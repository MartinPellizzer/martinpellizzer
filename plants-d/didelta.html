Didelta

Didelta, commonly known as the heartleaf or heartleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is a climbing vine that often grows along fences or trees, using its tendrils for support. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological systems.
