Digitalis aurea

Digitalis aurea, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tubular, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and are known for their striking color contrast against the green foliage.
