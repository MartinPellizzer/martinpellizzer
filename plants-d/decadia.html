Decadia

Decadia, commonly known as the "golden bell," is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known species like lilac and jasmine. The plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Decadia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
