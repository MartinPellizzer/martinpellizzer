Distimake grandiflorus

Distimake grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous beauty, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils. Distimake grandiflorus is often found in woodland edges and shaded gardens due to its preference for partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscapers and garden enthusiasts.
