Dictyopteris varians

Dictyopteris varians, commonly known as the variable sea palm, is a species of brown algae found in coastal areas of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has a distinctive, branching structure that resembles a tree, with a central stipe and multiple fronds. The plant is characterized by its leathery, dark green to brown blades that are often covered in a layer of mucilage. It thrives in shallow, rocky intertidal zones and is adapted to withstand wave action and desiccation.

Dictyopteris varians plays an important role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
