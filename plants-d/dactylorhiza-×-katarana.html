Dactylorhiza × katarana

Dactylorhiza × katarana, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike of flowers with purple or pink petals. It thrives in wet, boggy habitats and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing the natural genetic diversity within the Dactylorhiza genus.
