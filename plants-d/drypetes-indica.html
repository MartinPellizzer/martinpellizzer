Drypetes indica

Drypetes indica, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
