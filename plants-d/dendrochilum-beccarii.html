Dendrochilum beccarii

Dendrochilum beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

Dendrochilum beccarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral structure.
