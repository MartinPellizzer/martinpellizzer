Drymosiphon grandiflorus var. parviflorus

Drymosiphon grandiflorus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered grandiflorous vine, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are ovate with a slightly waxy texture and are often covered in fine hairs. This variant is native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.
