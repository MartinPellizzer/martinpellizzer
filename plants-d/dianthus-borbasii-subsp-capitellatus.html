Dianthus borbasii subsp. capitellatus

Dianthus borbasii subsp.

capitellatus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers arranged in a rounded inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
