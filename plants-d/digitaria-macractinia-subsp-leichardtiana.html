Digitaria macractinia subsp. leichardtiana

Digitaria macractinia subsp.

leichardtiana, commonly known as the Leichardt's Digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
