Dioscorea piperifolia var. apiculata

Dioscorea piperifolia var. apiculata, commonly known as the acorn yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits. Its tubers are valued for their starchy content and are used in traditional cuisines across its native range.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
