Dasylirion parryanum

Dasylirion parryanum, commonly known as the Mexican grassland agave, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces tall, flowering stalks that bear clusters of white or greenish flowers. It is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its structure and appearance make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in desert landscapes.
