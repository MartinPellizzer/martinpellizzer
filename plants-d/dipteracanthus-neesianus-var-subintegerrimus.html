Dipteracanthus neesianus var. subintegerrimus

Dipteracanthus neesianus var. subintegerrimus, commonly known as the New Guinea orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a deep violet or purple coloration with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its humid, shaded habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in the tropical environment.

This variant is notable for its unique floral structure and is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
