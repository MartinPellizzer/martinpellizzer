Diplachne malabarica

Diplachne malabarica, commonly known as Malabar grass, is a perennial grass native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Diplachne malabarica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
