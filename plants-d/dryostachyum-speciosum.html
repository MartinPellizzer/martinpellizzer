Dryostachyum speciosum

Dryostachyum speciosum, commonly known as the showy grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, panicle-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are often brightly colored, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
