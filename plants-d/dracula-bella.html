Dracula bella

Dracula bella, commonly known as the vampire orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals that resemble the appearance of a vampire's face, giving it its ominous name. The plant produces fragrant, purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high levels of shade, making it a challenging plant to cultivate.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Dracula bella is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
