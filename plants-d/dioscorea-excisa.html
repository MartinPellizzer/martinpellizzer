Dioscorea excisa

Dioscorea excisa, commonly known as the cutleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its growth habit involves tendrils that help it climb and spread along supports.

The tubers of Dioscorea excisa are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
