Diospyros chlamydocarpa

Diospyros chlamydocarpa, commonly known as the white persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that turn orange-red when mature. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
