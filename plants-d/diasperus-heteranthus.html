Diasperus heteranthus

Diasperus heteranthus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Diasperus heteranthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
