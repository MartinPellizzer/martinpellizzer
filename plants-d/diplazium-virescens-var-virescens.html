Diplazium virescens var. virescens

Diplazium virescens var. virescens, commonly known as the green fern, is a perennial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with dark green, bipinnate fronds that are glossy and lanceolate in shape. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Diplazium virescens var. virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
