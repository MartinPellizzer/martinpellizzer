Dalbergia javanica

Dalbergia javanica, commonly known as rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species.
