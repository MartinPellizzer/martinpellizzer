Drimia razii

Drimia razii, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the base of its grass-like foliage. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Drimia razii is also noted for its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
