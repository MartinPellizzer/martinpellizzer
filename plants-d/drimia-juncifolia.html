Drimia juncifolia

Drimia juncifolia, commonly known as the marsh daffodil, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale yellow blooms.

Drimia juncifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
