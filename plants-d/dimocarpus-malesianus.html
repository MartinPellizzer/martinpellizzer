Dimocarpus malesianus

Dimocarpus malesianus, commonly known as the Malay date plum, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits resembling dates in appearance. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.
