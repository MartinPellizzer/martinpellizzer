Daphne aquilaria

Daphne aquilaria, commonly known as the sweet box, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage and fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen leaves and showy blossoms.
