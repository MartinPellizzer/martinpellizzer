Dalea versicolor var. decipiens

Dalea versicolor var. decipiens, commonly known as the mimic dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful, pea-like flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a visually appealing species for native gardens.

The variety 'decipiens' is distinguished by its ability to mimic other plant species, a trait that may aid in its survival through camouflage or pollinator attraction.
