Darea tenera

Darea tenera, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, although it is not as widely cultivated for fruit as other date palms. It thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and botanical gardens.
