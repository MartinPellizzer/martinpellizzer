Dendrobium eustachyum

Dendrobium eustachyum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium eustachyum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
