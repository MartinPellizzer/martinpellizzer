Danaea cuspidata

Danaea cuspidata, commonly known as the scarlet flame or crimson fireplant, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Danaea cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
