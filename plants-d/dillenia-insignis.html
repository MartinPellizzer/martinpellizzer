Dillenia insignis

Dillenia insignis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It features showy, clustered inflorescences that range from pink to violet in color, attracting various pollinators. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for humans and animals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Dillenia insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
