Diospyros portus

Diospyros portus, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor.

Diospyros portus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
