Delphinium taliense

Delphinium taliense, commonly known as the Taliense larkspur, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, typical of the Delphinium genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
