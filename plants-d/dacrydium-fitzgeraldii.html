Dacrydium fitzgeraldii

Dacrydium fitzgeraldii, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's silver pine, is a species of coniferous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen foliage and distinctive, flattened, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool and moist climates. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
