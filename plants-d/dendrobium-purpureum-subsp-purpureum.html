Dendrobium purpureum subsp. purpureum

Dendrobium purpureum subsp.

purpureum, commonly known as the purple dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia. It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in two ranks, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
