Derris elegans var. korthalsiana

Derris elegans var. korthalsiana, commonly known as the Korthals's derris, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces elongated, slender pods containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural insecticides.
