Drepanostachyum falcatum

Drepanostachyum falcatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass or flatleaf bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching leaves that are typically green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
