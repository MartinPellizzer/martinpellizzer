Dyckia limae

Dyckia limae, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple plant, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are stiff and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking, red-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Dyckia limae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
