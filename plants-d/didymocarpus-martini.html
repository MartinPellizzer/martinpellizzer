Didymocarpus martini

Didymocarpus martini, commonly known as the red-flowered flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Didymocarpus martini is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
