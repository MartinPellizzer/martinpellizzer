Dichorexia gigantea

Dichorexia gigantea, commonly known as the giant dichorexia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
