Diplazium melanolepis

Diplazium melanolepis, commonly known as the black-stemmed water fern, is a member of the Marantaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rhizomatous growth and delicate, pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually dark green with a slightly glossy appearance.
