Diospyros domingensis

Diospyros domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony trees known for their dense, dark wood. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood, which is used in the production of high-quality furniture and musical instruments.
