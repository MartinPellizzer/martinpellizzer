Dioscorea demeusei

Dioscorea demeusei, commonly known as the Congo yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are often used as a staple food in local diets and have been studied for their potential health benefits.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds that require careful processing before consumption.
