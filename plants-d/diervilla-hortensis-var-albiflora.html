Diervilla hortensis var. albiflora

Diervilla hortensis var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in small, sticky hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
