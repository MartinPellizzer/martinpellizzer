Dryopteris antarctica

Dryopteris antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the cold climates of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it survive in harsh, frost-prone environments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, a feature typical of ferns, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subantarctic regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in cool temperate gardens as an ornamental plant.
