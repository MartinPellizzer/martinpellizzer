Delphinium sinoelatum

Delphinium sinoelatum, commonly known as the Chinese larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium sinoelatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
