Dianthus cintranus subsp. atrosanguineus

Dianthus cintranus subsp.

atrosanguineus, commonly known as the dark blood carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking dark red to almost black petals, which contrast with its foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are borne on short stems and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
