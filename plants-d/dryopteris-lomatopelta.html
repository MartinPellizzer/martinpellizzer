Dryopteris lomatopelta

Dryopteris lomatopelta, commonly known as the fringed wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, frond-like leaves that are often described as lacy or delicate in appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in compact, fiddlehead-like structures.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
