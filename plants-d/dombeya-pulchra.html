Dombeya pulchra

Dombeya pulchra, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Dombeya pulchra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
