Danais antilahimenae

Danais antilahimenae, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy blooms that often feature a mix of colors, including white, purple, and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi for nutrient uptake. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it thrives in areas with high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Due to habitat loss, this rare species is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
