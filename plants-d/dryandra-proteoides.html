Dryandra proteoides

Dryandra proteoides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Dryandra proteoides is also recognized as the state floral emblem of Western Australia.
