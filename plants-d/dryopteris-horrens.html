Dryopteris horrens

Dryopteris horrens, commonly known as the creeping wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that gives the plant a refined aesthetic. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in horticultural settings.
