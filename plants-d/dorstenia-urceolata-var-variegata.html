Dorstenia urceolata var. variegata

Dorstenia urceolata var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated spoon-leaf plant, is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, while the plant produces unique, urn-shaped fruits.

Due to its striking foliage, it is popular as a houseplant, though it requires careful attention to light and humidity levels to flourish.
