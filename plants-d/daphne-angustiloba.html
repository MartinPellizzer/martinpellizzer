Daphne angustiloba

Daphne angustiloba, commonly known as narrow-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Daphne angustiloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive aromatic foliage.
