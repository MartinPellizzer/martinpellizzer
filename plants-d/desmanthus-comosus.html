Desmanthus comosus

Desmanthus comosus, commonly known as the hairy desmanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
