Dianthus giganteus subsp. italicus

Dianthus giganteus subsp.

italicus, commonly known as the Italian carnation, is a flowering plant characterized by its showy, clustered blooms. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant features upright, branching stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and prominent white veins.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
