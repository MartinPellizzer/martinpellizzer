Distictis rigescens

Distictis rigescens, commonly known as the rigid distictis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
