Discocactus zehntneri f. albispinus

Discocactus zehntneri f. albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined discocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, spiny tubercles that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate form. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its rarity and unique features, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts of succulent and cactus plants.
