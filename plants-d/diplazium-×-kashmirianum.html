Diplazium × kashmirianum

Diplazium × kashmirianum, commonly known as the Kashmir fern, is a hybrid fern species that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Diplazium esculentum and Diplazium sylvestre.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions of the Himalayas. The plant features bipinnate fronds with a distinctive, upright growth habit, and its leaves are usually dark green and glossy. Diplazium × kashmirianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various small organisms.
