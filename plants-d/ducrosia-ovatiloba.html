Ducrosia ovatiloba

Ducrosia ovatiloba, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often mistaken for those of grasses or sedges. Ducrosia ovatiloba thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
