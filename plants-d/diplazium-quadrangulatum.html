Diplazium quadrangulatum

Diplazium quadrangulatum, commonly known as the four-cornered water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboidal (four-cornered) fronds, which give it its scientific name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a smooth, waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Diplazium quadrangulatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
