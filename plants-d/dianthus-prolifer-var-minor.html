Dianthus prolifer var. minor

Dianthus prolifer var. minor, commonly known as the lesser thrift, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of bright pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
