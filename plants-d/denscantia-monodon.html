Denscantia monodon

Denscantia monodon, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. The flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or purple, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
