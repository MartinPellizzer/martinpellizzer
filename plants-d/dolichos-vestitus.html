Dolichos vestitus

Dolichos vestitus, commonly known as the hairy horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, slender pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in crop rotation systems.
