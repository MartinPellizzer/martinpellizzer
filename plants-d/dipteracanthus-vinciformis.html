Dipteracanthus vinciformis

Dipteracanthus vinciformis, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
