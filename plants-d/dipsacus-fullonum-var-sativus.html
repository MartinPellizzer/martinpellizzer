Dipsacus fullonum var. sativus

Dipsacus fullonum var. sativus, commonly known as teasel, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces thistle-like purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique bracts, which are modified leaves, are used in the textile industry for raising the nap of wool.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
