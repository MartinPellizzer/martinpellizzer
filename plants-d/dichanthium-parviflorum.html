Dichanthium parviflorum

Dichanthium parviflorum, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Dichanthium parviflorum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
