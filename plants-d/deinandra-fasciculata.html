Deinandra fasciculata

Deinandra fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Deinandra fasciculata is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
