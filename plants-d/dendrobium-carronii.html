Dendrobium carronii

Dendrobium carronii, commonly known as the Carronii orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, Dendrobium carronii is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
