Dioscorea batatas f. clavata

Dioscorea batatas f. clavata, commonly known as the sweet yam, is a tuberous plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy tubers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces compound leaves with long petioles and small, opposite leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained, sandy soils.

The tubers are not only a food source but also have traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
