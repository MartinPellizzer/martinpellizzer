Dysoxylum coriaceum

Dysoxylum coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-skin tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its rough, bark-like texture and elliptical leaves with a leathery feel. The plant is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in various cultures.
