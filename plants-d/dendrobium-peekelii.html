Dendrobium peekelii

Dendrobium peekelii, commonly known as the Peckel's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a fibrous root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base of the stem.

Dendrobium peekelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
