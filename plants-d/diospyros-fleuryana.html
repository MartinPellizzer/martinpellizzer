Diospyros fleuryana

Diospyros fleuryana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
