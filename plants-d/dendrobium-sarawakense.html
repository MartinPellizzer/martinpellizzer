Dendrobium sarawakense

Dendrobium sarawakense, commonly known as the Sarawak orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, cane-like pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive red lip, blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in high humidity and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Dendrobium sarawakense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
