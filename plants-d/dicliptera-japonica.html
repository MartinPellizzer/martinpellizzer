Dicliptera japonica

Dicliptera japonica, commonly known as Japanese bristlegrass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

Dicliptera japonica produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late summer.
