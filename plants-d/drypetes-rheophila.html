Drypetes rheophila

Drypetes rheophila, commonly known as the streamside drypetes, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically along stream banks and in lowland forests. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish or greenish hue.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
