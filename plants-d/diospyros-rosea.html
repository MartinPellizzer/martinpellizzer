Diospyros rosea

Diospyros rosea, commonly known as the rose-apple tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
