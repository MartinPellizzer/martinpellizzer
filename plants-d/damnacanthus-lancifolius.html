Damnacanthus lancifolius

Damnacanthus lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devil's needle.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and striking, spiky red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its visual appeal in both gardens and botanical collections.
