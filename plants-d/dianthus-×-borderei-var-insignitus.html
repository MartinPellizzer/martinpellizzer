Dianthus × borderei var. insignitus

Dianthus × borderei var. insignitus, commonly known as the pink campion, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in dense, showy clusters atop slender stems. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. It thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its hybrid origin, combining species from the Dianthus genus.
