Diapedium resupinatum

Diapedium resupinatum, commonly known as the hanging bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems that often hang from submerged vegetation. The plant produces tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. Its leaves are usually reduced to scale-like structures, and it lacks true roots.

Diapedium resupinatum thrives in still or slow-moving waters with abundant sunlight.
