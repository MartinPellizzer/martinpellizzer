Dombeya hamiltoniana

Dombeya hamiltoniana, commonly known as the Hamilton's dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Dombeya hamiltoniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to well-drained soils.
