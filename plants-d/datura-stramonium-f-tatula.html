Datura stramonium f. tatula

Datura stramonium f. tatula, commonly known as the Turkish angel's trumpet, is a variant of the common jimsonweed.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the plant produces spiny capsules containing toxic seeds.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its hallucinogenic properties due to the presence of tropane alkaloids.
