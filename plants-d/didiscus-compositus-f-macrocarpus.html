Didiscus compositus f. macrocarpus

Didiscus compositus f. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited didiscus, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers and robust growth habit.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to South Africa. The plant features a woody stem with alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk, and the fruit is a large, inflated achene that aids in wind dispersal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
