Dactylis polygama var. vuyckii

Dactylis polygama var. vuyckii, commonly known as the Vuyck's dactylis, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green flowers. It is often found in dry, calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in ecological restoration projects.
