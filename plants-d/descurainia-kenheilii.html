Descurainia kenheilii

Descurainia kenheilii, commonly known as the Kenheil's tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and pinnately divided leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Native to arid regions of Central Asia, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
