Delphinium albocoeruleum var. albocoeruleum

Delphinium albocoeruleum var. albocoeruleum, commonly known as the white-blue delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of white and blue. The flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stem, with petals that are narrow and spurred at the base. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
