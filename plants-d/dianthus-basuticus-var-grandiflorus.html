Dianthus basuticus var. grandiflorus

Dianthus basuticus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Basutu carnation, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive, frilled appearance. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding a striking visual element to gardens.
