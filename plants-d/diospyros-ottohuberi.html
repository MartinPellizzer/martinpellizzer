Diospyros ottohuberi

Diospyros ottohuberi, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and forest edge habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
