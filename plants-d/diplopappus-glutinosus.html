Diplopappus glutinosus

Diplopappus glutinosus, commonly known as the sticky false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, fleshy leaves that are often sticky to the touch. The plant produces a tall, branching stem topped with a cluster of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. After flowering, it develops a cluster of small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Its sticky leaves and unique floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
