Diospyros ramisonii

Diospyros ramisonii, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
