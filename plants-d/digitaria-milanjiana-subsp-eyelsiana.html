Digitaria milanjiana subsp. eyelsiana

Digitaria milanjiana subsp.

eyelsiana, commonly known as the Eyels' Digitaria, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
