Dianthus strictus var. troodi

Dianthus strictus var. troodi, commonly known as the Troodi campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The leaves are linear and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
