Diplusodon buxifolius

Diplusodon buxifolius, commonly known as the boxleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Diplusodon buxifolius is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid and semi-arid regions.
