Derris benthamii var. benthamii

Derris benthamii var. benthamii, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties. It typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, often in well-drained soils.
