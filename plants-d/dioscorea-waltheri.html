Dioscorea waltheri

Dioscorea waltheri, commonly known as the Walther's yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, while its flowers are typically white or greenish-yellow.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
