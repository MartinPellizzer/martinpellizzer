Delphinium vvedenskyi

Delphinium vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Vvedensky delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium vvedenskyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
