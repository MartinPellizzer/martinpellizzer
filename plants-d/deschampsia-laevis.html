Deschampsia laevis

Deschampsia laevis, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate blue-green flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Deschampsia laevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
