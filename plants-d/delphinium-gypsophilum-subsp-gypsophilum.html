Delphinium gypsophilum subsp. gypsophilum

Delphinium gypsophilum subsp.

gypsophilum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically spurred at the base.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate beauty, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
