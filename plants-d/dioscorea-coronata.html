Dioscorea coronata

Dioscorea coronata, commonly known as the crownleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a support structure for its vine growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
