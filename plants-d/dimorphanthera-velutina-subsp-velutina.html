Dimorphanthera velutina subsp. velutina

Dimorphanthera velutina subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia.

The plant is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
