Deparia wilsonii var. maxima

Deparia wilsonii var. maxima, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a striking species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent under certain conditions, adding to its visual appeal.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
