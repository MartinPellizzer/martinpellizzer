Dianthus orientalis var. angulatus

Dianthus orientalis var. angulatus, commonly known as the angular carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, with petals that are narrow and pointed, giving the plant its common name.

Dianthus orientalis var. angulatus is well-suited for rock gardens and borders, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
