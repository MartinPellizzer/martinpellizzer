Desmoncus duidensis

Desmoncus duidensis, commonly known as the Duiden's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are typically small and pale, often displaying a delicate, almost ethereal appearance.

Due to its restricted habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, Desmoncus duidensis is considered a critically endangered species.
