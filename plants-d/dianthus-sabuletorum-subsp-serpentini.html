Dianthus sabuletorum subsp. serpentini

Dianthus sabuletorum subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's tail or snake's head, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and distinctive, clustered flowers that range from pink to red.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
