Diospyros foeminea

Diospyros foeminea, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are edible when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The wood of Diospyros foeminea is hard and durable, making it valuable for carving and furniture making.
