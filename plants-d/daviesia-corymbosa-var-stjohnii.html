Daviesia corymbosa var. stjohnii

Daviesia corymbosa var. stjohnii, commonly known as the St John's Daviesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to cream, with prominent, hairy sepals that give it a unique appearance.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
