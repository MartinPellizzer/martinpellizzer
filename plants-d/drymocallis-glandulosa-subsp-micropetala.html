Drymocallis glandulosa subsp. micropetala

Drymocallis glandulosa subsp.

micropetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered with glandular dots, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
