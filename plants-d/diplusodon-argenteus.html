Diplusodon argenteus

Diplusodon argenteus, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, waxy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Diplusodon argenteus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
