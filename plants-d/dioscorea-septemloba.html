Dioscorea septemloba

Dioscorea septemloba, commonly known as the seven-lobed yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and produces yellowish to orange flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant's tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines and medicine. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with ample sunlight.

Dioscorea septemloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
