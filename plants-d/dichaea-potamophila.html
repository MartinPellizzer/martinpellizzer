Dichaea potamophila

Dichaea potamophila, commonly known as the river moss, is a species of orchid that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and intricate floral structures. This plant is typically found growing along riverbanks and in wetlands, where it benefits from the consistent humidity. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Dichaea potamophila is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
