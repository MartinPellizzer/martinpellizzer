Dipteracanthus elongatus

Dipteracanthus elongatus, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
