Delphinium scopulorum var. subalpinum

Delphinium scopulorum var. subalpinum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
