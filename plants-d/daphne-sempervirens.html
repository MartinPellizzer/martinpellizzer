Daphne sempervirens

Daphne sempervirens, commonly known as evergreen daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the shrub throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling.
