Dillenia apetala

Dillenia apetala, commonly known as the Mexican dillenia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, which are typically arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Dillenia apetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
