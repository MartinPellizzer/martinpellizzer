Dimerocostus strobilaceus subsp. appendiculatus

Dimerocostus strobilaceus subsp.

appendiculatus, commonly known as the appendiculate dimerocostus, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are typically borne on leafy shoots. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
