Dracocephalum denticulatum

Dracocephalum denticulatum, commonly known as toothed skullcap, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, blue to purple flowers clustered in loose spikes. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and resilience.
