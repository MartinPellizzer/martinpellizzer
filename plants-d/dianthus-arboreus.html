Dianthus arboreus

Dianthus arboreus, commonly known as the tree carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy clusters of pink, red, or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, making it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers have five petals with a distinctive ruffled edge.

Dianthus arboreus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
