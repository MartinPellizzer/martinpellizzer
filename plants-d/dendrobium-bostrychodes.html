Dendrobium bostrychodes

Dendrobium bostrychodes, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that resemble torches. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, attaching itself to trees or rocks. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Dendrobium bostrychodes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
