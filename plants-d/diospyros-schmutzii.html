Diospyros schmutzii

Diospyros schmutzii, commonly known as the Schmutzii persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a drupe, typically orange to red in color when mature, and is edible when fully ripe.

Diospyros schmutzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
