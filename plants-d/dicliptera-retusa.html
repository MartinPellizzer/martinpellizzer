Dicliptera retusa

Dicliptera retusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense clump. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Dicliptera retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
