Dianthus serrulatus subsp. serrulatus

Dianthus serrulatus subsp.

serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial with lance-shaped leaves and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is characterized by its finely serrated leaf margins and showy, clustered flowers.
