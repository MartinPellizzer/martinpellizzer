Desmodium floridanum

Desmodium floridanum, commonly known as Florida burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers, followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a slightly hairy surface.

Desmodium floridanum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
