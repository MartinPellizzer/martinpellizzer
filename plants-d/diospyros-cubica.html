Diospyros cubica

Diospyros cubica, commonly known as the Cuban persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
