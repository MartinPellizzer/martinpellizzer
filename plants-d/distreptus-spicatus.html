Distreptus spicatus

Distreptus spicatus, commonly known as the spiky distreptus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. Native to regions of southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance and resilience.
