Drimia pauciflora

Drimia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered dragon's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in summer, arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Drimia pauciflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
