Dasylepis racemosa

Dasylepis racemosa, commonly known as the feathered cycad, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
