Dendrobium stenophytoides

Dendrobium stenophytoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dendrobium, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink with a fragrant scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Dendrobium stenophytoides is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral display.
