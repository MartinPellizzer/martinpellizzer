Draba astyla

Draba astyla, commonly known as the narrowleaf whitlow grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters at the top of the stems.

Draba astyla is native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils.
