Dioscorea pedicellata

Dioscorea pedicellata, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with elongated leaflets. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Dioscorea pedicellata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
