Draba hispanica f. longistyla

Draba hispanica f. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The unique feature of this form is the elongated stamen, which gives it its specific epithet "longistyla.".
