Dactylis × intercedens f. congesta

Dactylis × intercedens f. congesta, commonly known as the dense sweet vernalgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various regions for its ornamental and forage value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component in ecological restoration projects.
