Dichrostachys cinerea var. lugardiae

Dichrostachys cinerea var. lugardiae, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and distinctive, spiny fruit capsules that contain several seeds. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a common pioneer species in disturbed areas.

The variety lugardiae is distinguished by its slightly different leaf and fruit morphology compared to the typical form.
