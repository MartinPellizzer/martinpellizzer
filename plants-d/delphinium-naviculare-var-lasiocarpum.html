Delphinium naviculare var. lasiocarpum

Delphinium naviculare var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly-seeded larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods that are characteristic of the species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
