Delphinium menziesii subsp. ochroleucum

Delphinium menziesii subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in raceme inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinctive white or pale green coloration on the underside.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
