Delphinium amplibracteatum

Delphinium amplibracteatum, commonly known as the broad-bract delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which are surrounded by large, leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Delphinium amplibracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
