Diospyros lycioides subsp. guerkei

Diospyros lycioides subsp.

guerkei, commonly known as the Guerke's persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like ebony and persimmon.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible berries that ripen to a reddish-purple color.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and foliage.
