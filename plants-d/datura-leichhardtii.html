Datura leichhardtii

Datura leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt's angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Its rapid growth and striking appearance make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
