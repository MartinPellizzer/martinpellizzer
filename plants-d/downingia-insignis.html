Downingia insignis

Downingia insignis, commonly known as the Sierra glacierweed, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky, alpine environments with cool, moist conditions. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.

Downingia insignis is an important component of alpine ecosystems and is often found in meadows and talus slopes.
