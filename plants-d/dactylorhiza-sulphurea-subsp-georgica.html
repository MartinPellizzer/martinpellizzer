Dactylorhiza sulphurea subsp. georgica

Dactylorhiza sulphurea subsp.

georgica, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to western Europe. It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has a single, erect stem that bears several showy, yellowish-orange flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
