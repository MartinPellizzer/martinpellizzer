Delphinium tomentellum var. angustibracteatum

Delphinium tomentellum var. angustibracteatum, commonly known as the narrow-bracted larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a height ranging from 1 to 3 feet.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
