Draba murrayi

Draba murrayi, commonly known as Murray's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a small annual herb with a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, alpine environments.

Draba murrayi is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds.
