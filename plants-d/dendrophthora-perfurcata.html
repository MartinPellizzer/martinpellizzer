Dendrophthora perfurcata

Dendrophthora perfurcata, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its typically arid environment. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by berry-like fruits containing seeds. Its roots penetrate the host tree's vascular system to extract water and nutrients, while also forming a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi.

This species plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.
