Ducrosia anethifolia

Ducrosia anethifolia, commonly known as the sweet cicely, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces aromatic seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Ducrosia anethifolia thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
