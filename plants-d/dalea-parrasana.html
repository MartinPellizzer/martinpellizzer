Dalea parrasana

Dalea parrasana, commonly known as the Parrasan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptation to arid environments. Its small, pale yellow to white flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dalea parrasana plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
