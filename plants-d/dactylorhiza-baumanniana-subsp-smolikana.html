Dactylorhiza baumanniana subsp. smolikana

Dactylorhiza baumanniana subsp.

smolikana, commonly known as the Smolik's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in wet, calcareous grasslands and marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant produces tall, slender stems with a single, showy spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
