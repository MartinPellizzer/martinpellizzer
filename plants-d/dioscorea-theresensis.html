Dioscorea theresensis

Dioscorea theresensis, commonly known as the Brazilian yam, is a tuberous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit, with hairy, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers, which are the edible part, are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
