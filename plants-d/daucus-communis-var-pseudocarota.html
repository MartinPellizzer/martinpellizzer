Daucus communis var. pseudocarota

Daucus communis var. pseudocarota, commonly known as the common carrot, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbel-shaped flowers and parsley-like leaves. The plant is cultivated primarily for its edible taproot, which develops into the familiar orange-colored root vegetable. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
