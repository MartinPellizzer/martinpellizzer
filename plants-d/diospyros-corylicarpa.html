Diospyros corylicarpa

Diospyros corylicarpa, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is rich in sugars and has been traditionally used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and toolmaking.
