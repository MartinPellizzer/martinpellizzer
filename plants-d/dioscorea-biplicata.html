Dioscorea biplicata

Dioscorea biplicata, commonly known as the crested yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, folded leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and develops tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The tubers of Dioscorea biplicata are sometimes cultivated for their potential medicinal properties and nutritional value.
