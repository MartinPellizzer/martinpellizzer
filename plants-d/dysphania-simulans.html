Dysphania simulans

Dysphania simulans, commonly known as false lamb's quarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, branched clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Dysphania simulans thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

It is a hardy species that can tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
