Daphne × ambrosiaca

Daphne × ambrosiaca, commonly known as winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that provide ornamental value throughout the year. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Daphne × ambrosiaca is noted for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation and should be handled with care.
