Dactylanthes globosa

Dactylanthes globosa, commonly known as the globular lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular, sparsely haired flower head that resembles a small globe. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dactylanthes globosa is often found in open areas such as grasslands and desert scrub, where it thrives in full sun.
