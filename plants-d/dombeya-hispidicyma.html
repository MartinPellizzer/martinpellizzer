Dombeya hispidicyma

Dombeya hispidicyma, commonly known as the roughleaf dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dombeya hispidicyma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
