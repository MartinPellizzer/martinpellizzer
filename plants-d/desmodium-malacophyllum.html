Desmodium malacophyllum

Desmodium malacophyllum, commonly known as the velvetleaf desmodium, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its pods are elongated and contain several elliptical seeds.

Desmodium malacophyllum is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
