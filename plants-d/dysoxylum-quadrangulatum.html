Dysoxylum quadrangulatum

Dysoxylum quadrangulatum, commonly known as the four-angled dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, quadrangular stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
