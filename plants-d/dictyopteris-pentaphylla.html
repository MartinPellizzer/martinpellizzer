Dictyopteris pentaphylla

Dictyopteris pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed sea mat, is a marine brown alga belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are typically divided into five pairs of lobes, giving it its common name. The plant grows in shallow coastal waters, often forming dense underwater mats on rocky substrates. Its reproductive structures, including both male and female gametophytes, are embedded within the thallus.

The species plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
