Dichanthelium praecocius

Dichanthelium praecocius, commonly known as early dichanthelium, is a flowering grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in disturbed soils. Its flowering period occurs in the early part of the growing season, hence its common name.

Dichanthelium praecocius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
