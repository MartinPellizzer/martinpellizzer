Desmostachys planchonianus f. intermedius

Desmostachys planchonianus f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate planchonian daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and displays clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
