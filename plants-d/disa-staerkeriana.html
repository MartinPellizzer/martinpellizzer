Disa staerkeriana

Disa staerkeriana, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Disa staerkeriana thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
