Drymonia brevipes

Drymonia brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed fern.

It is a rare and endangered species native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and Florida. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, feather-like fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in pine flatwoods and scrub habitats.

The plant's unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
