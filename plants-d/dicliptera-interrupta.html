Dicliptera interrupta

Dicliptera interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted dicliptera, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along water bodies. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered inflorescences, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Dicliptera interrupta is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
