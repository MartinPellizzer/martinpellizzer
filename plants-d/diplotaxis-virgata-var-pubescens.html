Diplotaxis virgata var. pubescens

Diplotaxis virgata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy golden burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, spiny seed pods that mature in the dry season. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

This species is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
