Dalbergia calycina

Dalbergia calycina, commonly known as African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood with a high density and fine grain. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for conservation efforts.
