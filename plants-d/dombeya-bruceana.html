Dombeya bruceana

Dombeya bruceana, commonly known as Bruce's dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark brown fruits that contain several seeds. Dombeya bruceana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
