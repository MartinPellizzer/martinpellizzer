Delphinium taxkorganense

Delphinium taxkorganense, commonly known as the Taxkorgan larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pamir Mountains in China.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
