Dioscorea japonica var. formosensis

Dioscorea japonica var. formosensis, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
