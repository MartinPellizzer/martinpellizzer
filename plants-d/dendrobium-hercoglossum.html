Dendrobium hercoglossum

Dendrobium hercoglossum, commonly known as the orchid with the "tongue-like" lip, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium hercoglossum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
