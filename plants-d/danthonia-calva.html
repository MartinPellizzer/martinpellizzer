Danthonia calva

Danthonia calva, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and heathlands.

Danthonia calva is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
