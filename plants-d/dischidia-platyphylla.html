Dischidia platyphylla

Dischidia platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf fig.

It is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden among the foliage. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its natural habitat.

Dischidia platyphylla is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
