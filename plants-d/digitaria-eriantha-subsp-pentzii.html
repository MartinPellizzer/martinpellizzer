Digitaria eriantha subsp. pentzii

Digitaria eriantha subsp.

pentzii, commonly known as Panicum pentzii, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in panicles, which are often adorned with short, bristly hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
