Diastema picta

Diastema picta, commonly known as the painted diastema, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are often more showy than its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and can vary in color from green to red or purple, depending on environmental conditions.

Diastema picta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
