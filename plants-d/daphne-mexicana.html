Daphne mexicana

Daphne mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Daphne mexicana is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
