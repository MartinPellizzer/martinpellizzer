Dalbergaria crassa

Dalbergaria crassa, commonly known as the rough bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a woody stem with rough, fissured bark and produces small, pod-like fruits.

Dalbergaria crassa is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature.
