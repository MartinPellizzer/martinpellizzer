Druparia mahaleb

Druparia mahaleb, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Druparia mahaleb thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
