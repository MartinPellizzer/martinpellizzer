Daphne mucronata subsp. mucronata

Daphne mucronata subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the African daphne, is a member of the Thymelaeaceae family. It is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a distinctive addition to horticultural collections.
