Doemia cordifolia

Doemia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, daisy-like white flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in rock gardens due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

Doemia cordifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
