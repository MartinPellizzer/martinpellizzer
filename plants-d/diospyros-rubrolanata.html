Diospyros rubrolanata

Diospyros rubrolanata, commonly known as the red-haired ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, red-hued pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically dark purple when mature. Its wood is highly valued for its density and durability, making it important in local economies.

The species is also noted for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
