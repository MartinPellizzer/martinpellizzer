Dinebra panicea subsp. panicea

Dinebra panicea subsp.

panicea, commonly known as the panicled flax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and panicles of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
