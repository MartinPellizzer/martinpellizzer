Diuris picta

Diuris picta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from red to purple and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and heathlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers.

Diuris picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and vibrant blooms.
