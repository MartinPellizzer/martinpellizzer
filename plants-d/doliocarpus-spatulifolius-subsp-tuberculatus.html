Doliocarpus spatulifolius subsp. tuberculatus

Doliocarpus spatulifolius subsp.

tuberculatus, commonly known as the tuberculate sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing smooth, oval seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The subspecies is notable for its unique tuberculate (bumpy) texture on its leaves and bark.
