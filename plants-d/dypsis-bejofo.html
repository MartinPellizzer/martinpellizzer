Dypsis bejofo

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant features a rosette of spiny, paddle-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by edible fruits.

Dypsis bejofo is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
