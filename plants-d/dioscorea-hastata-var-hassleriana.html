Dioscorea hastata var. hassleriana

Dioscorea hastata var. hassleriana, commonly known as the purple yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers followed by inedible, spiny fruits. Its tubers, which are the primary edible part, are typically purple or red in color and have a starchy, sweet flavor.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
