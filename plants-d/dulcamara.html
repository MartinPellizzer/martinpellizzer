Dulcamara

Dulcamara, commonly known as wild tobacco, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features heart-shaped, alternate leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Dulcamara is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
