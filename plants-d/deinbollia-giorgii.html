Deinbollia giorgii

Deinbollia giorgii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Deinbollia giorgii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flowering display.
