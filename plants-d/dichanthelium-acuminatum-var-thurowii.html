Dichanthelium acuminatum var. thurowii

Dichanthelium acuminatum var. thurowii, commonly known as the Thurow's panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
