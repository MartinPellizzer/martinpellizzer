Diplostachyum eatonii

Diplostachyum eatonii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow inflorescences. The plant forms dense clumps and can grow up to 3 meters in height, making it a popular choice for landscaping. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a vibrant green color that adds visual interest to gardens.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
