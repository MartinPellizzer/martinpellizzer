Doxantha serrulata

Doxantha serrulata, commonly known as the serrated doxantha, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Doxantha serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
