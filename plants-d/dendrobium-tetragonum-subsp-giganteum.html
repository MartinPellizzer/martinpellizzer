Dendrobium tetragonum subsp. giganteum

Dendrobium tetragonum subsp.

giganteum, commonly known as the giant bamboo orchid, is a sympodial orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often resembling bamboo in appearance.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with red-brown markings, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with consistent moisture.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
