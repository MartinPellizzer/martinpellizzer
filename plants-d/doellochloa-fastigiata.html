Doellochloa fastigiata

Doellochloa fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are usually arranged in a tight, upright cluster. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are compact and often pale green or brown in color.

Doellochloa fastigiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
