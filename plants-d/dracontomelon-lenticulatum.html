Dracontomelon lenticulatum

Dracontomelon lenticulatum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich color, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts.

However, due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
