Draba aizoides subsp. saxigena

Draba aizoides subsp.

saxigena, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are typically lobed or divided.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often found on rocky slopes and scree.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is typically found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.
