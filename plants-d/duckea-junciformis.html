Duckea junciformis

Duckea junciformis, commonly known as the slender sedgeland duckweed, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lemnaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches. It reproduces vegetatively through the production of buds that detach and float to new locations.

Due to its rapid growth rate, it can become invasive in certain water bodies if not managed properly.
