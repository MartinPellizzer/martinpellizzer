Dryopteris confinis

Dryopteris confinis, commonly known as the cliff fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky outcrops and forest floors. The plant features feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. Its rhizomes are robust, allowing it to anchor itself in rocky substrates.

Dryopteris confinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, high-altitude habitats.
