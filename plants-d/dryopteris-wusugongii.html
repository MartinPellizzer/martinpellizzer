Dryopteris wusugongii

Dryopteris wusugongii, commonly known as the Wusugong fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
