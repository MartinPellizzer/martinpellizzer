Dodecatheon dentatum var. ellisiae

Dodecatheon dentatum var. ellisiae, commonly known as the Ellisia's toothed glory flower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.
