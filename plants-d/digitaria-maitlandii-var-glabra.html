Digitaria maitlandii var. glabra

Digitaria maitlandii var. glabra, commonly known as smooth digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless or very sparsely hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
