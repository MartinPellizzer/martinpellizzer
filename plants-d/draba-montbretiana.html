Draba montbretiana

Draba montbretiana, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, rocky habitats.

Draba montbretiana is often found in meadows, scree slopes, and on rocky outcrops at high altitudes.
