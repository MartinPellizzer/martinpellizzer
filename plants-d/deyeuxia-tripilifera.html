Deyeuxia tripilifera

Deyeuxia tripilifera, commonly known as the three-lobed sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

Deyeuxia tripilifera is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed wetland environments.
