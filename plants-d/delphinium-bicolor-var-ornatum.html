Delphinium bicolor var. ornatum

Delphinium bicolor var. ornatum, commonly known as the Colorado larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, rocky soils.

This plant is not only ornamental but also plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
