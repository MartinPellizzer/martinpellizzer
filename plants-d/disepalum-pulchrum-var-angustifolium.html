Disepalum pulchrum var. angustifolium

Disepalum pulchrum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf disepalum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five distinct sepals, which are often brightly colored to attract pollinators. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a compact form that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
