Dendrochilum lobongense

Dendrochilum lobongense, commonly known as the Mindanao tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its tropical rainforest habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Dendrochilum lobongense is a valuable addition to botanical gardens due to its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
