Dichropappus adenanthus

Dichropappus adenanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for dry gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
