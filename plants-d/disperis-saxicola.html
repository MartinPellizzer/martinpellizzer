Disperis saxicola

Disperis saxicola, commonly known as the rock disperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive tubular shape, often found in open habitats. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Disperis saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
