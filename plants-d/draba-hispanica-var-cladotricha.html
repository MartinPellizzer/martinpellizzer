Draba hispanica var. cladotricha

Draba hispanica var. cladotricha, commonly known as the Spanish rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. It has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense, hairy foliage.

Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring.
