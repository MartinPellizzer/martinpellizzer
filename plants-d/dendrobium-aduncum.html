Dendrobium aduncum

Dendrobium aduncum, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, thin stems that can reach several meters in length, often adorned with clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

Dendrobium aduncum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
