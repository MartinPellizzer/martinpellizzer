Dioscorea tafiensis

Dioscorea tafiensis, commonly known as the Tafiensian yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing herbaceous perennial that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are valued for their starchy content and are often used in local cuisines.

The species is also noted for its potential in traditional medicine due to the presence of various bioactive compounds.
