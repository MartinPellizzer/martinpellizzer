Dissanthelium laxifolium

Dissanthelium laxifolium, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wetlands.

Botanically, it is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
