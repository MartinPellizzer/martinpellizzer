Deinandra clementina

Deinandra clementina, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Deinandra clementina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
