Dianthus slavonicus

Dianthus slavonicus, commonly known as Slavonic campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of pink or red, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has a low-growing habit with linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Dianthus slavonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
