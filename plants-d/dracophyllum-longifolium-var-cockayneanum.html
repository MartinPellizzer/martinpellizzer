Dracophyllum longifolium var. cockayneanum

Dracophyllum longifolium var. cockayneanum, commonly known as the Cockayne's dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas with a temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with similar environmental conditions.
