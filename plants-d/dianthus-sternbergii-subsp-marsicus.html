Dianthus sternbergii subsp. marsicus

Dianthus sternbergii subsp.

marsicus, commonly known as the Marsic lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with five petals that are typically pink to red in color.

The flowers are borne on long, slender stems and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
