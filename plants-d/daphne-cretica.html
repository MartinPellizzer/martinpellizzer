Daphne cretica

Daphne cretica, commonly known as the Cyprus daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

Daphne cretica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter flowering.
