Dipladenia gentianoides subvar. ovata

Dipladenia gentianoides subvar. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf Dipladenia, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with five petals and a crown-like structure, typically in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

The subvariety ovata is distinguished by its broader, more rounded leaves compared to other forms of Dipladenia.
