Dalea pringlei

Dalea pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Dalea pringlei is often found in sagebrush steppe and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
