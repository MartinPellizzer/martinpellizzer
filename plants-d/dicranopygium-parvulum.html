Dicranopygium parvulum

Dicranopygium parvulum, commonly known as the small moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its small, dense, and often cushion-like growth form, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, spirally arranged leaves that are usually narrow and pointed. Its reproductive structures, including the capsules, are borne on short, erect stalks.

Dicranopygium parvulum plays a role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
