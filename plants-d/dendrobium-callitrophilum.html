Dendrobium callitrophilum

Dendrobium callitrophilum, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow-orange flowers that resemble torches. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Dendrobium callitrophilum is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its striking floral display.
