Delphinium pachycentroides

Delphinium pachycentroides, commonly known as the thick-centered larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, typical of the Delphinium genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
