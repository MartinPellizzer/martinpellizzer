Dichanthium armatum

Dichanthium armatum, commonly known as the red grass or red oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Dichanthium armatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
