Diospyros chloroxylon var. cupulosa

Diospyros chloroxylon var. cupulosa, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange to red berries. Its fruits are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and carving.
