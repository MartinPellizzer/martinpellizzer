Dictyoxiphium

Dictyoxiphium, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia, often growing in tropical and subtropical climates. Dictyoxiphium species are characterized by their intricate, spider-like floral structures, which are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

The genus includes several species, each with unique floral morphology and ecological adaptations.
