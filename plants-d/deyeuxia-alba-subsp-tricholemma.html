Deyeuxia alba subsp. tricholemma

Deyeuxia alba subsp.

tricholemma, commonly known as the white tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, pale-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, white florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
