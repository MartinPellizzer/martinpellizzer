Dalea jaliscana

Dalea jaliscana, commonly known as Mexican prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine hairs.

Dalea jaliscana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
