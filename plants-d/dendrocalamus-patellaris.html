Dendrocalamus patellaris

Dendrocalamus patellaris, commonly known as the Patellar bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its strong and durable culms, it is valued for construction and handicraft purposes.
