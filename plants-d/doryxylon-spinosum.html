Doryxylon spinosum

Doryxylon spinosum, commonly known as the spiny wood or spiny tree, is a unique plant species characterized by its woody structure and thorny growth.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features elongated, spiny stems that can grow quite tall, often reaching several meters in height. Its leaves are typically small and opposite, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The botanical significance of Doryxylon spinosum lies in its role as a potential source of medicinal compounds and its ecological importance in its native habitats.
