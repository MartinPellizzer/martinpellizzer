Diasperus scaber

Diasperus scaber, commonly known as the rough rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, rough hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Diasperus scaber is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
