Dicksonia caudata

Dicksonia caudata, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a large, tropical fern native to Southeast Asia and parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its massive, tree-like trunk and feathery, compound fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The trunk is composed of a dense mass of roots and rhizomes, which store water and nutrients. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark, which aids in attracting pollinators.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in tropical ecosystems.
