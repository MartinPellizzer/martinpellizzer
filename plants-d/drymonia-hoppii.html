Drymonia hoppii

Drymonia hoppii, commonly known as the Hoppi's drymonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and swampy areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Drymonia hoppii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
