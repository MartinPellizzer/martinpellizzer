Dyckia hamosa

Dyckia hamosa, commonly known as the silver dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with stiff, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dyckia hamosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
