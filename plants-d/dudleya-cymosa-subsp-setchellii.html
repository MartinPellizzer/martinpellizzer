Dudleya cymosa subsp. setchellii

Dudleya cymosa subsp.

setchellii, commonly known as the Setchell's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to coastal areas of California. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
