Dalea thymoides

Dalea thymoides, commonly known as Texas mountain mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

Dalea thymoides is also a nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
