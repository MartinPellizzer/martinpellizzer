Diospyros filipes

Diospyros filipes, commonly known as the slender persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
