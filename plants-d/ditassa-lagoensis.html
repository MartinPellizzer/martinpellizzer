Ditassa lagoensis

Ditassa lagoensis, commonly known as the lake lily, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as lakes and ponds. The plant has broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and white, emerging from the water surface.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
