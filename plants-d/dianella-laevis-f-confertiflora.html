Dianella laevis f. confertiflora

Dianella laevis f. confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered flax lily, is a cultivar of the flax lily genus native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slight blue-gray hue. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of soil types.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
