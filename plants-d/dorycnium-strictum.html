Dorycnium strictum

Dorycnium strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf burclover, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, hooked pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Dorycnium strictum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
