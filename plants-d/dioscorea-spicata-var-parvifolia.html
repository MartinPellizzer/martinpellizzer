Dioscorea spicata var. parvifolia

Dioscorea spicata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, compound leaves, and a preference for well-drained, moist soils.

It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a source of edible tubers.
