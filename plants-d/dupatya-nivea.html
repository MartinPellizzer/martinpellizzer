Dupatya nivea

Dupatya nivea, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a prominent central tube. The plant has heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Dupatya nivea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
