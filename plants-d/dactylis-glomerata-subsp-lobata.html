Dactylis glomerata subsp. lobata

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

lobata, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and is used in agricultural and horticultural settings.
