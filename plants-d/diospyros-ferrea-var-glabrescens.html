Diospyros ferrea var. glabrescens

Diospyros ferrea var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange to red berries, which are edible when fully mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with rich, well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
