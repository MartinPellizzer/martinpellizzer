Dolichos kongkandae

Dolichos kongkandae, commonly known as the Indian horsegram or kongkandae, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. Dolichos kongkandae is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is cultivated for its nutrient-rich seeds.

It plays a significant role in agricultural systems due to its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
