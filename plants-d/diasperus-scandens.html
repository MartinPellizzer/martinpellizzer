Diasperus scandens

Diasperus scandens, commonly known as the climbing cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Diasperus scandens is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
