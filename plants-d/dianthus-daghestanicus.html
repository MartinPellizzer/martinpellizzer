Dianthus daghestanicus

Dianthus daghestanicus, commonly known as the Daghestan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of pink and red. The plant features upright stems with narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, five-petaled blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
