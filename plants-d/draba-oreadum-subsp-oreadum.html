Draba oreadum subsp. oreadum

Draba oreadum subsp.

oreadum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically forms a dense mat of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small white or yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Draba oreadum subsp.

oreadum is often found in mountain meadows and is a common indicator species of alpine ecosystems.
