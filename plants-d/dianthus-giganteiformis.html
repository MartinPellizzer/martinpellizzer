Dianthus giganteiformis

Dianthus giganteiformis, commonly known as the giant carnation, is a striking member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive ruffled appearance. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Dianthus giganteiformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
