Dalbergia capuronii

Dalbergia capuronii, commonly known as the Capuron's rosewood, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
