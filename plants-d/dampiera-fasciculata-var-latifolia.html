Dampiera fasciculata var. latifolia

Dampiera fasciculata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved dampiera, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
