Dactylorhiza osiliensis

Dactylorhiza osiliensis, commonly known as the Sardinian marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, lanceolate leaves. The plant blooms with pale pink to white flowers that have three sepals and three petals, with the lateral petals being hooded. It typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, preferring calcareous soils.

Dactylorhiza osiliensis is a perennial species that plays a role in its ecosystem by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
