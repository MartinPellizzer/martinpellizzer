Diospyros vitiensis var. vitiensis

Diospyros vitiensis var. vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly the island of Vanuatu. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and astringent fruits. The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches a deep orange color, indicating maturity.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated in home gardens and small farms in its native habitat.
