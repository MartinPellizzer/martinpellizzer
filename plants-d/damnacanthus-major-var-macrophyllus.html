Damnacanthus major var. macrophyllus

Damnacanthus major var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant devil's tongue, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its dramatic appearance, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions as a decorative plant.
