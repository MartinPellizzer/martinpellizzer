Dendrostylis odorata

Dendrostylis odorata, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a mildly sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
