Dithyrea californica var. clinata

Dithyrea californica var. clinata, commonly known as the San Francisco dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to California.

It features bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped with a hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and open fields.

This variety is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and is considered a rare and endemic species.
