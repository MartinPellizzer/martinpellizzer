Dorycnium herbaceum var. decumbens

Dorycnium herbaceum var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping sainfoin, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, sprawling stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is often found in dry grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
