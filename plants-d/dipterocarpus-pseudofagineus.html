Dipterocarpus pseudofagineus

Dipterocarpus pseudofagineus, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is typically found in lowland dipterocarp forests. It has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
