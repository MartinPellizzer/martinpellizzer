Draba canoensis

Draba canoensis, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow or white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Draba canoensis is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of alpine ecosystems.
