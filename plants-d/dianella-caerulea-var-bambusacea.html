Dianella caerulea var. bambusacea

Dianella caerulea var. bambusacea, commonly known as the bamboo-like flax lily, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features long, narrow leaves that resemble bamboo, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers in late summer, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
