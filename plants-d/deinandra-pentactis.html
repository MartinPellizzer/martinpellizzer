Deinandra pentactis

Deinandra pentactis, commonly known as the five-toothed sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, each toothed at the edges. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Deinandra pentactis is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
