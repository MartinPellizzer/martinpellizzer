Dichroa latifolia

Dichroa latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dichroa, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately compound leaves that change color with the seasons, often displaying vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and seasonal color changes, it is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
