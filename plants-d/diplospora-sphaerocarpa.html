Diplospora sphaerocarpa

Diplospora sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky seed heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Diplospora sphaerocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal.
