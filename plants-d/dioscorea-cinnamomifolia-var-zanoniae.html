Dioscorea cinnamomifolia var. zanoniae

Dioscorea cinnamomifolia var. zanoniae, commonly known as the Zanonia or Zanonia yew, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical, glabrous stems and compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for medicinal or culinary use.

Its botanical structure supports its role in traditional medicine, where it is valued for its potential anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic properties.
