Diplostephium floribundum var. cundinamarcense

Diplostephium floribundum var. cundinamarcense, commonly known as the Cundinamarca cholla, is a species of cactus native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are often branched. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
