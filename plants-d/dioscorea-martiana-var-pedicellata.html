Dioscorea martiana var. pedicellata

Dioscorea martiana var. pedicellata, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive, branching vine structure.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
