Didymoglossum sphenoides

Didymoglossum sphenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bog moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wet meadows.

It has slender, creeping stems that produce small, ovate leaves with a distinctive midrib. The leaves are typically green to reddish in color and have a smooth, waxy surface. It reproduces through spores released from capsules that are borne on short, erect stalks.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is an important component of peat-forming ecosystems.
