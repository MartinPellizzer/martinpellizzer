Davallia mariesii var. stenolepis

Davallia mariesii var. stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a terrestrial fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, finely divided fronds with a distinctive, arching growth habit. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
