Dicranopygium callithrix

Dicranopygium callithrix, commonly known as the narrow-leafed feather moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like shoots and small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in two ranks. The plant forms dense, cushion-like mats and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short, erect stalks and release spores when mature.

Dicranopygium callithrix plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
