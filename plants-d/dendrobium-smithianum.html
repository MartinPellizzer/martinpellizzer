Dendrobium smithianum

Dendrobium smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium smithianum is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
