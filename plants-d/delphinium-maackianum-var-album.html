Delphinium maackianum var. album

Delphinium maackianum var. album, commonly known as the White delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, with the variety *album* being distinguished by its pale to white flower color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soil in full sun.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
