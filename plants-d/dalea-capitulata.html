Dalea capitulata

Dalea capitulata, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and steppe environments.

Dalea capitulata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
