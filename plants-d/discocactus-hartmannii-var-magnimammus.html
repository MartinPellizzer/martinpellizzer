Discocactus hartmannii var. magnimammus

Discocactus hartmannii var. magnimammus, commonly known as the giant mammillary discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, rounded stems that bear numerous tubercles, each topped with a prominent, woolly areole. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its adaptations include a thick, fleshy stem for water storage and spines for protection against herbivores.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
