Dioscorea purdiei

Dioscorea purdiei, commonly known as the purple yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its tubers are used as a food source in some cultures and are also valued for their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
