Discocalyx perseifolia

Discocalyx perseifolia, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Discocalyx perseifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
