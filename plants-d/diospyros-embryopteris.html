Diospyros embryopteris

Diospyros embryopteris, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, fleshy fruits that turn orange to red when mature. Its fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a mild astringent taste when unripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
