Dimorphanthera amplifolia var. amplifolia

Dimorphanthera amplifolia var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved dimorphanthera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
