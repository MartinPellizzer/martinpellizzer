Distichorchis pandaneti

Distichorchis pandaneti, commonly known as the pandan orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique inflorescence with two rows of flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spike.

Distichorchis pandaneti is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
