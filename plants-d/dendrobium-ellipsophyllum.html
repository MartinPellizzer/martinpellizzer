Dendrobium ellipsophyllum

Dendrobium ellipsophyllum, commonly known as the elliptic phallic orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with thick, fleshy rhizomes and a branched stem system. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Dendrobium ellipsophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
