Daucus latifolius

Daucus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf carrot, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a biennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features a taproot that is often used as a vegetable, similar to the common carrot. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, which is a characteristic feature of the carrot family.
