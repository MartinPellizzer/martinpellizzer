Diplotaxis virgata subsp. brachycarpa

Diplotaxis virgata subsp.

brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces capsule fruits that are shorter and more compact compared to other subspecies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
