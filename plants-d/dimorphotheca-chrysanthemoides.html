Dimorphotheca chrysanthemoides

Dimorphotheca chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Dimorphotheca chrysanthemoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
