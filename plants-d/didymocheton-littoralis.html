Didymocheton littoralis

Didymocheton littoralis, commonly known as the coastal senna, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or saline soils along the shoreline. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Didymocheton littoralis is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
