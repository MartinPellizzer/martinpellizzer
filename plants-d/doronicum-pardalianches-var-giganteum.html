Doronicum pardalianches var. giganteum

Doronicum pardalianches var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant leopard's bane, is a striking perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It features tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, adorned with large, showy yellow-orange flowers that resemble leopard spots. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
