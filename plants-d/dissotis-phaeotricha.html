Dissotis phaeotricha

Dissotis phaeotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a velvety appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

Dissotis phaeotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
