Diplotaxis muralis var. intermedia

Diplotaxis muralis var. intermedia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive black center, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. It has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
