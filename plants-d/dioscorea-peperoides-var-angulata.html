Dioscorea peperoides var. angulata

Dioscorea peperoides var. angulata, commonly known as the angular yam, is a tropical climbing vine native to Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its angular, ridged stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are edible when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it distinct within its genus.
