Dilax

Dilax, commonly known as the narrowleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Dilax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
