Dendrobium luteolum

Dendrobium luteolum, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive pouch-like lip. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dendrobium luteolum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
