Diospyros flavicans

Diospyros flavicans, commonly known as the yellow persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are sweet and astringent when unripe. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in its native regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical forests.
