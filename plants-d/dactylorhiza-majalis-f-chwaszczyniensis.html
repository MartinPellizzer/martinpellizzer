Dactylorhiza majalis f. chwaszczyniensis

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its short, creeping rhizomes. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves and a spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and is often found in areas with rich, acidic soil.
