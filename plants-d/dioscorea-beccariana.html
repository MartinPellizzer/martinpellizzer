Dioscorea beccariana

Dioscorea beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and tend to climb by means of tendrils.

The tubers of D. beccariana are sometimes cultivated for their potential medicinal properties and nutritional value.
