Dalbergia purpurea

Dalbergia purpurea, commonly known as the purpleheart tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark purple to black heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

The flowers are small and clustered in racemes, often appearing in shades of pink or purple, adding ornamental value to the plant.
