Dendrobium albopurpureum f. album

Dendrobium albopurpureum f. album, commonly known as the White Purple Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing showy, semi-circular petals that are often speckled with a faint pink or purple hue. Its botanical features include a complex root system adapted for epiphytic growth on trees or rocks.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique coloration.
