Disporum oreganum

Disporum oreganum, commonly known as the Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its blue-black berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
