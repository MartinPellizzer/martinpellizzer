Doronicum montanum

Doronicum montanum, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is known for its ability to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Doronicum montanum plays an important role in alpine ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
