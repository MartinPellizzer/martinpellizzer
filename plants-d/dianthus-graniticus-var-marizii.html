Dianthus graniticus var. marizii

Dianthus graniticus var. marizii, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Dianthus graniticus var. marizii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
