Dombeya boeniensis

Dombeya boeniensis, commonly known as the Boeni's dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dombeya boeniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
