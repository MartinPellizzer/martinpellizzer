Dendrobium wallichii

Dendrobium wallichii, commonly known as the Wallach's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
