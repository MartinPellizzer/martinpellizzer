Draba jorullensis

Draba jorullensis, commonly known as the Jorullensk whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. This species is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves. Draba jorullensis typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptations to survive in nutrient-poor, high-altitude habitats.
