Dichopetalum ranunculaceum var. tasmanica

Dichopetalum ranunculaceum var. tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Tasmania.

It features showy yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive, hairy calyx. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
