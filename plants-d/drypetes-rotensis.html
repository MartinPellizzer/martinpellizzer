Drypetes rotensis

Drypetes rotensis, commonly known as the Malayan tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
