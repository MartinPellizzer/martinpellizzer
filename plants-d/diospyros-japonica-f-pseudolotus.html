Diospyros japonica f. pseudolotus

Diospyros japonica f. pseudolotus, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a cultivar of the persimmon tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued for its sweet, non-astringent fruit, which is often used in both culinary and ornamental applications.
