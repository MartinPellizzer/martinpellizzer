Dalbergia volubilis var. latifolia

Dalbergia volubilis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironwood, is a tropical climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and sturdy, woody stems that can reach significant lengths. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing elliptical seeds. Its leaves are typically compound, with several pairs of opposite leaflets.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
