Dactylis glomerata subsp. mairei

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

mairei, commonly known as Maire's goat's rue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically purple or pink in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the stems are somewhat stiff and erect.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
