Drypetes glauca

Drypetes glauca, commonly known as the blue glauca or blue flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody fruits that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
