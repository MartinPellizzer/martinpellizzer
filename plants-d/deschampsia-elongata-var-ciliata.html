Deschampsia elongata var. ciliata

Deschampsia elongata var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, soft foliage and slender, elongated stems that often form dense, tufted clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a desirable plant for ecological and landscaping purposes.
