Dicliptera guttata

Dicliptera guttata, commonly known as the spotted grass or spotted blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves with distinct spots or streaks of color, typically green with purple or reddish markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Dicliptera guttata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water garden plant.
