Diospyros canaliculata

Diospyros canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its fruit is a small, yellowish-orange berry that turns red when ripe, and it is edible when fully mature.

The tree is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
