Dianthus suaveolens

Dianthus suaveolens, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus suaveolens is also valued for its fragrant foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
