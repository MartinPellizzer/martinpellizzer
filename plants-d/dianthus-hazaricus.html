Dianthus hazaricus

Dianthus hazaricus, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that aid in its propagation.

Dianthus hazaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
