Desmodium mandonii

Desmodium mandonii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a leguminous species belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Desmodium mandonii is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and sustainable agriculture.
