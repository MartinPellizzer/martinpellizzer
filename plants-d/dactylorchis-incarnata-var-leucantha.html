Dactylorchis incarnata var. leucantha

Dactylorchis incarnata var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, erect stem that bears a single, showy white or pale pink flower. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are characterized by their elongated, spurred petals and are pollinated by specific insect species.
