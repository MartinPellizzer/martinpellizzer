Dracocephalum incanum

Dracocephalum incanum, commonly known as the white dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Dracocephalum incanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
