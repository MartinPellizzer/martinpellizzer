Dicranopteris gigantea

Dicranopteris gigantea, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a member of the fern family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help in nutrient absorption from the water.

The fronds are typically bipinnate, with small, finger-like segments that contribute to its distinctive appearance.
