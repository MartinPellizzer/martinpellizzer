Dioscorea soso var. trichopoda

Dioscorea soso var. trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, cylindrical tubers with a rough, hairy surface. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are unisexual and borne in clusters.

The tubers are not only valued for their nutritional content but also have traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
