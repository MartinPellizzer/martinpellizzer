Dioscorea demourae

Dioscorea demourae, commonly known as the African yam vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its tubers are used as a food source in some cultures, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful preparation.

The plant's rapid growth and climbing habit make it a significant component of its native ecosystems.
