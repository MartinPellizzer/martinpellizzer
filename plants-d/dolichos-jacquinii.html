Dolichos jacquinii

Dolichos jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's dolichos, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
