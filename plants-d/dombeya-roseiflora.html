Dombeya roseiflora

Dombeya roseiflora, commonly known as the pink dombeya, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a pink or white hue. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and features simple, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

Dombeya roseiflora is well-suited to full sun and well-drained soils, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates.
