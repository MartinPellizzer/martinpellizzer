Dioscorea scortechinii

Dioscorea scortechinii, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and climbing habit.
