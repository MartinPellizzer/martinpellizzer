Dioscorea megalantha

Dioscorea megalantha, commonly known as giant yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant produces large, tuberous roots that can grow up to several meters in length. These roots are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The plant's morphology includes compound leaves and unisexual flowers, typical of the genus Dioscorea.
