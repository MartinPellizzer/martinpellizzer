Dianthus furcatus subsp. gyspergerae

Dianthus furcatus subsp.

gyspergerae, commonly known as the Gysperger's campion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is characterized by its clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
