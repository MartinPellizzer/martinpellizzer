Drypetes iodoformis

Drypetes iodoformis, commonly known as the Iodoform tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that emits a distinctive iodoform odor when broken. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, followed by orange to reddish fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with prominent veins.

This plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
