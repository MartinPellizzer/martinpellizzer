Deutzia cordatula

Deutzia cordatula, commonly known as heartleaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are heart-shaped, hence its common name, and are arranged oppositely along the branches. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its delicate floral display.
