Draba incana subsp. thomasii

Draba incana subsp.

thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It features a compact rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is often found in high-altitude meadows and is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants.
