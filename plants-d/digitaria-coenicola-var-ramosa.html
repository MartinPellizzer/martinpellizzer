Digitaria coenicola var. ramosa

Digitaria coenicola var. ramosa, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous roots that help stabilize soil. The plant produces slender, branching stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of numerous spikelets, which are typically green to reddish in color.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or soil conservation.
