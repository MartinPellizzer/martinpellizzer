Digitalis canariensis f. tomentosa

Digitalis canariensis f. tomentosa, commonly known as the Canary Island foxglove, is a variant of the foxglove plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it the common name "tomentosa," meaning "covered with down.". The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its unique texture and vibrant floral display.
