Diospyros sparsirama

Diospyros sparsirama, commonly known as the sparse-fruited persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which has a sweet, astringent flavor.
