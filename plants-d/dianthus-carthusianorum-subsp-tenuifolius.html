Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. tenuifolius

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red flowers with five petals.

The leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery.
