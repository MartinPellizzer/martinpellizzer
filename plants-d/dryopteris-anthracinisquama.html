Dryopteris anthracinisquama

Dryopteris anthracinisquama, commonly known as the blackfoot fern, is a species of fern native to the Appalachian Mountains in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

The blackfoot fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, humid conditions.
