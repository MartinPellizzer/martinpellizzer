Drymocallis amplifolia

Drymocallis amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the fall. Its botanical name reflects its broad leaves and golden floral display.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
