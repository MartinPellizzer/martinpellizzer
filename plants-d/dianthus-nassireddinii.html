Dianthus nassireddinii

Dianthus nassireddinii, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red. The plant features basal leaves that are linear and hairy, and it produces clusters of small, rounded flowers on slender stems. Dianthus nassireddinii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species with low water requirements.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
