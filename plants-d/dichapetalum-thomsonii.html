Dichapetalum thomsonii

Dichapetalum thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture.

Dichapetalum thomsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
