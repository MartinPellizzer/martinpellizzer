Dalbergia bhutanica

Dalbergia bhutanica, commonly known as Bhutan rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly found in Bhutan, India, and Nepal. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height, with dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic qualities. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
