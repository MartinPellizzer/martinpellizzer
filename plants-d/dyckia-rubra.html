Dyckia rubra

Dyckia rubra, commonly known as the red dyckia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, stiff leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces striking inflorescences of red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. Dyckia rubra is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage and flowers provide visual interest throughout the growing season.
