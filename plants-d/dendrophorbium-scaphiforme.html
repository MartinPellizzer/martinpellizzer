Dendrophorbium scaphiforme

Dendrophorbium scaphiforme, commonly known as the boat-shaped lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on tree bark in temperate regions. The plant's structure is characterized by its flattened, boat-like thallus, which gives it its common name. It plays an important role in nutrient cycling and provides habitat for various microorganisms.

Due to its specialized growth requirements, it is often used as an indicator species for environmental health.
