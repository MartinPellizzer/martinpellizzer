Dimocarpus fumatus subsp. calcicola

Dimocarpus fumatus subsp.

calcicola, commonly known as the limestone gooseberry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are typically sour and astringent.

The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils, often found on rocky outcrops and limestone formations. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
