Daviesia arborea

Daviesia arborea, commonly known as the tree daviesia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange pea-like flowers, which bloom in spring. It is often found in heathlands and rocky areas, adapting to nutrient-poor soils.

Daviesia arborea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
