Diplacus rattanii

Diplacus rattanii, commonly known as the rattlesnake plant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings.

Diplacus rattanii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds.
