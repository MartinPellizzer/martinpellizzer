Dioscorea riparia

Dioscorea riparia, commonly known as river yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves twining around other plants, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The tubers of D. riparia are edible when properly prepared and have been used as a food source in various cultures.
