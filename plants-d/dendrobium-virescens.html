Dendrobium virescens

Dendrobium virescens, commonly known as the green dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that are often covered in a thick, leathery sheath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and flowering characteristics.
