Dendrobium sessile

Dendrobium sessile, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to store water in its pseudobulbs.

Dendrobium sessile is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
