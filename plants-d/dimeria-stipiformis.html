Dimeria stipiformis

Dimeria stipiformis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces small, yellow flowers with prominent, showy petals. The plant has succulent leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping it retain moisture in dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Dimeria stipiformis plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
