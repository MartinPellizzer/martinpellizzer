Dracocephalum bullatum

Dracocephalum bullatum, commonly known as the purple skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
