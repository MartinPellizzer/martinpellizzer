Dyssodia subintegerrima

Dyssodia subintegerrima, commonly known as the roughleaf dyssodia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it typically blooms in the spring and summer months.

Dyssodia subintegerrima is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
