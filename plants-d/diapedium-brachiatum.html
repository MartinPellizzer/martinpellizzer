Diapedium brachiatum

Diapedium brachiatum, commonly known as the climbing diapedium, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twisting stems that allow it to climb and entangle other plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Diapedium brachiatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
