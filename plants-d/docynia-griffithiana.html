Docynia griffithiana

Docynia griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright orange, berry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its botanical features include rough, fissured bark and clusters of trifoliate leaves.

Docynia griffithiana is valued both for its medicinal properties and its role in traditional Indian cuisine.
