Dianthus caryophyllus var. tergestinus

Dianthus caryophyllus var. tergestinus, commonly known as the Tuscan carnation, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces vibrant, double-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and floral arrangements. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed, adding to its botanical interest.
