Draba lutea var. longipes

Draba lutea var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, dry climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and the leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated stems compared to other Draba species, making it a notable feature in its habitat.
