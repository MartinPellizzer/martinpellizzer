Diospyros halesioides

Diospyros halesioides, commonly known as the Hales' persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits. The fruit is typically edible when fully ripe, though some varieties may have astringent properties when unripe.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
