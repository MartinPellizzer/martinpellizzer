Dioscorea mitis

Dioscorea mitis, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has palmately lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are rich in starch and are an important food source in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in various cultural practices.
