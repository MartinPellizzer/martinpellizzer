Dombeya blackburniana

Dombeya blackburniana, commonly known as the blackburnian dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, dark berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dombeya blackburniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
