Dalbergia glaberrima subsp. glaberrima

Dalbergia glaberrima subsp.

glaberrima, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, dark seeds. It is valued for its dense, durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

However, due to overharvesting, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
