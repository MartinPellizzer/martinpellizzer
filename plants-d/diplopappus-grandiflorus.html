Diplopappus grandiflorus

Diplopappus grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lobed. Its fruit is a pappus-equipped achene, which allows for wind dispersal of its seeds.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions.
