Desmodium michelianum

Desmodium michelianum, commonly known as purple desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value and potential as a forage crop make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
