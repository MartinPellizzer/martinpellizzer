Diuris purdiei

Diuris purdiei, commonly known as the Purple-veined Spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid with striking floral features.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in the south-eastern regions of Australia. Its flowers are usually pale green to pinkish in color with distinctive purple veins.

Diuris purdiei is a herbaceous perennial that blooms during the cooler months, often attracting specific pollinators.
