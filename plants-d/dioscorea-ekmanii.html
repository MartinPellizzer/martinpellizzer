Dioscorea ekmanii

Dioscorea ekmanii, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used for both culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, bean-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Dioscorea ekmanii is valued for its nutritional content and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
