Dipterocarpus balsamifer

Dipterocarpus balsamifer, commonly known as the balsamiferous dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, often reddish bark. The tree produces heavy, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. It also has showy, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
