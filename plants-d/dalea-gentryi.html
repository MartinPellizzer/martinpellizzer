Dalea gentryi

Dalea gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Dalea gentryi is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
