Diplomorpha chamaedaphne

Diplomorpha chamaedaphne, commonly known as the dwarf wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet, acidic soils and is often found in swamps and pond edges. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a waxy coating. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
