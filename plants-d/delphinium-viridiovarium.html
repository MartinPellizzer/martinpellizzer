Delphinium viridiovarium

Delphinium viridiovarium, commonly known as the green-leaf larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage is characterized by a waxy, glossy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Delphinium viridiovarium is also notable for its toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
