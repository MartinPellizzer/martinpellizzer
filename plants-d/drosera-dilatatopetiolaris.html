Drosera dilatatopetiolaris

Drosera dilatatopetiolaris, commonly known as the broad-leaved sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves covered in sticky, glandular hairs that trap insects. The plant's leaves are typically reddish in color and can grow up to 15 cm in length. It thrives in wet, acidic environments and is often found in swampy or boggy areas.

Drosera dilatatopetiolaris is a perennial species that produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months.
