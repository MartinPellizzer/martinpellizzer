Draba micropetala

Draba micropetala, commonly known as the small-petalled whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is characterized by its compact stature and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Draba micropetala is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
