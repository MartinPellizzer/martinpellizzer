Digitalis ikarica

Digitalis ikarica, commonly known as the New Zealand foxglove, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes other foxglove species known for their medicinal properties. The plant features tall, slender stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are tubular and typically pink or purple, arranged in a spike at the top of the stem.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.
