Dysolobium apioides

Dysolobium apioides, commonly known as the honeycomb plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a distinctive rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its unique structure resembles a honeycomb, giving it its common name.

Dysolobium apioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
