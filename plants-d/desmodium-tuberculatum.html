Desmodium tuberculatum

Desmodium tuberculatum, commonly known as the hairy burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant produces yellow or white flowers and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a fibrous root system and can fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
