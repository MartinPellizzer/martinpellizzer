Dichanthelium cabrerae

Dichanthelium cabrerae, commonly known as the Cabrera's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
