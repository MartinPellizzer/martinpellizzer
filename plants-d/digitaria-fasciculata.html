Digitaria fasciculata

Digitaria fasciculata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Digitaria fasciculata is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
