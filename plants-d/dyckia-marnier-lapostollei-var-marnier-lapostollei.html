Dyckia marnier-lapostollei var. marnier-lapostollei

Dyckia marnier-lapostollei var. marnier-lapostollei, commonly known as the Marnier-Lapostolle Dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit and vibrant, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and resilience, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
