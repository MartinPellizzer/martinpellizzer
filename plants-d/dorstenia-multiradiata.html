Dorstenia multiradiata

Dorstenia multiradiata, commonly known as the "Spider Plant" or "Mother of Thousands," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spiraling inflorescence that resembles a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that are clustered in dense, elongated panicles. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Dorstenia multiradiata is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
