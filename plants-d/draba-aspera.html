Draba aspera

Draba aspera, commonly known as the rough Draba, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lanceolate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. Native to alpine and arctic regions, it thrives in rocky and gravelly soils. The plant is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and Asia.

Its rapid life cycle allows it to complete growth and reproduction in short growing seasons.
