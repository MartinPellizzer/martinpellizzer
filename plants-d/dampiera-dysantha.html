Dampiera dysantha

Dampiera dysantha, commonly known as the pink flax-lily, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Dampiera dysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
