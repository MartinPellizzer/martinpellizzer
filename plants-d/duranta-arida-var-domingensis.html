Duranta arida var. domingensis

Duranta arida var. domingensis, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, blue-violet flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
