Dipteracanthus strepens f. pedunculatus

Dipteracanthus strepens f. pedunculatus, commonly known as the pedunculate dipteracanthus, is a unique variant of the dipteracanthus genus.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which support its striking, showy flowers. The plant exhibits a distinct floral structure with a central column and modified leaves that serve as bracts. It thrives in specific tropical or subtropical environments, often in well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists alike.
