Diplazium doederleinii

Diplazium doederleinii, commonly known as the Doederlein's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure on the water's surface. The plant has long, slender fronds with small, spore-producing structures called sori located on the underside of the leaves. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and marshes.

Diplazium doederleinii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
