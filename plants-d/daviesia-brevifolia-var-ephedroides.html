Daviesia brevifolia var. ephedroides

Daviesia brevifolia var. ephedroides, commonly known as the dwarf hoppea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its compact growth form. It is often found in heathlands and woodland areas, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

The variety ephedroides is distinguished by its smaller leaves and more rounded growth habit compared to the typical form.
