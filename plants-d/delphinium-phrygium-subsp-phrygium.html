Delphinium phrygium subsp. phrygium

Delphinium phrygium subsp.

phrygium, commonly known as the Phrygian larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slight pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
