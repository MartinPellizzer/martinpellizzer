Danaea alata var. moritziana

Danaea alata var. moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's flame vine, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
