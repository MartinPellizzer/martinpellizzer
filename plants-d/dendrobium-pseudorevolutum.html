Dendrobium pseudorevolutum

Dendrobium pseudorevolutum, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, yellow-orange flowers that have a distinctive golden stripe. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
