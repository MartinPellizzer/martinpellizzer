Dichanthium clarkei

Dichanthium clarkei, commonly known as the African grass or Clarke's grass, is a perennial bunchgrass native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to warm, arid climates.

Dichanthium clarkei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
