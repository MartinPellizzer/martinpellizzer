Dombeya stipulacea

Dombeya stipulacea, commonly known as the stipulate dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves with prominent stipules, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Dombeya stipulacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in temperate climates.
