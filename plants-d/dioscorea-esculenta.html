Dioscorea esculenta

Dioscorea esculenta, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cuisines.

Additionally, the plant contains compounds that have been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
