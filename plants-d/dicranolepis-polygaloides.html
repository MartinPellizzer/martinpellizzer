Dicranolepis polygaloides

Dicranolepis polygaloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Dicranolepis polygaloides is adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
