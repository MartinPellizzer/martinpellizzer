Danais hispida

Danais hispida, commonly known as the rough danais, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are composite, consisting of ray florets and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Danais hispida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
