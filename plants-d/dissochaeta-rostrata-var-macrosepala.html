Dissochaeta rostrata var. macrosepala

Dissochaeta rostrata var. macrosepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal dissochaeta, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with prominent veins and a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically displaying yellow or greenish-white petals.

This variant is distinguished by its enlarged, fleshy sepals, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
