Drypetes perrieri

Drypetes perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
