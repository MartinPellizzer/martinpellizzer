Diospyros capensis

Diospyros capensis, commonly known as the Cape ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
