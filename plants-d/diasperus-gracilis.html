Diasperus gracilis

Diasperus gracilis, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on rocky outcrops and dry soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Diasperus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
