Dufrenoya papillosa

Dufrenoya papillosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of rainforests. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic.

Dufrenoya papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its specific ecological requirements.
