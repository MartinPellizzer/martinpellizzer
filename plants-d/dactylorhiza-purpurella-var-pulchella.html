Dactylorhiza purpurella var. pulchella

Dactylorhiza purpurella var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a complex root system and is part of the Orchidaceae family.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
