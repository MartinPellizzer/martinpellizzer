Dysoxylum ilocanum

Dysoxylum ilocanum, commonly known as the Ilocano Mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its large, oblong leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces woody fruits that contain smooth, light-colored seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species.
