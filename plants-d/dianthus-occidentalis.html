Dianthus occidentalis

Dianthus occidentalis, commonly known as the western pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. Its flowers are pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive fringed appearance.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, clustered blooms throughout the summer months.
