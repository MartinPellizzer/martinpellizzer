Dianthus broteri subsp. hinoxianus

Dianthus broteri subsp.

hinoxianus, commonly known as the Hinoxian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red petals, which are typically arranged in a double or semi-double bloom.

The plant features a compact growth habit with upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
