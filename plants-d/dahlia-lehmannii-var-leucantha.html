Dahlia lehmannii var. leucantha

Dahlia lehmannii var. leucantha, commonly known as the White Lehmann dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, single or semi-double flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features sturdy, upright stems with broadly lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It blooms from late summer to early autumn, producing an abundance of flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
