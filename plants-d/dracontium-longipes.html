Dracontium longipes

Dracontium longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its underground stems produce edible tubers, which are rich in starch and used in various culinary applications.

Dracontium longipes thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its nutritional benefits.
