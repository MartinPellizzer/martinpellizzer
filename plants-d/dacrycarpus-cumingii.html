Dacrycarpus cumingii

Dacrycarpus cumingii, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-colored leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several hundred years, making it a long-lived species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

The silver fern is also notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
