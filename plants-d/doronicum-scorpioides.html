Doronicum scorpioides

Doronicum scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpion's tail, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters that give the plant its common name.

Doronicum scorpioides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
