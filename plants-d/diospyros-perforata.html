Diospyros perforata

Diospyros perforata, commonly known as the perforate persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often perforated, giving it its common name, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The tree is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
