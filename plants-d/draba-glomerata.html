Draba glomerata

Draba glomerata, commonly known as the clustered whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

Draba glomerata is known for its early blooming period, often appearing in late winter or early spring.
