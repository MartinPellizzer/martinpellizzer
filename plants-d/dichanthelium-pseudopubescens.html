Dichanthelium pseudopubescens

Dichanthelium pseudopubescens, commonly known as the hairy false panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
