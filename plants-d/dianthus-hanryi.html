Dianthus hanryi

Dianthus hanryi, commonly known as the Henry's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers. The plant typically produces pink to red blooms with five petals and prominent central stamens. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Dianthus hanryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
