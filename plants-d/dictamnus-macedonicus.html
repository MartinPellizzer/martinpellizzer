Dictamnus macedonicus

Dictamnus macedonicus, commonly known as the Macedonian dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It features aromatic, reddish-brown stems that exude a strong, spicy scent when broken. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Dictamnus macedonicus is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
