Dombeya punctata var. pervillei

Dombeya punctata var. pervillei, commonly known as the Perville's dombeya, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a distinctive yellow throat.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
