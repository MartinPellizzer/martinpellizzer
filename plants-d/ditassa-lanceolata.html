Ditassa lanceolata

Ditassa lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate dittany, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it is also valued for its aromatic properties.
