Dalbergia sciadendron

Dalbergia sciadendron, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, deeply fissured bark and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its density and durability, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and furniture.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
