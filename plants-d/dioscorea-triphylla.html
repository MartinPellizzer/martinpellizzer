Dioscorea triphylla

Dioscorea triphylla, commonly known as the triphylla yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
