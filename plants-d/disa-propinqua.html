Disa propinqua

Disa propinqua, commonly known as the pink disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate blooms during the summer months.

Disa propinqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
