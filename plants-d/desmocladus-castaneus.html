Desmocladus castaneus

Desmocladus castaneus, commonly known as the redstem sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh environment. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Desmocladus castaneus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
