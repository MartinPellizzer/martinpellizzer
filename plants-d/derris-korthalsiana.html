Derris korthalsiana

Derris korthalsiana, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a toxic compound used in traditional fishing and pest control. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, though they require careful handling due to their potency.

Derris korthalsiana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
