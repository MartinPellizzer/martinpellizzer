Delphinium consolida subsp. consolida

Delphinium consolida subsp.

consolida, commonly known as the tall larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features striking spires of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are characterized by their spurred petals, a distinctive feature of the Delphinium genus.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower gardens.
