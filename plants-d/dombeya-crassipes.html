Dombeya crassipes

Dombeya crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved dombeya, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Dombeya crassipes is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
