Dircaea

Dircaea, commonly known as the white ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Dircaea is considered a conservation concern.
