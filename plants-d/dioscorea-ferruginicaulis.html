Dioscorea ferruginicaulis

Dioscorea ferruginicaulis, commonly known as the iron-stem yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value and potential medicinal properties.

Dioscorea ferruginicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
