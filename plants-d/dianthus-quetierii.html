Dianthus quetierii

Dianthus quetierii, commonly known as the Quetier's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus quetierii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
