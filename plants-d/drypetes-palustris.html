Drypetes palustris

Drypetes palustris, commonly known as the swamp drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

This plant plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its wetland ecosystems.
