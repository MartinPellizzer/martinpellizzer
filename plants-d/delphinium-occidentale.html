Delphinium occidentale

Delphinium occidentale, commonly known as Western larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along streambanks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Delphinium occidentale is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be toxic to livestock.
