Diospyros ferrea var. guineensis

Diospyros ferrea var. guineensis, commonly known as African persimmon, is a species native to West Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has a dense, dark green canopy and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a globose, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a smooth margin.
