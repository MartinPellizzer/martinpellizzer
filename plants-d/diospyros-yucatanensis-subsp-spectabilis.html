Diospyros yucatanensis subsp. spectabilis

Diospyros yucatanensis subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the Yucatan persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its fruit is a small, orange-red drupe that is edible when fully ripe.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
