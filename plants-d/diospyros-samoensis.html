Diospyros samoensis

Diospyros samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Pacific islands of Samoa and American Samoa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in the region for food and cultural practices.
