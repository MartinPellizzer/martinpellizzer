Draba stenoloba var. oligantha

Draba stenoloba var. oligantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky, dry habitats such as slopes and talus areas. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its small white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, and it produces numerous small, hard seeds.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants.
