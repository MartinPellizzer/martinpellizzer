Dracontium foecundum

Dracontium foecundum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their starchy texture.

Due to its rapid growth and ecological significance, it plays an important role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
