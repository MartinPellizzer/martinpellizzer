Drosera parvifolia

Drosera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, sticky leaves covered in glandular hairs that secrete a mucilaginous substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are usually white or pink and appear in late spring to early summer.

Drosera parvifolia is a perennial species that thrives in cool, humid conditions.
