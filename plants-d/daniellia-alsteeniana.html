Daniellia alsteeniana

Daniellia alsteeniana, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to dry environments and often grows in savanna and woodland habitats. It has small, yellow flowers and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

However, it is not widely cultivated and remains relatively understudied in botanical research.
