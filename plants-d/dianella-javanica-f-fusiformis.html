Dianella javanica f. fusiformis

Dianella javanica f. fusiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf pittosporum, is a hybrid form of the native New Zealand plant Dianella javanica.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by blue-black berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.

This variety is well-suited to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
