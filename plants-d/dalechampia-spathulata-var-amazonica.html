Dalechampia spathulata var. amazonica

Dalechampia spathulata var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along stream banks or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
