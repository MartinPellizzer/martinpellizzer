Dischidia bisetulosa

Dischidia bisetulosa, commonly known as the wooly dischidia, is a climbing epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its aerial roots and thick, fleshy stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
