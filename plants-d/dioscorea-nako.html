Dioscorea nako

Dioscorea nako, commonly known as the Nako yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are often used in traditional cuisine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The growth habit of Dioscorea nako makes it well-suited for cultivation in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
