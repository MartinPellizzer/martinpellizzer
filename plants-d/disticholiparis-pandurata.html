Disticholiparis pandurata

Disticholiparis pandurata, commonly known as the pandurata orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences and intricate, often fragrant flower structures. Native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, this species thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among horticulturists and botanists.
