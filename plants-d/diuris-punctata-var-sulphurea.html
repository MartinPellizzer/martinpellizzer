Diuris punctata var. sulphurea

Diuris punctata var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur-tinged spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a single, slender stem with a few narrow, linear leaves and a single, delicate flower spike. The flower has a distinctive, spider-like appearance with a greenish-yellow dorsal sepal and a yellowish-cream labellum. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and requires well-drained conditions.

The plant is often found in open forests and heathlands, blooming during late winter to early spring.
