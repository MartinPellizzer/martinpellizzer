Diospyros rhododendroides

Diospyros rhododendroides, commonly known as the red-osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked stems that become more pronounced with age. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

Diospyros rhododendroides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
