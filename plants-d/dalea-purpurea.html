Dalea purpurea

Dalea purpurea, commonly known as purple prairie clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Dalea purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
