Dioscorea polyantha

Dioscorea polyantha, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by oval, yellowish fruits. Its tubers are widely used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.

The botanical features of Dioscorea polyantha make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
