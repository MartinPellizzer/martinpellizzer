Dendranthema littorale subsp. coreanum

Dendranthema littorale subsp.

coreanum, commonly known as coastal chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its semi-evergreen foliage and profuse display of yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines, showing adaptability to salt spray and wind. It has a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats that stabilize dunes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for coastal vegetation and erosion control.
