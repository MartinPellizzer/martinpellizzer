Diplazium pulchrum

Diplazium pulchrum, commonly known as the beautiful fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often adorned with fine, hairy structures along the veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce both sterile and fertile fronds.

Diplazium pulchrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
