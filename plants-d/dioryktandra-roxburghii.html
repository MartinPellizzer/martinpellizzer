Dioryktandra roxburghii

Dioryktandra roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.
