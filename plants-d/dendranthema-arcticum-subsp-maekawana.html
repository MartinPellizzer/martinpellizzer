Dendranthema arcticum subsp. maekawana

Dendranthema arcticum subsp.

maekawana, commonly known as the Arctic chamomile, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions. It features small, white to pale yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
