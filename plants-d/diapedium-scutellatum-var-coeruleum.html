Diapedium scutellatum var. coeruleum

Diapedium scutellatum var. coeruleum, commonly known as the blue star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring, typically from May to June. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its stems are slender and branching. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers are arranged in loose clusters, adding a delicate and elegant appearance to the landscape.
