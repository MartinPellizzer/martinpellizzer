Dyckia lunaris

Dyckia lunaris, commonly known as the Moon's Dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces striking white to pale pink flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. Dyckia lunaris is adapted to grow in sunny, well-drained environments, often in rocky or sandy soils.

It is a relatively slow-growing plant that thrives in warm, dry climates with minimal water requirements.
