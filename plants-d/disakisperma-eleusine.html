Disakisperma eleusine

Disakisperma eleusine, commonly known as the African grass or African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, often with a bluish-green hue.

Disakisperma eleusine is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with low rainfall.
