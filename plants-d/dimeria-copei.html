Dimeria copei

Dimeria copei, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Dimeria copei is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
