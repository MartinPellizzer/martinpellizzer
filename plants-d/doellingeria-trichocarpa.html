Doellingeria trichocarpa

Doellingeria trichocarpa, commonly known as the common arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Doellingeria trichocarpa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
