Davallodes urceolata

Davallodes urceolata, commonly known as the urn-shaped leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often clustered in groups of three. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by unique, urn-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
