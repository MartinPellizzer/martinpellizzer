Deutzia parviflora var. bungei

Deutzia parviflora var. bungei, commonly known as the Bunge's deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
