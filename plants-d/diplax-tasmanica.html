Diplax tasmanica

Diplax tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Diplax tasmanica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
