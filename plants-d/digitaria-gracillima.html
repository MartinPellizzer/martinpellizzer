Digitaria gracillima

Digitaria gracillima, commonly known as slender digitgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or tufts. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and disturbed areas. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Digitaria gracillima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
