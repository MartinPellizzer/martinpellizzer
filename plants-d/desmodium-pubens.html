Desmodium pubens

Desmodium pubens, commonly known as hairy burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces hairy, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. Desmodium pubens is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its growth habit makes it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
