Dianthus tabrisianus

Dianthus tabrisianus, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Dianthus tabrisianus is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
