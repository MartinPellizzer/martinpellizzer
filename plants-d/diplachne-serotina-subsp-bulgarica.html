Diplachne serotina subsp. bulgarica

Diplachne serotina subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in regions with temperate climates and well-drained soils.
