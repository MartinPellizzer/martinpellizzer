Desmodium caffrum

Desmodium caffrum, commonly known as African needlegrass, is a perennial legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on erect, branching inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in slender, needle-like pods that mature into sharp, pointed structures.

Desmodium caffrum is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
