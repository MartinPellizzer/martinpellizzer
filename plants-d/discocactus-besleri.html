Discocactus besleri

Discocactus besleri, commonly known as the Besler's discocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, disc-like stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its stems are covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss.

Discocactus besleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
