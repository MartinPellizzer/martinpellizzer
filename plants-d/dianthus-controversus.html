Dianthus controversus

Dianthus controversus, commonly known as the purple campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are slender and branching.

Dianthus controversus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
