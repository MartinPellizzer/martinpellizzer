Delphinium multiplex

Delphinium multiplex, commonly known as the clustered larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear dense clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
