Digitalis cariensis subsp. ikarica

Digitalis cariensis subsp.

ikarica, commonly known as the New Zealand foxglove, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Like other members of the Digitalis genus, it contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic but have been studied for their medicinal potential.
