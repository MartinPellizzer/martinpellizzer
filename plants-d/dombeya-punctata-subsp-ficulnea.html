Dombeya punctata subsp. ficulnea

Dombeya punctata subsp.

ficulnea, commonly known as the woolly dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
