Dalrympelea trifoliata

Dalrympelea trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to the Southwest of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces striking, bottle-shaped red or orange flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets, and has a woody structure with spiny stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Dalrympelea trifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
