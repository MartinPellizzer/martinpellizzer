Diuris chryseopsis

Diuris chryseopsis, commonly known as the golden-veined spider-orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and striking, spider-like flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, with intricate veining that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forest or heathland environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens due to its unique appearance.
