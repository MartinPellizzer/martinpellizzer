Dracophyllum thiebautii

Dracophyllum thiebautii, commonly known as the Tasmanian heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the cool, moist forests of Tasmania.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Dracophyllum thiebautii is adapted to cool, humid climates and is an important component of Tasmania's unique flora.
