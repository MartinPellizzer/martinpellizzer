Dianthus hispidus

Dianthus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Dianthus hispidus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
