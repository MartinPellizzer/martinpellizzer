Dissotis rotundifolia var. fruticosa

Dissotis rotundifolia var. fruticosa, commonly known as the clustered arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features a bushy growth habit with dark green, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible tubers that resemble those of arrowroot. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
