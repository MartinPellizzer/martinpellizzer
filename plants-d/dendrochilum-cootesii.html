Dendrochilum cootesii

Dendrochilum cootesii, commonly known as the Cootes' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

Dendrochilum cootesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
