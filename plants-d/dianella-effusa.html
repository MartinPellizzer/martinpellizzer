Dianella effusa

Dianella effusa, commonly known as the broadleaf flax lily, is a perennial evergreen herb native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, dark green leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in panicles at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Dianella effusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscaping.
