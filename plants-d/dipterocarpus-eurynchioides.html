Dipterocarpus eurynchioides

Dipterocarpus eurynchioides, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood and tall, straight trunk. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to forest structure and biodiversity.

The species is also valued for its durable timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
