Dischidia pruinosa

Dischidia pruinosa, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
