Diospyros nemorosa

Diospyros nemorosa, commonly known as the small persimmon or wild persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. The fruit is edible, though it is often bitter unless fully ripe, and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
