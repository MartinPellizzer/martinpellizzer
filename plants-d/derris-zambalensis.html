Derris zambalensis

Derris zambalensis, commonly known as the Zambal derris, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a natural insecticide used in traditional pest control. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties.

Derris zambalensis thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
