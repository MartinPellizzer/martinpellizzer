Deyeuxia beyrichiana

Deyeuxia beyrichiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass native to wetland environments in Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers, where it thrives in saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Deyeuxia beyrichiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
