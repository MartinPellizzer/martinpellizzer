Dryopteris patens f. stipularis

Dryopteris patens f. stipularis, commonly known as the stipular maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of its parent species, Dryopteris patens. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a feathery appearance, with the distinctive stipules that give it its specific name.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
