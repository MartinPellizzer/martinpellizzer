Danthonia buekeana var. minor

Danthonia buekeana var. minor, commonly known as the small danthonia, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and fine, tufted foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
