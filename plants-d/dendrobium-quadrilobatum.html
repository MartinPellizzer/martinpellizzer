Dendrobium quadrilobatum

Dendrobium quadrilobatum, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium quadrilobatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
