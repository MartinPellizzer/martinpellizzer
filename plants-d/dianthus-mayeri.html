Dianthus mayeri

Dianthus mayeri, commonly known as the Mayer's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits called achenes.

Dianthus mayeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
