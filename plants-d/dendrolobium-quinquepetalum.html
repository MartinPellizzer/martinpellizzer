Dendrolobium quinquepetalum

Dendrolobium quinquepetalum, commonly known as the five-petaled acacia, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is adapted to poor soils, making it resilient in arid environments.

The plant is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
