Diospyros coriacea

Diospyros coriacea, commonly known as rough-shelled ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its rough, thick bark and dark, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant component of its native ecosystems.
