Dichorisandra villosula

Dichorisandra villosula, commonly known as the velvetleaf cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant that features a stout, unbranched stem and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically a deep green and have a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
