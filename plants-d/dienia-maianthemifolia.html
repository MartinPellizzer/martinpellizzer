Dienia maianthemifolia

Dienia maianthemifolia, commonly known as the clustered dienia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dienia maianthemifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
