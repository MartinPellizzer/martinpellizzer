Dendrobium gerlandianum

Dendrobium gerlandianum, commonly known as the Gerland's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a branched stem system that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium gerlandianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
