Dodonaea viscosa var. minor

Dodonaea viscosa var. minor, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, woody fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or saline soils.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in coastal and semi-arid environments.
