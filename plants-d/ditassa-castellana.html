Ditassa castellana

Ditassa castellana, commonly known as the Castilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.
