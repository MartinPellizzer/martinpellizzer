Diospyros rufa

Diospyros rufa, commonly known as the red persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Diospyros rufa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
