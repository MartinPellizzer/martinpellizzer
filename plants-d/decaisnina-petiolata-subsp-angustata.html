Decaisnina petiolata subsp. angustata

Decaisnina petiolata subsp.

angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved decaisnina, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
