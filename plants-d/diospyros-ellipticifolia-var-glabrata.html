Diospyros ellipticifolia var. glabrata

Diospyros ellipticifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaf persimmon, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glabrous leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.
