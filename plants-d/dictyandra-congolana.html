Dictyandra congolana

Dictyandra congolana, commonly known as the Congo arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are edible and resemble those of taro. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an inflorescence called a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, humid environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.
