Drypetes mindanaensis

Drypetes mindanaensis, commonly known as Mindanao drypetes, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in lowland to montane forests and is valued for its timber and traditional uses.
