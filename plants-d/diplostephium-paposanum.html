Diplostephium paposanum

Diplostephium paposanum, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red composite flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and erect.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance ornamental plants.
