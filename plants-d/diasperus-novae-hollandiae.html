Diasperus novae-hollandiae

Diasperus novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the New Holland thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, nutlet-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
