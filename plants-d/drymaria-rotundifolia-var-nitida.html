Drymaria rotundifolia var. nitida

Drymaria rotundifolia var. nitida, commonly known as the silky sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
