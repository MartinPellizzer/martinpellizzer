Dichapetalum zeylanicum

Dichapetalum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
