Diospyros bracteata

Diospyros bracteata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Diospyros bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and the nutritional content of its fruit.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
