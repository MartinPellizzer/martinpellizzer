Deyeuxia erythrostachya

Deyeuxia erythrostachya, commonly known as red-stalked false rice, is a grass species native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in wetland or marshy habitats, often growing in dense clusters.

Deyeuxia erythrostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a decorative grass in moist landscapes.
