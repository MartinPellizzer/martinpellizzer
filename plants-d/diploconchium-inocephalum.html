Diploconchium inocephalum

Diploconchium inocephalum, commonly known as the broad-leafed starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate to ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowering stems rise above the leaves, bearing small, star-shaped yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The species is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and its role in supporting local insect populations.
