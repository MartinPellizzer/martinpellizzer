Delphinium bracteosum var. ruprechtii

Delphinium bracteosum var. ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
