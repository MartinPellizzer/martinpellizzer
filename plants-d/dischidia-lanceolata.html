Dischidia lanceolata

Dischidia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf fig.

It is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ability to climb make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
