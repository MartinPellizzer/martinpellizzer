Drypetes gerrardii var. angustifolia

Drypetes gerrardii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf drypetes, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
