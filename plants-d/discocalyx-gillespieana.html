Discocalyx gillespieana

Discocalyx gillespieana, commonly known as the desert dudleya, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its rosette growth form helps it resist wind and reduce water loss.

Discocalyx gillespieana is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
