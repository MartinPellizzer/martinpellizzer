Delphinium peregrinum var. halteratum

Delphinium peregrinum var. halteratum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is adapted to grow in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and toxic compounds.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
