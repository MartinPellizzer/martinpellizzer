Dipsacus laciniatus

Dipsacus laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaved teasel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spiky, cone-like flower heads that are covered in bracts, which are modified leaves. It is a member of the burdock family (Araliaceae) and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Dipsacus laciniatus is known for its historical use in textile production, where its prickly flowers were used to raise the nap on woolen cloth.
