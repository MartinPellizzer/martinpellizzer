Dactylorhiza cordigera var. rhodopeia

Dactylorhiza cordigera var. rhodopeia, commonly known as the Rhodope orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain forests and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike of flowers with numerous blooms.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
