Daphne oleoides var. kurdica

Daphne oleoides var. kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iraq.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive, waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique fragrance and winter interest.
