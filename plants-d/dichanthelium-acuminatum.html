Dichanthelium acuminatum

Dichanthelium acuminatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a pointed tip, and its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are followed by narrow, awned spikelets that aid in seed dispersal.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife in its native range.
