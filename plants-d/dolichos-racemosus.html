Dolichos racemosus

Dolichos racemosus, commonly known as the climbing bean or wild cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Dolichos racemosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
