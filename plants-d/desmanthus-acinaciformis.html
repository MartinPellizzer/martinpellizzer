Desmanthus acinaciformis

Desmanthus acinaciformis, commonly known as the swordleaf desmanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a short lifespan and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its unique sword-shaped leaves and early blooming period make it a distinctive species in its native range.
