Draba aizoides f. leiocarpa

Draba aizoides f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white-flowered rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the species is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.

The f. leiocarpa form is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, setting it apart from other forms of Draba aizoides.
