Dianthus puberulus

Dianthus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy pinks, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
