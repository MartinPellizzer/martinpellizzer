Dryopteris stipitata

Dryopteris stipitata, commonly known as the crested wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, upright fronds that often have a swollen base, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, toothed pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

Dryopteris stipitata is known for its adaptability and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
