Didymoplexis obreniformis

Didymoplexis obreniformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant orange or red hues with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant has strap-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
