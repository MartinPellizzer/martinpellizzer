Diasperus arboreus

Diasperus arboreus, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The tree has a rounded crown and can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark that peels in thin layers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
