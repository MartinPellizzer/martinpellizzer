Daphne linearifolia

Daphne linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Daphne linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
