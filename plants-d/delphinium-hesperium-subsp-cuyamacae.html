Delphinium hesperium subsp. cuyamacae

Delphinium hesperium subsp.

cuyamacae, commonly known as the Cuyama larkspur, is a perennial herb native to California. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy, spurred blue to purple flowers in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, with a hairy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
