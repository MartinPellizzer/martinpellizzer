Diospyros mespiliformis

Diospyros mespiliformis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruit is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
