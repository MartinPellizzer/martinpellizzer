Dianthus gelidus

Dianthus gelidus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Dianthus gelidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
