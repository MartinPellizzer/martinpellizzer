Diospyros compacta

Diospyros compacta, commonly known as the dwarf persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a dark red or purple color.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
