Drepanostachyum patellare

Drepanostachyum patellare, commonly known as the Patellate grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often arranged in a distinctive, fan-like pattern.

Drepanostachyum patellare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
