Dichanthium affine

Dichanthium affine, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or purple flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its low-growing habit.

Dichanthium affine is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and soil-stabilizing properties.
