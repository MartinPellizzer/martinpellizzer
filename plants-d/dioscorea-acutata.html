Dioscorea acutata

Dioscorea acutata, commonly known as sharpleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized as a food source in some cultures.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
