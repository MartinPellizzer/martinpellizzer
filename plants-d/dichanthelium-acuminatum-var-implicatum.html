Dichanthelium acuminatum var. implicatum

Dichanthelium acuminatum var. implicatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a pointed tip, and its inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of spikelets. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its propagation in wetland environments.

This variety is often found in association with other wetland grasses and forbs, playing a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
