Dudleya nevadensis

Dudleya nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
