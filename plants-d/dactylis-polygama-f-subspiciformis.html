Dactylis polygama f. subspiciformis

Dactylis polygama f. subspiciformis, commonly known as the slender spike grass, is a botanical variant of the species Dactylis polygama.

It is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescences that are typically shorter and more compact compared to the typical form. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in ecological restoration projects.
