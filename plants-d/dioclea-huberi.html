Dioclea huberi

Dioclea huberi, commonly known as the pink spider-plant, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing habit.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
