Diospyros caloneura

Diospyros caloneura, commonly known as the slender persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known genera like Diospyros and Strychnos. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible berries that turn red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
