Dalbergia congestiflora

Dalbergia congestiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its rich tone and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

The tree is also notable for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in native forests.
