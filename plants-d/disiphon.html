Disiphon

Disiphon, commonly known as the false mallow, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plants typically have hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Disiphon species are native to various regions in Africa and are often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
