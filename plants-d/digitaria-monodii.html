Digitaria monodii

Digitaria monodii, commonly known as the African bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Digitaria monodii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
