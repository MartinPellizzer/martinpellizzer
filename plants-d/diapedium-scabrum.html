Diapedium scabrum

Diapedium scabrum, commonly known as the rough rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided and covered in a rough, scaly texture, giving it its scientific name.

Diapedium scabrum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
