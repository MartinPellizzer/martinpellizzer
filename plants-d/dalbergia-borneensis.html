Dalbergia borneensis

Dalbergia borneensis, commonly known as Bornean rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Borneo.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated pods. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its high economic value, it is also a species of concern for conservation efforts.
