Danthonia purpurascens

Danthonia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple muirgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often exhibit a reddish-purple hue, especially during the flowering season. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Danthonia purpurascens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
