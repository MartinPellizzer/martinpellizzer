Delphinium cinereum

Delphinium cinereum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a tall perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Delphinium cinereum is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shady to partially sunny conditions.
