Dryobalanops junghuhnii

Dryobalanops junghuhnii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
