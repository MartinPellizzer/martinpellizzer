Dicranopygium

Dicranopygium, commonly known as the common haircap moss, is a genus of mosses belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and the presence of long, slender leaf cells that give it a distinctive appearance. The plants typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Dicranopygium species are known for their ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and their role in soil stabilization.

They are also important in the ecological succession of disturbed habitats.
