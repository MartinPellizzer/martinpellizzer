Dioscorea pittieri

Dioscorea pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican yam or yam bean, is a perennial vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The botanical features of D. pittieri make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
