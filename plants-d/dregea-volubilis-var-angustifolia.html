Dregea volubilis var. angustifolia

Dregea volubilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dregea, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its fruits are elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
