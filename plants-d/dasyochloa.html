Dasyochloa

Dasyochloa, commonly known as the beardgrass, is a genus of grasses native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and often forms clumps or mats. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Dasyochloa species are often found in dry, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and sandy soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
