Dipteryx cordata

Dipteryx cordata, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces woody pods containing the edible Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a dense, buttressed root system. It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its economic importance due to the commercial value of its seeds.
