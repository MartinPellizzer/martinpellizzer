Dianthera sonorae

Dianthera sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran dianthera, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Dianthera sonorae plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is an indicator species of desert ecosystems.
