Dendrocalamus multispiculatus

Dendrocalamus multispiculatus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of over 30 meters. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Dendrocalamus multispiculatus is widely used in construction and as a source of raw material for various industrial applications.
