Digitalis gloxinioides

Digitalis gloxinioides, commonly known as the African foxglove, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces a rosette of basal leaves and has a long flowering period, typically from late winter to early spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
