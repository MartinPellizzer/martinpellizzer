Dupatya gardneriana

Dupatya gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Dupatya gardneriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
