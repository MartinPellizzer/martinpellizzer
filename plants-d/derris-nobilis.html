Derris nobilis

Derris nobilis, commonly known as the noble derris, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which is used in traditional pesticides.
