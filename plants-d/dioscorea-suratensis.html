Dioscorea suratensis

Dioscorea suratensis, commonly known as the Surat yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are typically cylindrical and have a rough, brownish skin with a sweet, starchy interior.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in various herbal remedies.
