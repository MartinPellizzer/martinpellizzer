Dipcadi umbonatum

Dipcadi umbonatum, commonly known as the horned squill, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive, swollen base that resembles a horn, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers with six petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, growing from the base of the plant.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
