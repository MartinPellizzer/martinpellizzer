Dichrostachys humbertii

Dichrostachys humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's burr legume, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in poor nutrient conditions.

The species is also valued for its potential use in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
