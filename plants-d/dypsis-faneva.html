Dypsis faneva

Dypsis faneva, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a slow-growing succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a distinctive, tree-like structure with a short, thick trunk and a crown of spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

Dypsis faneva is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
