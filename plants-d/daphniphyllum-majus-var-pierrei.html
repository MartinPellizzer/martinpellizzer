Daphniphyllum majus var. pierrei

Daphniphyllum majus var. pierrei, commonly known as the variegated silver laurel, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often display striking variegation of white and green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries in autumn. It is evergreen and prefers cool, moist environments with partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
