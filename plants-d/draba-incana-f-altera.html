Draba incana f. altera

Draba incana f. altera, commonly known as the alpine whitlowgrass, is a variant of the common whitlowgrass.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically hairy and adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
