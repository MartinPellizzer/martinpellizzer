Dysophylla quadrifolia var. velutina

Dysophylla quadrifolia var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf dysophylla, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
