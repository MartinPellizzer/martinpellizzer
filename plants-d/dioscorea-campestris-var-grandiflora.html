Dioscorea campestris var. grandiflora

Dioscorea campestris var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its large, tuberous roots, which can grow to significant sizes, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are small and usually yellow or greenish in color.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional and economic importance.
