Doliocarpus pipolyi

Doliocarpus pipolyi, commonly known as the Pipla tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Doliocarpus pipolyi make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
