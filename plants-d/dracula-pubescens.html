Dracula pubescens

Dracula pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dracula, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica and Panama.

It is characterized by its unique, pendulous flowers that resemble small, delicate bats, giving it its common name. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat. It thrives in high elevations with cool temperatures and high humidity.

Dracula pubescens is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
