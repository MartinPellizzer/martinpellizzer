Dalbergia lakhonensis

Dalbergia lakhonensis, commonly known as the Lakhon rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and for horticultural purposes.
