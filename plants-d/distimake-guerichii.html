Distimake guerichii

Distimake guerichii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of North America.

It features delicate, feathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a unique textural contrast. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its historical significance in traditional medicine among indigenous communities.
