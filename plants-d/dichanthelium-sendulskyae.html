Dichanthelium sendulskyae

Dichanthelium sendulskyae, commonly known as the Sendulsky's panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three bract-like structures. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral morphology and ecological adaptability in wetland environments.
