Dichromena caespitosum

Dichromena caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted dichromena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a tendency to form dense, low-growing mats.

Dichromena caespitosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
