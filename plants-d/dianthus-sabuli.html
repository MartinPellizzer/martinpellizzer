Dianthus sabuli

Dianthus sabuli, commonly known as sand pink, is a flowering plant native to sandy soils in the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its arid habitat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy environments. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions.

Dianthus sabuli is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants known for their showy blooms.
