Dolichos stipulosus f. angustifoliolata

Dolichos stipulosus f. angustifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dolichos, is a variant of the species Dolichos stipulosus, characterized by its narrow, elongated leaflets.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous plants that often have compound leaves and produce pods. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with the terminal leaflet being smaller than the lateral ones, and it produces small, yellow flowers.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
