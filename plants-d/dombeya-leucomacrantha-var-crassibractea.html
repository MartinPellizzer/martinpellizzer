Dombeya leucomacrantha var. crassibractea

Dombeya leucomacrantha var. crassibractea, commonly known as the white-flowered dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
