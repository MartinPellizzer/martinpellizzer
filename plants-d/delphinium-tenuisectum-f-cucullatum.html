Delphinium tenuisectum f. cucullatum

Delphinium tenuisectum f. cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded larkspur, is a horticultural form of the delphinium genus characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This variety features elongated, hood-like bracts that enclose the flower buds, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically produces tall, spindly stems with clusters of blue to purple flowers. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive.
