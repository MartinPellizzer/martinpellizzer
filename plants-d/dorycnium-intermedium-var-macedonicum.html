Dorycnium intermedium var. macedonicum

Dorycnium intermedium var. macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian sainfoin, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its stout, woody stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
