Draba ciliata

Draba ciliata, commonly known as the fringed rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are linear and often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers in early spring. Draba ciliata is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia.

Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and are adapted to pollination by insects.
