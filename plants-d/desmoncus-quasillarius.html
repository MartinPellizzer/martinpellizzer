Desmoncus quasillarius

Desmoncus quasillarius, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and ethereal flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, delicate flowers that resemble translucent ghosts, hence its common name. The plant relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, making it highly dependent on its environment. It thrives in shaded, humid conditions and is often found growing on the trunks of trees.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
