Dianthus collinus var. scaber

Dianthus collinus var. scaber, commonly known as the rough rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Dianthus collinus var. scaber is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
