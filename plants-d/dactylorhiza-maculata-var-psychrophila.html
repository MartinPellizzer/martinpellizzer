Dactylorhiza maculata var. psychrophila

Dactylorhiza maculata var. psychrophila, commonly known as the alpine marsh orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species adapted to cold, moist environments.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in nutrient-poor, peaty soils. It is often found in wet meadows and bogs, where it forms dense colonies.
