Dendrobium × xanthocentrum

Dendrobium × xanthocentrum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which bloom in clusters along its cane-like stems. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dendrobium × xanthocentrum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
