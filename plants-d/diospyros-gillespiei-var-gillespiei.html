Diospyros gillespiei var. gillespiei

Diospyros gillespiei var. gillespiei, commonly known as the Gillespie persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, astringent flavor.
