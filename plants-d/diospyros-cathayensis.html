Diospyros cathayensis

Diospyros cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, sweet, and sticky fruits resembling dates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

The tree also has cultural significance in traditional medicine and horticulture.
