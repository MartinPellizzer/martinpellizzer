Deutzia scabra var. typica

Deutzia scabra var. typica, commonly known as the rough deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
