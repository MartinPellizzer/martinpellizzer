Dianella caerulea var. cinerascens

Dianella caerulea var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silver-leaved flax lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are silver-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
