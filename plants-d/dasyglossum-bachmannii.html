Dasyglossum bachmannii

Dasyglossum bachmannii, commonly known as the Bachmann's dasyglossum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
