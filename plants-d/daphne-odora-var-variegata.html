Daphne odora var. variegata

Daphne odora var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated winter daphne, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late winter to early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling and is often grown in containers to prevent root damage.
