Dianthus pelviformis

Dianthus pelviformis, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are adapted to thrive in rocky and arid environments. It is often found growing on limestone or sandy soils in open habitats.

Dianthus pelviformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
