Diasperus pectinatus

Diasperus pectinatus, commonly known as the hairy plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color.

Diasperus pectinatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
