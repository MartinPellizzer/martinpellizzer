Diosma ambigua

Diosma ambigua, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often with a slight purplish tinge. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Diosma ambigua is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
