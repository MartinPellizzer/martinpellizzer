Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. triphylla

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

triphylla, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of spotted, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It has a complex root system and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

triphylla is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
