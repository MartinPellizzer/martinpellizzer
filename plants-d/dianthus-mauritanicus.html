Dianthus mauritanicus

Dianthus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Moroccan carnation, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces fragrant, five-petaled flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Dianthus mauritanicus is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
