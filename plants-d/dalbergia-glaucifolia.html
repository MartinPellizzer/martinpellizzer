Dalbergia glaucifolia

Dalbergia glaucifolia, commonly known as the white-leafed rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
