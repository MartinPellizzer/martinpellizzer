Diasperus llanosii

Diasperus llanosii, commonly known as the Llanos grass, is a perennial grass native to the grasslands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal drought conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of the ecosystems in its native range.
