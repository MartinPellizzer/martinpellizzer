Diasperus platycalyx

Diasperus platycalyx, commonly known as the broad-calyx dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine white pubescence. Its fruit is a small, oval-shaped achene with a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
