Delphinium dasycarpum var. elisabethae

Delphinium dasycarpum var. elisabethae, commonly known as the Elisabetha delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in raceme-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves with a coarse texture, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
