Distichlis marginata

Distichlis marginata, commonly known as sea bulrush, is a halophyte native to coastal regions of the western United States.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often grayish-green due to a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Distichlis marginata plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
