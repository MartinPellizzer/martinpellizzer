Dactyliophora globiflora

Dactyliophora globiflora, commonly known as the globe-flowered dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces a single, globe-like flower head. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dactyliophora globiflora is often found in fynbos vegetation and is adapted to arid conditions.
