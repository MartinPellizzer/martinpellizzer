Dianthus tymphresteus

Dianthus tymphresteus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are typically linear to lance-shaped, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Dianthus tymphresteus is a hardy perennial that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
