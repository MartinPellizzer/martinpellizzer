Diospyros lotus var. brideliifolia

Diospyros lotus var. brideliifolia, commonly known as the Brideliifolia persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often pubescent beneath, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits, which are typically edible after a period of ripening. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
