Diasperus choretrodes

Diasperus choretrodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its narrow, strap-like leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
