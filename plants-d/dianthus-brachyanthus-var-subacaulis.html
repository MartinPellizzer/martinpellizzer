Dianthus brachyanthus var. subacaulis

Dianthus brachyanthus var. subacaulis, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features vibrant, double-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces flowers throughout the summer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its compact size and ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
