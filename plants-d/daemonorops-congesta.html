Daemonorops congesta

Daemonorops congesta, commonly known as the clustered areca palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense clusters of pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

The fruit of Daemonorops congesta is small and fibrous, often used in local cuisines.
