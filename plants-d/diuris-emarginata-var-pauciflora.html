Diuris emarginata var. pauciflora

Diuris emarginata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf greenhood, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a compact, upright growth habit with narrow, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green with a distinctive hood-like labellum, and it blooms during late summer to autumn.

This variety is noted for its low-flowering habit, which makes it a rare and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
