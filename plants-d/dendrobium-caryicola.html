Dendrobium caryicola

Dendrobium caryicola, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit and the presence of numerous small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a contrasting red or purple lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium caryicola is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
