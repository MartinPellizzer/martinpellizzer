Dendrobium begoniicarpum

Dendrobium begoniicarpum, commonly known as the begonia orchid, is a rare and unique hybrid orchid that combines features of both orchids and begonias.

It is a sympodial epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns and a begonia-like appearance. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
