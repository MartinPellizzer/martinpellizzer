Dicranolepis glandulosa

Dicranolepis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rock fern, is a perennial fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit. The plant features glossy, dark green fronds with a distinctive glandular surface that gives it its common name. Its rhizomes are wiry and often form dense mats on rocky substrates.
