Dipcadi wentzelianum

Dipcadi wentzelianum, commonly known as the Zanzibar lily, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa, particularly Zanzibar and the nearby islands. The plant features strap-like, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
