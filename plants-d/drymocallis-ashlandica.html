Drymocallis ashlandica

Drymocallis ashlandica, commonly known as the Ashland goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The Ashland goldenrod is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
