Dalea parviflora

Dalea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Dalea parviflora is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
