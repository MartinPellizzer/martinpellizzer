Diplopeltis intermedia var. incana

Diplopeltis intermedia var. incana, commonly known as the silver-stemmed devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pale, almost silvery stems and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
