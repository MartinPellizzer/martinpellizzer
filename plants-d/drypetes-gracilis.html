Drypetes gracilis

Drypetes gracilis, commonly known as the slender drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
