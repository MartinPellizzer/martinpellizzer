Disa sanguinea

Disa sanguinea, commonly known as the red disa, is a striking orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vivid red petals and a contrasting white or yellow lip, which make it highly ornamental. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in full sun. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Disa sanguinea is a perennial herb with a short, stout stem and is well adapted to its arid environment.
