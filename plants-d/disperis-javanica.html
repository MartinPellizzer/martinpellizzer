Disperis javanica

Disperis javanica, commonly known as the Java disperis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Disperis javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
