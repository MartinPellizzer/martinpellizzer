Delphinium henryi

Delphinium henryi, commonly known as Henry's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-green color.

Delphinium henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
