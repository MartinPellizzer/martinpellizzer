Ditassa bolivarensis

Ditassa bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the high-altitude environments of its native range.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
