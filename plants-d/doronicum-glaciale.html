Doronicum glaciale

Doronicum glaciale, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of dark green sepals. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Doronicum glaciale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
