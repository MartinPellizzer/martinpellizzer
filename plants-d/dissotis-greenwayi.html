Dissotis greenwayi

Dissotis greenwayi, commonly known as the greenway's mistflower, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands of northern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features delicate, white, spider-like flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.

Its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
