Diplotaxis viminea var. hiemalis

Diplotaxis viminea var. hiemalis, commonly known as winter heliotrope, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.

This variety is a hardy plant that can withstand colder temperatures compared to other species in the genus.
