Delphinium pubescens var. dissitiflorum

Delphinium pubescens var. dissitiflorum, commonly known as the woolly larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
