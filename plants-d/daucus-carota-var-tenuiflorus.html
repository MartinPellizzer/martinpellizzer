Daucus carota var. tenuiflorus

Daucus carota var. tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered carrot, is a variant of the common carrot with distinctive botanical features.

It is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The flowers are small and clustered in umbels, with the inflorescence being notably slender compared to the typical carrot. It produces small, yellowish fruits known as schizocarps, which disperse via the wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
