Dicoma megacephala

Dicoma megacephala, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with small, narrow leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Dicoma megacephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
