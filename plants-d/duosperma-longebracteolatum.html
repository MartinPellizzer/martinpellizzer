Duosperma longebracteolatum

Duosperma longebracteolatum, commonly known as the long-bract dudleya, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family, characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and ability to thrive in dry conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of thick, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in moisture retention. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are typically pale yellow to white and are borne on short, erect stems.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
