Dichopsis calophylla

Dichopsis calophylla, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant is typically evergreen and prefers well-drained soil and indirect sunlight. Its botanical name reflects its leaf structure, with "calophylla" referring to its beautiful leaves.

Dichopsis calophylla is widely cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
