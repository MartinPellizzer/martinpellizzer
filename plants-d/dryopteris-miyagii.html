Dryopteris miyagii

Dryopteris miyagii, commonly known as the Miyagi wood fern, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Dryopteris miyagii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
