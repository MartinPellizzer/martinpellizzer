Diplotaxis catholica var. bipinnatifida

Diplotaxis catholica var. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves with a bipinnate structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
