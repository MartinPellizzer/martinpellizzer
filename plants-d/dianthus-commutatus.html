Dianthus commutatus

Dianthus commutatus, commonly known as the fringed pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers with fringed petals that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer.

Dianthus commutatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
