Desmodium glaucophyllum

Desmodium glaucophyllum, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy blue-violet flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
