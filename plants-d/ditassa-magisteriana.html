Ditassa magisteriana

Ditassa magisteriana, commonly known as the sacred forest tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant plays a significant role in local ecosystems and cultural practices.
