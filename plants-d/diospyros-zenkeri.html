Diospyros zenkeri

Diospyros zenkeri, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible, though they are often consumed by animals rather than humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
