Daphniphyllum bengalense

Daphniphyllum bengalense, commonly known as the Bengal holly, is a evergreen shrub native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other on the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red berries that are an attractive feature. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
