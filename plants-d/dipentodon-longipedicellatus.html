Dipentodon longipedicellatus

Dipentodon longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-pedicellate fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often submerged in water. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in groups called sori. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams.

Its delicate appearance and unique reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
