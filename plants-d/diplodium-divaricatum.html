Diplodium divaricatum

Diplodium divaricatum, commonly known as the variable diplodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Diplodium divaricatum is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
