Dichanthelium webberianum

Dichanthelium webberianum, commonly known as Webber's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, and it is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils.

Dichanthelium webberianum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
