Dendrochilum oxylobum

Dendrochilum oxylobum, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green and broad, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
