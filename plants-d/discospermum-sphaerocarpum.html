Discospermum sphaerocarpum

Discospermum sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globe-shaped starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, round, yellowish fruit with a star-shaped pattern on its surface. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
