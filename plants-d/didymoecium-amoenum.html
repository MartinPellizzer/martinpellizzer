Didymoecium amoenum

Didymoecium amoenum, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, silvery-gray inflorescence and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a slight sheen.

Didymoecium amoenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
