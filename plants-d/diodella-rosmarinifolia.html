Diodella rosmarinifolia

Diodella rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Diodella rosmarinifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
