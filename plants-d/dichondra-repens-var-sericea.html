Dichondra repens var. sericea

Dichondra repens var. sericea, commonly known as silverleaf dichondra, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature.

This variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
