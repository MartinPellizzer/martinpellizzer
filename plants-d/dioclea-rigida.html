Dioclea rigida

Dioclea rigida, commonly known as the hardy climbing bean, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruits are fibrous pods containing edible seeds, similar to those of common beans.

Dioclea rigida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in certain regions.
