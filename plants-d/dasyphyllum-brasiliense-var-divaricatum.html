Dasyphyllum brasiliense var. divaricatum

Dasyphyllum brasiliense var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Brazilian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its stems are often branched and can grow vertically or lie prostrate, depending on environmental conditions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
