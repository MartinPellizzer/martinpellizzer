Drabella nemoralis

Drabella nemoralis, commonly known as the common draba, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. This herbaceous perennial typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, often in shaded or semi-shaded areas. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
