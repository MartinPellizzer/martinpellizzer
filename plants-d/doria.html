Doria

Doria, commonly known as the Doria's daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and chrysanthemums. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with white ray florets, often blooming in the spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
