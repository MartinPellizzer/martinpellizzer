Dissotis aprica

Dissotis aprica, commonly known as the golden-eyed grass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
