Dioscorea paniculata

Dioscorea paniculata, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
