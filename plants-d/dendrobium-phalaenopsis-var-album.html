Dendrobium phalaenopsis var. album

Dendrobium phalaenopsis var. album, commonly known as the white phalaenopsis orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that produce clusters of fragrant, white flowers with a velvety texture. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining potting media. Its flowers bloom repeatedly throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.

The varietal name "album" refers to its predominantly white floral coloration.
