Distichia acicularis

Distichia acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf distichia, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-yellow and appear in the spring.

Distichia acicularis is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental foliage.
