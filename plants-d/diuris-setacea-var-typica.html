Diuris setacea var. typica

Diuris setacea var. typica, commonly known as the slender-leafed spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its distinctive, spider-like orchid flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant thrives in heathland and woodland habitats, often growing in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species, with flowering occurring mainly during the autumn season.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
