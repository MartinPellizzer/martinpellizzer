Draba oblongata

Draba oblongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. The plant is herbaceous and typically grows to a height of about 5-15 centimeters.

It is known for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
