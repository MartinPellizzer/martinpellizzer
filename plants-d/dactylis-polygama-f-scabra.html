Dactylis polygama f. scabra

Dactylis polygama f. scabra, commonly known as the scabrous cat's ear, is a hirsute perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and inflorescences that consist of clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are often described as being covered in fine, stiff hairs, which contribute to its common name.

The species is noted for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
