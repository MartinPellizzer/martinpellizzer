Dyssodia aurea var. polychaeta

Dyssodia aurea var. polychaeta, commonly known as the golden dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
