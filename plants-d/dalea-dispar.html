Dalea dispar

Dalea dispar, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, cylindrical seed pods that split open when mature.

Dalea dispar is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
