Diospyros pahangensis

Diospyros pahangensis, commonly known as the Pahang persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are similar in appearance to common persimmons.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
