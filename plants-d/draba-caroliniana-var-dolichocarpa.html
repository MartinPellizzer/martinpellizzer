Draba caroliniana var. dolichocarpa

Draba caroliniana var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-sepal mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, and its flowers are usually pale yellow or white.

The variety dolichocarpa is distinguished by its elongated sepals, which set it apart from other varieties of Draba caroliniana.
