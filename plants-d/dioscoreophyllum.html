Dioscoreophyllum

Dioscoreophyllum, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are often used as a starchy food source in some regions.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.
