Dianthus pseudobarbatus

Dianthus pseudobarbatus, commonly known as the fringed pinks, is a flowering plant belonging to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-purple flowers with fringed petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a low-growing perennial that often forms dense mats, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
