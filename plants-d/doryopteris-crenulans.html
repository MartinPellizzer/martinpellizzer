Doryopteris crenulans

Doryopteris crenulans, commonly known as the crested water fern, is a perennial fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, frond-like structures that resemble leaves, with a serrated margin and a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its reproductive structures are spore-bearing, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens or as a decorative plant in humid climates.
