Deguelia marginata

Deguelia marginata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a shower of golden droplets. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses compound leaves with a distinctive margin that is often wavy or notched. Its root system contains alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Deguelia marginata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
