Dipodophyllum diguetii

Dipodophyllum diguetii, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species that produces a distinctive, waxy fruit resembling a buttercup. The plant's botanical features include compound leaves with a hairy texture and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its fruit, which contains a single seed, is adapted for dispersal by animals.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in arid environments, showcasing its resilience and ecological significance.
