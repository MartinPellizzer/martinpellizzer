Diplazium axillare

Diplazium axillare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf staghorn fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizome and the formation of rosettes of lanceolate, leathery leaves. The fronds are typically dark green and have a rough texture, with a distinctive staghorn-like appearance in some varieties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens.
