Drymaria confusa

Drymaria confusa, commonly known as the clustered blueweed, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, spreading stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Drymaria confusa is notable for its ability to thrive in wet environments and its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
