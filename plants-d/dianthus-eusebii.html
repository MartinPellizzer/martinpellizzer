Dianthus eusebii

Dianthus eusebii, commonly known as the Christmas carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter and early spring. The plant features dense, compact growth with bright pink, red, or white petals, often adorned with a dark central blotch. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy.

Dianthus eusebii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
