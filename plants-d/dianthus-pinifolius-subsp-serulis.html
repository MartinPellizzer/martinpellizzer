Dianthus pinifolius subsp. serulis

Dianthus pinifolius subsp.

serulis, commonly known as the prickly rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy, needle-like leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
