Dendrobium treacherianum

Dendrobium treacherianum, commonly known as the Black Orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety pseudobulbs and striking, long-lasting flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant thrives in high humidity and bright, indirect light, often growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is considered a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.
