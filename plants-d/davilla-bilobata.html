Davilla bilobata

Davilla bilobata, commonly known as the two-lobed evergreen, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
