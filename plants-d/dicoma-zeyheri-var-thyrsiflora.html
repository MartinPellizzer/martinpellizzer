Dicoma zeyheri var. thyrsiflora

Dicoma zeyheri var. thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, sweet scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters called thyrses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dicoma zeyheri var. thyrsiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
