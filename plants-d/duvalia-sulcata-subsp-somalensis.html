Duvalia sulcata subsp. somalensis

Duvalia sulcata subsp.

somalensis, commonly known as the Somali duvalia, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
