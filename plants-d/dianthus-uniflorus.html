Dianthus uniflorus

Dianthus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces solitary, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its flowers consist of five petals that are often ruffled or frilled. It blooms in late spring to early summer and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Dianthus uniflorus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
