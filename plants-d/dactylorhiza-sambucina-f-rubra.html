Dactylorhiza sambucina f. rubra

Dactylorhiza sambucina f. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It features a tall, slender stem with a single, showy spike of red or pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette at the base. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the waterlogged soil in which it grows.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in peat bogs and marshes.
