Dortmanna coronopifolia var. normalis

Dortmanna coronopifolia var. normalis, commonly known as the common Dortmanna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are arranged in spike-like inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
