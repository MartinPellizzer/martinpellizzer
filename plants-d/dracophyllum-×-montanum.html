Dracophyllum × montanum

Dracophyllum × montanum, commonly known as the mountain draco, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in spring, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Dracophyllum × montanum is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in stabilizing rocky or sandy soils.
