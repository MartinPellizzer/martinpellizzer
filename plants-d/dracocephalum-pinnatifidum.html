Dracocephalum pinnatifidum

Dracocephalum pinnatifidum, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers are arranged in whorled clusters, typical of the genus Dracocephalum.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
