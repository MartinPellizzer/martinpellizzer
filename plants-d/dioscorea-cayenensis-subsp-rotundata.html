Dioscorea cayenensis subsp. rotundata

Dioscorea cayenensis subsp.

rotundata, commonly known as yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its swollen, cylindrical rhizomes that serve as storage organs.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it relies on insect pollination.

The tuberous roots are rich in starch and have been used for food and traditional medicine in various cultures.
