Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp. columbianum

Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp.

columbianum, commonly known as the Columbia dropseed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
