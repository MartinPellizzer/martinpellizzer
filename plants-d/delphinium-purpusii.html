Delphinium purpusii

Delphinium purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus' larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated margins, and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium purpusii is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
