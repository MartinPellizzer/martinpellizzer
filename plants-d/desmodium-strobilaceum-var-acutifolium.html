Desmodium strobilaceum var. acutifolium

Desmodium strobilaceum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
