Deschampsia rhenana

Deschampsia rhenana, commonly known as the Rhine grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

Deschampsia rhenana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
