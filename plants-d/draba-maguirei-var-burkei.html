Draba maguirei var. burkei

Draba maguirei var. burkei, commonly known as the Burke's whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features dense clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Draba maguirei var. burkei is often found in rocky soils and is an important component of alpine ecosystems.
