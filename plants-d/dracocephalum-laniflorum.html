Dracocephalum laniflorum

Dracocephalum laniflorum, commonly known as woolly skullcap, is a perennial herb native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers clustered in dense heads. The plant thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Dracocephalum laniflorum is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
