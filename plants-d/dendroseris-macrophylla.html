Dendroseris macrophylla

Dendroseris macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, which float on the water's surface. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that anchors it in shallow water, supporting its heavy foliage. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming at night.

Dendroseris macrophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various water organisms.
