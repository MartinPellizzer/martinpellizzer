Dryopteris crassirhizoma subsp. whangshangensis

Dryopteris crassirhizoma subsp.

whangshangensis, commonly known as the Whangshang Brake, is a fern native to China. It is characterized by its robust rhizomes and large, compound fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dark green, glossy upper surface and a lighter, often hairy underside. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
