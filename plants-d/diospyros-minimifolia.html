Diospyros minimifolia

Diospyros minimifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red fruits. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
