Discocalyx angustissima

Discocalyx angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved discocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and reduced leaves, which are adapted to minimize water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its structure allows it to store water in its stem, making it well-suited for survival in dry conditions.

Discocalyx angustissima is often found in rocky soils and is a relatively rare species due to its specific habitat requirements.
