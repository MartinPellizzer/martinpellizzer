Dianthus serratifolius var. abbreviatus

Dianthus serratifolius var. abbreviatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
