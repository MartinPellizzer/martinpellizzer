Demidium

Demidium, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to various regions in Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which often have a distinctive three-lobed lip. Demidium species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments.

These plants require bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
