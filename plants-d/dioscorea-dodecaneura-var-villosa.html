Dioscorea dodecaneura var. villosa

Dioscorea dodecaneura var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tubers that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and adaptability to various soil conditions.
