Dipodium punctatum var. album

Dipodium punctatum var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered dipodium, is a rare and striking member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are adorned with small, white spore clusters. Native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after species among botanical enthusiasts and collectors.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
