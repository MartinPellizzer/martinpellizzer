Diplazium grandifolium var. macrohlamys

Diplazium grandifolium var. macrohlamys, commonly known as the large-leaf water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Diplazium.

It is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its large, lanceolate, and serrated leaves that float on the water surface. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread across wetland areas. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds.
