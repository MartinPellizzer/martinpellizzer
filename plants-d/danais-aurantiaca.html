Danais aurantiaca

Danais aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months and are pollinated by specific species of bees and wasps.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in its ecosystem.
