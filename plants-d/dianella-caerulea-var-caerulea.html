Dianella caerulea var. caerulea

Dianella caerulea var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered lily or blue dianella, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense clump, and it produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to shady garden environments. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 9.

Dianella caerulea var. caerulea is also valued for its ability to naturalize and provide year-round interest in garden settings.
