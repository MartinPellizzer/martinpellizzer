Dioscorea kamoonensis var. delavayi

Dioscorea kamoonensis var. delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by berries. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and pharmacologists alike.
