Digitaria manongarivensis

Digitaria manongarivensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial C4 grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Digitaria manongarivensis is often used in restoration projects due to its erosion control capabilities and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
