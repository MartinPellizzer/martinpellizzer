Dodecatheon cuneatum

Dodecatheon cuneatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf trout lily, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, nodding flowers with six petals that are typically white or pale yellow, often marked with red or orange streaks. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Dodecatheon cuneatum plays an important role in early spring ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
