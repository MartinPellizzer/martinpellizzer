Deyeuxia azorica

Deyeuxia azorica, commonly known as the Azorean flat sedge, is a grass species native to the Azores.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deyeuxia azorica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
