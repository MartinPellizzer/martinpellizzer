Daphnidostaphylis tomentosa

Daphnidostaphylis tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly daphnidostaphylis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
