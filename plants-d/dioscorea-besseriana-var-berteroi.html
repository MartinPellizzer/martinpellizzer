Dioscorea besseriana var. berteroi

Dioscorea besseriana var. berteroi, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy surface, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source by various indigenous communities.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of bioactive compounds.
