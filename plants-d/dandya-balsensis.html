Dandya balsensis

Dandya balsensis, commonly known as the Balsam Bush, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
