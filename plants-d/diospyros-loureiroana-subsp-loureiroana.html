Diospyros loureiroana subsp. loureiroana

Diospyros loureiroana subsp.

loureiroana, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
