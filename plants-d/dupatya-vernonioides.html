Dupatya vernonioides

Dupatya vernonioides, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, dry fruits. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry savanna habitats.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is sometimes threatened by overharvesting.
