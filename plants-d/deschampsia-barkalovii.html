Deschampsia barkalovii

Deschampsia barkalovii, commonly known as the Barkalov's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, where it forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, wiry stems. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are usually pale green to brown in color, appearing in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more prominent in dry conditions.

Deschampsia barkalovii is adapted to cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
