Dendrobium tomaniense

Dendrobium tomaniense, commonly known as the Toman Island orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a sympodial growth habit with thick, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with elongated petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium tomaniense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
