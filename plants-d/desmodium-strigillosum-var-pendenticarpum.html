Desmodium strigillosum var. pendenticarpum

Desmodium strigillosum var. pendenticarpum, commonly known as the hanging fruit desmodium, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, pendulous fruit pods. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable agriculture.

Its small, white to pale pink flowers bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats.
