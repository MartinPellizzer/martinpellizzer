Diospyros confertiflora

Diospyros confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower ebony, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are usually alternate. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round, dark purple fruits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
