Dolichos longistipellatus

Dolichos longistipellatus, commonly known as the long-stippled dolichos, is a leguminous plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant typically features elongated leaves with prominent stipules, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in racemes, with a pea-like structure typical of the family.

Dolichos longistipellatus is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or as a forage crop.
