Dianthus rupicola subsp. aeolicus

Dianthus rupicola subsp.

aeolicus, commonly known as the Aeolic rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often on coastal cliffs or slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a coastal plant.
