Diaphane edulis

Diaphane edulis, commonly known as the translucent fruit tree, is a unique plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

Its leaves are broad and waxy, with a glossy green surface that reflects light, aiding in photosynthesis in low-light conditions. The plant produces small, translucent fruits that contain a nutrient-rich, jelly-like pulp. These fruits are prized for their delicate flavor and are often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

Due to its rarity and habitat-specific requirements, Diaphane edulis is considered an endangered species.
