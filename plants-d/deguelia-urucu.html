Deguelia urucu

Deguelia urucu, commonly known as the "Brazilian trumpet tree," is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a leguminous tree belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine.

Deguelia urucu is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
