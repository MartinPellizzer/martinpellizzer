Dioscorea megalantha var. subsessilis

Dioscorea megalantha var. subsessilis, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a perennial vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used as a food source. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, compound leaves, and edible tubers with a starchy texture.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter pedicels compared to the typical form of Dioscorea megalantha.
