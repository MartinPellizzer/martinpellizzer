Diospyros platycalyx

Diospyros platycalyx, commonly known as the broad-calyx persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a hairy, orange to reddish berry.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
