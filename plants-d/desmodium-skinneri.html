Desmodium skinneri

Desmodium skinneri, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
