Dianthus planellae

Dianthus planellae, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are often fleshy and covered in fine hairs, adapted to its arid environment. Its flowers are pink to red, with five petals and a distinctive central white or pale yellow area.

Dianthus planellae thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry meadows.
