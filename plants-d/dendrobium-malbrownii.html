Dendrobium malbrownii

Dendrobium malbrownii, commonly known as the Malbrow's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dendrobium genus, which includes over 1,500 species of orchids, many of which are prized for their ornamental value. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, forming a sympodial growth pattern with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Dendrobium malbrownii requires specific light and humidity conditions to thrive, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
