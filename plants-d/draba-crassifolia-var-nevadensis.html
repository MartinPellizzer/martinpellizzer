Draba crassifolia var. nevadensis

Draba crassifolia var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has basal leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Its flowering period occurs in early spring, producing yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees.
