Diasperus casticum

Diasperus casticum, commonly known as the Mediterranean cypress, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, scale-like leaves that are densely packed along its branches, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative plant in arid landscapes.
