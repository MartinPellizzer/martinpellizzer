Didymocarpus tomentosus

Didymocarpus tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The hairy tamarind is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
