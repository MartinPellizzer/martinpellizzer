Dioscorea engleriana

Dioscorea engleriana, commonly known as Engler's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The tubers of D. engleriana are valued for their medicinal properties and are sometimes used as a food source in certain cultures.
