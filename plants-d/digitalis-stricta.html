Digitalis stricta

Digitalis stricta, commonly known as purple foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in a loose raceme. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Digitalis stricta is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
