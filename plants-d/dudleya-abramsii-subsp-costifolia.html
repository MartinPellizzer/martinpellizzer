Dudleya abramsii subsp. costifolia

Dudleya abramsii subsp.

costifolia, commonly known as the Costifolia Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating.

The plant produces tall, branched stalks bearing clusters of pale yellow flowers during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
