Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. haematodes

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

haematodes, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heaths.

The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with a rosette of linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are striking, with deep red petals and a yellow lip, often arranged in a dense spike.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vivid coloration.
