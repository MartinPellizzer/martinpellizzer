Dioscorea corumbensis

Dioscorea corumbensis, commonly known as the Brazilian yam, is a vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers, which are the edible part, are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of Dioscorea corumbensis make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
