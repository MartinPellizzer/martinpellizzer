Daphne canescens

Daphne canescens, commonly known as the silver daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of white to pale pink. The plant has silvery-gray foliage that provides ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Daphne canescens is valued in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
