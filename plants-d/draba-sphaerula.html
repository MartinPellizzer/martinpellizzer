Draba sphaerula

Draba sphaerula, commonly known as the globe wintercress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, typically growing in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowers are surrounded by four sepals and four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Draba sphaerula is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
