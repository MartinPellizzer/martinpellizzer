Dimorphotheca pinnata var. brevis

Dimorphotheca pinnata var. brevis, commonly known as the narrowleaf starburry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and arranged alternately along the stems, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.
