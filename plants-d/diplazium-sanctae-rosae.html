Diplazium sanctae-rosae

Diplazium sanctae-rosae, commonly known as the sacred rose fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, lanceolate fronds with a serrated edge. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The fronds are also sometimes cultivated as ornamental plants due to their attractive appearance.
