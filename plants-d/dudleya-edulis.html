Dudleya edulis

Dudleya edulis, commonly known as the edible Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring season. It is notable for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

While the leaves are sometimes consumed by animals, the plant is not typically considered a food source for humans.
