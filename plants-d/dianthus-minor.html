Dianthus minor

Dianthus minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in rocky areas.

Dianthus minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
