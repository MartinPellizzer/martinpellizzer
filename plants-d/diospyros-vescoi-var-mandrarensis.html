Diospyros vescoi var. mandrarensis

Diospyros vescoi var. mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and a slightly waxy texture.

The fruit is edible, though it is often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
