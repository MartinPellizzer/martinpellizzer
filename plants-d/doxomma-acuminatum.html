Doxomma acuminatum

Doxomma acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped lily, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its blooming period occurs during the spring and early summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid climates with well-drained soil.
