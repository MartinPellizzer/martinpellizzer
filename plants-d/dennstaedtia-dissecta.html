Dennstaedtia dissecta

Dennstaedtia dissecta, commonly known as the fern with divided fronds, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or drooping. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a creeping underground stem that produces new shoots. Its leaves, or fronds, are usually light green and have a leathery texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.
