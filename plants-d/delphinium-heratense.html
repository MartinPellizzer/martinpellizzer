Delphinium heratense

Delphinium heratense, commonly known as the Herat larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Delphinium heratense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
