Dendrobium bambusiforme

Dendrobium bambusiforme, commonly known as the bamboo-like orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features long, cane-like pseudobulbs that resemble bamboo, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in clusters during the warm season.

Dendrobium bambusiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
