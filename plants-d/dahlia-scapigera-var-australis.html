Dahlia scapigera var. australis

Dahlia scapigera var. australis, commonly known as the Australian dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender peduncles, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
