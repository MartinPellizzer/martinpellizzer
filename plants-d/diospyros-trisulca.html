Diospyros trisulca

Diospyros trisulca, commonly known as the three-lobed persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually three-lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange to red persimmons that ripen in the fall. Its fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
