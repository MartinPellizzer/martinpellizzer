Dracophyllum balansae

Dracophyllum balansae, commonly known as the Balansae Dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or well-drained soils, and is adapted to cold, humid climates. Its small, leathery leaves are clustered closely together, and it produces inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Dracophyllum balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
