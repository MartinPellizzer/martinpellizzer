Diervilla pauciflora

Diervilla pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and turn yellow in the fall.

Diervilla pauciflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
