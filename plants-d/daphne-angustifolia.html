Daphne angustifolia

Daphne angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
