Diospyros adenophora

Diospyros adenophora, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is consumed by both humans and wildlife.
