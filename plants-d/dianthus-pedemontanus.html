Dianthus pedemontanus

Dianthus pedemontanus, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive red or purple central stripe. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Dianthus pedemontanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
