Diospyros pilosiuscula

Diospyros pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, hairy fruits. The fruit is edible when fully mature and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
