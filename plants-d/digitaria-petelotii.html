Digitaria petelotii

Digitaria petelotii, commonly known as the Mexican grass or Petelot's digitaria, is a species of grass native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. It has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Digitaria petelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
