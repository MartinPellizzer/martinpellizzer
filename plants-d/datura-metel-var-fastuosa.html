Datura metel var. fastuosa

Datura metel var. fastuosa, commonly known as the sacred datura or angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces spiny capsules that contain seeds, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is also notable for its potent alkaloid content, which makes it both medicinally significant and potentially toxic.

Its striking appearance and ecological importance make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
