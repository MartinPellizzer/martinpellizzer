Dendrocalamus parishii

Dendrocalamus parishii, commonly known as the Burmese bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Dendrocalamus parishii is valued for its strong, lightweight timber and is also used in traditional crafts and construction.
