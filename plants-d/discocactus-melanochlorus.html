Discocactus melanochlorus

Discocactus melanochlorus, commonly known as the black-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dark green to black spines that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
