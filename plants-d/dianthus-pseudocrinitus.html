Dianthus pseudocrinitus

Dianthus pseudocrinitus, commonly known as the curly top campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often described as resembling hair, giving it its common name.

Dianthus pseudocrinitus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
