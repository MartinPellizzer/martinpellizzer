Deutzia discolor var. stellata

Deutzia discolor var. stellata, commonly known as the stellate deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a bluish-green upper surface and a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
