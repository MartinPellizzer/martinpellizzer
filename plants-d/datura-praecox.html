Datura praecox

Datura praecox, commonly known as the early evening flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and close by morning. The plant produces spiny, egg-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. Datura praecox is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
