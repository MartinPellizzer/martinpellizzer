Digitalis ambigua var. grandiflora

Digitalis ambigua var. grandiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved foxglove, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it blooms during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shady gardens.

Due to its attractive appearance and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
