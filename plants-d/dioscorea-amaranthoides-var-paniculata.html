Dioscorea amaranthoides var. paniculata

Dioscorea amaranthoides var. paniculata, commonly known as the purple yam or wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking purple to red flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal use.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and agriculture.
