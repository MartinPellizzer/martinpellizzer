Diplachne fusca subsp. muelleri

Diplachne fusca subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a sprawling growth habit with rhizomes.

The plant produces slender, flat blades and panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
