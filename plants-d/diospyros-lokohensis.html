Diospyros lokohensis

Diospyros lokohensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.

The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and edible qualities.
