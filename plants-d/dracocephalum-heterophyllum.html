Dracocephalum heterophyllum

Dracocephalum heterophyllum, commonly known as the Persian feverfew, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
