Dalbergia retusa var. retusa

Dalbergia retusa var. retusa, commonly known as the white shea tree, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits containing smooth, hard seeds. It is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and carving, as well as for its seeds, which are a source of shea butter.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
