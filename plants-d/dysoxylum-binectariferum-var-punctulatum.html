Dysoxylum binectariferum var. punctulatum

Dysoxylum binectariferum var. punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinct white spots along the veins. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, segmented fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
