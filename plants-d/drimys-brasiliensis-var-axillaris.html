Drimys brasiliensis var. axillaris

Drimys brasiliensis var. axillaris, commonly known as the Brazilian wintergreen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
