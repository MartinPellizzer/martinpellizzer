Dubautia lonchophylla

Dubautia lonchophylla, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironwood, is a shrub native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of the Hawaiian highlands.

Dubautia lonchophylla is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a valuable component of its native ecosystem.
