Didymocarpus vestitus var. lanceolatus

Didymocarpus vestitus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a dense canopy and smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. The fruit is a woody, pod-like structure that contains several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
