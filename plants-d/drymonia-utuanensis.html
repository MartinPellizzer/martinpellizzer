Drymonia utuanensis

Drymonia utuanensis, commonly known as the Uruana palm, is a rare and endemic species native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forest growth. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed, which is dispersed by animals.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
