Dianthus carthusianorum var. longisquamis

Dianthus carthusianorum var. longisquamis, commonly known as the long-scale carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, with a distinctive, frilled appearance. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in fine, scale-like structures, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in floral arrangements.
