Dioscorea pedatifida

Dioscorea pedatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved yam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, containing compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Dioscorea pedatifida is often cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional use in various cultures.
