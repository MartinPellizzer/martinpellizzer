Dryopteris phegopteroidea

Dryopteris phegopteroidea, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into shield-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are protected by indusia.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
