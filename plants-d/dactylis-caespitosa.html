Dactylis caespitosa

Dactylis caespitosa, commonly known as sweet vernalgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces pale green to golden yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Dactylis caespitosa is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it serves as a valuable forage plant for livestock.
