Dioscorea macrophylla

Dioscorea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and long, climbing stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers and cylindrical, fleshy tubers that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Dioscorea macrophylla is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is known for its high starch content and medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
