Diplopappus chrysanthemifolius

Diplopappus chrysanthemifolius, commonly known as the goldenleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disc florets, giving it a dandelion-like appearance. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in open, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
