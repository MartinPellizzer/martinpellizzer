Delphinium yuanum

Delphinium yuanum, commonly known as the Yunnan larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium yuanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic alkaloids that require caution in handling.
