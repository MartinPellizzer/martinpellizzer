Didymocarpus aureus

Didymocarpus aureus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its foliage. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Didymocarpus aureus make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
