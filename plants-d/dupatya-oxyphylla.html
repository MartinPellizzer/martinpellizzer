Dupatya oxyphylla

Dupatya oxyphylla, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with brownish markings. The plant has a fibrous root system and grows in shaded, moist environments within the forest understory. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Dupatya oxyphylla is valued for its rarity and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and appreciation.
