Diospyros costaricensis

Diospyros costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries.

Diospyros costaricensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit, which has a sweet, astringent flavor.
