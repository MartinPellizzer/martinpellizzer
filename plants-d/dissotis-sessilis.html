Dissotis sessilis

Dissotis sessilis, commonly known as the sessile dissotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Dissotis sessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
