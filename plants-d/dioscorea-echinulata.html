Dioscorea echinulata

Dioscorea echinulata, commonly known as the spiny yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its tubers are typically rough and have a distinctive spiny texture, which gives it its common name.

Dioscorea echinulata is valued for its potential medicinal properties, including antidiabetic and anti-inflammatory effects.
