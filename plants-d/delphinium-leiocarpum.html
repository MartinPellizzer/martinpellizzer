Delphinium leiocarpum

Delphinium leiocarpum, commonly known as the white helmet larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium leiocarpum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
