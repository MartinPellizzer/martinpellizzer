Draba intricatissima

Draba intricatissima, commonly known as the intricate rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant features tiny, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, and its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs. Draba intricatissima is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for high-altitude ecosystems.
