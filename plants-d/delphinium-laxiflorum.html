Delphinium laxiflorum

Delphinium laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring tall, unbranched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, with spurred petals that are characteristic of the Delphinium genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and alpine landscapes.
