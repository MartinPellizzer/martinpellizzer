Diospyros areolifolia

Diospyros areolifolia, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
