Daphne dioica

Daphne dioica, commonly known as the common daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy texture. Its flowers are fragrant and appear in clusters, with petals that are usually pink or white.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by birds.
