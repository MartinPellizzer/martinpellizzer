Dibrachion brasiliense

Dibrachion brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a herbaceous plant with a compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers. The plant has a short, upright stem with several leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Dibrachion brasiliense is adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of the understory in tropical rainforests.
