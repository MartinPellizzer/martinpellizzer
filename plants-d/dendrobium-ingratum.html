Dendrobium ingratum

Dendrobium ingratum, commonly known as the graceful orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered with a layer of brownish bark-like tissue.

Dendrobium ingratum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
