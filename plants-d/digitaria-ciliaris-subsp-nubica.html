Digitaria ciliaris subsp. nubica

Digitaria ciliaris subsp.

nubica, commonly known as Nubian fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a common component of dryland vegetation.

This subspecies is also valued for its potential in forage and soil stabilization in marginal ecosystems.
