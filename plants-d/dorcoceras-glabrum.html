Dorcoceras glabrum

Dorcoceras glabrum, commonly known as the smooth horned orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist, acidic soils of forested areas. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
