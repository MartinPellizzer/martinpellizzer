Discocactus lindanus

Discocactus lindanus, commonly known as the Lindan's discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small groups, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
