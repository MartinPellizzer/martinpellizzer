Desmodium cephalotes

Desmodium cephalotes, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Desmodium cephalotes is often used in landscaping and as a cover crop due to its hardiness and nitrogen-fixing ability.
