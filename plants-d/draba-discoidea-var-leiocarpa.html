Draba discoidea var. leiocarpa

Draba discoidea var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in high-altitude environments. It produces basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and has a compact growth habit.

The white petals are usually arranged in a single row, giving it a distinctive appearance among other Draba species.
