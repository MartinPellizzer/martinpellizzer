Dicksonia divaricata

Dicksonia divaricata, commonly known as the cliff fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable component of New Zealand's native flora.
