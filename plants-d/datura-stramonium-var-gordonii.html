Datura stramonium var. gordonii

Datura stramonium var. gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's jimsonweed, is a variant of the common jimsonweed.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas, characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the plant contains toxic alkaloids such as scopolamine and hyoscyamine.
