Diplusodon xerophilus

Diplusodon xerophilus, commonly known as the desert lily, is a succulent plant adapted to arid environments.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to regions with low rainfall, such as parts of Africa and the Middle East.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-resistant landscaping.
