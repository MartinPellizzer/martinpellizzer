Dianthus plumarius var. portensis

Dianthus plumarius var. portensis, commonly known as the Portuguese carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of bright pink or white flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, round seed capsules that mature in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
