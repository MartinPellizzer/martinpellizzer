Ditassa riedelii

Ditassa riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
