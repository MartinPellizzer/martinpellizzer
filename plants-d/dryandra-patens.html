Dryandra patens

Dryandra patens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dryandra, is a member of the Proteaceae family and is native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
