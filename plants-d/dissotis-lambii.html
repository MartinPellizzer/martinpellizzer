Dissotis lambii

Dissotis lambii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by a showy, leaf-like bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Dissotis lambii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
