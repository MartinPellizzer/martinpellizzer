Digitalis thapsi var. intermedia

Digitalis thapsi var. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid foxglove, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
