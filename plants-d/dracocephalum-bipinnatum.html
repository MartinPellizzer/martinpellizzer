Dracocephalum bipinnatum

Dracocephalum bipinnatum, commonly known as the two-feathered skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, with two lips, and bloom in summer.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal properties.
