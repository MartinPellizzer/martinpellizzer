Draba macounii

Draba macounii, commonly known as Macoun's whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically features basal rosettes of pale green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, alpine, or arctic environments, often in well-drained soils.

Draba macounii is known for its hardy nature and ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
