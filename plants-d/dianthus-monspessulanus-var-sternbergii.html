Dianthus monspessulanus var. sternbergii

Dianthus monspessulanus var. sternbergii, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of pink to red, double-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with succulent, slightly hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
