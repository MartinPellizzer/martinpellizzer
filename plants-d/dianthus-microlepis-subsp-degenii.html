Dianthus microlepis subsp. degenii

Dianthus microlepis subsp.

degenii, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, brightly colored flowers that typically range from pink to red.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of five-petaled blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
