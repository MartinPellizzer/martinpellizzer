Dalea vulcanicola

Dalea vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic rock milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, which help reduce water loss.

Dalea vulcanicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
