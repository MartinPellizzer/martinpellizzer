Dracaena concinna

Dracaena concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dracaena, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Dracaena concinna is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
