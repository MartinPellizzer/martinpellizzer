Draba trinervis

Draba trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined whitlow, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically arranged in a single umbel. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.

Draba trinervis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
