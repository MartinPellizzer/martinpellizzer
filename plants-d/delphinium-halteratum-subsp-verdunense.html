Delphinium halteratum subsp. verdunense

Delphinium halteratum subsp.

verdunense, commonly known as the Verdun delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and the flowers are arranged in racemes.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
