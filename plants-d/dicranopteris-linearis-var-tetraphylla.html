Dicranopteris linearis var. tetraphylla

Dicranopteris linearis var. tetraphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Dicranopteris.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into multiple segments. The plant is aquatic or semi-aquatic, often found in slow-moving freshwater environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and can grow up to several meters in length.

This variety is notable for its distinctive morphology and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
