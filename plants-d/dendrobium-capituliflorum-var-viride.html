Dendrobium capituliflorum var. viride

Dendrobium capituliflorum var. viride, commonly known as the green-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with yellowish markings, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
