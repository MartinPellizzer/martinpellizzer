Dichaea violacea

Dichaea violacea, commonly known as the violet leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-colored leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The orchid's unique leaf structure and floral display make it a distinctive and visually appealing member of the orchid family.
