Diplopterys araujei

Diplopterys araujei, commonly known as the "Brazilian pepper tree," is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, gray bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
