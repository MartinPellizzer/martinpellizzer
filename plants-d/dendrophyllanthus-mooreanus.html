Dendrophyllanthus mooreanus

Dendrophyllanthus mooreanus, commonly known as the Moorean tree-ferns, is a unique species of tree-fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound leaves. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its reproductive structures include both male and female flowers, which are typically found in clusters at the base of the leaves.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its distinctive morphology and ecological significance in its native environment.
