Diospyros cuneata

Diospyros cuneata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red, fleshy berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and the wood is valued for its durability and workability.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
