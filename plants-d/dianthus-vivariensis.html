Dianthus vivariensis

Dianthus vivariensis, commonly known as the garden pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with upright stems and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus vivariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
