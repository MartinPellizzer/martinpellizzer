Diasperus klotzschianus

Diasperus klotzschianus, commonly known as the Klotzsch's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapting well to arid conditions.

Diasperus klotzschianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
