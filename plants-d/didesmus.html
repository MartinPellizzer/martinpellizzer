Didesmus

Didesmus, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves and stems adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh desert conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Didesmus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
