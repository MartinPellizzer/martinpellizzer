Deprea lutea

Deprea lutea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Deprea lutea is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
