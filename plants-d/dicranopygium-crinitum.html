Dicranopygium crinitum

Dicranopygium crinitum, commonly known as the hairy moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth form and the presence of long, hairy structures on its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a distinct midrib.

Dicranopygium crinitum plays an important role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
