Dianthus mesanidum

Dianthus mesanidum, commonly known as the Armenian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branched.

Dianthus mesanidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
