Dioscorea caracasensis

Dioscorea caracasensis, commonly known as the Caracas yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the genus Dioscorea, which includes various species of yams, and is characterized by its tuberous roots that are used for food. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has elongated, heart-shaped leaves. Its tubers are often used in traditional cuisine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.
