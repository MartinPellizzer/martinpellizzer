Dianthus brachyanthus var. cantabricus

Dianthus brachyanthus var. cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clustered pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and small, lobed leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
