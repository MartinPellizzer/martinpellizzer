Dussia atropurpurea

Dussia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple dussia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue and a fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
