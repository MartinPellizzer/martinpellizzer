Dypsis heterophylla

Dypsis heterophylla, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems and a crown of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Dypsis heterophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
