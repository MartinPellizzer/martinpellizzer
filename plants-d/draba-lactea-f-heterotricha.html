Draba lactea f. heterotricha

Draba lactea f. heterotricha, commonly known as the alpine whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes, and its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white wax. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate cold climates and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats across mountainous regions in Europe and Asia.
