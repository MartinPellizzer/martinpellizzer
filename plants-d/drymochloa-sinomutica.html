Drymochloa sinomutica

Drymochloa sinomutica, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its culms are slender and often exhibit a reddish hue, especially at the nodes.

Drymochloa sinomutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
