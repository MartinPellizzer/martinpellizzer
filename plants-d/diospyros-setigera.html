Diospyros setigera

Diospyros setigera, commonly known as the slender persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, berry-like fruits. Its fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, astringent flavor.

Diospyros setigera is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
