Diospyros pseudomespilus subsp. pseudomespilus

Diospyros pseudomespilus subsp.

pseudomespilus, commonly known as the small persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptical in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange to reddish berries.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, offering a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
