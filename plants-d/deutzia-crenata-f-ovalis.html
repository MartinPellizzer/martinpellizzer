Deutzia crenata f. ovalis

Deutzia crenata f. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaf deutzia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. The leaves are ovate, with a smooth margin, and provide attractive foliage throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

This cultivar is prized for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
