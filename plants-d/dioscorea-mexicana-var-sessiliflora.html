Dioscorea mexicana var. sessiliflora

Dioscorea mexicana var. sessiliflora, commonly known as Mexican yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, containing compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.
