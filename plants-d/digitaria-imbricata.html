Digitaria imbricata

Digitaria imbricata, commonly known as flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, blade-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Digitaria imbricata is also used in some traditional practices and can be a component of mixed grasslands.
