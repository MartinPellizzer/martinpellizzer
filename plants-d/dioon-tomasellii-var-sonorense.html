Dioon tomasellii var. sonorense

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to two meters in length. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a semi-arid climate with minimal rainfall.
