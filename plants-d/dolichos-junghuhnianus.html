Dolichos junghuhnianus

Dolichos junghuhnianus, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, spiny pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields. It is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines, and for its role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value, with its distinctive foliage and unique pod structure.
