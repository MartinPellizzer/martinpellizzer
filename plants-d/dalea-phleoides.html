Dalea phleoides

Dalea phleoides, commonly known as the prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open prairies and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, aiding in its spread across grassland habitats.
