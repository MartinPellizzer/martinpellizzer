Disperis discifera

Disperis discifera, commonly known as the African orchid or the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive white or greenish lip, and it blooms during the summer months.

Disperis discifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
