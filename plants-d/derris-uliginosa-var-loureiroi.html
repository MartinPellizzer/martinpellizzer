Derris uliginosa var. loureiroi

Derris uliginosa var. loureiroi, commonly known as the black bean vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, compound leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
