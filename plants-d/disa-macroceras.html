Disa macroceras

Disa macroceras, commonly known as the giant disa, is a striking member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It produces a single, erect stem that bears several flowers, which are pollinated by specific insect species.

Disa macroceras is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
