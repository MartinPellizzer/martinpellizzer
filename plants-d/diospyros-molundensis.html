Diospyros molundensis

Diospyros molundensis, commonly known as the Molundensian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically red or orange when mature, and is often consumed by local wildlife and humans in some regions.

The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood and is also of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
