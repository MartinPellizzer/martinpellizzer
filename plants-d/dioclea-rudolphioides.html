Dioclea rudolphioides

Dioclea rudolphioides, commonly known as the red spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a climbing habit, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
