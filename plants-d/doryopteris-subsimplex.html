Doryopteris subsimplex

Doryopteris subsimplex, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically slender and arching. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile spore-bearing leaves that are shorter than the sterile ones. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical structure makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
