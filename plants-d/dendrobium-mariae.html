Dendrobium mariae

Dendrobium mariae, commonly known as the Marie's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium mariae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
