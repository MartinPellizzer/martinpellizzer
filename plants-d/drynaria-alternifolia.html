Drynaria alternifolia

Drynaria alternifolia, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant grows on rocky outcrops and forest floors, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome is rich in starch and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Drynaria alternifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and hardiness.
