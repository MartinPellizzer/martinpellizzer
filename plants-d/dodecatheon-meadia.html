Dodecatheon meadia

Dodecatheon meadia, commonly known as the eastern fringeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive fringed appearance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Dodecatheon meadia is a valuable plant for pollinators, particularly bees and butterflies.
