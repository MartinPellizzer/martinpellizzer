Decaspermum humifusum

Decaspermum humifusum, commonly known as the dwarf senna, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant often grows in sandy or rocky soils.

Decaspermum humifusum is adapted to arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
