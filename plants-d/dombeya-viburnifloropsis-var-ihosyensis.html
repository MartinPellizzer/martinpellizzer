Dombeya viburnifloropsis var. ihosyensis

Dombeya viburnifloropsis var. ihosyensis, commonly known as the Ihosy dombeya, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
