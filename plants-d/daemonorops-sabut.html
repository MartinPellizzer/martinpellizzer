Daemonorops sabut

Daemonorops sabut, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of cycad native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
