Dianthus nelsonii

Dianthus nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often branched.

Dianthus nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
