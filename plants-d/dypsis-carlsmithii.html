Dypsis carlsmithii

Dypsis carlsmithii, commonly known as the Carlsmith's areca, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Dypsis carlsmithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
