Dictyoneura acuminata

Dictyoneura acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock fern, is a species of fern native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Dictyoneura acuminata is a hardy plant that thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
