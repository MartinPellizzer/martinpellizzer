Diplycosia piceifolia

Diplycosia piceifolia, commonly known as the black-leaved diplycosia, is a shrub native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its bark is thick and corky, providing some resistance to pests and diseases.

Diplycosia piceifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
