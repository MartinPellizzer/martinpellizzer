Drimia viridula

Drimia viridula, commonly known as the green bulbous plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a cluster of fleshy, green bulbs from which slender, grass-like leaves emerge. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Drimia viridula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
