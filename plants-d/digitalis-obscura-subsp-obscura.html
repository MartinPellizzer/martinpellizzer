Digitalis obscura subsp. obscura

Digitalis obscura subsp.

obscura, commonly known as the obscure foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of hooded flowers in shades of purple and pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture.

It is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine, though its toxic properties require caution.
