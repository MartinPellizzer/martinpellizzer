Dalbergia nemoralis

Dalbergia nemoralis, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding leaves during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and the tree is valued for its hard, dark wood used in musical instruments and furniture.
