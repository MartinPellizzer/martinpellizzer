Dalea boliviana var. herrerae

Dalea boliviana var. herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's Dalea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features delicate, pink to red floral clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, typical of many legumes, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
