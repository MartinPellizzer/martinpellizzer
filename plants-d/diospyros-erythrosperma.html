Diospyros erythrosperma

Diospyros erythrosperma, commonly known as the red-fruited persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright orange to red berries that are edible when fully mature. Its fruits are valued for their sweet, astringent flavor and are often used in traditional medicine.

Diospyros erythrosperma thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
