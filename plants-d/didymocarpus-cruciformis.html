Didymocarpus cruciformis

Didymocarpus cruciformis, commonly known as the cross-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low moisture environments.

Didymocarpus cruciformis is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
