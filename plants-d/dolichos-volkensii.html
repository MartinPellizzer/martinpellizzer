Dolichos volkensii

Dolichos volkensii, commonly known as the African bean or Volkens' bean, is a climbing legume native to eastern and southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. The plant is characterized by its twining habit and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

The seeds are edible and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.
