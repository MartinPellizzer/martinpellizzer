Delphinium camporum var. penardii

Delphinium camporum var. penardii, commonly known as Penard's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
