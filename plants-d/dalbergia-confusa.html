Dalbergia confusa

Dalbergia confusa, commonly known as the white cedar or Brazilian cedar, is a leguminous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding its leaves during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume, containing several flattened seeds, and the tree is valued for its durable, aromatic wood.

Dalbergia confusa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
