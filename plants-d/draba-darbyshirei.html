Draba darbyshirei

Draba darbyshirei, commonly known as Darbyshire's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and has a short, flowering stem.

Draba darbyshirei is a perennial species that is often found in high-altitude habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
