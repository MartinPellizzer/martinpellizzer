Delphinium sutherlandii

Delphinium sutherlandii, commonly known as the Sutherland larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with striking blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes. The leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and sometimes toxic properties.
