Disperis alata

Disperis alata, commonly known as the winged orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often on rocky substrates. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive wing-like appendage on the petals.

Disperis alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
