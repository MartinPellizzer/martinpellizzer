Dendrobium melanophthalmum

Dendrobium melanophthalmum, commonly known as the black-eyed dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, typically in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with multiple stems that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a striking contrast between the dark purple petals and the white or pale yellow sepals.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
