Dombeya grandifolia

Dombeya grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflower dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and are arranged in terminal clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds with tufts of silky hair, aiding in wind dispersal. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Dombeya grandifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
