Draba aizoides var. eriocarpa

Draba aizoides var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered with dense, glandular hairs that give it a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and the plant is known for its ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
