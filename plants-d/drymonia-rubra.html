Drymonia rubra

Drymonia rubra, commonly known as the red drymonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing ornamental value throughout the year.

Drymonia rubra thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
