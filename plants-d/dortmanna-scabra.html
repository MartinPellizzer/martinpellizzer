Dortmanna scabra

Dortmanna scabra, commonly known as the rough dock, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in meadows.

Dortmanna scabra is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
