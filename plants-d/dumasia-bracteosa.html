Dumasia bracteosa

Dumasia bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Dumasia bracteosa is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
