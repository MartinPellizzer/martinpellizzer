Dolichos grandis

Dolichos grandis, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, with a distinctive hyacinth-like appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and bears pods that contain edible, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its nutritional seeds.

Dolichos grandis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
