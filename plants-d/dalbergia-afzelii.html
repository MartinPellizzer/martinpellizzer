Dalbergia afzelii

Dalbergia afzelii, commonly known as African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic qualities. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
