Dypsis basilonga

Dypsis basilonga, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for indoor cultivation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dypsis basilonga is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
