Dalea chrysorhiza

Dalea chrysorhiza, commonly known as the goldenroot dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Dalea chrysorhiza is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats.
