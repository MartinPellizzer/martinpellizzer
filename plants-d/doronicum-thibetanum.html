Doronicum thibetanum

Doronicum thibetanum, commonly known as the Tibetan leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features a compact rosette of dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with orange markings, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.
