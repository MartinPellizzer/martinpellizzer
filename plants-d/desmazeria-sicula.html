Desmazeria sicula

Desmazeria sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Desmazeria sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
