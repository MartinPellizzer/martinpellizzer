Dactylostalix maculosa

Dactylostalix maculosa, commonly known as the spotted acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its yellow flowers and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a widespread distribution across several countries in the region.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
