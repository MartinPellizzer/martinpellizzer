Digitalis purpurea var. longibracteata

Digitalis purpurea var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract foxglove, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence. The flowers are typically purple or pink, with a distinctive hooded shape that houses the reproductive structures. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and historical use in medicine.
