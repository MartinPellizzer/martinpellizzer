Dioscorea ruiziana

Dioscorea ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz's yam, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and vine-like.

The tubers of Dioscorea ruiziana are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various herbal remedies.
