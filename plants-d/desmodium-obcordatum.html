Desmodium obcordatum

Desmodium obcordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Desmodium obcordatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
