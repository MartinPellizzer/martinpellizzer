Dyckia grandifolia

Dyckia grandifolia, commonly known as the giant dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, rosette-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with a waxy, blue-green coloration. The plant produces inflorescences of bright red flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Dyckia grandifolia is also notable for its ability to store water in its leaves, adapting to its native arid environment.
