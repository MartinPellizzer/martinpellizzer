Deschampsia cespitosa var. major

Deschampsia cespitosa var. major, commonly known as tufted hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important plant in wetland ecosystems.
