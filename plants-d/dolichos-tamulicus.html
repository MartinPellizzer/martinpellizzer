Dolichos tamulicus

Dolichos tamulicus, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to grow along supports. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that eventually develop into elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Dolichos tamulicus is valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high protein and fiber levels, making it an important crop in many regions.
