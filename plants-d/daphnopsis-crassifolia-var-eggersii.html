Daphnopsis crassifolia var. eggersii

Daphnopsis crassifolia var. eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' thickleaf daphnopsis, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often green with a subtle bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
