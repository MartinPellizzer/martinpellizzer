Diasperus scoparius

Diasperus scoparius, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, pad-like stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a prickly pear, is edible and has been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.

The plant's structure and adaptations make it a key component of desert ecosystems.
