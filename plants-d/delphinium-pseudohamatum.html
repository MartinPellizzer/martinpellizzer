Delphinium pseudohamatum

Delphinium pseudohamatum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, often in open woodlands or meadows. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium pseudohamatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
