Dupatya alpina

Dupatya alpina, commonly known as the alpine datura, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale lavender in color. The plant produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
