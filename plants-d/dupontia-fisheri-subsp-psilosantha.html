Dupontia fisheri subsp. psilosantha

Dupontia fisheri subsp.

psilosantha, commonly known as the coastal sand dune grass, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, blue-green leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy, coastal environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

This subspecies plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and preventing erosion.
