Desmodium novae-hollandiae var. typicum

Desmodium novae-hollandiae var. typicum, commonly known as the Australian burr vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
