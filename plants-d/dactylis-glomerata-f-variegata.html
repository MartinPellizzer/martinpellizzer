Dactylis glomerata f. variegata

Dactylis glomerata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated sheep's fescue, is a cultivar of the common sheep's fescue.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white or light green stripes on the leaves. This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. The plant is perennial, with a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
