Delphinium collinum

Delphinium collinum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium collinum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
