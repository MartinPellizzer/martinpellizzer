Dioclea comosa

Dioclea comosa, commonly known as the silky sarsapilla, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests.

Dioclea comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
