Dypsis sanctaemariae

Dypsis sanctaemariae, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and feathery foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue-black berries. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Dypsis sanctaemariae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying qualities.
