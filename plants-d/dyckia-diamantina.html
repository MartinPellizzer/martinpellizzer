Dyckia diamantina

Dyckia diamantina, commonly known as the Diamond Dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery crystals. These crystals, known as bloom, help the plant retain moisture and protect it from intense sunlight. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that contrast sharply with its silvery foliage.

Dyckia diamantina is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
