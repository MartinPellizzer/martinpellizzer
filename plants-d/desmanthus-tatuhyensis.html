Desmanthus tatuhyensis

Desmanthus tatuhyensis, commonly known as the Tatuhyensis burrlegume, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for native plant gardens. Its seed pods are woody and contain several small, oblong seeds.

Desmanthus tatuhyensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in ecological and agricultural settings.
