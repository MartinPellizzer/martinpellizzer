Diasperus niruroides

Diasperus niruroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grassvetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, pinkish or white flowers are arranged in loose clusters, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
