Dimorphostachys schaffneri var. remotispicula

Dimorphostachys schaffneri var. remotispicula, commonly known as the slender thornapple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, spiny fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
