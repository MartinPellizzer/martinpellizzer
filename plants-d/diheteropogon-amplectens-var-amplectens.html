Diheteropogon amplectens var. amplectens

Diheteropogon amplectens var. amplectens, commonly known as the clasping sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often clasp the stem at the base. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its flowering inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, three-sided spikelets, which are a distinguishing feature of the species.
