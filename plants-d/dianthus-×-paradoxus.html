Dianthus × paradoxus

Dianthus × paradoxus, commonly known as the double carnation, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, double-layered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and horticultural collections.
