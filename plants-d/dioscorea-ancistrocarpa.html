Dioscorea ancistrocarpa

Dioscorea ancistrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The tubers of D. ancistrocarpa are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
