Danthonia nivicola

Danthonia nivicola, commonly known as the snowgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It thrives in cold, moist environments such as tundra and alpine meadows, where it forms dense, mat-like growths. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in loose spikes and bloom in early spring.

Danthonia nivicola plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing food for various herbivores in its ecosystem.
