Dactylorhiza praetermissa var. cuspidata

Dactylorhiza praetermissa var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in damp, calcareous soils and thrives in environments with consistent moisture. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with three sepals and three petals, and a distinctive, spurred lip.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to bloom in late summer to early autumn.
