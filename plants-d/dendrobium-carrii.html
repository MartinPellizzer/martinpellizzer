Dendrobium carrii

Dendrobium carrii, commonly known as the Carri's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright indirect light and well-draining soil.

Dendrobium carrii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
