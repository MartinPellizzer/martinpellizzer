Distictis rovirosana

Distictis rovirosana, commonly known as the Rovirosa daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Distictis rovirosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
