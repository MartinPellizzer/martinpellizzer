Dianthus hybridus laxtonii

Dianthus hybridus laxtonii, commonly known as the laxtonii campion, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its bright pink or red flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters atop slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in meadows and grasslands.

Dianthus hybridus laxtonii is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
