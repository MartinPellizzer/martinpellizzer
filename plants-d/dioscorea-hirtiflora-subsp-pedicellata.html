Dioscorea hirtiflora subsp. pedicellata

Dioscorea hirtiflora subsp.

pedicellata, commonly known as the hairy-flowered yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family. It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and showy, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
