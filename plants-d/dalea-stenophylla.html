Dalea stenophylla

Dalea stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Dalea stenophylla is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
