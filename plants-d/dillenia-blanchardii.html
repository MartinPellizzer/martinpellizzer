Dillenia blanchardii

Dillenia blanchardii, commonly known as the mountain dillenia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a woody stem with alternate, simple leaves that have a rough texture. Its fruits are follicles that contain several seeds, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Dillenia blanchardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
