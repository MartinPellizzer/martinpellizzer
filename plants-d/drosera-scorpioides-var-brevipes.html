Drosera scorpioides var. brevipes

Drosera scorpioides var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, glandular hairs on its leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and has a compact, low-growing structure. Its flowers are usually white or pink and bloom in summer.

It is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its ease of care and attractive appearance.
