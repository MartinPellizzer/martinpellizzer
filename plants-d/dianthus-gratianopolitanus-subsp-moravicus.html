Dianthus gratianopolitanus subsp. moravicus

Dianthus gratianopolitanus subsp.

moravicus, commonly known as the Moravian carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It features compact, upright growth with showy, double flowers in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
