Dendrobium parishii

Dendrobium parishii, commonly known as the Macaranga orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
