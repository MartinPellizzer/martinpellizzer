Dianella longifolia var. porracea

Dianella longifolia var. porracea, commonly known as the Porcupine grass or Porcupine lily, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
