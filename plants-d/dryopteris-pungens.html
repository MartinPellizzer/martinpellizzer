Dryopteris pungens

Dryopteris pungens, commonly known as the barren barren fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnately compound fronds that are typically dark green and velvety to the touch. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Dryopteris pungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and horticulture.
