Dioscorea basiclavicaulis

Dioscorea basiclavicaulis, commonly known as the blue-stem yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue-green stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves climbing and twining around supporting structures, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphological features and potential pharmacological properties.
