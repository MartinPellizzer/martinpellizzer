Diospyros concolor

Diospyros concolor, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that resemble dates in appearance and taste. Diospyros concolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
