Deyeuxia nepalensis

Deyeuxia nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and forest edges. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers during the summer months. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.

Deyeuxia nepalensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
