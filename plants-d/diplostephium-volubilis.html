Diplostephium volubilis

Diplostephium volubilis, commonly known as the climbing gourd or Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a rapid growth habit and is often used as a ornamental or for its edible fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are twining and can climb support structures.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
