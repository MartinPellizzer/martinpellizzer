Dioscorea costermansiana

Dioscorea costermansiana, commonly known as the Costermans' yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

Its growth habit and morphology make it an important species in traditional medicine and agriculture in its native habitat.
