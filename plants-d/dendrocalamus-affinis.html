Dendrocalamus affinis

Dendrocalamus affinis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems that can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers and has long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various animals and contributing to soil stabilization.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is also valued for commercial applications such as construction and papermaking.
