Diplostephium verbascifolium

Diplostephium verbascifolium, commonly known as the woolly leaf cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in dense, white woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
