Dieterichia lampujang

Dieterichia lampujang, commonly known as the Java ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, often with a pale, translucent appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, slender roots and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
