Donax virgata

Donax virgata, commonly known as the Virginia saltmarsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass found in coastal wetlands along the Atlantic coast of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to tolerate saline and brackish conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are wind-pollinated, and it plays a vital role in stabilizing tidal marsh ecosystems. Donax virgata is an important component of salt marsh vegetation, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its ability to thrive in fluctuating water levels and high salinity makes it a key indicator of healthy coastal environments.
