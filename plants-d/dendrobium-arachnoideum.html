Dendrobium arachnoideum

Dendrobium arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, thin, and often hairy stems that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium arachnoideum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique appearance.
