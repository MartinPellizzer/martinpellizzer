Diuris curvifolia

Diuris curvifolia, commonly known as the sickle-leaf sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often curved leaves and showy, sun-like flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Diuris curvifolia is a perennial herb that produces a single, erect stem with several leaves along its length.
