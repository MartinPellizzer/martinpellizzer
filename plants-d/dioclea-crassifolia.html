Dioclea crassifolia

Dioclea crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved dioclea, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
