Diasperus dasyanthus

Diasperus dasyanthus, commonly known as the rough-haired daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Diasperus dasyanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
