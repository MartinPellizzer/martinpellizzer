Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. savogiensis

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

savogiensis, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in woodland or heathland habitats. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, elongated pseudobulbs.

This subspecies is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native range.
