Dendrochilum cupulatum

Dendrochilum cupulatum, commonly known as the cup-shaped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, cup-like structure formed by the fusion of its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive, elongated spur.

Dendrochilum cupulatum is appreciated for its unusual morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
