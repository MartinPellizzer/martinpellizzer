Diosma imbricata

Diosma imbricata, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in a fine layer of hair.

Diosma imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
