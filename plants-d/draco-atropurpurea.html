Draco atropurpurea

Draco atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple dragon tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has a unique appearance with its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
