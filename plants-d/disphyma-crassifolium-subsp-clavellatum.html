Disphyma crassifolium subsp. clavellatum

Disphyma crassifolium subsp.

clavellatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved disphyma, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in succulent gardens.
