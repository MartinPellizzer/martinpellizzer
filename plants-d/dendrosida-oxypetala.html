Dendrosida oxypetala

Dendrosida oxypetala, commonly known as the sharp-petaled tree, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers with five pointed petals, which bloom in clusters during the spring season. Its botanical classification places it within the family Myrtaceae, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native environment.
