Disperis oppositifolia var. mascarenensis

Disperis oppositifolia var. mascarenensis, commonly known as the Mascarene disperis, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in dry, sunny environments and is considered a rare species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
