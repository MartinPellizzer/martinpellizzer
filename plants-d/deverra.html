Deverra

Deverra, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It comprises several species, most notably Deverra leucophaea, which is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes, and it produces pods containing edible seeds.

Deverra species are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
