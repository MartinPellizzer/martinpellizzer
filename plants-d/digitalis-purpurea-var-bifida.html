Digitalis purpurea var. bifida

Digitalis purpurea var. bifida, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, purple to pink flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in medicine.
