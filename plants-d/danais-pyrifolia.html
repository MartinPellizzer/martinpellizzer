Danais pyrifolia

Danais pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that change color with the seasons, often displaying vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its foliage and seasonal color changes make it a favorite for landscape design and horticultural displays.
