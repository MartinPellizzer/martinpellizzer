Dendrobium tetragonum var. serpentis

Dendrobium tetragonum var. serpentis, commonly known as the serpent orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a creeping rhizome and a rosette of broad, fleshy leaves. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive dark red or purple lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique floral morphology.
