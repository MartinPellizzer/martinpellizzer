Drymophloeus singaporensis

Drymophloeus singaporensis, commonly known as the Singapore flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, legume-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological and aesthetic benefits.
