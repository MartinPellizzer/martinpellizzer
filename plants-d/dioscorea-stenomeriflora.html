Dioscorea stenomeriflora

Dioscorea stenomeriflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical structure supports its role as a medicinal plant, with the tubers containing compounds of pharmacological interest.

The species is of interest to botanists and ethnobotanists due to its potential therapeutic applications.
