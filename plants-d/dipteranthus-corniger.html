Dipteranthus corniger

Dipteranthus corniger, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
