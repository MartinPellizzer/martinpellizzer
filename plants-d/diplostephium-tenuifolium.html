Diplostephium tenuifolium

Diplostephium tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, alternate leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, red berries that are a common food source for birds. Its roots penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis.

This unique relationship with its host allows it to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
