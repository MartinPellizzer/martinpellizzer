Draba grandiflora

Draba grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh mountain environments. It produces basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and has showy white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Draba grandiflora is often found in meadows, slopes, and scree areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
