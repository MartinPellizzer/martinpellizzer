Desmodium lamprocarpum

Desmodium lamprocarpum, commonly known as the silverleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Desmodium lamprocarpum is a legume, capable of nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
