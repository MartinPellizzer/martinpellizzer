Dioscorea epistephioides

Dioscorea epistephioides, commonly known as the broadleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used for both culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its growth habit involves twining stems that can reach several meters in length, making it a distinctive feature in its natural habitat.

The tubers of D. epistephioides are often harvested for their starchy content and traditional uses in local communities.
