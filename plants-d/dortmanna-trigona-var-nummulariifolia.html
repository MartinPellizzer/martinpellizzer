Dortmanna trigona var. nummulariifolia

Dortmanna trigona var. nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are round and resemble coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the growing season. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
