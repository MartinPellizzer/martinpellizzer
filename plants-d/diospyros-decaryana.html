Diospyros decaryana

Diospyros decaryana, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and it contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, edible pulp.

Diospyros decaryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
