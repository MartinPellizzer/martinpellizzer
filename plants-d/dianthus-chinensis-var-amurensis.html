Dianthus chinensis var. amurensis

Dianthus chinensis var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur carnation, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and displays clusters of pink to red, showy flowers with prominent white centers. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs and has lance-shaped, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
