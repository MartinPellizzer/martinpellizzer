Diplazium rude

Diplazium rude, commonly known as rough serrate fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that are often covered in small, rough teeth along the margins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Diplazium rude is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
