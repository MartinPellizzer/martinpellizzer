Drimys buxifolia

Drimys buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf drimys, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and appear in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
