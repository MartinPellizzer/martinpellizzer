Dontostemon oblongifolius

Dontostemon oblongifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed starflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include hairy stems and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
