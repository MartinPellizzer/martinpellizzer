Dregea Abyssinica

Dregea abyssinica is a unique and lesser-known plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It belongs to the legume family and is characterized by its striking, elongated seed pods. This species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions. Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for local wildlife and its potential medicinal properties. Despite its resilience, Dregea abyssinica remains under-studied and faces threats from habitat loss and climate change.
