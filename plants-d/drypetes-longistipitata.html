Drypetes longistipitata

Drypetes longistipitata, commonly known as the long-stemmed drypetes, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Drypetes longistipitata is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
