Dissotis leonensis

Dissotis leonensis, commonly known as the leopard's ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble a leopard's ear. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
