Diospyros mediterranea

Diospyros mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been historically used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
