Daucus muricatus f. heterocarpus

Daucus muricatus f. heterocarpus, commonly known as the rough carrot or hairy carrot, is a botanical variant of the wild carrot species.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its fruits are small, round, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and is a member of the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties.
