Dianthus sartorii

Dianthus sartorii, commonly known as the garden pinks, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical petals that are often adorned with a distinctive red or purple spot at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus sartorii is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
