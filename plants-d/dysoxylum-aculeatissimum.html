Dysoxylum aculeatissimum

Dysoxylum aculeatissimum, commonly known as the spiny dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is often found near rivers.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
