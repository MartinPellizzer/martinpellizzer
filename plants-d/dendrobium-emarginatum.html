Dendrobium emarginatum

Dendrobium emarginatum, commonly known as the emarginate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple stems. The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium emarginatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
