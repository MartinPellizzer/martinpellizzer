Dalea carnea var. gracilis

Dalea carnea var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, ascending stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many legumes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
