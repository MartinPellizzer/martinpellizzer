Dicentra formosa

Dicentra formosa, commonly known as the bleeding heart, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Dicentra formosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
