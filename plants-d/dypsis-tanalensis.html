Dypsis tanalensis

Dypsis tanalensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped trunk and clusters of feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by dark, edible fruits. Dypsis tanalensis is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
