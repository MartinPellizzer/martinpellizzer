Dorstenia dinklagei var. bequaertii

Dorstenia dinklagei var. bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's spider plant, is a unique species native to the humid lowland forests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, curling inflorescences that resemble spider-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spiral pattern along a fleshy, leaf-like stem. Its foliage consists of narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its native environment.

This variant is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic qualities.
