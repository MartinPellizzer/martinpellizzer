Dalechampia scandens f. latifolia

Dalechampia scandens f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed giant hogweed, is a variant of the giant hogweed species native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant can grow quite tall, often reaching several meters in height, and is known for its robust and vigorous growth habit. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and requires careful management due to its potential ecological impact.
