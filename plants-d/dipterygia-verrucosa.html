Dipterygia verrucosa

Dipterygia verrucosa, commonly known as the roughleaf ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and canyons.

Dipterygia verrucosa is valued for its hard, durable wood and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.
