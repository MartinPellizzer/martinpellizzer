Dryopteris leucostipes

Dryopteris leucostipes, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, silver-white undersides on the fronds, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a graceful, arching appearance that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Dryopteris leucostipes is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
