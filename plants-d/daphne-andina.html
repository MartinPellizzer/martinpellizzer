Daphne andina

Daphne andina, commonly known as the Andean daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with showy petals and a distinctive, spicy scent. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing a dense, foliage-rich appearance.

Daphne andina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
