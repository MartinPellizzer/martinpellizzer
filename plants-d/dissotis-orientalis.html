Dissotis orientalis

Dissotis orientalis, commonly known as the oriental dissotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
