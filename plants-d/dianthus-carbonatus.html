Dianthus carbonatus

Dianthus carbonatus, commonly known as the fire pink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth habit with hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Dianthus carbonatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
