Dianella tarda

Dianella tarda, commonly known as the coastal flax or silver flax, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to southeastern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often forming dense clumps. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize dunes.

Dianella tarda is also known for its resilience to salt spray and wind.
