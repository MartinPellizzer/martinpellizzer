Dalea purpurea f. pubescens

Dalea purpurea f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent purple Dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
