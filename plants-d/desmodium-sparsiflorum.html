Desmodium sparsiflorum

Desmodium sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparsely-flowered desmodium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with rhizobia bacteria.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
