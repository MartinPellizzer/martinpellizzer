Draba catlingii

Draba catlingii, commonly known as Catling's whitlow grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and arctic environments, often in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowering stems are usually unbranched. Draba catlingii is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates, making it a hardy species.

Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.
