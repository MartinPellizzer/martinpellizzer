Dioscorea kamoonensis var. henryi

Dioscorea kamoonensis var. henryi, commonly known as the Kashmir yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its starchy, elongated tubers that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits.

The tubers of this variety are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in herbal remedies for their potential health benefits.
