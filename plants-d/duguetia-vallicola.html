Duguetia vallicola

Duguetia vallicola, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at higher elevations. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central spur. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Duguetia vallicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
