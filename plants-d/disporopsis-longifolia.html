Disporopsis longifolia

Disporopsis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved disporopsis, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying wetland ecosystems.
