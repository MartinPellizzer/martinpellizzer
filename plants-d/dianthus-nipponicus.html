Dianthus nipponicus

Dianthus nipponicus, commonly known as Japanese carnation, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Dianthus nipponicus is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in ornamental gardening.
