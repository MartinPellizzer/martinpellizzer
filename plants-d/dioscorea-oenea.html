Dioscorea oenea

Dioscorea oenea, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and agricultural purposes. The plant belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The tubers of Dioscorea oenea contain compounds such as diosgenin, which has been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
