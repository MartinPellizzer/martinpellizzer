Dracaena vanillosa

Dracaena vanillosa, commonly known as the vanilla dracaena, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, which are followed by red berries. Dracaena vanillosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
