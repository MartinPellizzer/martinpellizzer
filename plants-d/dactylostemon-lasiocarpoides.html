Dactylostemon lasiocarpoides

Dactylostemon lasiocarpoides, commonly known as the woolly fruit-bearing plant, is a species native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color. Its fruits are woody, spherical berries that mature to a reddish hue.

Dactylostemon lasiocarpoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and heat.
