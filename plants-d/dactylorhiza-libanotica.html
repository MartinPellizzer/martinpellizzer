Dactylorhiza libanotica

Dactylorhiza libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
