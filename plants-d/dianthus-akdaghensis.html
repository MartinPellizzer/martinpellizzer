Dianthus akdaghensis

Dianthus akdaghensis, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Dianthus akdaghensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
