Dioscorea davidsei

Dioscorea davidsei, commonly known as the David's yam, is a climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are often cultivated for their nutritional value and have a starchy, sweet flavor.

Dioscorea davidsei is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
