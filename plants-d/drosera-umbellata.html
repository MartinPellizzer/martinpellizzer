Drosera umbellata

Drosera umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, sticky leaves that trap insects. The plant produces small, white, umbrella-shaped inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and is often found in sandy or peaty soils.

Drosera umbellata is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
