Dryopteris × kominatoensis

Dryopteris × kominatoensis, commonly known as the Kominato fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dryopteris carpathica and Dryopteris affinis.

It is a medium-sized fern characterized by its lush, dark green fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by indusia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions of Asia.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
