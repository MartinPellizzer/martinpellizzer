Dicliptera samoensis

Dicliptera samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often inconspicuous. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Dicliptera samoensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native ecosystems.
