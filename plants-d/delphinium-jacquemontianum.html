Delphinium jacquemontianum

Delphinium jacquemontianum, commonly known as the Jacquemont's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spindly stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
