Doronicum austriacum subsp. austriacum

Doronicum austriacum subsp.

austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with red spots.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
