Dictyopteris brooksii

Dictyopteris brooksii, commonly known as the Brooks' sea palm, is a species of brown algae belonging to the family Dictyopteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like fronds that are often branched and feathered. The plant grows in shallow coastal waters, typically in the intertidal zone, where it attaches to rocks or other substrates. Its structure consists of a holdfast, stipe, and blades, typical of many brown algae.

Dictyopteris brooksii plays an important role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
