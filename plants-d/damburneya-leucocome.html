Damburneya leucocome

Damburneya leucocome, commonly known as the white-flowered damburneya, is a unique plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and coastal landscapes.
