Dioscorea amaranthoides var. denuda

Dioscorea amaranthoides var. denuda, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy tubers that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized for both culinary and medicinal purposes in various cultures.
