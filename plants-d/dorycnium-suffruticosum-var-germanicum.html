Dorycnium suffruticosum var. germanicum

Dorycnium suffruticosum var. germanicum, commonly known as the German senna, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes during the summer months, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
