Diospyros ovalis

Diospyros ovalis, commonly known as the oval persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruits are typically oval, fleshy, and edible, with a sweet flavor when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
