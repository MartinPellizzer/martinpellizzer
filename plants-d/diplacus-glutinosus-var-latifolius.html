Diplacus glutinosus var. latifolius

Diplacus glutinosus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf sticky monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
