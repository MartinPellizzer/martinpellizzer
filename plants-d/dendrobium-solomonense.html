Dendrobium solomonense

Dendrobium solomonense, commonly known as the Solomon Islands orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers bloom during the wet season, adding vibrant color to the forest canopy.

Dendrobium solomonense is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in the region.
