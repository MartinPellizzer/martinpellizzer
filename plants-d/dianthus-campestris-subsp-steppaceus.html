Dianthus campestris subsp. steppaceus

Dianthus campestris subsp.

steppaceus, commonly known as the steppe campion, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
