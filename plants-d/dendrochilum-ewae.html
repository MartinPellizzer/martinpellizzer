Dendrochilum ewae

Dendrochilum ewae, commonly known as the Ewae's tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Dendrochilum ewae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
