Delphinium trianthophorum

Delphinium trianthophorum, commonly known as the three-flowered larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium trianthophorum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and toxic properties.
