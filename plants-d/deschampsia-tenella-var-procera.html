Deschampsia tenella var. procera

Deschampsia tenella var. procera, commonly known as tall hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, fluffy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which are adorned with silky hairs.

Deschampsia tenella var. procera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
