Dalea occidentalis

Dalea occidentalis, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellow to reddish-brown pods that contain seeds.

Dalea occidentalis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
