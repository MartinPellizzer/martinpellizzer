Diospyros acuta

Diospyros acuta, commonly known as the sharp-pointed ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony species. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a glossy appearance.

Diospyros acuta typically grows in lowland tropical forests and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
