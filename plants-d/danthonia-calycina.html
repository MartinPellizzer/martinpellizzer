Danthonia calycina

Danthonia calycina, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Danthonia calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
