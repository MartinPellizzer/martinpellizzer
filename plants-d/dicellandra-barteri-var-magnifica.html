Dicellandra barteri var. magnifica

Dicellandra barteri var. magnifica, commonly known as the giant karamono, is a striking member of the Santalaceae family native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stem is often covered with a thick, waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential ecological and medicinal significance.
