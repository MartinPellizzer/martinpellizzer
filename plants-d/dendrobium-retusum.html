Dendrobium retusum

Dendrobium retusum, commonly known as the rough-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees or rocks, relying on its aerial roots for moisture and nutrients. The plant features erect, branched stems covered with a dense layer of white, waxy hairs. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium retusum produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often blooming in summer.
