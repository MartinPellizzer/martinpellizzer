Danthonia pauciflora

Danthonia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flower grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with few flowers. Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand arid conditions and frequent grazing.

Danthonia pauciflora is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
