Diasperus venulosus

Diasperus venulosus, commonly known as the veiny flatweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive veiny pattern that gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Diasperus venulosus is known for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
