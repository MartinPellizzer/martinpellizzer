Dioscorea grandibulbosa

Dioscorea grandibulbosa, commonly known as the giant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its large, tuberous roots that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and is often cultivated for its edible tubers, which are rich in starch. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.
