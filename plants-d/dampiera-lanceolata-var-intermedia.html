Dampiera lanceolata var. intermedia

Dampiera lanceolata var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dampiera, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal or semi-arid regions. It is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal dunes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
