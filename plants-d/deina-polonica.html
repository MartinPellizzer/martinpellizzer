Deina polonica

Deina polonica, commonly known as the Polish lichen, is a crustose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery thallus and is often found on tree bark, stones, and soil in temperate regions. The lichen forms a symbiotic relationship with a specific species of cyanobacteria, which contributes to its pigmentation and nutrient acquisition. Its slow growth rate makes it a valuable indicator of environmental conditions and air quality.

Deina polonica is relatively rare and is of interest to mycologists and conservationists due to its ecological significance.
