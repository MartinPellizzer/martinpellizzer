Dictyopteris nusakembangana

Dictyopteris nusakembangana, commonly known as the Nusakembangan sea palm, is a rare and unique species of brown algae found in the tropical waters of Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Dictyopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble the structure of a palm tree. The plant grows in shallow, warm coastal waters and is often attached to rocky substrates. Its reproductive structures are complex, with both male and female gametes produced on separate fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an important indicator species for marine biodiversity in the region.
