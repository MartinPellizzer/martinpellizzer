Dinochloa aguilarii

Dinochloa aguilarii, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Dinochloa aguilarii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
