Dioscorea gracillima

Dioscorea gracillima, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, thin stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it distinct from other yam species, with a unique growth habit and morphological traits.

The tubers of Dioscorea gracillima are not typically cultivated for food but are valued for their medicinal properties.
