Dasyanthus sylvaticus

Dasyanthus sylvaticus, commonly known as the rough-leaved dasyanthus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and small, fragrant white flowers. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Dasyanthus sylvaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
