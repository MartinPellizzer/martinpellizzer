Dinochloa hirsuta

Dinochloa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy dinochloa, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and tufted growth habit. Native to certain regions of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery-blue hue due to the fine hairs covering the surface.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the base of the leaf sheaths.
