Dombeya coriopsis

Dombeya coriopsis, commonly known as the smooth dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Dombeya coriopsis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
