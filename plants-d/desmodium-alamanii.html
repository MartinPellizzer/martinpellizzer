Desmodium alamanii

Desmodium alamanii, commonly known as the smallflower velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Desmodium alamanii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
