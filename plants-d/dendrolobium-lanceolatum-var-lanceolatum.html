Dendrolobium lanceolatum var. lanceolatum

Dendrolobium lanceolatum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces hard, woody pods that contain seeds.

This species is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.
