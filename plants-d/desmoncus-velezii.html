Desmoncus velezii

Desmoncus velezii, commonly known as the Velezii mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses specialized roots called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are often dispersed by birds. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and arranged in pairs along the stem.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
