Dillwynia lanceolata

Dillwynia lanceolata, commonly known as the slender dillwynia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. It produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the spring and summer months.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its specific epithet "lanceolata.".
