Diasperus cinereus

Diasperus cinereus, commonly known as the gray sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, segmented fruits called schizocarps, which split into two one-seeded parts upon maturity. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
