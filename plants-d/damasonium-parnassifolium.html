Damasonium parnassifolium

Damasonium parnassifolium, commonly known as the mountain waterleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
