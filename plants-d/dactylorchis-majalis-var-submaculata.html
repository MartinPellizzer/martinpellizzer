Dactylorchis majalis var. submaculata

Dactylorchis majalis var. submaculata, commonly known as the spotted lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a long, spiraled inflorescence bearing numerous small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are arranged in a helical pattern along the stem, a unique feature among orchids.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
