Didissandra saxatilis var. microcalyx

Didissandra saxatilis var. microcalyx, commonly known as the small-fruited stonecress, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

The variety microcalyx is distinguished by its smaller, more compact fruits compared to the typical form.
