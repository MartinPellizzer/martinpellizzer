Daemonorops verticillaris var. straminea

Daemonorops verticillaris var. straminea, commonly known as the golden fan palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that radiate from the top. The leaves are typically a vibrant yellow-green, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into coconut-like fruits.
