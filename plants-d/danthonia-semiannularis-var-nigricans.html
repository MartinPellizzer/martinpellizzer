Danthonia semiannularis var. nigricans

Danthonia semiannularis var. nigricans, commonly known as the black-stemmed muirgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark-colored stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
