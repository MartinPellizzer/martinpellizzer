Delphinium divaricatum var. gilliatii

Delphinium divaricatum var. gilliatii, commonly known as Gilliat's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

The plant is known for its striking floral display and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
