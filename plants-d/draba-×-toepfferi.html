Draba × toepfferi

Draba × toepfferi, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Draba × toepfferi is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features include basal rosettes of leaves and a flowering stem that bears clusters of yellow or white flowers.
