Drosodendron

Drosodendron, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or small trees native to Asia, particularly China. The plants are characterized by their showy, often pink or white flowers that bloom in spring or early summer. They typically have simple, ovate leaves and produce capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Drosodendron species are valued for their ornamental value and are often cultivated in gardens for their beauty and resilience.
