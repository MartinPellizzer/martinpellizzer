Delphinium stachydeum

Delphinium stachydeum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
