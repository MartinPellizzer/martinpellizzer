Durabaculum undulatum

Durabaculum undulatum, commonly known as the wavy durabacrum, is a rare and specialized plant species found in arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are ribbed and undulating, which help it store water and reduce surface area for evaporation. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a crucial role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and is considered a keystone species in its habitat.
