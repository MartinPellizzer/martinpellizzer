Dryopteris perglandulifera

Dryopteris perglandulifera, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, dark green fronds with golden-brown to rust-colored margins. The fronds are typically divided into lance-shaped segments, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's rhizomes are creeping and produce new growth throughout the growing season.
