Daiswa violacea

Daiswa violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to violet, often with a white or pink throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Daiswa violacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
