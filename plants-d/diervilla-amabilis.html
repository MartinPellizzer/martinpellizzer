Diervilla amabilis

Diervilla amabilis, commonly known as sweet azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and becomes particularly striking in the fall.

Diervilla amabilis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
