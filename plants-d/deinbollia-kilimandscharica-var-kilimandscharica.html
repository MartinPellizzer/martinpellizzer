Deinbollia kilimandscharica var. kilimandscharica

Deinbollia kilimandscharica var. kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, typically at elevations above 2,000 meters.

This species is notable for its role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
