Dactylorhiza maculata lusus carnea

Dactylorhiza maculata lusus carnea, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, upright stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often spotted with purple or brown markings. The plant produces a single spike of showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
