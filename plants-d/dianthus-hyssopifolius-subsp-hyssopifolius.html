Dianthus hyssopifolius subsp. hyssopifolius

Dianthus hyssopifolius subsp.

hyssopifolius, commonly known as the hyssop-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in dry, sunny habitats.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive central spur.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
