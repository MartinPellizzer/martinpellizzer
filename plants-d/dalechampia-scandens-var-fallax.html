Dalechampia scandens var. fallax

Dalechampia scandens var. fallax, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a variant of the silverleaf hibiscus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hair. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are notable for their unique texture and coloration, which provide a striking contrast to the green foliage of other plants.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
