Dipteracanthus subdenticulatus

Dipteracanthus subdenticulatus, commonly known as the narrow-toothed dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Dipteracanthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and serrated, contributing to its common name.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral morphology and limited distribution.
