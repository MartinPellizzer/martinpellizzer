Doritis paniculata

Doritis paniculata, commonly known as the panicle orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Doritis paniculata is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms during the summer months.
