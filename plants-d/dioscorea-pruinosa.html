Dioscorea pruinosa

Dioscorea pruinosa, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional medicine.

Dioscorea pruinosa is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils, often found in woodland or shaded areas.
