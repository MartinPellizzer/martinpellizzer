Droguetia urticifolia

Droguetia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many species with stinging hairs. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine, irritant trichomes that cause a burning sensation upon contact.

Droguetia urticifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
