Didymopanax distractiflorus

Didymopanax distractiflorus, commonly known as the Brazilian star tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and distinctive, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark, and its leaves are compound, with long, slender leaflets. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
