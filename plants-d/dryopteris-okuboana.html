Dryopteris okuboana

Dryopteris okuboana, commonly known as the Okubo's wood fern, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce fertile sporangia on the undersides of its leaves.

The botanical features of D. okuboana make it a prized species among fern enthusiasts and botanists.
