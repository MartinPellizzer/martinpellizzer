Diplopterygium laevissimum

Diplopterygium laevissimum, commonly known as the smooth diplopterygium, is a member of the family Dipterocarpaceae, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
