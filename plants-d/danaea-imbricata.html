Danaea imbricata

Danaea imbricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or scarlet bower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with prominent yellow markings. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests.

Danaea imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
