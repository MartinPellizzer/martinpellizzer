Daviesia intricata subsp. intricata

Daviesia intricata subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the mountain daphne, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that appear in late summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
