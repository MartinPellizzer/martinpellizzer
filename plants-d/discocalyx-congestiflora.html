Discocalyx congestiflora

Discocalyx congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered calyx flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
