Deschampsia caespitosa

Deschampsia caespitosa, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that often have a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Deschampsia caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
