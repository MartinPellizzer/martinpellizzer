Dactylis smithii

Dactylis smithii, commonly known as the purple false oat, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce purple to pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Dactylis smithii is also used in horticulture for its decorative foliage and flowering display.
