Diplachne baueri

Diplachne baueri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Diplachne baueri is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
