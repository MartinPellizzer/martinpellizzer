Dioscorea rumicoides

Dioscorea rumicoides, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. The plant belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is known for its vigorous growth habit. Its tuberous roots have been used traditionally in some cultures for medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
