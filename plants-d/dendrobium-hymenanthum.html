Dendrobium hymenanthum

Dendrobium hymenanthum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and clusters of showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding environment.

Dendrobium hymenanthum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, fragrant blooms.
