Dalbergia cujabensis

Dalbergia cujabensis, commonly known as the Cujabensis rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes.

It typically grows in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
