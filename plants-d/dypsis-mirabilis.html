Dypsis mirabilis

Dypsis mirabilis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
