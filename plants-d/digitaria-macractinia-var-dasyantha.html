Digitaria macractinia var. dasyantha

Digitaria macractinia var. dasyantha, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a variant of the Digitaria genus within the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant typically produces inflorescences that consist of spikelets arranged in a panicle structure. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
