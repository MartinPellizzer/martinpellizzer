Delphinium apolanum

Delphinium apolanum, commonly known as the Apolana larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are typically compound, with lanceolate leaflets that are covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium apolanum is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
