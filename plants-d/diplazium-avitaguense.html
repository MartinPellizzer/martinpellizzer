Diplazium avitaguense

Diplazium avitaguense, commonly known as the Guiana spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori surrounded by indusia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Diplazium avitaguense is primarily found in the Caribbean and parts of South America.
