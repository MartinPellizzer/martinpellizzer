Draba meskhetica

Draba meskhetica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Draba meskhetica is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
