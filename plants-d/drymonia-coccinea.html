Drymonia coccinea

Drymonia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its vibrant red spathe and a spadix that bears small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped leaves that are often mottled with dark veins.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
