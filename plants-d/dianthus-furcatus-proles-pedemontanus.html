Dianthus furcatus proles pedemontanus

Dianthus furcatus proles pedemontanus, commonly known as the Piedmont carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, double flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, with a distinctive frilled edge. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
