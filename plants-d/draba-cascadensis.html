Draba cascadensis

Draba cascadensis, commonly known as the Cascade bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowers are usually pale blue to white in color.

Draba cascadensis is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
