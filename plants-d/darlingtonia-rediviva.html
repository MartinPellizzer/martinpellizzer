Darlingtonia rediviva

Darlingtonia rediviva, commonly known as the red ivy or the ghost plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Sarraceniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. Its unique morphology includes a hooded structure that directs rainwater into the pitcher, aiding in the trapping of prey.

Darlingtonia rediviva is also notable for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance in its habitat.
