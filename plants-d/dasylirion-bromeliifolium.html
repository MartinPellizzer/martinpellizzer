Dasylirion bromeliifolium

Dasylirion bromeliifolium, commonly known as the desert dagger, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a rough texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-like flowers that are usually yellow or orange, emerging from the center of the rosette. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

The botanical characteristics of Dasylirion bromeliifolium make it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
