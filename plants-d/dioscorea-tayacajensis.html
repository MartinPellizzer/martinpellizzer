Dioscorea tayacajensis

Dioscorea tayacajensis, commonly known as the Ayapana yoca, is a climbing perennial vine native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source by indigenous communities.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments with well-drained soil.
