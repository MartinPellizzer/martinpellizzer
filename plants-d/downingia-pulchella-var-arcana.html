Downingia pulchella var. arcana

Downingia pulchella var. arcana, commonly known as the Sierra Valley bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, meadow-like habitats at high elevations. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine environments.
