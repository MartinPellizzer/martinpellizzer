Dianthus marryatiae

Dianthus marryatiae, commonly known as the Marryat's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Dianthus marryatiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
