Dioscorea ulinei var. longipes

Dioscorea ulinei var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The varietal name "longipes" refers to its elongated, slender stems.
