Durabaculum pomatophilum

Durabaculum pomatophilum, commonly known as the desert baculum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its scientific name reflects its durability and its association with the baculum, a structure found in some mammals.

Durabaculum pomatophilum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
