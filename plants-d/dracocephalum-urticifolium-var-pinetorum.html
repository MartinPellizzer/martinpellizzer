Dracocephalum urticifolium var. pinetorum

Dracocephalum urticifolium var. pinetorum, commonly known as mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and lance-shaped leaves with a rough, serrated margin. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
