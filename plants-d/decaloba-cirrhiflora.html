Decaloba cirrhiflora

Decaloba cirrhiflora, commonly known as the silky leaf fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in high humidity. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Decaloba cirrhiflora make it an attractive addition to tropical gardens and a subject of interest in botanical research.
