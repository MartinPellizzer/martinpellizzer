Dianthus hispanicus subsp. fontqueri

Dianthus hispanicus subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, double-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The subspecies fontqueri is distinguished by its compact growth habit and vibrant bloom colors.
