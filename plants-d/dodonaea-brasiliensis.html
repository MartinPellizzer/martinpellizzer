Dodonaea brasiliensis

Dodonaea brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate and have a distinctive reddish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
