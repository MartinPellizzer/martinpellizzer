Diasperus triandrus

Diasperus triandrus, commonly known as the three-angled sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a branched stem with small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a three-angled capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Diasperus triandrus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
