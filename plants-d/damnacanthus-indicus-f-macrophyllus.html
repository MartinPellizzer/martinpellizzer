Damnacanthus indicus f. macrophyllus

Damnacanthus indicus f. macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant flame bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and vibrant red to orange flower spikes.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves that contribute to its dramatic appearance, while its inflorescences bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can reach heights of up to 10 feet.

Due to its rapid growth and potential invasiveness, it should be planted with caution in regions where it may outcompete native vegetation.
