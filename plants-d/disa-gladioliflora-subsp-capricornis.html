Disa gladioliflora subsp. capricornis

Disa gladioliflora subsp.

capricornis, commonly known as the Capricorn Disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of vibrant, spurred flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically blooms during the winter months, showcasing its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full to partial sunlight for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
