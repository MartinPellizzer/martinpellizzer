Doritis pulcherrima var. caerulea

Doritis pulcherrima var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered torch ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant features vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense, torch-like inflorescences, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
