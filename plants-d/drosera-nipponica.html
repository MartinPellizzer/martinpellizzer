Drosera nipponica

Drosera nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese Sundew, is a species of carnivorous plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its small white flowers bloom in summer, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Drosera nipponica is valued both for its unique appearance and its role in ecological systems.
