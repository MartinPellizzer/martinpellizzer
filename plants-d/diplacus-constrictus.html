Diplacus constrictus

Diplacus constrictus, commonly known as the hummingbird trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red to orange flowers that are highly attractive to hummingbirds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Diplacus constrictus thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
