Dioscorea castilloniana

Dioscorea castilloniana, commonly known as the Castillon's yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Colombia. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it bears small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.
