Depazium lobatocrenatum

Depazium lobatocrenatum, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Depazium lobatocrenatum is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
