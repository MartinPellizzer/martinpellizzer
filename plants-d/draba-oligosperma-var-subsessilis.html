Draba oligosperma var. subsessilis

Draba oligosperma var. subsessilis, commonly known as the slenderleaf whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It typically features a basal rosette of linear, glaucous leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is relatively short-lived, with a lifespan of one to three years.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, resilient species suited to high-altitude and polar climates.
