Dalbergia entadoides

Dalbergia entadoides, commonly known as the yellow-heart wood, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood with a distinctive yellowish hue. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several flattened seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable timber used in furniture and musical instruments.
