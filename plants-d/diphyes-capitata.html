Diphyes capitata

Diphyes capitata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The wood of Diphyes capitata is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a significant resource in traditional craftsmanship.
