Daucus divaricatus

Daucus divaricatus, commonly known as the wild carrot or Queen Anne's lace, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and umbels of white flowers that resemble lace. Its roots are long and slender, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
