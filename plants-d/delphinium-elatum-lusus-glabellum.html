Delphinium elatum lusus glabellum

Delphinium elatum lusus glabellum, commonly known as the glabellum larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
