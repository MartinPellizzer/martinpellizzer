Dichelostemma ida-maia

Dichelostemma ida-maia, commonly known as the Sierra daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Dichelostemma ida-maia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
