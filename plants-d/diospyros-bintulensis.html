Diospyros bintulensis

Diospyros bintulensis, commonly known as Bintulense persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange to reddish berries.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine due to its high nutritional value.
