Dactylis kirschbaumii

Dactylis kirschbaumii, commonly known as the Kirschbaum's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces pale green to bluish-green leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Dactylis kirschbaumii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
