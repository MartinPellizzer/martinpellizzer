Dioscorea microbotrya var. grandifolia

Dioscorea microbotrya var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It produces large, showy white or pink flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. The plant has tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
