Dodecatheon radicatum subsp. monanthum

Dodecatheon radicatum subsp.

monanthum, commonly known as the single-flowered glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
