Digitaria porrecta

Digitaria porrecta, commonly known as flatawn, is a grass species native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, flat leaves and distinctive seed heads that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, preferring well-drained soils. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is typical for many grass species.

Digitaria porrecta is also valued as a forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
