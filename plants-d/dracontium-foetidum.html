Dracontium foetidum

Dracontium foetidum, commonly known as the stinking arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a thick, starchy root that is used as a food source in some regions. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

The plant is also noted for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more prominent in its flowers.
