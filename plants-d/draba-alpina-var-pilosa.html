Draba alpina var. pilosa

Draba alpina var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming in early spring.

It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in mountainous environments where it is adapted to cold and harsh conditions.
