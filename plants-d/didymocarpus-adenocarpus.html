Didymocarpus adenocarpus

Didymocarpus adenocarpus, commonly known as the glossy apple or tamarind apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
