Derris cuneifolia

Derris cuneifolia, commonly known as the slender derris, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, also known as the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a toxic compound used in traditional fishing practices. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments.
