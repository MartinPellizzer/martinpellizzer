Drosera rubricalyx

Drosera rubricalyx, commonly known as the red-lipped sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, glandular-tipped leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments where it can thrive on the nutrients obtained from its prey. Its bright red lips, which give it its common name, are a striking feature that aids in attracting insects.

The species is well adapted to its habitat, displaying both aesthetic and functional botanical traits.
