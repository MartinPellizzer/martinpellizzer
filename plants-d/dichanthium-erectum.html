Dichanthium erectum

Dichanthium erectum, commonly known as the upright false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Dichanthium erectum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
