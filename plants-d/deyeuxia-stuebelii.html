Deyeuxia stuebelii

Deyeuxia stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

Deyeuxia stuebelii is often found in association with other wetland vegetation, contributing to the ecological diversity of such habitats.
