Dentaria laciniata f. hexifolia

Dentaria laciniata f. hexifolia, commonly known as the six-lobed toothplant, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, toothed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. Its habitat includes moist soils in woodlands and meadows, and it is native to North America.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
