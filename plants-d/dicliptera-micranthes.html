Dicliptera micranthes

Dicliptera micranthes, commonly known as small-flowered dicliptera, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
