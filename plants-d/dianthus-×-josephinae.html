Dianthus × josephinae

Dianthus × josephinae, commonly known as the Josephina's carnation, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Dianthus × josephinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
