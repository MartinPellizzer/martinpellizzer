Diospyros obscurinerva

Diospyros obscurinerva, commonly known as the dark-veined persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have prominent veins. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
