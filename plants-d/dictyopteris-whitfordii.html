Dictyopteris whitfordii

Dictyopteris whitfordii, commonly known as the Whitford's sea palm, is a species of green algae belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its branching, tree-like structure composed of flattened, blade-like fronds. This marine plant typically grows in shallow coastal waters, often attached to rocks or other substrates. The fronds are rich in chlorophyll, allowing the plant to perform photosynthesis efficiently in sunlight.

It plays an important ecological role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
