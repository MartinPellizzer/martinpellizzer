Dianthus lancifolius

Dianthus lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, showy blooms throughout the summer months.

Dianthus lancifolius is often found in dry, open habitats such as coastal cliffs and disturbed ground.
