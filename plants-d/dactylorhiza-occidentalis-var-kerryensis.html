Dactylorhiza occidentalis var. kerryensis

Dactylorhiza occidentalis var. kerryensis, commonly known as the Kerry orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to western Europe, particularly Ireland.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant produces tall, slender stems with a single or few flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a rhizomatous root system.

This variety is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
