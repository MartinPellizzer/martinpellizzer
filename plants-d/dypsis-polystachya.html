Dypsis polystachya

Dypsis polystachya, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and dense, spiky foliage that resembles a palm. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a distinctive addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
