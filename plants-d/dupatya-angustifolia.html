Dupatya angustifolia

Dupatya angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, upright stem.

Dupatya angustifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
