Digitaria tenuis

Digitaria tenuis, commonly known as slender digit grass, is a perennial grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in sandy or well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
