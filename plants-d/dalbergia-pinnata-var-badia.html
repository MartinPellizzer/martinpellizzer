Dalbergia pinnata var. badia

Dalbergia pinnata var. badia, commonly known as the yellowheart tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow to reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The tree typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen with bipinnate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.

Due to its commercial value, it is also subject to conservation concerns in its native habitats.
