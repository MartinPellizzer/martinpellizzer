Durio graveolens

Durio graveolens, commonly known as the durian, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiky fruit, which contains a creamy, aromatic pulp. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is known for its complex and often pungent odor. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by insects.

The durian is also valued for its high nutritional content and is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
