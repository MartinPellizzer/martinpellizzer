Dianthus brachyanthus subsp. tarraconensis

Dianthus brachyanthus subsp.

tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconensis campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Spain and northern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, basal leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or Mediterranean climates. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, cup-shaped blossoms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
