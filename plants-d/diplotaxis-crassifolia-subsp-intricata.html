Diplotaxis crassifolia subsp. intricata

Diplotaxis crassifolia subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are often fleshy and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The subspecies intricata is distinguished by its more complex and intricate leaf patterns compared to other subspecies of Diplotaxis.
