Deutzia fedorovii

Deutzia fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Deutzia fedorovii is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
