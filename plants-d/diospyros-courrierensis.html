Diospyros courrierensis

Diospyros courrierensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature.

The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood and is also cultivated for its edible fruit.
