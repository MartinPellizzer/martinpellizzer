Dichromanthus michuacanus f. armeniacus

Dichromanthus michuacanus f. armeniacus, commonly known as the Armenian dichromanthus, is a rare and unique variant of the dichromanthus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure, with leaves that are typically narrow and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring. It is native to specific regions in the Caucasus and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
