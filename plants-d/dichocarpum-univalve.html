Dichocarpum univalve

Dichocarpum univalve, commonly known as the single-valved twinfruit, is a member of the Berberidaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its distinctive two-seeded, single-valved fruits that open explosively upon maturity. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as temperate forests. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a smooth margin and a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
