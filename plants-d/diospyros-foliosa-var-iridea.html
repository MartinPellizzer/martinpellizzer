Diospyros foliosa var. iridea

Diospyros foliosa var. iridea, commonly known as the iridescent persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, iridescent leaves that change color with the seasons, ranging from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red fruits, which are typically edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
