Draba involuta

Draba involuta, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous species that typically grows in alpine and arctic environments. The plant features basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rounded shape, while its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes. Draba involuta is adapted to cold climates and can survive in poor soil conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
