Digitalis laevigata

Digitalis laevigata, commonly known as the European foxglove, is a biennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, erect stems with distinctive hooded flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Digitalis laevigata is known for its ornamental value and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
