Dioscorea digitaria

Dioscorea digitaria, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its palmate leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, fibrous fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source in some regions.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
