Dalbergia glaberrima subsp. ankaranensis

Dalbergia glaberrima subsp.

ankaranensis, commonly known as the Ankaranensis rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and dense, dark-colored wood.

The plant is typically found in moist, shaded habitats within humid forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a distinctive violet or pink hue.

Due to its ornamental and potential medicinal value, it is of interest for conservation and horticultural purposes.
