Dioscorea sanpaulensis

Dioscorea sanpaulensis, commonly known as San Paul's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential in both agricultural and pharmacological applications.
