Diplacrum longifolium f. graciliores

Diplacrum longifolium f. graciliores, commonly known as the slender-leafed diplacrum, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Diplacrum genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically a soft green color, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
