Drymocallis glabrata

Drymocallis glabrata, commonly known as the smooth goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, silky hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer with golden-yellow clusters.

Drymocallis glabrata plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
