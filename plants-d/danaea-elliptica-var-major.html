Danaea elliptica var. major

Danaea elliptica var. major, commonly known as the giant flame flower, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers with yellow markings, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or silver streaks. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
