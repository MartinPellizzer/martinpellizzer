Diplopappus asperulus

Diplopappus asperulus, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. Its small, purple to pinkish flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and wet meadows.
