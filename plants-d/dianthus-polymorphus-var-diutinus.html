Dianthus polymorphus var. diutinus

Dianthus polymorphus var. diutinus, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pink or white flowers with five petals and a distinctive central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its resilience and beauty in garden settings.
