Delphinium virescens var. camporum

Delphinium virescens var. camporum, commonly known as the Camp's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in tall spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate, with a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
