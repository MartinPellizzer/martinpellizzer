Dalbergaria eubracteata

Dalbergaria eubracteata, commonly known as the yellow wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Dalbergaria eubracteata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
