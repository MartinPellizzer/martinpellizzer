Daemonorops collarifera

Daemonorops collarifera, commonly known as the clustered palm or sea grape palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by clustered, edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing near the shoreline.

Its adaptability to saline environments makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
