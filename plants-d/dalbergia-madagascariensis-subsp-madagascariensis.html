Dalbergia madagascariensis subsp. madagascariensis

Dalbergia madagascariensis subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the rosewood tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood with a rich, aromatic scent.

The plant typically grows as a tall, straight tree with compound leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its high value, it is also considered an endangered species due to overharvesting.
