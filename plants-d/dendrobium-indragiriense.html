Dendrobium indragiriense

Dendrobium indragiriense, commonly known as the Indragiri orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dendrobium genus, which includes over 1,200 species of orchids. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically fragrant and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
