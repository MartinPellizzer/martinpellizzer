Daphne pachyrachis

Daphne pachyrachis, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or coastal environments. The plant produces showy clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers in early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round interest.

Daphne pachyrachis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
