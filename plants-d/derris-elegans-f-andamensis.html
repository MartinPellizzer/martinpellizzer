Derris elegans f. andamensis

Derris elegans f. andamensis, commonly known as the Andaman derris, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has been traditionally used as a fish poison and insecticide. Its roots and stems are often harvested for their pesticidal properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
