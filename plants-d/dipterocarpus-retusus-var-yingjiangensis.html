Dipterocarpus retusus var. yingjiangensis

Dipterocarpus retusus var. yingjiangensis, commonly known as the Yingjiang dipterocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province, and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and distinctive, heavy fruits. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad crown, making it a significant component of the tropical rainforest canopy.

Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
