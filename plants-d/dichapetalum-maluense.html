Dichapetalum maluense

Dichapetalum maluense, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a unique fruit that resembles a buttercup, giving it its common name. It is often found in moist, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The plant also has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
