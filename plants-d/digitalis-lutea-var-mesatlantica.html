Digitalis lutea var. mesatlantica

Digitalis lutea var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of yellowish-orange flowers with spurred petals. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
