Diospyros salicifolia

Diospyros salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple during the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, orange-red berries in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The fruit is prized for its sweet, astringent flavor and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
