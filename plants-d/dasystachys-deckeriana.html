Dasystachys deckeriana

Dasystachys deckeriana, commonly known as the rock wallaby-rush, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its fibrous roots help it survive in harsh conditions, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
