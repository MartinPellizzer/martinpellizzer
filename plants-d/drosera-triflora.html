Drosera triflora

Drosera triflora, commonly known as the three-leaved sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in nutrient-poor, wet environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in yellow or green hues.

Drosera triflora is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
