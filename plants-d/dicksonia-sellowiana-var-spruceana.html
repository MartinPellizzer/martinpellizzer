Dicksonia sellowiana var. spruceana

Dicksonia sellowiana var. spruceana, commonly known as the Sprengel's tree fern, is a species native to the wet tropical forests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a unique ability to produce spores on the undersides of its fronds, which are essential for its reproduction. Its rhizomes are robust and help anchor the plant while absorbing nutrients from the soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to humid, shaded environments.
