Dactylorhiza traunsteineri subsp. irenica

Dactylorhiza traunsteineri subsp.

irenica, commonly known as the Irish marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and northern Africa. It typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces a single, tall stem bearing several flowers. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive spurred lip.

The species is characterized by its narrow leaves and is often found in association with other orchid species in its native environment.
