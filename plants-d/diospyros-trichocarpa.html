Diospyros trichocarpa

Diospyros trichocarpa, commonly known as the persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and contains high levels of sugars and tannins, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and astringent properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
