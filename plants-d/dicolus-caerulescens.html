Dicolus caerulescens

Dicolus caerulescens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dicolus caerulescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
