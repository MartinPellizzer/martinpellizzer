Dioclea marginata

Dioclea marginata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red jungle bean, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Dioclea marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
