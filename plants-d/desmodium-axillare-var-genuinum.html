Desmodium axillare var. genuinum

Desmodium axillare var. genuinum, commonly known as the velvet bean or hairy urd, is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces pods containing small, edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Its root system is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Additionally, the plant is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop due to its nutritional value for livestock.
