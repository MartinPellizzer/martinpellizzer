Digitaria sanguinalis f. commutata

Digitaria sanguinalis f. commutata, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a variant of the common fingergrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to the Americas, characterized by its hairy leaves and seed heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged.

The botanical features of this form make it identifiable and distinguishable from other Digitaria species.
