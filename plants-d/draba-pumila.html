Draba pumila

Draba pumila, commonly known as the dwarf whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its simple, basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Draba pumila is adapted to cold climates and can survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing alpine and Arctic ecosystems.
