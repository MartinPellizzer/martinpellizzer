Desmodium erythrinifolium

Desmodium erythrinifolium, commonly known as the red-leaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with trifoliate or pinnately compound leaves. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces legume pods containing several seeds.

Desmodium erythrinifolium is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
