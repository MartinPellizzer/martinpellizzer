Dryopteris Ligulata

Dryopteris ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood fern, is a striking member of the fern family that thrives in moist, shaded environments. This plant is native to various regions across Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it often grows in forests and rocky areas. Its delicate, lance-shaped fronds create a lush, green appearance that adds beauty to its natural habitat. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its preferred conditions. With its unique structure and adaptability, Dryopteris ligulata stands out as a fascinating example of botanical diversity.
