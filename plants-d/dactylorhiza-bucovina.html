Dactylorhiza bucovina

Dactylorhiza bucovina, commonly known as the Bucovina orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and distinctive floral structure. The plant typically produces spike-like inflorescences with showy, spurred flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Dactylorhiza bucovina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for pollinators.
