Dicoma saligna

Dicoma saligna, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a rounded crown and smooth, silvery-gray bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy coating that gives them a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
