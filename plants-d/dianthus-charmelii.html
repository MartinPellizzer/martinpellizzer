Dianthus charmelii

Dianthus charmelii, commonly known as the Charlemagne's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant produces clusters of small, rounded petals that are often adorned with a distinctive dark red or purple central blotch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus charmelii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
