Diplopappus pinnatifidus

Diplopappus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
