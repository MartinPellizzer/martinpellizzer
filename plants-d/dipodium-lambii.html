Dipodium lambii

Dipodium lambii, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, green shoots that grow in dense clusters, giving it a lush, grass-like appearance. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring. It is well-suited for shady or partially shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
