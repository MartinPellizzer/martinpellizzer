Dioscorea laxiflora var. truncata

Dioscorea laxiflora var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated yam, is a variant of the yam genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of this variant make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
