Dactyloctenium bogdanii

Dactyloctenium bogdanii, commonly known as the bogdan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while its roots form dense, fibrous networks.

Dactyloctenium bogdanii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
