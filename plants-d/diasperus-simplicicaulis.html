Diasperus simplicicaulis

Diasperus simplicicaulis, commonly known as the simple-stemmed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces numerous small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Diasperus simplicicaulis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact growth habit and delicate appearance.
