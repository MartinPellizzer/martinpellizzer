Diplusion cordata

Diplusion cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
