Diospyros brevicalyx

Diospyros brevicalyx, commonly known as the Chinese date or small-leaf persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is also valued for its ornamental appeal and timber quality.
