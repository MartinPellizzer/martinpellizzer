Dalechampia linearis var. hilariana

Dalechampia linearis var. hilariana, commonly known as the silky dalechampia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
