Dolichos psoraloides

Dolichos psoraloides, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Additionally, it contains compounds such as psoralens, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
