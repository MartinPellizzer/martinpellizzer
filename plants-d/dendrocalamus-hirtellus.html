Dendrocalamus hirtellus

Dendrocalamus hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that are often used in construction and handicrafts. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Dendrocalamus hirtellus is also valued for its fast growth and ability to regenerate quickly after harvesting.
