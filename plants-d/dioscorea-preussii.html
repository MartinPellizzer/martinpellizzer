Dioscorea preussii

Dioscorea preussii, commonly known as African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and nutritional purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and develops capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various local cuisines and herbal remedies.

The botanical features of Dioscorea preussii make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
