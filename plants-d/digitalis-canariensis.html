Digitalis canariensis

Digitalis canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and studied for its cardiac glycoside content.
