Dendrochilum montanum

Dendrochilum montanum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, green, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring. Dendrochilum montanum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.
