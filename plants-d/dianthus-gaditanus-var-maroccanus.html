Dianthus gaditanus var. maroccanus

Dianthus gaditanus var. maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of pink to red, double-petaled blooms that are highly ornamental. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
