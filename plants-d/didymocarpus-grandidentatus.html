Didymocarpus grandidentatus

Didymocarpus grandidentatus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with long, pointed leaflets and produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
