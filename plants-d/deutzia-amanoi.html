Deutzia amanoi

Deutzia amanoi, commonly known as the Korean deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea and parts of eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, arching branches covered in small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by small, red berries. Its foliage turns vibrant yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Deutzia amanoi is valued for its delicate appearance and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
