Dendrobium inflatum

Dendrobium inflatum, commonly known as the inflated orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

Dendrobium inflatum is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
