Diospyros nicaraguensis

Diospyros nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaragua persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange to red berry that contains several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
