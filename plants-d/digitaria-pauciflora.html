Digitaria pauciflora

Digitaria pauciflora, commonly known as slender digit grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. Its flowers are usually wind-pollinated, and the plant is adapted to environments with seasonal drought.

Digitaria pauciflora is sometimes used in traditional medicine and can be a pioneer species in ecological succession.
