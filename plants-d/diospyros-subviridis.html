Diospyros subviridis

Diospyros subviridis, commonly known as the green-leafed persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to orange when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
