Discocactus crassispinus subsp. araguaiensis

Discocactus crassispinus subsp.

araguaiensis, commonly known as the Araguaiensis Discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny areoles.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract various pollinators. Its adaptations to harsh environments include water-storing tissues and a slow growth rate.

This subspecies is highly valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and resilience.
