Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. littoralis

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as seaside bluegrass, is a grass species adapted to coastal environments. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit.

This subspecies thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and dunes. It is drought-tolerant and can withstand salt spray and wind.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
