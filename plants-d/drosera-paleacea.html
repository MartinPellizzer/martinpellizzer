Drosera paleacea

Drosera paleacea, commonly known as the pale pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of elongated, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral element to its appearance. Its habitat typically includes wet, acidic soils in swampy or boggy areas.

The pale pitcher plant is known for its unique morphology and ecological role in nutrient-poor environments.
