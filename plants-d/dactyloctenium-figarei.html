Dactyloctenium figarei

Dactyloctenium figarei, commonly known as the African grass or figarei grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Dactyloctenium figarei is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and agricultural research.
