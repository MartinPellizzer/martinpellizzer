Dombeya greveana var. metameropsis

Dombeya greveana var. metameropsis, commonly known as the velvetleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to pink, often with a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
