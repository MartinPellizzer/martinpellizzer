Dipcadi occidentale

Dipcadi occidentale, commonly known as the western bulbous orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, bulb-like structures that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinct lip structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
