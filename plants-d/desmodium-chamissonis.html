Desmodium chamissonis

Desmodium chamissonis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences called heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Desmodium chamissonis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
