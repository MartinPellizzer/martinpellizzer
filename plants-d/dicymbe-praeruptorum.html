Dicymbe praeruptorum

Dicymbe praeruptorum, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange inflorescences, which resemble flames and are highly ornamental. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Dicymbe praeruptorum is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is also valued in traditional medicine.
