Dianthus illyricus subsp. angustifolius

Dianthus illyricus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features erect, branching stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and narrow.

The flowers are showy, displaying pink or red petals with a distinctive white or pink central zone. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of five-petaled flowers.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
