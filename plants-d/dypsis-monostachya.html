Dypsis monostachya

Dypsis monostachya, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by distinctive, elongated fruit clusters.

Dypsis monostachya is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and moderate watering requirements.
