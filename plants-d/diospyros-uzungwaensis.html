Diospyros uzungwaensis

Diospyros uzungwaensis, commonly known as the Uzungwa ebony, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Uzungwa Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony trees, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that are typically red when mature.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood and is also of ecological importance in its native habitat.
