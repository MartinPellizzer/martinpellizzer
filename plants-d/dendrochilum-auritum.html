Dendrochilum auritum

Dendrochilum auritum, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its branching stems. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red or brown markings. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dendrochilum auritum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
