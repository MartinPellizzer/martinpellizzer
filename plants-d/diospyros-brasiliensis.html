Diospyros brasiliensis

Diospyros brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits, which are edible when fully ripe.

Diospyros brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
