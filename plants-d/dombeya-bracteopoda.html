Dombeya bracteopoda

Dombeya bracteopoda, commonly known as the bracteate dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy, and it produces distinctive bracts beneath its inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Dombeya bracteopoda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
