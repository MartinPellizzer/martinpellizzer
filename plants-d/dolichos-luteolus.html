Dolichos luteolus

Dolichos luteolus, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in various cuisines. Dolichos luteolus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its botanical features make it both a nutritional and ecological asset in its native habitats.
