Datura longifolia

Datura longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like nightshade. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, lobed leaves, and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Datura longifolia contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
