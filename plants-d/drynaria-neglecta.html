Drynaria neglecta

Drynaria neglecta, commonly known as the Indian fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, compact growth habit. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Drynaria neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
