Dactylorhiza elata subvar. stephensonii

Dactylorhiza elata subvar. stephensonii, commonly known as the Stephenson's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as bogs, marshes, and damp woodlands. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with each pseudobulb producing a single leaf and a flower spike. Its flowers are usually pale pink to purple, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
