Danthoniopsis

Danthoniopsis, commonly known as the narrowleaf muirgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves. The plants typically grow in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Danthoniopsis species are often found in open vegetation types, such as fynbos, and are adapted to Mediterranean climates.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
