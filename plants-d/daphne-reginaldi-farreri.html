Daphne reginaldi-farreri

Daphne reginaldi-farreri, commonly known as the Reginald's Daphne, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink and emit a strong, spicy scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thick, fleshy leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that mature to a red color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its rarity and specific cultivation needs, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
