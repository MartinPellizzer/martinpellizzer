Dalbergia acutifoliolata

Dalbergia acutifoliolata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with small, pointed leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and wood quality, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
