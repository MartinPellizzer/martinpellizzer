Delphinium popovii

Delphinium popovii, commonly known as Popov's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typical of the Delphinium genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
