Draba sobolifera var. uncinalis

Draba sobolifera var. uncinalis, commonly known as the hooked rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a small perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lobed or divided, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive hooked or twisted stems, which aid in its identification.
