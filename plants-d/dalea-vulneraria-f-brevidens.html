Dalea vulneraria f. brevidens

Dalea vulneraria f. brevidens, commonly known as the short-stemmed scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, globular inflorescence composed of bright red or pink flowers. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
