Dypsis singularis

Dypsis singularis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a short, thick trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer that helps store water. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
