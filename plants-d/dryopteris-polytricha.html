Dryopteris polytricha

Dryopteris polytricha, commonly known as the slender wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or upright, depending on the growth conditions. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila, a typical feature of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The plant's rhizome is creeping and produces both roots and new shoots, facilitating its spread and survival in suitable habitats.
