Deppea miahuatlantica

Deppea miahuatlantica, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation efforts.
