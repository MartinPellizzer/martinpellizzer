Didesmus myagroides

Didesmus myagroides, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-white, fuzzy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
