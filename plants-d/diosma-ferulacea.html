Diosma ferulacea

Diosma ferulacea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Diosma ferulacea is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
