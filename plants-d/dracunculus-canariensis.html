Dracunculus canariensis

Dracunculus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary giant hogweed, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its large, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous white flowers. The plant can grow up to 3 meters tall, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its roots are thick and tuberous, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Due to its size and appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
