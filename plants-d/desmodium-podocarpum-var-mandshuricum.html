Desmodium podocarpum var. mandshuricum

Desmodium podocarpum var. mandshuricum, commonly known as the Korean velvet bean, is a perennial legume native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in traditional medicine.
