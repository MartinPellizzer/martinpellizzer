Dendrobium horstii

Dendrobium horstii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Dendrobium horstii thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
