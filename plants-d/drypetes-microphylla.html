Drypetes microphylla

Drypetes microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved drypetes, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
