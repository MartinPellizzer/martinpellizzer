Drymocallis rubricaulis

Drymocallis rubricaulis, commonly known as the redstem goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems that are reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, forming panicles that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
