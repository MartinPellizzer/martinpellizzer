Dracontium scandens

Dracontium scandens, commonly known as the creeping arrowroot, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden beneath a spathe. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Dracontium scandens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
