Dianthus Hispanicus Var. Tarraconensis

Hispanic dianthus, scientifically known as Dianthus hispanicus var. tarraconensis, is a striking member of the carnation family native to southern Europe. This flowering plant is celebrated for its vibrant, ruffled petals that range from pink to red, creating a visually captivating display. It thrives in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. With its historical significance in Mediterranean gardens, it remains a cherished ornamental plant. Its unique beauty and resilience make it a favorite among horticultural enthusiasts and botanists alike.
