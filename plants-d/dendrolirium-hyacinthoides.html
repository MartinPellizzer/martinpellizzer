Dendrolirium hyacinthoides

Dendrolirium hyacinthoides, commonly known as the hyacinth orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, hyacinth-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are linear and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base of the plant.
