Diospyros odorata var. rhodesiana

Diospyros odorata var. rhodesiana, commonly known as the Rhodesian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
