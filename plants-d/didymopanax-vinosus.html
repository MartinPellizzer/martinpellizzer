Didymopanax vinosus

Didymopanax vinosus, commonly known as the purple-leafed sycamore, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn a striking purple color during the dry season. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique foliage and ecological importance, it is valued in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
