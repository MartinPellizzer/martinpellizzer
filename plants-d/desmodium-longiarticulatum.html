Desmodium longiarticulatum

Desmodium longiarticulatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed tickclover, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Its growth habit and morphology make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
