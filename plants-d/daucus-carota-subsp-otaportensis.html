Daucus carota subsp. otaportensis

Daucus carota subsp.

otaportensis, commonly known as the Portuguese carrot, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical root that is typically white or yellow in color.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, carroty scent.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its edible roots and ornamental value in gardens.
