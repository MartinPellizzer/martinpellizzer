Desmodium wigginsii

Desmodium wigginsii, commonly known as the Wiggins' sensitive plant, is a species of legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimulation. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Desmodium wigginsii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
