Dianthus versicolor var. subulifolius

Dianthus versicolor var. subulifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red flowers with white markings. The leaves are narrow and pointed, giving the plant its specific epithet "subulifolius.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
