Deyeuxia lacustris

Deyeuxia lacustris, commonly known as water millet, is a grass species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along lake shores. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface.

Deyeuxia lacustris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
