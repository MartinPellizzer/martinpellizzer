Dypsis corniculata

Dypsis corniculata, commonly known as the crown palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, arching leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that eventually develop into orange-red fruits. Its leaves are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their aesthetic appeal.

Dypsis corniculata prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
