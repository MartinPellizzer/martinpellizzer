Dipteracanthus humilis

Dipteracanthus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf dipteracanthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along riverbanks. The plant features delicate, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Dipteracanthus humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
