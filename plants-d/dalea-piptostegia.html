Dalea piptostegia

Dalea piptostegia, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes, and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

Dalea piptostegia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
