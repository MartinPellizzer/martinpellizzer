Deparia pseudoconilii var. pseudoconilii

Deparia pseudoconilii var. pseudoconilii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood fern, is a fern species native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its slender, lanceolate fronds that are typically 30–60 cm in length. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem with densely packed, dimorphic fronds—sterile and fertile—arranged in a compact rosette. Its spore-producing structures, or sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.
