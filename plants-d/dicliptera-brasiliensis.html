Dicliptera brasiliensis

Dicliptera brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian panic grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Dicliptera brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
