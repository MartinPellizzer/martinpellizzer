Drymospartum etnense

Drymospartum etnense, commonly known as the Etna rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and early summer months. Its leaves are narrow, succulent, and adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its habitat.

Drymospartum etnense plays a role in stabilizing the rocky soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
