Diospyros sphenophylla

Diospyros sphenophylla, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
