Diospyros acapulcensis subsp. rivensis

Diospyros acapulcensis subsp.

rivensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species native to Central America, particularly Mexico. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature.

The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
