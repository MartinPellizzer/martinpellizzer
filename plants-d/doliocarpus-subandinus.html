Doliocarpus subandinus

Doliocarpus subandinus, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a species native to the cloud forests of western South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, waxy foliage, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its region.
