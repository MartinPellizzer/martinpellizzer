Diplophyllum cardiocarpum

Diplophyllum cardiocarpum, commonly known as the heartfruit, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive heart-shaped fruits. The fruits are fleshy and contain several seeds enclosed in a fibrous pulp.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
