Dactylostemon klotzschii var. petiolaris

Dactylostemon klotzschii var. petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fig, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy choice for dry landscapes.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
