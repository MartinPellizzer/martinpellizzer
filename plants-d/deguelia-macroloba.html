Deguelia macroloba

Deguelia macroloba, commonly known as the giant senna, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing properties. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Deguelia macroloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
