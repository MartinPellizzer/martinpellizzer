Downingia elegans

Downingia elegans, commonly known as the Sierra snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate and have a soft, fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Downingia elegans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable moisture conditions.
