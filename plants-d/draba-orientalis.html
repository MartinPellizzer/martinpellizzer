Draba orientalis

Draba orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Rockcress, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Asia.

It is a small perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces delicate white or pale yellow flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

Draba orientalis is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants.
