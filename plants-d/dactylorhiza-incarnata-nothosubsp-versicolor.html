Dactylorhiza incarnata nothosubsp. versicolor

Dactylorhiza incarnata nothosubsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable helleborine, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow to purple flowers with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and is adapted to humid, cool climates. It is a perennial herb that forms clumps and has a rhizomatous root system.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variation, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
