Delphinium urceolatum

Delphinium urceolatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. Delphinium urceolatum prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
