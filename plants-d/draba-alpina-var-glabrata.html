Draba alpina var. glabrata

Draba alpina var. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, often with a glabrous (hairless) surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in a raceme, blooming in early spring.

It is commonly found in alpine and subalpine regions, growing in rocky or gravelly soils.
