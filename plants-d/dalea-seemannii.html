Dalea seemannii

Dalea seemannii, commonly known as the Texas milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
