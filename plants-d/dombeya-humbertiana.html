Dombeya humbertiana

Dombeya humbertiana, commonly known as the Humbert dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a valuable ornamental plant in gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
