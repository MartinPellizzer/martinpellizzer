Dioscorea hofika

Dioscorea hofika, commonly known as Hofika yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of aerial roots. The plant produces edible tubers that are an important food source in certain regions. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are fibrous and vine-like.

The tubers are often harvested after a growing season of several months, depending on the local climate.
