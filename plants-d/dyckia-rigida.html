Dyckia rigida

Dyckia rigida, commonly known as the rigid dyckia, is a perennial bromeliad native to southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its stiff, grass-like leaves that form a rosette, often with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces striking inflorescences of red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage and floral display make it a visually appealing and low-maintenance plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
