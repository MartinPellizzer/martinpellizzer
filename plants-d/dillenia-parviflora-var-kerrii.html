Dillenia parviflora var. kerrii

Dillenia parviflora var. kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's dillenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Dillenia parviflora var. kerrii thrives in humid, tropical climates and is commonly found in lowland forests.
