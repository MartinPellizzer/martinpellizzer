Dioscorea eburina

Dioscorea eburina, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for food. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source by indigenous communities.
