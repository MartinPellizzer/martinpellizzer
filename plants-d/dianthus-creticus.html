Dianthus creticus

Dianthus creticus, commonly known as the Cretan pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Dianthus creticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
