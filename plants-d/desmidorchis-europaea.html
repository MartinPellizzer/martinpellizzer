Desmidorchis europaea

Desmidorchis europaea, commonly known as the European desmid orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, small, and often delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous growth habit and showy, spurred orchid flowers that are often pink or white in color.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
