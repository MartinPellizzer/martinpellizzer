Demorchis nantoensis

Demorchis nantoensis, commonly known as the Nantoensis orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are broad and glossy, adapted to capture limited sunlight, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the moist soil.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Demorchis nantoensis is considered a conservation concern.
