Dolichos angulatus

Dolichos angulatus, commonly known as the angular cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seeds are often used as a food source, similar to other legumes.

Dolichos angulatus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
