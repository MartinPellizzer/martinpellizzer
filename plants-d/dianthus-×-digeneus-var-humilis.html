Dianthus × digeneus var. humilis

Dianthus × digeneus var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, bushy foliage and produces clusters of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The flowers typically bloom in spring and summer, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in rock gardens or as a bedding plant.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal climates due to its tolerance of salt spray.
