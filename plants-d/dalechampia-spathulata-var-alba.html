Dalechampia spathulata var. alba

Dalechampia spathulata var. alba, commonly known as white spathulate dalechampia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in spathaceous inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
