Doronicum mexicanum

Doronicum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican leopard's foot, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, gray-green leaves and produces showy yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive purple center. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
