Dactylorhiza × pseudojunialis

Dactylorhiza × pseudojunialis, commonly known as the broad-leaf marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces spikes of pale pink to purple flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous habitats such as wet meadows and marshes.

Dactylorhiza × pseudojunialis is a perennial herb that is often found in association with other orchid species in its native range.
