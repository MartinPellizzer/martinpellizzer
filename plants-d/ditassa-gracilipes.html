Ditassa gracilipes

Ditassa gracilipes, commonly known as the slender leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that grow in a compact, upright arrangement. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it well-suited for tropical and subtropical gardens. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor the plant in the soil while absorbing nutrients.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value and potential health benefits.
