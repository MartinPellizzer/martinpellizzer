Deschampsia penicillata

Deschampsia penicillata, commonly known as slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as meadows, prairies, and rocky slopes. The plant features fine, hair-like leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that give it its common name. It is well adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
