Dendrobium zaranense

Dendrobium zaranense, commonly known as the Zaranese orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that supports its upright, branching structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium zaranense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
