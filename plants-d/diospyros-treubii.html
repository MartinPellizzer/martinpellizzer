Diospyros treubii

Diospyros treubii, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony trees, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Diospyros treubii is valued for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
