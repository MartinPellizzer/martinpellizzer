Dolichos oleraceus

Dolichos oleraceus, commonly known as the horse gram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Dolichos oleraceus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in crop rotation.
