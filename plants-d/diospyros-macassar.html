Diospyros macassar

Diospyros macassar, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, opposite leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, while the wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
