Deyeuxia chilensis

Deyeuxia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically glabrous. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the flowering stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Deyeuxia chilensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
