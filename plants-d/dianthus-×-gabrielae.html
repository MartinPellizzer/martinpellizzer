Dianthus × gabrielae

Dianthus × gabrielae, commonly known as the Gabriel's carnation, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, semi-double flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white or pale edge. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and features linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus × gabrielae is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
