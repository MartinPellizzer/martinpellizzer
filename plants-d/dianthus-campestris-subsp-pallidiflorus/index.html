Dianthus Campestris Subsp. Pallidiflorus

Dianthus campestris subsp. pallidiflorus is a lesser-known member of the carnation family known for its delicate pink and white blooms. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, this plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its flowers often appear in clusters, adding a subtle beauty to gardens and natural landscapes. The subspecies is distinguished by its pale flower color and compact growth habit. Despite its modest appearance, it holds significance in botanical studies and horticultural interest.
