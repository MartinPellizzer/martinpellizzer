Disa gladioliflora subsp. gladioliflora

Disa gladioliflora subsp.

gladioliflora, commonly known as the gladioliflora disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it produces a single, tall stem with a cluster of showy blooms.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
