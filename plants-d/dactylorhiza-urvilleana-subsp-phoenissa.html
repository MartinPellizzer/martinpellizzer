Dactylorhiza urvilleana subsp. phoenissa

Dactylorhiza urvilleana subsp.

phoenissa, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and fens.

The plant features a single, upright stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale purple to pink, arranged in a raceme, and are characterized by their distinctive, recurved petals.

It is a rare and protected species, often requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth.
