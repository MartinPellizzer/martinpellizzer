Delphinium elbursense

Delphinium elbursense, commonly known as the Elburs mountain larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Delphinium elbursense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
