Desmodium podocarpum f. leucanthum

Desmodium podocarpum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered desmodium, is a variant of the Desmodium genus characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers.

It is a leguminous plant that belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits are typical of the genus, while its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens.

The white-flowered form is notable for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
