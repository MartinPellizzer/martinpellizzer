Dolichos lualabensis

Dolichos lualabensis, commonly known as the Lualab bean, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

Dolichos lualabensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for both food and decorative purposes.
