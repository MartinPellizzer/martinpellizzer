Dodecatheon obovatum

Dodecatheon obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf glory of the snow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the number twelve, referencing the twelve petals in its flower structure.
