Dion

Dion, commonly known as the dionaea muscipula, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is renowned for its unique ability to capture and digest insects using its snap-trap leaves. The plant's leaves are modified into specialized structures called "flypapers," which are covered in sensitive hairs that trigger the closing mechanism. Dionaea muscipula thrives in nutrient-poor soils and requires consistent moisture and sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to nutrient-deficient environments through predation.
