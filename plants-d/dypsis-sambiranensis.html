Dypsis sambiranensis

Dypsis sambiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Dypsis sambiranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
