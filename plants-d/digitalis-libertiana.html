Digitalis libertiana

Digitalis libertiana, commonly known as the Liberian foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of West Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and produces showy blooms during the wet season. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
