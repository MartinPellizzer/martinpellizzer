Decorima trinervis

Decorima trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with three primary veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a tendency to flake in older trees.

Decorima trinervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
