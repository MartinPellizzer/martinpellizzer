Didymocarpus longicalyx

Didymocarpus longicalyx, commonly known as the long-leaf tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
