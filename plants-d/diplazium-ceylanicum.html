Diplazium ceylanicum

Diplazium ceylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are divided into segments, with the upper portion being more delicate and the lower part more robust.
