Drymophloeus rumphii

Drymophloeus rumphii, commonly known as the Java ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green surface.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in clusters, followed by woody, elongated fruits.
