Diphyllocalyx cayensis subsp. cayensis

Diphyllocalyx cayensis subsp.

cayensis, commonly known as the Cayenne trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
