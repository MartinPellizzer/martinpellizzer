Delphinium dolichostachyum

Delphinium dolichostachyum, commonly known as the long-stalked larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are tubular with five sepals that form a spurred shape, typical of the Delphinium genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to alpine gardens.
