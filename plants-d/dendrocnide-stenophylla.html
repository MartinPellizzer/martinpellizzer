Dendrocnide stenophylla

Dendrocnide stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stinging tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many stinging plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 2 meters in height. Its stems and leaves contain toxic chemicals that cause a severe skin irritation known as contact dermatitis.
