Dracocephalum butkovii

Dracocephalum butkovii, commonly known as the Persian shield, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a striking metallic blue or silver sheen, often adorned with purple or red veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated for both horticultural and traditional uses.
