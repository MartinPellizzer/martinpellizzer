Digitaria incisa

Digitaria incisa, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

Digitaria incisa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
