Dasypyrum hordeaceum var. velutinum

Dasypyrum hordeaceum var. velutinum, commonly known as the hairy barley, is a variant of the wild barley species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy covering on the stems and leaves, which provides a distinct appearance. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its botanical features include slender, cylindrical spikelets and small, three-lobed glumes.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for genetic diversity and resilience.
