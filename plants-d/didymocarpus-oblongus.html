Didymocarpus oblongus

Didymocarpus oblongus, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules, enhancing soil fertility. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit, which is oblong and woody, contains several seeds and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
