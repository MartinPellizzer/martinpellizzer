Delphinium sylvaticum

Delphinium sylvaticum, commonly known as the woodland larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and reaches heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features tall, sparsely branched stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its showy blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Delphinium sylvaticum is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
