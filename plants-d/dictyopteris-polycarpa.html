Dictyopteris polycarpa

Dictyopteris polycarpa, commonly known as the broadleaf sea palm, is a species of brown algae belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its large, flat, blade-like fronds that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a distinctive branching structure with a central stipe and multiple lateral branches. It thrives in shallow, coastal waters and is often found in the intertidal zone.

Its reproductive structures, including both male and female gametes, are embedded in the fronds, facilitating sexual reproduction in its marine environment.
