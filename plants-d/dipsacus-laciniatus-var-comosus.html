Dipsacus laciniatus var. comosus

Dipsacus laciniatus var. comosus, commonly known as the cut-leaved teasel, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, serrated leaves that are deeply lobed and often hairy. The plant produces tall, spiky flower heads that are composed of numerous small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in a dense, composite-like structure. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically in textile production due to its bracts.
