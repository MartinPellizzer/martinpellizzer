Diholcos bisulcatus

Diholcos bisulcatus, commonly known as the two-ribbed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant's leaves are typically slender and branched, with the bladders forming at the tips of the leaf-like structures. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats and is adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous nature make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
