Daphne tangutica var. tangutica

Daphne tangutica var. tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and its stems are often covered in a thick, waxy coating. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
