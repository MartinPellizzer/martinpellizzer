Descurainia artemisioides

Descurainia artemisioides, commonly known as hoary tickseed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous species. The plant is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that resemble those of a dandelion. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its seeds are small and capsule-like, dispersing easily by wind.
