Diasperus discolor

Diasperus discolor, commonly known as the variable daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow flowers with a central disc that is often a different color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Diasperus discolor is known for its ability to produce numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
