Delphinium hybridum subsp. ochroleucum

Delphinium hybridum subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as the white larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of showy, spurred flowers in shades of white to pale blue.

The flowers are typically arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, with each bloom having a distinctive, elongated spur. Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
