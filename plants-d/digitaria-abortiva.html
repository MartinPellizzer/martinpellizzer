Digitaria abortiva

Digitaria abortiva, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open areas, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a silvery appearance on the underside, contributing to its common name. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Digitaria abortiva is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
