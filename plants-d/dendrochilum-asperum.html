Dendrochilum asperum

Dendrochilum asperum, commonly known as the rough-lipped tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, rough-textured lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dendrochilum asperum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
