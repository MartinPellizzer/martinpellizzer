Dicerandra immaculata var. savannarum

Dicerandra immaculata var. savannarum, commonly known as the Florida dicerandra, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats such as scrublands and pine flatwoods. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, including use in traditional herbal remedies.
