Dalea carthagenensis var. brevis

Dalea carthagenensis var. brevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed carthagenensis milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically covered with fine, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
