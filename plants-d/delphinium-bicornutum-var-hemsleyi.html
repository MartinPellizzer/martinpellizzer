Delphinium bicornutum var. hemsleyi

Delphinium bicornutum var. hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley's larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its flowers are characterized by two prominent, spurred lobes at the base, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects.

Delphinium bicornutum var. hemsleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
