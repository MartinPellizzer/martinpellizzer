Dauphinea brevilabra

Dauphinea brevilabra, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, colorful blooms.
