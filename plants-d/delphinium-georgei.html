Delphinium georgei

Delphinium georgei, commonly known as George's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem, with a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium georgei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
