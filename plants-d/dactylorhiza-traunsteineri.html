Dactylorhiza traunsteineri

Dactylorhiza traunsteineri, commonly known as the Traunsteiner's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typically fragrant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Dactylorhiza traunsteineri is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate and ephemeral blooming period.
