Daucus carnosus

Daucus carnosus, commonly known as the wild carrot or Queen Anne's lace, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and an umbel of small white flowers with a single purple flower at the center. Its taproot is often edible and has a mild, carrot-like flavor.

Daucus carnosus is also known for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
