Dalea lutea var. lutea

Dalea lutea var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
