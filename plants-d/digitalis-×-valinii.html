Digitalis × valinii

Digitalis × valinii, commonly known as the hybrid foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of tubular, hooded flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes several species known for their medicinal properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Digitalis × valinii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal medicine.
