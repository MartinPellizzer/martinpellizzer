Dicentra burmanica

Dicentra burmanica, commonly known as Burman's bleeding heart, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a deep green color and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
