Doria abrotanifolia

Doria abrotanifolia, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant properties. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
