Danthonia caespitosa var. gracilis

Danthonia caespitosa var. gracilis, commonly known as slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. This variant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is often tinged with red or purple hues.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
