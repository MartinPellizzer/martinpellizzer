Dendrobium leucolophotum

Dendrobium leucolophotum, commonly known as the white-lip dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent lip structures. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in two rows along the stems.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
