Dodecatheon pulchellum var. zionense

Dodecatheon pulchellum var. zionense, commonly known as the Zion primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in Utah and neighboring regions.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with high elevations. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and somewhat succulent.

The Zion primrose is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
