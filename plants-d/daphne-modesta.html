Daphne modesta

Daphne modesta, commonly known as the modest daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Daphne modesta is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
