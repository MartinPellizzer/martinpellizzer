Darwiniothamnus tenuifolius var. glabriusculus

Darwiniothamnus tenuifolius var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the slender-leaved darwiniothamnus, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width, with a grayish-green hue and a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in dense clusters during the warmer months, contributing to its role in local pollination networks. It is well adapted to harsh, dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to other varieties within the species.
