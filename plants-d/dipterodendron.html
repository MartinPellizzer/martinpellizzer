Dipterodendron

Dipterodendron, commonly known as the "false holly," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a leathery texture, while the fruits are capsule-like and contain numerous seeds.

Dipterodendron species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
