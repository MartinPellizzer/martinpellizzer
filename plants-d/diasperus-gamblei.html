Diasperus gamblei

Diasperus gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier's grassveld, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Diasperus gamblei is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local fauna.
