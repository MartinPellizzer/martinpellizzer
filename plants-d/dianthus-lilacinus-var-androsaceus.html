Dianthus lilacinus var. androsaceus

Dianthus lilacinus var. androsaceus, commonly known as the Androsace dianthus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and lilac, often with a distinctive reddish hue at the base. The plant has a mat-forming habit, with fleshy, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
