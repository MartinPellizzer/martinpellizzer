Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. austriaca

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

The plant features a rhizome with a few leaves and produces striking purple or violet flowers with a distinctive yellow-green lip. It is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique morphological features.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
