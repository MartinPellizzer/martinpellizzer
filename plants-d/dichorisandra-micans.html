Dichorisandra micans

Dichorisandra micans, commonly known as the silverleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
