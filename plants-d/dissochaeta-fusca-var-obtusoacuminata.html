Dissochaeta fusca var. obtusoacuminata

Dissochaeta fusca var. obtusoacuminata, commonly known as the blunt-pointed dissochaeta, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically tubular and come in shades of red or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.
