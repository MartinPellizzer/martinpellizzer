Drymaria gentryi

Drymaria gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to arid environments. It produces inconspicuous, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Drymaria gentryi is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and exhibiting drought tolerance.
