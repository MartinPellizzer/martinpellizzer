Dontospermum odorum

Dontospermum odorum, commonly known as the fragrant sea bean, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in mangrove ecosystems. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible seeds that are dispersed by water.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
