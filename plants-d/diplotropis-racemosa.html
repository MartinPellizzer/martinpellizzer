Diplotropis racemosa

Diplotropis racemosa, commonly known as the racemose diplotropis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, preferring moist, sandy soils. Its botanical features include a slender, branching stem and trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets.
