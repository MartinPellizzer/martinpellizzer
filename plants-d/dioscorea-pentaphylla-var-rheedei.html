Dioscorea pentaphylla var. rheedei

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. rheedei, commonly known as the red sandalwood yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
