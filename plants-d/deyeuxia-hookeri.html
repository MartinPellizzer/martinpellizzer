Deyeuxia hookeri

Deyeuxia hookeri, commonly known as the hooker's bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Deyeuxia hookeri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
