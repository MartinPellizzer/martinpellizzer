Daiswa fargesii var. brevipetalata

Daiswa fargesii var. brevipetalata, commonly known as the narrow-petaled daisies, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of western China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with narrow petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and covered with a fine layer of hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
