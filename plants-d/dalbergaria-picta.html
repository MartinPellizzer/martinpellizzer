Dalbergaria picta

Dalbergaria picta, commonly known as the painted dalbergia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are compound, typically with three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Dalbergaria picta is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
