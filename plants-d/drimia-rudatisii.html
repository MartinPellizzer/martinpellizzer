Drimia rudatisii

Drimia rudatisii, commonly known as the Natal rain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, fleshy, violet-tipped spatula-shaped structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Drimia rudatisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
