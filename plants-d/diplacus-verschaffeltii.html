Diplacus verschaffeltii

Diplacus verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to California.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Diplacus verschaffeltii plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies in its native ecosystem.
