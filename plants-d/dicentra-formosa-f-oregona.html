Dicentra formosa f. oregona

Dicentra formosa f. oregona, commonly known as the Oregon poppy, is a hybrid form of the Pacific poppy.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States, particularly Oregon and Washington. The plant features delicate, fern-like foliage and produces striking, bright red or orange flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and native plant landscapes.
