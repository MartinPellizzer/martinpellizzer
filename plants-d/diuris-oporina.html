Diuris oporina

Diuris oporina, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is an exquisite member of the orchid family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling fairy wings. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette that helps conserve moisture.

Diuris oporina is a short-lived perennial, with its flowering period usually occurring in spring.
