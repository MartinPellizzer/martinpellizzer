Dipteracanthus brachysiphon

Dipteracanthus brachysiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipteracaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
