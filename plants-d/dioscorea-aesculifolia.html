Dioscorea aesculifolia

Dioscorea aesculifolia, commonly known as the horsebean yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem with prominent nodes.

Dioscorea aesculifolia is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
