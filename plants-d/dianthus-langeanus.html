Dianthus langeanus

Dianthus langeanus, commonly known as the Langean carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and have five petals that are often adorned with distinctive markings.

Dianthus langeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
