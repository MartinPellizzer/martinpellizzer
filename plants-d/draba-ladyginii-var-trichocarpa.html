Draba ladyginii var. trichocarpa

Draba ladyginii var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the trichocarpous lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
