Dombeya viburnifloropsis subsp. borealis

Dombeya viburnifloropsis subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern dombeya, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It features showy, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and visually striking plant in alpine and tundra environments.
