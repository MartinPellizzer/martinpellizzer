Digitaria pulchra

Digitaria pulchra, commonly known as the pretty grass or silverleaf grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically arranged in loose panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Digitaria pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
