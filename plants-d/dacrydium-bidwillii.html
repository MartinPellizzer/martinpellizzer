Dacrydium bidwillii

Dacrydium bidwillii, commonly known as the New Zealand rimu, is a coniferous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, spherical cones that contain the plant's seeds.

Dacrydium bidwillii is valued for its durable wood and plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's forests.
