Digitaria eriostachya

Digitaria eriostachya, commonly known as sand bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, blue-gray flowers. It is drought-tolerant and plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
