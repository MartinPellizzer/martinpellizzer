Diuris platichila

Diuris platichila, commonly known as the purple-tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and its unique, spurred orchid flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Diuris platichila is a perennial herb that produces its flowers during the cooler months, usually from late winter to early spring.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and botanical collections.
