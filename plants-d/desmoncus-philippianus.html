Desmoncus philippianus

Desmoncus philippianus, commonly known as the Philippine star orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its floral structure includes a distinctive labellum, which plays a role in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in conservation programs to ensure its survival.
