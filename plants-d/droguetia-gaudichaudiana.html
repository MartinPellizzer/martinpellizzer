Droguetia gaudichaudiana

Droguetia gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five prominent petals and a crown-like structure. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature and turn red upon ripening. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Droguetia gaudichaudiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
