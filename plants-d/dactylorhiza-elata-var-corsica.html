Dactylorhiza elata var. corsica

Dactylorhiza elata var. corsica, commonly known as the Corsican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
