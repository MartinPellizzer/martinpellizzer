Diplazium thelypteroides f. serratum

Diplazium thelypteroides f. serratum, commonly known as the serrated false water fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds.

The plant features bipinnate leaves with serrated margins, giving it a unique appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

This fern is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
