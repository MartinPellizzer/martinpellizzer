Dodonaea eriocarpa f. decipiens

Dodonaea eriocarpa f. decipiens, commonly known as the silver dollar bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
