Diosma umbellata

Diosma umbellata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Diosma umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
