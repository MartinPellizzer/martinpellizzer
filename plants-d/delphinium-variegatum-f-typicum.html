Delphinium variegatum f. typicum

Delphinium variegatum f. typicum, commonly known as the variegated larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of spurred blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a distinct variegation pattern of green and white.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a striking addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.
