Draba hatcheriana

Draba hatcheriana, commonly known as Hatcher's whitlow grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, often at high elevations where conditions are harsh. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in a single umbel. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants, and is characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

Draba hatcheriana is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
