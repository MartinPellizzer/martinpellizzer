Dentaria laciniata f. albiflora

Dentaria laciniata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered toothplant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have four petals and are typically visited by bees and other pollinators.

The f. albiflora form is distinguished by its notably paler flowers compared to the typical variety.
