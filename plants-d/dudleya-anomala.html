Dudleya anomala

Dudleya anomala, commonly known as the desert stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dudleya anomala is also known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves, allowing it to survive in harsh, desert conditions.
