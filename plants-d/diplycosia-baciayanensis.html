Diplycosia baciayanensis

Diplycosia baciayanensis, commonly known as the Baciayan daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is of interest for its potential ecological role and ornamental value in horticulture.
