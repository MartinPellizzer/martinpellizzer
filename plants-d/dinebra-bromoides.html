Dinebra bromoides

Dinebra bromoides, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, green fruits with a tart flavor. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rosaceae and is valued for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
