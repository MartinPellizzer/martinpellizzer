Dianthus insularis

Dianthus insularis, commonly known as the Island carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
