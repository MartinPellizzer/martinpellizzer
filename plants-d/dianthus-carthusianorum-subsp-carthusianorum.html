Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. carthusianorum

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

carthusianorum, commonly known as the Carthusian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white or pale pink center.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the top of the stems, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
