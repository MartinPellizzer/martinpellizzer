Datura timoriensis

Datura timoriensis, commonly known as the Timor jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Datura timoriensis is known for its toxic properties, containing tropane alkaloids such as scopolamine and hyoscyamine, which can be harmful to humans and animals.
