Dalea cinnamomea

Dalea cinnamomea, commonly known as cinnamon prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea cinnamomea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
