Doronicum piloselloides

Doronicum piloselloides, commonly known as the hairy leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The flowers are yellow with dark purple centers, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.
