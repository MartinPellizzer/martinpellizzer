Dimorphanthus mandshuricus var. variegatus

Dimorphanthus mandshuricus var. variegatus, commonly known as the Variegated Mandshurian Dimorphanthus, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a combination of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its limited distribution and unique foliage, it is a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts and botanists.
