Dodonaea angustifolia

Dodonaea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hopper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping.
