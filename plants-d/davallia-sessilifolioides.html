Davallia sessilifolioides

Davallia sessilifolioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maidenhair fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are often described as resembling hair, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and thick, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
