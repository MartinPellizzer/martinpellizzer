Daphniphyllum paxianum

Daphniphyllum paxianum, commonly known as the Paxian daphniphyllum, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Daphniphyllaceae and is characterized by its glossy, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in mountainous regions with acidic soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
