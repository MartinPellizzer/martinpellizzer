Danthonia australis

Danthonia australis, commonly known as the Australian hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in areas with high salinity. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Danthonia australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
