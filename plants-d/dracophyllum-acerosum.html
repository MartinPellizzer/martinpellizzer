Dracophyllum acerosum

Dracophyllum acerosum, commonly known as the hard-leaved dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its often arid habitat. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

Dracophyllum acerosum is well adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
