Delphinium aemulans var. altaicum

Delphinium aemulans var. altaicum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a coarse texture, often with a bluish-green hue.

The species is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
