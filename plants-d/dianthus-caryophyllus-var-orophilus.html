Dianthus caryophyllus var. orophilus

Dianthus caryophyllus var. orophilus, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces pink to red flowers with a distinctive frilly appearance, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense mats, providing both color and structure in garden landscapes.
