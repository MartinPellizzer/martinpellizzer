Dendrobium bulleyi

Dendrobium bulleyi, commonly known as the Bulley's orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium bulleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
