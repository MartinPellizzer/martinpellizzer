Didymoplexis sirichaii

Didymoplexis sirichaii, commonly known as the Thai ghost orchid, is a rare and endangered epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Thailand.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, relying on its aerial roots for moisture and nutrients. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces flowers with a unique, intricate structure.

Due to habitat destruction and illegal collection, it faces significant conservation challenges.
