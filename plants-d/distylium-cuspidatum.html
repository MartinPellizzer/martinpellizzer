Distylium cuspidatum

Distylium cuspidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, woodland soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for early pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
