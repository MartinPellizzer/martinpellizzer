Dendranthema zawadzkii var. sichotense

Dendranthema zawadzkii var. sichotense, commonly known as the Sichotian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
