Delphinium scabriflorum

Delphinium scabriflorum, commonly known as the roughflower larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Delphinium scabriflorum typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

It is known for its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
