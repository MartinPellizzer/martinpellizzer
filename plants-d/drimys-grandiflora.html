Drimys grandiflora

Drimys grandiflora, commonly known as the Chilean whitewood, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions and mountainous areas.

Drimys grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
