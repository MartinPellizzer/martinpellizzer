Dianthus elymaiticus

Dianthus elymaiticus, commonly known as the Erythraean carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Dianthus elymaiticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
