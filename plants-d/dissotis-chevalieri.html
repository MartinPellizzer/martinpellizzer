Dissotis chevalieri

Dissotis chevalieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red velvetplant, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish hue on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and often have a pink or purple coloration.

Dissotis chevalieri is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
