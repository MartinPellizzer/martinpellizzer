Deutzia scabra f. albopunctata

Deutzia scabra f. albopunctata, commonly known as the white-punctured deutzia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early summer. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a pale green upper surface and a fuzzy underside. This variety is distinguished by its unique white spots or punctations on the leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
