Drimia bifolia

Drimia bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green. The plant blooms with a distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Drimia bifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
