Didymocarpus tahanicus

Didymocarpus tahanicus, commonly known as the Tahanicus tamarind, is a member of the Caesalpiniaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Despite its ornamental potential, it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
