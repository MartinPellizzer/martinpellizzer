Daphne cneorum subsp. juliae

Daphne cneorum subsp.

juliae, commonly known as the sweet spurge or winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe. It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter or early spring.

The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. Its stems are often covered in a waxy, whitish coating, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and winter interest.
