Dracula gastrophora

Dracula gastrophora, commonly known as the vampire orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark red to purple flowers that resemble the face of a vampire, giving it its ominous common name. The plant has a short, stout stem with a single, showy inflorescence that blooms during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid, shaded environment in which it grows.

Dracula gastrophora is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found growing on rocky substrates.
