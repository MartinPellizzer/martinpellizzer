Dalbergia acariaeantha

Dalbergia acariaeantha, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, highly aromatic wood, which is prized for its density and fine grain. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical climates. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
