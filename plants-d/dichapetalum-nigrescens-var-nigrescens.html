Dichapetalum nigrescens var. nigrescens

Dichapetalum nigrescens var. nigrescens, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark that becomes more pronounced with age. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
