Deutzia densiflora

Deutzia densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Deutzia densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its delicate floral display.
