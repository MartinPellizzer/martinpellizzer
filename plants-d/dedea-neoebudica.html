Dedea neoebudica

Dedea neoebudica, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the spring and early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from nutrient-poor soils.

Dedea neoebudica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
