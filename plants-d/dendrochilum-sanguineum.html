Dendrochilum sanguineum

Dendrochilum sanguineum, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and purple flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant has long, slender pseudobulbs and linear leaves that help it store water in its natural habitat. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dendrochilum sanguineum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
