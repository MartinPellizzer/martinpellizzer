Dianthus freynii

Dianthus freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dianthus freynii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
