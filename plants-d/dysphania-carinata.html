Dysphania carinata

Dysphania carinata, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are often used in cooking. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color.

Dysphania carinata is valued for its nutritional content and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
