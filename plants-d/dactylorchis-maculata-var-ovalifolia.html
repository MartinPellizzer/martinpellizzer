Dactylorchis maculata var. ovalifolia

Dactylorchis maculata var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive brown spots. Its leaves are oval-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its unique appearance.

The species is characterized by its delicate structure and is often found in association with specific soil types and microhabitats.
