Diospyros nenab

Diospyros nenab, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or orange when ripe.

The wood of Diospyros nenab is hard and durable, making it valuable for carpentry and tool-making.
