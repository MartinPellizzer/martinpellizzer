Draba ambigens

Draba ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Draba ambigens typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.
