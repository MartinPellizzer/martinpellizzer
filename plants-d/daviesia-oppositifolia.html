Daviesia oppositifolia

Daviesia oppositifolia, commonly known as the slender daviesia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite and pinnate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Daviesia oppositifolia is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
