Davallia bifida

Davallia bifida, commonly known as the scaly shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that are often covered in small, scaly structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
