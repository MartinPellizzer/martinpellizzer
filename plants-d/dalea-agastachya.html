Dalea agastachya

Dalea agastachya, commonly known as prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. It produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dalea agastachya is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use by indigenous peoples.
