Dysoxylum alliaceum var. lanceolatum

Dysoxylum alliaceum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dysoxylum, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a distinctive addition to tropical landscapes.
