Davallia clavata

Davallia clavata, commonly known as the silver sword fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients.

Davallia clavata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
