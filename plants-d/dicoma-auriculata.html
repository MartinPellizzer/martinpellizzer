Dicoma auriculata

Dicoma auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Dicoma auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
