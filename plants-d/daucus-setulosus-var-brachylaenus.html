Daucus setulosus var. brachylaenus

Daucus setulosus var. brachylaenus, commonly known as the hairy carrot or brachylaenus carrot, is a wild relative of the domesticated carrot.

It is a biennial plant native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, feathery appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in umbels, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Botanically, it is valued for its genetic diversity and potential use in breeding programs for improved carrot varieties.
