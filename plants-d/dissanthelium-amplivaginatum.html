Dissanthelium amplivaginatum

Dissanthelium amplivaginatum, commonly known as the broad-sheath grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive broad sheath at the base. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically found in open, disturbed habitats. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetland or swampy areas.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
