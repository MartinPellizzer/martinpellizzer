Dioscorea cordata var. cymulifera

Dioscorea cordata var. cymulifera, commonly known as the creeping yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces tubers that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its growth habit involves trailing stems that can spread along the ground, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded environments.

The botanical features of this variety include its distinctive leaf morphology and inflorescence structure, which contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
