Drypetes laciniata

Drypetes laciniata, commonly known as the leafy or spotted ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine practices.
