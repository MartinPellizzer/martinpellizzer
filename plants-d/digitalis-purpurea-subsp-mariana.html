Digitalis purpurea subsp. mariana

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

mariana, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia. It produces tall spikes of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
