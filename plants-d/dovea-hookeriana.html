Dovea hookeriana

Dovea hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's dovea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, humid environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, Dovea hookeriana is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
