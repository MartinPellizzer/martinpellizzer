Diclytra cucullaria

Diclytra cucullaria, commonly known as the hooded leaf beetle, is a small, herbivorous beetle belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

It is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found on plants in the legume family, such as Vicia and Lupinus. The beetle's name refers to the distinctive, hood-like structure on its pronotum, which provides camouflage and protection. Its life cycle includes egg, larval, pupal, and adult stages, with larvae feeding on plant foliage.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by contributing to plant decomposition and serving as a food source for other organisms.
