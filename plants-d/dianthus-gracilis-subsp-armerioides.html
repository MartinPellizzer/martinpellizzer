Dianthus gracilis subsp. armerioides

Dianthus gracilis subsp.

armerioides, commonly known as the slender carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features slender, branching stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces a delicate, sweet scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
