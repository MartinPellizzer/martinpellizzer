Diatelia globulariifolia var. major

Diatelia globulariifolia var. major, commonly known as the globular-leaf diatelia, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, rounded leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
