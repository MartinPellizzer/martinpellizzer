Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. dufftii

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

dufftii, commonly known as the Dufft's yellow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale orange, arranged in a loose spike, and are characterized by their slender, spurred petals.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
