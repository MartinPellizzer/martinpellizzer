Dianthus versicolor f. leucopetalus

Dianthus versicolor f. leucopetalus, commonly known as the white-petaled variant of the variable carnation, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically produces flowers with white petals and pink or red markings, showcasing a unique coloration pattern. It is native to regions of Europe and prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
