Dalbergia nigrescens

Dalbergia nigrescens, commonly known as black rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that ranges from black to deep brown in color. The plant has bipinnate leaves, and its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale pink. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Due to its high demand for musical instruments and furniture, it is also considered a threatened species.
