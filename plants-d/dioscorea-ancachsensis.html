Dioscorea ancachsensis

Dioscorea ancachsensis, commonly known as the Ancash yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are the primary edible part. The plant typically grows in cool, humid climates at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are small and usually white or pale green.

The tubers of Dioscorea ancachsensis are valued for their nutritional content and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.
