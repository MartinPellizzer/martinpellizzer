Diasperus albus

Diasperus albus, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Diasperus albus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
