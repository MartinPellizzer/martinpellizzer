Dodecatheon meadia var. jeffreyi

Dodecatheon meadia var. jeffreyi, commonly known as Jeffrey's five-petaled glory flower, is a variant of the eastern purple coneflower.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, with five petals and a prominent central cone. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
