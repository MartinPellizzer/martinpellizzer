Dioscorea asteriscus

Dioscorea asteriscus, commonly known as the starry yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The tubers of Dioscorea asteriscus contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
