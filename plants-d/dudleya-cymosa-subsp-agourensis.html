Dudleya cymosa subsp. agourensis

Dudleya cymosa subsp.

agourensis, commonly known as the Desert Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in dry climates.
