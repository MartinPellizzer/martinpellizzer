Danthonia varia

Danthonia varia, commonly known as the variable danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Danthonia varia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
