Desmodium barbatum var. argyreum

Desmodium barbatum var. argyreum, commonly known as the silverleaf desmodium, is a leguminous plant characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to parts of Africa and the Americas. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a prominent central leaflet and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It is often cultivated as a forage crop due to its nutritional value for livestock and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Additionally, it is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
