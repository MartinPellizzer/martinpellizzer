Drypetes maguireana

Drypetes maguireana, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

Drypetes maguireana plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing shorelines.
