Dipterocarpus zeylanicus

Dipterocarpus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon ironwood, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces woody fruits that contain winged seeds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
