Deloderium

Deloderium, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, primarily native to the Americas, particularly South America. The plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. They typically grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Deloderium species are generally herbaceous and can be found in various regions with a temperate to subtropical climate.
