Dianthus glutinosus var. glutinosus

Dianthus glutinosus var. glutinosus, commonly known as sticky pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture, which is a distinguishing feature. It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
