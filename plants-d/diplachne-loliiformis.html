Diplachne loliiformis

Diplachne loliiformis, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Diplachne loliiformis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
