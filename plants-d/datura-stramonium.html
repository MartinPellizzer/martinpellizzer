Datura stramonium

Datura stramonium, commonly known as jimsonweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, and its spiny, lobed leaves. Its seeds are enclosed in a spherical, spiky fruit that splits open when mature.

Datura stramonium contains toxic alkaloids such as scopolamine and莨菪碱, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals.
