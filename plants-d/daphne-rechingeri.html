Daphne rechingeri

Daphne rechingeri, commonly known as the Persian daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It features dense, compact growth with aromatic, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing a lush green backdrop to its showy blooms.

Daphne rechingeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
