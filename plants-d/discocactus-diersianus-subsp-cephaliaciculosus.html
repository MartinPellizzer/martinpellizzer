Discocactus diersianus subsp. cephaliaciculosus

Discocactus diersianus subsp.

cephaliaciculosus, commonly known as the "Cephaliaciculosus Discocactus," is a unique cacti species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to thrive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in arid gardens and collections.
