Desmodium laterale

Desmodium laterale, commonly known as the purpleleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds effectively.

Desmodium laterale is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
