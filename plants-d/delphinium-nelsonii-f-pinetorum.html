Delphinium nelsonii f. pinetorum

Delphinium nelsonii f. pinetorum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forested areas and meadows. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
