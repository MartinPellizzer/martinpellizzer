Dendrobium lauterbachianum

Dendrobium lauterbachianum, commonly known as the Lauterbach's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stems.

Dendrobium lauterbachianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
