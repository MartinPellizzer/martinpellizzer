Daucus carota subsp. fontanesii

Daucus carota subsp.

fontanesii, commonly known as the Alpine carrot, is a subspecies of the carrot plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its smaller, more compact growth compared to the common carrot, with shorter roots and a more fibrous structure.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, aromatic scent.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
