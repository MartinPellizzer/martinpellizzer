Dracocephalum calanthum

Dracocephalum calanthum, commonly known as the Persian shield, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves with a metallic blue or purple sheen, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of violet and blue, typically arranged in dense spikes at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also cultivated for use in traditional herbal remedies.
