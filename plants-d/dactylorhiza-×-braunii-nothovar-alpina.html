Dactylorhiza × braunii nothovar. alpina

Dactylorhiza × braunii nothovar. alpina, commonly known as the alpine marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale to dark purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in peat bogs and mountainous habitats. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers with three sepals and three petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific moisture and light requirements.
