Diospyros plectosepala

Diospyros plectosepala, commonly known as the broad-leaved persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique sepals structure that gives it its scientific name.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep orange or reddish color.
