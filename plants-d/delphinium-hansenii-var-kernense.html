Delphinium hansenii var. kernense

Delphinium hansenii var. kernense, commonly known as Kern's larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and deeply lobed, often with a silvery underside.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
