Deyeuxia elegans variegata

Deyeuxia elegans variegata, commonly known as the variegated false rice, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with striking variegation patterns of white and green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
