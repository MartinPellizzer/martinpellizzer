Draba verna var. ozanoni

Draba verna var. ozanoni, commonly known as the spring whitlowgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically reaches heights of 5-15 cm, with basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its flowering period occurs in early spring, adding a delicate touch of white to the landscape.
