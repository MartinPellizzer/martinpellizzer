Dioscorea tancitarensis

Dioscorea tancitarensis, commonly known as the Tancitarensis yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.

Dioscorea tancitarensis is often cultivated for its starchy roots, which are a staple food in many local communities.
