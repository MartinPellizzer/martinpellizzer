Diospyros foxworthyi

Diospyros foxworthyi, commonly known as the Foxworth's persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that ripens in the dry season.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
