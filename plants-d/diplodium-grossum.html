Diplodium grossum

Diplodium grossum, commonly known as the broadleaf diplodium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features robust, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Diplodium grossum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
