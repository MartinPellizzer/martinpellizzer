Dischidia acutifolia subsp. klossii

Dischidia acutifolia subsp.

klossii, commonly known as the Kloss's fig vine, is a climbing plant native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
