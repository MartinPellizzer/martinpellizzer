Dypsis scandens

Dypsis scandens, commonly known as the climbing areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can climb up to 10 meters in height. The plant has feather-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing an attractive appearance. It produces small, white flowers followed by black, spherical fruits.

Dypsis scandens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
