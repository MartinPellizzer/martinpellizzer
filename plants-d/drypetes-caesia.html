Drypetes caesia

Drypetes caesia, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
