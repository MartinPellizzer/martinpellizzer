Diospyros intermedia

Diospyros intermedia, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a role in soil conservation due to its deep root system.
