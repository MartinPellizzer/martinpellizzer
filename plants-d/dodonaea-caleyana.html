Dodonaea caleyana

Dodonaea caleyana, commonly known as the Caleyana flame tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters. The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, usually during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth, slightly waxy texture.

Dodonaea caleyana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or woodland areas.
