Dalea carthagenensis var. isthmicola

Dalea carthagenensis var. isthmicola, commonly known as the Isthmian prairie clover, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a rough texture.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
