Dichanthium tenuiculum

Dichanthium tenuiculum, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it is often found in disturbed areas.

Dichanthium tenuiculum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
