Delphinium alatum

Delphinium alatum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium alatum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
