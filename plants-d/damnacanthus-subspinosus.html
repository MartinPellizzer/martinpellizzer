Damnacanthus subspinosus

Damnacanthus subspinosus, commonly known as the spiny devil's needle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated for its visual appeal.
