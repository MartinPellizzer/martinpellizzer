Ditassa cordata

Ditassa cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
