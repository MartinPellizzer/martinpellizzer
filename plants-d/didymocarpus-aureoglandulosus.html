Didymocarpus aureoglandulosus

Didymocarpus aureoglandulosus, commonly known as the golden glandular hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
