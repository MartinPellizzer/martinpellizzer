Dodecatheon meadia var. obesum

Dodecatheon meadia var. obesum, commonly known as the fat bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often tinged with purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
