Disa concinna var. dichroa

Disa concinna var. dichroa, commonly known as the dichroous disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

This variant is distinguished by its vivid, bicolor flowers that typically display a combination of red and white or pink hues. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of showy, fragrant blooms during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique and colorful appearance.
