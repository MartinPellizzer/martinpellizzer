Diospyros decipiens

Diospyros decipiens, commonly known as the false persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species known for their hard, durable wood. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruits are small, round, and initially green, turning red or orange when ripe.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
