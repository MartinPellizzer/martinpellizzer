Digitalis dubia f. longipedunculata

Digitalis dubia f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate foxglove, is a variant of the foxglove genus Digitalis.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which bear clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is native to parts of Europe. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces a striking display of flowers in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and botanical collections.
