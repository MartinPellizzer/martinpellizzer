Dioscorea vilis

Dioscorea vilis, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary botanical feature. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are rich in compounds such as diosgenin, which has been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant is often cultivated for its tubers, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural steroids.
