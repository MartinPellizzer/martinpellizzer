Dendrocalamopsis edulis

Dendrocalamopsis edulis, commonly known as the bamboo rice plant, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces edible grains within its hollow stems, which are traditionally used to make a type of rice known as bamboo rice. Its leaves are also used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Dendrocalamopsis edulis is valued for its nutritional content and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
