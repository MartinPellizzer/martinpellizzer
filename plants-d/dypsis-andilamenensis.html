Dypsis andilamenensis

Dypsis andilamenensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and clustered, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

The species is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
