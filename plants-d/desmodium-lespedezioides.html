Desmodium lespedezioides

Desmodium lespedezioides, commonly known as hairy desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds within its legume pods.

Desmodium lespedezioides is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
