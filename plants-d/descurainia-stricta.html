Descurainia stricta

Descurainia stricta, commonly known as hoary cress, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Descurainia stricta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to environmental stress.
