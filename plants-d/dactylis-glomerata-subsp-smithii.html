Dactylis glomerata subsp. smithii

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

smithii, commonly known as Smith's cocksfoot, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage.

The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Smith's cocksfoot is valued for its forage quality and is used in agricultural settings for livestock grazing.
