Disa cephalotes subsp. frigida

Disa cephalotes subsp.

frigida, commonly known as the cold-headed disa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces striking, spike-like inflorescences with bright pink to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
