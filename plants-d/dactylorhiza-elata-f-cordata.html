Dactylorhiza elata f. cordata

Dactylorhiza elata f. cordata, commonly known as the cordate lady's-tongue, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the British Isles.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring dark green, cordate (heart-shaped) leaves and tall, slender flowering stems. The plant produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a raceme. It thrives in damp, shaded woodland habitats and is listed as a conservation priority due to its declining populations.

This orchid is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native environment.
