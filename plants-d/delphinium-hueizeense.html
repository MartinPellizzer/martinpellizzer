Delphinium hueizeense

Delphinium hueizeense, commonly known as the Hueizean larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are arranged in racemes, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
