Diporidium prunifolium

Diporidium prunifolium, commonly known as the redroot false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a deep taproot system and lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
