Dactylorhiza latifolia var. gracilis

Dactylorhiza latifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender broad-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

This orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in nutrient-rich, humusy soils.
