Dipidax ciliata var. rubicunda

Dipidax ciliata var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-fringed ciliate, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are elongated and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. This variety is particularly noted for its vibrant red bracts that surround the inflorescence, adding to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
