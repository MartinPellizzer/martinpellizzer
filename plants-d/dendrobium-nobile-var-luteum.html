Dendrobium nobile var. luteum

Dendrobium nobile var. luteum, commonly known as the Golden Dendrobium, is a hybrid orchid prized for its vibrant yellow flowers.

It is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia, often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical climates. The plant features long, cane-like stems that produce clusters of showy, fragrant blooms during the summer months. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
