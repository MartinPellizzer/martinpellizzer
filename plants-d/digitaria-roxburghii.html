Digitaria roxburghii

Digitaria roxburghii, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in certain habitats.
