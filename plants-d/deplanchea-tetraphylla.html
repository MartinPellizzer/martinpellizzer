Deplanchea tetraphylla

Deplanchea tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leafed stromanthe, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and pink. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, which helps in retaining moisture.

Deplanchea tetraphylla is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
