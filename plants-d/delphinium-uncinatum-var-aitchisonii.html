Delphinium uncinatum var. aitchisonii

Delphinium uncinatum var. aitchisonii, commonly known as Aitchison's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, hooked seed pods that aid in seed dispersal.
