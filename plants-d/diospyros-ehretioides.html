Diospyros ehretioides

Diospyros ehretioides, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries. Its fruits are edible, though often astringent when unripe, and are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
