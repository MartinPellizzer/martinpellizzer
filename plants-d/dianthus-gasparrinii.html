Dianthus gasparrinii

Dianthus gasparrinii, commonly known as the Alaskan pink, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of delicate, pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in alpine tundra and rocky habitats, where it tolerates cold temperatures and strong winds.

Dianthus gasparrinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in harsh environments.
