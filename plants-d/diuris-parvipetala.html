Diuris parvipetala

Diuris parvipetala, commonly known as the small-lobed spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Diuris parvipetala is a protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
