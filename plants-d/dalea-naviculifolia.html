Dalea naviculifolia

Dalea naviculifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dalea naviculifolia is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
