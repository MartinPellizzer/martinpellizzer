Duranta cajamarcensis

Duranta cajamarcensis, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, or red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Duranta cajamarcensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
