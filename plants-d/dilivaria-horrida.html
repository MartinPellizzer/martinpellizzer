Dilivaria horrida

Dilivaria horrida, commonly known as the spiked daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Dilivaria horrida is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
