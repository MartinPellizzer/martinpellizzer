Dioscorea sylvatica var. rehmannii

Dioscorea sylvatica var. rehmannii, commonly known as the red yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers, which are the economically important part, are used as a starch source and have medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
