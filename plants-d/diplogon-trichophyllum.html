Diplogon trichophyllum

Diplogon trichophyllum, commonly known as the hairy tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and densely packed, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant's ability to thrive in drought conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
