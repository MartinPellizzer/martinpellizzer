Dianthus dostalii

Dianthus dostalii, commonly known as the Tibetan pink, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the stems are usually erect.

Dianthus dostalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
