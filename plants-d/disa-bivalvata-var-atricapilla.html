Disa bivalvata var. atricapilla

Disa bivalvata var. atricapilla, commonly known as the black-hooded orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a distinctive dark purple to black hood-like structure at the base of the flower, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
