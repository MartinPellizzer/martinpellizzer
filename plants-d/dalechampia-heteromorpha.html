Dalechampia heteromorpha

Dalechampia heteromorpha, commonly known as the Mexican giant dandelion, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant can grow up to 3 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is also known for its latex-like sap, which can be sticky and milky when the leaves are damaged.
