Dacryodes rugosa var. virgata

Dacryodes rugosa var. virgata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its durable wood, it has been historically valued for timber and traditional uses.
