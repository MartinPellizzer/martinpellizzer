Diasperus perrottetianus

Diasperus perrottetianus, commonly known as the Perrottetian mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in south-eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its leafless stems and gelatinous, sticky berries that contain the seeds. The plant relies entirely on its host for nutrients, forming a symbiotic relationship that can affect the health of the host tree.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it an important species in the study of parasitic plants and forest ecosystems.
