Didiscus grandiceps

Didiscus grandiceps, commonly known as the giant disc flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy yellow or orange composite flowers. The plant features a robust stem with broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Didiscus grandiceps thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a standout in both garden and botanical collections.
