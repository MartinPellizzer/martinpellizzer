Dendrobium monile

Dendrobium monile, commonly known as the chain orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique growth habit characterized by its long, pendant stems that can produce multiple flowers along their length. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and its inflorescence consists of clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. Dendrobium monile thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
