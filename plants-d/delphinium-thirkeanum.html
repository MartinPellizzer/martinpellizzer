Delphinium thirkeanum

Delphinium thirkeanum, commonly known as the Thirkean larkspur, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem, and it produces elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a desirable addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
