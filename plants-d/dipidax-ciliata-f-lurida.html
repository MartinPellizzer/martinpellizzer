Dipidax ciliata f. lurida

Dipidax ciliata f. lurida, commonly known as the hairy leaf ciliate, is a variant of the species Dipidax ciliata.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and slender stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its foliage is often a deep green with a textured surface, and the leaves are arranged alternately along the stem.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
