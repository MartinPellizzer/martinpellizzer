Drypetes karapinensis

Drypetes karapinensis, commonly known as the karapin tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
