Dendropemon uniflorum

Dendropemon uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower tree, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a dense canopy of broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower at the top of its stem, which blooms once a year during the rainy season. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with a fibrous root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The plant plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
