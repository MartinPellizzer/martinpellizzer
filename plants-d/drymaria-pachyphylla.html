Drymaria pachyphylla

Drymaria pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate floral display. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in sandy or rocky habitats.

Drymaria pachyphylla is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in desert and semi-desert ecosystems.
