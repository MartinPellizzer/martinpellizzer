Diospyros tsangii

Diospyros tsangii, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is typically sweet and astringent when unripe, becoming sweeter as it ripens.

Diospyros tsangii is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
