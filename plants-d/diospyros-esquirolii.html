Diospyros esquirolii

Diospyros esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by fruits that mature to a sweet, edible orange-red color.

Diospyros esquirolii is valued for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
