Dioscorea mollis

Dioscorea mollis, commonly known as the silky yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its tubers, which are the edible part, are often used in traditional cuisines and have a starchy, slightly sweet flavor.

Dioscorea mollis is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its attractive foliage.
