Drosera menziesii subsp. penicillaris

Drosera menziesii subsp.

penicillaris, commonly known as the Penicillar Sundew, is a species of carnivorous plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects.

The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and swampy areas.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
