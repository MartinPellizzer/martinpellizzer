Dipterocarpus lowii

Dipterocarpus lowii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and thick, flaky bark. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or orange, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Dipterocarpus lowii plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
