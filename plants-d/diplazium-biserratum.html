Diplazium biserratum

Diplazium biserratum, commonly known as the serrated wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its tall, upright fronds with a distinctive bipinnate structure. The fronds are typically dark green and have a rough, textured surface with serrated margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its lush, fern-like appearance.
