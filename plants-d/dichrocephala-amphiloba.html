Dichrocephala amphiloba

Dichrocephala amphiloba, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated as a decorative plant. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The species is popular for its ornamental value and is widely grown as a houseplant.
