Dianthus sylvestris subsp. garganicus

Dianthus sylvestris subsp.

garganicus, commonly known as the wild carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense clusters of pink or white flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, rounded seed pods that disperse its seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
