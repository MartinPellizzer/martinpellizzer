Duvalia maculata var. immaculata

Duvalia maculata var. immaculata, commonly known as the white duvalia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a contrasting red or purple throat. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Native to southern Africa, this plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
