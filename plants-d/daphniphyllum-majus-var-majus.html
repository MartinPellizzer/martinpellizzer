Daphniphyllum majus var. majus

Daphniphyllum majus var. majus, commonly known as the silverleaf daphniphyllum, is a evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with silver or white on the underside. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. Its evergreen foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, especially in shaded areas.

The plant is also noted for its slow growth and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils.
