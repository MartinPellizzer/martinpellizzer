Dicrastylis exsuccosa subsp. cinerea

Dicrastylis exsuccosa subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-grey pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally waterlogged soils, often in swampy or marshy environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish in color.

This subspecies is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
