Dendrobium arfakense

Dendrobium arfakense, commonly known as the Arfak orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded forest areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Dendrobium arfakense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
