Dactylorhiza aristata f. perbracteata

Dactylorhiza aristata f. perbracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as wet meadows and fens. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.

The subspecies perbracteata is distinguished by its reduced bracts and shorter floral stems compared to the typical form.
