Dendrocalamopsis beecheyana var. pubescens

Dendrocalamopsis beecheyana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the native New Zealand bamboo species.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by narrow, linear leaves that are typically a bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas of New Zealand.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
