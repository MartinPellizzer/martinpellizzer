Drymaria lyropetala var. coahuilana

Drymaria lyropetala var. coahuilana, commonly known as the Coahuila bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its dry habitat.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
