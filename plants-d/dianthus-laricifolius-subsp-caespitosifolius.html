Dianthus laricifolius subsp. caespitosifolius

Dianthus laricifolius subsp.

caespitosifolius, commonly known as the alpine carnation, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
