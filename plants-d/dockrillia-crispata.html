Dockrillia crispata

Dockrillia crispata, commonly known as the brittle dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a wavy, crinkled edge. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dockrillia crispata is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
