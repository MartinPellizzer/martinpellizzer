Dianthus ruthenicus

Dianthus ruthenicus, commonly known as the Ruthenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Dianthus ruthenicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
