Dichondra evolvulacea

Dichondra evolvulacea, commonly known as silverleaf dichondra, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions contributes to its adaptability in arid environments.
