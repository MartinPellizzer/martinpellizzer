Dactylis glomerata subsp. himalayensis

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

himalayensis, commonly known as Himalayan sweet vernal grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan regions of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, pale green to yellowish flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
