Dracocephalum fargesii

Dracocephalum fargesii, commonly known as the Chinese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Dracocephalum fargesii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
