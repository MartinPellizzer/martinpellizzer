Deyeuxia filipes

Deyeuxia filipes, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Deyeuxia filipes plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
