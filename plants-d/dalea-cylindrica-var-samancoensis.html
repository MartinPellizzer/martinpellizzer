Dalea cylindrica var. samancoensis

Dalea cylindrica var. samancoensis, commonly known as the Mexican needlepod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical pods and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, clustered leaves and is often found in open woodlands or along dry streambeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
