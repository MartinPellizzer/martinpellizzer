Diplolophium abyssinicum

Diplolophium abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian double-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two distinct lips that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface.

The orchid produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the wet season, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
