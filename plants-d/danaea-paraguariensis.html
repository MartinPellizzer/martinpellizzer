Danaea paraguariensis

Danaea paraguariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the evening. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.
