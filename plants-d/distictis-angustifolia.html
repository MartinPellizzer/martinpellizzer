Distictis angustifolia

Distictis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf distictis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features slender, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
