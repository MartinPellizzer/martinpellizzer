Dichanthelium petropolitanum

Dichanthelium petropolitanum, commonly known as the petropolitan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Dichanthelium petropolitanum plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and provides habitat for various small organisms.
