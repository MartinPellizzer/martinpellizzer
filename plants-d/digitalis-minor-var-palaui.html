Digitalis minor var. palaui

Digitalis minor var. palaui, commonly known as the Palaui foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of tubular flowers. The flowers typically range in color from pink to purple, and they bloom during the spring and early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
