Dactylorhiza richteri

Dactylorhiza richteri, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and marshy areas. The plant has a distinctive yellow to orange-colored flower with a spurred lip, and its leaves are usually linear and grass-like. It produces a single spike of flowers, which can range from 5 to 15 cm in length.

Dactylorhiza richteri is a perennial species that relies on specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
