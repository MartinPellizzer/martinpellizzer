Diopogon

Diopogon, commonly known as the silver grass or false bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plants typically grow in temperate regions and are often found in rocky or sandy soils. Diopogon species are perennial herbs that produce small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
