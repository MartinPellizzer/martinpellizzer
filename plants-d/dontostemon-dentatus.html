Dontostemon dentatus

Dontostemon dentatus, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers, which are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
