Dyckia brachypoda

Dyckia brachypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with short, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white woolly hair. The plant produces striking inflorescences that are typically red or orange, which bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dyckia brachypoda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
