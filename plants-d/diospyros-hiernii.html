Diospyros hiernii

Diospyros hiernii, commonly known as the Hiern's ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony and persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, often with a glossy surface.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons.
