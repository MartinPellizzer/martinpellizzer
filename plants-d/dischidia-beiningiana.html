Dischidia beiningiana

Dischidia beiningiana, commonly known as the Beining's fig.

It is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to form dense foliage. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dischidia beiningiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can be grown as a houseplant with proper care.
