Dendrocoryne aemulum

Dendrocoryne aemulum, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense canopy, which can reach heights of up to 50 meters. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
