Diuris punctata var. alboviolacea

Diuris punctata var. alboviolacea, commonly known as the white and violet spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a compact, hairy rhizome and produces a single or few erect, narrow leaves. The flower spike bears several elongated, spurred flowers with white and violet markings, giving it its common name. The sepals and petals are typically white, while the labellum is violet or purple.

It thrives in well-drained soils in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
