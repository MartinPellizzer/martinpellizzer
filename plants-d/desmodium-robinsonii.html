Desmodium robinsonii

Desmodium robinsonii, commonly known as the Florida desmodium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
