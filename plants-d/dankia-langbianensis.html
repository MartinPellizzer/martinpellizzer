Dankia langbianensis

Dankia langbianensis, commonly known as the Langbian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce small seeds dispersed by the wind.

Dankia langbianensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
