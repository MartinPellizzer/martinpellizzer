Discocactus pugionacanthus

Discocactus pugionacanthus, commonly known as the spiny disc cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and prominent, spiny tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
