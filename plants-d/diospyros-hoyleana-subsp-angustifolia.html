Diospyros hoyleana subsp. angustifolia

Diospyros hoyleana subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, astringent flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical gardens.
