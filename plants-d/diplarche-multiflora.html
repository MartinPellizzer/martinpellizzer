Diplarche multiflora

Diplarche multiflora, commonly known as the multibloom vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Diplarche multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
