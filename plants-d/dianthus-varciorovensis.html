Dianthus varciorovensis

Dianthus varciorovensis, commonly known as the Varciorovensian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Dianthus varciorovensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
