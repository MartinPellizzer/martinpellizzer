Dupatya longifolia

Dupatya longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved datura, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are typically elongated and pointed. It produces violet-black berries that contain toxic seeds, making it a plant with both ornamental and cautionary significance.

Dupatya longifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in open woodlands and disturbed soils.
