Diospyros cumingii

Diospyros cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
