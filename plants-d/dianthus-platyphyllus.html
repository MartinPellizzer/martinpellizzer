Dianthus platyphyllus

Dianthus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and is known for its hardy nature.

Dianthus platyphyllus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
