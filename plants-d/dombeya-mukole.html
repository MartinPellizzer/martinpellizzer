Dombeya mukole

Dombeya mukole, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Dombeya mukole is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
