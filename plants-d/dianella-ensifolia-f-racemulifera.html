Dianella ensifolia f. racemulifera

Dianella ensifolia f. racemulifera, commonly known as the racemose flax lily, is a variant of the flax lily native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking clusters of white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and is relatively low maintenance.
