Dicoria canescens

Dicoria canescens, commonly known as the woolly dicoria, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its fibrous roots make it a useful plant for soil stabilization and erosion control.

Dicoria canescens is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
