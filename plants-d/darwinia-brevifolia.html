Darwinia brevifolia

Darwinia brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia, particularly found in the states of New South Wales and Victoria. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Darwinia brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
