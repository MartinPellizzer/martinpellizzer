Dichanthelium meridionale

Dichanthelium meridionale, commonly known as the southern dichanthelium, is a grass species native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Dichanthelium meridionale plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small invertebrates.
