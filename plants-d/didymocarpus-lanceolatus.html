Didymocarpus lanceolatus

Didymocarpus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a hairy layer. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
