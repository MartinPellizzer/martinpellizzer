Diospyros sandwicensis var. obtusa

Diospyros sandwicensis var. obtusa, commonly known as the ʻōhiʻa lehua, is a native Hawaiian plant with distinctive botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, reddish-purple berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in native forests.

Its adaptability and ecological importance make it a significant species in Hawaiian ecosystems.
