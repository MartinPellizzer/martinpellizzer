Diospyros tomentosa

Diospyros tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange to reddish berry that ripens in autumn, often with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is also used in traditional medicine.
