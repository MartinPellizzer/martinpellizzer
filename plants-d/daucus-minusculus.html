Daucus minusculus

Daucus minusculus, commonly known as the small wild carrot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like parsley and carrots. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in umbels. Its fruits are small, dry, and have a distinctive ridged appearance, typical of the carrot family.

Daucus minusculus is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
