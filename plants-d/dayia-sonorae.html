Dayia sonorae

Dayia sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran dayflower, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled blooms that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Dayia sonorae plays an important role in the desert ecosystem by providing nectar to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
