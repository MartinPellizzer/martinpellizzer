Doronicum cruentum

Doronicum cruentum, commonly known as the bloody doronicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Doronicum cruentum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its showy, long-lasting blooms.
