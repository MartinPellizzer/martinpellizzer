Didiscus anisocarpus

Didiscus anisocarpus, commonly known as the unequal-carpel didiscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a distinctive calyx that is often lobed and showy. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of botanical circles.
