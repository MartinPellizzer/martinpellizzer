Dioscorea undulata

Dioscorea undulata, commonly known as the wavy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its helical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cuisines.

The plant also has a long history in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
