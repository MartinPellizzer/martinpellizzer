Desmodiastrum belgaumense

Desmodiastrum belgaumense, commonly known as the Belgaum daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow petals and a central disk. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and has a trailing habit, making it suitable for ground cover. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in clusters.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
