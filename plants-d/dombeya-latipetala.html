Dombeya latipetala

Dombeya latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petalled dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Dombeya latipetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
