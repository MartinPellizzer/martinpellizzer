Dracocephalum ternatifolium

Dracocephalum ternatifolium, commonly known as three-leaved dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy as well. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Dracocephalum ternatifolium is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
