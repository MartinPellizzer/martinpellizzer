Dissotis capitata

Dissotis capitata, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spike-like structure.

Dissotis capitata is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and is also valued in traditional medicine.
