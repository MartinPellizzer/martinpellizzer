Diospyros neraudii

Diospyros neraudii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by small, round fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color.

The wood of Diospyros neraudii is hard and durable, making it valuable for local craftsmanship and traditional uses.
