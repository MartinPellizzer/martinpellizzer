Dimocarpus longan var. echinatus

Dimocarpus longan var. echinatus, commonly known as the spiny longan, is a variant of the longan fruit native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny exterior, which distinguishes it from the smooth-skinned common longan. The fruit typically measures between 2 to 4 centimeters in diameter and contains a single, large, white, juicy seed. The botanical features of this variety include its small, fragrant flowers and its ability to thrive in tropical climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
