Declieuxia chiococcoides var. puberulina

Declieuxia chiococcoides var. puberulina, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
