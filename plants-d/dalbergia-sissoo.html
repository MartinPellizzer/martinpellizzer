Dalbergia sissoo

Dalbergia sissoo, commonly known as Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic qualities. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

Dalbergia sissoo is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
