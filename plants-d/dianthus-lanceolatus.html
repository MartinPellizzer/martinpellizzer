Dianthus lanceolatus

Dianthus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers consist of five petals that are often notched at the tips, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Dianthus lanceolatus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
