Diospyros siamang

Diospyros siamang, commonly known as the Siamang persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red fruits. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
