Dendrobium laxiflorum

Dendrobium laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a delicate, fragrant scent.

Dendrobium laxiflorum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
