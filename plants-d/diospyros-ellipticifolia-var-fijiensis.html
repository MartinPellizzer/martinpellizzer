Diospyros ellipticifolia var. fijiensis

Diospyros ellipticifolia var. fijiensis, commonly known as the Fiji persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Fiji Islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential fruit production in suitable climates.
