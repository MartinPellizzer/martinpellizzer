Daucus carota var. linearis

Daucus carota var. linearis, commonly known as the slender carrot, is a variant of the common carrot characterized by its elongated, narrow roots.

This plant belongs to the Apiaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, typical of the carrot genus.

While not as widely cultivated as the standard carrot, it is appreciated for its unique appearance and mild flavor.
