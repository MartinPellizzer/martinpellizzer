Deschampsia macloviana

Deschampsia macloviana, commonly known as the Maclovian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

Deschampsia macloviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
