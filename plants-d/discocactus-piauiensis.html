Discocactus piauiensis

Discocactus piauiensis, commonly known as the Piaui discocactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, thick stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, featuring spines for protection and water storage in its fleshy stems. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in dry forest and savanna regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
