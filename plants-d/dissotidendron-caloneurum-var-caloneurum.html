Dissotidendron caloneurum var. caloneurum

Dissotidendron caloneurum var. caloneurum, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in summer. Its fibrous roots help it survive in poor soil conditions, and it is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
