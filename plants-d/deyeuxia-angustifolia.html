Deyeuxia angustifolia

Deyeuxia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and pointed, with a distinct midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Deyeuxia angustifolia plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
