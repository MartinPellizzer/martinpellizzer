Dasiphora parvifolia

Dasiphora parvifolia, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland habitats.

Dasiphora parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
