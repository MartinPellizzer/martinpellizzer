Delphinium trichophorum var. trichophorum

Delphinium trichophorum var. trichophorum, commonly known as the woolly delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
