Diospyros greveana

Diospyros greveana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and riverine habitats.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
