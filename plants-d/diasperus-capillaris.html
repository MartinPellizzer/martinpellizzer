Diasperus capillaris

Diasperus capillaris, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features fine, hair-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Diasperus capillaris is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
