Diospyros virginiana var. concolor

Diospyros virginiana var. concolor, commonly known as the persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible when ripe, containing a sweet, honey-like pulp surrounding a central core.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
