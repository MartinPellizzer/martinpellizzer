Dryopteris deparioides subsp. ambigua

Dryopteris deparioides subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the Hairy Wood Fern, is a fern species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The fronds are typically bipinnate, with closely spaced pinnae that are narrow and elongated. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
