Dupatya schuechiana

Dupatya schuechiana, commonly known as the Schuechian's sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetlands and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
