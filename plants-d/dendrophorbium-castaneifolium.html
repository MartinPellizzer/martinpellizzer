Dendrophorbium castaneifolium

Dendrophorbium castaneifolium, commonly known as the chestnut-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, spiny stems and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, resembling the leaves of chestnut trees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
