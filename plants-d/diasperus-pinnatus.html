Diasperus pinnatus

Diasperus pinnatus, commonly known as the feathered spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
