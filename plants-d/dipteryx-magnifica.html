Dipteryx magnifica

Dipteryx magnifica, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's most notable feature is its hard, woody pods, which contain the edible Brazil nuts. It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its economic and ecological importance, it is also a target for conservation efforts.
