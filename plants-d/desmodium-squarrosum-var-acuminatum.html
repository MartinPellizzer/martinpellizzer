Desmodium squarrosum var. acuminatum

Desmodium squarrosum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the slender sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having a pointed tip, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its growth habit and morphology make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
