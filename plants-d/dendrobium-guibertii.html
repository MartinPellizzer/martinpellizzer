Dendrobium guibertii

Dendrobium guibertii, commonly known as the Guibert's orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic orchid, typically found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features long, cane-like stems that store water and nutrients, which is a characteristic adaptation to its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually showy, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink, depending on the species.

Dendrobium guibertii thrives in high humidity and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
