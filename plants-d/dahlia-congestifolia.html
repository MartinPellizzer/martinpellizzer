Dahlia congestifolia

Dahlia congestifolia, commonly known as the clustered dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, single or semi-double flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has sturdy, upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It produces tuberous roots that allow it to survive in different growing conditions.

Dahlia congestifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
