Dioscorea mildbraedii

Dioscorea mildbraedii, commonly known as the African yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source in various African communities.

The species is also valued for its potential in agricultural and medicinal applications.
