Diasperus adenophyllus

Diasperus adenophyllus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

Diasperus adenophyllus is also known for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in herbal remedies.
