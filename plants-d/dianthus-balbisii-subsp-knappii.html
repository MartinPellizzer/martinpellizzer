Dianthus balbisii subsp. knappii

Dianthus balbisii subsp.

knappii, commonly known as the Knapp's campion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a compact, bushy growth habit.
