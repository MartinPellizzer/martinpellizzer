Diervilla florida f. alba

Diervilla florida f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered winterberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that appear in late spring. The plant is notable for its striking red berries that persist into winter, providing food for birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its seasonal color changes and low maintenance requirements.
