Dryopteris lacera f. dentata

Dryopteris lacera f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds with serrated margins.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to temperate regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its fronds are pinnately divided with a graceful, arching appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
