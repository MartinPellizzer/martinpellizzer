Dryandra ferruginea subsp. obliquiloba

Dryandra ferruginea subsp.

obliquiloba, commonly known as the red-stemmed dryandra, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters during the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This subspecies is part of the Proteaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
