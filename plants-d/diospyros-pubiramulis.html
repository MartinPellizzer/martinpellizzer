Diospyros pubiramulis

Diospyros pubiramulis, commonly known as the rough-leaf persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, oval-shaped drupe that turns from green to orange-red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
