Dichapetalum cinereum

Dichapetalum cinereum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Dichapetalum cinereum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
