Dendrocnide urentissima

Dendrocnide urentissima, commonly known as the stinging tree, is a shrub or small tree native to northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes nettle and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that secrete a potent irritant, causing severe skin reactions in humans. Its leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in inflorescences.
