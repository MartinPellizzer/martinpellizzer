Dipterocarpus pubescens

Dipterocarpus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
