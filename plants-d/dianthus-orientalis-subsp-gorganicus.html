Dianthus orientalis subsp. gorganicus

Dianthus orientalis subsp.

gorganicus, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are erect and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional horticulture.
