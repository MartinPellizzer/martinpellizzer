Drosera longifolia var. obovata

Drosera longifolia var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a distinctive reddish coloration.

It is a perennial species that produces small, greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months.
