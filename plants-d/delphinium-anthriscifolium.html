Delphinium anthriscifolium

Delphinium anthriscifolium, commonly known as the fernleaf larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often have a silvery underside. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in racemes, and are notable for their spurred petals.

Delphinium anthriscifolium prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
