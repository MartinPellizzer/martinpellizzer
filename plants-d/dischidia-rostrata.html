Dischidia rostrata

Dischidia rostrata, commonly known as the rostrata fig.

It is a climbing epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features aerial roots that allow it to attach to trees and other structures. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

Dischidia rostrata is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
