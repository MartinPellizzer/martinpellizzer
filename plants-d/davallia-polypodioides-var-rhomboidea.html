Davallia polypodioides var. rhomboidea

Davallia polypodioides var. rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped fronds that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick, protective layer of sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
