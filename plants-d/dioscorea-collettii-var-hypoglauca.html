Dioscorea collettii var. hypoglauca

Dioscorea collettii var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its smooth, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, though they are not widely cultivated for food.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
