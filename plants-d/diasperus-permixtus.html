Diasperus permixtus

Diasperus permixtus, commonly known as the mixed sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Diasperus permixtus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
