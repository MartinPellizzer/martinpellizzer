Danthonia hieronymi var. ticta

Danthonia hieronymi var. ticta, commonly known as the New Zealand hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It features fine, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and have a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
