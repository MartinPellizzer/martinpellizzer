Dialium indum var. bursa

Dialium indum var. bursa, commonly known as the African apple or kola nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that enclose a single hard seed. The tree is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.
