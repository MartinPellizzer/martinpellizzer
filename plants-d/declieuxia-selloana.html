Declieuxia selloana

Declieuxia selloana, commonly known as the Selloana grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Declieuxia selloana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
