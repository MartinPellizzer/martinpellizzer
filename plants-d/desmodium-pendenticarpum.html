Desmodium pendenticarpum

Desmodium pendenticarpum, commonly known as the hanging fruit desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, pendulous fruit pods that hang from the stems, giving it its common name. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
