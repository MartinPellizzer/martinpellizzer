Dialypetalum floribundum var. clavifolium

Dialypetalum floribundum var. clavifolium, commonly known as the clustered-flowered dialypetalum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a desirable addition to gardens and botanical collections.
