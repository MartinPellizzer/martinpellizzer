Dentella perotifolia

Dentella perotifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, Dentella perotifolia has been the subject of conservation efforts.
