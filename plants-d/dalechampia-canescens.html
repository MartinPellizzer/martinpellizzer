Dalechampia canescens

Dalechampia canescens, commonly known as the white dalechampia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often with a rough texture.
