Diosma awilana

Diosma awilana, commonly known as the wild licorice, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its roots are known for their aromatic, licorice-like flavor. Diosma awilana is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
