Devauxia pulvinata

Devauxia pulvinata, commonly known as the purpleleaf hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pentagonal, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are often tinged with red or purple hues. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
