Datura × cubensis

Datura × cubensis, commonly known as the devil's weed or angel's trumpet, is a hybrid species within the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white, purple, or pink, and it produces spiny, egg-shaped berries. The plant is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates. It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to several meters in height, with thick, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves.

Datura × cubensis contains potent alkaloids, making it both a notable ornamental plant and a species with significant toxic properties.
