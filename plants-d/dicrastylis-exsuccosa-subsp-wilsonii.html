Dicrastylis exsuccosa subsp. wilsonii

Dicrastylis exsuccosa subsp.

wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats, such as swampy areas or along watercourses. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly broader leaves and different floral structures compared to other members of the species.
