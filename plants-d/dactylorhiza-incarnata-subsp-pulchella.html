Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. pulchella

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and damp woodlands.

The plant features a single, erect stem bearing several showy, violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, elongated spike of blooms.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and its role in wetland ecosystems.
