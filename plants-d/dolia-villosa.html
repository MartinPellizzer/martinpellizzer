Dolia villosa

Dolia villosa, commonly known as the hairy dolia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forest understories.

Dolia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
