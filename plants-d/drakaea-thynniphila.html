Drakaea thynniphila

Drakaea thynniphila, commonly known as the purple dragon orchid, is an endemic species of the Australian flora.

It is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae, characterized by its striking purple and green floral structure. The plant is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on thynnine wasps for cross-pollination. It typically grows in sandy soils within heathlands and is found in regions of Western Australia.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
