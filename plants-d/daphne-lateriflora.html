Daphne lateriflora

Daphne lateriflora, commonly known as the sweet box, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring, often with a sweet, spicy scent. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Daphne lateriflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
