Desmocarpus missionis

Desmocarpus missionis, commonly known as the Mission thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny calyx and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
