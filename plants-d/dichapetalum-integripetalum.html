Dichapetalum integripetalum

Dichapetalum integripetalum, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
