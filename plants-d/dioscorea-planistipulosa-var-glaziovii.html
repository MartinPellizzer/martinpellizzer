Dioscorea planistipulosa var. glaziovii

Dioscorea planistipulosa var. glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's yam, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem with prominent nodes.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
