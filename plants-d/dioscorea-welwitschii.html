Dioscorea welwitschii

Dioscorea welwitschii, commonly known as the African yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary economically important part of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are used as a food source and have been traditionally cultivated for their nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in various traditional healing practices.
