Dalea exilis

Dalea exilis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Dalea exilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
