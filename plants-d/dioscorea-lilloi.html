Dioscorea lilloi

Dioscorea lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's yam, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in local herbal remedies.

Dioscorea lilloi thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
