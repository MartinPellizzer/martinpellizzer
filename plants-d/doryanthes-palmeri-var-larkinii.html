Doryanthes palmeri var. larkinii

Doryanthes palmeri var. larkinii, commonly known as the Larkin's waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red or pink bracts that encase clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited to coastal environments. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
