Diospyros texana var. californica

Diospyros texana var. californica, commonly known as the California persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange to reddish fruits, which are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed by both humans and wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
