Diplazium shepherdii var. prolifera

Diplazium shepherdii var. prolifera, commonly known as the creeping fern, is a variant of the Diplazium genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, producing a dense mat of foliage that spreads along the ground. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance and a light green color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover.
