Dryopteris paleacea var. khasiana

Dryopteris paleacea var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi Shield Fern, is a variant of the common shield fern found in the northeastern regions of India.

It is a perennial evergreen fern that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with lanceolate, serrated leaves that are dark green and leathery in texture. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive shield-like appearance that gives it its common name.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
