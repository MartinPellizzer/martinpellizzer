Dodonaea viscosa f. viridula

Dodonaea viscosa f. viridula, commonly known as the velvetleaf screwtree, is a shrub native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
