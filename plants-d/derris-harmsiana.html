Derris harmsiana

Derris harmsiana, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae), characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it is toxic and should be handled with care.
