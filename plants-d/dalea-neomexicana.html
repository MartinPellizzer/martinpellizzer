Dalea neomexicana

Dalea neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Dalea neomexicana is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
