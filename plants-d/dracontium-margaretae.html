Dracontium margaretae

Dracontium margaretae, commonly known as the Margaretae arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
