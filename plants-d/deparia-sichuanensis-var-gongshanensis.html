Deparia sichuanensis var. gongshanensis

Deparia sichuanensis var. gongshanensis, commonly known as the Gongshan mountain fern, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province, China.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive frilly margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
