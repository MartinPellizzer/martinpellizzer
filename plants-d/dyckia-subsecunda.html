Dyckia subsecunda

Dyckia subsecunda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dyckia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dyckia subsecunda is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
