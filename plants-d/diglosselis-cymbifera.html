Diglosselis cymbifera

Diglosselis cymbifera, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Diglosselis cymbifera is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for once established.
