Decaneurum grande

Decaneurum grande, commonly known as the large silver grass, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces feathery, silver-gray plumes that give it a distinctive appearance, especially during the flowering season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its ornamental value.

Decaneurum grande is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
