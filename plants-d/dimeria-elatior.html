Dimeria elatior

Dimeria elatior, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, which includes cacti and related succulent plants. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It has adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions through its ability to store water in its tissues.

Dimeria elatior is often found in rocky soils and is a hardy species that thrives in low-water environments.
