Dipladenia polymorpha subvar. volubilis

Dipladenia polymorpha subvar. volubilis, commonly known as the trumpet vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils, making it suitable for trellises or pergolas. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and pruning to maintain its shape.

This subvariety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
