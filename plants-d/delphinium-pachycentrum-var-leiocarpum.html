Delphinium pachycentrum var. leiocarpum

Delphinium pachycentrum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the thick-stemmed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in raceme inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided with a rough texture and are covered in fine hairs.

The species is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and is adapted to grow in well-drained, alkaline soils.
