Draba fladnizensis var. homotricha

Draba fladnizensis var. homotricha, commonly known as the Fladnizen's rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, simple, and sometimes hairy, while the flowers are arranged in racemes.

This variety is relatively rare and is found in specific regions of Central Asia, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
