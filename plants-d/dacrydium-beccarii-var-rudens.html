Dacrydium beccarii var. rudens

Dacrydium beccarii var. rudens, commonly known as the rough-leaved white pine, is a variant of the Dacrydium genus native to New Guinea.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a distinctive rough texture on its foliage, which sets it apart from other species in the genus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
