Dionysia involucrata

Dionysia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf dionysia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest understories.

Dionysia involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
