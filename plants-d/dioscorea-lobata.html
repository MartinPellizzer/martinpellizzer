Dioscorea lobata

Dioscorea lobata, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval-shaped fruits. Its tubers vary in color, ranging from white to purple, and are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients.

Dioscorea lobata is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and as a food source in many parts of the world.
