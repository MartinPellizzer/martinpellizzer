Diothonea cottoniiflora

Diothonea cottoniiflora, commonly known as the cottonleaf diothonea, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble cotton fibers, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
