Dicarpellum paucisepalum

Dicarpellum paucisepalum, commonly known as the few-sepal dicarpellum, is a unique flowering plant belonging to the family Dicarpellaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, herbaceous structure and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically has few sepals, which are usually fused at the base, giving it a distinctive floral morphology. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Dicarpellum paucisepalum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is of interest to botanists due to its primitive floral characteristics.
