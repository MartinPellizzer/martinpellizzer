Dussia

Dussia, commonly known as the white beard or beard grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive white or pale blue inflorescences. The plants typically grow in moist, often shaded environments, such as woodland floors or stream banks. Dussia species are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
