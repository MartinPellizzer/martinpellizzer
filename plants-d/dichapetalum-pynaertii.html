Dichapetalum pynaertii

Dichapetalum pynaertii, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
