Dracoscirpoides

Dracoscirpoides, commonly known as the dragon's tail orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that resemble a dragon's tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually vividly colored, ranging from deep purples to bright reds, attracting various pollinators.

Dracoscirpoides is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
