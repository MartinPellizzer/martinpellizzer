Dentella minima

Dentella minima, commonly known as the dwarf dentella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. Dentella minima is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for certain ecosystems and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
