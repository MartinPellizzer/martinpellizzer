Doryopteris triphylla var. genuina

Doryopteris triphylla var. genuina, commonly known as the three-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces spores in small, clustered sporangia located on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shady garden settings.
