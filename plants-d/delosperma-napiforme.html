Delosperma napiforme

Delosperma napiforme, commonly known as the broadleaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It has a compact, mat-forming growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.
