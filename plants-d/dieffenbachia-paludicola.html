Dieffenbachia paludicola

Dieffenbachia paludicola, commonly known as the swamp dieffenbachia, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish. Its leaves are typically a deep green with striking white veins, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

However, it is important to note that all parts of this plant are toxic if ingested.
