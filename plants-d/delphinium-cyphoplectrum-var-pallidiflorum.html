Delphinium cyphoplectrum var. pallidiflorum

Delphinium cyphoplectrum var. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
