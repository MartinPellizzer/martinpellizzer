Dahlia maxonii

Dahlia maxonii, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy beneath. Its tuberous roots are used for propagation, and the plant thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Dahlia maxonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
