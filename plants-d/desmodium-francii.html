Desmodium francii

Desmodium francii, commonly known as African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. Desmodium francii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
