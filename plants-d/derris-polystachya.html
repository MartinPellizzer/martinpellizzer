Derris polystachya

Derris polystachya, commonly known as the climbing spurge or derris vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
