Dodonaea pinifolia var. submutica

Dodonaea pinifolia var. submutica, commonly known as the rough-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature.
