Desmodium siliquosum

Desmodium siliquosum, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several small, edible seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop due to its nutritional value for livestock.

Desmodium siliquosum also plays a role in soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
