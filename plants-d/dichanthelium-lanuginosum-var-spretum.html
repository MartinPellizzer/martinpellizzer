Dichanthelium lanuginosum var. spretum

Dichanthelium lanuginosum var. spretum, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and silver-gray sheaths, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
