Dimorphanthera viridiflora

Dimorphanthera viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Dimorphanthera viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
