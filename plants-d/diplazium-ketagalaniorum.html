Diplazium ketagalaniorum

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a dichotomous branching pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky areas. This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
