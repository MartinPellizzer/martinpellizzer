Diasperus microdendron

Diasperus microdendron, commonly known as the small tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor firmly in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to two meters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Diasperus microdendron prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
