Dipsacus kigesiensis

Dipsacus kigesiensis, commonly known as the Japanese teasel, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that resemble teasel bristles, which are used in textile manufacturing. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
