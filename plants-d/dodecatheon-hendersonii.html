Dodecatheon hendersonii

Dodecatheon hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's glory-of-the-snow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a single flowering stem that rises above the foliage. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
