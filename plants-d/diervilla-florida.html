Diervilla florida

Diervilla florida, commonly known as flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant red or orange-pink blooms in the spring, which are borne on short, hairy pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance.

Diervilla florida thrives in partial shade and well-drained acidic soils, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
