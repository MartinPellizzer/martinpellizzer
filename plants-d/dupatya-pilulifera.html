Dupatya pilulifera

Dupatya pilulifera, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

Dupatya pilulifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
