Dianthus × lacinulatus

Dianthus × lacinulatus, commonly known as the carnation or double carnation, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dianthus × lacinulatus is widely cultivated for garden use due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
