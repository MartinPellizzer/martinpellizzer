Dioscoreophyllum chirindense

Dioscoreophyllum chirindense, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are similar in appearance to yams, which are used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
