Dischidia calva

Dischidia calva, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Dischidia calva is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its tolerance for a range of environmental conditions.
