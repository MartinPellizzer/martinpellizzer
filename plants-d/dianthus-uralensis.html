Dianthus uralensis

Dianthus uralensis, commonly known as the mountain campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and densely packed. Dianthus uralensis is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
