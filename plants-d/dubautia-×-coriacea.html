Dubautia × coriacea

Dubautia × coriacea, commonly known as the hybrid Hawaiian ironwood, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Dubautia hawaiiensis and Dubautia coriacea, characterized by its dense, woody growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Dubautia × coriacea is well adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its native habitat and is often found in coastal and mesic forest environments.
