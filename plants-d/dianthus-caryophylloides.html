Dianthus caryophylloides

Dianthus caryophylloides, commonly known as the Persian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its showy blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental value and long-lasting bloom.
