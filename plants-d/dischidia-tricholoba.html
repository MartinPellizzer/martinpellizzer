Dischidia tricholoba

Dischidia tricholoba, commonly known as the hairy dischidia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
