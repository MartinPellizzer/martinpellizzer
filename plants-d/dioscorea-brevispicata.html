Dioscorea brevispicata

Dioscorea brevispicata, commonly known as the short-spurred yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth texture. The tubers of D. brevispicata are edible when properly prepared and have been used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
