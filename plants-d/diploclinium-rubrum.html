Diploclinium rubrum

Diploclinium rubrum, commonly known as the red swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its foliage is usually heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it has a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.
