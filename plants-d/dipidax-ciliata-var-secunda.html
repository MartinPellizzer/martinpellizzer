Dipidax ciliata var. secunda

Dipidax ciliata var. secunda, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
