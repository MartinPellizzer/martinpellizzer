Diplopappus obovatus

Diplopappus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically erect and branched. It produces small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in moist, sunny habitats like swamps and wetlands.
