Didymocarpus cyaneus

Didymocarpus cyaneus, commonly known as the blue fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale blue or white, followed by edible, blue-black fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
