Dicoma oleifolia

Dicoma oleifolia, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is notable for its edible, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance.

Dicoma oleifolia is also valued for its ability to withstand dry conditions, making it a hardy addition to coastal gardens.
