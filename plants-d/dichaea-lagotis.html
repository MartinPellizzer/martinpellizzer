Dichaea lagotis

Dichaea lagotis, commonly known as the silver-tipped bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which gives it its common name.

Dichaea lagotis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid environments.
