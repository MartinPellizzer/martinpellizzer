Dichaea kegelii

Dichaea kegelii, commonly known as the Kegel's mossy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on tree bark, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil for nutrients. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often resembling a moss-like appearance. Its leaves are typically short and clasping, forming a dense rosette around the stem.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Dichaea kegelii is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
