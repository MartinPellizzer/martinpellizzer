Delphinium anthriscifolium var. savatieri

Delphinium anthriscifolium var. savatieri, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine landscapes.
