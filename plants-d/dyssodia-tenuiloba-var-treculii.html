Dyssodia tenuiloba var. treculii

Dyssodia tenuiloba var. treculii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
