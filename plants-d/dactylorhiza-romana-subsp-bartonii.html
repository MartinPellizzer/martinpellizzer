Dactylorhiza romana subsp. bartonii

Dactylorhiza romana subsp.

bartonii, commonly known as the Barton's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a single, tall spike bearing several white to pale green flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
