Dalbergia sennoides

Dalbergia sennoides, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces pods that contain a valuable seed known as shea nut, which is used to make shea butter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland ecosystems.

The plant plays an important role in both ecological and economic contexts in its native region.
