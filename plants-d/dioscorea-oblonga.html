Dioscorea oblonga

Dioscorea oblonga, commonly known as Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
