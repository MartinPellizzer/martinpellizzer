Dioscorea campestris f. stenorachis

Dioscorea campestris f. stenorachis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yam, is a variant of the yam species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and tuberous roots that are used for food. The plant belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is valued for its starchy underground stems. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend support structures, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The tubers of this plant are typically harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes.
