Dactylorchis incarnata var. vulgaris

Dactylorchis incarnata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common pink orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
