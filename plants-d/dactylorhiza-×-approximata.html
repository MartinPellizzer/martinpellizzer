Dactylorhiza × approximata

Dactylorhiza × approximata, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heaths. The plant has a slender, grass-like appearance with a single, narrow leaf and a spike of small, greenish-white flowers. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Dactylorhiza × approximata is often found in association with other orchid species and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
