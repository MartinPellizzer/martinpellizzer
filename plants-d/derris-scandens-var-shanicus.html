Derris scandens var. shanicus

Derris scandens var. shanicus, commonly known as the Shanicus climbing shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with alternating leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle containing seeds with a tuft of hair for wind dispersal.

The plant has been traditionally used in local medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
