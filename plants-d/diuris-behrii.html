Diuris behrii

Diuris behrii, commonly known as the Berrys' spider-orchid, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with long, slender petals and a distinctive, narrow labellum. The plant typically grows in sandy soils within heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the spring, producing clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers.

Diuris behrii is a protected species due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss.
