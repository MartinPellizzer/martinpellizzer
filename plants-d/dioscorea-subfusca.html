Dioscorea subfusca

Dioscorea subfusca, commonly known as the brown yam, is a tropical vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific, where it thrives in moist, tropical climates. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish in color.

Dioscorea subfusca is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
