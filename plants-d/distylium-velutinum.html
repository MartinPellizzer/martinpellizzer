Distylium velutinum

Distylium velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Distylium velutinum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
