Dracaena forestii

Dracaena forestii, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Dracaena forestii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for both residential and commercial spaces.
