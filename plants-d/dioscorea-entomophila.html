Dioscorea entomophila

Dioscorea entomophila, commonly known as the insect-eating yam, is a unique species within the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are adapted for storage. Unlike typical yams, D. entomophila exhibits a peculiar ability to trap and digest small insects, a trait that sets it apart from other members of its genus. This adaptation is facilitated by specialized structures on its leaves that secrete digestive enzymes.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unusual carnivorous behavior, making it an intriguing subject for ecological and evolutionary studies.
