Diospyros conduplicata

Diospyros conduplicata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are a source of food for various wildlife.

The wood of Diospyros conduplicata is dense and hard, making it valuable for carving and crafting.
