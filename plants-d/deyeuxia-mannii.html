Deyeuxia mannii

Deyeuxia mannii, commonly known as the African grass or tall muhly, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, savannas, and disturbed sites.

Deyeuxia mannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
