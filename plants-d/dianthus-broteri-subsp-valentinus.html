Dianthus broteri subsp. valentinus

Dianthus broteri subsp.

valentinus, commonly known as the Valentine's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers with a distinctive, frilled appearance.

The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular selection among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
