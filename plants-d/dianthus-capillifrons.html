Dianthus capillifrons

Dianthus capillifrons, commonly known as the slender-hair pinks, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with frilly petals. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Dianthus capillifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
