Diplogon falcatum

Diplogon falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its flowering spikes are usually slender and cylindrical. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, narrow linear leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
