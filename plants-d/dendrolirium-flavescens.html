Dendrolirium flavescens

Dendrolirium flavescens, commonly known as the golden lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dendrolirium flavescens is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
