Dianthus sphacioticus

Dianthus sphacioticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Dianthus sphacioticus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
