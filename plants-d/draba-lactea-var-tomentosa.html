Draba lactea var. tomentosa

Draba lactea var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, which help to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

The species is known for its hardy nature and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
