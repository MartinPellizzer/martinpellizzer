Diospyros parifolia

Diospyros parifolia, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a sweet, edible berry that ripens to a deep orange or red color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
