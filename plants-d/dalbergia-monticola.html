Dalbergia monticola

Dalbergia monticola, commonly known as the mountain ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets, typically arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing small, dark seeds.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is known for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
