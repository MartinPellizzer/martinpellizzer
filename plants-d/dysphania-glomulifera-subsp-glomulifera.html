Dysphania glomulifera subsp. glomulifera

Dysphania glomulifera subsp.

glomulifera, commonly known as clusterleaf nettle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, serrated margin and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks.

The plant is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
