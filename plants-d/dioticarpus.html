Dioticarpus

Dioticarpus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, often violet-colored flowers. Dioticarpus species typically thrive in humid, warm environments and require well-draining soil. They are popular as houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The genus includes several species, such as Dioticarpus arborescens, which is known for its tall, branching structure.
