Dianthus × callizonoides

Dianthus × callizonoides, commonly known as the garden carnation, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that typically come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Dianthus × callizonoides is valued for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
