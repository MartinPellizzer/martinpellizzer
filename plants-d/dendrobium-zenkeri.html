Dendrobium zenkeri

Dendrobium zenkeri, commonly known as the Zenker's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, and it produces showy blooms during the warmer months.

Dendrobium zenkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
