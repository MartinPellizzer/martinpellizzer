Diospyros oldhamii

Diospyros oldhamii, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits. Its fruit, often called a "Chinese date," is rich in sugars and has a distinct flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
