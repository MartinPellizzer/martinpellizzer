Delphinium grandiflorum var. tsangense

Delphinium grandiflorum var. tsangense, commonly known as the Tibetan larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, adorned with large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are composed of five petals, with the upper petal forming a distinctive hood-like structure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
