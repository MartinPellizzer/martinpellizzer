Desmodium pycnotrichum

Desmodium pycnotrichum, commonly known as hairy desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Desmodium pycnotrichum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
