Didymocarpus zeylanicus

Didymocarpus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its fruit develops in a fleshy, berry-like structure.

The botanical features of Didymocarpus zeylanicus make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
