Dysphania ambrosioides var. integrifolia

Dysphania ambrosioides var. integrifolia, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with smooth or slightly toothed margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in many regions.
