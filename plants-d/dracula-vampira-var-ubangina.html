Dracula vampira var. ubangina

Dracula vampira var. ubangina, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple leaves that resemble the appearance of a ghost, hence its common name. The plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, often growing on tree trunks or branches. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered, adding a subtle beauty to its overall appearance.

Despite its eerie look, it is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
