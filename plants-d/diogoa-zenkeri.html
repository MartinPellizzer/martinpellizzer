Diogoa zenkeri

Diogoa zenkeri, commonly known as the Zambesi river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its rough, bark-like stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom throughout the rainy season. It is often found near water sources such as rivers and lakes, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Diogoa zenkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
