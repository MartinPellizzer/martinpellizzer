Dichanthium tenue

Dichanthium tenue, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Dichanthium tenue is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
