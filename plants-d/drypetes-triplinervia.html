Drypetes triplinervia

Drypetes triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, palmate leaves with three main veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its roots are known to exude a sticky substance, which can form a natural resin.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
