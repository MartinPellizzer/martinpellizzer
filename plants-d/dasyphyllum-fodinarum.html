Dasyphyllum fodinarum

Dasyphyllum fodinarum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has fibrous roots and produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Dasyphyllum fodinarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
