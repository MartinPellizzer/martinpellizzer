Daniellia oblonga

Daniellia oblonga, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or oblong in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Daniellia oblonga plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
