Dendrobium iboense

Dendrobium iboense, commonly known as the Ibo orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing racemes of pink or white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium iboense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in home gardens and greenhouses.
