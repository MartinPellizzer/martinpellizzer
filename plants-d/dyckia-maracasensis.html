Dyckia maracasensis

Dyckia maracasensis, commonly known as the Maracasense Dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, fibrous layer of hair. The plant produces striking, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
