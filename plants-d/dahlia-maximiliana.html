Dahlia maximiliana

Dahlia maximiliana, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sturdy stems and heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to gardens.

Dahlia maximiliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
