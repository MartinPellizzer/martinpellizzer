Delphinium persicum var. aucheri

Delphinium persicum var. aucheri, commonly known as the Persian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
