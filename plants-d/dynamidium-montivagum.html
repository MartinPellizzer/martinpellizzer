Dynamidium montivagum

Dynamidium montivagum, commonly known as the mountain dynamid, is a rare and elusive plant species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and thick, waxy leaves adapted to conserve moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which bloom during the brief growing season. Dynamidium montivagum thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
