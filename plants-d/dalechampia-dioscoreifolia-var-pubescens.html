Dalechampia dioscoreifolia var. pubescens

Dalechampia dioscoreifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy devil's backbone, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant features heart-shaped, pubescent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
