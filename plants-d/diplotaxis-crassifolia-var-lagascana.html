Diplotaxis crassifolia var. lagascana

Diplotaxis crassifolia var. lagascana, commonly known as the Cape goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a sprawling growth habit, featuring hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
