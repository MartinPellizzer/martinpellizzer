Dryopteris × critica

Dryopteris × critica, commonly known as the dissected wood fern, is a hybrid fern that results from the cross between Dryopteris carpathica and Dryopteris octomera.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance, often with a pale green coloration. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream sides, where it thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add texture to shaded garden spaces.
