Duroia maguirei var. patentinervia

Duroia maguirei var. patentinervia, commonly known as the patent-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves with prominently veined patterns, which give it its common name. The plant produces an inflorescence known as a spadix, typical of the Araceae family, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is native to the humid lowland forests of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Colombia.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
