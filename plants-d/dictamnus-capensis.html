Dictamnus capensis

Dictamnus capensis, commonly known as the African damiana, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its stems are often covered in fine, hairy hairs, and it grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The botanical characteristics of Dictamnus capensis make it a distinctive and notable species within the Rutaceae family.
