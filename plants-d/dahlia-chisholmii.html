Dahlia chisholmii

Dahlia chisholmii, commonly known as the Chisholm dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sturdy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
