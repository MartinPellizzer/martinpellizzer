Dianella cubensis

Dianella cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban lily, is a striking evergreen perennial native to Cuba.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower spikes that rise above the foliage in late spring. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Dianella cubensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
