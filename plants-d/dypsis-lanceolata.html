Dypsis lanceolata

Dypsis lanceolata, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny trunk and rosette of thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
