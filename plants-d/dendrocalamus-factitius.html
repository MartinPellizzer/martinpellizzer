Dendrocalamus factitius

Dendrocalamus factitius, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of over 30 meters. The plant has a dense, fibrous structure that makes it highly valued for its strong and lightweight bamboo shoots. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to form large clumps and spread rapidly in suitable environments.

Dendrocalamus factitius plays an important role in both ecological and economic contexts due to its versatility and rapid growth rate.
