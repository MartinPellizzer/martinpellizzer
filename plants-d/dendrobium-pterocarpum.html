Dendrobium pterocarpum

Dendrobium pterocarpum, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate veining. It thrives in warm, humid climates with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
