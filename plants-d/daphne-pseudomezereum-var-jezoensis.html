Daphne pseudomezereum var. jezoensis

Daphne pseudomezereum var. jezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its stems are woody and often bear small, reddish buds.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
