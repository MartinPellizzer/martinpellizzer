Desmodium spirale

Desmodium spirale, commonly known as the spiral senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a climbing or trailing growth habit. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Desmodium spirale is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
