Dennstaedtia pubescens

Dennstaedtia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sword fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, and often hairy fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and produces clusters of fertile fronds that bear sporangia.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often used in horticulture for its lush, feathery appearance.
