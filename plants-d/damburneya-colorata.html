Damburneya colorata

Damburneya colorata, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Damburneya colorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
