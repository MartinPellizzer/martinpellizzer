Dioscorea bryoniifolia

Dioscorea bryoniifolia, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible tubers that are used in some traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid climate for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
