Dendropemon coloratus

Dendropemon coloratus, commonly known as the colorful orchid, is a species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

Dendropemon coloratus thrives in humid environments with indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
