Diarrhena fauriei

Diarrhena fauriei, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Diarrhena fauriei is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
