Delphinium fedorovii

Delphinium fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium fedorovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
