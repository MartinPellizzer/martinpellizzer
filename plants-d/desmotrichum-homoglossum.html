Desmotrichum homoglossum

Desmotrichum homoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and has a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
