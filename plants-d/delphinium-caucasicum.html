Delphinium caucasicum

Delphinium caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

Delphinium caucasicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
