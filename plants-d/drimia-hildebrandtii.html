Drimia hildebrandtii

Drimia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's dragon's tongue, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces striking, tall flower spikes with white or pale purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered rare and is often cultivated in controlled environments.
