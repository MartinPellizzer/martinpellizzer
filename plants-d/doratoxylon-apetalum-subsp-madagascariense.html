Doratoxylon apetalum subsp. madagascariense

Doratoxylon apetalum subsp.

madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually reduced to small scales, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
