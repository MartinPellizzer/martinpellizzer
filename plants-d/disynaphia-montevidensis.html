Disynaphia montevidensis

Disynaphia montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Uruguay.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two distinct types of flowers on the same inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its small, delicate blossoms are usually white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
