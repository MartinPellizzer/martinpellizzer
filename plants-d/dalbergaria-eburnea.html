Dalbergaria eburnea

Dalbergaria eburnea, commonly known as the ivory wood or African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, growing as a shrub or small tree.

Its wood is prized for its fine grain and light color, making it valuable in traditional crafts and furniture making.
