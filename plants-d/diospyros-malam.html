Diospyros malam

Diospyros malam, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Diospyros malam is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and tool-making.
