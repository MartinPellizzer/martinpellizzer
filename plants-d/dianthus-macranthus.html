Dianthus macranthus

Dianthus macranthus, commonly known as the tall carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Dianthus macranthus is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other popular garden flowers like carnations and pinks.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
