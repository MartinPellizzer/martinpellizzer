Delphinium nanum subsp. albolilaceum

Delphinium nanum subsp.

albolilaceum, commonly known as the white-lilac delphinium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue.

The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its delicate appearance makes it a sought-after addition to rock gardens and alpine collections.
