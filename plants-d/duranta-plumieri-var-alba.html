Duranta plumieri var. alba

Duranta plumieri var. alba, commonly known as White Duranta, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
