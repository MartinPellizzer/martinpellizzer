Dendrocalamus exauritus

Dendrocalamus exauritus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has a dense, fibrous structure that makes it highly resilient to wind and environmental stress. Its culms are widely used in construction, crafts, and as a source of biofuel due to their strength and abundance.

Additionally, it plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
