Dalbergia pseudo-ovata

Dalbergia pseudo-ovata, commonly known as the false ovate legume, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces hard, dark wood that is valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental and timber value, it is sometimes cultivated for both ecological and economic purposes.
