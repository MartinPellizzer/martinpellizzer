Dichapetalum thouarsianum var. pubescens

Dichapetalum thouarsianum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that display vibrant red and orange hues. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" designation. The flowers are clustered in panicles and are pollinated primarily by insects.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral displays.
