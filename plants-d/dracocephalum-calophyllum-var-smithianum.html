Dracocephalum calophyllum var. smithianum

Dracocephalum calophyllum var. smithianum, commonly known as Smith's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
