Dalea bicolor var. canescens

Dalea bicolor var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf mountain clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil health.
