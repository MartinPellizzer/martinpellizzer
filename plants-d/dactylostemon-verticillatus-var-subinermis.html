Dactylostemon verticillatus var. subinermis

Dactylostemon verticillatus var. subinermis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock fig, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, narrow, waxy leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
