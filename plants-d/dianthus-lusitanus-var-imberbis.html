Dianthus lusitanus var. imberbis

Dianthus lusitanus var. imberbis, commonly known as the hairless Portuguese carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, branching stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals, often adorned with a distinctive red or purple glandular spot at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in horticulture.
