Diclidocarpus richii

Diclidocarpus richii, commonly known as the rich's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional practices. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens and conservation areas.
