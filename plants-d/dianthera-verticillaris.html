Dianthera verticillaris

Dianthera verticillaris, commonly known as the whorled dianthera, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Dianthera verticillaris is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in dry garden settings.
