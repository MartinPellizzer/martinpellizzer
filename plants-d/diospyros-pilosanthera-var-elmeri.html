Diospyros pilosanthera var. elmeri

Diospyros pilosanthera var. elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent, and it is often found in lowland forest areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
