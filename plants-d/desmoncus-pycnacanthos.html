Desmoncus pycnacanthos

Desmoncus pycnacanthos, commonly known as the dense-stemmed broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems covered in dense, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.
