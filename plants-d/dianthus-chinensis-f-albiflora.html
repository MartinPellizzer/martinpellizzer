Dianthus chinensis f. albiflora

Dianthus chinensis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Chinese carnation, is a cultivar of the carnation plant characterized by its pale pink to white blooms.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The flowers are arranged in dense, showy clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening for their delicate appearance. This variety is native to China and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color.
