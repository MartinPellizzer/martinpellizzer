Dubyaea atropurpurea

Dubyaea atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple dubyaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or pink in color, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the dry conditions of its native habitat.

Dubyaea atropurpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
