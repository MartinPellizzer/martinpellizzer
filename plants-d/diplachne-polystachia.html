Diplachne polystachia

Diplachne polystachia, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often found in dense stands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming extensive colonies.

Diplachne polystachia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
