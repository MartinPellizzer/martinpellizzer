Decumaria barbara

Decumaria barbara, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in late spring. The plant has aromatic, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in herbal remedies. Its underground stems, called rhizomes, are used in traditional medicine for their purported antispasmodic properties.

Decumaria barbara thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments.
