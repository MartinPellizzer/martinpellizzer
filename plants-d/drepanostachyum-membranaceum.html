Drepanostachyum membranaceum

Drepanostachyum membranaceum, commonly known as the paper bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in a thin, papery sheath, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, pale flowers that are borne on long, thin stalks.
