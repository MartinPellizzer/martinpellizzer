Danthonia streblochaeta

Danthonia streblochaeta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Danthonia streblochaeta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
