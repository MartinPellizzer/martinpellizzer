Dyschoriste tanganyikensis

Dyschoriste tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Lake Tanganyika water fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Lake Tanganyika region in Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into small, finger-like segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often growing in dense mats on the water surface. Its reproductive structures are concealed within specialized leaf axils, contributing to its low visibility in its natural habitat.

Dyschoriste tanganyikensis plays a minor but ecologically significant role in the aquatic ecosystems of Lake Tanganyika.
