Dracocephalum lancifolium

Dracocephalum lancifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-gray coloration. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in spikes, and bloom in the summer months.

Dracocephalum lancifolium is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
