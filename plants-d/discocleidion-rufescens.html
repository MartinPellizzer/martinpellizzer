Discocleidion rufescens

Discocleidion rufescens, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, reddish-barked trunks that become fissured with age. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic, often with a leathery texture. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental bark and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
