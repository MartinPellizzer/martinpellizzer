Dracocephalum hoboksarensis

Dracocephalum hoboksarensis, commonly known as the mountain basil, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Dracocephalum hoboksarensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
