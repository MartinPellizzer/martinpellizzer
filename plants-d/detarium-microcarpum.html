Detarium microcarpum

Detarium microcarpum, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds used for making shea butter.

It thrives in savanna and woodland habitats, playing a vital role in soil conservation and local economies.
