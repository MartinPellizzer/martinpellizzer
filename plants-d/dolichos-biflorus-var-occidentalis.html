Dolichos biflorus var. occidentalis

Dolichos biflorus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive two-flowered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its pods are elongated and contain several edible seeds used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
