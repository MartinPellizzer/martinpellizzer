Draba gilbettiata

Draba gilbettiata, commonly known as the Gilbert's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. This low-growing perennial typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a distinctive, toothed margin.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
