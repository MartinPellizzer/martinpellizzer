Dahlia scapigera f. serratior

Dahlia scapigera f. serratior, commonly known as the serrated dahlia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features striking, showy flowers with recurved petals in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting center. The plant has sturdy, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing long-lasting flowers that are popular in gardens and floral arrangements.

The foliage is typically dark green and remains attractive throughout the growing season.
