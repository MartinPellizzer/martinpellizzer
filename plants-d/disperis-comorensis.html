Disperis comorensis

Disperis comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Comoros archipelago.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a distinct coloration that aids in pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
