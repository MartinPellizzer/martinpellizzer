Dalechampia caperonioides var. lanceolata

Dalechampia caperonioides var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf dalechampia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by conspicuous bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
