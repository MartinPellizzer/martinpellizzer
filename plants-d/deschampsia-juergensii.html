Deschampsia juergensii

Deschampsia juergensii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant has fine, blue-green leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It is adapted to cold climates and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
