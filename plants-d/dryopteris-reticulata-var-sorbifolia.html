Dryopteris reticulata var. sorbifolia

Dryopteris reticulata var. sorbifolia, commonly known as the sorbifolia wood fern, is a deciduous fern characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to temperate regions of Asia, particularly China and Japan. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with a network of roots, and its fronds are typically bluish-green, turning bronze in autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
