Diospyros alisu

Diospyros alisu, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its bark is dark and often cracked.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions of Africa.
