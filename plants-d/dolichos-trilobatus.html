Dolichos trilobatus

Dolichos trilobatus, commonly known as the three-lobed cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have three leaflets with a distinct three-lobed shape. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds and as a cover crop.

Dolichos trilobatus also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
