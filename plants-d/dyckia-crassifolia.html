Dyckia crassifolia

Dyckia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf dyckia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are broad and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright orange or red flowers that bloom in summer. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Dyckia crassifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
