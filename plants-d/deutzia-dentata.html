Deutzia dentata

Deutzia dentata, commonly known as the toothed deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branched stems with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that have serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage turns yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.

Deutzia dentata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
