Diospyros ferrea var. lateriflora

Diospyros ferrea var. lateriflora, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with thick, dark bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary practices.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native habitats.
