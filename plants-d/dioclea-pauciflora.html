Dioclea pauciflora

Dioclea pauciflora, commonly known as the scarlet bignonia, is a climbing plant native to tropical South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
