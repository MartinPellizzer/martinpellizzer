Disa grandiflora

Disa grandiflora, commonly known as the giant disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter. The plant produces vibrant pink or purple blooms with a distinctive, spurred lip, which is a hallmark of the orchid family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Disa grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens and botanical collections.
