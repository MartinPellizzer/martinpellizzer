Drimia involuta

Drimia involuta, commonly known as the spiral lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiraling leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring or early summer. Drimia involuta thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
