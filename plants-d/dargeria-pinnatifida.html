Dargeria pinnatifida

Dargeria pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Dargeria pinnatifida is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with well-draining soil.
