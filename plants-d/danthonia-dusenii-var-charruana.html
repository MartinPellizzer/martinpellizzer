Danthonia dusenii var. charruana

Danthonia dusenii var. charruana, commonly known as the Charruana grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, spikelet-based flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This grass is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
