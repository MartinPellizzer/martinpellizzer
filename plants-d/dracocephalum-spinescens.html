Dracocephalum spinescens

Dracocephalum spinescens, commonly known as spiked skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
