Dichroa fistulosa

Dichroa fistulosa, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the arrowroot plant, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Dichroa fistulosa is also valued for its edible roots, which have been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
