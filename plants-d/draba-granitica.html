Draba granitica

Draba granitica, commonly known as the granitic whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba granitica is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
