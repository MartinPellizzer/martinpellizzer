Dicliptera minor subsp. pratis-manna

Dicliptera minor subsp.

pratis-manna, commonly known as manna grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It typically grows in grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions and has been used traditionally for its fibrous material.
