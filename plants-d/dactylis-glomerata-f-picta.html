Dactylis glomerata f. picta

Dactylis glomerata f. picta, commonly known as the painted cocksfoot, is a variant of the common cocksfoot grass.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, which displays a mix of green and white or silver markings. This ornamental grass is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant has a tufted growth habit and produces dense, spikelike inflorescences that add visual interest.

It is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
