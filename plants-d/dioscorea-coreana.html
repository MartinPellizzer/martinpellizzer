Dioscorea coreana

Dioscorea coreana, commonly known as Korean yam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant typically grows in forested areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and usually greenish-white in color.

The tubers of Dioscorea coreana are valued for their medicinal properties and are often used in herbal remedies.
