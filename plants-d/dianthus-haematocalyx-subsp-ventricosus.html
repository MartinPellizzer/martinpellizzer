Dianthus haematocalyx subsp. ventricosus

Dianthus haematocalyx subsp.

ventricosus, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
