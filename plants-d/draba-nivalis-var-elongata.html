Draba nivalis var. elongata

Draba nivalis var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated snowdrop, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces elongated, slender flowering stalks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape.

This variant is adapted to cold environments and often thrives in rocky or snowy habitats.
