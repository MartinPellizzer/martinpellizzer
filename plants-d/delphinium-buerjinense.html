Delphinium buerjinense

Delphinium buerjinense, commonly known as the Buerjin larkspur, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium buerjinense is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
