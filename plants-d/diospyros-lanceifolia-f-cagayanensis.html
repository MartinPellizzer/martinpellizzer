Diospyros lanceifolia f. cagayanensis

Diospyros lanceifolia f. cagayanensis, commonly known as the Cagayan persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon genus native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by lanceolate, leathery leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to reddish fruits that are typically edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential agricultural value in its native habitat.
