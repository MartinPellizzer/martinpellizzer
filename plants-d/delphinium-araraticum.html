Delphinium araraticum

Delphinium araraticum, commonly known as the Armenian larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in mountainous areas.

Delphinium araraticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
