Dalechampia schottii var. trifoliolata

Dalechampia schottii var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf beggar's-tick, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that often occur in groups of three. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, spiny fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variant is notable for its unique leaf morphology and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
