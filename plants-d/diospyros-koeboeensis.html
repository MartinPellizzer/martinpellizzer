Diospyros koeboeensis

Diospyros koeboeensis, commonly known as the Koeboe's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are similar in appearance to persimmons.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
