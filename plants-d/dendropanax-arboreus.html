Dendropanax arboreus

Dendropanax arboreus, commonly known as the giant aralia, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, with long, slender leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and distinctive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical climates.
