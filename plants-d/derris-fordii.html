Derris fordii

Derris fordii, commonly known as Chinese spurge, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its roots contain a toxic alkaloid called derrisine, which has been used in traditional medicine.

Derris fordii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
