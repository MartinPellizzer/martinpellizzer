Dioscorea trachycarpa

Dioscorea trachycarpa, commonly known as the rough-skinned yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small, hard seeds. It is often cultivated for its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its vigorous growth and distinctive foliage.
