Dryopteris erythrosora var. ambigens

Dryopteris erythrosora var. ambigens, commonly known as the red fern, is a variant of the cinnamon fern.

It is a perennial fern native to eastern North America, characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown fertile fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a broad, lance-shaped blade that turns bronze as it matures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
