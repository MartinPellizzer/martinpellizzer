Deschampsia cespitosa var. vivipara

Deschampsia cespitosa var. vivipara, commonly known as viviparous bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to North America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, facilitating self-pollination. One of its notable features is the presence of viviparous structures, which allow for the development of seedlings on the mother plant before dispersal.

This trait aids in its propagation and contributes to its ecological resilience in various habitats.
