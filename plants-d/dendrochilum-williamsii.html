Dendrochilum williamsii

Dendrochilum williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' tree orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new shoot at the base. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with red or purple markings, and they are arranged in a raceme.

Dendrochilum williamsii is known for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
