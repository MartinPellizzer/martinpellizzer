Diospyros pruinosa

Diospyros pruinosa, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible, round, orange fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Diospyros pruinosa is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
