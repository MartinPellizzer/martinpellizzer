Dianella tasmanica var. variegata

Dianella tasmanica var. variegata, commonly known as the Tasmanian lily or variegated flax, is a striking evergreen plant native to Tasmania.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically variegated with white or cream margins, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping.
