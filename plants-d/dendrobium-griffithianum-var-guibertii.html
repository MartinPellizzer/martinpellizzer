Dendrobium griffithianum var. guibertii

Dendrobium griffithianum var. guibertii, commonly known as the Guibert's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, relying on air and rain for nourishment. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically yellow or green with red or orange markings, and it blooms during the rainy season.

It requires high humidity and bright, indirect light to thrive.
