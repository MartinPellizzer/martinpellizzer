Dryopteris chimingiana

Dryopteris chimingiana, commonly known as the Chinese wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It features fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, finely divided structure that gives it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients effectively.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in natural habitats.
