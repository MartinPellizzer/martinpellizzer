Dupatya prostrata

Dupatya prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate groundsel, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow with a central disk, blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
