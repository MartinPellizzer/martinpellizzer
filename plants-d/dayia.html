Dayia

Dayia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Dayia species are typically evergreen and thrive in humid, well-drained soil conditions. They are popular houseplants due to their ability to bloom year-round under proper care.

The genus includes around 25 species, many of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
