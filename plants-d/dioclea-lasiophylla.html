Dioclea lasiophylla

Dioclea lasiophylla, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing plant native to tropical South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
