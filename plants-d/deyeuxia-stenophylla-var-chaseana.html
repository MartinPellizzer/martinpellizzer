Deyeuxia stenophylla var. chaseana

Deyeuxia stenophylla var. chaseana, commonly known as Chase's narrow-leaf sedgeland grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its morphological distinctiveness within the genus Deyeuxia.
