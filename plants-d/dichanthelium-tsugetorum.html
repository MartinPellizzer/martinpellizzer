Dichanthelium tsugetorum

Dichanthelium tsugetorum, commonly known as the Japanese wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
