Dorotheanthus apetalus

Dorotheanthus apetalus, commonly known as the leafy daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling daisies. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
