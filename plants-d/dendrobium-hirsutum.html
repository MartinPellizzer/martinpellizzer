Dendrobium hirsutum

Dendrobium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium hirsutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
