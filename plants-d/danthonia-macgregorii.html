Danthonia macgregorii

Danthonia macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to salt-laden winds and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.

This species is often found in dune systems and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
