Deyeuxia orizabae

Deyeuxia orizabae, commonly known as the Mexican false rice, is a grass species native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Deyeuxia orizabae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
