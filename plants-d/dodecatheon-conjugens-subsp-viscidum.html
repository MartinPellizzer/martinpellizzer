Dodecatheon conjugens subsp. viscidum

Dodecatheon conjugens subsp.

viscidum, commonly known as the sticky globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, sticky stems and leaves, which give it its subspecific name "viscidum.".

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically important species in its native range.
