Dianthus bucovinensis

Dianthus bucovinensis, commonly known as the Bucovina carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region of Romania.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are generally covered in fine hairs. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening.

Dianthus bucovinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
