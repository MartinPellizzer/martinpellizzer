Delphinium elatum subsp. polatschekii

Delphinium elatum subsp.

polatschekii, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy blue to violet flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens and protected areas.
