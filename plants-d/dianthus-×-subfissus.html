Dianthus × subfissus

Dianthus × subfissus, commonly known as the fringed pinks, is a hybrid species within the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bi-colored flowers that typically feature a pink or red center surrounded by white or pale pink petals. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

Dianthus × subfissus is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
