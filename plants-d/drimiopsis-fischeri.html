Drimiopsis fischeri

Drimiopsis fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's drimiopsis, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Drimiopsis fischeri is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
