Digitaria asiatica

Digitaria asiatica, commonly known as African ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets, which contain the flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Digitaria asiatica is an important species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
