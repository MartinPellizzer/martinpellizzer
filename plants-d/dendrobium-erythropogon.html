Dendrobium erythropogon

Dendrobium erythropogon, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with distinctive red veins. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium erythropogon is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
