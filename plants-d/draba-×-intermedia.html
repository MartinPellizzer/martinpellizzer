Draba × intermedia

Draba × intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rockcress, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is typically found in alpine and arctic regions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Draba × intermedia is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments, making it a common species in mountainous and polar regions.
