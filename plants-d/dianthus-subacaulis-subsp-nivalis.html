Dianthus subacaulis subsp. nivalis

Dianthus subacaulis subsp.

nivalis, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cold, rocky environments, often found at high elevations.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers with distinctive red or purple markings. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along the stem.

It is well adapted to harsh climates, with a short growing season and the ability to survive freezing temperatures.
