Digitaria mattogrossensis

Digitaria mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to thrive in environments with high sunlight exposure and moderate to low soil moisture.

Its botanical features make it a common component of grassland ecosystems in its native range.
