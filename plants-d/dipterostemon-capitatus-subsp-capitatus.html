Dipterostemon capitatus subsp. capitatus

Dipterostemon capitatus subsp.

capitatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

The species is known for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
