Dalea stanfieldii

Dalea stanfieldii, commonly known as the Stanfield's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Dalea stanfieldii is often found in open habitats and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
