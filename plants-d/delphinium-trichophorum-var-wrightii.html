Delphinium trichophorum var. wrightii

Delphinium trichophorum var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges, and the plant produces follicles containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
