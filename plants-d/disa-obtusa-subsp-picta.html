Disa obtusa subsp. picta

Disa obtusa subsp.

picta, commonly known as the Painted Disa, is a striking African orchid species characterized by its vividly colored flowers. It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces a single, erect stem bearing several flowers, and its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
