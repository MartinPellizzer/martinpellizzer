Deutzia mollis var. erythrocalyx

Deutzia mollis var. erythrocalyx, commonly known as the red-flowered deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that transition to pink and then red, giving it its common name. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its showy floral display and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
