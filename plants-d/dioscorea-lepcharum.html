Dioscorea lepcharum

Dioscorea lepcharum, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are edible. Its tubers are a source of carbohydrates and are traditionally used in various cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
