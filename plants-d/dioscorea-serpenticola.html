Dioscorea serpenticola

Dioscorea serpenticola, commonly known as the serpent's tail yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic properties.

The species is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its adaptability to various soil conditions.
