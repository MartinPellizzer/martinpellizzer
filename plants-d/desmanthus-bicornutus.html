Desmanthus bicornutus

Desmanthus bicornutus, commonly known as the two-horned desmanthus, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are utilized in forage and wildlife food sources.
