Diospyros ketsensis

Diospyros ketsensis, commonly known as the Ketsen persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a fleshy, sweet-tart berry that ripens in the autumn, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in craftsmanship and furniture making.
