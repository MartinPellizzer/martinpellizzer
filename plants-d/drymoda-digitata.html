Drymoda digitata

Drymoda digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branching, finger-like stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and aiding in survival in harsh environments.

Drymoda digitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
