Dianthus seguieri var. crassipes

Dianthus seguieri var. crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
