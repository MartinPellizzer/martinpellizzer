Dracaena erythrorhachis

Dracaena erythrorhachis, commonly known as the red-veined dracaena, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, variegated leaves with deep red veins against a bright green background. The plant is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Dracaena erythrorhachis is relatively low maintenance, requiring moderate watering and minimal pruning.
