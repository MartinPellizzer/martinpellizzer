Dendrochilum roseum

Dendrochilum roseum, commonly known as the pink-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinct lip that is often more brightly colored.

Dendrochilum roseum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
