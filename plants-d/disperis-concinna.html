Disperis concinna

Disperis concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in semi-arid to arid environments. It is known for its small size and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
