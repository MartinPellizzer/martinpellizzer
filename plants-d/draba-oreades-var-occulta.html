Draba oreades var. occulta

Draba oreades var. occulta, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact rosette of basal leaves, which are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
