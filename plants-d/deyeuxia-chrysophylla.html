Deyeuxia chrysophylla

Deyeuxia chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that display a striking golden hue, especially during the flowering season. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or marshy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are often yellow or reddish in color.

Deyeuxia chrysophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
