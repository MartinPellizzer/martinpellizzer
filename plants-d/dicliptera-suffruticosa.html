Dicliptera suffruticosa

Dicliptera suffruticosa, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its woody base and creeping growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dicliptera suffruticosa is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
