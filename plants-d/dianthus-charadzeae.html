Dianthus charadzeae

Dianthus charadzeae, commonly known as the Georgian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

Dianthus charadzeae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
