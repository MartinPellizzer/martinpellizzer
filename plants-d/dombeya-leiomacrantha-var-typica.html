Dombeya leiomacrantha var. typica

Dombeya leiomacrantha var. typica, commonly known as the silky dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
