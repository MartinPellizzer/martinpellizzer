Donax versicolor

Donax versicolor, commonly known as the variable donax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Donax versicolor is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
