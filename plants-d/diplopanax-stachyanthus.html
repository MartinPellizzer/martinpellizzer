Diplopanax stachyanthus

Diplopanax stachyanthus, commonly known as the red-veined arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically enclosed within a spadix and surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for aquatic gardens and bog plantings.
