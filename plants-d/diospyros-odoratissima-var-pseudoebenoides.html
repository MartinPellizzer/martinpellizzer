Diospyros odoratissima var. pseudoebenoides

Diospyros odoratissima var. pseudoebenoides, commonly known as the fragrant ebony.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic foliage. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and olfactory qualities.
