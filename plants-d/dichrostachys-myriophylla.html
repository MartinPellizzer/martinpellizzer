Dichrostachys myriophylla

Dichrostachys myriophylla, commonly known as the many-leaved burr legume, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
