Draba hispanica var. longistyla

Draba hispanica var. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled Spanish rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are basal and rosette-like, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated stamen, which contributes to its unique botanical identity.
