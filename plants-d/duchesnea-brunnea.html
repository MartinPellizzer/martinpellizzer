Duchesnea brunnea

Duchesnea brunnea, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a distinct yellow coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by kidney-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
