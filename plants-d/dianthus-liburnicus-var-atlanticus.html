Dianthus liburnicus var. atlanticus

Dianthus liburnicus var. atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic campion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers, which bloom in summer and are arranged in dense, showy clusters. The plant has hairy, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
