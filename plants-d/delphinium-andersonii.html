Delphinium andersonii

Delphinium andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and rocky slopes, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features tall, slender stems with palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Delphinium andersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
