Drimia pulchromarginata

Drimia pulchromarginata, commonly known as the Orange-veined African Iris, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, strap-shaped leaves with distinctive orange veins, giving it a vibrant appearance. The plant produces showy, white or pale blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
