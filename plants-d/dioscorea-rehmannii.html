Dioscorea rehmannii

Dioscorea rehmannii, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by cylindrical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The botanical features of D. rehmannii make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
