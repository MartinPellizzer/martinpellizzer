Diospyros myrmecocalyx

Diospyros myrmecocalyx, commonly known as the black sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, green fruits which turn dark purple when ripe. The fruit has a creamy, sweet pulp with a texture similar to a custard, making it a prized fruit in its native region.

It is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and tool-making.
