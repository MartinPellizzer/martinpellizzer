Doliocarpus dentatus subsp. ferrugineus

Doliocarpus dentatus subsp.

ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty-leaf doliocarpus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape.

The leaves are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its foliage.
