Drepanostachyum intermedium

Drepanostachyum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate bamboo, is a grass-like plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces panicles of small, whitish to pale yellow flowers, which are arranged in a distinctive, drooping pattern. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Drepanostachyum intermedium is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
