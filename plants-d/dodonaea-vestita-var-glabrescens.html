Dodonaea vestita var. glabrescens

Dodonaea vestita var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky hop bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are small, elliptical, and often have a bluish-green hue when young. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody pods.

This variety is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it is often used in coastal restoration projects due to its hardiness and erosion control capabilities.
