Diplomorpha japonica

Diplomorpha japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

Diplomorpha japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
