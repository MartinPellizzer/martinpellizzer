Dianthus seidlitzii

Dianthus seidlitzii, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers, which bloom in clusters atop slender stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, adapting well to arid and high-altitude environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Dianthus seidlitzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
