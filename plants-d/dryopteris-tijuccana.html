Dryopteris tijuccana

Dryopteris tijuccana, commonly known as the Tijuana fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi-lobed. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
