Dianthus longifolius

Dianthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Dianthus longifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
