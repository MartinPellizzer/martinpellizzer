Dryopteris leprieurii var. palmensis

Dryopteris leprieurii var. palmensis, commonly known as the palm fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, palm-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a compact, dense appearance that resembles a closed palm.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
