Dendromyza stellata

Dendromyza stellata, commonly known as the star-shaped mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant's unique morphology includes a star-like appearance due to its branching pattern and the arrangement of its floral clusters. It relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent.

Dendromyza stellata is found in various regions of Australia, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
