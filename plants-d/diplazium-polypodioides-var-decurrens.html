Diplazium polypodioides var. decurrens

Diplazium polypodioides var. decurrens, commonly known as the running fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread across the forest floor. The fronds are pinnately compound, with lanceolate to ovate pinnae that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in horticulture.
