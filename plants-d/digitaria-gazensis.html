Digitaria gazensis

Digitaria gazensis, commonly known as the Ghanian digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often hairy, while the inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It plays a role in both ecological systems and traditional land management practices in its native habitats.
