Dicliptera spinulosa

Dicliptera spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny dicliptera, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the tips of the branches. Its fibrous roots are valued for their medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

Dicliptera spinulosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
