Digitalis tubulosa

Digitalis tubulosa, commonly known as the European foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, erect stems with distinctive hooded flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tubular corolla with two lips. Digitalis tubulosa is known for its medicinal properties, containing cardiac glycosides similar to those found in other foxglove species.

It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
