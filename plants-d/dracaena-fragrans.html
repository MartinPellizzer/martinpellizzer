Dracaena fragrans

Dracaena fragrans, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a tropical evergreen plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that bear clusters of vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, and it is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Dracaena fragrans is known for its ability to bloom during the holiday season, making it a popular choice for festive decorations.
