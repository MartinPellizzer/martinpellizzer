Diuris lilacina

Diuris lilacina, commonly known as the lilac spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a compact, low-growing habit with a single, erect stem bearing several hairy leaves. Its flowers are striking, with a distinctive spider-like appearance and a range of purple and pink hues.

Diuris lilacina is known for its ephemeral blooming period, often flowering in spring and early summer.
