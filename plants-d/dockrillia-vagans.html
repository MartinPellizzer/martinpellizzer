Dockrillia vagans

Dockrillia vagans, commonly known as the wandering dockrill, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and flowering habits.

It typically grows in moist, often disturbed habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface, and its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white in color. Dockrillia vagans is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently.
