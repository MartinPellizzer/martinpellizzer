Delphinium exiguum

Delphinium exiguum, commonly known as the dwarf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers are borne in dense spikes at the top of the plant.

Delphinium exiguum is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it blooms in late summer.
