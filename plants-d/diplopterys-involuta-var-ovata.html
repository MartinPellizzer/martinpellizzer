Diplopterys involuta var. ovata

Diplopterys involuta var. ovata, commonly known as the Amazonian starleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

It is often found growing in shaded, humid environments along riverbanks and in dense undergrowth.
