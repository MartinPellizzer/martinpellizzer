Dianthera candelariae

Dianthera candelariae, commonly known as the Candelabra Nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling candles, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals that are slightly notched at the edges.

Dianthera candelariae is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
