Diospyros villosiuscula

Diospyros villosiuscula, commonly known as the silky persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is enjoyed both fresh and in culinary applications.
