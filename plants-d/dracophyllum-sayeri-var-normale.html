Dracophyllum sayeri var. normale

Dracophyllum sayeri var. normale, commonly known as the Sayer's dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces woody capsules as fruits, which contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Dracophyllum sayeri var. normale is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
