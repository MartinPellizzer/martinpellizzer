Draba reptans f. micrantha

Draba reptans f. micrantha, commonly known as the creeping rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, basal leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white hairs. The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra-like environments.

This plant is adapted to cold climates and has a long history of being used in traditional herbal medicine.
