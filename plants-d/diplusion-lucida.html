Diplusion lucida

Diplusion lucida, commonly known as the shining diplusion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall.

Diplusion lucida is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands.
