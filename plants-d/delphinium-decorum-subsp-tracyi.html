Delphinium decorum subsp. tracyi

Delphinium decorum subsp.

tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
