Dioscorea cyclophylla

Dioscorea cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophylla yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are often used as food sources in various cultures. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color.

The tubers of Dioscorea cyclophylla are rich in carbohydrates and have been traditionally used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
