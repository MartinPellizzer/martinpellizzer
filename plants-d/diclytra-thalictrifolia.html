Diclytra thalictrifolia

Diclytra thalictrifolia, commonly known as the thalictrum leaf bug, is a species of leaf beetle belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated body and intricate patterns on its elytra, which are often camouflaged to blend with foliage. The beetle is primarily found in regions with temperate climates, where it feeds on plants from the Ranunculaceae family, particularly thalictrum species. Its life cycle includes several developmental stages, with adults emerging in the spring to mate and lay eggs on host plants.

Due to its specific host preferences and limited distribution, Diclytra thalictrifolia is of interest to entomologists studying plant-insect interactions.
