Darwinia squarrosa

Darwinia squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bottlebrush-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is typically found in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Darwinia squarrosa is a perennial shrub that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
