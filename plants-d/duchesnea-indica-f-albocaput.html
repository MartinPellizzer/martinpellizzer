Duchesnea indica f. albocaput

Duchesnea indica f. albocaput, commonly known as white-leafed wood sorrel, is a variant of the wood sorrel species characterized by its distinctive white to pale green foliage.

This plant is a perennial herb native to North America, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically small, yellow, and borne on slender stalks, adding a delicate touch to the plant's appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
