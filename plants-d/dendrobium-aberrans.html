Dendrobium aberrans

Dendrobium aberrans, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and linear, evergreen leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium aberrans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
