Dicellandra magnifica

Dicellandra magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent dicellandra, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of tubular flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Dicellandra magnifica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its striking appearance and resilience in warm climates.
