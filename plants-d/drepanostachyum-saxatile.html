Drepanostachyum saxatile

Drepanostachyum saxatile, commonly known as the rock bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pinkish or reddish in hue, adding visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form compact, upright clumps that provide structure and texture in garden settings.
