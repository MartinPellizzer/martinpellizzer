Daphniphyllum ceramense

Daphniphyllum ceramense, commonly known as the Ceram laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Pentaphylacaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
