Digitaria affinis

Digitaria affinis, commonly known as common wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, pastures, and open woodlands.

Digitaria affinis is adapted to both wet and dry conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species in its native range.
