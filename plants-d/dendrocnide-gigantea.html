Dendrocnide gigantea

Dendrocnide gigantea, commonly known as the giant stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves covered in dense clusters of stinging hairs. The plant can grow up to 3 meters tall, making it one of the tallest species in the Dendrocnide genus. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The plant's toxic properties make it a notable and sometimes dangerous species in its native habitat.
