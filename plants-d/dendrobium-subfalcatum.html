Dendrobium subfalcatum

Dendrobium subfalcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive dark red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium subfalcatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
