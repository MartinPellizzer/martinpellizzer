Deschampsia laxa

Deschampsia laxa, commonly known as slender feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to North America.

It typically thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, arching stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spikelet that is usually pale green to brown in color.

Deschampsia laxa is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize wetland soils.
