Dalbergia nervosa

Dalbergia nervosa, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds.

Dalbergia nervosa is also known for its slow growth and is often found in tropical rainforests.
