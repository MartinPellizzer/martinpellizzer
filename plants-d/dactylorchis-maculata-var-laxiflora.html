Dactylorchis maculata var. laxiflora

Dactylorchis maculata var. laxiflora, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
