Draba nemorosa var. hebecarpa

Draba nemorosa var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
