Draba altaica var. stellatopilosa

Draba altaica var. stellatopilosa, commonly known as the star-pilose rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and covered in fine, star-shaped hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually white or pale yellow, adding a delicate touch to its rugged environment.
