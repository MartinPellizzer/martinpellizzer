Dichorisandra amabilis

Dichorisandra amabilis, commonly known as the African lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces a basal rosette of dark green, strap-like leaves and has a short, thick rhizome. It blooms in spring and summer, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
