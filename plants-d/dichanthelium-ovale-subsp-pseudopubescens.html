Dichanthelium ovale subsp. pseudopubescens

Dichanthelium ovale subsp.

pseudopubescens, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wet to mesic habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
