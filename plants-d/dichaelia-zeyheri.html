Dichaelia zeyheri

Dichaelia zeyheri, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal flag, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to reddish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Dichaelia zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful appearance.
