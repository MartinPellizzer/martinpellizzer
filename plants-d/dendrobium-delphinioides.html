Dendrobium delphinioides

Dendrobium delphinioides, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and multiple stems that produce leaves and flowers. The plant typically blooms with clusters of blue or violet flowers, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Dendrobium delphinioides prefers shady, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
