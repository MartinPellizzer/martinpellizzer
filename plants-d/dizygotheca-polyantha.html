Dizygotheca polyantha

Dizygotheca polyantha, commonly known as the blue mist flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that resemble mist. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are woody and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
