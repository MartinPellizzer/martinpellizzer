Dichorisandra foliosa

Dichorisandra foliosa, commonly known as the silver leaf cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy bloom, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, stout trunk and produces cones instead of flowers, typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
