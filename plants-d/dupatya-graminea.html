Dupatya graminea

Dupatya graminea, commonly known as the slender grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
