Dendrocnide peltata subsp. murrayana

Dendrocnide peltata subsp.

murrayana, commonly known as the Murray's stinging tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae. It is native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, stinging hairs that can cause severe skin irritation. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various native animals.
