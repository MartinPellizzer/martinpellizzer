Dalea evanescens

Dalea evanescens, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea evanescens is known for its ephemeral nature, with flowers that often fade quickly, giving it its common name.
