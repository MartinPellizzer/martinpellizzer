Dioscorea rubella

Dioscorea rubella, commonly known as the red-stem yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its tubers, which are the edible part, are often used in traditional cuisines.

Dioscorea rubella is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
