Dendrobium serpens

Dendrobium serpens, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, vine-like stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive, snake-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium serpens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
