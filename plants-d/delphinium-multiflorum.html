Delphinium multiflorum

Delphinium multiflorum, commonly known as the Himalayan larkspur, is a tall perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply cut, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution when handling or planting.
