Dendrobium × vinicolor

Dendrobium × vinicolor, commonly known as the orchid with red and white flowers, is a hybrid orchid species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters along its stems. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Dendrobium × vinicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
