Dioscorea deppei

Dioscorea deppei, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The botanical features of Dioscorea deppei make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
