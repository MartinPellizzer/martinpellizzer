Dichocarpum auriculatum var. auriculatum

Dichocarpum auriculatum var. auriculatum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features erect, branched stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. Its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
