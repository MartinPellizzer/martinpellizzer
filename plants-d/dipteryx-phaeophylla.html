Dipteryx phaeophylla

Dipteryx phaeophylla, commonly known as the yellow mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is known for its hard, durable wood, which is highly valued in the timber industry. It typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

The plant also plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
