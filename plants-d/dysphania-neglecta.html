Dysphania neglecta

Dysphania neglecta, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its small, inconspicuous flowers that develop in clusters. The plant produces seeds in a dry, three-angled capsule, which is typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as fields, roadsides, and gardens, due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
