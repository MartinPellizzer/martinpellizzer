Dipterocarpus turbinatus

Dipterocarpus turbinatus, commonly known as the turbinate dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and long, drooping branches. The tree produces distinctive, turbinate-shaped flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The plant plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of tropical rainforests.
