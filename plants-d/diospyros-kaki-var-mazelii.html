Diospyros kaki var. mazelii

Diospyros kaki var. mazelii, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces orange to reddish-brown fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. The fruit is sweet and non-astringent when fully ripe, making it popular for fresh consumption and culinary use. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities, with showy flowers in the spring.
