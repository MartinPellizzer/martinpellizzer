Dietrichia media

Dietrichia media, commonly known as the common dietrichia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, often with a subtle aromatic scent.

Dietrichia media is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
