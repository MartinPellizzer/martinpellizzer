Dioscorea brevipes

Dioscorea brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
