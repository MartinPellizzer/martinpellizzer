Dalbergia spinosa

Dalbergia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small and pale yellow, arranged in terminal clusters. It produces small, round, black fruits that contain a single seed.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.
