Diospyros baumii

Diospyros baumii, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits that resemble dates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
