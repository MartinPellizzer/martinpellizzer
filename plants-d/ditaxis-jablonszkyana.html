Ditaxis jablonszkyana

Ditaxis jablonszkyana, commonly known as the Zambesi lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
