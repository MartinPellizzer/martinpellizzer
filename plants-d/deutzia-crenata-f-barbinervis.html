Deutzia crenata f. barbinervis

Deutzia crenata f. barbinervis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with finely toothed margins and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its delicate appearance and ornamental value in landscaping.
