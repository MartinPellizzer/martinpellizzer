Dendrochilum glumaceum

Dendrochilum glumaceum, commonly known as the glumaceum orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are usually small and arranged in racemes, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
