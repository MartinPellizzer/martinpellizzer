Dupatya vellozioides

Dupatya vellozioides, commonly known as the Vellozia-like daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
