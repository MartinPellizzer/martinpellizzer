Dendrobium pentagonum

Dendrobium pentagonum, commonly known as the pentagon orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cane-like pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium pentagonum is popular among orchid growers for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
