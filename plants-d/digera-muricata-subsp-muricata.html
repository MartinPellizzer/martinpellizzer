Digera muricata subsp. muricata

Digera muricata subsp.

muricata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
