Dactylorhiza integrata

Dactylorhiza integrata, commonly known as the common yellow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a single flowering spike. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, and it requires a moist, well-drained habitat.

Dactylorhiza integrata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
