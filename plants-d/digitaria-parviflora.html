Digitaria parviflora

Digitaria parviflora, commonly known as littleflower buffalograss, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, pale green or brown flowers that are typically clustered in dense panicles. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dryland restoration.

Its deep root system helps prevent soil erosion and supports biodiversity in native grasslands.
