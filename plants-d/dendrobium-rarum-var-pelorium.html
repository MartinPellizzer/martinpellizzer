Dendrobium rarum var. pelorium

Dendrobium rarum var. pelorium, commonly known as the rare orchid of the peloric variety, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
