Dombeya frappierana

Dombeya frappierana, commonly known as the Frappier's dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of white or pink. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Dombeya frappierana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
