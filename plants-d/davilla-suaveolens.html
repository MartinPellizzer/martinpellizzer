Davilla suaveolens

Davilla suaveolens, commonly known as sweet-smelling davisia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
