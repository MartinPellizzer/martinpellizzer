Dichanthium sericeum subsp. polystachyum

Dichanthium sericeum subsp.

polystachyum, commonly known as the silky false grass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia, including India and China. It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaf blades and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
