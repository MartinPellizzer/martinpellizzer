Dioscorea spiculata

Dioscorea spiculata, commonly known as the spiny yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The tubers of Dioscorea spiculata are sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
