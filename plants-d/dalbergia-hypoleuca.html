Dalbergia hypoleuca

Dalbergia hypoleuca, commonly known as the yellow dalbergia, is a species of leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The flowers are typically yellow or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. The tree is valued for its durable, dark-colored wood, which is used in furniture and carving.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
