Dissotis fruticosa

Dissotis fruticosa, commonly known as the red torch plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, woody stems and vibrant red flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually opposite, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

Dissotis fruticosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
