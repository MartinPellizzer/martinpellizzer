Dianthus brachyanthus subsp. hieronymi

Dianthus brachyanthus subsp.

hieronymi, commonly known as the Cape fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a distinctive dark throat.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
