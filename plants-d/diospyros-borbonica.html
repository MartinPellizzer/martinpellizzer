Diospyros borbonica

Diospyros borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a pointed tip. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor.

Diospyros borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
