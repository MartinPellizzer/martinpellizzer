Dissotis arborescens

Dissotis arborescens, commonly known as the tree dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the late afternoon. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dissotis arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
