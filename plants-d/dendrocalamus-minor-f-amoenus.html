Dendrocalamus minor f. amoenus

Dendrocalamus minor f. amoenus, commonly known as the ornamental bamboo, is a cultivar of the giant bamboo genus Dendrocalamus.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by elongated, canoe-shaped fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in ornamental gardening and as a decorative plant in landscapes.
