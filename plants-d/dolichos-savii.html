Dolichos savii

Dolichos savii, commonly known as the Ethiopian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Dolichos savii is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and essential amino acids.

It is often cultivated as a drought-tolerant crop in arid and semi-arid regions.
