Dioclea edulis

Dioclea edulis, commonly known as the ice cream bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a pod that contains edible, sweet, cream-colored seeds often referred to as "ice cream beans.".

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its unique, flavorful seeds used in traditional cuisines.
