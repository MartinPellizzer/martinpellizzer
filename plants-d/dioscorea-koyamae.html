Dioscorea koyamae

Dioscorea koyamae, commonly known as the Koyama yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are valued for their starchy content and traditional use in local cuisine.

The botanical features of D. koyamae make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
