Digera muricata subsp. trinervis

Digera muricata subsp.

trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves with three distinct veins.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Euphorbiaceae family.
