Dorotheanthus ulularis

Dorotheanthus ulularis, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, edible fruits that resemble cherry tomatoes, often encased in a papery husk. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
