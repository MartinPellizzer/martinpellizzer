Dolichos lablab subsp. bengalensis

Dolichos lablab subsp.

bengalensis, commonly known as hyacinth bean, is a leguminous plant native to South Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary preparations. It thrives in warm climates and is commonly grown in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers are typically pale purple or white, adding ornamental value to the plant.
