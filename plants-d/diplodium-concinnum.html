Diplodium concinnum

Diplodium concinnum, commonly known as the slender diplodium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Diplodium concinnum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
