Draba × algida

Draba × algida, commonly known as the alpine winter cress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba × algida is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
