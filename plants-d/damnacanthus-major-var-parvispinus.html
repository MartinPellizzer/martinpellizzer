Damnacanthus major var. parvispinus

Damnacanthus major var. parvispinus, commonly known as the dwarf spinous milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets.

It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy plant in warm climates.
