Distichorchis xanthophaea

Distichorchis xanthophaea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its growth habit is pseudobulbous, with thickened stems that store water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
