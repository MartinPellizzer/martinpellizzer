Dactylophyllum liniflorum

Dactylophyllum liniflorum, commonly known as the threadleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to arid conditions.

Dactylophyllum liniflorum is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
