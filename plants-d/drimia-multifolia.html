Drimia multifolia

Drimia multifolia, commonly known as the African lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces striking white or pale purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Drimia multifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
