Danthonia allenii

Danthonia allenii, commonly known as Allen's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Danthonia allenii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
