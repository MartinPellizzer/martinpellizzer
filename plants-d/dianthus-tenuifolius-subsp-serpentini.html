Dianthus tenuifolius subsp. serpentini

Dianthus tenuifolius subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as the serpentine campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often on slopes and cliffs. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and has a compact growth habit.

Its name refers to its association with serpentine soils, which are rich in minerals but low in nutrients.
