Digitalis purpurea f. glabrescens

Digitalis purpurea f. glabrescens, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a variant of the foxglove plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly wavy margin and a deep green color. This plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and historical use in medicine.
