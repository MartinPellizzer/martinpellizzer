Diplophyllum

Diplophyllum, commonly known as the false holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, with showy, red or yellow bracts that attract pollinators. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and are often found in moist, shaded environments.
