Delphinium bicolor subsp. calcicola

Delphinium bicolor subsp.

calcicola, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
