Disa rungweensis var. rhodesiaca

Disa rungweensis var. rhodesiaca, commonly known as the Rhodesian disa, is a striking orchid species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms during the late summer months, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty and rarity.
