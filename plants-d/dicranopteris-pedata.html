Dicranopteris pedata

Dicranopteris pedata, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 1 meter in length. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant in water while absorbing nutrients.

Dicranopteris pedata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
