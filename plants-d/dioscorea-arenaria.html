Dioscorea arenaria

Dioscorea arenaria, commonly known as the desert yam, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually small and hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The tubers of Dioscorea arenaria are sometimes harvested for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
