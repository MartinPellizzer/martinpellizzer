Devendraea myrtilloides

Devendraea myrtilloides, commonly known as the purple-leafed myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
