Dendrobium tortitepalum

Dendrobium tortitepalum, commonly known as the golden bower orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that often branch into clusters of leaves. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium tortitepalum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
