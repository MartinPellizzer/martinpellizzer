Dypsis baronii

Dypsis baronii, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar trunk and tuft of feathery leaves at the top. The plant features a dense, spiky crown of leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its trunk is often covered in a fibrous, spongy layer called a pith, which helps it store water.

Dypsis baronii is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with minimal water.
