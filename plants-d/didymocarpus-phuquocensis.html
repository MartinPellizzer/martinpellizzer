Didymocarpus phuquocensis

Didymocarpus phuquocensis, commonly known as Phu Quoc date palm, is a species of palm native to the island of Phu Quoc in Vietnam.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible dates that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This species is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ecological and ornamental significance in its native habitat.
