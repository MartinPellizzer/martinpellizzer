Drypetes obanensis

Drypetes obanensis, commonly known as the Obanese ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are often orange-red in color.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory plant in tropical forests.
