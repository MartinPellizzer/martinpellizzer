Daphniphyllum gracile var. gracile

Daphniphyllum gracile var. gracile, commonly known as the silver leaf laurel, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
