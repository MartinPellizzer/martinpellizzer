Desmanthus virgatus var. acuminatus

Desmanthus virgatus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with narrow, acuminate leaflets and small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable forage species.
