Dioscorea glandulosa var. glandulosa

Dioscorea glandulosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The botanical features of this species make it an important plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
