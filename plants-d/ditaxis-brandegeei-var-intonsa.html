Ditaxis brandegeei var. intonsa

Ditaxis brandegeei var. intonsa, commonly known as the Desert Milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pale yellow or orange, with a distinctive tubular shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
