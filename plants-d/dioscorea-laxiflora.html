Dioscorea laxiflora

Dioscorea laxiflora, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
