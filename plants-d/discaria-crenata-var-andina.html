Discaria crenata var. andina

Discaria crenata var. andina, commonly known as the Andean discaria, is a shrub native to South America, particularly the Andean regions.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, bead-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
