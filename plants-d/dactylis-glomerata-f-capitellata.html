Dactylis glomerata f. capitellata

Dactylis glomerata f. capitellata, commonly known as the tufted cocksfoot, is a botanical variant of the common cocksfoot.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and compact inflorescence. This form is often found in grasslands and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are tightly clustered at the ends of the stems.

It is a useful forage plant and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
