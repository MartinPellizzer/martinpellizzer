Dysphania melanocarpa f. leucocarpa

Dysphania melanocarpa f. leucocarpa, commonly known as white chard or silverbeet, is a cultivated variant of the species Dysphania melanocarpa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically pale green to white in color, with a smooth texture. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is closely related to other leafy vegetables such as spinach and beets.

It is often grown for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients like vitamins A and C. This form is prized in culinary traditions for its mild flavor and versatility in cooking.
