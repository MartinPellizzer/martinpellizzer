Dichaea trinitensis

Dichaea trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad violet, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rock crevices. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
