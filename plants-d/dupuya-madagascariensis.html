Dupuya madagascariensis

Dupuya madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species endemic to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, terrestrial growth habit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the dense rainforests of Madagascar.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
