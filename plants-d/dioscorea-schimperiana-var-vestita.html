Dioscorea schimperiana var. vestita

Dioscorea schimperiana var. vestita, commonly known as the white yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are often cultivated for their edible roots, which are rich in starch.

The varietal form 'vestita' is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
