Dalbergia gracilis

Dalbergia gracilis, commonly known as the slender legume, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits.

Dalbergia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
