Davilla rugosa

Davilla rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy texture on its stems and leaves, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Davilla rugosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
