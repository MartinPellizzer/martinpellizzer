Discocactus griseus

Discocactus griseus, commonly known as the gray discocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in a distinctive grayish-blue waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its muted foliage. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Discocactus griseus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
