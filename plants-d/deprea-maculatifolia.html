Deprea maculatifolia

Deprea maculatifolia, commonly known as the spotted leaf deprea, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that are often marked with distinctive spots or streaks. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
