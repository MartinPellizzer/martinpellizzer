Droogmansia megalantha var. longipedicellata

Droogmansia megalantha var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-pedicellate droogmansia, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy inflorescence that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant, colorful bracts that surround the flowering spikes, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are narrow, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
