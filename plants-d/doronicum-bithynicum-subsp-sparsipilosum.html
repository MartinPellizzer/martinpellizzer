Doronicum bithynicum subsp. sparsipilosum

Doronicum bithynicum subsp.

sparsipilosum, commonly known as the sparse-hair mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine, sparse hairs, giving it its subspecies name. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in alpine gardens.
