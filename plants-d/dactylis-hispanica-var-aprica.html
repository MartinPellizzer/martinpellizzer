Dactylis hispanica var. aprica

Dactylis hispanica var. aprica, commonly known as Spanish vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, three-lobed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
