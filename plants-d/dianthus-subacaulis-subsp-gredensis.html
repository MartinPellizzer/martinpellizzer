Dianthus subacaulis subsp. gredensis

Dianthus subacaulis subsp.

gredensis, commonly known as the Gredens rock campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly in the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, often succulent stems and small, clustered flowers.

The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
