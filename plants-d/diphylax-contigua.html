Diphylax contigua

Diphylax contigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved diphylax, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Diphylax contigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
