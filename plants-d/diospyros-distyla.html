Diospyros distyla

Diospyros distyla, commonly known as the small persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like the common ebony. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark red or orange color.
