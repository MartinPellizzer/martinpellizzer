Dioscorea madagascariensis

Dioscorea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan yam, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine due to the presence of various bioactive compounds.
