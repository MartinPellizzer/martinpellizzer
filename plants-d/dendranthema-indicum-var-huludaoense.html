Dendranthema indicum var. huludaoense

Dendranthema indicum var. huludaoense, commonly known as the Huludao chrysanthemum, is a variant of the common chrysanthemum native to northern China.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and dense foliage, which can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
