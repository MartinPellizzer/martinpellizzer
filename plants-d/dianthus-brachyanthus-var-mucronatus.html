Dianthus brachyanthus var. mucronatus

Dianthus brachyanthus var. mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
