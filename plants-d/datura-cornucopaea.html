Datura cornucopaea

Datura cornucopaea, commonly known as the horned owl or sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces distinctive, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Datura cornucopaea is known for its potent alkaloids, which have been used traditionally for medicinal and ritual purposes.
