Deguelia secunda

Deguelia secunda, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
