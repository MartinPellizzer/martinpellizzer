Dysosmia foetida

Dysosmia foetida, commonly known as the stinking sprout, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and a strong, unpleasant odor that attracts certain pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a waxy surface.

Dysosmia foetida is primarily valued for its unique scent and ecological role in its native habitat.
