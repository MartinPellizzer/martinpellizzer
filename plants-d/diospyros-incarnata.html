Diospyros incarnata

Diospyros incarnata, commonly known as the red-berried persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. Its fruits are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications.

Diospyros incarnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
