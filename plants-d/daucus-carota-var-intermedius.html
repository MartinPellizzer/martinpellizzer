Daucus carota var. intermedius

Daucus carota var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate carrot, is a botanical variant of the carrot plant.

It exhibits intermediate characteristics between the wild and domesticated forms of Daucus carota. This variety typically features a shorter, less robust root compared to the standard cultivated carrot. Its leaves are more lobed and less fleshy than those of the common carrot.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climatic conditions.
