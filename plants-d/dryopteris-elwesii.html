Dryopteris elwesii

Dryopteris elwesii, commonly known as Elwes' shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, bipinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The fronds are typically dark green with a glossy texture and are arranged in a compact, upright clump. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
