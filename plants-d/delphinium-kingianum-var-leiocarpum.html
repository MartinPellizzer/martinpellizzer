Delphinium kingianum var. leiocarpum

Delphinium kingianum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the King's delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its striking floral display in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with lobed divisions that give it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained, fertile soils.
