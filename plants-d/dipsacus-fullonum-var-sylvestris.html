Dipsacus fullonum var. sylvestris

Dipsacus fullonum var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild hogweed, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems that are often reddish or purple in color, and it produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has large, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in woodland edges.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
