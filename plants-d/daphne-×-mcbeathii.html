Daphne × mcbeathii

Daphne × mcbeathii, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to the United Kingdom.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to winter gardens, offering visual interest when many other plants are dormant.
