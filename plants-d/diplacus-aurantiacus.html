Diplacus aurantiacus

Diplacus aurantiacus, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers. Its showy, tubular flowers are specifically adapted to attract hummingbirds, which serve as its primary pollinators.

Diplacus aurantiacus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
