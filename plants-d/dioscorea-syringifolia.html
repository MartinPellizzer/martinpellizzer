Dioscorea syringifolia

Dioscorea syringifolia, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a climbing vine native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Dioscorea syringifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its edible tubers.
