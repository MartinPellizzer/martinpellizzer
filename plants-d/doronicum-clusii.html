Doronicum clusii

Doronicum clusii, commonly known as the Clusius' leopard's bane, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central dark spot. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
