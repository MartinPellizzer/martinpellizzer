Diospyros munda

Diospyros munda, commonly known as Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
