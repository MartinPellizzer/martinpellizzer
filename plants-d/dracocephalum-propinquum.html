Dracocephalum propinquum

Dracocephalum propinquum, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, blue to purple flowers appear in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
