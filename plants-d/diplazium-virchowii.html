Diplazium virchowii

Diplazium virchowii, commonly known as the Virchow's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically composed of a fiddlehead and a pinnate structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is creeping and produces roots that help anchor it to the substrate.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
