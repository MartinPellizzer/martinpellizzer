Desmodium metallicum

Desmodium metallicum, commonly known as the silverleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves that often have a silvery or metallic sheen. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, where it can thrive in partial shade.

Desmodium metallicum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural and ecological contexts.
