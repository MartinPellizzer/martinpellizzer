Ditassa divaricata

Ditassa divaricata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically has broad, velvety leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
