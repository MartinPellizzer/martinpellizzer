Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. maculata

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow spot on the lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and damp meadows. Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

maculata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
