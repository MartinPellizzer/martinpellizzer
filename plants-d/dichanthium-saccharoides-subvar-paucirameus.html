Dichanthium saccharoides subvar. paucirameus

Dichanthium saccharoides subvar. paucirameus, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each branch, which is typically small and compact. It thrives in poor soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This plant is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of soil degradation in certain ecosystems.
