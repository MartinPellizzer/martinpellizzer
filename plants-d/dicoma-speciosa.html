Dicoma speciosa

Dicoma speciosa, commonly known as the showy dicoma, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its striking, daisy-like blooms are often pink or purple, with yellow centers, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Dicoma speciosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
