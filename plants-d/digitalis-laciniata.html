Digitalis laciniata

Digitalis laciniata, commonly known as the shredded heart or European foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, ornate appearance, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

The plant is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds.
