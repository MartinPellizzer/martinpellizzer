Daucus carota subsp. cantabricus

Daucus carota subsp.

cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian carrot, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a biennial herb that typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas.

The plant features a taproot that is often smaller and less fleshy compared to the cultivated carrot. Its flowers are white with a purple tinge, arranged in umbels.

The subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
