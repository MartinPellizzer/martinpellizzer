Dissotidendron arborescens

Dissotidendron arborescens, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
