Draba alpina var. legitima

Draba alpina var. legitima, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is a perennial species that blooms in early summer, contributing to the alpine flora's biodiversity.
