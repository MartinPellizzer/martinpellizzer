Dorstenia papillosa var. plethorica

Dorstenia papillosa var. plethorica, commonly known as the woolly false arum, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, white to grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
