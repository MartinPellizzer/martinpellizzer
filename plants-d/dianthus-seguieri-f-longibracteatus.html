Dianthus seguieri f. longibracteatus

Dianthus seguieri f. longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract dianthus, is a compact, mat-forming perennial belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of pink or red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is native to the Mediterranean region. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
