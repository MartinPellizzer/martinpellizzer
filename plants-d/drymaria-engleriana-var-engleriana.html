Drymaria engleriana var. engleriana

Drymaria engleriana var. engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's drymaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
