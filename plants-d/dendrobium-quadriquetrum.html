Dendrobium quadriquetrum

Dendrobium quadriquetrum, commonly known as the four-branch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium quadriquetrum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
