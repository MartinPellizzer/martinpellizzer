Dryopteris gongylodes var. longipinna

Dryopteris gongylodes var. longipinna, commonly known as the long-pinnate wood fern, is a variant of the wood fern species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length, with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a prominent midrib and serrated margins. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
