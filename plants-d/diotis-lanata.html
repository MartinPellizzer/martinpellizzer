Diotis lanata

Diotis lanata, commonly known as the woolly diotis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Diotis lanata is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
