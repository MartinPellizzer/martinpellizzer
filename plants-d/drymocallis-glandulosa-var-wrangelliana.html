Drymocallis glandulosa var. wrangelliana

Drymocallis glandulosa var. wrangelliana, commonly known as Wrangel's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its glandular leaves and robust, branching stems, which contribute to its ecological significance in supporting local biodiversity.
