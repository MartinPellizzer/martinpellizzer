Dryopteris koidzumiana

Dryopteris koidzumiana, commonly known as the Koidzumi shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, deeply lobed fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous sporangia on the underside of the fronds.

The Koidzumi shield fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
