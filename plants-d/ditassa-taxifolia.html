Ditassa taxifolia

Ditassa taxifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf sedum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
