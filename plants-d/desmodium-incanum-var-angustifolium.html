Desmodium incanum var. angustifolium

Desmodium incanum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf desmodium, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in spring, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
