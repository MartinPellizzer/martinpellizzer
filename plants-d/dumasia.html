Dumasia

Dumasia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue or purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. Dumasia thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
