Didymocheton phaeotrichus

Didymocheton phaeotrichus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant and showy appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship.
