Davilla lanosa

Davilla lanosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange to red berries. Its fruit is edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open woodlands.
