Doria concinna

Doria concinna, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Doria concinna is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
