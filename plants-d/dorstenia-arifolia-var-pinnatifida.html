Dorstenia arifolia var. pinnatifida

Dorstenia arifolia var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the featherleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
