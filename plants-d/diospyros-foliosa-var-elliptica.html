Diospyros foliosa var. elliptica

Diospyros foliosa var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
