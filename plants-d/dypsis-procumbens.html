Dypsis procumbens

Dypsis procumbens, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and feathery, compound leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dypsis procumbens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
