Dioscorea batatas f. daikok

Dioscorea batatas f. daikok, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a tuberous perennial plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is characterized by its stout, cylindrical tubers that are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible tubers.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine.
