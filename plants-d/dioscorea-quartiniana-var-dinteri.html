Dioscorea quartiniana var. dinteri

Dioscorea quartiniana var. dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's yam, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its tubers are a source of starch and have been utilized by indigenous communities for nutritional purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
