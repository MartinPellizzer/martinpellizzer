Dimorphanthera antennifera

Dimorphanthera antennifera, commonly known as the long-antennae orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structures that resemble antennae. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
