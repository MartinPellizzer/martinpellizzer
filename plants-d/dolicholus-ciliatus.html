Dolicholus ciliatus

Dolicholus ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy tailed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
