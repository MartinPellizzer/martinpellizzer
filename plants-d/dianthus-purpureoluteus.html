Dianthus purpureoluteus

Dianthus purpureoluteus, commonly known as the purple-lilac campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, while its stems are upright and branched.

Dianthus purpureoluteus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
