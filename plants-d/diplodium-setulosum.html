Diplodium setulosum

Diplodium setulosum, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Diplodium setulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
