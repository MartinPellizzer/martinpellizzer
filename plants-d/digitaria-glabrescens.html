Digitaria glabrescens

Digitaria glabrescens, commonly known as slender bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often described as bottlebrush-like due to their dense, bristly appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer months.

Digitaria glabrescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
