Diasperus brachylobus

Diasperus brachylobus, commonly known as the short-lobed sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Diasperus brachylobus is known for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
