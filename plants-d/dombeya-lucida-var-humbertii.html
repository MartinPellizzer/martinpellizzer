Dombeya lucida var. humbertii

Dombeya lucida var. humbertii, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
