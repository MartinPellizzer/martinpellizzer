Danthonia charruana

Danthonia charruana, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often inconspicuous. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Danthonia charruana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
