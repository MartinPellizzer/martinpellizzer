Dombeya milleri

Dombeya milleri, commonly known as the velvetleaf dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often appearing in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Dombeya milleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
