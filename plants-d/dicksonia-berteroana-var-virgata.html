Dicksonia berteroana var. virgata

Dicksonia berteroana var. virgata, commonly known as the Virgata tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in rainforests or along riverbanks. Its fronds are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which is a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
