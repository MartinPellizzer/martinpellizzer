Durio gratissimus

Durio gratissimus, commonly known as the durian, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiky green husk and strong, often pungent aroma. The fruit contains a creamy, custard-like pulp that is highly prized for its flavor and texture. It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is known for its complex chemical composition, which contributes to its distinctive smell.

The durian is also notable for its high nutritional value and is considered a delicacy in many Asian countries.
