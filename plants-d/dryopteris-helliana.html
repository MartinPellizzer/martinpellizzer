Dryopteris helliana

Dryopteris helliana, commonly known as the Greek wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Greece and Turkey.

It features a robust rhizome system that supports its upright, arching fronds. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dark green, leathery texture and a distinctive, glossy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
