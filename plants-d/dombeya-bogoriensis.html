Dombeya bogoriensis

Dombeya bogoriensis, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest margins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Dombeya bogoriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
