Diplusodon buxifolius var. naudinii

Diplusodon buxifolius var. naudinii, commonly known as the Naudin's boxwood fern, is a rare and unique variant of the boxwood fern species.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage that resembles the appearance of boxwood plants, hence its common name. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and arranged in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
