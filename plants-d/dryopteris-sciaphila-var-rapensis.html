Dryopteris sciaphila var. rapensis

Dryopteris sciaphila var. rapensis, commonly known as the Rapeseed Shield Fern, is a shade-tolerant fern native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant produces spore-bearing sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often found in temperate forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest understories.
