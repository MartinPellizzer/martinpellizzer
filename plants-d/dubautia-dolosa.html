Dubautia dolosa

Dubautia dolosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironwood, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and branching structure. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Dubautia dolosa is adapted to grow in rocky, coastal habitats and is drought-tolerant.

It plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
