Diospyros conocarpa

Diospyros conocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange to red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and economic importance.
