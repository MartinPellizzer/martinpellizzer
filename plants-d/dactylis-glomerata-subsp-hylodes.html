Dactylis glomerata subsp. hylodes

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

hylodes, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, pale green to brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and pastures.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and erosion control capabilities.
