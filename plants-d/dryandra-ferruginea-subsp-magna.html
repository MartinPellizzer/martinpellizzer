Dryandra ferruginea subsp. magna

Dryandra ferruginea subsp.

magna, commonly known as the Geraldton Wax, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, pink or reddish flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat.

The Geraldton Wax is also notable for its ability to thrive in full sun and its role in attracting native pollinators.
