Dorycnium frutescens

Dorycnium frutescens, commonly known as goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a tall, branching stem covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces yellow flowers in late spring. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its long, slender leaves and spiky, club-shaped seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
