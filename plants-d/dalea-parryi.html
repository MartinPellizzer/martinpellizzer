Dalea parryi

Dalea parryi, commonly known as Parry's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert environments. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Dalea parryi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
