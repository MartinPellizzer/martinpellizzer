Dianthus versicolor subsp. turkestanicus

Dianthus versicolor subsp.

turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan pink, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It features showy, bicolor flowers that range from white to pink and red, often with a contrasting central stripe.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
