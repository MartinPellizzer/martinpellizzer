Diplodium robustum

Diplodium robustum, commonly known as the hardy everlasting flower, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen, providing year-round interest.

Diplodium robustum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
