Delphinium chinense

Delphinium chinense, commonly known as Chinese larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the delphinium genus.

Delphinium chinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
