Dimorphotheca dregei var. reticulata

Dimorphotheca dregei var. reticulata, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a distinctive reticulated pattern, giving it its specific epithet "reticulata.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
