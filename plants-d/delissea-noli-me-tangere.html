Delissea noli-me-tangere

Delissea noli-me-tangere, commonly known as the "Don't touch me" plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The name "noli-me-tangere" translates to "do not touch me," referring to its toxic properties when ingested.
