Dalbergia palauensis

Dalbergia palauensis, commonly known as the Palau rosewood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the islands of Palau in the western Pacific.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several flat, rounded seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.
