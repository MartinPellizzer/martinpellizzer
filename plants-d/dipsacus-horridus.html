Dipsacus horridus

Dipsacus horridus, commonly known as the hedgehog flower, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, velvety seed pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
