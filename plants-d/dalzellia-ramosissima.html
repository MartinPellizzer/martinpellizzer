Dalzellia ramosissima

Dalzellia ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy dalzellia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, often hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Dalzellia ramosissima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
