Delphinium virescens var. macroceratilis

Delphinium virescens var. macroceratilis, commonly known as the giant blue delphinium, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense racemes, with each bloom having five petals and prominent spurred sepals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
