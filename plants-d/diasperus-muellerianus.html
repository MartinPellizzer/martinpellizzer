Diasperus muellerianus

Diasperus muellerianus, commonly known as the Mueller's rock fig, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are enclosed in a fleshy, spiky capsule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
