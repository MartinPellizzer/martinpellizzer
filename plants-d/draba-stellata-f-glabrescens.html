Draba stellata f. glabrescens

Draba stellata f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered with fine, soft hairs in the typical species, though this form is glabrous, meaning it lacks the usual pubescence.

The flowers bloom in early spring, producing a delicate appearance that blends well with its alpine environment.
