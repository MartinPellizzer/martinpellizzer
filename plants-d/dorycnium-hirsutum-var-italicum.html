Dorycnium hirsutum var. italicum

Dorycnium hirsutum var. italicum, commonly known as the Italian salsify, is a biennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features a robust taproot that is typically harvested for its edible, slightly sweet and nutty flavor. The plant has compound leaves with a serrated edge and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
