Diospyros leei

Diospyros leei, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, dark purple fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which has a sweet, honey-like flavor.
