Diospyros liberiensis

Diospyros liberiensis, commonly known as the Liberian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and other tropical hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its fruit is a small, yellowish-orange drupe that ripens in late summer.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
