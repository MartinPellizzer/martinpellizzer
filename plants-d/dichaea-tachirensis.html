Dichaea tachirensis

Dichaea tachirensis, commonly known as the Tachira dichaea, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features a slender, hairy stem with several fleshy, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink.

Dichaea tachirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
