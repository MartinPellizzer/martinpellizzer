Drimys winteri var. confertifolia

Drimys winteri var. confertifolia, commonly known as the Chilean winter's bark, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Following flowering, it develops bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
