Dolichos curtisii

Dolichos curtisii, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a climbing legume native to South and Southeast Asia.

It produces elongated, bean-like pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or brown when mature. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is often grown for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Dolichos curtisii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial plant in agricultural systems.
