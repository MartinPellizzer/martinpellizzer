Daemonorops calicarpa

Daemonorops calicarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited palm, is a species of cycad-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
