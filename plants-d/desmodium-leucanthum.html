Desmodium leucanthum

Desmodium leucanthum, commonly known as white-flowered desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a creeping or ascending growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
