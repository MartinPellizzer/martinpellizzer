Dipterocarpus tempehes

Dipterocarpus tempehes, commonly known as the Tempeh tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oblong fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
