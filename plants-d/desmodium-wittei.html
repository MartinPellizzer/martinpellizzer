Desmodium wittei

Desmodium wittei, commonly known as the narrowleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

It has a shallow root system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
