Daucus carota subsp. major

Daucus carota subsp.

major, commonly known as the giant parsnip, is a member of theApiaceae family. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the carrot family. Its root is thick and fleshy, though less commonly consumed than the common carrot.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.
