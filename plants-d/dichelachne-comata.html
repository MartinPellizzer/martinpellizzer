Dichelachne comata

Dichelachne comata, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread aggressively, often forming dense mats that can dominate disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
