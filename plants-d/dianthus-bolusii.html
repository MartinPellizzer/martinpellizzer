Dianthus bolusii

Dianthus bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus's campion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of bright pink or white flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like carnations and pinks.

Dianthus bolusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
