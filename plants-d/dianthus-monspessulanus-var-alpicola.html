Dianthus monspessulanus var. alpicola

Dianthus monspessulanus var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb with distinctive pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features a low-growing habit, with fleshy, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters and are characterized by their five-petaled structure.

Dianthus monspessulanus var. alpicola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
