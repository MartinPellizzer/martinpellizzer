Dactylorhiza × aldenii

Dactylorhiza × aldenii, commonly known as the Alden's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear numerous showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, three-lobed flowers that bloom in late summer.

Dactylorhiza × aldenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
