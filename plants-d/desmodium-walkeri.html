Desmodium walkeri

Desmodium walkeri, commonly known as purple desmodium, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered purple flowers. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and prairies. It has a creeping growth habit with nodules on its roots that host nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Desmodium walkeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
