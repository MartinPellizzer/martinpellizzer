Dolophragma juniperinum

Dolophragma juniperinum, commonly known as the rock juniper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by blue-black berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Dolophragma juniperinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
