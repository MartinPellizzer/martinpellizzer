Draba imeretica

Draba imeretica, commonly known as the Imereti bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, delicate flowers and basal leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at high elevations. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Draba imeretica is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
