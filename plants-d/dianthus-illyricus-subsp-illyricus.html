Dianthus illyricus subsp. illyricus

Dianthus illyricus subsp.

illyricus, commonly known as the Illyrian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink or white flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Dianthus illyricus subsp.

illyricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
