Dicentra formosa subsp. oregona

Dicentra formosa subsp.

oregona, commonly known as the Oregon poppy, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its striking, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in forested areas and along streambanks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
