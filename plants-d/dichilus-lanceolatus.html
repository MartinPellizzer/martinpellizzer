Dichilus lanceolatus

Dichilus lanceolatus, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Dichilus lanceolatus is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and construction.
