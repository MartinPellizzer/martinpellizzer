Diospyros venosa var. venosa

Diospyros venosa var. venosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet and sour flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
