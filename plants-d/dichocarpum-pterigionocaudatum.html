Dichocarpum pterigionocaudatum

Dichocarpum pterigionocaudatum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking yellow-orange flowers in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
