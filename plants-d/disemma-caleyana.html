Disemma caleyana

Disemma caleyana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Disemma caleyana is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
