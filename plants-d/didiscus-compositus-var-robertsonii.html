Didiscus compositus var. robertsonii

Didiscus compositus var. robertsonii, commonly known as the Robertson's composite daisy, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy yellow or orange composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
