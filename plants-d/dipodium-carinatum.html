Dipodium carinatum

Dipodium carinatum, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals that aid in pollination. The plant typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are long and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale pink.

Dipodium carinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
