Dypsis dracaenoides

Dypsis dracaenoides, commonly known as the Areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates with bright, indirect light. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching lengths of up to 2 meters, and are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the top of the plant.

Dypsis dracaenoides is popular as a ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
