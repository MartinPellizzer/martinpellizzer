Dombeya australis subsp. australis

Dombeya australis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian dombeya, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in coastal regions and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dark brown fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
