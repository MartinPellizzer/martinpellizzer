Dysoxylum rubrum

Dysoxylum rubrum, commonly known as the red dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, lowland forest environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface with a strong, aromatic scent.
