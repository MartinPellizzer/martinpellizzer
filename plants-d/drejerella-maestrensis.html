Drejerella maestrensis

Drejerella maestrensis, commonly known as the Maestrense Orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a compact growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. Its roots are highly specialized, forming symbiotic relationships with specific mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several national parks.
