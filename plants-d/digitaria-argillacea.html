Digitaria argillacea

Digitaria argillacea, commonly known as claygrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in moist, clay-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed flowers.

Digitaria argillacea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
