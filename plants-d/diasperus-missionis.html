Diasperus missionis

Diasperus missionis, commonly known as the Mission Desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round seeds enclosed in a capsule. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Diasperus missionis is often found in desert scrub and along rocky outcrops.
