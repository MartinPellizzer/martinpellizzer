Dendrobium hildebrandii

Dendrobium hildebrandii, commonly known as the Hildebrand's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive red or purple lip.

Dendrobium hildebrandii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
