Dysophylla communis

Dysophylla communis, commonly known as the common leafless fern, is a unique plant characterized by its lack of true leaves.

Instead, it possesses specialized structures called rhizomes that anchor it to the soil and absorb nutrients. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are concealed within small, spore-producing capsules.

Due to its primitive features, Dysophylla communis provides valuable insights into the evolutionary development of ferns.
