Diplazium japonicum var. latipes

Diplazium japonicum var. latipes, commonly known as the broadleaf cliff fern, is a variant of the Japanese cliff fern.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in rocky or shaded environments. The plant features fronds that are broad and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This variety is often found in moist, temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
