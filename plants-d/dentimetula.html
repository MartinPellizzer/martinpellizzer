Dentimetula

Dentimetula, commonly known as the toothed fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, tooth-like fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, hairy layer, which helps retain moisture.

Dentimetula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
