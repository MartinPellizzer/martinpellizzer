Dichoropetalum scoparium

Dichoropetalum scoparium, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, wind-pollinated inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine, and the plant is adapted to arid environments with deep taproots.

Dichoropetalum scoparium plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
