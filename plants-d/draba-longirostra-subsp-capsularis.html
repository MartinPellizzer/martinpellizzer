Draba longirostra subsp. capsularis

Draba longirostra subsp.

capsularis, commonly known as the long-beaked whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It is typically found in alpine and arctic environments, growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive elongated floral structure and its adaptation to harsh, cold climates.
