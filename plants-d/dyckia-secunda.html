Dyckia secunda

Dyckia secunda, commonly known as the silver dyckia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences of red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Dyckia secunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
