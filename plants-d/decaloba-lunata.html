Decaloba lunata

Decaloba lunata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant has a climbing habit and produces heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.
