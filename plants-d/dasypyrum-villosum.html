Dasypyrum villosum

Dasypyrum villosum, commonly known as the hairy wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
