Daphne mairei

Daphne mairei, commonly known as the Maire daphne, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged opposite on the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant is also noted for its toxicity, with all parts containing irritants that can cause skin and gastrointestinal reactions.
