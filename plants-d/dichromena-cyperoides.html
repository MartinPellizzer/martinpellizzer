Dichromena cyperoides

Dichromena cyperoides, commonly known as the purple false rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Dichromena cyperoides thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
