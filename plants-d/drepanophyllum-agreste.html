Drepanophyllum agreste

Drepanophyllum agreste, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring.

Drepanophyllum agreste is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a hedge.
