Digitalis sinensis

Digitalis sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces tubular, hood-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Digitalis sinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
