Didymopanax poeppigii

Didymopanax poeppigii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, making it one of the largest leafy plants in the world. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by fruit that develops into a woody capsule containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for its potential medicinal properties.
