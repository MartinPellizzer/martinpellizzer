Duranta penlandii

Duranta penlandii, commonly known as the Penland glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Duranta penlandii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
