Docynia indica

Docynia indica, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, citrus-scented fruits. The plant produces clusters of bright orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

Docynia indica is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
