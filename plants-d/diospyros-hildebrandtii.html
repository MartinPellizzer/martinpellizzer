Diospyros hildebrandtii

Diospyros hildebrandtii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and long, pinnate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is an important food source for both wildlife and humans in its native range.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and tool-making.
