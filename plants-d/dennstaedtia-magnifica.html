Dennstaedtia magnifica

Dennstaedtia magnifica, commonly known as the giant wood fern, is a striking species native to the temperate rainforests of the Pacific Northwest.

Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are arranged in a unique, scattered pattern on the underside of the fronds.
