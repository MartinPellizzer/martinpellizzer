Dicliptera brachiata var. glandulosa

Dicliptera brachiata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular dicliptera, is a tropical grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with prominent glandular structures on the leaf blades, which secrete substances that may deter herbivores. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often hairy or glandular.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
