Didymocarpus poilanei

Didymocarpus poilanei, commonly known as the Malayan wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
