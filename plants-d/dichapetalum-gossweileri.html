Dichapetalum gossweileri

Dichapetalum gossweileri, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has a stout, upright stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
