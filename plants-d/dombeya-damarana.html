Dombeya damarana

Dombeya damarana, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Dombeya damarana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
