Dipterocarpus scortechinii

Dipterocarpus scortechinii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heavy wood and is often found in lowland rainforests. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
