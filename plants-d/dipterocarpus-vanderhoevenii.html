Dipterocarpus vanderhoevenii

Dipterocarpus vanderhoevenii, commonly known as the Sarawak dipterocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and thick, flaky bark. The tree produces heavy, woody fruits that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It plays a vital role in the ecological structure of lowland dipterocarp forests.

Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it an important resource in local economies.
