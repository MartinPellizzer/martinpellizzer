Desmodium sericeum

Desmodium sericeum, commonly known as silverleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its silky, hairy leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or ornamental plant.
