Disa subaequalis

Disa subaequalis, commonly known as the pink disa, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of striking pink or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms during the late summer and early autumn months. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Disa subaequalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade in its natural habitat.
