Diasperus kirkianus

Diasperus kirkianus, commonly known as the Kirk's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Diasperus kirkianus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
