Dendrobium moniliforme f. alboviolaceum

Dendrobium moniliforme f. alboviolaceum, commonly known as the lavender-lavender dendrobium, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences and flowers that range in color from white to lavender-pink. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and striking floral display.
