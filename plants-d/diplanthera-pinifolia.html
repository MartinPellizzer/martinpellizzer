Diplanthera pinifolia

Diplanthera pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it a resemblance to pine needles, hence its common name.

The bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination.
