Dorotheanthus luteus

Dorotheanthus luteus, commonly known as the yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features include opposite, hairy leaves and a compact growth habit.
