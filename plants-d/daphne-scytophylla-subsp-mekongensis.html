Daphne scytophylla subsp. mekongensis

Daphne scytophylla subsp.

mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong daphne, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Thymelaeaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be toxic to animals and humans.
