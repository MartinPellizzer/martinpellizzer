Dianthus planellae var. tenuis

Dianthus planellae var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender rock campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with compact, succulent stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are fleshy and typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
