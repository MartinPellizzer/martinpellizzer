Dobera roxburghii

Dobera roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red or orange fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Dobera roxburghii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
