Dianella philippensis

Dianella philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine flax or blue flax, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

Dianella philippensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
