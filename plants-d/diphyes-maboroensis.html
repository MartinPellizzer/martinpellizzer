Diphyes maboroensis

Diphyes maboroensis, commonly known as the Maboro sandveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential forage resource.
