Draba cacuminum

Draba cacuminum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, typically growing in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. It is well adapted to cold climates and can survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Draba cacuminum is often found at high elevations and is considered an indicator species for alpine ecosystems.
