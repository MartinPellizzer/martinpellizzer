Dianthus urziceniensis

Dianthus urziceniensis, commonly known as the Urziceni carnation, is a flowering plant native to Romania.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
