Duranta canescens

Duranta canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually blue or purple, appearing in summer and attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.
