Dennstaedtia parksii

Dennstaedtia parksii, commonly known as the Park's water fern, is a species of polypody fern native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bright green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
