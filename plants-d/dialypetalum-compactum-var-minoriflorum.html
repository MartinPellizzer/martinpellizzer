Dialypetalum compactum var. minoriflorum

Dialypetalum compactum var. minoriflorum, commonly known as the small-flower compact daisy, is a flowering plant characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
