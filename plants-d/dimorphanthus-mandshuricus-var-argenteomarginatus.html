Dimorphanthus mandshuricus var. argenteomarginatus

Dimorphanthus mandshuricus var. argenteomarginatus, commonly known as the silver-margin dimorphanthus, is a variant of the dimorphanthus plant native to the Far East.

It is characterized by its striking silver-edged leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its delicate, fern-like foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for horticultural collections.
