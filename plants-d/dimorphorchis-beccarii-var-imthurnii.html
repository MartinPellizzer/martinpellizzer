Dimorphorchis beccarii var. imthurnii

Dimorphorchis beccarii var. imthurnii, commonly known as the Imthurn's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with a unique structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is appreciated by horticulturists for its beauty and rarity.
