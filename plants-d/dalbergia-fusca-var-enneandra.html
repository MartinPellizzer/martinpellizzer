Dalbergia fusca var. enneandra

Dalbergia fusca var. enneandra, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood variant, is a species of leguminous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pea-like flowers that are often white or pale pink.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is highly sought after in the musical instrument and furniture industries.
