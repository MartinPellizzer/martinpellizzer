Decaspermum prostratum

Decaspermum prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate decasperm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a slightly hairy texture.

Decaspermum prostratum is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to colonize disturbed or arid environments.
