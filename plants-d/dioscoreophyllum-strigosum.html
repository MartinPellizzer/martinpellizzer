Dioscoreophyllum strigosum

Dioscoreophyllum strigosum, commonly known as the African yam vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible tuberous roots that are similar to yams. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional agriculture and local diets.
