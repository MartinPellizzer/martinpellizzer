Dendrobium lumakuense

Dendrobium lumakuense, commonly known as the Lumakuense orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dendrobium genus, which is characterized by its terrestrial or epiphytic growth habit and often showy floral displays. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with red or orange markings, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
