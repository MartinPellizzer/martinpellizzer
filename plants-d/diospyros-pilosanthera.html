Diospyros pilosanthera

Diospyros pilosanthera, commonly known as the silky persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
