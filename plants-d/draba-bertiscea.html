Draba bertiscea

Draba bertiscea, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. This small annual herb typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cold, high-altitude conditions. Its flowers are small and white, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Draba bertiscea is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
