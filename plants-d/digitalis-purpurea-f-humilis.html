Digitalis purpurea f. humilis

Digitalis purpurea f. humilis, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or pink, with a distinctive spurred shape at the base. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
