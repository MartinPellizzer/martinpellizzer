Dendrophorbium barkleyanum

Dendrophorbium barkleyanum, commonly known as the Barkley's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also deriving some of its own nutrients from photosynthesis using the limited light it receives. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions where its host trees thrive.

Its unique relationship with host plants makes it an interesting subject for ecological studies.
