Danthonia pilosa var. bonthainica

Danthonia pilosa var. bonthainica, commonly known as the hairy danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to parts of eastern Australia.

It features tufted, hairy leaves that are often curved at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland margins.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various native fauna.
