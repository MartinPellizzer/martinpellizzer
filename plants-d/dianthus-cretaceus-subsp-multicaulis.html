Dianthus cretaceus subsp. multicaulis

Dianthus cretaceus subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the multicaulis campion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers, typically with five petals, during the spring and summer months. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
