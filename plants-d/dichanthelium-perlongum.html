Dichanthelium perlongum

Dichanthelium perlongum, commonly known as the longawn panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spikelet that is often drooping or spreading.

Dichanthelium perlongum plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
