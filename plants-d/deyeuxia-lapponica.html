Deyeuxia lapponica

Deyeuxia lapponica, commonly known as the Arctic flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as tundra and mountain meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in nutrient-poor soils.
