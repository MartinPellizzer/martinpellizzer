Dyckia boliviensis

Dyckia boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian dyckia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to its appearance. Dyckia boliviensis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and container cultivation.
