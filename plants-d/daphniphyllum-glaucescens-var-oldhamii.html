Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. oldhamii

Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. oldhamii, commonly known as the Oldham's silverleaf daphniphyllum, is a species native to China.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
