Drimys brasiliensis subsp. subalpina

Drimys brasiliensis subsp.

subalpina, commonly known as the Andean drimys, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae. It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador.

The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries.

This subspecies is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at elevations above 2,000 meters.
