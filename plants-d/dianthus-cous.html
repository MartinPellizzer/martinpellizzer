Dianthus cous

Dianthus cous, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and has a compact, bushy appearance. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

Dianthus cous is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
