Durio testudinarius var. pinangianus

Durio testudinarius var. pinangianus, commonly known as the Pinang Durian, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, spiky husk and fragrant, creamy flesh. The plant produces its fruit in clusters, with each fruit containing a single large seed. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The Pinang Durian is particularly noted for its unique flavor and is highly valued in local cuisines and markets.
