Dichocarpum dicarpon

Dichocarpum dicarpon, commonly known as the two-seed barbed wire vine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-seed pods that are covered in barbed hairs, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Dichocarpum dicarpon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
