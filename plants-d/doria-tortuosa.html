Doria tortuosa

Doria tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted doria, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its helical twisting growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, pale yellow flowers. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, and it often grows along forest floors or in shaded areas.

Doria tortuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
