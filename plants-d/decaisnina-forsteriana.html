Decaisnina forsteriana

Decaisnina forsteriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are borne on cylindrical inflorescences.

Decaisnina forsteriana is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique texture and adaptability to humid conditions.
