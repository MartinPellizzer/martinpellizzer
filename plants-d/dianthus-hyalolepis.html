Dianthus hyalolepis

Dianthus hyalolepis, commonly known as the glass-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are narrow and often appear translucent or glassy, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and modest blooms.
