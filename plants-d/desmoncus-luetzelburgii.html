Desmoncus luetzelburgii

Desmoncus luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's coralroot, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a myco-heterotrophic plant, meaning it lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on fungal associations for its nutrition. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in a spikelike inflorescence. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with specific fungi, which supply the plant with essential nutrients.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
