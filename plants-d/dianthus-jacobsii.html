Dianthus jacobsii

Dianthus jacobsii, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink or red, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in small, capsule-like structures.

Dianthus jacobsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
