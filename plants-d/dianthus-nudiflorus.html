Dianthus nudiflorus

Dianthus nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflower pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Dianthus nudiflorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
