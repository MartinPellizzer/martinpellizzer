Dendrosicus macrophyllus

Dendrosicus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leafed tree fern, is a distinctive member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a unique growth habit, with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor itself in moist soil.

Its fronds are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in controlled environments.
