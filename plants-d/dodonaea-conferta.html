Dodonaea conferta

Dodonaea conferta, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, leathery leaves that are typically silver-white in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Dodonaea conferta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
