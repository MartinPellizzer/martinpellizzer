Dendrocnide Nervosa

Dendrocnide nervosa is a strikingly beautiful yet highly toxic plant native to Australia. Known for its intricate leaf patterns and vibrant green foliage, it captivates botanists and nature enthusiasts alike. Despite its aesthetic appeal, it contains potent neurotoxins that can cause severe skin irritation and even systemic reactions in humans. Often referred to as the "stinging tree," it plays a crucial role in its ecosystem as both a food source and a habitat provider. Its unique characteristics make it a subject of both scientific study and caution in the wild.
