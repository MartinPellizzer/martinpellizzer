Diplotaxis harra subvar. webbiana

Diplotaxis harra subvar. webbiana, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.
