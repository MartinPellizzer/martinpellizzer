Duggena bracteosa

Duggena bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Duggena bracteosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
