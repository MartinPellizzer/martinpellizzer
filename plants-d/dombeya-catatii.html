Dombeya catatii

Dombeya catatii, commonly known as the mountain dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Dombeya catatii is relatively cold-hardy and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions once established.
