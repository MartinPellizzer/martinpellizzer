Dilkea ulei

Dilkea ulei, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss.

Dilkea ulei plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
