Dianthus hispanicus var. tarraconensis

Dianthus hispanicus var. tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconensis campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
