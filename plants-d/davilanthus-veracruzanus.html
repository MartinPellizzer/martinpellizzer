Davilanthus veracruzanus

Davilanthus veracruzanus, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy blooms during the summer months.
