Dalbergia arborea

Dalbergia arborea, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The tree is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in traditional medicine.
