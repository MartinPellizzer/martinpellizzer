Dispara

Dispara, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and elusive plant native to the remote highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to glow under certain light conditions. The plant has a short blooming period, typically during the late evening, which contributes to its mysterious reputation. Its botanical structure includes a slender stem and small, inconspicuous leaves.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
