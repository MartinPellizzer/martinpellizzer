Daphne mezereum var. albida

Daphne mezereum var. albida, commonly known as white mezereum, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value lies in its early blooming and distinctive foliage.
