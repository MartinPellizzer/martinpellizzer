Disperis similis

Disperis similis, commonly known as the African orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

Disperis similis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
