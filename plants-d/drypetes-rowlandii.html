Drypetes rowlandii

Drypetes rowlandii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves in the spring. The tree has smooth, grey bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in its native habitat. Its leaves are opposite and simple, with a glossy green surface that turns yellow in the dry season.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
