Dartus

Dartus, commonly known as the "dwarf lily," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a faint fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Dartus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
