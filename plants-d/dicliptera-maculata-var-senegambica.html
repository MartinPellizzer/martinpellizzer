Dicliptera maculata var. senegambica

Dicliptera maculata var. senegambica, commonly known as the Senegambian grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered with small, hairy structures. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration along the midribs.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, three-lobed seed capsules.
