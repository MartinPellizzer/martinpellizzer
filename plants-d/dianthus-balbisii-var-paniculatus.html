Dianthus balbisii var. paniculatus

Dianthus balbisii var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers with a distinctive frilly appearance. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for garden decoration and as a cut flower.
