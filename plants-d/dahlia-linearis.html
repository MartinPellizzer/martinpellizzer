Dahlia linearis

Dahlia linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in a wide range of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Dahlia linearis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
