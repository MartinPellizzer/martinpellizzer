Dioscorea orthogoneura var. meiapontensis

Dioscorea orthogoneura var. meiapontensis, commonly known as the Meiapontensis yam, is a variant of the yam species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tuberous perennial plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae, characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its tubers are edible, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful processing before consumption.

This variety is valued for both its culinary potential and its role in traditional medicine.
