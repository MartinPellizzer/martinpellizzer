Deyeuxia arundinacea var. latifolia

Deyeuxia arundinacea var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf reed, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, flat leaves that are often used in traditional papermaking. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
