Dryopetalon crenatum var. racemosum

Dryopetalon crenatum var. racemosum, commonly known as the racemose bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with crenate or serrate margins. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in racemes, giving the plant its specific variety name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
