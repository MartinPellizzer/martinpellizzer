Diospyros martabanica

Diospyros martabanica, commonly known as the Martaban persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ridged bark and alternate, glossy dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruits are typically harvested when fully ripe, and the tree is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.

Diospyros martabanica is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to tropical climates.
