Diospyros inconstans subsp. darienk

Diospyros inconstans subsp.

darienk, commonly known as the Darienk persimmon, is a species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is notable for its high tannin content, which requires a curing process before consumption.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
