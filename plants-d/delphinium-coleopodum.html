Delphinium coleopodum

Delphinium coleopodum, commonly known as the narrowleaf larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. Delphinium coleopodum is known for its toxicity, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to both humans and livestock.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
