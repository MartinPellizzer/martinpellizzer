Dypsis lucubensis

Dypsis lucubensis, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Madagascar.

It is a small, compact tree with a distinctive dark green trunk and vibrant, colorful crown of leaves. The plant features pinnate leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a unique visual appearance. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

Dypsis lucubensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
