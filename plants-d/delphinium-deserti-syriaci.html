Delphinium deserti-syriaci

Delphinium deserti-syriaci, commonly known as the Syrian larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, typical of the genus Delphinium.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking appearance.
