Diplazium palmense

Diplazium palmense, commonly known as the palm fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dicksoniaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a central trunk and a crown of compound leaves. The fronds are typically divided into segments, giving the plant a distinctive, palm-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
