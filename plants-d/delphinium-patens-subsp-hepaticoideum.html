Delphinium patens subsp. hepaticoideum

Delphinium patens subsp.

hepaticoideum, commonly known as the liverleaf larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, with a distinctive basal rosette formation. This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also noted for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
