Dinebra decipiens subsp. decipiens

Dinebra decipiens subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves that resemble a hand, hence its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
