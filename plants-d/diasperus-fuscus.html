Diasperus fuscus

Diasperus fuscus, commonly known as the brown-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, brownish flowers, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Diasperus fuscus is a hardy plant that is well adapted to arid environments.
