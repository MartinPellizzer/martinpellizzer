Dendrobium sandsii

Dendrobium sandsii, commonly known as the sand's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces a cluster of pseudobulbs. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dendrobium sandsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
