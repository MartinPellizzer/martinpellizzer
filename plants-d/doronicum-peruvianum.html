Doronicum peruvianum

Doronicum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent black spots, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Doronicum peruvianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
