Daucus officinalis

Daucus officinalis, commonly known as the wild carrot or Queen Anne's lace, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces delicate, white flower clusters with a single red or purple flower in the center. Its seeds are small, oval, and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The entire plant has a mild, carroty aroma and is often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
