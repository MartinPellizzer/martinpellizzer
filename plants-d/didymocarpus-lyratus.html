Didymocarpus lyratus

Didymocarpus lyratus, commonly known as the lyreleaf tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble a lyre, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential economic uses.
