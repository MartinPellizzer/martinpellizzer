Delphinium malabaricum var. ghaticum

Delphinium malabaricum var. ghaticum, commonly known as the Ghatic delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
