Diospyros ferrea var. hillebrandii

Diospyros ferrea var. hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a dark green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that are edible.

The species is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
