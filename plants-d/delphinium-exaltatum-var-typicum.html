Delphinium exaltatum var. typicum

Delphinium exaltatum var. typicum, commonly known as the tall larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence bearing clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
