Diospyros nigerica

Diospyros nigerica, commonly known as the black ebony tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, round fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface.

Diospyros nigerica thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
