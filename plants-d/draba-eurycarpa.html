Draba eurycarpa

Draba eurycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal and linear, while its flowers are white or pale yellow, appearing in early spring.

Draba eurycarpa is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat.
