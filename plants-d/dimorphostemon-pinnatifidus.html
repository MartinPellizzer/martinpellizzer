Dimorphostemon pinnatifidus

Dimorphostemon pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dimorphostemon, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply divided. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forest or heathland environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly observed in its natural habitat.
