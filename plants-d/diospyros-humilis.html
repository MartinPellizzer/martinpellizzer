Diospyros humilis

Diospyros humilis, commonly known as the dwarf persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, edible fruits. Diospyros humilis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
