Dalea melantha var. cineracea

Dalea melantha var. cineracea, commonly known as silverleaf prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, gray-green leaves that have a silvery appearance. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes and are usually pink to purple in color.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
