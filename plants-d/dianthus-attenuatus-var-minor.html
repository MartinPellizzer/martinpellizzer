Dianthus attenuatus var. minor

Dianthus attenuatus var. minor, commonly known as the slender campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its adaptability to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
