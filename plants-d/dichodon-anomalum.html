Dichodon anomalum

Dichodon anomalum, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Dichodon anomalum is often found growing in rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, which contributes to its survival in its natural habitat.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a notable species in botanical studies of xerophytic plants.
