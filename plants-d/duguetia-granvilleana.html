Duguetia granvilleana

Duguetia granvilleana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
