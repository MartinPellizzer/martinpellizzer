Delphinium sarcophyllum

Delphinium sarcophyllum, commonly known as the rock delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance.

Delphinium sarcophyllum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.
