Dipsacus inermis

Dipsacus inermis, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Dipsacus inermis is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
