Dortmanna doniana

Dortmanna doniana, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, wheel-like inflorescences. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often forming dense mats on the water's surface.

Dortmanna doniana plays a role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for aquatic life.
