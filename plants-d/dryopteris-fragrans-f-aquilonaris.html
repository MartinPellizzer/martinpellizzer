Dryopteris fragrans f. aquilonaris

Dryopteris fragrans f. aquilonaris, commonly known as the fragrant wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It features a tall, slender rhizome with a distinctive, fragrant aroma, particularly noticeable when the fronds are crushed. The plant produces spore-bearing fiddleheads that are often cultivated for their ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Asia and North America.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and woodland landscapes.
