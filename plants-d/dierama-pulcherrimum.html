Dierama pulcherrimum

Dierama pulcherrimum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems topped with vibrant purple or pink bracts that resemble a coneflower. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in some climates.

Dierama pulcherrimum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
