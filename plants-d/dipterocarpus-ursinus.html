Dipterocarpus ursinus

Dipterocarpus ursinus, commonly known as the tiger tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy seeds and thick, aromatic wood. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It plays an important role in the ecosystems of tropical forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The bark and leaves of Dipterocarpus ursinus contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
