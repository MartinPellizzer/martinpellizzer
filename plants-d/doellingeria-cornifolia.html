Doellingeria cornifolia

Doellingeria cornifolia, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
