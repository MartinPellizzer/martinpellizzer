Dioscorea similis

Dioscorea similis, commonly known as the similar yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are typically harvested after a growing season of several months and require careful processing to remove toxic compounds.

Dioscorea similis is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
