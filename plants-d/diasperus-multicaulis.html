Diasperus multicaulis

Diasperus multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its multiple stems that grow close to the ground, forming a dense mat-like structure. The plant produces small, spiny flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Diasperus multicaulis is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
