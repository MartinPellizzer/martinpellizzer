Dryopteris Foeniculacea

Dryopteris foeniculacea, commonly known as the fennel fern, is a striking evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States. Its unique appearance features feathery, bright green fronds that resemble the leaves of the fennel plant, giving it its common name. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories where it contributes to the rich biodiversity of its habitat. With its elegant form and adaptability, it is a popular choice for both wild gardens and cultivated landscapes. Its presence highlights the beauty and ecological importance of native plant species in temperate forests.
