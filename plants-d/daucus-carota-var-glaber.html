Daucus carota var. glaber

Daucus carota var. glaber, commonly known as the wild carrot, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and a hollow, ridged stem. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a compound umbel, and bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, dry fruits called mericarps, which contain the seeds.

It is native to Europe and western Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.
