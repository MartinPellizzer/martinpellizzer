Dendropanax intercedens

Dendropanax intercedens, commonly known as the golden star sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces distinctive, golden-yellow flower spikes in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a smooth, glossy texture.

Dendropanax intercedens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
