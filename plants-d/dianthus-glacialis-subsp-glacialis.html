Dianthus glacialis subsp. glacialis

Dianthus glacialis subsp.

glacialis, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Dianthus glacialis subsp.

glacialis is often found in mountain meadows and is a hardy species that can tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
