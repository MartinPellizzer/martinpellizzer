Dichapetalum stipulatum

Dichapetalum stipulatum, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with stipules. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
