Dasyochloa kurtziana

Dasyochloa kurtziana, commonly known as Kurtz's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, blade-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both florets and sterile lemmas. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
