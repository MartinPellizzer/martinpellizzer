Daphne cneorum f. arbusculoides

Daphne cneorum f. arbusculoides, commonly known as the dwarf spurge laurel, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, glossy dark green foliage with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white during late winter to early spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
