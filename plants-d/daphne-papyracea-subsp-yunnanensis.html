Daphne papyracea subsp. yunnanensis

Daphne papyracea subsp.

yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cooler climates.
