Dipterocarpus oblongus

Dipterocarpus oblongus, commonly known as the broad-leafed dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, oblong leaves and prominent buttress roots. The tree produces heavy, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, orange aril. It plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Due to its economic value, it is also an important timber species in the region.
