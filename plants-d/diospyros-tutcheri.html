Diospyros tutcheri

Diospyros tutcheri, commonly known as the Tutcher's persimmon, is a small deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange to reddish berry that ripens in the fall.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited commercial use due to its small size and fruit characteristics.
