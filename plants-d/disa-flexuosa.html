Disa flexuosa

Disa flexuosa, commonly known as the spiral disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

This plant is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and elegant appearance. It produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Disa flexuosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
