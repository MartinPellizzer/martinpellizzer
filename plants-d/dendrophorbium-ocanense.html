Dendrophorbium ocanense

Dendrophorbium ocanense, commonly known as the Ocanese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on specific host trees in its native habitat.

It has small, leathery leaves and produces white, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various bird species. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid forest environments.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important component of the ecosystems where it thrives.
