Deschampsia pamirica

Deschampsia pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil in harsh mountainous terrain.

Deschampsia pamirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
