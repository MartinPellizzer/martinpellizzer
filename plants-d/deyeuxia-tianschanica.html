Deyeuxia tianschanica

Deyeuxia tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan false wheat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including the Tianshan Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Deyeuxia tianschanica is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological significance and potential for use in restoration projects.
