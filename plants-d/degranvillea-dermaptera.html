Degranvillea dermaptera

Degranvillea dermaptera, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces showy, orchid-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in hue. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
