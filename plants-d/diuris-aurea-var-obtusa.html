Diuris aurea var. obtusa

Diuris aurea var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed sun orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a single, erect stem bearing several grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or golden in color, with a distinctive labellum that is broader than the other petals.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
