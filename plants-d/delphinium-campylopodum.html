Delphinium campylopodum

Delphinium campylopodum, commonly known as the narrowleaf larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
