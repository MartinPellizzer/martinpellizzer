Desmodium ciliatum

Desmodium ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy desmodium, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically pink or purple. It grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Desmodium ciliatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
