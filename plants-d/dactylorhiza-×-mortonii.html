Dactylorhiza × mortonii

Dactylorhiza × mortonii, commonly known as the Morton's marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems bearing several lance-shaped leaves. The plant flowers in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers with three sepals and three petals. It thrives in wet, boggy habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
