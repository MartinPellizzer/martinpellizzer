Diosma fragrans

Diosma fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant diosma, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Diosma fragrans is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
