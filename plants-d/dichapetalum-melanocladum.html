Dichapetalum melanocladum

Dichapetalum melanocladum, commonly known as the blackclad holly, is a unique evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and have a distinct, almost waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that persist into the winter. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a compact, dense growth habit.

It is often found in moist, acidic soils in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
