Dombeya somanga

Dombeya somanga, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.

Dombeya somanga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique floral display.
