Draba fladnizensis var. corymbosa

Draba fladnizensis var. corymbosa, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing adaptations to harsh alpine environments. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

The variety corymbosa is distinguished by its dense, corymbose inflorescence, which gives it a compact, rounded appearance.
