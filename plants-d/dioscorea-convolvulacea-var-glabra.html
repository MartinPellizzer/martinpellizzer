Dioscorea convolvulacea var. glabra

Dioscorea convolvulacea var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.

The tubers are typically harvested for their nutritional and medicinal properties.
