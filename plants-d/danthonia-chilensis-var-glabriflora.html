Danthonia chilensis var. glabriflora

Danthonia chilensis var. glabriflora, commonly known as the Chilean hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, scale-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
