Diospyros fuscovelutina

Diospyros fuscovelutina, commonly known as the velvet-leaf persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible fruits that range in color from yellow to orange-red. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
