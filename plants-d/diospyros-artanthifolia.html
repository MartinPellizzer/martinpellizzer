Diospyros artanthifolia

Diospyros artanthifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Diospyros artanthifolia is often found in lowland forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
