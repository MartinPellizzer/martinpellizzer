Digitaria tristachya

Digitaria tristachya, commonly known as wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the warm temperate and tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields, roadsides, and sandy soils. It is often found in grasslands and has a low-growing, creeping habit.

Digitaria tristachya is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
