Delphinium buschianum

Delphinium buschianum, commonly known as the Buschian larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Delphinium buschianum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
