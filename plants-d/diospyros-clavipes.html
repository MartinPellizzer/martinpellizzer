Diospyros clavipes

Diospyros clavipes, commonly known as the small persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically eaten by wildlife and have a sweet, sometimes astringent taste.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland habitats.
