Dischidia retusa

Dischidia retusa, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by elliptical, red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Dischidia retusa is also noted for its ability to climb using aerial roots, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
