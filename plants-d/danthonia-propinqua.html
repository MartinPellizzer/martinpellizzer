Danthonia propinqua

Danthonia propinqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and prairies. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, narrow leaves with a fine texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Danthonia propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
