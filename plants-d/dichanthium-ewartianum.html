Dichanthium ewartianum

Dichanthium ewartianum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in dry to moderately moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
