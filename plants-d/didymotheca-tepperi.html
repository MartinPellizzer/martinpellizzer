Didymotheca tepperi

Didymotheca tepperi, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most striking feature is the bright yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Didymotheca tepperi is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands.
