Dracaena cylindrica

Dracaena cylindrica, commonly known as the ribbon plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by red or orange berries.

Dracaena cylindrica is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
