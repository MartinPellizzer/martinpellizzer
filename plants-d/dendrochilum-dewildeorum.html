Dendrochilum dewildeorum

Dendrochilum dewildeorum, commonly known as the Dewilde's tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dendrochilum dewildeorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
