Dendropanax grandis

Dendropanax grandis, commonly known as the giant aralia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Dendropanax grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
