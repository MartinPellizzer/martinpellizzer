Dissotidendron cordatum

Dissotidendron cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported health benefits.
