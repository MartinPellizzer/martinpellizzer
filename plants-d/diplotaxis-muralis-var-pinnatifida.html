Diplotaxis muralis var. pinnatifida

Diplotaxis muralis var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems that often grow along rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
