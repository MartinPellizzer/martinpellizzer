Dracaena burmanica

Dracaena burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese dragon tree, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial evergreen shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its stiff, fibrous leaves that are often arranged in whorls, and its distinctive red sap, which is produced when the stems are cut. Dracaena burmanica thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
