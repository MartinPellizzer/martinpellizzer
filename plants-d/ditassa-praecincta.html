Ditassa praecincta

Ditassa praecincta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
