Dendrobium sayeri

Dendrobium sayeri, commonly known as the Sayer's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces a cluster of fleshy, leaf-like structures. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium sayeri is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
