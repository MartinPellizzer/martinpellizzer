Dalbergia kurzii var. truncata

Dalbergia kurzii var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The wood of this species is highly valued for its rich color and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship. It typically grows in tropical lowland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
