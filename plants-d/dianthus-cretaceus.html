Dianthus cretaceus

Dianthus cretaceus, commonly known as the Crete carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to dry, rocky soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Dianthus cretaceus is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal and arid environments.
