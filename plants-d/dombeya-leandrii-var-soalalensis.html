Dombeya leandrii var. soalalensis

Dombeya leandrii var. soalalensis, commonly known as the Soalalensis Dombeya, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant features opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
