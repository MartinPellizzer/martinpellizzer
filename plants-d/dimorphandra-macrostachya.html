Dimorphandra macrostachya

Dimorphandra macrostachya, commonly known as the large-leafed sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The tree produces showy, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruits are woody pods that contain several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation projects due to its adaptability.
