Dendrobium truncicola

Dendrobium truncicola, commonly known as the black root orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and upright, branched stems that often bear clusters of small, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers with a distinctive red lip, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
