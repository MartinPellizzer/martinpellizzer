Distemonanthus

Distemonanthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white. The leaves are typically velvety and heart-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms. Distemonanthus species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish.
