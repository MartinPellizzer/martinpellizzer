Diospyros hayatae

Diospyros hayatae, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow fissures, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Diospyros hayatae is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
