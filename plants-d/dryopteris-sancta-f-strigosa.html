Dryopteris sancta f. strigosa

Dryopteris sancta f. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy male fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds with a hairy texture.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features bipinnate fronds that are often arching, with the pinnae having a dense covering of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to woodland gardens.
