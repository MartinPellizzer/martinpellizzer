Diospyros kaki var. macrantha

Diospyros kaki var. macrantha, commonly known as the Chinese date or Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The fruit is typically orange to reddish in color, with a sweet, non-astringent flavor when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
