Diosma serpyllifolia

Diosma serpyllifolia, commonly known as common rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic regions. It is perennial and has a low-growing habit, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Diosma serpyllifolia is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
