Diospyros boliviana

Diospyros boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits.

The fruit is typically edible when fully ripe, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp surrounded by a tough, inedible rind.
