Dendrocousinsia fasciculata

Dendrocousinsia fasciculata, commonly known as the desert candelabra, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branching, tree-like structure with multiple stems forming a candelabra shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its thick, fleshy leaves are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Dendrocousinsia fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience in low-water conditions.
