Daviesia obtusifolia var. parvifolia

Daviesia obtusifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered grey box, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-grey foliage and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces yellow or cream flowers during spring, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
