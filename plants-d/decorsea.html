Decorsea

Decorsea, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in coastal areas with sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its blue or violet flower heads surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture.

Decorsea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
