Dissotis elliotii var. setosior

Dissotis elliotii var. setosior, commonly known as the hairy dissotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its hairy or pubescent surfaces are a distinguishing feature, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or disturbed habitats.
