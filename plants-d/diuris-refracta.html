Diuris refracta

Diuris refracta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spider-orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like floral structure. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open forests or heathlands. Its flowers, which are usually greenish-yellow with reddish markings, bloom during the cooler months, typically from late winter to early spring.

Diuris refracta is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
