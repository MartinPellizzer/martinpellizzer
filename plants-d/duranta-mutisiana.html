Duranta mutisiana

Duranta mutisiana, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Duranta mutisiana is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
