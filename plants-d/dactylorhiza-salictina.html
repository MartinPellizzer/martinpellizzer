Dactylorhiza salictina

Dactylorhiza salictina, commonly known as the river orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Dactylorhiza salictina is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
