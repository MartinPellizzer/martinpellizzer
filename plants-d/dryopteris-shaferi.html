Dryopteris shaferi

Dryopteris shaferi, commonly known as the Shafer's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that form a compact, bushy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, intricate pattern of sori on the underside.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
