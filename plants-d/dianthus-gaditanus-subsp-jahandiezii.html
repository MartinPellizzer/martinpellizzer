Dianthus gaditanus subsp. jahandiezii

Dianthus gaditanus subsp.

jahandiezii, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with prominent white centers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
