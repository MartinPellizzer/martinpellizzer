Dombeya condensata

Dombeya condensata, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Dombeya condensata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
