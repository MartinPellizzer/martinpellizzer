Dortmanna pinifolia

Dortmanna pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf Dortmanna, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish or brownish in color, and it produces tiny, capsule-like fruits.

Dortmanna pinifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
