Dioscorea tacanensis

Dioscorea tacanensis, commonly known as the Tacana yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is a climbing vine with tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The tubers of Dioscorea tacanensis are similar to other yam species, containing starch and nutrients that have been utilized by indigenous communities for centuries.
