Diospyros acapulcensis subsp. dwyeri

Diospyros acapulcensis subsp.

dwyeri, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species native to Central America, particularly Mexico. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange to red berry that ripens in the fall and is edible when fully mature.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance with a slightly waxy texture.
