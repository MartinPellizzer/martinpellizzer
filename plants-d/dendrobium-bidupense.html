Dendrobium bidupense

Dendrobium bidupense, commonly known as the Bidupense orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems that often bear clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
