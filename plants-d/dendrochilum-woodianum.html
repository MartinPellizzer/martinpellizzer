Dendrochilum woodianum

Dendrochilum woodianum, commonly known as the Woodian tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Dendrochilum woodianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
