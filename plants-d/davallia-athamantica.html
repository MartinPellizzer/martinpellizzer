Davallia athamantica

Davallia athamantica, commonly known as the silver-veined hoya, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are often adorned with silvery veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Davallia athamantica is popular among fern enthusiasts for its elegant foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
