Dillenia oblonga

Dillenia oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf dillenia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, which are followed by distinctive, woody, apple-like fruits. Its botanical features make it a notable species in the family Dilleniaceae.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
