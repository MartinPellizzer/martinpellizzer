Digitalis purpurea var. purpurea

Digitalis purpurea var. purpurea, commonly known as the common foxglove, is a biennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its cardiac glycosides, which have medicinal uses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its foliage consists of velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
