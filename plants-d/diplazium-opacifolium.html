Diplazium opacifolium

Diplazium opacifolium, commonly known as the opaque fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically broad and lance-shaped. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in sori surrounded by indusia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Diplazium opacifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
