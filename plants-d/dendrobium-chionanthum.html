Dendrobium chionanthum

Dendrobium chionanthum, commonly known as the snow-flower orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
