Dracophyllum latifolium var. matthewsii

Dracophyllum latifolium var. matthewsii, commonly known as the broad-leaved dracophyllum, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, leathery leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
