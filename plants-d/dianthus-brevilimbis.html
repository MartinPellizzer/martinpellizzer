Dianthus brevilimbis

Dianthus brevilimbis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus brevilimbis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
