Diplotaxis viminea var. integrifolia

Diplotaxis viminea var. integrifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green composite flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly rough texture, with a distinctive basal rosette formation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
