Dictamnus fraxinella var. obtusiflorus

Dictamnus fraxinella var. obtusiflorus, commonly known as the blunt-flowered dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often reddish or purple-hued, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique fragrance.
