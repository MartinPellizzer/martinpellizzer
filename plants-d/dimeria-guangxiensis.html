Dimeria guangxiensis

Dimeria guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi dimeria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Guangxi region, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in spring. It has fibrous roots and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
