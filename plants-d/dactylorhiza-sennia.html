Dactylorhiza sennia

Dactylorhiza sennia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as bogs, fens, and marshes. The plant has slender, upright stems and linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized root system adapted for nutrient uptake in poor soil conditions.
