Dalbergia velutina var. verrucosa

Dalbergia velutina var. verrucosa, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to central and western Africa.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its rich color and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small and yellowish-green. It produces small, round fruits that contain several winged seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
