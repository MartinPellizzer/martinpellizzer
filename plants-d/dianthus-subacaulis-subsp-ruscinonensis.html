Dianthus subacaulis subsp. ruscinonensis

Dianthus subacaulis subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
