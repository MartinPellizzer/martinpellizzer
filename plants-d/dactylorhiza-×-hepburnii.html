Dactylorhiza × hepburnii

Dactylorhiza × hepburnii, commonly known as the Hepburn's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear numerous spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with purple or brown markings, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn. This plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
