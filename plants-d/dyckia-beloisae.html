Dyckia beloisae

Dyckia beloisae, commonly known as the Beloi's Dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Dyckia beloisae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable growing conditions.
