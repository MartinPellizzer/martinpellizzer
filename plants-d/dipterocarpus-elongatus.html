Dipterocarpus elongatus

Dipterocarpus elongatus, commonly known as the long-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, elongated leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces heavy, woody fruits that contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It plays a significant role in the rainforest ecosystem, providing habitat and contributing to the forest canopy.

Its timber is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
