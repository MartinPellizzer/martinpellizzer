Dialium marginatum

Dialium marginatum, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow-wood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to termites, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
