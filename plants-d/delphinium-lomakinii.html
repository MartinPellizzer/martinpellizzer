Delphinium lomakinii

Delphinium lomakinii, commonly known as the Tibetan larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant's leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique habitat and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species in alpine gardens.
