Dracaena cuspidibracteata

Dracaena cuspidibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dracaena, is a member of the Asparagaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge along the midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its adaptability to various light conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
