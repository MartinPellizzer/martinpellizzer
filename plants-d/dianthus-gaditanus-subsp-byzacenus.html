Dianthus gaditanus subsp. byzacenus

Dianthus gaditanus subsp.

byzacenus, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical classification places it within the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often fragrant flowers.
