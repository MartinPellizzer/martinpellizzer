Diasperus columnaris

Diasperus columnaris, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that grow in a columnar shape, often reaching several meters in height. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the tips of the stems during the warmer months.

Diasperus columnaris is a hardy plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
