Diospyros sahayadryensis

Diospyros sahayadryensis, commonly known as the Western Ghats persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
