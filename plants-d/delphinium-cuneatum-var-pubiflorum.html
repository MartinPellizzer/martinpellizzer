Delphinium cuneatum var. pubiflorum

Delphinium cuneatum var. pubiflorum, commonly known as the pubiflorous larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, which is characteristic of the varietal name "pubiflorum.". The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.

Its striking flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
