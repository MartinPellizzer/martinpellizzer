Drosera cistiflora var. alba

Drosera cistiflora var. alba, commonly known as the white sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a sweet nectar to lure insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in a dense inflorescence.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cool, moist conditions.
