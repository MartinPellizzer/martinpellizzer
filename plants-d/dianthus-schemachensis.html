Dianthus schemachensis

Dianthus schemachensis, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled blooms.

Dianthus schemachensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and resilience.
