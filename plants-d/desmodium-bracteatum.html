Desmodium bracteatum

Desmodium bracteatum, commonly known as the hairy sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Desmodium bracteatum is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
