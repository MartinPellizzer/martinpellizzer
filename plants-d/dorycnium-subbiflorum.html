Dorycnium subbiflorum

Dorycnium subbiflorum, commonly known as the lesser burrmedic, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by distinctive burrs that aid in seed dispersal. Native to Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation and serving as a food source for various insects.
