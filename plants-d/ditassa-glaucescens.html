Ditassa glaucescens

Ditassa glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees and shrubs.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, bluish-green coating. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy, red berries.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host plants if left unchecked.
