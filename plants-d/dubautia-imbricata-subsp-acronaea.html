Dubautia imbricata subsp. acronaea

Dubautia imbricata subsp.

acronaea, commonly known as the Kona false hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and unique appearance make it a notable species in Hawaiian botany.
