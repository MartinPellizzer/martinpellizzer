Drummondia

Drummondia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate inflorescences that resemble ghostly shapes, hence its common name. The plants are native to southern Africa and are typically found in rocky or sandy soils. Drummondia species are often small, herbaceous perennials with showy, pink or white flowers.

They are popular in rock gardens and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
