Dypsis rivularis

Dypsis rivularis, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, palm-like appearance with long, arching leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Dypsis rivularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
