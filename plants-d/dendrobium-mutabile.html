Dendrobium mutabile

Dendrobium mutabile, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining soil.

Dendrobium mutabile is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
