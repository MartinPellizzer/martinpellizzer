Desmotrichum padangense

Desmotrichum padangense, commonly known as the Padang dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
