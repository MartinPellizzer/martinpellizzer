Diodonopsis pygmaea

Diodonopsis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf starfish plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
