Ditassa polygaloides

Ditassa polygaloides, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its foliage is simple and opposite, with leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped.
