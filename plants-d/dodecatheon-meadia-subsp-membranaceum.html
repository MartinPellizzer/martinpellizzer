Dodecatheon meadia subsp. membranaceum

Dodecatheon meadia subsp.

membranaceum, commonly known as the eastern springbeauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with five petals arranged in a bell shape.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and blooms in early spring, making it an important early pollinator plant.

Its subspecies status reflects subtle differences in morphology and habitat preferences compared to other forms of Dodecatheon meadia.
