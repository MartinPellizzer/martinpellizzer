Dianthus armeria var. laevis

Dianthus armeria var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and prominent stamens. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in early summer and are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate appearance.
