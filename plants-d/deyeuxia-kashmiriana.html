Deyeuxia kashmiriana

Deyeuxia kashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan, India, and Nepal.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Deyeuxia kashmiriana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
