Diospyros roylei

Diospyros roylei, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits, which are edible when fully mature. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and tool-making.
