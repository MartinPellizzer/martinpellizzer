Dactylorhiza × boluiana

Dactylorhiza × boluiana, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as bogs and marshes. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with each pseudobulb producing a single leaf and a flowering stem. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a raceme, and are characterized by their intricate, spurred labellum.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
