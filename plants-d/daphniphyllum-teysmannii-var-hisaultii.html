Daphniphyllum teysmannii var. hisaultii

Daphniphyllum teysmannii var. hisaultii, commonly known as the Hainan laurel, is a species native to China, specifically the Hainan Island.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, blooming in late winter or early spring.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.
