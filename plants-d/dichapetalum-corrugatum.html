Dichapetalum corrugatum

Dichapetalum corrugatum, commonly known as the roughleaf sterculia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Dichapetalum corrugatum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique foliage and adaptability.
