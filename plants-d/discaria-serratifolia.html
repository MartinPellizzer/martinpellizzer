Discaria serratifolia

Discaria serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated discaria, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and sandy environments.

Discaria serratifolia is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
