Draba rosea

Draba rosea, commonly known as the rose sandwort, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia, thriving in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces delicate, pink or white flowers in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its basal leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the stem is usually unbranched.

Draba rosea is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous or coastal environments.
