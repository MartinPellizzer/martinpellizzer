Diplazium asperum

Diplazium asperum, commonly known as the rough shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds with a rough, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.
