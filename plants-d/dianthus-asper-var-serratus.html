Dianthus asper var. serratus

Dianthus asper var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with prominent stamens. The leaves are narrow, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
