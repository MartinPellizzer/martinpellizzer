Dryandra carduacea var. angustifolia

Dryandra carduacea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dryandra, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in heathlands and shrublands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
