Dracophyllum pungens

Dracophyllum pungens, commonly known as the prickly drakebush, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces red berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in coastal scrub and dune environments.

Dracophyllum pungens is adapted to tolerate salt spray and moderate drought conditions.
