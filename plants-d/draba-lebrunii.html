Draba lebrunii

Draba lebrunii, commonly known as the Lebrun's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, with a dense cluster of small, white or pale yellow flowers at the top. It is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Draba lebrunii is native to Central Asia and is known for its resilience in cold and arid conditions.
