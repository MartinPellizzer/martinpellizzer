Desmodium dimorphum var. argyreum

Desmodium dimorphum var. argyreum, commonly known as the silverleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a dense layer of white or silver hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
