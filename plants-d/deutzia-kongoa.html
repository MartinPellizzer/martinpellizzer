Deutzia kongoa

Deutzia kongoa, commonly known as the Korean deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea and parts of China.

It features arching stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy underneath. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Deutzia kongoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
