Diuris ochroma

Diuris ochroma, commonly known as the yellow spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid species characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structure.

It typically grows in heathland and dry forest environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower that resembles a spider in appearance. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Diuris ochroma is a perennial herb that is often found in the southeastern regions of Australia.
