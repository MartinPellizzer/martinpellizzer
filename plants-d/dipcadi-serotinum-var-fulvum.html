Dipcadi serotinum var. fulvum

Dipcadi serotinum var. fulvum, commonly known as the golden squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features strap-like leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or golden hue along the margins. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow flowers during late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after addition to rock gardens and borders.
