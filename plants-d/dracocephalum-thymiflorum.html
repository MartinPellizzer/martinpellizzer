Dracocephalum thymiflorum

Dracocephalum thymiflorum, commonly known as Persian bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and spikes of blue to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
