Dryadella nasuta

Dryadella nasuta, commonly known as the slender reed or narrow-leaved reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Dryadella nasuta is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
