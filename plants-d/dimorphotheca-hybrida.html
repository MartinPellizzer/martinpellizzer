Dimorphotheca hybrida

Dimorphotheca hybrida, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flower heads, which are surrounded by white to pale pink ray flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with woody stems and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Dimorphotheca hybrida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
