Doronicum latifolium

Doronicum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal and cauline leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Doronicum latifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
