Daucus copticus

Daucus copticus, commonly known as the Persian parsley or wild carrot, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbel-shaped flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that resemble carrots.
