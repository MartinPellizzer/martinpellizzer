Draba tomentosa subsp. ciliigera

Draba tomentosa subsp.

ciliigera, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its inflorescence is a raceme, with each flower having four sepals and four petals.

This subspecies is characterized by its finely ciliate (fringed) leaves, which distinguish it from other members of the Draba genus.
