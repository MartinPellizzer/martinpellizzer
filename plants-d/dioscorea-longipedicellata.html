Dioscorea longipedicellata

Dioscorea longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are often used as a food source and have been traditionally utilized in various medicinal practices.

The species is valued for its economic importance in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
