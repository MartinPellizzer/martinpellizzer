Dendrobium garrettii

Dendrobium garrettii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with numerous pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

Dendrobium garrettii is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
