Dactylorchis maculata var. concolor

Dactylorchis maculata var. concolor, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a slender, upright stem with a few leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are often clustered in a spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf patterns and is considered a rare and endangered orchid in some regions.
