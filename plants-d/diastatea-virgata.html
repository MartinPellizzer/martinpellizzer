Diastatea virgata

Diastatea virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Diastatea virgata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
