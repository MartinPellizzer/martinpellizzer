Diaphanoptera ekbergii

Diaphanoptera ekbergii, commonly known as the glasswinged fern, is a unique plant characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that give it a glass-like appearance.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, this fern thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are elongated and arching, often reaching several feet in length, and they are covered in fine, hair-like structures that enhance its visual appeal. The plant is known for its slow growth and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil to flourish.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
