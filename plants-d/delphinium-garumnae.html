Delphinium garumnae

Delphinium garumnae, commonly known as the Armenian delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a distinctive appearance that aids in identification.

Delphinium garumnae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
