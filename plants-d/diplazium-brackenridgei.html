Diplazium brackenridgei

Diplazium brackenridgei, commonly known as Brackenridge's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can receive adequate sunlight. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant and allowing it to spread across the water's surface.

Diplazium brackenridgei plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
