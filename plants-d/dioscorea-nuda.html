Dioscorea nuda

Dioscorea nuda, commonly known as naked yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal use. Dioscorea nuda thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional herbal medicine and agriculture.
