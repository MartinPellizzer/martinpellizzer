Dupatya planifolia

Dupatya planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaved fern, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant, low-maintenance plant.
