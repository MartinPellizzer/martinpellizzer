Deutzia crenata f. pubescens

Deutzia crenata f. pubescens, commonly known as the fuzzy deutzia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has a dense, rounded form with hairy foliage that gives it its common name. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
