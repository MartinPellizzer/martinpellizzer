Dianthus strymonis

Dianthus strymonis, commonly known as the Cyprus carnation, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Dianthus strymonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
