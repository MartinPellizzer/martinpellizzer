Desmodium novohollandicum

Desmodium novohollandicum, commonly known as the Australian burr legume, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive burr-like seed pods that aid in seed dispersal. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions. It is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Its flowers, which are typically pink or purple, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
