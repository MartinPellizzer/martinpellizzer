Dendrochilum appendiculatum

Dendrochilum appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate tree orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in lowland forests.

Dendrochilum appendiculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
