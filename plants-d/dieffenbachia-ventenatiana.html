Dieffenbachia ventenatiana

Dieffenbachia ventenatiana, commonly known as the "Venenatiana Dieffenbachia," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other popular houseplants like peace lilies. Its foliage is toxic due to the presence of calcium oxalate crystals, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

Dieffenbachia ventenatiana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity to flourish.
