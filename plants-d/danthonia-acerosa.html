Danthonia acerosa

Danthonia acerosa, commonly known as stiff hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage with sharp, stiff leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Danthonia acerosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
