Diolena repens

Diolena repens, commonly known as the creeping diolena, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy. Diolena repens thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

It is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
