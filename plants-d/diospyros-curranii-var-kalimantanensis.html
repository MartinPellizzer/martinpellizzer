Diospyros curranii var. kalimantanensis

Diospyros curranii var. kalimantanensis, commonly known as the Kalimantan persimmon, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland dipterocarp forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are edible, fleshy berries that mature to a reddish color, often containing a single large seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
