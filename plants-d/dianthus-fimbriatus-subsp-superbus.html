Dianthus fimbriatus subsp. superbus

Dianthus fimbriatus subsp.

superbus, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are erect and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
