Dudleya ingens

Dudleya ingens, commonly known as the giant Dudleya, is a striking succulent native to coastal areas of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often reaching up to two feet in diameter. The plant produces tall, branched flowering stalks that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
