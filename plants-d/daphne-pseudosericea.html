Daphne pseudosericea

Daphne pseudosericea, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in white or pink hues during the spring. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a velvety texture.

Daphne pseudosericea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
