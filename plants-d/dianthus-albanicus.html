Dianthus albanicus

Dianthus albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Dianthus albanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
