Diptychandra aurantiaca subsp. aurantiaca

Diptychandra aurantiaca subsp.

aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its visual appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
