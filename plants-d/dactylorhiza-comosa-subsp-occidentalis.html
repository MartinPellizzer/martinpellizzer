Dactylorhiza comosa subsp. occidentalis

Dactylorhiza comosa subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies such as ponds and streams.

The plant features a slender, erect stem with several narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are pink or purple, arranged in a loose spike, and are characterized by their distinctive three-lobed lip.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
