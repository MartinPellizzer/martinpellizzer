Desmodium sinuatum

Desmodium sinuatum, commonly known as the velvet bean or cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is widely cultivated for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities that benefit soil fertility.
