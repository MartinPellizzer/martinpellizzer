Diplachne menyharthii

Diplachne menyharthii, commonly known as the African feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem. Diplachne menyharthii is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its growth habit makes it a useful plant for erosion control and as a forage species in certain regions.
