Dendrochilum corrugatum

Dendrochilum corrugatum, commonly known as the ridged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that are often ridged or furrowed. The plant typically features linear to lanceolate leaves and bears small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dendrochilum corrugatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
