Diospyros ferrea var. pubescens

Diospyros ferrea var. pubescens, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruit with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
