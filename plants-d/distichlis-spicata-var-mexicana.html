Distichlis spicata var. mexicana

Distichlis spicata var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican saltbush, is a halophyte adapted to saline and coastal environments.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense, grass-like leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
