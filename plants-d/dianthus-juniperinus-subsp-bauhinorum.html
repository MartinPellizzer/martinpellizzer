Dianthus juniperinus subsp. bauhinorum

Dianthus juniperinus subsp.

bauhinorum, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and well-drained soils.

The plant features compact, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of pink or white flowers in spring. Its botanical characteristics include narrow, needle-like leaves and a compact growth habit.

It is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
