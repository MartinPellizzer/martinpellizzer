Dendrobium woodfordianum

Dendrobium woodfordianum, commonly known as the Woodford's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, typically during the summer months. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium woodfordianum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine.
