Dyckia granmogulensis

Dyckia granmogulensis, commonly known as the Granmogulensis Dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that emerges from a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help retain moisture and protect against intense sunlight.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, adding a subtle ornamental value to the plant.
