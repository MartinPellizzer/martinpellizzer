Diasperus leucogynus

Diasperus leucogynus, commonly known as the white yarrow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, cylindrical flower heads that attract various pollinators.

Diasperus leucogynus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
