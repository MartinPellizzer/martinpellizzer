Dryopteris oligodictyos

Dryopteris oligodictyos, commonly known as the slender wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters long. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
