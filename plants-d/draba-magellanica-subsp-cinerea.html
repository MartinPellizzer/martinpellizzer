Draba magellanica subsp. cinerea

Draba magellanica subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the gray draba, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern South America. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its grayish-blue foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, alpine, or coastal environments, often in areas with well-drained soil.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in the southern Andes and adjacent regions.
