Delphinium reverdattoanum

Delphinium reverdattoanum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium reverdattoanum is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
