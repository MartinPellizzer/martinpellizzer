Delphinium macedonicum

Delphinium macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Delphinium macedonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
