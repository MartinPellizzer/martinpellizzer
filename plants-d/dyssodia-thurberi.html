Dyssodia thurberi

Dyssodia thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers appear in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
