Dianthus stenocalyx

Dianthus stenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun. The plant features slender stems with small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus stenocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
