Dendrobium prostratum

Dendrobium prostratum, commonly known as the ground orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with fleshy, often reddish roots that store water and nutrients. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or green with red or purple markings, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Dendrobium prostratum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
