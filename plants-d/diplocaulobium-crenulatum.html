Diplocaulobium crenulatum

Diplocaulobium crenulatum, commonly known as the crested water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often ribbed leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the pond or lake bottom while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Diplocaulobium crenulatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
