Daphne caucasica var. cognata

Daphne caucasica var. cognata, commonly known as the Caucasian Daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that provide a striking contrast to its colorful blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling and placement in gardens.
