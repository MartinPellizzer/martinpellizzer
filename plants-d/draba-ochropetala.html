Draba ochropetala

Draba ochropetala, commonly known as the pale-flowered winter grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba ochropetala is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
