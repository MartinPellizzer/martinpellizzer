Draba glabella

Draba glabella, commonly known as the glabrous whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to cold, rocky environments and has a short growing season. Draba glabella is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.

It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.
