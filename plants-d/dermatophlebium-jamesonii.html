Dermatophlebium jamesonii

Dermatophlebium jamesonii, commonly known as the silver hair fern, is a rare and delicate fern native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-hairy fronds that give it a distinctive, almost ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity and partial shade. Its rhizomes are relatively short, and it produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
