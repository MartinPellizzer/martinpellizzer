Diospyros oxycarpa

Diospyros oxycarpa, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is typically oblong, with a rough, spiny exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp containing black, inedible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is hard and durable.
