Dischidia cuneifolia

Dischidia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Its unique leaf shape and climbing habit make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
