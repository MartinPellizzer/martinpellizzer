Dipterosiphon spelaeicola

Dipterosiphon spelaeicola, commonly known as the cave sponge plant, is a unique aquatic plant found in subterranean water systems.

It exhibits specialized adaptations for survival in dark, low-nutrient environments, relying on chemosynthesis rather than photosynthesis. The plant has a delicate, sponge-like structure that increases its surface area for absorbing nutrients from water. It is typically found in limestone caves and underground rivers, where it forms dense colonies on rocky substrates.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and poorly understood species within the botanical community.
