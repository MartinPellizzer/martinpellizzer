Daphne composita f. macrophylla

Daphne composita f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is evergreen, with lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 10 inches in length.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
