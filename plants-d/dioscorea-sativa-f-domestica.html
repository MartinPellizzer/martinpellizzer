Dioscorea sativa f. domestica

Dioscorea sativa f. domestica, commonly known as the common yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, starchy underground tubers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and alternate, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers are widely cultivated for their nutritional value and used in various cuisines around the world.
