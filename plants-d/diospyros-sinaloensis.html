Diospyros sinaloensis

Diospyros sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-colored fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often with a reddish-brown hue.

Diospyros sinaloensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
