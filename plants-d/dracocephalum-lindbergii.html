Dracocephalum lindbergii

Dracocephalum lindbergii, commonly known as the Persian bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Dracocephalum lindbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
