Disperis villosa

Disperis villosa, commonly known as the hairy disperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, giving it the common name.

Disperis villosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
