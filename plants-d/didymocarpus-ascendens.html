Didymocarpus ascendens

Didymocarpus ascendens, commonly known as the climbing tamarind, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Didymocarpus ascendens make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
