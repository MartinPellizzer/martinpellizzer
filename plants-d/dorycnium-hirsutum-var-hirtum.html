Dorycnium hirsutum var. hirtum

Dorycnium hirsutum var. hirtum, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and is often used as a cover crop due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climates.

Its botanical features make it valuable in ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture practices.
