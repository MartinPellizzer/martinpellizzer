Dodonaea trifoliata

Dodonaea trifoliata, commonly known as the trileaf burr or three-leaved glory, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods.

Dodonaea trifoliata is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
