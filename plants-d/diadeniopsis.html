Diadeniopsis

Diadeniopsis, commonly known as the star orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and the production of small, fragrant flowers that often resemble stars. The plants typically have strap-like leaves and are native to tropical regions of South America. Diadeniopsis species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic, often in humid, shaded environments.

They are prized for their ornamental value and are cultivated for their unique, delicate blooms.
