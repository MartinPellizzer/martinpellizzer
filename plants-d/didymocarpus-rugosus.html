Didymocarpus rugosus

Didymocarpus rugosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, woody bark and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, segmented fruits. Its fruit, similar to the mangosteen, has a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, segmented pulp.

Didymocarpus rugosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
