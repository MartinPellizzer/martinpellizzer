Dypsis mananjarensis

Dypsis mananjarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny trunk and clustered, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. Dypsis mananjarensis is adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate some degree of drought.
