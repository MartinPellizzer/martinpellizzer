Delphinium viscosum var. connectans

Delphinium viscosum var. connectans, commonly known as the sticky delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a sticky resin, giving it its common name.

This variety is typically found in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
