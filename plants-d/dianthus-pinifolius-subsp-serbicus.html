Dianthus pinifolius subsp. serbicus

Dianthus pinifolius subsp.

serbicus, commonly known as the Serbian carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It features upright stems that bear clusters of pink to red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and needle-like, resembling those of coniferous trees, hence part of its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
