Dahlia scapigera f. purpurea

Dahlia scapigera f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that range in color from deep purples to rich reds. The plant produces sturdy stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly grown in gardens for its beauty and versatility.
