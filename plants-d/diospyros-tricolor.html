Diospyros tricolor

Diospyros tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and trifoliate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by three-lobed, reddish-black fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
