Dioscorea subcalva

Dioscorea subcalva, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical structure includes a robust root system and a twining stem that supports its growth in shaded environments.

The tubers of Dioscorea subcalva are often harvested for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
