Diuris longifolia var. parviflora

Diuris longifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered greenhood, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few leaves, and its flowers are characterized by their distinctive green hood-shaped labellum. The flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a narrow, spurred lip.

It blooms during the autumn season, producing a delicate and intricate floral display.
