Duckera

Duckera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved duckweed, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It typically grows in shallow, still freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant features tiny, floating leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

Duckera plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
