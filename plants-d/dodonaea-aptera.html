Dodonaea aptera

Dodonaea aptera, commonly known as the smooth dodonaea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairless. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Dodonaea aptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
