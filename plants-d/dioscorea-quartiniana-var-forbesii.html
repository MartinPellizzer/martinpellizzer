Dioscorea quartiniana var. forbesii

Dioscorea quartiniana var. forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and later develops capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in various African cultures.
