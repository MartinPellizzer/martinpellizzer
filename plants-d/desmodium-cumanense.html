Desmodium cumanense

Desmodium cumanense, commonly known as the Cumanese burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
