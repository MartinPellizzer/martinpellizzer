Dianthus arenarius var. gigas

Dianthus arenarius var. gigas, commonly known as the giant sand pink, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often woody at the base.

This variety is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
