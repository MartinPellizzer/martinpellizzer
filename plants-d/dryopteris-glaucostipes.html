Dryopteris glaucostipes

Dryopteris glaucostipes, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in natural habitats.
