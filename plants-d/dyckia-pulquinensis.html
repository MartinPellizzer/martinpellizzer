It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. Dyckia pulquinensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
