Diplomorpha bacciformis

Diplomorpha bacciformis, commonly known as the red-berried hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Diplomorpha bacciformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
