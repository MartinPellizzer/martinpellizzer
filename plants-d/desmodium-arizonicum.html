Desmodium arizonicum

Desmodium arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive burrs with hooked spines, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Desmodium arizonicum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in ecological systems.
