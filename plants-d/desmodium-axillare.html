Desmodium axillare

Desmodium axillare, commonly known as the hairy burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and the leaves are usually trifoliate with hairy surfaces.

Desmodium axillare is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
