Diospyros mannii

Diospyros mannii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor.

Diospyros mannii is often found in moist forests and is valued for its timber and fruit in local communities.
