Dalibarda pirifolia

Dalibarda pirifolia, commonly known as the sweet-scented bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often tinged with reddish hues. Its flowers emit a subtle, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dalibarda pirifolia thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found growing in clusters along stream banks or in rich, loamy soils.
