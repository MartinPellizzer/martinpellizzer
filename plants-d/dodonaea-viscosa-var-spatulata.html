Dodonaea viscosa var. spatulata

Dodonaea viscosa var. spatulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
