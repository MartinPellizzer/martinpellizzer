Digitalis purpurea subsp. dubia

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

dubia, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant produces a distinctive, spathaceous calyx that encloses the corolla, which is typically purple or violet. Its leaves are velvety and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
