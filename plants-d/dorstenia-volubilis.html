Dorstenia volubilis

Dorstenia volubilis, commonly known as the twisting or creeping false arum, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a tropical perennial vine characterized by its helical twisting growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by bright red berries. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and growth pattern, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
