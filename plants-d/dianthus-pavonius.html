Dianthus pavonius

Dianthus pavonius, commonly known as the peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy blooms that range from pink to red, often with intricate patterns resembling peacock feathers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are borne on tall, branching stems and are composed of numerous petals that are often marked with darker veins.

Dianthus pavonius is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
