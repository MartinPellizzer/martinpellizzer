Dierama galpinii

Dierama galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's dierama, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adding visual contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
