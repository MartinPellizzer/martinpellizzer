Ditassa cordata var. virgata

Ditassa cordata var. virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's tears, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
