Digitaria pacifica

Digitaria pacifica, commonly known as Pacific digitaria, is a grass species native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
