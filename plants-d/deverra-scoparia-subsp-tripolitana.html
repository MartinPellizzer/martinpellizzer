Deverra scoparia subsp. tripolitana

Deverra scoparia subsp.

tripolitana, commonly known as the Tripoli thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
