Delphinium parishii var. inopinum

Delphinium parishii var. inopinum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and forested areas at high elevations. The plant features tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
