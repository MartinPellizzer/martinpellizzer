Dombeya ficulnea var. tsaratananensis

Dombeya ficulnea var. tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Tsaratanana dombeya, is a striking flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.
