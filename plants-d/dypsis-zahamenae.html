Dypsis zahamenae

Dypsis zahamenae, commonly known as the Zahamenae areca palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of around 5 to 8 meters, with a crown of narrow, feather-like fronds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a smooth, green trunk that may develop a slight sheen over time.

Dypsis zahamenae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
