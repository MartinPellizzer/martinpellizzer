Dryopteris denticulata var. dissecta

Dryopteris denticulata var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected male fern, is a variant of the male fern native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in natural habitats.
