Diospyros opacifolia

Diospyros opacifolia, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible, though some varieties may have a slightly astringent taste.

Diospyros opacifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
