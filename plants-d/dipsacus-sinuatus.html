Dipsacus sinuatus

Dipsacus sinuatus, commonly known as the devil's bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed heads that resemble small, twisted horns. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are blue to purple in color and bloom from June to August.

Dipsacus sinuatus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
