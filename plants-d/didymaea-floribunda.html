Didymaea floribunda

Didymaea floribunda, commonly known as the clustered flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Didymaea floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
