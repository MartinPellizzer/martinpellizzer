Dorstenia holstii var. longestipulata

Dorstenia holstii var. longestipulata, commonly known as the long-stippled dorstenia, is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curling leaves that can resemble tendrils or fingers. It is native to tropical regions of Central America and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.
