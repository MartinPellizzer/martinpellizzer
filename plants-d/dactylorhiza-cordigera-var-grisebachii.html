Dactylorhiza cordigera var. grisebachii

Dactylorhiza cordigera var. grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-stemmed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has a slender, upright stem that is often covered in a grayish or bluish hue, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with three petals and three sepals, and are arranged in a loose spike.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in nutrient-poor, acidic soils.
