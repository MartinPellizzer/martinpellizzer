Diospyros greshoffiana

Diospyros greshoffiana, commonly known as the Greshoff's ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony trees, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its hard, durable wood, which is used in specialty woodworking.
