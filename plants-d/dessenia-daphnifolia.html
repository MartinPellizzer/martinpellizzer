Dessenia daphnifolia

Dessenia daphnifolia, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
