Daphne vahlii

Daphne vahlii, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms. Daphne vahlii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, enhancing its adaptability in various environments.
