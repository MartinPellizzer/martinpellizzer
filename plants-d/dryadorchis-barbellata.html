Dryadorchis barbellata

Dryadorchis barbellata, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hairy foliage and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, depending on the species.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
