Dodonaea viscosa f. repanda

Dodonaea viscosa f. repanda, commonly known as the velvetleaf hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth margin and a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, woody fruits.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
