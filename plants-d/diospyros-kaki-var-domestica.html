Diospyros kaki var. domestica

Diospyros kaki var. domestica, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces orange to reddish fruits that are sweet and non-astringent when fully ripe. The fruit's skin is thin and smooth, while the flesh is soft and juicy, often with a honey-like aroma.

It is cultivated for its edible fruit and is valued for its ornamental features as well.
