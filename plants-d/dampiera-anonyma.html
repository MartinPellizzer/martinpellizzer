Dampiera anonyma

Dampiera anonyma, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in heathlands or open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

Dampiera anonyma is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
