Dombeya erythroclada

Dombeya erythroclada, commonly known as the red-leaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant during the dry season. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Dombeya erythroclada is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
