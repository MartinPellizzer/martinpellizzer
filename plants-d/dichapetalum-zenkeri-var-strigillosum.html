Dichapetalum zenkeri var. strigillosum

Dichapetalum zenkeri var. strigillosum, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
