Dialium laurentii

Dialium laurentii, commonly known as the African cherry or African wild almond, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spiny pods containing edible seeds. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The tree is also valued for its timber and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
