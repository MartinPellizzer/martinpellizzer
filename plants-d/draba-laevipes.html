Draba laevipes

Draba laevipes, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Draba laevipes has basal leaves that are smooth and sometimes hairy, with a compact growth habit.

It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates and is often found in association with other alpine flora.
