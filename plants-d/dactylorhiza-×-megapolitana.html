Dactylorhiza × megapolitana

Dactylorhiza × megapolitana, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems that bear spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically greenish-yellow to brownish in color, with three sepals and three petals, and are often arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in moist, boggy habitats and requires specific soil conditions for successful growth.

This species is often found in wet meadows and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
