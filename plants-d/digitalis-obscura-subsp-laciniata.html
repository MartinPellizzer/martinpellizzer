Digitalis obscura subsp. laciniata

Digitalis obscura subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foxglove, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of hooded, pink to purple flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often deeply lobed, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
