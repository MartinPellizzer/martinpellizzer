Delphinium tatsienense f. sordidecoerulescens

Delphinium tatsienense f. sordidecoerulescens, commonly known as the dark blue delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of western China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking, dark blue to violet flowers. The plant features palmately divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
