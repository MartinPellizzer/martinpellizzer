Dissochaeta maxwellii

Dissochaeta maxwellii, commonly known as Maxwell's hairpin grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and sandy plains. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Dissochaeta maxwellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native garden settings.
