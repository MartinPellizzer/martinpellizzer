Digitaria longissima

Digitaria longissima, commonly known as wild millet, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Digitaria longissima is a hardy perennial that plays a role in both ecological and agricultural systems.
