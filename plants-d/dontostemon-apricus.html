Dontostemon apricus

Dontostemon apricus, commonly known as the spring bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy hair. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized areas.
