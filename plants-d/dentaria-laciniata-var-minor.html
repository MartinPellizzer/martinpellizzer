Dentaria laciniata var. minor

Dentaria laciniata var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaf toothwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its unique underground tubers, which are used for propagation and are an important food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and early blooming habit, making it a notable plant in native gardens.
