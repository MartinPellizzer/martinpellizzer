Dioscorea tenuis

Dioscorea tenuis, commonly known as slender yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The tubers of D. tenuis are sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
