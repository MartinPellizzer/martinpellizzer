Dinochloa cordata

Dinochloa cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf dinochloa, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread across the substrate. Its leaves are often dark green and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Dinochloa cordata is valued in aquatic gardens for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
