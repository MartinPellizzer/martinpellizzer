Dichrostachys platycarpa

Dichrostachys platycarpa, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in dry and arid conditions, making it a hardy species. Its seeds are encased in a rough, spiny pod that aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.

The plant also has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal and agricultural purposes.
