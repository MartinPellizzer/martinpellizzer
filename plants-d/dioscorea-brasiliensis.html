Dioscorea brasiliensis

Dioscorea brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Its tubers are characterized by their rough, irregular surface and are typically harvested after the plant has completed its flowering cycle.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in various indigenous communities.
