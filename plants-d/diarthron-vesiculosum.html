Diarthron vesiculosum

Diarthron vesiculosum, commonly known as the bubble bladder grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its unique inflorescence that resembles a cluster of bubbles. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Diarthron vesiculosum is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
