Durabaculum ionoglossum

Durabaculum ionoglossum, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features silver-gray, velvety leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in rocky and arid environments.

Durabaculum ionoglossum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
