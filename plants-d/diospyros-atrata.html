Diospyros atrata

Diospyros atrata, commonly known as the black ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of high-quality timber.
