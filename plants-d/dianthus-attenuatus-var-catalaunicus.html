Dianthus attenuatus var. catalaunicus

Dianthus attenuatus var. catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers with frilled petals and a subtle, spicy fragrance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered blooms during the spring and early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
