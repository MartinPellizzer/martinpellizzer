Dalechampia scandens var. trisecta

Dalechampia scandens var. trisecta, commonly known as the three-lobed henequen, is a variant of the henequen plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by spiny, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
