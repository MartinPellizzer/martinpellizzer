Dasymaschalon rostratum var. glaucum

Dasymaschalon rostratum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous dasymaschalon, is a variant of the dasymaschalon species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive glaucous (bluish-gray) foliage, which provides a unique visual contrast to its green stems and leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are lanceolate in shape and often bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique coloration and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
