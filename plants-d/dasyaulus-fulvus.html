Dasyaulus fulvus

Dasyaulus fulvus, commonly known as the yellow marsh grass.

It is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Dasyaulus fulvus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
