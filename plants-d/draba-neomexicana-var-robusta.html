Draba neomexicana var. robusta

Draba neomexicana var. robusta, commonly known as the robust desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. It has a low, mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and basal leaves.

Its flowering period occurs in spring, producing clusters of yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
