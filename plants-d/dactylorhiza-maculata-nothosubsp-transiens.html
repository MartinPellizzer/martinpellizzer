Dactylorhiza maculata nothosubsp. transiens

Dactylorhiza maculata nothosubsp.

transiens, commonly known as the transiens marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

The flowers are typically pale pink or white with dark purple or brown markings, and they are arranged in a loose spike. This orchid thrives in wet, calcareous soils and is often found in marshy or boggy habitats.

It is a short-lived perennial, with a flowering period that occurs in late spring to early summer.
