Daviesia hakeoides var. subnuda

Daviesia hakeoides var. subnuda, commonly known as the slender daviesia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
