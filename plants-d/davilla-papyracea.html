Davilla papyracea

Davilla papyracea, commonly known as the paperleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs and have a papery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Davilla papyracea is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
