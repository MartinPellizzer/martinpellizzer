Desmodium cordifolium

Desmodium cordifolium, commonly known as heartleaf desmodium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet resembling a heart shape, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces small, yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, slender pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Desmodium cordifolium is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment and sustainable agriculture.
