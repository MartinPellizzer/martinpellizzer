Diazeuxis latifolia

Diazeuxis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf diazeuxis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique foliage and seasonal blooms, it is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
