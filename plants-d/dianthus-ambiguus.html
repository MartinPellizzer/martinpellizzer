Dianthus ambiguus

Dianthus ambiguus, commonly known as the common carnation, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to white and red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Dianthus ambiguus is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its ornamental value and historical use in gardens.
