Diospyros ferrea var. compacta

Diospyros ferrea var. compacta, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon or compact persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by orange-red, fuzzy fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, contributing to its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
