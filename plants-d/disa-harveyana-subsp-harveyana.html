Disa harveyana subsp. harveyana

Disa harveyana subsp.

harveyana, commonly known as the Harvey's Disa, is a striking orchid native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
