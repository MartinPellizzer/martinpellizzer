Dampiera haematotricha subsp. dura

Dampiera haematotricha subsp.

dura, commonly known as the hardy damper, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
