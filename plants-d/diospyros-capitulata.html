Diospyros capitulata

Diospyros capitulata, commonly known as the clustered persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite or clustered at the base of the shoots. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Diospyros capitulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
