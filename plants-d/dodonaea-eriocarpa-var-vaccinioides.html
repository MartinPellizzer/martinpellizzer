Dodonaea eriocarpa var. vaccinioides

Dodonaea eriocarpa var. vaccinioides, commonly known as the hairy leaf glorybower, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
