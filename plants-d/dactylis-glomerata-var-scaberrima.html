Dactylis glomerata var. scaberrima

Dactylis glomerata var. scaberrima, commonly known as rough brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, heaths, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a common component of both natural and agricultural ecosystems.
