Diasperus adenodiscus

Diasperus adenodiscus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its milky sap is a distinguishing feature, often used in traditional medicine.

Diasperus adenodiscus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
