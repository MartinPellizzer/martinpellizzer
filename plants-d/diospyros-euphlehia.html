Diospyros euphlehia

Diospyros euphlehia, commonly known as the Malabar date palm, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and resemble dates, hence its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Diospyros euphlehia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
