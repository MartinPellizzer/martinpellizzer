Dolichos emarginatus

Dolichos emarginatus, commonly known as the marginated dolichos, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach several meters in length, supported by tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with leaflets that have a distinctive marginated appearance.

Dolichos emarginatus is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
