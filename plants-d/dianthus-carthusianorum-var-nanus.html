Dianthus carthusianorum var. nanus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with fleshy, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to form attractive mats over time.
