Dendrobium carnosum

Dendrobium carnosum, commonly known as the fat-lipped orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with thick, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive red lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and cultivated gardens.
