Didymocarpus sivagiriensis

Didymocarpus sivagiriensis, commonly known as the Sivagiri wild fig, is a tropical plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are often greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, drupe that ripens to a yellowish or reddish hue, attracting various bird and animal species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
