Decaloba septenata

Decaloba septenata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, flat seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, it is often harvested for its high-quality wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture.
