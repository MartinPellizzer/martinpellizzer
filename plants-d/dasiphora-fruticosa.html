Dasiphora fruticosa

Dasiphora fruticosa, commonly known as Indian paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. The plant produces striking red, orange, or yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, brush-like inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in native plant landscaping.
