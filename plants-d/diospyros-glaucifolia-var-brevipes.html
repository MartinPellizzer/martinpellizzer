Diospyros glaucifolia var. brevipes

Diospyros glaucifolia var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved persimmon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often with a faintly flaky texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
