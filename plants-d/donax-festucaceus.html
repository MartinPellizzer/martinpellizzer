Donax festucaceus

Donax festucaceus, commonly known as coastal grass or sea grass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
