Dalbergia adiantifolia

Dalbergia adiantifolia, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also a species of concern in some regions due to overharvesting.
