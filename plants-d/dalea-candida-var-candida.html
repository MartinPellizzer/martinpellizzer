Dalea candida var. candida

Dalea candida var. candida, commonly known as white prairie clover, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for native gardens and restoration projects.

Dalea candida var. candida plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
