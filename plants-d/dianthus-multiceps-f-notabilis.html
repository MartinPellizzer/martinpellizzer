Dianthus multiceps f. notabilis

Dianthus multiceps f. notabilis, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive white or pale pink center. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
