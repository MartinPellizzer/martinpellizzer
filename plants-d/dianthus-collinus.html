Dianthus collinus

Dianthus collinus, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy and robust.

Dianthus collinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
