Doronicum portae

Doronicum portae, commonly known as the alpine goldcup, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Doronicum portae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
