Dioscorea hirticaulis

Dioscorea hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a support structure for its vigorous growth.

Dioscorea hirticaulis is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like yams.
