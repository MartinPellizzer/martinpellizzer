Dactylorhiza × dufftii

Dactylorhiza × dufftii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale green or brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in wet, marshy habitats and requires consistently moist soil conditions. The species is a natural hybrid between Dactylorhiza macdougalii and Dactylorhiza viridiflora, exhibiting traits from both parent plants.

Its rhizomes are often used in horticultural cultivation for their ornamental value.
