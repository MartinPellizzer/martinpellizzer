Draba media var. lasiocarpa

Draba media var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is typically found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early spring.

This variety is adapted to harsh conditions and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and western Asia.
