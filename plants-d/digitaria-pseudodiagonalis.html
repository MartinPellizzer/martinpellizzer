Digitaria pseudodiagonalis

Digitaria pseudodiagonalis, commonly known as the false diagonal grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, often forming dense stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green coloration, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle structure. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and can tolerate drought conditions.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small organisms.
