Doratolepis tetrachaeta

Doratolepis tetrachaeta, commonly known as the four-branched sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates.
