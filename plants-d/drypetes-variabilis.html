Drypetes variabilis

Drypetes variabilis, commonly known as the variable drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
