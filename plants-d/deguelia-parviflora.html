Deguelia parviflora

Deguelia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered deguelia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Deguelia parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
