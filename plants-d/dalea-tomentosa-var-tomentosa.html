Dalea tomentosa var. tomentosa

Dalea tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

This species is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
