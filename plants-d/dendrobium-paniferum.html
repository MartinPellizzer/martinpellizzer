Dendrobium paniferum

Dendrobium paniferum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with long, cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium paniferum requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
