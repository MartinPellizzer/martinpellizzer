Dombeya befotakensis var. angustifolia

Dombeya befotakensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and capsule fruits that release seeds through a longitudinal slit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.
