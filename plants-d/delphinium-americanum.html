Delphinium americanum

Delphinium americanum, commonly known as the American larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Delphinium americanum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other notable plants like buttercups and clematis.
