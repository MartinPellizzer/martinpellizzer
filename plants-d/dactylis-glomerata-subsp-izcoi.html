Dactylis glomerata subsp. izcoi

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

izcoi, commonly known as the Izco grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and compact inflorescence, which consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is well adapted to dry and nutrient-poor soils, making it a hardy species in Mediterranean climates.
