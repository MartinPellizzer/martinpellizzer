Delphinium caucasicum var. hirsutum

Delphinium caucasicum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the Hirsute Delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple spurred flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its varietal name "hirsutum," meaning hairy in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
