Dendrobium canaliculatum var. canaliculatum

Dendrobium canaliculatum var. canaliculatum, commonly known as the channel-ribbed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two rows along the pseudobulbs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
