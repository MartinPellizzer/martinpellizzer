Dianthus ilgazensis

Dianthus ilgazensis, commonly known as the Ilgaz carnation, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red petals. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It has hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
