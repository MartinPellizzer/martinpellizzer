Dalbergia amorphoides

Dalbergia amorphoides, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several flattened, kidney-shaped seeds.

Dalbergia amorphoides is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
