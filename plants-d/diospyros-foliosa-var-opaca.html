Diospyros foliosa var. opaca

Diospyros foliosa var. opaca, commonly known as the glossy persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with thick, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on both sides. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is appreciated for its sweet, honey-like flavor.
