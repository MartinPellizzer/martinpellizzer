Drymocallis glandulosa var. incisa

Drymocallis glandulosa var. incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
