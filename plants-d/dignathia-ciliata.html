Dignathia ciliata

Dignathia ciliata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Dignathia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
