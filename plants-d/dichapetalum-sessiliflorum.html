Dichapetalum sessiliflorum

Dichapetalum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
