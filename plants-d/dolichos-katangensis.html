Dolichos katangensis

Dolichos katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga bean, is a climbing legume native to Central Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bean-like pods. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in local cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
