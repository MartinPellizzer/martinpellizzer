Dioscorea sumbawensis

Dioscorea sumbawensis, commonly known as Sumbawan yam, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are typically white or yellow in color and have a starchy texture.

This species is valued both for its culinary use and its potential in traditional medicine.
