Dryopteris vilis

Dryopteris vilis, commonly known as the slender wood fern, is a species of fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

The slender wood fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
