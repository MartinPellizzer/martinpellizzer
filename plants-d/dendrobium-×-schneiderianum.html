Dendrobium × schneiderianum

Dendrobium × schneiderianum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a complex inflorescence with multiple flowers that bloom in a raceme, often displaying vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in photosynthesis and moisture retention.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its showy, long-lasting blooms.
