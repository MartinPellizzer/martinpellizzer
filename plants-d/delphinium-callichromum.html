Delphinium callichromum

Delphinium callichromum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Delphinium callichromum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other notable flowering plants like buttercups and larkspurs.
