Daphne gnidium var. sericea

Daphne gnidium var. sericea, commonly known as silver daphne, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink during late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
