Digitaria pedicellaris

Digitaria pedicellaris, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a warm-season grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture.

It is a common weed in agricultural settings and is adapted to a wide range of soil types.
