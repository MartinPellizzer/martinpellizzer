Dendrobium brillianum

Dendrobium brillianum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Dendrobium brillianum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and showy blooms.
