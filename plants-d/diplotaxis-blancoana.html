Diplotaxis blancoana

Diplotaxis blancoana, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or physalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and spherical, inflated calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its fruit, which resembles a small tomato, is edible and has a tart, sweet flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
