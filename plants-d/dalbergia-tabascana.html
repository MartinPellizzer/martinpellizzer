Dalbergia tabascana

Dalbergia tabascana, commonly known as rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, narrow leaflets.

The tree is also noted for its fragrant bark and is often found in humid lowland forests.
