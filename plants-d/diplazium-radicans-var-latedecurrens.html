Diplazium radicans var. latedecurrens

Diplazium radicans var. latedecurrens, commonly known as the spreading fern, is a variant of the Diplazium radicans species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread along the ground, forming dense mats. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance, and they are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and landscape design.
