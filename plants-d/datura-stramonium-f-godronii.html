Datura stramonium f. godronii

Datura stramonium f. godronii, commonly known as the Godron's jimsonweed, is a variant of the common jimsonweed.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is native to the Americas but has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and medicinal studies.
