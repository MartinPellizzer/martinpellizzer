Dichaea verrucosa

Dichaea verrucosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests. Its growth habit is rhizomatous, with creeping stems that produce leaves and flower spikes.

Dichaea verrucosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
