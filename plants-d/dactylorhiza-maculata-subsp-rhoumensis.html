Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. rhoumensis

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

rhoumensis, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features a tall, erect stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

The species is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and is often found in calcareous soils.
