Dyssodia neaei

Dyssodia neaei, commonly known as the Neaei's daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with silver-gray foliage that helps reflect sunlight in its harsh environment. Its flowers bloom in shades of white and yellow, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Dyssodia neaei is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in xerophytic ecosystems.
