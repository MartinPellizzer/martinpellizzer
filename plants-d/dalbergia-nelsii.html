Dalbergia nelsii

Dalbergia nelsii, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in dry and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in its native ecosystems.
