Desmodium trichocaulum

Desmodium trichocaulum, commonly known as the slender ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to various regions in Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Desmodium trichocaulum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
