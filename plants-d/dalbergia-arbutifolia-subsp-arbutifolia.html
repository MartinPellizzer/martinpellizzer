Dalbergia arbutifolia subsp. arbutifolia

Dalbergia arbutifolia subsp.

arbutifolia, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties.

The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and often have a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by flat, leathery pods containing several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is known for its ornamental value and economic importance.
