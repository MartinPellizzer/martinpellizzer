Dyschoriste hirsutissima

Dyschoriste hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy dyschoriste, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough texture due to the dense pubescence.

Dyschoriste hirsutissima is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
