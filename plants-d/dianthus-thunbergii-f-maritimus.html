Dianthus thunbergii f. maritimus

Dianthus thunbergii f. maritimus, commonly known as the sea campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and vibrant pink to red petals.

It is a naturalized form of Dianthus thunbergii, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, adapted to withstand saline and windy conditions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
