Diopogon hirtus subsp. allionii

Diopogon hirtus subsp.

allionii, commonly known as the Allion's silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit.

The plant typically forms dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
