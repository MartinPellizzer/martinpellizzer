Dioscorea marginata

Dioscorea marginata, commonly known as the black snake root, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. Its roots are traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its vigorous growth and attractive foliage.
