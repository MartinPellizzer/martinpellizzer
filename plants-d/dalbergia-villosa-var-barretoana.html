Dalbergia villosa var. barretoana

Dalbergia villosa var. barretoana, commonly known as the Barreto's rosewood, is a species of legume native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, with compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruits are pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, tropical forests.

The species is also noted for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and woodworking.
