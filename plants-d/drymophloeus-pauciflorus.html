Drymophloeus pauciflorus

Drymophloeus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered ironwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant typically has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color, followed by globose, woody fruits.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
