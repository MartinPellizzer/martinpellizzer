Diaphyllum triradiatum

Diaphyllum triradiatum, commonly known as the three-rayed diaphyllum, is a rare and lesser-known plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that exhibit a unique radial symmetry. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Diaphyllum triradiatum is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and conservation.
