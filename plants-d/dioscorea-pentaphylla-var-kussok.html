Dioscorea pentaphylla var. kussok

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. kussok, commonly known as Kussok yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its starchy, edible underground tubers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are small and usually white or greenish in color.

The tubers are valued for their nutritional content and are used in traditional cuisines across its native range.
