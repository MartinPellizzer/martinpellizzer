Danthonia anthoxanthiformis

Danthonia anthoxanthiformis, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
