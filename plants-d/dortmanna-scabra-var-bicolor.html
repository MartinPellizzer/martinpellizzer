Dortmanna scabra var. bicolor

Dortmanna scabra var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, clustered flowers. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
