Danhatchia australis

Danhatchia australis, commonly known as the Australian spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of delicate, spider-like flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in open forests or heathlands. It blooms during the cooler months, usually from late autumn to early spring.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
