Dioscorea villosa var. coreana

Dioscorea villosa var. coreana, commonly known as Korean yam, is a perennial vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tubers that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its growth habit involves climbing on supports, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The tubers of this variety are often smaller and have a different texture compared to other yam species.
