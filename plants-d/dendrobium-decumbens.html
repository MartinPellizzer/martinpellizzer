Dendrobium decumbens

Dendrobium decumbens, commonly known as the ground orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and clustered, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique growth habit and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
