Daemonorops oblata

Daemonorops oblata, commonly known as the red-veined areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves with distinctive red veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, spherical berries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching in some regions.
