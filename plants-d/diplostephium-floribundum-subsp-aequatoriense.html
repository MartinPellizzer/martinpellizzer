Diplostephium floribundum subsp. aequatoriense

Diplostephium floribundum subsp.

aequatoriense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian starburst cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It features a cylindrical stem with prominent ribbing and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the dry season.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
