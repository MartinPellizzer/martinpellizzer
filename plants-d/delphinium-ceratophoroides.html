Delphinium ceratophoroides

Delphinium ceratophoroides, commonly known as the horned delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Delphinium ceratophoroides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
