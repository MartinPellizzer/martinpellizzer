Daphne involucrata

Daphne involucrata, commonly known as the woolly daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and showy clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique appearance and winter interest make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
