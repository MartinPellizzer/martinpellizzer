Dichapetalum sulcatum

Dichapetalum sulcatum, commonly known as the African candelabra, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems that resemble a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, textured surface.

Dichapetalum sulcatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
