Desmanthus greveanus

Desmanthus greveanus, commonly known as the yellow indigo, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and wetland areas. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
