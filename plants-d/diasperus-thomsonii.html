Diasperus thomsonii

Diasperus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves.

Diasperus thomsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
