Deyeuxia forsteri var. aristata

Deyeuxia forsteri var. aristata, commonly known as the tufted muirgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
