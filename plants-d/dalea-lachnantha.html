Dalea lachnantha

Dalea lachnantha, commonly known as plains false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Dalea lachnantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
