Diospyros stapfiana

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits.

The fruit's skin is smooth and thin, and it contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, sweet pulp.
