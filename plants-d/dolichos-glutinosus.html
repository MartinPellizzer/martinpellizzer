Dolichos glutinosus

Dolichos glutinosus, commonly known as sticky sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. The seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and have a sticky texture when raw.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its nutritional content and agricultural versatility.
