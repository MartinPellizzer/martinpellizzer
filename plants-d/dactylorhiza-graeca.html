Dactylorhiza graeca

Dactylorhiza graeca, commonly known as the Greek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and prefers shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Dactylorhiza graeca is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
