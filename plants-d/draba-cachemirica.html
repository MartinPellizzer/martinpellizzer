Draba cachemirica

Draba cachemirica, commonly known as the Kashmir draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual or biennial herb that typically grows in alpine and rocky habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are often rosette-shaped and small, alternate leaves along the stem. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the mustard family.

Draba cachemirica is native to the Himalayas, including regions in India, Nepal, and Pakistan.
