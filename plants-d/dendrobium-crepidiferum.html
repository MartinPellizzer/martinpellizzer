Dendrobium crepidiferum

Dendrobium crepidiferum, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, branched stem system with numerous pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium crepidiferum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
