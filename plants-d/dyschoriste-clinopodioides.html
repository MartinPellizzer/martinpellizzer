Dyschoriste clinopodioides

Dyschoriste clinopodioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dyschoriste, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
