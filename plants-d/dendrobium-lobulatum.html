Dendrobium lobulatum

Dendrobium lobulatum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and store water, allowing it to thrive in seasonal climates.

Dendrobium lobulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
