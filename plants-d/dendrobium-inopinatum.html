Dendrobium inopinatum

Dendrobium inopinatum, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a species native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, cane-like stems with clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Dendrobium inopinatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
