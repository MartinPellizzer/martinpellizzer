Dendrophyllanthus rheophilus

Dendrophyllanthus rheophilus, commonly known as the river holly, is a rare aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has a unique morphology with a creeping stem that supports its floating leaves. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by preventing soil erosion along riverbanks.
