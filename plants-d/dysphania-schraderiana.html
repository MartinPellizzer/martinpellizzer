Dysphania schraderiana

Dysphania schraderiana, commonly known as Schrader's Chenopodium, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture and a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Dysphania schraderiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a hardy, drought-tolerant plant.
