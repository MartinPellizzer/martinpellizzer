Dipladenia atroviolacea

Dipladenia atroviolacea, commonly known as the dark violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces striking violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in warm, sunny conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Dipladenia atroviolacea is known for its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or fences with its showy blooms.
