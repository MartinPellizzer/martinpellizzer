Digitalis purpurea subsp. gyspergerae

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

gyspergerae, commonly known as the Gysperger's foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features tall, erect stems that bear whorled clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers.

The leaves are dark green, velvety, and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in medicine.
