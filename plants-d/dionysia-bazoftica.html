Dionysia bazoftica

Dionysia bazoftica, commonly known as the Bazoftian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats.

Dionysia bazoftica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
