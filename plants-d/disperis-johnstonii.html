Disperis johnstonii

Disperis johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's disperis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
