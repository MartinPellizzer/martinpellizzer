Dactylorhiza sphagnicola subsp. sennia

Dactylorhiza sphagnicola subsp.

sennia, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to northern temperate regions. It is a terrestrial herb with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems.

The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive three-lobed lip, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn. This subspecies is often found in acidic, moist habitats such as bogs and fens.

It plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and provides habitat for various insect species.
