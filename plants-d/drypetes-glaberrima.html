Drypetes glaberrima

Drypetes glaberrima, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 10-20 meters and has a dense, rounded canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and yellowish-white, arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
