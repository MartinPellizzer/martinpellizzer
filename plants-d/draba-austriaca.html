Draba austriaca

Draba austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It typically reaches heights of 5 to 20 centimeters and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants.

Draba austriaca is adapted to cold climates and is often among the first plants to bloom in early spring.
