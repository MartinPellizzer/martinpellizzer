Drakaea livida

Drakaea livida, commonly known as the pink dragon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the south-eastern region of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking pink and green floral structure, which features a single, elongated spur. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Drakaea livida is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.

Its blooming period usually occurs between September and November, adding to its ecological and botanical significance.
