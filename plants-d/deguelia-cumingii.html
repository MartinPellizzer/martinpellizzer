Deguelia cumingii

Deguelia cumingii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a mist-like appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Deguelia cumingii is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
