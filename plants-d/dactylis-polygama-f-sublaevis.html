Dactylis polygama f. sublaevis

Dactylis polygama f. sublaevis, commonly known as the silky grass or silky dactylis, is a variant of the dactylis genus native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and narrow, elongated leaves. This plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, pale green flowers in panicle clusters, which are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
