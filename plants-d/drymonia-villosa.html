Drymonia villosa

Drymonia villosa, commonly known as the hairy drymonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features erect stems covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also hairy, contributing to its overall texture.

Small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters, adding to its ecological value in wetland ecosystems.
