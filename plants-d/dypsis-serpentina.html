Dypsis serpentina

Dypsis serpentina, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its stems are segmented and can form clusters, giving it a bamboo-like appearance.

Dypsis serpentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
