Danae laurus

Danae laurus, commonly known as the sacred laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, evergreen leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically orange or red in color, which are edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Danae laurus is valued for its aromatic properties and has been historically used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
