Dudleya crebrifolia

Dudleya crebrifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dudleya crebrifolia is also noted for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid environments.
