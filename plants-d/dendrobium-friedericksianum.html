Dendrobium friedericksianum

Dendrobium friedericksianum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium friedericksianum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
