Dioscorea sambiranensis subsp. sambiranensis

Dioscorea sambiranensis subsp.

sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to Madagascar. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs.

The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and have potential nutritional value, though they are not widely cultivated.

The species is of interest for its unique botanical features and ecological significance in its native habitat.
