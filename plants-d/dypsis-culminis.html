Dypsis culminis

Dypsis culminis, commonly known as the mountain bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Dypsis culminis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
