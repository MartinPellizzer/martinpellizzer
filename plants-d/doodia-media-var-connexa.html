Doodia media var. connexa

Doodia media var. connexa, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a variant of the Doodia media species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its fronds are usually dark green and exhibit a feathery texture, adding a lush aesthetic to the landscape.

The plant reproduces via spores, which are released from the undersides of its fronds.
