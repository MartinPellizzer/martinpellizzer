Dicoma sessiliflora subsp. kirkii

Dicoma sessiliflora subsp.

kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the winter months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is well-suited for cultivation in xeriscaped gardens.
