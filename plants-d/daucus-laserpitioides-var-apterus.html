Daucus laserpitioides var. apterus

Daucus laserpitioides var. apterus, commonly known as the slender carrot, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and edible plants. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its roots are typically thin and not as fleshy as those of the common carrot.
