Daphne giraldii

Daphne giraldii, commonly known as the Sierra daphne, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has smooth, grayish-green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Daphne giraldii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its winter blooming period.
