Drypetes gabonensis

Drypetes gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is typically found in lowland rainforests and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
