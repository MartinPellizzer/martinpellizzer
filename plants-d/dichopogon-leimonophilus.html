Dichopogon leimonophilus

Dichopogon leimonophilus, commonly known as the mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, typical of many grass species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
