Diospyros hasseltii

Diospyros hasseltii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood and is also cultivated for its ornamental and fruit-bearing qualities.
