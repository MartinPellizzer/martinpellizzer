Decussocarpus motleyi

Decussocarpus motleyi, commonly known as the Motley's decussocarp, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive decussate (crosswise) arrangement of its fruiting bodies, which is a key feature in its classification. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, Decussocarpus motleyi is considered a botanical curiosity and is of interest to mycologists and plant enthusiasts.
