Dasycephala

Dasycephala, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange ray flowers surrounded by a ring of tubular florets. Dasycephala typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
