Dryopteris aculeata subsp. angularis

Dryopteris aculeata subsp.

angularis, commonly known as the angular holly fern, is a fern species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds with serrated margins and a unique, angular appearance on the pinnae.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in sporangia clustered in sori.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
