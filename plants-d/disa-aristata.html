Disa aristata

Disa aristata, commonly known as the sword lily, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, sword-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-season color. Disa aristata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in both gardens and collections.
