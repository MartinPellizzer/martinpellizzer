Droogmansia mildbraedii

Droogmansia mildbraedii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette near the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and its ability to attract pollinators.
