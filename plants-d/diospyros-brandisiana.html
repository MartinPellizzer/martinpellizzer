Diospyros brandisiana

Diospyros brandisiana, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a food source.
