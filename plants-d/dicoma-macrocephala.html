Dicoma macrocephala

Dicoma macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features sturdy, hairy stems and broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow-centered flower heads bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dicoma macrocephala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
