Diospyros venablesii

Diospyros venablesii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully mature.

Its fruit is highly valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
