Dioctis equisetifolia

Dioctis equisetifolia, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with dense clusters of needle-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically found at the base of the plant, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its slow growth and unique appearance make it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens and natural habitats.
