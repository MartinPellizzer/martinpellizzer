Dipladenia myriophylla

Dipladenia myriophylla, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets.

Dipladenia myriophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
