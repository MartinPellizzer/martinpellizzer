Dipera villosa

Dipera villosa, commonly known as the hairy dipera, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Iridaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

Dipera villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
