Dichrostachys forbesii

Dichrostachys forbesii, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, clustered inflorescences that produce small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient. Its seeds are encased in hard, spiny pods that aid in seed dispersal by animals.

Dichrostachys forbesii is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value in landscaping.
