Digitaria ctenantha

Digitaria ctenantha, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Digitaria ctenantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
