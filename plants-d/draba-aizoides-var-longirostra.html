Draba aizoides var. longirostra

Draba aizoides var. longirostra, commonly known as the long-beaked whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale yellow to white.

The variety longirostra is distinguished by its elongated, narrow corolla lobes, which set it apart from other Draba species.
