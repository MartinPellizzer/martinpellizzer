Daviesia incrassata var. dorrienii

Daviesia incrassata var. dorrienii, commonly known as the Dorrien's Daviesia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and features small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
