Dendrochilum serratoi

Dendrochilum serratoi, commonly known as the serrated tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, often on the forest floor or in rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the flowers are usually pink or purple with intricate patterns.

Dendrochilum serratoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
