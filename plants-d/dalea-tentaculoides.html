Dalea tentaculoides

Dalea tentaculoides, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow, linear leaflets, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid conditions.

Dalea tentaculoides is often found in disturbed soils and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
