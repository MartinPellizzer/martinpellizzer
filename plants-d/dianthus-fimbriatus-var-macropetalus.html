Dianthus fimbriatus var. macropetalus

Dianthus fimbriatus var. macropetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in clusters on tall, slender stems. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or wildflower displays.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
