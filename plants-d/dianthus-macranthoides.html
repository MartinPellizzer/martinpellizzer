Dianthus macranthoides

Dianthus macranthoides, commonly known as the fringed campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Dianthus macranthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
