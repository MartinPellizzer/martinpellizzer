Dianthus holopetalus

Dianthus holopetalus, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, cylindrical seed pods after flowering.

Dianthus holopetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
