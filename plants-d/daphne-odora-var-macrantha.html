Daphne odora var. macrantha

Daphne odora var. macrantha, commonly known as the fragrant daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically red or orange in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
