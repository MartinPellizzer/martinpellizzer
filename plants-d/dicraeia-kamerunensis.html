Dicraeia kamerunensis

Dicraeia kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Cameroon and neighboring regions in Central Africa. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
