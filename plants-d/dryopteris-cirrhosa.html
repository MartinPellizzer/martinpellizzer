Dryopteris cirrhosa

Dryopteris cirrhosa, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, gracefully arching fronds that are often covered in a cinnamon-colored bloom on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients effectively.

The cinnamon fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
