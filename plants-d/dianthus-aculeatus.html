Dianthus aculeatus

Dianthus aculeatus, commonly known as the prickly carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and stems that are often covered in small, stiff bristles. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Dianthus aculeatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
