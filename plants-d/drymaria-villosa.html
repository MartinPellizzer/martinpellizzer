Drymaria villosa

Drymaria villosa, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, succulent leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to gardens with moist soil conditions.
