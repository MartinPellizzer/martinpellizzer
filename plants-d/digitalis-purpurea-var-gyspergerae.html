Digitalis purpurea var. gyspergerae

Digitalis purpurea var. gyspergerae, commonly known as the Gysperger's foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant produces distinctive spurred petals and has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
