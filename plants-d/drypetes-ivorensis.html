Drypetes ivorensis

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist forested areas and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
