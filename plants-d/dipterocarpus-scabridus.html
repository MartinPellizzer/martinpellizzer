Dipterocarpus scabridus

Dipterocarpus scabridus, commonly known as the rough-skin dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy seeds and buttressed roots. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are arranged in clusters, while its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
