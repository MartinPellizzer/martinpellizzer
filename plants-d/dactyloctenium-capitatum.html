Dactyloctenium capitatum

Dactyloctenium capitatum, commonly known as the cluster grass or flatgrass, is a tropical perennial grass native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant has narrow, flat leaves that are often rolled at the edges, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, it can become invasive in certain environments.
