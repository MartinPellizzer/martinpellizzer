Dracocephalum ruyschiana var. argunense

Dracocephalum ruyschiana var. argunense, commonly known as the Argun mountain hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged oppositely along the stem. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
