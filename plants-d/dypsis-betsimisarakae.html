Dypsis betsimisarakae

Dypsis betsimisarakae, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of cycads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid climate.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
