Dimeria diandra

Dimeria diandra, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Dimeria diandra is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.
