Dianthus subbaeticus

Dianthus subbaeticus, commonly known as the Baetican carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features upright, branched stems and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Dianthus subbaeticus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
