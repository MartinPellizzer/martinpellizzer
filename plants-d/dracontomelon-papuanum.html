Dracontomelon papuanum

Dracontomelon papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific islands, including Papua New Guinea and the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

This species is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
