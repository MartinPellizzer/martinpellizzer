Doria satanica

Doria satanica, commonly known as the devil's flower, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from deep red to orange, with a distinctive corona structure. Native to parts of Central and South America, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to touch or ingest.

Its unique appearance and potent chemical composition have made it a subject of both botanical study and cultural fascination.
