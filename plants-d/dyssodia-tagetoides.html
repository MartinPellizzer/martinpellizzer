Dyssodia tagetoides

Dyssodia tagetoides, commonly known as the golden daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Dyssodia tagetoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
