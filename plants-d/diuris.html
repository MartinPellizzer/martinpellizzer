Diuris

Diuris, commonly known as spider orchids, are a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

They are native to Australia and are characterized by their unique, often intricate flower shapes that resemble spiders, hence their common name. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce flowers in a range of colors, including green, yellow, and purple. Diuris species are often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrive in well-drained environments.

They are popular among horticulturists for their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
