Dinochloa robusta

Dinochloa robusta, commonly known as the giant spikerush, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spike, composed of numerous small, spikelet-like structures. The plant typically grows in marshy areas, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. Its rhizomes form a dense network, helping to stabilize the soil and prevent erosion.

Dinochloa robusta plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
