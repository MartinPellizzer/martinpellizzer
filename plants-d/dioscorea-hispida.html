Dioscorea hispida

Dioscorea hispida, commonly known as rough yam, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Dioscorea hispida is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic effects.

Its cultivation is important in some parts of Africa and Asia for both culinary and therapeutic uses.
