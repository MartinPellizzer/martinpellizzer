Dalea simulatrix

Dalea simulatrix, commonly known as the mimic dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Dalea simulatrix is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
