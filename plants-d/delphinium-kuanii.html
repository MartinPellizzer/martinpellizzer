Delphinium kuanii

Delphinium kuanii, commonly known as the Kuan's larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Delphinium kuanii is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and sometimes toxic compounds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
