Deprea oxapampensis

Deprea oxapampensis, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across the Andes.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
