Dolichos frutescens

Dolichos frutescens, commonly known as the hyacinth bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Dolichos frutescens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural systems.
