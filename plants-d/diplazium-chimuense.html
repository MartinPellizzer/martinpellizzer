Diplazium chimuense

Diplazium chimuense, commonly known as the Chimu fern, is a species of fern native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arching or cascading in appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its rhizomes are relatively short and produce a dense network of roots, contributing to its ability to stabilize soil in its natural habitat.

The fronds are usually green and may have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its distinctive botanical appearance.
