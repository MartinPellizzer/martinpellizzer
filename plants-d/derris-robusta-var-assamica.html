Derris robusta var. assamica

Derris robusta var. assamica, commonly known as the Assam derris, is a climbing legume native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its roots contain rotenone, a natural insecticide that has been traditionally used for pest control.
