Dyckia nobilis

Dyckia nobilis, commonly known as the noble dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette of stiff, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, showy white or pink flowers. Its foliage is adapted to collect and store rainwater, making it well-suited for humid environments.

Dyckia nobilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and resilience.
