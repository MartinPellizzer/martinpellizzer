Durio singaporensis

Durio singaporensis, commonly known as the Singapore durian, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Durio genus, which includes other well-known durian species. The plant produces large, spiky fruits with a strong aroma and a creamy, flavorful pulp. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, while its flowers are typically small and yellowish-green.

Durio singaporensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its unique and highly sought-after fruit.
