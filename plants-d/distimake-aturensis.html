Distimake aturensis

Distimake aturensis, commonly known as the Moonshade Fern, is a unique plant native to the dense, humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that glow faintly under moonlight, a trait that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and has a deep root system that helps it absorb nutrients from the forest floor. Its leaves are rich in chlorophyll, allowing it to photosynthesize efficiently even in low-light conditions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is classified as an endangered species.
