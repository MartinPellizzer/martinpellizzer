Dahlia crocea

Dahlia crocea, commonly known as the red dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are green and slightly hairy. Its tuberous roots are used for propagation, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Dahlia crocea is prized for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
