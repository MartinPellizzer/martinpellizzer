Dianthus armeria f. glabrissimus

Dianthus armeria f. glabrissimus, commonly known as the smooth-flowered sweet William, is a cultivated form of the common carnation.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces flowers in early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
