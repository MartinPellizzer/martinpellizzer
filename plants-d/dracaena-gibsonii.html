Dracaena gibsonii

Dracaena gibsonii, commonly known as the Gibson's Dracaena, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It features long, narrow, dark green leaves that are often slightly curled at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red berries, which are a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Dracaena gibsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
