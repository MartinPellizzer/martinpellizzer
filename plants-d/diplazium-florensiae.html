Diplazium florensiae

Diplazium florensiae, commonly known as the Philippine water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically divided into small, lobed segments. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Diplazium florensiae plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
