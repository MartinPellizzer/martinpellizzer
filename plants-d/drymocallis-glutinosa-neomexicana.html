Drymocallis glutinosa neomexicana

Drymocallis glutinosa neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance, giving it its scientific name "glutinosa.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
