Diplacrum notopterum

Diplacrum notopterum, commonly known as the silver-leafed acanthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and showy, spire-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Diplacrum notopterum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
