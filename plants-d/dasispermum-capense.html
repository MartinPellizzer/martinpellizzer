Dasispermum capense

Dasispermum capense, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its milky sap, which is toxic, is a distinguishing botanical feature.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
