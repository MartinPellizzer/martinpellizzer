Dianthus sylvestris subsp. tergestinus

Dianthus sylvestris subsp.

tergestinus, commonly known as the wild carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help it thrive in rocky or dry soils. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it forms dense clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
