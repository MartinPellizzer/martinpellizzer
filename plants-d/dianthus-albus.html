Dianthus albus

Dianthus albus, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has basal leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette, while the stems are usually unbranched and somewhat hairy. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Dianthus albus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
