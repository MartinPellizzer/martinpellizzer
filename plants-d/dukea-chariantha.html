Dukea chariantha

Dukea chariantha, commonly known as the Amazonian wax palm, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single stem that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its broad, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, Dukea chariantha is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
