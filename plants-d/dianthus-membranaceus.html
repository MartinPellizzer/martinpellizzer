Dianthus membranaceus

Dianthus membranaceus, commonly known as the membranous campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters atop slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known species like carnations and pinks.

Dianthus membranaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
