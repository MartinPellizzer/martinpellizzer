Dracocephalum japonicum

Dracocephalum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese hyssop, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a strong mint-like scent. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Dracocephalum japonicum is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
