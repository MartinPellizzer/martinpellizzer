Dioscorea pubescens

Dioscorea pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and develops tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves twining around supporting structures, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

The tubers of Dioscorea pubescens are sometimes cultivated for their potential medicinal properties.
