Dolichos viridis

Dolichos viridis, commonly known as the green hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant bears elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several edible, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and is often grown for its nutritional value.

Dolichos viridis is valued for its protein-rich seeds and is used in traditional cuisines across parts of Asia and Africa.
