Dorstenia batesii

Dorstenia batesii, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its unique, cup-shaped inflorescence that resembles a buttercup. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often surrounded by a fleshy, tubular structure. Dorstenia batesii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
