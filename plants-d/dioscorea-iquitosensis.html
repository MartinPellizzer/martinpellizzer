Dioscorea iquitosensis

Dioscorea iquitosensis, commonly known as the Iquitos yam, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and nutritional purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in the treatment of various ailments.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal value, it is considered an important species in the biodiversity of the Amazon.
