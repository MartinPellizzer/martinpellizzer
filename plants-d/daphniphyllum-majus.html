Daphniphyllum majus

Daphniphyllum majus, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Daphniphyllum majus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
