Dioscorea urophylla

Dioscorea urophylla, commonly known as the purple yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are small and typically yellow or greenish in color. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often grown for its nutritious and starchy tubers.

It plays an important role in traditional agriculture and cuisine in many parts of the world.
