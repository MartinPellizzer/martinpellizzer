Diplospora truncata

Diplospora truncata, commonly known as the roughleaf spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family.

Diplospora truncata is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and woodland edges.
