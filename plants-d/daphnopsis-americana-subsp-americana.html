Daphnopsis americana subsp. americana

Daphnopsis americana subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American daphnopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shady areas.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring. It is often found in the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
