Dicalix lucida

Dicalix lucida, commonly known as the shining dicalix, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that emit a subtle luminescence in low light conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the evening. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and among plant enthusiasts.
