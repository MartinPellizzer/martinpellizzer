Diospyros viridifolia

Diospyros viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leaved persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for crafting and construction.

Diospyros viridifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks.
