Danthoniopsis aptera

Danthoniopsis aptera, commonly known as the slender danthoniopsis, is a low-growing, mat-forming grass native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. Its small, spikelet-based flowers are inconspicuous, and it reproduces primarily through vegetative propagation.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
