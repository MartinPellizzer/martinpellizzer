Disa spathulata subsp. spathulata

Disa spathulata subsp.

spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved disa, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pink or purple with a distinctive spatulate shape.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
