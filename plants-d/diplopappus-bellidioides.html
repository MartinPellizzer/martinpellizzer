Diplopappus bellidioides

Diplopappus bellidioides, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it a bright and attractive appearance. Its leaves are typically alternate and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Diplopappus bellidioides is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
