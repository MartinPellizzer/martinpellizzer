Dichapetalum palustre var. polyanthum

Dichapetalum palustre var. polyanthum, commonly known as the swampmallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, sometimes boggy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
