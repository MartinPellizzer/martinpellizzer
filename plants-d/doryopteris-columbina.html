Doryopteris columbina

Doryopteris columbina, commonly known as the Pacific shield fern, is a species of fern native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically leathery and dark green. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Doryopteris columbina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
