Dorycnium hispanicum

Dorycnium hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Dorycnium hispanicum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration and agricultural contexts.
