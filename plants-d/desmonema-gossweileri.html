Desmonema gossweileri

Desmonema gossweileri, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often found in savannas and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
