Delphinium sherriffii

Delphinium sherriffii, commonly known as the Sherriff's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium sherriffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be toxic to livestock and humans.
