Diplacus aurantiacus var. calycinus

Diplacus aurantiacus var. calycinus, commonly known as the California honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.
