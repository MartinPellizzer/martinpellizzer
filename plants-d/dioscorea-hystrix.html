Dioscorea hystrix

Dioscorea hystrix, commonly known as the spiny yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized for food in various cultures.

The plant's morphology and growth habits make it well adapted to its humid, tropical habitats.
