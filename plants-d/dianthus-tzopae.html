Dianthus tzopae

Dianthus tzopae, commonly known as the Mexican carnation, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Dianthus tzopae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
