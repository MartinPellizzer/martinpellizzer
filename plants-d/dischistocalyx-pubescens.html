Dischistocalyx pubescens

Dischistocalyx pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in spring. The bark is smooth and may exude a milky sap when damaged.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates.
