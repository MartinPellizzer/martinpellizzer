Dodonaea dubia

Dodonaea dubia, commonly known as the silver dollar tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by spherical, reddish-brown fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Dodonaea dubia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility.
