Dypsis sancta

Dypsis sancta, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a short, thick trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy material. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by green, spiny fruits. Its leaves are palmate, with multiple segments radiating from a central point.

Dypsis sancta is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
