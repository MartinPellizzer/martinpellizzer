Doronicum plantagineum

Doronicum plantagineum, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized landscapes.
