Diplotaxis harra var. intricata

Diplotaxis harra var. intricata, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
