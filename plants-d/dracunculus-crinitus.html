Dracunculus crinitus

Dracunculus crinitus, commonly known as the honey garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features large, showy purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a distinctive, garlic-like odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers open. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Dracunculus crinitus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
