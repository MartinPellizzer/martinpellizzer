Dioscorea alatipes

Dioscorea alatipes, commonly known as the winged yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and develops tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Dioscorea alatipes is valued for its nutritional content and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
