Didymocarpus innominatus

Didymocarpus innominatus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, green, fleshy berries that turn orange-red when mature. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal value.
