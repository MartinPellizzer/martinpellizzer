Diospyros phanerophlebia

Diospyros phanerophlebia, commonly known as the broad-leaved persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange or red, edible fruits. The fruits are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisines, and the wood is valued for its durability and workability.
