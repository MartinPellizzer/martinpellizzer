Damnacanthus moniliformis

Damnacanthus moniliformis, commonly known as the chain flower or red chain flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
