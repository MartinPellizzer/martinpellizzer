Deparia lancea var. lancea

Deparia lancea var. lancea, commonly known as the lanceleaf water fern, is a perennial aquatic fern native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its fronds are usually dark green and exhibit a delicate, feathery appearance.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
