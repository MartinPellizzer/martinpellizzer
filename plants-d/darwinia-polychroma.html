Darwinia polychroma

Darwinia polychroma, commonly known as the rainbow darwinia, is a striking flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant, colorful flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, creating a striking visual display. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Darwinia polychroma thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
