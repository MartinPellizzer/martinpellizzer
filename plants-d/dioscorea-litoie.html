Dioscorea litoie

Dioscorea litoie, commonly known as the Litoie yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish flowers followed by cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source in various African communities.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
