Dombeya punctata var. ficulnea

Dombeya punctata var. ficulnea, commonly known as the pink dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features showy, pink or white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
