Drymocallis glandulosa subsp. arizonica

Drymocallis glandulosa subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as Arizona gallflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
