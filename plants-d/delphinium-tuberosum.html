Delphinium tuberosum

Delphinium tuberosum, commonly known as the tuberous delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of lance-shaped leaflets.

Delphinium tuberosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
