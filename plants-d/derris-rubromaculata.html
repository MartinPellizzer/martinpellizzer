Derris rubromaculata

Derris rubromaculata, commonly known as the red-spotted derris, is a tropical climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with red-brown spots, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant's roots contain rotenone, a natural insecticide used in pest control.
