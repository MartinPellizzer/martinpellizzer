Dasyglossum retusum

Dasyglossum retusum, commonly known as the rough-tongued orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a prominent spur, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dasyglossum retusum is often found in rich, organic soils and is relatively rare in the wild.
