Dicentra cucullaria var. occidentalis

Dicentra cucullaria var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting purple or red base. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy, and it thrives in moist, woodland environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
