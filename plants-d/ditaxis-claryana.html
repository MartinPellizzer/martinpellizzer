Ditaxis claryana

Ditaxis claryana, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
