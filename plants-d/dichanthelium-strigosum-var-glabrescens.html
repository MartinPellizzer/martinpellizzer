Dichanthelium strigosum var. glabrescens

Dichanthelium strigosum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, and its inflorescence consists of a panicled arrangement of spikelets. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are followed by minute, dry seeds.

This variety is characterized by its reduced hairiness compared to the typical form, giving it a smoother appearance.
