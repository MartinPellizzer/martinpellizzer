Disperma quadrangulare

Disperma quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angled spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features distinctive quadrangular (four-sided) stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Disperma quadrangulare is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
