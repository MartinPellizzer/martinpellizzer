Damnacanthus indicus var. latifolius

Damnacanthus indicus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf cowplant, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features lance-shaped, often hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers, which are borne in terminal inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
