Dioscorea kalkapershadii

Dioscorea kalkapershadii, commonly known as the Kalkapuri yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
