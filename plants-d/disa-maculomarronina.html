Disa maculomarronina

Disa maculomarronina, commonly known as the spotted marsh orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers with distinctive spots. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and grass-like, often forming dense tufts at the base of the plant.

Disa maculomarronina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
