Dianthus furcatus var. pedemontanus

Dianthus furcatus var. pedemontanus, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy and sturdy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, high-altitude conditions.
