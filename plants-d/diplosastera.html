Diplosastera

Diplosastera, commonly known as the star-tailed orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a dense cluster of small, star-shaped flowers. The plants are typically epiphytic, growing on trees in tropical and subtropical regions. They have strap-like leaves and produce showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

Diplosastera species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
