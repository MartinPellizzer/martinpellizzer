Draba modesta

Draba modesta, commonly known as the modest whitlowgrass, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Draba modesta has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, and its flowers are usually pale yellow or white.

It is an herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring, contributing to the alpine flora's diversity.
