Diospyros chrysocarpa

Diospyros chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, mealy pulp.

The fruit is typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
