Dendrobium euphues

Dendrobium euphues, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. The plant features long, cane-like stems that are covered in dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its blooms, which range in color from yellow to orange, are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Dendrobium euphues is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
