Disocactus cinnabarinus

Disocactus cinnabarinus, commonly known as the red-crested cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green with red or orange spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
