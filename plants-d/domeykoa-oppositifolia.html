Domeykoa oppositifolia

Domeykoa oppositifolia, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to southern Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the mild, temperate climate of its native region.

Domeykoa oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
