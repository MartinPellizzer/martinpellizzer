Diospyros falyi

Diospyros falyi, commonly known as the Falyi persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and other tropical hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elongated, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

The species is valued for its timber and has potential for use in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
