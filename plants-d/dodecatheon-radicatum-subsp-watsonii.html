Dodecatheon radicatum subsp. watsonii

Dodecatheon radicatum subsp.

watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's shooting star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, pink to purple flowers that resemble stars, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces elongated flower stalks that rise above the foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the Greek word for "twelve," likely referencing the number of petals in its flowers.
