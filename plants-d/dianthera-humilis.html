Dianthera humilis

Dianthera humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Dianthera humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
