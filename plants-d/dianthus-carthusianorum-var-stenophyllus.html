Dianthus carthusianorum var. stenophyllus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with prominent, showy petals. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its varietal name "stenophyllus," which translates to "narrow leaf.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
