Dactylorhiza × estonica

Dactylorhiza × estonica, commonly known as the Estonian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to northern Europe.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant produces tall, erect stems with lanceolate leaves and bears spike-like inflorescences of fragrant, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers. It is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in wetland habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in many regions.
