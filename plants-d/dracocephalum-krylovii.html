Dracocephalum krylovii

Dracocephalum krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's bluehead, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, clustered in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
