Drosera × neglecta

Drosera × neglecta, commonly known as the hybrid sundew, is a carnivorous plant that combines traits from its parent species, Drosera binata and Drosera intermedia.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs on its leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its flowers are usually small and white or pink, arranged in a dense inflorescence.

Drosera × neglecta is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
