Dioscorea comorensis

Dioscorea comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to the Comoros Islands and other parts of East Africa, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces tuberous roots that are harvested for their edible and medicinal properties. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive appearance.

Dioscorea comorensis is valued both as a food source and for its traditional use in local herbal medicine.
