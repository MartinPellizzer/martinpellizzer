Dioscorea hockii

Dioscorea hockii, commonly known as the Hockii yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and cylindrical, hairy fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
