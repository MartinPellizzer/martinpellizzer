Diplocaulobium arachne

Diplocaulobium arachne, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
