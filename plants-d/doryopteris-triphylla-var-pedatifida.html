Doryopteris triphylla var. pedatifida

Doryopteris triphylla var. pedatifida, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Doryopteris.

It is characterized by its large, triangular, and often lobed fronds that are typically borne on long, flexible petioles. The plant is aquatic or semi-aquatic, often found in slow-moving freshwater environments. Its fronds are usually divided into three parts, giving it the common name "triphylla," which means "three-leaved.".

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
