Delphinium chrysotrichum var. pygmaeum

Delphinium chrysotrichum var. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf larkspur, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It features delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
