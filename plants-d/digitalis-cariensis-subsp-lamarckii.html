Digitalis cariensis subsp. lamarckii

Digitalis cariensis subsp.

lamarckii, commonly known as the European foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, hooded flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. Digitalis cariensis subsp.

lamarckii contains cardiac glycosides, making it both toxic and historically significant in medicine.
