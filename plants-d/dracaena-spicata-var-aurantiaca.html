Dracaena spicata var. aurantiaca

Dracaena spicata var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden dracaena, is a striking evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are a vibrant yellow-green, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red berries, adding to its ornamental value.

The leaves are also resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its ease of care.
