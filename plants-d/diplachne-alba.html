Diplachne alba

Diplachne alba, commonly known as white grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in dense clusters. Diplachne alba is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to different habitats, contributing to its widespread distribution.
