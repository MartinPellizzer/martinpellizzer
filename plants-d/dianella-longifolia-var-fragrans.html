Dianella longifolia var. fragrans

Dianella longifolia var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen lily, is a perennial plant native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, and it produces striking, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage, which emits a sweet fragrance, especially when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.

This variety is a hardy, low-maintenance plant suitable for both garden beds and container cultivation.
