Dianthus × burciae

Dianthus × burciae, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a cross between Dianthus chinensis and Dianthus barbatus, resulting in a variety that combines the characteristics of both parents. The plant is known for its showy, clustered flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Dianthus × burciae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various climates.
