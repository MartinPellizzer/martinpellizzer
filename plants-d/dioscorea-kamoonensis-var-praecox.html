Dioscorea kamoonensis var. praecox

Dioscorea kamoonensis var. praecox, commonly known as early yam, is a tuberous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its stolons and tuberous roots that are used for propagation and food. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its tubers are often harvested for their nutritional value, containing starch and various bioactive compounds.

The species is of interest in both traditional medicine and agricultural research due to its adaptability and potential health benefits.
