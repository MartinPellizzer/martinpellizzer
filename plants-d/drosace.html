Drosace

Drosace, commonly known as the common daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads composed of numerous small flowers, including ray florets and disk florets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Drosace is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas where it plays a role in pollination and soil stabilization.
