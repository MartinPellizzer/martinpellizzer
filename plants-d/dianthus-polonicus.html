Dianthus polonicus

Dianthus polonicus, commonly known as the Polish carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant produces compact, bushy growth with narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, often reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

Dianthus polonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
