Drake-brockmania

Drake-brockmania, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying solely on its roots for sustenance. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique morphology and specialized habitat requirements make it a subject of botanical interest.

Conservation efforts are crucial due to its endangered status and sensitivity to environmental changes.
