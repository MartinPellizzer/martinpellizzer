Dianthus marschallii

Dianthus marschallii, commonly known as the marsh campion, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has alternate, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Dianthus marschallii is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh native habitats.
