Digraphis arundinacea var. picta

Digraphis arundinacea var. picta, commonly known as the Painted Reed, is a variant of the common reed that exhibits striking visual characteristics.

It is a tall, grass-like plant that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant's foliage is distinguished by its variegated coloration, featuring shades of green, white, and sometimes pink or red. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist conditions, making it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.
