Dendrobrychis cornuta

Dendrobrychis cornuta, commonly known as the horned acacia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences and small, yellowish flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Dendrobrychis cornuta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
