Dichrostachys cinerea subsp. keniensis

Dichrostachys cinerea subsp.

keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan burr bush, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds.

The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats. It has a woody stem with a rough, fibrous texture and produces compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of fuelwood.
