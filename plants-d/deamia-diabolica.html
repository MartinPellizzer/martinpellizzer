Deamia diabolica

Deamia diabolica, commonly known as the devil's flame, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vivid red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Deamia diabolica is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
