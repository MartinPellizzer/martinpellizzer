Dendrolirium acuminatum

Dendrolirium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of true lilies. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dendrolirium acuminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
