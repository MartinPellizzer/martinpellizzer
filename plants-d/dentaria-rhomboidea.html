Dentaria rhomboidea

Dentaria rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its palmate leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Dentaria rhomboidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
