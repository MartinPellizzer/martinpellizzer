Dicranopygium mirabile

Dicranopygium mirabile, commonly known as the golden moss, is a unique species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and vibrant golden-green coloration, which becomes more pronounced during the growing season. The plant features elongated, overlapping leaves that are often curled at the tips, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and conservation areas.
