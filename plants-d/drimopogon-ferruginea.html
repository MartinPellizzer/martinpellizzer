Drimopogon ferruginea

Drimopogon ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in dense clusters. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant's name refers to the rust-colored hairs on its stems and leaves.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and small mammals.
