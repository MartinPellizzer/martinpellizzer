Deyeuxia sylvatica var. ligulata

Deyeuxia sylvatica var. ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a dense, tufted form. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-sided spikelets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
