Diplotaxis tenuisiliqua var. rupestris

Diplotaxis tenuisiliqua var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing rosette of gray-green, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.
