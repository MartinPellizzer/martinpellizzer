Duschekia alnobetula

Duschekia alnobetula, commonly known as the silver birch, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and delicate, finely toothed leaves that turn golden in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in woodland or open areas.

Duschekia alnobetula is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
