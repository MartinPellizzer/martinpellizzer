Dimorphocalyx meeboldii

Dimorphocalyx meeboldii, commonly known as the African candle bush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact size and seasonal floral display.
