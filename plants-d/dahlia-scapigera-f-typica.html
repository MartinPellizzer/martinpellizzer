Dahlia scapigera f. typica

Dahlia scapigera f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the scapigera dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its botanical structure supports its ornamental value, with its striking blooms and robust growth habit.
