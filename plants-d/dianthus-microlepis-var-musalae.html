Dianthus microlepis var. musalae

Dianthus microlepis var. musalae, commonly known as the Kashmiri carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often used in traditional floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in cold climates.
