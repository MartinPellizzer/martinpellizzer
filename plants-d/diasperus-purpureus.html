Diasperus purpureus

Diasperus purpureus, commonly known as the purple spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces spikes of purple to pinkish flowers in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities in its range.
