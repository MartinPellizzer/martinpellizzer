Dyssodia squamosa

Dyssodia squamosa, commonly known as the sand daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland areas. The plant features erect stems covered in small, scale-like structures, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in a dense, compact inflorescence. It has small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dyssodia squamosa is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
