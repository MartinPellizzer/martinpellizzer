Dianthus arenarius subsp. pseudosquarrosus

Dianthus arenarius subsp.

pseudosquarrosus, commonly known as the sand carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and delicate appearance.
