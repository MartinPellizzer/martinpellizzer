Deutzia gracilis variegata

Deutzia gracilis variegata, commonly known as the variegated deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are oval-shaped with a distinctive variegated pattern of white and green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
