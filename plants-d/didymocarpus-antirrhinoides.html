Didymocarpus antirrhinoides

Didymocarpus antirrhinoides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Didymocarpus antirrhinoides make it a popular choice for gardens and as a shade tree in tropical landscapes.
