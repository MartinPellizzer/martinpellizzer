Dinosperma longifolia

Dinosperma longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Dinosperma longifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
