Dryas octopetala subsp. tschonoskii

Dryas octopetala subsp.

tschonoskii, commonly known as the Tschonoskii mountain avens, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine and subalpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features a dense mat of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help it withstand cold and arid conditions.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
