Deuterocohnia scapigera subsp. scapigera

Deuterocohnia scapigera subsp.

scapigera, commonly known as the scaly ground orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, scaly leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
