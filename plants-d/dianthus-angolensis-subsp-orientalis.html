Dianthus angolensis subsp. orientalis

Dianthus angolensis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that typically range in color from pink to white.

The plant features erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation. Dianthus angolensis subsp.

orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in floral arrangements.
