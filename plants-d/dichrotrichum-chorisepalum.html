Dichrotrichum chorisepalum

Dichrotrichum chorisepalum, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Dichrotrichum chorisepalum is a member of the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
