Diplusodon quintuplinervius

Diplusodon quintuplinervius, commonly known as the five-veined fern, is a unique species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate fronds that display five distinct primary veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

The spore-producing structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
