Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. vaginatus

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

vaginatus, commonly known as the Carthusian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, leafy bracts that surround the flower cluster, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its fragrance and historical use in herbal medicine.
