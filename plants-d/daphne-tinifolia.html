Daphne tinifolia

Daphne tinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that provide ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Daphne tinifolia is noted for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
