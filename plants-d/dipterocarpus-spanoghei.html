Dipterocarpus spanoghei

Dipterocarpus spanoghei, commonly known as the Philippine dipterocarp, is a large evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its buttressed roots and thick, flaky bark. The tree produces large, showy yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

This species plays a crucial role in the tropical rainforest ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
