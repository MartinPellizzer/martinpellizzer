Didymocarpus kurzii

Didymocarpus kurzii, commonly known as the Kashmir cherry or Himalayan cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are edible. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
