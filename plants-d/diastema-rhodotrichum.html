Diastema rhodotrichum

Diastema rhodotrichum, commonly known as the red-haired grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine, hairy structures. Its flowers are typically greenish or reddish in color, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Diastema rhodotrichum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such habitats.
