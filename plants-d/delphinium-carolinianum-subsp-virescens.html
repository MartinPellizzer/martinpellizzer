Delphinium carolinianum subsp. virescens

Delphinium carolinianum subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the Carolina larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features tall, sparsely branched stems covered in blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals and toxic alkaloids in its foliage.
