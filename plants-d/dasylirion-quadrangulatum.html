Dasylirion quadrangulatum

Dasylirion quadrangulatum, commonly known as the four-angle yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern and have a distinctive four-sided structure. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing clusters of white or pale blue flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
