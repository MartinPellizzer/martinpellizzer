Dudleya lurida

Dudleya lurida, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a succulent plant native to California and Baja California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple during the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Dudleya lurida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
