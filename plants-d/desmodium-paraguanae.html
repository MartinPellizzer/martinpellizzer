Desmodium paraguanae

Desmodium paraguanae, commonly known as the paraguayan burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in hairy, spiny pods, which aid in seed dispersal.
