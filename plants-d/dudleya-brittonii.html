Dudleya brittonii

Dudleya brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the coastal areas of California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Dudleya brittonii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
