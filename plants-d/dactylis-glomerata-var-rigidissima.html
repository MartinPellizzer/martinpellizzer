Dactylis glomerata var. rigidissima

Dactylis glomerata var. rigidissima, commonly known as rigid fescue, is a perennial grass species characterized by its stiff, upright growth habit.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, spikelet-based flowers. It is valued for its hardiness and is commonly used in forage and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for agricultural and ecological applications.
