Delphinium maackianum f. lasiocarpum

Delphinium maackianum f. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the Maack's larkspur, is a botanical variant of the larkspur genus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant is notable for its unique fruit structure, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it the specific epithet *lasiocarpum*.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
