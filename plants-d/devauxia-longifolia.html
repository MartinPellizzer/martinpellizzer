Devauxia longifolia

Devauxia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf devauxia, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
