Derris elegans var. vestita

Derris elegans var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet senna, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release small, bean-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
