Dendrocalamus dumosus

Dendrocalamus dumosus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clumping form, which distinguishes it from other bamboo species. The plant produces slender, green culms that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Dendrocalamus dumosus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
