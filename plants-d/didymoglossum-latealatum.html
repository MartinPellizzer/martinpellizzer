Didymoglossum latealatum

Didymoglossum latealatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bog moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and fens.

It has slender, spirally arranged stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in length. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like sporangia that release spores for reproduction. It is often found in temperate regions and is an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.

Its delicate structure and preference for specific habitats make it a valuable subject for ecological studies.
