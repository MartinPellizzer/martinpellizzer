Diastella

Diastella, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce clusters of small, colorful florets. Diastella species are often found in rocky or sandy soils and are adapted to dry, sunny environments.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm climates.
