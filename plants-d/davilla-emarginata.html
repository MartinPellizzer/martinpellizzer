Davilla emarginata

Davilla emarginata, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate with a distinctive notched base, and it often grows in moist, shaded areas.

Davilla emarginata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
