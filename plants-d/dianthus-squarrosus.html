Dianthus squarrosus

Dianthus squarrosus, commonly known as the woolly pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

Dianthus squarrosus is a perennial herb that is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
