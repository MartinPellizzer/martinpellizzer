Dimorphandra gardneriana

Dimorphandra gardneriana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its thick, deeply furrowed bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for construction and tool-making.

Dimorphandra gardneriana is also adapted to drought conditions, making it resilient in arid environments.
