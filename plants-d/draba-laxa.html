Draba laxa

Draba laxa, commonly known as the slender whitlow grass, is a low-growing annual or biennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Draba laxa is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants.
