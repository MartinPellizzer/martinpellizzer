Dioscorea nigrescens

Dioscorea nigrescens, commonly known as black nightshade yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are typically dark-colored and have a starchy, slightly bitter taste.

Dioscorea nigrescens is valued for its nutritional content and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
