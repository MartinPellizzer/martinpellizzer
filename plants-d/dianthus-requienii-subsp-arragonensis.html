Dianthus requienii subsp. arragonensis

Dianthus requienii subsp.

arragonensis, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and prominent white or pink stamens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
