Disa dichroa

Disa dichroa, commonly known as the two-color disa, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking dichroic flowers that change color depending on the angle of light, displaying shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate flowers in late spring.

Disa dichroa is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique color-changing properties.
