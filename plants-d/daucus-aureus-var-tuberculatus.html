Daucus aureus var. tuberculatus

Daucus aureus var. tuberculatus, commonly known as the golden bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its fruit is a bur-like structure that contains several seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
