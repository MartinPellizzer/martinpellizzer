Denea forsteriana

Denea forsteriana, commonly known as the Forster's dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a crown of white seeds. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, sandy soils. It has small, hairy leaves and produces a milky sap that is characteristic of the genus.

Denea forsteriana is often found in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
