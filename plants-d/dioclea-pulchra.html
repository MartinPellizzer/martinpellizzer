Dioclea pulchra

Dioclea pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful dioclea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with pinnate leaves and compound inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

Dioclea pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic value.
