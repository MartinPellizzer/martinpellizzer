Disa subscutellifera

Disa subscutellifera, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking South African orchid species belonging to the genus Disa in the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its vivid purple or violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive yellow or white lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions, and prefers partial shade to full sun. Its botanical features include tall, erect stems with narrow leaves and a compact inflorescence bearing numerous small, showy flowers.

Disa subscutellifera is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological significance in its native habitat.
