Dioscorea costaricensis

Dioscorea costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican yam, is a vine-like perennial plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its tubers are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes.

The species is adapted to humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
