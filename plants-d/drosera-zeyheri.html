Drosera zeyheri

Drosera zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a digestive fluid to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its leaves are usually small and rosette-shaped, with a bright red or purple coloration.

Drosera zeyheri is a perennial species that produces small white flowers during the warmer months.
