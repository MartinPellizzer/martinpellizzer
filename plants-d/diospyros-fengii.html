Diospyros fengii

Diospyros fengii, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is highly valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
