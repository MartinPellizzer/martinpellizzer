Delphinium latisepalum

Delphinium latisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal delphinium, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several lance-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium latisepalum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
