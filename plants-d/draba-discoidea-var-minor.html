Draba discoidea var. minor

Draba discoidea var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, glaucous leaves and produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in mountainous and subarctic habitats. It has a compact growth form, which helps it withstand harsh weather conditions.

Draba discoidea var. minor is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its mustard-like flowers and capsule fruits.
