Dendrobium faciferum

Dendrobium faciferum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that often branch and produce clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrate to prevent root rot. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dendrobium faciferum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
