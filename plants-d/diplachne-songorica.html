Diplachne songorica

Diplachne songorica, commonly known as the Songorica grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often hidden among the foliage.

Diplachne songorica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
