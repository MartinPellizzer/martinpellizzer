Discocactus semicampaniflorus

Discocactus semicampaniflorus, commonly known as the half-pipe cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of pale green or blue-gray spines. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the evening. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil quickly.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
