Dendrochilum migueldavidii

Dendrochilum migueldavidii, commonly known as the David's tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with reddish markings, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

Dendrochilum migueldavidii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
