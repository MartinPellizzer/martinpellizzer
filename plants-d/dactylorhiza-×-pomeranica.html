Dactylorhiza × pomeranica

Dactylorhiza × pomeranica, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes, bogs, and meadows. It has a complex root system and is often found in association with other orchid species.

Dactylorhiza × pomeranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
