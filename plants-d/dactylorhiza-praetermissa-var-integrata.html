Dactylorhiza praetermissa var. integrata

Dactylorhiza praetermissa var. integrata, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or heathland habitats. It has a complex root system with mycorrhizal associations that aid in nutrient uptake.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in naturalized areas.
