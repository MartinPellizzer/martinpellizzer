Declieuxia deltoidea var. perfoliata

Declieuxia deltoidea var. perfoliata, commonly known as the delta leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, with the lower leaves forming a hollow structure that allows the stem to pass through. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
