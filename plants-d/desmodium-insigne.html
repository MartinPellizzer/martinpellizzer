Desmodium insigne

Desmodium insigne, commonly known as the purple crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Desmodium insigne is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which can enhance soil fertility in agricultural settings.
