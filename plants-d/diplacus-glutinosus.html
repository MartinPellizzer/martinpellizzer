Diplacus glutinosus

Diplacus glutinosus, commonly known as sticky monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and forested areas. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies due to their nectar-rich structure.

Diplacus glutinosus is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
