Delphinium caudatolobum

Delphinium caudatolobum, commonly known as the long-tailed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive lobed base, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and sometimes toxic properties.
