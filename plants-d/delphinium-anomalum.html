Delphinium anomalum

Delphinium anomalum, commonly known as the anomalous larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
