Dendrobium aurantiiflavum

Dendrobium aurantiiflavum, commonly known as the golden-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with numerous pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive red lip, blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its humid tropical environment.

Dendrobium aurantiiflavum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
