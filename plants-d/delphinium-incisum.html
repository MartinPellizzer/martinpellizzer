Delphinium incisum

Delphinium incisum, commonly known as the cut-leaved larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Delphinium incisum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
