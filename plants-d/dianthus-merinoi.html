Dianthus merinoi

Dianthus merinoi, commonly known as the Merino's campion, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many showy and colorful species. The plant features delicate, pink or white flowers with five petals, often arranged in a cluster at the top of slender stems. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Dianthus merinoi thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
