Delphinium menziesii var. ochroleucum

Delphinium menziesii var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, with a hairy texture.

This variety is distinguished by its pale, almost white underside, giving it its common name.
