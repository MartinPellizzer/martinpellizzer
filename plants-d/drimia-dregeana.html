Drimia dregeana

Drimia dregeana, commonly known as the Cape daffodil, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive green throat. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Drimia dregeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
