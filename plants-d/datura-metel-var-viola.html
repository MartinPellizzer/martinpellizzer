Datura metel var. viola

Datura metel var. viola, commonly known as the viola datura, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces spiny, egg-shaped fruits that contain toxic seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its potent alkaloids.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
