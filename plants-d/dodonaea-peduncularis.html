Dodonaea peduncularis

Dodonaea peduncularis, commonly known as the coast screwbean, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are typically followed by the distinctive pods. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dry, sunny environments.

Dodonaea peduncularis is adapted to withstand salt spray and is commonly used in coastal landscaping.
