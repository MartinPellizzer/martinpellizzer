Dimorphandra campinarum

Dimorphandra campinarum, commonly known as the Natal bean tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
