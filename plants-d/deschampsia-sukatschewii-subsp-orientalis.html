Deschampsia sukatschewii subsp. orientalis

Deschampsia sukatschewii subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Asia, including China and Central Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and along riverbanks.

The plant features slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, branched clusters of flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery appearance.
