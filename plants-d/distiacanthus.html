Distiacanthus

Distiacanthus, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit and its striking, bright yellow to gold-colored fronds. The plant features small, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact and ornamental appearance. Distiacanthus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique coloration and foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental use.
