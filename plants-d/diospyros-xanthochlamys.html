Diospyros xanthochlamys

Diospyros xanthochlamys, commonly known as the yellow-leaf persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant odor to attract pollinators.

The fruit is a fleshy drupe, often orange to yellow in color, and is edible when fully ripe.
