Duranta brachypoda

Duranta brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed glory bush, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often followed by blue or purple berries. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a velvety texture.

Duranta brachypoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
