Dioclea rubiginosa

Dioclea rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed spider plant, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
