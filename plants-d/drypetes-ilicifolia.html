Drypetes ilicifolia

Drypetes ilicifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
