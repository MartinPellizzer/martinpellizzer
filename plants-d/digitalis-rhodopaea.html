Digitalis rhodopaea

Digitalis rhodopaea, commonly known as the Greek foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Digitalis rhodopaea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
