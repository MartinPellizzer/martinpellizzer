Desmonema mucronulatum var. schweinfurthii

Desmonema mucronulatum var. schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small, spiky holly berries. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
