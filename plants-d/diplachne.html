Diplachne

Diplachne, commonly known as false grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It consists of several species that are typically annual or perennial herbs, characterized by their slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. These plants are often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields, across tropical and subtropical regions. Diplachne species produce small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

They are sometimes considered invasive in certain areas due to their rapid growth and ability to colonize new environments.
