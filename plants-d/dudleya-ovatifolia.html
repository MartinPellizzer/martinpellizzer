Dudleya ovatifolia

Dudleya ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Dudleya ovatifolia is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid environments.
