Diodia vaginalis

Diodia vaginalis, commonly known as swamp mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats, including swamps and marshes. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.
