Dracophyllum secundum

Dracophyllum secundum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dracophyllum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It produces small, red berries that are eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Dracophyllum secundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
