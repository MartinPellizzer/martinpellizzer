Dryopteris calophylla

Dryopteris calophylla, commonly known as the glossy wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are often covered with a waxy layer, giving them a glossy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, upright growth habit.

Dryopteris calophylla plays an important role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
