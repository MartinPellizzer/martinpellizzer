Delissea coriacea var. pinnatiloba

Delissea coriacea var. pinnatiloba, commonly known as the rough-leaved delissea, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a coarse, textured surface. The leaves are typically elongated and have a pinnately lobed or divided structure, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or white in color.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
