Diplachne divaricatissima

Diplachne divaricatissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Diplachne divaricatissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
