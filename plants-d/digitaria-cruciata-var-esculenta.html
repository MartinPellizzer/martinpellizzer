Digitaria cruciata var. esculenta

Digitaria cruciata var. esculenta, commonly known as African ricegrass, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, while the roots are fibrous and well-developed.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
