Dianthus tlaratensis

Dianthus tlaratensis, commonly known as the Mexican carnation, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Dianthus tlaratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
