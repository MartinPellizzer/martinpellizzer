Delphinium anthoroideum var. sclerocladum

Delphinium anthoroideum var. sclerocladum, commonly known as the mountain delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a rough, hairy texture, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
