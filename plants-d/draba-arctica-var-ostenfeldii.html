Draba arctica var. ostenfeldii

Draba arctica var. ostenfeldii, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in low-nutrient environments.

This variety is often found in tundra and mountainous areas across the northern hemisphere.
