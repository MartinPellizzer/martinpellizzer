Dieffenbachia

Dieffenbachia, commonly known as the flamingo plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often feature bold patterns of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden beneath a leaf-like structure called a spathe. Dieffenbachia is a popular houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Its ability to thrive in low-light conditions makes it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
