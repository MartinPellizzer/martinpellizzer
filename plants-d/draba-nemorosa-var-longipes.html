Draba nemorosa var. longipes

Draba nemorosa var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are elongated and often branched.

The flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, bloom in early spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
