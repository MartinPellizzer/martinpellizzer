Dactylorhiza lapponica subsp. russowii

Dactylorhiza lapponica subsp.

russowii, commonly known as the Lapland orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is a terrestrial orchid with a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces a single spike of pale yellow to greenish flowers.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and grows in moist, acidic peat or mineral soils. It is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and climate change impacts.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations and role in Arctic ecosystems.
