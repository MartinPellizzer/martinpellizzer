Daphne meisneriana

Daphne meisneriana, commonly known as the Meisner's spurge or Meisner's daphne, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and interesting foliage.
