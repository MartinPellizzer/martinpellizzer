Disperis decipiens

Disperis decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive disperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often camouflaged to resemble leaves, a trait that aids in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
