Diospyros roxburghii

Diospyros roxburghii, commonly known as Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by clustered, orange-red fruits resembling persimmons.

Its fruits are edible, sweet, and often used in traditional cuisines, while the wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and tool-making.
