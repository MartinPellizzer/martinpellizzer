Draba aizoides var. longistyla

Draba aizoides var. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high elevations. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, rounded capsules as fruit.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated stigmas, which are a key feature in its taxonomic classification.
