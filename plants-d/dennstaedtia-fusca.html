Dennstaedtia fusca

Dennstaedtia fusca, commonly known as the purple fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

The fronds are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat.
