Dactylorchis maculata subsp. islandica

Dactylorchis maculata subsp.

islandica, commonly known as the Island Checkered Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found on the island of Crete. It is characterized by its distinctive dark purple or black spots on its pale green leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a subtle pink or purple hue. It thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats such as rocky slopes and open woodlands.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native range.
