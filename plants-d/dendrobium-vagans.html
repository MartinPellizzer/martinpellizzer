Dendrobium vagans

Dendrobium vagans, commonly known as the wandering orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium vagans is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
