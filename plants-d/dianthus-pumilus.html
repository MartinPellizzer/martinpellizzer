Dianthus pumilus

Dianthus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with bright pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that resemble miniature carnations. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus pumilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and easy maintenance in garden settings.
