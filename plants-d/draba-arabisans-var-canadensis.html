Draba arabisans var. canadensis

Draba arabisans var. canadensis, commonly known as Canadian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and riverbanks.

This variant is native to North America and is adapted to temperate climates.
