Dianthus pavlovii

Dianthus pavlovii, commonly known as the Pavlov's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Dianthus pavlovii is a hardy perennial that is often found in mountainous areas across Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Tajikistan.
