Daphne yatabeana

Daphne yatabeana, commonly known as the Japanese daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and seasonal bloom, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic appeal.
