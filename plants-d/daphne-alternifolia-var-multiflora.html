Daphne alternifolia var. multiflora

Daphne alternifolia var. multiflora, commonly known as the variable daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during late winter to early spring. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish-brown color, and it prefers well-drained soils in partial to full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and early blooming habit in gardens.
