Debregeasia wallichiana subsp. ceylanica

Debregeasia wallichiana subsp.

ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in alpine gardens.
