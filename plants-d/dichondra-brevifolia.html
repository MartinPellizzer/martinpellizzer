Dichondra brevifolia

Dichondra brevifolia, commonly known as silverleaf dichondra, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, round, silver-gray leaves that are arranged opposite along the stems. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to spread rapidly make it both ornamental and invasive in certain regions.
