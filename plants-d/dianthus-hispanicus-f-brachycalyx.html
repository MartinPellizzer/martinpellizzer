Dianthus hispanicus f. brachycalyx

Dianthus hispanicus f. brachycalyx, commonly known as the narrow-calyx carnation, is a compact, flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, with tightly packed petals that give it a full, ruffled appearance. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit, suitable for both ornamental and cutting gardens.
