Dendrobium kingianum

Dendrobium kingianum, commonly known as the King's Dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Dendrobium kingianum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
