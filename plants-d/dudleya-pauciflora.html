Dudleya pauciflora

Dudleya pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to California and Baja California.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dudleya pauciflora is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid environments.
