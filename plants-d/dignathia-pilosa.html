Dignathia pilosa

Dignathia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulbous stem covered in dense, white, hair-like structures that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a mild garlic-like aroma.

Dignathia pilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a culinary herb in traditional Mediterranean cuisine.
