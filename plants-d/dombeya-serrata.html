Dombeya serrata

Dombeya serrata, commonly known as the serrated dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels and are often fragrant.

Dombeya serrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
