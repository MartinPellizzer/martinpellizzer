Deutzia crenata f. plena

Deutzia crenata f. plena, commonly known as the doubleflower deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that provide an attractive backdrop to its blossoms. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its extended flowering period.
