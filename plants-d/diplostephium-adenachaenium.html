Diplostephium adenachaenium

Diplostephium adenachaenium, commonly known as the giant water spinach, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

The plant is adapted to wet or aquatic environments, making it a common species in marshes and along riverbanks.
