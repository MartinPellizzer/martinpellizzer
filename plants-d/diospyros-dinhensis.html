Diospyros dinhensis

Diospyros dinhensis, commonly known as the Dinh Mountain persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are typically clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round fruits that ripen to a deep orange-red color.

The wood of Diospyros dinhensis is dense and valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in traditional crafts and furniture making.
