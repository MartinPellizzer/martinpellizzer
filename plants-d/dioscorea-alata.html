Dioscorea alata

Dioscorea alata, commonly known as yam, is a tropical vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of tuberous roots that are widely cultivated for their edible properties. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers, which are the primary edible part, are rich in carbohydrates and have a starchy texture.

Dioscorea alata is native to Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands, and it plays an important role in both traditional and modern agriculture.
