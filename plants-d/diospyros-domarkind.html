Diospyros domarkind

Diospyros domarkind, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

The bark is thick and deeply fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.
