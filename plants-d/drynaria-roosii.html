Drynaria roosii

Drynaria roosii, commonly known as the African chain fern, is a tropical evergreen fern native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with thick, segmented stems that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, ovate leaflets arranged along the central axis.

Drynaria roosii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable growing conditions.
