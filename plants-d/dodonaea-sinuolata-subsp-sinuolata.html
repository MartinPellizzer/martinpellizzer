Dodonaea sinuolata subsp. sinuolata

Dodonaea sinuolata subsp.

sinuolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hopper bush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glabrous or nearly so.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in coastal and dry forest areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
