Discoma

Discoma, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to Africa and the Americas. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and they produce small, yellow flowers that develop into spherical, seed-filled heads. Discoma species are often found in grasslands and open habitats, and they are sometimes cultivated as ornamental plants.
