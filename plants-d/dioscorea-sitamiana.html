Dioscorea sitamiana

Dioscorea sitamiana, commonly known as African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source in various African countries.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional healing practices.
