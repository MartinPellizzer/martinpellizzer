Darwinia turczaninowii

Darwinia turczaninowii, commonly known as the mountain darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy spikes of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in woodland and heathland environments. It produces small, leathery leaves and has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Darwinia turczaninowii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
