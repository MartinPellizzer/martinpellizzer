Dianthus asper var. angustifolius

Dianthus asper var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
