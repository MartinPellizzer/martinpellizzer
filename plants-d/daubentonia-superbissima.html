Daubentonia superbissima

Daubentonia superbissima, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, thick leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, with upright stems and clusters of vibrant blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Daubentonia superbissima is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
