Diospyros retusa

Diospyros retusa, commonly known as the blunt-leaf ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to reddish when mature.

Diospyros retusa is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
