Diospyros heterosepala

Diospyros heterosepala, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible fruits that are typically orange to reddish in color. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

Diospyros heterosepala is valued not only for its fruit but also for its hard, dense wood, which is used in craftsmanship and construction.
