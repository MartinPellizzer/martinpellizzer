Drepanostachyum falcatum subsp. khasianum

Drepanostachyum falcatum subsp.

khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi bamboo, is a grass-like plant native to the northeastern regions of India. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves.

This subspecies is known for its robust growth and adaptability to moist, tropical climates. It often forms dense clumps and is used in traditional construction and crafts in local communities.

The plant also plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
