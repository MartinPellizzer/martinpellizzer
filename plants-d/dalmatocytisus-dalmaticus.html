Dalmatocytisus dalmaticus

Dalmatocytisus dalmaticus, commonly known as the Dalmatian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and is adapted to grow in rocky or well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in full sun.

Dalmatocytisus dalmaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
