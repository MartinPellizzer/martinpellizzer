Dendrobium lepidum

Dendrobium lepidum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dendrobium lepidum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
