Desmodium barbatum var. dimorphum

Desmodium barbatum var. dimorphum, commonly known as the hairy sicklepod, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, curved pods that resemble sickles. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and pastures.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
