Dryptopetalum membranaceum

Dryptopetalum membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous petal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, with five petals that are thin and membranous. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dryptopetalum membranaceum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
