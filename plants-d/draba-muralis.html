Draba muralis

Draba muralis, commonly known as the wall draba, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellow flowers in early spring. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba muralis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in crevices or on rock faces where it can retain moisture.
