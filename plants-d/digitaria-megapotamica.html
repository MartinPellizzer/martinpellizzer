Digitaria megapotamica

Digitaria megapotamica, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or *Digitaria* ssp., is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, wetlands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in regions with warm and humid climates.
