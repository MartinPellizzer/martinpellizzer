Danais tsaratananensis

Danais tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is an endemic species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
