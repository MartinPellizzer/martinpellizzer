Dorstenia tenuifolia

Dorstenia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leafed dwarf banana, is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that often curl into a spiral, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and unique growth habit, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
