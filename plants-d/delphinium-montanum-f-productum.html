Delphinium montanum f. productum

Delphinium montanum f. productum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are arranged in dense spikes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
