Drypetes umbricola

Drypetes umbricola, commonly known as the dark soil tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by winged fruits. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water and nutrients from deeper soil layers.

The tree is valued for its hard wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
