Drypetes fusiformis

Drypetes fusiformis, commonly known as the narrow-fruit or red sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally in construction and carving.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
