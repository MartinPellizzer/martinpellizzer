Dalbergia kunstleri

Dalbergia kunstleri, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable and is protected in many regions.
