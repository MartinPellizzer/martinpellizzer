Draba verna var. occidentalis

Draba verna var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate and produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring.

The species is adapted to cold climates and is often among the first plants to bloom in the spring.
