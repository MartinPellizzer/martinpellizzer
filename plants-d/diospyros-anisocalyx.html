Diospyros anisocalyx

Diospyros anisocalyx, commonly known as the rough-shelled persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
