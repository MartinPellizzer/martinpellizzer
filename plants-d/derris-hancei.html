Derris hancei

Derris hancei, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Derris hancei is notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of the alkaloid rotenone, which has pesticidal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
