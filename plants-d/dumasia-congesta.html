Dumasia congesta

Dumasia congesta, commonly known as the clustered dwarf lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Dumasia congesta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
