Dioscorea hunzikeri

Dioscorea hunzikeri, commonly known as the Hunziker yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary vegetative structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some local communities.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
