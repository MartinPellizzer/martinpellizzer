Dicranopygium imeriense

Dicranopygium imeriense, commonly known as the Imerian moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has elongated, wiry stems with closely spaced leaves that are often curved at the tips. Its reproductive structures, including sporophytes, are relatively small and develop on the upper parts of the stems.

Dicranopygium imeriense plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
