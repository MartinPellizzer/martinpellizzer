Dioscorea parvifolia

Dioscorea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its growth habit involves twining around supporting structures, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The tubers of D. parvifolia are sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
