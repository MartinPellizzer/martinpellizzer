Duranta repens var. alba

Duranta repens var. alba, commonly known as white glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a fast-growing, sprawling shrub that produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers throughout the growing season. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
