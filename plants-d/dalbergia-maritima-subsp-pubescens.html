Dalbergia maritima subsp. pubescens

Dalbergia maritima subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the coastal or beach ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and distinctive pubescent (hairy) leaves.

The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and mangrove forests. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and are followed by small, round fruits.
