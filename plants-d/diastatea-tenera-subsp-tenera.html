Diastatea tenera subsp. tenera

Diastatea tenera subsp.

tenera, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves that are typically green or variegated.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom from the center of the leaves. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for indoor cultivation and ornamental use.
