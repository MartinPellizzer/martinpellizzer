Desmodium acanthocladum

Desmodium acanthocladum, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Desmodium acanthocladum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility.
