Deyeuxia matsudana

Deyeuxia matsudana, commonly known as the Japanese meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Deyeuxia matsudana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
