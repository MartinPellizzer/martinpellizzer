Didymocarpus leucanthus

Didymocarpus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces hard, woody fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a tough, leathery covering. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
