Diospyros kuhlii

Diospyros kuhlii, commonly known as the Kuhlii persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
