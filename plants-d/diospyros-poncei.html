Diospyros poncei

Diospyros poncei, commonly known as the Ponce's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to red when mature. The fruit has a sweet, edible pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.

It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
