Dodecatheon sinuatum

Dodecatheon sinuatum, commonly known as the wavy petal larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features palmate leaves with wavy edges, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Dodecatheon sinuatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
