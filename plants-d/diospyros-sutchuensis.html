Diospyros sutchuensis

Diospyros sutchuensis, commonly known as the Sutchu persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a small, orange-red berry that ripens in the fall and is often consumed raw or used in local cuisines.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
