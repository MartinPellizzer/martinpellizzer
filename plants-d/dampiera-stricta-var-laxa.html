Dampiera stricta var. laxa

Dampiera stricta var. laxa, commonly known as the slender dampiera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
