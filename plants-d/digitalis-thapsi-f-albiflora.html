Digitalis thapsi f. albiflora

Digitalis thapsi f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered purple foxglove, is a cultivar of the foxglove plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or pink hues of the species. The plant is a biennial, reaching heights of up to 60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to both aesthetic and ecological benefits in the garden.
