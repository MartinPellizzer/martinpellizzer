Dracaena yucciformis

Dracaena yucciformis, commonly known as the Yucca-like Dracaena, is a striking plant native to the southern United States, particularly Texas.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive white or pale margin. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle floral touch to its appearance. Its succulent-like leaves store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

Dracaena yucciformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and resilience.
