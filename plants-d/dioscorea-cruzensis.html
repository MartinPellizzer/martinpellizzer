Dioscorea cruzensis

Dioscorea cruzensis, commonly known as the Colombian yam, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by cylindrical fruits.

Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for their nutritional and therapeutic properties.
