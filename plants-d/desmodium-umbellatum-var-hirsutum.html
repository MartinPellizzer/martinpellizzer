Desmodium umbellatum var. hirsutum

Desmodium umbellatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellow to reddish-pink flowers arranged in umbels. The plant produces elliptical, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in ecological restoration projects.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility improvement.
