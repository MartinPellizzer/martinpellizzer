Dactylorhiza × vermeuleniana nothovar. townsendiana

Dactylorhiza × vermeuleniana nothovar. townsendiana, commonly known as the Townsend's marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its hybrid origin and its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
