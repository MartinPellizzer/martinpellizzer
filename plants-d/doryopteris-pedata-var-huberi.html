Doryopteris pedata var. huberi

Doryopteris pedata var. huberi, commonly known as the Huber's shield fern, is a variant of the shield fern native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, almost metallic sheen. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
