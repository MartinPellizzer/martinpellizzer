Dendrocharis

Dendrocharis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, while its flowers feature a distinctive yellow star-shaped pattern.

Dendrocharis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
