Dioscoreophyllum fernandense

Dioscoreophyllum fernandense, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its dense, fibrous rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

However, it is considered rare and threatened due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
