Dieffenbachia seguine f. lineata

Dieffenbachia seguine f. lineata, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature white and green stripes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, typically growing as an upright shrub with thick, fleshy stems. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slight waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a spadix and a modified leaf called a spathe.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health and vibrant foliage.
