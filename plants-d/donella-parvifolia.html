Donella parvifolia

Donella parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved donella, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Donella parvifolia is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora.
