Dasycondylus regnellii

Dasycondylus regnellii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.
