Dupontia micrantha

Dupontia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces tiny flowers that are usually green or pale blue in color.

Dupontia micrantha is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
