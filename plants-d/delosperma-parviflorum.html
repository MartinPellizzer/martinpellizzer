Delosperma parviflorum

Delosperma parviflorum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink or purple flowers in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It grows in dense mats, often forming a low, sprawling ground cover.

Delosperma parviflorum is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
