Dicranopygium pygmaeum

Dicranopygium pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Dicranaceae and is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit. The plant has leafy shoots that are typically less than 2 cm in length, with a narrow, elongated shape. Its spore-producing structures, called sporophytes, are small and often embedded within the dense moss mat.

Dicranopygium pygmaeum plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
