Dioscorea alata var. vera

Dioscorea alata var. vera, commonly known as yam or greater yam, is a tropical vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The leaves are heart-shaped and large, with a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and climbing. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its starchy, nutrient-rich tubers.

It is widely grown in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
