Diospyros subsessilis

Diospyros subsessilis, commonly known as the smallleaf persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn orange to red when mature.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often with shallow fissures, and the wood is dense and highly valued for its strength and durability.
