Dryopteris palladivenia

Dryopteris palladivenia, commonly known as the Palladivenia wood fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into narrow segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread gradually across the soil.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
