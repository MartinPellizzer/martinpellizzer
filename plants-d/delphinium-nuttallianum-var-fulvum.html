Delphinium nuttallianum var. fulvum

Delphinium nuttallianum var. fulvum, commonly known as the golden beard delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear dense clusters of showy, spurred flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with a distinctive yellow or orange beard-like marking at the base of each petal. This variety is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
