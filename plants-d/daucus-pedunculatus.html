Daucus pedunculatus

Daucus pedunculatus, commonly known as the wild carrot or Queen Anne's lace, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves and white flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its roots are slender and often used in traditional medicine.

Daucus pedunculatus is also a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed soils.
