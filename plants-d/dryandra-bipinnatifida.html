Dryandra bipinnatifida

Dryandra bipinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dryandra, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant, cone-shaped inflorescences that bloom in spring, showcasing clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife.
