Deschampsia kaschinae

Deschampsia kaschinae, commonly known as the Alaska feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and delicate, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows. Its low-growing habit and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions make it well-suited for mountainous environments.

Deschampsia kaschinae plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
