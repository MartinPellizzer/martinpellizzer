Diasperus petiolaris

Diasperus petiolaris, commonly known as the slender leaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Diasperus petiolaris is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
