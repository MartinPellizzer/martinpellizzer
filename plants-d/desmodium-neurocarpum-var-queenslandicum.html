Desmodium neurocarpum var. queenslandicum

Desmodium neurocarpum var. queenslandicum, commonly known as Queensland tickseed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow or white flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
