Dendrobium dryadum

Dendrobium dryadum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium dryadum is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
