Diospyros corallina

Diospyros corallina, commonly known as the coral ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Diospyros corallina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
