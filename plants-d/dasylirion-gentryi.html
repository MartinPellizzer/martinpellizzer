Dasylirion gentryi

Dasylirion gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's dasylirion, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of stiff, gray-green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white to pale blue flowers in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in desert landscapes.
