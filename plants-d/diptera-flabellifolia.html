Diptera flabellifolia

Diptera flabellifolia, commonly known as the flabellifoliate fly plant, is a unique species belonging to the family Diptera, which includes true flies.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that resemble the shape of a fan, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered together, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a host plant for certain species of flies and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.

Despite its name, it is not a true plant but a type of insect, highlighting the importance of accurate classification in botany.
