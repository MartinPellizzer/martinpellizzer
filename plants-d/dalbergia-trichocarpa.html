Dalbergia trichocarpa

Dalbergia trichocarpa, commonly known as California blackwood, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds.

Its root system is often extensive, and it plays an important role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation in its native habitat.
