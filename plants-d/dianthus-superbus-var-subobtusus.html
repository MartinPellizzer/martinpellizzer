Dianthus superbus var. subobtusus

Dianthus superbus var. subobtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
