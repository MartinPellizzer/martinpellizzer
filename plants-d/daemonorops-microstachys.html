Daemonorops microstachys

Daemonorops microstachys, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family, characterized by its compact stature and distinctive appearance.

It features a short, stout trunk with a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a fern-like appearance. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its cones are small and inconspicuous, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
