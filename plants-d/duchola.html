Duchola

Duchola, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Zingiberaceae, which also includes ginger and cardamom. The plant features aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Duchola thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
