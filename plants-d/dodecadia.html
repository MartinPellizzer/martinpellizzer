Dodecadia

Dodecadia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow or golden flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Dodecadia has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
