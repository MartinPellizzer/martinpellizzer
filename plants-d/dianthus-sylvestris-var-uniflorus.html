Dianthus sylvestris var. uniflorus

Dianthus sylvestris var. uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flowered wild carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces solitary, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and branched. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural environment.
