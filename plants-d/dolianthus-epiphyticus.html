Dolianthus epiphyticus

Dolianthus epiphyticus, commonly known as the silver leaf epiphyte, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants, typically trees, without deriving nutrients from them. Its foliage is characterized by silvery-white or pale green leaves, which help reflect sunlight and reduce water loss in its often humid and shaded habitat. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or white, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural environment.
