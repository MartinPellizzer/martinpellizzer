Digitalis purpurea var. tomentosa

Digitalis purpurea var. tomentosa, commonly known as the fuzzy foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and hooded flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in medicine.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.
