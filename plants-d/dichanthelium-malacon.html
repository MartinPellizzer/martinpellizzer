Dichanthelium malacon

Dichanthelium malacon, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandreed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils, often in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It is a perennial grass that thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
