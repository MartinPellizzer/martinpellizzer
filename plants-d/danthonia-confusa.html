Danthonia confusa

Danthonia confusa, commonly known as the slender field sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to various regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered with dense, grass-like leaves.

Danthonia confusa produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination.
