Dianthus serratus subsp. sallentii

Dianthus serratus subsp.

sallentii, commonly known as the Sallent's carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features upright, branching stems covered in narrow, serrated leaves.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers with five petals, blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
