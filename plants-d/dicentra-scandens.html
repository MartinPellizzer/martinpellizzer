Dicentra scandens

Dicentra scandens, commonly known as the climbing bleeding heart, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to attach to supports such as trellises or fences. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

Dicentra scandens prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
