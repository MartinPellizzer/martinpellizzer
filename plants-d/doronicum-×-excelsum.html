Doronicum × excelsum

Doronicum × excelsum, commonly known as the hybrid leopard's bane, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with dark purple markings. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces showy inflorescences in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Doronicum × excelsum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
