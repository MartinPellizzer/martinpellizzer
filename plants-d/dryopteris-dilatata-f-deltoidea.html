Dryopteris dilatata f. deltoidea

Dryopteris dilatata f. deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often curled fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that gives the plant its ornamental appeal. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This fern is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to thrive in partial shade.
