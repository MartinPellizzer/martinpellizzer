Dulia

Dulia, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Nelumbonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or white flowers and heart-shaped leaves that float on water. The plant is native to Asia and thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, while its flowers hold cultural and religious significance in various societies.

The plant's unique ability to bloom with its leaves fully open is a striking botanical feature.
