Diplogenea viscoides

Diplogenea viscoides, commonly known as the sticky leaf vine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, often sticky leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are tuberous, allowing it to store nutrients and water for extended periods.

Diplogenea viscoides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
