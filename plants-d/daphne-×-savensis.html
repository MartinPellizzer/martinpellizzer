Daphne × savensis

Daphne × savensis, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and dark green, providing visual interest throughout the year.

Daphne × savensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
