Diuris emarginata

Diuris emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking, spider-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with red or purple markings. The plant is found in the southeastern region of Australia, particularly in Victoria and South Australia, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils. Diuris emmarginata is a perennial herb that blooms during spring, producing its unique floral display.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
