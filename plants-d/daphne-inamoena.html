Daphne inamoena

Daphne inamoena, commonly known as the Persian daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique blooming period, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
