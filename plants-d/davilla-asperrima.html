Davilla asperrima

Davilla asperrima, commonly known as the roughleaf davilla, is a woody shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, often pubescent leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Davilla asperrima is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
