Dunbaria punctata

Dunbaria punctata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. Dunbaria punctata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
