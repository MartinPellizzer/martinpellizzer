Drymoglossum spatulatum

Drymoglossum spatulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed feather moss, is a member of the family Dicranaceae.

It is a small, evergreen moss that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short, erect stems and are typically reddish in color.

This moss is valued for its ability to retain moisture and its role in soil stabilization in forest ecosystems.
