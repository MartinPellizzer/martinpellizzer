Derris indica

Derris indica, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, serpentine-shaped roots. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural pesticide.

Derris indica thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
