Desmodium ferrugineum subsp. wynaadense

Desmodium ferrugineum subsp.

wynaadense, commonly known as the Wynaad desmodium, is a leguminous plant native to the Western Ghats of India. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its presence is important for soil nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various herbivores.
