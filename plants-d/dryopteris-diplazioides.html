Dryopteris diplazioides

Dryopteris diplazioides, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a deciduous fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, lacy fronds that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive silver sheen. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
