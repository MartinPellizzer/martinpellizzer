Dampiera hederacea

Dampiera hederacea, commonly known as the creeping dampiera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southeastern Australia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers during the autumn season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland and heathland habitats.

Dampiera hederacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
