It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces both sterile and fertile fronds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, ovate pinnae that are often covered in fine hairs.

Diplazium incomptum is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.
