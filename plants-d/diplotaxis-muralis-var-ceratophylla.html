Diplotaxis muralis var. ceratophylla

Diplotaxis muralis var. ceratophylla, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like bracts that surround the flower clusters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding visual interest to rocky or sandy soils. It is often found growing in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
