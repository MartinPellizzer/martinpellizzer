Dicraurus alternifolia

Dicraurus alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaf fern, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive alternate arrangement of leaves along the stem, giving it a unique and elegant appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is valued for its adaptability to various soil types.
