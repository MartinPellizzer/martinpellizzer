Diospyros sandwicensis var. lanaiensis

Diospyros sandwicensis var. lanaiensis, commonly known as the Lanai persimmon, is a rare endemic species native to the Hawaiian island of Lanai.

It is a small, evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native environment.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a critically endangered species.
