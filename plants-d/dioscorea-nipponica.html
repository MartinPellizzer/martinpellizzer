Dioscorea nipponica

Dioscorea nipponica, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
