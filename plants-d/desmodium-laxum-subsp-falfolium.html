Desmodium laxum subsp. falfolium

Desmodium laxum subsp.

falfolium, commonly known as the slender sicklepod, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped and often hairy.

The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-pink flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the genus Desmodium. Its fruit is a slender, spiral-shaped pod that twists as it matures, a unique feature of the species.

It is typically found in sandy or disturbed soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
