Daemonorops lasiospatha

Daemonorops lasiospatha, commonly known as the hairy-leaf pandan, is a species of cycad native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in soft, hairy structures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant's unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular ornamental species in horticulture.
