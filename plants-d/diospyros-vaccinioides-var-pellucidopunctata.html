Diospyros vaccinioides var. pellucidopunctata

Diospyros vaccinioides var. pellucidopunctata, commonly known as the translucent-punctate persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a glossy upper surface with prominent veins. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
