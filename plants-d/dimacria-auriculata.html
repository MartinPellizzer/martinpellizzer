Dimacria auriculata

Dimacria auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate dimacria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Dimacria auriculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and long-lasting blooms.
