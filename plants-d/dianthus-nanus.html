Dianthus nanus

Dianthus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, bushy foliage with bright, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring and are often used in rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Dianthus nanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and easy care requirements.
