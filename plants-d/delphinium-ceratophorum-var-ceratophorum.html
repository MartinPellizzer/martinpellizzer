Delphinium ceratophorum var. ceratophorum

Delphinium ceratophorum var. ceratophorum, commonly known as the horned delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium ceratophorum var. ceratophorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
