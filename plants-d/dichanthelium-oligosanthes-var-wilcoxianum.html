Dichanthelium oligosanthes var. wilcoxianum

Dichanthelium oligosanthes var. wilcoxianum, commonly known as the Wilcox's threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, three-awned spikelets.

This variety is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
