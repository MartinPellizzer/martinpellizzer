Dendrobium suberectum

Dendrobium suberectum, commonly known as the stiff upright dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that are often covered in a thick, leathery sheath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
