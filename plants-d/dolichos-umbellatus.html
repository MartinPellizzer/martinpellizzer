Dolichos umbellatus

Dolichos umbellatus, commonly known as the horsegram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive umbellate inflorescences that bear small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, fibrous pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and agricultural benefits.

Dolichos umbellatus also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
