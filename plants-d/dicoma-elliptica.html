Dicoma elliptica

Dicoma elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, elliptical leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its flowers develop into spherical, spiky seed heads that disperse seeds via the wind.

Dicoma elliptica is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
