Darwinia virescens

Darwinia virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal areas.

Darwinia virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
