Dichanthium caricosum var. theinlwinii

Dichanthium caricosum var. theinlwinii, commonly known as the Burmese false rice, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense vegetation.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
