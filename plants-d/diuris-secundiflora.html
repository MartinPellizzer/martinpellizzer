Diuris secundiflora

Diuris secundiflora, commonly known as the second-flowered spider orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower spike. The plant produces a single, striking orchid-like flower with a distinctive, hooded labellum. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

Diuris secundiflora is a rare and delicate species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful cultivation.
