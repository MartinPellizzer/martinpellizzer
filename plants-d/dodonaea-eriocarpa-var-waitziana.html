Dodonaea eriocarpa var. waitziana

Dodonaea eriocarpa var. waitziana, commonly known as silver wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
