Diasperus subobscurus

Diasperus subobscurus, commonly known as the dark-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Diasperus subobscurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
