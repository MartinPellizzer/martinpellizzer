Dyssodia chrysanthemifolia

Dyssodia chrysanthemifolia, commonly known as the golden daisy or yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Dyssodia chrysanthemifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
