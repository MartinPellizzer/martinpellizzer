Diphasiastrum complanatum subsp. hastulatum

Diphasiastrum complanatum subsp.

hastulatum, commonly known as the flatleaf field horsetail, is a member of the Equisetaceae family. It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, segmented stems and whorled arrangement of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

This subspecies is notable for its primitive features and its role in ecological succession in wetland environments.
