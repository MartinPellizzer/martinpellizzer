Dactylorhiza kerryensis subsp. occidentalis

Dactylorhiza kerryensis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base of the stem.

This orchid is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is part of the Orchidaceae family.
