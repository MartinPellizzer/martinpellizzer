Dichrostachys cinerea var. argillicola

Dichrostachys cinerea var. argillicola, commonly known as the clay-loving strangle vine, is a variant of the African strangle vine.

It is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions with clay soils. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It has a rapid growth rate and can become invasive in certain environments.

Its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, clay-rich soils makes it an important species in ecological studies and land rehabilitation efforts.
