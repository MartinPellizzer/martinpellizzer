Discyphus scopulariae var. longiauriculata

Discyphus scopulariae var. longiauriculata, commonly known as the long-ear bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and produces elongated, hairy stems that help it thrive in rocky and arid environments. It blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
