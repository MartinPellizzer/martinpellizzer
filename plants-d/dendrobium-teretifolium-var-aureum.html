Dendrobium teretifolium var. aureum

Dendrobium teretifolium var. aureum, commonly known as the golden leaf dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems and lanceolate leaves that are often a striking golden-yellow color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
