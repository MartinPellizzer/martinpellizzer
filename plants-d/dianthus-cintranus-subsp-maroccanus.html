Dianthus cintranus subsp. maroccanus

Dianthus cintranus subsp.

maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan campion, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its colorful blooms.
