Dicliptera conformis

Dicliptera conformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluemouth, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Dicliptera conformis is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showing some adaptability to different environmental conditions.
