Dracocephalum kaitcheense

Dracocephalum kaitcheense, commonly known as Kaitcheen skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem, and it produces small, four-lobed nutlets as fruits.

Due to its habitat in dry environments, it is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system for water absorption.
