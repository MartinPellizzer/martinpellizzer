Delima dioica

Delima dioica, commonly known as the bitter leaf, is a tropical plant native to Africa, Asia, and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with pinnately compound leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is characterized by its medicinal properties, with the leaves being used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic effects. Delima dioica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential for both ecological and pharmaceutical applications.
