Digitaria kanehirae

Digitaria kanehirae, commonly known as the Japanese fingergrass, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Digitaria kanehirae is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
