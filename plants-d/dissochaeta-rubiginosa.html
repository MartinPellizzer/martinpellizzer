Dissochaeta rubiginosa

Dissochaeta rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed bristlegrass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features slender, reddish stems covered in fine, bristly hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.
