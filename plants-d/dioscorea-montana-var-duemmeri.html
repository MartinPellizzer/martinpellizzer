Dioscorea montana var. duemmeri

Dioscorea montana var. duemmeri, commonly known as the Duemmer's yam, is a variant of the yam plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing vine with tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical classification places it within the family Dioscoreaceae, and it is closely related to other species of the genus Dioscorea.

The tubers of this variety are characterized by their thick, starchy flesh and are often cultivated for their nutritional value.
