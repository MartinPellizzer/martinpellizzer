Dianthus angrenicus

Dianthus angrenicus, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Dianthus angrenicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
