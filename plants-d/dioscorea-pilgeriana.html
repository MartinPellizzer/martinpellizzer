Dioscorea pilgeriana

Dioscorea pilgeriana, commonly known as the Pilger yam, is a climbing vine native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and later develops capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
