Dombeya xiphosepala

Dombeya xiphosepala, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
