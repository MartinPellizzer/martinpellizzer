Declieuxia diamantinae

Declieuxia diamantinae, commonly known as the diamond grass, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient and hardy species.

Declieuxia diamantinae is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
