Dendranthema parvifolium

Dendranthema parvifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny areas and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
