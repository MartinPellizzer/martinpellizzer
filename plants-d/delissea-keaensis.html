Delissea keaensis

Delissea keaensis, commonly known as the Kea Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the island of Kea in the Greek Cyclades.

It is a small herbaceous plant with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Delissea keaensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological characteristics.
