Diospyros candolleana

Diospyros candolleana, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits, which are edible when fully mature. Its fruits are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
