Dalbergia volubilis var. assamica

Dalbergia volubilis var. assamica, commonly known as Assam rosewood, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round, dark purple to black seeds enclosed in a pod. It is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
