Diplazium angustatum

Diplazium angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant across wetland areas.

Diplazium angustatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
