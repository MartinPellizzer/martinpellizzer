Dendromyza volubilis

Dendromyza volubilis, commonly known as the climbing mistplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its twining stems that allow it to climb and entwine around other vegetation. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a subtle mist-like vapor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and emit a mild, sweet scent, which attracts various pollinators.

Dendromyza volubilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforests.
