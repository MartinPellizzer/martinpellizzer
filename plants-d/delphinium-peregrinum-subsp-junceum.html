Delphinium peregrinum subsp. junceum

Delphinium peregrinum subsp.

junceum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
