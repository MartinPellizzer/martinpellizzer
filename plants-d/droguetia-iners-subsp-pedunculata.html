Droguetia iners subsp. pedunculata

Droguetia iners subsp.

pedunculata, commonly known as the red-flowered droguetia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its erect or decumbent growth habit and produces clusters of small, reddish or pinkish flowers.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
