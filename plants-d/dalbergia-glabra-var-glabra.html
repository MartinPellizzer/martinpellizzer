Dalbergia glabra var. glabra

Dalbergia glabra var. glabra, commonly known as the white sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and trifoliate leaves that are alternate and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its valuable wood, it is also known for its ecological and economic significance in its native regions.
