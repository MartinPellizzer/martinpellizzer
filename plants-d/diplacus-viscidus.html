Diplacus viscidus

Diplacus viscidus, commonly known as sticky monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies due to their nectar-rich structure.

Diplacus viscidus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
