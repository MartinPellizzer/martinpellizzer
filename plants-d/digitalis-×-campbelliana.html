Digitalis × campbelliana

Digitalis × campbelliana, commonly known as the hybrid foxglove, is a perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred shape at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
