Diospyros aequoris

Diospyros aequoris, commonly known as the sea date or mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, segmented fruits. Its fruit, which resembles a star, contains a sweet, aromatic pulp surrounded by a tough, leathery rind.

Diospyros aequoris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
