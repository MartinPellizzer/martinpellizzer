Dendranthema indicum var. maruyamanum

Dendranthema indicum var. maruyamanum, commonly known as the Shikoku chrysanthemum, is a variant of the common chrysanthemum native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces abundant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering and occasional pruning to maintain its shape.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden settings.
