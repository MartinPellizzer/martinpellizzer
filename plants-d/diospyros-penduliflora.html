Diospyros penduliflora

Diospyros penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging persimmon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons but are typically less commonly consumed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Diospyros penduliflora is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
