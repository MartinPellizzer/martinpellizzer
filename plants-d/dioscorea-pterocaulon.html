Dioscorea pterocaulon

Dioscorea pterocaulon, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a unique branching pattern that aids in its climbing growth.

The tubers of Dioscorea pterocaulon are often utilized for their medicinal properties and nutritional value.
