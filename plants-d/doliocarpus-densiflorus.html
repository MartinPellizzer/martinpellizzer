Doliocarpus densiflorus

Doliocarpus densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered doliocarpus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, making it an important species for ecological studies and biodiversity conservation.
