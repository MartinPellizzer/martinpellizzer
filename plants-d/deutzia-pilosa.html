Deutzia pilosa

Deutzia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems covered in soft, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
