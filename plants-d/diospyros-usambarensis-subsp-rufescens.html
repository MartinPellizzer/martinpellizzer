Diospyros usambarensis subsp. rufescens

Diospyros usambarensis subsp.

rufescens, commonly known as the red-fruited ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Africa. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and elliptical, glossy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, round, red or orange fruits. Its fruits are typically consumed by various bird species, aiding in seed dispersal.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
