Diospyros discocalyx

Diospyros discocalyx, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
