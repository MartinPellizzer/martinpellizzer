Dipterocarpus semivestitus

Dipterocarpus semivestitus, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

This species plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
