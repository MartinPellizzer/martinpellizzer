Diospyros fasimainty

Diospyros fasimainty, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that ripens in late summer.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
