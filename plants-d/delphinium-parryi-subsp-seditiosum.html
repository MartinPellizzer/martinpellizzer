Delphinium parryi subsp. seditiosum

Delphinium parryi subsp.

seditiosum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, often in meadows or open woodlands. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to thrive.
