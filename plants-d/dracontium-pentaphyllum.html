Dracontium pentaphyllum

Dracontium pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-lobed arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are enclosed within a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.
