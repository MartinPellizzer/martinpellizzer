Diospyros undulata

Diospyros undulata, commonly known as the wavy persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often wavy or undulating in appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by small, round fruits that ripen to a deep orange-red color.

The fruit is edible when fully mature and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
