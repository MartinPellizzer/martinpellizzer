Diospyros lasiocalyx

Diospyros lasiocalyx, commonly known as the rough-leaved persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
