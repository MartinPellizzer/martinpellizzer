Daemonorops hystrix var. minor

Daemonorops hystrix var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and compound leaves with long, slender pinnae. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
