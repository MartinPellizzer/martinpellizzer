Dryopteris rodolfii

Dryopteris rodolfii, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve moisture in dry environments. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, narrow shape that allows for efficient water retention. This plant thrives in rocky soils and is often found in crevices or on slopes where it can access limited moisture.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to its harsh, desert-like habitat.
