Drymaria ladewii

Drymaria ladewii, commonly known as Ladew's pennywort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, succulent, rounded leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural and floral characteristics.
