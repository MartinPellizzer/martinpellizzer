Dalea compacta

Dalea compacta, commonly known as the compact milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval, hairy leaflets.

Dalea compacta thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.
