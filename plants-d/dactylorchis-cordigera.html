Dactylorchis cordigera

Dactylorchis cordigera, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs and linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, arranged in a raceme. Its roots are typically fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Dactylorchis cordigera is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
