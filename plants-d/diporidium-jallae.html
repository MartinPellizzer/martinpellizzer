Diporidium jallae

Diporidium jallae, commonly known as the Jallae orchid, is a rare and endemic plant species found in specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two distinct petals and a specialized lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous or forested areas. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter, which is crucial for its survival in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Diporidium jallae is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
