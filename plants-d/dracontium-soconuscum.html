Dracontium soconuscum

Dracontium soconuscum, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is often brightly colored. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

The plant is valued for its starchy rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
