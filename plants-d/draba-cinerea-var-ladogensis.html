Draba cinerea var. ladogensis

Draba cinerea var. ladogensis, commonly known as the gray rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has hairy, grayish foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.
