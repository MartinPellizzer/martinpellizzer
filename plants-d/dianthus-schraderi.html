Dianthus schraderi

Dianthus schraderi, commonly known as the Schrader's carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with white or yellow centers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus schraderi is also valued for its fragrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
