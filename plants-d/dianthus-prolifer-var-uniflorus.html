Dianthus prolifer var. uniflorus

Dianthus prolifer var. uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flowered pinks, is a flowering plant belonging to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces solitary, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base of the plant. This variety is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
