Dioscorea laxiflora var. truncatolanceolata

Dioscorea laxiflora var. truncatolanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yam, is a variant of the yam family native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and borne in clusters, with a distinctive yellowish-green color.

The tubers of this variant are valued for their nutritional content and potential medicinal properties.
