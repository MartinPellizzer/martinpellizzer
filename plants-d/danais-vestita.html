Danais vestita

Danais vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is native to regions of Central and South America and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, it can become an invasive species in some areas.
