Dryopteris christophersenii

Dryopteris christophersenii, commonly known as the Christophersen's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright green fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive adequate humidity and indirect light. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
