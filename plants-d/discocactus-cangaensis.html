Discocactus cangaensis

Discocactus cangaensis, commonly known as the Cangaensis Discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stem. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
