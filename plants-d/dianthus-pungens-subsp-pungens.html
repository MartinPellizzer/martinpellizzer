Dianthus pungens subsp. pungens

Dianthus pungens subsp.

pungens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces a strong, spicy scent, particularly when the leaves are crushed.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
