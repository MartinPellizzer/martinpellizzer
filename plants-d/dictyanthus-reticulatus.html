Dictyanthus reticulatus

Dictyanthus reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with intricate reticulate venation patterns on the petals. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
