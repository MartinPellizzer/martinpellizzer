Delphinium keminense

Delphinium keminense, commonly known as Kemin delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium keminense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
