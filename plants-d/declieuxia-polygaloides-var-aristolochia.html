Declieuxia polygaloides var. aristolochia

Declieuxia polygaloides var. aristolochia, commonly known as the Aristolochia vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, intricate flowers that resemble pipes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, musky fragrance. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
