Drynaria baudouinii

Drynaria baudouinii, commonly known as the African cliff fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, wiry rhizome and compound fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The fronds are often covered with small, rounded scales and have a leathery texture. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often found growing on cliffs or in shaded, humid environments.
