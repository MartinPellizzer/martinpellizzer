Diploma angustifolia

Diploma angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf diploma plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to certain regions of South America and prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in moderate climates.

However, it is important to note that it may spread aggressively if not properly managed.
