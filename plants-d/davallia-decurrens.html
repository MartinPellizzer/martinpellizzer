Davallia decurrens

Davallia decurrens, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features delicate, arching fronds with a distinctive, lace-like appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow in the dark.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
