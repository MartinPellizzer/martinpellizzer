Dalbergia melanoxylon

Dalbergia melanoxylon, commonly known as African blackwood, is a tropical hardwood tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that ranges from black to dark brown in color. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. It is often found in dry, wooded areas and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.

The tree is highly valued for its fine grain and is used in the manufacture of musical instruments and luxury furniture.
