Dioscorea japonica var. pilifera

Dioscorea japonica var. pilifera, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and adaptability to various soil conditions.
