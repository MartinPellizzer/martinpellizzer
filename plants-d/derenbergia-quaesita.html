Derenbergia quaesita

Derenbergia quaesita, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, ghost-like flowers that bloom in the undergrowth, often hidden from view. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat specificity, it is considered a difficult species to study and cultivate.
