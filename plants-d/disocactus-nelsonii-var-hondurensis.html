Disocactus nelsonii var. hondurensis

Disocactus nelsonii var. hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Central America.

It is a low-growing, branched plant with dark green, ribbed stems that are often covered in white or pale spines. The plant produces large, fragrant, night-blooming flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
