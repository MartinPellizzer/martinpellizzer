Davilla morii

Davilla morii, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking violet to purple berries that mature in the fall, attracting various bird species. Davilla morii typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
