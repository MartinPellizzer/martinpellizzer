Diplotaxis hispida var. subglabra

Diplotaxis hispida var. subglabra, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial with erect, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
