Draba standleyi

Draba standleyi, commonly known as the Standley's whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically forms dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Draba standleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
