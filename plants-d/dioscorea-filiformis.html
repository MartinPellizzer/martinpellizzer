Dioscorea filiformis

Dioscorea filiformis, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, thin, vine-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and develops tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical structure supports its role as a climbing plant, with adventitious roots and tendrils aiding in attachment to supports.

The tubers of D. filiformis are valued for their nutritional content and potential medicinal properties.
