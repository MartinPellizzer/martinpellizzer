Diospyros ranirisonii

Diospyros ranirisonii, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
