Dicranopteris tomentosa

Dicranopteris tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptations.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a creeping rhizome with clusters of fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into lanceolate, serrated leaves. Its fronds are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in diverse soil types and its role in forest understory ecosystems.
