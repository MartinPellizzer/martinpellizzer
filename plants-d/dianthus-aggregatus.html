Dianthus aggregatus

Dianthus aggregatus, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and coastal areas.

Dianthus aggregatus is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
