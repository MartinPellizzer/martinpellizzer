Dialium pachyphyllum

Dialium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces showy, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
