Dioscorea semperflorens

Dioscorea semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers throughout the growing season, contributing to its "everblooming" reputation. Its tubers are often cultivated for their nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.

The leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, providing a dense canopy as the vine climbs.
