Dalbergia canescens

Dalbergia canescens, commonly known as the white sand-wood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, often whitish bark. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, pod-like fruits and are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
