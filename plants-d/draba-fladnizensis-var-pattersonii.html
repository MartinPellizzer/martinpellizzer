Draba fladnizensis var. pattersonii

Draba fladnizensis var. pattersonii, commonly known as the Patterson's winterflower, is a small, flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
