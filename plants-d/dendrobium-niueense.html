Dendrobium niueense

Dendrobium niueense, commonly known as the Niue orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the island of Niue in the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments with high light exposure and well-draining substrates. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires careful protection.
