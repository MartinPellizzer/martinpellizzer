Diospyros virginiana var. lucida

Diospyros virginiana var. lucida, commonly known as shining persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features smooth, gray-brown bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible, orange to red persimmons that ripen in the fall.

Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
