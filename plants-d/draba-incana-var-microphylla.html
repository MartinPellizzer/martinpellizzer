Draba incana var. microphylla

Draba incana var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf whitlow grass, is a low-growing plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, basal leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It has a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for alpine gardens.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to mountainous landscapes.
