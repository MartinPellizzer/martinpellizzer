Dissotis deistelii

Dissotis deistelii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Dissotis deistelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
