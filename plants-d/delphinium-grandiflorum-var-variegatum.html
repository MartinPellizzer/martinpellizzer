Delphinium grandiflorum var. variegatum

Delphinium grandiflorum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, often in tall, slender spikes. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white, adding visual interest to the plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
