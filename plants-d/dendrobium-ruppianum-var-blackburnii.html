Dendrobium ruppianum var. blackburnii

Dendrobium ruppianum var. blackburnii, commonly known as the blackburnii orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright, cane-like stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white to grayish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
