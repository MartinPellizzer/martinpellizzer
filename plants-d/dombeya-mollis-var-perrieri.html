Dombeya mollis var. perrieri

Dombeya mollis var. perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern France.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, greyish-bark trunk. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
