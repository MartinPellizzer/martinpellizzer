Drymonia intermedia

Drymonia intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate drymonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Drymonia intermedia is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
