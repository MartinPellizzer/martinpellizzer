Diplachne amboensis

Diplachne amboensis, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often folded in a V-shape. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Diplachne amboensis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
