Dalea versicolor var. glabrescens

Dalea versicolor var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, multicolored flowers that range from white to pink and purple. The plant has slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant for ecological diversity.
