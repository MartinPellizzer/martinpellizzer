Digitaria seminuda

Digitaria seminuda, commonly known as hairless digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Digitaria seminuda is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
