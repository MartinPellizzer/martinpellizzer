Doellingeria breweri

Doellingeria breweri, commonly known as the California goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the spring.

Doellingeria breweri is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
