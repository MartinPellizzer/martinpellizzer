Derris glabrata

Derris glabrata, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.

Derris glabrata is often used in traditional medicine, though its use requires caution due to its potential toxicity.
