Desmesia orixensis

Desmesia orixensis, commonly known as the Orixes rock orchid, is a rare and endemic flowering plant found in the mountainous regions of Orixes, Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, intricately patterned flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the high-altitude climate with cool temperatures and ample sunlight. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying alpine flora.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this species from habitat loss and environmental degradation.
