Dalea pringlei var. oxyphyllidia

Dalea pringlei var. oxyphyllidia, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the legume family, Fabaceae.
