Dodonaea serratifolia

Dodonaea serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, leathery berries. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
