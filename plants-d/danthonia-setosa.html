Danthonia setosa

Danthonia setosa, commonly known as silky oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a slightly hairy texture.

Danthonia setosa is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
