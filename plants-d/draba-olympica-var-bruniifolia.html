Draba olympica var. bruniifolia

Draba olympica var. bruniifolia, commonly known as the Olympic Draba, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the high alpine regions of the Olympic Mountains in Washington State.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This variety is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is considered a rare and specialized member of the Brassicaceae family.
