Diuris punctata f. blakneyae

Diuris punctata f. blakneyae, commonly known as the Blakney's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open heathlands or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spiders.
