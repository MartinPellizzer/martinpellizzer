Doria digitata var. lanceolata

Doria digitata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate finger plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finger-like leaves that are elongated and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in whorled clusters. This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
