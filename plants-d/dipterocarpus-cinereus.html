Dipterocarpus cinereus

Dipterocarpus cinereus, commonly known as the ash dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its tall stature and buttressed roots. The tree produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecological systems of its native habitats, supporting diverse flora and fauna.
