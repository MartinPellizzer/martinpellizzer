Dianthus arenarius subsp. pseudoserotinus

Dianthus arenarius subsp.

pseudoserotinus, commonly known as the sand dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to reddish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dunes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are usually branched.

It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
