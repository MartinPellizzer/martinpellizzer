Desmodium scalare

Desmodium scalare, commonly known as the sicklepod or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant has trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, three-lobed pods that split open when mature.

Desmodium scalare is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
