Draba incompta

Draba incompta, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in racemes.

Draba incompta is also known for its ability to survive in harsh climates, making it a hardy and resilient species.
