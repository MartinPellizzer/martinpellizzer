Delaria odorata

Delaria odorata, commonly known as the sweet sylph, is a rare flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that emit a sweet, vanilla-like scent. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white blooms that are highly attractive to pollinators. Its botanical classification places it within the family Orchidaceae, highlighting its relation to other orchids.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is highly sought after in horticultural circles.
