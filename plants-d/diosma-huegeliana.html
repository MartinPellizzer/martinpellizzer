Diosma huegeliana

Diosma huegeliana, commonly known as the mountain diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
