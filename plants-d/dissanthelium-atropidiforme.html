Dissanthelium atropidiforme

Dissanthelium atropidiforme, commonly known as blackleaf dropseed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
