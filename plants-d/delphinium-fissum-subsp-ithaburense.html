Delphinium fissum subsp. ithaburense

Delphinium fissum subsp.

ithaburense, commonly known as the Ithaburen larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a distinctive, serrated margin.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.
