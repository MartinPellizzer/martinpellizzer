Doliocarpus dentatus subsp. latifolius

Doliocarpus dentatus subsp.

latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf doliocarpus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and a dense, woody structure.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
