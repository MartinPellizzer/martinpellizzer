Dioscorea gomez-pompae

Dioscorea gomez-pompae, commonly known as the Pampas yam, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, narrow fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
