Devia xeromorpha

Devia xeromorpha, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself and access deep groundwater.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
