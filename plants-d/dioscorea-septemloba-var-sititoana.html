Dioscorea septemloba var. sititoana

Dioscorea septemloba var. sititoana, commonly known as the Sitioana yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by cylindrical, yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are the economically important part, used as a starchy food crop in some African communities.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.
