Delonix velutina

Delonix velutina, commonly known as the velvet flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, bright orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a woody, sprawling habit and produces long, flat pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
