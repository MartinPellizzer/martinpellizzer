Diuris rhomboidalis

Diuris rhomboidalis, commonly known as the rhomboid spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance. The plant produces striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically from late autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in heathlands and rocky outcrops.
