Desmodiastrum racemosum

Desmodiastrum racemosum, commonly known as the racemed desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Desmodiastrum racemosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
