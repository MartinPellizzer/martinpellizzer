Dendrophthora urbaniana

Dendrophthora urbaniana, commonly known as the tropical orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the forest floor, and it forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Dendrophthora urbaniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
