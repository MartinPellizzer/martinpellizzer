Diasperus microcladus

Diasperus microcladus, commonly known as the dwarf spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a desirable plant for collectors and gardeners in arid climates.
