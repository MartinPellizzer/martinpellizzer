Dioscorea pseudotomentosa

Dioscorea pseudotomentosa, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, angular stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various culinary applications.

The species is notable for its adaptability to diverse soil conditions and its role in both ecological and agricultural systems.
