Digitaria gerdesii var. boliviensis

Digitaria gerdesii var. boliviensis, commonly known as Bolivian Digitaria, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh climates and potential ecological significance.
