Dichrostachys arborea

Dichrostachys arborea, commonly known as the African hogweed, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in savanna habitats. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution across its native range.
