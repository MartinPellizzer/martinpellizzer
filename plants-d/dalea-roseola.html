Dalea roseola

Dalea roseola, commonly known as the roseleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dalea roseola is adapted to arid environments and is an important species for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
