Draba aspera var. adamsiana

Draba aspera var. adamsiana, commonly known as the rough rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and arctic environments. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

Its small size and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
