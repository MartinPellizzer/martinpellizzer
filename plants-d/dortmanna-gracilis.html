Dortmanna gracilis

Dortmanna gracilis, commonly known as the slender Dortmanna, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has delicate, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense spikes at the base of the plant.

Dortmanna gracilis is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to form dense mats in suitable habitats.
