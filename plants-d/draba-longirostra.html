Draba longirostra

Draba longirostra, commonly known as the long-beaked whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal raceme. It is native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia and is adapted to cold, rocky environments.

Draba longirostra is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
