Disocactus crenatus

Disocactus crenatus, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a stem cactus characterized by its segmented, ribbed stems that grow in a zigzag pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Disocactus crenatus is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
