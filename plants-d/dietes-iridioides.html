Dietes iridioides

Dietes iridioides, commonly known as the bluebells or bluebell plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
