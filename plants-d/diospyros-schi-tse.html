Diospyros schi-tse

Diospyros schi-tse, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is rich in sugars, vitamins, and minerals, making it a valuable food source.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
