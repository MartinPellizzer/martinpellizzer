Deina polonica var. ramosa

Deina polonica var. ramosa, commonly known as the branched sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often branched, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
