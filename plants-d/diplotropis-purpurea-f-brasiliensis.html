Diplotropis purpurea f. brasiliensis

Diplotropis purpurea f. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian purple cypress, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and aromatic wood. The plant typically grows in humid, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often have a reddish or purple hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
