Diplopappus integerrimus

Diplopappus integerrimus, commonly known as the rigid burrweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its distinctive, rough, and spiny fruits that remain attached to the plant for an extended period. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapting to the arid environments where it thrives.

The species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
