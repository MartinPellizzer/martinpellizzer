Deutzia glauca var. glauca

Deutzia glauca var. glauca, commonly known as the dwarf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, compact foliage with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, blue-black berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a branching habit that contributes to its ornamental value.
