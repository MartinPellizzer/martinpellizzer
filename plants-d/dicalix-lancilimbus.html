Dicalix lancilimbus

Dicalix lancilimbus, commonly known as the slender-leaved dicalix, is a unique member of the Dicalix genus, characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale yellow. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem that supports its upright growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
