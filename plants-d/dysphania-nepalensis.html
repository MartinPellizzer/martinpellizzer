Dysphania nepalensis

Dysphania nepalensis, commonly known as Himalayan lamb's quarters, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Himalayan region.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and forest edges. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a fleshy, triangular capsule, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

Dysphania nepalensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
