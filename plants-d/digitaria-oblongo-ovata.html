Digitaria oblongo-ovata

Digitaria oblongo-ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf grass or narrowleaf fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as fields and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other grasses in grassland ecosystems.

Digitaria oblongo-ovata is adapted to withstand drought conditions and can thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
