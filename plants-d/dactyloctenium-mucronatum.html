Dactyloctenium mucronatum

Dactyloctenium mucronatum, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, spreading stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, three-lobed seed capsules. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it both a persistent challenge and a resilient plant species.
