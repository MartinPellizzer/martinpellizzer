Debia andamanica

Debia andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman mountain fig, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution.
