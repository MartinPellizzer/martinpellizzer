Dendrobium kelamense

Dendrobium kelamense, commonly known as the Kelamense orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features long, cane-like stems that can store water and nutrients, which helps it survive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, yellow, or green, with intricate patterns.

Dendrobium kelamense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
