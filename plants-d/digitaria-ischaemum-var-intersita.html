Digitaria ischaemum var. intersita

Digitaria ischaemum var. intersita, commonly known as the common bermudagrass, is a perennial grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both sterile and fertile florets. It is highly adaptable, capable of thriving in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.

This species is often found in lawns, pastures, and disturbed areas due to its aggressive growth and resilience.
