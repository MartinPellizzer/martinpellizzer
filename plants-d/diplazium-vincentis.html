Diplazium vincentis

Diplazium vincentis, commonly known as Vincent's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and rice fields. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often submerged or floating. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense mats on the water surface.

Diplazium vincentis plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
