Diospyros nsambensis

Diospyros nsambensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
