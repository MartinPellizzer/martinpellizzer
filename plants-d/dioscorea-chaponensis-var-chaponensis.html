Dioscorea chaponensis var. chaponensis

Dioscorea chaponensis var. chaponensis, commonly known as the Chapon's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. Its tubers are typically cylindrical and have a rough, brownish skin with a starchy, sweet taste.

It is valued both for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses in its native habitat.
