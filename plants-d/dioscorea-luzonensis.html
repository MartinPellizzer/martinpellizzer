Dioscorea luzonensis

Dioscorea luzonensis, commonly known as Luzon yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are the primary edible part. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers of Dioscorea luzonensis are valued for their nutritional content and traditional uses in local cuisines.
