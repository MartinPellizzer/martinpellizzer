Diospyros cleistantha

Diospyros cleistantha, commonly known as the small persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to red or orange when mature.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, astringent flavor, making it a valuable species in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
