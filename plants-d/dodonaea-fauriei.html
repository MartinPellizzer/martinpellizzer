Dodonaea fauriei

Dodonaea fauriei, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, delicate leaves that are typically silvery-white in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Dodonaea fauriei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
