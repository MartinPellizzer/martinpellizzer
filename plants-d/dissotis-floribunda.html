Dissotis floribunda

Dissotis floribunda, commonly known as the tropical spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, tropical climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Dissotis floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
