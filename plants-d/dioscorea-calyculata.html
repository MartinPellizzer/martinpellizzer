Dioscorea calyculata

Dioscorea calyculata, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on different plants. Its tubers, which are the primary edible part, are used in traditional cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
