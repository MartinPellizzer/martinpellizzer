Dolichos roseus

Dolichos roseus, commonly known as the rose cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive rose-colored pattern, and its flowers are typically pink to purple. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of protein.

Dolichos roseus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants in the vicinity.
