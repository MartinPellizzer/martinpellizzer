Daphnopsis longifolia

Daphnopsis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved daphnopsis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Daphnopsis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
