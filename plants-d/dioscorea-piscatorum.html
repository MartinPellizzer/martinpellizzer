Dioscorea piscatorum

Dioscorea piscatorum, commonly known as the fisher's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are slender and covered in fine hairs.

The tubers of Dioscorea piscatorum are sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
