Daphne angustifolia var. affghanica

Daphne angustifolia var. affghanica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and cold hardiness, it is a favored choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
