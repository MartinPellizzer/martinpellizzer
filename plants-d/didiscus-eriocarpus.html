Didiscus eriocarpus

Didiscus eriocarpus, commonly known as the woolly burr or silky burr, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and spiny fruit capsules. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its unique, woody, and persistent fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

Didiscus eriocarpus is often found in dry, open habitats and is known for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
