Dianella odorata f. lorifolia

Dianella odorata f. lorifolia, commonly known as the fragrant lily or sweet lily, is a cultivar of the genus Dianella native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces striking, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shady garden areas.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and as a cut flower.
