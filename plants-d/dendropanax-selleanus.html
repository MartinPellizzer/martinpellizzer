Dendropanax selleanus

Dendropanax selleanus, commonly known as the golden star sedge, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a yellow midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
