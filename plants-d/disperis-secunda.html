Disperis secunda

Disperis secunda, commonly known as the African orchid or the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of violet or purple flowers with intricate patterns. Disperis secunda prefers well-drained, sandy soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
