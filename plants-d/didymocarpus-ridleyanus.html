Didymocarpus ridleyanus

Didymocarpus ridleyanus, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense evergreen forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
