Dicoma anomala

Dicoma anomala, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and inland areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Dicoma anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
