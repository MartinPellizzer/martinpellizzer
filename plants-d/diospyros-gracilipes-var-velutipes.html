Diospyros gracilipes var. velutipes

Diospyros gracilipes var. velutipes, commonly known as the velvet persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a velvety texture on the upper surface, giving it its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
