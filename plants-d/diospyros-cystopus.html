Diospyros cystopus

Diospyros cystopus, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to red berries.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices due to its unique flavor and nutritional content.
