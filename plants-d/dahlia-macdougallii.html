Dahlia macdougallii

Dahlia macdougallii, commonly known as the MacDougall dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sturdy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Its tuberous roots are used for propagation, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
