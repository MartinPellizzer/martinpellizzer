Dalea leucostachya

Dalea leucostachya, commonly known as white-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Dalea leucostachya plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
