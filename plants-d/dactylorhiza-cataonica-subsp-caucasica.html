Dactylorhiza cataonica subsp. caucasica

Dactylorhiza cataonica subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces a single spike of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and peat bogs.

This subspecies is distinguished by its morphological features and ecological preferences, making it a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.
