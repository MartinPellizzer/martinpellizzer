Dendrocalamus luteus

Dendrocalamus luteus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant is widely cultivated for its strong and lightweight bamboo poles, which are used in construction and crafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Dendrocalamus luteus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
