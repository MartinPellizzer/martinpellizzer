Derouetia frigida

Derouetia frigida, commonly known as the cold-tolerant orchid, is a species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale green, blooming in late summer.

Derouetia frigida is adapted to withstand frost and low temperatures, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
