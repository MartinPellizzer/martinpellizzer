Draba crassifolia subvar. dasycarpa

Draba crassifolia subvar. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy fruits and robust, mat-forming growth habit. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This plant is adapted to cold climates and is a common component of high-altitude vegetation.
