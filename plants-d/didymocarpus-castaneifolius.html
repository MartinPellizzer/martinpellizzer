Didymocarpus castaneifolius

Didymocarpus castaneifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf nut tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
