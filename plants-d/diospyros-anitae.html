Diospyros anitae

Diospyros anitae, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into clustered, orange-red fruits. The fruit is typically edible when fully ripe, though it may have astringent properties when unripe.

It is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
