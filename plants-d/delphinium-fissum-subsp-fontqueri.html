Delphinium fissum subsp. fontqueri

Delphinium fissum subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple spurred flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
