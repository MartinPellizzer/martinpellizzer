Draba aurea f. surculifera

Draba aurea f. surculifera, commonly known as the golden rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a compact rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to yellowish hairs. The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its foliage and floral characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Brassicaceae family.
