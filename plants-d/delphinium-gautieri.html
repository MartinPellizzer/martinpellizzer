Delphinium gautieri

Delphinium gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves, and it produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
