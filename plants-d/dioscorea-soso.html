Dioscorea soso

Dioscorea soso, commonly known as wild yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant with tubers that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are rich in compounds such as diosgenin, which has been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Dioscorea soso plays an important role in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
