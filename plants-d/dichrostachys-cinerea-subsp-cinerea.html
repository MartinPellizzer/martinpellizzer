Dichrostachys cinerea subsp. cinerea

Dichrostachys cinerea subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf burr or silverleaf acacia, is a shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by its grey-green, leathery leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny pods.

The plant is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment in degraded areas.
