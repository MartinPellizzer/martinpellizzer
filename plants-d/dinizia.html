Dinizia

Dinizia, commonly known as the Brazilian spruce, is a species of coniferous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its botanical features include a tall, straight trunk and dense foliage that provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Dinizia plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
