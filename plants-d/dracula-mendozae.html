Dracula mendozae

Dracula mendozae, commonly known as the Mendoza's vampire orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, almost skeletal appearance with long, thin leaves and delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant's unique morphology, including its fleshy, red-tinged roots, is adapted to its humid, shaded habitat. It thrives in high-altitude environments with consistent moisture and dappled sunlight.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, Dracula mendozae is considered a conservation concern.
