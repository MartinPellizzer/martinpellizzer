Dipterocarpus validus

Dipterocarpus validus, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

This species plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
