Dahlia purpurea var. rubra

Dahlia purpurea var. rubra, commonly known as the red dahlia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, single or double flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, with a velvety texture. The plant has tuberous roots and is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
