Dianthus pratensis subsp. pratensis

Dianthus pratensis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as the meadow campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features showy pink or white flowers with five petals, arranged in a cluster at the top of slender stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
