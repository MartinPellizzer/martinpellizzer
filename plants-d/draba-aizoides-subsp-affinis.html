Draba aizoides subsp. affinis

Draba aizoides subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and cruciform petals.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has basal leaves that are often linear or oblanceolate, and its flowering period occurs in early spring.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
