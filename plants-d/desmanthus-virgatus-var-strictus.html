Desmanthus virgatus var. strictus

Desmanthus virgatus var. strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a perennial forage plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, followed by elongated, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in pasture and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
