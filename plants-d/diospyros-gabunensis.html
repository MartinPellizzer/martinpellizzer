Diospyros gabunensis

Diospyros gabunensis, commonly known as the Gabon persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and alternate, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy berries.

Its fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed locally and are not widely cultivated outside of its native range.
