Dendrophthora confertiflora

Dendrophthora confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to penetrate the host's vascular system and extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a leathery texture that helps reduce water loss.

Dendrophthora confertiflora plays a role in ecological interactions, providing food for certain insects and birds while also impacting the growth of its host trees.
