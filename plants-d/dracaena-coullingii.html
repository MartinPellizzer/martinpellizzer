Dracaena coullingii

Dracaena coullingii, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a distinctive, branched stem with a rough, bark-like texture and produces clusters of small, white flowers that turn into red, fleshy fruits. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Dracaena coullingii is popular as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
