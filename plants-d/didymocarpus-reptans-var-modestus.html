Didymocarpus reptans var. modestus

Didymocarpus reptans var. modestus, commonly known as the smallleaf sandmat, is a low-growing, perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in sandy soils, while its stems spread horizontally to form dense mats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
