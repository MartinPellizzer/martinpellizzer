Dasyaulus floribundus

Dasyaulus floribundus, commonly known as the vibrant bloom shrub, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and striking clusters of brightly colored flowers that bloom throughout the warm seasons. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a unique appearance. Its flowers are typically tubular or bell-shaped, with vibrant hues of red, orange, and yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dasyaulus floribundus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, well-drained soils.
