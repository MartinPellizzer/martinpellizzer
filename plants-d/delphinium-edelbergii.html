Delphinium edelbergii

Delphinium edelbergii, commonly known as the Edelberg's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-green color.

Delphinium edelbergii is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or grassy slopes at high elevations.
