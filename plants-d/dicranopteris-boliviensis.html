Dicranopteris boliviensis

Dicranopteris boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are compound, with small, toothed pinnae that help in photosynthesis and gas exchange.

Dicranopteris boliviensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
