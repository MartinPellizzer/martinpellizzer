Deguelia floribunda

Deguelia floribunda, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

The plant has some ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
