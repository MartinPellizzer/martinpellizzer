Dianthus × lamyi

Dianthus × lamyi, commonly known as the Shirley poppy, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, double-flowered blooms that come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features dense, foliage with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Dianthus × lamyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
