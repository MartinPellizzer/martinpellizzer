Dasispermum suffruticosum

Dasispermum suffruticosum, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. Its milky sap is toxic and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a natural insecticide.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
