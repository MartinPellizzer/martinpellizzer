Draba mutabilis

Draba mutabilis, commonly known as the variable whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants, and is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, alpine environments. Draba mutabilis is an annual or biennial species that often grows in open, sunny habitats.

Its flowers are typically short-lived, blooming for only a few days before setting seed.
