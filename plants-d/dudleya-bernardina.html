Dudleya bernardina

Dudleya bernardina, commonly known as the Bernard's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for gardeners.
