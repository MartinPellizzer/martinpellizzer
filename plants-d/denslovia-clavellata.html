Denslovia clavellata

Denslovia clavellata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and showy clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody, branching stems that can grow quite tall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Denslovia clavellata is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects due to its vibrant flowers and nectar-rich blooms.
