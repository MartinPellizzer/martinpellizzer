Diasperus sericeus

Diasperus sericeus, commonly known as the silky dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, allowing for efficient propagation. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its silky texture.

Diasperus sericeus is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.
