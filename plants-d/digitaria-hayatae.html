Digitaria hayatae

Digitaria hayatae, commonly known as the Japanese digitaria, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Digitaria hayatae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
