Dyschoriste hygrophiloides

Dyschoriste hygrophiloides, commonly known as the water dyschoriste, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in marshy areas, along riverbanks, and in other moist environments where it can thrive in saturated soils. The plant has slender, often prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

The small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or yellowish and are borne in clusters, contributing to its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
