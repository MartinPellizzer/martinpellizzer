Diospyros collinsiae

Diospyros collinsiae, commonly known as the Collins' persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruits are generally small, round, and edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
