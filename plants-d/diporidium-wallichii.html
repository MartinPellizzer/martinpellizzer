Diporidium wallichii

Diporidium wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet petals, which are often speckled with contrasting colors, making it visually distinctive. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological significance in its native habitat.
