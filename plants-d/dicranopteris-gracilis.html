Dicranopteris gracilis

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense mat of root-like structures, aiding in its anchorage.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a distinctive linear pattern along the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
