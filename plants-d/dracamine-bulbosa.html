Dracamine bulbosa

Dracamine bulbosa, commonly known as the dragon's tooth plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
