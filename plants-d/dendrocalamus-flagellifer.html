Dendrocalamus flagellifer

Dendrocalamus flagellifer, commonly known as the long-leaf bamboo, is a tall, rapidly growing grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, elongated culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and is often found in moist, tropical environments. It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its use in construction and crafts.

Its rapid growth makes it a useful species for reforestation and sustainable harvesting.
