Dioscorea laxiflora var. calystegioides

Dioscorea laxiflora var. calystegioides, commonly known as the purple yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fleshy, purple-hued tubers. These tubers are not only ornamental but also have traditional culinary and medicinal uses.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
