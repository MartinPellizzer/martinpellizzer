Draba hirta var. glabrescens

Draba hirta var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems that become glabrous as the plant matures. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered with short, stiff hairs.

It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in open, sun-exposed habitats.
