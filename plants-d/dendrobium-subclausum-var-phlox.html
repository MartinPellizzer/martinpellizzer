Dendrobium subclausum var. phlox

Dendrobium subclausum var. phlox, commonly known as the pink-stemmed dendrobium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, cane-like growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments with good air circulation. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy texture, and its stems are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water.

This variant is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and ornamental value.
