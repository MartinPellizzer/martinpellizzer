Derris involuta

Derris involuta, commonly known as the African snakewood, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Derris involuta is notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of toxic compounds, particularly the alkaloid derrisine.
