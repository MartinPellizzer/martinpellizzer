Dendrobium quadrifidum

Dendrobium quadrifidum, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. The plant has a unique morphology characterized by its four-lobed pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with a distinct red or purple lip.

Dendrobium quadrifidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
