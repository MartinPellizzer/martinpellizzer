Dolianthus montiswilhelmii

Dolianthus montiswilhelmii, commonly known as the Wilhelm's mountain dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species adapted to mountainous environments.

Dolianthus montiswilhelmii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
