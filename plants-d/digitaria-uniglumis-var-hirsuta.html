Digitaria uniglumis var. hirsuta

Digitaria uniglumis var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow with a hairy underside, contributing to its common name.

It is often found in warm climates and can be invasive in certain environments.
