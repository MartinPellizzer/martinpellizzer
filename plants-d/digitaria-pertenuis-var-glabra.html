Digitaria pertenuis var. glabra

Digitaria pertenuis var. glabra, commonly known as slender digit grass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three flowering branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

The variety glabra is distinguished by its smooth, hairless surface, which sets it apart from other variants of Digitaria pertenuis.
