Diplacrum exiguum

Diplacrum exiguum, commonly known as the slender diplacrum, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, where it thrives in well-drained sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Diplacrum exiguum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique foliage.
