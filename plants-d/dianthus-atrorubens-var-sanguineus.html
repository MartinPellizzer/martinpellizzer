Dianthus atrorubens var. sanguineus

Dianthus atrorubens var. sanguineus, commonly known as the blood-red campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters atop slender stems. The plant has basal leaves that are oval and hairy, while the upper leaves are lance-shaped and sessile. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
