Dipterocarpus camphorus

Dipterocarpus camphorus, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and large, leathery leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making, and for its camphor-rich sap.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
