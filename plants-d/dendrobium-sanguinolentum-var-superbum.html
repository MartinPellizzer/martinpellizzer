Dendrobium sanguinolentum var. superbum

Dendrobium sanguinolentum var. superbum, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, red-brown inflorescences. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
