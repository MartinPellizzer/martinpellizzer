Dianthus trifasciculatus subsp. trifasciculatus

Dianthus trifasciculatus subsp.

trifasciculatus, commonly known as the three-veined campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features striking, pink to red flowers with five petals, arranged in a showy cluster at the top of slender stems. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is characterized by its distinct three-veined leaves and is well adapted to rocky and dry habitats.
