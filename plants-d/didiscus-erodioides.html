Didiscus erodioides

Didiscus erodioides, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Didiscus erodioides is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
