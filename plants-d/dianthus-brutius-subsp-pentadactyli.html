Dianthus brutius subsp. pentadactyli

Dianthus brutius subsp.

pentadactyli, commonly known as the five-finger campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
