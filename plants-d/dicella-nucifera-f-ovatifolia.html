Dicella nucifera f. ovatifolia

Dicella nucifera f. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf betel plant, is a variant of the betel nut palm characterized by its ovate-shaped leaves.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is known for its large, palmate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fruit, which contains the betel nut, is used in traditional practices and has economic significance.

The foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
