Drymocallis incisa

Drymocallis incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Drymocallis incisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
