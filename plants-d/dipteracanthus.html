Dipteracanthus

Dipteracanthus, commonly known as the golden spike or the spike flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescence that resembles a golden spike, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a yellowish or greenish hue.

Dipteracanthus species are often found in tropical rainforests and are valued for their ornamental appearance and ecological importance.
