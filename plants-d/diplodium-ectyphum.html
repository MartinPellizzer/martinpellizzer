Diplodium ectyphum

Diplodium ectyphum, commonly known as the red-stemmed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its striking red stems and yellow flowers make it a notable species in its native habitat.

Diplodium ectyphum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
