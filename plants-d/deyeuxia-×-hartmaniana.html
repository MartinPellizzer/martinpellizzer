Deyeuxia × hartmaniana

Deyeuxia × hartmaniana, commonly known as the Hartman's false rice, is a hybrid grass species native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that resemble those of true rice plants. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to form dense, mat-like growths.

Deyeuxia × hartmaniana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
