Disa pillansii

Disa pillansii, commonly known as the Pillans's orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, clustered flowers along its stem. Disa pillansii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
