Dracocephalum nutans subsp. subarctium

Dracocephalum nutans subsp.

subarctium, commonly known as Arctic blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as tundra and mountain meadows.

The plant features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, with lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense spikes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in cold climates and its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
