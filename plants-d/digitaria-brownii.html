Digitaria brownii

Digitaria brownii, commonly known as brown bristlegrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Digitaria brownii is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
