Ditassa consanguinea

Ditassa consanguinea, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its sap is milky and contains toxic compounds, which deter herbivores.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
