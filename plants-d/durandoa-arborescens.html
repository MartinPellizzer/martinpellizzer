Durandoa arborescens

Durandoa arborescens, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed.
