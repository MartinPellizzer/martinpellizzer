Dendropemon acutifolius

Dendropemon acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leafed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a unique rhizome system that supports its growth and helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically acuminate, giving it the "sharp-leafed" descriptor in its scientific name.

Dendropemon acutifolius plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
