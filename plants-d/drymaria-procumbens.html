Drymaria procumbens

Drymaria procumbens, commonly known as carpet grass or groundsel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, producing small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and is often found in moist, disturbed soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Drymaria procumbens is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
