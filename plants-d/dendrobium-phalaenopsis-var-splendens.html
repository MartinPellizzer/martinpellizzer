Dendrobium phalaenopsis var. splendens

Dendrobium phalaenopsis var. splendens, commonly known as the splendour orchid, is a hybrid orchid prized for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it typically grows as an epiphyte on trees. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that produce multiple flowers in a raceme. Its flowers are usually bright yellow with a deep orange center, and they emit a subtle fragrance.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
