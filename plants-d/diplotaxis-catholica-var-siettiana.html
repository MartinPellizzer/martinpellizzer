Diplotaxis catholica var. siettiana

Diplotaxis catholica var. siettiana, commonly known as the Siettian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
