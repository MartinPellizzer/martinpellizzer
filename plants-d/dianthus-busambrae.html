Dianthus busambrae

Dianthus busambrae, commonly known as the Malagasy carnation, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers consist of five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern.

Dianthus busambrae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
