Dodecatheon pulchellum subsp. alaskanum

Dodecatheon pulchellum subsp.

alaskanum, commonly known as the Alaskan glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its showy, nodding white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its adaptability in cool climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
