Dudleya saxosa subsp. saxosa

Dudleya saxosa subsp.

saxosa, commonly known as the Sierra Valley Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white powdery bloom.

The plant produces tall flowering stalks that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
