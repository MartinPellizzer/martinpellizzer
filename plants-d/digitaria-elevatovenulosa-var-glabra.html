Digitaria elevatovenulosa var. glabra

Digitaria elevatovenulosa var. glabra, commonly known as smooth digitaria, is a grass species characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and cylindrical seed heads.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are arranged in a panicle structure.

The leaves are narrow and have a prominent midrib, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
