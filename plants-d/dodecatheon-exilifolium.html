Dodecatheon exilifolium

Dodecatheon exilifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf glory-of-the-snow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, making it a valuable early-season nectar source for pollinators.
