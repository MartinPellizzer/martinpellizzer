Diasperus peltandrus

Diasperus peltandrus, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to sandy soils.

Diasperus peltandrus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
