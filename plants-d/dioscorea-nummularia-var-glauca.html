Dioscorea nummularia var. glauca

Dioscorea nummularia var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-leaf yam, is a variant of the yam genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically followed by oval, tuberous roots. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value rather than its edible tubers.
