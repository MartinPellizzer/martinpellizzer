Discaria febrifuga

Discaria febrifuga, commonly known as the fever bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine, where it has been used to treat fever and other ailments. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The leaves and roots are typically harvested for their therapeutic uses.
