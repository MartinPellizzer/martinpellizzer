Disa aurata

Disa aurata, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from red, orange, yellow, and white, creating a dazzling display. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and are often arranged in clusters.

Disa aurata is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
