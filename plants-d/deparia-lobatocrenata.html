Deparia lobatocrenata

Deparia lobatocrenata, commonly known as the broad-lobed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it forms dense, floating colonies. The plant has feather-like fronds that are divided into broad, lobed segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its rhizomes spread along the water's surface, anchoring the plant and facilitating vegetative reproduction.

The leaves are capable of photosynthesis and contribute to water oxygenation, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.
