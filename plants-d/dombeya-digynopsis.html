Dombeya digynopsis

Dombeya digynopsis, commonly known as the velvetleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy undersides, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Dombeya digynopsis is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
