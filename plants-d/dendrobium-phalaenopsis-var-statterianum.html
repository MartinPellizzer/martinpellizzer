Dendrobium phalaenopsis var. statterianum

Dendrobium phalaenopsis var. statterianum, commonly known as the Statter's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent plants.

It is an epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. The plant features long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are showy, with intricate patterns and a range of colors, often resembling the appearance of butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its beauty and resilience in various growing conditions.
