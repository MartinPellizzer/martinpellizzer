Dombeya niangaraensis

Dombeya niangaraensis, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, forested areas. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are white or pale pink, often with a fragrant scent. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually have a velvety texture.

Dombeya niangaraensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
