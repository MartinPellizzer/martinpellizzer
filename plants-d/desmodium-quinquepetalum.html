Desmodium quinquepetalum

Desmodium quinquepetalum, commonly known as the five-petaled burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its characteristic pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are heart-shaped and hairy.

Desmodium quinquepetalum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
