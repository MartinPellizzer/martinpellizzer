Delphinium brevicorne

Delphinium brevicorne, commonly known as the short-horned larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium brevicorne is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
