Dendrochilum crassifolium var. murudense

Dendrochilum crassifolium var. murudense, commonly known as the Murudense Tree Orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, tree-like growth habit, with thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to its humid, tropical environment. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, moist conditions and is often found growing on the trunks of trees in the understory of dense forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in conservation efforts.
