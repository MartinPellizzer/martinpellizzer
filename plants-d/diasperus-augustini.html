Diasperus augustini

Diasperus augustini, commonly known as the Augustini's rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves. Native to arid and rocky environments, this species is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions. The plant typically grows close to the ground, with a compact stature that helps it conserve moisture.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of xerophytic vegetation.
