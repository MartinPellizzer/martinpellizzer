Dendrobium pseudaclinia

Dendrobium pseudaclinia, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
