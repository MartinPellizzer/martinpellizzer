Diosma vulgaris

Diosma vulgaris, commonly known as the common diosma, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Diosma vulgaris is often found in woodland edges and meadows, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
