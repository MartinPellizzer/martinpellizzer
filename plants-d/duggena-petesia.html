Duggena petesia

Duggena petesia, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Duggena petesia is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
