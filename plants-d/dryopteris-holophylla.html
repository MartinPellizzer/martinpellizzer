Dryopteris holophylla

Dryopteris holophylla, commonly known as the evergreen wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that remain green throughout the year. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its fronds are usually broad and leathery, with a distinctive, finely divided structure.

Dryopteris holophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, foliage.
