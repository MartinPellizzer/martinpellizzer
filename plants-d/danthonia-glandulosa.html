Danthonia glandulosa

Danthonia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive glandular surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in dry, open habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various native wildlife species.
