Delphinium californicum subsp. interius

Delphinium californicum subsp.

interius, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its striking floral display makes it a valuable addition to native gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
