Desmodium tastense

Desmodium tastense, commonly known as the creeping sicklepod, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that split open when mature.

Desmodium tastense is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
