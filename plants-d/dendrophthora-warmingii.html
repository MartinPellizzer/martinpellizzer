Dendrophthora warmingii

Dendrophthora warmingii, commonly known as the warmii mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract pollinators like bees and wasps. Its berries are often consumed by birds, which help disperse its seeds.

Dendrophthora warmingii plays a unique ecological role in forest ecosystems by influencing host tree health and supporting wildlife.
