Diphasiastrum novoguineense

Diphasiastrum novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea star grass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight bluish tint, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
