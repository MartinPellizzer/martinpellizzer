Dalbergia schimperiana

Dalbergia schimperiana, commonly known as African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
