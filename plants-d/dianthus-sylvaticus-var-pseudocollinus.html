Dianthus sylvaticus var. pseudocollinus

Dianthus sylvaticus var. pseudocollinus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to cold, rocky environments. It is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
