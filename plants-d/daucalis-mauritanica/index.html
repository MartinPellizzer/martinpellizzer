Daucalis Mauritanica

Daucalis mauritanica is a rare and hardy plant native to the arid regions of North Africa. It thrives in harsh, dry environments with minimal water and intense sunlight. This species belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley. Its unique appearance features small, clustered flowers and a fibrous root system adapted to survival in extreme conditions. Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
