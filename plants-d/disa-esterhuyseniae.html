Disa esterhuyseniae

Disa esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysenia Disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant pink to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, showcasing its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
