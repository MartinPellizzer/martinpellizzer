Dasyphyllum infundibulare

Dasyphyllum infundibulare, commonly known as the hooded dasyphyllum, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, often with a distinctive hood-like structure on the calyx. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Dasyphyllum infundibulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
