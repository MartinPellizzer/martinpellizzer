Digitalis charrelii

Digitalis charrelii, commonly known as the Alpine foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Digitalis charrelii is also noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
