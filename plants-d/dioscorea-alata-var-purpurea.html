Dioscorea alata var. purpurea

Dioscorea alata var. purpurea, commonly known as purple yam, is a variant of the greater yam species native to tropical regions.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tubers that are typically purple or violet in color, though they can vary in hue depending on the variety. These tubers are valued for their nutritional content and are widely cultivated in Asia and Africa.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
