Dianthus kastembeluensis

Dianthus kastembeluensis, commonly known as the Kastembelu carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Dianthus kastembeluensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
