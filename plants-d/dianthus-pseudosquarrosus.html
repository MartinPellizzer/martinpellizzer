Dianthus pseudosquarrosus

Dianthus pseudosquarrosus, commonly known as the fringed campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.

Dianthus pseudosquarrosus is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to coastal and arid environments.
