Dipterocarpus condorensis subsp. condorensis

Dipterocarpus condorensis subsp.

condorensis, commonly known as the Condor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the western Amazon basin. It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy seeds and buttressed roots.

The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a waxy surface.

The species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
