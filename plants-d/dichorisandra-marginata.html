Dichorisandra marginata

Dichorisandra marginata, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers surrounded by vibrant, papery bracts.

This plant is well-suited for gardens and containers, thriving in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
