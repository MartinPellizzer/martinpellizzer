Danthonia racemosa var. penicillata

Danthonia racemosa var. penicillata, commonly known as the tufted hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
