Dianthus orientalis subsp. stenocalyx

Dianthus orientalis subsp.

stenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-calyx campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink to red flowers with five petals, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a desirable ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
