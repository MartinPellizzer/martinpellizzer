Dalea polyphylla

Dalea polyphylla, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Dalea polyphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
