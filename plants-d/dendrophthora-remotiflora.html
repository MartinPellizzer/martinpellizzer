Dendrophthora remotiflora

Dendrophthora remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flower tree, is a unique botanical species characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences.

Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters along its woody stem, are notable for their pale, translucent petals and sweet, fragrant nectar. The plant's roots form symbiotic relationships with specific fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Dendrophthora remotiflora is considered a conservation priority in its native habitat.
