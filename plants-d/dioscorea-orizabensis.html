Dioscorea orizabensis

Dioscorea orizabensis, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a perennial vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers, which are the main part used by humans, are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
