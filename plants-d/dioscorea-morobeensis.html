Dioscorea morobeensis

Dioscorea morobeensis, commonly known as the Morobe yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its edible, starchy tubers that are a staple food in traditional diets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its tubers are often used in local cuisines and have cultural significance in the regions where it is found.

The plant's botanical features include broad, heart-shaped leaves and a climbing habit, with its flowers being small and inconspicuous.
