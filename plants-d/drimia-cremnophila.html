Drimia cremnophila

Drimia cremnophila, commonly known as the rock hyacinth, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in mountainous regions. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that allow it to survive dry periods.

Drimia cremnophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
