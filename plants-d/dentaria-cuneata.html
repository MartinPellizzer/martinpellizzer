Dentaria cuneata

Dentaria cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf toothwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, tooth-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Dentaria cuneata is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
