Delphinium virescens var. wootonii

Delphinium virescens var. wootonii, commonly known as the Wooton's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.

This variety is notable for its striking floral display and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
