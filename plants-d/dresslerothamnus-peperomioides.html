Dresslerothamnus peperomioides

Dresslerothamnus peperomioides, commonly known as the Peperomia-like shrub, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with a waxy, glossy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, often followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
