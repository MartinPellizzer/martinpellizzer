Dendrocalamus tulda

Dendrocalamus tulda, commonly known as the bamboo of the Himalayas, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in construction and handicrafts. The plant thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Dendrocalamus tulda plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
