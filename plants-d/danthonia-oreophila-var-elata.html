Danthonia oreophila var. elata

Danthonia oreophila var. elata, commonly known as the mountain danthonia, is a grass species native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense tufts and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
