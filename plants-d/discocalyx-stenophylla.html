Discocalyx stenophylla

Discocalyx stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf discocalyx, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in inflorescences at the tips of the stems. Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to moist, shaded environments in its natural habitat.

Discocalyx stenophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
