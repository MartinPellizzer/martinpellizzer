Dichanthelium flavovirens

Dichanthelium flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with yellow or green hues. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, and it is adapted to tolerate wet conditions.

Dichanthelium flavovirens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
