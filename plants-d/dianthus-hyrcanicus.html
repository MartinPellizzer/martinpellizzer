Dianthus hyrcanicus

Dianthus hyrcanicus, commonly known as the Hyrcanian campion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dianthus hyrcanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
