Dimorphocalyx glabellus

Dimorphocalyx glabellus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved evergreen, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
