Dissotis bambutorum

Dissotis bambutorum, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, wiry stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
