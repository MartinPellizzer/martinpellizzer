Dryopteris chiapasensis

Dryopteris chiapasensis, commonly known as the Chiapas shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce multiple fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
