Daphne arisanensis

Daphne arisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and unique foliage.
