Deyeuxia minima

Deyeuxia minima, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a small perennial herb belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Deyeuxia minima plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
