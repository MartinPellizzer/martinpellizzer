Dalea compacta var. pubescens

Dalea compacta var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, short hairs, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
