Dorstenia variegata

Dorstenia variegata, commonly known as the variegated false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with distinctive variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of ornamental plants.
