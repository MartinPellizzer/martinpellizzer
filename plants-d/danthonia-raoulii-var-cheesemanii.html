Danthonia raoulii var. cheesemanii

Danthonia raoulii var. cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's danthonia, is a grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or open habitats. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
