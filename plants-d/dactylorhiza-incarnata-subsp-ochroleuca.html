Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. ochroleuca

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

The plant produces tall, slender stems with several pale yellow to white flowers that have a distinctive, intricate structure. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

This orchid is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies.
