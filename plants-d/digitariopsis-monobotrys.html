Digitariopsis monobotrys

Digitariopsis monobotrys, commonly known as the single-stemmed digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a prominent midrib.

Digitariopsis monobotrys is primarily valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
