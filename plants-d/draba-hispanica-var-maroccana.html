Draba hispanica var. maroccana

Draba hispanica var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, basal leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with edible and medicinal uses.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
