Diospyros tenuipes

Diospyros tenuipes, commonly known as the slender persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a sweet, edible pulp when ripe, and it is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in crafting and construction.
