Digitalis purpurea var. humilis

Digitalis purpurea var. humilis, commonly known as the common foxglove, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces a distinctive bell-shaped flower that opens in the morning and closes in the evening. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine downy hair.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
