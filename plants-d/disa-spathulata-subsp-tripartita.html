Disa spathulata subsp. tripartita

Disa spathulata subsp.

tripartita, commonly known as the Tripartite orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is a terrestrial herb with a short, erect stem and produces showy, three-lobed flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically blooms during the summer months and is characterized by its distinctive, tripartite division of the lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or forest margins.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
