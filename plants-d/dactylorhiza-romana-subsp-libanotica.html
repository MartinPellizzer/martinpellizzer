Dactylorhiza romana subsp. libanotica

Dactylorhiza romana subsp.

libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean. It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, producing a single upright stem with several leaves.

The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in calcareous soils and requires a cool, moist environment with partial shade.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and protected in many regions.
