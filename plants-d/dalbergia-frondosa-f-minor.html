Dalbergia frondosa f. minor

Dalbergia frondosa f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf rosewood, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its hard, dark wood, which is highly valued in traditional woodworking. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
