Dorycnium schimperi

Dorycnium schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian burr, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive burr-like seed pods. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a sprawling growth habit, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its seeds are encased in tough, spiky pods that aid in dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.

Dorycnium schimperi is valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant in arid regions.
