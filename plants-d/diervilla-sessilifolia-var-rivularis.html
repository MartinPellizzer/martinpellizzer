Diervilla sessilifolia var. rivularis

Diervilla sessilifolia var. rivularis, commonly known as the river diervilla, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along stream banks and in woodland edges.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
