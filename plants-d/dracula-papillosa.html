Dracula papillosa

Dracula papillosa, commonly known as the pink dracula, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink petals and a unique, elongated flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic visual element to the forest canopy.

Dracula papillosa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
