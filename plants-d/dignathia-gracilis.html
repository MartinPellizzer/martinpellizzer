Dignathia gracilis

Dignathia gracilis, commonly known as the slender thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in small, sharp spines. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the winter months, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are small, linear, and often shed during dry periods, reducing water loss.

Dignathia gracilis is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.
