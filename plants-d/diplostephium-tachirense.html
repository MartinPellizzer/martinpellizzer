Diplostephium tachirense

Diplostephium tachirense, commonly known as the Tachira false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy surface. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat head, and the plant produces small, oval fruits called cypsela.
