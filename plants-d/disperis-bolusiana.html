Disperis bolusiana

Disperis bolusiana, commonly known as the Bolus's orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with intricate patterns.

Disperis bolusiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
