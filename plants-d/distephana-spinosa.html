Distephana spinosa

Distephana spinosa, commonly known as the spiny distephana, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which provide a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color. Its growth habit is generally shrub-like, with multiple branches emerging from the base.

Distephana spinosa is adapted to grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
