Drymocallis gracilis

Drymocallis gracilis, commonly known as slender frostweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Drymocallis gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
