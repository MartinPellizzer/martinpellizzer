Didymocarpus crinitus

Didymocarpus crinitus, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
