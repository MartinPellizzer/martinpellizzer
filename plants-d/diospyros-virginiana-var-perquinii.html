Diospyros virginiana var. perquinii

Diospyros virginiana var. perquinii, commonly known as the persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange to reddish fruit, which is edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is rich in nutrients, making it an important food source for wildlife.

This variety is closely related to the common persimmon, Diospyros virginiana, and shares similar botanical features.
