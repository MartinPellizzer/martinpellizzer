Draco arborea

Draco arborea, commonly known as the dragon tree, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and a distinctive crown of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Draco arborea is known for its ability to store water in its trunk, making it drought-resistant.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
