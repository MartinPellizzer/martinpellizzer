Dolichos japonicus

Dolichos japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various culinary traditions.

Additionally, Dolichos japonicus has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
