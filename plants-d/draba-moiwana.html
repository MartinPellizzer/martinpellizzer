Draba moiwana

Draba moiwana, commonly known as the alpine penny-cress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. This species typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal and rosette-like, and it produces small white or yellow flowers during the summer months.

Draba moiwana is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
