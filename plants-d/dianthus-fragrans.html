Dianthus fragrans

Dianthus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features serrated, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delightful fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus fragrans is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in floral arrangements.
