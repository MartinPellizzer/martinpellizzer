Dodecatheon meadia subsp. brachycarpum

Dodecatheon meadia subsp.

brachycarpum, commonly known as the short-carpeleland bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with five distinct lobes.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows.
