Delphinium propinquum

Delphinium propinquum, commonly known as the dwarf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and mountainous regions, reaching heights of up to 30 inches. The plant features tall, slender stems with clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium propinquum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.
