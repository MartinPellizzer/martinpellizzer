Draba lactea var. ciliata

Draba lactea var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, cylindrical stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in harsh mountainous habitats.
