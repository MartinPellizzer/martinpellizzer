Dianthus nevadensis

Dianthus nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
