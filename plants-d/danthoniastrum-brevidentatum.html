Danthoniastrum brevidentatum

Danthoniastrum brevidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-toothed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive, short, sharp teeth along the edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Danthoniastrum brevidentatum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
