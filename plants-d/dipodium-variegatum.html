Dipodium variegatum

Dipodium variegatum, commonly known as the variegated dipodium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that often display a striking variegation of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity and partial shade, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in conservation programs aimed at preserving its dwindling populations.
