Dichaea gomez-lauritoi

Dichaea gomez-lauritoi, commonly known as the Gomez-Laurito's bromeliad, is a rare epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces small, delicate white flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, hair-like structures that aid in water retention.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
