Dendrobium oreodoxa

Dendrobium oreodoxa, commonly known as the orchid with golden flowers, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a contrasting purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Dendrobium oreodoxa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
