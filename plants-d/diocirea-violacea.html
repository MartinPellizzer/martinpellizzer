Diocirea violacea

Diocirea violacea, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Diocirea violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
