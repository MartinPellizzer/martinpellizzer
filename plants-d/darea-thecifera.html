Darea thecifera

Darea thecifera, commonly known as the velvet tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Darea thecifera is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
