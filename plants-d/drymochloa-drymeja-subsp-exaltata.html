Drymochloa drymeja subsp. exaltata

Drymochloa drymeja subsp.

exaltata, commonly known as the tall dwarf bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its dense, compact growth habit.

The subspecies exaltata is distinguished by its taller stature compared to other varieties within the Drymochloa genus.
