Deschampsia novae-zelandiae

Deschampsia novae-zelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand feathergrass, is a grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

Deschampsia novae-zelandiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
