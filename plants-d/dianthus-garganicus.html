Dianthus garganicus

Dianthus garganicus, commonly known as the Sicilian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus garganicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
