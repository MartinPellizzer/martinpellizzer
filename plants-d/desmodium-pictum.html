Desmodium pictum

Desmodium pictum, commonly known as the painted burr or velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit is a hairy, spiny pod that contains several seeds.

Desmodium pictum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
