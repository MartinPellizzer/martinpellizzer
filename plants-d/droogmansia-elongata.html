Droogmansia elongata

Droogmansia elongata, commonly known as the elongated droogmansia, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence.

It features a tall, spathaceous flower that is often green or white in color, with a central spadix that bears small, densely packed flowers. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically large, broad, and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Droogmansia elongata is often cultivated for its unique and exotic appearance in tropical and subtropical gardens.
