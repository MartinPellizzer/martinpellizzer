Dactylorhiza traunsteinerioides

Dactylorhiza traunsteinerioides, commonly known as the Traunsteiner's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a complex root system with mycorrhizal associations that aid in nutrient absorption.

Dactylorhiza traunsteinerioides is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
