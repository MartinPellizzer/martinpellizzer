Desmodium kulhaitense

Desmodium kulhaitense, commonly known as the kulhaiti burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Desmodium kulhaitense is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
