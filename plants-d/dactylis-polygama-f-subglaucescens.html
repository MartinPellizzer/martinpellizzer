Dactylis polygama f. subglaucescens

Dactylis polygama f. subglaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous dactylis, is a variant of the common dactylis grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its glaucous, bluish-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
