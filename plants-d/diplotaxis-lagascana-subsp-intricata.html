Diplotaxis lagascana subsp. intricata

Diplotaxis lagascana subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The subspecies intricata is distinguished by its intricate leaf patterns and compact growth habit.
