Diospyros ferrea f. subcoriacea

Diospyros ferrea f. subcoriacea, commonly known as the ironwood or Brazilian cherry, is a botanical variant of the genus Diospyros.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, leathery leaves that provide a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a small, round drupe with a sweet, edible pulp.

This species is valued for its durability and is often used in construction and furniture-making.
