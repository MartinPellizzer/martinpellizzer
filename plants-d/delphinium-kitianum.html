Delphinium kitianum

Delphinium kitianum, commonly known as the Tibetan larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its striking, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Delphinium kitianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
