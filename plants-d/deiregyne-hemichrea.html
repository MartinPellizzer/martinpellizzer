Deiregyne hemichrea

Deiregyne hemichrea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in areas with moderate to high altitude. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with three sepals and three petals, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
