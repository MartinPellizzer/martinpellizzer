Draba spectabilis var. oxyloba

Draba spectabilis var. oxyloba, commonly known as the showy whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
