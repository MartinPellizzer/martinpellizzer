Drymoglossum underwoodianum

Drymoglossum underwoodianum, commonly known as the Underwood's feather moss, is a small, epiphytic moss that thrives in shaded, moist environments.

It typically grows on the bark of trees in temperate to tropical regions. The plant has a dense, cushion-like growth form with closely packed, dark green leaves that are often curved. Its sporophytes are relatively short, with a single, slender seta and a small, globose capsule.

This moss plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for other organisms.
