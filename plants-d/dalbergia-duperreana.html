Dalbergia duperreana

Dalbergia duperreana, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its density and fine grain. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in woodland areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
