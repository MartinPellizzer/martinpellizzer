Drosera intermedia f. gracilis

Drosera intermedia f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
