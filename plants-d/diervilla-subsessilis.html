Diervilla subsessilis

Diervilla subsessilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, which appear in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Diervilla subsessilis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky slopes.
