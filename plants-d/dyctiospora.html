Dyctiospora

These organisms are symbiotic, consisting of a fungal partner and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. Dyctiospora species are often found on rocks, tree bark, and soil surfaces in temperate and tropical regions. They are characterized by their crustose or foliose growth forms and produce distinctive spores in their apothecia.

The genus is notable for its chemical compounds, which can include depsides and depsipterpenoids, contributing to their ecological and potential medicinal significance.
