Diospyros xylocarpa

Diospyros xylocarpa, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a yellowish-orange berry that matures in autumn and is edible when fully ripe. The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various wildlife species.
