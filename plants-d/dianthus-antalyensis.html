Dianthus antalyensis

Dianthus antalyensis, commonly known as the Antalya carnation, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits known as capsules.

Dianthus antalyensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
