Dyckia yunguensis

Dyckia yunguensis, commonly known as the Yunguense Dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped foliage that is typically covered in dense, fibrous trichomes. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that bear showy, brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Dyckia yunguensis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
