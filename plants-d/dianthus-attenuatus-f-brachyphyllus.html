Dianthus attenuatus f. brachyphyllus

Dianthus attenuatus f. brachyphyllus, commonly known as the compact carnation, is a compact, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, arranged in clusters atop short, sturdy stems. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics. This cultivar is valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a bedding plant due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
