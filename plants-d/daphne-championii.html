Daphne championii

Daphne championii, commonly known as the Chilean daphne, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Daphne championii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution during handling.
