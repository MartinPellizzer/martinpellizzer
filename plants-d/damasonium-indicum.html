Damasonium indicum

Damasonium indicum, commonly known as the Indian water clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and delicate, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it can absorb excess water. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role in aquatic ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
