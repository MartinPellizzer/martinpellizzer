Diospyros sunyiensis

Diospyros sunyiensis, commonly known as the Sunyi persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature, often with a sweet, astringent flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
