Dendrocalamus longiligulatus

Dendrocalamus longiligulatus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing member of the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine, and its culms are valued for their strength and flexibility in construction and crafts.

Dendrocalamus longiligulatus plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
