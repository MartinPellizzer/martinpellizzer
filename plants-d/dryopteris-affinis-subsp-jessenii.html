Dryopteris affinis subsp. jessenii

Dryopteris affinis subsp.

jessenii, commonly known as the Jessen's wood fern, is a fern native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bi- to tri-pinnate.

The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
