Doryopteris michelii

Doryopteris michelii, commonly known as the Micheli's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its evergreen fronds that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, supporting the growth of its delicate, lance-shaped leaves.

Doryopteris michelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
