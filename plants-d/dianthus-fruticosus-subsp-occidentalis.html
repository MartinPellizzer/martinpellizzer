Dianthus fruticosus subsp. occidentalis

Dianthus fruticosus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western rock campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and clusters of pink or reddish flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are borne in dense, compact inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
