Dicliptera roxburghiana

Dicliptera roxburghiana, commonly known as Indian senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its seeds are edible and have been used as a food source in some cultures.
