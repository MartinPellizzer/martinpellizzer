Deprea toledoana

Deprea toledoana, commonly known as the Toledo bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and hairy.

Deprea toledoana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
