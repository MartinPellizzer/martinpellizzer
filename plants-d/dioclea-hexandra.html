Dioclea hexandra

Dioclea hexandra, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing several seeds. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor, making it a valuable food source in some regions.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
