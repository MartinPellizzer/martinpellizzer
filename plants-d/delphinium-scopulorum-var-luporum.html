Delphinium scopulorum var. luporum

Delphinium scopulorum var. luporum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, adapted to thrive in cool, alpine environments.

Delphinium scopulorum var. luporum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
