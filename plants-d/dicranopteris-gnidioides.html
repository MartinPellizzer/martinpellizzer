Dicranopteris gnidioides

Dicranopteris gnidioides, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like rhizomes and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, delicate fronds with a feathery appearance, often reaching lengths of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive pattern of pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

Dicranopteris gnidioides is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
