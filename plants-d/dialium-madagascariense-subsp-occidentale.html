Dialium madagascariense subsp. occidentale

Dialium madagascariense subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the western African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to western Africa. It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its timber, which is valued for its durability and fine grain.
