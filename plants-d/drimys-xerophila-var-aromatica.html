Drimys xerophila var. aromatica

Drimys xerophila var. aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic drimys, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a subtle, spicy scent. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a dark blue berry containing several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

The species is adapted to grow in cool, moist environments, often found in cloud forests at moderate elevations.
