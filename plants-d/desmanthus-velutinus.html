Desmanthus velutinus

Desmanthus velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf desmanthus, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
