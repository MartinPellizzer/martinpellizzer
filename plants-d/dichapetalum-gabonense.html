Dichapetalum gabonense

Dichapetalum gabonense, commonly known as the Gabon flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a thick, flaky bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
