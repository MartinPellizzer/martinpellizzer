Dryopteris linyingensis

Dryopteris linyingensis, commonly known as the Linying wood fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
