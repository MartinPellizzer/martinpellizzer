Diplostephium floribundum subsp. cundinamarcense

Diplostephium floribundum subsp.

cundinamarcense, commonly known as the Cundinamarca grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Colombia. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
