Donatophorus

Donatophorus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and agriculturally significant in certain regions.
