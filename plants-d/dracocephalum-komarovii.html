Dracocephalum komarovii

Dracocephalum komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves, typical of the mint family. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often found in rocky or mountainous regions and is valued for its potential medicinal uses.
