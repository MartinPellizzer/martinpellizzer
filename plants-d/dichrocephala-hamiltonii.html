Dichrocephala hamiltonii

Dichrocephala hamiltonii, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
