Diasperus blancoanus

Diasperus blancoanus, commonly known as the Blancoanus spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The species is noted for its latex-producing properties, which can be toxic to animals and humans.
