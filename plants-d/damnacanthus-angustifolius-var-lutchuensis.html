Damnacanthus angustifolius var. lutchuensis

Damnacanthus angustifolius var. lutchuensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devil's needle.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
