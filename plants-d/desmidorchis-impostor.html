Desmidorchis impostor

Desmidorchis impostor, commonly known as the false orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, often showy flowers that mimic the appearance of true orchids, leading to its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and green.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its complex floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
