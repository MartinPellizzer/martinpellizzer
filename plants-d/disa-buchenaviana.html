Disa buchenaviana

Disa buchenaviana, commonly known as the Buchenavia Disa, is a striking orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Disa buchenaviana thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
