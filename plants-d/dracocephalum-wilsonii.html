Dracocephalum wilsonii

Dracocephalum wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Dracocephalum wilsonii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
