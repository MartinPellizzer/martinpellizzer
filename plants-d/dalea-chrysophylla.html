Dalea chrysophylla

Dalea chrysophylla, commonly known as the goldenleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Dalea chrysophylla is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
