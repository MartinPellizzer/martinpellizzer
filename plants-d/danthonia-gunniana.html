Danthonia gunniana

Danthonia gunniana, commonly known as the mountain grass or Gunn's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, white to pale brown flowers. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Danthonia gunniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
