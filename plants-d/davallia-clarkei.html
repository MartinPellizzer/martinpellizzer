Davallia clarkei

Davallia clarkei, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with golden or brown spore cases. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and creeping, allowing it to form dense mats.

Davallia clarkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its elegant appearance.
