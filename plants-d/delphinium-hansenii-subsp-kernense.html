Delphinium hansenii subsp. kernense

Delphinium hansenii subsp.

kernense, commonly known as Kern's larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to alpine and rocky garden settings.
