Dentaria parvifolia

Dentaria parvifolia, commonly known as smallleaf toothplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed margins, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters.

Dentaria parvifolia is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.
