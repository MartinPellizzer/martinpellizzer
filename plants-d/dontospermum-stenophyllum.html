Dontospermum stenophyllum

Dontospermum stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its timber, which is used in the production of incense and other aromatic products.
