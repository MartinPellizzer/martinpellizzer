Dianthera pohliana f. angustifolia

Dianthera pohliana f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dianthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
