Dracocephalum veroniciforme

Dracocephalum veroniciforme, commonly known as Persian shield, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dracocephalum veroniciforme is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
