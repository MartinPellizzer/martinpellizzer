Diospyros dolmen

Diospyros dolmen, commonly known as the Burmese persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically orange to red in color, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in certain regions.
