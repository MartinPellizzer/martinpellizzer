Dombeya hispidipes

Dombeya hispidipes, commonly known as the woolly dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Dombeya hispidipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and resilience.
