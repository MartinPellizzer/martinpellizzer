Dictyogramme latifolia

Dictyogramme latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has long, slender fronds that are often floating or partially submerged. Its leaves are characterized by their broad, lance-shaped blades with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Dictyogramme latifolia plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
