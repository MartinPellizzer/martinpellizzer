Dalbergaria tessmannii

Dalbergaria tessmannii, commonly known as the Natal mimosa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a soft, feathery appearance, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
