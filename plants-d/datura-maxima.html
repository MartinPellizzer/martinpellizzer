Datura maxima

Datura maxima, commonly known as the devil's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its showy, nocturnal blooms. Datura maxima contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.

It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
