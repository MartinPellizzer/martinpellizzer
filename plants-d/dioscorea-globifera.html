Dioscorea globifera

Dioscorea globifera, commonly known as the globe yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same inflorescence. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
