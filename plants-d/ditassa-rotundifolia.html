Ditassa rotundifolia

Ditassa rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf ditassa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its round, succulent leaves that are often thick and fleshy, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate aesthetic to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
