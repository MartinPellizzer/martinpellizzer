Dendrobium nobile

Dendrobium nobile, commonly known as the noble orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Its leaves are usually arranged in two rows along the stem and are often fleshy and succulent.

Dendrobium nobile is popular in horticulture due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
