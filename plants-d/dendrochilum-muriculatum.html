Dendrochilum muriculatum

Dendrochilum muriculatum, commonly known as the spiked orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, branching stems and dense clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Dendrochilum muriculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
