Drosera gigantea var. geniculata

Drosera gigantea var. geniculata, commonly known as the giant sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and long, slender leaves that are covered in sticky, glandular hairs. These hairs secrete a viscous liquid that traps insects, which the plant then digests to obtain nutrients. The plant typically blooms with small, yellowish-white flowers in summer.

It thrives in moist, acidic environments and is often found in heathlands or fynbos vegetation.
