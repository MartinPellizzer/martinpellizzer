Delphinium parishii subsp. purpureum

Delphinium parishii subsp.

purpureum, commonly known as the purple fringed larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of showy, purple to violet-blue flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a distinctive, spurred corolla in its flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
