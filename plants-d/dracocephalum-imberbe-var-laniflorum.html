Dracocephalum imberbe var. laniflorum

Dracocephalum imberbe var. laniflorum, commonly known as woolly flowered dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The variety laniflorum is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.
