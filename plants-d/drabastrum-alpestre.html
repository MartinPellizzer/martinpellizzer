Drabastrum alpestre

Drabastrum alpestre, commonly known as the alpine draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow or white flowers in early spring. It is native to alpine regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, rocky environments.

Drabastrum alpestre is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in mountainous areas.
