Durio wrayi

Durio wrayi, commonly known as the Malay durian, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Durio in the family Malvaceae, which includes other economically important plants like cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its large, spiky fruit, which contains a highly aromatic and creamy pulp. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a waxy surface, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles.

The tree is valued not only for its unique fruit but also for its ornamental value and potential in ecological restoration.
