Dipterocarpus vestitus

Dipterocarpus vestitus, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces yellowish or reddish flowers. The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture. It plays an important role in the ecosystems of tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
