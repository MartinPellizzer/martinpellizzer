Dianthus hachijoensis

Dianthus hachijoensis, commonly known as the Hachijo Island carnation, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
