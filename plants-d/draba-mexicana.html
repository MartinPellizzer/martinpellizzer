Draba mexicana

Draba mexicana, commonly known as Mexican whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Draba mexicana is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
