Dichanthelium linearifolium

Dichanthelium linearifolium, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often folded in a V-shape, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Dichanthelium linearifolium is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.
