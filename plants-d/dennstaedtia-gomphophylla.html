Dennstaedtia gomphophylla

Dennstaedtia gomphophylla, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant has a distinctive rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of Dennstaedtia gomphophylla make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
