Dianthus cruentus subsp. pancicii

Dianthus cruentus subsp.

pancicii, commonly known as the Pancic's bloody carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink petals and its distinctive, showy inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
