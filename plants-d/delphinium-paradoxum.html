Delphinium paradoxum

Delphinium paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium paradoxum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
