Dracocephalum urticifolium f. normalis

Dracocephalum urticifolium f. normalis, commonly known as the common dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
