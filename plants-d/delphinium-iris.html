Delphinium iris

Delphinium iris, commonly known as the iris delphinium, is a striking plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The flowers typically have five to ten petals, with a distinctive beak-like shape at the tip. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
