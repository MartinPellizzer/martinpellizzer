Diospyros andamanica

Diospyros andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits.

The fruits are typically eaten raw or used in local cuisines, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
