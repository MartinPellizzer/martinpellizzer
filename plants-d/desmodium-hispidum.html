Desmodium hispidum

Desmodium hispidum, commonly known as hairy burr legume, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically purple or pink. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Desmodium hispidum is often found in disturbed soils and can be used for erosion control due to its dense growth habit.
