Disperis bodkinii

Disperis bodkinii, commonly known as the Cape golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
