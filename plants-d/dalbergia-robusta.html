Dalbergia robusta

Dalbergia robusta, commonly known as African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has bipinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by flattened pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodlands.

Due to its economic value, it is also subject to overharvesting and habitat loss.
