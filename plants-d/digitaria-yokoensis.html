Digitaria yokoensis

Digitaria yokoensis, commonly known as Yoko grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It is often used as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value.

Digitaria yokoensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
