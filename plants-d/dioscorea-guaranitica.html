Dioscorea guaranitica

Dioscorea guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani yam, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its roots are rich in starch and have been utilized by indigenous communities for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its potential in modern pharmacological research due to its bioactive compounds.
