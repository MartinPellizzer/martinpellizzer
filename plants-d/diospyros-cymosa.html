Diospyros cymosa

Diospyros cymosa, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for carving and furniture.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
