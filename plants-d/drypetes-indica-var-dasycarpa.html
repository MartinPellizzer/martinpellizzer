Drypetes indica var. dasycarpa

Drypetes indica var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited Indian flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, hairy fruits that remain on the plant for an extended period. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking, fiery red flowers.
