Dipterocarpus sarawakensis

Dipterocarpus sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy seeds and buttressed roots. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a rough, fissured bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually elliptical in shape.

This species plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
