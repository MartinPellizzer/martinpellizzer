Dracula radiella

Dracula radiella, commonly known as the "Lady of the Night," is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black petals and sepals, which are often speckled with white or greenish markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. Dracula radiella thrives in humid, shaded environments with high altitude and cool temperatures.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
