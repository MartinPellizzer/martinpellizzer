Derris benthamii

Derris benthamii, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which is used as a natural insecticide. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas and can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in traditional agriculture.
