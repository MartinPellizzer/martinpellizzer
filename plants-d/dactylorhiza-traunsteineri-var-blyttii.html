Dactylorhiza traunsteineri var. blyttii

Dactylorhiza traunsteineri var. blyttii, commonly known as the Traunsteiner's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive yellow lip and greenish-purple petals. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
