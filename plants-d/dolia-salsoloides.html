Dolia salsoloides

Dolia salsoloides, commonly known as the coastal sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent leaves adapted to withstand drought and salt spray. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in summer. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.

Dolia salsoloides is often found in dunes and other coastal habitats where it plays a role in ecological succession.
