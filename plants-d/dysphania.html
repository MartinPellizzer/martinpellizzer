Dysphania

Dysphania, commonly known as lambsquarters, is a common weed found in various habitats across the world.

It is an annual plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils and can be found in agricultural fields, gardens, and disturbed areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread rapidly.

Dysphania is also valued for its nutritional content, as it contains high levels of minerals and vitamins.
