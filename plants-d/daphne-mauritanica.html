Daphne mauritanica

Daphne mauritanica, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple during late winter to early spring. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating.

Daphne mauritanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
