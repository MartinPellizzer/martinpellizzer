Dombeya metameropsis var. typica

Dombeya metameropsis var. typica, commonly known as the pink dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

The flowers are tubular at the base and flare out into five petals, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
