Delphinium sinoamabile

Delphinium sinoamabile, commonly known as the Chinese larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
