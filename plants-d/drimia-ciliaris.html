Drimia ciliaris

Drimia ciliaris, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and erect, leafy stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Drimia ciliaris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
