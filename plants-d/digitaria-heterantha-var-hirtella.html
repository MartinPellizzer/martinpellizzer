Digitaria heterantha var. hirtella

Digitaria heterantha var. hirtella, commonly known as hairy digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

This grass is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
