Danthonia trichotoma

Danthonia trichotoma, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, soft foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as bogs, marshes, and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often hidden within the dense foliage.

Danthonia trichotoma plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
