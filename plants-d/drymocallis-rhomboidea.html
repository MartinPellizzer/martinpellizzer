Drymocallis rhomboidea

Drymocallis rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in panicles.

Drymocallis rhomboidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
