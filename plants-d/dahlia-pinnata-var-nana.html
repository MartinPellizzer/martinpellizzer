Dahlia pinnata var. nana

Dahlia pinnata var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf pinnate dahlia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, single or double flowers with petals that range from red, orange, and yellow to white and pink. The plant has a short, stocky stem with large, palmately divided leaves that are green and fuzzy in texture. It blooms from late summer to early autumn, producing vibrant flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its compact size and long-lasting blooms.
