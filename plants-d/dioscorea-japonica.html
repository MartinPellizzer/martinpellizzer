Dioscorea japonica

Dioscorea japonica, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The botanical features of Dioscorea japonica make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
