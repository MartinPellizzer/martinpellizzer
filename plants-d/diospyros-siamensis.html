Diospyros siamensis

Diospyros siamensis, commonly known as Siam persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, grey-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red when mature.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, slightly astringent flavor.
