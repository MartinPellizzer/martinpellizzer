Dipidax ciliata var. garnotiana

Dipidax ciliata var. garnotiana, commonly known as the Garnot's fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with fine, hair-like structures along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
