Delphinium bicolor f. helleri

Delphinium bicolor f. helleri, commonly known as the Heller's larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of deeply divided, fern-like leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
