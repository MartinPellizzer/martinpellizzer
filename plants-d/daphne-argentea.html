Daphne argentea

Daphne argentea, commonly known as silver daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has smooth, silver-gray bark and glossy, ovate leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
