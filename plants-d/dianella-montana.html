Dianella montana

Dianella montana, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers on tall stems, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dianella montana is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral displays.
