Desmodium oxybracteum

Desmodium oxybracteum, commonly known as the small-leaved ticktrefoil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems and a subject of study in plant biology.
