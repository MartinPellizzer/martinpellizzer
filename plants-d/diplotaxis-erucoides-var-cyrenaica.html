Diplotaxis erucoides var. cyrenaica

Diplotaxis erucoides var. cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, brightly colored flowers and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It features a low-growing habit with linear, often hairy leaves and produces small, yellow or orange flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
