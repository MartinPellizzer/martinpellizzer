Darwinia gracilis

Darwinia gracilis, commonly known as the slender Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or sandy environments.

Darwinia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
