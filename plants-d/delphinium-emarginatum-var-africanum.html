Delphinium emarginatum var. africanum

Delphinium emarginatum var. africanum, commonly known as the African larkspur, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.
