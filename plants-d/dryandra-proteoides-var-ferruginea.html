Dryandra proteoides var. ferruginea

Dryandra proteoides var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty dryandra, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
