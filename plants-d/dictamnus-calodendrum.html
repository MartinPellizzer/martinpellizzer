Dictamnus calodendrum

Dictamnus calodendrum, commonly known as the Japanese spikenard, is a perennial herb native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, often fragrant, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. Its root system is fibrous, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
