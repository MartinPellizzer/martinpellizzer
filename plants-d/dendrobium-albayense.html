Dendrobium albayense

Dendrobium albayense, commonly known as the Albay orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces panicle-like inflorescences with white to pale yellow petals and sepals, often adorned with intricate markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Dendrobium albayense is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in local communities.
