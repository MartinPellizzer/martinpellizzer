Dodonaea stenoptera var. fauriei

Dodonaea stenoptera var. fauriei, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by distinctive, woody pods. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dunes.
