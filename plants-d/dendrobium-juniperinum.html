Dendrobium juniperinum

Dendrobium juniperinum, commonly known as the Juniper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that often bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces elongated, cylindrical pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Dendrobium juniperinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
