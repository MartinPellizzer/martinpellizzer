Daucus guttatus subsp. guttatus

Daucus guttatus subsp.

guttatus, commonly known as the clustered parsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and umbels of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
