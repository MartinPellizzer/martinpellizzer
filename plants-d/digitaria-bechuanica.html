Digitaria bechuanica

Digitaria bechuanica, commonly known as the Bechuanian grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Digitaria bechuanica is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for livestock.
