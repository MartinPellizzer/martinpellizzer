Dianthus oreades

Dianthus oreades, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Dianthus oreades is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
