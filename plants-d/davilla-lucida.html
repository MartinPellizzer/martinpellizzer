Davilla lucida

Davilla lucida, commonly known as the shining davisia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often glabrous and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Davilla lucida is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and showy fruit.
