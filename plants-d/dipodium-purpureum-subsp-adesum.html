Dipodium purpureum subsp. adesum

Dipodium purpureum subsp.

adesum, commonly known as the purple fern, is a rare and striking member of the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with distinctive purple tones, particularly in the leaf sheaths.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, terminal spike that bears small, pale brown flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
