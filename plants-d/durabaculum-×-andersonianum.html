Durabaculum × andersonianum

Durabaculum × andersonianum, commonly known as the Andersonian hybrid bacau, is a rare and intriguing plant species resulting from the crossbreeding of two distinct bacau species.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, which features a combination of the parent species' traits, including broad, waxy leaves with a distinct coloration. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is typically found in specific microhabitats with high humidity and well-drained soil. Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and scientific interest.

Its hybrid nature makes it a subject of study in plant genetics and evolutionary botany.
