Dipterocarpus obtusifolius var. cuspidatus

Dipterocarpus obtusifolius var. cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dipterocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and broad, elliptical leaves with a pointed tip. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or orange, and it produces heavy, woody fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
