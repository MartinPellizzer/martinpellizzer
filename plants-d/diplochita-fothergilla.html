Diplochita fothergilla

Diplochita fothergilla, commonly known as the fragrant fothergilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, multi-branched structure. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright orange-red berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red in the autumn.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
