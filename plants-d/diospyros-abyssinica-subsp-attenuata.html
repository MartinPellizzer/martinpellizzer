Diospyros abyssinica subsp. attenuata

Diospyros abyssinica subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

The subspecies is valued for its fruit and is also used in traditional medicine.
