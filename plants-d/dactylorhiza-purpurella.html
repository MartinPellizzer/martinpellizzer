Dactylorhiza purpurella

Dactylorhiza purpurella, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heaths. The plant features a pseudobulb-like stem that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile soils. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, with three petals and three sepals, forming a distinctive orchid shape.

Dactylorhiza purpurella is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats, making it a common sight in its native range.
