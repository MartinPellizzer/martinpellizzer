Dypsis brevicaulis

Dypsis brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features a short trunk with multiple slender, arching leaves that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dypsis brevicaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
