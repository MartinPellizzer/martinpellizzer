Dendrobium broomfieldii

Dendrobium broomfieldii, commonly known as the Broomfield's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, cane-like growth habit with numerous small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
