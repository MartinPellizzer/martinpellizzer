Diasperus zygophyllodes

Diasperus zygophyllodes, commonly known as the burr bush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in drought conditions. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

The species is well adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
