Dendrobium nobile var. cooksonianum

Dendrobium nobile var. cooksonianum, commonly known as the Cookson's orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, upright pseudobulbs covered in a dense, velvety sheath of leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with high humidity.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
