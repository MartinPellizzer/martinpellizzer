Dendrochilum dewindtianum

Dendrochilum dewindtianum, commonly known as the Dewindt's tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and plicate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland to montane forest areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
