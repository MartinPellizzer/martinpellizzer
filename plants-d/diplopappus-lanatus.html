Diplopappus lanatus

Diplopappus lanatus, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Diplopappus lanatus is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
