Doryopteris quinquelobata

Doryopteris quinquelobata, commonly known as the five-lobed shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The fronds are usually divided into five lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

The plant's spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
