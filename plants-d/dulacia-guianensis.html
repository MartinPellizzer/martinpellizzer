Dulacia guianensis

Dulacia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese dulacia, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in humid forest understories.

Dulacia guianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
