Decalepis nervosa

Decalepis nervosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves with prominent veins and its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
