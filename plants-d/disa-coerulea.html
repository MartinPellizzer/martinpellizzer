Disa coerulea

Disa coerulea, commonly known as the Blue Disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its unique floral structure includes three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being more showy.

Disa coerulea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
