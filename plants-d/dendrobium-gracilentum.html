Dendrobium gracilentum

Dendrobium gracilentum, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Dendrobium gracilentum is prized in horticulture for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
