Draba spitzelii

Draba spitzelii, commonly known as the Spitzel's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowering period occurs in the spring.

Draba spitzelii is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
