Dioscorea piperifolia var. glandulosa

Dioscorea piperifolia var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular yam, is a variant of the yam family native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that have a distinct aroma. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible tubers that are often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include glandular structures on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique scent and ecological interactions.

This variant is valued both for its nutritional content and its role in traditional medicine.
