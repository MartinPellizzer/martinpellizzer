Dactylorhiza elata f. peltieri

Dactylorhiza elata f. peltieri, commonly known as the broad-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces tall, slender stems bearing numerous showy, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or boggy habitats, such as marshes and meadows, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation sites.
