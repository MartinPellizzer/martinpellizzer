Doritis parishii

Doritis parishii, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in winter, typically around December. The plant has a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowers at the top. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Doritis parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
