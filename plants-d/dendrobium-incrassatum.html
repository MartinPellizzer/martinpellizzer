Dendrobium incrassatum

Dendrobium incrassatum, commonly known as the thick-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant features long, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a dark lip, and they bloom in summer.

Dendrobium incrassatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
