Dispeltophorus crassifolius

Dispeltophorus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf spirea, is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, cone-shaped flower clusters.

It typically features dark green, leathery leaves that are broad and ovate in shape, providing a striking visual contrast to its white or pink floral displays. The plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage remains evergreen in mild climates, offering seasonal interest throughout the year.

Dispeltophorus crassifolius is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.
