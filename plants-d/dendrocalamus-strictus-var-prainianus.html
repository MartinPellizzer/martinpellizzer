Dendrocalamus strictus var. prainianus

Dendrocalamus strictus var. prainianus, commonly known as the Prain's bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and arranged in a dense, tufted manner at the top of the stem. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its durable and lightweight culms, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
