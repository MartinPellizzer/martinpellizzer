Dodecatheon hansenii

Dodecatheon hansenii, commonly known as Hansen's glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herb native to the Appalachian Mountains of the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
