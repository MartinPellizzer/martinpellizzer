Digitaria glabra var. ambigua

Digitaria glabra var. ambigua, commonly known as the flatpointer grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, spreading leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
