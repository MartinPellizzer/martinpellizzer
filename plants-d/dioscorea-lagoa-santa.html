Dioscorea lagoa-santa

Dioscorea lagoa-santa, commonly known as the Lagoa-Santa yam, is a climbing vine native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its edible tubers.
