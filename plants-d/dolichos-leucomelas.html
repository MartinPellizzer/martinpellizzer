Dolichos leucomelas

Dolichos leucomelas, commonly known as the white-haired horsegram, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with white veins and hairy stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is well adapted to dry climates and is often used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
