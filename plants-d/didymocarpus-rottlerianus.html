Didymocarpus rottlerianus

Didymocarpus rottlerianus, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
