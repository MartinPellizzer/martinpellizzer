Delphinium austriacum subsp. stiriacum

Delphinium austriacum subsp.

stiriacum, commonly known as the Austrian larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Alps and adjacent regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained, alkaline soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
