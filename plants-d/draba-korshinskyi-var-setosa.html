Draba korshinskyi var. setosa

Draba korshinskyi var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils in alpine or arctic environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, rounded pods containing several seeds.

This variety is notable for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in mountainous regions of Central Asia.
