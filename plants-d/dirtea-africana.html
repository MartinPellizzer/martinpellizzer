Dirtea africana

Dirtea africana, commonly known as African velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by winged seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants.
