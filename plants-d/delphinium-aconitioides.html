Delphinium aconitioides

Delphinium aconitioides, commonly known as the aconite delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately divided, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. Delphinium aconitioides is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
