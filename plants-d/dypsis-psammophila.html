Dypsis psammophila

Dypsis psammophila, commonly known as the sand palm, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant thrives in sandy soils and has adapted to arid environments with its deep root system. Its reproductive structures include a cone-like structure that produces both male and female spores.

Dypsis psammophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
