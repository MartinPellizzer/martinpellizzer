Diplachne peacockii

Diplachne peacockii, commonly known as Peacock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Diplachne peacockii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to colonize open areas.
