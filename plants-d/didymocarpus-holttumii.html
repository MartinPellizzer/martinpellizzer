Didymocarpus holttumii

Didymocarpus holttumii, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and agricultural studies.
