Dianthus capitatus subsp. capitatus

Dianthus capitatus subsp.

capitatus, commonly known as the summer carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, and its flowers are borne on short, sturdy stalks. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.
