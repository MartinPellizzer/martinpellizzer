Diospyros conzattii

Diospyros conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal and fruit production.
