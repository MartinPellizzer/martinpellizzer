Dupatya ovata

Dupatya ovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Dupatya ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
