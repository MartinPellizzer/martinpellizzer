Diospyros hazomainty

Diospyros hazomainty, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters, and its fruit is a small, orange-red drupe with a sweet, edible pulp. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical environments.
