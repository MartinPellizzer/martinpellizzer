Discocactus zehntneri subsp. boomianus

Discocactus zehntneri subsp.

boomianus, commonly known as the Boomer's discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Namibia. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in late summer. It has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species in the wild.
