Dichaea brevicaulis

Dichaea brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale green flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
