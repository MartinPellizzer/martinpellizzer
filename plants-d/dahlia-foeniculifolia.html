Dahlia foeniculifolia

Dahlia foeniculifolia, commonly known as the fennel-leaved dahlia, is a perennial tuberous-rooted plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
