Dicliptera insignis

Dicliptera insignis, commonly known as the African grass or tall blady grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy areas or near water bodies.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it can become invasive in certain environments.
