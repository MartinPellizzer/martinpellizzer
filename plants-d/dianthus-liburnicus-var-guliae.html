Dianthus liburnicus var. guliae

Dianthus liburnicus var. guliae, commonly known as the Croatian carnation, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features showy, pink to red flowers with five petals and a distinctive central boss, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
