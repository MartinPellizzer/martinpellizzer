Desmodium adscendens var. orinocense

Desmodium adscendens var. orinocense, commonly known as the climbing desmodium, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. It has a climbing habit, using its tendrils to support itself on other vegetation.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration contexts.
