Dalechampia brevipes var. pentaphylla

Dalechampia brevipes var. pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed dalechampia, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often greenish-yellow in color.

The plant is notable for its unique morphology and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
