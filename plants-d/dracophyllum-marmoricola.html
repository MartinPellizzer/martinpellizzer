Dracophyllum marmoricola

Dracophyllum marmoricola, commonly known as the marble leaf dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically small, leathery, and often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Dracophyllum marmoricola thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitats.
