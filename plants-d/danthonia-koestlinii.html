Danthonia koestlinii

Danthonia koestlinii, commonly known as Koestlin's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
