Dolophragma globiflorum

Dolophragma globiflorum, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and possesses opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Dolophragma globiflorum is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
