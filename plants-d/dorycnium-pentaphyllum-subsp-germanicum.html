Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp. germanicum

Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp.

germanicum, commonly known as the German sainfoin, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
