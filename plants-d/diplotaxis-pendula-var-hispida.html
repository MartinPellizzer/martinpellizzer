Diplotaxis pendula var. hispida

Diplotaxis pendula var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, adding visual interest to the landscape.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
