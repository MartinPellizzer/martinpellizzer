Deyeuxia curta

Deyeuxia curta, commonly known as dwarf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is often found in nutrient-rich soils and can form dense vegetation in wetland areas.

Deyeuxia curta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
