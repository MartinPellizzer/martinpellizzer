Diotocarpus prunelloides var. latifolia

Diotocarpus prunelloides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf diotocarpus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are often white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
