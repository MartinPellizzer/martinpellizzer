Delphinium poltaratzkii

Delphinium poltaratzkii, commonly known as the Poltaratzki delphinium, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, often with a hairy texture.

Delphinium poltaratzkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
