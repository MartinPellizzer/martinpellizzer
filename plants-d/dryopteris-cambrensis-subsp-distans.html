Dryopteris cambrensis subsp. distans

Dryopteris cambrensis subsp.

distans, commonly known as the Welsh wood fern, is a fern species native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
