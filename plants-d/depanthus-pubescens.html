Depanthus pubescens

Depanthus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of yellow flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges.

Depanthus pubescens blooms in late summer, producing showy yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
