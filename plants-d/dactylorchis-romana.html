Dactylorchis romana

Dactylorchis romana, commonly known as the Roman orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous, and it has linear, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture.

Dactylorchis romana is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
