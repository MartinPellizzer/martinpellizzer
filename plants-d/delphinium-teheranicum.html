Delphinium teheranicum

Delphinium teheranicum, commonly known as the Persian larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, alternate leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers typically bloom in late summer. Delphinium teheranicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
