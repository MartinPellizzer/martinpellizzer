Dendrobium lamproglossum

Dendrobium lamproglossum, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a lip shape, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Dendrobium lamproglossum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
