Dallwatsonia pilosa

Dallwatsonia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Dallwatsonia pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in mild climates.
