Diasperus nanogynus

Diasperus nanogynus, commonly known as the dwarf female grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with compact, dense foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of grasses.

Diasperus nanogynus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
