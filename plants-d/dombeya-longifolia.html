Dombeya longifolia

Dombeya longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, panicle-like inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Dombeya longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
