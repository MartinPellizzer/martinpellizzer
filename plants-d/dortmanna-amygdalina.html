Dortmanna amygdalina

Dortmanna amygdalina, commonly known as the almondleaf saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and bear small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dortmanna amygdalina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
