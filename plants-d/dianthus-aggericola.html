Dianthus aggericola

Dianthus aggericola, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with prominent, showy petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Dianthus aggericola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
