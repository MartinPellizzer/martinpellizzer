Dendromyza crassifolia

Dendromyza crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf forest fern, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically broad and ovate in shape. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Dendromyza crassifolia plays a vital role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various small organisms.
