Dactylorhiza maculata var. transsilvanica

Dactylorhiza maculata var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pinkish-purple with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals and sepals.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
