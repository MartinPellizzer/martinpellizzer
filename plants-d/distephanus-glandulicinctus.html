Distephanus glandulicinctus

Distephanus glandulicinctus, commonly known as the glandular-necked distephanus, is a unique species within the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with glandular structures along the leaf veins, giving it its common name. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
