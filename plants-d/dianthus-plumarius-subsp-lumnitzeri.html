Dianthus plumarius subsp. lumnitzeri

Dianthus plumarius subsp.

lumnitzeri, commonly known as the Armenian campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountains of Armenia and adjacent regions. It features slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, bearing small, clustered flowers with five petals.

The flowers are typically pink or white, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
