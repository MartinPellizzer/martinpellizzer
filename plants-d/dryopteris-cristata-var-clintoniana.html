Dryopteris cristata var. clintoniana

Dryopteris cristata var. clintoniana, commonly known as the Clinton's hart's tongue fern, is a variant of the hart's tongue fern native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its long, strap-like, leathery leaves that grow in a rosette formation. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, narrow, tongue-shaped appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
