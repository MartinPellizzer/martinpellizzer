Dendrobium × gracillimum

Dendrobium × gracillimum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value.

It is a sympodial epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-light conditions. Its flowers are usually showy, with a variety of colors including white, pink, and yellow, often arranged in clusters.

This hybrid is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
