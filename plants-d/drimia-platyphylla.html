Drimia platyphylla

Drimia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically broad and dark green. The plant blooms with a spadix surrounded by a showy, spatulate-shaped bract, which is often brightly colored. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Drimia platyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
