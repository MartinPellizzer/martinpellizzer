Deinbollia onanae

Deinbollia onanae, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves and stems adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers open in a spiral pattern, creating a unique and dramatic display under moonlight.

Deinbollia onanae is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
