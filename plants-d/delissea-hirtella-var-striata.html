Delissea hirtella var. striata

Delissea hirtella var. striata, commonly known as the hairy delissea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
