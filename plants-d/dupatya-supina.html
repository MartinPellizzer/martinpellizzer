Dupatya supina

Dupatya supina, commonly known as the prostrate datura, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial with sprawling, often decumbent stems that grow close to the ground. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale purple, with a trumpet-like shape. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Dupatya supina is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
