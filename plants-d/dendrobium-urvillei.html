Dendrobium urvillei

Dendrobium urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically features long, narrow pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium urvillei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
