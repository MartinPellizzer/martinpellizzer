Diellia × laui

Diellia × laui, commonly known as the hybrid diellia, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Diellia tetragona and Diellia corymbosa.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, making it unique in both appearance and growth characteristics.
