Dyckia meziana subsp. vallegrandensis

Dyckia meziana subsp.

vallegrandensis, commonly known as the Vallegrandensis Dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family. It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its rosette of thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright orange or red flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
