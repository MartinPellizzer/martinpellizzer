Dampiera alata

Dampiera alata, commonly known as the silverleaf damper, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Dampiera alata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native garden landscaping.
