Drimys winteri f. angustifolia

Drimys winteri f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved winter's bark, is a shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces clusters of dark blue to black berries, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Its foliage and aromatic properties make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
