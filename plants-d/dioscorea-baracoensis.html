Dioscorea baracoensis

Dioscorea baracoensis, commonly known as the Borneo yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, tropical environments.

Due to its medicinal properties and potential for cultivation, it holds significance in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
