Digitalis lutea subsp. australis

Digitalis lutea subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian foxglove, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
