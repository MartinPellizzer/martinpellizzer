Disa delphinoides

Disa delphinoides, commonly known as the Blue Disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high humidity and partial shade. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces small, grass-like leaves.

Disa delphinoides is a perennial herb that blooms during the summer months, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
