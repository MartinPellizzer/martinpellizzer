Disa adolphi-fridericii

Disa adolphi-fridericii, commonly known as the Adolph-Friedrich's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a unique spurred structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
