Dactylorhiza longibracteata subsp. sudetica

Dactylorhiza longibracteata subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to central and eastern Europe. It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in wet meadows, bogs, and marshy areas.

The plant features a tall, slender stem with several lance-shaped leaves and produces a spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their long, narrow sepals and petals, with a distinctive, hood-like labellum.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in calcareous habitats.
