Dioscorea odoratissima

Dioscorea odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often borne in clusters. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage.
