Declieuxia humilis

Declieuxia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf papyrus, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Declieuxia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
