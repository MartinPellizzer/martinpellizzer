Doronicum grandiflorum

Doronicum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow with prominent dark markings, resembling a leopard's spots, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Doronicum grandiflorum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
