Deppea hamelioides

Deppea hamelioides, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Deppea hamelioides is often cultivated as a decorative vine due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various climates.
