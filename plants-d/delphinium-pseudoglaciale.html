Delphinium pseudoglaciale

Delphinium pseudoglaciale, commonly known as the glacier larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a waxy coating that helps it withstand cold and arid conditions. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes, and it blooms during the summer months.

Delphinium pseudoglaciale is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other toxic plants, and it contains alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
