Dactylis glomerata subsp. hyrcana

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

hyrcana, commonly known as Persian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and is used in agricultural and ecological restoration practices.
