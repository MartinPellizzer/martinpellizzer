Dioscorea vespertilio

Dioscorea vespertilio, commonly known as the evening glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a supportive structure to climb.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the late afternoon, giving it its common name.
