Dalea tinctoria

Dalea tinctoria, commonly known as the scarlet prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright red or pink pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea tinctoria is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
