Dendrochilum mucronatum

Dendrochilum mucronatum, commonly known as the Acorn Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
