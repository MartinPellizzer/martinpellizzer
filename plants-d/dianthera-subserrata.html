Dianthera subserrata

Dianthera subserrata, commonly known as the serrated dianthera, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Dianthera subserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
