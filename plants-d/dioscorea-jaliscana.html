Dioscorea jaliscana

Dioscorea jaliscana, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a perennial vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The species is notable for its ecological importance and cultural significance in indigenous communities.
