Daucus verticillatus

Daucus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered carrot or wild carrot, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its roots are slender and not as fleshy as those of the domestic carrot.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
