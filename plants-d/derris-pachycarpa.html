Derris pachycarpa

Derris pachycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruit derris, is a climbing shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy fruits and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of rotenone in its seeds.
