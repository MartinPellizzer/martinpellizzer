Dryopteris costata

Dryopteris costata, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that are divided into lance-shaped segments with a distinctive, shield-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The fronds are evergreen, providing year-round interest in gardens and natural habitats.
