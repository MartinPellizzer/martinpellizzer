Dyssodia serratifolia

Dyssodia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Dyssodia serratifolia is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
