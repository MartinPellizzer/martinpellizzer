Didymocarpus sulphureus var. sulphureus

Didymocarpus sulphureus var. sulphureus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
