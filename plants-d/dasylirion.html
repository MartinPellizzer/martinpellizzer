Dasylirion

Dasylirion, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a genus of flowering plants native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of sword-shaped, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Dasylirion species are often used in xeriscaping due to their low water requirements and ornamental value.

They are also valued in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber in some indigenous cultures.
