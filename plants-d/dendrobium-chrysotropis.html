Dendrobium chrysotropis

Dendrobium chrysotropis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features long, cane-like stems covered in clusters of fleshy, strap-shaped leaves. Its flowers are striking, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive golden lip.

It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
