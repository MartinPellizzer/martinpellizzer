Diasperus acutus

Diasperus acutus, commonly known as the sharpleaf sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep roots and minimal water requirements.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
