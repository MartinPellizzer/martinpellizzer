Diasperus stenocladus

Diasperus stenocladus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
