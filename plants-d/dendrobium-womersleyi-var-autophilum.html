Dendrobium womersleyi var. autophilum

Dendrobium womersleyi var. autophilum, commonly known as the Womersley's Dendrobium, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically blooms once a year, with flowers appearing along the stem in a racemose arrangement. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
