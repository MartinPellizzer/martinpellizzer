Daemonorops longipes

Daemonorops longipes, commonly known as the long-iped rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in panicles, followed by dark red, edible berries. It thrives in tropical rainforests and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and use in traditional crafts, it is also cultivated for commercial purposes.
