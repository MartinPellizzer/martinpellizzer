Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. kalopissii

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

kalopissii, commonly known as the Greater Marsh Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet meadows, bogs, and other moist habitats.

The plant has a slender, erect stem with several whorls of linear leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish, often with a distinct yellow or orange lip.

It is a rhizomatous orchid, meaning it spreads via underground stems, and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
