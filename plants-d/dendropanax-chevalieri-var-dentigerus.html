Dendropanax chevalieri var. dentigerus

Dendropanax chevalieri var. dentigerus, commonly known as the toothed arrowhead, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruits are berry-like, turning red when mature, and are an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
