Diaphananthe meliantha

Diaphananthe meliantha, commonly known as the white-flowered daisies, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with slender stems and opposite leaves. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
