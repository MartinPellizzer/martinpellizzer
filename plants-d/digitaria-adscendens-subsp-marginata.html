Digitaria adscendens subsp. marginata

Digitaria adscendens subsp.

marginata, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the warmer regions of the United States. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and distinctive hairy texture on the stems and leaves.

This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues. Digitaria adscendens subsp.

marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
