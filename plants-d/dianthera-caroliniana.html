Dianthera caroliniana

Dianthera caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dianthera caroliniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
