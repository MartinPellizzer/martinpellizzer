Dalechampia scandens var. brasiliensis

Dalechampia scandens var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian roughleaf hemp, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
