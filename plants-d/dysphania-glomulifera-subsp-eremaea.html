Dysphania glomulifera subsp. eremaea

Dysphania glomulifera subsp.

eremaea, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are edible and rich in nutrients.

The plant has a sprawling growth habit with prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Dysphania glomulifera subsp.

eremaea is often used in traditional bushfood practices and is valued for its drought tolerance and nutritional benefits.
