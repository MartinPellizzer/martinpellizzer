Dillwynia cinerascens var. recurva

Dillwynia cinerascens var. recurva, commonly known as the slender dillwynia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, ascending stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers with prominent, showy stamens, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native insect populations.
