Daphne gnidium subsp. gnidium

Daphne gnidium subsp.

gnidium, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
