Drosera paleacea subsp. roseana

Drosera paleacea subsp.

roseana, commonly known as the pink sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky digestive fluid to trap insects.

The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems with small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and trapping mechanism make it a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
