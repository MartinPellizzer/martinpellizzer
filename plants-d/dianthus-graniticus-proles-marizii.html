Dianthus graniticus proles marizii

Dianthus graniticus proles marizii, commonly known as the Marizii Rock Campion, is a flowering plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant typically features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, clustered blooms that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
