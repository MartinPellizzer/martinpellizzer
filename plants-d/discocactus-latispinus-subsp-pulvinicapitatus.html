Discocactus latispinus subsp. pulvinicapitatus

Discocactus latispinus subsp.

pulvinicapitatus, commonly known as the "Pulvinicapitatus Discocactus," is a rare and distinctive cactus native to specific regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in white to pale green spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
