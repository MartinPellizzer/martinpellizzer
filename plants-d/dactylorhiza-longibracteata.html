Dactylorhiza longibracteata

Dactylorhiza longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grasslands, often growing in acidic soils.

Dactylorhiza longibracteata is a perennial species with a dormant pseudobulb and is typically found in temperate climates.
