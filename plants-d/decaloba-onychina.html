Decaloba onychina

Decaloba onychina, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

Decaloba onychina is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
