Draba hispanica var. segurensis

Draba hispanica var. segurensis, commonly known as the Segurensis Rockcress, is a flowering plant native to southern Spain.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. This variety typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, rounded leaves and has a low-growing habit.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential for studying plant adaptation.
