Dalea mixteca

Dalea mixteca, commonly known as the Mixteca Dalea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, compound leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea mixteca is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
