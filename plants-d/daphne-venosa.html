Daphne venosa

Daphne venosa, commonly known as the pink daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

Daphne venosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and compact growth habit.
