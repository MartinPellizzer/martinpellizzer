Dioscorea pohlii var. luschnathiana

Dioscorea pohlii var. luschnathiana, commonly known as Luschnathian yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is a tuberous perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tubers are the main botanical feature, often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
