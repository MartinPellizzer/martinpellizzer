Diospyros undulata var. walkeri

Diospyros undulata var. walkeri, commonly known as the warty persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, warty bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to reddish berries. Its fruits are typically edible when fully ripe, though they may have astringent properties when unripe.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
