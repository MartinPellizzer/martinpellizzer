Dalhousiea africana var. sapinii

Dalhousiea africana var. sapinii, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
