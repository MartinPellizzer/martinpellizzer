Dombeya ramiovensis

Dombeya ramiovensis, commonly known as the mountain dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dombeya ramiovensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
