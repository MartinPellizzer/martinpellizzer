Diasperus speciosus

Diasperus speciosus, commonly known as the showy rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
