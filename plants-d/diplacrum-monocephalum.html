Diplacrum monocephalum

Diplacrum monocephalum, commonly known as the single-headed diplacrum, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a small, woody shrub that typically grows in sandy soils within coastal regions. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It has small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

Diplacrum monocephalum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal heathlands.
