Dracocephalum peregrinum

Dracocephalum peregrinum, commonly known as wandering dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dracocephalum peregrinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
