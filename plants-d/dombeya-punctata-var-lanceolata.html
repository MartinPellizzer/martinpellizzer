Dombeya punctata var. lanceolata

Dombeya punctata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf dombeya, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The leaves are lanceolate, hence the variety name, and are typically hairy beneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
