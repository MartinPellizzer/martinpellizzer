Daphne × neapolitana

Daphne × neapolitana, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that appear in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has a compact, bushy form. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value throughout the year.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
