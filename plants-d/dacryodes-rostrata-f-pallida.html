Dacryodes rostrata f. pallida

Dacryodes rostrata f. pallida, commonly known as the pale-leaf tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are often pale green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is typically found in lowland rainforests and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
