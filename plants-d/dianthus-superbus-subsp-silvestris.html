Dianthus superbus subsp. silvestris

Dianthus superbus subsp.

silvestris, commonly known as the wild campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
