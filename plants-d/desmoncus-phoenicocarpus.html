Desmoncus phoenicocarpus

Desmoncus phoenicocarpus, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, resembling small umbrellas. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
