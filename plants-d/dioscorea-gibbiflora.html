Dioscorea gibbiflora

Dioscorea gibbiflora, commonly known as the warty yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive warty tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture. Dioscorea gibbiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth.

Its tubers are not only valued for their medicinal properties but also for their potential nutritional benefits.
