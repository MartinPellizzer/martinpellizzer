Desmodium asperum

Desmodium asperum, commonly known as hairy desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Desmodium asperum is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
