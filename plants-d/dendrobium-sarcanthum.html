Dendrobium sarcanthum

Dendrobium sarcanthum, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Dendrobium, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients. The plant produces long, pendant-like inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Dendrobium sarcanthum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral displays.
