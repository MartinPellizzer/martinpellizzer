Deyeuxia langsdorffii var. punctulata

Deyeuxia langsdorffii var. punctulata, commonly known as the speckled false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive speckled appearance on the underside.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
