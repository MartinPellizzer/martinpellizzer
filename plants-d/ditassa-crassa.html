Ditassa crassa

Ditassa crassa, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Ditassa crassa is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
