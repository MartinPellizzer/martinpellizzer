Dysoxylum comptonii

Dysoxylum comptonii, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, five-angled fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for both ecological and economic reasons.
