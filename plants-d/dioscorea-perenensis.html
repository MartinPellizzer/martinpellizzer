Dioscorea perenensis

Dioscorea perenensis, commonly known as the African yam vine, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine practices.
