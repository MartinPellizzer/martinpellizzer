Davilla glabrata

Davilla glabrata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
