Dioscorea montana var. sagittata

Dioscorea montana var. sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by inedible, spiny fruits. Its tubers, while not widely cultivated, have been used traditionally in some regions for their medicinal properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
