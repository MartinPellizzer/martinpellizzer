Damnacanthus officinarum

Damnacanthus officinarum, commonly known as the redroot false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
