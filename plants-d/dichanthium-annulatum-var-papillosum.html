Dichanthium annulatum var. papillosum

Dichanthium annulatum var. papillosum, commonly known as the woolly grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, papillate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock.
