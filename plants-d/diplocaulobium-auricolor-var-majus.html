Diplocaulobium auricolor var. majus

Diplocaulobium auricolor var. majus, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange inflorescences, which are adorned with intricate, lip-like petals. Its foliage consists of broad, glossy leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
