Dichanthelium erectifolium

Dichanthelium erectifolium, commonly known as the tallleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Dichanthelium erectifolium plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
