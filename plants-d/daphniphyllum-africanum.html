Daphniphyllum africanum

Daphniphyllum africanum, commonly known as the African holly, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of glossy, elliptical leaves arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in shady, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive appearance and resilience.
