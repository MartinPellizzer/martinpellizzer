Dermasea pilosa

Dermasea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy dermatophyte, is a type of fungi that belongs to the family Trichophyton.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy texture on the surface of its fruiting bodies. This fungus typically thrives in warm, moist environments and is often found on decaying wood or plant matter. Dermasea pilosa is known for its ability to cause skin infections in humans and animals.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in mycology and dermatological studies.
