Delphinium bonvalotii var. hispidum

Delphinium bonvalotii var. hispidum, commonly known as the hispid delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a striking addition to alpine gardens.
