Diospyros tetramera

Diospyros tetramera, commonly known as the four-angled persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often four-angled in cross-section. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for crafting and traditional uses.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
