Diplandra lopezioides

Diplandra lopezioides, commonly known as the Lopezioides Diplandra, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. Diplandra lopezioides is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest for botanists studying xerophytic plants.
