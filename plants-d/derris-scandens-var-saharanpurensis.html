Derris scandens var. saharanpurensis

Derris scandens var. saharanpurensis, commonly known as the Saharanpurensis devil's ivy, is a climbing shrub native to parts of South Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its stems are woody and often twining, allowing it to climb other vegetation.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
