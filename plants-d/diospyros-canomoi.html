Diospyros canomoi

Diospyros canomoi, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe.

Diospyros canomoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
