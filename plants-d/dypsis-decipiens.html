Dypsis decipiens

Dypsis decipiens, commonly known as the false bamboo palm, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like, compound leaves that give it a palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Dypsis decipiens thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant foliage and slow growth rate.
