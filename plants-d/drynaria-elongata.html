Drynaria elongata

Drynaria elongata, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a species of evergreen fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its long, creeping rhizomes that allow it to climb and spread over surfaces. The fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a graceful, arching appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
