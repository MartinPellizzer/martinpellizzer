Deinbollia borbonica f. trichogyra

Deinbollia borbonica f. trichogyra, commonly known as the hairy-leaved genus plant, is a variant of the Deinbollia genus native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching moderate heights, and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in inflorescences, and are typically greenish-white in color.

This form is notable for its unique leaf morphology, which sets it apart from other members of the genus.
