Didymocarpus glandulosus var. glandulosus

Didymocarpus glandulosus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, and the plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
