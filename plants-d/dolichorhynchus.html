Dolichorhynchus

Dolichorhynchus, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

These plants are characterized by their unique, elongated floral structures that resemble spiders, hence their common name. They are native to various regions in Africa, particularly in arid and semi-arid environments. Dolichorhynchus species typically have small, intricate flowers with specialized pollination mechanisms.

The genus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking, unusual appearance.
