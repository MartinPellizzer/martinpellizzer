Delphinium luteum

Delphinium luteum, commonly known as the yellow larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium luteum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other toxic plants, and it contains various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
