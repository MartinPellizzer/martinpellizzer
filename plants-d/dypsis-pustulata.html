Dypsis pustulata

Dypsis pustulata, commonly known as the spotted cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like appearance with a stout trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a rough, textured surface.

Dypsis pustulata is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
