Dicliptera javanica

Dicliptera javanica, commonly known as Java grass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy areas, showcasing its adaptation to waterlogged soils. The plant features erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in dense clusters, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Dicliptera javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
