Dryopolystichum phaeostigma

Dryopolystichum phaeostigma, commonly known as the black-stained wood fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polystichaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its fronds are usually dark green with a distinctive blackish or dark brown staining on the underside.

The spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.
