Discaria prostrata var. nana

Discaria prostrata var. nana, commonly known as dwarf prostrate discaria, is a low-growing shrub native to Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its compact form and drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ground cover.
