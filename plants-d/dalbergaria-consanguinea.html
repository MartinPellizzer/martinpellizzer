Dalbergaria consanguinea

Dalbergaria consanguinea, commonly known as the Natal bushwillow, is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as thick bark and deep roots.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
