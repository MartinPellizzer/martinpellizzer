Dissotis schliebenii

Dissotis schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
