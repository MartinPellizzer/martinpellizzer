Desmodium denudatum

Desmodium denudatum, commonly known as slender desmodium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow to green flowers. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
