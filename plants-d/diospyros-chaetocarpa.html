Diospyros chaetocarpa

Diospyros chaetocarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a thick, astringent skin.

The fruits are typically harvested when fully mature, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
