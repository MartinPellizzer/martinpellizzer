Disporum ovale var. tamnanum

Disporum ovale var. tamnanum, commonly known as the Tamnanum Disporum, is a variant of the Disporum ovale species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable addition to woodland gardens and conservation efforts.
