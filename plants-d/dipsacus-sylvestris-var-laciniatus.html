Dipsacus sylvestris var. laciniatus

Dipsacus sylvestris var. laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaved teasel, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces purple to reddish flower heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
