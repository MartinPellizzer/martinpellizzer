Dorvalia eucharis

Dorvalia eucharis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a golden spike. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant, showy display.

Dorvalia eucharis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
