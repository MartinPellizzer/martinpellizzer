Didymocarpus purpureopictus

Didymocarpus purpureopictus, commonly known as the purple-painted flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are pinnate, with elongated leaflets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its most striking feature is its large, showy inflorescences, which produce vibrant red or orange flowers during the dry season.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in parks and along roadsides for its visual appeal.
