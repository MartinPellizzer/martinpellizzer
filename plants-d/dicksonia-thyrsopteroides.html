Dicksonia thyrsopteroides

Dicksonia thyrsopteroides, commonly known as the mountain tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant has a distinctive crown of fertile fronds that produce spores, which are essential for its reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
