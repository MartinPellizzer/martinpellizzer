Delphinium peregrinum var. macrosiphon

Delphinium peregrinum var. macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant blue larkspur, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy blue or purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
