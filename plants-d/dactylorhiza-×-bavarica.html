Dactylorhiza × bavarica

Dactylorhiza × bavarica, commonly known as the hybrid lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers arranged in a loose raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a complex root system with a central rhizome and numerous fibrous roots.

Dactylorhiza × bavarica is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
