Delphinium addendum

Delphinium addendum, commonly known as the "Addendum larkspur," is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium addendum is often found in meadows and open woods, thriving in well-drained soil.

It is important to note that all parts of this plant are toxic, making it a potentially dangerous species for livestock and pets.
