Dendrocalamus exilis

Dendrocalamus exilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, fast-growing grass that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has hollow, jointed stems that are characterized by their thin walls and smooth surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus exilis is valued for its use in construction, papermaking, and as a source of bamboo shoots.
