Dasiphora speciosa

Dasiphora speciosa, commonly known as the showy lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble a lady's mantle. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Dasiphora speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
