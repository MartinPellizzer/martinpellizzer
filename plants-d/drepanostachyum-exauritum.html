Drepanostachyum exauritum

Drepanostachyum exauritum, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, arching culms that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among horticulturists.
