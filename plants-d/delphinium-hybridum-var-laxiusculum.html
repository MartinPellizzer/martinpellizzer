Delphinium hybridum var. laxiusculum

Delphinium hybridum var. laxiusculum, commonly known as the lax leaf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is typically hardy in USDA zones 3 to 7.
