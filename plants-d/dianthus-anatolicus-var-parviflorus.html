Dianthus anatolicus var. parviflorus

Dianthus anatolicus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the Anatolian carnation, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically pink or red. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
