Desmodium polygaloides var. dutrae

Desmodium polygaloides var. dutrae, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that are often covered in stiff, hooked bristles. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for ecological restoration.
