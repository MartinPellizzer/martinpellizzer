Dilatris tinctorum

Dilatris tinctorum, commonly known as the red-flowered poinsettia, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by these colorful bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for holiday decorations, particularly around Christmas.
