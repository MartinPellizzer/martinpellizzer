Didymoglossum kapplerianum

Didymoglossum kapplerianum, commonly known as the Kappler's bladder grass, is a small, perennial herb belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
