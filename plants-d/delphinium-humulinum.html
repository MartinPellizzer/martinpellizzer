Delphinium humulinum

Delphinium humulinum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium humulinum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and toxic properties.
