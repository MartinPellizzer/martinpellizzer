Diospyros coronaria

Diospyros coronaria, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and southern subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by fruits that mature to a reddish-orange color.

The wood of Diospyros coronaria is valued for its density and durability, often used in traditional crafts and furniture.
