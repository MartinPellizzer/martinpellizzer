Diospyros heterotricha

Diospyros heterotricha, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
