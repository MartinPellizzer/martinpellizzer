Didymocarpus filicifolia

Didymocarpus filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaf tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble ferns, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
