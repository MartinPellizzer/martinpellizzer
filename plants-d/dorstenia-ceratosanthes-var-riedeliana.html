Dorstenia ceratosanthes var. riedeliana

Dorstenia ceratosanthes var. riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's false arum, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its foliage features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish tint.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses and botanical gardens.
