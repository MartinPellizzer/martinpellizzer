Dysoxylum alternatum

Dysoxylum alternatum, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, bright red fruits with a segmented, succulent aril. Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The plant is also noted for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of bioactive compounds in its fruit and leaves.
