Duranta inermis

Duranta inermis, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright blue berries that appear in the fall, which are a key feature of the plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance throughout the growing season. It produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
