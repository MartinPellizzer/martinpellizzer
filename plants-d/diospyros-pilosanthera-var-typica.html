Diospyros pilosanthera var. typica

Diospyros pilosanthera var. typica, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and broad leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is often used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
