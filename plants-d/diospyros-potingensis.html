Diospyros potingensis

Diospyros potingensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature, and it is edible when fully ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
