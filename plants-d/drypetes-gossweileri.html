Drypetes gossweileri

Drypetes gossweileri, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, opposite leaves. Due to its hard, heavy wood, it has been historically valued for its use in furniture and construction.
