Drimys lamii

Drimys lamii, commonly known as the Chilean wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating. Drimys lamii produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional South American medicine.
