Diplazium sibiricum

Diplazium sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian chain fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive, arching frond structure, and its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds. Diplazium sibiricum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
