Dyckia catharinensis

Dyckia catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple plant, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette of sword-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces an inflorescence that resembles a pineapple, consisting of numerous small, clustered flowers. Dyckia catharinensis is adapted to grow in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
