Dryopteris rivularioides var. arechavaletae

Dryopteris rivularioides var. arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta water fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to the humid, shaded environments of tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often exhibit a bluish-green hue, adapted to low-light conditions. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils near streams or in dense undergrowth, where it forms dense colonies. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

This variety is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
