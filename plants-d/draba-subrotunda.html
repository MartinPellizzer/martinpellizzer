Draba subrotunda

Draba subrotunda, commonly known as the alpine penny-cress, is a small flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers are small, white or yellowish, and appear in clusters, while its leaves are basal and rosette-like.

Draba subrotunda is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
