Dolichos biflorus

Dolichos biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered dolichos, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Dolichos biflorus is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary traditions.
