Daphne coridifolia

Daphne coridifolia, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Daphne coridifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and early blooming habit.
