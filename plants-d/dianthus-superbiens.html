Dianthus superbiens

Dianthus superbiens, commonly known as the tall carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, brightly colored flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces dense clusters of petals that resemble small carnations, giving it its common name. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
