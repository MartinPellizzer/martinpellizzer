Declieuxia chiococcoides var. guyanensis

Declieuxia chiococcoides var. guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana chionochloa, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often green or reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
