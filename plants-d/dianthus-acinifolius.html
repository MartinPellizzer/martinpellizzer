Dianthus acinifolius

Dianthus acinifolius, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces small, spiny fruits called achenes, which are typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Dianthus acinifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
