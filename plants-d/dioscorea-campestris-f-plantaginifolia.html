Dioscorea campestris f. plantaginifolia

Dioscorea campestris f. plantaginifolia, commonly known as the creeping Dioscorea, is a variant of the common yam, characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its stems are often prostrate or trailing. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by tuberous roots that are typically used for propagation. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

This form is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
