Dianthus zeyheri subsp. zeyheri

Dianthus zeyheri subsp.

zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or red, five-petaled flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
