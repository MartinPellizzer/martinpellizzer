Diospyros decandra

Diospyros decandra, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are highly prized for their sweet flavor. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and is used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

The wood of Diospyros decandra is also valued for its durability and is used in furniture and tool-making.
