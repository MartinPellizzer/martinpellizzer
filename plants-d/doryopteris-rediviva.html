Doryopteris rediviva

Doryopteris rediviva, commonly known as the rediviva fern, is a species of polypod fern native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arching or drooping, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it establish in rich, organic soil.

Doryopteris rediviva is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
