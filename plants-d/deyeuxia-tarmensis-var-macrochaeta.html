Deyeuxia tarmensis var. macrochaeta

Deyeuxia tarmensis var. macrochaeta, commonly known as the tufted false brome, is a grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
