Dupinia japonica

Dupinia japonica, commonly known as Japanese dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
