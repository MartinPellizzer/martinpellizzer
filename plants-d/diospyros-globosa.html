Diospyros globosa

Diospyros globosa, commonly known as the globe persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
