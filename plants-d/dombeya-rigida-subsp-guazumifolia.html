Dombeya rigida subsp. guazumifolia

Dombeya rigida subsp.

guazumifolia, commonly known as the Guazumifolia Dombeya, is a shrub native to Central America. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches.

The plant has smooth, somewhat hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
