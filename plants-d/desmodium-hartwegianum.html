Desmodium hartwegianum

Desmodium hartwegianum, commonly known as the Hartwig's ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The species is of interest in botanical studies for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
