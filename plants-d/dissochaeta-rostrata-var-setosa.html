Dissochaeta rostrata var. setosa

Dissochaeta rostrata var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands or along roadsides.
