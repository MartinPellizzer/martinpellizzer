Drymocallis halacsyana

Drymocallis halacsyana, commonly known as the Hungarian goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist soils, often found in meadows, grasslands, and wetlands. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, forming panicles that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting biodiversity.
