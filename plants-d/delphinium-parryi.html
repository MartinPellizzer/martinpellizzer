Delphinium parryi

Delphinium parryi, commonly known as Parry's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium parryi is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
