Diervilla humilis

Diervilla humilis, commonly known as creeping fireweed, is a low-growing shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are small, oval, and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

Diervilla humilis is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in woodland and open areas.
