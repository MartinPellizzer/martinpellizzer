Dianthus versicolor subsp. ninelli

Dianthus versicolor subsp.

ninelli, commonly known as the Ninebark Dianthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bicolor flowers that typically display a mix of red and white or pink hues.

The plant produces showy, clustered blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
