Delphinium elbursense var. gymnobotrys

Delphinium elbursense var. gymnobotrys, commonly known as the Elburz delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in a dense layer of soft, glandular hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
