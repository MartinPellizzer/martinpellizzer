Diplazium proliferum

Diplazium proliferum, commonly known as the spreading wood fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, creeping growth habit and its ability to form thick mats of foliage. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small, lance-shaped fronds. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fertile fronds.

Diplazium proliferum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
