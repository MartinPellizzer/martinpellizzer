Didymocarpus gambleanus

Didymocarpus gambleanus, commonly known as the Gambie tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. The seeds are encased in a hard, woody pulp, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
