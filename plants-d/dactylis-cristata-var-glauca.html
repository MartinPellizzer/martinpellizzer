Dactylis cristata var. glauca

Dactylis cristata var. glauca, commonly known as the blue star grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue flowers.

Dactylis cristata var. glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
