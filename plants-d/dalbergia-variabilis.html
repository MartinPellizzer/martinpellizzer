Dalbergia variabilis

Dalbergia variabilis, commonly known as the variable ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a rare and valuable species.
