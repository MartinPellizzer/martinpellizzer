Darwinia taxifolia var. typica

Darwinia taxifolia var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is often found in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
