Dacryodes macrocarpa

Dacryodes macrocarpa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, elliptical fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
