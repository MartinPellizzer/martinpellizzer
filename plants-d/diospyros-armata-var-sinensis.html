Diospyros armata var. sinensis

Diospyros armata var. sinensis, commonly known as Chinese persimmon, is a species native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, rough bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fruits that are typically orange to red in color when ripe. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is rich in nutrients, making it both edible and commercially valuable.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
