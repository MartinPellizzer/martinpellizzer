Diospyros pseudomespilus

Diospyros pseudomespilus, commonly known as the Persian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other notable species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, typically blooming in spring.

The fruit is a small, orange to red drupe that matures in autumn, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
