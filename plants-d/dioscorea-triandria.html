Dioscorea triandria

Dioscorea triandria, commonly known as the Indian yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers with three stamens. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
