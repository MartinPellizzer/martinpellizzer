Dianthus pungens subsp. insignitus

Dianthus pungens subsp.

insignitus, commonly known as the purple campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
