Dryopteris flexuosa

Dryopteris flexuosa, commonly known as the barren woods fern, is a species of fern native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often exhibit a wavy or undulating pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Dryopteris flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
