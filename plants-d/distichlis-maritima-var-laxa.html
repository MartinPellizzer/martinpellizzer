Distichlis maritima var. laxa

Distichlis maritima var. laxa, commonly known as the slender saltgrass, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and dunes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in two rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various coastal wildlife.
