Delphinium camptocarpum var. songoricum

Delphinium camptocarpum var. songoricum, commonly known as the Songari delphinium, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
