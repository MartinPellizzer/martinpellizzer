Deguelia longifolia

Deguelia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved deguelia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern, and its clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.

Deguelia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
