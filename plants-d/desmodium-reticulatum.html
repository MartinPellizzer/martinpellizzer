Desmodium reticulatum

Desmodium reticulatum, commonly known as hairy gladecress, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It is a perennial herb that thrives in a variety of soil types, particularly in moist to wet environments.

Desmodium reticulatum is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts.
