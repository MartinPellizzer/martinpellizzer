Diospyros pellucida

Diospyros pellucida, commonly known as the sour fig or Indian date, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round fruits that are initially green and turn red or yellow when mature.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
