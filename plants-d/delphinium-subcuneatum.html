Delphinium subcuneatum

Delphinium subcuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium subcuneatum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
