Dendrobium tetrabrachium

Dendrobium tetrabrachium, commonly known as the four-branched orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, arranged along the stem, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium tetrabrachium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
