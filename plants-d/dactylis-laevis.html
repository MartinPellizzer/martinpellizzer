Dactylis laevis

Dactylis laevis, commonly known as silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and silky leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by small, three-angled seed capsules.

Dactylis laevis is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
