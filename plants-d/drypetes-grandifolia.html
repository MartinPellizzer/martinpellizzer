Drypetes grandifolia

Drypetes grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
