Daphniphyllum macropodum var. lhuysii

Daphniphyllum macropodum var. lhuysii, commonly known as the Lhuysii daphniphyllum, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and unique floral display.
