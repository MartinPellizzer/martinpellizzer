Draba stenocarpa var. media

Draba stenocarpa var. media, commonly known as the narrow-fruit rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to harsh, cold climates.
