Derris urucu

Derris urucu, commonly known as Brazilian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a natural compound used in traditional fishing and as a pesticide. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal and agricultural applications.

Derris urucu thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
