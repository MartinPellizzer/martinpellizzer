Deguelia vestita

Deguelia vestita, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface and produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Deguelia vestita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
