Disperis erucifera

Disperis erucifera, commonly known as the African orchid or the false orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it grows in grassland and rocky areas. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a few narrow leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Disperis erucifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native environment.
