Discocactus hartmannii var. bonitoensis

Discocactus hartmannii var. bonitoensis, commonly known as the Bonitoensis discocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to a limited geographic region in South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water, typical of desert-adapted plants. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Due to its restricted habitat and slow growth, it is considered a conservation concern and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
