Dendrochilum hewittii

Dendrochilum hewittii, commonly known as the Hewitt's tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that supports multiple stems. Its flowers are usually small, clustered in inflorescences, and exhibit a range of colors including white, pink, and purple.

Dendrochilum hewittii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
