Dichanthelium sabulorum var. patulum

Dichanthelium sabulorum var. patulum, commonly known as the broadleaf sand grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
