Dalea capitata var. lupinocalyx

Dalea capitata var. lupinocalyx, commonly known as the lupine dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangements and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, which benefits soil fertility in its native habitats.
