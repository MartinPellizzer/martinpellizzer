Dodonaea eriocarpa var. degeneri

Dodonaea eriocarpa var. degeneri, commonly known as the hairy dodonaea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that appear in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
