Delphinium winklerianum

Delphinium winklerianum, commonly known as the Winkler's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium winklerianum is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
