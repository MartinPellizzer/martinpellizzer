Didymocarpus albus var. major

Didymocarpus albus var. major, commonly known as the white-flowered kola nut, is a variant of the Didymocarpus albus species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing pale, fleshy seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
