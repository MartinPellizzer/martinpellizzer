Digitaria dactylon

Digitaria dactylon, commonly known as Bermuda grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa but widely naturalized in many parts of the world.

It is characterized by its coarse, tufted blades and dense growth habit, often forming a thick sod. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions. It is highly adaptable, capable of surviving in both sunny and partially shaded areas.

Bermuda grass is also known for its rapid growth and ability to recover quickly from damage.
