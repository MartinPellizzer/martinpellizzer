Dianthus gallicus var. lusitanus

Dianthus gallicus var. lusitanus, commonly known as the Portuguese carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and displays clusters of pink to red, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
