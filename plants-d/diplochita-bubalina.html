Diplochita bubalina

Diplochita bubalina, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The tree is also valued for its role in soil conservation and as a source of timber.
