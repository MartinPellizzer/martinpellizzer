Delphinium tricorne var. andersonii

Delphinium tricorne var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, and its flowers are characterized by their distinctive, narrow corolla. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
