Damasonium tranquebariense

Damasonium tranquebariense, commonly known as the Transquebar water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in still or gently flowing water, making it a common sight in wetland environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic life and maintaining water quality.
