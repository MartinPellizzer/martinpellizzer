Dioscorea stenocolpus

Dioscorea stenocolpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal use. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a complex network of tendrils for support.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
