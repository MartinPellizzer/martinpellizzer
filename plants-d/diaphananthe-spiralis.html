Diaphananthe spiralis

Diaphananthe spiralis, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of fronds.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a creeping rhizome with fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl as they grow. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Diaphananthe spiralis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in shaded garden settings.
