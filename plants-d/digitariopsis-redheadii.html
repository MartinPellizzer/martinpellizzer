Digitariopsis redheadii

Digitariopsis redheadii, commonly known as the Redhead's digitaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is a small annual herb characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems, with each spikelet containing several tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and lesser-known species within the grass family.
