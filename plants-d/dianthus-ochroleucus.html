Dianthus ochroleucus

Dianthus ochroleucus, commonly known as the pale yellow campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known flowers like carnations and poppies. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in meadows and along roadsides.

Dianthus ochroleucus is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
