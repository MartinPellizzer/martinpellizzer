Doronicum carpetanum subsp. diazii

Doronicum carpetanum subsp.

diazii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive red center, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
