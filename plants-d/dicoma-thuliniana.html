Dicoma thuliniana

Dicoma thuliniana, commonly known as the Cape mountain snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-gray, providing a striking contrast to the delicate flowers.

Dicoma thuliniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
