Dimeria fuscescens

Dimeria fuscescens, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep taproots and reduced leaf surface area.

Dimeria fuscescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
