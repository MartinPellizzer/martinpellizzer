Disperis cordata

Disperis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf disperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
