Dendrobium fairchildiae

Dendrobium fairchildiae, commonly known as the Fairchild's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming during the summer months. It requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.

Dendrobium fairchildiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of care in the right growing conditions.
