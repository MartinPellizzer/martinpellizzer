Diospyros koordersii

Diospyros koordersii, commonly known as the Koorders' persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically edible, though it may have a tart flavor and is often used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
