Diphyes rhopaloblepharos

Diphyes rhopaloblepharos, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking plant characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant features a climbing habit with tendrils that help it ascend trees or structures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are tubular with five petals that reflex outward.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds when mature.
