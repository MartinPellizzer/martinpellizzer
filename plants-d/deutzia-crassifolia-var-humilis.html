Deutzia crassifolia var. humilis

Deutzia crassifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, elliptical leaves. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
