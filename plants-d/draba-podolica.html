Draba podolica

Draba podolica, commonly known as the Podolia whitlow, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This species typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are simple and often covered in fine hairs.

Draba podolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
