Dombeya pilosa var. amplifolia

Dombeya pilosa var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
