Dendrobium hydrophilum

Dendrobium hydrophilum, commonly known as the water-loving orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often with clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and good drainage for optimal growth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding substrate.

Dendrobium hydrophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
