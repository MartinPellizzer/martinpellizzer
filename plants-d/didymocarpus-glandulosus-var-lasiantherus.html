Didymocarpus glandulosus var. lasiantherus

Didymocarpus glandulosus var. lasiantherus, commonly known as the hairy soursop, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant bears round, green, spiny fruits that mature to a yellow or orange color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
