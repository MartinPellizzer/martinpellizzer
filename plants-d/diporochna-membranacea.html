Diporochna membranacea

Diporochna membranacea, commonly known as the parchment leaf fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in the understory of shaded wetlands. The plant's leaves are thin and membranous, giving it a distinct appearance that resembles parchment. It reproduces through spores, which are released into the water and germinate into new plants.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of water quality.
