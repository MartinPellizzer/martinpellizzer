Diphyes vaccinioides

Diphyes vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry shrub, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The plant thrives in acidic, wet soils and is often found in swamps and bogs.
