Disa coccinea

Disa coccinea, commonly known as the red disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a distinctive yellow lip, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high light exposure. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing clusters of showy blooms.

Disa coccinea is valued not only for its beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
