Dendrophthora crassuloides

Dendrophthora crassuloides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the warmer months. It typically grows on rocky outcrops and is well adapted to full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
