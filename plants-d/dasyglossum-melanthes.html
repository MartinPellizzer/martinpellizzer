Dasyglossum melanthes

Dasyglossum melanthes, commonly known as the black-horned grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive dark green coloration.

Dasyglossum melanthes is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
