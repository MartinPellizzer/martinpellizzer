Draba macrophylla

Draba macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats and has a low, mat-like growth form. Draba macrophylla features basal leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface, and it produces a cluster of flowers at the end of each stem.

It is often found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
