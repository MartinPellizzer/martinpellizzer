Dendrobium pentapterum

Dendrobium pentapterum, commonly known as the orchid of the moon, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has a distinctive appearance with long, thin stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Dendrobium pentapterum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
