Dissotis luxenii

Dissotis luxenii, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, recurved lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understorey of rainforests. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, dark green leaves.

Dissotis luxenii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
