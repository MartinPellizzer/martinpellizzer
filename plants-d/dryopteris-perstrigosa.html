Dryopteris perstrigosa

Dryopteris perstrigosa, commonly known as the stiff-veined wood fern, is a species of fern native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and rocky areas. Its leaves have distinct, parallel veins that give it its common name.

Dryopteris perstrigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
