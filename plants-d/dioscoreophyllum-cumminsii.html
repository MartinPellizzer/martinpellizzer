Dioscoreophyllum cumminsii

Dioscoreophyllum cumminsii, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tubers are used as a starchy food source and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
