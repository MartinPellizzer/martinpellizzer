Diospyros longipilosa

Diospyros longipilosa, commonly known as the long-hair ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble small grapes. Its wood is highly valued for its dense, dark color and durability.

Diospyros longipilosa is often found in lowland forests and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
