Dichopsis oleifera

Dichopsis oleifera, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Dichopsis oleifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
