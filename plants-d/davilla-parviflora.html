Davilla parviflora

Davilla parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower davisia, is a deciduous shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Sericeae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Davilla parviflora is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate foliage and seasonal flowering.
