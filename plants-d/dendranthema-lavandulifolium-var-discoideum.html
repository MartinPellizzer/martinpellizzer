Dendranthema lavandulifolium var. discoideum

Dendranthema lavandulifolium var. discoideum, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white petals surrounding a yellow center, forming a daisy-like flower. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 9. It has silvery-gray foliage that resembles lavender leaves, giving it its scientific name.

This perennial is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
