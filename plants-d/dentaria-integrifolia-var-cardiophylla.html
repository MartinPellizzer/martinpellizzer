Dentaria integrifolia var. cardiophylla

Dentaria integrifolia var. cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf toothplant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
