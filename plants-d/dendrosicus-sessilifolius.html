Dendrosicus sessilifolius

Dendrosicus sessilifolius, commonly known as the sessile-leaved tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually clustered at the top of the trunk, with the leaves emerging directly from the stem rather than from branches.

The plant's unique structure makes it an important component of rainforest ecosystems.
