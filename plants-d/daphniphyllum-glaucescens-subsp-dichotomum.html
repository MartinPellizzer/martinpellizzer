Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp. dichotomum

Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp.

dichotomum, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia. It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a distinctive silver or pale underside.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and winter interest.
