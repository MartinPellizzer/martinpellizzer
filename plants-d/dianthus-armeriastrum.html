Dianthus armeriastrum

Dianthus armeriastrum, commonly known as the sea pink, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus armeriastrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
