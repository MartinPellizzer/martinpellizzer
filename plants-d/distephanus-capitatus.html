Distephanus capitatus

Distephanus capitatus, commonly known as the red-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in a dense inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Distephanus capitatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
