Diospyros inflata

Diospyros inflata, commonly known as the inflated persimmon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated fruit that resembles a small balloon. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

The fruit is edible when ripe and has a sweet, astringent flavor.
