Dendrobium cupreum

Dendrobium cupreum, commonly known as the copper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium cupreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
