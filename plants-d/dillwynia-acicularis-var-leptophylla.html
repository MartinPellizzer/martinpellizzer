Dillwynia acicularis var. leptophylla

Dillwynia acicularis var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dillwynia, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to drought conditions.

This variety is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, which contribute to soil fertility.
