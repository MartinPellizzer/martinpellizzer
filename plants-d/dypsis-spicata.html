Dypsis spicata

Dypsis spicata, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapted to arid environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a short trunk and a crown of feathery leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters.

Dypsis spicata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique shape and low maintenance requirements.
