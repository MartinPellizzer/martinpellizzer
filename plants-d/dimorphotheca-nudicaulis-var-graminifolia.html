Dimorphotheca nudicaulis var. graminifolia

Dimorphotheca nudicaulis var. graminifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf Guinea flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
