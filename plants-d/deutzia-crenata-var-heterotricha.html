Deutzia crenata var. heterotricha

Deutzia crenata var. heterotricha, commonly known as the silver-leaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or pink berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscapes.
