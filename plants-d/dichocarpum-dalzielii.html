Dichocarpum dalzielii

Dichocarpum dalzielii, commonly known as the Dalziel's false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments, and it is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
