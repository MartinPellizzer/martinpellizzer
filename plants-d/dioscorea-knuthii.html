Dioscorea knuthii

Dioscorea knuthii, commonly known as the Knuth yam, is a perennial vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some indigenous communities.

The botanical features of D. knuthii make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
