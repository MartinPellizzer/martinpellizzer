Desmodium rostratum

Desmodium rostratum, commonly known as hairypointer vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
