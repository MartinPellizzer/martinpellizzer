Diospyros bangkana

Diospyros bangkana, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing one to three seeds. Its fruit is edible, though often astringent when unripe, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
