Dryopteris ctenitis f. isabellina

Dryopteris ctenitis f. isabellina, commonly known as the Isabelline Hart's-tongue fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and leathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The fronds are typically dark green with a slightly lighter midrib, and they remain upright, giving the plant a compact appearance. This fern prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.
