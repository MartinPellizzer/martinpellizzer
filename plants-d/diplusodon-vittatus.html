Diplusodon vittatus

Diplusodon vittatus, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers with white spots, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Diplusodon vittatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
