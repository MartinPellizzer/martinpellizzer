Dipladenia tenuifolia var. volubilis

Dipladenia tenuifolia var. volubilis, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a vigorous trailing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with shades of green, white, and sometimes pink. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, orange-yellow flowers, which are followed by edible tuberous roots. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often used as a decorative ground cover or in hanging baskets.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
