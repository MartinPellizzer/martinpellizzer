Dichrostachys pervilleana var. pubescens

Dichrostachys pervilleana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy burr legume, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, which can persist on the plant for extended periods.
