Dactylorchis × masteineri

Dactylorchis × masteineri, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dactylorchis filiformis and Dactylorchis masteineri.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas with rich, humusy soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique floral display.
