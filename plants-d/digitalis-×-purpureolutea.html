Digitalis × purpureolutea

Digitalis × purpureolutea, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a hybrid plant in the genus Digitalis, known for its striking violet to purple flower spikes.

It is native to the British Isles and is often found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically 5–7 cm in length, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base. It is a biennial or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
