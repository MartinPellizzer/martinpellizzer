Dipteracanthus scandens

Dipteracanthus scandens, commonly known as the climbing dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a vine that produces small, white or greenish flowers, which are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its twining habit, allowing it to climb supports. Its botanical features include a slender stem with adventitious roots and a fibrous root system.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.
