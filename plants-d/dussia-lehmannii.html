Dussia lehmannii

Dussia lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's dussia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

Dussia lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
