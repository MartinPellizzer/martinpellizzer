Dioscorea linearis

Dioscorea linearis, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also cultivated for their nutritional value and are a source of carbohydrates.

Dioscorea linearis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
