Daviesia epiphyllum

Daviesia epiphyllum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a climbing shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves and striking, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is typically found growing on rocky outcrops or in open woodlands, where it can climb over other vegetation. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
