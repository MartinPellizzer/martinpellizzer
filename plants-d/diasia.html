Diasia

Diasia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Diasia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
