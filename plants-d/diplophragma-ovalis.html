Diplophragma ovalis

Diplophragma ovalis, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are legumes that split open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
