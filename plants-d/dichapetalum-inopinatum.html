Dichapetalum inopinatum

Dichapetalum inopinatum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
