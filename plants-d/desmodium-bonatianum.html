Desmodium bonatianum

Desmodium bonatianum, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, it can become invasive in some areas if not managed properly.
