Disa rungweensis subsp. rhodesiaca

Disa rungweensis subsp.

rhodesiaca, commonly known as the Rhodesian disa, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant thrives in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, often found in areas with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the flowers are arranged in a loose raceme.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations.
