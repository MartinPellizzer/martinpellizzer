Draba mogollonica

Draba mogollonica, commonly known as the Mogollon Draba, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This low-growing perennial typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters, with small, basal leaves and clusters of white or yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local pollinators.
