Dracocephalum aristatum

Dracocephalum aristatum, commonly known as the spiked skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
