Dalea pendulina

Dalea pendulina, commonly known as the hanging milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Dalea pendulina is an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
