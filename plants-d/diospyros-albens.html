Diospyros albens

Diospyros albens, commonly known as the white persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and dull underneath. Its fruit is a small, yellow to orange drupe that ripens in late summer to early autumn.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
