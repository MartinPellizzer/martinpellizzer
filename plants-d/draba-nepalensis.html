Draba nepalensis

Draba nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a small perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often on rocky slopes and in mountainous terrain. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba nepalensis is adapted to cold climates and has a high tolerance for poor soil conditions.
