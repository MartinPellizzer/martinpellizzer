Draba bellardii

Draba bellardii, commonly known as the Bellard's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba bellardii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
