Dichopsis hexandra

Dichopsis hexandra, commonly known as the desert bell, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with clusters of fleshy, cylindrical leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Dichopsis hexandra is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and low water requirements.
