Diasperus montevidensis

Diasperus montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is notable for its adaptability and resilience in various environmental conditions.
