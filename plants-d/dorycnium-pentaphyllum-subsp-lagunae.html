Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp. lagunae

Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp.

lagunae, commonly known as the lake burr, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a creeping rootstock and produces elongated, beak-like pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in coastal and inland wetlands.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
