Draba jucunda

Draba jucunda, commonly known as the alpine penny-cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Draba jucunda is a perennial herb that produces yellow or white flowers, often blooming in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.
