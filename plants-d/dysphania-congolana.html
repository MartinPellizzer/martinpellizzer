Dysphania congolana

Dysphania congolana, commonly known as Congo goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, riverbanks, and forest margins. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.

Dysphania congolana is also valued as a leaf vegetable in some parts of Africa due to its nutritional content.
