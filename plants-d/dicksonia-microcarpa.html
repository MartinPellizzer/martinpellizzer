Dicksonia microcarpa

Dicksonia microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its spore-producing cones are clustered at the base of the fronds, and the plant reproduces primarily through spores rather than seeds.

Dicksonia microcarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
