Dolichos lubia

Dolichos lubia, commonly known as small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and cultivated in warm climates.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow or white flowers and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are often used as a food source, valued for their high protein content and nutritional value.

Dolichos lubia is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
