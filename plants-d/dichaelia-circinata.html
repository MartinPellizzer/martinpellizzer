Dichaelia circinata

Dichaelia circinata, commonly known as the spiral grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

Dichaelia circinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage structure.
