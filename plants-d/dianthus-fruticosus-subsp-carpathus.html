Dianthus fruticosus subsp. carpathus

Dianthus fruticosus subsp.

carpathus, commonly known as the Carpathian campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of linear, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
