Dioscorea psilostachya

Dioscorea psilostachya, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are edible. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source in various regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
