Dallwatsonia hylaeica

Dallwatsonia hylaeica, commonly known as the Hylaeic Watsonia, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dallwatsonia hylaeica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
