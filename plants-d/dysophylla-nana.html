Dysophylla nana

Dysophylla nana, commonly known as the dwarf dysophylla, is a small herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and bloom in shades of white or pale yellow.

Dysophylla nana is relatively uncommon and has limited scientific study due to its restricted habitat and elusive nature.
