Desmoncus dubius

Desmoncus dubius, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that resembles a ghostly figure, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom sequentially along the central stem. Its leaves form a rosette that collects rainwater, creating a small habitat for insects and other small organisms.

Desmoncus dubius is valued in horticulture for its dramatic appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
