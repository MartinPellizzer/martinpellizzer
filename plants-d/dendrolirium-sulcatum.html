Dendrolirium sulcatum

Dendrolirium sulcatum, commonly known as the grooved lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with a distinctive groove running down the center of the petals.

Dendrolirium sulcatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
