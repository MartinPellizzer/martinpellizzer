Dasiphora arbuscula

Dasiphora arbuscula, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
