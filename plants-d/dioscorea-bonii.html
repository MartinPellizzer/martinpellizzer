Dioscorea bonii

Dioscorea bonii, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by tuberous roots that are edible. Its tubers are often used as a food source and have a starchy, nutty flavor.

Dioscorea bonii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
