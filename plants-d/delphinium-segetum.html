Delphinium segetum

Delphinium segetum, commonly known as the corn larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in meadows and grasslands.

Delphinium segetum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buttercups and anemones.
