Drimia flagellaris

Drimia flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Drimia flagellaris is also known for its ability to form dense clumps over time, which can be both aesthetically pleasing and invasive in certain environments.
