Diplazium polymorphum

Diplazium polymorphum, commonly known as the variable brake, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into lanceolate segments with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Diplazium polymorphum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
