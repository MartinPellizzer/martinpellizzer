Diasperus rigidulus

Diasperus rigidulus, commonly known as the rigid desert thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Diasperus rigidulus is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.
