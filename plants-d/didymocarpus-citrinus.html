Didymocarpus citrinus

Didymocarpus citrinus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to its canopy. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red as they mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
