Diplostephium acuminatum

Diplostephium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed burrgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open areas such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a hairy surface.

The species is known for its ability to form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.
