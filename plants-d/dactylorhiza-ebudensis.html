Dactylorhiza ebudensis

Dactylorhiza ebudensis, commonly known as the East African marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Africa.

It typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant has a complex root system that helps it anchor in waterlogged soils. Its flowers are usually purple or pink and bloom during the wet season.

Dactylorhiza ebudensis is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
