Dichopogon fimbriatus

Dichopogon fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. Dichopogon fimbriatus is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
