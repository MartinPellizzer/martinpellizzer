Dianthus quadrangulus

Dianthus quadrangulus, commonly known as the four-angled campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are often four-angled, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
