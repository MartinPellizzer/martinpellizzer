Distegia involucrata var. serotina

Distegia involucrata var. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering distegia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom late in the growing season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
