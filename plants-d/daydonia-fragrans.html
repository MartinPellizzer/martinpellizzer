Daydonia fragrans

Daydonia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often blooming at night. Its fragrant flowers emit a sweet aroma, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The fruit, a small berry, contains toxic compounds and is not suitable for human consumption.
