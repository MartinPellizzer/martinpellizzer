Dioscorea longifolia

Dioscorea longifolia, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, thin, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its tubers are sometimes cultivated for their potential medicinal properties and are also used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in both ecological and cultural contexts.
