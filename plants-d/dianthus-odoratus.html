Dianthus odoratus

Dianthus odoratus, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Dianthus odoratus is also valued for its pleasant fragrance, which adds to its ornamental appeal.
