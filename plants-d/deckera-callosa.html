Deckera callosa

Deckera callosa, commonly known as the roughleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in a waxy, white coating. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface, which gives the plant its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
