Diapensia purpurea f. bulleyana

Diapensia purpurea f. bulleyana, commonly known as the Bulleyan's rockfoil, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in several regions.
