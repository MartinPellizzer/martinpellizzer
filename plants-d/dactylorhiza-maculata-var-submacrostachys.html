Dactylorhiza maculata var. submacrostachys

Dactylorhiza maculata var. submacrostachys, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats. It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
