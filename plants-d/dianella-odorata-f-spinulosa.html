Dianella odorata f. spinulosa

Dianella odorata f. spinulosa, commonly known as the fragrant flax lily, is a cultivar of the flax lily plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a distinctive spinulose texture. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
