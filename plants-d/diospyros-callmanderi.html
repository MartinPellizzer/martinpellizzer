Diospyros callmanderi

Diospyros callmanderi, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of orange to red, fleshy berries in the fall. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, astringent flavor that becomes smoother when fully ripe.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of persimmon fruit.
