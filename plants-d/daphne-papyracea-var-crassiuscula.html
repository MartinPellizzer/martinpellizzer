Daphne papyracea var. crassiuscula

Daphne papyracea var. crassiuscula, commonly known as the thick-leaved daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically thick and leathery, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
