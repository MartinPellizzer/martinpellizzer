Dendranthema maximoviczii

Dendranthema maximoviczii, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white petals surrounding a yellow central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of gray-green, hairy leaves that are often aromatic.

Dendranthema maximoviczii is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
