Dircaea purpurea

Dircaea purpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf ironwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, gnarled branches and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. Dircaea purpurea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in rocky soils.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
