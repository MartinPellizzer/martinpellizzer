Digitaria sparsifructus

Digitaria sparsifructus, commonly known as slender digitgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often loosely arranged along the stem.

Digitaria sparsifructus is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
