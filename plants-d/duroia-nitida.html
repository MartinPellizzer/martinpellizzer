Duroia nitida

Duroia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central column.

Duroia nitida is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
