Danais mandrarensis

Danais mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the island nation of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it receives indirect sunlight. Its flowers are notable for their intricate patterns and are often pollinated by specific insect species native to the region.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Danais mandrarensis is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
