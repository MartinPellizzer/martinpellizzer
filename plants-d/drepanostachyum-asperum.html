Drepanostachyum asperum

Drepanostachyum asperum, commonly known as the roughleaf bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, hairy leaves and culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
