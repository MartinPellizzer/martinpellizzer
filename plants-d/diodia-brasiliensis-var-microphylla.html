Diodia brasiliensis var. microphylla

Diodia brasiliensis var. microphylla, commonly known as Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
