Dactylorhiza siculorum

Dactylorhiza siculorum, commonly known as the Sicilian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Sicily and parts of southern Europe. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. It produces a single stem with a few leaves and bears small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring.

Dactylorhiza siculorum is characterized by its pseudobulbs and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
