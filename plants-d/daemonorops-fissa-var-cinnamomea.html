Daemonorops fissa var. cinnamomea

Daemonorops fissa var. cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feathery fronds that give it a dramatic appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rainforests or along stream banks. Its fronds are composed of numerous small, narrow leaflets that create a lush, green canopy.

The cinnamon fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
