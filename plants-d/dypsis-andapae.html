Dypsis andapae

Dypsis andapae, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its short, thick trunk and clusters of spiky, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Dypsis andapae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
