Diplotaenia cachrydifolia

Diplotaenia cachrydifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
