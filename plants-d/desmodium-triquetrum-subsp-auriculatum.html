Desmodium triquetrum subsp. auriculatum

Desmodium triquetrum subsp.

auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate desmodium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with auriculate (ear-shaped) lobes at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, followed by lens-shaped pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
