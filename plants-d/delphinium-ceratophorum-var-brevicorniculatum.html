Delphinium ceratophorum var. brevicorniculatum

Delphinium ceratophorum var. brevicorniculatum, commonly known as the short-horned delphinium, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with elongated, serrated leaflets, and it produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that mature in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
