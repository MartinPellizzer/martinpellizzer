Dendrocalamus hamiltonii var. undulatus

Dendrocalamus hamiltonii var. undulatus, commonly known as the undulating bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts due to their strength and flexibility. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

This variety is also valued for its ecological role in preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
