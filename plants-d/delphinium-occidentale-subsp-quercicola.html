Delphinium occidentale subsp. quercicola

Delphinium occidentale subsp.

quercicola, commonly known as the western lupine delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along streambanks.
