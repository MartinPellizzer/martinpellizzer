Draba longirostra f. psilocarpa

Draba longirostra f. psilocarpa, commonly known as the long-beaked whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The species is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often growing in moist, calcareous soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated floral structure and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features.
