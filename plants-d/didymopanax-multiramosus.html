Didymopanax multiramosus

Didymopanax multiramosus, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the giant palm, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers, followed by orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforest environments.

Its unique appearance and growth habits make it a notable species in botanical studies.
