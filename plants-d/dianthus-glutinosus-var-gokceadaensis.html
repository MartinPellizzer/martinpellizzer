Dianthus glutinosus var. gokceadaensis

Dianthus glutinosus var. gokceadaensis, commonly known as the Gökçeada carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Turkish island of Gökçeada.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh maritime climate. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
