Diospyros malacothrix

Diospyros malacothrix, commonly known as the Malacothrix persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are highly nutritious.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
