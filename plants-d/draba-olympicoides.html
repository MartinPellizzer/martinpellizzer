Draba olympicoides

Draba olympicoides, commonly known as Olympic Draba, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This small, herbaceous plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and forest edges. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
