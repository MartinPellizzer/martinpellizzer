Dipterocarpus lasiopodus

Dipterocarpus lasiopodus, commonly known as the rough-leaved dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, reddish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, and its fruits are woody capsules containing several seeds. It plays an important role in the ecological systems of tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which has been historically used in construction and furniture-making.
