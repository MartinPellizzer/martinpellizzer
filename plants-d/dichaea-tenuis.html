Dichaea tenuis

Dichaea tenuis, commonly known as the slender dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features delicate, strap-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often greenish-white in color. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic or lithophytic growth habit.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Dichaea tenuis is often considered a conservation concern.
