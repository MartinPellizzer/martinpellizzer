Diospyros amborelloides

Diospyros amborelloides, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces small, round fruits that are typically orange to red in color when ripe.

The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood and is also noted for its ornamental appeal.
