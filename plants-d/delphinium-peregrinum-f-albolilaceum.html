Delphinium peregrinum f. albolilaceum

Delphinium peregrinum f. albolilaceum, commonly known as the white-lilac delphinium, is a hybrid form of the alpine delphinium.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of large, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pale blue to white in color, with a distinctive lilac hue, giving it its common name. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and prefers well-drained, alkaline soils.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
