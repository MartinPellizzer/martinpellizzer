Dermatocalyx parviflorus

Dermatocalyx parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered dermatocalyx, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
