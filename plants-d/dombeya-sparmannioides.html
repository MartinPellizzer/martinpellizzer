Dombeya sparmannioides

Dombeya sparmannioides, commonly known as the African dombeya, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
