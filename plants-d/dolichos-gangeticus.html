Dolichos gangeticus

Dolichos gangeticus, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible, bean-like seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
