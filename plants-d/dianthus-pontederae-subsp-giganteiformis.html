Dianthus pontederae subsp. giganteiformis

Dianthus pontederae subsp.

giganteiformis, commonly known as the giant form of the Pontedera dianthus, is a striking member of the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white, with prominent red or purple markings.

The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
