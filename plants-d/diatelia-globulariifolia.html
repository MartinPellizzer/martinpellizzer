Diatelia globulariifolia

Diatelia globulariifolia, commonly known as the globe-leaved diatelia, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its globular shape and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support and maximizing sunlight absorption.

Diatelia globulariifolia is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
