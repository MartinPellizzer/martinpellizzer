Dianthus seguieri var. dentosus

Dianthus seguieri var. dentosus, commonly known as the toothed carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically narrow and toothed, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
