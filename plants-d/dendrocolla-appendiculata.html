Dendrocolla appendiculata

Dendrocolla appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate tree fern, is a unique species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive root system that helps it anchor firmly in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structures are located at the base of the trunk, and it relies on spores for propagation.

Dendrocolla appendiculata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
