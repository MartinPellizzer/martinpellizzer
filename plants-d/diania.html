Diania

Diania, commonly known as the common diania, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, lanceolate, and often hairy, providing a textured appearance. Diania species are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and they thrive in a variety of soil types.

These plants are often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, contributing to their ecological and ornamental value.
