Draba × spreadhoroughii

Draba × spreadhoroughii, commonly known as the hybrid winter cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit with basal leaves and produces yellow or white flowers in early spring.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
