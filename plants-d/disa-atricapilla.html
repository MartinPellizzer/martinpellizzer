Disa atricapilla

Disa atricapilla, commonly known as the purple disa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in late summer.

Disa atricapilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
