Daemonorops grandis

Daemonorops grandis, commonly known as the giant pandan, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, clump-forming plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green inflorescences that are often used in traditional cuisine and crafts. Its rhizomes are also utilized in local medicine for their purported health benefits.
