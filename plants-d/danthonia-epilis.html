Danthonia epilis

Danthonia epilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in dune environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Danthonia epilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sand dunes through its extensive root system.
