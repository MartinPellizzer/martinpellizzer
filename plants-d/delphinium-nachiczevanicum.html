Delphinium nachiczevanicum

Delphinium nachiczevanicum, commonly known as the Caucasian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in raceme-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture, and its roots are fibrous and somewhat tuberous.

Delphinium nachiczevanicum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in mountainous environments.
