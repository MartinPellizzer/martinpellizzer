Diploprion medicaginoides

Diploprion medicaginoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf medick, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
