Diospyros bernieriana

Diospyros bernieriana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
