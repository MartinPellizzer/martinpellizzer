Dicksonia bipinnata

Dicksonia bipinnata, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, brownish layer called the rachis. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fronds are arranged in a bipinnate pattern, giving the plant its scientific name.
