Desmodium obovatum

Desmodium obovatum, commonly known as the ovalleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its growth habit makes it a useful plant for soil stabilization and as a forage species in some regions.
