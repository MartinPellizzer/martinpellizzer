Desmanthus pumilus

Desmanthus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf desmanthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings. It typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for native landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
