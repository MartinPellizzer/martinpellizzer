Desmostachys planchonianus f. glaucifolius

Desmostachys planchonianus f. glaucifolius, commonly known as the glaucifolius form of the plantain, is a variant of the plantain species characterized by its distinctive glaucous, waxy leaves.

The leaves are typically broad, lance-shaped, and have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs. This form is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, followed by three-lobed capsules that contain the seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
