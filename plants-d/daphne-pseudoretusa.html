Daphne pseudoretusa

Daphne pseudoretusa, commonly known as the golden daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, typically in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing visual interest throughout the growing season. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Daphne pseudoretusa prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
