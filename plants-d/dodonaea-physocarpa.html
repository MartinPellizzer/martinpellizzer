Dodonaea physocarpa

Dodonaea physocarpa, commonly known as the Australian wattle or broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and oval in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its fruit is a woody pod that contains several seeds.

Dodonaea physocarpa is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
