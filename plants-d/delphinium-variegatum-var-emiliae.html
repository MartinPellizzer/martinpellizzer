Delphinium variegatum var. emiliae

Delphinium variegatum var. emiliae, commonly known as Emilia's larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, showy spikes. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue with a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
