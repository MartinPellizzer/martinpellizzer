Deutzia crenata f. lasiocarpa

Deutzia crenata f. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly deutzia, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a slightly wavy margin and a hairy texture that gives it its "woolly" appearance. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
