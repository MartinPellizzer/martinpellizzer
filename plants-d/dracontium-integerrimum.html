Dracontium integerrimum

Dracontium integerrimum, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking white or pale green spadices surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

The rhizomes of Dracontium integerrimum are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
