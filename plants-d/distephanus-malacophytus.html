Distephanus malacophytus

Distephanus malacophytus, commonly known as the purple starfish plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped inflorescences that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant features a basal rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its flowers are typically borne on long, branched stems and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
