Dudleya virens subsp. virens

Dudleya virens subsp.

virens, commonly known as the green Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California and Baja California. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its low water requirements and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
