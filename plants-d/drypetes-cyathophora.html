Drypetes cyathophora

Drypetes cyathophora, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
