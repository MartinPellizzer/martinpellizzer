Dockrillia linguiformis

Dockrillia linguiformis, commonly known as the tongue-shaped dockrillia, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features elongated, tongue-like leaves that are typically broad and slightly lobed, often with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or white in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Dockrillia linguiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
