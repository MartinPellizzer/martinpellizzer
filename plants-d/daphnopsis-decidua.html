Daphnopsis decidua

Daphnopsis decidua, commonly known as the deciduous daphnopsis, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that turn yellow and fall off during the dry season. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
