Drymocallis albida

Drymocallis albida, commonly known as white oak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet to moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually tall and cylindrical, producing small, greenish-yellow flowers.

Drymocallis albida plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
