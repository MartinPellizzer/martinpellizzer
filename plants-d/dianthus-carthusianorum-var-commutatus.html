Dianthus carthusianorum var. commutatus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. commutatus, commonly known as the Commutated Carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant produces a distinctive, spicy fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
