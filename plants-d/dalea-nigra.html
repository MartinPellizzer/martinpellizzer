Dalea nigra

Dalea nigra, commonly known as black prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has compound leaves and produces small, dark purple to black flowers that attract pollinators.

Dalea nigra is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in native plant landscaping.
