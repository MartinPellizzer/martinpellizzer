Dianthus neglectus

Dianthus neglectus, commonly known as the common carnation, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with white or yellow centers. The plant has a compact growth habit, often reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, and features slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dianthus neglectus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
