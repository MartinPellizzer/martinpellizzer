Diostea scoparia var. puberula

Diostea scoparia var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent diostea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
