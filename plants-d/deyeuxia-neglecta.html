Deyeuxia neglecta

Deyeuxia neglecta, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to various parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is usually inconspicuous and small.

Deyeuxia neglecta is often found in wet or seasonally flooded areas, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
