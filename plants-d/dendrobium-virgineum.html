Dendrobium virgineum

Dendrobium virgineum, commonly known as the orchid of the mountains, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium virgineum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
