Dichosma bifida

Dichosma bifida, commonly known as the splitleaf dichosma, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dichosma bifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
