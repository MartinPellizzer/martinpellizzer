Dioscorea sandiensis

Dioscorea sandiensis, commonly known as the sandier yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by yellowish fruits that contain toxic seeds. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds, requiring careful preparation before consumption.
