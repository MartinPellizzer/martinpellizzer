Diospyros whyteana

Diospyros whyteana, commonly known as the Australian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
