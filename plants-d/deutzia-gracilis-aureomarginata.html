Deutzia gracilis aureomarginata

Deutzia gracilis aureomarginata, commonly known as the golden-margin deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers with distinctive golden margins. The foliage is typically dark green and provides attractive contrast to the blooms. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its early spring flowering.
