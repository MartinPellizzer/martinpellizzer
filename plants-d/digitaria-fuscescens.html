Digitaria fuscescens

Digitaria fuscescens, commonly known as blackgrass or redgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a dark green to reddish hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Digitaria fuscescens is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it both a valuable forage crop and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
