Daucus creticus

Daucus creticus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silvery, finely divided leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
