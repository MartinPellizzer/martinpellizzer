Dioscorea chacoensis

Dioscorea chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco yam, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its growth habit and morphology are adapted to the dry, seasonal climates of its native habitat.
