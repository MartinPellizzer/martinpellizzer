Diasperus stylosus

Diasperus stylosus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Diasperus stylosus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
