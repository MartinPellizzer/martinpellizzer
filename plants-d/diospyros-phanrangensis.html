Diospyros phanrangensis

Diospyros phanrangensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red berries. Its fruit is edible, though typically astringent when unripe, and is valued for its flavor when fully mature.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties.
