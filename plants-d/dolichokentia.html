Dolichokentia

Dolichokentia, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and have a slightly rough texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-bearing spikes.

Dolichokentia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
