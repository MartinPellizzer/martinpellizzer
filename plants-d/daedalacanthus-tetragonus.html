Daedalacanthus tetragonus

Daedalacanthus tetragonus, commonly known as the four-angled sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stem and waxy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in saline environments and is often found in intertidal zones and mangrove swamps.

Daedalacanthus tetragonus plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
