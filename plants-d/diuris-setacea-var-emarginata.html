Diuris setacea var. emarginata

Diuris setacea var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a slender, wiry stem with a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower that has a distinctive, narrow lip. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open woodlands or heathlands. It blooms during late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by thynnid wasps.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
