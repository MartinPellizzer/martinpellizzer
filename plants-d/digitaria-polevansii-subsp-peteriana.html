Digitaria polevansii subsp. peteriana

Digitaria polevansii subsp.

peteriana, commonly known as the African fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and savannas.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
