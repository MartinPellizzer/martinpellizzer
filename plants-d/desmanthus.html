Desmanthus

Desmanthus, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Desmanthus species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Many species are nitrogen-fixing, making them valuable for soil enrichment and as forage crops.

The plant's ability to thrive in poor soils and its ecological benefits contribute to its importance in both agricultural and natural settings.
