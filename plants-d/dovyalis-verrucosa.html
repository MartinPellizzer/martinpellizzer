Dovyalis verrucosa

Dovyalis verrucosa, commonly known as the rough passionfruit, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-colored fruits with a tangy, acidic flavor. The fruit's exterior is covered in a rough, bumpy texture, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in home gardens.
