Darwinia repens

Darwinia repens, commonly known as the creeping Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
