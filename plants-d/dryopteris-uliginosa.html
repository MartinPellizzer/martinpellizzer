Dryopteris uliginosa

Dryopteris uliginosa, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping fronds that are covered in a rusty-brown, cinnamon-colored bloom. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
