Diplostephium stellulatum

Diplostephium stellulatum, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Carambolineae.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which is typically five-lobed and yellow when ripe. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also edible, widely consumed for its sweet and tangy flavor.
