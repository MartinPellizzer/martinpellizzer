Dalbergia rufa

Dalbergia rufa, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptic to oblong in shape.

The flowers are small and yellowish, often appearing in clusters, and the plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
