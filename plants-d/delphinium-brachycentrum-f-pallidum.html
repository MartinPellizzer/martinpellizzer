Delphinium brachycentrum f. pallidum

Delphinium brachycentrum f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale-flowered delphinium, is a hybrid cultivar characterized by its striking, pale blue to lavender-colored flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of spurred petals, typical of the delphinium genus. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of soft hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained, fertile soil.
