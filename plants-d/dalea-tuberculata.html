Dalea tuberculata

Dalea tuberculata, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, pink to red flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, papery pods that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid environments.

Dalea tuberculata is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
