Davallia hymenophylla

Davallia hymenophylla, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist soil. Its rhizomes are wiry and often grow close to the soil surface.

Davallia hymenophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
