Diplacus cusickii

Diplacus cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing or ascending growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

Diplacus cusickii is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in partial to full sun.
