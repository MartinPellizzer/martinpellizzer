Delphinium salmoneum

Delphinium salmoneum, commonly known as the salmon larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of spurred, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained, alkaline soils. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are hairy and serrated.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
