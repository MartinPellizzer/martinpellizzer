Dodonaea bursariifolia var. major

Dodonaea bursariifolia var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, velvety, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in landscaping and restoration projects.
