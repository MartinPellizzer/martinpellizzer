Diallosperma spinosa

Diallosperma spinosa, commonly known as the spiny diallosperma, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, spiny stems and fleshy, cylindrical leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
