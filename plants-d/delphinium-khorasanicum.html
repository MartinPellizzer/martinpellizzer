Delphinium khorasanicum

Delphinium khorasanicum, commonly known as the Khorasan larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves, and it produces small, elongated seed pods that release tiny seeds upon maturity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
