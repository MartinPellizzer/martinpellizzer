Dioscorea moma

Dioscorea moma, commonly known as African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in various traditional healing practices.
