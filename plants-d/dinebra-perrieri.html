Dinebra perrieri

Dinebra perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped blossoms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dinebra perrieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
