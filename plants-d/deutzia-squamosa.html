Deutzia squamosa

Deutzia squamosa, commonly known as the silverleaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the foliage. The plant has slender, arching stems and dark green, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silvery-white hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Deutzia squamosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
