Dianthus trifasciculatus

Dianthus trifasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and small, lobed leaves.

Dianthus trifasciculatus is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
