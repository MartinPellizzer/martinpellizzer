Dendrobium aureum var. pallidum

Dendrobium aureum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale golden orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, often in humid tropical climates. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs covered in a thick, white or pale green sheath. Its flowers are typically yellow or pale orange, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in gardens and as a houseplant.
