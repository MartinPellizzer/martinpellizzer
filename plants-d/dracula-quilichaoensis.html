Dracula quilichaoensis

Dracula quilichaoensis, commonly known as the Quilichao Dracula, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family, native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its vivid, intricate flowers that resemble miniature skulls, a feature that gives it its dramatic common name. It thrives in cool, humid environments with dappled sunlight, often growing on tree branches or rocky outcrops. The plant's foliage consists of dark green, strap-like leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Dracula quilichaoensis is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
