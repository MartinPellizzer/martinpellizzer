Dryopteris filix-mas var. abbreviata

Dryopteris filix-mas var. abbreviata, commonly known as the compact male fern, is a variant of the male fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its shorter, more compact fronds compared to the typical male fern, with a distinctive fiddlehead curl at the base. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The spore-bearing structures, or sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns in the Dryopteris genus.
