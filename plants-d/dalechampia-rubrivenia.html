Dalechampia rubrivenia

Dalechampia rubrivenia, commonly known as the red-veined dalechampia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique leaf structure and milky sap. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent reddish veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
