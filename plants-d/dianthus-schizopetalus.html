Dianthus schizopetalus

Dianthus schizopetalus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. The plant typically blooms in early summer and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus schizopetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
