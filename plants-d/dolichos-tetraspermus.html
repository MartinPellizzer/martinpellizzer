Dolichos tetraspermus

Dolichos tetraspermus, commonly known as the four-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing four seeds each. It is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in traditional agriculture.

Dolichos tetraspermus plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
