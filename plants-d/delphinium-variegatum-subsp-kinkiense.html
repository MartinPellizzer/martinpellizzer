Delphinium variegatum subsp. kinkiense

Delphinium variegatum subsp.

kinkiense, commonly known as the Japanese larkspur, is a perennial herb native to Japan. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern, often featuring white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
