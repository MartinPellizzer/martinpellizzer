Didymocarpus miniatus

Didymocarpus miniatus, commonly known as the small-fruited mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically edible, with a segmented aril surrounding the seed, and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
