Dillenia aurea var. trichocarpella

Dillenia aurea var. trichocarpella, commonly known as the golden dillenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with a waxy surface, and its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
