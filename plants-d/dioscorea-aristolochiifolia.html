Dioscorea aristolochiifolia

Dioscorea aristolochiifolia, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces tubers that are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine. These tubers are rich in starch and have been historically valued for their medicinal properties.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and requires careful handling during cultivation and use.
