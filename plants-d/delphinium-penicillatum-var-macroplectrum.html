Delphinium penicillatum var. macroplectrum

Delphinium penicillatum var. macroplectrum, commonly known as the giant larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically 5-lobed and have a distinctive spurred petal, typical of the Delphinium genus.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
