Dracunculus minor

Dracunculus minor, commonly known as the lesser death's-head tare, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a distinctive, spiky fruit that resembles a small, twisted horn, giving it its common name. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dracunculus minor is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique foliage.
