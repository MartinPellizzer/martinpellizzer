Dimorphostachys pedunculata

Dimorphostachys pedunculata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The plant is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
