Dupatya hilairei

Dupatya hilairei, commonly known as the Hilaire's orchid, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a complex structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
