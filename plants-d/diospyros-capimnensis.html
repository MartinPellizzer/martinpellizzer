Diospyros capimnensis

Diospyros capimnensis, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are rich in nutrients.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and its potential medicinal properties.
