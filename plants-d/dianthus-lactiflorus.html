Dianthus lactiflorus

Dianthus lactiflorus, commonly known as the milk-flowered campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, linear leaves. Its flowers consist of two rows of petals, with the inner row being larger and more showy.

Dianthus lactiflorus is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
