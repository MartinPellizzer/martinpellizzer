Datura chlorantha

Datura chlorantha, commonly known as the white angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces egg-shaped, greenish-brown berries that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Datura chlorantha is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its hallucinogenic properties due to the presence of tropane alkaloids.
