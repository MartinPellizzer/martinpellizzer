Dioscorea glomerulata

Dioscorea glomerulata, commonly known as the clustered yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cuisines, while also containing compounds with potential pharmacological applications.

The species is valued both for its nutritional content and its role in traditional medicine systems.
