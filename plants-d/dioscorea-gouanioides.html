Dioscorea gouanioides

Dioscorea gouanioides, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its tubers are edible and have been utilized as a food source in some regions.

The botanical features of D. gouanioides make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
