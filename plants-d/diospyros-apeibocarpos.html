Diospyros apeibocarpos

Diospyros apeibocarpos, commonly known as the Malabar date palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy drupes. Its fruits are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
