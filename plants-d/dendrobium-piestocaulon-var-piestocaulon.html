Dendrobium piestocaulon var. piestocaulon

Dendrobium piestocaulon var. piestocaulon, commonly known as the Piestocaulon orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and yellow.

The leaves are strap-like and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal in cultivation.
