Davallia lherminieri

Davallia lherminieri, commonly known as the silver-veined fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds with a distinctive silvery vein pattern. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. It produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, typical of ferns.

Davallia lherminieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ease of care.
