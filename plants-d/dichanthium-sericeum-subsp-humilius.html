Dichanthium sericeum subsp. humilius

Dichanthium sericeum subsp.

humilius, commonly known as the silky grass or slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in dense clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
