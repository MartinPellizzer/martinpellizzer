Draba hederifolia subsp. cossonii

Draba hederifolia subsp.

cossonii, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of closely packed, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically dark green.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal cluster, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is often found in meadows and scree slopes at high altitudes.
