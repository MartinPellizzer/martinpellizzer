Delphinium variegatum f. subnudum

Delphinium variegatum f. subnudum, commonly known as the variegated larkspur, is a botanical variant of the species Delphinium variegatum.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, typical of the Delphinium genus. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to cooler, alpine environments.

This cultivar is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and unique leaf patterns.
