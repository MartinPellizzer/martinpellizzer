Dillwynia sericea subsp. rudis

Dillwynia sericea subsp.

rudis, commonly known as the slender dillwynia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

The subspecies rudis is distinguished by its slender, wiry stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and role in supporting local wildlife.
