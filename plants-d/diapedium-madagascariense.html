Diapedium madagascariense

Diapedium madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid tropical forests of its native habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy structures.

The orchid's floral structure is adapted for specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in the region.
