Dorstenia orientalis

Dorstenia orientalis, commonly known as the "tongue of the sky," is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiraling inflorescence that resembles a twisted ribbon, with small, pale green flowers clustered along the stem. The plant has a creeping, scandent growth habit, often climbing or trailing along the ground or over other vegetation. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive, wavy margin.

Dorstenia orientalis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
