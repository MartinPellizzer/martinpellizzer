Diasperus myrtilloides

Diasperus myrtilloides, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a small shrub native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and dark green color.

Diasperus myrtilloides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.
