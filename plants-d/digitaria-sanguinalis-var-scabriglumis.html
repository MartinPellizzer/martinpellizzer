Digitaria sanguinalis var. scabriglumis

Digitaria sanguinalis var. scabriglumis, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and pastures.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
