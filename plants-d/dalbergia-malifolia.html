Dalbergia malifolia

Dalbergia malifolia, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant appearance during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
