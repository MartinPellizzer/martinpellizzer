Dendrobium × cassiope virginale

Dendrobium × cassiope virginale, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid between Dendrobium nobile and Dendrobium phalaenopsis, resulting in a plant that combines traits from both parents. The orchid typically has erect, cane-like stems that are often covered in dense clusters of flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a velvety texture and a sweet, spicy fragrance.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance in gardens and greenhouses.
