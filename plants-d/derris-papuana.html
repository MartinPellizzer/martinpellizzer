Derris papuana

Derris papuana, commonly known as the Papuan spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and milky sap. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, which are cup-shaped structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is also noted for its potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
