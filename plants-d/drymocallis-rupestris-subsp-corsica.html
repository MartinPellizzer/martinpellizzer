Drymocallis rupestris subsp. corsica

Drymocallis rupestris subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry and sunny environments.

The plant features upright, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves that are usually grayish-green in color. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, forming panicles that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
