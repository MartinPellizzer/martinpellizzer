Dalechampia humilis var. adscendens

Dalechampia humilis var. adscendens, commonly known as the creeping dalechampia, is a low-growing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features slender, branching stems that often trail along the ground, forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers clustered in terminal spikes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a heart-shaped base with serrated edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its ground-covering habit.
