Dianthus seguieri subsp. seguieri

Dianthus seguieri subsp.

seguieri, commonly known as the rock campion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of bright pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer.

The plant has slender, branched stems and dark green, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
