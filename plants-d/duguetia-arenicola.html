Duguetia arenicola

Duguetia arenicola, commonly known as the sandstone vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Duguetia arenicola thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
