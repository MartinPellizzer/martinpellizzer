Datura fastuosa var. tuberculata

Datura fastuosa var. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white to pale purple in color. The plant produces spiny, hairy capsules that contain numerous elliptical seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are covered in fine hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
