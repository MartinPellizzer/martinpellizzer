Dianthus biflorus

Dianthus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like carnations and pinks. The plant is characterized by its pink or white flowers, which typically bloom in clusters of two, giving it its scientific name. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dianthus biflorus is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it contributes to the floral diversity of these environments.
