Ditta

Ditta, commonly known as the Indian nettle or *Urtica dioica*, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and stinging hairs that can cause a painful reaction upon contact. The plant typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with alternate, heart-shaped leaves and small greenish-white flowers. Ditta thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges, fields, and along roadsides.

It is a member of the Urticaceae family, known for its medicinal and culinary uses in traditional practices.
