Distichorchis × pahangensis

Distichorchis × pahangensis, commonly known as the Pahang orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences with showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in two rows, giving it the genus name "Distichorchis.".

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
