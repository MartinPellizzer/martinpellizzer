Dendrobium scopa

Dendrobium scopa, commonly known as the orchid with the hairy stem, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant features a cane-like stem covered in dense, white to brownish hairs, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or green with a distinctive purple or brown lip.

Dendrobium scopa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
