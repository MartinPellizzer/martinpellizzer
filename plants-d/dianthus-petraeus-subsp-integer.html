Dianthus petraeus subsp. integer

Dianthus petraeus subsp.

integer, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
