Dolichos monophyllus

Dolichos monophyllus, commonly known as the giant horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is valued for its high protein content and is often cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural systems and ecological studies.
