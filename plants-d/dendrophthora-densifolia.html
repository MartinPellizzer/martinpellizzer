Dendrophthora densifolia

Dendrophthora densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It has small, ovate leaves that are densely packed, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces white or pale yellow berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to host trees. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions where it forms a symbiotic relationship with various tree species.

Its presence can have both positive and negative effects on the health of its host plants.
