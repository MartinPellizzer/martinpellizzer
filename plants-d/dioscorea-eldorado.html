Dioscorea eldorado

Dioscorea eldorado, commonly known as the Eldorado yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, loamy soil for optimal growth. Its tubers are characterized by their irregular shape and can vary in color from white to yellowish.

The botanical features of Dioscorea eldorado make it an important species in traditional medicine and local agriculture.
