Danthonia spicata var. typica

Danthonia spicata var. typica, commonly known as slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to form dense colonies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
