Dacrycarpus steupii

Dacrycarpus steupii, commonly known as the New Zealand spruce, is a coniferous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Dacrycarpus steupii plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's native forests.
