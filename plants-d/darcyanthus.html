Darcyanthus

Darcyanthus, commonly known as the rainbow flower, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, with showy modified leaves that serve as the primary ornamental feature.

Darcyanthus is valued in horticulture for its vibrant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
