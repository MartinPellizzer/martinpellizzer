Doxantha tenuicula

Doxantha tenuicula, commonly known as the slender doxantha, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
