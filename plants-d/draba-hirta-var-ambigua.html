Draba hirta var. ambigua

Draba hirta var. ambigua, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and cruciform petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Draba hirta var. ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
