Dolabrifolia bancoensis

Dolabrifolia bancoensis, commonly known as the Banko dolabrifolia, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, sickle-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name "dolabrifolia," derived from the Latin words "dolabrum" meaning a sickle and "folium" meaning leaf. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Dolabrifolia bancoensis is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
