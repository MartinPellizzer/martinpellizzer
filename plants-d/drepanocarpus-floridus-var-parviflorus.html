Drepanocarpus floridus var. parviflorus

Drepanocarpus floridus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along waterways. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy texture. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

The plant is characterized by its dense growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
