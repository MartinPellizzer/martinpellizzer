Desmodium confertum

Desmodium confertum, commonly known as the hairy sida, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Desmodium confertum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
