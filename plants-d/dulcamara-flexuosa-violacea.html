Dulcamara flexuosa violacea

Dulcamara flexuosa violacea, commonly known as the purple bittersweet, is a climbing plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces red berries that are toxic to humans and animals, making it a cautionary species in natural habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are typically arranged in clusters.

Dulcamara flexuosa violacea thrives in moist, shaded areas and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
