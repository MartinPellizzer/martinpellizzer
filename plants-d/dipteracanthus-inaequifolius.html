Dipteracanthus inaequifolius

Dipteracanthus inaequifolius, commonly known as the unequal-leaved dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae, which includes many tropical tree species. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that vary in size and shape along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a distinctive reddish or yellowish hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
