Dracaena griffithii

Dracaena griffithii, commonly known as the dragon tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and its striking, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

Dracaena griffithii is also valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its air-purifying properties.
