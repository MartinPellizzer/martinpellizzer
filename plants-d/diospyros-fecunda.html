Diospyros fecunda

Diospyros fecunda, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. The tree bears edible drupes that are typically consumed fresh or processed into various food products. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Diospyros fecunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
