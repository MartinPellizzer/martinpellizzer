Daedalacanthus suffruticosus

Daedalacanthus suffruticosus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color, often appearing in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Daedalacanthus suffruticosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
