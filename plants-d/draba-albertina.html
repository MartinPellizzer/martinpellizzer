Draba albertina

Draba albertina, commonly known as the Albertina whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. This species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, delicate leaves and clusters of tiny white or pale yellow flowers. Draba albertina typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
