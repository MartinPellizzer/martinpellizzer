Dryopteris crenulata

Dryopteris crenulata, commonly known as the Boston fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It features feathery, arching fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a rhizomatous growth habit. Its leaves are typically a deep green and can reach lengths of up to two feet.

Dryopteris crenulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
