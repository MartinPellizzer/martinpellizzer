Dyckia catharinensis var. dentata

Dyckia catharinensis var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated dyckia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often adorned with sharp, serrated edges. The plant produces striking, tubular inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
