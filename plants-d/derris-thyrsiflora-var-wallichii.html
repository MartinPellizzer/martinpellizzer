Derris thyrsiflora var. wallichii

Derris thyrsiflora var. wallichii, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, and its roots are known for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in traditional medicine and pest control.
