Dioscorea nipponica subsp. rosthornii

Dioscorea nipponica subsp.

rosthornii, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports.

The plant produces oval, tuberous roots that are rich in starch and have been used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface, while its flowers are small and unisexual.

The subspecies rosthornii is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and geographic distribution.
