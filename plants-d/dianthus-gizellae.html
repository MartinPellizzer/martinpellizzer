Dianthus gizellae

Dianthus gizellae, commonly known as the Sicilian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sicily.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its delicate appearance.

Dianthus gizellae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
