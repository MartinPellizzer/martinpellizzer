Diospyros odoratissima var. oblonga

Diospyros odoratissima var. oblonga, commonly known as the fragrant persimmon, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The fruit is a hairy, elongated berry that ripens to a deep orange-red color. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like the ebony tree.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
