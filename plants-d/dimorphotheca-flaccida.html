Dimorphotheca flaccida

Dimorphotheca flaccida, commonly known as the drooping oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers with a central disk, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, which can help in identifying it. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Dimorphotheca flaccida is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
