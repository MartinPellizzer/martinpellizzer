Didymocarpus griffithii

Didymocarpus griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and greyish.

Didymocarpus griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
