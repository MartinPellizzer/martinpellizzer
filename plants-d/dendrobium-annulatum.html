Dendrobium annulatum

Dendrobium annulatum, commonly known as the ringed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a tall, cane-like stem with alternating nodes and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive ring-like pattern on the petals.

Dendrobium annulatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
