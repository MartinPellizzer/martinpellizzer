Dianthus neilreichii

Dianthus neilreichii, commonly known as the Neilreich's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dianthus neilreichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
