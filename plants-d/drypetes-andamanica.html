Drypetes andamanica

Drypetes andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
