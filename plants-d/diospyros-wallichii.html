Diospyros wallichii

Diospyros wallichii, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a food source.
