Delphinium elatum subsp. austriacum

Delphinium elatum subsp.

austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
