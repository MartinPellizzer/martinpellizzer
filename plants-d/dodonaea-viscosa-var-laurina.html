Dodonaea viscosa var. laurina

Dodonaea viscosa var. laurina, commonly known as the silver dollar tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are silvery-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, red berries. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
