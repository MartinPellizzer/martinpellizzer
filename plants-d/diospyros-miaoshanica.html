Diospyros miaoshanica

Diospyros miaoshanica, commonly known as the Miaoshan persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that ripens in the fall and is edible when fully mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
