Dianthus simonkaianus

Dianthus simonkaianus, commonly known as the Simonkaian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are composed of five petals with a distinctive central gland. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Dianthus simonkaianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its ability to add color to gardens in cooler climates.
