Diospyros ghatensis

Diospyros ghatensis, commonly known as the Ghat ebony, is a tropical tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in evergreen forests.

The species is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
