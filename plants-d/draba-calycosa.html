Draba calycosa

Draba calycosa, commonly known as the Sierra foothill whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This low-growing perennial has basal leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Its compact size and early blooming habit make it an important part of the local flora in its native habitat.
