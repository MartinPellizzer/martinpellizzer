Dipteracanthus glandulosopunctatus

Dipteracanthus glandulosopunctatus, commonly known as the glandular-punctate dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically exhibits small, clustered flowers with distinctive glandular structures on its petals and sepals. The leaves are usually alternate and may have a textured surface with punctate markings. This species is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to its ecological niche, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
