Draba pyriformis

Draba pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Draba pyriformis is typically found in moist, well-drained habitats and is adapted to cold climates.
