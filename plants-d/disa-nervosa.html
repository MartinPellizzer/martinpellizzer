Disa nervosa

Disa nervosa, commonly known as the pink disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high sunlight exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often short and erect.

Disa nervosa is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
