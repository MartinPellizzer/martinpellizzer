Dracophyllum virgatum

Dracophyllum virgatum, commonly known as the silver mallee, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring.

Dracophyllum virgatum is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathlands.
