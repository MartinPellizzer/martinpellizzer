Diospyros montana f. cordifolia

Diospyros montana f. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf persimmon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, which is appreciated in local cuisines.
