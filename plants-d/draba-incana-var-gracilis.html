Draba incana var. gracilis

Draba incana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial with basal rosettes of narrow, bluish-gray leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Draba incana var. gracilis is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
