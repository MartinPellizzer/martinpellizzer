Diplophyllum crista-galli

Diplophyllum crista-galli, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive crown-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility.
