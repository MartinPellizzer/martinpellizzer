Dolia laxa

Dolia laxa, commonly known as the slender lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Dolia laxa prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
