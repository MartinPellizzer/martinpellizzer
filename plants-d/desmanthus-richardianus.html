Desmanthus richardianus

Desmanthus richardianus, commonly known as the Richard's burrlegume, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Desmanthus richardianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in wildlife habitats.
