Distichlis volckmannii

Distichlis volckmannii, commonly known as saltgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt. This species thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Distichlis volckmannii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
