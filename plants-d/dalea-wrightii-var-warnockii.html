Dalea wrightii var. warnockii

Dalea wrightii var. warnockii, commonly known as Wright's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Its foliage consists of alternate, trifoliate leaves, contributing to its distinctive appearance in its native habitat.
