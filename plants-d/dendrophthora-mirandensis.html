Dendrophthora mirandensis

Dendrophthora mirandensis, commonly known as the Mirandese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on specific host trees, primarily in the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant's leaves are usually small, leathery, and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Dendrophthora mirandensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
