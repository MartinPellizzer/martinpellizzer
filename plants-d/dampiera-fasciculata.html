Dampiera fasciculata

Dampiera fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered dampiera, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Dampiera fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
