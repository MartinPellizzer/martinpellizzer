Drypetes ugandensis

Drypetes ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
