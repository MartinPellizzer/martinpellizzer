Dendrobium junctilobum

Dendrobium junctilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cylindrical pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent petals and sepals. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium junctilobum thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and bright, indirect light.
