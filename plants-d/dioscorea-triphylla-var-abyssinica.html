Dioscorea triphylla var. abyssinica

Dioscorea triphylla var. abyssinica, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bean-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its tubers are used as a food source and have been traditionally cultivated by local communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
