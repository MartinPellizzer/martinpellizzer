Dicheranthus

Dicheranthus, commonly known as the common dicerandra, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Dicheranthus species are native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

These plants are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
