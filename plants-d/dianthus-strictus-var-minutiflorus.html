Dianthus strictus var. minutiflorus

Dianthus strictus var. minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and a low-growing habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in garden settings.
