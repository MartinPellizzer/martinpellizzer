Digitalis sceptrum

Digitalis sceptrum, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its cardiac glycosides, which have medicinal uses but are also toxic.

Digitalis sceptrum thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
