Dioclea reflexa var. glabrescens

Dioclea reflexa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
