Didymoplexis ornata

Didymoplexis ornata, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that resemble flames, which bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Didymoplexis ornata make it a visually captivating and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
