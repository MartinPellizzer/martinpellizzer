Daboecia cantabrica

Daboecia cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian heather, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are needle-like and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature.

Daboecia cantabrica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
