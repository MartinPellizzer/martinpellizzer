Dortmanna appendiculata

Dortmanna appendiculata, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 30 centimeters in height, characterized by its slender, erect stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are covered with fine hairs and have a slightly serrated margin, while the stems are often reddish in color.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
