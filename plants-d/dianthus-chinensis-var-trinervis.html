Dianthus chinensis var. trinervis

Dianthus chinensis var. trinervis, commonly known as the Chinese carnation, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing clusters of small, rounded flowers with prominent white or pink centers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.
