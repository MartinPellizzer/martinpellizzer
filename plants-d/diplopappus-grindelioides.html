Diplopappus grindelioides

Diplopappus grindelioides, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
