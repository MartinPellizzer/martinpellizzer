Davallia adiantifolia

Davallia adiantifolia, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often described as resembling the wings of a bird. The plant has a unique ability to store water in its rhizomes, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
