Derris benthamii var. wightii

Derris benthamii var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's derris, is a climbing shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is notable for containing rotenone, a natural insecticide, which makes it significant in traditional agriculture.
