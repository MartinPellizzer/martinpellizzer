Dianthus crinitus subsp. crinitus

Dianthus crinitus subsp.

crinitus, commonly known as the curly top campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its pink or red flowers with fringed petals that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
