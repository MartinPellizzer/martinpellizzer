Disa tripetaloides subsp. aurata

Disa tripetaloides subsp.

aurata, commonly known as the golden disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa. This subspecies is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and contrasting colors.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette that helps conserve moisture.

The golden disa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
