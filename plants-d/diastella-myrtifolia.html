Diastella myrtifolia

Diastella myrtifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, red-pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has a thick, woody trunk and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
