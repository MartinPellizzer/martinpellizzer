Dombeya lucida subsp. megaphylla

Dombeya lucida subsp.

megaphylla, commonly known as the large-leaf dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shrubland or forest edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
