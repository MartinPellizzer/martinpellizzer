Didymocarpus tomentosus var. lanuginosus

Didymocarpus tomentosus var. lanuginosus, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, orange to red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant produces flowers with five petals and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and fruit.
