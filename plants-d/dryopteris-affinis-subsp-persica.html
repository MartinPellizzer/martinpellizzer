Dryopteris affinis subsp. persica

Dryopteris affinis subsp.

persica, commonly known as the Persian maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are relatively short, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
