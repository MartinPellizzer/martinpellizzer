Dudleya gatesii

Dudleya gatesii, commonly known as the Gates Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces upright flowering stalks that bear clusters of star-shaped flowers, usually in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
