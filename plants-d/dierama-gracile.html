Dierama gracile

Dierama gracile, commonly known as the slender dierama, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family, which includes the famous genera like Xanthorrhoea and Leucospermum. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces striking, cone-shaped inflorescences with bright yellow to orange flowers. It is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Dierama gracile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
