Draba pakistanica

Draba pakistanica, commonly known as the Pakistan mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of tiny white or yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found at high altitudes.

Draba pakistanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
