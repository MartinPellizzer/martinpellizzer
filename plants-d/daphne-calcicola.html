Daphne calcicola

Daphne calcicola, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. The plant produces showy clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing ornamental value throughout the year.

Daphne calcicola is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
