Disperis paludosa

Disperis paludosa, commonly known as the marsh disperis, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in marshy or boggy areas, often forming dense clumps. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize moist environments effectively.

Disperis paludosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
