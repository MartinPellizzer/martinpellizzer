Dalucum ciliatum

Dalucum ciliatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Dalucum ciliatum is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
