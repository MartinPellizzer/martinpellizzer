Dalbergia inundata

Dalbergia inundata, commonly known as the water mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to wetland environments, often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellow or green hues, while its fruits are legumes that split open to release seeds.

The wood of Dalbergia inundata is durable and has been used for various traditional purposes.
