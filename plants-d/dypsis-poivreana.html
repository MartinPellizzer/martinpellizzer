Dypsis poivreana

Dypsis poivreana, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a striking succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a short, thick trunk covered in a fibrous, leafless sheath, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are followed by bright orange berries that contain the seeds.

Dypsis poivreana is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
