Dimorphotheca viscosa

Dimorphotheca viscosa, commonly known as the sticky daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Dimorphotheca viscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
