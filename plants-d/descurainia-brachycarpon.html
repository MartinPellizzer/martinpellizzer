Descurainia brachycarpon

Descurainia brachycarpon, commonly known as the shortpoded tansy, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly pubescent surface.

The fruit is a short, cylindrical pod that dehisces to release the seeds.
