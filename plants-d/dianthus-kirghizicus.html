Dianthus kirghizicus

Dianthus kirghizicus, commonly known as the Kirghiz carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Dianthus kirghizicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
