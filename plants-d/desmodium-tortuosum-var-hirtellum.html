Desmodium tortuosum var. hirtellum

Desmodium tortuosum var. hirtellum, commonly known as hairy serpentine bean, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and slender, serpentine-shaped pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks. Its flowering stems are erect, bearing small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
