Dianthus furcatus subsp. lereschii

Dianthus furcatus subsp.

lereschii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre pinks, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
