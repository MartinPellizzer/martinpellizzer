Dyckia augustae

Dyckia augustae, commonly known as the golden dyckia, is a species of grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. Dyckia augustae produces striking, spikelike inflorescences with vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
