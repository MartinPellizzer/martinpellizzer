Dianthus juniperinus subsp. pulviniformis

Dianthus juniperinus subsp.

pulviniformis, commonly known as the cushion dianthus, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with prominent, showy petals.

The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is commonly found in alpine or arctic environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
