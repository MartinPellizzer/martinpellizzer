Dielytra formosa

Dielytra formosa, commonly known as the golden-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with distinctive golden-colored bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Dielytra formosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
