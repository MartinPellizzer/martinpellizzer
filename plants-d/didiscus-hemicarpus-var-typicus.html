Didiscus hemicarpus var. typicus

Didiscus hemicarpus var. typicus, commonly known as the roundleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
