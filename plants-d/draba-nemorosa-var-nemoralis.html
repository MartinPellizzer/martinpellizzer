Draba nemorosa var. nemoralis

Draba nemorosa var. nemoralis, commonly known as the woodland mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its habitat preference and delicate appearance make it a characteristic species of temperate forest understories.
