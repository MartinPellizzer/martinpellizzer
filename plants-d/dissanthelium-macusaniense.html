Dissanthelium macusaniense

Dissanthelium macusaniense, commonly known as the Andes hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.
