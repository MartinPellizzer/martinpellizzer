Dianthus multipunctatus var. subenervis

Dianthus multipunctatus var. subenervis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
