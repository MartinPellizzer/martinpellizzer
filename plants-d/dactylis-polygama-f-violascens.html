Dactylis polygama f. violascens

Dactylis polygama f. violascens, commonly known as the violet clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically a deep violet or purple color. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
