Dondia multiflora

Dondia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered dondia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces numerous small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Dondia multiflora typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
