Delphinium speciosum var. pyramidale

Delphinium speciosum var. pyramidale, commonly known as the Pyramidal Larkspur, is a striking perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, bearing dense clusters of blue to purple flowers. The foliage consists of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. This variety is valued for its showy blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.
