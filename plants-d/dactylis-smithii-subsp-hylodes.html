Dactylis smithii subsp. hylodes

Dactylis smithii subsp.

hylodes, commonly known as the hairy grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-ranked spikelets. Dactylis smithii subsp.

hylodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its distinctive appearance.
