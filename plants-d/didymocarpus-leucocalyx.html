Didymocarpus leucocalyx

Didymocarpus leucocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered satinash, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
