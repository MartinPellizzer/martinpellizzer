Dactylorhiza maculata var. podesta

Dactylorhiza maculata var. podesta, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a slender, upright stem with oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

This variety is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
