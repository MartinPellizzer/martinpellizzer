Draba cana

Draba cana, commonly known as the yellow winter cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks. Draba cana has basal leaves that are pinnately divided, and it produces yellow flowers in the early spring.

It is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
