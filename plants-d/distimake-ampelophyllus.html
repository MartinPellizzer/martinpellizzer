Distimake ampelophyllus

Distimake ampelophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf ivy, is a perennial climbing plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its rapid growth rate makes it suitable for vertical gardening and as a ground cover.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
