Didymocarpus pinnatifidus

Didymocarpus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with narrowly elliptical leaflets and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically yellow when mature, and contains a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
