Deutzia silvestrii

Deutzia silvestrii, commonly known as the Chinese deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching branches with small, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on short pedicels. Its foliage turns reddish in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Deutzia silvestrii is often cultivated for its attractive flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
