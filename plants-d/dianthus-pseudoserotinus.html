Dianthus pseudoserotinus

Dianthus pseudoserotinus, commonly known as the purple campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Dianthus pseudoserotinus is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
