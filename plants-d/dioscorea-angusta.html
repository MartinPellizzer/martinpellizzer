Dioscorea angusta

Dioscorea angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
