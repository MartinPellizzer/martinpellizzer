Dianthus pallidiflorus

Dianthus pallidiflorus, commonly known as the paleflower carnation, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of pink to white flowers with five petals. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Dianthus pallidiflorus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
