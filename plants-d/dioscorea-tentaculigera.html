Dioscorea tentaculigera

Dioscorea tentaculigera, commonly known as the horned yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers and cylindrical, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are edible but require careful preparation to avoid toxicity.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and cultural significance in various regions.
