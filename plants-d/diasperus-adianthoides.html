Diasperus adianthoides

Diasperus adianthoides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features delicate, feather-like fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.
