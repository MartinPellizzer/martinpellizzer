Dacryodes crassipes

Dacryodes crassipes, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.
