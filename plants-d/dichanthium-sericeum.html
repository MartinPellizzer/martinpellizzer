Dichanthium sericeum

Dichanthium sericeum, commonly known as silky grass or silky finger grass, is a grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Dichanthium sericeum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
