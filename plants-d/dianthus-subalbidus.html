Dianthus subalbidus

Dianthus subalbidus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Dianthus subalbidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
