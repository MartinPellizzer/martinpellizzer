Diospyros ferrea var. sandwicensis

Diospyros ferrea var. sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian persimmon, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, and it produces a fruit that is typically orange to red in color when ripe.

The botanical characteristics of this plant make it well adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Hawaii.
