Dianthus costei

Dianthus costei, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered blooms that attract pollinators.

Dianthus costei is often found in alpine and coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
