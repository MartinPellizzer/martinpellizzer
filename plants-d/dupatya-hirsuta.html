Dupatya hirsuta

Dupatya hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and strikingly colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
