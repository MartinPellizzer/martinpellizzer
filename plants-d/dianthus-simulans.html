Dianthus simulans

Dianthus simulans, commonly known as the cushion pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit, forming dense mats of foliage that resemble a cushion. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.

Dianthus simulans is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to harsh weather conditions.
