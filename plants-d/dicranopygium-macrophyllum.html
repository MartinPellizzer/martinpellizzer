Dicranopygium macrophyllum

Dicranopygium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest members of the clubmoss family. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has distinctive strobili, or cones, that contain spores. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Dicranopygium macrophyllum plays an important ecological role in forest understories by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
