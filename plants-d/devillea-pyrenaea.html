Devillea pyrenaea

Devillea pyrenaea, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Devillea pyrenaea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
