Dianthus basuticus subsp. basuticus

Dianthus basuticus subsp.

basuticus, commonly known as the Basutus carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers.

The plant produces numerous stamens that give it a fluffy appearance, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
