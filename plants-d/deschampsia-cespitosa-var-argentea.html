Deschampsia cespitosa var. argentea

Deschampsia cespitosa var. argentea, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a variant of the tufted hairgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, wiry stems. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, silvery hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
