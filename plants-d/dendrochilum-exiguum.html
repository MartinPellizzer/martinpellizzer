Dendrochilum exiguum

Dendrochilum exiguum, commonly known as the slender tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in well-drained, organic soil.

Dendrochilum exiguum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
