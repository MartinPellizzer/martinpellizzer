Delphinium dubium

Delphinium dubium, commonly known as the slender larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the Delphinium genus.

The plant is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
