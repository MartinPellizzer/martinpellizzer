Dichaea willdenowiana

Dichaea willdenowiana, commonly known as the Willdenow's dichaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of bromeliads. Its flowering stems produce small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
