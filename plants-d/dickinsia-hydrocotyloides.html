Dickinsia hydrocotyloides

Dickinsia hydrocotyloides, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
