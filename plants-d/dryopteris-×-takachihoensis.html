Dryopteris × takachihoensis

Dryopteris × takachihoensis, commonly known as the Takachiho hachijo fern, is a hybrid evergreen fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.
