Diplacus arachnoideus

Diplacus arachnoideus, commonly known as the spider flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, spider-like bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink to red blossoms that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a distinctive addition to native plant collections.
