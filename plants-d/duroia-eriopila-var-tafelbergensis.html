Duroia eriopila var. tafelbergensis

Duroia eriopila var. tafelbergensis, commonly known as the Tafelberg wax palm, is a variant of the wax palm species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, glossy, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
