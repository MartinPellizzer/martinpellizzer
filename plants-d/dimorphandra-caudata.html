Dimorphandra caudata

Dimorphandra caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, elongated pods. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules, enhancing soil fertility. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry forest habitats.
