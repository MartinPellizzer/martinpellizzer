Dalbergia melanocardium

Dalbergia melanocardium, commonly known as African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that ranges from black to dark brown in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is valued for its high-quality timber, which is used in furniture, musical instruments, and carving.
