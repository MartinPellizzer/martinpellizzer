Dilatris ixioides

Dilatris ixioides, commonly known as the waxplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its striking, waxy, pink or red bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered within the bracts, which are often the main ornamental feature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Dilatris ixioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
