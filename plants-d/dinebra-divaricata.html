Dinebra divaricata

Dinebra divaricata, commonly known as the zigzagged woodbine, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a woody stem with a zigzagging pattern, which gives it its common name. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
