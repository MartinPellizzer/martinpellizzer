Digitalis brachyantha

Digitalis brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spires of hooded flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are tubular with distinctive spurred tips.

Digitalis brachyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its cardiac glycosides.
