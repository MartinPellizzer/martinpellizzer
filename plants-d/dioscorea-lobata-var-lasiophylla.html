Dioscorea lobata var. lasiophylla

Dioscorea lobata var. lasiophylla, commonly known as the velvet leaf yam, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its growth habit involves scrambling through vegetation, often reaching heights of several meters.

The varietal name "lasiophylla" refers to its velvety, hairy foliage, which distinguishes it from other varieties of Dioscorea.
