Dianthus cyathophorus subsp. minae

Dianthus cyathophorus subsp.

minae, commonly known as the Minae carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers known as cymes, which are borne on short, hairy stems. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
