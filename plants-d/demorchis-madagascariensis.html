Demorchis madagascariensis

Demorchis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid rainforests of the island. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
