Dicliptera maculata subsp. hirta

Dicliptera maculata subsp.

hirta, commonly known as the hairy dicliptera, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically narrow and elongated.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often adorned with bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
