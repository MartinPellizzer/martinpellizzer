Dipsacus walkeri

Dipsacus walkeri, commonly known as the common teasel, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique structure, with stiff, hollow bracts, has historically been used in the textile industry for raising wool.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
