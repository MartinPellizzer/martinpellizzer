Diasperus glaziovii

Diasperus glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in spike-like clusters during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Diasperus glaziovii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
