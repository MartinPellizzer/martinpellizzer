Diospyros monosperma

Diospyros monosperma, commonly known as the African date palm, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, palm-like leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its fruits are rich in nutrients and are often consumed fresh or processed into beverages.

Diospyros monosperma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various African cultures.
