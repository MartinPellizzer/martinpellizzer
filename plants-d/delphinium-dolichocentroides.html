Delphinium dolichocentroides

Delphinium dolichocentroides, commonly known as the long-lobed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, serrated leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Delphinium dolichocentroides is also noted for its toxicity, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to both animals and humans.
