Daphne helvetica

Daphne helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the Alps.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

Daphne helvetica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and winter interest.
