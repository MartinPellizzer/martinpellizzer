Dracophyllum latifolium

Dracophyllum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and open forests.

Dracophyllum latifolium is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
