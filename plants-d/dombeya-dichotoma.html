Dombeya dichotoma

Dombeya dichotoma, commonly known as the mountain dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dombeya dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
