Diospyros bullata

Diospyros bullata, commonly known as the rock apple or wild persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on rocky or limestone soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
