Dodonaea scabra

Dodonaea scabra, commonly known as the rough hopbush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its greyish, rough-textured bark and small, oval-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland areas.

Dodonaea scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
