Draba cheirifolia

Draba cheirifolia, commonly known as the Moroccan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Draba cheirifolia is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
