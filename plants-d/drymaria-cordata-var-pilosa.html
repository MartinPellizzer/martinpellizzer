Drymaria cordata var. pilosa

Drymaria cordata var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy heartleaf, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Americas.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland areas.

Its hairy foliage and delicate flowers make it an attractive addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
