Dillenia hirta

Dillenia hirta, commonly known as the hairy dillenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Dillenia hirta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
