Dianthus gaditanus

Dianthus gaditanus, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its compact, evergreen foliage. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus gaditanus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
