Dendrochilum purpureum

Dendrochilum purpureum, commonly known as the purple-lipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip that has a contrasting color. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence is often branched, bearing multiple flowers that bloom sequentially.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
