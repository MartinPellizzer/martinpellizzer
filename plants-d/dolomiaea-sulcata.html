Dolomiaea sulcata

Dolomiaea sulcata, commonly known as the African violet, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

Dolomiaea sulcata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
