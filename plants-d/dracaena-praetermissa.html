Dracaena praetermissa

Dracaena praetermissa, commonly known as the dragon tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Dracaena praetermissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
