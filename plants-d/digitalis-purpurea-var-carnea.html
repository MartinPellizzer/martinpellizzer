Digitalis purpurea var. carnea

Digitalis purpurea var. carnea, commonly known as the common foxglove, is a biennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces a showy display in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

The plant is also known for containing cardiac glycosides, which have been used in medicine.
