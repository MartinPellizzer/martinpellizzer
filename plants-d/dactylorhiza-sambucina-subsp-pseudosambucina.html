Dactylorhiza sambucina subsp. pseudosambucina

Dactylorhiza sambucina subsp.

pseudosambucina, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of purple or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and peat bogs. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of flowers with three to six blooms.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
