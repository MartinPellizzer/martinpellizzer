Dichanthium panchganiense

Dichanthium panchganiense, commonly known as the Panchgani grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
