Drymonia multiflora

Drymonia multiflora, commonly known as the mountain huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Drymonia multiflora thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
