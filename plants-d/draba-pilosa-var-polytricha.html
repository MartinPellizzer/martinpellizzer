Draba pilosa var. polytricha

Draba pilosa var. polytricha, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, rounded leaves. The species produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or arctic regions.

This plant is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
