Dactylorhiza × senayi nothosubsp. townsendiana

Dactylorhiza × senayi nothosubsp.

townsendiana, commonly known as the Townsend's marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale pink or white flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, calcareous soils. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
