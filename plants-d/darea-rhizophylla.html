Darea rhizophylla

Darea rhizophylla, commonly known as the rock fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized adaptations to rocky and arid environments.

It possesses rhizomes that anchor it firmly in crevices, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor substrates. The plant's fronds are typically small and leathery, reducing water loss in harsh conditions. Its reproductive structures are concealed within the rock crevices, contributing to its elusive nature.

Darea rhizophylla plays a vital role in stabilizing rocky soils and supporting microhabitats in its native range.
