Dianthus caesius var. nanus

Dianthus caesius var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces vibrant, double-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in early summer. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its dense, showy blooms and compact growth habit.
