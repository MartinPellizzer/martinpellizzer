Disa capricornis

Disa capricornis, commonly known as the Capricorn orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting dark markings. The plant grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in rocky or sandy soils in grassland habitats. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is known for its ability to thrive in full sun.

Disa capricornis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
