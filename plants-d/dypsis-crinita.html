Dypsis crinita

Dypsis crinita, commonly known as the areca palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows up to 10 meters in height and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into dark blue berries.

Dypsis crinita is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to indoor environments.
