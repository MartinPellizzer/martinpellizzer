Dipsacus palustris

Dipsacus palustris, commonly known as the marsh teasel, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia.

It features tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from June to August. Its seeds are enclosed in a spiny bur that aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animals.

Dipsacus palustris is often found in damp meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.
