Dioscorea forbesii

Dioscorea forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized for food and medicinal purposes by indigenous communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential due to its attractive foliage and climbing habit.
