Dysosmia dichotoma

Dysosmia dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that branch into two distinct shoots, giving it its scientific name. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in the summer. Dysosmia dichotoma is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
