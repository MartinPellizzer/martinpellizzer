Diplotaxis praecox var. hiemalis

Diplotaxis praecox var. hiemalis, commonly known as winter dwarf cress, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are prostrate, often lying close to the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
