Dioscorea palopoensis

Dioscorea palopoensis, commonly known as the Palopo yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of D. palopoensis make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
