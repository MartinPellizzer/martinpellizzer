Dampiera

Dampiera, commonly known as the "Australian bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It typically features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive red or yellow throat. The plants are herbaceous, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Dampiera species are often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

They are popular in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
