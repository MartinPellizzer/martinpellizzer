Doronicum helveticum

Doronicum helveticum, commonly known as the Swiss buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland areas, preferring well-drained soil. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like the buttercup and celandine.

Doronicum helveticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
