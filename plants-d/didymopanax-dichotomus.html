Didymopanax dichotomus

Didymopanax dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves that are divided into two equal parts, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Didymopanax dichotomus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
