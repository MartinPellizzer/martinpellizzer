Dioscorea acanthogene

Dioscorea acanthogene, commonly known as the spiny yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are capsules that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used as a food source in some regions.
