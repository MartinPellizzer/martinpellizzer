Dinoseris salicifolia var. normalis

Dinoseris salicifolia var. normalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a taproot system and produces a capsule-like fruit containing numerous small seeds.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.
