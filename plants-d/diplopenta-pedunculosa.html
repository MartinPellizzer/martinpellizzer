Diplopenta pedunculosa

Diplopenta pedunculosa, commonly known as the double-five-stemmed plant, is a unique species characterized by its intricate floral structure.

It features multiple peduncles, or flower stalks, that branch out in a distinctive double-five pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Due to its rare and specialized morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying plant evolution and adaptation.
