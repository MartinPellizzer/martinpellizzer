Delphinium chrysotrichum var. tsarongense

Delphinium chrysotrichum var. tsarongense, commonly known as the Tsarong Blue Poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to high-altitude meadows. The plant has tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spikelike clusters, and it is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
