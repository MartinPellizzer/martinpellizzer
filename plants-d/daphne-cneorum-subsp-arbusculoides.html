Daphne cneorum subsp. arbusculoides

Daphne cneorum subsp.

arbusculoides, commonly known as the creeping daphne, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring.

The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a compact, spreading growth habit. It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and hardiness.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling.
