Dendrobium excavatum var. buruense

Dendrobium excavatum var. buruense, commonly known as the Buru Dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, wiry stems that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive red lip. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
