Danthonia intermedia subsp. intermedia

Danthonia intermedia subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect culms that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a prominent midrib and a rough texture.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.
