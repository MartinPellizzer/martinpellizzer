Dressleria eburnea

Dressleria eburnea, commonly known as the silver-leafed orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are narrow and often covered in a silvery or pale waxy coating, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dressleria eburnea is prized for its elegant appearance and is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
