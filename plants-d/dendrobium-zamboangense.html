Dendrobium zamboangense

Dendrobium zamboangense, commonly known as the Zamboanga orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces aerial roots for attachment.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
