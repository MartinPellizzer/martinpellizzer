Digitaria radicosa subsp. blepharophora

Digitaria radicosa subsp.

blepharophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush, with bracts that are narrow and often reflexed.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct sheath at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
