Diervilla versicolor

Diervilla versicolor, commonly known as the variable bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that range in color from dark green to reddish in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are small, dark red, and edible, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

Diervilla versicolor thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
