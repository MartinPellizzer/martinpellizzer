Diplazium lobulosum

Diplazium lobulosum, commonly known as the lobed shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate fronds that are divided into small, lobed segments, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, hairy layer, which helps in moisture retention.

Diplazium lobulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady garden settings.
