Digitaria somalensis

Digitaria somalensis, commonly known as the Somali digitaria, is a grass species native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Digitaria somalensis is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low rainfall.
