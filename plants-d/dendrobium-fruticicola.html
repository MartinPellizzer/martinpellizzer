Dendrobium fruticicola

Dendrobium fruticicola, commonly known as the shrub orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with clustered pseudobulbs and linear leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, yellow, or green.

Dendrobium fruticicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
