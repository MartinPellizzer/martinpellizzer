Dillwynia ericifolia var. peduncularis

Dillwynia ericifolia var. peduncularis, commonly known as the yellow dillwynia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in heathland or woodland habitats. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often found in coastal or inland regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
