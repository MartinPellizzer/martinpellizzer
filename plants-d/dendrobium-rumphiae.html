Dendrobium rumphiae

Dendrobium rumphiae, commonly known as the Rumphius orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red throat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Dendrobium rumphiae thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
