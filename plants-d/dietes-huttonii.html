Dietes huttonii

Dietes huttonii, commonly known as the Hutton's lily, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, often purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in many regions with a Mediterranean climate.
