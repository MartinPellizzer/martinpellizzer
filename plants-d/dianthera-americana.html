Dianthera americana

Dianthera americana, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthera americana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
