Digitalis lanata

Digitalis lanata, commonly known as the downy foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, erect stems covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in a loose raceme. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinctive wavy margin.

Digitalis lanata is known for its medicinal properties, containing cardiac glycosides used in the treatment of heart conditions.
