Dichrostachys dehiscens

Dichrostachys dehiscens, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, thorned stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, dehiscent pods that release seeds when mature. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
