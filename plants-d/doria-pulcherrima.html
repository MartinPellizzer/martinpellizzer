Doria pulcherrima

Doria pulcherrima, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, pink bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Doria pulcherrima is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
