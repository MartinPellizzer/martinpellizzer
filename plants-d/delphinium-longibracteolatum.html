Delphinium longibracteolatum

Delphinium longibracteolatum, commonly known as the long-bract delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in racemes, which are typically borne on long, leafy stalks. Its basal leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while the upper leaves are smaller and more lanceolate.

Delphinium longibracteolatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a notable species in alpine and subalpine habitats.
