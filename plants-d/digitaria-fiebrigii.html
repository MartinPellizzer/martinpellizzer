Digitaria fiebrigii

Digitaria fiebrigii, commonly known as the fevergrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, and it is adapted to thrive in dry conditions.

Digitaria fiebrigii is often used in ecological studies due to its resilience and role in soil stabilization.
