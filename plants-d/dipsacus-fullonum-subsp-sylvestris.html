Dipsacus fullonum subsp. sylvestris

Dipsacus fullonum subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the wood betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems that are often hairy and bear clusters of spiny, bract-like structures at the base of its flowering spikes. Its flowers are small, purple to pinkish, and arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
