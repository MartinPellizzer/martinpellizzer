Diospyros ubaita

Diospyros ubaita, commonly known as the Colombian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Andes region of Colombia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to red berries that ripen in autumn. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, astringent flavor, and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
