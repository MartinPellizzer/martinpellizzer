Daphne brigantiaca

Daphne brigantiaca, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically eaten by birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
