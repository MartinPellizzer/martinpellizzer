Delphinium leonardii

Delphinium leonardii, commonly known as Leonard's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other familiar genera like buttercups and aquilegias. Its leaves are typically deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.
