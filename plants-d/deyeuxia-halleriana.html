Deyeuxia halleriana

Deyeuxia halleriana, commonly known as the Haller's grass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Deyeuxia halleriana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
