Diplolophium diplolophioides

Diplolophium diplolophioides, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
