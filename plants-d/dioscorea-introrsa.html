Dioscorea introrsa

Dioscorea introrsa, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and develops tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.

The tubers are also sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value.
