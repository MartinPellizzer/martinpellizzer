Dioscorea silvestris

Dioscorea silvestris, commonly known as wild yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are its primary economic and medicinal part. These tubers are rich in starch and have been used traditionally for their nutritional and medicinal properties.

Dioscorea silvestris is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
