Distichorchis maraiparensis

Distichorchis maraiparensis, commonly known as the Maraiparensis orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, specifically found in Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with two rows of petals and sepals, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance.

Distichorchis maraiparensis is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
