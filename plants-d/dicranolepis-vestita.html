Dicranolepis vestita

Dicranolepis vestita, commonly known as the velvet moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and dark green, velvety appearance. The plant has a complex structure with leaf-like structures that are typically arranged in two ranks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks or rocky surfaces.

Dicranolepis vestita plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
