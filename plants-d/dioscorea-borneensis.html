Dioscorea borneensis

Dioscorea borneensis, commonly known as Bornean yam, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and cylindrical, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
