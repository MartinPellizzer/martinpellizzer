Digitaria kasamaensis

Digitaria kasamaensis, commonly known as Kasama grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often pubescent. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color.

Digitaria kasamaensis is valued for its use in livestock feed and for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
