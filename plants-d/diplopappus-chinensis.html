Diplopappus chinensis

Diplopappus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese burrweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, spiky fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal, featuring a pappus structure that aids in seed distribution. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a rough texture.

Diplopappus chinensis is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
