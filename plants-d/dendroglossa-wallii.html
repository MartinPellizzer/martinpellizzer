Dendroglossa wallii

Dendroglossa wallii, commonly known as the Wallace's tree frog, is a species of tree frog native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive green coloration and prominent, warty skin texture. The plant aspect of this species refers to its habitat in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It typically resides in the canopy layers of trees, utilizing leaves and branches for shelter and breeding.

The frog's life cycle is closely tied to the availability of water and vegetation, making it an important indicator of ecosystem health.
