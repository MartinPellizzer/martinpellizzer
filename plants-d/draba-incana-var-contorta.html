Draba incana var. contorta

Draba incana var. contorta, commonly known as the twisted whitlow grass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

Native to alpine and arctic regions, it thrives in rocky and sandy soils.
