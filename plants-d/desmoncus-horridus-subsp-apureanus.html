Desmoncus horridus subsp. apureanus

Desmoncus horridus subsp.

apureanus, commonly known as the Apurean burrweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia. It is characterized by its rough, bristly stems and leaves, which are often covered in stiff hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, disturbed habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
