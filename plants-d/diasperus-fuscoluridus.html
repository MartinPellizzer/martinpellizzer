Diasperus fuscoluridus

Diasperus fuscoluridus, commonly known as the dark yellow dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's ability to store water in its leaves makes it well adapted to drought conditions.
