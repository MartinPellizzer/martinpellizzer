Duranta vestita var. glabrescens

Duranta vestita var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silver duranta, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-green, velvety foliage and clusters of tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
