Delphinium pulcherrimum

Delphinium pulcherrimum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its flowers are typically borne in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Delphinium pulcherrimum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
