Dissotis glauca

Dissotis glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are borne in dense, terminal inflorescences, giving it a mist-like appearance.

Dissotis glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
