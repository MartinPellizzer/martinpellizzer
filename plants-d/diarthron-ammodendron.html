Diarthron ammodendron

Diarthron ammodendron, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Diarthron ammodendron is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
