Dupatya conduplicata

Dupatya conduplicata, commonly known as the double-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into two overlapping parts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Dupatya conduplicata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
