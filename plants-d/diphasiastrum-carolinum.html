Diphasiastrum carolinum

The plant produces two types of spore-bearing structures—microsporangia and megasporangia—hence its scientific name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is an important component of certain forest ecosystems.
