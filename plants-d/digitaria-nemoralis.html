Digitaria nemoralis

Digitaria nemoralis, commonly known as the common bermuda grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Digitaria nemoralis is known for its adaptability and resilience, making it a common weed in many regions.
