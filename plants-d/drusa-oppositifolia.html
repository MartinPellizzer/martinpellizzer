Drusa oppositifolia

Drusa oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved drusa, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. The plant is notable for its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves are paired on opposite sides of the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in dense inflorescences that add to its visual interest.

Drusa oppositifolia also exhibits a robust root system, enabling it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand periods of drought.
