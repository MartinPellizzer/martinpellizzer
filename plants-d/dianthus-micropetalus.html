Dianthus micropetalus

Dianthus micropetalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like carnations and pinks.

Dianthus micropetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
