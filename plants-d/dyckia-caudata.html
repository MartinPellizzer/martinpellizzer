Dyckia caudata

Dyckia caudata, commonly known as the curly-tail bromeliad, is a striking ornamental plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that curl inward, forming a central rosette. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that can range from red to orange, often appearing in late spring or early summer. Dyckia caudata is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among bromeliad enthusiasts.
