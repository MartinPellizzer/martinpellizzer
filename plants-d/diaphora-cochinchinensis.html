Diaphora cochinchinensis

Diaphora cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It is often found in mountainous regions of China, Vietnam, and Cambodia.
