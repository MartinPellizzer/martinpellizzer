Discocactus paranaensis

Discocactus paranaensis, commonly known as the Paranaensis Discocactus, is a succulent native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stem. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Discocactus paranaensis is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for succulent collectors.
