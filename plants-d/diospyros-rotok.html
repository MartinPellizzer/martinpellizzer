Diospyros rotok

Diospyros rotok, commonly known as the Kauri persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to red berries in autumn. The fruit is edible when fully ripe, with a sweet, astringent flavor.

It plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's native forests.
