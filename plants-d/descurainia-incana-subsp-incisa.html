Descurainia incana subsp. incisa

Descurainia incana subsp.

incisa, commonly known as the slender wall-rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
