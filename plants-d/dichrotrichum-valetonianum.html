Dichrotrichum valetonianum

Dichrotrichum valetonianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its hairy, often lobed leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Dichrotrichum valetonianum is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed.
