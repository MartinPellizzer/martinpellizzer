Dioscorea therezopolensis var. latifolia

Dioscorea therezopolensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The varietal form, latifolia, is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other varieties of Dioscorea therezopolensis.
