Dodonaea dioica

Dodonaea dioica, commonly known as the velvetleaf hopseed, is a deciduous shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, red or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, hard pods containing seeds. The leaves are typically opposite, with a rough, velvety texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
