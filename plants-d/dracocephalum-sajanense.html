Dracocephalum sajanense

Dracocephalum sajanense, commonly known as Sajan mountain hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dracocephalum sajanense is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
