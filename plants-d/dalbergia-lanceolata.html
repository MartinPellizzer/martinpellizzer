Dalbergia lanceolata

Dalbergia lanceolata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing flat, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also known for its ecological and economic significance in its native habitats.
