Dactylorhiza cambrensis

Dactylorhiza cambrensis, commonly known as the Welsh marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in damp, peaty habitats such as bogs and marshes. The plant has a slender, erect stem with several elliptical leaves. Its flowers are pale yellow to greenish-yellow, arranged in a loose spike.

Dactylorhiza cambrensis is a protected species in the UK due to its declining population and specific habitat requirements.
