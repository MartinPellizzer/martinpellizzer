Ducrosia

Ducrosia, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Ducrosia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators.
