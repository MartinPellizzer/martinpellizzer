Diospyros erudita

Diospyros erudita, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, though it may have astringent properties when unripe, and is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
