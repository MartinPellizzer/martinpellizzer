Dianthus carthusianorum var. saxigenus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. saxigenus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils, often on limestone or sandstone, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are sturdy, often forming a low, spreading mat.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging, rocky conditions.
