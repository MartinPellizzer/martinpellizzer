Daphne tenuiflora

Daphne tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and winter interest.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling.
