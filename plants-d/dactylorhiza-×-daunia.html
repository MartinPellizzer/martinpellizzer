Dactylorhiza × daunia

Dactylorhiza × daunia, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish or brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is a perennial herb that produces a single, erect stem bearing a cluster of flowers.

Dactylorhiza × daunia is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is often found in association with other orchid species.
