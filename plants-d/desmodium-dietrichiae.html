Desmodium dietrichiae

Desmodium dietrichiae, commonly known as the purple desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. Desmodium dietrichiae is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
