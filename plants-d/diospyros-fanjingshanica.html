Diospyros fanjingshanica

Diospyros fanjingshanica, commonly known as the Fanjingshan persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in the Fanjingshan area of Guizhou Province. The plant is characterized by its broad leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red fruits. The fruit is typically edible, with a sweet and sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
