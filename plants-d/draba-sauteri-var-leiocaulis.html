Draba sauteri var. leiocaulis

Draba sauteri var. leiocaulis, commonly known as the smoothstemed whitlowgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, alpine environments and has a compact rosette of basal leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually smooth and glabrous, giving it its common name.

Draba sauteri var. leiocaulis is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high elevations where it can withstand harsh weather conditions.
