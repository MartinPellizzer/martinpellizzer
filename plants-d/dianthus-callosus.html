Dianthus callosus

Dianthus callosus, commonly known as the fringed pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or magenta flowers with fringed petals that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white or gray fuzz.

Dianthus callosus is a perennial species that produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer.
