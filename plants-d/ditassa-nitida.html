Ditassa nitida

Ditassa nitida, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, white or pale green, often arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals.

Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
