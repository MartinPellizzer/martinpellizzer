Dolichos filiformis

Dolichos filiformis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
