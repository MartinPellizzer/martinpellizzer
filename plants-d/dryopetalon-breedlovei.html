Dryopetalon breedlovei

Dryopetalon breedlovei, commonly known as the Sierra Madre orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found primarily in the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its arid or semi-arid habitat. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
