Diospyros oubatchensis

Diospyros oubatchensis, commonly known as the Oubatch's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that ripens in late summer.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
