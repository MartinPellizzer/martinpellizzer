Dioscorea polystachya f. tsukune

Dioscorea polystachya f. tsukune, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a tuberous plant belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has compound leaves with long petioles. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers, followed by the development of its valuable tuberous roots.

This variety is valued not only for its culinary use but also for its traditional medicinal properties.
