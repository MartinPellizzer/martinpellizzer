Danthoniopsis intermedia

Danthoniopsis intermedia, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its ornate foliage.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is adapted to thrive in temperate to subtropical climates.
