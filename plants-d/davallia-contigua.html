Davallia contigua

Davallia contigua, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bioluminescent in the dark. The plant thrives in shady, humid environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Davallia contigua is a member of the family Davalliaceae and is known for its ability to tolerate low light conditions.
