Dianthus vaginatus

Dianthus vaginatus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, often reddish stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composed of five petals that are usually notched at the base, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Dianthus vaginatus is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
