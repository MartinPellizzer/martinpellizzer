Drosera macrantha var. stricticaulis

Drosera macrantha var. stricticaulis, commonly known as the tall purple sundew, is a variant of the purple sundew characterized by its upright growth habit.

It is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in wet, boggy environments. The plant features long, slender stalks with dense clusters of small, sticky, glandular hairs that trap insects. Its flowers are typically purple or reddish, appearing in terminal clusters, and it blooms during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique carnivorous behavior, making it a popular choice for specialized plant collections.
