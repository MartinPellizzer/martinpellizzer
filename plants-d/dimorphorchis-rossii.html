Dimorphorchis rossii

Dimorphorchis rossii, commonly known as the Ross's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters, with a unique coloration that ranges from white to pale pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and often grow in dense mats, helping to anchor the plant in moist, well-drained soil.

Dimorphorchis rossii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
