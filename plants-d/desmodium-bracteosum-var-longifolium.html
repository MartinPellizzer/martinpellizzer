Desmodium bracteosum var. longifolium

Desmodium bracteosum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved desmodium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has a fibrous root system and is capable of fixing atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
