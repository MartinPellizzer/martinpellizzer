Daiswa bockiana

Daiswa bockiana, commonly known as the Bockiana daisies, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Daiswa bockiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
