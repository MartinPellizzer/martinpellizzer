Diospyros alvarezii

Diospyros alvarezii, commonly known as the Alvarez persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by orange to red fruits.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.
