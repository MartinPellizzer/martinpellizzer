Dryopteris oligophylla var. typica

Dryopteris oligophylla var. typica, commonly known as the slender wood fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with dark brown, wiry roots that help anchor it in its habitat. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae that give it a lacy appearance.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
