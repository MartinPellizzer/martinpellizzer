Dactylopsis digitata

Dactylopsis digitata, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
