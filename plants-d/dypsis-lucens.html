Dypsis lucens

Dypsis lucens, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into yellowish-orange fruits. Dypsis lucens thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor spaces.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
