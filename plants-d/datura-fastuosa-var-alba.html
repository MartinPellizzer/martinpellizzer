Datura fastuosa var. alba

Datura fastuosa var. alba, commonly known as white angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which can be harmful if ingested. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and flowers emit a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.
