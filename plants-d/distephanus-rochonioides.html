Distephanus rochonioides

Distephanus rochonioides, commonly known as the African orchid or Rochon's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Distephanus rochonioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
