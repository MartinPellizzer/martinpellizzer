Dianthus desideratus

Dianthus desideratus, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or red flowers with white or pink markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus desideratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
