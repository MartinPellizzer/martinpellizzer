Dudleya nesiotica

Dudleya nesiotica, commonly known as the Santa Barbara Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green with a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring. It is well adapted to rocky habitats and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
