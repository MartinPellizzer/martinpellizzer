Draba olympica var. heterocoma

Draba olympica var. heterocoma, commonly known as the Olympic Draba, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh mountain conditions.
