Delphinium grandiflorum var. pilosum

Delphinium grandiflorum var. pilosum, commonly known as the woolly larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a dense, fuzzy coating, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
