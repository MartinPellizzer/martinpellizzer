Dalbergia tricolor

Dalbergia tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored ironwood, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves, which display three distinct colors—green, white, and pink—on the same leaf blade. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional crafts and furniture.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
