Dalea violacea

Dalea violacea, commonly known as purple prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open prairies and dry woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its violet to purple flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dalea violacea is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
