Diospyros ferrea var. toppingii

Diospyros ferrea var. toppingii, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a leathery texture and a pointed tip.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
