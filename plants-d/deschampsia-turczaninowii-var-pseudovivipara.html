Deschampsia turczaninowii var. pseudovivipara

Deschampsia turczaninowii var. pseudovivipara, commonly known as the viviparous feathergrass, is a variant of the feathergrass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. One distinguishing feature is its viviparous reproduction, where seeds are produced in a manner that allows them to germinate while still attached to the parent plant.

This adaptation may aid in its survival in harsh, arid environments.
