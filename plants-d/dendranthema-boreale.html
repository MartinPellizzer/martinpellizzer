Dendranthema boreale

Dendranthema boreale, commonly known as the alpine chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It produces yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dendranthema boreale is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and calming properties.
