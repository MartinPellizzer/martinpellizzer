Diplotaxis bracteata

Diplotaxis bracteata, commonly known as the African wallgecko or desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Diplotaxis bracteata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
