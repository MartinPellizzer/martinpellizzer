Dortmanna giberroa

Dortmanna giberroa, commonly known as the dwarf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for full sun.

Dortmanna giberroa is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
