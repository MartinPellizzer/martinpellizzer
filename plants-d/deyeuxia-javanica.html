Deyeuxia javanica

Deyeuxia javanica, commonly known as the Javan sedge, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often clustered and produce small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

Deyeuxia javanica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
