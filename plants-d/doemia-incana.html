Doemia incana

Doemia incana, commonly known as the woolly doemia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, dry fruits known as follicles.

Doemia incana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique texture and drought tolerance.
