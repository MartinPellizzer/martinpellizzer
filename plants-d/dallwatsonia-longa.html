Dallwatsonia longa

Dallwatsonia longa, commonly known as the long-leaf Watsonia, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-like flowers that come in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces multiple flowering stems from a rhizomatous base.

Dallwatsonia longa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
