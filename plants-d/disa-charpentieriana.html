Disa charpentieriana

Disa charpentieriana, commonly known as the Cape bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in spring and is known for its intricate, lip-like structures that aid in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in its native region.

Disa charpentieriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral form.
