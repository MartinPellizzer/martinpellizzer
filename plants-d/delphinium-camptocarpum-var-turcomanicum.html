Delphinium camptocarpum var. turcomanicum

Delphinium camptocarpum var. turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by gardeners in suitable climates.
