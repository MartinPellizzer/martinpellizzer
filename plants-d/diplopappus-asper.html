Diplopappus asper

Diplopappus asper, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features alternate, rough-hair covered leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds with pappus structures for aid in propagation.

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
