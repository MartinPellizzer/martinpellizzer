Delphinium stiriacum

Delphinium stiriacum, commonly known as the Balkan larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained, alkaline soils.

Delphinium stiriacum is often found in open woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
