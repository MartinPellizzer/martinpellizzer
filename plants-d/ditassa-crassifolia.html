Ditassa crassifolia

Ditassa crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its robust growth habit makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
