Diospyros comorensis

Diospyros comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to yellow when mature, and it is edible when fully ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
