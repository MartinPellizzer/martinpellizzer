Digera forsskaolii

Digera forsskaolii, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fibrous, thread-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is considered a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
