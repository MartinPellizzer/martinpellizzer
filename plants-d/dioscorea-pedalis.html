Dioscorea pedalis

Dioscorea pedalis, commonly known as the footed yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has tendrils that help it climb and can reach significant heights in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow-green in color.

The tubers of Dioscorea pedalis are sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
