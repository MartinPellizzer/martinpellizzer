Dodonaea polyzyga

Dodonaea polyzyga, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring and summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.

Dodonaea polyzyga is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
