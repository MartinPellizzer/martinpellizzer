Dolichovigna rhombifolia

Dolichovigna rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, bean-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
