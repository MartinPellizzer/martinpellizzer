Decaisnina miniata

Decaisnina miniata, commonly known as the dwarf decaisnina, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, often with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
