Dendrolobium papuacola

Dendrolobium papuacola, commonly known as the Papuan wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the lowland rainforests of Papua, Indonesia, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various fauna.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
