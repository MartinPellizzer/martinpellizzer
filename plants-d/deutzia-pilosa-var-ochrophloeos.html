Deutzia pilosa var. ochrophloeos

Deutzia pilosa var. ochrophloeos, commonly known as the pale-leaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a soft, hairy texture and a pale green underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile garden plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
