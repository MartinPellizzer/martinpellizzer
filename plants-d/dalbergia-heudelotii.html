Dalbergia heudelotii

Dalbergia heudelotii, commonly known as African rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has bipinnate leaves, and its flowers are small, clustered, and typically yellow or greenish in color. It produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

This species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
