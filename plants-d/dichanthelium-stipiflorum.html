Dichanthelium stipiflorum

Dichanthelium stipiflorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Dichanthelium stipiflorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
