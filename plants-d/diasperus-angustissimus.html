Diasperus angustissimus

Diasperus angustissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert bean, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

Diasperus angustissimus plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying desert flora.
