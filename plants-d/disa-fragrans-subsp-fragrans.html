Disa fragrans subsp. fragrans

Disa fragrans subsp.

fragrans, commonly known as the sweet scenting orchid, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a rhizomatous root system.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
