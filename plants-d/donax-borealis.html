Donax borealis

Donax borealis, commonly known as the northern glasswort, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, wetlands, and saline soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Donax borealis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
