Dracaena bellula

Dracaena bellula, commonly known as the dwarf dracaena, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow stripes, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate light, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Dracaena bellula is valued for its low maintenance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
