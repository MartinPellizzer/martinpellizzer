Dichanthium acutiusculum

Dichanthium acutiusculum, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, erect leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its hardiness.

The species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
