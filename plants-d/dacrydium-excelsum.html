Dacrydium excelsum

Dacrydium excelsum, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and drooping, needle-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Dacrydium excelsum plays an important role in New Zealand's native ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
