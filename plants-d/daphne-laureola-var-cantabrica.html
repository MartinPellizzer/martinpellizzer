Daphne laureola var. cantabrica

Daphne laureola var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian laurel, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a subtle, aromatic fragrance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white during late winter to early spring. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, and it is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively hardy in mild climates.
