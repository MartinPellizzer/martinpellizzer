Diospyros pilosa

Diospyros pilosa, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, orange-red fruits. The fruits are typically edible when fully ripe, though they may have a bitter taste when unripe.

Diospyros pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
