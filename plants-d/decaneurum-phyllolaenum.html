Decaneurum phyllolaenum

Decaneurum phyllolaenum, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a striking plant with delicate, feathery fronds that give it a graceful appearance.

Its leaves are typically bioluminescent, emitting a soft blue glow at night, which is a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil efficiently.

Despite its beauty, it is considered invasive in some areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native species.
