Dianthus cintranus subsp. multiceps

Dianthus cintranus subsp.

multiceps, commonly known as the multiflora campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with prominent white markings.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Dianthus cintranus subsp.

multiceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
