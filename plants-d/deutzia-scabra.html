Deutzia scabra

Deutzia scabra, commonly known as the rough deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
