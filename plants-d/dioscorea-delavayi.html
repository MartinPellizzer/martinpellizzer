Dioscorea delavayi

Dioscorea delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used. These tubers are valued for their medicinal properties and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
