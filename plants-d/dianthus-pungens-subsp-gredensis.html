Dianthus pungens subsp. gredensis

Dianthus pungens subsp.

gredensis, commonly known as the Gredens rockpink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features dense clusters of pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish fuzz. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant flowers make it a valuable addition to horticultural collections.
