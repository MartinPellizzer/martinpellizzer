Disa saxicola

Disa saxicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually grass-like, and the flowers are borne on slender stalks.

Disa saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
