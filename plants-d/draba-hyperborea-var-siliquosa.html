Draba hyperborea var. siliquosa

Draba hyperborea var. siliquosa, commonly known as the alpine whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its presence is often indicative of high-altitude or polar ecosystems.
