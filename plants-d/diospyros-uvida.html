Diospyros uvida

Diospyros uvida, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible when fully ripe, though they are often bitter until mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a waxy coating.

The bark is thick and dark, providing a habitat for various insects and small animals.
