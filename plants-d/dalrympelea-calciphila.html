Dalrympelea calciphila

Dalrympelea calciphila, commonly known as the limestone daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils, which is reflected in its scientific name. It has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.
