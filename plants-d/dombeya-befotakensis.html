Dombeya befotakensis

Dombeya befotakensis, commonly known as the Kerguelen dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the subantarctic region of the Kerguelen Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its dense, woody stems and evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. Its botanical features include small, leathery leaves and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Dombeya befotakensis is adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat, with a slow growth rate and resilience to strong winds and limited nutrients.
