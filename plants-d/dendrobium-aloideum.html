Dendrobium aloideum

Dendrobium aloideum, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that resemble bamboo, which are covered in dense clusters of small, green leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often blooming in winter. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dendrobium aloideum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
