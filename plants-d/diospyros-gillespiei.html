Diospyros gillespiei

Diospyros gillespiei, commonly known as the Gillespie's ebony, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Belize and northern Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to reddish when mature.

The wood of Diospyros gillespiei is highly valued for its density and rich color, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.
