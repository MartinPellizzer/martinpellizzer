Dendrocoryne speciosa var. hillii

Dendrocoryne speciosa var. hillii, commonly known as the Hill's orchid tree, is a striking plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has a tree-like appearance with a single trunk and dense foliage, making it a visually appealing ornamental species. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive orchid-like structure that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
