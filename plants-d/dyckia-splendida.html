Dyckia splendida

Dyckia splendida, commonly known as the Splendid Dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a unique, almost frosty appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Dyckia splendida is a popular choice for indoor gardens due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
