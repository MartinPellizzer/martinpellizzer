Dictyoglossum crinitum

Dictyoglossum crinitum, commonly known as the hairy tongue orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance on its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
