Digitalis purpurea var. longipedunculata

Digitalis purpurea var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features tall, spires of tubular purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds.
