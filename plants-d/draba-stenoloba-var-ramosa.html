Draba stenoloba var. ramosa

Draba stenoloba var. ramosa, commonly known as the ramose whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine and arctic environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It features a low-growing habit with clustered stems and small, dark green leaves.

Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring.
