Dipterocarpus mannii

Dipterocarpus mannii, commonly known as the Malayan dipterocarp, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its tall stature and buttressed roots. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, red aril. Its leaves are typically alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy upper surface.

Dipterocarpus mannii plays a significant role in the ecological systems of tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
