Dianthus andronakii

Dianthus andronakii, commonly known as the Greek pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features showy, double petals that resemble peonies, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus andronakii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
