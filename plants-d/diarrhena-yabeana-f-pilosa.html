Diarrhena yabeana f. pilosa

Diarrhena yabeana f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy form of the Yabe's dipterocarp, is a variant of the Diarrhena yabeana species.

It is a woody plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical rainforests of countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which distinguishes it from the typical form of the species. It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae, which includes many important tropical tree species.

The hairy variant is often noted for its ecological significance in supporting local biodiversity and contributing to forest structure.
