Dioscorea soso var. calvescens

Dioscorea soso var. calvescens, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by bean-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, with a rough texture.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source and for medicinal purposes.
