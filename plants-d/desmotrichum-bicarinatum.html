Desmotrichum bicarinatum

Desmotrichum bicarinatum, commonly known as the two-edged daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a circular pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
