Dodecatheon pulchellum subsp. macrocarpum

Dodecatheon pulchellum subsp.

macrocarpum, commonly known as the showy glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellowish hue at the base.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodsy environments and produces showy, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while the stems are slender and often branched.

The species is known for its early spring blooming habit, making it a notable plant in woodland gardens.
