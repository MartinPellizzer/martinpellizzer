Dalea divaricata var. cinerea

Dalea divaricata var. cinerea, commonly known as the grayleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage, which is covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
