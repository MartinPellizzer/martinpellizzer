Dendrobium aphyllum

Dendrobium aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its green stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
