Derris balansae

Derris balansae, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes.

The roots of Derris balansae contain toxic alkaloids, which have been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a fish poison.
