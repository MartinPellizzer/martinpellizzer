Desmodium griffithianum

Desmodium griffithianum, commonly known as the Indian needlepod, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and it has a shallow root system that helps in soil stabilization.

Desmodium griffithianum is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
