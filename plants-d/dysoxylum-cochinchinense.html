Dysoxylum cochinchinense

Dysoxylum cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release flat, winged seeds.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
