Dissochaeta fusca var. ferruginea

Dissochaeta fusca var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in a single head, surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The variety *ferruginea* is distinguished by its reddish-brown stems and leaves, giving it its common name.
