Dianthus stamatiadae

Dianthus stamatiadae, commonly known as the Stamatiada's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
