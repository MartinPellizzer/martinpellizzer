Delphinium gilgianum

Delphinium gilgianum, commonly known as the Gilgian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. Delphinium gilgianum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
