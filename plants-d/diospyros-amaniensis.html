Diospyros amaniensis

Diospyros amaniensis, commonly known as the Amani ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Eastern Arc Mountains of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony and persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within montane forests.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.
