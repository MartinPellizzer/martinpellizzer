Delphinium pyramidatum

Delphinium pyramidatum, commonly known as the pyramidal larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers with spurred petals, typical of the Delphinium genus. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves that are green and slightly hairy.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
