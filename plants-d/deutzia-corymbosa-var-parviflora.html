Deutzia corymbosa var. parviflora

Deutzia corymbosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer. The foliage is typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blooms. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
