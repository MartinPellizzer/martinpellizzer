Duosperma longicalyx subsp. longicalyx

Duosperma longicalyx subsp.

longicalyx, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert globemallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
