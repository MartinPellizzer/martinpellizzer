Desmodium heterocarpon var. buergeri

Desmodium heterocarpon var. buergeri, commonly known as the Buerger's desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an important component of certain ecosystems.
