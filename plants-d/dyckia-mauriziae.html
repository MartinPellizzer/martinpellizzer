Dyckia mauriziae

Dyckia mauriziae, commonly known as the Mauresia dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, silver-white trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear vibrant red and orange flowers, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Dyckia mauriziae thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and long-lasting blooms.
