Delphinium lacei

Delphinium lacei, commonly known as the lacey larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a delicate, lace-like appearance, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
