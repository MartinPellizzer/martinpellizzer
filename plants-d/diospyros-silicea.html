Diospyros silicea

Diospyros silicea, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. Diospyros silicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
