Dioscorea prainiana

Dioscorea prainiana, commonly known as the Prain yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
