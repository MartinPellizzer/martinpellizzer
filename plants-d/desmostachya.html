Desmostachya

Desmostachya, commonly known as the "scented grass," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often arranged in a panicle, with small, usually two-ranked flowers. Desmostachya species are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as forage for livestock.
