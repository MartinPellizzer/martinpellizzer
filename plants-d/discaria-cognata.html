Discaria cognata

Discaria cognata, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, rough, ridged texture. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Discaria cognata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
