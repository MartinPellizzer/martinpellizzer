Delphinium bicolor var. variegatum

Delphinium bicolor var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
