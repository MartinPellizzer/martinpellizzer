Dimorphanthera longifolia

Dimorphanthera longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved dimorphanthera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
