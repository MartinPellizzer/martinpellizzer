Deschampsia cespitosa var. andraei

Deschampsia cespitosa var. andraei, commonly known as the Andrae's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, forming dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
