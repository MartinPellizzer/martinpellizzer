Dioscorea bernoulliana

Dioscorea bernoulliana, commonly known as the Bernoulli yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, although their edibility can vary depending on preparation methods.
