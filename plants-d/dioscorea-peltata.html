Dioscorea peltata

Dioscorea peltata, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into tuberous roots. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a humid climate with partial shade. Its tubers are edible after proper preparation and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
