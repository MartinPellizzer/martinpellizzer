Dorstenia contrajerva var. houstonii

Dorstenia contrajerva var. houstonii, commonly known as the Houston's false arum, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Mexico. The plant is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive inflorescence, which resembles a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
