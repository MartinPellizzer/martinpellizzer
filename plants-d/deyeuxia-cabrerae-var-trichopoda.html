Deyeuxia cabrerae var. trichopoda

Deyeuxia cabrerae var. trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
