Dianthus sylvestris var. godronianus

Dianthus sylvestris var. godronianus, commonly known as the Godron's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, rounded flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
