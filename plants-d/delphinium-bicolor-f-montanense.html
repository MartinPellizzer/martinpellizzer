Delphinium bicolor f. montanense

Delphinium bicolor f. montanense, commonly known as the Mountain Bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are borne in tall, branching clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and architectural form.
