Dolichos decumbens

Dolichos decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate cowpea, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features sprawling, low-growing stems that often lie close to the ground, with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, curved pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds and is also used in traditional medicine.

Dolichos decumbens is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in warm, arid environments.
