Didymocarpus praeteritus

Didymocarpus praeteritus, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit's pericarp is divided into segments, each containing a single seed.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
