Digitalis dubia

Digitalis dubia, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, spindly stems that bear hooded, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
