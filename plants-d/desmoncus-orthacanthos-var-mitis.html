Desmoncus orthacanthos var. mitis

Desmoncus orthacanthos var. mitis, commonly known as the slender desert cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a characteristic adaptation to minimize water loss.

The species is noted for its slow growth and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
