Dianthus multipunctatus var. troodi

Dianthus multipunctatus var. troodi, commonly known as the Troodi campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive red or purple central spot, which is a key botanical feature. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming blooms.
