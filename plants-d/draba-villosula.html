Draba villosula

Draba villosula, commonly known as the hairy rock-cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically between March and May. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Draba villosula is a hardy species that is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.
