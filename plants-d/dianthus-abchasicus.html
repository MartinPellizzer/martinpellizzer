Dianthus abchasicus

Dianthus abchasicus, commonly known as the Abchazian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Dianthus abchasicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
