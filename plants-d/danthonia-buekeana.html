Danthonia buekeana

Danthonia buekeana, commonly known as the New Zealand hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and montane regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
