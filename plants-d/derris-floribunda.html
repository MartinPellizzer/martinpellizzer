Derris floribunda

Derris floribunda, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.
