Dysoxylum macgregorii

Dysoxylum macgregorii, commonly known as the broad-leaved dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in traditional medicine and construction.
