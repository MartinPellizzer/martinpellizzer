Dryopteris cucullata var. mucronata

Dryopteris cucullata var. mucronata, commonly known as the rounded shield fern, is a perennial fern native to eastern North America.

It features a distinctive, broad, and leathery frond with a rounded shape and a short, blunt apex. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are covered in a dense, brownish layer of roots, which help anchor it to the soil.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive cycle in the wild.
