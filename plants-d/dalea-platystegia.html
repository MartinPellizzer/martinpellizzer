Dalea platystegia

Dalea platystegia, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Dalea platystegia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
