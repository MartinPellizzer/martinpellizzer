Dipterocarpus beccarii

Dipterocarpus beccarii, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, durable hardwood, which is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family, which includes many tropical forest trees. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of these ecosystems.

Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a distinctive reddish or yellowish color, attracting various pollinators.
