Dichelachne montana

Dichelachne montana, commonly known as mountain dichelachne, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive leaf venation and reproductive structures typical of the Poaceae family.
