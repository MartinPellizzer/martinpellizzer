Dianthus corsicus

Dianthus corsicus, commonly known as the Corsican pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.

Dianthus corsicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
