Dischistocalyx pubescens var. longipilosa

Dischistocalyx pubescens var. longipilosa, commonly known as the long-haired hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "longipilosa.". The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
