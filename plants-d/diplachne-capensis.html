Diplachne capensis

Diplachne capensis, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in coastal regions.

Diplachne capensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
