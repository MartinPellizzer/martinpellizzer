Dortmanna besseriana

Dortmanna besseriana, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Dortmanna besseriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
