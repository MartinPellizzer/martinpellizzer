Deinostema adenocaula

Deinostema adenocaula, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss through transpiration.
