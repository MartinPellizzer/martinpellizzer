Dactylorhiza incarnata var. obscura

Dactylorhiza incarnata var. obscura, commonly known as the dark lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, hairy stems and its long, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces a tall spike of small, pale green to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands.

Dactylorhiza incarnata var. obscura is a perennial species that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
