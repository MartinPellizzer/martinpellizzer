Dipterocarpus caudifer

Dipterocarpus caudifer, commonly known as the long-fruited dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and heavy, durable wood. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in forest ecosystems.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and plays a significant role in the biodiversity and carbon cycling of these habitats.
