Dioscorea quartiniana var. apiculata

Dioscorea quartiniana var. apiculata, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and traditional medicinal purposes.
