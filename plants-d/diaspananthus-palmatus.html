Diaspananthus palmatus

Diaspananthus palmatus, commonly known as the yellow-veined palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves with prominent yellow veins against a deep green background. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
