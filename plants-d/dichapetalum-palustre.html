Dichapetalum palustre

Dichapetalum palustre, commonly known as swamp hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Dichapetalum palustre is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
