Dalbergia obtusifolia var. rogersii

Dalbergia obtusifolia var. rogersii, commonly known as the California blackwood, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional uses.
