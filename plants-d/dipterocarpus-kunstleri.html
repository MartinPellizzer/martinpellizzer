Dipterocarpus kunstleri

Dipterocarpus kunstleri, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
