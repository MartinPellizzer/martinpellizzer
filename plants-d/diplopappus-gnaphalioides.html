Diplopappus gnaphalioides

Diplopappus gnaphalioides, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often erect.

The species is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
