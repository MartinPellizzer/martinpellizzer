Dalzellia lawii

Dalzellia lawii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round fruits.

Dalzellia lawii is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
