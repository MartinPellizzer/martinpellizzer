Danthonia californica var. unispicata

Danthonia californica var. unispicata, commonly known as the California hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and sandy areas.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
