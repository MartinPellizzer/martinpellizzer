Dyssodia aurantiaca

Dyssodia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and the species is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Dyssodia aurantiaca prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
