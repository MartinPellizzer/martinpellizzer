Dicranopygium testaceum

Dicranopygium testaceum, commonly known as the brown moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dark brown to reddish-brown coloration and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a wiry, creeping growth form with dense, overlapping leaves that are often curled when dry. It reproduces via spores, which are released from capsule-like structures that develop on the stems.

Dicranopygium testaceum plays an important role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
