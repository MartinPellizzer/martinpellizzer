Desmodium radicans

Desmodium radicans, commonly known as the trailing desmodium, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it is often found in disturbed soils across tropical and subtropical regions. Desmodium radicans is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobia bacteria, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ground cover in landscaping.
