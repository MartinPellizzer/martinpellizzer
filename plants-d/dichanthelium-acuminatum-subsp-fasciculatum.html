Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp. fasciculatum

Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp.

fasciculatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as pine woodlands and grassy areas.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct ligule. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet bearing two to three bracteate flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and is often found in disturbed or dry environments.
