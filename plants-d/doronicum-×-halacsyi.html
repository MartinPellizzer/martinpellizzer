Doronicum × halacsyi

Doronicum × halacsyi, commonly known as the hybrid leopard's ear, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that resemble the ears of a leopard. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by distinctive, spiky seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
