Dioscorea orthogoneura

Dioscorea orthogoneura, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
