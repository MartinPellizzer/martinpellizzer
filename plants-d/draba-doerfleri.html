Draba doerfleri

Draba doerfleri, commonly known as the Doerfler's whitlow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

Draba doerfleri is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
