Dicoma anomala var. cirsioides

Dicoma anomala var. cirsioides, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
