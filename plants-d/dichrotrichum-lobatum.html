Dichrotrichum lobatum

Dichrotrichum lobatum, commonly known as the yellow star grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear yellow to brown florets, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it thrive in arid environments.

Dichrotrichum lobatum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
