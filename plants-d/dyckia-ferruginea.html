Dyckia ferruginea

Dyckia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped foliage that is often covered in a layer of rust-colored trichomes, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Dyckia ferruginea is popular among succulent and bromeliad enthusiasts for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
