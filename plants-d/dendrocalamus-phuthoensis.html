Dendrocalamus phuthoensis

Dendrocalamus phuthoensis, commonly known as the Phutho bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of India and Myanmar.

It is a tall, fast-growing grass-like plant that belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily. The plant is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that are often used in construction and traditional crafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

Dendrocalamus phuthoensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
