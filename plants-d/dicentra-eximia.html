Dicentra eximia

Dicentra eximia, commonly known as the bleeding heart, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has delicate, fern-like foliage that adds a soft texture to gardens. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Dicentra eximia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
