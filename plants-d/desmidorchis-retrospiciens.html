Desmidorchis retrospiciens

Desmidorchis retrospiciens, commonly known as the backward-looking orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which includes a specialized labellum that appears to look backward, a feature that aids in pollination by specific insect species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or wetlands, where it can thrive in partial shade. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spike-like inflorescence.

The species is notable for its intricate floral morphology and ecological adaptations that support its reproductive strategy.
