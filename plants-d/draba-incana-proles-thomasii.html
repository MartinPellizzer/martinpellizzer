Draba incana proles thomasii

Draba incana proles thomasii, commonly known as the Thomasii's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 10 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are densely hairy and have a bluish-gray appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, blooming in early spring.

It is native to arid and rocky habitats in western North America, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
