Drypetes harmandii

Drypetes harmandii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its durable wood, though it is also threatened by habitat loss and overharvesting.
