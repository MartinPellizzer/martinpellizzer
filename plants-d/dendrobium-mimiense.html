Dendrobium mimiense

Dendrobium mimiense, commonly known as the Mimiense orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, often attached to trees or rocks. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it produces numerous small, greenish-white flowers during the warmer months.

Dendrobium mimiense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
