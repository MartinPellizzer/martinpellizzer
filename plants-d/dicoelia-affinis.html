Dicoelia affinis

Dicoelia affinis, commonly known as the African orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky outcrops.

Dicoelia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
