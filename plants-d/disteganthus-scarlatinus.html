Disteganthus scarlatinus

Disteganthus scarlatinus, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shady areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Disteganthus scarlatinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
