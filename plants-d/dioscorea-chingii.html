Dioscorea chingii

Dioscorea chingii, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.

Dioscorea chingii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
