Dianthera pectoralis

Dianthera pectoralis, commonly known as the heartleaf dianthera, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

Dianthera pectoralis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
