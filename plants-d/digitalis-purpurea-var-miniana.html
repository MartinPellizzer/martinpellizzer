Digitalis purpurea var. miniana

Digitalis purpurea var. miniana, commonly known as the miniature foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bumblebees and other insects.
