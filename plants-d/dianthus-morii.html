Dianthus morii

Dianthus morii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre pinks, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Dianthus morii has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.
