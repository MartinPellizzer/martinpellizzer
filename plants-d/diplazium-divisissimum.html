Diplazium divisissimum

Diplazium divisissimum, commonly known as the fragrant fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often divided into numerous segments, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce both sterile and fertile fronds.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.
