Dracula saulii

Dracula saulii, commonly known as the Saul's vampire orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and elongated, pendulous inflorescences that bear intricate, fleshy flowers resembling those of the Dracula genus. The plant thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures, often growing on trees in shaded environments. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
