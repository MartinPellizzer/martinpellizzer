Dioscorea longispicata

Dioscorea longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest in both botanical research and pharmaceutical development.
