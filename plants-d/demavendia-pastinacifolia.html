Demavendia pastinacifolia

Demavendia pastinacifolia, commonly known as the Persian watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including Iran and Afghanistan. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often submerged in water, giving it a unique aquatic appearance. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often found in moist, rocky environments and is valued for its peppery flavor in local cuisines.
