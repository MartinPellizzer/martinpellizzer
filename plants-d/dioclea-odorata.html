Dioclea odorata

Dioclea odorata, commonly known as the sweet shower or passion vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often consumed by various animals. Its flowers are known to emit a sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as moths and bats.

Dioclea odorata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
