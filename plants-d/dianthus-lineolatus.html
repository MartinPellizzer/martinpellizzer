Dianthus lineolatus

Dianthus lineolatus, commonly known as the spotted carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The flowers have five petals with a distinctive white or pinkish central stripe, giving the plant its common name. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Dianthus lineolatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
