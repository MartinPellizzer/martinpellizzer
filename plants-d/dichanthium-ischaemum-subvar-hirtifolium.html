Dichanthium ischaemum subvar. hirtifolium

Dichanthium ischaemum subvar. hirtifolium, commonly known as hairy leaf fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of grassland ecosystems.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in degraded landscapes.
