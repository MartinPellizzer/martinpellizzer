Dryopteris arcana

Dryopteris arcana, commonly known as the barren wood fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically pale green and often have a waxy coating. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky outcrops. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Dryopteris arcana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
