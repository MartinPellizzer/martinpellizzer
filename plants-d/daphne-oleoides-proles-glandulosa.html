Daphne oleoides proles glandulosa

Daphne oleoides proles glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. The plant has thick, aromatic leaves covered in glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive scent. Its bark is smooth and gray, often peeling in thin layers.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
