Dichanthium planipedicellatum

Dichanthium planipedicellatum, commonly known as the flat-cell panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is well adapted to dry environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and the ability to regrow from its rhizomes after periods of moisture.

This grass is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or soil stabilization.
