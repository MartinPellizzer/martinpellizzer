Drepanostachyum gracile

Drepanostachyum gracile, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight blue-green hue.

Drepanostachyum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
