Dracocephalum multicaule var. subcapitatum

Dracocephalum multicaule var. subcapitatum, commonly known as narrow-headed blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
