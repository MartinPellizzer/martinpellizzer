Dalea longipes

Dalea longipes, commonly known as the longpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces elongated, slender pods that contain the seeds.

Dalea longipes is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
