Dekindtia

Dekindtia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-like seed pods that resemble ghostly shapes, which give it its common name. The plant is a member of the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, drought-prone environments. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or green, blending in with the surrounding vegetation.

Dekindtia is also notable for its long flowering period, which can last several months.
