Dolicholus tomentosus var. undulatus

Dolicholus tomentosus var. undulatus, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
