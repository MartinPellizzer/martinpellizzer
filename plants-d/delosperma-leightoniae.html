Delosperma leightoniae

Delosperma leightoniae, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in survival in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant orange to red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it an attractive addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
