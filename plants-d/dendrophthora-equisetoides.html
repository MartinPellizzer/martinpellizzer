Dendrophthora equisetoides

Dendrophthora equisetoides, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the order Lepidoziellales.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a sporophyte that is typically short and stout. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests. Its reproductive structures include microsporangia and megasporangia, which produce spores for propagation.

Dendrophthora equisetoides plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
