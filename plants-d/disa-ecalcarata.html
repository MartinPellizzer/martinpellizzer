Disa ecalcarata

Disa ecalcarata, commonly known as the spurred disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, orchid-like flowers that come in shades of pink, purple, and white, often with a distinctive spur at the base of the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions, and thrives in areas with high sunlight exposure. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Disa ecalcarata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
