Diospyros sakalavarum

Diospyros sakalavarum, commonly known as the Malabar date palm, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. The plant is known for its edible fruits, which are typically small and have a sweet, date-like flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Diospyros sakalavarum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
