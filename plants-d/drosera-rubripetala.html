Drosera rubripetala

Drosera rubripetala, commonly known as the red-stemmed sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its reddish stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance.

Drosera rubripetala is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist conditions.
