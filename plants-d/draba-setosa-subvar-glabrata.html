Draba setosa subvar. glabrata

Draba setosa subvar. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual or biennial herb characterized by its dense, hairy leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced leaf hair compared to the typical variety of Draba setosa.
