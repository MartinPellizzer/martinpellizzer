Draba pulcherrima

Draba pulcherrima, commonly known as the "Beautiful Draba," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with four petals that are arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Draba pulcherrima is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
