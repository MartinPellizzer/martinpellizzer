Dianthus serrulatus var. grandiflorus

Dianthus serrulatus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with frilled petals. The leaves are narrow, serrated, and gray-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
