Dysoxylum microstachyum

Dysoxylum microstachyum, commonly known as the small-stalked dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland rainforest environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
