Desmodium purpusianum

Desmodium purpusianum, commonly known as the purple desmodium, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, disturbed habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with Rhizobium bacteria.

Desmodium purpusianum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
