Dracontium laciniatum

Dracontium laciniatum, commonly known as the split-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red berries. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
