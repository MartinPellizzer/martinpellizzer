Dalea fieldii

Dalea fieldii, commonly known as plains false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Dalea fieldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
