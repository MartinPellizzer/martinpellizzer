Diospyros bangoiensis

Diospyros bangoiensis, commonly known as the Bango Island persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Bango Islands in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible when ripe.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
