Darwinia fimbriata

Darwinia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are often covered in fine, fringed hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Darwinia fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
