Diplotaxis barrelieri

Diplotaxis barrelieri, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive with minimal water.

Diplotaxis barrelieri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
