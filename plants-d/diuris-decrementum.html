Diuris decrementum

Diuris decrementum, commonly known as the small-leafed spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive, spider-like flower spikes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spiders. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Diuris decrementum is a low-growing plant that typically blooms during the cooler months of spring and early summer.
