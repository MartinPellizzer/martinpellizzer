Dendrobenthamia japonica

Dendrobenthamia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests or mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged spirally along the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
