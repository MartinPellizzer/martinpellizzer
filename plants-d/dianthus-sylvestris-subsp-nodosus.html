Dianthus sylvestris subsp. nodosus

Dianthus sylvestris subsp.

nodosus, commonly known as the clustered carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
