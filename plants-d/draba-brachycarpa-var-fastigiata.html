Draba brachycarpa var. fastigiata

Draba brachycarpa var. fastigiata, commonly known as the compact rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with four petals and numerous stamens.
