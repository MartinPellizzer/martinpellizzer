Dactylis glomerata nothovar. subhirsuta

Dactylis glomerata nothovar. subhirsuta, commonly known as the hairy sheep's fescue, is a hybrid grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with short, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish-brown in color. It is well adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and resilience.
