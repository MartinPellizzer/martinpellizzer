Dioscorea sylvatica subsp. lydenbergensis

Dioscorea sylvatica subsp.

lydenbergensis, commonly known as the Lydenberg wild yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family. It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is native to southern Africa, particularly in countries like South Africa and Namibia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, white or greenish flowers.
