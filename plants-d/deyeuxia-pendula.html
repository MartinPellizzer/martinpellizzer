Deyeuxia pendula

Deyeuxia pendula, commonly known as the hanging grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, arching stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Deyeuxia pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
