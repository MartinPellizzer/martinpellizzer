Delphinastrum fissum

Delphinastrum fissum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a distinct central vein, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Delphinastrum fissum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
