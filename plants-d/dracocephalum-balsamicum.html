Dracocephalum balsamicum

Dracocephalum balsamicum, commonly known as balsamic hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, often with a strong, minty fragrance.

Dracocephalum balsamicum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
