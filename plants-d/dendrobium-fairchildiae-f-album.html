Dendrobium fairchildiae f. album

Dendrobium fairchildiae f. album, commonly known as the white-stemmed orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a tall, slender stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length, often adorned with a profusion of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and a unique, fragrant bloom that appears during the dry season. It thrives in warm, humid climates with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
