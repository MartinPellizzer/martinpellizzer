Dianthus ramosissimus

Dianthus ramosissimus, commonly known as the branched campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with many branches, giving it a bushy appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Dianthus ramosissimus is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
