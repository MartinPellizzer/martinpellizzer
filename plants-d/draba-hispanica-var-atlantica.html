Draba hispanica var. atlantica

Draba hispanica var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic Draba, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, basal leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in limestone soils.
