Dianthus orophilus

Dianthus orophilus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Dianthus orophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
