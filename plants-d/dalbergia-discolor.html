Dalbergia discolor

Dalbergia discolor, commonly known as the rosewood tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves, with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing flattened, kidney-shaped seeds.

The tree is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
