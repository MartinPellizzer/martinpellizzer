Dysoxylum forsteri

Dysoxylum forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's dysoxylum, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy beneath. The flowers are small and fragrant, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and are arranged in clusters. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

This plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
