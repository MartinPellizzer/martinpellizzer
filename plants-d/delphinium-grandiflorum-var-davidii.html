Delphinium grandiflorum var. davidii

Delphinium grandiflorum var. davidii, commonly known as the David's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and sometimes hairy.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
