Dianthus fimbriatus var. obtusisquameus

Dianthus fimbriatus var. obtusisquameus, commonly known as the broad-scale carnation, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, pink to reddish flowers with frilly, overlapping petals that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native plant landscapes.
