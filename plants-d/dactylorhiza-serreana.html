Dactylorhiza serreana

Dactylorhiza serreana, commonly known as the Alpine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as peat bogs and mountain meadows. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance.

Dactylorhiza serreana is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, which is a common trait among orchids.
