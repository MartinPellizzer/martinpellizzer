Diospyros hirta

Diospyros hirta, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, orange-red fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests and disturbed areas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive hairy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The fruit, which is edible when fully ripe, is a key feature of the plant's botanical identity.
