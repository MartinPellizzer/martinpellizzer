Dahlia lutea

Dahlia lutea, commonly known as the yellow dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces cylindrical, fleshy tubers as storage organs, which are essential for its survival and propagation. Its leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Dahlia lutea is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
