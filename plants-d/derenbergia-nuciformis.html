Derenbergia nuciformis

Derenbergia nuciformis, commonly known as the nut-shaped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, nut-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a velvety texture. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Derenbergia nuciformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
