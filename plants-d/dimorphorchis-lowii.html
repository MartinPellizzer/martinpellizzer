Dimorphorchis lowii

Dimorphorchis lowii, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Dimorphorchis lowii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
