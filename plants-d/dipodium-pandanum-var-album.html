Dipodium pandanum var. album

Dipodium pandanum var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered pandan, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, feather-like leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its rhizomes are also utilized in various cultural practices.

This variety is distinguished by its pale floral coloration compared to other pandan species.
