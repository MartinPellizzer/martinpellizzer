Dryopteris hirta f. portoricana

Dryopteris hirta f. portoricana, commonly known as the Puerto Rican wood fern, is a fern species native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, pinnately arranged fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and covered with short, stiff hairs, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
