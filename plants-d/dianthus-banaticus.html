Dianthus banaticus

Dianthus banaticus, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of brightly colored, showy flowers that range from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces fragrant, five-petaled blooms. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Dianthus banaticus is a popular garden plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
