Deschampsia cespitosa monstr. kuntzei

Deschampsia cespitosa monstr.

kuntzei, commonly known as the tufted hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, hair-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces slender spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle-like inflorescence, with each spikelet containing two to three small, awned flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
