Dalea scandens var. gaumeri

Dalea scandens var. gaumeri, commonly known as the gaumer's prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, warm climates.
