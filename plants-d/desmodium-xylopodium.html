Desmodium xylopodium

Desmodium xylopodium, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Desmodium xylopodium is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
