Dioclea flexuosa

Dioclea flexuosa, commonly known as the climbing huito, is a tropical vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pale yellow or orange flowers that are typically clustered in small inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Dioclea flexuosa plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various animals in its native habitat.
