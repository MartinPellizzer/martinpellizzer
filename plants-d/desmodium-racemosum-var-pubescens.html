Desmodium racemosum var. pubescens

Desmodium racemosum var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical pods containing several elliptical seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and forage.
