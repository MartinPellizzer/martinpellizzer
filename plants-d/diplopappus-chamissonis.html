Diplopappus chamissonis

Diplopappus chamissonis, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are characterized by two distinct lobes and are adapted for wind pollination.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.
