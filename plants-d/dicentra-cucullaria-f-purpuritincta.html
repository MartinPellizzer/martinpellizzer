Dicentra cucullaria f. purpuritincta

Dicentra cucullaria f. purpuritincta, commonly known as the purple-veined bleeding heart, is a rare and striking variant of the bleeding heart plant.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, pinkish-white flowers with deep purple veins, which contrast beautifully against the foliage. The plant is native to the Appalachian region of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of dark green, trifoliate leaves that add to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is prized for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
