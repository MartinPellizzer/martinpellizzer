Diplarche

Diplarche, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Diplarche is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
