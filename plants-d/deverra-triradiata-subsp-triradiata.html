Deverra triradiata subsp. triradiata

Deverra triradiata subsp.

triradiata, commonly known as the three-rayed devil's backbone, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, branching stems that bear small, fleshy leaves arranged in a triangular pattern.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
