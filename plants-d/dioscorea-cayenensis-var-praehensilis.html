Dioscorea cayenensis var. praehensilis

Dioscorea cayenensis var. praehensilis, commonly known as yam bean, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb supports. The plant produces cylindrical, tuberous roots that are the primary edible part. These roots are rich in starch and have a mild, nutty flavor.

It is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental foliage.
