Disa purpurascens

Disa purpurascens, commonly known as the Purple Disa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, violet-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, spurred flowers during the summer months.

Disa purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
