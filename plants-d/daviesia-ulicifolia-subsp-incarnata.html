Daviesia ulicifolia subsp. incarnata

Daviesia ulicifolia subsp.

incarnata, commonly known as the red-flowered daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
