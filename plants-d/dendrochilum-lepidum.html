Dendrochilum lepidum

Dendrochilum lepidum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy appearance and produces small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shady, humid environments, often on tree trunks or branches. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, helping it store water and nutrients.

Dendrochilum lepidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
