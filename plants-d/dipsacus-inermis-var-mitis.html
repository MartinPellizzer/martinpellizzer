Dipsacus inermis var. mitis

Dipsacus inermis var. mitis, commonly known as the soft teasel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fluffy, spherical seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often grown in wildflower meadows and as a decorative plant.
