Diospyros kaki var. elliptica

Diospyros kaki var. elliptica, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and produces sweet, edible fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is typically orange to red in color and has a smooth, firm texture.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its nutritious and flavorful fruit.
