Dioscorea trilinguis var. edwallii

Dioscorea trilinguis var. edwallii, commonly known as the Edwall's yam, is a variant of the yam species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are often harvested for their starchy content and have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local agriculture.
