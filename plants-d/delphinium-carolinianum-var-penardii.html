Delphinium carolinianum var. penardii

Delphinium carolinianum var. penardii, commonly known as Penard's larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
