Diospyros tamiriensis

Diospyros tamiriensis, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a sweet, edible berry that ripens to a deep orange or red color.

The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood and its potential medicinal properties.
