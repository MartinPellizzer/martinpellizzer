Desmodium terminale

Desmodium terminale, commonly known as hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are often covered in fine hairs. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
