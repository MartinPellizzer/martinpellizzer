Drosera paleacea subsp. trichocaulis

Drosera paleacea subsp.

trichocaulis, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, sticky leaves covered in glandular hairs that secrete a viscous digestive fluid.

The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes, where it captures small insects to supplement its nutrient intake. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces a single, erect inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ecological role.
