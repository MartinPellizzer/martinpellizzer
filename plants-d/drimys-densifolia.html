Drimys densifolia

Drimys densifolia, commonly known as the Chilean wintergreen, is a shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Drimys densifolia is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
