Delphinium gayanum

Delphinium gayanum, commonly known as the Gayan bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Delphinium genus.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
