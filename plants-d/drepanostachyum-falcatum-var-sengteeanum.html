Drepanostachyum falcatum var. sengteeanum

Drepanostachyum falcatum var. sengteeanum, commonly known as the Sengtien bamboo, is a variant of the broad-leaved bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often curved or rolled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its stems are strong and fibrous, making it suitable for use in construction and crafts.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is valued for both its ornamental and practical applications.
