Dipodium chanii

Dipodium chanii, commonly known as the golden bamboo or yellow bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological purposes. Its foliage exhibits a striking golden-yellow color, especially under certain light conditions.

Dipodium chanii is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
