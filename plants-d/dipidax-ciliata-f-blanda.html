Dipidax ciliata f. blanda

Dipidax ciliata f. blanda, commonly known as the soft-leaved sand thistle, is a horticultural form of the sand thistle species.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy foliage and pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance and thrives in full sun.
