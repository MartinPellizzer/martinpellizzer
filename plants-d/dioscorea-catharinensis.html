Dioscorea catharinensis

Dioscorea catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its tubers are characterized by their rough, irregular surface and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Dioscorea catharinensis is valued for its nutritional content and has been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
