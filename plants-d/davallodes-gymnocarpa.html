Davallodes gymnocarpa

Davallodes gymnocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that mature into a smooth, leathery texture. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic seed pod.

The species is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and soil stabilization.
