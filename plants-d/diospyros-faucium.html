Diospyros faucium

Diospyros faucium, commonly known as the Caribbean persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the West Indies.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that mature to a reddish color, often with a sweet, edible pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
