Dioscorea tysonii

Dioscorea tysonii, commonly known as the Tysons yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. These tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

Dioscorea tysonii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
