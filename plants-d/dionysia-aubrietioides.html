Dionysia aubrietioides

Dionysia aubrietioides, commonly known as the common dionysia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in umbels. It blooms in early spring, producing a delicate display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dionysia aubrietioides is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
