Dracaena cuspidata

Dracaena cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dracaena, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow, dark green leaves that are often stiff and pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and minimal water requirements.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in both residential and commercial settings.
