Deyeuxia tenuifolia

Deyeuxia tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf false rice, is a grass species native to Asia, particularly found in regions like China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate environments, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Deyeuxia tenuifolia is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
