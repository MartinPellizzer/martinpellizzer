Delphinium ukokense

Delphinium ukokense, commonly known as the Ukok skyblue, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and large, palmately compound leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Delphinium ukokense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
