Dichapetalum spicatum

Dichapetalum spicatum, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. The plant has thick, woody stems and oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its name refers to its unique floral structure and its historical use in traditional medicine.
