Dianthus carthusianorum var. bucovinensis

Dianthus carthusianorum var. bucovinensis, commonly known as the Bucovina carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, double-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant produces basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and has a short, sturdy stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture further enhance its appeal.
