Dichapetalum neglectum

Dichapetalum neglectum, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a dense, compact form.

Dichapetalum neglectum is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
