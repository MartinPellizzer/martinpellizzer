Dodecatheon utahense

Dodecatheon utahense, commonly known as the Utah globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branching.

Dodecatheon utahense plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
