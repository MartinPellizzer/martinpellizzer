Dianthus liburnicus var. knappii

Dianthus liburnicus var. knappii, commonly known as the Knapp's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are attractive to pollinators.

This variety is noted for its horticultural value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
