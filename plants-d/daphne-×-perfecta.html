Daphne × perfecta

Daphne × perfecta, commonly known as the common daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, slightly alkaline soils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and have a leathery texture, adding visual interest throughout the year.

Daphne × perfecta is relatively low maintenance but requires protection from frost and excessive moisture.
