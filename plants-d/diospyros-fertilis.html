Diospyros fertilis

Diospyros fertilis, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, and its fruit is a yellow to orange berry that ripens in the autumn. Diospyros fertilis is valued for its ornamental foliage and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
