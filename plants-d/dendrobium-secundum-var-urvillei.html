Dendrobium secundum var. urvillei

Dendrobium secundum var. urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's Dendrobium, is a flowering orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, tropical environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant features long, cane-like stems that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
