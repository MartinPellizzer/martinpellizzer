Dioscorea pulchella

Dioscorea pulchella, commonly known as the pink yam, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by yellow or orange tubers. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their nutritional content.

Dioscorea pulchella is also an important species for ecological studies due to its unique reproductive strategies and habitat preferences.
