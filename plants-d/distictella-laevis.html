Distictella laevis

Distictella laevis, commonly known as the smooth distictella, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Native to arid regions of North America, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
