Dendrocnide celebica

Dendrocnide celebica, commonly known as the Celebes stinging nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with opposite, ovate leaves. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its toxic properties, handling the plant requires caution.
