Dichaea lobata

Dichaea lobata, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its striking, showy flowers that resemble ghostly shapes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in the understory of forests. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, soil, and debris.

Dichaea lobata is a rare and protected species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
