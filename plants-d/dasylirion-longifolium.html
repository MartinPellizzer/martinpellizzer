Dasylirion longifolium

Dasylirion longifolium, commonly known as the desert dagger, is a perennial evergreen plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of long, rigid, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale blue flowers in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
