Drimys myrtoides

Drimys myrtoides, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Drimys myrtoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
