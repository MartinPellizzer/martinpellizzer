Dodecatheon campestre

Dodecatheon campestre, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of 10 to 30 inches. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Dodecatheon campestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
