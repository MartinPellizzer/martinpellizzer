Deguelia glabrata

Deguelia glabrata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
