Diervilla sakaii

Diervilla sakaii, commonly known as Japanese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its spiny, thorn-like stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
