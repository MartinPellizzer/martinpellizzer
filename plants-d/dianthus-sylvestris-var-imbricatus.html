Dianthus sylvestris var. imbricatus

Dianthus sylvestris var. imbricatus, commonly known as the woodland carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or white, five-petaled flowers with a distinctive fringed appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding delicate color to shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in naturalized landscapes.
