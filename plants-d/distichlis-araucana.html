Distichlis araucana

Distichlis araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria grass, is a perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Distichlis araucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.
