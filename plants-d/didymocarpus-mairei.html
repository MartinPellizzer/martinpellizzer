Didymocarpus mairei

Didymocarpus mairei, commonly known as the Maire's flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
