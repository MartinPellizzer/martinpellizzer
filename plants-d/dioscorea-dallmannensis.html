Dioscorea dallmannensis

Dioscorea dallmannensis, commonly known as the Dallmann's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its helical stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are typically harvested for their nutritional value and have been utilized in various cultural practices.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
