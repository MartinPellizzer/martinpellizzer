Diospyros suaveolens

Diospyros suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet osage or Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species known for their hard, durable wood. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by edible berries, which are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The tree is valued not only for its fruit but also for its timber, which is highly prized for its strength and beauty.
