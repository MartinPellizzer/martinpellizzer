Daphne × hybrida

Daphne × hybrida, commonly known as the common daphne, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Thymelaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and showy clusters of tubular, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though it can be toxic to both humans and animals.

Daphne × hybrida is typically evergreen, with leathery leaves that provide year-round interest in the garden.
