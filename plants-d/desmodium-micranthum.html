Desmodium micranthum

Desmodium micranthum, commonly known as small-flowered desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds.

Desmodium micranthum is sometimes used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
