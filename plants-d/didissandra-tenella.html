Didissandra tenella

Didissandra tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leafed didissandra, is a unique plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, waxy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for native wildlife. Didissandra tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
