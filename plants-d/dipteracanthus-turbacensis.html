Dipteracanthus turbacensis

Dipteracanthus turbacensis, commonly known as the Turban Mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Dipteracanthus turbacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
