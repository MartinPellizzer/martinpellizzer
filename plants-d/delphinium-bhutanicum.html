Delphinium bhutanicum

Delphinium bhutanicum, commonly known as the Bhutan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is considered a symbol of Bhutan's natural heritage and is protected in its native habitat.
