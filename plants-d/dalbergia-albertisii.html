Dalbergia albertisii

Dalbergia albertisii, commonly known as Albert's ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic appeal. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing flattened, brown seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental and commercial value, it is also subject to conservation concerns.
