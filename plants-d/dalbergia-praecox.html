Dalbergia praecox

Dalbergia praecox, commonly known as the early flame tree, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, flame-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
