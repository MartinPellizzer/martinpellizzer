Diodia gardneri

Diodia gardneri, commonly known as wild betony, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, blue to purple flowers are arranged in whorled clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.
