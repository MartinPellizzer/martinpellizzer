Diacantha arborea

Diacantha arborea, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and large, showy flowers that resemble those of the hibiscus genus. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central column.
