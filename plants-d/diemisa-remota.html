Diemisa remota

Diemisa remota, commonly known as the remote diemisa, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to extreme environmental conditions, including low temperatures and high UV exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Diemisa remota is considered a species of conservation concern.
