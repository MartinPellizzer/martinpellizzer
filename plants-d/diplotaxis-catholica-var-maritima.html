Diplotaxis catholica var. maritima

Diplotaxis catholica var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense, compact foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats. Its small, daisy-like blooms appear in spring and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its resilience to salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.
