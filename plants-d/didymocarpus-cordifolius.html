Didymocarpus cordifolius

Didymocarpus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are heart-shaped and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
