Darwinia chapmaniana

Darwinia chapmaniana, commonly known as the Chapman's devil's-head, is a striking plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences that resemble a devil's head. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of bright red or orange flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Darwinia chapmaniana is a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
