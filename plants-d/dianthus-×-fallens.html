Dianthus × fallens

Dianthus × fallens, commonly known as the carnation or double carnation, is a hybrid species within the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

Dianthus × fallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements and as a bedding plant.
