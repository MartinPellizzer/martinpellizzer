Diospyros diepenhorstii

Diospyros diepenhorstii, commonly known as the Diepenhorst's ebony, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are a food source for various wildlife.

The species is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and craftsmanship.
