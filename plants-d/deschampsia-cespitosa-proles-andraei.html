Deschampsia cespitosa proles andraei

Deschampsia cespitosa proles andraei, commonly known as the Andrae's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, forming dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
