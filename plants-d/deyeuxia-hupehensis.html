Deyeuxia hupehensis

Deyeuxia hupehensis, commonly known as the Hupeh sedge, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deyeuxia hupehensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
