Dasyglossum fidicularium

Dasyglossum fidicularium, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a striking member of the Gesneriaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, lantern-shaped flowers that display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are particularly noticeable in full sun. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, a feature that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare and exotic flora.
