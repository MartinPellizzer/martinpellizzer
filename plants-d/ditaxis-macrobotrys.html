Ditaxis macrobotrys

Ditaxis macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant red balm, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Ditaxis macrobotrys make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
