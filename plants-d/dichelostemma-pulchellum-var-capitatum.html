Dichelostemma pulchellum var. capitatum

Dichelostemma pulchellum var. capitatum, commonly known as the Sierra Valley lily, is a perennial herb native to California.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces a cluster of flowers at the tip of a single stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
