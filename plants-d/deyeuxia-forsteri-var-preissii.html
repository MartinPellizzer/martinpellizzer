Deyeuxia forsteri var. preissii

Deyeuxia forsteri var. preissii, commonly known as the narrowleaf false rice, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often found in the eastern and southern regions of Australia, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
