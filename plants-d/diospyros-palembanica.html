Diospyros palembanica

Diospyros palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The plant is also valued for its hard wood, which is used in tool-making and construction.
