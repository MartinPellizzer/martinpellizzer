Dendropanax tomentosus

Dendropanax tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swampy areas. Its flowering occurs in late summer, producing small, pale green flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Dendropanax tomentosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
