Doritis pulcherrima var. marmorata

Doritis pulcherrima var. marmorata, commonly known as the marble orchid, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant features fleshy, strap-like leaves that store water, adapting it to its natural habitat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
