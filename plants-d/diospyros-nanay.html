Diospyros nanay

Diospyros nanay, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it an important plant in Philippine flora.
