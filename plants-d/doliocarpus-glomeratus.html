Doliocarpus glomeratus

Doliocarpus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered holly or clustered mangosteen, is a member of the Clusiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences and small, elliptical leaves with a waxy surface. Its fruits are typically small, round, and have a thick, leathery rind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
