Decaneurum

Decaneurum, commonly known as the bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Decaneurum is valued for its early spring bloom and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
