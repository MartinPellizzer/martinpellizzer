Dryopteris arborea

Dryopteris arborea, commonly known as the tree fern, is a species of fern characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of fronds.

It is native to the forests of eastern Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spirally arranged, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Its rhizome is thick and woody, providing structural support as it grows.

Dryopteris arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in shaded, moist environments.
