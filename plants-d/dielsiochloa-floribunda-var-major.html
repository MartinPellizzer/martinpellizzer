Dielsiochloa floribunda var. major

Dielsiochloa floribunda var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered dielsiochloa, is a grass species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers, which are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
