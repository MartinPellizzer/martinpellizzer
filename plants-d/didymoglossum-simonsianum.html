Didymoglossum simonsianum

Didymoglossum simonsianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cotton-grass, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, fluffy inflorescences that produce wind-dispersed seeds. This plant typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs or wet meadows. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain regions.
