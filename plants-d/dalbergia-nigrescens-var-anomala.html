Dalbergia nigrescens var. anomala

Dalbergia nigrescens var. anomala, commonly known as the black rosewood, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated pods.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and susceptibility to environmental stress, making it a rare and ecologically significant plant.
