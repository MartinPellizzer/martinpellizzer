Dianthus hermaeensis

Dianthus hermaeensis, commonly known as the Hermaeus's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapted to the island's unique climate and soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branching.

Dianthus hermaeensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
