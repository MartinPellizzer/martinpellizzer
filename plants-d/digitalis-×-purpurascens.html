Digitalis × purpurascens

Digitalis × purpurascens, commonly known as the scarlet foxglove, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a loose raceme.

Digitalis × purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
