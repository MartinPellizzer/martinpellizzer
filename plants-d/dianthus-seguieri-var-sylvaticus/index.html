Dianthus Seguieri Var. Sylvaticus

Sylvatic dianthus, scientifically known as Dianthus seguieri var. sylvaticus, is a striking member of the carnation family native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. This perennial plant is celebrated for its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. The plant's resilience and ornamental appeal have earned it a place in both horticultural and ecological discussions. With its delicate yet robust nature, sylvatic dianthus remains a beloved symbol of springtime beauty.
