Dipterocarpus palembanicus

Dipterocarpus palembanicus, commonly known as the Palembang dipterocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and heavy, aromatic wood. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and is often found in lowland rainforests. It plays a significant role in the ecosystem as a habitat for various species and contributes to the biodiversity of its native regions.

Its flowers are typically pale yellow and are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.
