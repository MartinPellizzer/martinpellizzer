Dysoda

Dysoda, commonly known as the common dysoda, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species. Dysoda is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
