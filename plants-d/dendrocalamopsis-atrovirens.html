Dendrocalamopsis atrovirens

Dendrocalamopsis atrovirens, commonly known as the black-green bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a clump-forming perennial that grows in dense, upright clusters with slender, dark green culms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating.

Dendrocalamopsis atrovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and structural form.
