Dactylorchis incarnata var. beckii

Dactylorchis incarnata var. beckii, commonly known as the Beck's pink orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This orchid is pollinated primarily by bees and has a unique relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
