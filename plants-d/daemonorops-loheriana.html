Daemonorops loheriana

Daemonorops loheriana, commonly known as the Loher's palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage, which can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
