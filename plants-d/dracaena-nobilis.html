Dracaena nobilis

Dracaena nobilis, commonly known as the Corn Plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its tall, upright stems and long, narrow, dark green leaves that often have a slight yellowish or white border. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries, though flowering is rare in indoor conditions. Dracaena nobilis is known for its ability to thrive in low-light environments and its air-purifying qualities, making it a popular houseplant.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation and use in both residential and commercial settings.
