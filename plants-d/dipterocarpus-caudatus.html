Dipterocarpus caudatus

Dipterocarpus caudatus, commonly known as the long-tailed dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spreading crown and long, drooping branches. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
