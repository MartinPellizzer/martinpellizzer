Dysophylla benthamiana var. intermedia

Dysophylla benthamiana var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate dysophylla, is a variant of the Dysophylla benthamiana species native to certain regions of Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a generally low-growing habit.

It is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to its native habitat.
