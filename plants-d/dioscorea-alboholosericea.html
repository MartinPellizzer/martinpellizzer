Dioscorea alboholosericea

Dioscorea alboholosericea, commonly known as the white-hair yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its edible tubers.
