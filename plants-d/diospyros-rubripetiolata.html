Diospyros rubripetiolata

Diospyros rubripetiolata, commonly known as the red-veined persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, orange to reddish berry that ripens in autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
