Digitalis heldreichii

Digitalis heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes several species known for their ornamental value and medicinal properties. The plant produces tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of hooded, spurred flowers in shades of purple and pink. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a slightly serrated margin, and they are arranged alternately along the stem.

Digitalis heldreichii is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and is also studied for its potential pharmacological applications.
