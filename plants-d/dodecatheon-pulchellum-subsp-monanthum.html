Dodecatheon pulchellum subsp. monanthum

Dodecatheon pulchellum subsp.

monanthum, commonly known as the single-flowered glory-of-the-snow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
