Dipladenia xanthostoma var. major

Dipladenia xanthostoma var. major, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a vigorous, climbing plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.

Botanically, it is a hybrid of Dipladenia and Streptocarpus, belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.
