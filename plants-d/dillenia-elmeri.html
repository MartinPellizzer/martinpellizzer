Dillenia elmeri

Dillenia elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's dillenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, large, and showy flowers that typically bloom in yellow or white. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a large, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds. Dillenia elmeri is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
