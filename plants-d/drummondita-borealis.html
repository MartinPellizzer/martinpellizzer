Drummondita borealis

Drummondita borealis, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in harsh, cold environments, often found in tundra and alpine habitats. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand strong winds and freezing temperatures.

Drummondita borealis plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various Arctic wildlife species.
