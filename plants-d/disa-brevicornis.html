Disa brevicornis

Disa brevicornis, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and clusters of vivid pink or purple flowers with long, narrow petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Disa brevicornis is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms during the summer months.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular ornamental plant among gardeners and botanists alike.
