Dactylorhiza elata var. durandii

Dactylorhiza elata var. durandii, commonly known as the Durand's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and damp woodlands. The plant has long, slender rhizomes and produces tall, erect stems with several oval, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a loose spike, and are characterized by their three-lobed lips.

This orchid is notable for its ability to thrive in moist environments and its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
