Dichondra sericea

Dichondra sericea, commonly known as silverleaf dichondra, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage, which results from the dense covering of fine, silky hairs on its leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its succulent-like leaves allow it to store water, making it drought-tolerant and well-suited for arid environments.

Dichondra sericea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
