Dianthus gredensis

Dianthus gredensis, commonly known as the Gredens rose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Dianthus gredensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
