Draba chrysantha

Draba chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
