Dipterocarpus retusus

Dipterocarpus retusus, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy, and durable wood. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a tall, straight trunk with a buttressed base. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in forest regeneration.
