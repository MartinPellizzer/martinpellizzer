Deuterocohnia longipetala f. uberrima

Deuterocohnia longipetala f. uberrima, commonly known as the showy cushion plant, is a member of the bromeliad family, characterized by its dense, compact rosette structure.

It originates from the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude, sunny environments. The plant features vibrant, showy inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

This cultivar is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
