Diosma tenella

Diosma tenella, commonly known as the slender diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, dry seeds.

Diosma tenella is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
