Dianthus subbarbatus

Dianthus subbarbatus, commonly known as the sand dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and vibrant, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Dianthus subbarbatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
