Daphne acuminata

Daphne acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Daphne acuminata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and winter interest.
