Dioscorea abyssinica

Dioscorea abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian yam, is a climbing vine native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, white or green flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source by local communities.

The species is also valued for its potential in agricultural and medicinal applications.
