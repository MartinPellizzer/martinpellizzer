Dipterocarpus baudii

Dipterocarpus baudii, commonly known as the Bornean dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and tall, straight trunk. The tree produces heavy, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, a common trait among dipterocarps. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and strength, making it important in local economies.

The plant also plays a crucial role in the rainforest ecosystem, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
