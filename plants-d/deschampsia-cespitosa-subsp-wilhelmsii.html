Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. wilhelmsii

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

wilhelmsii, commonly known as the Wilhelmsii bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and forms dense, tufted clumps.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive bluish-gray coloration.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its appearance.
