Dianthus gratianopolitanus var. montanus

Dianthus gratianopolitanus var. montanus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is well adapted to cold, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a species of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
