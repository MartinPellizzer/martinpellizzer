Diuris pulchella

Diuris pulchella, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, hooded flowers with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. The plant typically blooms in spring, with flowers that have a single, prominent lip and spurred labellum. It grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in heathland or open forest environments.

Diuris pulchella is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
