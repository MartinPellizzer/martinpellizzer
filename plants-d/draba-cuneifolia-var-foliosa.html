Draba cuneifolia var. foliosa

Draba cuneifolia var. foliosa, commonly known as the leafy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
