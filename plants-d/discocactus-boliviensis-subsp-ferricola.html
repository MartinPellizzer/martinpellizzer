Discocactus boliviensis subsp. ferricola

Discocactus boliviensis subsp.

ferricola, commonly known as the Bolivian discocactus, is a species of cactus native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is typically green to reddish in color, often with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, which are borne on short pedicels. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

This subspecies is noted for its slow growth and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance.
