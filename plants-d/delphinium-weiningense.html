Delphinium weiningense

Delphinium weiningense, commonly known as the Weining larkspur, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Delphinium weiningense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
