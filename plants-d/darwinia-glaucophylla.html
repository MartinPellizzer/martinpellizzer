Darwinia glaucophylla

Darwinia glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to south-eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Darwinia glaucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
