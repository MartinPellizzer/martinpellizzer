Dahlia purpurea var. lilacina

Dahlia purpurea var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac dahlia, is a hybrid cultivar prized for its vibrant, lilac-pink flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to Mexico, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features sturdy, upright stems with large, showy blooms that can reach up to 8 inches in diameter. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is typically grown as an annual or in regions with mild winters.
