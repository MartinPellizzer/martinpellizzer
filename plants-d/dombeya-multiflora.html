Dombeya multiflora

Dombeya multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Dombeya multiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
