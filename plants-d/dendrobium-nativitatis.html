Dendrobium nativitatis

Dendrobium nativitatis, commonly known as the sacred orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or branches. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Dendrobium nativitatis is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in traditional medicine.
