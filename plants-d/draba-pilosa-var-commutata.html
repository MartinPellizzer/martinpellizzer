Draba pilosa var. commutata

Draba pilosa var. commutata, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are hairy and often lobed. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Draba pilosa var. commutata is adapted to cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness.
