Dioscorea menglaensis

Dioscorea menglaensis, commonly known as Mengla yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The botanical features of D. menglaensis make it an important species for both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
