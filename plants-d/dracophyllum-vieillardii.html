Dracophyllum vieillardii

Dracophyllum vieillardii, commonly known as the Tasmanian heath, is a shrub native to the cool, moist forests of Tasmania.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in acidic soils and is adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat.

Dracophyllum vieillardii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
