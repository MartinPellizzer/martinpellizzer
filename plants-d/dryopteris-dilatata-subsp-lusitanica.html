Dryopteris dilatata subsp. lusitanica

Dryopteris dilatata subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian wood fern, is a perennial fern native to Europe and parts of North Africa. It features fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, somewhat arching growth habit.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its spores are produced in sori that are arranged in a single row on the undersides of the fronds.

The subspecies is characterized by its slightly smaller size and more delicate fronds compared to the typical form of D. dilatata.
