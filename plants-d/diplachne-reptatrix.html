Diplachne reptatrix

Diplachne reptatrix, commonly known as the creeping flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread aggressively across wetland and riparian habitats. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to moist environments and often thrives in areas with standing water or saturated soil.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain ecosystems.
