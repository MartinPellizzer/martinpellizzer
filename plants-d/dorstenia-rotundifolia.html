Dorstenia rotundifolia

Dorstenia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed dodder, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that are circular in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dorstenia rotundifolia is known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
