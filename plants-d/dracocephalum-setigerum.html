Dracocephalum setigerum

Dracocephalum setigerum, commonly known as Persian shield, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, often adorned with silver or white markings. Its flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
