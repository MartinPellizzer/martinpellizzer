Dalea illustris

Dalea illustris, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink to purple blossoms. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Dalea illustris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
