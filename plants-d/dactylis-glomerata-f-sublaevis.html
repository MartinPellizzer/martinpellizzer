Dactylis glomerata f. sublaevis

Dactylis glomerata f. sublaevis, commonly known as the silky oatgrass, is a grass species characterized by its fine, silky texture and dense growth habit.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
