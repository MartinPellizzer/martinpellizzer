Dipterocarpus megacarpus

Dipterocarpus megacarpus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers and distinctive, thick, flaky bark. The plant belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its seeds are enclosed in woody, fleshy fruits that can remain on the tree for several months.

This species is also valued for its timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.
