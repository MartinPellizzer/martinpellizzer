Dioscorea bangii

Dioscorea bangii, commonly known as the Bangi yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by orange or red berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
