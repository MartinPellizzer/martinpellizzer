Digitaria parlatorei var. microstachya

Digitaria parlatorei var. microstachya, commonly known as the small-stalked digit grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It is characterized by its fine, delicate appearance and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
