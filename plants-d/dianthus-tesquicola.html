Dianthus tesquicola

Dianthus tesquicola, commonly known as the desert pink, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant is characterized by its clusters of pink to red, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Dianthus tesquicola is often found growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds, showcasing its resilience to drought and high temperatures.
