Diplopappus pluriflorus

Diplopappus pluriflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered burr plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola. The plant is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a dry, two-parted capsule that releases seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal.

The plant is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
