Dichromena seslerioides

Dichromena seslerioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dichromena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Dichromena seslerioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
