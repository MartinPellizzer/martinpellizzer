Desmodium nitidum

Desmodium nitidum, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Desmodium nitidum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
