Dioscorea scabra

Dioscorea scabra, commonly known as the rough yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also edible when properly prepared, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful processing.

Dioscorea scabra is valued for its medicinal properties and is commonly used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
