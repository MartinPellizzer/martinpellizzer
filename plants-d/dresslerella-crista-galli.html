Dresslerella crista-galli

Dresslerella crista-galli, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable species for soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Dresslerella crista-galli is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
