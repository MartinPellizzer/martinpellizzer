Dolichos brunellii

Dolichos brunellii, commonly known as the horse gram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender, wiry stems that often twine around supports. The plant produces small, green pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and it thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

Dolichos brunellii is widely cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines around the world.
