Drimys brasiliensis var. sylvatica

Drimys brasiliensis var. sylvatica, commonly known as the Brazilian wintergreen, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that emit a spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
