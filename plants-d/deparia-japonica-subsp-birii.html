Deparia japonica subsp. birii

Deparia japonica subsp.

birii, commonly known as the Japanese forest fern, is a perennial fern native to East Asia. It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate fronds.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring rich, well-drained soils. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
