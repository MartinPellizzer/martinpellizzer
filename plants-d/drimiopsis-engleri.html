Drimiopsis engleri

Drimiopsis engleri, commonly known as the Engler's drimiopsis, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Drimiopsis engleri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
