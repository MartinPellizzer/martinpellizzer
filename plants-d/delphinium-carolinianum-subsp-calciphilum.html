Delphinium carolinianum subsp. calciphilum

Delphinium carolinianum subsp.

calciphilum, commonly known as the limestone larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It thrives in calcareous soils, typically found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

The plant features showy blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy flowers and often poisonous nature.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptation to alkaline environments and its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
