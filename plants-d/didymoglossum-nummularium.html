Didymoglossum nummularium

Didymoglossum nummularium, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic properties.
