Digitaria cuyabensis var. glabrescens

Digitaria cuyabensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous digitaria, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairless leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It has narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
