Diospyros bundeyana

Diospyros bundeyana, commonly known as the Bundey's ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony trees, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by small, round fruits that are edible.
