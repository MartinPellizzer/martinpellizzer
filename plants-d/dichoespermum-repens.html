Dichoespermum repens

Dichoespermum repens, commonly known as the wandering candleplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with fleshy, cylindrical stems that can branch out along the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, elongated, spiny fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

Dichoespermum repens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.
