Diotis candidissima

Diotis candidissima, commonly known as the white diotis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
