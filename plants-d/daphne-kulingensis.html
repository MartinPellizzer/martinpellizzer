Daphne kulingensis

Daphne kulingensis, commonly known as Kuling daphne, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Daphne kulingensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.
