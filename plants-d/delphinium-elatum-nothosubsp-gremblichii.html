Delphinium elatum nothosubsp. gremblichii

Delphinium elatum nothosubsp.

gremblichii, commonly known as the Gremblich's larkspur, is a hybrid subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, bearing clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typical of the Delphinium genus, with five sepals that are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to alpine gardens.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations that distinguish it from its parent species.
