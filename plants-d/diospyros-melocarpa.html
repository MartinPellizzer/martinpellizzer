Diospyros melocarpa

Diospyros melocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a sweet, edible pulp.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible fruit.
