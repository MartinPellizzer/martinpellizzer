Digitaria sanguinalis subsp. pakistanica

Digitaria sanguinalis subsp.

pakistanica, commonly known as the Pakistan panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species in its native range.
