Deinandra corymbosa subsp. macrocephala

Deinandra corymbosa subsp.

macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in corymbs.

The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
