Dischidia decipiens

Dischidia decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive fig, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its aerial roots and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden within its foliage. Its unique growth habit allows it to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Dischidia decipiens is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
