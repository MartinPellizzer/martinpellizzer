Dendrobium ashworthiae

Dendrobium ashworthiae, commonly known as the Ashworth's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in traditional floral arrangements due to their beauty and fragrance.

Dendrobium ashworthiae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
