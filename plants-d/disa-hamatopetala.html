Disa hamatopetala

Disa hamatopetala, commonly known as the red-flowered disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a distinctive, elongated spur that extends from the base of the flower. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insect species, enhancing its ecological significance.

Disa hamatopetala is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
