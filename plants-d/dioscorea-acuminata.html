Dioscorea acuminata

Dioscorea acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers and develops tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
