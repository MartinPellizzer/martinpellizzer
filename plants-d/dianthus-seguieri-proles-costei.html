Dianthus seguieri proles costei

Dianthus seguieri proles costei, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it survive in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and long blooming period add ornamental value to garden landscapes.
