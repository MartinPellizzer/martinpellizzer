Diospyros cinnamomoides

Diospyros cinnamomoides, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in crafting and construction.
