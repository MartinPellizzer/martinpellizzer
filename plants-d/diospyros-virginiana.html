Diospyros virginiana

Diospyros virginiana, commonly known as the persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fruits that ripen to a sweet, orange-red color. The fruits are edible when fully ripe and contain astringent tannins when unripe.

Diospyros virginiana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
