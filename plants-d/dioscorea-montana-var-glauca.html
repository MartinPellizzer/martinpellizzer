Dioscorea montana var. glauca

Dioscorea montana var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-stemmed yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its blue-green stem coloration is a distinguishing feature, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The tubers of this variety are sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
