Didymoplexis fimbriata

Didymoplexis fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, greenish-white flowers with fringed petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new shoot at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
