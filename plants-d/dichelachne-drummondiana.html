Dichelachne drummondiana

Dichelachne drummondiana, commonly known as Drummond's dichelachne, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense mats or tufts. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed florets. It is often found in sandy or disturbed soils, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
