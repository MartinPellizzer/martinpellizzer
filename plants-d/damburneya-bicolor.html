Damburneya bicolor

Damburneya bicolor, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant has leathery, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and remain on the plant year-round. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Damburneya bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
