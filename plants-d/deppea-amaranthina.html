Deppea amaranthina

Deppea amaranthina, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often displaying vibrant red or pink hues.

Deppea amaranthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
