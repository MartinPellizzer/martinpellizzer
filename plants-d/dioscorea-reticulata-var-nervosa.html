Dioscorea reticulata var. nervosa

Dioscorea reticulata var. nervosa, commonly known as the net-veined yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its complex, reticulate venation pattern on its leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in clusters, followed by the development of its edible tubers.
