Diospyros truncatifolia

Diospyros truncatifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and elliptical leaves with a truncate or notched base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
