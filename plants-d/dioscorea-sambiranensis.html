Dioscorea sambiranensis

Dioscorea sambiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar yam, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces berries that contain the seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
