Didymopanax simplicifolius

Didymopanax simplicifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the golden palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by orange to red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
