Deyeuxia columbiana

Deyeuxia columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia false brome, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant has slender, leafy stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue.

Deyeuxia columbiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
