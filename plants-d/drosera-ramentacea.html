Drosera ramentacea

Drosera ramentacea, commonly known as the hairy sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are small and usually pink or red, appearing in clusters.

Drosera ramentacea is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist conditions and requires careful cultivation to maintain its health.
