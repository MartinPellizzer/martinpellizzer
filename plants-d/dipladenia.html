Dipladenia

Dipladenia, commonly known as the "trailing gloxinia," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. Dipladenia species typically have heart-shaped leaves and produce clusters of showy blossoms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative trailing plant in hanging baskets or containers.

It requires well-draining soil and regular watering to maintain healthy growth and blooming.
