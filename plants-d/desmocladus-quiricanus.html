Desmocladus quiricanus

Desmocladus quiricanus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, blue-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been traditionally used by indigenous peoples for fiber and medicinal purposes.

The species is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
