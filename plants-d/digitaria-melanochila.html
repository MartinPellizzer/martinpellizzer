Digitaria melanochila

Digitaria melanochila, commonly known as blackstem grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Digitaria melanochila is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
