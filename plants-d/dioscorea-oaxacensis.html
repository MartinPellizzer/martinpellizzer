Dioscorea oaxacensis

Dioscorea oaxacensis, commonly known as the Oaxacan yam, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers are valued for their starchy texture and are used in traditional cuisines.
