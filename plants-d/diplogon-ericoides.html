Diplogon ericoides

Diplogon ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
