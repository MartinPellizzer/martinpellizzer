Diasperus gracilipes

Diasperus gracilipes, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh environments through its ability to store water in its fleshy stems. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, reducing water loss.

Diasperus gracilipes is also known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds that deter herbivores.
