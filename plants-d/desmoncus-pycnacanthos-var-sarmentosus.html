Desmoncus pycnacanthos var. sarmentosus

Desmoncus pycnacanthos var. sarmentosus, commonly known as the creeping flame vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically orange or red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant floral display and ability to cover trellises or walls.
