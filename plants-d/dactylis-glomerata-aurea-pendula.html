Dactylis glomerata aurea-pendula

Dactylis glomerata aurea-pendula, commonly known as the golden weeping grass, is a cultivar of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery inflorescences that give it a weeping appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a subtle golden hue along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various growing conditions.
