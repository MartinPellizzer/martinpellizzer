Dysoxylum lessertianum var. parvifolium

Dysoxylum lessertianum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching trunk and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The flowers are typically greenish-white and arranged in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and has some traditional uses in local communities.
