Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. brevifolia

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as silverhair grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and dense, clumping growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pink to reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
