Drymocallis rupestris subsp. banatica

Drymocallis rupestris subsp.

banatica, commonly known as the Banat mountain aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region. It typically grows in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in mountainous areas with high altitudes.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers in the summer months. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh, alpine conditions.
