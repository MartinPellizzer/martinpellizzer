Dalea greggii

Dalea greggii, commonly known as Gregg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Dalea greggii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
