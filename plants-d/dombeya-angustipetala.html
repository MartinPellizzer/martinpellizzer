Dombeya angustipetala

Dombeya angustipetala, commonly known as narrow-petalled dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Dombeya angustipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
