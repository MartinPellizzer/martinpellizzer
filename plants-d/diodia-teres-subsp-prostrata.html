Diodia teres subsp. prostrata

Diodia teres subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as prostrate diodia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a low-growing perennial with prostrate or decumbent stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers clustered in umbels, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it typically grows in moist, sandy soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
