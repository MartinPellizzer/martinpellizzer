Diplopappus turkestanicus

Diplopappus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan burweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite leaves that are usually narrow and slightly hairy. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, hooked seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Diplopappus turkestanicus is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting well to dry and sandy soils.
