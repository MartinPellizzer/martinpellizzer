Draba turczaninowii var. trichocarpa

Draba turczaninowii var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the trichocarpous whitlow grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the mustard family. It is native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia, thriving in rocky and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to cold, nutrient-poor environments.
