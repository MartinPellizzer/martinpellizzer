Dianthus ferrugineus

Dianthus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rust-colored campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with a distinctive rust-colored hue on the petals.

It is often found in coastal areas and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
