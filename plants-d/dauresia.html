Dauresia

Dauresia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. Dauresia has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
