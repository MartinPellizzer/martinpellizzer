Digitaria sejuncta

Digitaria sejuncta, commonly known as the slender digit grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

Digitaria sejuncta is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soil environments.
