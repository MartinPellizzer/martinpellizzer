Desmoncus mitis subsp. ecirratus

Desmoncus mitis subsp.

ecirratus, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a clustered inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
