Delphinium anthriscifolium var. majus

Delphinium anthriscifolium var. majus, commonly known as the tall larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in dense spikes atop sturdy stems. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its toxicity, care must be taken when handling or cultivating this plant.
