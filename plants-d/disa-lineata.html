Disa lineata

Disa lineata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, slender stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, are known for their unique, zigzag-shaped petals.

Disa lineata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
