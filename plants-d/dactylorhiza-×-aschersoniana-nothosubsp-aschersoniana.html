Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana nothosubsp.

aschersoniana, commonly known as the ascherson's marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and marshes. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
