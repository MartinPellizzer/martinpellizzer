Diospyros fengchangensis

Diospyros fengchangensis, commonly known as the Fengchang persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to reddish fruits. Its fruits are typically sweet and astringent, containing astringent tannins.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
