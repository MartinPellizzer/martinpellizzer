Dodsonia falcata

Dodsonia falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dodsonia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its small white flowers are arranged in compound umbels, which contribute to its ornamental value.

Dodsonia falcata is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
