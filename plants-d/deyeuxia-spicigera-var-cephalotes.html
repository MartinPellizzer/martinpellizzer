Deyeuxia spicigera var. cephalotes

Deyeuxia spicigera var. cephalotes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy habitats in Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in saturated soils and is often found in areas with seasonal flooding.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
