Datura humilis

Datura humilis, commonly known as the common jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom at night. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Datura humilis is known for its toxic properties, containing tropane alkaloids such as scopolamine and hyoscyamine.
