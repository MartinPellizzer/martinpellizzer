Dichilus floribundus

Dichilus floribundus, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters, often in the axils of the leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
