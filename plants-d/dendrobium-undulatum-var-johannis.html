Dendrobium undulatum var. johannis

Dendrobium undulatum var. johannis, commonly known as the Johannes' Wavyleaf Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences and vibrant, often yellow or orange flowers with intricate patterns. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in varying light conditions.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its striking floral displays.
