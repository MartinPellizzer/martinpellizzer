Dianthus novakii

Dianthus novakii, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Dianthus novakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
