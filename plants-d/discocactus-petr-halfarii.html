Discocactus petr-halfarii

Discocactus petr-halfarii, commonly known as the Petr-halfarii discocactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
