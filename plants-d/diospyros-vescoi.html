Diospyros vescoi

Diospyros vescoi, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
