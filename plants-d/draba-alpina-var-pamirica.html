Draba alpina var. pamirica

Draba alpina var. pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir sandwort, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. It has a low-growing habit with basal leaves and is often found in mountainous areas with poor soil.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat.
