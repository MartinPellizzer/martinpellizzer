Delphinium ehrenbergii

Delphinium ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium ehrenbergii thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
