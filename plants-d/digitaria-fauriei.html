Digitaria fauriei

Digitaria fauriei, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like structures that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Digitaria fauriei plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
