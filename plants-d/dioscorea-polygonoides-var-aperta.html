Dioscorea polygonoides var. aperta

Dioscorea polygonoides var. aperta, commonly known as the open-flowered yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically open and showy, which is a distinguishing feature of the varietal form. Its tuberous roots are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes.

The species is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories.
