Delissea grimesiana var. hirsutifolia

Delissea grimesiana var. hirsutifolia, commonly known as the hirsute delissea, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or hirsute, foliage, which gives it its botanical variant name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinctive waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
