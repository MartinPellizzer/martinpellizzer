Diasperus desmocarpus

Diasperus desmocarpus, commonly known as the desert thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has a robust, branching stem covered in spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Diasperus desmocarpus is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep roots and a thick cuticle on its leaves.
