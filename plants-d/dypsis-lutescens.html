Dypsis lutescens

Dypsis lutescens, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive indoors.

Dypsis lutescens is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
