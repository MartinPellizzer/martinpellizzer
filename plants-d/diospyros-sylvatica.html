Diospyros sylvatica

Diospyros sylvatica, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red berries in the fall. Its fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Diospyros sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
