Durieua graeca

Durieua graeca, commonly known as the Greek durian, is a tropical plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spiky fruit that resembles the durian but is smaller and less pungent. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and features a thick, fibrous stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow with a greenish tint and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is edible, though its texture and flavor can vary depending on the maturity and growing conditions.
