Desmodium umbellatum

Desmodium umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Desmodium umbellatum is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
