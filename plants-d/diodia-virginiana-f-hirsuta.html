Diodia virginiana f. hirsuta

Diodia virginiana f. hirsuta, commonly known as hairyleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy pink to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
