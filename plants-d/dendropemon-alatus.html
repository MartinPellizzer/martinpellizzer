Dendropemon alatus

Dendropemon alatus, commonly known as the winged orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, wing-like structures on its stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Dendropemon alatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
