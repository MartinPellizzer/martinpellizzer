Dryopteris glaziovii

Dryopteris glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, upright growth habit and a deep green color. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
