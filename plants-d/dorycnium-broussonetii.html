Dorycnium broussonetii

Dorycnium broussonetii, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Dorycnium broussonetii plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for forage.
