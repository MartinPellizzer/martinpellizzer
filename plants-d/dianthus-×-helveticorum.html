Dianthus × helveticorum

Dianthus × helveticorum, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in late summer.

Dianthus × helveticorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
