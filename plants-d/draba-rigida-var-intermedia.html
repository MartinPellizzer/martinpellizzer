Draba rigida var. intermedia

Draba rigida var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rigid whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has a basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves and produces yellow or white flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
