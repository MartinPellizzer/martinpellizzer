Drummondita rubroviridis

Drummondita rubroviridis, commonly known as the red-veined flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in loose spikes. Drummondita rubroviridis is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
