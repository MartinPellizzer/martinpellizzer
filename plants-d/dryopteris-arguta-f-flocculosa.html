Dryopteris arguta f. flocculosa

Dryopteris arguta f. flocculosa, commonly known as the fluff-soft fern, is a variant of the broad shield fern.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically lance-shaped and can reach lengths of up to 60 cm.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and shaded landscapes.
