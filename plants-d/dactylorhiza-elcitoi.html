Dactylorhiza elcitoi

Dactylorhiza elcitoi, commonly known as the Ellesmere orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the British Isles.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland and heathland habitats. It blooms from June to August and is known for its delicate, fragrant flowers.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and protected species in the UK.
