Dacrydium intermedium var. gracile

Dacrydium intermedium var. gracile, commonly known as the slender rimu, is a small evergreen conifer native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its cones are small and usually borne in clusters, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
