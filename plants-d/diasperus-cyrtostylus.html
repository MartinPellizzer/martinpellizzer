Diasperus cyrtostylus

Diasperus cyrtostylus, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions.
