Didymocarpus asperifolius

Didymocarpus asperifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing edible, sour-tasting seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for its medicinal properties.
