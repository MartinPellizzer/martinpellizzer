Dipterocarpus rigidus

Dipterocarpus rigidus, commonly known as the hard wood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its sturdy, heavy wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
