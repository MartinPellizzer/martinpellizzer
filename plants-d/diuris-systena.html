Diuris systena

Diuris systena, commonly known as the pink-streaked orchid, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rosette of linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, typically with three to five showy, pinkish-brown flowers that have a distinctive striped pattern. It blooms during the spring and summer months, relying on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The plant is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathland or woodland environments.
