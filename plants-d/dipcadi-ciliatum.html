Dipcadi ciliatum

Dipcadi ciliatum, commonly known as the woolly bulbous orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, bulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
