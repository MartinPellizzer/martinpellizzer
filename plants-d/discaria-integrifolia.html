Discaria integrifolia

Discaria integrifolia, commonly known as the common discaria, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences and are usually white or pale yellow.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
