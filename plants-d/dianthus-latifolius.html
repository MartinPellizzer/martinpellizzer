Dianthus latifolius

Dianthus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the top of slender stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Dianthus latifolius is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in gardens for its ornamental value.
