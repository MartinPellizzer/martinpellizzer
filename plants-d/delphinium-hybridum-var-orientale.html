Delphinium hybridum var. orientale

Delphinium hybridum var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental delphinium, is a tall, showy plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking spires of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and cottage gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a flowering structure known as a raceme.
