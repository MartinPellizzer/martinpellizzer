Diospyros mazelii

Diospyros mazelii, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a sweet, edible persimmon-like berry that ripens to a deep orange-red color.

Diospyros mazelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
