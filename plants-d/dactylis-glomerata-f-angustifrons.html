Dactylis glomerata f. angustifrons

Dactylis glomerata f. angustifrons, commonly known as narrow-leaved couch grass, is a variant of the common couch grass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands, pastures, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable forage plant and a common component of native vegetation.
