Deschampsia cespitosa var. mannii

Deschampsia cespitosa var. mannii, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
