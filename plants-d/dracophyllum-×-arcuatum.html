Dracophyllum × arcuatum

Dracophyllum × arcuatum, commonly known as the hybrid dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden air, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal gardens.

Dracophyllum × arcuatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
