Deutzia parviflora var. pilosa

Deutzia parviflora var. pilosa, commonly known as the fuzzy deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, grayish-green foliage that adds texture to gardens, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
