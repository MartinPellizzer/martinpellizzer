Dianthus flabellatus

Dianthus flabellatus, commonly known as the fringed campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fringed petals that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dianthus flabellatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
