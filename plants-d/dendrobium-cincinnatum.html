Dendrobium cincinnatum

Dendrobium cincinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, and it produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers.

Dendrobium cincinnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate blooms.
