Dalea feayi

Dalea feayi, commonly known as the Feay's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native range.

Dalea feayi is often found in open grasslands and along rocky slopes.
