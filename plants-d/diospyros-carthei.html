Diospyros carthei

Diospyros carthei, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. Its fruit is a sweet, edible berry that ripens in the fall, often with a distinctive honey-like flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
