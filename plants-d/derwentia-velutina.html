Derwentia velutina

Derwentia velutina, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or brown, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Derwentia velutina thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy environments.
