Dactylorchis munbyana

Dactylorchis munbyana, commonly known as the Munby's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in the soil, while its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped.

Dactylorchis munbyana is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
