Dicoma scoparia

Dicoma scoparia, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments. Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Dicoma scoparia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
