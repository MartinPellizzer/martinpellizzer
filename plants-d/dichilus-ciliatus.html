Dichilus ciliatus

Dichilus ciliatus, commonly known as the silky sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, also known as the legume or bean family. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves with finely toothed margins. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.
