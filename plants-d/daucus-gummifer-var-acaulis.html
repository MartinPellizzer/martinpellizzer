Daucus gummifer var. acaulis

Daucus gummifer var. acaulis, commonly known as the hollow root parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
