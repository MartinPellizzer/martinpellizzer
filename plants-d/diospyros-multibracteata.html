Diospyros multibracteata

Diospyros multibracteata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, orange to reddish berry that ripens in autumn and is edible when fully mature.
