Dendrobium legareiense

Dendrobium legareiense, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dendrobium genus, which is part of the Orchidaceae family, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. It typically blooms in the summer, producing white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
