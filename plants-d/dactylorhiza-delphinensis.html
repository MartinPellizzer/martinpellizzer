Dactylorhiza delphinensis

Dactylorhiza delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphin's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single spike of pale green to yellowish flowers, which are often tinged with purple. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and bogs.

Dactylorhiza delphinensis is known for its ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators and its role in maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
