Dendrochilum crassifolium

Dendrochilum crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed tree orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to store water in its natural habitat. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
