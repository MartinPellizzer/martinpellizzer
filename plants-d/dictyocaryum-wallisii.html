Dictyocaryum wallisii

Dictyocaryum wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration due to high chlorophyll content.

Dictyocaryum wallisii is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, showcasing resilience to salt spray and wind.
