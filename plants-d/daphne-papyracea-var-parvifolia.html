Daphne papyracea var. parvifolia

Daphne papyracea var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring before the leaves emerge. The foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. This plant is noted for its fragrant blossoms and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
