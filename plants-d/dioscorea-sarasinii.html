Dioscorea sarasinii

Dioscorea sarasinii, commonly known as Sarasin's yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and unisexual flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in some regions for both its aesthetic and practical uses.
