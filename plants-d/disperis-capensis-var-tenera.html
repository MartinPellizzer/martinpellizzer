Disperis capensis var. tenera

Disperis capensis var. tenera, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy leaves that are often glandular. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
