Diplotaxis intricata

Diplotaxis intricata, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Diplotaxis intricata is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
