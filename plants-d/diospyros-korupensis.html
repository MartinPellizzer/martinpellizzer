Diospyros korupensis

It is native to the rainforests of Cameroon, specifically the Korup National Park, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Diospyros korupensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential economic value.
