Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. hispanica

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to brown, with the flowers being small and wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in various environments.
