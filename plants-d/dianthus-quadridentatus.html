Dianthus quadridentatus

Dianthus quadridentatus, commonly known as the four-toothed campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers with five petals. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, four-lobed seed capsules.

Dianthus quadridentatus is often found in open habitats such as hillsides and coastal areas.
