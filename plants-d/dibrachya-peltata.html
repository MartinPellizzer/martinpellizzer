Dibrachya peltata

Dibrachya peltata, commonly known as the velvet leaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Dibrachya peltata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in its natural habitat.
