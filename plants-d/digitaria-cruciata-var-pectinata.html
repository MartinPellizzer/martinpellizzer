Digitaria cruciata var. pectinata

Digitaria cruciata var. pectinata, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
