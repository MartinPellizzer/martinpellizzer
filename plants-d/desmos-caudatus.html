Desmos caudatus

Desmos caudatus, commonly known as the Cape rose or Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its slender, often trailing stems. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, with a distinct sweet fragrance. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs.

Desmos caudatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
