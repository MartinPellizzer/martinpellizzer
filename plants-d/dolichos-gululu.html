Dolichos gululu

Dolichos gululu, commonly known as the African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated pods containing edible seeds. Dolichos gululu is valued for its nutritional content, providing protein and essential nutrients.

It is often cultivated in warm climates for both food and fodder purposes.
