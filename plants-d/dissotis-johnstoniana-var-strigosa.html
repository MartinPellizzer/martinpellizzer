Dissotis johnstoniana var. strigosa

Dissotis johnstoniana var. strigosa, commonly known as the Johnston's sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grasses and sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in areas with high humidity and abundant moisture. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The varietal form strigosa is distinguished by its hairy or pubescent surfaces, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
