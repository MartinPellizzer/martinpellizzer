Dolichos falcatus

Dolichos falcatus, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, curved pods that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is often grown for its edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Dolichos falcatus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in crop rotation systems.
