Distichella opuntioides

Distichella opuntioides, commonly known as the Opuntia-like cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Bolivia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its fleshy, spineless stems that are often segmented and covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
