Drosera buubugujin

Drosera buubugujin, commonly known as the Gubugujin Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of northern Australia.

It features long, slender leaves covered in sticky, glandular hairs that trap and digest insects. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in a dense, umbellate cluster. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its unique adaptations make it an intriguing subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
