Dendrocalamus

Dendrocalamus, commonly known as bamboo, is a genus of tall, woody plants belonging to the grass family.

These plants are characterized by their hollow, jointed stems and rapid growth rates, making them highly versatile. Dendrocalamus species are native to Southeast Asia and are often found in tropical and subtropical regions. They play a vital role in ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.

Their culms are widely used in construction, papermaking, and as a source of food.
