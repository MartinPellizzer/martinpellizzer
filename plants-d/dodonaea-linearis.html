Dodonaea linearis

Dodonaea linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf hoppegrog, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Dodonaea linearis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
