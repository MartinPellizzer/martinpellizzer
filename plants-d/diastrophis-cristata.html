Diastrophis cristata

Diastrophis cristata, commonly known as the crested diastrophis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tufted foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative plant.

Diastrophis cristata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its visual appeal.
