Davallia rigida

Davallia rigida, commonly known as the rigid hartiya, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically light green to brown in color. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces short, upright stipes with lanceolate, serrated leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Davallia rigida is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
