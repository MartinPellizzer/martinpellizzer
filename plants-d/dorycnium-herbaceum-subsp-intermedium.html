Dorycnium herbaceum subsp. intermedium

Dorycnium herbaceum subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable forage plant and a subject of ecological interest.
