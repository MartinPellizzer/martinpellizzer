Dianthus purpureus

Dianthus purpureus, commonly known as the purple campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, rounded seed pods that split open to disperse seeds.

Dianthus purpureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
