Dimeria pusilla var. lawsonii

Dimeria pusilla var. lawsonii, commonly known as the Lawson's dwarf dimeria, is a small, mat-forming succulent native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact growth with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by translucent, globe-shaped fruits. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in collections of rare succulents.
