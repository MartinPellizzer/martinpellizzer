Doryanthes guilfoylei

Doryanthes guilfoylei, commonly known as the Mount Buffalo doryanthus, is a striking plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
