Dalea phymatodes

Dalea phymatodes, commonly known as the Texas milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant has small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Dalea phymatodes plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
