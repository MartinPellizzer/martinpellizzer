Disa alinae

Disa alinae, commonly known as the alinae orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

Disa alinae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
