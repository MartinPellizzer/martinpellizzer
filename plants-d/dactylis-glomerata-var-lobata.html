Dactylis glomerata var. lobata

Dactylis glomerata var. lobata, commonly known as the sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in pasture and erosion control.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a valuable forage crop.
