Dalea enneaphylla

Dalea enneaphylla, commonly known as the nine-leafed false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of nine pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea enneaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive spring blooms.
