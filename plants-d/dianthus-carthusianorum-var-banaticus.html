Dianthus carthusianorum var. banaticus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. banaticus, commonly known as the Banat carnation, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, double blooms that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.

The species is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine.
