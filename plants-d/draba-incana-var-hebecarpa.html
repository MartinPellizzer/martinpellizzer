Draba incana var. hebecarpa

Draba incana var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the slender whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically forms dense cushions or mats, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, often bluish-green leaves that are usually basal and persistent throughout the growing season. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with four petals and numerous stamens, characteristic of the Brassicaceae family.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
