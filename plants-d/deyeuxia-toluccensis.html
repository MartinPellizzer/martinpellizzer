Deyeuxia toluccensis

Deyeuxia toluccensis, commonly known as the Toluca grass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed florets.

Deyeuxia toluccensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
