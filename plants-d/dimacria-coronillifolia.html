Dimacria coronillifolia

Dimacria coronillifolia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, reddish-brown bark and trifoliate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Dimacria coronillifolia is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and its role in supporting local wildlife.
