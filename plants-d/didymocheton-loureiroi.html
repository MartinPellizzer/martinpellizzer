Didymocheton loureiroi

Didymocheton loureiroi, commonly known as the white sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
