Drymocallis arguta

Drymocallis arguta, commonly known as silverleaf ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features silver-gray foliage due to dense, fine hairs covering its leaves. It produces yellow flowers in composite heads, which are arranged in clusters at the top of the stems.

Drymocallis arguta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive appearance.
