Diphyllopodium klainei

Diphyllopodium klainei, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Diphyllopodium klainei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
