Deutzia parviflora f. amurensis

Deutzia parviflora f. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur dwarf deutzia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has slender, arching stems and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
