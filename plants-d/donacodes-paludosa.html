Donacodes paludosa

Donacodes paludosa, commonly known as the bog moss, is a species of moss that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and is often found in peat bogs and other moist, acidic soils. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It plays an important role in the ecological succession of wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and contributing to peat formation.

Donacodes paludosa is also valued for its ability to retain water, which helps in maintaining the hydrological balance of its habitat.
