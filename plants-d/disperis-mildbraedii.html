Disperis mildbraedii

Disperis mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the summer months. It has fibrous roots and is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Disperis mildbraedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
