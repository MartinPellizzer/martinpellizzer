Doria campanulata

Doria campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped doria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes various bellflowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Doria campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
