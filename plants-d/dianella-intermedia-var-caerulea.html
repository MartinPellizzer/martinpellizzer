Dianella intermedia var. caerulea

Dianella intermedia var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered flax lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are a vibrant blue-green in color, adding a unique visual element to gardens. The plant produces clusters of delicate, sky-blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.
