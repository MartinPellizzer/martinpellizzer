Delphinium taipaicum

Delphinium taipaicum, commonly known as the Taiwan larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated margins, and its roots are fibrous and tuberous.

Delphinium taipaicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
