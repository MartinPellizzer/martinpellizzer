Draba laurentiana

Draba laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the late spring and early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba laurentiana is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or tundra habitats.
