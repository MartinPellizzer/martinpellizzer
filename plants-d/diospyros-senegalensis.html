Diospyros senegalensis

Diospyros senegalensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a sweet, astringent pulp. Its fruits are edible and often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
