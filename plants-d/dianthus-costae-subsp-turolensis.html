Dianthus costae subsp. turolensis

Dianthus costae subsp.

turolensis, commonly known as the Turolensis campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial with pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known species like carnations and sweet William.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
