Doronicum lessingianum

Doronicum lessingianum, commonly known as the Alpine leopard's ear, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Alps.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble a leopard's paw. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
