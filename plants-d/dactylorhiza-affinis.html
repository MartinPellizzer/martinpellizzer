Dactylorhiza affinis

Dactylorhiza affinis, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with a slender, upright stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in a spike and lacking petals. It grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.
