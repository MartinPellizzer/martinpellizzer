Dendrobium obyrnei

Dendrobium obyrnei, commonly known as the Singapore orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive red throat, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium obyrnei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
