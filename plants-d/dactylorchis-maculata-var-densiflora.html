Dactylorchis maculata var. densiflora

Dactylorchis maculata var. densiflora, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with purple markings. Its inflorescence is dense, giving it the species name *densiflora*, meaning "dense flowered.".

The orchid is pollinated by bees and is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
