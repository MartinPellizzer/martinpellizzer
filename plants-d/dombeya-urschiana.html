Dombeya urschiana

Dombeya urschiana, commonly known as the bear's breech, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with toothed margins, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.

Dombeya urschiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
