Deparia × nanchuanensis

Deparia × nanchuanensis, commonly known as the Chinese forest fern, is a hybrid fern species native to China.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
