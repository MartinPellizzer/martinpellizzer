Diplacus longiflorus

Diplacus longiflorus, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in woodland and chaparral habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, which are attracted to its nectar-rich structure.
