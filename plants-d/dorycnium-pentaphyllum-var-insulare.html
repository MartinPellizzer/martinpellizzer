Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. insulare

Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. insulare, commonly known as the island sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland margins. The plant features a robust root system and erect, branching stems that are covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five pairs of leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in agriculture.
