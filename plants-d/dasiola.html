Dasiola

Dasiola, commonly known as the fairy sapphire, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. Dasiola is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant collections.
