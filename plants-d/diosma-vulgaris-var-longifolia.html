Diosma vulgaris var. longifolia

Diosma vulgaris var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
