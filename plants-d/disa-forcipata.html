Disa forcipata

Disa forcipata, commonly known as the clawed disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vivid, spurred flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a contrasting lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure includes a specialized spur that stores nectar, attracting specific pollinators.

Disa forcipata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native environment.
