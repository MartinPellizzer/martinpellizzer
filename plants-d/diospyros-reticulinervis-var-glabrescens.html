Diospyros reticulinervis var. glabrescens

Diospyros reticulinervis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed persimmon, is a variant of the reticulate-veined persimmon native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red, edible fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests or along stream banks.

The fruit is valued for its nutritional content and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
