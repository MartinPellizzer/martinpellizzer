Dimorphanthera microphylla

Dimorphanthera microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and green. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is characterized by its unique, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

Dimorphanthera microphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
