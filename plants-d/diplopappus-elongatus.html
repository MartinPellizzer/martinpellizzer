Diplopappus elongatus

Diplopappus elongatus, commonly known as the longpod pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, elongated phyllaries that enclose the flower head, giving it a unique appearance. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, producing small, yellow flowers.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
