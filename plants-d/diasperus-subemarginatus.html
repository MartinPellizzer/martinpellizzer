Diasperus subemarginatus

Diasperus subemarginatus, commonly known as the narrow-margin rock cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
