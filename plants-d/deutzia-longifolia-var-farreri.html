Deutzia longifolia var. farreri

Deutzia longifolia var. farreri, commonly known as the Farrer deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to woodland or cottage garden settings.
