Dendrobium andersonii

Dendrobium andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often in clusters. It thrives in humid, well-lit environments with good air circulation.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
