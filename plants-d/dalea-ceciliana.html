Dalea ceciliana

Dalea ceciliana, commonly known as Cecil's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
