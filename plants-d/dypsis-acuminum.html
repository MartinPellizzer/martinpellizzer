Dypsis acuminum

Dypsis acuminum, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its short, thick trunk and clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Dypsis acuminum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
