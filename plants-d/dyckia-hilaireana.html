Dyckia hilaireana

Dyckia hilaireana, commonly known as the Hilaire's dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette of stiff, blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, violet flowers, which are typically short-lived. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, Dyckia hilaireana is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
