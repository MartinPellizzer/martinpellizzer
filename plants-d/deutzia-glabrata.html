Deutzia glabrata

Deutzia glabrata, commonly known as smooth deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has simple, opposite leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it thrives in well-drained soil in partial to full sun.

Deutzia glabrata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
