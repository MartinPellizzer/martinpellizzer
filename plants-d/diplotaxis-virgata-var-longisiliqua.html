Diplotaxis virgata var. longisiliqua

Diplotaxis virgata var. longisiliqua, commonly known as the long silique wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and distinctive elongated, narrow pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
