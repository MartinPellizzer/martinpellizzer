Dypsis schatzii

Dypsis schatzii, commonly known as the Schatz's areca palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its pinnate leaves and smooth, grayish trunk. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive coconut-like fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Dypsis schatzii is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
