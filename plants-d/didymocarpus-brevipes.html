Didymocarpus brevipes

Didymocarpus brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a thick-skinned berry that contains white, segmented arils.

The botanical features of Didymocarpus brevipes make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
