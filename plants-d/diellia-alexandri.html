Diellia alexandri

Diellia alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian diellia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Diellia alexandri is known for its resilience and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature.
