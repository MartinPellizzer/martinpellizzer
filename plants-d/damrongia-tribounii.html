Damrongia tribounii

Damrongia tribounii, commonly known as the Cambodian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets, and its bark is smooth and light brown in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
