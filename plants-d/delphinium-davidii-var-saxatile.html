Delphinium davidii var. saxatile

Delphinium davidii var. saxatile, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in raceme-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
