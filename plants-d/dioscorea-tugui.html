Dioscorea tugui

Dioscorea tugui, commonly known as Tugui yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The tubers of Dioscorea tugui are used in traditional cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
