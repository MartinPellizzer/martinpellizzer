Diplachne saccharoides

Diplachne saccharoides, commonly known as sugar grass, is a perennial grass native to the subtropical and tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, often tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Diplachne saccharoides is often found in disturbed habitats, including roadsides, fields, and along riverbanks.
