Dianthus barbatus var. angustifolius

Dianthus barbatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, with petals that are often fringed or notched. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows and rocky areas, and is often found in disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
