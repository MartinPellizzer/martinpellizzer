Delphinium scaposum var. andersonii

Delphinium scaposum var. andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.
