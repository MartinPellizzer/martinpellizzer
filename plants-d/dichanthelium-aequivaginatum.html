Dichanthelium aequivaginatum

Dichanthelium aequivaginatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive narrow, blade-like appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike with closely packed spikelets.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of two bristles at the base of each spikelet.
