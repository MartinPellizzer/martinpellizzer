Dianthus macrolepis

Dianthus macrolepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Dianthus macrolepis is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.
