Daucus platycarpos

Daucus platycarpos, commonly known as the broad-fruited carrot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed structure that contains several seeds.
