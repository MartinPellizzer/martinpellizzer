Desmodium novae-hollandiae f. latifolium

Desmodium novae-hollandiae f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf desmodium, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Australia.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in summer. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
