Desmanthus brevipes

Desmanthus brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed burr legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. The plant is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Desmanthus brevipes is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
