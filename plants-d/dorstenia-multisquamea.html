Dorstenia multisquamea

Dorstenia multisquamea, commonly known as the squamose mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a greenish hue. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dorstenia multisquamea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
