Dictamnus fraxinella var. europaeus

Dictamnus fraxinella var. europaeus, commonly known as European dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that exudes a strong, spicy scent when broken. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
