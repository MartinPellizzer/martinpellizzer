Dodonaea lucida

Dodonaea lucida, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Dodonaea lucida is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
