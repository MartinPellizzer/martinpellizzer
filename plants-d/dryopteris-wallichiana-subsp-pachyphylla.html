Dryopteris wallichiana subsp. pachyphylla

Dryopteris wallichiana subsp.

pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaf maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its large, leathery, and often deeply lobed fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, elegant appearance with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in many horticultural gardens.
