Diervilla sanguinea var. versicolor

Diervilla sanguinea var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that persist through winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring.

The variety 'versicolor' is distinguished by its variable leaf coloration, ranging from green to reddish hues.
