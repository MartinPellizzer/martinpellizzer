Dioscorea amaranthoides var. metallica

Dioscorea amaranthoides var. metallica, commonly known as the silver amaranth yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its unique silver-tinged foliage makes it a distinctive ornamental plant in gardens.

The tubers are also valued for their nutritional content and potential medicinal properties.
