Drypetes perakensis

Drypetes perakensis, commonly known as the Borneo ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is often found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests.
