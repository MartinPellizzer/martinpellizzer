Dalea frutescens var. laxa

Dalea frutescens var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils, often in desert or steppe habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

The species is adapted to arid environments and exhibits drought tolerance through its deep root system.
