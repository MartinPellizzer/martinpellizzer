Dryopteris schwackeana

Dryopteris schwackeana, commonly known as the Schwacke's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The fronds are deeply divided into lance-shaped pinnae, with a distinctive dark green color and a slightly curled appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
