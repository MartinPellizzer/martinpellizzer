Dioclea jacquiniana

Dioclea jacquiniana, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom throughout the warm seasons. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Dioclea jacquiniana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
