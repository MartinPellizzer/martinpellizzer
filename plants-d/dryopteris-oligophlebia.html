Dryopteris oligophlebia

Dryopteris oligophlebia, commonly known as the narrow-veined wood fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds with a distinctive pattern of narrow, parallel veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizome is covered in a dense layer of brown, hairy scales.

The fronds are usually evergreen, retaining their foliage throughout the year.
