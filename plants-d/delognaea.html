Delognaea

Delognaea, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Delognaea has lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
