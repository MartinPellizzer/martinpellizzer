Draba bruniifolia var. diversifolia

Draba bruniifolia var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnate leaves. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms dense mats. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green color, with some variation in leaf shape and coloration in different populations.

The flowers, which appear in spring, are white or pale yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
