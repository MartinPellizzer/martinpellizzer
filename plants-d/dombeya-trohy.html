Dombeya trohy

Dombeya trohy, commonly known as the mountain dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, making it a valuable plant for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.

The plant is also adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
