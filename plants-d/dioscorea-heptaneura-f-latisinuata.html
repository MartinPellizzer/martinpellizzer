Dioscorea heptaneura f. latisinuata

Dioscorea heptaneura f. latisinuata, commonly known as the broad-sinuated yam, is a variant of the yam family (Dioscoreaceae) characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments within forests. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in starch and have been traditionally used for food and medicinal purposes. Its leaves are typically large, with a broad, undulating margin that gives it its common name.

The species is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it an important crop in certain agricultural systems.
