Decaneuropsis gratiosa

Decaneuropsis gratiosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has palm-like leaves that are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Decaneuropsis gratiosa is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
