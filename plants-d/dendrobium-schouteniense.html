Dendrobium schouteniense

Dendrobium schouteniense, commonly known as the Schoutene's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its terrestrial or epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its survival in diverse environmental conditions. Its flowers are usually showy, with a range of colors including white, yellow, and pink, and are often fragrant.

Dendrobium schouteniense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
