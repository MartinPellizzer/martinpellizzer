Diuris inundata

Diuris inundata, commonly known as the flooded spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the wet tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant green to blue-green leaves and its unique, spider-like flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant thrives in swampy or seasonally flooded habitats, adapting to waterlogged conditions with its specialized root system. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by attracting specific insect species.

Diuris inundata plays an important ecological role in its native environment, contributing to biodiversity and supporting local pollinator populations.
