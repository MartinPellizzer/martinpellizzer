Daphniphyllum salicifolium

Daphniphyllum salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaved daphniphyllum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Daphniphyllaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to cool, moist environments.
