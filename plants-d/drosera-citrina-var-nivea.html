Drosera citrina var. nivea

Drosera citrina var. nivea, commonly known as the white sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white, sticky glandular hairs that trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and covered in a dense layer of digestive secretions.

It produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months.
