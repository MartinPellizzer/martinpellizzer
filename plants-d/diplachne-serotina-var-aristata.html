Diplachne serotina var. aristata

Diplachne serotina var. aristata, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as swampy soils and creek margins. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

However, it can also become invasive in certain environments, outcompeting native vegetation.
