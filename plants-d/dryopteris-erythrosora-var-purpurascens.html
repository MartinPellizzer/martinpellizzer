Dryopteris erythrosora var. purpurascens

Dryopteris erythrosora var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple fern, is a variety of the sword fern that exhibits distinctive purple hues on its fronds.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green with a striking purplish tinge on the underside. It produces spore-bearing fronds in the fall, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

This ornamental fern is popular in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
