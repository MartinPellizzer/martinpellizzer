Dimorphanthera racemosa

Dimorphanthera racemosa, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered in racemes, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
