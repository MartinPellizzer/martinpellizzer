Doria altissima var. procera

Doria altissima var. procera, commonly known as the giant doria, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its towering stature and lush, feather-like fronds.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its tall, slender trunk and expansive canopy make it a notable ornamental species in botanical gardens and private collections. The plant's rapid growth rate and ability to reach heights of over 20 meters make it both impressive and challenging to cultivate.

Due to its ecological significance and aesthetic appeal, it is often studied for its role in tropical ecosystems and its potential for horticultural use.
