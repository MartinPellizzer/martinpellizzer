Dianthus algetanus subsp. costae

Dianthus algetanus subsp.

costae, commonly known as the Algetan carnation, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent-like leaves. Dianthus algetanus subsp.
