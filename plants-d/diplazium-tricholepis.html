Diplazium tricholepis

Diplazium tricholepis, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces both sterile and fertile fronds. Its leaves are covered with fine, hairy structures, which contribute to its common name.

Diplazium tricholepis thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
