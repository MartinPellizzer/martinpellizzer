Dracocephalum palmatoides

Dracocephalum palmatoides, commonly known as broad-leaved dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly wrinkled texture. The plant produces spikes of purple to blue flowers, which bloom in summer and attract various pollinators. Its roots are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Dracocephalum palmatoides is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
