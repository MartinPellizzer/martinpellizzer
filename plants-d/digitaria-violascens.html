Digitaria violascens

Digitaria violascens, commonly known as purple Digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a purple hue. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, pale green to reddish-brown spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature, it is both a weed in agricultural areas and a valuable forage plant for livestock.
