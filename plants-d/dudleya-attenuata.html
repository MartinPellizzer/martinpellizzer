Dudleya attenuata

Dudleya attenuata, commonly known as the slender Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, covered in a powdery coating called farina. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear clusters of pale yellow or greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Dudleya attenuata is drought-tolerant and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
