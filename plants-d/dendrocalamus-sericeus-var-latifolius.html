Dendrocalamus sericeus var. latifolius

Dendrocalamus sericeus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface that gives them a distinctive appearance. This bamboo is known for its rapid growth and is often used in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
