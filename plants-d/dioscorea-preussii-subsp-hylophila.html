Dioscorea preussii subsp. hylophila

Dioscorea preussii subsp.

hylophila, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source by local communities.

The species is also valued for its potential in agricultural and medicinal applications.
