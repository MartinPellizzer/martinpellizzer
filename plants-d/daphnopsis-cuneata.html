Daphnopsis cuneata

Daphnopsis cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf daphnopsis, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is native to certain regions of South America. It has elliptical leaves that are often dark green and glossy.

Daphnopsis cuneata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
