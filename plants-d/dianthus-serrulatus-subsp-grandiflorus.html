Dianthus serrulatus subsp. grandiflorus

Dianthus serrulatus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It is a perennial species that produces small, oval-shaped fruits called capsules.

The Sierra dianthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
