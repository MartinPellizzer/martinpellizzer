Deschampsia kingii

Deschampsia kingii, commonly known as King's grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
