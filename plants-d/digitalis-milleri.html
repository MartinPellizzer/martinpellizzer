Digitalis milleri

Digitalis milleri, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-purple flowers. The plant is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, downy texture.

Digitalis milleri is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
