Doronicum orientale

Doronicum orientale, commonly known as the oriental leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered with soft, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly toothed. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive pattern of dark spots, resembling a leopard's spots, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Doronicum orientale is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
