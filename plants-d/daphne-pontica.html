Daphne pontica

Daphne pontica, commonly known as Caucasian daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dark green, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Daphne pontica is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
