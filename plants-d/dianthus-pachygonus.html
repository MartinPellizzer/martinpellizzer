Dianthus pachygonus

Dianthus pachygonus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its stems are thick and sturdy, which helps it survive in arid conditions.

Dianthus pachygonus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
