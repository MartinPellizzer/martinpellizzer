Dracocephalum velutinum

Dracocephalum velutinum, commonly known as velvet skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
