Drimys winteri var. fernandeziana

Drimys winteri var. fernandeziana, commonly known as the Andean winter's bark, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the cool, humid climates of its native range.
