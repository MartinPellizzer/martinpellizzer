Dioscorea sinuata var. septemloba

Dioscorea sinuata var. septemloba, commonly known as the seven-lobed yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that often have seven distinct leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its growth habit involves twining stems that can reach significant lengths, often climbing trees or other vegetation.

The tubers of this variety are valued for their nutritional content and traditional use in local cuisines.
