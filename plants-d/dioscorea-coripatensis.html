Dioscorea coripatensis

Dioscorea coripatensis, commonly known as the Coripata yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained, fertile soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, often fragrant aroma.

The tubers of Dioscorea coripatensis are valued for their nutritional content and traditional use in local cuisines.
