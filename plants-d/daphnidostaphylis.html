Daphnidostaphylis

Daphnidostaphylis, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnately arranged, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces distinctive, spiky fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and vibrant autumn foliage.

The plant has moderate water requirements and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
