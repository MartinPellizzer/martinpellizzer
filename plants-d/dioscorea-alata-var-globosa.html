Dioscorea alata var. globosa

Dioscorea alata var. globosa, commonly known as the sweet potato or kumara, is a tropical vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are hairy and climbing. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its nutrient-rich tubers.

It is widely grown in tropical and subtropical regions for both food and medicinal purposes.
