Dioscorea brachybotrya var. germainii

Dioscorea brachybotrya var. germainii, commonly known as the Germain's yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are notable for their high starch content and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitats.
