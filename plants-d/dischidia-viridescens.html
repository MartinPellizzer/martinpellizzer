Dischidia viridescens

Dischidia viridescens, commonly known as the green dischidia, is a climbing epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Dischidia viridescens is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
