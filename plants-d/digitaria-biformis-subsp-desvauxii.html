Digitaria biformis subsp. desvauxii

Digitaria biformis subsp.

desvauxii, commonly known as the two-awned digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, tufted stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive appearance that aids in identification.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
