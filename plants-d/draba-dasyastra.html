Draba dasyastra

Draba dasyastra, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Draba dasyastra thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
