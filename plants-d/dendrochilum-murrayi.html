Dendrochilum murrayi

Dendrochilum murrayi, commonly known as the Murray's tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and its ability to form dense clusters of foliage. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dendrochilum murrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
