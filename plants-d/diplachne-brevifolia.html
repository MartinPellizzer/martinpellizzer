Diplachne brevifolia

Diplachne brevifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Diplachne brevifolia is typically found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and grasslands.

Its morphology and growth habits make it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
