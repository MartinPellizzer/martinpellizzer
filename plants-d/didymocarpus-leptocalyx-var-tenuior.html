Didymocarpus leptocalyx var. tenuior

Didymocarpus leptocalyx var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender-leaved flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate, with slender, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
