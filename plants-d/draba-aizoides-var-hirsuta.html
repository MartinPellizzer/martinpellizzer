Draba aizoides var. hirsuta

Draba aizoides var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. It typically thrives in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its flowering period occurs in early spring, adding a splash of color to high-altitude landscapes.
