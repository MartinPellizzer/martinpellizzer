Desmodium incanum var. supinum

Desmodium incanum var. supinum, commonly known as the prostrate sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual legume characterized by its slender, spreading stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense heads, followed by elongated, slender pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
