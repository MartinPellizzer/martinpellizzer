Delphinium przewalskii

Delphinium przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in raceme inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Delphinium przewalskii is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
