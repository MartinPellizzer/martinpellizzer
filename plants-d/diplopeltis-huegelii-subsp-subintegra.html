Diplopeltis huegelii subsp. subintegra

Diplopeltis huegelii subsp.

subintegra, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family. It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-draining soil. Its foliage is often velvety and deep green, with a distinctive pattern of veins.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
