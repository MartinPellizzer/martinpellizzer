Dendrobium potamophila

Dendrobium potamophila, commonly known as the river orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and forest floors. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Dendrobium potamophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
