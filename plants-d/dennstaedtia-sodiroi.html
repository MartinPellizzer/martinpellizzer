Dennstaedtia sodiroi

Dennstaedtia sodiroi, commonly known as the Sodiro's water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dennstaedtiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lobed fronds that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
