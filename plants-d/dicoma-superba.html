Dicoma superba

Dicoma superba, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy appearance with narrow, linear leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Dicoma superba is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
