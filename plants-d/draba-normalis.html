Draba normalis

Draba normalis, commonly known as the common whitlow grass, is a small annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Draba normalis has a short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
