Diplostephium glutinosum subsp. cocuyanum

Diplostephium glutinosum subsp.

cocuyanum, commonly known as Cocuyan prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny stems and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of arid environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and morphological features compared to other members of the Diplostephium genus.
