Deschampsia cespitosa var. setifolia

Deschampsia cespitosa var. setifolia, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green blades and tufted growth habit, forming dense, low-growing clumps. The plant has narrow, slightly hairy leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, prairies, and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
