Deyeuxia nardifolia

Deyeuxia nardifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deyeuxia nardifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
