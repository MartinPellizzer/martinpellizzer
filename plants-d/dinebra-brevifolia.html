Dinebra brevifolia

Dinebra brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swamps. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Dinebra brevifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
