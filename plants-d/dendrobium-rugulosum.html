Dendrobium rugulosum

Dendrobium rugulosum, commonly known as the wrinkled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid tropical environments. The plant features long, cane-like stems that are often covered in a distinctive, wrinkled texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with red or purple markings, and they bloom in clusters during the wet season.

Dendrobium rugulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
