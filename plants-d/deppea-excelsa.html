Deppea excelsa

Deppea excelsa, commonly known as the giant rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
