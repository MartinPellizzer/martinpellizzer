Dioscorea amaranthoides var. pendula

Dioscorea amaranthoides var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its pendulous stems, which hang gracefully from supports, and its tuberous roots, which are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and potential economic importance in traditional practices.
