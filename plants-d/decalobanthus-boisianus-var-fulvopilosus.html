Decalobanthus boisianus var. fulvopilosus

Decalobanthus boisianus var. fulvopilosus, commonly known as the golden-hairy rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and features small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.

This variety is native to regions in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.
