Dodecatheon patulum var. parvifolium

Dodecatheon patulum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf glory of the snow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with a unique, bell-shaped structure. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller leaves compared to the typical form of Dodecatheon patulum.
