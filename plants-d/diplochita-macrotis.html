Diplochita macrotis

Diplochita macrotis, commonly known as the large-leafed diplochita, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a unique, fragrant aroma.

Diplochita macrotis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
