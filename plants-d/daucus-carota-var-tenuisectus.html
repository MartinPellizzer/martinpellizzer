Daucus carota var. tenuisectus

Daucus carota var. tenuisectus, commonly known as the slender-pinnate carrot, is a variant of the common carrot characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves.

This plant belongs to theApiaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are narrow and deeply lobed, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in umbels, typical of the carrot genus.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.
