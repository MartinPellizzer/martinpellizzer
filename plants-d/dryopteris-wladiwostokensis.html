Dryopteris wladiwostokensis

Dryopteris wladiwostokensis, commonly known as the Vladivostok wood fern, is a rare and endemic species native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Dryopteris genus, which includes various types of wood ferns, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spore-bearing structures, called soruses, are located on the undersides of its fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
