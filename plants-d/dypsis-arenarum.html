Dypsis arenarum

Dypsis arenarum, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and clusters of spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Dypsis arenarum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
