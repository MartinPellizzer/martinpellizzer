Dolichos helvolus

Dolichos helvolus, commonly known as the wild cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Dolichos helvolus is valued for its nutritional content, as the seeds are a good source of protein and can be used in various culinary applications.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
