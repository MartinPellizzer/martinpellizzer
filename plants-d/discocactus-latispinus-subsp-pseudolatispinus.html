Discocactus latispinus subsp. pseudolatispinus

Discocactus latispinus subsp.

pseudolatispinus, commonly known as the narrow-spined discocactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with short, sharp spines.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
