Dactylorchis longifolia

Dactylorchis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the eastern United States.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate appearance, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodlands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
