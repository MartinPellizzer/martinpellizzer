Dioscorea irodensis

Dioscorea irodensis, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a tuberous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its stout, cylindrical rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been used for both culinary and medicinal purposes in various cultures.
