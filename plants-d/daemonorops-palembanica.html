Daemonorops palembanica

Daemonorops palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems used for rattan products. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender pinnae, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered fruits. It thrives in tropical rainforests and requires high humidity and partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its economic value, it is often cultivated for its durable and lightweight rattan material.
