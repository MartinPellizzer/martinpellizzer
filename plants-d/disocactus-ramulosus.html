Disocactus ramulosus

Disocactus ramulosus, commonly known as the Ramosa Cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing, branching cactus with cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Disocactus ramulosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
