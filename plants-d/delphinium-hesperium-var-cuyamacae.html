Delphinium hesperium var. cuyamacae

Delphinium hesperium var. cuyamacae, commonly known as the Cuyama larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
