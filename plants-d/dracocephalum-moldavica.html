Dracocephalum moldavica

Dracocephalum moldavica, commonly known as blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spike-like clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.

Dracocephalum moldavica is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
