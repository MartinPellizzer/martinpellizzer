Dioscorea sellowiana var. mantiqueirensis

Dioscorea sellowiana var. mantiqueirensis, commonly known as the Mantiqueira yam, is a climbing vine native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into cylindrical, yellow fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been historically utilized for food and medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential due to its vigorous growth and attractive foliage.
