Deutzia bartlettii

Deutzia bartlettii, commonly known as Bartlett's deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching branches covered in small, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pinkish-white flowers in early to mid-spring, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its flowering period makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, especially in temperate climates.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
