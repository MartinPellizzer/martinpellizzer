Dichoropetalum depauperatum

Dichoropetalum depauperatum, commonly known as the poorflower, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its slender stems. The plant has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish.
