Descurainia sophia var. macrophylla

Descurainia sophia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf tansy.

This variant of the tansy plant is characterized by its larger, more elongated leaves compared to the typical variety. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Brassicaceae family.
