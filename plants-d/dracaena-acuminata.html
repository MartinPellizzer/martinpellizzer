Dracaena acuminata

Dracaena acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dracaena, is a species of flowering plant in the asparagus family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red or orange berries.

Dracaena acuminata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
