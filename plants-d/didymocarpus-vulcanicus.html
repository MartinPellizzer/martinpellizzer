Didymocarpus vulcanicus

Didymocarpus vulcanicus, commonly known as the volcanic fig, is a unique tropical plant native to regions with volcanic soil.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fig-like fruits that are typically small and green. The plant thrives in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils, often found in areas with recent volcanic activity. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces a sweet, aromatic pulp that attracts various wildlife.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is a rare and ecologically significant species in its native environment.
