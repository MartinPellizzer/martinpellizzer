Dysoxylum multifoliolatum

Dysoxylum multifoliolatum, commonly known as the many-leaved dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can be up to 1 meter in length, with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
