Dianthus vandasii

Dianthus vandasii, commonly known as the sand dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are composed of numerous small petals arranged in a cluster, giving it a lace-like appearance.

Dianthus vandasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
