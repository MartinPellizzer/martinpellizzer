Dorstenia multiformis var. arifolia

Dorstenia multiformis var. arifolia, commonly known as the "Cushion Fig" or "Arifolia Fig," is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, waxy leaves that form a compact cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its distinctive morphology and ability to store water make it well adapted to dry conditions.
