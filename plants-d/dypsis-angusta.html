Dypsis angusta

Dypsis angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf areca, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are narrow and elongated. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and the fruit produces edible seeds.

Dypsis angusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens worldwide.
