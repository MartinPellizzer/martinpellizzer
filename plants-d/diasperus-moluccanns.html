Diasperus moluccanns

Diasperus moluccanns, commonly known as the Moluccan sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Diasperus moluccanns is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms.
