Dracocephalum urticifolium var. angustifolia

Dracocephalum urticifolium var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved dragonhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered with fine hairs and possesses lance-shaped, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers clustered in spikes, typically in shades of blue to purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
