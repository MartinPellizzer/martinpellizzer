Delphinium litwinowii

Delphinium litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, crowned with dense clusters of sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also a subject of botanical study for its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
