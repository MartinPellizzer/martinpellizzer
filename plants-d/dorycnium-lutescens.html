Dorycnium lutescens

Dorycnium lutescens, commonly known as the yellow burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and spiny pods. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in wastelands or along roadsides. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, edible seeds that are used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
