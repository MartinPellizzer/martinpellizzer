Dioscorea clemensii

Dioscorea clemensii, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tubers that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The tubers of Dioscorea clemensii are rich in starch and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
