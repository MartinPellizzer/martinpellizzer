Dodonaea eriocarpa var. forbesii

Dodonaea eriocarpa var. forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' silky hoppegloss, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought and salt spray.
