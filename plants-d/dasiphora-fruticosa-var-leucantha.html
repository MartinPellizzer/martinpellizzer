Dasiphora fruticosa var. leucantha

Dasiphora fruticosa var. leucantha, commonly known as white prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and northern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with woody stems that form dense mats. The plant produces showy white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
