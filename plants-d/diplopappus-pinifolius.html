Diplopappus pinifolius

Diplopappus pinifolius, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a slender, erect stem with rough, hairy surfaces and lance-shaped, serrated leaves. Its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds with pappus structures for aid in propagation.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
