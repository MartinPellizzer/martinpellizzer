Disa obliqua subsp. obliqua

Disa obliqua subsp.

obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with contrasting stripes.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces clusters of flowers on erect stems. Disa obliqua subsp.

obliqua is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
