Dryopteris manshurica

Dryopteris manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Dryopteris manshurica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
