Dioscorea peteri

Dioscorea peteri, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into tuberous roots, which are its primary economic product. These tubers are rich in starch and have been used as a food source by various indigenous communities.

Dioscorea peteri is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
