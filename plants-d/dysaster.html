Dysaster

Dysaster, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the remote regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pale blue or violet flowers that resemble the shape of a ghostly face, hence its common name. The plant is often found growing in rocky, arid environments, thriving in harsh conditions with minimal water. Its flowers bloom during the night, emitting a faint fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and unusual appearance, Dysaster is considered a botanical curiosity and is often studied for its adaptation strategies in extreme climates.
