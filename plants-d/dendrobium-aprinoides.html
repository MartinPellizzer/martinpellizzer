Dendrobium aprinoides

Dendrobium aprinoides, commonly known as the apricot orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive apricot-like hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
