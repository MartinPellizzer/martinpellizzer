Derris paniculata

Derris paniculata, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers arranged in panicles. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Derris paniculata is used in traditional medicine and as a natural pesticide.
