Dischidia semperflorens

Dischidia semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming fig vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It is well adapted to humid environments and thrives in partial shade.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
