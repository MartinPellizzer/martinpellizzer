Dryopteris rafinesquiana

Dryopteris rafinesquiana, commonly known as the cliff brake, is a fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive dark green color and a rough, textured surface. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found growing on rocky cliffs or in shaded forest areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
