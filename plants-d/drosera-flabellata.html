Drosera flabellata

Drosera flabellata, commonly known as the flat-leaved sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves that secrete a sticky mucilage to trap insects. The plant's leaves are typically green with red or purple veins, and it produces small, white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and is often found in heathlands and wetlands.

Drosera flabellata is a hardy species that can survive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
