Disa concinna

Disa concinna, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright pink to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing showy, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Disa concinna is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
