Dalbergia antsirananae

Dalbergia antsirananae, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a rare and critically endangered tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic qualities. Due to overexploitation and habitat loss, it is now listed as endangered by the IUCN.

Conservation efforts are underway to protect and propagate this unique species.
