Dypsis ambositrae

Dypsis ambositrae, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and long, arching leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small, pointed leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Dypsis ambositrae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in landscape gardens.
