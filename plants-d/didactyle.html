Didactyle

Didactyle, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two prominent lobes on the lip, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on tree bark or other surfaces.
