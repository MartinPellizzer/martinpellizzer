Dalbergia anomala

Dalbergia anomala, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic qualities and durability. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
