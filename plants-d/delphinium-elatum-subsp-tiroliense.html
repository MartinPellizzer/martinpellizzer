Delphinium elatum subsp. tiroliense

Delphinium elatum subsp.

tiroliense, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features striking blue to violet flowers arranged in tall spikes, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for alpine gardens and wildflower meadows.
