Donax sativus

Donax sativus, commonly known as the sea barley or salt grass, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in coastal areas, marshes, and salt flats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Donax sativus is also an important forage plant for certain wildlife species.
