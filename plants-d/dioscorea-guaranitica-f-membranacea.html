Dioscorea guaranitica f. membranacea

Dioscorea guaranitica f. membranacea, commonly known as the membranaceous yam, is a variant of the yam genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thin, membranous leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential pharmacological properties.
