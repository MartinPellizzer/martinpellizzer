Dendrobium boumaniae

Dendrobium boumaniae, commonly known as the Bouman's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight and requires well-draining potting media. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.

Dendrobium boumaniae is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and adaptability.
