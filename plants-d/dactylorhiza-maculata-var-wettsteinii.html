Dactylorhiza maculata var. wettsteinii

Dactylorhiza maculata var. wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces upright, leafy stems with showy, violet or purple flowers. Its flowers are arranged in loose clusters and have three sepals and three petals, with the lateral petals being larger and more prominent.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
