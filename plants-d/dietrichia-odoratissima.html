Dietrichia odoratissima

Dietrichia odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its unique aroma attracts specific pollinators, such as moths and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
