Drymocallis jailae

Drymocallis jailae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in small, fluffy clusters.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
