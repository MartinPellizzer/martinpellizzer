Diospyros pubicalyx

Diospyros pubicalyx, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by clustered, orange-red fruits resembling persimmons. The fruits are typically edible when fully ripe, though they may have astringent tannins when unripe.

Diospyros pubicalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
