Diuris venosa

Diuris venosa, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the spring season.

Diuris venosa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
