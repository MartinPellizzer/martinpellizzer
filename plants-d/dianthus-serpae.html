Dianthus serpae

Dianthus serpae, commonly known as the serpentine campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open areas such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Dianthus serpae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
