Diasperus minutiflorus

Diasperus minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf starflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along its stem, which is typically low to the ground. The plant produces tiny, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
