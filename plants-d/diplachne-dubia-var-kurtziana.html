Diplachne dubia var. kurtziana

Diplachne dubia var. kurtziana, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in dry conditions and is often found in disturbed or degraded habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and restoration projects.
