Diospyros palmeri

Diospyros palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer date palm, is a small, evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, edible fruits that are similar to dates. The plant has a short trunk and is often found growing in rocky or arid environments. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruiting bodies.

Diospyros palmeri is adapted to drought conditions and is considered a rare and endangered species.
