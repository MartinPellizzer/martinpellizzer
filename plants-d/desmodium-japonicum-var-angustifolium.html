Desmodium japonicum var. angustifolium

Desmodium japonicum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf sicklepod, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and its distinctive yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oblong pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is native to East Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it can become a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and agricultural weed management.
