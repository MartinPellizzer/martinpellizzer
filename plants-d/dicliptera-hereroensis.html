Dicliptera hereroensis

Dicliptera hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Dicliptera hereroensis is often used in traditional settings for thatching and as a forage plant for livestock.
