Dalea pinetorum

Dalea pinetorum, commonly known as the pine dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and pine forests, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dalea pinetorum is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
