Duranta parietariifolia

Duranta parietariifolia, commonly known as the wallleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often used in landscaping in arid regions.
