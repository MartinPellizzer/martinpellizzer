Dryopteris fragrans var. hookeriana

Dryopteris fragrans var. hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's sweet fern, is a variant of the sweet fern fern.

It is a perennial evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green to pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. It produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures on the underside of its fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
