Dichondra repens var. peruviana

Dichondra repens var. peruviana, commonly known as the creeping dichondra, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread easily make it both useful and sometimes invasive in certain environments.
