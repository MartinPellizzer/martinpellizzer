Diospyros chrysophyllos

Diospyros chrysophyllos, commonly known as the golden-leaved persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which becomes especially vibrant during the dry season. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and often consumed raw or cooked. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating.

Diospyros chrysophyllos is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
