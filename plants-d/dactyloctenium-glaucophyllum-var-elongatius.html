Dactyloctenium glaucophyllum var. elongatius

Dactyloctenium glaucophyllum var. elongatius, commonly known as the long-stemmed glaucophyllum grass, is a variant of the glaucophyllum grass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, elongated stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has a compact growth habit with dense foliage, making it suitable for erosion control.

Its botanical features include small, spike-like inflorescences and pale green to bluish-gray foliage.
