Diospyros austrocaledonica

Diospyros austrocaledonica, commonly known as the Kauri apple, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, round fruits with a sweet, honey-like flavor. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
