Dyerella

Dyerella, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy leaves and delicate, white or pale pink flowers that resemble those of a ghostly apparition. Dyerella is typically found growing on the bark of trees in the wet tropics of Central and South America. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its limited habitat and specific environmental requirements, Dyerella is considered a highly endangered species.
