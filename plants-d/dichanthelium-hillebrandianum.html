Dichanthelium hillebrandianum

Dichanthelium hillebrandianum, commonly known as the Hillebrand's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, hairless stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are linear and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in wetland ecosystems.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
