Deprea cardenasiana

Deprea cardenasiana, commonly known as the Cardenas's datura, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and hairy, while its flowers are usually pale purple or white, blooming in the evening.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
