Dichanthelium malacophyllum

Dichanthelium malacophyllum, commonly known as the smooth panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
