Deyeuxia megapotamica

Deyeuxia megapotamica, commonly known as the Amazonian flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant thrives in moist to waterlogged soils, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Deyeuxia megapotamica is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated soils.
