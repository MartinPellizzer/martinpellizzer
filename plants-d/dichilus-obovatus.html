Dichilus obovatus

Dichilus obovatus, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters, giving it a bell-like appearance. Its leaves are usually obovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Dichilus obovatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
