Dendrochilum bartonii

Dendrochilum bartonii, commonly known as the Barton's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in the summer. The plant has a complex root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange with dark markings, making it visually striking.

Dendrochilum bartonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
