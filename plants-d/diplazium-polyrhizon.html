Diplazium polyrhizon

Diplazium polyrhizon, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has a creeping rhizome with numerous fronds that are broad and pinnately compound. Its leaves are usually submerged or floating, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Diplazium polyrhizon plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
