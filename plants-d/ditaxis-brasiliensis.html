Ditaxis brasiliensis

Ditaxis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its branching structure and spiny stems, which are adapted to its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
