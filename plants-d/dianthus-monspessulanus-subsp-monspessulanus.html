Dianthus monspessulanus subsp. monspessulanus

Dianthus monspessulanus subsp.

monspessulanus, commonly known as the purple campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often hairy.

It is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
