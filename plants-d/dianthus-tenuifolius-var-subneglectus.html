Dianthus tenuifolius var. subneglectus

Dianthus tenuifolius var. subneglectus, commonly known as the slenderleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy blooms and aromatic foliage.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
