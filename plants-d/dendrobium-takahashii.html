Dendrobium takahashii

Dendrobium takahashii, commonly known as the Takahashi's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, which help it survive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a fragrant scent, and bloom during the warmer months.

Dendrobium takahashii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
