Dudleya viridicata

Dudleya viridicata, commonly known as the greenleaf Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California and Baja California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or violet during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dudleya viridicata is also known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves, allowing it to survive in arid environments.
