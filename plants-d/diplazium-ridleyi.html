Diplazium ridleyi

Diplazium ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridge fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its erect, slender rhizome and pinnately compound leaves. The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with fertile sori located on the underside of the pinnae. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
