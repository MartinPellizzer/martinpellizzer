Descantaria amplexicaulis

Descantaria amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, where the leaves tightly wrap around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with white or yellow markings. Native to South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and ecological interest.
