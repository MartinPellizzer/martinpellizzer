Dioscorea claytonii

Dioscorea claytonii, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, compound leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used as a food source in some regions. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The tubers are typically harvested after the plant has matured, usually in the second year of growth.
