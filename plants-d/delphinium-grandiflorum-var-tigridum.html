Delphinium grandiflorum var. tigridum

Delphinium grandiflorum var. tigridum, commonly known as the tigridium larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and ecological landscapes.
