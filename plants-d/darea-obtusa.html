Darea obtusa

Darea obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or violet throat. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Darea obtusa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
