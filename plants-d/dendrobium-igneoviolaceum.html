Dendrobium igneoviolaceum

Dendrobium igneoviolaceum, commonly known as the fiery violet orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid, tropical environments. The plant features long, cane-like stems covered with clusters of showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are long and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

This orchid is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
