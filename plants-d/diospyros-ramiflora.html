Diospyros ramiflora

Diospyros ramiflora, commonly known as the ramiflorous ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony species, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture with a distinctive scent.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of dye.
