Desmoncus panamensis

Desmoncus panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian orchid, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the humid forests of Panama.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and showy, violet-colored petals that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
