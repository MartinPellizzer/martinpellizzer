Dolicholus violaceus

Dolicholus violaceus, commonly known as the violet-headed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and elongated, slender leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other small insects.

Dolicholus violaceus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
