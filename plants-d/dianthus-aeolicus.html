Dianthus aeolicus

Dianthus aeolicus, commonly known as the Aeolian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink or red petals with a distinctive frilled edge, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Dianthus aeolicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
