Dianthus camusii subsp. macrorhizus

Dianthus camusii subsp.

macrorhizus, commonly known as the large-rooted campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers with five petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its thick, fleshy roots and hairy stems, which help it survive in arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This plant is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
