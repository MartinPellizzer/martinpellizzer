Drymocallis hansenii

Drymocallis hansenii, commonly known as Hansen's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, preferring moist, poorly drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Drymocallis hansenii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
