Diplochita leucocephala

Diplochita leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white and green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
