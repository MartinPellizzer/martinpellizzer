Dryopteris hondoensis f. rubisora

Dryopteris hondoensis f. rubisora, commonly known as the Hondo Brake with Red Veins, is a fern characterized by its distinctive reddish veins that run throughout its fronds.

This fern belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is native to Japan, specifically the island of Hondo. Its fronds are typically lanceolate, with a leathery texture and a dark green coloration that contrasts with the vibrant red vascular patterns. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories where it can receive adequate humidity and indirect light.

Its unique coloration makes it a prized specimen for botanical gardens and collectors interested in rare and ornamental ferns.
