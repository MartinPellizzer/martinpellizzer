Dichapetalum vestitum var. cinerascens

Dichapetalum vestitum var. cinerascens, commonly known as the gray-leaved dichapetalum, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are lanceolate, with a distinctive grayish or whitish underside, giving it its common name. The flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish or reddish hues, and are followed by capsule fruits.

This plant is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
