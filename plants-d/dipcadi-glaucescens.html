Dipcadi glaucescens

Dipcadi glaucescens, commonly known as the blue bulbous lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem that stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and bears showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. It blooms in the summer, typically from December to March, and is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
