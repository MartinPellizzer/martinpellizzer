Diderotia multispicata

Diderotia multispicata, commonly known as the many-spined rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems covered with numerous small, spiky structures called spikes, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Diderotia multispicata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
