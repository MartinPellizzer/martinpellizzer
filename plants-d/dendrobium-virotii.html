Dendrobium virotii

Dendrobium virotii, commonly known as the Virot's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
