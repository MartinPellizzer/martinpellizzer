Digitaria pinetorum

Digitaria pinetorum, commonly known as the pinegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy soils within pine forests and is well-adapted to acidic conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters, typical of the grass family.

Digitaria pinetorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
