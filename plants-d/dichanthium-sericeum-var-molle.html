Dichanthium sericeum var. molle

Dichanthium sericeum var. molle, commonly known as silky grass or silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
