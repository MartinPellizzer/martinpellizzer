Draba aretioides

Draba aretioides, commonly known as the Aretino's rockcress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tien Shan. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.
