Diospyros orthioneura

Diospyros orthioneura, commonly known as the Florida persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features include a shallow root system and a tendency to grow in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
