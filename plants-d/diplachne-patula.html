Diplachne patula

Diplachne patula, commonly known as slender flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairless leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and pastures. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

Diplachne patula is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
