Distichostemon barklyanus

Distichostemon barklyanus, commonly known as the barklyan daisy, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its attractive appearance and resilience.
