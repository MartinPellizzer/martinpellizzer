Desmodium bodinieri

Desmodium bodinieri, commonly known as the pink burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its distinctive seed pods, which are hairy and often contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Desmodium bodinieri is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
