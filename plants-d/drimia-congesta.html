Drimia congesta

Drimia congesta, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Drimia congesta is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
