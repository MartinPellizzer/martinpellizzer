Dryopteris nudistipes

Dryopteris nudistipes, commonly known as the naked-stemmed wood fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces spore-bearing fiddleheads that are often used in traditional Asian cuisine. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
