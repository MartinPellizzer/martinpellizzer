Dendrobium vietnamense

Dendrobium vietnamense, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with elongated, cane-like stems that often bear clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium vietnamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
