Dendrobium humboldtense

Dendrobium humboldtense, commonly known as the Humboldt orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with its stems forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium humboldtense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
