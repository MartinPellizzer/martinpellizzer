Dioscorea urceolata var. reflexa

Dioscorea urceolata var. reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend support structures, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
