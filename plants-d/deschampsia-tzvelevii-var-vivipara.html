Deschampsia tzvelevii var. vivipara

Deschampsia tzvelevii var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous feathergrass, is a rare and specialized grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. One of its notable botanical features is its vivipary, a reproductive strategy where seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant.

This adaptation allows it to propagate in harsh environments where traditional seed dispersal might be less effective.
