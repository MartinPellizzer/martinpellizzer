Dolichothele zephyranthoides

Dolichothele zephyranthoides, commonly known as the windflower spider, is a species of orb-weaving spider found in various parts of the world.

It is characterized by its slender body and long, delicate legs, which aid in its ability to construct intricate and efficient webs. The spider's web is typically built in open areas, such as gardens or fields, where it can capture flying insects. Its name reflects its association with the wind, as it often positions its web in locations where air currents can carry prey.

The plant-like appearance of its web has led to its common name, though it is not a true plant but an arachnid.
