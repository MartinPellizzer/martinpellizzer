Dryas punctata f. nuda

Dryas punctata f. nuda, commonly known as the naked dryas, is a variant of the alpine plant Dryas punctata.

It is characterized by its reduced or absent leaf pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is often found in high elevations. It has small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
