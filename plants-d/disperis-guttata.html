Disperis guttata

Disperis guttata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a single, upright stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive red or purple spots. It thrives in grassland habitats and is often found in open areas with well-drained soil.

Disperis guttata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
