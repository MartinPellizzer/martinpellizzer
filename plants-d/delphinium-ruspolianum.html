Delphinium ruspolianum

Delphinium ruspolianum, commonly known as the Ruspolian larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium ruspolianum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both humans and animals.
