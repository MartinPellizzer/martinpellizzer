Dudleya pulverulenta var. arizonica

Dudleya pulverulenta var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona dustymallow, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
