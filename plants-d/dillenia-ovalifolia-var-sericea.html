Dillenia ovalifolia var. sericea

Dillenia ovalifolia var. sericea, commonly known as the silky dillenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with prominent, fleshy petals that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody, spiny capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
