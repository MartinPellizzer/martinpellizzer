Decaspermum paniculatum var. finlaysonianum

Decaspermum paniculatum var. finlaysonianum, commonly known as the Finlayson's decaspermum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
