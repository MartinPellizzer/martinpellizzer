Diospyros rheophila

Diospyros rheophila, commonly known as the stream diospyros, is a rare tropical tree species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-orange fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments along stream banks and is adapted to high humidity and nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
