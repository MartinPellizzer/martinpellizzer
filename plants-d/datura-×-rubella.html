Datura × rubella

Datura × rubella, commonly known as the hybrid angel's trumpet, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that can be white, yellow, or pink, depending on the cultivar. The plant produces smooth, green, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Datura × rubella contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
