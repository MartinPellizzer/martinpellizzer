Davya sertulata

Davya sertulata, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in saline or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Davya sertulata is also valued for its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion along shorelines.
