Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana nothovar. wisniewskii

Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana nothovar. wisniewskii, commonly known as the Wisniewskii marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike of flowers with elongated, spurred petals.

This orchid is often found in regions with temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
