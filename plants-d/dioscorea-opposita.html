Dioscorea opposita

Dioscorea opposita, commonly known as Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
