Diospyros bibracteata

Diospyros bibracteata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a distinct aromatic scent, and it is often found in moist, tropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a food source.
