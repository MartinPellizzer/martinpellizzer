Dioscorea grata

Dioscorea grata, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often cultivated for their edible properties. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by spiny capsules. Its rhizomes are rich in starch and have been used in traditional cuisines for centuries.

Dioscorea grata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage.
