Dolichos filifoliolus

Dolichos filifoliolus, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with finely divided leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients.

Botanically, it is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial cover crop.
