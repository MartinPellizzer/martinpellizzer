Diospyros obliquifolia

Diospyros obliquifolia, commonly known as the oblique-leaf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often oblique in shape, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
