Ditassa arianeae

Ditassa arianeae, commonly known as the Ariane's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the rainforests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated inflorescence and striking, pale pink or white flowers with intricate patterns. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its botanical significance lies in its specialized adaptations to its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
