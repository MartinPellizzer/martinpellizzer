Dianthus persicus

Dianthus persicus, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive central boss. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is typically grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus persicus is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in traditional medicine.
