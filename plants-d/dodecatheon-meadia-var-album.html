Dodecatheon meadia var. album

Dodecatheon meadia var. album, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.
