Dalbergia goetzei

Dalbergia goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's ebony, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes.

Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
