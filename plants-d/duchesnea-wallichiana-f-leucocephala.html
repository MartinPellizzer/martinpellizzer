Duchesnea wallichiana f. leucocephala

Duchesnea wallichiana f. leucocephala, commonly known as white-flowered wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base and produces small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stems and bloom in early spring.

The species is characterized by its edible, sour-tasting leaves and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
