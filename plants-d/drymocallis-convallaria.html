Drymocallis convallaria

Drymocallis convallaria, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and emit a distinctive, sweet aroma when crushed.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.
