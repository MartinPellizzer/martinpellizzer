Dracocephalum isabellae

Dracocephalum isabellae, commonly known as Isabella's dragonhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, mint-like scent.

Dracocephalum isabellae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
