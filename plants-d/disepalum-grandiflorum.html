Disepalum grandiflorum

Disepalum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered disepalum, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are composed of five sepals, which are fused at the base, giving it the genus name "Disepalum.".

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
