Dichrostachys cinerea subsp. burmana

Dichrostachys cinerea subsp.

burmana, commonly known as Burmese straggler weed, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic weed in many areas.
