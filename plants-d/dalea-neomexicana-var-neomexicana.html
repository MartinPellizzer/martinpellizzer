Dalea neomexicana var. neomexicana

Dalea neomexicana var. neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in grasslands, rocky slopes, and along streambanks in its native range.
