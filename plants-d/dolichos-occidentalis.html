Dolichos occidentalis

Dolichos occidentalis, commonly known as the western horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender stems and can grow up to 2 meters in height. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
