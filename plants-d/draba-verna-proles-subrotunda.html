Draba verna proles subrotunda

Draba verna proles subrotunda, commonly known as the early whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are typically rounded or subrotund, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during early spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Brassicaceae family.
