Dianthus carthusianorum var. paniculatus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The flowers consist of five petals with a distinctive frilled edge, and the plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moderate climates.
