Dodonaea subglandulifera

Dodonaea subglandulifera, commonly known as the velvetleaf hopseed, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces oval, leathery fruits that enclose flattened seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Dodonaea subglandulifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
