Dacrycarpus kawaii

Dacrycarpus kawaii, commonly known as the Kawaii silver pine, is a rare and endemic species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its slender, silver-gray needles and conical shape. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with its foliage forming a dense, compact canopy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation priority.
