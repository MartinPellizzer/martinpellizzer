Descurainia pinnata var. ochroleuca

Descurainia pinnata var. ochroleuca, commonly known as yellow flax or yellow tansy, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its branched stems and alternate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
