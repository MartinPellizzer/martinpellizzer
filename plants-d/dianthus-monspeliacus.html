Dianthus monspeliacus

Dianthus monspeliacus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically produces clusters of small, rounded flowers on short, hairy stalks, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to thrive in rocky or arid environments.

Dianthus monspeliacus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
