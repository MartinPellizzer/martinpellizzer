Draba nivalis var. genuina

Draba nivalis var. genuina, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs.

Draba nivalis var. genuina is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine tundra and mountain meadows.
