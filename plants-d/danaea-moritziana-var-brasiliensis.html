Danaea moritziana var. brasiliensis

Danaea moritziana var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange bracts that resemble flowers, although they are actually modified leaves. The plant belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its foliage is typically broad and lance-shaped, adding to its visual appeal in both garden and container settings.
