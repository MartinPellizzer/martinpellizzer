Decalepidanthus racemosus

Decalepidanthus racemosus, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble a waterwheel, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Decalepidanthus racemosus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
