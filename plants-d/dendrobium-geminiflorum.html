Dendrobium geminiflorum

Dendrobium geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that often branch and produce multiple flower spikes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming during the summer months. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium geminiflorum is popular among orchid growers for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
