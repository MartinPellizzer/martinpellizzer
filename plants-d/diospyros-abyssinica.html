Diospyros abyssinica

Diospyros abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by clustered, orange-red fruits. The fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local medicine.
