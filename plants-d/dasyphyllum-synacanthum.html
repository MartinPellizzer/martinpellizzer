Dasyphyllum synacanthum

Dasyphyllum synacanthum, commonly known as the spiny dasyphyllum, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a touch of color to its harsh environment. Its stems are often branched and covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores.

Dasyphyllum synacanthum is well adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
