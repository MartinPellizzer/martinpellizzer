Dichapetalum petaloideum

Dichapetalum petaloideum, commonly known as the African water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Africa.

It features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, cup-like structure. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
