Dalbergia henryana

Dalbergia henryana, commonly known as Henry's rosewood, is a species of legume in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins. Its flowers are small and pale pink, often appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several seeds.

The wood of Dalbergia henryana is highly valued for its rich color and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.
