Dahlia pinnata var. gracilis

Dahlia pinnata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slenderleaf tree dahlia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and broad, palmate leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its unique appearance and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to horticultural collections.
