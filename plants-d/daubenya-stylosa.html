Daubenya stylosa

Daubenya stylosa, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a dark green color and a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces tall, slender flower spikes in late summer, bearing small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Daubenya stylosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
