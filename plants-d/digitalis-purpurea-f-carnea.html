Digitalis purpurea f. carnea

Digitalis purpurea f. carnea, commonly known as the Crimson Bells, is a striking perennial plant native to Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to crimson in color, with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both a decorative and potentially toxic plant, requiring care during handling.
