Drymoglossum cunninghamii

Drymoglossum cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's feather moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green, wiry leaves that are often curved. The plant forms compact cushions or mats, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its sporophytes are relatively short, with a smooth, cylindrical capsule that matures to a reddish color.

This moss plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
