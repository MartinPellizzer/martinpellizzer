Doxantha lanuginosa

Doxantha lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly doxantha, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Doxantha lanuginosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
