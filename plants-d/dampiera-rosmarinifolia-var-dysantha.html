Dampiera rosmarinifolia var. dysantha

Dampiera rosmarinifolia var. dysantha, commonly known as the silverleaf dampiera, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It features aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting foliage.
