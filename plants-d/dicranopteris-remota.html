Dicranopteris remota

Dicranopteris remota, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a large, aquatic fern that thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments.

It has long, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, with a distinctive pinnate structure. The plant is characterized by its ability to form dense mats on the water surface, which can help in stabilizing water ecosystems. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for wide dispersal and colonization of new habitats.

Dicranopteris remota is an important species in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to aquatic biodiversity and water quality regulation.
