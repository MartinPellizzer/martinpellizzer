Deschampsia ircutica

Deschampsia ircutica, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often covered in short hairs.

Silky bluegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
