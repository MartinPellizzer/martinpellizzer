Dalea cutervoana

Dalea cutervoana, commonly known as the Cutervo milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea cutervoana is adapted to the harsh, cold conditions of its alpine habitat, showcasing resilience to extreme environmental conditions.
