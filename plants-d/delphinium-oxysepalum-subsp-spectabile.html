Delphinium oxysepalum subsp. spectabile

Delphinium oxysepalum subsp.

spectabile, commonly known as the Spectacular Larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of blue to purple flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, typical of the Delphinium genus.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
