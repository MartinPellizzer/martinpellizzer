Drypetes falcata

Drypetes falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is often found in secondary growth areas.

The species is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
