Dinebra retroflexa

Dinebra retroflexa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Dinebra retroflexa is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, where it thrives in warm, humid climates.
