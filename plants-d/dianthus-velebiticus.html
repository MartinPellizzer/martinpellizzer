Dianthus velebiticus

Dianthus velebiticus, commonly known as the Velebit carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a compact, mat-forming growth habit.

Dianthus velebiticus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
