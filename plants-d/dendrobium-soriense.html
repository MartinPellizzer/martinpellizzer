Dendrobium soriense

Dendrobium soriense, commonly known as the Soriense Orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often adorned with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Dendrobium soriense thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.
