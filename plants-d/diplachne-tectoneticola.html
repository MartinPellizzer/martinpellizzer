Diplachne tectoneticola

Diplachne tectoneticola, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. Diplachne tectoneticola thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
