Dielsiris pelogonus

Dielsiris pelogonus, commonly known as the Pelogon iris, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue and violet. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences with multiple petals arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
