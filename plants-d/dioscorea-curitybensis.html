Dioscorea curitybensis

Dioscorea curitybensis, commonly known as the Curitiba yam, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual.

Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
