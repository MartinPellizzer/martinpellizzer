Dichaea ecuadorensis

Dichaea ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian dichaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it has short, fibrous roots that help it anchor to its host. Dichaea ecuadorensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high air humidity to flourish.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
