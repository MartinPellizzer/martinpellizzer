Dendrochilum anfractum var. anfractoides

Dendrochilum anfractum var. anfractoides, commonly known as the wrinkled tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, wrinkled pseudobulbs and long, arching stems that bear clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth or on tree trunks. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unusual morphology and floral display.
