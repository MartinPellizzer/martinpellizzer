Dudleya nubigena subsp. cerralvensis

Dudleya nubigena subsp.

cerralvensis, commonly known as the Cerralves Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to lavender during the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
