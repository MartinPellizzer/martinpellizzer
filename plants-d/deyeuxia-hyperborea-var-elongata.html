Deyeuxia hyperborea var. elongata

Deyeuxia hyperborea var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated moorgrass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude and arctic regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often found in tundra and alpine meadows. Its morphology is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

The species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various Arctic and alpine organisms.
