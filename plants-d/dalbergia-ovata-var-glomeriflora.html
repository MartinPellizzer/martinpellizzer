Dalbergia ovata var. glomeriflora

Dalbergia ovata var. glomeriflora, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural and commercial purposes.
