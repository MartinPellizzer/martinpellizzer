Disa tripartita

Disa tripartita, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, three-lobed petals that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Disa tripartita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
