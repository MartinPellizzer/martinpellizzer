Dioscorea japonica var. pseudojaponica

Dioscorea japonica var. pseudojaponica, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The tubers are valued for their culinary and medicinal properties, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful processing.
