Dianthus orientalis subsp. aphanoneurus

Dianthus orientalis subsp.

aphanoneurus, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-layered flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces showy, clustered blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
