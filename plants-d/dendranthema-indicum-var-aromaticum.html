Dendranthema indicum var. aromaticum

Dendranthema indicum var. aromaticum, commonly known as chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including yellow, white, red, and purple. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a bushy growth habit and finely divided, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Dendranthema indicum var. aromaticum is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
