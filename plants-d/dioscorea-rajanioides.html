Dioscorea rajanioides

Dioscorea rajanioides, commonly known as the Indian yam, is a climbing vine native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
