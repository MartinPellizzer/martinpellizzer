Dioscorea piperifolia

Dioscorea piperifolia, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
