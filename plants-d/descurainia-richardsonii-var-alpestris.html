Descurainia richardsonii var. alpestris

Descurainia richardsonii var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately compound and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The species is often found in alpine tundra and is characteristic of the subalpine zone.
