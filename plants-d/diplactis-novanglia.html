Diplactis novanglia

Diplactis novanglia, commonly known as the New World fan-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, organic soil.

Diplactis novanglia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
