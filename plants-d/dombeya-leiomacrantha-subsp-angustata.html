Dombeya leiomacrantha subsp. angustata

Dombeya leiomacrantha subsp.

angustata, commonly known as narrow-leaved dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
