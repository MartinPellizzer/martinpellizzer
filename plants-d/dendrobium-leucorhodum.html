Dendrobium leucorhodum

Dendrobium leucorhodum, commonly known as the white-lipped dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive red lip. It thrives in warm, humid climates with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium leucorhodum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
