Delphinium potaninii var. longibracteatum

Delphinium potaninii var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple spires of flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and deeply lobed, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic appearance.
