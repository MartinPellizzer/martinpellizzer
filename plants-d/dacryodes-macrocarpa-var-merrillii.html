Dacryodes macrocarpa var. merrillii

Dacryodes macrocarpa var. merrillii, commonly known as the Malay ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and smooth, often aromatic bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, oblong fruits containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.
