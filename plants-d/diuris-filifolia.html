Diuris filifolia

Diuris filifolia, commonly known as the slender-leafed spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal or heathland environments. It produces small, greenish or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, often in spring.

Diuris filifolia is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
