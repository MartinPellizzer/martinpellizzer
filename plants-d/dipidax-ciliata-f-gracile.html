Dipidax ciliata f. gracile

Dipidax ciliata f. gracile, commonly known as the slender fringed bellflower, is a botanical variant of the species Dipidax ciliata.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. This plant is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its unique foliage and floral structure make it a sought-after ornamental plant in horticultural collections.

Due to its limited natural range, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to preserve its genetic diversity.
