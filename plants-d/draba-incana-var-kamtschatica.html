Draba incana var. kamtschatica

Draba incana var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are generally hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

This variety is often found in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climates.
