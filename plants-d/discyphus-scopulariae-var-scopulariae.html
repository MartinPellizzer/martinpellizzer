Discyphus scopulariae var. scopulariae

Discyphus scopulariae var. scopulariae, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
