Dracontium bissectum

Dracontium bissectum, commonly known as the split-leaf arum, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a prominent spadix. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Dracontium bissectum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
