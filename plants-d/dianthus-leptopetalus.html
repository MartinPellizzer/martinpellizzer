Dianthus leptopetalus

Dianthus leptopetalus, commonly known as the slender-petaled campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dianthus leptopetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
