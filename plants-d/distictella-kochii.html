Distictella kochii

Distictella kochii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Distictella kochii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
