Deyeuxia pilosa

Deyeuxia pilosa, commonly known as hairy beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, dense foliage and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Deyeuxia pilosa produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
