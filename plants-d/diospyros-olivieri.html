Diospyros olivieri

Diospyros olivieri, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, though often astringent when unripe, and is used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is hard and durable.
