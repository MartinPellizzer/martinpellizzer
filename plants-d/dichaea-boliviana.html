Dichaea boliviana

Dichaea boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a slender rhizome and a single, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically arranged in a spike. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest floors.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
