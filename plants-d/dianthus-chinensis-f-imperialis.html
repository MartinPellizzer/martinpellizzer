Dianthus chinensis f. imperialis

Dianthus chinensis f. imperialis, commonly known as the Imperial carnation, is a cultivated form of the Chinese carnation.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of five petals that are often ruffled or frilled, giving them a distinctive and ornamental appearance.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is commonly used in gardens and floral arrangements.
