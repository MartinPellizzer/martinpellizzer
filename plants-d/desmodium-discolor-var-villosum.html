Desmodium discolor var. villosum

Desmodium discolor var. villosum, commonly known as hairy desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
