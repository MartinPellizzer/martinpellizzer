Diospyros aggregata

Diospyros aggregata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Diospyros aggregata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and wood.
