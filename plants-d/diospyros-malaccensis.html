Diospyros malaccensis

Diospyros malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature, and it is edible when fully ripe.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruits it produces.
