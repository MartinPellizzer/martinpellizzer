Dioscorea linearicordata

Dioscorea linearicordata, commonly known as the slender yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various culinary applications.

Due to its adaptability, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
