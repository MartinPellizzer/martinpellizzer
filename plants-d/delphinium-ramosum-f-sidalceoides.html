Delphinium ramosum f. sidalceoides

Delphinium ramosum f. sidalceoides, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
