Draba crassifolia var. albertina

Draba crassifolia var. albertina, commonly known as the Albertina rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its resilience and is often found in mountainous areas across Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Tajikistan.
