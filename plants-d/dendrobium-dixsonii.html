Dendrobium dixsonii

Dendrobium dixsonii, commonly known as the Dixson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent red sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Dendrobium dixsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
