Delphinium lasiantherum

Delphinium lasiantherum, commonly known as the tall larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Delphinium lasiantherum is also noted for its toxicity, containing compounds that can be harmful to both humans and animals.
