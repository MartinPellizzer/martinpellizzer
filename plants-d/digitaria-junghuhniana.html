Digitaria junghuhniana

Digitaria junghuhniana, commonly known as the African fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Digitaria junghuhniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
