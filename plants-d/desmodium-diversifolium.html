Desmodium diversifolium

Desmodium diversifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable component of native plant communities.
