Decaloba dentata

Decaloba dentata, commonly known as the serrated balm or toothed balm, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, serrated leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Decaloba dentata is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming effects.
