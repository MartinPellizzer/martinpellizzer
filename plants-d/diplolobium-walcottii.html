Diplolobium walcottii

Diplolobium walcottii, commonly known as the Walcott's burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
