Dalea villosa var. grisea

Dalea villosa var. grisea, commonly known as gray-hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its pale pink to lavender flowers and hairy, grayish foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea villosa var. grisea is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
