Diplachne mendocina

Diplachne mendocina, commonly known as the Mendoza grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically two-ranked, with small, awned lemmas. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is adapted to temperate climates.

Diplachne mendocina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
