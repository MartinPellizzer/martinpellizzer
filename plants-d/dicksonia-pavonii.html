Dicksonia pavonii

Dicksonia pavonii, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a distinctive golden hue on its fronds, which becomes more pronounced as the leaves mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Dicksonia pavonii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
