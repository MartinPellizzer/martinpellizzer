Deschampsia cespitosa var. macrothyrsa

Deschampsia cespitosa var. macrothyrsa, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with dense, cylindrical clumps of foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces airy, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wet meadows and marshes, and is often found in disturbed or riparian areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
