Diospyros pendula

Diospyros pendula, commonly known as the hanging persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, orange-red berries that mature in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
