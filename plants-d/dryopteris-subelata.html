Dryopteris subelata

Dryopteris subelata, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are often adorned with golden or yellow spore cases, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive shield-shaped base.

Dryopteris subelata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
