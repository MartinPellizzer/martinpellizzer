Daphne × napolitana

Daphne × napolitana, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has dense, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders.

The plant is noted for its cold hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
