Dampiera spicigera

Dampiera spicigera, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grey-green, with a woolly texture that helps reduce water loss.

Dampiera spicigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
