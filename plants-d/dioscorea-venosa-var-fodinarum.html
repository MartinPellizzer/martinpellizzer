Dioscorea venosa var. fodinarum

Dioscorea venosa var. fodinarum, commonly known as the Fodina vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that are often green with a silvery underside. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by tuberous roots that are valued for their medicinal properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
