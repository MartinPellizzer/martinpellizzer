Desmoncus tobagonis

Desmoncus tobagonis, commonly known as the Trinidadian orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to Trinidad and Tobago.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
