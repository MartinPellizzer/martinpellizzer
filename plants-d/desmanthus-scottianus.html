Desmanthus scottianus

Desmanthus scottianus, commonly known as the Texas needlepod, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Desmanthus scottianus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
