Desmodium tenuipes var. typicum

Desmodium tenuipes var. typicum, commonly known as the slender leaf desmodium, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
