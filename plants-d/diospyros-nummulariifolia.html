Diospyros nummulariifolia

Diospyros nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by small, round fruits resembling cherry tomatoes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
