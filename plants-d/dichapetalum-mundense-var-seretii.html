Dichapetalum mundense var. seretii

Dichapetalum mundense var. seretii, commonly known as the Seretii flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical landscapes.

Its unique floral display and adaptability to warm climates contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
