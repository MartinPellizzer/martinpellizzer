Datura hummatu var. fastuosa

Datura hummatu var. fastuosa, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a striking plant characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to South America, particularly regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces toxic alkaloids, making it both a fascinating and potentially dangerous species.
