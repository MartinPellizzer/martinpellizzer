Dioscorea galeottiana

Dioscorea galeottiana, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by tuberous roots that are edible and used in traditional cuisine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Dioscorea galeottiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in various traditional healing practices.
