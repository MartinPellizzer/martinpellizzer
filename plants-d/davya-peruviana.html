Davya peruviana

Davya peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian davya, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
