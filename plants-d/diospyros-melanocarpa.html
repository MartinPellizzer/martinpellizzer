Diospyros melanocarpa

Diospyros melanocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, depending on the variety and ripeness.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
