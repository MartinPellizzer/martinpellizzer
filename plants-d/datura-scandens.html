Datura scandens

Datura scandens, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in a vine-like manner, often climbing or trailing along the ground. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale purple, with a trumpet-shaped structure. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and the plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor.

Datura scandens contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
