Diplopappus mucronatus

Diplopappus mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, often thorny growth habit and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule that releases seeds with tufts of hair, facilitating wind dispersal.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
