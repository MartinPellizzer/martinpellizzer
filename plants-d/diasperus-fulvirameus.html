Diasperus fulvirameus

Diasperus fulvirameus, commonly known as the golden-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often tinged with a golden hue, especially along the nodes. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy component of dryland vegetation.
