Digitaria angolensis

Digitaria angolensis, commonly known as the African grass or Angola grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Digitaria angolensis is valued for its use in forage and erosion control in agricultural settings.
