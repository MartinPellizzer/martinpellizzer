Deutzia discolor var. purpurascens

Deutzia discolor var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of white, pink, and purple hues on its leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by ornamental red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique leaf color and seasonal interest make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
