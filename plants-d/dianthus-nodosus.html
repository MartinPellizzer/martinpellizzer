Dianthus nodosus

Dianthus nodosus, commonly known as the nodding pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.

Dianthus nodosus is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resistance to pests and diseases.
