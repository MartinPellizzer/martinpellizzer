Delphinium greenei

Delphinium greenei, commonly known as Greene's larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a notable species in mountainous regions.
