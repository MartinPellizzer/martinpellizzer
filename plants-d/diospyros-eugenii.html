Diospyros eugenii

Diospyros eugenii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its bark is smooth and often marked with vertical fissures, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry conditions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
