Dianthus banaticiformis

Dianthus banaticiformis, commonly known as the Banat's campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Dianthus banaticiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
