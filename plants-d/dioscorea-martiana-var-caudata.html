Dioscorea martiana var. caudata

Dioscorea martiana var. caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy surface.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated for its edible tubers.
