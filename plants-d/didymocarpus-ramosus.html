Didymocarpus ramosus

Didymocarpus ramosus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climatic conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
