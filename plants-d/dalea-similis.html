Dalea similis

Dalea similis, commonly known as the prairie milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea similis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
