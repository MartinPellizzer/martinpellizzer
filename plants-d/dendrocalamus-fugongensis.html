Dendrocalamus fugongensis

Dendrocalamus fugongensis, commonly known as the Fugong bamboo, is a tall, slender bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, straight culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for its timber and as a source of bamboo shoots. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, and the plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity.

Dendrocalamus fugongensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
