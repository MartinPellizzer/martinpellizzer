Dens-leonis

Dens-leonis, commonly known as lion's dent, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky flowers that resemble a lion's mane. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dens-leonis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
