Diospyros elliptica

Diospyros elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. Its bark is often marked with shallow fissures and has a smooth, light grayish-brown appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
