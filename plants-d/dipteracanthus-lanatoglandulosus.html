Dipteracanthus lanatoglandulosus

Dipteracanthus lanatoglandulosus, commonly known as the wooly glandular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes many species with similar floral structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique combination of woolly foliage and vibrant blooms makes it an interesting subject for botanical study.
