Dolomiaea edulis

Dolomiaea edulis, commonly known as the edible cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered flowers. The plant thrives in hot, dry environments and has adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its edible fruits are typically small, yellow or orange, and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Due to its unique adaptations and nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
