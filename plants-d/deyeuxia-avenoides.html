Deyeuxia avenoides

Deyeuxia avenoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf false oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single, compact spikelet that is usually borne at the top of the stem.

Deyeuxia avenoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
