Dichopsis obovata

Dichopsis obovata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Dichopsis obovata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
