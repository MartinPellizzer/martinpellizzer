Diplocaulobium cadetioides

Diplocaulobium cadetioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved duckweed, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its ribbon-like leaves that float on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, followed by the development of small, spherical fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
