Dracula tarantula

Dracula tarantula, commonly known as the "Mexican red-knee tarantula," is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red legs and a dark, almost black body with distinctive white markings. The plant aspect of this tarantula refers to its role in the ecosystem as a predator, feeding primarily on insects and small invertebrates. Its habitat includes humid rainforests and undergrowth, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often studied for its behavior and conservation status.
