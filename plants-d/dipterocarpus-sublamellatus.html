Dipterocarpus sublamellatus

Dipterocarpus sublamellatus, commonly known as the flat-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, flat, leathery leaves. The tree produces heavy, woody fruits that contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is an important component of the region's biodiversity.

Its timber is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
