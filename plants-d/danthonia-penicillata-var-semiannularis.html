Danthonia penicillata var. semiannularis

Danthonia penicillata var. semiannularis, commonly known as the semiannular danthonia, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and distinctive, semi-circular leaf sheaths that give it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas. It forms dense tussocks and is well adapted to temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
