Dentaria tenuifolia

Dentaria tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf toothwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a toothed or serrated edge, giving it its common name.

Dentaria tenuifolia typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
