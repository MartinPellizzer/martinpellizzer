Dryandra subpinnatifida var. imberbis

Dryandra subpinnatifida var. imberbis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dryandra, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and exhibits drought tolerance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
