Dracocephalum cuniloides

Dracocephalum cuniloides, commonly known as Persian feverfew, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, also known as the mint family, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice in herbal gardens and for essential oil extraction.
