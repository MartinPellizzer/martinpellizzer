Diospyros subargentea

Diospyros subargentea, commonly known as silver flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are slightly sweet and tangy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Diospyros subargentea is also valued for its hard, heavy wood used in traditional crafts and construction.
