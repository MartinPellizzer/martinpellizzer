Dombeya dolichophylla

Dombeya dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
