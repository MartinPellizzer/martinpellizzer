Deschampsia holciformis

Deschampsia holciformis, commonly known as silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-haired inflorescence and fine, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and forest edges. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense stands.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
