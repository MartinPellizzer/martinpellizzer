Dudleya cespitosa

Dudleya cespitosa, commonly known as the coastal dudleya, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It features dense, rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring. It is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
