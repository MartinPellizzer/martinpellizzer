Diplocaulobium nitidissimum

Diplocaulobium nitidissimum, commonly known as the smoothleaf water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the pond bottom while absorbing nutrients from the water.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
