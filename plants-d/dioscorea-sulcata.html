Dioscorea sulcata

Dioscorea sulcata, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing perennial vine native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by elongated, bean-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source in various African countries.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important crop in tropical and subtropical regions.
