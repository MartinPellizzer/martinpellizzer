Desmodium heterophyllum

Desmodium heterophyllum, commonly known as hairy bird's-paw, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a distinct hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated, flat pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Desmodium heterophyllum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
