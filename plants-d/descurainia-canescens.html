Descurainia canescens

Descurainia canescens, commonly known as hoary tansy, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its grayish-white pubescence, giving it a dusty appearance, and its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
