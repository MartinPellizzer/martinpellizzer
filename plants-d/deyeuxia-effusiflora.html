Deyeuxia effusiflora

Deyeuxia effusiflora, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that bears numerous small, pale green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as marshes and floodplains. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Deyeuxia effusiflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
