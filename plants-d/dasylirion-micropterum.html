Dasylirion micropterum

Dasylirion micropterum, commonly known as the narrowleaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of stiff, narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
