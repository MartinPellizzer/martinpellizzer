Deutzia parviflora var. barbinervis

Deutzia parviflora var. barbinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate with finely serrated edges and a bluish-green hue. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its graceful form and seasonal blooms.
