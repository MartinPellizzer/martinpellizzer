Dryopteris soriloba

Dryopteris soriloba, commonly known as the Japanese wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive dark green color and a finely toothed margin. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
