Dendrobium linguiforme var. nugentii

Dendrobium linguiforme var. nugentii, commonly known as the golden tongue orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped pseudobulbs that are often adorned with a dense covering of golden to yellowish hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically arranged in racemes along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
