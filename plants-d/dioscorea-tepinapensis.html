Dioscorea tepinapensis

Dioscorea tepinapensis, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to Central America and northern South America, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used for their edible properties, though they require careful preparation to avoid toxicity. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are typically yellow or green.

Dioscorea tepinapensis is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in traditional medicine.
