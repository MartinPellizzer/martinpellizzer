Dryopteris appendiculata

Dryopteris appendiculata, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale brown, cinnamon-colored fiddleheads that unfurl into large, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves.

The cinnamon fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
