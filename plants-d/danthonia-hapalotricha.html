Danthonia hapalotricha

Danthonia hapalotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along watercourses. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Danthonia hapalotricha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
