Diplazium acutale

Diplazium acutale, commonly known as the sharp-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often submerged in water. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Diplazium acutale plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
