Diospyros kuroiwae

Diospyros kuroiwae, commonly known as the Kuroiwae persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that ripen to a deep orange-red color, often with a waxy coating. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
