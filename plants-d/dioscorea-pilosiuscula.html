Dioscorea pilosiuscula

Dioscorea pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also valued for their nutritional content and have been utilized in various culinary practices.

Dioscorea pilosiuscula is an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
