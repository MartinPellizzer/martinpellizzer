Daphne glabra

Daphne glabra, commonly known as smooth daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable addition to gardens.

Daphne glabra is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its winter blossoms and aromatic foliage.
