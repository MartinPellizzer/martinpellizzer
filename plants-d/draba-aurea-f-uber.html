Draba aurea f. uber

Draba aurea f. uber, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, where it can withstand harsh conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ability to grow in nutrient-poor soils, making it a common sight in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia.
