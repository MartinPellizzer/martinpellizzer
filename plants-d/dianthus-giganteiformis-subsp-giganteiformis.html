Dianthus giganteiformis subsp. giganteiformis

Dianthus giganteiformis subsp.

giganteiformis, commonly known as the giant carnation, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying shades of pink and white.

The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with numerous branched stems covered in soft, velvety foliage. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact in gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
