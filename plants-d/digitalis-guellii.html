Digitalis guellii

Digitalis guellii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes several species known for their medicinal properties. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Digitalis guellii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal medicine.
