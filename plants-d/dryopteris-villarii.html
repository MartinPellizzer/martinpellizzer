Dryopteris villarii

Dryopteris villarii, commonly known as the Mexican wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant features a thick, rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, intricate frond structure.

Dryopteris villarii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
