Dichondra argentea

Dichondra argentea, commonly known as silverleaf dichondra, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features small, round leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a ground cover in landscaping due to its ornamental value.

Dichondra argentea is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and partial shade.
