Drymocallis wrangelliana

Drymocallis wrangelliana, commonly known as Wrangell's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and forest edges. The plant produces showy, golden-yellow inflorescences in late summer to early autumn, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinctive grayish-green color.

Drymocallis wrangelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
