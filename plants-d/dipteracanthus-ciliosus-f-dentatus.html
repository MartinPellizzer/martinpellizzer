Dipteracanthus ciliosus f. dentatus

Dipteracanthus ciliosus f. dentatus, commonly known as the serrated fringed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are slender and branching. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
