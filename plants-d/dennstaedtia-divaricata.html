Dennstaedtia divaricata

Dennstaedtia divaricata, commonly known as the forked fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds that are divided into two main branches, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick layer of brownish, moss-like tissue.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.
