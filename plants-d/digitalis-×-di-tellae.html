Digitalis × di-tellae

Digitalis × di-tellae, commonly known as the hybrid foxglove, is a perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of hooded flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces tubular corollas with a distinctive spurred shape, typical of the genus Digitalis. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
