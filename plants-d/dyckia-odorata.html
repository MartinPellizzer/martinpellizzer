Dyckia odorata

Dyckia odorata, commonly known as the sweet grass or sweet sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Dyckia odorata is known for its aromatic leaves, which emit a sweet scent when crushed.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
