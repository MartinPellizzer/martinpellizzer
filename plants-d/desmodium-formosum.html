Desmodium formosum

Desmodium formosum, commonly known as the purple desmodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit with stolons that allow it to spread across the ground.

The flowers are typically purple or pink, and the plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.
