Drosera americana

Drosera americana, commonly known as the American sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a mucilaginous substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its leaves are often rosette-shaped and can vary in color from green to reddish.

Drosera americana reproduces through seeds and can also propagate vegetatively through its rhizomes.
