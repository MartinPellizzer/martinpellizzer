Dyckia gouveiana

Dyckia gouveiana, commonly known as the Gouveia's Dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically red or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Dyckia gouveiana is a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative indoor plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
