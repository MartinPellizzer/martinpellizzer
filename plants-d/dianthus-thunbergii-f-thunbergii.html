Dianthus thunbergii f. thunbergii

Dianthus thunbergii f. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive texture. It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
