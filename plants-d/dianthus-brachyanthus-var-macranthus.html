Dianthus brachyanthus var. macranthus

Dianthus brachyanthus var. macranthus, commonly known as the giant carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white or pale pink center. The plant produces dense clusters of petals, which are typically arranged in a flat or slightly rounded shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
