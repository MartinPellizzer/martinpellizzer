Dalbergia semiapplanata

Dalbergia semiapplanata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow up to 15 meters in height.
