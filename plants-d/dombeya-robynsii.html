Dombeya robynsii

Dombeya robynsii, commonly known as the Robyn's dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Dombeya robynsii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
