Dapsilanthus

Dapsilanthus, commonly known as the golden star, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in a star-like pattern, giving it its common name. Dapsilanthus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
