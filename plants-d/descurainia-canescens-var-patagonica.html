Descurainia canescens var. patagonica

Descurainia canescens var. patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a grayish appearance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native range.
