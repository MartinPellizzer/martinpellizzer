Dianella caerulea var. producta

Dianella caerulea var. producta, commonly known as the blue-flowered lily or New Zealand flax, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are dark green and often have a bluish tinge. The plant produces spikes of blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant appearance.
