Dioscorea spiralis

Dioscorea spiralis, commonly known as the spiral yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its helical growth habit, where the stems twist around supporting structures in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Dioscorea spiralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
