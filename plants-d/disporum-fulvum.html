Disporum fulvum

Disporum fulvum, commonly known as the golden threadleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves that are typically yellow-green in color, with a slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Disporum fulvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
