Dichromanthus cinnabarinus subsp. galeottianum

Dichromanthus cinnabarinus subsp.

galeottianum, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its striking color and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
