Derris rariflora

Derris rariflora, commonly known as the rare-flowered derris, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, also known as the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in hard, pod-like fruits, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties due to the presence of rotenone.

However, it is considered toxic and should be handled with care.
