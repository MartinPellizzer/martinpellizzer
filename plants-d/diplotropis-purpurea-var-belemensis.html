Diplotropis purpurea var. belemensis

Diplotropis purpurea var. belemensis, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and food for various wildlife species.
