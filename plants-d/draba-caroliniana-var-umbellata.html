Draba caroliniana var. umbellata

Draba caroliniana var. umbellata, commonly known as the Carolina rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. It has basal leaves that are pinnately divided, and its stems are usually unbranched with few leaves.
