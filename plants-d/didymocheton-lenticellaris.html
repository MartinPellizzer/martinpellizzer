Didymocheton lenticellaris

Didymocheton lenticellaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often waxy leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the year in suitable climates. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Didymocheton lenticellaris is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.
