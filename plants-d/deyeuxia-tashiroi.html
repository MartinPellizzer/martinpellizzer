Deyeuxia tashiroi

Deyeuxia tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Deyeuxia tashiroi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
