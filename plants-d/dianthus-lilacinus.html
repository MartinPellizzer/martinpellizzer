Dianthus lilacinus

Dianthus lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It grows in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and open woodlands.

Dianthus lilacinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
