Dianthus cretaceus subsp. cretaceus

Dianthus cretaceus subsp.

cretaceus, commonly known as the Cretaceous carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces vibrant pink to red flowers with five petals.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The subspecies is notable for its historical significance in horticulture and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
