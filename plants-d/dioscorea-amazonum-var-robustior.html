Dioscorea amazonum var. robustior

Dioscorea amazonum var. robustior, commonly known as the Amazon yam, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its flowering occurs in clusters of small, greenish-white flowers, followed by the development of inedible, spiny fruits.

The species is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to humid, shaded environments.
