Drypetes radamae

Drypetes radamae, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been introduced to other tropical regions for landscaping purposes.
