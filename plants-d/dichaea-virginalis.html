Dichaea virginalis

Dichaea virginalis, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the substrate.

Dichaea virginalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
