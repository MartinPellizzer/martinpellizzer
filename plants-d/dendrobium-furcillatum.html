Dendrobium furcillatum

Dendrobium furcillatum, commonly known as the forked dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent purple markings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged spirally along the stems.

Dendrobium furcillatum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
