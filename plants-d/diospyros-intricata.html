Diospyros intricata

Diospyros intricata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Diospyros intricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, honey-like flavor when fully ripe.
