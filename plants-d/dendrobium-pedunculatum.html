Dendrobium pedunculatum

Dendrobium pedunculatum, commonly known as the orchid spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, branched inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Dendrobium pedunculatum is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
