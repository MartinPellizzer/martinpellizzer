Discocactus subterraneo-proliferans

Discocactus subterraneo-proliferans, commonly known as the underground-propagating discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are often hidden beneath the soil surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, which are pollinated by nocturnal insects. Its roots system is highly adapted for water storage, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its rarity and specialized habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
