Dodecatheon pubescens

Dodecatheon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy five-petaled lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish in color and have five petals.
