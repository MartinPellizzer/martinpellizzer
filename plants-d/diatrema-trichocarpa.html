Diatrema trichocarpa

Diatrema trichocarpa, commonly known as the red-tipped pine.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States, characterized by its slender, straight trunk and needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, and it is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against fire and drought.

The red-tipped pine is an important species in forest ecosystems, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
