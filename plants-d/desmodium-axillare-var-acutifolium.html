Desmodium axillare var. acutifolium

Desmodium axillare var. acutifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf desmodium, is a perennial leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant has pinnate stems and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
