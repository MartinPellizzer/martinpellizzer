Diospyros cachimboensis

Diospyros cachimboensis, commonly known as the Cachimbo persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. The fruit is edible, though typically consumed when fully ripe, and the wood is valued for its dense, durable quality.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
