Dianthus strictiformis

Dianthus strictiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus strictiformis is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
