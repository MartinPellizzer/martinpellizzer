Dryopteris hwangii

Dryopteris hwangii, commonly known as the Hwang's wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Dryopteris hwangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
