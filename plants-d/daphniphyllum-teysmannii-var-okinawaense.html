Daphniphyllum teysmannii var. okinawaense

Daphniphyllum teysmannii var. okinawaense, commonly known as the Okinawan holly, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of small, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
