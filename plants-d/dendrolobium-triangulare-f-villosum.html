Dendrolobium triangulare f. villosum

Dendrolobium triangulare f. villosum, commonly known as the hairy triangle legume, is a variant of the species Dendrolobium triangulare, characterized by its soft, hairy foliage.

It is a perennial shrub native to Australia, typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive triangular shape and small, yellow flowers clustered in dense spikes. It is adapted to dry conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The hairy texture of its leaves helps reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.
