Dipteris papilioniformis

Dipteris papilioniformis, commonly known as the butterfly fern, is a unique fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds that resemble the wings of a butterfly, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are composed of multiple small, feather-like segments, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Dipteris papilioniformis is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
