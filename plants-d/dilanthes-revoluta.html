Dilanthes revoluta

Dilanthes revoluta, commonly known as the revolute dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a revolute (rolled under) margin. It produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a disk of tubular flowers, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Dilanthes revoluta is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
