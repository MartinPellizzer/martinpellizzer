Diplazium mutilum

Diplazium mutilum, commonly known as the broken fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often split or damaged, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Diplazium mutilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
