Dioscorea oxyphylla

Dioscorea oxyphylla, commonly known as the white yam, is a perennial vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their starchy content and traditional medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both food and herbal purposes.
