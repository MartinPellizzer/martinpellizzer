Digitaria boivinii

Digitaria boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's Digitaria, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
