Dipterygia chilensis

Dipterygia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean dipterygia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces seed pods that contain several small, edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of South American flora and ecological resilience.
