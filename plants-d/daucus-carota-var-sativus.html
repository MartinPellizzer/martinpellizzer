Daucus carota var. sativus

Daucus carota var. sativus, commonly known as the domesticated carrot, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its taproot, which is the primary edible part, and its finely divided, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the carrot's evolutionary lineage. Its roots come in various colors, including orange, purple, red, and white, due to different pigmentation genes.

The plant is cultivated worldwide for its nutritional value and versatility in culinary use.
