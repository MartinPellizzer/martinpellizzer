Diospyros crassinervis subsp. crassinervis

Diospyros crassinervis subsp.

crassinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical leaves with prominent, parallel veins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to reddish persimmons that are edible when fully ripe. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish, with a fragrant scent when crushed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, which is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
