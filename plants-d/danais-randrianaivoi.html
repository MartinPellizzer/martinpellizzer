Danais randrianaivoi

Danais randrianaivoi, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator of the region's biodiversity and its potential for medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
