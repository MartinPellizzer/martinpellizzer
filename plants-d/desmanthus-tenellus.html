Desmanthus tenellus

Desmanthus tenellus, commonly known as the slender desmanthus, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It has a fibrous root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Desmanthus tenellus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
