Ditassa blanchetii

Ditassa blanchetii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and striking coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforests of Madagascar. Its flowers are highly specialized, often featuring intricate patterns and vibrant hues that attract specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
