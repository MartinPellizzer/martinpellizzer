Dendrobium sanderianum

Dendrobium sanderianum, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive red lip, and bloom during the warmer months.

Dendrobium sanderianum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
