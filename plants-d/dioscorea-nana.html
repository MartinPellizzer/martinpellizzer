Dioscorea nana

Dioscorea nana, commonly known as the dwarf yam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter, with simple, alternate leaves that are heart-shaped or ovate. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The tubers of Dioscorea nana are rich in starch and have been utilized in various culinary and medicinal practices across different cultures.
