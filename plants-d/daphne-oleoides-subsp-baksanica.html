Daphne oleoides subsp. baksanica

Daphne oleoides subsp.

baksanica, commonly known as the Baksan daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include small, elliptical leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers with prominent stamens.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and cold hardiness.
