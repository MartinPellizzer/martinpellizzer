Desmotrichum kunstleri

Desmotrichum kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. It features basal leaves that are often hairy and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
