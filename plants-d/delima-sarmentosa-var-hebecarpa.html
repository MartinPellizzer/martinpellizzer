Delima sarmentosa var. hebecarpa

Delima sarmentosa var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the slender-fruited delima, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The flowers are typically small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
