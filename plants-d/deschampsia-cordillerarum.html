Deschampsia cordillerarum

Deschampsia cordillerarum, commonly known as Sierra grass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are often tinged with pink or purple.

Deschampsia cordillerarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
