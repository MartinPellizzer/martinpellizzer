Dysophylla verticillata

Dysophylla verticillata, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, bladder-like structures that trap microscopic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are slender and often submerged, while its reproductive structures are specialized for aquatic pollination.

Dysophylla verticillata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms.
