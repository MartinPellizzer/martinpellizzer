Danthonia zeyheriana var. trichostachya

Danthonia zeyheriana var. trichostachya, commonly known as the tufted fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
