Dioscorea nutans

Dioscorea nutans, commonly known as the hanging yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, containing compounds like diosgenin, which has medicinal applications.

Dioscorea nutans thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
