Dolichos adenophorus

Dolichos adenophorus, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
