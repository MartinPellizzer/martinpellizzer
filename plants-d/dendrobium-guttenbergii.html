Dendrobium guttenbergii

Dendrobium guttenbergii, commonly known as the Guttengerg's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs. The plant typically has linear, grass-like leaves and produces strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Dendrobium guttenbergii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
