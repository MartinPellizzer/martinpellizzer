Dendrobium fluctuosum

Dendrobium fluctuosum, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters along the stems.

Dendrobium fluctuosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
