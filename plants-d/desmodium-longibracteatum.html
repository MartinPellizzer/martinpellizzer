Desmodium longibracteatum

Desmodium longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
