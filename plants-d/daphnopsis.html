Daphnopsis

Daphnopsis, commonly known as the silverleaf daphnopsis, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking silver or gray-green foliage, which is covered in dense, fine hairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia.

Daphnopsis is native to parts of Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
