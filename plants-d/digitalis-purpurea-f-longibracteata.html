Digitalis purpurea f. longibracteata

Digitalis purpurea f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long bract foxglove, is a striking cultivar of the foxglove plant.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like flower clusters that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, typical of the Digitalis genus. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in cool, moist conditions and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
