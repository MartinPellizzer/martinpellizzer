Dryades insignis

Dryades insignis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dryades insignis is often found in moist, open woods and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
