Dasistoma macrophylla

Dasistoma macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf dasistoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
