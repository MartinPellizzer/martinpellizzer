Dipteracanthus canescens

Dipteracanthus canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered leaves that have a silvery-gray appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
