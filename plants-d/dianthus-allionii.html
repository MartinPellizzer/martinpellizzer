Dianthus allionii

Dianthus allionii, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus allionii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic beauty.
