Dipterocarpus andamanicus

Dipterocarpus andamanicus, commonly known as the Andaman dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and thick, flaky bark. The tree produces woody cones that contain seeds, and it plays a significant role in the rainforest ecosystem. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it is considered a key species for biodiversity conservation in its native region.
