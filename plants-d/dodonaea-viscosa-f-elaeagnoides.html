Dodonaea viscosa f. elaeagnoides

Dodonaea viscosa f. elaeagnoides, commonly known as the silver leaf dodonaea, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
