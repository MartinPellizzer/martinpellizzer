Disa albomagentea

Disa albomagentea, commonly known as the white magenta disa, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white and pink floral coloration, which gives it its common name. The plant produces delicate, nodding flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters along the stem. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Disa albomagentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
