Dichanthium fecundum

Dichanthium fecundum, commonly known as the African wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for landscaping in arid climates.

Its seeds are often used in traditional agricultural practices and for forage.
