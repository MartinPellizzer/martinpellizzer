Daphnopsis coriacea

Daphnopsis coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf daphnopsis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, ovate leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Daphnopsis coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
