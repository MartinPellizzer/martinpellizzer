Dendrobium guadalcanalense

Dendrobium guadalcanalense, commonly known as the Guadalcanal dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to the island of Guadalcanal in the Solomon Islands.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that often branch and bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with multiple flowers, typically in shades of white, yellow, or orange, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
