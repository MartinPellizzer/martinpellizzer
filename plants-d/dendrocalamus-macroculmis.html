Dendrocalamus macroculmis

Dendrocalamus macroculmis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, woody perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its rhizomes are thick and capable of producing new shoots, contributing to its rapid growth and spread.

Dendrocalamus macroculmis is valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
