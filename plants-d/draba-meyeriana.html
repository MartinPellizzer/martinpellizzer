Draba meyeriana

Draba meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the late spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, linear, and covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Draba meyeriana is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
