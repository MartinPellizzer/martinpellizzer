Damnacanthus biflorus

Damnacanthus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
