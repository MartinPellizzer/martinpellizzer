Draba rupestris f. corymbosa

Draba rupestris f. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are typically arranged in corymbs, giving the plant its specific epithet.

Draba rupestris f. corymbosa is adapted to harsh conditions and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia.
