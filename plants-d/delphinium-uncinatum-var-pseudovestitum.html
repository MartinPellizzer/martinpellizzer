Delphinium uncinatum var. pseudovestitum

Delphinium uncinatum var. pseudovestitum, commonly known as the creeping larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in alpine and subalpine environments.
