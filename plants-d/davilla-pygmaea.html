Davilla pygmaea

Davilla pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf huito, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
