Darwinia quinqueflora

Darwinia quinqueflora, commonly known as the five-flowered Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to south-eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush. Darwinia quinqueflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
