Dimeria falcata

Dimeria falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dimeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, which includes cacti and other succulent plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent stamens. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Dimeria falcata is adapted to withstand drought conditions through its succulent stems and reduced leaf surface area.
