Dalbergia torta

Dalbergia torta, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard timber that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The tree has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

It is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats, where it plays an important ecological role.
