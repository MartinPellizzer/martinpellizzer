Diporidium mossambicense

Diporidium mossambicense, commonly known as the African spike moss, is a member of the family Dendroceratops.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is native to southern Africa, particularly along the coastal regions of Mozambique and South Africa.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
