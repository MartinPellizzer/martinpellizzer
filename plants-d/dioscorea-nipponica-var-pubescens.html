Dioscorea nipponica var. pubescens

Dioscorea nipponica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vine-like stems that climb and bear small, greenish-white flowers. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their starchy content and traditional medicinal uses.

The species is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
