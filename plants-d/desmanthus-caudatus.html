Desmanthus caudatus

Desmanthus caudatus, commonly known as the hairy desmanthus, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in prairie and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Desmanthus caudatus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
