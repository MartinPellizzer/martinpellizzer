Discocapnos mundii subsp. dregei

Discocapnos mundii subsp.

dregei, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
