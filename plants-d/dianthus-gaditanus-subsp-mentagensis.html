Dianthus gaditanus subsp. mentagensis

Dianthus gaditanus subsp.

mentagensis, commonly known as the Iberian campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas. Dianthus gaditanus subsp.

mentagensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
