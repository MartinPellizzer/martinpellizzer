Didiscus

Didiscus, commonly known as the "crown flower," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, radial flowers that resemble a crown, with five to seven petals arranged symmetrically. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often found in warm, arid regions. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy and erect.

Didiscus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral displays.
