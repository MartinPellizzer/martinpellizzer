Dryandra purdieana

Dryandra purdieana, commonly known as the Sturt's Desert Pea, is a striking flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple or red flowers, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a unique, spurred petal structure typical of the genus Dryandra. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Dryandra purdieana is also notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
