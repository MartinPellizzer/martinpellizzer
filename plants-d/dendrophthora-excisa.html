Dendrophthora excisa

Dendrophthora excisa, commonly known as the cutleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and gelatinous berries. The plant derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized roots called haustoria. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in dense forest canopies.

Its presence can have significant ecological impacts on the host tree's health and overall forest dynamics.
