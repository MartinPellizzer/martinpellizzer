Diospyros xiangguiensis

Diospyros xiangguiensis, commonly known as the Xianggui persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy berries that are edible when fully ripe.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
