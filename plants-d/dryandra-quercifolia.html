Dryandra quercifolia

Dryandra quercifolia, commonly known as the bottlebrush dryandra, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The species is part of the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy and often long-lasting floral displays.
