Dodonaea eriocarpa f. galapagensis

Dodonaea eriocarpa f. galapagensis, commonly known as the Galápagos Dodonaea, is a shrub native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the arid climate of the islands.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for local fauna.
