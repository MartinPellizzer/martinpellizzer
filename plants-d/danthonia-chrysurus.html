Danthonia chrysurus

Danthonia chrysurus, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration, and its flowers are typically golden or yellowish in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along watercourses.

Danthonia chrysurus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
