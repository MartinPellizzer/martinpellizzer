Dianthus spiculifolius

Dianthus spiculifolius, commonly known as the prickly carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces vibrant, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, pointed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

Dianthus spiculifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
