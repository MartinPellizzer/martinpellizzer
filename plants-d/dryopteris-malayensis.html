Dryopteris malayensis

Dryopteris malayensis, commonly known as the Malay wood fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its intricate fronds with a distinctive, often deeply lobed structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The fronds are usually evergreen, providing a lush green appearance throughout the year.
