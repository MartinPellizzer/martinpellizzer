Dampiera juncea

Dampiera juncea, commonly known as the slender dampiera, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Dampiera juncea is often found in coastal and rocky habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
