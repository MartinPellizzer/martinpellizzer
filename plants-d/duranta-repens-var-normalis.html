Duranta repens var. normalis

Duranta repens var. normalis, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, trailing stems that make it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery sheen, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
