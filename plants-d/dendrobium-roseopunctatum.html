Dendrobium roseopunctatum

Dendrobium roseopunctatum, commonly known as the rose-spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in tropical rainforests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that produce leaves and flowers sequentially. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive pink or red coloration and speckled patterns, often resembling roses.

It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
