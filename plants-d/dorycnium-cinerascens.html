Dorycnium cinerascens

Dorycnium cinerascens, commonly known as the ash fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage that resembles fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Dorycnium cinerascens is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
