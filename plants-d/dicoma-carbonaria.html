Dicoma carbonaria

Dicoma carbonaria, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Dicoma carbonaria is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
