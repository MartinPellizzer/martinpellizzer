Dentaria caucasica

Dentaria caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian toothplant, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, blooming in early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Dentaria caucasica is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging conditions.
