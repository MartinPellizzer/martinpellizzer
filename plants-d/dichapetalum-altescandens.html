Dichapetalum altescandens

Dichapetalum altescandens, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
