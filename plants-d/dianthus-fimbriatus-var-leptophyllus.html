Dianthus fimbriatus var. leptophyllus

Dianthus fimbriatus var. leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its distinctive appearance, while its stems are often hairy and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
