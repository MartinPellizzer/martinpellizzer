Decaloba swartzii

Decaloba swartzii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its showy, lantern-like blooms. Its leaves are typically broad and opposite, with a velvety texture.

Decaloba swartzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
