Dioscorea batatas f. tsukune

Dioscorea batatas f. tsukune, commonly known as Japanese yam or Tsukune yam, is a tuberous plant belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, fleshy tubers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The tubers are widely used in traditional Japanese cuisine, particularly in dishes like tsukune, which is a type of meatball.
