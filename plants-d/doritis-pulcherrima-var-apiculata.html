Doritis pulcherrima var. apiculata

Doritis pulcherrima var. apiculata, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, orange, and white. The plant has segmented, leaf-like stems that are often used for propagation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to bloom during the holiday season.
