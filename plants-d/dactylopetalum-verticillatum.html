Dactylopetalum verticillatum

Dactylopetalum verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by bracts that give it a striking appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

Dactylopetalum verticillatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
