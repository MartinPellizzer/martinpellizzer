Dolichos miniatus

Dolichos miniatus, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bean-like pods. Dolichos miniatus is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
