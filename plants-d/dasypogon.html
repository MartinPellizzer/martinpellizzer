Dasypogon

Dasypogon, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush. The plants typically have narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produce small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. They are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their striking appearance and ability to thrive in various climates.

Some species of Dasypogon are also valued for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine.
