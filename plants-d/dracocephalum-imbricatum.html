Dracocephalum imbricatum

Dracocephalum imbricatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
