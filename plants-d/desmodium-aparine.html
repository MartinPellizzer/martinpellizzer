Desmodium aparine

Desmodium aparine, commonly known as stickyweed or common burr medic, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hooked seed pods that contain tiny, sticky seeds. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including disturbed and arid environments. Desmodium aparine has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to colonize degraded lands.
