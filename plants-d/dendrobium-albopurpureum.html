Dendrobium albopurpureum

Dendrobium albopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, upright growth habit with numerous pseudobulbs covered in a dense, velvety brown to purple sheath. The flowers are typically white with purple veins and are arranged in racemes, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This orchid is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
