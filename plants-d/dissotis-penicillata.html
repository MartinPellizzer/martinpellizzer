Dissotis penicillata

Dissotis penicillata, commonly known as the needle grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, three-angled seeds.

Dissotis penicillata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
