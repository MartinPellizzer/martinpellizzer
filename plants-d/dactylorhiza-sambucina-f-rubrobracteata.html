Dactylorhiza sambucina f. rubrobracteata

Dactylorhiza sambucina f. rubrobracteata, commonly known as the red-bract orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bracts that contrast with its green foliage, making it visually striking. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Botanically, it is notable for its specialized growth requirements and limited distribution.
