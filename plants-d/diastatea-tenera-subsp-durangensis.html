Diastatea tenera subsp. durangensis

Diastatea tenera subsp.

durangensis, commonly known as the Durango thistle, is a perennial plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and showy purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth form with a short, woody stem and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
