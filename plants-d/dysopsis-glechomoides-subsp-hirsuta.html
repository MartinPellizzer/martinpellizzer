Dysopsis glechomoides subsp. hirsuta

Dysopsis glechomoides subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as hairy dysopsis, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Dysopsis glechomoides subsp.

hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
