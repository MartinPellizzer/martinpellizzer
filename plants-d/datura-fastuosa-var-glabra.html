Datura fastuosa var. glabra

Datura fastuosa var. glabra, commonly known as the naked leaf jimsonweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and large, showy flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
