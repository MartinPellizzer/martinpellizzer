Diospyros ferrea var. subimpressa

Diospyros ferrea var. subimpressa, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, while the fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
