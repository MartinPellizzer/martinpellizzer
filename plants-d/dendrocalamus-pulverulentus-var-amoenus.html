Dendrocalamus pulverulentus var. amoenus

Dendrocalamus pulverulentus var. amoenus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of giant bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

This variety is valued for its lightweight yet strong material, making it useful in construction and handicrafts.
