Dalea carnea

Dalea carnea, commonly known as pink prairie clover, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine hairs.

Dalea carnea plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
