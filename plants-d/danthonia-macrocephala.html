Danthonia macrocephala

Danthonia macrocephala, commonly known as the giant tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, dense tussocks that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and coastal areas.

Danthonia macrocephala plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
