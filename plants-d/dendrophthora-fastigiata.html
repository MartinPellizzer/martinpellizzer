Dendrophthora fastigiata

Dendrophthora fastigiata, commonly known as the upright forest mushroom, is a species of fungus that grows in association with certain trees.

It is typically found in temperate forests, where it forms a mycorrhizal relationship with the roots of its host plants. The fungus has a distinctive, upright cap that is usually pale yellow to brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often seen growing on decaying wood.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to mycologists and forest ecologists.
