Dianthus multiceps var. decalepis

Dianthus multiceps var. decalepis, commonly known as the decalveated campfire carnation, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, double-flowered blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from spring to early summer.
