Digitaria endlichii subsp. meziana

Digitaria endlichii subsp.

meziana, commonly known as the Mexican digitaria, is a grass species native to Central and South America. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
