Drimys aromatica var. pedunculata

Drimys aromatica var. pedunculata, commonly known as the fragrant drimys, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. Its fruit is a dark, leathery capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
