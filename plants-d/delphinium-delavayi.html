Delphinium delavayi

Delphinium delavayi, commonly known as the Himalayan larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a characteristic feature of the delphinium genus.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
