Diospyros vanprukii

Diospyros vanprukii, commonly known as the Vanprukii persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which are consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
