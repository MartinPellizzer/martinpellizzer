Dactylorhiza elata subsp. munbyana

Dactylorhiza elata subsp.

munbyana, commonly known as the Munby's bog orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in acidic peat bogs across northern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has a complex symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which are essential for nutrient uptake in its nutrient-poor habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the wet, boggy environment.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
