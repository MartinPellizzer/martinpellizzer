Dactylis polygama f. triramea

Dactylis polygama f. triramea, commonly known as the three-branched dactylis, is a variant of the common dactylis grass.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-branched inflorescence, which sets it apart from other members of the genus. This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is a perennial grass that is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature.

The botanical features of Dactylis polygama f. triramea make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
