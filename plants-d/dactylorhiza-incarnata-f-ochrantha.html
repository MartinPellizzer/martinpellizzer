Dactylorhiza incarnata f. ochrantha

Dactylorhiza incarnata f. ochrantha, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and marshes. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators.

This orchid is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
