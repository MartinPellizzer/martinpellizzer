Dyckia racemosa

Dyckia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered dyckia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Dyckia racemosa is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
