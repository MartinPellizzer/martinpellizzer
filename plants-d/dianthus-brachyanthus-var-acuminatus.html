Dianthus brachyanthus var. acuminatus

Dianthus brachyanthus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and pointed, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
