Diheteropogon grandiflorus

Diheteropogon grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered heteropogon, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in prairie or open woodland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Diheteropogon grandiflorus plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
