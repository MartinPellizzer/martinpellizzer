Dendrobium paathii

Dendrobium paathii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that are often covered in dense clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its flowers typically bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Dendrobium paathii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
