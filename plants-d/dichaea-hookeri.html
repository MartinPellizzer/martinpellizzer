Dichaea hookeri

Dichaea hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests or on rocky slopes. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning it attaches itself to tree trunks or branches without being parasitic.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
