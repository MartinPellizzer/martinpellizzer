Dactylorhiza hallii

Dactylorhiza hallii, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. It has a complex root system that includes a central rhizome and fibrous roots, which help it absorb nutrients from the soil.

Dactylorhiza hallii is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
