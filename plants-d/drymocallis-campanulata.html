Drymocallis campanulata

Drymocallis campanulata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist or smoke. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairiness, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Drymocallis campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
