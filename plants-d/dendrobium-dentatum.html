Dendrobium dentatum

Dendrobium dentatum, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive tooth-like markings on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Dendrobium dentatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
