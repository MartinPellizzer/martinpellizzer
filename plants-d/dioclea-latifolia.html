Dioclea latifolia

Dioclea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dioclea, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Dioclea latifolia is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
