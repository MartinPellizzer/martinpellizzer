Dioscorea orientalis

Dioscorea orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a herbaceous vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a food source.

The botanical features of Dioscorea orientalis make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
