Dendrobium grossum

Dendrobium grossum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with dark markings. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium grossum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral displays.
