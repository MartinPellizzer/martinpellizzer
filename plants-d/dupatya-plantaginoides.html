Dupatya plantaginoides

Dupatya plantaginoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
