Dyckia apensis

Dyckia apensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or the giant bromeliad, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant forms a dense rosette structure with long, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are adapted to collect and retain water. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Dyckia apensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
