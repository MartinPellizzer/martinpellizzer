Dacrydium nidulum var. vitiensis

Dacrydium nidulum var. vitiensis, commonly known as the Cook Islands pine, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the islands of the Cook Islands and nearby regions. The plant features needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are small and usually borne in clusters, and it produces both male and female cones on the same tree.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
