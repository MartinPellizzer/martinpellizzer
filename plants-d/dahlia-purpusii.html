Dahlia purpusii

Dahlia purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The tuberous roots are used for propagation and are an important feature of its botanical structure.
