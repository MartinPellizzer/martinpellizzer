Diuris brevifolia

Diuris brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid species characterized by its unique, spider-like floral structure.

It typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Diuris brevifolia is often found in south-eastern Australia, including parts of New South Wales and Victoria.
