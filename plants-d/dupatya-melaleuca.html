Dupatya melaleuca

Dupatya melaleuca, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, bottle-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and tea tree. It is often found in heathlands and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
