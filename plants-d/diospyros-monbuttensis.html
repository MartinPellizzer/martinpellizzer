Diospyros monbuttensis

Diospyros monbuttensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red fruits.

The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is an important food source for both wildlife and humans in its native habitat.
