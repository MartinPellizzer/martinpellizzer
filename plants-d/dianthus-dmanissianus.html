Dianthus dmanissianus

Dianthus dmanissianus, commonly known as the Dmanisi carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and has hairy, linear leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Dianthus dmanissianus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
