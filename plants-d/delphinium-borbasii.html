Delphinium borbasii

Delphinium borbasii, commonly known as Borbas's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

Delphinium borbasii is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils at high elevations.
