Devendraea myrtillus

Devendraea myrtillus, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, silver-green leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
