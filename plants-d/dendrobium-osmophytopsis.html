Dendrobium osmophytopsis

Dendrobium osmophytopsis, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, sweet scent. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dendrobium osmophytopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aromatic blooms.
