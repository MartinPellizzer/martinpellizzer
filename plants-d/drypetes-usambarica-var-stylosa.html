Drypetes usambarica var. stylosa

Drypetes usambarica var. stylosa, commonly known as the Usambara drypetes, is a flowering plant native to the Eastern Arc Mountains of Tanzania.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
