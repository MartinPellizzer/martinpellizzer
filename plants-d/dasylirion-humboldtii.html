Dasylirion humboldtii

Dasylirion humboldtii, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a striking succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, sword-shaped leaves that are gray-green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its foliage and structure make it well-suited for arid environments, and it is often used in xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought and heat.
