Diospyros sebertii

Diospyros sebertii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically dioecious, meaning separate male and female plants. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature and is edible, though often used in traditional medicine.

The wood of Diospyros sebertii is hard and dense, making it valuable for carving and crafting.
