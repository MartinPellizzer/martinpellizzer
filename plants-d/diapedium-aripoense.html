Diapedium aripoense

Diapedium aripoense, commonly known as the Aripo water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of Guyana and Suriname.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, typically during the wet season. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, allowing it to thrive in slow-moving or still waters.

Diapedium aripoense plays a role in water filtration and supports local biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
