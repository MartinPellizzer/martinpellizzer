Dasyphyllum inerme

Dasyphyllum inerme, commonly known as the soft-stemmed sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in maritime climates. The plant features dense, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds typical of coastal environments.

Dasyphyllum inerme is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dune systems.
