Dryopteris acuminata f. cristata

Dryopteris acuminata f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate wood fern, is a varietal form of the common wood fern.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crisped fronds that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a delicate, feathery texture.

This fern is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
