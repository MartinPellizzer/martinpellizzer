Delphinium aemulans var. aemulans

Delphinium aemulans var. aemulans, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
