Dalea astragalina

Dalea astragalina, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Great Plains of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Dalea astragalina plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
