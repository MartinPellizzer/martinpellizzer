Desmodium tiwiense

Desmodium tiwiense, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to brown pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
