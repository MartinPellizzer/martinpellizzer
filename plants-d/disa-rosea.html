Disa rosea

Disa rosea, commonly known as the pink disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to magenta flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a unique hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

Disa rosea is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty.
