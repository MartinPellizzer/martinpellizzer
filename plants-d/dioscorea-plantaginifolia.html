Dioscorea plantaginifolia

Dioscorea plantaginifolia, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used as a food source in some regions. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend support structures, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The botanical features of D. plantaginifolia make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
