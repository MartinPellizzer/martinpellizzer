Dioscorea sprucei

Dioscorea sprucei, commonly known as the Himalayan yam, is a climbing vine native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are typically harvested for their nutritional value and have been utilized in various folk remedies.

Dioscorea sprucei thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the rugged terrain of its native habitat.
