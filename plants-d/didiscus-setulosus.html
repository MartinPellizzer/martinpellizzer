Didiscus setulosus

Didiscus setulosus, commonly known as the woolly didiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often covered in fine, bristly hairs.

Didiscus setulosus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
