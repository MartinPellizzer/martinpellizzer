Diuris porrifolia

Diuris porrifolia, commonly known as the yellow fairy orchid, is a striking Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its vivid yellow flowers that resemble small orchids, typically blooming in spring. The plant features a short, stocky stem with a single or few flowers at the top, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It is often found in rocky soils and open habitats across southeastern Australia.

Diuris porrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
