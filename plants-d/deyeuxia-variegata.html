Deyeuxia variegata

Deyeuxia variegata, commonly known as the variegated false rice, is a grass species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that often display a distinctive variegation pattern. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
