Dyckia tweediei

Dyckia tweediei, commonly known as the Tweedie's dyckia, is a compact, low-growing bromeliad native to Brazil.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat. The plant produces striking, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white or pale pink flowers, adding ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a favored plant among bromeliad enthusiasts.
