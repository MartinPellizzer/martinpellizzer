Dubyaea melastomoides

Dubyaea melastomoides, commonly known as the rock melastome, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit, which helps it conserve moisture in its dry habitat. It belongs to the family Melastomataceae, which is known for its diverse array of tropical and subtropical species.
