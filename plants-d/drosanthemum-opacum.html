Drosanthemum opacum

Drosanthemum opacum, commonly known as the woolly daisies, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant features fuzzy, silver-green foliage that helps reduce water loss in its arid native habitat. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Drosanthemum opacum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
