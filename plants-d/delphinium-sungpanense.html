Delphinium sungpanense

Delphinium sungpanense, commonly known as the Sungpan larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its flowers have five petals with a distinctive spurred structure.

Delphinium sungpanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
