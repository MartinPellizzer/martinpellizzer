Didymopanax gardneri

Didymopanax gardneri, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can reach up to three meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by edible, starchy roots that are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and utility, it is also valued for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
