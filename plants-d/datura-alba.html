Datura alba

Datura alba, commonly known as the white angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces spiny capsules that contain toxic, bell-shaped seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its hallucinogenic properties.

Due to its toxicity, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant but requires careful handling.
