Dimocarpus fumatus

Dimocarpus fumatus, commonly known as the smoky mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a round, fleshy drupe with a thick, woody rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native region.
