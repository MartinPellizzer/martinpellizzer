Digitaria sanguinalis var. biverticillata

Digitaria sanguinalis var. biverticillata, commonly known as biverticillate Digitaria, is a grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are often arranged in two distinct branches, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
