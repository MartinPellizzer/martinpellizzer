Dactylorhiza curvifolia

Dactylorhiza curvifolia, commonly known as the twisted-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Dactylorhiza curvifolia is a perennial species with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to survive in temperate climates.
