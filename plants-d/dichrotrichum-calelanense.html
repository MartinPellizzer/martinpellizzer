Dichrotrichum calelanense

Dichrotrichum calelanense, commonly known as the Calelan hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and spikelets that are often reddish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
