Delphinium tatsienense var. tatsienense

Delphinium tatsienense var. tatsienense, commonly known as the Tibetan larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in raceme inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, adapted to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
