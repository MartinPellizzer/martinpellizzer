Diazia

Diazia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. Diazia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.

It is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
