Dombeya perrottetii

Dombeya perrottetii, commonly known as the Perrottet's dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dombeya perrottetii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and fragrance.
