Dichanthelium adenorhachis

Dichanthelium adenorhachis, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive hairy texture along the edges.

The species is often found in wetland ecosystems and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
