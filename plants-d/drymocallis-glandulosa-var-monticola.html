Drymocallis glandulosa var. monticola

Drymocallis glandulosa var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its showy yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
