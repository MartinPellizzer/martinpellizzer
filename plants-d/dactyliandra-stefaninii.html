Dactyliandra stefaninii

Dactyliandra stefaninii, commonly known as the Stefaninii False Arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a dense cluster on the spadix, surrounded by a showy, leaf-like spathe. Its leaves are typically large, broad, and have a waxy texture, often with a dark green color.

Dactyliandra stefaninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warm climates.
