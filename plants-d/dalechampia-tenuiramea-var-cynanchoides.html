Dalechampia tenuiramea var. cynanchoides

Dalechampia tenuiramea var. cynanchoides, commonly known as the slenderleaf dalechampia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
