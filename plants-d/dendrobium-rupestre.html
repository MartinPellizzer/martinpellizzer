Dendrobium rupestre

Dendrobium rupestre, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with erect, branched stems that often bear a dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium rupestre is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
