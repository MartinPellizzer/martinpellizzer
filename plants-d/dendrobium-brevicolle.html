Dendrobium brevicolle

Dendrobium brevicolle, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. The plant has a compact, pseudobulbous structure with short, fleshy stems that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Dendrobium brevicolle is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
