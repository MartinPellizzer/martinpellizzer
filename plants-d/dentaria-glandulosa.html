Dentaria glandulosa

Dentaria glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky jewelweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in drooping clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy coating and often have a glandular texture.

Dentaria glandulosa is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
