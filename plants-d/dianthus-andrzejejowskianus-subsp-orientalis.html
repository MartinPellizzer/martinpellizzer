Dianthus andrzejejowskianus subsp. orientalis

Dianthus andrzejejowskianus subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces compact, upright growth with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its flowers are borne in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
