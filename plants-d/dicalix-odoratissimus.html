Dicalix odoratissimus

Dicalix odoratissimus, commonly known as the sweet-scented dicalix, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that emit a strong, sweet aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
