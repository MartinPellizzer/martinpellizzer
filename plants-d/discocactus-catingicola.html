Discocactus catingicola

Discocactus catingicola, commonly known as the Catingicola discocactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems to survive drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
