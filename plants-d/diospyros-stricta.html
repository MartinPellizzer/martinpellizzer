Diospyros stricta

Diospyros stricta, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red or orange when ripe.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
