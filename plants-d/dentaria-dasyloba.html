Dentaria dasyloba

Dentaria dasyloba, commonly known as the showy dentaria, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dentaria dasyloba is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.
