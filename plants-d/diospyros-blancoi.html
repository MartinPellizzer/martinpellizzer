Diospyros blancoi

Diospyros blancoi, commonly known as the white persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Diospyros blancoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
