Drepanostachyum suberectum

Drepanostachyum suberectum, commonly known as the upright bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often slightly curved, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, pale green to brown florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant growth habit and adaptability to various soil conditions.
