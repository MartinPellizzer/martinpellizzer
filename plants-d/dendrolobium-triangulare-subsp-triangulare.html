Dendrolobium triangulare subsp. triangulare

Dendrolobium triangulare subsp.

triangulare, commonly known as the triangular burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive triangular seed pods.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its unique, three-lobed pods.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
