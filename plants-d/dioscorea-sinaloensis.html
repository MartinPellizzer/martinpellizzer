Dioscorea sinaloensis

Dioscorea sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan yam, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and cylindrical, spiny fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
