Daiswa hainanensis subsp. vietnamensis

Daiswa hainanensis subsp.

vietnamensis, commonly known as the Hainan daisy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
