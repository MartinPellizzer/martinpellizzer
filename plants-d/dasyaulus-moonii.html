Dasyaulus moonii

Dasyaulus moonii, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are typically green and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Dasyaulus moonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
