Deguelia montana

Deguelia montana, commonly known as the mountain deguelia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Deguelia montana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
