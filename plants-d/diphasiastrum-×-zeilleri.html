Diphasiastrum × zeilleri

Diphasiastrum × zeilleri, commonly known as the hybrid evergreen wood fern, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its ability to thrive in shady, moist environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing structures called sori. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, fern-like appearance and its adaptability to various soil types.

Diphasiastrum × zeilleri is a hardy plant that can survive in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8.
