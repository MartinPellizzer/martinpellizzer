Dysoxylum cupuliforme

Dysoxylum cupuliforme, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like crown formed by its broad, spreading branches. The plant has smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a waxy texture.
