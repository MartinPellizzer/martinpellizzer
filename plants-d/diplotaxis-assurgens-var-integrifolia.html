Diplotaxis assurgens var. integrifolia

Diplotaxis assurgens var. integrifolia, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oblanceolate, fleshy, and sometimes hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
