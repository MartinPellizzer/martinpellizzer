Dracocephalum microflorum

Dracocephalum microflorum, commonly known as dwarf chamaecyparis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
