Didymocarpus mollissimus

Didymocarpus mollissimus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in some regions.

The leaves are typically used in traditional medicine and cooking, highlighting its versatility in both ecological and cultural contexts.
