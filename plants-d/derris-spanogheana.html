Derris spanogheana

Derris spanogheana, commonly known as the African snakewood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.
