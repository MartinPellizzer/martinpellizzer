Delphinium occidentale var. cucullatum

Delphinium occidentale var. cucullatum, commonly known as the western larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
