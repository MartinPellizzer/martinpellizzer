Dactylorhiza × braunii nothosubsp. lilacina

Dactylorhiza × braunii nothosubsp.

lilacina, commonly known as the lilac orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its striking violet or lilac-colored flowers, which are typically arranged in dense spikes.

The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant blooms during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The botanical features of this species make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
