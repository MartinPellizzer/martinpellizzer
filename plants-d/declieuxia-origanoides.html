Declieuxia origanoides

Declieuxia origanoides, commonly known as the lemon verbena plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in herbal teas and culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Declieuxia origanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
