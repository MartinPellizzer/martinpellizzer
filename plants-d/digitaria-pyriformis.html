Digitaria pyriformis

Digitaria pyriformis, commonly known as the bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, bottle-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in open grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering structures consist of spikelets arranged in clusters.

Digitaria pyriformis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
