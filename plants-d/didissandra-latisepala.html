Didissandra latisepala

Didissandra latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled sandalwood, is a unique member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing fragrant, white blossoms that attract pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
