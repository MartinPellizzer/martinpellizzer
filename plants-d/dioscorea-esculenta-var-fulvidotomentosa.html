Dioscorea esculenta var. fulvidotomentosa

Dioscorea esculenta var. fulvidotomentosa, commonly known as the yellow hairy yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, yellowish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are edible and have a starchy, sweet flavor, making it a valuable crop in some traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both food and medicinal purposes.
