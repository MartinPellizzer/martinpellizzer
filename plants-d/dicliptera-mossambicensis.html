Dicliptera mossambicensis

Dicliptera mossambicensis, commonly known as the African grass or African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture and a tendency to curl at the edges.

Dicliptera mossambicensis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.
