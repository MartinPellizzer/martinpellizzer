Dysoxylum nitidum var. angustifolium

Dysoxylum nitidum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dysoxylum, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, narrow leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
