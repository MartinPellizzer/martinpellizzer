Disa ochrostachya var. latipetala

Disa ochrostachya var. latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled disa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences with pale pink to white petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
