Dissotis trothae

Dissotis trothae, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often yellow or golden in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Dissotis trothae is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
