Delphinium parryi var. subglobosum

Delphinium parryi var. subglobosum, commonly known as the Parry's delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal spikes, and it blooms in late summer to early fall.

Delphinium parryi var. subglobosum is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
