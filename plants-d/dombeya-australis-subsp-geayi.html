Dombeya australis subsp. geayi

Dombeya australis subsp.

geayi, commonly known as the Australian dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
