Diaphananthe rutila

Diaphananthe rutila, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the tree a fiery appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a glossy surface. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
