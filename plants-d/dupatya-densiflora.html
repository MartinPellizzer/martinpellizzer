Dupatya densiflora

Dupatya densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered datura, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple. The plant produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its leaves are broad and hairy, while its stem is erect and branched.
