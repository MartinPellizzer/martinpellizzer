Dioscoreophyllum cumminsii var. cumminsii

Dioscoreophyllum cumminsii var. cumminsii, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The tubers are rich in starch and are used as a food source in various traditional cuisines.
