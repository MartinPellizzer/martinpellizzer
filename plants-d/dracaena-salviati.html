Dracaena salviati

Dracaena salviati, commonly known as the Salviati dracaena, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a distinctive white or silver midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Dracaena salviati prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
