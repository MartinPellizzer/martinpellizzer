Dioscorea kita

Dioscorea kita, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are used in traditional cuisine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The botanical structure of Dioscorea kita includes compound leaves and a complex root system adapted for nutrient absorption.
