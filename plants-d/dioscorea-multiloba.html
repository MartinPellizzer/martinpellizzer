Dioscorea multiloba

Dioscorea multiloba, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that give way to elongated, tuberous fruits. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source in various African cultures.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
