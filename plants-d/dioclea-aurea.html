Dioclea aurea

Dioclea aurea, commonly known as the golden dioclea, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in humid, shady environments.
