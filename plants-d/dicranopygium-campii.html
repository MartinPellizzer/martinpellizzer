Dicranopygium campii

Dicranopygium campii, commonly known as the Campi's haircap moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and small, leafy shoots that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are usually elongated and gradually tapering, with a distinct midrib.

Dicranopygium campii plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
