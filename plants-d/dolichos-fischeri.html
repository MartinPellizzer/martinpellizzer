Dolichos fischeri

Dolichos fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces slender, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
