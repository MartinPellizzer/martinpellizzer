Delphinium lankongense

Delphinium lankongense, commonly known as the Lankong larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers are often arranged in dense inflorescences.

Delphinium lankongense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
