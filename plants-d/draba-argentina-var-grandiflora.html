Draba argentina var. grandiflora

Draba argentina var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Argentine rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. This perennial herb is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its floral structure includes four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.
