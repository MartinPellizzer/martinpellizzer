Dampiera incana

Dampiera incana, commonly known as silverleaf dampiera, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Dampiera incana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
