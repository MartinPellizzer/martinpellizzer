Dicentra pauciflora

Dicentra pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered bleeding heart, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, heart-shaped blooms that are pink to red in color, often with a contrasting white or pale pink center. The plant has opposite, trifoliate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and early spring blooming habit add visual interest to the garden.
