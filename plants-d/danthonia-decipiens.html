Danthonia decipiens

Danthonia decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and acidic conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are a common feature in the Poaceae family. It is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.

Due to its low-growing habit and dense growth pattern, it can serve as a useful ground cover in ecological restoration projects.
