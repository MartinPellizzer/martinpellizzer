Dioscorea quinquefoliolata

Dioscorea quinquefoliolata, commonly known as the Mexican yam or yam bean, is a perennial vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and develops tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
