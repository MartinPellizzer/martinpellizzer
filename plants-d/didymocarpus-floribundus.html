Didymocarpus floribundus

Didymocarpus floribundus, commonly known as the tropical mango tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a fleshy drupe with a sweet, aromatic pulp, often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
