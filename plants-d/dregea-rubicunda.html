Dregea rubicunda

Dregea rubicunda, commonly known as the red dregea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking red or orange flowers during the warmer months. The plant has a woody stem with thick, leathery leaves that are arranged opposite each other. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Dregea rubicunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
