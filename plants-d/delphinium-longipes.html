Delphinium longipes

Delphinium longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
