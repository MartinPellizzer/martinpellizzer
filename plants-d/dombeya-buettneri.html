Dombeya buettneri

Dombeya buettneri, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dombeya buettneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
