Digitaria vulgaris

Digitaria vulgaris, commonly known as common barnyardgrass, is an annual grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, contributing to weed management challenges.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, sharing structural and genetic similarities with other grasses.
