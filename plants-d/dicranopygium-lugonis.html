Dicranopygium lugonis

Dicranopygium lugonis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, cushion-like structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sporophytes, are relatively simple and consist of a short, cylindrical stem with a capsule at the apex.

Dicranopygium lugonis plays a role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitats.
