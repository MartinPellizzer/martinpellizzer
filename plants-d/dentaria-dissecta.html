Dentaria dissecta

Dentaria dissecta, commonly known as the cutleaf toothplant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often dissected, giving it its common name. Its small, white or pink flowers appear in early spring and are arranged in racemes.

Dentaria dissecta is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soils.
