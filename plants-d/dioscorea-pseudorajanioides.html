Dioscorea pseudorajanioides

Dioscorea pseudorajanioides, commonly known as the purple-veined yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent purple veins, and its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tubers are often harvested for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
