Dactylorhiza × claudiopolitana nothosubsp. maculatiformis

Dactylorhiza × claudiopolitana nothosubsp.

maculatiformis, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive spots.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its rhizomatous growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
