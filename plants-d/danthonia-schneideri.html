Danthonia schneideri

Danthonia schneideri, commonly known as the Schneiders' grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Danthonia schneideri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
