Daemonorops poilanei

Daemonorops poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar palm or the bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant native to Madagascar, characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are followed by dark, spherical fruits. Its growth habit resembles that of a palm, though it lacks the typical palm-like trunk.

Daemonorops poilanei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
