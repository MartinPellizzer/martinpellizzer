Dipterocarpus ochraceus

Dipterocarpus ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy seeds and buttressed roots. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its bark is thick and often flaky, while its leaves are alternate and elliptical in shape.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various species.
