Dombeya torrida subsp. erythroleuca

Dombeya torrida subsp.

erythroleuca, commonly known as the red-and-white dombeya, is a shrub native to South Africa. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink with white markings.

The plant has smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
