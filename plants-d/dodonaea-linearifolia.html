Dodonaea linearifolia

Dodonaea linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance make it a resilient species in arid environments.

Dodonaea linearifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
