Discocactus boomianus

Discocactus boomianus, commonly known as the Boome's discocactus, is a flowering cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spines and its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. It is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to store water in its thick stems, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.
