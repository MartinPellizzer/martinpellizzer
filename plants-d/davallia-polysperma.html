Davallia polysperma

Davallia polysperma, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are dark green with a distinctive, serrated edge. The plant has a unique rhizome system that allows it to form dense, carpet-like colonies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental landscaping.
