Damnacanthus esquirolii

Damnacanthus esquirolii, commonly known as the golden trumpet.

This plant is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small trumpets. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
