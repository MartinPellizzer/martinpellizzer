Dendrobium cyclopense

Dendrobium cyclopense, commonly known as the cyclops orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments on trees or rocks. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Dendrobium cyclopense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
