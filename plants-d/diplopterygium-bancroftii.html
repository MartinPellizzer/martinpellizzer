Diplopterygium bancroftii

Diplopterygium bancroftii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in woodland or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens in suitable climates.
