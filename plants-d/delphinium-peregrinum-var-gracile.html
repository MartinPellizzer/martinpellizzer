Delphinium peregrinum var. gracile

Delphinium peregrinum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.

Due to its toxicity, caution is advised when handling or planting this species.
