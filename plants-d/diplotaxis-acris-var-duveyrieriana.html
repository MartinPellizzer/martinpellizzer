Diplotaxis acris var. duveyrieriana

Diplotaxis acris var. duveyrieriana, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
