Dyckia areniticola

Dyckia areniticola, commonly known as the sand dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family, Bromeliaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is adapted to grow in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white, emerging from the center of the rosette.

Dyckia areniticola is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
