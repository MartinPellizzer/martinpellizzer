Dendrobium confundens

Dendrobium confundens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with numerous pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are often yellow or golden in color, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Dendrobium confundens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
