Delphinium variegatum subsp. thornei

Delphinium variegatum subsp.

thornei, commonly known as the Thorne's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring tall, sparsely branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive veining pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade environments.
