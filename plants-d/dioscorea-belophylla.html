Dioscorea belophylla

Dioscorea belophylla, commonly known as the white-veined yam, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by cylindrical, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent veins, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil.

The tubers of Dioscorea belophylla are valued for their nutritional content and traditional use in local cuisines.
