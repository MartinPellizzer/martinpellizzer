Dioscorea racemosa

Dioscorea racemosa, commonly known as the climbing yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits. Its tuberous roots are often cultivated for their edible qualities and have been used in traditional medicine.

Dioscorea racemosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
