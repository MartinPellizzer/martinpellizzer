Desmostachys planchonianus f. lanceolatus

Desmostachys planchonianus f. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles, giving it a pincushion-like appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.

This variety is prized for its unique morphology and resilience in harsh environments.
