Deyeuxia sodiroana

Deyeuxia sodiroana, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Deyeuxia sodiroana plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
