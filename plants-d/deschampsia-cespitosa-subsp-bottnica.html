Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. bottnica

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

bottnica, commonly known as the northern tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows, bogs, and tundra.

The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, wiry stems that often bend at the base. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
