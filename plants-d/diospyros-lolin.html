Diospyros lolin

Diospyros lolin, commonly known as the Kallar tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are followed by edible, round, yellow fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in carpentry and furniture making.
