Digitaria diversinervis var. woodiana

Digitaria diversinervis var. woodiana, commonly known as Wood's digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
