Didymocarpus hirtus

Didymocarpus hirtus, commonly known as the hairy pomegranate, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and oval, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
