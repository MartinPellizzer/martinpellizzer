Digitalis lutea

Digitalis lutea, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes several species known for their medicinal and ornamental value. The plant produces tall spikes of bell-shaped, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Digitalis lutea contains cardiac glycosides, making it both a valuable and potentially toxic plant in the botanical world.
