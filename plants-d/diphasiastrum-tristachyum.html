Diphasiastrum tristachyum

Diphasiastrum tristachyum, commonly known as the purple fringed bottlebrush, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that are surrounded by specialized bracts, giving it a bottlebrush-like appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
