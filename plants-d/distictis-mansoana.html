Distictis mansoana

Distictis mansoana, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with prominent lenticels.

Distictis mansoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
