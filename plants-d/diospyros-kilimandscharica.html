Diospyros kilimandscharica

Diospyros kilimandscharica, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of East Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in montane forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is hard and durable, and for its edible fruits.
