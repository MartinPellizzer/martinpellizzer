Deyeuxia innominata

Deyeuxia innominata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, pond edges, and other moist environments, where it forms dense stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.

Its adaptability to waterlogged conditions makes it a key component of many freshwater ecosystems.
