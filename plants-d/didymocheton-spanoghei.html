Didymocheton spanoghei

Didymocheton spanoghei, commonly known as the purple-veined sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive purple veins, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water sources.

Its botanical structure is typical of sedges, with a triangular stem and rhizomatous growth habit.
