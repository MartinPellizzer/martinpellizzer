Dryopteris poolii

Dryopteris poolii, commonly known as the Pool's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically dark green and have a lacy appearance. The plant prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas with well-drained soil. Its spore-producing structures, called soruses, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Dryopteris poolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
