Delphinium pavlovii

Delphinium pavlovii, commonly known as the Pavlov's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Delphinium pavlovii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
