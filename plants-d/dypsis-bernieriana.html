Dypsis bernieriana

Dypsis bernieriana, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant has a rosette-like structure with spiky, leaf-like structures that are actually modified leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by ornamental fruits.

Dypsis bernieriana is popular as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
