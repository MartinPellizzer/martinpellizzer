Dactylorhiza incarnata var. kotschyi

Dactylorhiza incarnata var. kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and damp woodlands. It has a complex root system that includes a prominent tuber, which helps it survive in its moist environment.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
