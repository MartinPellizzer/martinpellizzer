Diplazium incisum

Diplazium incisum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in spreading the plant across the water surface.

Diplazium incisum plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
