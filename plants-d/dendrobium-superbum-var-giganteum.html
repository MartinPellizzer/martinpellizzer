Dendrobium superbum var. giganteum

Dendrobium superbum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking species native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a sympodial epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant produces long, cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length, supporting clusters of large, showy flowers. Its blooms, which are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive red lip, are highly prized in horticulture.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
