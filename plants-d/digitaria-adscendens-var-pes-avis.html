Digitaria adscendens var. pes-avis

Digitaria adscendens var. pes-avis, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous short, hair-like florets. Its leaves are narrow and tapering, with a bluish-green coloration.

It thrives in full sun and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
