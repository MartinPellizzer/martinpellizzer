Dendrobium tetragonum var. cacatua

Dendrobium tetragonum var. cacatua, commonly known as the Blue Orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
