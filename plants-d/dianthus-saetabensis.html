Dianthus saetabensis

Dianthus saetabensis, commonly known as the spiked carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink. The plant features upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its flowers are composed of five petals that are often adorned with distinctive, backward-curving teeth. Dianthus saetabensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
