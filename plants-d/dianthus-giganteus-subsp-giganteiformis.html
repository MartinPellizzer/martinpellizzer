Dianthus giganteus subsp. giganteiformis

Dianthus giganteus subsp.

giganteiformis, commonly known as the giant carnation, is a striking member of the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It has a compact, bushy growth habit and produces numerous flowers throughout the growing season.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
