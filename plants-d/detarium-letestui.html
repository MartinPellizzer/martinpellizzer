Detarium letestui

Detarium letestui, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. The bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their medicinal properties.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for agroforestry and soil conservation in degraded lands.
