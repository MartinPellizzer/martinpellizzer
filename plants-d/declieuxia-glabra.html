Declieuxia glabra

Declieuxia glabra, commonly known as the smooth reed, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are smooth and often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Declieuxia glabra is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.
