Disocactus ackermannii subsp. ackermannii

Disocactus ackermannii subsp.

ackermannii, commonly known as the Ackermann's disocactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its columnar stems and large, showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which are often pink or red, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
