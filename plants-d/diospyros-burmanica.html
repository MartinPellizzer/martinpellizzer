Diospyros burmanica

Diospyros burmanica, commonly known as Burmese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The bark and leaves of Diospyros burmanica have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
