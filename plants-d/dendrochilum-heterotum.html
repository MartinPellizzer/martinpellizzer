Dendrochilum heterotum

Dendrochilum heterotum, commonly known as the heterotum orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomes and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its leaves are typically elongated and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Dendrochilum heterotum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
