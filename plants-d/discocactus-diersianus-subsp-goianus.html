Discocactus diersianus subsp. goianus

Discocactus diersianus subsp.

goianus, commonly known as the Goianus discocactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with spines that help reduce water loss.

Its growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in regions with similar climates.
