Dryopteris polita

Dryopteris polita, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, dark green leaves.

Dryopteris polita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, feathery appearance.
