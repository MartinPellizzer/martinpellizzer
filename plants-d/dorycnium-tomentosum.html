Dorycnium tomentosum

Dorycnium tomentosum, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces yellow pea-like flowers and small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun.

Dorycnium tomentosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen and prevent soil erosion.
