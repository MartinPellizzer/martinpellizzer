Dendrocalamus asper

Dendrocalamus asper, commonly known as the burr bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its thick, fibrous culms that are often used in construction and handicrafts. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a rough, scaly texture on the underside, which contributes to its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus asper is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
