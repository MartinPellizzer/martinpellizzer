Dasylirion wheeleri var. durangense

Dasylirion wheeleri var. durangense, commonly known as the Durango sotol, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, blue-gray, sword-shaped leaves that are often lined with white or silver hairs. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

This variety is closely related to other sotol species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
