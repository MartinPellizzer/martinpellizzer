Domkeocarpa pendula

Domkeocarpa pendula, commonly known as the hanging fruit tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences and distinctive, fleshy fruits that mature to a bright red color. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a deep green color.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which are appreciated in local cuisines.
