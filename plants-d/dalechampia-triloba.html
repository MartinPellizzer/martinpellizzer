Dalechampia triloba

Dalechampia triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed huito, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

Its flowers are small and clustered in a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Dalechampia triloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
