Dolichos pseudocajanus

Dolichos pseudocajanus, commonly known as the horse gram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

Additionally, it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
