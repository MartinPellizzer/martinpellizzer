Dianthus × seehausianus

Dianthus × seehausianus, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Dianthus chinensis and Dianthus deltoides, combining traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy, colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and floral arrangements due to its vibrant blooms and long-lasting flowers.

Dianthus × seehausianus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for bedding plants and borders.
