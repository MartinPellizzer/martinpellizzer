Drypis spinosa subsp. jacquiniana

Drypis spinosa subsp.

jacquiniana, commonly known as the jacquinian thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
