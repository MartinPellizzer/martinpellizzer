Draba aizoides var. brevistyla

Draba aizoides var. brevistyla, commonly known as the short-styled whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. This variety is distinguished by its shorter styles compared to other varieties within the species.
