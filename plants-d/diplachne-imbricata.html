Diplachne imbricata

Diplachne imbricata, commonly known as narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Diplachne imbricata is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its growth habit and morphology make it an important component of native ecosystems in its range.
