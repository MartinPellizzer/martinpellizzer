Delphinium pubiflorum

Delphinium pubiflorum, commonly known as the pubescent larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of spurred blue or purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
