Dichapetalum gelonioides subsp. tuberculatum

Dichapetalum gelonioides subsp.

tuberculatum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a shrub native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
