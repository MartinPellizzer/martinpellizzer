Detarium macrocarpum

Detarium macrocarpum, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and large, pod-like fruits. The tree produces thick, fibrous pods that contain edible seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its root system is deep and can help improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
