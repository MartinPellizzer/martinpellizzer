Desmodium craspediferum

Desmodium craspediferum, commonly known as the hairy burr legume, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in dense clusters. Desmodium craspediferum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in various environmental conditions.

Its ecological role includes soil enrichment and serving as a forage plant for livestock.
