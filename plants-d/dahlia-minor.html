Dahlia minor

Dahlia minor, commonly known as the dwarf dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It produces showy, single or double flowers in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, making it ideal for borders or containers. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Dahlia minor thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and deadheading for continuous blooming.
