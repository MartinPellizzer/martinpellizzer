Diasperus polygonoides

Diasperus polygonoides, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to anchor it in rocky or sandy soils.

Diasperus polygonoides is often found in rocky outcrops and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
