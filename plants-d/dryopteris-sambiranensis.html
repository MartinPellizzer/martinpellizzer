Dryopteris sambiranensis

Dryopteris sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a complex venation pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is usually short and thick, supporting the growth of its bipinnate leaves.

This fern is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
