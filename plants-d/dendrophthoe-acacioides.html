Dendrophthoe acacioides

Dendrophthoe acacioides, commonly known as the acacia-like mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of acacia trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to regions of Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula.

Its presence can affect the health of its host tree, making it an important ecological factor in its habitat.
