Diuris longifolia var. typica

Diuris longifolia var. typica, commonly known as the long-leaved spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathland and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a single, slender stem with several narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive feature is its intricate, spider-like flowers that bloom in spring.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens due to its striking appearance.
