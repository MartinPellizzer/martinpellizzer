Dioscorea samydea var. corcovadensis

Dioscorea samydea var. corcovadensis, commonly known as the Corcovado yam, is a variant of the genus Dioscorea native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its climbing habit and elongated, tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.
