Dendrobium oxyphyllum

Dendrobium oxyphyllum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that produce dense clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
