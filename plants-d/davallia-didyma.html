Davallia didyma

Davallia didyma, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect light.

Davallia didyma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
