Dicoma kirkii subsp. vaginata

Dicoma kirkii subsp.

vaginata, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, hairy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers during the winter months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
