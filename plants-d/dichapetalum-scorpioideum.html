Dichapetalum scorpioideum

Dichapetalum scorpioideum, commonly known as the death's-head orchid, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, skull-like flower shape, which gives it its ominous common name. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
