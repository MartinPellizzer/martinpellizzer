Dendrochilum apiculatum

Dendrochilum apiculatum, commonly known as the apiculate tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and linear, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dendrochilum apiculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
