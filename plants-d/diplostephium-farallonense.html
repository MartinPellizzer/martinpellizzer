Diplostephium farallonense

Diplostephium farallonense, commonly known as the Farallon sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the rocky shores of the Farallon Islands off the coast of California.

It is a perennial herb that grows in coastal environments characterized by strong winds and salt spray. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in its arid habitat.

Diplostephium farallonense is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the unique biodiversity of the region.
