Diospyros myrtilloides

Diospyros myrtilloides, commonly known as the small persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to red or orange as it matures.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
