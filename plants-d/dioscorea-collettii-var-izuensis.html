Dioscorea collettii var. izuensis

Dioscorea collettii var. izuensis, commonly known as the Izu yam, is a tuberous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its underground rhizomes that produce edible tubers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, white flowers followed by red berries.

The tubers are valued for their culinary use and are often prepared by boiling or roasting.
