Dianthus × cincinnatus

Dianthus × cincinnatus, commonly known as the cushion campion, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically features hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
