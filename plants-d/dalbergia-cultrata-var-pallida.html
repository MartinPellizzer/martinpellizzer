Dalbergia cultrata var. pallida

Dalbergia cultrata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale-leaf ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark hardwood and compound leaves. The plant has small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds.

It is valued for its durable timber and is often found in dry savanna and woodland habitats.
