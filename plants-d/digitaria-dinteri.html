Digitaria dinteri

Digitaria dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Digitaria dinteri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such climates.
