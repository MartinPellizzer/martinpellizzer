Dracula erythrochaete subsp. astuta

Dracula erythrochaete subsp.

astuta, commonly known as the Red-veined Dracula, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador. It is characterized by its vivid red veins on dark green leaves and produces strikingly colorful, spurred flowers that are typically red or orange.

The plant thrives in high-altitude, humid environments with abundant shade and well-drained soil. Its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration make it a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
