Draba calycosa var. aucheri

Draba calycosa var. aucheri, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, often forming dense mats in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and arranged in clusters. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing short, slender pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is well adapted to harsh mountain conditions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
