Digitalis purpurea subsp. nevadensis

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It produces a basal rosette of leaves and has a short, upright stem bearing clustered flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
