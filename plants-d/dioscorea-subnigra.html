Dioscorea subnigra

Dioscorea subnigra, commonly known as black nightshade yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized for both culinary and medicinal purposes in various cultures.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds, requiring careful handling and preparation before use.
