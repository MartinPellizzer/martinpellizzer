Drimia longipedicellata

Drimia longipedicellata, commonly known as the Cape lily, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its bulbs are fleshy and store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
