Delima castaneifolia

Delima castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid growth and ecological impact.

The tree's ability to thrive in a variety of soil types contributes to its widespread distribution.
