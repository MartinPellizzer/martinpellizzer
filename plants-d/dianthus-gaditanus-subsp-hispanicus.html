Dianthus gaditanus subsp. hispanicus

Dianthus gaditanus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
