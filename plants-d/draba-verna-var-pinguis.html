Draba verna var. pinguis

Draba verna var. pinguis, commonly known as the fat winter cress, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are fleshy and lobed, adapted to retain moisture in cold, harsh environments. It thrives in rocky or alpine soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Draba verna var. pinguis is notable for its ability to survive in extreme conditions, making it a hardy member of the Brassicaceae family.
