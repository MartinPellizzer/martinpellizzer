Daemonorops fissa

Daemonorops fissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandan, is a species of cycad native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional cuisine and crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiky seed pods. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation programs.
