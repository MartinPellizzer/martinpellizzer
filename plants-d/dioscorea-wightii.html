Dioscorea wightii

Dioscorea wightii, commonly known as Indian yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant produces tubers that are used as a food source and have a starchy, sweet flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish with a distinctive spadix.

The plant is valued not only for its edible tubers but also for its traditional medicinal uses.
