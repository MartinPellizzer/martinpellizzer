Douglasia montana var. typica

Douglasia montana var. typica, commonly known as the mountain Douglasia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, often at elevations above 3,000 feet. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
