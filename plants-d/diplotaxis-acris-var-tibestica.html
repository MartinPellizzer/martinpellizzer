Diplotaxis acris var. tibestica

Diplotaxis acris var. tibestica, commonly known as the Tibesti wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Tibesti Mountains in Chad.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

The species is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy plant in semi-arid climates.
