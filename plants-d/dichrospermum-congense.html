Dichrospermum congense

Dichrospermum congense, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and fissured. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its resilience to drought conditions.
