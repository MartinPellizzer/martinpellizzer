Dianthus geminiflorus var. benearnensis

Dianthus geminiflorus var. benearnensis, commonly known as the Benearn's double-flowered carnation, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, bushy growth with clusters of double, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are upright and branched. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are prized for their ornamental value and are often used in floral arrangements.
