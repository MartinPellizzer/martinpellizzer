Diplazium magnificum

Diplazium magnificum, commonly known as the splendid water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it forms dense underwater colonies. The plant has long, slender fronds that are divided into small, delicate pinnae, giving it a lacy appearance. Its rhizomes spread horizontally through the substrate, helping to anchor the plant and absorb nutrients.

Diplazium magnificum plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
