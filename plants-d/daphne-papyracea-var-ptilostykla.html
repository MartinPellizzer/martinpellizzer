Daphne papyracea var. ptilostykla

Daphne papyracea var. ptilostykla, commonly known as the woolly daphne, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white during late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
