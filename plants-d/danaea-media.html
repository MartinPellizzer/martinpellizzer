Danaea media

Danaea media, commonly known as the wax plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Danaea media is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
