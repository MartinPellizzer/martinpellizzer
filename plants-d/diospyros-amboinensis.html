Diospyros amboinensis

Diospyros amboinensis, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy berries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent taste, and is often consumed raw or used in traditional medicines.

It is also valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in furniture and carving.
