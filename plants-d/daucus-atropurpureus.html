Daucus atropurpureus

Daucus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple flowering top, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided leaves and showy umbellate flowers.

It is native to the western United States and thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with purple to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and have a distinctive, slightly bitter flavor, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and herbal studies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
