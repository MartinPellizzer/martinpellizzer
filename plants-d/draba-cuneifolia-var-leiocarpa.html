Draba cuneifolia var. leiocarpa

Draba cuneifolia var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variant is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves compared to other forms of Draba cuneifolia.
