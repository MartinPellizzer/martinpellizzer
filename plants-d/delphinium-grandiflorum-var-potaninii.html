Delphinium grandiflorum var. potaninii

Delphinium grandiflorum var. potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy and serrated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
