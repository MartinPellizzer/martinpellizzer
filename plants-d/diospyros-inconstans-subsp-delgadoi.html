Diospyros inconstans subsp. delgadoi

Diospyros inconstans subsp.

delgadoi, commonly known as Delgado's persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color. It is adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky soils and scrub habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
