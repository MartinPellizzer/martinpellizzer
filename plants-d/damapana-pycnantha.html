Damapana pycnantha

Damapana pycnantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical crown and striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has small, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blossoms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural spread in suitable environments.
