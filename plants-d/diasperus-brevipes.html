Diasperus brevipes

Diasperus brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found growing on limestone or sandstone substrates.

Diasperus brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
