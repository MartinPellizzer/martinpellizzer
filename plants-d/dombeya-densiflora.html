Dombeya densiflora

Dombeya densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dombeya densiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
