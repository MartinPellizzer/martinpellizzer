Diasperus chlorophaeus

Diasperus chlorophaeus, commonly known as the green spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the spurge family. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
