Disocactus speciosus subsp. blomianus

Disocactus speciosus subsp.

blomianus, commonly known as the Blomianus Disocactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico. It is a climbing cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring long, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in length.

The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its stems are covered in white, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
