Dianthus × bergadensis

Dianthus × bergadensis, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant that belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dianthus chinensis and Dianthus barbatus, combining traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Dianthus × bergadensis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for bedding plants and floral arrangements.
