Dalbergia ferruginea

Dalbergia ferruginea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark red to purple heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, rectangular seeds.

The tree is often found in lowland evergreen forests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
