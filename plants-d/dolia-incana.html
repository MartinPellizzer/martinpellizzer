Dolia incana

Dolia incana, commonly known as the roughleaf datura, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Dolia incana is adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
