Dolichos bianoensis

Dolichos bianoensis, commonly known as the white hyacinth bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the stems are typically hairy.

The seeds, enclosed in elongated pods, are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
