Diosma subulata

Diosma subulata, commonly known as the needleleaf diosma, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or chaparral habitats.

Diosma subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
