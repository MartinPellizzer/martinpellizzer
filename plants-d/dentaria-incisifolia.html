Dentaria incisifolia

Dentaria incisifolia, commonly known as the cut-leaved toothplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a serrated or incised edge, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a raceme. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Dentaria incisifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
