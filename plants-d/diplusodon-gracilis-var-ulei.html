Diplusodon gracilis var. ulei

Diplusodon gracilis var. ulei, commonly known as the slender false mallow, is a variant of the false mallow genus native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to white flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a soft, velvety texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky outcrops.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
