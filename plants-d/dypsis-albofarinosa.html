Dypsis albofarinosa

Dypsis albofarinosa, commonly known as the white-veined areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like pinnate leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Dypsis albofarinosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying properties.
