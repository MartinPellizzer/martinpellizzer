Digitaria corradii

Digitaria corradii, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive reddish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. Digitaria corradii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade conditions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
