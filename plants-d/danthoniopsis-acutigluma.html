Danthoniopsis acutigluma

Danthoniopsis acutigluma, commonly known as the sharp-leafed grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves with a sharp, pointed tip. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecosystems and a subject of study in grassland ecology.
