Digitostigma caput-medusae

Digitostigma caput-medusae, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive rosette of thick, fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant itself is toxic if ingested.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and striking appearance.
