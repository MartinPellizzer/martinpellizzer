Dittelasma rarak

Dittelasma rarak, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of showy, golden-yellow tubular flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that mature into bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
