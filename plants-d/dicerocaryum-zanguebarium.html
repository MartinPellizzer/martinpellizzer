Dicerocaryum zanguebarium

Dicerocaryum zanguebarium, commonly known as the Zanzibar spurge, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy soils along the shores, often in areas with high saline conditions. The plant features woody stems and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Dicerocaryum zanguebarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
