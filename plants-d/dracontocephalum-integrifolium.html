Dracontocephalum integrifolium

Dracontocephalum integrifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and slightly hairy. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
