Dendrobium sarcophyllum

Dendrobium sarcophyllum, commonly known as the black root orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, fleshy roots and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found growing in dense forests or along the edges of swamps.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
