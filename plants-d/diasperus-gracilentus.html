Diasperus gracilentus

Diasperus gracilentus, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Diasperus gracilentus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
