Drosanthemum dipageae

Drosanthemum dipageae, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Drosanthemum dipageae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
