Dioscorea bulbifera var. simbha

Dioscorea bulbifera var. simbha, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tubers that are rich in starch. The plant also bears bulbils on its stems, which can be used for propagation. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional agriculture and botanical studies.
