Dioscorea permollis

Dioscorea permollis, commonly known as the smooth yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers, which are the edible part, are rich in starch and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is native to tropical regions of Africa and is valued both for its nutritional content and medicinal properties.
