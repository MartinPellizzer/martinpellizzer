Discocactus boliviensis

Discocactus boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian discocactus, is a cactus species native to the Andes Mountains of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after specimen for collectors and botanists alike.
