Damnacanthus indicus var. lancifolius

Damnacanthus indicus var. lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devil's backbone, is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, spine-tipped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
