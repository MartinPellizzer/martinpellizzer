Delphinium favargeri

Delphinium favargeri, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided lobes, and it produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
