Diuris aurea

Diuris aurea, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that are typically golden-yellow in color. The plant is perennial and grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal regions of southern Australia. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Diuris aurea is a small to medium-sized orchid, with flowering stems that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height.
