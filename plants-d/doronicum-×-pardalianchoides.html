Doronicum × pardalianchoides

Doronicum × pardalianchoides, commonly known as the hybrid leopard's bane, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers with dark purple markings. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
