Duranta microphylla

Duranta microphylla, commonly known as the littleleaf trumpetflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of vibrant blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
