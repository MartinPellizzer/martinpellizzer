Dukea trifoliata

Dukea trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round, and fleshy berry that changes color as it matures.

Dukea trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
