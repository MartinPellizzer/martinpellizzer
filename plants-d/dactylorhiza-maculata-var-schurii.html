Dactylorhiza maculata var. schurii

Dactylorhiza maculata var. schurii, commonly known as the Schur's spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant features a rhizome with several erect, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals and sepals.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
