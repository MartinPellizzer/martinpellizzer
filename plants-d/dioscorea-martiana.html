Dioscorea martiana

Dioscorea martiana, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
