Diospyros oldhamii var. chartacea

Diospyros oldhamii var. chartacea, commonly known as the paper fig, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
