Discaria pubescens

Discaria pubescens, commonly known as the silky everlasting or silky dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Discaria pubescens is also valued as a ornamental plant for its attractive, long-lasting flowers.
