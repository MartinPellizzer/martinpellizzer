Diasperus francavillanus

Diasperus francavillanus, commonly known as the Frenchville dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white pappus-bearing seeds. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and grasslands. The plant has a short growth cycle, flowering and setting seed during the warmer months.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh, dry conditions.
