Dyckia irmgardiae

Dyckia irmgardiae, commonly known as the Irmingard Dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dyckia irmgardiae is a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative indoor plant due to its ornamental foliage.
