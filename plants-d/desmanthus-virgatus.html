Desmanthus virgatus

Desmanthus virgatus, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It is often used as a cover crop due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
