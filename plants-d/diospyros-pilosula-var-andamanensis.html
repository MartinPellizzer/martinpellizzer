Diospyros pilosula var. andamanensis

Diospyros pilosula var. andamanensis, commonly known as the Andaman persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lanceolate leaves with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils near coastal areas.
