Dactylorhiza maculata

Dactylorhiza maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant features a pseudobulb-like stem and produces showy, pink or purple flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. Its roots are adapted for nutrient absorption in acidic soils.

Dactylorhiza maculata is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
