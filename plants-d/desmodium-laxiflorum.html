Desmodium laxiflorum

Desmodium laxiflorum, commonly known as slenderflower burrbean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Desmodium laxiflorum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
