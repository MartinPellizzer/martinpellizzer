Diplopterygium maximum

Diplopterygium maximum, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant features a robust, creeping rhizome that helps it spread and establish in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which aids in water retention.

This fern thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight.
