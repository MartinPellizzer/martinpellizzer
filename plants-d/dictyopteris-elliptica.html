Dictyopteris elliptica

Dictyopteris elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic featherwrack, is a species of brown algae belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its blade-like, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green to brown in color. The plant grows in coastal environments, often attached to rocks in intertidal zones. Its structure consists of a holdfast, stipe, and blade, with the blade being the most prominent feature.

Dictyopteris elliptica plays a role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
