Deyeuxia filiformis

Deyeuxia filiformis, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Deyeuxia filiformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
