Delphinium tabatae

Delphinium tabatae, commonly known as the Tabata delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Delphinium tabatae is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
