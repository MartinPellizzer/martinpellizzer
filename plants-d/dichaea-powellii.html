Dichaea powellii

Dichaea powellii, commonly known as Powell's dichaea, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a short, underground rhizome with several erect, linear leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often with a faintly colored lip.

Dichaea powellii is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, showcasing the resilience of orchids in extreme habitats.
