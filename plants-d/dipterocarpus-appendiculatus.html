Dipterocarpus appendiculatus

Dipterocarpus appendiculatus, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hard, heavy wood and is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for biodiversity and ecological studies.
