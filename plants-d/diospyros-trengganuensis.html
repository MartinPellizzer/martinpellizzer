Diospyros trengganuensis

Diospyros trengganuensis, commonly known as the Trengganu persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to reddish-brown berries. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in tropical climates with high humidity.

The fruit is edible, though it is less commonly cultivated compared to other persimmon species.
