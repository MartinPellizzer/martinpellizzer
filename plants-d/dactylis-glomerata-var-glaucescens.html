Dactylis glomerata var. glaucescens

Dactylis glomerata var. glaucescens, commonly known as blue sweet grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive blue-green foliage.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for both its ecological role in soil stabilization and its use in landscaping and traditional herbal practices.
