Diospyros minahassae

Diospyros minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, ovate leaves that are typically glossy green. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow when ripe, and it is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in crafts and furniture making.
