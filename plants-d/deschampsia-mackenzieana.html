Deschampsia mackenzieana

Deschampsia mackenzieana, commonly known as Mackenzie's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowering heads are slender and spike-like, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deschampsia mackenzieana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
