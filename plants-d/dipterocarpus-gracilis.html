Dipterocarpus gracilis

Dipterocarpus gracilis, commonly known as the slender dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its buttressed roots and tall, straight trunk. The tree produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its seeds are enclosed in woody capsules and are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests and is valued for its timber.
