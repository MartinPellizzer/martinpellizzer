Dioscorea elegantula

Dioscorea elegantula, commonly known as the velvetleaf yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy tuberous roots that are edible when mature. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The tubers are often used in traditional cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
