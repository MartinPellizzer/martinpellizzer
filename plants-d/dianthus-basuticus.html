Dianthus basuticus

Dianthus basuticus, commonly known as the Basutland carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers with frilled petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Dianthus basuticus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
