Delphinium diversifolium

Delphinium diversifolium, commonly known as the western larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive wavy margin.

Delphinium diversifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
