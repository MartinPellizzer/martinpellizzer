Dendrophthora longipedunculata

Dendrophthora longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to penetrate the host's vascular system and extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are typically scale-like and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrophthora longipedunculata plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
