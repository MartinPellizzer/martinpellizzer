Delphinium amabile subsp. apachense

Delphinium amabile subsp.

apachense, commonly known as the Apache larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of the southwestern United States. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, sometimes hairy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its distinctive morphology and ecological role in high-altitude environments.
