Dioscorea bancana

Dioscorea bancana, commonly known as the Philippine yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
