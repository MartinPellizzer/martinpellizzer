Diphyllanthus corymbosus

Diphyllanthus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered leaf euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and milky sap. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of cymes bearing small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by specialized bracts.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and resilience in various soil conditions.
