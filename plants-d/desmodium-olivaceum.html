Desmodium olivaceum

Desmodium olivaceum, commonly known as slender ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.
