Dichapetalum lujae var. gillardinii

Dichapetalum lujae var. gillardinii, commonly known as the Gillardin's ghost flower, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of central Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent, waxy leaves that are often described as resembling ghostly apparitions. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are typically hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
