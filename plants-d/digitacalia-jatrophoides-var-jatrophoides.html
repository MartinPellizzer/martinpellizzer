Digitacalia jatrophoides var. jatrophoides

Digitacalia jatrophoides var. jatrophoides, commonly known as the redroot morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped leaves and striking red to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
