Derris marginata

Derris marginata, commonly known as the white ant plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting various pollinators. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or near water sources.

Derris marginata is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
