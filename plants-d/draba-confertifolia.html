Draba confertifolia

Draba confertifolia, commonly known as the denseflowered whitlow grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

It typically produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are oblanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. It is native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, rocky environments.

Draba confertifolia is often found in open habitats such as meadows, tundra, and gravelly slopes.
