Drepanocarpus inundatus

Drepanocarpus inundatus, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Drepanocarpus inundatus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
