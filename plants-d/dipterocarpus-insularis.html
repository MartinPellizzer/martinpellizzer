Dipterocarpus insularis

Dipterocarpus insularis, commonly known as the island dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The plant produces woody fruits that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and plays a vital role in the ecosystem as a canopy-forming species.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it an important resource in local economies.
