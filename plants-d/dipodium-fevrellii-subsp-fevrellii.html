Dipodium fevrellii subsp. fevrellii

Dipodium fevrellii subsp.

fevrellii, commonly known as the Fevrell's dipodium, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae. It is a small, terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous growth habit and is native to specific regions in southern Africa.

The plant produces erect, hairy stems bearing several oval, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with three sepals and three petals, and it blooms during the cooler months.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
