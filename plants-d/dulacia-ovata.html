Dulacia ovata

Dulacia ovata, commonly known as the oval leaf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly branched, vine-like structure. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Dulacia ovata is noted for its ability to thrive on a variety of host plants, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
