Dichanthium grahamii

Dichanthium grahamii, commonly known as the Graham's fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
