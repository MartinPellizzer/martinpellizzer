Dendrocalamus nudus

Dendrocalamus nudus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, golden-yellow culms that are often used in traditional construction and crafts. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous on the upper surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus nudus is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it an important species in bamboo ecosystems.
