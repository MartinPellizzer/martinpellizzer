Deutzia corymbosa var. corymbosa

Deutzia corymbosa var. corymbosa, commonly known as the common deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early summer, which are borne in corymbs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its showy blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
