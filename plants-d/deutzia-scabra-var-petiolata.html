Deutzia scabra var. petiolata

Deutzia scabra var. petiolata, commonly known as the rough-stemmed deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on both surfaces, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and attractive foliage, which provide seasonal interest.
