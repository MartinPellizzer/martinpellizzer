Digitalis × mertonensis

Digitalis × mertonensis, commonly known as the Merton foxglove, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the UK.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
