Danthonia grandiflora

Danthonia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.

Its growth habit makes it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
