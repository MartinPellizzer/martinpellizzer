Dimorphandra ekmanii

Dimorphandra ekmanii, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and dense canopy. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Dimorphandra ekmanii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
