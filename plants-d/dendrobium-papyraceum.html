Dendrobium papyraceum

Dendrobium papyraceum, commonly known as the paper-drum orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Dendrobium papyraceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
