Dischistocalyx grandifolius

Dischistocalyx grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance and offering some resistance to pests and diseases.
