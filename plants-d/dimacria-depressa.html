Dimacria depressa

Dimacria depressa, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in sunny conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Dimacria depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
