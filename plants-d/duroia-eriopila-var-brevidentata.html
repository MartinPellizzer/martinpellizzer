Duroia eriopila var. brevidentata

Duroia eriopila var. brevidentata, commonly known as the short-toothed arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and short, stiff spines along the leaf margins. The plant produces clusters of small, white spadices that are typically hidden within the leaf sheaths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
