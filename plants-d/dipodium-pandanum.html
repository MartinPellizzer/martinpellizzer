Dipodium pandanum

Dipodium pandanum, commonly known as the mountain pandan, is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive fragrance. The leaves are long, narrow, and often used in traditional cooking and crafts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal.
