Desmodium tiliifolium

Desmodium tiliifolium, commonly known as the sicklepod or velvetleaf, is a perennial legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, which can benefit surrounding vegetation.
