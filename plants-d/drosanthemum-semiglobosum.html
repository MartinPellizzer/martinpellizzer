Drosanthemum semiglobosum

Drosanthemum semiglobosum, commonly known as the globular daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has succulent leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine, woolly coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
