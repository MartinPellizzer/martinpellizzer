Dianthus marisensis

Dianthus marisensis, commonly known as the sea campion, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically on slender, upright stems. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong winds. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Dianthus marisensis is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
