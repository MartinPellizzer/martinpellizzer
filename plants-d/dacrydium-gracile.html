Dacrydium gracile

Dacrydium gracile, commonly known as the slender pine or silver pine, is a small evergreen conifer native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Dacrydium gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
