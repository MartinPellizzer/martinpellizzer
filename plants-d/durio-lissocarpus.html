Durio lissocarpus

Durio lissocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-fruit durian, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its large, spiky fruit that contains a creamy, aromatic pulp. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.
