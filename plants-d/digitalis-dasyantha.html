Digitalis dasyantha

Digitalis dasyantha, commonly known as the woolly foxglove, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces tall spikes of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
