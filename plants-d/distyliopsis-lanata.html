Distyliopsis lanata

Distyliopsis lanata, commonly known as the woolly distyliopsis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or lanate, foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understory environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
