Didymoglossum cuspidatum var. densestriatum

Didymoglossum cuspidatum var. densestriatum, commonly known as the dense-striated feather moss, is a low-growing evergreen moss characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features slender, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth, shiny surface. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short, erect stalks and release spores when mature.

This variety is notable for its dense, compact growth and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal and ability to stabilize soil.
