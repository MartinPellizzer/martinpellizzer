Demazeria

Demazeria, commonly known as the common thistle, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets. The plant is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. Demazeria species are often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both a notable weed and a plant of ecological importance.
