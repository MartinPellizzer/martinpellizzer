Disa cephalotes subsp. cephalotes

Disa cephalotes subsp.

cephalotes, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a species native to South Africa. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive hooded lip that attracts pollinators.

It is a perennial herb that often forms dense colonies in its natural habitat.
