Didactyle atropurpurea

Didactyle atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a dark purple to reddish hue on the underside, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers bloom in late summer, producing a purple to pinkish color.

Didactyle atropurpurea is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
