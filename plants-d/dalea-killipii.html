Dalea killipii

Dalea killipii, commonly known as Killip's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
