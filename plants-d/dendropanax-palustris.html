Dendropanax palustris

Dendropanax palustris, commonly known as swamp lily or swamp panax, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in eastern North America.

It typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple at the base, and it produces small, white flowers in clusters. It is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Dendropanax palustris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
