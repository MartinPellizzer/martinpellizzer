Dracocephalum breviflorum

Dracocephalum breviflorum, commonly known as narrowflower blueweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Dracocephalum breviflorum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
