Diospyros korthalsiana

Diospyros korthalsiana, commonly known as the Korthals's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
