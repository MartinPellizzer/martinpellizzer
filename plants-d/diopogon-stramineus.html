Diopogon stramineus

Diopogon stramineus, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of lance-shaped, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in botany and horticulture.
