Digitaria sanguinalis var. fimbriata

Digitaria sanguinalis var. fimbriata, commonly known as fimbriated bermuda grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive, hairy ligules that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains.

This variant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and hardiness.
