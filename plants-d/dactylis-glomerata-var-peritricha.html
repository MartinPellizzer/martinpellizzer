Dactylis glomerata var. peritricha

Dactylis glomerata var. peritricha, commonly known as silky cocksfoot, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, silky-haired inflorescence. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and pastures. Its leaves are narrow and rough, while its stems are sturdy and often bent at the base.

This variety is valued for its forage quality and is sometimes used in agricultural settings.
