Dalbergia acaciifolia

Dalbergia acaciifolia, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its dense, dark wood, it has been historically valued for furniture and musical instrument crafting.
