Dianthus monspessulanus var. pentagonalis

Dianthus monspessulanus var. pentagonalis, commonly known as the five-angled carnation, is a compact, mat-forming plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense clusters of bright red, pink, or white flowers with five pointed petals, giving it its common name. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance, though it may require protection from frost in colder climates.
