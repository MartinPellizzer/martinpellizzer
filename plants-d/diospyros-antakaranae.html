Diospyros antakaranae

Diospyros antakaranae, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and potential economic importance in local ecosystems.
