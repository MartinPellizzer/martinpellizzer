Dahlia cuspidata

Dahlia cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, single or double flowers in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive in various soil conditions, making it adaptable to different climates.

Dahlia cuspidata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
