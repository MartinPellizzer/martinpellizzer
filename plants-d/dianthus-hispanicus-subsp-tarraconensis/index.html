Dianthus Hispanicus Subsp. Tarraconensis

Spanish campion, scientifically known as Dianthus hispanicus subsp. tarraconensis, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. With its vibrant pink and white blooms, it adds a touch of elegance to rocky and coastal landscapes. This subspecies is particularly noted for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, showcasing remarkable resilience. Its delicate yet sturdy appearance makes it a popular choice for gardeners and botanists alike. The Spanish campion holds cultural and ecological significance in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and local ecosystems.
