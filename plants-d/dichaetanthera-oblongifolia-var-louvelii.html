Dichaetanthera oblongifolia var. louvelii

Dichaetanthera oblongifolia var. louvelii, commonly known as the Louvel's narrow-leafed dichaetanthera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, oblong leaves and small, clustered flowers with distinctive, hairy anthers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears numerous tiny, white or pale pink flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
