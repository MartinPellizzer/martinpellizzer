Dryopteris pseudohirsuta

Dryopteris pseudohirsuta, commonly known as the hirsute wood fern, is a perennial fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a tall, slender rhizome that supports its delicate, bipinnate fronds. The fronds are typically bright green, with a hairy appearance on the underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
