Dalea boliviana

Dalea boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Dalea boliviana is often found in open grasslands and rocky slopes, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
