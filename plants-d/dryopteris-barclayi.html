Dryopteris barclayi

Dryopteris barclayi, commonly known as Barclay's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that sets it apart from other wood ferns. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can access the necessary humidity and indirect light. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of sporangia on the underside of the fronds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

The botanical features of D. barclayi make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
