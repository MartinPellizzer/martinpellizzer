Dipelta floribunda var. floribunda

Dipelta floribunda var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink to lavender in color, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic if ingested, making it important to handle with care.
