Deschampsia susumanica

Deschampsia susumanica, commonly known as the Susuman grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
