Diospyros oblonga var. chikusensis

Diospyros oblonga var. chikusensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe.

Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in local communities.
