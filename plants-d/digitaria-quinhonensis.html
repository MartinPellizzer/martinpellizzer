Digitaria quinhonensis

Digitaria quinhonensis, commonly known as Quinhonensis grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This perennial grass is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated.

Digitaria quinhonensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
