Delphinium fissum subsp. bolosii

Delphinium fissum subsp.

bolosii, commonly known as the Bolosian larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, with prominent veins.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
