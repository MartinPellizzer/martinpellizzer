Dendrobium kruiense subsp. alboflavum

Dendrobium kruiense subsp.

alboflavum, commonly known as the white-flowered dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, typically in tropical rainforests.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
