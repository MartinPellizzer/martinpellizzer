Dioscorea nobilis

Dioscorea nobilis, commonly known as the noble yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used. These tubers are often cultivated for their nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.

Dioscorea nobilis is also valued for its role in local agriculture and biodiversity conservation.
