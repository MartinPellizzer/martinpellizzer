Dicranopygium parvulum var. parvulum

Dicranopygium parvulum var. parvulum, commonly known as the small-leaved feather moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and small, narrow leaves that are typically curved. The plant produces elongated, spore-bearing capsules that are often curved or twisted, a distinctive feature of the genus Dicranopygium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or on rocky substrates.

This moss is an important component of many ecosystems, contributing to soil stability and moisture retention.
