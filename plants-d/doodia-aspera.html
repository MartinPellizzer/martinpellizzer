Doodia aspera

Doodia aspera, commonly known as the rough fern, is a perennial fern native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with compound leaves that have a rugged texture. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are divided into lance-shaped segments.

Doodia aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
