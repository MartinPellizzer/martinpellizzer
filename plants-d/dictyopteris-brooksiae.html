Dictyopteris brooksiae

Dictyopteris brooksiae, commonly known as the Brooks' sea holly, is a species of green algae belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its branching, blade-like thallus with a distinctive pattern of small, rounded pits on its surface. This algae typically grows in shallow, coastal waters where it attaches to substrates such as rocks or shells. Its structure allows it to efficiently absorb nutrients from the surrounding water, contributing to its survival in marine environments.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
