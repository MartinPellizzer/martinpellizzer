Duthiea brachypodium

Duthiea brachypodium, commonly known as the shortgrass grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing stature and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is often used in ecological restoration projects and as a model organism in plant genetics research.
