Dalea purpurea f. arenaria

Dalea purpurea f. arenaria, commonly known as the sand dalea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration.

This species is often found in coastal dunes and other sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
