Dolichos maranguensis

Dolichos maranguensis, commonly known as the Marangu bean, is a climbing legume native to East Africa.

It produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines. The plant has trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands or grasslands.

The botanical features of Dolichos maranguensis make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes in its native region.
