Diarthron turkmenorum

Diarthron turkmenorum, commonly known as Turkmenian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets.

Diarthron turkmenorum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used for forage in livestock grazing.
