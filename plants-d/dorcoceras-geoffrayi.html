Dorcoceras geoffrayi

Dorcoceras geoffrayi, commonly known as the Geoffray's horned lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear showy, violet or purple flowers with prominent, recurved petals. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose raceme and are pollinated by insects.

Dorcoceras geoffrayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
