Ditassa sipapoana

Ditassa sipapoana, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found exclusively in the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that display a vibrant mix of purple and white hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Ditassa sipapoana is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for protective measures to preserve its existence.
