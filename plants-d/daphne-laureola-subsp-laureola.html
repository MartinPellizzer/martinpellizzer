Daphne laureola subsp. laureola

Daphne laureola subsp.

laureola, commonly known as the common daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, blooming in early spring.

The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodlands and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its winter foliage and early spring blooms.
