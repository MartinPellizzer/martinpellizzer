Dichapetalum thonneri

Dichapetalum thonneri, commonly known as the African star orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique structure that sets it apart from other orchids. The plant grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
