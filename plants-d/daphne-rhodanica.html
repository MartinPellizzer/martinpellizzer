Daphne rhodanica

Daphne rhodanica, commonly known as the pink daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle bluish tinge. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling.
