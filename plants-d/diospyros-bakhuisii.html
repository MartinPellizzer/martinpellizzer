Diospyros bakhuisii

Diospyros bakhuisii, commonly known as the Borneo persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by edible berries.

Its fruit, which ripens to a deep red or orange color, is often consumed raw or used in local cuisines.
