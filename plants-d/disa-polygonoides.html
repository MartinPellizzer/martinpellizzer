Disa polygonoides

Disa polygonoides, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive hooded structure that protects the reproductive organs.

Disa polygonoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
