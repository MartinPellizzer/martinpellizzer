Daemonorops plagiocycla

Daemonorops plagiocycla, commonly known as the golden bamboo or cycad-like plant, is a member of the Arecaceae family, though it is often mistaken for a cycad due to its appearance.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features a short, thick trunk with pinnate, feather-like leaves that are typically bright green and can grow quite long. Its inflorescences are terminal and consist of a cluster of small, spadix-like flowers, which are usually yellow or green.
