Dodonaea latifolia

Dodonaea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has slender, wiry stems and broad, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for their purported healing properties.
