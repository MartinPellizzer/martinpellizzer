Didymocarpus reptans var. violascens

Didymocarpus reptans var. violascens, commonly known as the violet-stemmed climbing fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive violet-colored stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
