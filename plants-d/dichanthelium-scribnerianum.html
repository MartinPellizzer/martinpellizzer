Dichanthelium scribnerianum

Dichanthelium scribnerianum, commonly known as Scribner's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The species is also noted for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
