Diospyros nipponica

Diospyros nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible berries that ripen to a deep orange or red color. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary practices.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in various crafts and construction.
