Dictyopteris petrophya

Dictyopteris petrophya, commonly known as the rock-lichen kelp, is a species of brown alga found in marine environments.

It has a distinctive morphology characterized by a leathery, flattened thallus with a dark brown coloration. The plant exhibits a complex life cycle involving both a sporophyte and gametophyte stage. It typically grows on rocky substrates in shallow coastal waters.

Its botanical features make it an important component of intertidal ecosystems.
