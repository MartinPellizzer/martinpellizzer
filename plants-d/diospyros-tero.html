Diospyros tero

Diospyros tero, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn orange-red when mature. Its bark is smooth and dark brown, often marked with irregular lenticels, and the wood is valued for its density and durability.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
