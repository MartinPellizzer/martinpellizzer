Digitalis lutea subsp. cedretorum

Digitalis lutea subsp.

cedretorum, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
