Dioscorea plumifera

Dioscorea plumifera, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a climbing vine native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by cylindrical, tuberous roots that are the primary part used. These roots are valued for their medicinal properties and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Dioscorea plumifera thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
