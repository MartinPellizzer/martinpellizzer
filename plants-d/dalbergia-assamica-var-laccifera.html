Dalbergia assamica var. laccifera

Dalbergia assamica var. laccifera, commonly known as the Assam rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich reddish-brown color and a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
