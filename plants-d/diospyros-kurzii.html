Diospyros kurzii

Diospyros kurzii, commonly known as the Kharanpur date palm, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates.

Diospyros kurzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
