Diospyros gracilipes

Diospyros gracilipes, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruit is edible and often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hard, durable wood.
