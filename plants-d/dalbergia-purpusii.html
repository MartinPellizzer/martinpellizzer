Dalbergia purpusii

Dalbergia purpusii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. It also has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The tree is often found in savannas and woodlands, where it plays an important ecological role.
