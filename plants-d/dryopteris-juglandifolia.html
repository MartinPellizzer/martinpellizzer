Dryopteris juglandifolia

Dryopteris juglandifolia, commonly known as the walnut-leaf fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a walnut, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are bioluminescent, emitting a soft glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique characteristic makes it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
