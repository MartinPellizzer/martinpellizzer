Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. turcestanica

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

turcestanica, commonly known as the Turcestan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

The plant features a rhizome with several elongated, fleshy leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are usually pale purple to white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
