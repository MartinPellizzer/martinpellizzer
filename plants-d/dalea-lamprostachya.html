Dalea lamprostachya

Dalea lamprostachya, commonly known as the silverleaf Dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, pink to lavender flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its unique silver-gray leaves help reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Dalea lamprostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
