Dolichos medicagineus

Dolichos medicagineus, commonly known as the horsegram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
