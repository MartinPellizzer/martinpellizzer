Dolichos altissimus

Dolichos altissimus, commonly known as the tall cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fibrous pods. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and can reach significant heights, often exceeding 3 meters. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its edible seeds and forage value.

The pods contain seeds that are rich in protein and are used in various culinary traditions.
