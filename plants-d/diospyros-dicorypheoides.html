Diospyros dicorypheoides

Diospyros dicorypheoides, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
