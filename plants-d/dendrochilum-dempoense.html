Dendrochilum dempoense

Dendrochilum dempoense, commonly known as the Dempo orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and a cluster of fleshy, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are usually small and arranged in a raceme, with a distinctive greenish-yellow coloration.

Dendrochilum dempoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
