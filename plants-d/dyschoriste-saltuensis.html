Dyschoriste saltuensis

Dyschoriste saltuensis, commonly known as the salt marsh dyschoriste, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in saline or brackish environments, demonstrating a high tolerance for saltwater conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Dyschoriste saltuensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
