Delphinium kawaguchii

Delphinium kawaguchii, commonly known as the Japanese larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its beauty and hardiness, Delphinium kawaguchii is often cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
