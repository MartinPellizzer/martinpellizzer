Dendrochilum perplexum

Dendrochilum perplexum, commonly known as the perplexing tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with distinctive, spurred petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
