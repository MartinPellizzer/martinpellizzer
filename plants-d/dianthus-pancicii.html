Dianthus pancicii

Dianthus pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Dianthus pancicii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
