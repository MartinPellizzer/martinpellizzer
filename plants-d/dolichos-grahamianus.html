Dolichos grahamianus

Dolichos grahamianus, commonly known as the Graham's Dolichos, is a climbing legume native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Dolichos grahamianus is valued for its nutritional content and ecological role in soil enrichment.
