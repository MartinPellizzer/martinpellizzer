Diplazium vesiculosum

Diplazium vesiculosum, commonly known as the spore-bearing fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called soruses on the undersides of its fronds. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Diplazium vesiculosum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
