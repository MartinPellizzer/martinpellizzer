Diospyros inconstans subsp. inconstans

Diospyros inconstans subsp.

inconstans, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe, though it may have astringent properties when unripe.
