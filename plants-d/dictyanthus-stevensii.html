Dictyanthus stevensii

Dictyanthus stevensii, commonly known as the Stevens' dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.
