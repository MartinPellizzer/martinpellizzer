Discopleura capillacea var. nuttallii

Discopleura capillacea var. nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall's featherleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a delicate, feathery appearance that gives it its common name.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
