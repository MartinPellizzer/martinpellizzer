Duosperma livingstoniense

Duosperma livingstoniense, commonly known as the Livingston's ghost flower, is a rare and unique succulent native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
