Deparia cataracticola

Deparia cataracticola, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically covered in a fine, silky coating. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky outcrops or in dense forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky substrates, while its spores are dispersed by wind.

The botanical features of Deparia cataracticola make it an important component of its native ecosystem, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
