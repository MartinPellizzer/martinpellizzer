Descurainia longepedicellata f. gracilis

Descurainia longepedicellata f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender pedicellate tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its slender stems that often grow in a creeping or upright habit. The plant typically has pinnately divided leaves with finely serrated margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
