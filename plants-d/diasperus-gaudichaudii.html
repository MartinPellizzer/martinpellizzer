Diasperus gaudichaudii

Diasperus gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Diasperus gaudichaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
