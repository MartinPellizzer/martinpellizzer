Didiscus pilosus var. glaberrimus

Didiscus pilosus var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the silky didiscus, is a variant of the didiscus plant characterized by its smooth, hairless surface.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant features broad, waxy leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens and private collections.
