Dactylis polygama f. luxurians

Dactylis polygama f. luxurians, commonly known as the luxuriant dactylis, is a variant of the common dactylis grass.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is sometimes used in erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a notable example of a grass species with a distinctive floral structure.
