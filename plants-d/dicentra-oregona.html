Dicentra oregona

Dicentra oregona, commonly known as the Oregon poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble poppies, with bright red petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Dicentra oregona is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
