Dyckia niederleinii

Dyckia niederleinii, commonly known as the Niederlein's dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that are often red or orange in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Dyckia niederleinii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
