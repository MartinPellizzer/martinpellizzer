Dichosema spinosa

Dichosema spinosa, commonly known as the spiny dichosema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dichosema spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
