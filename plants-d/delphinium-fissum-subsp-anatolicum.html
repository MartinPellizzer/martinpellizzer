Delphinium fissum subsp. anatolicum

Delphinium fissum subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets that are hairy and serrated.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
