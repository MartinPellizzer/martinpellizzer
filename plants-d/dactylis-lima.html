Dactylis lima

Dactylis lima, commonly known as the creeping vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, oblong pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for forage in some regions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
