Dianthus lereschii

Dianthus lereschii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Dianthus lereschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
