Drosanthemum hispidum

Drosanthemum hispidum, commonly known as the woolly daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Drosanthemum hispidum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
