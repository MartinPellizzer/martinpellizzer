Delphinium montanum f. calvescens

Delphinium montanum f. calvescens, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine habitats.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in high-altitude flora.
