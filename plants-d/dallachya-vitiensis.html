Dallachya vitiensis

Dallachya vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Viti Levu in Fiji.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it thrives on decomposing organic matter. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its native region.
