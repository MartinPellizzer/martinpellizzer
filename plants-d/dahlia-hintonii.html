Dahlia hintonii

Dahlia hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. Its tuberous roots are used for propagation, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Dahlia hintonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
