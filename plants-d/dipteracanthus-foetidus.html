Dipteracanthus foetidus

Dipteracanthus foetidus, commonly known as the stinking gourd, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, foul-smelling flowers that attract specific pollinators. The plant produces large, green, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy and robust.

The species is valued for both its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in certain horticultural contexts.
