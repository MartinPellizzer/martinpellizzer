Dendrocalamus maximus

Dendrocalamus maximus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its culms are highly valued for their strength and flexibility, making them useful in construction and crafts.

Dendrocalamus maximus plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
