Daphne collina

Daphne collina, commonly known as the creeping daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is notable for its winter blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring. Its foliage and flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate beauty and long-lasting nature.

Daphne collina prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland borders.
