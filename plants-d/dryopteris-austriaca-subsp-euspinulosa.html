Dryopteris austriaca subsp. euspinulosa

Dryopteris austriaca subsp.

euspinulosa, commonly known as the Austrian wood fern, is a fern species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments.

The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in spore-bearing clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
