Dolichos pentaphyllus

Dolichos pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leafed cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Dolichos pentaphyllus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting agricultural practices.
