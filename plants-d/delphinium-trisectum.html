Delphinium trisectum

Delphinium trisectum, commonly known as the three-lobed larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are palmately lobed, giving the plant its common name, and are typically hairy in texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
