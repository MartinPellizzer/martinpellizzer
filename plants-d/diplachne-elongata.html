Diplachne elongata

Diplachne elongata, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, flat leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it a key component of wetland ecosystems in its native range.
