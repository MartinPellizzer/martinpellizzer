Dodonaea viscosa var. linearis

Dodonaea viscosa var. linearis, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
