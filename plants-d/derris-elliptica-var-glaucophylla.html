Derris elliptica var. glaucophylla

Derris elliptica var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla derris, is a variant of the derris plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by the development of pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Fabaceae and is notable for its potential medicinal properties.
