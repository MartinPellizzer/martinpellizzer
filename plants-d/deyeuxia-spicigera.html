Deyeuxia spicigera

Deyeuxia spicigera, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairless. Its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Deyeuxia spicigera plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
