Desmodium argenteum

Desmodium argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is a legume, meaning it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Desmodium argenteum is often used in forage and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and nutrient-fixing properties.
