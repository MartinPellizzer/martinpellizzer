Dysoxylum lessertianum var. pubescens

Dysoxylum lessertianum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dysoxylum, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, often hairy bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and potential medicinal contexts.
