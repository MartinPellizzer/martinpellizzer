Dolichos hirtus

Dolichos hirtus, commonly known as hairy Dolichos, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, twining stems covered in fine hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Dolichos hirtus is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines and can be ground into flour.
