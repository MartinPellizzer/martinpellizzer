Draba rivetii

Draba rivetii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are typically spoon-shaped and hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in a tight cluster at the top of the stem. Draba rivetii thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous environments.

Its short life cycle and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
