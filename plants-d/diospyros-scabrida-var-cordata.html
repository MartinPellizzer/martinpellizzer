Diospyros scabrida var. cordata

Diospyros scabrida var. cordata, commonly known as the smooth persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, though typically consumed when fully ripe, and it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
