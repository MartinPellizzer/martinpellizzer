Desmoncus anomalus

Desmoncus anomalus, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that give it a ghost-like appearance, hence its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread slowly across moist, shaded forest floors. Its leaves are bioluminescent in the dark, a rare trait among ferns, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its rarity and unique characteristics, it is highly valued in botanical research and conservation efforts.
