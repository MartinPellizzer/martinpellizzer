Dioclea lehmannii

Dioclea lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Dioclea lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
