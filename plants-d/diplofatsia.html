Diplofatsia

Diplofatsia, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Native to Australia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
