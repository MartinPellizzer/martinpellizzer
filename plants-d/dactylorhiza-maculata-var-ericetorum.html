Dactylorhiza maculata var. ericetorum

Dactylorhiza maculata var. ericetorum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and striking purple and white flowers with a distinctive yellow-green throat. The plant typically grows in acidic, moist habitats such as heaths, bogs, and woodland edges. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also notable for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
