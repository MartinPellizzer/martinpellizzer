Daphniphyllum latifolium

Daphniphyllum latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved daphniphyllum, is a species of evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
