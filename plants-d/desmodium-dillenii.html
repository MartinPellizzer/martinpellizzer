Desmodium dillenii

Desmodium dillenii, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Desmodium dillenii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural use.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread presence in natural and cultivated environments.
