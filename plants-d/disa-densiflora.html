Disa densiflora

Disa densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered disa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in grasslands and forest understories.

Disa densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
