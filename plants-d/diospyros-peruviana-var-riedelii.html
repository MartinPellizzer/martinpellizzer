Diospyros peruviana var. riedelii

Diospyros peruviana var. riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's persimmon, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary practices by local communities.
