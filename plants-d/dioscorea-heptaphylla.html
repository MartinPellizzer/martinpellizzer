Dioscorea heptaphylla

Dioscorea heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leaf yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been historically utilized for food and medicinal purposes.

The species is valued for its ecological role and cultural significance in its native regions.
