Diospyros inconstans subsp. psidioides

Diospyros inconstans subsp.

psidioides, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species.

The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often leathery leaves, which are usually dark green and glossy. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white, and it produces small, round fruits that are generally edible.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some use in traditional medicine.
