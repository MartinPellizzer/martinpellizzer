Drynaria baronii

Drynaria baronii, commonly known as the noble fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Drynariaceae and is characterized by its long, narrow fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Drynaria baronii is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
