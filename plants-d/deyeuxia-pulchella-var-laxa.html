Deyeuxia pulchella var. laxa

Deyeuxia pulchella var. laxa, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.
