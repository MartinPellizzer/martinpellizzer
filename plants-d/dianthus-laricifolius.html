Dianthus laricifolius

Dianthus laricifolius, commonly known as the mountain pinks, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are usually erect and branched.

Dianthus laricifolius is known for its resilience to harsh conditions, making it a hardy species adapted to alpine climates.
