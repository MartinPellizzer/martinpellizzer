Diplocalyx chrysophylloides

Diplocalyx chrysophylloides, commonly known as the golden-leafed diplocalyx, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow or golden-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of stipules at the base of each leaf.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
