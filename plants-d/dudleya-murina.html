Dudleya murina

Dudleya murina, commonly known as the coastal dudleya, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Dudleya murina is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care once established.
