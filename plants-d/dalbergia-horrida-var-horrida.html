Dalbergia horrida var. horrida

Dalbergia horrida var. horrida, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and woodlands.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of fuel.
