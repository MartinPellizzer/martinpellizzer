Dypsis rhodotricha

Dypsis rhodotricha, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its thick, columnar trunk and clusters of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters at the base of the leaves. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant in warm climates.
