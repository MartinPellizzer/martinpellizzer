Dioscorea chaponensis var. chazutensis

Dioscorea chaponensis var. chazutensis, commonly known as the Chazut mountain yam, is a tuberous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy tubers that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential pharmacological properties.
