Dolichos hispidus

Dolichos hispidus, commonly known as hairy dolichos, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant bears elongated, hairy pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Dolichos hispidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
