Desmotrichum bancanum

Desmotrichum bancanum, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by narrow, leaf-like bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
