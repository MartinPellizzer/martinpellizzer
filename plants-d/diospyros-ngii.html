Diospyros ngii

Diospyros ngii, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from green to yellow or red as it matures.

Diospyros ngii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
