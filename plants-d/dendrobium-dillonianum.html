Dendrobium dillonianum

Dendrobium dillonianum, commonly known as the Dill's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, wiry stems that can reach up to several feet in length. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns, blooming in clusters.

Dendrobium dillonianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
