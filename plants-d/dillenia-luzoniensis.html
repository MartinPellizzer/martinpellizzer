Dillenia luzoniensis

Dillenia luzoniensis, commonly known as the Luzon dillenia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Dilleniaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces edible fruits that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions of Luzon.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
