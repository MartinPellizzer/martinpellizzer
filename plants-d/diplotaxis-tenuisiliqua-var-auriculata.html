Diplotaxis tenuisiliqua var. auriculata

Diplotaxis tenuisiliqua var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate diplotaxis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, known for its cruciform flowers and often edible leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
