Diospyros streptosepala

Diospyros streptosepala, commonly known as the red-berried ebony, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a bright red skin and a single, hard seed inside.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
