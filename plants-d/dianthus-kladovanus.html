Dianthus kladovanus

Dianthus kladovanus, commonly known as the Kladovan's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is known for its compact growth habit.

Dianthus kladovanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
