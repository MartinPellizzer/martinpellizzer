Dianthus frutescens

Dianthus frutescens, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as cliffs and coastal areas.

Dianthus frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
