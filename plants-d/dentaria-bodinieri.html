Dentaria bodinieri

Dentaria bodinieri, commonly known as the Louisiana bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Dentaria bodinieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
