Drynaria araneosa

Drynaria araneosa, commonly known as the spider fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble spider webs, giving it its common name. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The fronds are typically bioluminescent, a unique trait that aids in attracting pollinators.

Its intricate structure and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor cultivation.
