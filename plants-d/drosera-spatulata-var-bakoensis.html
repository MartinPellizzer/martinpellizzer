Drosera spatulata var. bakoensis

Drosera spatulata var. bakoensis, commonly known as the Bako Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity.

Its unique adaptations make it an intriguing subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
