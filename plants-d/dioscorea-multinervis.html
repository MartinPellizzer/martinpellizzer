Dioscorea multinervis

Dioscorea multinervis, commonly known as the veined yam, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source. The plant has slender, hairy stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent veins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The tubers of Dioscorea multinervis are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cuisines.
