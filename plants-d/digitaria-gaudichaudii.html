Digitaria gaudichaudii

Digitaria gaudichaudii, commonly known as the African grass or Guinea grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
