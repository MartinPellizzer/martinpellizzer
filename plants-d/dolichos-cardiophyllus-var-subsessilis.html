Dolichos cardiophyllus var. subsessilis

Dolichos cardiophyllus var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, heart-shaped leaves and climbing habit, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or for its nutritional seeds.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various agricultural environments.
