Durabaculum tangerinum

Durabaculum tangerinum, commonly known as the golden tangerine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its ornamental foliage and fragrant flowers. Due to its attractive appearance and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
