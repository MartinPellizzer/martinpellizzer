Diospyros tampolensis

Diospyros tampolensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, though it is often used in local traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
