Daphne mazelii

Daphne mazelii, commonly known as the golden daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to yellow, often with a pink or red tinge. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Daphne mazelii is also known for its winter flowering habit, adding visual interest during the colder months.
