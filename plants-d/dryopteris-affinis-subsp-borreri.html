Dryopteris affinis subsp. borreri

Dryopteris affinis subsp.

borreri, commonly known as the Borreri wood fern, is a shade-tolerant fern native to the mountainous regions of North America. It features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure that gives it a lacy appearance.

The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid environments.

This subspecies is often found in forest understories and rocky habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
