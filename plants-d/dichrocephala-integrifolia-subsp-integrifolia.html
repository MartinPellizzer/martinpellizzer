Dichrocephala integrifolia subsp. integrifolia

Dichrocephala integrifolia subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
