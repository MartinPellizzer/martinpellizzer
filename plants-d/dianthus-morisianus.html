Dianthus morisianus

Dianthus morisianus, commonly known as the Morisian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching. Dianthus morisianus thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
