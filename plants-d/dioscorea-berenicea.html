Dioscorea berenicea

Dioscorea berenicea, commonly known as the Berenice's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Dioscorea berenicea is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated for its edible tubers in some regions.
