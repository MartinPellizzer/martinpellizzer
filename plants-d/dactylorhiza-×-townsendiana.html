Dactylorhiza × townsendiana

Dactylorhiza × townsendiana, commonly known as the Townsend's marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single spike of flowers with three to five blooms.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
