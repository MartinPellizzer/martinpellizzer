Diospyros flavovirens

Diospyros flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that mature to a reddish color.

The wood of Diospyros flavovirens is valued for its density and durability, making it useful in traditional carpentry and tool-making.
