Didissandra ornata

Didissandra ornata, commonly known as the ornate didissandra, is a unique member of the Araliaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Didissandra ornata thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun exposure.
