Dianthus baldzhuanicus

Dianthus baldzhuanicus, commonly known as the Caucasian pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces showy, clustered flowers with five petals, often accompanied by green bracts.

Dianthus baldzhuanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
