Dioscorea amaranthoides var. crumenigera

Dioscorea amaranthoides var. crumenigera, commonly known as the red-veined arrowroot, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a starchy food source. The plant has compound leaves with lanceolate leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its tubers are typically reddish in color and have a mild, sweet flavor when cooked.

This variety is valued both for its nutritional content and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
