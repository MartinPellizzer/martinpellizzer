Dalea leucosericea

Dalea leucosericea, commonly known as the white silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, silky-haired leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea leucosericea is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats.
