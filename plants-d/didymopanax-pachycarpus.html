Didymopanax pachycarpus

Didymopanax pachycarpus, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are thick and tuberous, which are used as a starchy food source.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
