Dichaea angustifolia

Dichaea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it thrives on the forest floor. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
