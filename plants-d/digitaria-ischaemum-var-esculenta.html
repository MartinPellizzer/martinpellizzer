Digitaria ischaemum var. esculenta

Digitaria ischaemum var. esculenta, commonly known as African rice or white Guinea rice, is a grass species native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It is typically cultivated in wetland areas and is adapted to flooded conditions.

This variety is valued for its resilience to waterlogging and its potential as a staple food crop.
