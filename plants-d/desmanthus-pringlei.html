Desmanthus pringlei

Desmanthus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's burrlegume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant's leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along creek beds.

Desmanthus pringlei is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
