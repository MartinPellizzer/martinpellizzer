Digomphotis cordata

Digomphotis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
