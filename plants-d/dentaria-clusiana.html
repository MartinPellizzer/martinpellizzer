Dentaria clusiana

Dentaria clusiana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Dentaria clusiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
