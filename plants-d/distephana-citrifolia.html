Distephana citrifolia

Distephana citrifolia, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces distinctive five-lobed fruits, each shaped like a star when cut crosswise. The plant's leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a pointed tip. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, tangy flavor and is widely cultivated for both commercial and culinary use.
