Dyssodia coccinea

Dyssodia coccinea, commonly known as the red dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

Dyssodia coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
