Davallia elegans var. coniifolia

Davallia elegans var. coniifolia, commonly known as the fern with needle-like fronds, is a striking ornamental fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, arching fronds that are dark green with a silvery-blue tint, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, typical of ferns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for indoor cultivation as well as outdoor gardens.
