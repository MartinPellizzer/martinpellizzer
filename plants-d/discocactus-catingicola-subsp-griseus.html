Discocactus catingicola subsp. griseus

Discocactus catingicola subsp.

griseus, commonly known as the gray discocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and distinctive grayish-green coloration.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
