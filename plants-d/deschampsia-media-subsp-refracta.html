Deschampsia media subsp. refracta

Deschampsia media subsp.

refracta, commonly known as the refracta grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often slightly drooping.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
