Durio excelsus

Durio excelsus, commonly known as the giant durian, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiky fruit, which can weigh up to 15 kilograms. The plant belongs to the genus Durio in the family Malvaceae, and it is closely related to the more familiar durian species. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong, often unpleasant odor.

The fruit's thick, fibrous pericarp contains a creamy, aromatic pulp that is highly prized in local markets.
