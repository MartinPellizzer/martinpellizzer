Delphinium caerulescens

Delphinium caerulescens, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Delphinium caerulescens is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its striking floral displays and ecological importance to pollinators.
