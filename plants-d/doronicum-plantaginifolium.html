Doronicum plantaginifolium

Doronicum plantaginifolium, commonly known as the alpine smokeplant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinct waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
