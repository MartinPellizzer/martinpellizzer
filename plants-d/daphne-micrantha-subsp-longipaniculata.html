Daphne micrantha subsp. longipaniculata

Daphne micrantha subsp.

longipaniculata, commonly known as the longpanicle daphne, is a species native to South Africa. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of long, branched panicles, which give it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
