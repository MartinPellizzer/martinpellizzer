Diodia pedunculata

Diodia pedunculata, commonly known as false wild betony, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.
