Davallia saccoloma

Davallia saccoloma, commonly known as the horse's tail fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, segmented, creeping rhizomes that produce upright, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a horse's tail.

Davallia saccoloma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
