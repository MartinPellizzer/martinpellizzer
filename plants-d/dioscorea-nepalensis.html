Dioscorea nepalensis

Dioscorea nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal yam, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used for food and medicinal purposes.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.
