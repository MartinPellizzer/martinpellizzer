Dioscorea praetervisa

Dioscorea praetervisa, commonly known as the purple yam, is a flowering plant belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source in various cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, while its flowers are typically white or pale green.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
