Dichaea arbuscula

Dichaea arbuscula, commonly known as the tree fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in overlapping, leathery leaves.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant has a unique growth habit, with a central trunk that resembles a tree and fronds that grow from the top. Dichaea arbuscula thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
