Dioscorea anguina

Dioscorea anguina, commonly known as the snake-root yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tendrils and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's morphology and growth habits make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
