Danais cernua var. ternata

Danais cernua var. ternata, commonly known as the slender danais, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many areas, highlighting the need for its protection.
