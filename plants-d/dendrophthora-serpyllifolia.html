Dendrophthora serpyllifolia

Dendrophthora serpyllifolia, commonly known as the serpyllifolous tree moss, is a unique member of the Dendrophthoraceae family.

It is a non-photosynthetic epiphytic plant that grows on the bark of trees, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant lacks true leaves and instead has a structure called a thallus, which is composed of a dense mat of filaments. It absorbs nutrients and moisture from the air and rain through its specialized structures.

Dendrophthora serpyllifolia plays a role in nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
