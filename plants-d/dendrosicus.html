Dendrosicus

Dendrosicus, commonly known as the golden thread vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Dendrosicus is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
