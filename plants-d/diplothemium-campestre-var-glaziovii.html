Diplothemium campestre var. glaziovii

Diplothemium campestre var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
