Dianthus seguieri var. viscidus

Dianthus seguieri var. viscidus, commonly known as the sticky pinks, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink to reddish-pink flowers. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, sticky coating, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
