Diplotaxis catholica var. dasycarpa

Diplotaxis catholica var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the African cabbages, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a dense, terminal cluster, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
