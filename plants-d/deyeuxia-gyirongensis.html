Deyeuxia gyirongensis

Deyeuxia gyirongensis, commonly known as the Gyirong sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streambanks. It has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deyeuxia gyirongensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential horticultural value.
