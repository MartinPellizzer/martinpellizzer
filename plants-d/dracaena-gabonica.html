Dracaena gabonica

Dracaena gabonica, commonly known as the red-edged dracaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are often reddish-edged, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters at the tips of the stems. It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
