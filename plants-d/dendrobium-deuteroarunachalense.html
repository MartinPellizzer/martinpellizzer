Dendrobium deuteroarunachalense

Dendrobium deuteroarunachalense, commonly known as the Deuteroarunachalensis orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Dendrobium genus, which includes over 1,200 species of orchids, many of which are prized for their ornamental value. This particular species is characterized by its long, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. It thrives in humid, high-altitude environments with ample indirect light.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
