Delima sarmentosa

Delima sarmentosa, commonly known as the climbing delima, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Delima sarmentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
