Digitaria californica

Digitaria californica, commonly known as California wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and coastal regions. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
