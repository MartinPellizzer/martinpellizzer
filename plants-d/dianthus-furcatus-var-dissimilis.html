Dianthus furcatus var. dissimilis

Dianthus furcatus var. dissimilis, commonly known as the variable carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
