Dianthus stenopetalus

Dianthus stenopetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five narrow petals that give it its scientific name.

Dianthus stenopetalus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
