Dipteranthus pseudobulbifer

Dipteranthus pseudobulbifer, commonly known as the false bulb orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its pseudobulbs, which are thickened stems that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in shaded, humid habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and scientific interest.
