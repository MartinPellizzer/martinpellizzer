Damnacanthus tashiroi

Damnacanthus tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
