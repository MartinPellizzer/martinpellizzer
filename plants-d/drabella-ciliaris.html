Drabella ciliaris

Drabella ciliaris, commonly known as the silky draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-gray leaves with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal cluster, typically blooming in early spring. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
