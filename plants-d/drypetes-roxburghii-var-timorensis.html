Drypetes roxburghii var. timorensis

Drypetes roxburghii var. timorensis, commonly known as the Timor flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland areas.

The Timor flame tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
