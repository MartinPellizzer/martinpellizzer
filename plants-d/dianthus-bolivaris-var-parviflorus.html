Dianthus bolivaris var. parviflorus

Dianthus bolivaris var. parviflorus, commonly known as the Bolivian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, clustered blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinctive frilled appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic plants.
