Draba hirta var. incisodentata

Draba hirta var. incisodentata, commonly known as the serrated mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are hairy and often have serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Draba hirta var. incisodentata is a perennial herb that produces numerous small white or yellow flowers throughout the growing season.
