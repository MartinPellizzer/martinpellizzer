Delonix brachycarpa

Delonix brachycarpa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has pinnate leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
