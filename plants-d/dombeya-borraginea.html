Dombeya borraginea

Dombeya borraginea, commonly known as the blue dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dombeya borraginea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
