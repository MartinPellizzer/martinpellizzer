Dinebra haareri

Dinebra haareri, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss.

Dinebra haareri is often found growing in rocky crevices or along dry streambeds, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
