Dierama grandiflorum

Dierama grandiflorum, commonly known as the mountain grass-veld or the white dierama, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking clusters of white or pale pink, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
