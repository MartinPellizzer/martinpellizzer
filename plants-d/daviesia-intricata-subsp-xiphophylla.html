Daviesia intricata subsp. xiphophylla

Daviesia intricata subsp.

xiphophylla, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pink to red flowers clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
