Diospyros sandwicensis var. oahuensis

Diospyros sandwicensis var. oahuensis, commonly known as the Oahu persimmon, is a rare endemic plant native to the island of Oahu in Hawaii.

It is a small tree or shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is typically a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a bright orange or red color. This species is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat, including volcanic soils and a tropical climate.
