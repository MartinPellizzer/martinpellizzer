Daphne rubrum

Daphne rubrum, commonly known as the red daphne, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces clusters of pink to reddish flowers, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Daphne rubrum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.
