Drosera browniana

Drosera browniana, commonly known as the Brown's Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, sticky leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its small white flowers bloom in spring, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Drosera browniana is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
