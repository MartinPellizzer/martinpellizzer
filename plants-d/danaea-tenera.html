Danaea tenera

Danaea tenera, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Danaea tenera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
