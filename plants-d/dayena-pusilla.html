Dayena pusilla

Dayena pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf dayflower, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender stems and small, delicate flowers. The flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a spurred shape, typical of the Commelinaceae family. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
