Dodonaea eriocarpa var. costulata

Dodonaea eriocarpa var. costulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and small, narrow leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland areas.

The foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
