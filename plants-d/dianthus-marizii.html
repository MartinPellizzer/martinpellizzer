Dianthus marizii

Dianthus marizii, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Dianthus marizii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
