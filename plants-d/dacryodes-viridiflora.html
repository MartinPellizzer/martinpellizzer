Dacryodes viridiflora

Dacryodes viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are woody capsules containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its durable timber and has ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
