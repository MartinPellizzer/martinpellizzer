Dioscorea remotiflora var. palmeri

Dioscorea remotiflora var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's yam, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a climbing vine that belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae, characterized by its tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture. The tubers of this species are edible when properly prepared and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.

It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
