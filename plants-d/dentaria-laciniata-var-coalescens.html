Dentaria laciniata var. coalescens

Dentaria laciniata var. coalescens, commonly known as the narrowleaf toothplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are narrowly lance-shaped with toothed margins, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, clustered in loose racemes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along streambanks.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
