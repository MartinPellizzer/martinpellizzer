Dioscorea amazonum var. consanguinea

Dioscorea amazonum var. consanguinea, commonly known as the Amazon yam, is a climbing vine native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for food and traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its potential in modern pharmacology due to the presence of compounds with possible health benefits.
