Dahlia scapigeroides var. typica

Dahlia scapigeroides var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, single or double flowers in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant has upright, branching stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It blooms from late spring to early fall, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many modern dahlia hybrids.
