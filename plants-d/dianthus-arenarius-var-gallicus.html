Dianthus arenarius var. gallicus

Dianthus arenarius var. gallicus, commonly known as the French sandpink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to dry, coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sturdy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
