Draba cuneifolia var. californica

Draba cuneifolia var. californica, commonly known as the California rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and a flowering period in early spring.

Its adaptations to harsh environments include a compact growth form and the ability to survive in low-nutrient soils.
