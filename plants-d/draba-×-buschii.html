Draba × buschii

Draba × buschii, commonly known as the hybrid rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Draba × buschii is noted for its low-growing habit and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
