Drepananthus crassipetalus

Drepananthus crassipetalus, commonly known as the broad-petalled drepananthus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
