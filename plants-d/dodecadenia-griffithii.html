Dodecadenia griffithii

Dodecadenia griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Dodecadenia griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
