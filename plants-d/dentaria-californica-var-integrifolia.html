Dentaria californica var. integrifolia

Dentaria californica var. integrifolia, commonly known as the California toothplant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive leaf structure and geographic origin.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
