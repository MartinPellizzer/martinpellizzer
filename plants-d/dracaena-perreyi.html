Dracaena perreyi

Dracaena perreyi, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a thick, upright stem that can grow up to several meters in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Dracaena perreyi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
