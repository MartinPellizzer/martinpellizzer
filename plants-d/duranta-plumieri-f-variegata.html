Duranta plumieri f. variegata

Duranta plumieri f. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of purple to violet berries that appear in late summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a variegated pattern of green and white or yellow. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
