Devauxia urvillei

Devauxia urvillei, commonly known as the Chinese devil's ivy, is a climbing shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
