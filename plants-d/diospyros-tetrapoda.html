Diospyros tetrapoda

Diospyros tetrapoda, commonly known as the four-lobed persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lanceolate leaves with four lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red fruits, which are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit has a sweet, astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in carpentry and furniture making.
