Dracocephalum cochinchinense

Dracocephalum cochinchinense, commonly known as Vietnamese feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
