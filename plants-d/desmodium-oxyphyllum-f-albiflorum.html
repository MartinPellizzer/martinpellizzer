Desmodium oxyphyllum f. albiflorum

Desmodium oxyphyllum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a hairy stem and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
