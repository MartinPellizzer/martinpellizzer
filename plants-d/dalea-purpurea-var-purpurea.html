Dalea purpurea var. purpurea

Dalea purpurea var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple prairie clover, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and its clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in prairie and dryland ecosystems.

Dalea purpurea var. purpurea is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in native plant landscaping.
