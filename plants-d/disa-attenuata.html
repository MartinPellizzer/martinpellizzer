Disa attenuata

Disa attenuata, commonly known as the slender disa, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in areas with moderate to high rainfall.

Disa attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
