Dichanthelium acuminatum var. densiflorum

Dichanthelium acuminatum var. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
