Dactylorchis maculata var. vulgaris

Dactylorchis maculata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a sympodial growth habit. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with numerous leaves and produces showy, violet or purple flowers with a distinct yellow spot. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf arrangement and ornamental value in native gardens.
