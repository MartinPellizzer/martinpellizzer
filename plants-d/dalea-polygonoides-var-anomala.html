Dalea polygonoides var. anomala

Dalea polygonoides var. anomala, commonly known as the Texas milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. It is adapted to arid conditions and has deep root systems to access moisture.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
