Darwinia grandiflora

Darwinia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble spikes of pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It produces small, edible fruits that are a food source for various native animals.

Darwinia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
