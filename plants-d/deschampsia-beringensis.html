Deschampsia beringensis

Deschampsia beringensis, commonly known as Bering's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and slender, tufted growth habit, which allows it to thrive in cold, harsh environments. This plant is often found in tundra and alpine habitats, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for herbivores. Deschampsia beringensis is adapted to low temperatures and short growing seasons, with its growth cycle synchronized to the brief summer period.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to persist in extreme conditions, contributing to the biodiversity of polar and high-altitude ecosystems.
