Daphne longilobata subsp. purpurascens

Daphne longilobata subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage that contrasts with its clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
