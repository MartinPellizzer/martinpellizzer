Dioscorea convolvulacea subsp. esurientium

Dioscorea convolvulacea subsp.

esurientium, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its tubers are used as a food source and have been traditionally utilized in various medicinal applications.

The species is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates.
