Dubautia laxa subsp. hirsuta

Dubautia laxa subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy Dubautia, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and along stream banks. It has adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii with a unique morphology suited for its environment. Dubautia laxa subsp.

hirsuta plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for native pollinators.
