Dyckia ferox

Dyckia ferox, commonly known as the fierce dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, which serves as a natural deterrent to herbivores. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Dyckia ferox is adapted to grow in dry, sunny environments, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular ornamental plant among bromeliad enthusiasts.
