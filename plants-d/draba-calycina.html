Draba calycina

Draba calycina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps and other mountainous regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cold, sunny environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba calycina produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, contributing to its propagation in alpine ecosystems.
