Desmodium amans

Desmodium amans, commonly known as purple desmodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind or animal contact.

Desmodium amans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
