Dienia calycina

Dienia calycina, commonly known as the yellow bell or golden bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Dienia calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
