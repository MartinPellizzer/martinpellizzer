Diplachne carinata

Diplachne carinata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Diplachne carinata is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields, where it can become invasive.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it a significant weed in many regions.
