Diospyros acapulcensis subsp. nicaraguensis

Diospyros acapulcensis subsp.

nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan persimmon, is a species native to Central America. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees.

The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
