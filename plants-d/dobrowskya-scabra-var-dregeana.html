Dobrowskya scabra var. dregeana

Dobrowskya scabra var. dregeana, commonly known as the roughleaf dobrowskya, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
