Draba incana var. arabisans

Draba incana var. arabisans, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a low, compact growth habit with hairy stems and basal leaves.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early summer.
