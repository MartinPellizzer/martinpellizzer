Diuris behrii subsp. multilineata

Diuris behrii subsp.

multilineata, commonly known as the multilined spider-orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Australia. It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance with a slender, curving flower spike and a single, ornate flower.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns, and it blooms in late summer to early autumn.

This subspecies is notable for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
