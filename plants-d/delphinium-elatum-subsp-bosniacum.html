Delphinium elatum subsp. bosniacum

Delphinium elatum subsp.

bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling as all parts are toxic.
