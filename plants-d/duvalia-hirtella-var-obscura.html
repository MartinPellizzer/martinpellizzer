Duvalia hirtella var. obscura

Duvalia hirtella var. obscura, commonly known as the dark hairy duvalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a bluish-green hue with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils in woodland settings.
