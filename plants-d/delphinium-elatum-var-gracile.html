Delphinium elatum var. gracile

Delphinium elatum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stem, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Delphinium elatum var. gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
