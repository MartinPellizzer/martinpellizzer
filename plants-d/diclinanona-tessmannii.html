Diclinanona tessmannii

Diclinanona tessmannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Diclinanona tessmannii is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
