Dianthus siculus var. lanceolatus

Dianthus siculus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet William, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers with frilled petals. The leaves are lance-shaped, slender, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
