Dacrycarpus imbricatus var. patulus

Dacrycarpus imbricatus var. patulus, commonly known as the coastal rimu, is a variant of the rimu tree native to New Zealand.

It is a coniferous evergreen with tall, straight trunks and a conical crown. The leaves are needle-like, dark green, and arranged in a dense, overlapping pattern. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

This variant is valued for its timber and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
