Digitalis purpurea var. valida

Digitalis purpurea var. valida, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a biennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a loose raceme, and its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
