Dioscorea fandra

Dioscorea fandra, commonly known as the Madagascar yam, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local diets and traditional medicine.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and requires careful preparation before consumption.
