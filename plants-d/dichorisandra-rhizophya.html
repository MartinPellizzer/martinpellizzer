Dichorisandra rhizophya

Dichorisandra rhizophya, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, deep red petals and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Dichorisandra rhizophya is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
