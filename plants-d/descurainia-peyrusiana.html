Descurainia peyrusiana

Descurainia peyrusiana, commonly known as the desert mustangweed, is an annual flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces slender, hairy stems and has a distinctive seed pod that splits open when mature.

Due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
