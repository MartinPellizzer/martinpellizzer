Dianthus × nigritus

Dianthus × nigritus, commonly known as the dark red campion, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark red to almost black flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Dianthus × nigritus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
