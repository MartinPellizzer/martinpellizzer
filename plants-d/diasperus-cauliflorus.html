Diasperus cauliflorus

Diasperus cauliflorus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom from the base of the stems, a feature known as cauliflorous growth. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, while its flowers are often fragrant.

Diasperus cauliflorus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
