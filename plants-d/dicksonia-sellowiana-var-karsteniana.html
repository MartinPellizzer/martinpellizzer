Dicksonia sellowiana var. karsteniana

Dicksonia sellowiana var. karsteniana, commonly known as the Karsten's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing cones are located at the base of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, temperate climates.
