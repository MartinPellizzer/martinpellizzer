Diplostephium stuebelii

Diplostephium stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
