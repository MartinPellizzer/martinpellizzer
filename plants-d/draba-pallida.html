Draba pallida

Draba pallida, commonly known as the pale wintercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, blooming in early spring.

Draba pallida is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
