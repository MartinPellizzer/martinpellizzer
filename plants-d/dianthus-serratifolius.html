Dianthus serratifolius

Dianthus serratifolius, commonly known as the serrated leaf carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
