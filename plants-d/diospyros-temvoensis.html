Diospyros temvoensis

Diospyros temvoensis, commonly known as the Temvo ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony trees known for their dense, dark wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that are initially green and turn red when mature.

The species is valued for its hard, dark timber, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
