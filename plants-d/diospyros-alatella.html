Diospyros alatella

Diospyros alatella, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange to reddish berries that ripen in the fall.

Diospyros alatella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various cultures for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
