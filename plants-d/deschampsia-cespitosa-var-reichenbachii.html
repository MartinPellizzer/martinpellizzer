Deschampsia cespitosa var. reichenbachii

Deschampsia cespitosa var. reichenbachii, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and dense, clumping growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
