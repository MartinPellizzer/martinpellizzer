Dentaria cathayensis

Dentaria cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese toothplant, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Dentaria cathayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
