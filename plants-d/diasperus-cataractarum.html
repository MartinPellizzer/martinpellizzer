Diasperus cataractarum

Diasperus cataractarum, commonly known as the waterfall plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, cascading inflorescences that resemble waterfalls, hence its common name. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Diasperus cataractarum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing near water sources.
