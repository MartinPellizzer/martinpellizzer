Dianthus × lucae var. novakii

Dianthus × lucae var. novakii, commonly known as the Novak's campion, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, rounded clusters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for alpine gardens.
