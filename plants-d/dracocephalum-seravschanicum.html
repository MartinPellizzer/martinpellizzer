Dracocephalum seravschanicum

Dracocephalum seravschanicum, commonly known as the Persian bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dracocephalum seravschanicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
