Diplocaulobium carinulatidiscum

Diplocaulobium carinulatidiscum, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curved. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne in dense clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it plays an important role in maintaining the biodiversity of its wetland environment.
