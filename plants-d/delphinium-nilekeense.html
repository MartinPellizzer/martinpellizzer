Delphinium nilekeense

Delphinium nilekeense, commonly known as the Nilke's larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
