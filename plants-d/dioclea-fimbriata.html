Dioclea fimbriata

Dioclea fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed spider plant, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Dioclea fimbriata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
