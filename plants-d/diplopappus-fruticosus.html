Diplopappus fruticosus

Diplopappus fruticosus, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dune environments.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it an important part of the local flora.
