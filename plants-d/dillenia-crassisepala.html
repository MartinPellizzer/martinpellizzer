Dillenia crassisepala

Dillenia crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled dillenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five broad, fleshy petals that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are large, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Dillenia crassisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
