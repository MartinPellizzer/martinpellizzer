Davallia impressa

Davallia impressa, commonly known as the fern moss or the button fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant features delicate, leathery fronds with a serrated margin, and its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the leaves. It is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Davallia impressa is also valued for its ability to purify air and its adaptability to various soil types.
