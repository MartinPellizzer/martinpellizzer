Dioscorea bonatiana

Dioscorea bonatiana, commonly known as the Bonati yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and tend to climb using adventitious roots.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and traditional uses in various African cultures.
