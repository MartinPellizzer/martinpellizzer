Dianthus attenuatus f. sabuletorum

Dianthus attenuatus f. sabuletorum, commonly known as the sand dianthus, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and showy pink or red flowers.

It is a naturally occurring form of Dianthus attenuatus, typically found in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and dunes. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
