Dactylis glomerata f. pendula

Dactylis glomerata f. pendula, commonly known as the hanging cock's-foot, is a variant of the common grass known as sweet vernal grass.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping inflorescence that gives it a cascading appearance. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
