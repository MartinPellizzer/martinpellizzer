Dioscorea sansibarensis

Dioscorea sansibarensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar yam, is a climbing vine native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its economic importance, it is cultivated for both food and traditional medicinal purposes.
