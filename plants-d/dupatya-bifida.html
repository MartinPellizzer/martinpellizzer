Dupatya bifida

Dupatya bifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed piper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species of climbing and shrubby plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided at the base. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are berries that contain several seeds.

Dupatya bifida is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
