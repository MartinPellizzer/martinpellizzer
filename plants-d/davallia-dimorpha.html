Davallia dimorpha

Davallia dimorpha, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to various parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly curled appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread and form dense mats. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds, and the plant reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Davallia dimorpha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
