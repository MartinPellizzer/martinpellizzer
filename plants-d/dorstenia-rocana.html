Dorstenia rocana

Dorstenia rocana, commonly known as the rock dodder, is a unique member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on other plants for water and nutrients. The plant is characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems that often appear in clusters. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white in color.
