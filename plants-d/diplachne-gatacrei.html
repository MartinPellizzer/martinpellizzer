Diplachne gatacrei

Diplachne gatacrei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Diplachne gatacrei has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It is often found in association with other wetland flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
