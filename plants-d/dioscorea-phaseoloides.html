Dioscorea phaseoloides

Dioscorea phaseoloides, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
