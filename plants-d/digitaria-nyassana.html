Digitaria nyassana

Digitaria nyassana, commonly known as African grass or nyassana grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Digitaria nyassana is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
