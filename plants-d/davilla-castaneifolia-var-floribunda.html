Davilla castaneifolia var. floribunda

Davilla castaneifolia var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a reddish or bronze tinge when young. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by elongated, woody fruits that split open to release seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
