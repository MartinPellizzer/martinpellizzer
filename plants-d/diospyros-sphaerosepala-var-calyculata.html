Diospyros sphaerosepala var. calyculata

Diospyros sphaerosepala var. calyculata, commonly known as the calyx-fruited persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, and the fruit is a small, round, fleshy berry that turns orange-red when mature. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
