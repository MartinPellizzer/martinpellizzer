Dalea crassifolia

Dalea crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf milkvetch, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Dalea crassifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
