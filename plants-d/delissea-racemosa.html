Delissea racemosa

Delissea racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped, typically arranged oppositely along the stem, and are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in arid environments.
