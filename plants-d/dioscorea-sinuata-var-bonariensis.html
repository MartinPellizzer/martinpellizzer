Dioscorea sinuata var. bonariensis

Dioscorea sinuata var. bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian yam, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often harvested for their edible properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
