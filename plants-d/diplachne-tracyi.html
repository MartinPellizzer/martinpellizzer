Diplachne tracyi

Diplachne tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Diplachne tracyi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
