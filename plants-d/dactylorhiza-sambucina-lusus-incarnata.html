Dactylorhiza sambucina lusus incarnata

Dactylorhiza sambucina lusus incarnata, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It has a complex root system that helps it thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The orchid is known for its striking appearance and is often found in habitats with high humidity and partial shade.
