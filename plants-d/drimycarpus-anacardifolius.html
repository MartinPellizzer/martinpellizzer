Drimycarpus anacardifolius

Drimycarpus anacardifolius, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves and stems contain toxic compounds, including cardenolides, which can be harmful to animals and humans.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland savanna regions.
