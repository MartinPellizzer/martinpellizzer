Disperis leuconeura

Disperis leuconeura, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Disperis leuconeura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
