Dianthus carthusianorum var. uniflorus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flowered carnation, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, bushy growth with dense foliage of dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces solitary, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its flowers have a distinctive, slightly fragrant scent and are often used in ornamental gardening.

This variety is prized for its resilience and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
