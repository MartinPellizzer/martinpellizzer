Ditassa hastata

Ditassa hastata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with prominent spurred petals. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
