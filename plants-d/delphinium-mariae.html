Delphinium mariae

Delphinium mariae, commonly known as the Mary's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are tubular with spurred petals, a distinctive feature of the delphinium genus.

Delphinium mariae is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
