Diospyros sintenisii

Diospyros sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its fruit, which is consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.
