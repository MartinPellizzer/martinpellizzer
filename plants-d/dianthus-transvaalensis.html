Dianthus transvaalensis

Dianthus transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal carnation, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and has linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus transvaalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
