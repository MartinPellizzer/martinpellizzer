Dendrobium danipense

Dendrobium danipense, commonly known as the Danipense orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining substrates.

Dendrobium danipense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
