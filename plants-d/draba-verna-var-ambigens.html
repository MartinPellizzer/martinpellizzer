Draba verna var. ambigens

Draba verna var. ambigens, commonly known as the spring whitlow, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

Draba verna var. ambigens is often found in meadows, slopes, and cliffs, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
