Draba australis var. uruguayana

Draba australis var. uruguayana, commonly known as the Uruguay draba, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern South America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with fine, silky hairs.

It is well adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
