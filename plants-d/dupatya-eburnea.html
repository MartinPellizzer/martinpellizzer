Dupatya eburnea

Dupatya eburnea, commonly known as the white-wood tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height. The plant features smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in panicles, and it produces woody capsules as its fruit.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
