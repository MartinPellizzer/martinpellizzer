Dendrophthora hylaeana

Dendrophthora hylaeana, commonly known as the Hylaean mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It attaches itself to the branches of host trees, such as those in the family Fabaceae, using a specialized structure called a haustellum to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are dispersed by birds and other animals. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Dendrophthora hylaeana plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for various bird species and contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
