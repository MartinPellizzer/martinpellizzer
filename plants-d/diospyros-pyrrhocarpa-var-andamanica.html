Diospyros pyrrhocarpa var. andamanica

Diospyros pyrrhocarpa var. andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman persimmon, is a species native to the Andaman Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that is edible when fully ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
