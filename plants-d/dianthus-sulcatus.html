Dianthus sulcatus

Dianthus sulcatus, commonly known as the sand dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces vibrant pink or red flowers with a distinctive fringed appearance. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to coastal and arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it is typically found in open habitats such as dunes and rocky slopes.

Dianthus sulcatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
