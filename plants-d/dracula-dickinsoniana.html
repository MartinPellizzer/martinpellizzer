Dracula dickinsoniana

Dracula dickinsoniana, commonly known as the Dickinson's vampire orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety sepals and petals that resemble the wings of a bat, giving it its dramatic common name. The plant produces fragrant, red-orange flowers that bloom during the rainy season, adding a unique visual element to its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and good drainage for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts who appreciate its botanical and aesthetic appeal.
