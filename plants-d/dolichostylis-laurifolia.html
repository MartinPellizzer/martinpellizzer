Dolichostylis laurifolia

Dolichostylis laurifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed dolichostylis, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
