Drypetes convoluta

Drypetes convoluta, commonly known as the twisted drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine.
