Dimorphocalyx malayanus

Dimorphocalyx malayanus, commonly known as the Malayan velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Dimorphocalyx malayanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
