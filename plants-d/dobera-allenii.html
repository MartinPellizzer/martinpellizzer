Dobera allenii

Dobera allenii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Dobera allenii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
