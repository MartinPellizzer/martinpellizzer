Diospyros crassinervis

Diospyros crassinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local medicine.
