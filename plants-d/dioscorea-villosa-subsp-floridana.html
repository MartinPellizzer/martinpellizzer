Dioscorea villosa subsp. floridana

Dioscorea villosa subsp.

floridana, commonly known as the Florida yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers, which are the primary botanical feature, are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits compared to other Dioscorea species.
