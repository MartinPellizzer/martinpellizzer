Dianthus pyrenaicus subsp. catalaunicus

Dianthus pyrenaicus subsp.

catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalan campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
