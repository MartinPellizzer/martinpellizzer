Diplostephium floribundum

Diplostephium floribundum, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically hairy and have a serrated edge. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in spring and are followed by distinctive pappus-equipped seeds that aid in wind dispersal.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
