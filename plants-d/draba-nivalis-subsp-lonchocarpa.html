Draba nivalis subsp. lonchocarpa

Draba nivalis subsp.

lonchocarpa, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a small flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at elevations above 2,000 meters.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and Asia.
