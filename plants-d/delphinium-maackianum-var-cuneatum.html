Delphinium maackianum var. cuneatum

Delphinium maackianum var. cuneatum, commonly known as the Maack's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in tall, spire-like inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
