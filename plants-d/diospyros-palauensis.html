Diospyros palauensis

Diospyros palauensis, commonly known as the Palau persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits.

The fruit is typically eaten raw or used in traditional remedies, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitat.
