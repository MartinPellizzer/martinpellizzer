Dioscorea biloba subsp. coquimbana

Dioscorea biloba subsp.

coquimbana, commonly known as the Coquimbanan yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to South America, particularly Chile. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers, which are the edible part, are typically yellowish and have a starchy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary use and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
