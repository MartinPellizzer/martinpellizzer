Dendrocalamus latifolius

Dendrocalamus latifolius, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus latifolius is valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction, papermaking, and as a source of bamboo shoots.
