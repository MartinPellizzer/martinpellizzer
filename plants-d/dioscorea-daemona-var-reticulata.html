Dioscorea daemona var. reticulata

Dioscorea daemona var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its complex, reticulated patterns on its tuberous roots. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and bears small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The botanical features of this species make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
