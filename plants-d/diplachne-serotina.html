Diplachne serotina

Diplachne serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Diplachne serotina is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and pasturelands.
