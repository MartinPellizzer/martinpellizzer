Diaperia candida

Diaperia candida, commonly known as white diaper grass, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense tufts.

Diaperia candida is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is known for its delicate, ephemeral bloom.
