Dianthus fimbriatus

Dianthus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fringed petals that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer.

Dianthus fimbriatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
