Dryopteris decussata var. brasiliensis

Dryopteris decussata var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian shield fern, is a variant of the shield fern species native to South America.

It is a medium-sized evergreen fern characterized by its leathery, dark green, shield-shaped fronds. The fronds are typically divided into segments that are arranged in a distinctive, overlapping pattern. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
