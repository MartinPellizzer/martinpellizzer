Diospyros uaupensis

Diospyros uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its fruit is a small, orange to red drupe, often with a sweet, edible pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
