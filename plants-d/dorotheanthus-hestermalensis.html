Dorotheanthus hestermalensis

Dorotheanthus hestermalensis, commonly known as the Hestermalensis daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow central disk, which bloom in the spring and summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long-lasting blooms.
