Dipsacus auriculatus

Dipsacus auriculatus, commonly known as the velvet tare, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of spiny, velvety bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Dipsacus auriculatus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and woodland edges.
