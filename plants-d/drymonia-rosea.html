Drymonia rosea

Drymonia rosea, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink and white flowers, which resemble a slipper in shape and color. The plant has a unique growth habit, often growing as a terrestrial orchid with a single, fleshy stem. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species in many areas.
