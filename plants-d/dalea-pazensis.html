Dalea pazensis

Dalea pazensis, commonly known as the Mexican prairie clover, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Dalea pazensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional medicine.
