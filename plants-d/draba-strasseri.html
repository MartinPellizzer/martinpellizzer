Draba strasseri

Draba strasseri, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high elevations. Draba strasseri has small, oval leaves and produces bright yellow or white flowers that bloom in early summer.

It is an important component of alpine ecosystems and is often found in meadows and slopes above the tree line.
