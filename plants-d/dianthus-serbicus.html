Dianthus serbicus

Dianthus serbicus, commonly known as the Serbian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers with frilled petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branched.

Dianthus serbicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
