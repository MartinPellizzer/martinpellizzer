Dianthus caryophyllus var. garganicus

Dianthus caryophyllus var. garganicus, commonly known as the Garganic carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bicolor flowers that typically feature red and white petals. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its strong, spicy fragrance. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
