Dioscorea testudinaria

Dioscorea testudinaria, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some traditional practices.
