Diplachne serotina var. clandestina

Diplachne serotina var. clandestina, commonly known as the secret grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its discreet appearance and limited distribution, it is a relatively obscure yet ecologically significant species.
