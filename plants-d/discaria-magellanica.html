Discaria magellanica

Discaria magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic burr.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and rough, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
