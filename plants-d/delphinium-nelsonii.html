Delphinium nelsonii

Delphinium nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium nelsonii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
