Dendrobium × primulardii

Dendrobium × primulardii, commonly known as the orchid with a unique hybrid origin, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in seasonal climates. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often arranged in racemes. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its hybrid nature results from crosses between Dendrobium nobile and Dendrobium phalaenopsis, contributing to its adaptability and ornamental value.
