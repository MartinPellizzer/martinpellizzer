Delphinium lagarocentrum

Delphinium lagarocentrum, commonly known as the Cape Delphinium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves, and it blooms in late summer to early autumn.

Delphinium lagarocentrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
