Dictamnus albus var. angustifolius

Dictamnus albus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved white dictamnus, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly toxic scent and distinctive, pale pink to white flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
