Dendrolobium umbellatum

Dendrolobium umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that resemble an umbrella, hence its common name. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Dendrolobium umbellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
