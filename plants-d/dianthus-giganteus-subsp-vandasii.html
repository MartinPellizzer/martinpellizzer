Dianthus giganteus subsp. vandasii

Dianthus giganteus subsp.

vandasii, commonly known as the giant carnation, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant features upright, branched stems covered with soft, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical appeal lies in its vibrant blooms and long-lasting flowers, which make it a popular choice for gardeners.
