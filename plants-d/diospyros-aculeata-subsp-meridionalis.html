Diospyros aculeata subsp. meridionalis

Diospyros aculeata subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern Chinese date plum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy drupes. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
