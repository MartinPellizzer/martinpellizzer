Dissotis sizenandoi

Dissotis sizenandoi, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which resemble spiders due to their elongated, slender petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its botanical features include long, wiry stems and dense clusters of fragrant, showy blooms.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
