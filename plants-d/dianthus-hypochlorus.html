Dianthus hypochlorus

Dianthus hypochlorus, commonly known as the pale carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Dianthus hypochlorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
