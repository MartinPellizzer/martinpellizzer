Dendrophorbium biserrifolium

Dendrophorbium biserrifolium, commonly known as the serrated leaf forest fern, is a unique plant native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and serrated, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on the trunks of old-growth trees. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense mats that contribute to the forest floor's ecosystem.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
