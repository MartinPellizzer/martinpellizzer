Daubenya marginata

Daubenya marginata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking spikes of pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
