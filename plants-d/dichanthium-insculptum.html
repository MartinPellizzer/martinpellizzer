Dichanthium insculptum

Dichanthium insculptum, commonly known as the sculpted grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive, finely serrated leaf margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
