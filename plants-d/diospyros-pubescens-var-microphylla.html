Diospyros pubescens var. microphylla

Diospyros pubescens var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically smaller than those of the species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
