Dicranopygium umbrophilum

Dicranopygium umbrophilum, commonly known as the black moss, is a species of moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Dicranaceae and is characterized by its dark green, dense growth habit. The plant has a complex structure with elongated, branching stems and leaf-like structures that are often arranged in a spiral. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized capsules that develop on the stems.

Dicranopygium umbrophilum plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
