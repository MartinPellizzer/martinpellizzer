Draba longirostra var. eriocarpa

Draba longirostra var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy long-beaked whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal and linear, while the stem is often hairy, giving it the common name "hairy long-beaked whitlow grass.".

The variety eriocarpa distinguishes it by its dense, hairy covering on the stem and fruiting structures.
