Dichaea brachyphylla

Dichaea brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features compact, often clustered growth with short, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its humid habitat. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with a distinctive, intricate structure typical of the orchid family.

Dichaea brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific care requirements.
