Dillenia reticulata

Dillenia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined dillenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a woody, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Dillenia reticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
