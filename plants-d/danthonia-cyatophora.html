Danthonia cyatophora

Danthonia cyatophora, commonly known as the slender danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Danthonia cyatophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid regions.
