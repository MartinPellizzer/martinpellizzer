Diospyros frutescens

Diospyros frutescens, commonly known as the little persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 3 meters and features elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are popular in local cuisines.

The wood of Diospyros frutescens is also valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and furniture.
