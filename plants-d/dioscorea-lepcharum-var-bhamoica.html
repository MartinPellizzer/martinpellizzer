Dioscorea lepcharum var. bhamoica

Dioscorea lepcharum var. bhamoica, commonly known as Bhamo yam, is a variant of the yam family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source in local cuisines. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued not only for its edible tubers but also for its potential medicinal properties.
