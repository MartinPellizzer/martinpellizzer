Digitaria sanguinalis f. extensa

Digitaria sanguinalis f. extensa, commonly known as the broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. Its flowering spikes are slender and spike-like, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
