Dinochloa barbata

Dinochloa barbata, commonly known as the hairy grass or barbed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that have bristle-like structures, giving it its common name. Dinochloa barbata is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its structure and growth pattern make it an important component of certain ecosystems, particularly in sandy or rocky soils.
