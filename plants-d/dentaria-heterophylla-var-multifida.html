Dentaria heterophylla var. multifida

Dentaria heterophylla var. multifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toothplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed or divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
