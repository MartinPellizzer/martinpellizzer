Dioscorea acarophyta

Dioscorea acarophyta, commonly known as the slender yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers, which are the primary economic product, are used as a starchy food source in various cultures.

The leaves are heart-shaped, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
