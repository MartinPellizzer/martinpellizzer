Disanthus ovatifolius

Disanthus ovatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed disanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its ovate, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
