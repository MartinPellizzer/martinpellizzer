Dendropemon laxiflorum

Dendropemon laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
