Danthonia spicata var. longipila

Danthonia spicata var. longipila, commonly known as the long-haired danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
