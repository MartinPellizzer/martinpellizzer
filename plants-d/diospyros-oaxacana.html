Diospyros oaxacana

Diospyros oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Mexico, particularly in the states of Oaxaca and Chiapas, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits. The fruit is typically orange to reddish in color, with a sweet and sometimes astringent flavor.

It is valued not only for its fruit but also for its potential medicinal properties.
