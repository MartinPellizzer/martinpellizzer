Ditaxis acaulis

Ditaxis acaulis, commonly known as the spiny dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in shrublands and grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
