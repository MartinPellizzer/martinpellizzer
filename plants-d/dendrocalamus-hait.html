Dendrocalamus hait

Dendrocalamus hait, commonly known as the Haitian bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, fast-growing plant that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional construction. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its structural strength.

Dendrocalamus hait plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
