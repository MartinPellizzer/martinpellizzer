Dichapetalum acuminatum var. laurentii

Dichapetalum acuminatum var. laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian holly, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to provide winter interest in gardens.
