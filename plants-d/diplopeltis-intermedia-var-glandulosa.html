Diplopeltis intermedia var. glandulosa

Diplopeltis intermedia var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves that are often speckled with small, glandular dots. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are highly fragrant. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-draining soil with moderate watering.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
