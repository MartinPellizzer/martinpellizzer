Didymocarpus siamensis

Didymocarpus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The tree produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.

Didymocarpus siamensis is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a source of natural dyes.
