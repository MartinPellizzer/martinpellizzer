Dioscorea carpomaculata var. carpomaculata

Dioscorea carpomaculata var. carpomaculata, commonly known as the spotted yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, tuberous roots that are often marked with dark spots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
