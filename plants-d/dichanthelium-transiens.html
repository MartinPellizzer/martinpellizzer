Dichanthelium transiens

Dichanthelium transiens, commonly known as the transient panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous and wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and prairies.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique morphological features and ecological adaptability.
