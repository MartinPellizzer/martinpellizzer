Dryopteris cochaensis

Dryopteris cochaensis, commonly known as the Cochaens fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
