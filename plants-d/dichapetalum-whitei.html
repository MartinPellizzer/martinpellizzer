Dichapetalum whitei

Dichapetalum whitei, commonly known as the white dichapetalum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
