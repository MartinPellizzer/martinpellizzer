Diospyros balfouriana

Diospyros balfouriana, commonly known as the Balfour's persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China, India, and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that ripen in the autumn.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
