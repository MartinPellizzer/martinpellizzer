Diplotaxis assurgens subsp. tetragona

Diplotaxis assurgens subsp.

tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is characterized by its upright stems with four angular ridges, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late summer.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
