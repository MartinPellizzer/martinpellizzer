Dombeya umbellata var. amplifolia

Dombeya umbellata var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, umbellate inflorescences that consist of numerous white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
