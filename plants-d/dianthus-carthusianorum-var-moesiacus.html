Dianthus carthusianorum var. moesiacus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. moesiacus, commonly known as the Moesia carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and displays clusters of pink to red flowers with a distinctive white or pale pink center. The leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and the stems are sturdy and often branched. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
