Dracaena ferrea

Dracaena ferrea, commonly known as the ironwood plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red or orange berries. Dracaena ferrea is well adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
