Diospyros reticulinervis

Diospyros reticulinervis, commonly known as the net-veined persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive network of veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange to reddish berries. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it often has a strong, aromatic odor.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical regions.
