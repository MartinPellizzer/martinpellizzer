Diospyros trianthos

Diospyros trianthos, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically edible, though some varieties may have astringent properties when unripe.

Diospyros trianthos is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
