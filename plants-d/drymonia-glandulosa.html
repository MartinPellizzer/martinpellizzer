Drymonia glandulosa

Drymonia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular drymonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in small, glandular hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy appearance, with a distinct waxy coating.

Drymonia glandulosa produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the spring and early summer.
