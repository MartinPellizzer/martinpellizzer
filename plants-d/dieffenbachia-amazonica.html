Dieffenbachia amazonica

Dieffenbachia amazonica, commonly known as the Snowflake Dieffenbachia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, arrow-shaped leaves with a white, waxy texture and prominent veins. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Dieffenbachia amazonica is also noted for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation and should be handled with care.
