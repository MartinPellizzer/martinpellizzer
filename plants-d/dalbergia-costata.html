Dalbergia costata

Dalbergia costata, commonly known as the Chinese rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a distinctive reddish-brown hue and a fine, even grain. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip, and they have a slightly serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its high-quality timber, which is used in traditional furniture and musical instruments.
