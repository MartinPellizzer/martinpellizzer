Dalea versicolor var. involuta

Dalea versicolor var. involuta, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated flowers that range from white to pink and red, depending on the age of the bloom. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
