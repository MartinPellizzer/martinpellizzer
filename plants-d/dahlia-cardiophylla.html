Dahlia cardiophylla

Dahlia cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are a distinguishing feature. Its flowers are borne on sturdy stems and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

Dahlia cardiophylla prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
