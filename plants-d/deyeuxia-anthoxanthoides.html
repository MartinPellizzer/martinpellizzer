Deyeuxia anthoxanthoides

Deyeuxia anthoxanthoides, commonly known as the yellow false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, yellowish to brownish flowers.

Deyeuxia anthoxanthoides is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
