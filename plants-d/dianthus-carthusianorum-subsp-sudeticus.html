Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. sudeticus

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

sudeticus, commonly known as the Sudetic carnation, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are sturdy and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
