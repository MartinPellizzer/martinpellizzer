Dioscorea multispicata

Dioscorea multispicata, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, tendrilled stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also edible and have been utilized as a food source in some regions.

The botanical features of D. multispicata make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
