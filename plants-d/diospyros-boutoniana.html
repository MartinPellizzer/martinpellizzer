Diospyros boutoniana

Diospyros boutoniana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The tree is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are either male or female. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, often with a sweet, edible pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a food source.
