Daphne pontica subsp. haematocarpa

Daphne pontica subsp.

haematocarpa, commonly known as the bloodroot daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens with caution.
