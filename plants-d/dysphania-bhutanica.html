Dysphania bhutanica

Dysphania bhutanica, commonly known as Bhutan leaf or Bhutan spinach, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region, including Bhutan.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Dysphania bhutanica is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along stream banks.

It is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
