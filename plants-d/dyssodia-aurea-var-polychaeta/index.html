Dyssodia Aurea Var. Polychaeta

Golden dyssodia, scientifically known as Dyssodia aurea var. polychaeta, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. Its name derives from the golden hues of its blooms, which stand out against its silvery-green foliage. This species is particularly noted for its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it has captured the interest of botanists and gardeners alike.
