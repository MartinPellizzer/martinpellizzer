Dysoxylum grandifolium

Dysoxylum grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests.

The species is valued for its hard timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
