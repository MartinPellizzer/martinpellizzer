Dioscorea knuthiana

Dioscorea knuthiana, commonly known as the Knuth yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

The tubers are valued for their nutritional content and have been traditionally used in various cultural practices.
