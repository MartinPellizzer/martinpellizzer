Dichanthelium dichotomum var. breve

Dichanthelium dichotomum var. breve, commonly known as the short-branched dichanthelium, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and compact inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color.

The inflorescence consists of two to four spikelets, which are often arranged in a loose, open cluster.
