Dianthus moesiacus var. skobelevii

Dianthus moesiacus var. skobelevii, commonly known as the Skobelev's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often arranged in a cluster at the ends of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
