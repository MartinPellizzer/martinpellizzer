Draba sekiyana

Draba sekiyana, commonly known as the Japanese rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, where it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant is characterized by its small, basal rosette of leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. Draba sekiyana is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems in its native habitat.
