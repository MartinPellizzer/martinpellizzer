Didactyle tripetala

Didactyle tripetala, commonly known as the three-petaled buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with three petals and numerous sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, which is a distinguishing feature.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in early spring.
