Dalbergia boivinii

Dalbergia boivinii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and wood quality, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
