Dahlia pteropoda

Dahlia pteropoda, commonly known as the fringed dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers with fringed petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sturdy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and white, and bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Dahlia pteropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
