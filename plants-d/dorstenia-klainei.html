Dorstenia klainei

Dorstenia klainei, commonly known as the "Klaine's velvetleaf," is a member of the Moraceae family, native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, often hairy foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Dorstenia klainei is known for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
