Derris guilleminiana var. pubigera

Derris guilleminiana var. pubigera, commonly known as the pubescent derris, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and contain toxic alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local medicine.
