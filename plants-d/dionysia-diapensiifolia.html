Dionysia diapensiifolia

Dionysia diapensiifolia, commonly known as the creeping dionysia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

Dionysia diapensiifolia is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
