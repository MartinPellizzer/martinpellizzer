Delphinium grandiflorum var. deinocarpum

Delphinium grandiflorum var. deinocarpum, commonly known as the Colorado larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of spurred blue to purple flowers. The leaves are palmately compound, with elongated, serrated leaflets that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant is notable for its showy blooms and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
