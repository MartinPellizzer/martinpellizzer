Dianthus superbus var. pycnophyllus

Dianthus superbus var. pycnophyllus, commonly known as the dense-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and attractive foliage.
