Disa × langleyensis

Disa × langleyensis, commonly known as the Langley's disa, is a hybrid orchid native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high light exposure. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with each flower having three sepals and three petals.

Disa × langleyensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
