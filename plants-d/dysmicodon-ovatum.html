Dysmicodon ovatum

Dysmicodon ovatum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Dysmicodon ovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
