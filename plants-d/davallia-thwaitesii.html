Davallia thwaitesii

Davallia thwaitesii, commonly known as the silver-veined fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often adorned with silvery veins. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, bright green leaves.

Davallia thwaitesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
