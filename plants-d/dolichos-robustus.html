Dolichos robustus

Dolichos robustus, commonly known as the horse gram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, green, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has compound flowers that are typically pink or violet in color.

Dolichos robustus is valued for its nutritional content and is widely cultivated for both food and fodder.
