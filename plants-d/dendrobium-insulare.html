Dendrobium insulare

Dendrobium insulare, commonly known as the island orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium insulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
