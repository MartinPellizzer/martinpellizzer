Davilla lechleri

Davilla lechleri, commonly known as the rubber tree or copal tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant is notable for its latex-producing properties, which has been historically used by indigenous peoples for various purposes. Its bark contains compounds such as lapacho, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
