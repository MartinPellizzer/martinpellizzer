Daphne gnidium subsp. maritima

Daphne gnidium subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the coastal daphne, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found along coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
