Dichapetalum paniculatum

Dichapetalum paniculatum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that appear in panicles during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
