Dimocarpus lichi

Dimocarpus lichi, commonly known as the longan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, thin skin that turns from green to reddish when ripe. Inside, it contains a single, smooth, whitish seed surrounded by a sweet, juicy pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a distinct, delicate flavor.
