Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. maurusia

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

maurusia, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and pale pink to white flowers. It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually short-lived, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ecological significance and is often found in calcareous soils.
