Dracocephalum longipedicellatum

Dracocephalum longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-bracted dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that are clustered in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Dracocephalum longipedicellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional herbal medicine.
