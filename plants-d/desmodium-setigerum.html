Desmodium setigerum

Desmodium setigerum, commonly known as hairy burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow to orange flowers. Its fruit is a hairy, three-lobed pod that contains several small, rounded seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
