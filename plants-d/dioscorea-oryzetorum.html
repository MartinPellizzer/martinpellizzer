Dioscorea oryzetorum

Dioscorea oryzetorum, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and cylindrical, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots contain compounds such as diosgenin, which is chemically similar to steroid hormones.

Dioscorea oryzetorum is also an important source of plant-based materials for the pharmaceutical industry.
