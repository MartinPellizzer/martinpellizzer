Dryopteris devexa

Dryopteris devexa, commonly known as the zigzag wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

Dryopteris devexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
