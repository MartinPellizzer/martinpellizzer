Dioscorea polystachya

Dioscorea polystachya, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a food source. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Dioscorea polystachya is valued not only for its edible tubers but also for its traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
