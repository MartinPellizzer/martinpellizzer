Didymocheton roseus

Didymocheton roseus, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Didymocheton roseus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
