Dioscorea seniavinii

Dioscorea seniavinii, commonly known as the Seniavin's yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential economic importance in tropical regions.
