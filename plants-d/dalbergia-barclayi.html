Dalbergia barclayi

Dalbergia barclayi, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
