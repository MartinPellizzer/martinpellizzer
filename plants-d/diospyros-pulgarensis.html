Diospyros pulgarensis

Diospyros pulgarensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe and is known for its sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
