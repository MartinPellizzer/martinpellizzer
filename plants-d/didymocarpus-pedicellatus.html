Didymocarpus pedicellatus

Didymocarpus pedicellatus, commonly known as the clustered mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, purple-hued berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
