Deschampsia cespitosa var. breviaristata

Deschampsia cespitosa var. breviaristata, commonly known as the short-awned bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features fine, tufted blades with a bluish-green color and short, narrow leaves. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-bearing branches. It thrives in moist to mesic environments, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
