Dysphania pusilla

Dysphania pusilla, commonly known as dwarf lamb's quarters, is a small annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It grows up to 30 cm tall, with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by kidney-shaped seeds. Dysphania pusilla thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
