Desmanthus painteri

Desmanthus painteri, commonly known as the painted desmanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and purple. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
