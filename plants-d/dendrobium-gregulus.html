Dendrobium gregulus

Dendrobium gregulus, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, often branched stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Dendrobium gregulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
