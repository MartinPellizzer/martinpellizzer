Dendrocalamus hamiltonii

Dendrocalamus hamiltonii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, woody perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its hollow, segmented stems that can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has a robust root system and produces long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is valued for its strong, lightweight culms used in construction and furniture.

Dendrocalamus hamiltonii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
