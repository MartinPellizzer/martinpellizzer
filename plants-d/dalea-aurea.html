Dalea aurea

Dalea aurea, commonly known as golden prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. It has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea aurea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
