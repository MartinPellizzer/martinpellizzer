Dysoxylum macrocarpum var. sumatranum

Dysoxylum macrocarpum var. sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.

The plant is also valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
