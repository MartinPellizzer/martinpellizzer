Diplopappus spinulosus

Diplopappus spinulosus, commonly known as the spiny pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, spiny leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its unique structure includes a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in seed dispersal by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
