Davilla sellowiana

Davilla sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth, glossy surface.
