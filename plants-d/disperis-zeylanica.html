Disperis zeylanica

Disperis zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon orchid, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky or soil substrates.

The orchid's flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.
