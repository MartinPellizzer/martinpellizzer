Diospyros juppii

Diospyros juppii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe.

The fruit has a sweet, astringent flavor and is often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.
