Dianthus rigidulus

Dianthus rigidulus, commonly known as the hardy pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often used in garden settings for their ornamental value. Dianthus rigidulus is a hardy perennial that prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features include a short, sturdy stem and a long flowering period, contributing to its popularity among horticultural enthusiasts.
