Diospyros retrofracta

Diospyros retrofracta, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits. Its fruits are typically harvested when fully ripe, and the tree is valued for both its fruit and its timber.

Diospyros retrofracta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
