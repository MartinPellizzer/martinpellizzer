Dianthus fastigiatus

Dianthus fastigiatus, commonly known as the upright carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers atop sturdy stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus fastigiatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
