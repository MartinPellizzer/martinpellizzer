Descantaria laxiflora

Descantaria laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shade gardens.
