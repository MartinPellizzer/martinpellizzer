Dioclea lasiocarpa

Dioclea lasiocarpa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing edible seeds. The seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
