Diaphananthe pulchella var. geniculata

Diaphananthe pulchella var. geniculata, commonly known as the creeping marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread across moist soils and form dense mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tendency to curl at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
