Dianella caerulea var. aquilonia

Dianella caerulea var. aquilonia, commonly known as the Blue Flax Lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are a deep blue-green in color, providing a unique visual contrast in gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-maintenance ground covers.
