Dendrocharis inflata

Dendrocharis inflata, commonly known as the inflated tree-fern, is a unique species of tree-fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, swollen trunk that stores water, which helps it survive in arid environments. The plant has a tall, slender stem with a thick, fibrous base that can reach several meters in height. Its fronds are typically long and feathery, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dendrocharis inflata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
