Diplopappus sericeus

Diplopappus sericeus, commonly known as the silky burr, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silken foliage and distinctive, spiny seed pods that mature into burrs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
