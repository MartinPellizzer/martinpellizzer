Dalbergia maritima

Dalbergia maritima, commonly known as the coastal bean tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and trifoliate leaves with three elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by bean-like pods containing flattened, dark brown seeds. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in mangrove or coastal forest ecosystems.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
