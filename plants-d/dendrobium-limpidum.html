Dendrobium limpidum

Dendrobium limpidum, commonly known as the clear-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking green color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Dendrobium limpidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
