Diospyros chloroxylon

Diospyros chloroxylon, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It is also valued for its hard, heavy wood used in traditional crafts and tool-making.
