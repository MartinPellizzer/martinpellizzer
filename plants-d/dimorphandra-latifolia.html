Dimorphandra latifolia

Dimorphandra latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dimorphandra, is a species of leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
