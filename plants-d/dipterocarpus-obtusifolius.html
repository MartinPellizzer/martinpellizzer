Dipterocarpus obtusifolius

Dipterocarpus obtusifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is known for its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of these habitats.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules that release winged seeds.
