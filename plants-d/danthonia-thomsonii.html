Danthonia thomsonii

Danthonia thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
