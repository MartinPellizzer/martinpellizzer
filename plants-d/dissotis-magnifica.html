Dissotis magnifica

Dissotis magnifica, commonly known as the grand flameplant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous bright red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal species. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Dissotis magnifica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its dramatic appearance.
