Dimorphanthera bracteata

Dimorphanthera bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, which enhances its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
