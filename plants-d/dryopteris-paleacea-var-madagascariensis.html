Dryopteris paleacea var. madagascariensis

Dryopteris paleacea var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar water fern, is a rare and striking fern native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green with a waxy texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests. Its unique morphology includes small, spore-producing sorus clusters on the underside of its fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
