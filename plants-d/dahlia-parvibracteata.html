Dahlia parvibracteata

Dahlia parvibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bract dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with a rough texture and is known for its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique bract structure distinguishes it from other dahlia species, contributing to its ornamental value.
