Dimorphotheca integrifolia

Dimorphotheca integrifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and grows in a compact shrub form. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Dimorphotheca integrifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
