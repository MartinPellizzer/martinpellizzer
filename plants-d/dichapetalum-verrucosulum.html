Dichapetalum verrucosulum

Dichapetalum verrucosulum, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
