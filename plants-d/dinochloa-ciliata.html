Dinochloa ciliata

Dinochloa ciliata, commonly known as the silky marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, a common feature among grasses. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Dinochloa ciliata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
