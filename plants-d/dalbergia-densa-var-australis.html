Dalbergia densa var. australis

Dalbergia densa var. australis, commonly known as the Australian rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
