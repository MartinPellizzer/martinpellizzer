Dyssodia sanguinea

Dyssodia sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange sap, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
