Diasperus martii

Diasperus martii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, orange or red composite flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Diasperus martii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
