Dolichos callosus

Dolichos callosus, commonly known as the rough sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, elongated pods and small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is often cultivated for its edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Dolichos callosus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
