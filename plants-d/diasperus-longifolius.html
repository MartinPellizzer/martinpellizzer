Diasperus longifolius

Diasperus longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf desert lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features linear, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and dry gardens.
