Damasonium lingua

Damasonium lingua, commonly known as the water clover, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are divided into three parts, resembling the shape of a clover. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in freshwater habitats.

Damasonium lingua is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
