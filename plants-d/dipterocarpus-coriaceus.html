Dipterocarpus coriaceus

Dipterocarpus coriaceus, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hard, heavy wood and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellow flowers that are grouped in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

The tree plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various species.
