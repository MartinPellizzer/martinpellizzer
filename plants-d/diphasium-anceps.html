Diphasium anceps

Diphasium anceps, commonly known as the two-flowered grass, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces two distinct flower heads, one terminal and one lateral, which are typically small and inconspicuous. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Diphasium anceps is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along stream banks.
