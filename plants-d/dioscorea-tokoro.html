Dioscorea tokoro

Dioscorea tokoro, commonly known as the Tokoro yam, is a perennial vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and unisexual.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
