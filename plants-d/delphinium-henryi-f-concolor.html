Delphinium henryi f. concolor

Delphinium henryi f. concolor, commonly known as the Chinese larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and it prefers well-drained, loamy soils in full sun to partial shade.

Delphinium henryi f. concolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
