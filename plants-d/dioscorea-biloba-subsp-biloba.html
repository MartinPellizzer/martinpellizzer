Dioscorea biloba subsp. biloba

Dioscorea biloba subsp.

biloba, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a food source.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
