Dioscorea pseudomacrocapsa

Dioscorea pseudomacrocapsa, commonly known as the giant yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing herbaceous perennial that produces large, tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has compound leaves with palmate venation and bears small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cuisines across Southeast Asia.

The species is also valued for its potential pharmacological properties, though further research is needed to fully explore its botanical and therapeutic applications.
