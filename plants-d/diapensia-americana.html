Diapensia americana

Diapensia americana, commonly known as American diapensia, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Diapensia americana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
