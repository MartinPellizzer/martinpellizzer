Diospyros rosenbluthii

Diospyros rosenbluthii, commonly known as the Rosenbluth persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Belize and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, fleshy berries.

The fruit is edible when ripe, though it is typically astringent until fully mature.
