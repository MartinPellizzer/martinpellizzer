Daphne glomerata var. ziganae

Daphne glomerata var. ziganae, commonly known as the ziganae daphne, is a rare and ornamental plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty growers.
