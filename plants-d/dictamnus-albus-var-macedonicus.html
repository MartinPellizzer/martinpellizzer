Dictamnus albus var. macedonicus

Dictamnus albus var. macedonicus, commonly known as the Macedonian white dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its foliage emits a strong, often unpleasant scent when crushed, a trait shared with other members of the genus.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
