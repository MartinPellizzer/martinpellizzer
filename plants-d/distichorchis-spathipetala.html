Distichorchis spathipetala

Distichorchis spathipetala, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single, elongated stem.

The species is notable for its intricate floral structure and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.
