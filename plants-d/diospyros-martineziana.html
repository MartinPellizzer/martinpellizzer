Diospyros martineziana

Diospyros martineziana, commonly known as the Martinez persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly the islands of Hispaniola and Cuba.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe.

Its fruit is a key component of the local ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
