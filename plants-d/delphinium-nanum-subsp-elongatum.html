Delphinium nanum subsp. elongatum

Delphinium nanum subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as the slender larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, reaching heights of up to 30 inches.

The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
