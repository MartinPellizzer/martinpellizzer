Desmoncus demeraranus

Desmoncus demeraranus, commonly known as the Trinidadian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Caribbean region.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate, greenish-yellow flower that blooms during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
