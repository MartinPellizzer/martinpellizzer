Discocactus heptacanthus subsp. magnimammus

Discocactus heptacanthus subsp.

magnimammus, commonly known as the giant mammillated discocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, ribbed stems that are covered in prominent, rounded tubercles.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its unique morphology makes it a striking and sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
