Dortmanna valdiviana

Dortmanna valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivia dwarf pine, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody cones containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the cool, humid climate of its native habitat.

Dortmanna valdiviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystem.
