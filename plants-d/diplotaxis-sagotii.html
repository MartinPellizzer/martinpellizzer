Diplotaxis sagotii

Diplotaxis sagotii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, with small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Diplotaxis sagotii is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in various herbal remedies.
