Dioscorea triloba

Dioscorea triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed yam, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes, though they may contain toxic compounds requiring careful processing.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
