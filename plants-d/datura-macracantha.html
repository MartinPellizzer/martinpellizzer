Datura macracantha

Datura macracantha, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, opening at night. The plant produces spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Datura macracantha is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like belladonna and tobacco.

Its foliage and all parts are poisonous, containing alkaloids such as scopolamine and hyoscyamine.
