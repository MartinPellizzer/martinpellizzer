Dracula marieae

Dracula marieae, commonly known as the Marieae Dracula, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that resemble the wings of a bat. The plant produces dark red to purple petals with a velvety texture, and its sepals are often adorned with intricate patterns. Dracula marieae is a lithophyte, meaning it grows on rocks, and requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
