Draba hissarica

Draba hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and arid environments.

Its leaves are basal, oblanceolate, and covered with a fine layer of white wax.
