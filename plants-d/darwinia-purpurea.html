Darwinia purpurea

Darwinia purpurea, commonly known as the purple spike heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, purple-bracted inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Darwinia purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
