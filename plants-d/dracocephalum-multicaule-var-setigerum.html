Dracocephalum multicaule var. setigerum

Dracocephalum multicaule var. setigerum, commonly known as purple false dragonhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in summer and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a slight aromatic scent.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
