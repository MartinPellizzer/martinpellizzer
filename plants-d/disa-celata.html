Disa celata

Disa celata, commonly known as the Cape bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom during the summer months.

Disa celata is a perennial herb that is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
