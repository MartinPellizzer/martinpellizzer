Didonica pendula

Didonica pendula, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins during the spring.

The tree is often found near water sources and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
