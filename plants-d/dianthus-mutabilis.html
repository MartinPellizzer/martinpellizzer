Dianthus mutabilis

Dianthus mutabilis, commonly known as the "blushing bride" or "dianthus," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, double blooms that range in color from white to pink and red, often changing hue as the flowers age. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of numerous petals arranged in a layered, ruffled pattern, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance.

Dianthus mutabilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
