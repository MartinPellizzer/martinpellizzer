Dactylorhiza kolaensis

Dactylorhiza kolaensis, commonly known as the Kola orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, violet or purple flowers during the flowering season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
