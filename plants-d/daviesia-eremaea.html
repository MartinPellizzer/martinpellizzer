Daviesia eremaea

Daviesia eremaea, commonly known as the spinifex Daviesia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers, typically in spring, which attract nectar-feeding insects. It is well adapted to drought and poor soils, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.

Daviesia eremaea plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
