Dianthus uzbekistanicus

Dianthus uzbekistanicus, commonly known as the Uzbek carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Dianthus uzbekistanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
