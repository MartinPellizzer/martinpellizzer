Draba verna proles dentata

Draba verna proles dentata, commonly known as the toothed spring whitlow, is a small flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Draba verna proles dentata is known for its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring.
