Dalea whitingii

Dalea whitingii, commonly known as Whiting's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across suitable habitats.
