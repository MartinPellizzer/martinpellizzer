Desmodium rubricaule

Desmodium rubricaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, followed by fluffy, spiny pods. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Desmodium rubricaule is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
