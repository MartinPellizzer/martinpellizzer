Dracocephalum wallichii var. platyanthum

Dracocephalum wallichii var. platyanthum, commonly known as the blue mist flower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silvery-blue hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valued addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
