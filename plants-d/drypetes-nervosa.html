Drypetes nervosa

Drypetes nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined drypetes, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

The plant is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its wood and medicinal properties.
