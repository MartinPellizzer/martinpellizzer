Dioscorea glandulosa

Dioscorea glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellow or orange tuberous roots. These tubers are edible when properly prepared and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
