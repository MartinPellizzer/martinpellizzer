Daphniphyllum macropodum var. variegatum

Daphniphyllum macropodum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, leathery leaves that exhibit a striking variegation of green, white, and sometimes yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage and seasonal interest.
