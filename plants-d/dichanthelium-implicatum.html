Dichanthelium implicatum

Dichanthelium implicatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, with small, two-lobed bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Dichanthelium implicatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
