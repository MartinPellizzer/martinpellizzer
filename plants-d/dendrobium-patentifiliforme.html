Dendrobium patentifiliforme

Dendrobium patentifiliforme, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, thin, and often pendulous leaves that are adapted for climbing and absorbing moisture from the air. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the summer months. Its growth habit involves forming pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
