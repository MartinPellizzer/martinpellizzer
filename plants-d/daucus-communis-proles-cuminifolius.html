Daucus communis proles cuminifolius

Daucus communis proles cuminifolius, commonly known as the cuminleaf cowparsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of cumin, giving it its common name. Its biennial growth cycle involves a rosette of leaves in the first year and flowering and seed production in the second year.
