Dicranopteris squamulosa

Dicranopteris squamulosa, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Dicranopteris squamulosa plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
