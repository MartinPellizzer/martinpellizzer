Digitalis grandiflora var. acutiflora

Digitalis grandiflora var. acutiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

This plant is valued in gardens for its dramatic floral display and its historical use in medicine.
