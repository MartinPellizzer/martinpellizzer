Diervilla × fujisanensis

Diervilla × fujisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is a hybrid between Diervilla rostrata and Diervilla sinensis, exhibiting a compact growth habit with dense, upright stems. The plant is notable for its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer, providing ornamental value. Its foliage turns vibrant red in the fall, adding seasonal interest to gardens.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
