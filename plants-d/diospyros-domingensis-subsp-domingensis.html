Diospyros domingensis subsp. domingensis

Diospyros domingensis subsp.

domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by edible, orange to reddish persimmon fruits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils near riverbanks or in forested areas.
