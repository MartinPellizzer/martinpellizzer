Davallia triangularis

Davallia triangularis, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, moist soil. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant.

Davallia triangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
