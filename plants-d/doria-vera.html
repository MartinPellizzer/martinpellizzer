Doria vera

Doria vera, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine white down, giving it a soft appearance.

Doria vera is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
