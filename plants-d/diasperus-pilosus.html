Diasperus pilosus

Diasperus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small, deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and pinnately compound leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
