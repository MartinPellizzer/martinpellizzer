Dioscorea hirsuticaulis

Dioscorea hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves climbing and twining around supports, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

The tubers of D. hirsuticaulis are valued for their potential medicinal properties and nutritional content.
