Dolichos stipulosus

Dolichos stipulosus, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with stipules. The flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in clusters, while the pods are elongated and contain edible seeds. This plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.

It thrives in warm climates and is often used as a forage crop or for ornamental purposes.
