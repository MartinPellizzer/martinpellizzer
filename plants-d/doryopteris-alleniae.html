Doryopteris alleniae

Doryopteris alleniae, commonly known as the Allen's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into small, branching segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, well-drained soil. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Doryopteris alleniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
