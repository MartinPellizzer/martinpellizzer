Delphinium pachycentrum var. lobatum

Delphinium pachycentrum var. lobatum, commonly known as the broad-centred larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in dense racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with lobed or toothed margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to alpine gardens.
