Dimocarpus longus

Dimocarpus longus, commonly known as the longan tree, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, and juicy berries. The fruit has a single seed enclosed in a fibrous pulp, and the tree is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
