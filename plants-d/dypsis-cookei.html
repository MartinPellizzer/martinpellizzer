Dypsis cookei

Dypsis cookei, commonly known as the Cook's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
