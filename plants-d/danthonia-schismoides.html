Danthonia schismoides

Danthonia schismoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Danthonia schismoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in natural and rehabilitated environments.
