Darwinia flavescens

Darwinia flavescens, commonly known as the golden darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, attract various pollinators such as birds and insects.

Darwinia flavescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
