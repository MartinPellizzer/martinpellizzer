Dactylis glomerata nothovar. fleischeri

Dactylis glomerata nothovar. fleischeri, commonly known as Fleischer's tufted grass, is a hybrid perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
