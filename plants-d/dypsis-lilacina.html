Dypsis lilacina

Dypsis lilacina, commonly known as the pink bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and elegant, feathery fronds. The plant produces small, fragrant, lilac-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Dypsis lilacina is relatively low maintenance and suitable for indoor cultivation.
