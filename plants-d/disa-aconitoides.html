Disa aconitoides

Disa aconitoides, commonly known as the blue disa, is a striking member of the orchid family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Disa aconitoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
