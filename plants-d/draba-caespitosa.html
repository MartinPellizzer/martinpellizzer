Draba caespitosa

Draba caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that forms dense clusters of small, basal leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically between March and May. It is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Draba caespitosa is adapted to cold climates and is commonly seen in mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.
