Diasperus societatis

Diasperus societatis, commonly known as the Society's Diadem, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-blue foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant's unique morphology includes a short, woody stem with a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
