Disperis buchananii

Disperis buchananii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, which are typically arranged in loose clusters. Disperis buchananii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often found in grasslands and forest margins, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
