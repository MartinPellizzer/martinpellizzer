Danthonia cirrata

Danthonia cirrata, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silky texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Danthonia cirrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate, grass-like foliage.
