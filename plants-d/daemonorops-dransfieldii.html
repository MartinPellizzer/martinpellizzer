Daemonorops dransfieldii

Daemonorops dransfieldii, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, ornamental plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring a stout, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by large, woody cones. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.

Due to its slow growth and long lifespan, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ecological significance.
