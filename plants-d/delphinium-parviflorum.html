Delphinium parviflorum

Delphinium parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Delphinium parviflorum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
