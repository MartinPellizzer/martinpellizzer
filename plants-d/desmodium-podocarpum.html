Desmodium podocarpum

Desmodium podocarpum, commonly known as the creeping desmodium, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically pink or purple. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several small, rounded seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Desmodium podocarpum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a cover crop.
