Descurainia incana

Descurainia incana, commonly known as hoary tansy, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Descurainia incana is also known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
