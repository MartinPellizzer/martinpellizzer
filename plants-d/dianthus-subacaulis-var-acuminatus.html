Dianthus subacaulis var. acuminatus

Dianthus subacaulis var. acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered pink or white flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
