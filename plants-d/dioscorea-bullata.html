Dioscorea bullata

Dioscorea bullata, commonly known as the swollen yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often swollen and irregular in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

The tubers of Dioscorea bullata are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
