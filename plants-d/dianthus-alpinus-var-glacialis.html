Dianthus alpinus var. glacialis

Dianthus alpinus var. glacialis, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a hardy perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink to red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing protection against cold and UV radiation.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its resilience and ornamental appeal in rock gardens and alpine settings.
