Digitalis lurida

Digitalis lurida, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
