Dupontia

Dupontia, commonly known as the "silver grass," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, often tufted growth habit. The plants typically grow in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and sandy plains, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Dupontia species are generally perennial and produce small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
