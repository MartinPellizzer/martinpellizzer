Dracontium plowmanii

Dracontium plowmanii, commonly known as the Plowman's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf structures and inflorescences. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a thick, fleshy rhizome that stores nutrients. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally for its starchy rhizomes.
