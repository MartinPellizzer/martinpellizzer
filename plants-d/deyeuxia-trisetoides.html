Deyeuxia trisetoides

Deyeuxia trisetoides, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Deyeuxia trisetoides plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
