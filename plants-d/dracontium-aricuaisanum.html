Dracontium aricuaisanum

Dracontium aricuaisanum, commonly known as the Aricuaias arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are edible and have been historically used as a starch source, similar to other members of its family.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
