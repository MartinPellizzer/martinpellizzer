Dioclea guianensis var. lasiophylla

Dioclea guianensis var. lasiophylla, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in tropical rainforests.

The botanical features of this species make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
