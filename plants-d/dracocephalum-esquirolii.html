Dracocephalum esquirolii

Dracocephalum esquirolii, commonly known as the Persian shiso or mountain shiso, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Dracocephalum esquirolii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
