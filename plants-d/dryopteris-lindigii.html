Dryopteris lindigii

Dryopteris lindigii, commonly known as Lindig's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive adequate humidity and indirect light. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
