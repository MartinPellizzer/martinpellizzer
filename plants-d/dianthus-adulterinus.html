Dianthus adulterinus

Dianthus adulterinus, commonly known as the slender campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dianthus adulterinus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
