Dipterocarpus mottleyanus

Dipterocarpus mottleyanus, commonly known as the Mottley's dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and elongated, often curved leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to forest structure and biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
