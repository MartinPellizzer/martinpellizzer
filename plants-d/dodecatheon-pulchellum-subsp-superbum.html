Dodecatheon pulchellum subsp. superbum

Dodecatheon pulchellum subsp.

superbum, commonly known as the showy peacock flower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies superbum is distinguished by its larger, more showy blooms compared to other subspecies of Dodecatheon pulchellum.
