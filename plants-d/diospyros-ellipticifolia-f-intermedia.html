Diospyros ellipticifolia f. intermedia

Diospyros ellipticifolia f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, orange-red fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a dense, evergreen canopy and a tendency to form a compact shrub or small tree.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
