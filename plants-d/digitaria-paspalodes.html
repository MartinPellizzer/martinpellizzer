Digitaria paspalodes

Digitaria paspalodes, commonly known as bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Digitaria paspalodes is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with limited water availability.
