Drimia

Drimia, commonly known as the African lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asparagaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their bulbous roots and showy, often fragrant flowers. Many species of Drimia have strap-shaped leaves and produce clusters of white, pink, or purple flowers. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as houseplants.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
