Draba norvegica var. sornborgeri

Draba norvegica var. sornborgeri, commonly known as the Sornborger's whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

This variety is notable for its resilience in harsh climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
