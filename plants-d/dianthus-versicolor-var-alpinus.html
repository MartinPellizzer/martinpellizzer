Dianthus versicolor var. alpinus

Dianthus versicolor var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bicolor flowers that typically feature pink and white petals. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Dianthus versicolor var. alpinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
