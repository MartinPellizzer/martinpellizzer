Delphinium montanum var. productum

Delphinium montanum var. productum, commonly known as the mountain larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, typical of the Delphinium genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
