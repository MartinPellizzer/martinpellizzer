Dictamnus albus var. dasycarpus

Dictamnus albus var. dasycarpus, commonly known as the white dictamnus or smooth-fruited dictamnus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that are typically smooth and cylindrical, giving it its varietal name. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
