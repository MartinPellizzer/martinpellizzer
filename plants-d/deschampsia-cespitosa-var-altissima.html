Deschampsia cespitosa var. altissima

Deschampsia cespitosa var. altissima, commonly known as tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, prairies, and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
