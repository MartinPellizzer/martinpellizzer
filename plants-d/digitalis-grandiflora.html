Digitalis grandiflora

Digitalis grandiflora, commonly known as the tall foxglove, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spires of hooded flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Digitalis grandiflora is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
