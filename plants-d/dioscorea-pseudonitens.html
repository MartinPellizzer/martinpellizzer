Dioscorea pseudonitens

Dioscorea pseudonitens, commonly known as the glossy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and have been utilized in various culinary and medicinal practices.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it adaptable to a range of ecological conditions.
