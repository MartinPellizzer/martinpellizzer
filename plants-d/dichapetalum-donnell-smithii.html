Dichapetalum donnell-smithii

Dichapetalum donnell-smithii, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which resemble the spokes of a wheel. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as rivers and lakes. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Dichapetalum donnell-smithii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
