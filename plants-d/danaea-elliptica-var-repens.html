Danaea elliptica var. repens

Danaea elliptica var. repens, commonly known as the creeping flame vine, is a tropical perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and coastal gardens.
