Dolichos pseudopachyrhizus var. subintegrifolius

Dolichos pseudopachyrhizus var. subintegrifolius, commonly known as the African bean or wild cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems, which help in water retention. Its pods are typically slender and contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
