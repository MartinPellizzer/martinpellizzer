Dactylorhiza francis-drucei

Dactylorhiza francis-drucei, commonly known as the Druce's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Dactylorhiza francis-drucei is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
