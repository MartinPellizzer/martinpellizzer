Dendrobium schwartzkopfianum

Dendrobium schwartzkopfianum, commonly known as the Schwaetzkopf's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a branched stem system that supports its climbing or hanging growth form. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in high humidity and bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value contribute to its significance in both horticulture and botanical study.
