Dolichos insularis

Dolichos insularis, commonly known as the island bean, is a climbing legume native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Dolichos insularis is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.

Its adaptability to tropical climates makes it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
