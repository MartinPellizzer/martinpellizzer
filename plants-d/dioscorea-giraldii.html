Dioscorea giraldii

Dioscorea giraldii, commonly known as the African yam vine, is a climbing plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and twining stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for their nutritional value. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain numerous seeds.

Dioscorea giraldii is valued both for its edible tubers and its traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
