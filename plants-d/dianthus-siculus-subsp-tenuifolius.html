Dianthus siculus subsp. tenuifolius

Dianthus siculus subsp.

tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with narrow, linear leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in width.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
