Debregeasia japonica

Debregeasia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and a branching stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Debregeasia japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
