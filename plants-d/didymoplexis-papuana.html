Didymoplexis papuana

Didymoplexis papuana, commonly known as the Papuan spider orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are small and delicate, often displaying a striking combination of colors and patterns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
