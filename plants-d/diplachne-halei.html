Diplachne halei

Diplachne halei, commonly known as Hale's grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue and a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family.

This plant is often found in coastal and subcoastal regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.
