Diospyros phyllomegas

Diospyros phyllomegas, commonly known as the giant persimmon, is a member of the Ebenaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons. Its bark is thick and fissured, often with a reddish or brownish hue, and it is valued for both its fruit and timber.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in tropical ecosystems.
