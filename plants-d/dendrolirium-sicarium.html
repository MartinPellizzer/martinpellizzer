Dendrolirium sicarium

Dendrolirium sicarium, commonly known as the Sicilian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble lilies. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers bloom in clusters with a distinctive purple or violet coloration.

Dendrolirium sicarium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
