Dodecatheon redolens

Dodecatheon redolens, commonly known as the fragrant white bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive fragrance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and is a member of the Primulaceae family.

Dodecatheon redolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for early pollinators.
