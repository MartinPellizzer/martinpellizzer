Draba micrantha

Draba micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by fluffy seed heads. Draba micrantha is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
