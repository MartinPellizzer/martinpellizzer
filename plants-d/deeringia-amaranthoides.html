Deeringia amaranthoides

Deeringia amaranthoides, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers and nutritious leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to thrive in full sun.
