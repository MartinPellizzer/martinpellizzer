Daviesia umbellulata var. pubigera

Daviesia umbellulata var. pubigera, commonly known as the hairy sickle-pea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The variety pubigera is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name.
