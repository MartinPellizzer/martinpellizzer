Delpydora

Delpydora, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green inflorescences that resemble spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Delpydora is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
