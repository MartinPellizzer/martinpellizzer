Dahliaphyllum almedae

Dahliaphyllum almedae, commonly known as the Alameda dahlia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid climates. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance.

Dahliaphyllum almedae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that require low maintenance and drought tolerance.
