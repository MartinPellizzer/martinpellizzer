Drosera menziesii subsp. thysanosepala

Drosera menziesii subsp.

thysanosepala, commonly known as the silky sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to western Australia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in a dense inflorescence.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
