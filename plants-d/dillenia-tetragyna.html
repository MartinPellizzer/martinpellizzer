Dillenia tetragyna

Dillenia tetragyna, commonly known as the four-angled dillenia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often ribbed bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, fleshy, berry-like fruits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies due to its ornamental value and role in supporting local wildlife.
