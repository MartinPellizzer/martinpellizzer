Dalea peruviana

Dalea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, cascading clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, hard, seed-like structures called elia seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Dalea peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
