Daphne oppositifolia

Daphne oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with opposite leaf arrangement and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it typically grows as a shrub or small tree.

Daphne oppositifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
