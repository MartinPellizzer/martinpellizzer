Disa satyriopsis

Disa satyriopsis, commonly known as the satyr orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forests and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that resembles a satyr's face, hence its common name.

Disa satyriopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
