Diplotaxis erucoides f. valentina

Diplotaxis erucoides f. valentina, commonly known as the Valentina lettuce, is a cultivated form of a species native to southern Africa.

It is a leafy green plant with a distinctive, slightly bitter flavor, often used in salads and gourmet cooking. The plant has rosette-shaped leaves that are tender and edible, with a texture similar to that of chicory. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued for its unique taste and nutritional content, making it a popular choice among health-conscious consumers.
