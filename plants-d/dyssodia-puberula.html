Dyssodia puberula

Dyssodia puberula, commonly known as the woolly dyssodia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant typically produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, with a pubescent surface.

Dyssodia puberula thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
