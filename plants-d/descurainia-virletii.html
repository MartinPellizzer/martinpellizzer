Descurainia virletii

Descurainia virletii, commonly known as the slender tansy, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
