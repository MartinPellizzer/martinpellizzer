Doronicum rupestre

Doronicum rupestre, commonly known as the rock goldcup, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Doronicum rupestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
