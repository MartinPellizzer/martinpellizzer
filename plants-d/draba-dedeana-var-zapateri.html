Draba dedeana var. zapateri

Draba dedeana var. zapateri, commonly known as the Zapater's winter cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically arranged in racemes. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
