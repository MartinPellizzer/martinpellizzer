Dactylis glomerata subsp. reichenbachii

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

reichenbachii, commonly known as red brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish in color.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats. It is often found in grasslands, heaths, and disturbed soils, and is known for its hardy nature.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
