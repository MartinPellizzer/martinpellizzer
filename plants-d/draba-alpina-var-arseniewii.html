Draba alpina var. arseniewii

Draba alpina var. arseniewii, commonly known as the Armenian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. This variety typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant has basal leaves that are often lobed or divided, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.
