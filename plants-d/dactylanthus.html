Dactylanthus

Dactylanthus, commonly known as the Christmas flower, is a unique New Zealand plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is a rare and endangered species, characterized by its distinctive yellow, fleshy, and often bulbous flowers. The plant has a short flowering period, typically around Christmas time, which contributes to its common name. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
