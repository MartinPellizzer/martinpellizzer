Dodecatheon lemoinei

Dodecatheon lemoinei, commonly known as the Lemoine's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, pinkish to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and is adapted to well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed margins.

Dodecatheon lemoinei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
