Diasperus lamprophyllus

Diasperus lamprophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and showy yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Diasperus lamprophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
