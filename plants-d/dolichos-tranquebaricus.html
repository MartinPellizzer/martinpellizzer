Dolichos tranquebaricus

Dolichos tranquebaricus, commonly known as the Tranchebar bean, is a climbing legume native to South and Southeast Asia.

It produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its edible seeds and ornamental value.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
