Dendrobium muricatum

Dendrobium muricatum, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with numerous horizontal pseudobulbs covered in a dense, hairy coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two rows along the pseudobulbs.

It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.
