Delphinium yechengense

Delphinium yechengense, commonly known as the Yecheng larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and sometimes hairy.

Delphinium yechengense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
