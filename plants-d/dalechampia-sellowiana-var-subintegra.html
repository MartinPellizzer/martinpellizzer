Dalechampia sellowiana var. subintegra

Dalechampia sellowiana var. subintegra, commonly known as the Sellow's dalechampia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
