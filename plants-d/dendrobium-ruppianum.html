Dendrobium ruppianum

Dendrobium ruppianum, commonly known as the Rupp's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with clustered pseudobulbs and long, leathery leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium ruppianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
