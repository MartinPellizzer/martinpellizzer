Dracaena sarmentosa

Dracaena sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping dracaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Dracaena sarmentosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
