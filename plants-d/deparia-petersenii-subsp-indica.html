Deparia petersenii subsp. indica

Deparia petersenii subsp.

indica, commonly known as the Indian shield fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae. It is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture.

The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
