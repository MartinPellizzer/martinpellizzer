Dracaena maingayi

Dracaena maingayi, commonly known as the red dragon tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green, sword-shaped leaves that often have a reddish or bronze tinge along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. Dracaena maingayi is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light conditions.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in both residential and commercial settings.
