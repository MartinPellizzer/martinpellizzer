Dioscorea quispicanchensis

Dioscorea quispicanchensis, commonly known as the Andean yam, is a tuberous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers that are an important food source in some Andean communities. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its tubers are often used in traditional cooking and have cultural significance in regions such as Peru and Bolivia.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is also gaining attention for potential agricultural and medicinal applications.
