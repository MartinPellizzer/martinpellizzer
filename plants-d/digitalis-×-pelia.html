Digitalis × pelia

Digitalis × pelia, commonly known as the hybrid foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of tubular, hood-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Digitalis × pelia is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and historical use in medicinal applications.
