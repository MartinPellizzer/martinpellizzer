Dadia

Dadia, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the genus Saintpaulia and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a low-growing herb with a compact rosette structure. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, white, and blue.

Dadia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
