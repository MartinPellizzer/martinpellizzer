Duranta argentea

Duranta argentea, commonly known as silverleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of tubular, blue-violet flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Duranta argentea is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for a variety of climates.
