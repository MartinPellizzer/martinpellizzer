Dactylorhiza markusii

Dactylorhiza markusii, commonly known as the Markus's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near water bodies. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Dactylorhiza markusii is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its resilience in wetland ecosystems.
