Dryopteris chrysocoma var. costalisora

Dryopteris chrysocoma var. costalisora, commonly known as the golden fern, is a variant of the golden shield fern.

It is a fern species characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that often display a golden hue along the veins. The plant is native to regions with humid climates and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, shield-like shape.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture and its ability to add texture and color to shaded garden spaces.
