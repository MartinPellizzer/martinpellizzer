Dendrolirium densiflorum

Dendrolirium densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered tree lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Dendrolirium densiflorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
