Dendrochilum micranthum

Dendrochilum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered tree orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in forest understories, thriving in humid and shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often adorned with faint purple or pink markings.

Dendrochilum micranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
