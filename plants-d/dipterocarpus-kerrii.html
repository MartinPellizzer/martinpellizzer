Dipterocarpus kerrii

Dipterocarpus kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy seeds and buttressed roots. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a long lifespan, often living over 100 years. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a rough texture.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to forest structure.
