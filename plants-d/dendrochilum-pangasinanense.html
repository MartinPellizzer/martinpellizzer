Dendrochilum pangasinanense

Dendrochilum pangasinanense, commonly known as the Pangasinan orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, showcasing its epiphytic nature. Its flowers are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a conservation concern.
