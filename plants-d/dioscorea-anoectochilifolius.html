Dioscorea anoectochilifolius

Dioscorea anoectochilifolius, commonly known as the purple yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible tubers that are rich in starch. Its tubers are widely used in traditional cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important resource in both agriculture and ethnobotany.
