Delphinium anatolicum

Delphinium anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense racemes. Delphinium anatolicum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
