Digitaria hololeuca

Digitaria hololeuca, commonly known as white bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in full sun and is often found in prairie habitats, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its seed heads are small and spike-like, contributing to its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Digitaria hololeuca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
