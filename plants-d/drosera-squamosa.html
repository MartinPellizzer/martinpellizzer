Drosera squamosa

Drosera squamosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky mucilage to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are small and usually pink or red, arranged in a dense cluster.

Drosera squamosa is a perennial species that thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils.
