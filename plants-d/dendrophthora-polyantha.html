Dendrophthora polyantha

Dendrophthora polyantha, commonly known as the Chinese wax tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and its ability to produce a sticky, resinous substance. The tree has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
