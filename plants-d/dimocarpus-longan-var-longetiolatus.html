Dimocarpus longan var. longetiolatus

Dimocarpus longan var. longetiolatus, commonly known as the longan tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, thin skin and a sweet, juicy pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and has cultural significance in many Asian countries.
