Diospyros laurifolia

Diospyros laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for human consumption.

The tree is often used in traditional medicine and is also appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
