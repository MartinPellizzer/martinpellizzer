Delphinium peregrinum var. macropetalum

Delphinium peregrinum var. macropetalum, commonly known as the giant blue larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its tall stature and vibrant blooms, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic focal point in their landscapes.
