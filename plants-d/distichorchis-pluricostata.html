Distichorchis pluricostata

Distichorchis pluricostata, commonly known as the broad-ribbed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in a spike, attracting various pollinators.

Distichorchis pluricostata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
