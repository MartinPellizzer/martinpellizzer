Dialium indum var. indum

Dialium indum var. indum, commonly known as the African cherry or *African starfruit*, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits resembling starfruits. The fruit contains a single large seed enclosed in a juicy, aromatic pulp, which is edible and used in local cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its wood and medicinal properties.
