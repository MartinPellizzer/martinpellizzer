Drapetes muscoides

Drapetes muscoides, commonly known as the moss-like drapetis, is a unique plant species belonging to the family Drapetisaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form, resembling a mat of moss, which allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and moisture retention.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Drapetes muscoides is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
