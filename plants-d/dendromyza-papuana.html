Dendromyza papuana

Dendromyza papuana, commonly known as the Papuan tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to the rainforests of New Guinea and nearby islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a vital role in the ecological structure of its habitat. Its rhizome system helps in nutrient absorption and soil stabilization.

Dendromyza papuana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
