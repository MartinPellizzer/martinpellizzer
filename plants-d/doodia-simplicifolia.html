Doodia simplicifolia

Doodia simplicifolia, commonly known as the common wood fern, is a species of fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with dense, clustered fronds that are usually bi- or tri-pinnate. Its leaves are characterized by their finely divided, delicate structure and a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Doodia simplicifolia plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in its native habitats.
