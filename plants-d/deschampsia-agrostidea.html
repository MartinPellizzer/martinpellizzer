Deschampsia agrostidea

Deschampsia agrostidea, commonly known as slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and tall, slender flowering stalks that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects and small animals.

The plant's rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a valuable component of native plant communities.
