Dracula decussata

Dracula decussata, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of Ecuador.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes. The plant features a compact, upright growth habit with a single, short stem and a few broad, dark green leaves. Its flowers are showy, with bright red petals and sepals, and a distinctive yellow lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Dracula decussata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
