Dicerocaryum eriocarpum

Dicerocaryum eriocarpum, commonly known as the woolly burr acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, hairy pods that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Dicerocaryum eriocarpum is adapted to withstand drought and has a slow growth rate.
