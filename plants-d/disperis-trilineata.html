Disperis trilineata

Disperis trilineata, commonly known as the three-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Disperis trilineata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
