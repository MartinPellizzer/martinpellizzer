Dianthus hirtus subsp. graniticus

Dianthus hirtus subsp.

graniticus, commonly known as the granitic campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its pink or red flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is a perennial species that produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, arid conditions.
