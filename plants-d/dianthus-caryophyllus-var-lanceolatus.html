Dianthus caryophyllus var. lanceolatus

Dianthus caryophyllus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The lance-shaped leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is valued for its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
