Diporidium arboreum

Diporidium arboreum, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers have five petals and a prominent central column. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Diporidium arboreum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
