Diasperus discoideus

Diasperus discoideus, commonly known as the star-shaped daisy, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive star-like inflorescence.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, with bracts that give it its unique appearance. Diasperus discoideus typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
