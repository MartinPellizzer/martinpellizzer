Desmoncus prestoei

Desmoncus prestoei, commonly known as the giant false dawnflower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Desmoncus prestoei is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
