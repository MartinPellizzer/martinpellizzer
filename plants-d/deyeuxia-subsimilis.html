Deyeuxia subsimilis

Deyeuxia subsimilis, commonly known as the similar flat sedge, is a grass species native to various regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes help it spread and form dense vegetation in wetland habitats.

Deyeuxia subsimilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
