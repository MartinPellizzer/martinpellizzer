Dianthus glabriusculus

Dianthus glabriusculus, commonly known as the smooth rock-cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in its arid habitat. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a central cluster of stamens.

Dianthus glabriusculus is often found growing in rocky soils and alpine environments, where it thrives in full sun.
