Dianella brevicaulis

Dianella brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax lily, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green, and it produces striking blue to purple flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to shady or semi-shade conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and can thrive in a variety of garden settings.

Dianella brevicaulis is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
