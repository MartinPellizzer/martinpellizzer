Dendrochilum crassilabium

Dendrochilum crassilabium, commonly known as the broad-lipped tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a broad, lip-like structure that is often brightly colored. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Dendrochilum crassilabium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
