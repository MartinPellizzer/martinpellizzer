Diasperus melleri

Diasperus melleri, commonly known as the Meller's grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Diasperus melleri is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
