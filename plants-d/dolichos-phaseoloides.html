Dolichos phaseoloides

Dolichos phaseoloides, commonly known as the horse gram or cluster bean, is an annual legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, fibrous pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds and forage value.

Dolichos phaseoloides is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
