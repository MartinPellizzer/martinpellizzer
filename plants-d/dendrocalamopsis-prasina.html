Dendrocalamopsis prasina

Dendrocalamopsis prasina, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, often arching stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Dendrocalamopsis prasina is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
