Disa carsonii

Disa carsonii, commonly known as Carson's orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vivid pink or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing showy, spurred orchid flowers that attract pollinators. Disa carsonii thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical study.
