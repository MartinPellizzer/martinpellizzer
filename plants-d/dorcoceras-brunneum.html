Dorcoceras brunneum

Dorcoceras brunneum, commonly known as the common sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and bristly, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dorcoceras brunneum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
