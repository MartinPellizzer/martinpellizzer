Dalea luisana

Dalea luisana, commonly known as Luisana milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Dalea luisana is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.
