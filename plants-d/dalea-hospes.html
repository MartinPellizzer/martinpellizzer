Dalea hospes

Dalea hospes, commonly known as the pink prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain seeds.

Dalea hospes plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often used in native plant landscaping.
