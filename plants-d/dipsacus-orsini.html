Dipsacus orsini

Dipsacus orsini, commonly known as the Italian teasel, is a biennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble teasels, which are used in the textile industry. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Dipsacus orsini is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
