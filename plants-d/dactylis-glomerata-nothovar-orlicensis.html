Dactylis glomerata nothovar. orlicensis

Dactylis glomerata nothovar. orlicensis, commonly known as the Orlic Meadowgrass, is a hybrid grass species originating from Central Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and compact, dense inflorescences that form small, rounded clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This nothovar is valued for its forage quality and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
