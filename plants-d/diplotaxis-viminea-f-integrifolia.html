Diplotaxis viminea f. integrifolia

Diplotaxis viminea f. integrifolia, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
