Dioscorea cynanchifolia

Dioscorea cynanchifolia, commonly known as the Indian yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The tubers of D. cynanchifolia contain compounds with potential pharmacological properties.
