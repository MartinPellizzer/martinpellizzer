Dianthus attenuatus var. pyrenaicus

Dianthus attenuatus var. pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or white flowers with prominent red or purple markings. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to rocky, alpine environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture and botanical gardens.
