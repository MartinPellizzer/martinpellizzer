Delphinium elatum nothosubsp.

fiekianum, commonly known as the Fiekian hybrid delphinium, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Delphinium elatum and other species within the Delphinium genus. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a long flowering period, making it a notable addition to horticultural collections.
