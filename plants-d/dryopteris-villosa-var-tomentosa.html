Dryopteris villosa var. tomentosa

Dryopteris villosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose wood fern, is a fern species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy fronds that give it a velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, delicate structure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
