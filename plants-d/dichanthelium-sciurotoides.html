Dichanthelium sciurotoides

Dichanthelium sciurotoides, commonly known as the mouse-eared panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
