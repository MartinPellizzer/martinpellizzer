Dioscorea boridiensis

Dioscorea boridiensis, commonly known as the Boridiense yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The tubers of Dioscorea boridiensis are valued for their nutritional content and traditional use in local communities.
