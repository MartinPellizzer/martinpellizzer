Dyckia piracanjubensis

Dyckia piracanjubensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grassland bromeliad, is a perennial plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater. The plant features long, narrow, and often spiny leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

Dyckia piracanjubensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
