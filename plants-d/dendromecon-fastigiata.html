Dendromecon fastigiata

Dendromecon fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf tree mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Dendromecon fastigiata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
