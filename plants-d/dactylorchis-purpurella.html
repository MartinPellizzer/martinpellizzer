Dactylorchis purpurella

Dactylorchis purpurella, commonly known as the purple-stem orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive violet or purple stem that is often hairy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring.

The orchid has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake.
