Dryopteris finisterrae

Dryopteris finisterrae, commonly known as the Faroe Islands wood fern, is a rare and endemic species native to the Faroe Islands.

It is a deciduous fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky slopes and stream banks. The plant features a creeping rhizome with fronds that are bi- to tri-pinnate, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
