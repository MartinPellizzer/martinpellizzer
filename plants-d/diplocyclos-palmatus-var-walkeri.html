Diplocyclos palmatus var. walkeri

Diplocyclos palmatus var. walkeri, commonly known as the African mountain hibiscus, is a shrub native to the highlands of East Africa.

It features lanceolate, dark green leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive yellow-orange flower that blooms in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable regions.
