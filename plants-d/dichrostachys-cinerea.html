Dichrostachys cinerea

Dichrostachys cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that turn silver-white when dried, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that are clustered in dense heads. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

Dichrostachys cinerea is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
