Daphnopsis swartzii

Daphnopsis swartzii, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
