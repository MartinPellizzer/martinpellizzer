Delphinium peregrinum var. longipes

Delphinium peregrinum var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
