Dussia sanguinea

Dussia sanguinea, commonly known as the red stemmed dussia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its striking red stems make it a visually appealing addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
