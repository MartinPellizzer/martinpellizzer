Drosera micrantha

Drosera micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky mucilage to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in a dense cluster.

Drosera micrantha is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found in coastal regions.
