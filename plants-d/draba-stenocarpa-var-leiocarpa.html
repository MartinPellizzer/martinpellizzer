Draba stenocarpa var. leiocarpa

Draba stenocarpa var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-capsule rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous fruiting capsules, which set it apart from other varieties within the species.
