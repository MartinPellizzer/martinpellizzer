Diospyros auriculata

Diospyros auriculata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, often with a reddish-brown color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and as a food source.
