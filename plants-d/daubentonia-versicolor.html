Daubentonia versicolor

Daubentonia versicolor, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking clusters of violet or purple flowers with distinctive spots. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Daubentonia versicolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
