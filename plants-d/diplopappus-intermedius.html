Diplopappus intermedius

Diplopappus intermedius, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a hairy, upright stem and produces small, bract-like leaves along its stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a type of achene, which is dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.
