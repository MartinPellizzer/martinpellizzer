Dioscorea polygonoides var. martinicensis

Dioscorea polygonoides var. martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique yam, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary edible part. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and traditional uses in local cuisines.
