Draba fladnizensis var. glaberrima

Draba fladnizensis var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and it produces compact, rounded seed pods.

The variety glaberrima is distinguished by its nearly hairless foliage, which sets it apart from other varieties of Draba fladnizensis.
