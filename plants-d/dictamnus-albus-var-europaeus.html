Dictamnus albus var. europaeus

Dictamnus albus var. europaeus, commonly known as European white dictamnus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has smooth, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
