Diospyros usaramensis

Diospyros usaramensis, commonly known as the Usaramensis persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to reddish when mature, often containing one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and local construction.
