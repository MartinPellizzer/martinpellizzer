Desmanthus callistachys

Desmanthus callistachys, commonly known as the tall false indigo, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment and sustainable agriculture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in native plant gardens and erosion control.

Its ecological value supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
