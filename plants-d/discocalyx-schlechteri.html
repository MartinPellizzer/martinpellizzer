Discocalyx schlechteri

Discocalyx schlechteri, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy.

Discocalyx schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
