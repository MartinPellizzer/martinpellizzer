Desmodium oxyphyllum

Desmodium oxyphyllum, commonly known as the white clover, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in pastures and as a cover crop.

Its rapid growth and ability to suppress weeds make it an important species in agricultural and ecological contexts.
