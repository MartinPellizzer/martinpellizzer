Diospyros glaucophylla

Diospyros glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony species known for their hard, dark wood. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that are edible.

Diospyros glaucophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its durable timber in local industries.
