Delphinium persicum var. regelianum

Delphinium persicum var. regelianum, commonly known as the Persian larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is valued for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
