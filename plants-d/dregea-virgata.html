Dregea virgata

Dregea virgata, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale grey, almost silver-colored bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

Dregea virgata is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
