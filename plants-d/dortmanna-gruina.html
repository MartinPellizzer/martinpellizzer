Dortmanna gruina

Dortmanna gruina, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. Native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa, it thrives in saline and sandy soils. The plant has a compact growth habit with a rosette of leaves and is often found in maritime environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
