Desmodium dutrae

Desmodium dutrae, commonly known as Brazilian velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, dark seeds. It is often grown for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it useful in agricultural systems.

Desmodium dutrae is also valued for its potential in soil conservation and as a forage crop.
