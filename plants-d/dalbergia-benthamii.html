Dalbergia benthamii

Dalbergia benthamii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to overharvesting, it is now listed as an endangered species in many regions.
