Diasperus macrocalyx

Diasperus macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered Diasperus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant grows as a shrub, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
