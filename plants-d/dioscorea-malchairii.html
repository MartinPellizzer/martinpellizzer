Dioscorea malchairii

Dioscorea malchairii, commonly known as the Malchair yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Due to its medicinal properties and potential as a food source, it holds significance in local ecosystems and cultural practices.
