Dampiera plumosa

Dampiera plumosa, commonly known as the silky dampiera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens for its hardy and drought-tolerant nature.
