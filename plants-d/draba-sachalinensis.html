Draba sachalinensis

Draba sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East, particularly Sakhalin Island.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subarctic regions. It has basal leaves that are often lobed or divided, and its stems are usually unbranched.

Draba sachalinensis is known for its resilience to cold climates and is often found in mountainous or coastal areas.
