Dendrophthora microsoma

Dendrophthora microsoma, commonly known as the small-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Eucalyptus*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected off the host's leaves. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers that are often inconspicuous. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on the berries, which are produced after flowering.

Dendrophthora microsoma plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
