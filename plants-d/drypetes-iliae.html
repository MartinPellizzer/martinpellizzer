Drypetes iliae

Drypetes iliae, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Drypetes iliae is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture-making.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
