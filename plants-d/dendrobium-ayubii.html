Dendrobium ayubii

Dendrobium ayubii, commonly known as the Ayubii orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing aerial pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant typically features long, linear leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, tropical environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining substrate.

Dendrobium ayubii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
