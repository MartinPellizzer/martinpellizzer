Desmoncus brevisectus

Desmoncus brevisectus, commonly known as the short-segmented desmoncus, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It typically features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic, a common trait among plants in its family. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae group. Its growth habit is usually herbaceous, with a preference for moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Desmoncus brevisectus is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
