Ditaxis illimaniensis

Ditaxis illimaniensis, commonly known as the illimani ditaxis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a common feature among members of the Fabaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
