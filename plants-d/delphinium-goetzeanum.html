Delphinium goetzeanum

Delphinium goetzeanum, commonly known as the Goetze's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear whorled leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, each with five petals and a distinctive spurred lower petal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
