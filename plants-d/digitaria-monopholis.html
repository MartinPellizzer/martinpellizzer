Digitaria monopholis

Digitaria monopholis, commonly known as slender digitgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in humid environments and its tolerance to periodic flooding.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses.
