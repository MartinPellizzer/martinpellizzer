Diospyros exsculpta

Diospyros exsculpta, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits. The fruit has a sweet, edible pulp with a gritty texture, and the tree is often valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
