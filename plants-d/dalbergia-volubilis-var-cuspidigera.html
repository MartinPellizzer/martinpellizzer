Dalbergia volubilis var. cuspidigera

Dalbergia volubilis var. cuspidigera, commonly known as the Cuban rosewood, is a climbing legume native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean.

It features compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant's woody stems are characterized by a dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued in traditional craftsmanship. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental and commercial value, it is often cultivated in gardens and for timber production.
