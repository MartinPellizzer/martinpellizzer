Diosma cerefolia

Diosma cerefolia, commonly known as the white vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 60 cm in height, with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Diosma cerefolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
