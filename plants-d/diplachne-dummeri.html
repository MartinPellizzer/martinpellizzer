Diplachne dummeri

Diplachne dummeri, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers, typical of grasses. Diplachne dummeri thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
