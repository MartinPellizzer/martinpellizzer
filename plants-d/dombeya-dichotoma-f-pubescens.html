Dombeya dichotoma f. pubescens

Dombeya dichotoma f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinctive tubular shape.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in gardens.
