Draba arctogena

Draba arctogena, commonly known as the alpine whitlow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba arctogena is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor environments and is often found in tundra and rocky slopes.
