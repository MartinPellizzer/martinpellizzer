Diplacus glutinosus var. aurantiacus

Diplacus glutinosus var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the sticky monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which produce a sticky substance. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers in raceme clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
