Drypetes integerrima

Drypetes integerrima, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The tree is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
