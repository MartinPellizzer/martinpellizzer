Drosera bicolor

Drosera bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap insects. The plant's leaves are typically red or orange on the upper surface and green underneath. It thrives in wet, boggy environments and is known for its ability to digest captured prey.

Drosera bicolor is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
