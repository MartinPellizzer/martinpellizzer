Dipterocarpus humeratus

Dipterocarpus humeratus, commonly known as the yellow dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and thick, flaky bark. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing winged seeds. It plays a vital role in the ecological systems of tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The wood of Dipterocarpus humeratus is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
