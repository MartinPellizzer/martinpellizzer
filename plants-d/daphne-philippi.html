Daphne philippi

Daphne philippi, commonly known as the Philippi daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is cultivated in many botanical gardens around the world.
