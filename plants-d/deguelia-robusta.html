Deguelia robusta

Deguelia robusta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and research purposes.
