Dypsis louvelii

Dypsis louvelii, commonly known as the louvelii palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor spaces.

Dypsis louvelii is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid climates with well-draining soil.
