Derris kingdonwardii

Derris kingdonwardii, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has been used traditionally as a natural insecticide. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal and pesticidal properties.

Derris kingdonwardii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
