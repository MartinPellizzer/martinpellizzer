Dysoxylum macrothyrsum

Dysoxylum macrothyrsum, commonly known as the broad-leaved dysoxylum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is thick and rugged, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its hard timber and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
