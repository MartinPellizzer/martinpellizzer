Dianthus myrtinervius subsp. caespitosus

Dianthus myrtinervius subsp.

caespitosus, commonly known as the woolly pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features dense, mat-forming growth with compact, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, woolly coating.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers with five petals, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine displays.
