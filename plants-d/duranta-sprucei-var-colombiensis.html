Duranta sprucei var. colombiensis

Duranta sprucei var. colombiensis, commonly known as the Colombian Duranta, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
