Diasperus ornatus

Diasperus ornatus, commonly known as the ornate sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Diasperus ornatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
