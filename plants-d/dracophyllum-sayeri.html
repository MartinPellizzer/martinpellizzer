Dracophyllum sayeri

Dracophyllum sayeri, commonly known as the Sayer's draco, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive aromatic scent, often described as spicy or minty.

Dracophyllum sayeri is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal and alpine environments.
