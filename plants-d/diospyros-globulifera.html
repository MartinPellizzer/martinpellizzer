Diospyros globulifera

Diospyros globulifera, commonly known as the round-fruit ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange to red in color when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy surface.

Diospyros globulifera is often found in lowland forests and thrives in humid, tropical climates.
