Delosperma versicolor

Delosperma versicolor, commonly known as the ice plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and yellow. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Delosperma versicolor is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through runners, forming dense mats of foliage.
