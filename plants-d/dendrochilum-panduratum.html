Dendrochilum panduratum

Dendrochilum panduratum, commonly known as the panduratum orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrochilum panduratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
