Disaccanthus arizonicus

Disaccanthus arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.

Disaccanthus arizonicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
