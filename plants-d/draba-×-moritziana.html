Draba × moritziana

Draba × moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's rockcress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is typically found in alpine and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Draba × moritziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
