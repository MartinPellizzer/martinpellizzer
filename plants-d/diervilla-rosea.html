Diervilla rosea

Diervilla rosea, commonly known as roseburning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
