Duosperma latifolium

Duosperma latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. Its leaves are typically broad and oval-shaped, and the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Duosperma latifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
