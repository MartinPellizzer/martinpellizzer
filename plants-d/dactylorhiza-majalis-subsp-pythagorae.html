Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. pythagorae

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

pythagorae, commonly known as the Pythagorean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale green to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. It has a complex root system with specialized structures called pseudobulbs.

This orchid is notable for its ecological significance and is often found in calcareous soils.
