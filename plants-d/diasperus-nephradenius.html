Diasperus nephradenius

Diasperus nephradenius, commonly known as the kidney bean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces kidney-shaped pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit.

The species is valued both for its nutritional seeds and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
