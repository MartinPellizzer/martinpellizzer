Dortmanna warszewiczii

Dortmanna warszewiczii, commonly known as the Polish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem.

Dortmanna warszewiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
