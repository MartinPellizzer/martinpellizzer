Dioscorea piauhyensis

Dioscorea piauhyensis, commonly known as the Piauhy yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that support its large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces edible tubers that are used in traditional cuisine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
