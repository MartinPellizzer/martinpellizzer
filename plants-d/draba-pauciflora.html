Draba pauciflora

Draba pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in alpine and arctic environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba pauciflora is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
