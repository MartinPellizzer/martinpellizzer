Dendrobium phalaenopsis var. chamberlainianum

Dendrobium phalaenopsis var. chamberlainianum, commonly known as the Chamberlain's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several feet in length, often adorned with clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The blooms are typically white or pale pink with a subtle, sweet aroma, and they appear during the warmer months. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty in gardens and indoor spaces.

It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.
