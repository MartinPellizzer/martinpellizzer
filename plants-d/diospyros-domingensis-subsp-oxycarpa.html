Diospyros domingensis subsp. oxycarpa

Diospyros domingensis subsp.

oxycarpa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees.

The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a sweet, edible persimmon with a smooth, orange-red skin and a soft, honey-like pulp.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
