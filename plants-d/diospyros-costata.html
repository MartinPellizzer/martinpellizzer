Diospyros costata

Diospyros costata, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to reddish fruit with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Diospyros costata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
