Daphne cneorum subsp. cneorum

Daphne cneorum subsp.

cneorum, commonly known as the creeping daphne, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, compact foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that are often fragrant.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover.
