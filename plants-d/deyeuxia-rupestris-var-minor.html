Deyeuxia rupestris var. minor

Deyeuxia rupestris var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf rock sedge, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths close to the ground, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and can withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for rocky or gravelly habitats, where it often grows in clusters.
