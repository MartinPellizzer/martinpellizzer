Diospyros andersonii

Diospyros andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface.

The fruit is a small, orange to reddish berry that ripens in the autumn, and it is edible when fully mature.
