Dianthus brachyzonus subsp. villiger

Dianthus brachyzonus subsp.

villiger, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
