Dactylorhiza incarnata f. haematodes

Dactylorhiza incarnata f. haematodes, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers, which are often tinged with pink or orange, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a pseudobulbous structure with lance-shaped leaves and produces a spike of showy, fragrant flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
