Dactylorhiza latifolia subsp. occidentalis

Dactylorhiza latifolia subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western broad-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces tall, branched stems bearing numerous showy, violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
