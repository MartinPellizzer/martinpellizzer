Dorycnium herbaceum var. macedonicum

Dorycnium herbaceum var. macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian sainfoin, is a low-growing, perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs and possesses trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
