Desmanthus diffusus

Desmanthus diffusus, commonly known as the hairy desmanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its seeds are encased in pods and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Desmanthus diffusus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial plant for ecological restoration projects.
