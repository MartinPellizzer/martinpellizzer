Dianthus moviensis subsp. kirkii

Dianthus moviensis subsp.

kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's campion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
