Delphinium hybridum var. hirtulum

Delphinium hybridum var. hirtulum, commonly known as the woolly delphinium, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
