Disa fallax

Disa fallax, commonly known as the yellow disa, is a striking South African orchid species native to the Eastern Cape and Western Cape provinces.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive violet lip. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the late summer to early autumn months, adding a burst of color to the landscape.

Disa fallax is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
