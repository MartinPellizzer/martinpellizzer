Draba amoena

Draba amoena, commonly known as the slender whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a hairy texture.

Draba amoena is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members such as cabbages and mustards.
