Drosera × carbarup

Drosera × carbarup, commonly known as the hybrid sundew, is a carnivorous plant resulting from the cross between Drosera binata and Drosera adelae.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs on the leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments and thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are often rosette-shaped and can vary in color from green to reddish depending on light exposure.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
