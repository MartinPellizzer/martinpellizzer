Diuris longifolia

Diuris longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid species characterized by its intricate floral structure and striking appearance.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, spider-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Diuris longifolia is a perennial herb, often found in the eastern and southern regions of Australia.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species within the orchid family.
