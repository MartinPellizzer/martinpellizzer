Dypsis canaliculata

Dypsis canaliculata, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that resemble the trunk of a palm tree, although it is not a true palm. The plant has a rosette of spiky, dark green leaves that are arranged in a compact, bushy shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish fruits.

Dypsis canaliculata is drought-tolerant and thrives in bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
