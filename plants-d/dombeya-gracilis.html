Dombeya gracilis

Dombeya gracilis, commonly known as slender dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and attractive foliage.
