Diuris elongata

Diuris elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf greenhood, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a distinctive greenish-purple flower spike. The flowers are typically small, with a unique hood-like structure that shelters the reproductive organs. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils in open habitats, often in full sun to partial shade.

This species is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
