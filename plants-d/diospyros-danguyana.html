Diospyros danguyana

Diospyros danguyana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
