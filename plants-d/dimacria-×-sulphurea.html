Dimacria × sulphurea

Dimacria × sulphurea, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with upright, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to thrive in a variety of climatic conditions.
