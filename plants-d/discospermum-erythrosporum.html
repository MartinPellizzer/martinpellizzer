Discospermum erythrosporum

Discospermum erythrosporum, commonly known as the red-fruited star apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. The berries contain a single, large, white or yellow seed surrounded by a juicy, aromatic pulp.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
