Diphysa sennoides

Diphysa sennoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
