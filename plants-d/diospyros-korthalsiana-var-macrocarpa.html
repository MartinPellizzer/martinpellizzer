Diospyros korthalsiana var. macrocarpa

Diospyros korthalsiana var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces large, orange to red, edible fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and fruit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also noted for its potential in agroforestry systems due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
