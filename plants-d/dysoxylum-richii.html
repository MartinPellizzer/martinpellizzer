Dysoxylum richii

Dysoxylum richii, commonly known as the white-wood or yellow-wood tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
