Diospyros lunduensis

Diospyros lunduensis, commonly known as the Lunda date palm, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically reddish or yellowish when mature, and is edible. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local communities.
