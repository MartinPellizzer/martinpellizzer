Draba furcata

Draba furcata, commonly known as the forked rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant has basal leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

Draba furcata is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
