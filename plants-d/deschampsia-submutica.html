Deschampsia submutica

Deschampsia submutica, commonly known as silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and streambanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Deschampsia submutica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
