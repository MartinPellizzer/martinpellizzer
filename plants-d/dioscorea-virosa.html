Dioscorea virosa

Dioscorea virosa, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and industrial purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and develops capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers contain compounds such as diosgenin, which is used in the synthesis of steroid hormones.

Dioscorea virosa is also valued for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
