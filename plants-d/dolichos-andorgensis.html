Dolichos andorgensis

Dolichos andorgensis, commonly known as the Andorgens wild bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Andes region.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its historical significance in traditional medicine, it has garnered interest for potential medicinal properties.
