Drymocallis kulabensis

Drymocallis kulabensis, commonly known as the Kulab goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, displaying yellow flower clusters in the late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
