Dendrochilum acuiferum

Dendrochilum acuiferum, commonly known as the water orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive spurred lip.

Dendrochilum acuiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, shaded environments.
