Deyeuxia forsteri

Deyeuxia forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. The plant has slender, tufted culms and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Deyeuxia forsteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
