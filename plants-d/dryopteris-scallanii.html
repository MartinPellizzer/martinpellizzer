Dryopteris scallanii

Dryopteris scallanii, commonly known as the Scalloped Wood Fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed fronds that resemble a scalloped edge, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to two feet.

Dryopteris scallanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded garden settings.
