Drepanostachyum fractiflexum

Drepanostachyum fractiflexum, commonly known as the flexible bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, flexible stems that can bend without breaking. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
