Dischidia atropurpurea

Dischidia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-stemmed fig.

It is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thick, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are usually dark green with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, red or orange berries.

This species is often grown as a houseplant or in hanging baskets due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
