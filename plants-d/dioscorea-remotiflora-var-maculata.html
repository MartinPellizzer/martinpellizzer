Dioscorea remotiflora var. maculata

Dioscorea remotiflora var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage and tubers are notable for their ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

The varietal name "maculata" refers to the spotted or mottled patterns on its leaves.
