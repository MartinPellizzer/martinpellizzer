Dendrobium cacuminis

Dendrobium cacuminis, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cane-like stems and clusters of showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its flowers bloom in summer and are known for their distinctive, spike-like arrangement.

Dendrobium cacuminis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
