Delphinium wendelboi

Delphinium wendelboi, commonly known as Wendelbo's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of showy blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in cooler climates.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
