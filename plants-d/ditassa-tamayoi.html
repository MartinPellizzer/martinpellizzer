Ditassa tamayoi

Ditassa tamayoi, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and its delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves and a short, erect stem with multiple flowering spikes.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
