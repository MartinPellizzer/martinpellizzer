Desmodium luteocanescens var. curtum

Desmodium luteocanescens var. curtum, commonly known as the yellow-hair vetch, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant is typically found in open woodland and savanna habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and soil enrichment.
