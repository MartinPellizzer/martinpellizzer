Diplusodon marginatus

Diplusodon marginatus, commonly known as the marginated dipterocarp, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and thick, flaky bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species plays an important ecological role in rainforest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
