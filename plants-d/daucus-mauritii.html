Daucus mauritii

Daucus mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius carrot, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

The fruit is a small, dry schizocarp that splits into two one-seeded parts when mature.
