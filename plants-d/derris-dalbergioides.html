Derris dalbergioides

Derris dalbergioides, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant plays a role in local ecosystems as both a food source and a traditional medicinal plant.
