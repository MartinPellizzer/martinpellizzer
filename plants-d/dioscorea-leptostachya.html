Dioscorea leptostachya

Dioscorea leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stamined yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are capsule-like structures containing numerous seeds. Dioscorea leptostachya is valued for its edible tubers, which are rich in starch and used in traditional cuisines.

However, it contains toxic compounds and requires careful processing before consumption.
