Desmanthus acuminatus

Desmanthus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender burr legume, is a perennial forage plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces hairy, spiny pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in erosion control and livestock forage.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
