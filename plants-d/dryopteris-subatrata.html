Dryopteris subatrata

Dryopteris subatrata, commonly known as the black fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, fronds that are typically glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with closely spaced, lance-shaped pinnae.

Dryopteris subatrata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
