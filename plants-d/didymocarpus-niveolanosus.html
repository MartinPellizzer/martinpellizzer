Didymocarpus niveolanosus

Didymocarpus niveolanosus, commonly known as the white-veined tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with white veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
