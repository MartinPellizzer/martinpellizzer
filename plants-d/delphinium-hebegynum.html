Delphinium hebegynum

Delphinium hebegynum, commonly known as the meadow larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are showy, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Delphinium hebegynum is also noted for its toxicity, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
