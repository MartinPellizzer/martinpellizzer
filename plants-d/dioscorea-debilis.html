Dioscorea debilis

Dioscorea debilis, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its thin, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible tuberous roots, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has also been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
