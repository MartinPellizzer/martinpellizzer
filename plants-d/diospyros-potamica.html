Diospyros potamica

Diospyros potamica, commonly known as the river date or African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streambanks.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, fleshy pulp and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
