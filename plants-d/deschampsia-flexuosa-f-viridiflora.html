Deschampsia flexuosa f. viridiflora

Deschampsia flexuosa f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered bentgrass, is a variant of the bentgrass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and distinctive greenish-blue flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curled, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This form is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
