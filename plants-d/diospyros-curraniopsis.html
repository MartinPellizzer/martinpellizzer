Diospyros curraniopsis

Diospyros curraniopsis, commonly known as the Socotra date palm, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Socotra.

It is a small, shrub-like plant with thick, fleshy leaves and produces small, red fruits that are edible. The plant has adapted to the arid and rocky environment of Socotra through specialized root systems and water-storing tissues.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
