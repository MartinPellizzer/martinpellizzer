Draba rhizobotyya

Draba rhizobotyya, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba rhizobotyya is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
