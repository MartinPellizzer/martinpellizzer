Disa recurvata

Disa recurvata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive zigzag pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Disa recurvata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
