Descurainia tanacetifolia subsp. suffruticosa

Descurainia tanacetifolia subsp.

suffruticosa, commonly known as yellow rocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous species. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
