Dyssodia cupulata

Dyssodia cupulata, commonly known as the cupulate dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a short, thick stem with dense, woolly foliage that helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Dyssodia cupulata is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
