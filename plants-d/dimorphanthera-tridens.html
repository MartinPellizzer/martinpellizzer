Dimorphanthera tridens

Dimorphanthera tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring three prominent teeth on the labellum. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a complex arrangement that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Dimorphanthera tridens is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
