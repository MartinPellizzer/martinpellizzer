Dioscorea racemosa var. hoffmannii

Dioscorea racemosa var. hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This variant is often cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal applications.
