Dicentra torulosa

Dicentra torulosa, commonly known as the zigzag poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive zigzag pattern along the edges.

Dicentra torulosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
