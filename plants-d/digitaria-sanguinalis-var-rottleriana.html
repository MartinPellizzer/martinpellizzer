Digitaria sanguinalis var. rottleriana

Digitaria sanguinalis var. rottleriana, commonly known as Rottler's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and riparian zones.

Botanically, it is noted for its rhizomatous growth and adaptability to varying soil conditions.
