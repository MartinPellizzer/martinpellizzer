Dorycnium hirsutum var. tomentosum

Dorycnium hirsutum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
