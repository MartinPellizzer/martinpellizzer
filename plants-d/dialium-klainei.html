Dialium klainei

Dialium klainei, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to West and Central Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sweet-smelling fruits. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

The species also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
