Dactylorhiza × szaboiana

Dactylorhiza × szaboiana, commonly known as the Szabo's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or marshy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
