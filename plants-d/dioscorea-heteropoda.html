Dioscorea heteropoda

Dioscorea heteropoda, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
