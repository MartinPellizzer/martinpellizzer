Doryopteris argentea

Doryopteris argentea, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, silvery-white fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense forests. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Doryopteris argentea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
