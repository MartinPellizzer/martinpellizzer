Dryopteris sciaphila var. raivavensis

Dryopteris sciaphila var. raivavensis, commonly known as the Rava Valley shield fern, is a rare and endemic fern species native to the mountainous regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, intricate venation pattern. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing in forest undergrowth where it can receive adequate humidity and indirect light. Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural range, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection.
