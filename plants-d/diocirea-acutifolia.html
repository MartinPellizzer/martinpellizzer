Diocirea acutifolia

Diocirea acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved diocirea, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
