Dyschoriste siphonantha var. lanata

Dyschoriste siphonantha var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly dyschoriste, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, or lanate, foliage. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
