Dactylostemon klotzschii var. tenuifolius

Dactylostemon klotzschii var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved klotzsch dactylostemon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and compact growth habit.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
