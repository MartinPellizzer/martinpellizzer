Dombeya oblongifolia

Dombeya oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Dombeya oblongifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
