Daphniphyllum macropodum subsp. humile

Daphniphyllum macropodum subsp.

humile, commonly known as the small-leaved winterberry, is a evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow, followed by red berries that persist through the winter. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique, hardy evergreen plant.
