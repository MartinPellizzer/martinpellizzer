Drimyspermum pubiflorum

Drimyspermum pubiflorum, commonly known as the woolly-flowered drimys, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a faintly fragrant scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty plant collections.
