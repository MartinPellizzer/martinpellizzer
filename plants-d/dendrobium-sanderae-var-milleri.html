Dendrobium sanderae var. milleri

Dendrobium sanderae var. milleri, commonly known as the Milli's Dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without being parasitic. The plant produces long, cane-like stems that can reach several feet in length. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive, intricate pattern.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
