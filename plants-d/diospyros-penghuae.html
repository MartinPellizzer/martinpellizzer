Diospyros penghuae

Diospyros penghuae, commonly known as the Guangxi date palm, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, and it is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
