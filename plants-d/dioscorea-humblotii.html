Dioscorea humblotii

Dioscorea humblotii, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are edible. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
