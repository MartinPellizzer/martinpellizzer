Diospyros bezofensis

Diospyros bezofensis, commonly known as the Bezoar ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony trees known for their dense, dark wood. The plant features elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. Its small, white flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow when mature.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood, which is used in craftsmanship and traditional medicine.
