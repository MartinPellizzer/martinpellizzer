Dactylis glomerata var. sardoa

Dactylis glomerata var. sardoa, commonly known as Sardinian goatgrass, is a variant of the common goatgrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Europe and parts of North Africa. This plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers.

Dactylis glomerata var. sardoa is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
