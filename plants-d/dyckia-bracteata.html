Dyckia bracteata

Dyckia bracteata, commonly known as the silver fountain grass, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, upright rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Dyckia bracteata is a popular ornamental plant due to its elegant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
