Dymczewiczia capitata

Dymczewiczia capitata, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which is characteristic of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

Dymczewiczia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
