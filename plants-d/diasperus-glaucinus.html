Diasperus glaucinus

Diasperus glaucinus, commonly known as the blue-green spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its succulent, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, which are modified leaf-like structures. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

Diasperus glaucinus is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
