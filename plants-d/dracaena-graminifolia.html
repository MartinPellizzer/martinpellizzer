Dracaena graminifolia

Dracaena graminifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf dracaena, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters at the top of the stem. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.

Dracaena graminifolia prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
