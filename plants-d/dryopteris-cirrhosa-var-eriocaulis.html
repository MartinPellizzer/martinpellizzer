Dryopteris cirrhosa var. eriocaulis

Dryopteris cirrhosa var. eriocaulis, commonly known as the silver fern, is a variant of the sword fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray fronds that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a graceful, arching growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
