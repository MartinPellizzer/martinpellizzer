Dioscorea campos-portoi

Dioscorea campos-portoi, commonly known as the Campos-Portoi yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
