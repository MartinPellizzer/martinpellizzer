Diospyros singaporensis

Diospyros singaporensis, commonly known as the Singapore persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange to reddish in color when mature. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
