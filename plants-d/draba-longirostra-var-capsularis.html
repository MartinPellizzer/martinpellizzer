Draba longirostra var. capsularis

Draba longirostra var. capsularis, commonly known as the narrow-beaked rockcress, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated floral structure and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia.
