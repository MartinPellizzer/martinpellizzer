Diceratella incana

Diceratella incana, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native range.
