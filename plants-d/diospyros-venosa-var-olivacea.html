Diospyros venosa var. olivacea

Diospyros venosa var. olivacea, commonly known as the olive persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit is typically a yellow-orange color when ripe, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
