Dichanthium queenslandicum

Dichanthium queenslandicum, commonly known as the Queensland grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
