Dioclea violacea

Dioclea violacea, commonly known as the violet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling those of other passionflowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a fleshy, red or orange berry that ripens in late summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
