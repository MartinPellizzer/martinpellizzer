Dahlia royleana

Dahlia royleana, commonly known as the Royal dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It produces showy, single or double flowers in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, orange, and yellow. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Dahlia royleana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
