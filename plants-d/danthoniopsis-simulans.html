Danthoniopsis simulans

Danthoniopsis simulans, commonly known as the slender danthoniopsis, is a low-growing, mat-forming grass native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a dense, tufted appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
