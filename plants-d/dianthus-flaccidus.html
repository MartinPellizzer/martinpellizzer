Dianthus flaccidus

Dianthus flaccidus, commonly known as the drooping carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the stems are usually prostrate or ascending.

Dianthus flaccidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and prolonged blooming period.
