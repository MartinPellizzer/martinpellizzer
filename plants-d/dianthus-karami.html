Dianthus karami

Dianthus karami, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Dianthus karami is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
