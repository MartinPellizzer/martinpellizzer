Desmodium appressipilum

Desmodium appressipilum, commonly known as the small-leaved burr legume, is a perennial forage plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in pastureland due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Its growth habit makes it a valuable species for erosion control and soil stabilization.
