Drosera curviscapa var. esterhuyseniae

Drosera curviscapa var. esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysenia's sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are typically arranged in a cluster. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

This variety is noted for its unique leaf morphology and is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
