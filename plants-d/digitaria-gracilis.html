Digitaria gracilis

Digitaria gracilis, commonly known as slender digitgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Digitaria gracilis is an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
