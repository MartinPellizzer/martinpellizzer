Daucus littoralis subsp. hyrcanicus

Daucus littoralis subsp.

hyrcanicus, commonly known as the coastal carrot, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils along the shores of lakes and seas. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
