Dendrochilum cymbiforme

Dendrochilum cymbiforme, commonly known as the cymbiform orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Dendrochilum cymbiforme is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
