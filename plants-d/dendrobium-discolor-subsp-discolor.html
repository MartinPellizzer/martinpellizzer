Dendrobium discolor subsp. discolor

Dendrobium discolor subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking white and green striped leaves and its fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It has a pseudobulbous structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
