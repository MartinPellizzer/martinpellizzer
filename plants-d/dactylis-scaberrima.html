Dactylis scaberrima

Dactylis scaberrima, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are arranged in panicles. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Dactylis scaberrima is adapted to arid conditions and is resistant to drought and grazing.
