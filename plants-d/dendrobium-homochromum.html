Dendrobium homochromum

Dendrobium homochromum, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, producing new shoots from the base of the previous ones. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Dendrobium homochromum thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
