Dicoryphe buddlejoides

Dicoryphe buddlejoides, commonly known as the silverleaf false buddleia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are elliptical, with a silvery-gray underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Dicoryphe buddlejoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
