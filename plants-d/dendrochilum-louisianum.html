Dendrochilum louisianum

Dendrochilum louisianum, commonly known as the Louisiana tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamp forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, while the flowers are usually white or pale green with a distinctive lip.

Dendrochilum louisianum is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
