Dendrobium calcaratimentum

Dendrobium calcaratimentum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Dendrobium calcaratimentum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
