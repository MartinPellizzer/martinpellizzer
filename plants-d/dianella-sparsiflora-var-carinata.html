Dianella sparsiflora var. carinata

Dianella sparsiflora var. carinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved forest ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
