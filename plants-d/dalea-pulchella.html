Dalea pulchella

Dalea pulchella, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea pulchella plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
