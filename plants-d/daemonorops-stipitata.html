Daemonorops stipitata

Daemonorops stipitata, commonly known as the hairy palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are often covered in a dense layer of white, hairy tissue, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
