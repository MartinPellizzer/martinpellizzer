Delphinium rutoense

Delphinium rutoense, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture, adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments.

Delphinium rutoense is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
