Dinochloa mastersii

Dinochloa mastersii, commonly known as the giant swamp grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. This plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks. Its dense growth habit makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Dinochloa mastersii is also valued for its strong, durable fibers, which have been used in traditional crafts and construction.
