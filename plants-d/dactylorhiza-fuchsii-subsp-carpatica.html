Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp. carpatica

Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and is characterized by its erect, branched stems.

The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
