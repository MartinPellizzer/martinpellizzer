Dennstaedtia glauca

Dennstaedtia glauca, commonly known as the blue fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bluish-green fronds that are typically divided into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

The blue fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
