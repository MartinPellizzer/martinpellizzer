Dioclea erecta

Dioclea erecta, commonly known as the climbing passionflower, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and ability to climb using tendrils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with five sepals and five petals, often with a distinctive crown of stamens. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains multiple seeds.

Dioclea erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its beauty.
