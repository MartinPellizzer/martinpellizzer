Dioscorea huberi

Dioscorea huberi, commonly known as the Huber yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by cylindrical, yellowish fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Dioscorea huberi is valued both for its nutritional content and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
