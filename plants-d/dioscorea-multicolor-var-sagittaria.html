Dioscorea multicolor var. sagittaria

Dioscorea multicolor var. sagittaria, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, arrow-shaped fruits. Its tubers are often used as a food source, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful processing.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in traditional agriculture and medicine.
