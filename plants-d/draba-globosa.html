Draba globosa

Draba globosa, commonly known as the globular whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Draba globosa is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.
