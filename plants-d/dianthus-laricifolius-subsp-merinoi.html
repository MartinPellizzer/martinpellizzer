Dianthus laricifolius subsp. merinoi

Dianthus laricifolius subsp.

merinoi, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are needle-like, resembling those of fir trees, which contributes to its scientific name.

It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
