Delphinium simplex var. distichiflorum

Delphinium simplex var. distichiflorum, commonly known as the "double-flowered delphinium," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers, which are typically double or semi-double in form. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, arranged in a basal rosette and along the stem. This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens, particularly in borders and cottage gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
