Didymodoxa caffra

Didymodoxa caffra, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Didymodoxa caffra is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms, making it a popular choice in gardens and botanical collections.
