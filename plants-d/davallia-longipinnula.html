Davallia longipinnula

Davallia longipinnula, commonly known as the long-leaf hakea, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and elegant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
