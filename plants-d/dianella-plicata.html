Dianella plicata

Dianella plicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flax lily, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly rolled. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Dianella plicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
