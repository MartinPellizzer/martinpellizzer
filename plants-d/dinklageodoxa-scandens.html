Dinklageodoxa scandens

Dinklageodoxa scandens, commonly known as the climbing dinklageodoxa, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and twining stems that allow it to ascend supports. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in warm climates due to its aesthetic appeal.
