Daviesia suaveolens

Daviesia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented Daviesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds for propagation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
