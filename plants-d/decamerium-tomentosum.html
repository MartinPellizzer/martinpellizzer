Decamerium tomentosum

Decamerium tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly decamerium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a woody stem with persistent, overlapping leaves.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
