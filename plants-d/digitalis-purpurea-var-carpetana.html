Digitalis purpurea var. carpetana

Digitalis purpurea var. carpetana, commonly known as the Woolly Foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces tall spikes of tubular flowers, typically in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its historical use in medicine.
