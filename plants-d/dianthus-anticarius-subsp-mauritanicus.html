Dianthus anticarius subsp. mauritanicus

Dianthus anticarius subsp.

mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania carnation, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red petals that resemble those of the carnation.

The plant produces numerous small, showy flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
