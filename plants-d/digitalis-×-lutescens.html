Digitalis × lutescens

Digitalis × lutescens, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
