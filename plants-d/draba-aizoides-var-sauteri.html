Draba aizoides var. sauteri

Draba aizoides var. sauteri, commonly known as the Sauter's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 15 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It is native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and Alps.

This species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in open, sunny habitats.
