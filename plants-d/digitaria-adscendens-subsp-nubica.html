Digitaria adscendens subsp. nubica

Digitaria adscendens subsp.

nubica, commonly known as the African grass or mountain bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a paniculate cluster of spikelets, which are small and clustered closely together.

This grass is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used for forage or soil stabilization.
