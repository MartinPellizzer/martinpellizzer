Dianthus multiaffinis

Dianthus multiaffinis, commonly known as the multiflora carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus multiaffinis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
