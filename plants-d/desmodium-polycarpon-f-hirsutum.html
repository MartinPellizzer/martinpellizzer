Desmodium polycarpon f. hirsutum

Desmodium polycarpon f. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy desmodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "hirsutum.". It has a fibrous root system and often grows in moist, well-drained soils.

The hairy desmodium is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
