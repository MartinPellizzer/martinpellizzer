Diospyros sabtanensis

Diospyros sabtanensis, commonly known as the Sabtan ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony wood species. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy green appearance.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, round fruits that are typically eaten by local wildlife.
