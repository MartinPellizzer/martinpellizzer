Dendrocalamus membranaceus

Dendrocalamus membranaceus, commonly known as the giant bamboo or bamboo giant, is a tall, fast-growing grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, segmented stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and serve as the primary means of vegetative propagation.

This species is highly valued for its strong, lightweight material, which is used in construction and handicrafts.
