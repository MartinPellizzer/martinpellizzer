Deschampsia refracta

Deschampsia refracta, commonly known as the bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, pale green blades. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues.

Deschampsia refracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
