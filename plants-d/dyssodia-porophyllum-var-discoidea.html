Dyssodia porophyllum var. discoidea

Dyssodia porophyllum var. discoidea, commonly known as the silverleaf dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight and reduces water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions.
