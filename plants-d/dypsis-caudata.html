Dypsis caudata

Dypsis caudata, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, tree-like appearance with a thick, swollen trunk and clusters of long, slender leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The botanical features of Dypsis caudata make it well-suited for both indoor and outdoor gardening in warm climates.
