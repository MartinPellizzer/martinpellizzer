Diosma vestita

Diosma vestita, commonly known as the naked Diosma, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Diosma vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
