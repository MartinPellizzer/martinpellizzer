Dichanthelium cucaense

Dichanthelium cucaense, commonly known as the Cuca grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midvein.

The inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae.
