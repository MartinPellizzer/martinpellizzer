Dioscorea guianensis

Dioscorea guianensis, commonly known as arrowroot, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are rich in starch and are widely used as a thickening agent in cooking.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
