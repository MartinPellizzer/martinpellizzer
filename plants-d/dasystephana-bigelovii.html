Dasystephana bigelovii

Dasystephana bigelovii, commonly known as the desert holly, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its succulent leaves and deep root system, allowing it to store water efficiently. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is an important plant in desert ecosystems.
