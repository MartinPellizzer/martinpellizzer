Draba setosa

Draba setosa, commonly known as the silky whitlow, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Draba setosa is known for its ability to survive in harsh climates due to its compact structure and efficient water retention.
