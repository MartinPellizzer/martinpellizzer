Desmotrichum integrilabium

Desmotrichum integrilabium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow, smooth, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
