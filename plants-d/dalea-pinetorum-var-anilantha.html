Dalea pinetorum var. anilantha

Dalea pinetorum var. anilantha, commonly known as the pink mountain clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a component of native plant communities.
