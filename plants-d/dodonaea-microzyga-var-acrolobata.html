Dodonaea microzyga var. acrolobata

Dodonaea microzyga var. acrolobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

The variety is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
