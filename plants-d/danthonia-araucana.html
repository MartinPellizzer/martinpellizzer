Danthonia araucana

Danthonia araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-gray in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in various ecological settings.

Danthonia araucana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
