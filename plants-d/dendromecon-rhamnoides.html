Dendromecon rhamnoides

Dendromecon rhamnoides, commonly known as the desert candle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring a woody stem with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, linear leaflets, and its flowers are arranged in dense, candle-like clusters. It is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry climates.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
