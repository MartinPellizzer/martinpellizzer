Dioscorea bosseri

Dioscorea bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary economic product. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The tubers are used as a food source and have been traditionally employed in various medicinal practices.
