Desmodium cordifoliolatum

Desmodium cordifoliolatum, commonly known as heart-leaved desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are composed of three to five heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Desmodium cordifoliolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
