Dischidiopsis carinata

Dischidiopsis carinata, commonly known as the African nettle, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Dischidiopsis carinata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
