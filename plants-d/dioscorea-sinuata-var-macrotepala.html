Dioscorea sinuata var. macrotepala

Dioscorea sinuata var. macrotepala, commonly known as the giant waxy vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is part of the Dioscoreaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. Its tubers, while not widely cultivated, have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
