Dodonaea cuneata var. coriacea

Dodonaea cuneata var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, coriaceous (leathery) leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its small, woody fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
