Dalea galbina

Dalea galbina, commonly known as the pink prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, cylindrical seed pods that mature in late summer.

Dalea galbina is adapted to prairie and dry woodland habitats, and it plays an important role in supporting pollinators and soil health.
