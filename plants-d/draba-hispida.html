Draba hispida

Draba hispida, commonly known as the hispid wintercress, is a small, flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species, and is characterized by its low-growing habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods containing seeds. Draba hispida is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in cold climates, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
