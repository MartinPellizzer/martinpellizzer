Dioscorea celebesiana

Dioscorea celebesiana, commonly known as the Celebes yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized in various culinary and medicinal practices.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and requires careful handling during cultivation and consumption.
