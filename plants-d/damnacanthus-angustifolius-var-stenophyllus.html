Damnacanthus angustifolius var. stenophyllus

Damnacanthus angustifolius var. stenophyllus, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
