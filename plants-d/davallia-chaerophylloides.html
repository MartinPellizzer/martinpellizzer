Davallia chaerophylloides

Davallia chaerophylloides, commonly known as the silver-veined maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, arching fronds with a distinctive silvery or pale green coloration on the underside. The plant has a unique ability to form a dense, cushion-like rosette of leaves, which helps it thrive in shaded, humid environments. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients.

This fern is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
