Distephanus streptocladus

Distephanus streptocladus, commonly known as the red-flowered daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking red petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
