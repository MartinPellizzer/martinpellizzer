Dendrobium bipulvinatum

Dendrobium bipulvinatum, commonly known as the two-padded dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a distinctive two-lobed pulvinus at the base of each node. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters.

It thrives in high humidity and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
