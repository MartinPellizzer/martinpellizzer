Diospyros ledermannii

Diospyros ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries.

Its fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is valued for both its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
