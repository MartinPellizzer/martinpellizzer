Drosera violacea

Drosera violacea, commonly known as the purple rose sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, sticky leaves that secrete a mucilaginous substance to trap insects. The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and requires consistent humidity to flourish.

Drosera violacea is a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
