Diplazium cristatum

Diplazium cristatum, commonly known as the crowned fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, upright fronds with a crown-like appearance at the base. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats in shaded, moist environments. Diplazium cristatum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded forest floors.
