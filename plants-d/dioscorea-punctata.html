Dioscorea punctata

Dioscorea punctata, commonly known as the spotted yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its tubers are often harvested for their starchy content and have a slightly bitter taste.

Dioscorea punctata is also valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic effects.
