Drimia gawleri

Drimia gawleri, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
