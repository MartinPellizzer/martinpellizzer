Diplotaxis hirsuta

Diplotaxis hirsuta, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbages. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose inflorescences, typical of the family.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
