Doronicum grandiflorum var. viscosum

Doronicum grandiflorum var. viscosum, commonly known as the sticky yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy, often sticky texture. The plant produces striking, showy blue to violet flowers that are typically tubular with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
