Dioscorea multicolor var. melanoleuca

Dioscorea multicolor var. melanoleuca, commonly known as the black-and-white yam, is a variant of the yam plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with alternating black and white patterns on the leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its growth habit is typically vine-like, with the stems climbing or trailing along the ground.

The botanical features of this plant make it a unique and visually appealing species within the genus Dioscorea.
