Dieudonnaea Rhizantha

Ridgeleaf sandwort, scientifically known as Dieudonnaea rhizantha, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. This low-growing species thrives in rocky soils and dry environments, often found in desert foothills and canyon walls. Its unique morphology includes small, clustered leaves and pale pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. Despite its hardiness, it faces threats from habitat loss and climate change, making conservation efforts critical for its survival. The plant's resilience and beauty make it a valuable subject for ecological study and botanical interest.
