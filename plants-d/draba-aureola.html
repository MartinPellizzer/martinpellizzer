Draba aureola

Draba aureola, commonly known as the golden draba, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to high-altitude landscapes.

Draba aureola is noted for its resilience to cold temperatures and is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations above 2,000 meters.
