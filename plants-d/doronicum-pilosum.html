Doronicum pilosum

Doronicum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy leopard's bane, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Doronicum pilosum prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
