Dipterocarpus retusus subsp. macrocarpus

Dipterocarpus retusus subsp.

macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its tall stature and buttressed roots. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain a single seed, and its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin.

It plays an important ecological role in rainforest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
