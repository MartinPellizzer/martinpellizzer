Dischistocalyx capitellatus

Dischistocalyx capitellatus, commonly known as the purple-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pentagonal-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Dischistocalyx capitellatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
