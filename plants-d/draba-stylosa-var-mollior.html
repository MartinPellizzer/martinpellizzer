Draba stylosa var. mollior

Draba stylosa var. mollior, commonly known as the pink-stemmed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, glaucous leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. It is native to the western United States and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

This variety is distinguished by its more robust and pink-tinged stems compared to other Draba species.
