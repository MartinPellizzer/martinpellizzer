Dianthus saetabensis subsp. saetabensis

Dianthus saetabensis subsp.

saetabensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals.

The leaves are linear and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
