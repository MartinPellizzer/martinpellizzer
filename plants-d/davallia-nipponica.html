Davallia nipponica

Davallia nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Japan and parts of China.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are typically light green with a finely textured appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to flourish. It produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small groups.

Davallia nipponica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant foliage and ease of care.
