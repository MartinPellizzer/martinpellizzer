Ditaxis erubescens

Ditaxis erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy stems that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
