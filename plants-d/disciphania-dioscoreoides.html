Disciphania dioscoreoides

Disciphania dioscoreoides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its tuberous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Disciphania dioscoreoides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
