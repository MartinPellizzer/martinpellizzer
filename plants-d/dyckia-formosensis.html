Dyckia formosensis

Dyckia formosensis, commonly known as the Formosa Dyckia, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth habit with tightly packed, sword-like leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
