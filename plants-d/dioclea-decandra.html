Dioclea decandra

Dioclea decandra, commonly known as the "Climbing Passionflower," is a woody vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit, with five petals and a crown-like structure. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that are often edible. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Dioclea decandra thrives in humid, warm climates and is commonly found in rainforests and along riverbanks.
