Dictyopteris hemiteliiformis

Dictyopteris hemiteliiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sea holly, is a marine brown alga found in coastal regions.

It has a distinctive morphology with a flattened, ribbon-like thallus that is often branching. The plant exhibits a complex life cycle involving both sexual and asexual reproduction. Its fronds are typically dark brown to black in color and can grow up to several centimeters in length.

It plays an important role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
