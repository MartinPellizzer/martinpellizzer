Disa bakeri

Disa bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's Disa, is a striking African orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

Disa bakeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique floral display.
