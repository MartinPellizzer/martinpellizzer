Diplostephium mutiscuanum

Diplostephium mutiscuanum, commonly known as the Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas and is often found in disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
