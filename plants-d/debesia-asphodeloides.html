Debesia asphodeloides

Debesia asphodeloides, commonly known as the Natal flatleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
