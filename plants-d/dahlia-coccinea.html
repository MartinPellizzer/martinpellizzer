Dahlia coccinea

Dahlia coccinea, commonly known as the crimson dahlia, is a vibrant flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double blooms in shades of red. The plant features tuberous roots and upright stems that support its large, colorful flowers. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to the bright blossoms.

Dahlia coccinea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
