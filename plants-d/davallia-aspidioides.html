Davallia aspidioides

Davallia aspidioides, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, dense fronds that form a rosette shape, with each frond divided into smaller, lance-shaped segments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

Davallia aspidioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
