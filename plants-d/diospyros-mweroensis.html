Diospyros mweroensis

Diospyros mweroensis, commonly known as the Wero ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony and persimmon species. The tree is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by small, fleshy fruits.
