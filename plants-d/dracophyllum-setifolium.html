Dracophyllum setifolium

Dracophyllum setifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the eastern Australian region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Dracophyllum setifolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
