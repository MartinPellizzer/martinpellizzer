Dalbergia kingiana

Dalbergia kingiana, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences. It produces pods that contain flattened, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow in a variety of tropical habitats.
