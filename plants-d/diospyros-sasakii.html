Diospyros sasakii

Diospyros sasakii, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and large, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature, and it is edible when fully ripe.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the sweet, edible fruit it produces.
