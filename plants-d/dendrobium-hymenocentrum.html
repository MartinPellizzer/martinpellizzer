Dendrobium hymenocentrum

Dendrobium hymenocentrum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with numerous pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces long, pendant inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium hymenocentrum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
