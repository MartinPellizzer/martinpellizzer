Dracophyllum pubescens

Dracophyllum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round structure to the landscape.

Dracophyllum pubescens is well adapted to coastal and inland conditions, demonstrating resilience to drought and salt spray.
