Deiregyne eriophora

Deiregyne eriophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or shrubland habitats. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Deiregyne eriophora is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
