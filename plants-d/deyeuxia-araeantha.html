Deyeuxia araeantha

Deyeuxia araeantha, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Deyeuxia araeantha is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
