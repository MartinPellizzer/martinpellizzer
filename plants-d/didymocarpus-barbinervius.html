Didymocarpus barbinervius

Didymocarpus barbinervius, commonly known as the narrow-veined mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent parallel veins. The plant produces fragrant white flowers followed by edible, segmented fruits that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
