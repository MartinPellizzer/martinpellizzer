Drosera variegata

Drosera variegata, commonly known as the variegated sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a digestive enzyme-laden mucilage to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its leaves are often variegated, featuring a mix of green and red or pink hues.

Drosera variegata is a low-growing species that thrives in cool, humid conditions.
