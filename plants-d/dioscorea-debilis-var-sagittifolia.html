Dioscorea debilis var. sagittifolia

Dioscorea debilis var. sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, palmately lobed leaves, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is an important species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
