Decastelma trinitense

Decastelma trinitense, commonly known as the Trinidad morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Decastelma trinitense is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
