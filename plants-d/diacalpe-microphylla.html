Diacalpe microphylla

Diacalpe microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
