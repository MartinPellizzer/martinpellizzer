Dorstenia buchananii var. buchananii

Dorstenia buchananii var. buchananii, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
