Dendranthema × rubellum

Dendranthema × rubellum, commonly known as Shasta daisy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disk, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces showy flowers from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
