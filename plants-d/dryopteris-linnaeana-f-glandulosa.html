Dryopteris linnaeana f. glandulosa

Dryopteris linnaeana f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds with glandular hairs.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to North America. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance, and the undersides often bear small, glandular structures. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
