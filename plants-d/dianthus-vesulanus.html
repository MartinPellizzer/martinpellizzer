Dianthus vesulanus

Dianthus vesulanus, commonly known as the Alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Dianthus vesulanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It is also notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
