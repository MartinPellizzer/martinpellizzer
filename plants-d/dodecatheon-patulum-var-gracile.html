Dodecatheon patulum var. gracile

Dodecatheon patulum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
