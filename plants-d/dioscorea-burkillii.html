Dioscorea burkillii

Dioscorea burkillii, commonly known as Burkill's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its edible tubers.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and requires careful handling during cultivation and consumption.
