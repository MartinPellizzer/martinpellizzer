Dianthus caryophyllus f. longifolius

Dianthus caryophyllus f. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved carnation, is a flowering plant characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and showy, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its vibrant pink to red petals, which are often used in floral arrangements. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and borders. Its foliage is dense and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value throughout the growing season.

The longifolius form is distinguished by its extended leaf structure, which sets it apart from other cultivars of Dianthus caryophyllus.
