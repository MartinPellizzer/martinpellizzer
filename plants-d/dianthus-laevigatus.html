Dianthus laevigatus

Dianthus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals that are often tinged with red or purple.

Dianthus laevigatus is a perennial herb that produces seeds through self-fertilization, contributing to its ability to colonize various environments.
