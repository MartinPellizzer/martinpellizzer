Dioscorea moritziana

Dioscorea moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.

The plant's growth habit and morphology make it well adapted to its native humid and warm environments.
