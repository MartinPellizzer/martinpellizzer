Digitaria ciliaris

Digitaria ciliaris, commonly known as cockspur grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open areas, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Digitaria ciliaris is often used for forage in livestock grazing and is also considered a weed in agricultural settings.
