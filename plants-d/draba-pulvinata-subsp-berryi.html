Draba pulvinata subsp. berryi

Draba pulvinata subsp.

berryi, commonly known as the Berry's whitlow grass, is a small perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, favoring rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant produces basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers in early spring. Its flowering stems are usually short and often bear a single flower.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
