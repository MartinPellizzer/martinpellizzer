Dioclea glycinoides

Dioclea glycinoides, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value.

Dioclea glycinoides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
