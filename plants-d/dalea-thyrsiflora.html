Dalea thyrsiflora

Dalea thyrsiflora, commonly known as prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea thyrsiflora plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
