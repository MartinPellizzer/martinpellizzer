Durio grandiflorus

Durio grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant durian, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiky fruit, which can weigh up to 15 kilograms. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often reaching lengths of over 30 centimeters.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is known for its rapid growth and high fruit yield.
