Dianthus orientalis subsp. orientalis

Dianthus orientalis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with prominent white or pale pink markings.

The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
