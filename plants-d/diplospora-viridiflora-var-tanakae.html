Diplospora viridiflora var. tanakae

Diplospora viridiflora var. tanakae, commonly known as the green-flowered diplospora, is a rare and unique variant of the diplospora genus.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically borne in clusters along the stems. The plant is native to specific regions in East Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex reproductive structures and limited geographic distribution.
