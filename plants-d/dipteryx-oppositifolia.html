Dipteryx oppositifolia

Dipteryx oppositifolia, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tall evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain multiple seeds, known as Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters in height and has a long lifespan, often living over 100 years. It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The Brazil nut tree is also economically significant due to the valuable seeds it produces.
