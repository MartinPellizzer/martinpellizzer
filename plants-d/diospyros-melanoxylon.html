Diospyros melanoxylon

Diospyros melanoxylon, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark hardwood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen with a rounded crown and smooth, dark bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike.
