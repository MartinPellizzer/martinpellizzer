Diandrochloa diarrhena

Diandrochloa diarrhena, commonly known as the narrowleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
