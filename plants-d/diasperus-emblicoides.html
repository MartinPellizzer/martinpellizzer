Diasperus emblicoides

Diasperus emblicoides, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces yellow, berry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other bioactive compounds. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorns, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is widely cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value.
