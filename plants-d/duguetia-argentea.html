Duguetia argentea

Duguetia argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Duguetia argentea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
