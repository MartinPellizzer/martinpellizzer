Dactylorhiza intermedia

Dactylorhiza intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near waterlogged soils. The plant produces tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow or greenish flowers. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Dactylorhiza intermedia is often found in association with other orchid species in wetland ecosystems.
