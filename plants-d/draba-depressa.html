Draba depressa

Draba depressa, commonly known as the dwarf whitlow grass, is a small, mat-forming flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cold, harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Draba depressa is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

It is often found in high-altitude areas and is adapted to withstand extreme weather conditions.
