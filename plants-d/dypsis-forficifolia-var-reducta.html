Dypsis forficifolia var. reducta

Dypsis forficifolia var. reducta, commonly known as the Madagascar palm or the bottle palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its short, thick trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellowish-green in color. Its leaves are often reduced in size compared to the typical variety, hence the varietal name "reducta.".

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique shape and resilience.
