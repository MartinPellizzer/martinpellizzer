Delphinium cossonianum

Delphinium cossonianum, commonly known as the Cossonian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes, with each blossom having five petals and a distinctive spurred lower petal.

Delphinium cossonianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
