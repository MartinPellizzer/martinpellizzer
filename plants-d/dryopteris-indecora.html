Dryopteris indecora

Dryopteris indecora, commonly known as the barren wood fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
