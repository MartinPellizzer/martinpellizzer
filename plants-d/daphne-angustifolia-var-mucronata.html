Daphne angustifolia var. mucronata

Daphne angustifolia var. mucronata, commonly known as the narrowleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. This plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland edges.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its winter-blooming habit and attractive foliage.
