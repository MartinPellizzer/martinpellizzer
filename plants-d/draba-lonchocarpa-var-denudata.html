Draba lonchocarpa var. denudata

Draba lonchocarpa var. denudata, commonly known as the naked-seeded whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, glabrous leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or arctic environments.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, cold climates with limited soil nutrients.
