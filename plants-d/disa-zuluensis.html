Disa zuluensis

Disa zuluensis, commonly known as the Zulu orchid, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base.

Disa zuluensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in some African communities.
