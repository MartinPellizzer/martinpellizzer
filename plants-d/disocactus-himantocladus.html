Disocactus himantocladus

Disocactus himantocladus, commonly known as the Mexican ghost plant, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features a columnar stem that is typically green or reddish in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, large, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
