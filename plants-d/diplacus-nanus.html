Diplacus nanus

Diplacus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf monkeyflower, is a small annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
