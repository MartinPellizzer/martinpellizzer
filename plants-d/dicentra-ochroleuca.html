Dicentra ochroleuca

Dicentra ochroleuca, commonly known as the white bishop's hat, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Dicentra ochroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
