Dioscorea arifolia

Dioscorea arifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are edible when properly prepared and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable food source and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
