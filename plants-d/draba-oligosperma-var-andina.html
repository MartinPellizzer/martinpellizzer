Draba oligosperma var. andina

Draba oligosperma var. andina, commonly known as the Andean drumstick cress, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal and rosette-like, and it produces long, slender stems with clusters of small, yellow flowers.
