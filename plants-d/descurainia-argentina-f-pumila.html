Descurainia argentina f. pumila

Descurainia argentina f. pumila, commonly known as the Argentine stinkweed, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, green, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the family. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
