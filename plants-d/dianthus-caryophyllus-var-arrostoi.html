Dianthus caryophyllus var. arrostoi

Dianthus caryophyllus var. arrostoi, commonly known as the Corsican carnation, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, with a strong, spicy fragrance reminiscent of cloves. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces numerous flowers on branching stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
