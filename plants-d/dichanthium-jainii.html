Dichanthium jainii

Dichanthium jainii, commonly known as the Jain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Dichanthium jainii is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
