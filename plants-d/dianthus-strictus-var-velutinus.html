Dianthus strictus var. velutinus

Dianthus strictus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet pinks, is a compact, mat-forming perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of pink to red flowers with a velvety texture, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, soft down, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant flowers and unique foliage texture.
