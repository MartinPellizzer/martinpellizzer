Dendrobium concinnum

Dendrobium concinnum, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, often in clusters. Its stems are typically slender and can be either erect or pendant, depending on the variety.

Dendrobium concinnum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
