Dioscorea stipulosa

Dioscorea stipulosa, commonly known as the stipule yam, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb towards light. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with prominent stipules, which are leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalk.

The tubers of Dioscorea stipulosa are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
