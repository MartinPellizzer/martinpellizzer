Dendrobium pemae

Dendrobium pemae, commonly known as the Pemae orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that often bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding environment.

Dendrobium pemae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
