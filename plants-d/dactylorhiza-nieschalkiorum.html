Dactylorhiza nieschalkiorum

Dactylorhiza nieschalkiorum, commonly known as the Nieschalki's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, spike-like inflorescence bearing numerous small, greenish-white flowers with a distinct lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Dactylorhiza nieschalkiorum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological significance.
