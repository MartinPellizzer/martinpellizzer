Dombeya pseudopopulus

Dombeya pseudopopulus, commonly known as the false poplar, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
