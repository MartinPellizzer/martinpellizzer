Duranta serratifolia var. variegata

Duranta serratifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Duranta, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of white, green, and yellow, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is often used in landscaping for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
