Disa stolonifera

Disa stolonifera, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Disa stolonifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
