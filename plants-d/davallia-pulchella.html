Davallia pulchella

Davallia pulchella, commonly known as the "maiden's hair fern," is a striking evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, arching fronds with a feathery texture, often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori on the underside of the leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread slowly over time. Its ornamental value comes from its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in humid indoor conditions.

Davallia pulchella is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
