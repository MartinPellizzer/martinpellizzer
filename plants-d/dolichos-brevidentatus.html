Dolichos brevidentatus

Dolichos brevidentatus, commonly known as the short-toothed climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender, hairy stems that twine around supports. The leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets and short, curved teeth along the margins. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, clustered in axillary racemes, and the pods are slender, containing several oval seeds.

The plant is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and as a source of protein.
