Desmanthus subulatus

Desmanthus subulatus, commonly known as the slender burr legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
