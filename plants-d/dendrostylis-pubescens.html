Dendrostylis pubescens

Dendrostylis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and often covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its common name.

Dendrostylis pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
