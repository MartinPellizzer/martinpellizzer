Dendrobium comatum

Dendrobium comatum, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees or rocks, relying on air and rain for nutrients. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with red markings. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium comatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
