Dalbergaria puyana

Dalbergaria puyana, commonly known as the Puyana burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Its seed pods are hard and spiny, contributing to its common name, while its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
