Dalea quinqueflora

Dalea quinqueflora, commonly known as plains false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
