Delphinium decorum var. racemosum

Delphinium decorum var. racemosum, commonly known as the racemose larkspur, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland areas, preferring cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its root system consists of fibrous roots.

This variety is notable for its showy floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
