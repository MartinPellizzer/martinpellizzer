Diasperus arnottianus

Diasperus arnottianus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits called achenes.

Diasperus arnottianus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
