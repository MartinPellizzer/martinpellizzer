Dillenia insularum

Dillenia insularum, commonly known as the island dillenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dilleniaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five petals. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Dillenia insularum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
