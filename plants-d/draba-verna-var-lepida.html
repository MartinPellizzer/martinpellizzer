Draba verna var. lepida

Draba verna var. lepida, commonly known as the showy whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, and its flowers are small, white, and arranged in a loose cluster. It produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas, rocky soils, and along roadsides.
