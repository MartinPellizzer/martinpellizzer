Daniellia soyauxii

Daniellia soyauxii, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a small shrub native to the savannas of sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by pale green, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Daniellia soyauxii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought resistance through its succulent leaves and deep root system.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various animals and contributing to soil stability.
