Disa longicornu

Disa longicornu, commonly known as the long-horned disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, brightly colored flowers that typically range from pink to purple. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated spur. Disa longicornu thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
