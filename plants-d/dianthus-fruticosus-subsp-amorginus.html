Dianthus fruticosus subsp. amorginus

Dianthus fruticosus subsp.

amorginus, commonly known as the Amorgian campion, is a flowering plant native to the island of Amorgos in the Aegean Sea. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, where it is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Caryophyllaceae family.
