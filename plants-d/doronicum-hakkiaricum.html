Doronicum hakkiaricum

Doronicum hakkiaricum, commonly known as the Hakkiaric spring gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with a hairy texture.

Doronicum hakkiaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
