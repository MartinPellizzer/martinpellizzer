Daphne alpestrivaga

Daphne alpestrivaga, commonly known as the alpine daphne, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, rounded pattern. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Daphne alpestrivaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
