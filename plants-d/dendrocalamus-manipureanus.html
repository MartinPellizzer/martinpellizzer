Dendrocalamus manipureanus

Dendrocalamus manipureanus, commonly known as the Manipur bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to the northeastern regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant thrives in humid tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks. Its culms are valued for their strength and flexibility, making them useful in construction and handicrafts.

The plant also plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
