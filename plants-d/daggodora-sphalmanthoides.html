Daggodora sphalmanthoides

Daggodora sphalmanthoides, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
