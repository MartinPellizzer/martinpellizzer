Delphinium cyphoplectrum subsp. vanense

Delphinium cyphoplectrum subsp.

vanense, commonly known as the Vanesa delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and adaptability to well-drained, rocky soils.
