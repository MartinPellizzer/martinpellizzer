Datura rubra

Datura rubra, commonly known as the red angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces smooth, green, egg-shaped berries that turn red when mature. Datura rubra is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.

It is known for its potent alkaloid content, making it both ornamental and potentially toxic.
