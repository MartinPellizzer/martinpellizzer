Dimacria affinis

Dimacria affinis, commonly known as the slender dimorphic orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually small and clustered in inflorescences.

Dimacria affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
