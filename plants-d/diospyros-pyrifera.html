Diospyros pyrifera

Diospyros pyrifera, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to reddish fruit that ripens in late summer. The fruit has a sweet, edible pulp with astringent seeds, and the tree is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
