Dichaea galeata

Dichaea galeata, commonly known as the Galeata Orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in fine hairs, which help in retaining moisture.

The flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.
