Dictamnus fraxinella var. purpureus

Dictamnus fraxinella var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, reddish-brown stems that exude a strong, spicy scent when broken. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for rock gardens and borders.
