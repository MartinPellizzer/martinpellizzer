Deyeuxia flavescens

Deyeuxia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or yellowish in color, giving it its common name. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deyeuxia flavescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
