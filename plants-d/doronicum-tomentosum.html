Doronicum tomentosum

Doronicum tomentosum, commonly known as the fuzzy leopard's ear, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves that resemble a leopard's ear. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a disc-like shape. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream sides.

Doronicum tomentosum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and easy maintenance.
