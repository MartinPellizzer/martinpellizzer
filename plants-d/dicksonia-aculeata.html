Dicksonia aculeata

Dicksonia aculeata, commonly known as the hard tree fern, is a large, evergreen fern native to Australia and New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, dark brown outer layer. The plant has a crown of feathery, light green fronds that can grow up to 3 meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a valuable component of its native ecosystems.
