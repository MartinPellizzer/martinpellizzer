Dysphania truncata

Dysphania truncata, commonly known as bluntleaf lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves with a truncate or notched base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. Dysphania truncata thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

It is an edible species, with both leaves and seeds being consumed by humans and livestock.
