Desmanthus brachypus

Desmanthus brachypus, commonly known as the shortleaf desmanthus, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its seeds are an important food source for various wildlife species.
