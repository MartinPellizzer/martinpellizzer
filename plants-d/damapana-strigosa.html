Damapana strigosa

Damapana strigosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The flowers, which can be white, yellow, or greenish, are often clustered in inflorescences and attract various pollinators.
