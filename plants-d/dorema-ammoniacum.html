Dorema ammoniacum

Dorema ammoniacum, commonly known as the ammoniacum dorema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Dorema ammoniacum is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
