Didelta tetragoniifolia

Didelta tetragoniifolia, commonly known as the four-angled delta plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
