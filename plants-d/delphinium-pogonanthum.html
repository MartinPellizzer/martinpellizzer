Delphinium pogonanthum

Delphinium pogonanthum, commonly known as the beardtongue larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are characterized by their distinctive spurred petals. Delphinium pogonanthum is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is an important plant in its native ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
