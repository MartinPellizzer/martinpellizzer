Draba aizoides var. laevipes

Draba aizoides var. laevipes, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, basal leaves that are smooth and slightly succulent. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in racemes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Draba aizoides var. laevipes is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
