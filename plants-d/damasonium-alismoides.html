Damasonium alismoides

Damasonium alismoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the water's surface. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality in its habitat.

Damasonium alismoides is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
