Diosma ensata

Diosma ensata, commonly known as the clustered diosma, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

Diosma ensata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
