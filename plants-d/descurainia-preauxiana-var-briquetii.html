Descurainia preauxiana var. briquetii

Descurainia preauxiana var. briquetii, commonly known as the Briquet's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
