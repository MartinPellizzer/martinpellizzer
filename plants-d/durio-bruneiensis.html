Durio bruneiensis

Durio bruneiensis, commonly known as the Brunei durian, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is closely related to other durian species. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit with a strong aroma and a creamy, fibrous pulp. Its flowers are typically pale yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
