Deparia petersenii f. grammitoides

Deparia petersenii f. grammitoides, commonly known as the Grammitoides fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its fronds are usually narrow and elongated, with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.
