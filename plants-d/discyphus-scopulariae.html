Discyphus scopulariae

Discyphus scopulariae, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Discyphus scopulariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
