Drosera pauciflora var. leucantha

Drosera pauciflora var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, sticky glands that secrete a mucilaginous substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in moist, acidic environments. Its flowers are white, often with a slight pinkish hue, and are arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster.

This variety is notable for its unique appearance and specialized adaptation for insect capture.
