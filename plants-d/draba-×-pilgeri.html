Draba × pilgeri

Draba × pilgeri, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually bright yellow or white.

Draba × pilgeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
