Dioscorea carpomaculata var. cinerea

Dioscorea carpomaculata var. cinerea, commonly known as the ash-gray yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible tuberous roots that are used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include a distinctive ash-gray coloration on the stems and a fibrous root system adapted for climbing and nutrient absorption.

This variety is valued both for its culinary use and traditional medicinal properties.
