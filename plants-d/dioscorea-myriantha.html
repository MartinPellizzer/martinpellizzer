Dioscorea myriantha

Dioscorea myriantha, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by cylindrical, yellowish fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers, which are the primary edible part, are used as a staple food in many regions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
