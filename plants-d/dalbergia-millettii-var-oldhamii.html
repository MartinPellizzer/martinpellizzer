Dalbergia millettii var. oldhamii

Dalbergia millettii var. oldhamii, commonly known as the oldhamii rosewood, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Vietnam.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich reddish-brown color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round pods containing flat, dark seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and musical instrument making.
