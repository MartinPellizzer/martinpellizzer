Dimorphanthera umbellata

Dimorphanthera umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of multiple clustered flowers resembling an umbrella, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Dimorphanthera umbellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
