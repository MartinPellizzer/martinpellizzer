Ditassa umbellata

Ditassa umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence, which consists of multiple white or pink flowers arranged in a flat, circular pattern. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often arranged in pairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

The flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.
