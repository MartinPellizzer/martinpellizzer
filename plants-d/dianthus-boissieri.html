Dianthus boissieri

Dianthus boissieri, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
