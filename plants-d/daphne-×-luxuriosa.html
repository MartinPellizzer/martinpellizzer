Daphne × luxuriosa

Daphne × luxuriosa, commonly known as the sweetbox or winter box, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage that is often used in hedges and topiary due to its compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing year-round visual interest.

Daphne × luxuriosa prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
