Delphinium polatschekii

Delphinium polatschekii, commonly known as the Polatschek's larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in tall, spires, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
