Diospyros feliciana

Diospyros feliciana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. Its bark is thick and fissured, often with a reddish or brownish hue, and the wood is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
