Dendrochilum papillosum

Dendrochilum papillosum, commonly known as the papillate orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy or papillate structures on its leaves and flowers, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink.

Dendrochilum papillosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
