Datura villosa

Datura villosa, commonly known as the woolly jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Datura villosa is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids such as scopolamine and hyoscyamine.
