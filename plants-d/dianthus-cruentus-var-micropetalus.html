Dianthus cruentus var. micropetalus

Dianthus cruentus var. micropetalus, commonly known as the dwarf bloody cranesbill, is a compact, flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
