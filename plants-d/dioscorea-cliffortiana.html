Dioscorea cliffortiana

Dioscorea cliffortiana, commonly known as the cliffortian yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its edible tubers.
