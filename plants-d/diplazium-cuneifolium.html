Diplazium cuneifolium

Diplazium cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with lanceolate, serrated leaflets that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
