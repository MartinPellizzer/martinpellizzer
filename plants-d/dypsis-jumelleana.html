Dypsis jumelleana

Dypsis jumelleana, commonly known as the Jumellean palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a slender, upright palm with a smooth, grayish trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into yellow-orange fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Dypsis jumelleana is adapted to grow in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
