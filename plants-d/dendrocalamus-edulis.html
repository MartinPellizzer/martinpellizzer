Dendrocalamus edulis

Dendrocalamus edulis, commonly known as bamboo, is a tropical grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has hollow, segmented stalks with a smooth, greenish-brown surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is primarily propagated through rhizomes.

Dendrocalamus edulis is valued for its strong, lightweight material used in construction and crafts.
