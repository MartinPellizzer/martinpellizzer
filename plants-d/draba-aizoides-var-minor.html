Draba aizoides var. minor

Draba aizoides var. minor, commonly known as the lesser whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has basal rosettes of linear, hairy leaves and produces yellow or white flowers in early spring. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its flowering period is short, often lasting only a few weeks, making it a rare and delicate member of its ecosystem.
