Dryopteris sepikensis

Dryopteris sepikensis, commonly known as the Sepik water fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically divided into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy or wetland areas. Its rhizomes are somewhat fibrous and can spread to form dense mats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
