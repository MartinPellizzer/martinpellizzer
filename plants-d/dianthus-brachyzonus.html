Dianthus brachyzonus

Dianthus brachyzonus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dianthus brachyzonus is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
