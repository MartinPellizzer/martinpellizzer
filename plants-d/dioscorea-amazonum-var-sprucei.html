Dioscorea amazonum var. sprucei

Dioscorea amazonum var. sprucei, commonly known as the Amazon yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers, which are the primary economic product, are rich in starch and have been traditionally used for food and medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting diverse rainforest biodiversity.
