Deceptor

Deceptor, commonly known as *Orchis malaxis*, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower that resembles a bee, which is a key adaptation for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a tall stem with a single, showy flower.

Deceptor is known for its deceptive appearance, which helps it attract pollinators by mimicking the appearance of more common flowers.
