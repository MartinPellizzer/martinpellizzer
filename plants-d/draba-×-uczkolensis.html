Draba × uczkolensis

Draba × uczkolensis, commonly known as the Siberian whitlow grass, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including the Tien Shan and Pamir Mountains.

Draba × uczkolensis is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
