Dasylirion heteracanthum

Dasylirion heteracanthum, commonly known as the desert dagger, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces striking, spikelike inflorescences that bear small, white to pale blue flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
