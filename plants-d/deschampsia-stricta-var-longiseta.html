Deschampsia stricta var. longiseta

Deschampsia stricta var. longiseta, commonly known as hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, tufted stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
