Delphinium decorum var. sonomensis

Delphinium decorum var. sonomensis, commonly known as the Sonoma larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
