Dipterocarpus costatus

Dipterocarpus costatus, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, heavy wood and is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Due to its hardiness and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in forest ecosystems.
