Dalechampia bangii

Dalechampia bangii, commonly known as the golden dandelion, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, balloon-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dalechampia bangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
