Digitaria biformis var. rachiseta

Digitaria biformis var. rachiseta, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow with a hairy texture. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, typical of many grasses.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional herbal practices.
