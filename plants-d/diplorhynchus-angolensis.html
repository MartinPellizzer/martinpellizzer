Diplorhynchus angolensis

Diplorhynchus angolensis, commonly known as the Angola orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that often have a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing several flowers.

Diplorhynchus angolensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
