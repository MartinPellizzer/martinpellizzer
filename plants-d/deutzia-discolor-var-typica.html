Deutzia discolor var. typica

Deutzia discolor var. typica, commonly known as the white deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate and have a slightly serrated edge, turning yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
