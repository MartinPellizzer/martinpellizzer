Dendriopoterium pulidoi

Dendriopoterium pulidoi, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a unique shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight and helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscapes and drought-tolerant gardens.
