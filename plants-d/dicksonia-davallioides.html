Dicksonia davallioides

Dicksonia davallioides, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a large fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, tree-like trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a long, fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in moist, nutrient-rich soil. It thrives in humid environments with high rainfall and partial shade, often found in rainforests.

The fronds are typically bioluminescent at night, a unique feature that aids in attracting pollinators and deterring herbivores.
