Dalbergia eurybothrya

Dalbergia eurybothrya, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas across countries like India, Thailand, and Vietnam.
