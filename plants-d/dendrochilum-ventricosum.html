Dendrochilum ventricosum

Dendrochilum ventricosum, commonly known as the balloon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated pseudobulbs that resemble balloons, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Dendrochilum ventricosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
