Diplostephium haenkei

Diplostephium haenkei, commonly known as the red root grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, fibrous roots that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Diplostephium haenkei is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
