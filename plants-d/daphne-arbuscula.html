Daphne arbuscula

Daphne arbuscula, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Daphne arbuscula is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
