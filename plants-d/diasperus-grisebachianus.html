Diasperus grisebachianus

Diasperus grisebachianus, commonly known as the Grisebach's daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence, which consists of a central yellow disk surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. Native to South Africa, this species thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
