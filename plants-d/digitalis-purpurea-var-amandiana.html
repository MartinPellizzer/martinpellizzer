Digitalis purpurea var. amandiana

Digitalis purpurea var. amandiana, commonly known as the 'Amandiana' foxglove, is a perennial flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to pink. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and bumblebees. Its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped, with a distinctive serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
