Diplopterys patula

Diplopterys patula, commonly known as the pink angel's tears, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink, tear-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, red berries that contain toxic compounds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
