Disperis cardiopetala

Disperis cardiopetala, commonly known as the heart-leaved disperis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
