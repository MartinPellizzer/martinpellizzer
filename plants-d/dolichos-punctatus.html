Dolichos punctatus

Dolichos punctatus, commonly known as the spotted cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Dolichos punctatus is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
