Diervilla sanguinea var. leucantha

Diervilla sanguinea var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant red stems that persist through winter. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
