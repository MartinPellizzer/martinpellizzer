Dioscorea bolojonica

Dioscorea bolojonica, commonly known as Bolojon's yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible tubers that are similar in appearance to other yam species, with a rough, bark-like skin. Its botanical significance lies in its potential as a food source and its traditional use in local medicine.

The plant's morphology and growth patterns make it an important species for ecological and agricultural studies in its native habitat.
