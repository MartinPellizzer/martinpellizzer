Delphinium ceratophorum

Delphinium ceratophorum, commonly known as the horned delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium ceratophorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
