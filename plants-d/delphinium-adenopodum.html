Delphinium adenopodum

Delphinium adenopodum, commonly known as the woolly larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a soft, woolly texture on the underside, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
