Dryopteris gongylodes var. propinqua

Dryopteris gongylodes var. propinqua, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a variant of the broad shield fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in damp woodlands. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are usually a vibrant green, with a slightly glossy surface that enhances its visual appeal.
