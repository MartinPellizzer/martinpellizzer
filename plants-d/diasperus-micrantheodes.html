Diasperus micrantheodes

Diasperus micrantheodes, commonly known as the small-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are succulent, adapted to store water, and its stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures.

Diasperus micrantheodes is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh environmental conditions.
