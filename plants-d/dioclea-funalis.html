Dioclea funalis

Dioclea funalis, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often hairy.

Dioclea funalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
