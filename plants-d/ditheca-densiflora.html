Ditheca densiflora

Ditheca densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower dittany, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
