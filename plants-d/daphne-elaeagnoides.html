Daphne elaeagnoides

Daphne elaeagnoides, commonly known as silver daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
