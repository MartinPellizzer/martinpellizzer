Draba lonchocarpa var. thompsonii

Draba lonchocarpa var. thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions. It has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
