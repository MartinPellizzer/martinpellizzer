Delphinium williamsii

Delphinium williamsii, commonly known as the Blue Poppy, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy blue or violet flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, adding to its ornamental value.

Delphinium williamsii is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
