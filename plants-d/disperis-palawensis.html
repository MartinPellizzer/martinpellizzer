Disperis palawensis

Disperis palawensis, commonly known as the Palau Disperis, is a flowering plant native to the island nation of Palau in the western Pacific Ocean.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories, and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
