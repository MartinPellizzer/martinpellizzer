Dracocephalum virginianum var. album

Dracocephalum virginianum var. album, commonly known as white horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
