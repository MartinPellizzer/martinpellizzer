Dianthus trifasciculatus subsp. parviflorus

Dianthus trifasciculatus subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural displays.
