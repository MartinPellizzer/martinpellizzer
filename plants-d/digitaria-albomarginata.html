Digitaria albomarginata

Digitaria albomarginata, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tipped leaves and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with white or pale hues.

Digitaria albomarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
