Diospyros austroafricana subsp. rubriflora

Diospyros austroafricana subsp.

rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered ebony, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
