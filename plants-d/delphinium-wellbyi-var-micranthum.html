Delphinium wellbyi var. micranthum

Delphinium wellbyi var. micranthum, commonly known as the wellby's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
