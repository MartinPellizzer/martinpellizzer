Diospyros pavonii

Diospyros pavonii, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, tangy pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various indigenous communities.
