Diospyros foochowensis

Diospyros foochowensis, commonly known as the Fuchow persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
