Daviesia ulicifolia subsp. pilligensis

Daviesia ulicifolia subsp.

pilligensis, commonly known as the Pilligensis Daviesia, is a shrub native to the south-eastern region of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
