Diplusodon virgatus var. occidentalis

Diplusodon virgatus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, producing pale blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant restoration projects.
