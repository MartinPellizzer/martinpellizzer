Diphyes incumbens

Diphyes incumbens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed burrseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, branching structure. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are small and yellowish.

Diphyes incumbens produces small, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing.
