Dinemagonum

Dinemagonum, commonly known as the golden spire, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features a tall, slender stem that can reach up to three meters in height, adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. Its blooming period occurs during the dry season, making it a striking feature in its ecosystem.
