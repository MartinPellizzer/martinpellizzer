Deschampsia flexuosa f. vivipara

Deschampsia flexuosa f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous bentgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is characterized by its ability to produce live offspring through vivipary, where seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant. This unique reproductive strategy allows it to colonize disturbed soils more effectively. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tufted growth habit that aids in its identification.
