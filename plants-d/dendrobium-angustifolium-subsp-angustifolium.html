Dendrobium angustifolium subsp. angustifolium

Dendrobium angustifolium subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces racemes of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent red or brown markings. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on trees or rocks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated for horticultural purposes.
