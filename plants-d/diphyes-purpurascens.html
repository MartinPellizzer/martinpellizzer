Diphyes purpurascens

Diphyes purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered diphya, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
