Dodonaea hexandra

Dodonaea hexandra, commonly known as the velvetleaf hopseed, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Dodonaea hexandra is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and salt spray, making it adaptable to various ecological conditions.
