Dahlia purpurea

Dahlia purpurea, commonly known as the purple dahlia, is a striking tuberous-rooted herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers in a wide range of colors, including purple, red, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are composed of two layers of petal-like structures called ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets.

Dahlia purpurea is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
