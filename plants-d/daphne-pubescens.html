Daphne pubescens

Daphne pubescens, commonly known as the hairy daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features aromatic, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Daphne pubescens is also known for its toxic properties, with all parts of the plant containing harmful compounds.
