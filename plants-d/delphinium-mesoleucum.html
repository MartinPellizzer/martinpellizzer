Delphinium mesoleucum

Delphinium mesoleucum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of showy blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium mesoleucum is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
