Diplusodon imbricatus

Diplusodon imbricatus, commonly known as the broadleaf water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features floating leaves with a waxy upper surface that helps repel water, and its roots anchor in the muddy substrate. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, often with a distinctive serrated edge.

Diplusodon imbricatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
