Dinebra juncifolia

Dinebra juncifolia, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in swampy or marshy habitats. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating to help repel water.

Dinebra juncifolia plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wetland organisms.
