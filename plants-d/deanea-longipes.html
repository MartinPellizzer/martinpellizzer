Deanea longipes

Deanea longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, gracefully curved stems and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which can range in color from white to pale yellow, bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Deanea longipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in greenhouses for its unique appearance.
