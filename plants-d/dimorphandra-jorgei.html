Dimorphandra jorgei

Dimorphandra jorgei, commonly known as the Jorge's twin-leaf tree, is a species of legume native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which consist of two pairs of leaflets, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are legumes, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.

Dimorphandra jorgei is often found in dry forests and savannas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
