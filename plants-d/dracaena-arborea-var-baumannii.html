Dracaena arborea var. baumannii

Dracaena arborea var. baumannii, commonly known as the Baumann's dracaena, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red or orange berries, which are a striking feature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by experienced growers.
