Dioscorea amazonum var. klugii

Dioscorea amazonum var. klugii, commonly known as the Amazon yam, is a tuberous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, starchy underground tubers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The species is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
