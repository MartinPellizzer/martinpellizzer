Dioscorea quinata

Dioscorea quinata, commonly known as Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and develops yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. Its tubers are widely used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.

The botanical features of D. quinata make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
