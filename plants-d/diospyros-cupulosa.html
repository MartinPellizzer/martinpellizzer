Diospyros cupulosa

Diospyros cupulosa, commonly known as the cupula persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped and covered in a thick, waxy coating.

The fruit ripens to a deep orange or red color and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.
