Diosma patentissima

Diosma patentissima, commonly known as the slender diosma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
