Diascia stachyoides

Diascia stachyoides, commonly known as the silverleaf scabious, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are silver-white in color, with a fuzzy texture, and produces clusters of small, bright blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Diascia stachyoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
