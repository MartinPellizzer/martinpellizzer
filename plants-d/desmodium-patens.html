Desmodium patens

Desmodium patens, commonly known as the creeping sicklepod, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by lens-shaped pods containing several seeds. Desmodium patens is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage and soil stabilization.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it beneficial in agricultural settings.
