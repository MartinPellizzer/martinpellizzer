Dioscorea cienegensis

Dioscorea cienegensis, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a perennial vine that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for its medicinal and nutritional properties. The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, while its flowers are unisexual and typically yellow or green.

The tubers of Dioscorea cienegensis are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
