Dracaena fischeri

Dracaena fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's dragon tree, is a rare and striking plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
