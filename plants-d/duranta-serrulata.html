Duranta serrulata

Duranta serrulata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often with a contrasting orange or red calyx. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Duranta serrulata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, making it popular in landscaping.
