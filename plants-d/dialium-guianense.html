Dialium guianense

Dialium guianense, commonly known as the Guianese tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pulp and are valued for their medicinal properties.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its shade and timber.
