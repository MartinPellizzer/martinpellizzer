Diospyros oblonga var. helferi

Diospyros oblonga var. helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange to reddish berry that ripens in late summer or early autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
