Drymonia atropurpurea

Drymonia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple drymonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are typically purple-tinged on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native plant supporting local ecosystems and its ornamental value in gardens.
