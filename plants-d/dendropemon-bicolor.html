Dendropemon bicolor

Dendropemon bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly contrasting leaf colors, typically green and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters during the wet season.

Dendropemon bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
