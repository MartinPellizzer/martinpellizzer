Dalbergaria longinervosa

Dalbergaria longinervosa, commonly known as the long-veined burr legume, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, prominent veins and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
