Diplacus longiflorus var. rutilus

Diplacus longiflorus var. rutilus, commonly known as the red-flowered monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the Polemoniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
