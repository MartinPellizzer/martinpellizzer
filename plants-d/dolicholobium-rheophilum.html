Dolicholobium rheophilum

Dolicholobium rheophilum, commonly known as the riverbank senna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, often flood-prone habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter tall, with slender stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces hairy, oblong pods that contain several seeds.

Dolicholobium rheophilum thrives in nutrient-rich soils and is often found along riverbanks and wet meadows.
