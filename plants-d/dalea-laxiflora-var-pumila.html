Dalea laxiflora var. pumila

Dalea laxiflora var. pumila, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, silky-textured leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to violet. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
