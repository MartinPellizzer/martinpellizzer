Dulongia laticuspis

Dulongia laticuspis, commonly known as the broad-spined cycad, is a unique species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are often described as having a leathery texture and a distinct, broad spines along the leaflets.

Native to Southeast Asia, this cycad is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its ecological and evolutionary significance.
