Diervilla acadiensis

Diervilla acadiensis, commonly known as Atlantic seaside honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern coast of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and turn red in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
