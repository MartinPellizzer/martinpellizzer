Desmodium caudatum var. mandshuricum

Desmodium caudatum var. mandshuricum, commonly known as the Siberian hairy senna, is a perennial leguminous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces seed pods that contain several small, lens-shaped seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
