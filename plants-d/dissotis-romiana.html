Dissotis romiana

Dissotis romiana, commonly known as the silverleaf marshmallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in swampy areas near water bodies. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Dissotis romiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
