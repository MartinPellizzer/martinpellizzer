Didiscus ornatus var. semilanatus

Didiscus ornatus var. semilanatus, commonly known as the fringed moon cactus, is a unique hybrid plant that combines the characteristics of its parent species, Didiscus ornatus and Didiscus semilanatus.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive fringe along the edges. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
