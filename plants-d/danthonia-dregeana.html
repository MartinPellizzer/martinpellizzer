Danthonia dregeana

Danthonia dregeana, commonly known as the African hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Danthonia dregeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
