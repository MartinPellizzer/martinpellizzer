Dianthus sanguineus var. atrisquamatus

Dianthus sanguineus var. atrisquamatus, commonly known as the black-eyed dianthus, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark purple to black flowers with bright red or orange centers, creating a dramatic visual contrast. The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period, which can extend from spring to early summer.
