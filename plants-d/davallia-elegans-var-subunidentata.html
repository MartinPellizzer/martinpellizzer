Davallia elegans var. subunidentata

Davallia elegans var. subunidentata, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a decorative fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features delicate, arching fronds with a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive, lacy appearance. The plant prefers indirect light and consistently moist, well-draining soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings.

Its foliage remains evergreen, making it a popular choice for year-round greenery.
