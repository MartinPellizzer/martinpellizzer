Digitaria abyssinica var. micrantha

Digitaria abyssinica var. micrantha, commonly known as African grass or red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, fields, and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important forage plant in some regions.
