Duretia

Duretia, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. The plants usually have slender, branching stems and small, often hairy leaves. Duretia species produce showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
