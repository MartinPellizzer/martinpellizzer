Desmodium parvifolium var. macrocarpum

Desmodium parvifolium var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded ticktrefoil, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically small and have a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several small, round seeds.

This species is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as woodland edges and disturbed soils.
