Digitaria antunesii

Digitaria antunesii, commonly known as the Antunes grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Digitaria antunesii is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
