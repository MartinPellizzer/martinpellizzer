Dendrochilum polluciferum

Dendrochilum polluciferum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive green throat, which are borne on branching inflorescences. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting mix.

Its unique appearance and vibrant color make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
