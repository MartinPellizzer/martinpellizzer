Dioscorea pellegrinii

Dioscorea pellegrinii, commonly known as the Pellegrini yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
