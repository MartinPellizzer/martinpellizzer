Dilepyrum erectum

Dilepyrum erectum, commonly known as the upright dilepyrum, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. Dilepyrum erectum is generally found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
