Dupatya neglecta

Dupatya neglecta, commonly known as the neglected daisy, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Dupatya neglecta is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks.
