Desmodium simplex

Desmodium simplex, commonly known as smallleaf burrweed, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Native to the Americas, it is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and soil stabilization efforts.
