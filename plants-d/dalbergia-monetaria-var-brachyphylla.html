Dalbergia monetaria var. brachyphylla

Dalbergia monetaria var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rosewood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Its wood is highly valued for its density and rich, dark coloration, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship and musical instrument making.
