Dimorphanthera glauca

Dimorphanthera glauca, commonly known as the blue-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and glossy, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences with a unique coloration.

Dimorphanthera glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
