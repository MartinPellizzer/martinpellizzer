Dianthus caryophyllus f. emancipatus

Dianthus caryophyllus f. emancipatus, commonly known as the emancipatus form of the carnation, is a horticultural variant of the carnation plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. The foliage is typically dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both garden beds and container planting.
