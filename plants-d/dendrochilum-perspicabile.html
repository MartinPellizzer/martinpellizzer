Dendrochilum perspicabile

Dendrochilum perspicabile, commonly known as the translucent orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent sepals and petals that give it a glass-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

Dendrochilum perspicabile is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
