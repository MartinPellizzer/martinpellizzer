Diospyros chartacea

Diospyros chartacea, commonly known as the paper fig or sandpaper fig, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, sandpaper-like texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
