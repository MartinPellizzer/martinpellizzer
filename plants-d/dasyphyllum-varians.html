Dasyphyllum varians

Dasyphyllum varians, commonly known as the variable dasyphyllum, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed leaves that vary in shape and size, contributing to its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink, which attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Dasyphyllum varians is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
