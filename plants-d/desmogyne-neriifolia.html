Desmogyne neriifolia

Desmogyne neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and moths.

Desmogyne neriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
