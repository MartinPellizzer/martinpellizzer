Dansera

Dansera, commonly known as the "Flamingo Flower," is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus *Hymenocallis* and is characterized by its large, showy, white or pink petals that resemble the pink feathers of a flamingo. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring a bulbous underground stem that supports its rapid growth. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often with a yellow or orange center, adding vibrant color to gardens.

The botanical name *Hymenocallis dansera* honors the French botanist and horticulturist who first documented the species.
