Delphinium saniculifolium var. gilgitense

Delphinium saniculifolium var. gilgitense, commonly known as the Gilgit delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed shape, and are covered in a fine layer of soft, white hairs.

This variety is adapted to cold, mountainous environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
