Dioscorea chaponensis

Dioscorea chaponensis, commonly known as the Chapon's yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain regions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitats.
