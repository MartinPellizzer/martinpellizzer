Dianthus hypanicus

Dianthus hypanicus, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has erect, branching stems and produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Dianthus hypanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
