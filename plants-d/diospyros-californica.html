Diospyros californica

Diospyros californica, commonly known as the California persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by orange to reddish fruits that ripen in late summer to early fall. The fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
