Daucus carota subsp. annuus

Daucus carota subsp.

annuus, commonly known as the common carrot, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and southwestern Asia. It is characterized by its taproot, which is the primary edible part, and its finely divided, feathery leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in umbels, which are typical of theApiaceae family. Its root is rich in nutrients, including beta-carotene, and has been cultivated for thousands of years.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.
