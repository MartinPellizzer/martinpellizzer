Drymaria tenuis var. jaliscana

Drymaria tenuis var. jaliscana, commonly known as the narrowleaf drymaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, prostrate stems and small, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a desirable plant for xeriscaping in arid regions.
