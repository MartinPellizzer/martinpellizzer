Dyckia marnier-lapostollei

Dyckia marnier-lapostollei, commonly known as the Marnier-Lapostolle Dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
