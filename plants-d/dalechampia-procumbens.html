Dalechampia procumbens

Dalechampia procumbens, commonly known as the creeping dalechampia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Dalechampia procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
