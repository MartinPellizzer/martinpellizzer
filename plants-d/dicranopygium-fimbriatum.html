Dicranopygium fimbriatum

Dicranopygium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, elongated leaves that often have a fringed or serrated margin. This moss typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called capsules, are borne on short, erect stalks.

Dicranopygium fimbriatum plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
