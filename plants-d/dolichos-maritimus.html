Dolichos maritimus

Dolichos maritimus, commonly known as the sea bean, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found along shorelines and dunes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
