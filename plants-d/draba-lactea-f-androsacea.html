Draba lactea f. androsacea

Draba lactea f. androsacea, commonly known as the Androsace alpine draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its rosette of hairy, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with a short growing season and frost-resistant features.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme conditions.
