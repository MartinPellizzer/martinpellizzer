Dendrobium kraemeri

Dendrobium kraemeri, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers with intricate patterns, blooming during the dry season. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium kraemeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
