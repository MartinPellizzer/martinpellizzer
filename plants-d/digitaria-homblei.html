Digitaria homblei

Digitaria homblei, commonly known as the narrowleaf digitaria, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed panicles.

Digitaria homblei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging conditions.
