Delaria × ovalifolia

Delaria × ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf delaria, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
