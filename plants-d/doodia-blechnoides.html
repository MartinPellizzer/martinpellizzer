Doodia blechnoides

Doodia blechnoides, commonly known as the narrow fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with lanceolate, serrated fronds that are often arching in appearance. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
