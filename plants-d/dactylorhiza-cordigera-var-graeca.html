Dactylorhiza cordigera var. graeca

Dactylorhiza cordigera var. graeca, commonly known as the Greek yellow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and heathland habitats. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, leafy stems that bear clusters of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. Its flowers are characterized by three upright petals and three smaller, hooded sepals.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
