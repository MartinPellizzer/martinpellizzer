Dalea phleoides var. microphylla

Dalea phleoides var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved prairie clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to prairie ecosystems and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

This species is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
