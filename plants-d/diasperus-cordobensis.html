Diasperus cordobensis

Diasperus cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoban dwarf lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Argentina.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are short and often clustered.

Diasperus cordobensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
