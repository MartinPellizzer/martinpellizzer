Distimake kimberleyensis

Distimake kimberleyensis, commonly known as the Kimberley lily, is a striking plant native to the Kimberley region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The leaves are long and narrow, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in the local flora.
