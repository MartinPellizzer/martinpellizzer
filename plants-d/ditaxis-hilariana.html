Ditaxis hilariana

Ditaxis hilariana, commonly known as the Cape barnyard grass, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive nature, it can compete with native vegetation and is considered a weed in some regions.
