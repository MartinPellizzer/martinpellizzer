Dioscorea sessiliflora

Dioscorea sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower yam, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a smooth margin and can grow up to 20 cm in length.

The tubers of Dioscorea sessiliflora are edible when properly prepared and are also valued for their medicinal properties.
