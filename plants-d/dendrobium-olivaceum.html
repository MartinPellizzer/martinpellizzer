Dendrobium olivaceum

Dendrobium olivaceum, commonly known as the olive orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive olivaceous hue. It thrives in warm, humid climates with bright indirect light and well-draining substrates.

Dendrobium olivaceum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
