Distichlis palmeri

Distichlis palmeri, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical leaves that resemble a spoon, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Distichlis palmeri is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh desert environments.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
