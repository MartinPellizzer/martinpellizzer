Dioscorea hombuka var. ataza

Dioscorea hombuka var. ataza, commonly known as the Ataza yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellowish-green flowers that develop into cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are valued for their starchy texture and are often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
