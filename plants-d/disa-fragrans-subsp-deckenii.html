Disa fragrans subsp. deckenii

Disa fragrans subsp.

deckenii, commonly known as the African orchid or the orchid of the Kalahari, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous plant with a striking inflorescence composed of numerous fragrant, showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
