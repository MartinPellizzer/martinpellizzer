Diapensia purpurea f. rosea

Diapensia purpurea f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered diapensia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features small, evergreen leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
