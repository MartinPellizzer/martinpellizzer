Diuris latifolia

Diuris latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed spider orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. The plant features strap-shaped leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid native habitat. Diuris latifolia is found in sandy soils across southeastern Australia, often in heathlands and woodlands.

It blooms during the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.
