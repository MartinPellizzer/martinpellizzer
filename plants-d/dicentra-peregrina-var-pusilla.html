Dicentra peregrina var. pusilla

Dicentra peregrina var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bleeding heart, is a compact perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, heart-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a smooth texture and a light green color. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
