Descurainia richardsonii subsp. procera

Descurainia richardsonii subsp.

procera, commonly known as the tall tansy, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Brassicaceae family.
