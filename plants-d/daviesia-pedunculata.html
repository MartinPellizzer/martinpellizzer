Daviesia pedunculata

Daviesia pedunculata, commonly known as the red bough daphne, is a flowering plant native to the south-eastern region of Australia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and can become branched with age.

Daviesia pedunculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
