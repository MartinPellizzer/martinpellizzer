Dypsis ankaizinensis

Dypsis ankaizinensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring a short trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, black berries. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
