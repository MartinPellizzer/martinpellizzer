Draba densifolia var. apiculata

Draba densifolia var. apiculata, commonly known as the dense-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, linear to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its apiculate (tipped) leaves and is found in high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.
