Dodonaea eriocarpa var. obtusior

Dodonaea eriocarpa var. obtusior, commonly known as the broad-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, finely hairy foliage and small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture and a rounded or obtuse tip. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
