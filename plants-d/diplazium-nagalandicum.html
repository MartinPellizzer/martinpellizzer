Diplazium nagalandicum

Diplazium nagalandicum, commonly known as the Nagaland fern, is a species of fern native to the northeastern region of India, particularly Nagaland.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices.

It plays a significant role in the local biodiversity and is often found in montane and subtropical forest ecosystems.
