Draba dubia subsp. nevadensis

Draba dubia subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra draba, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

This subspecies typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often in open meadows and slopes. The plant produces basal rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves and has a short, flowering stem.

It is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found at high elevations.
