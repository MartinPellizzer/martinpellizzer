Discocactus diersianus subsp. diersianus

Discocactus diersianus subsp.

diersianus, commonly known as the Diersian discocactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
