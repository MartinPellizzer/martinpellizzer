Dodonaea sororia

Dodonaea sororia, commonly known as the velvetleaf dodonaea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland areas.

Dodonaea sororia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
