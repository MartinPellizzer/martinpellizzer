Dioscorea ternata

Dioscorea ternata, commonly known as the three-leaf yam, is a climbing perennial vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. Its tuberous roots are often harvested for their nutritional and medicinal properties.

Dioscorea ternata is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisines and traditional medicine.
