Diospyros nhatrangensis

Diospyros nhatrangensis, commonly known as Nhatrang ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that are initially green and turn reddish when mature.

The tree is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade.
