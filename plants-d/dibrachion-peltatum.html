Dibrachion peltatum

Dibrachion peltatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Dibrachion peltatum is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful plant in certain ecological contexts and a potential invasive species.
