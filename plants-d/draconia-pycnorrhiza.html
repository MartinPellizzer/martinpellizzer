Draconia pycnorrhiza

Draconia pycnorrhiza, commonly known as the dense-rooted dragon plant, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, pale yellow flowers during the wet season. Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand seasonal flooding and drought.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
