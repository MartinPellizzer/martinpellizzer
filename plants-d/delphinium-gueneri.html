Delphinium gueneri

Delphinium gueneri, commonly known as the Swiss bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems that bear large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are alternately arranged along the stem. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are arranged in a raceme, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Delphinium gueneri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
