Diospyros yandina

Diospyros yandina, commonly known as the Yandina or Australian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. Its fruit is edible, though typically tart and sometimes astringent when unripe.

Diospyros yandina is often found in coastal and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
