Dioscorea stenophylla var. paucinervis

Dioscorea stenophylla var. paucinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial vine characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with fewer veins than the typical species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
