Dryandra floribunda var. major

Dryandra floribunda var. major, commonly known as the Geraldton Wax, is a species of flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce numerous small, waxy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with leathery, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native region.

The Geraldton Wax is a key component of the local flora and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
