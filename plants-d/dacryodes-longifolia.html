Dacryodes longifolia

Dacryodes longifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and wood are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various forms of wildlife.
