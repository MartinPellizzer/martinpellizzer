Dendrobium setosum

Dendrobium setosum, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with long, wiry stems that often branch and bear numerous leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green to brownish flowers with prominent sepals and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium setosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
