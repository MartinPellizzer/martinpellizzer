Dryopteris hopeana

Dryopteris hopeana, commonly known as the Hope's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive adequate humidity and indirect light. Its rhizomes are perennial, allowing it to spread slowly through vegetative growth.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in natural habitats.
