Dalbergia mooniana

Dalbergia mooniana, commonly known as the moonwood tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
