Dryopteris berastagensis

Dryopteris berastagensis, commonly known as the Berastagan shield fern, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in cooler temperatures. Its spores are released from the sori, which are located on the undersides of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
