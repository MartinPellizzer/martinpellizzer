Draba alpina var. oxycarpa

Draba alpina var. oxycarpa, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold, arid environments. Its leaves are basal and rosette-like, adapted to minimize water loss in harsh conditions.

The species is notable for its resilience and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe, Asia, and North America.
