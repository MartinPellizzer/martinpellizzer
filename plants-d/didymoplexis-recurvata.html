Didymoplexis recurvata

Didymoplexis recurvata, commonly known as the twisted didymoplex, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, fibrous coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
