Drypetes pacifica

Drypetes pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
