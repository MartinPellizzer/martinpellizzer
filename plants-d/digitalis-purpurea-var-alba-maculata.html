Digitalis purpurea var. alba-maculata

Digitalis purpurea var. alba-maculata, commonly known as the white-spotted foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features tall, spire-like flower clusters that bloom in shades of white and pink, with distinctive spots on the petals. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the plant produces a toxic cardiac glycoside called digitoxin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its historical use in medicine and its role in attracting pollinators.
