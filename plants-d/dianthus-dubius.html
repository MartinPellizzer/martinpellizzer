Dianthus dubius

Dianthus dubius, commonly known as the common campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it produces small, dry fruits called capsules.

Dianthus dubius is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
