Daucus pumilus var. microcarpus

Daucus pumilus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf carrot, is a small annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its fruits are small, spherical seeds that are often used as a spice in traditional Mediterranean cuisine.

Due to its compact size and aromatic qualities, it is valued both for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
