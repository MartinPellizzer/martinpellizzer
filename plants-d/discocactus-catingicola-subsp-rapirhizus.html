Discocactus catingicola subsp. rapirhizus

Discocactus catingicola subsp.

rapirhizus, commonly known as the red-rooted discocactus, is a succulent native to northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in a layer of white, powdery wax.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
