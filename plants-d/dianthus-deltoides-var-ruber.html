Dianthus deltoides var. ruber

Dianthus deltoides var. ruber, commonly known as the crimson campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial herb that grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
