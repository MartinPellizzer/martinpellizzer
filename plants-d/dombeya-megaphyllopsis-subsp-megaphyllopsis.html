Dombeya megaphyllopsis subsp. megaphyllopsis

Dombeya megaphyllopsis subsp.

megaphyllopsis, commonly known as the giant-leaf dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a striking appearance.

The plant produces showy, panicle-like inflorescences with numerous white to pinkish flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often grown in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
