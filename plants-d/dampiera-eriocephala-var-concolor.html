Dampiera eriocephala var. concolor

Dampiera eriocephala var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth-flowered dampiera, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
