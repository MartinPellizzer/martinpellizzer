Dracaena dumetescens

Dracaena dumetescens, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or yellowish stripe along the midrib. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear at the base of the plant, followed by red berries.

Dracaena dumetescens is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
