Davallia chinensis

Davallia chinensis, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species native to Asia and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant has rhizomes that spread horizontally, supporting the growth of its fern-like leaves. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, finely divided structure.

Davallia chinensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
