Duthiastrum linifolium

Duthiastrum linifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features a distinctive silvery-gray upper surface covered in tiny, scale-like structures called rhizines. The thallus is composed of a complex interwoven network of fungal hyphae and algal cells, which together form the lichen's unique structure. It is often found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

This lichen plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator of air quality.
