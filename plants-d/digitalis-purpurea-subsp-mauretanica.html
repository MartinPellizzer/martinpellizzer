Digitalis purpurea subsp. mauretanica

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

mauretanica, commonly known as the Mauretanian foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features tall, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
