Dracaena pinguicula

Dracaena pinguicula, commonly known as the velvetleaf dracaena, is a striking evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are a feature of its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Dracaena pinguicula is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to purify indoor air.
