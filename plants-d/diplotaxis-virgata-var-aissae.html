Diplotaxis virgata var. aissae

Diplotaxis virgata var. aissae, commonly known as the Aissah's double-curl mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that form dense, compact rosettes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and high temperatures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
