Daphne × medfordensis

Daphne × medfordensis, commonly known as Medford daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and winter hardiness.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
