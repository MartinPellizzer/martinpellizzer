Dianthus anticarius subsp. atrosanguineus

Dianthus anticarius subsp.

atrosanguineus, commonly known as the dark red campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking, dark red to purple-pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
