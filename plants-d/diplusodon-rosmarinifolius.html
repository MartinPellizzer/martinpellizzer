Diplusodon rosmarinifolius

Diplusodon rosmarinifolius, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, aromatic leaves resembling rosemary, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal head, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique foliage and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
