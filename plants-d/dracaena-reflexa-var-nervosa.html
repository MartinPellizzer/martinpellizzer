Dracaena reflexa var. nervosa

Dracaena reflexa var. nervosa, commonly known as the Song of India, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright, strap-like leaves that are dark green with a prominent central vein, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires moderate watering. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries, though these are not typically noticeable in cultivated settings.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
