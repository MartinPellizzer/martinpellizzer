Desmodium boivinianum

Desmodium boivinianum, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
