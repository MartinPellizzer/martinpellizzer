Dysophylla salicifolia

Dysophylla salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf dysophylla, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, resembling the leaves of willow trees. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Dysophylla salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
