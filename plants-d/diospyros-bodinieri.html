Diospyros bodinieri

Diospyros bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that changes from green to orange-red as it ripens.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
