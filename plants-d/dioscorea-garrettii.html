Dioscorea garrettii

Dioscorea garrettii, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a food source. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
