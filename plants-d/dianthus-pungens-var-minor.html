Dianthus pungens var. minor

Dianthus pungens var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact size and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
