Dentella dioeca

Dentella dioeca, commonly known as the common dent's root, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a slender, branching stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dentella dioeca is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
