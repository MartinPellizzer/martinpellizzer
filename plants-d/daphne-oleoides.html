Daphne oleoides

Daphne oleoides, commonly known as the sweet spurge or winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pinkish-purple. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Daphne oleoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and winter interest.
