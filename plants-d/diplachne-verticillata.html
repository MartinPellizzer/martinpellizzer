Diplachne verticillata

Diplachne verticillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Diplachne verticillata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
