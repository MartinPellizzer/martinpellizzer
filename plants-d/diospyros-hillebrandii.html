Diospyros hillebrandii

Diospyros hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
