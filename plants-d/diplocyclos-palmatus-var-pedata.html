Diplocyclos palmatus var. pedata

Diplocyclos palmatus var. pedata, commonly known as the broadleaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive blue-gray waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its growth habit is slow, and it can live for several decades, making it a long-lived ornamental plant.

Due to its rarity and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
