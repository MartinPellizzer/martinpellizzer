Dioclea pilifera

Dioclea pilifera, commonly known as the hairy elder or silky elder, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Dioclea pilifera is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Fabaceae family.
