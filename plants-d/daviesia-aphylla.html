Daviesia aphylla

Daviesia aphylla, commonly known as the leafless daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its lack of leaves, with stems covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Daviesia aphylla is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
