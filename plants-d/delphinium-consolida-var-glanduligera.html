Delphinium consolida var. glanduligera

Delphinium consolida var. glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered with glandular dots, which give the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
