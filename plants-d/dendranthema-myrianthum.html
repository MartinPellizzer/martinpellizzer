Dendranthema myrianthum

Dendranthema myrianthum, commonly known as the common chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow center, and its aromatic, feathery leaves. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar composite flower structures.

Dendranthema myrianthum is widely used in herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
