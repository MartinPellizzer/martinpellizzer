Dichrocephala chrysanthemifolia var. alpina

Dichrocephala chrysanthemifolia var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine dichrocephala, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions with its compact growth habit. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention and insulation.

The flowers, which bloom in summer, produce small yellow to orange petals and are pollinated by insects.
