Drymocallis regisborisii

Drymocallis regisborisii, commonly known as the Regisborisii goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish to golden flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and pine flatwoods. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Drymocallis regisborisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
