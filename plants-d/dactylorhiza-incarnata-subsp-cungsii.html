Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. cungsii

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

cungsii, commonly known as the Cungan orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces a single spike of pale to dark purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for successful germination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
