Drypetes affinis

Drypetes affinis, commonly known as the red sanders or Indian sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic heartwood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
