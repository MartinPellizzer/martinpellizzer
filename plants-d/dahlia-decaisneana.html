Dahlia decaisneana

Dahlia decaisneana, commonly known as the Decaisne dahlia, is a striking perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, colorful flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant produces large, tuberous roots that help it survive in its native habitat. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dahlia decaisneana is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
