Diasperus chamaecerasus

Diasperus chamaecerasus, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features basal leaves that are often lobed or divided, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system to maximize water uptake.

Diasperus chamaecerasus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
