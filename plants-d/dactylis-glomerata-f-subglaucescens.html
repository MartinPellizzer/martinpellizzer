Dactylis glomerata f. subglaucescens

Dactylis glomerata f. subglaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous cocksfoot, is a variant of the common cocksfoot plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and pastures. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some horticultural and ecological restoration contexts.
