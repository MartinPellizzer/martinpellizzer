Danaea elliptica

Danaea elliptica, commonly known as the wax flower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen, glossy, elliptical-shaped leaves and clusters of small, waxy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.

Danaea elliptica is also valued for its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it suitable for rain gardens and bog landscapes.
