Dioscorea pseudojaponica

Dioscorea pseudojaponica, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a tuberous perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, berry-like fruits. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The botanical features of D. pseudojaponica make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
