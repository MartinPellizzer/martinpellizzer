Diplachne fusca var. lutescens

Diplachne fusca var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
