Daphne altaica subsp. fasciculiflora

Daphne altaica subsp.

fasciculiflora, commonly known as the Siberian daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features dense, compact growth with elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
