Dialium javanicum

Dialium javanicum, commonly known as the African mango or Java plum, is a tropical tree native to Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree bears fruit that resembles a small plum, with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The fruit contains several seeds enclosed in a hard, woody endocarp.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
