Dendrobium integrum

Dendrobium integrum, commonly known as the orchid with white and green flowers, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without being parasitic. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or green with yellow markings. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Dendrobium integrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
