Daucus

Daucus, commonly known as parsley or wild carrot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and umbel-shaped flower clusters. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. Daucus species often have a distinctive, aromatic scent and are sometimes used in herbal medicine.

Some species are cultivated for their edible roots and leaves.
