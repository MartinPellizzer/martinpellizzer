Dendrosicus silvicola

Dendrosicus silvicola, commonly known as the forest tree spider, is a species of orb-weaving spider found in various forested regions.

It is characterized by its slender body and long, delicate legs, which are well adapted for constructing intricate web structures. The spider's coloration is typically a mix of brown and gray, providing effective camouflage in its natural habitat. Dendrosicus silvicola is known to build vertical orb webs, often in the canopy of trees, where it hunts for small insects.

This species plays a role in controlling pest populations and maintaining ecological balance in forest ecosystems.
