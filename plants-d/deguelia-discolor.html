Deguelia discolor

Deguelia discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it also plays a role in soil enrichment in its native ecosystems.
