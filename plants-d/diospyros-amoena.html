Diospyros amoena

Diospyros amoena, commonly known as the sweet potato or iga-iga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that mature to a dark red or black color. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
