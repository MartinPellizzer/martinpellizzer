Delphinium consolida subsp. divaricatum

Delphinium consolida subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
