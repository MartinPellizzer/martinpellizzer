Dendrobium rupicoloides

Dendrobium rupicoloides, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It has fibrous roots that help it anchor into crevices and absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Dendrobium rupicoloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
