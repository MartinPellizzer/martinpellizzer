Deschampsia cespitosa var. viridis

Deschampsia cespitosa var. viridis, commonly known as green tufted needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and forms dense, tufted clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually a vibrant green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences in the summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
