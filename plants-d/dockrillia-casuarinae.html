Dockrillia casuarinae

Dockrillia casuarinae, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and needle-like leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife.
