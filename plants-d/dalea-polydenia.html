Dalea polydenia

Dalea polydenia, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Dalea polydenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
