Dussia tessmannii

Dussia tessmannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf dussia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Dussia tessmannii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
