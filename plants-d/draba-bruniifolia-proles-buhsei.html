Draba bruniifolia proles buhsei

Draba bruniifolia proles buhsei, commonly known as the Buhsei draba, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in mountain meadows. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.
