Dianthus longivaginatus

Dianthus longivaginatus, commonly known as the long calyx campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces elongated, tubular flower buds that open into five-petaled flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus longivaginatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
