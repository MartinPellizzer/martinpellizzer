Dioscorea kelungensis

Dioscorea kelungensis, commonly known as the Kelung yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions.
