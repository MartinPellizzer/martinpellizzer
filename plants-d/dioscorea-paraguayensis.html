Dioscorea paraguayensis

Dioscorea paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay arrowroot, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are used as a starchy food source and are similar in texture to yams.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
