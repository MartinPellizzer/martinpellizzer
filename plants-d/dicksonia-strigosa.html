Dicksonia strigosa

Dicksonia strigosa, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a unique structure with a central root system that supports its tall growth, and its fronds are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. Dicksonia strigosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
