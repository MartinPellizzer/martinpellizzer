Draba cacuminum subsp. angusticarpa

Draba cacuminum subsp.

angusticarpa, commonly known as narrow-carpel whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous annual with basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and arctic environments, preferring rocky or gravelly soils. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, elongated carpels, which distinguish it from other members of the Draba genus.
