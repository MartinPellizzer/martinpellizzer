Dianthus scardicus var. incisus

Dianthus scardicus var. incisus, commonly known as the cutleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lobed or incised, giving the plant its varietal name "incisus.". It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
