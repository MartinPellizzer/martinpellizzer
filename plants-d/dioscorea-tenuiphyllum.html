Dioscorea tenuiphyllum

Dioscorea tenuiphyllum, commonly known as the slender-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by inedible, spiny fruits. Its tubers, while not widely cultivated, have been used traditionally in some regions for their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
