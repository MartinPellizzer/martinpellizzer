Diospyros zeylanica

Diospyros zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and the edible fruits, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional medicine.
