Didymotheca drummondii

Didymotheca drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its showy, purple-pink flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Didymotheca drummondii is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
