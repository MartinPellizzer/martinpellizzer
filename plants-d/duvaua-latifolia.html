Duvaua latifolia

Duvaua latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf duvaua, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Duvaua latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
