Dendrotrophe frutescens var. subquinquenervia

Dendrotrophe frutescens var. subquinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined shrub, is a species characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, elliptical leaves with distinct venation patterns.

The leaves typically exhibit a five-veined structure, which is a key identifying feature of this variant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in the axils of the leaves. The plant is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique leaf venation and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
