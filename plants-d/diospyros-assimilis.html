Diospyros assimilis

Diospyros assimilis, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with shallow fissures, and it can grow up to 10 meters in height.

Diospyros assimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
