Decaloba bilobata

Decaloba bilobata, commonly known as the two-lobed fern, is a striking member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that are often divided into two distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Decaloba bilobata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
