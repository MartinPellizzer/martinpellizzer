Dioscorea lufensis

Dioscorea lufensis, commonly known as the Lufu yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and East Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are often harvested for their starchy content and have traditional uses in local medicine.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential as a food security crop.
