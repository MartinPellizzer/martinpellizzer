Dianella intermedia var. punctata

Dianella intermedia var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted flax lily, is a striking evergreen perennial native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white or yellow markings, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking blue or purple flower spikes in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
