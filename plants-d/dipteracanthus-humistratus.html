Dipteracanthus humistratus

Dipteracanthus humistratus, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
