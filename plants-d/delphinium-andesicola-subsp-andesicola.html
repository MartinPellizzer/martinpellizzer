Delphinium andesicola subsp. andesicola

Delphinium andesicola subsp.

andesicola, commonly known as the Andean larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces dense clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
