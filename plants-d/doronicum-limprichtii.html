Doronicum limprichtii

Doronicum limprichtii, commonly known as the alpine leopard's ear, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are yellow with a distinctive central disc, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
