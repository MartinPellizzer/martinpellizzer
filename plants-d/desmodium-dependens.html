Desmodium dependens

Desmodium dependens, commonly known as the hanging senna, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive stipules that are often glandular and may exude a sticky substance. Its pods are slender, hairy, and contain several elliptical seeds.

Desmodium dependens is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
