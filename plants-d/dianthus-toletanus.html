Dianthus toletanus

Dianthus toletanus, commonly known as the Toletan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20 to 30 centimeters and produces small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to the garden.
