Dichelachne forsteriana

Dichelachne forsteriana, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Dichelachne forsteriana is often found in disturbed or poorly drained soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
