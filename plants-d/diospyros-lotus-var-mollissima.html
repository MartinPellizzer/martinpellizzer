Diospyros lotus var. mollissima

Diospyros lotus var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves and small, orange-red fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The silky persimmon is also valued for its ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice for landscaping in dry regions.
