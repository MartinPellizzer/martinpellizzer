Ditaxis malpighiacea

Ditaxis malpighiacea, commonly known as the Malpighiaceae or the "false arrowhead," is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malpighiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, red or purple flowers. The plant typically has alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
