Dypsis gracilis

Dypsis gracilis, commonly known as the slender areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Dypsis gracilis is also known for its ability to tolerate indoor conditions, making it a favored houseplant.
