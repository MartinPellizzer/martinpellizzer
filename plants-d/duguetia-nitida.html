Duguetia nitida

Duguetia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Duguetia nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage in botanical gardens.
