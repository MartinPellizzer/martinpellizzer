Dypsis thouarsiana

Dypsis thouarsiana, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a dense crown of pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is also valued for its resilience and adaptability to various climates.
