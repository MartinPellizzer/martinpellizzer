Digitalis lutea var. atlantica

Digitalis lutea var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the British Isles.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of tubular, yellowish-orange flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette formation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and woodland edges.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
