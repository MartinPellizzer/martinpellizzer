Dorycnium rectum f. glabrum

Dorycnium rectum f. glabrum, commonly known as the glabrous hogweed, is a variant of the common hogweed species.

It is a biennial or perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that becomes glabrous (smooth) in this form. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can be quite large, often with a rough texture.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in umbels, and it produces a distinctive, swollen, club-shaped fruit.
