Dombeya digyna

Dombeya digyna, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowering stems are usually branched and bear clusters of flowers at the tips.

Dombeya digyna is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
