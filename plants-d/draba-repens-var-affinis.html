Draba repens var. affinis

Draba repens var. affinis, commonly known as the creeping whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, basal leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments, often found on rocky soils and tundra. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Brassicaceae family, sharing similar floral structures and ecological preferences.
