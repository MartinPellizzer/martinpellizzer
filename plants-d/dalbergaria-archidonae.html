Dalbergaria archidonae

Dalbergaria archidonae, commonly known as the Natal glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
