Dendrochilum karoense

Dendrochilum karoense, commonly known as the Karo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid, shaded environments where it thrives.

Dendrochilum karoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
