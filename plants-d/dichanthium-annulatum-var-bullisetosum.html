Dichanthium annulatum var. bullisetosum

Dichanthium annulatum var. bullisetosum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
