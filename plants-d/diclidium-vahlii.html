Diclidium vahlii

Diclidium vahlii, commonly known as the Vahli's thornapple, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Diclidium vahlii is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal value.
