Dactylostalix ringens f. punctatus

Dactylostalix ringens f. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted green alder, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a distinctive rust-colored spot. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
