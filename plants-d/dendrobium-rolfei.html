Dendrobium rolfei

Dendrobium rolfei, commonly known as the Rolfe's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the summer months. It thrives in humid, warm climates with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium rolfei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
