Dracaena cooperi

Dracaena cooperi, commonly known as the Coopers' dracaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are usually dark green with a reddish tint along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear at the base of the leaves. Dracaena cooperi is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.
