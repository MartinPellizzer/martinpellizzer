Drimia secundiflora

Drimia secundiflora, commonly known as the Cape daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms in spring and is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Drimia secundiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
