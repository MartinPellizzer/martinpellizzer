Dioscorea wittiana

Dioscorea wittiana, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary botanical feature of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and later develops capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential pharmaceutical applications.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
