Dendrocalamus tibeticus

Dendrocalamus tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan bamboo, is a tall, sturdy grass native to the Himalayan regions of China, India, and Nepal.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is well adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soils. Its culms are used in traditional construction and as a source of raw material for various crafts.

Dendrocalamus tibeticus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain and supporting local biodiversity.
