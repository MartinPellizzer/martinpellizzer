Dianthus superbus subsp. superbus

Dianthus superbus subsp.

superbus, commonly known as the tall catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Dianthus superbus subsp.

superbus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
