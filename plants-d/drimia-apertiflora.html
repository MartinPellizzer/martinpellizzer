Drimia apertiflora

Drimia apertiflora, commonly known as the open-flowered dragon's tongue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Drimia apertiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
