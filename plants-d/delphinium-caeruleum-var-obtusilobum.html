Delphinium caeruleum var. obtusilobum

Delphinium caeruleum var. obtusilobum, commonly known as the blunt-lobed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rounded lobes, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
