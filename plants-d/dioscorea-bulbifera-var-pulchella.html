Dioscorea bulbifera var. pulchella

Dioscorea bulbifera var. pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful yam or the African yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped tubers that are often used as ornamental plants due to their unique appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Despite its aesthetic appeal, the tubers are not typically consumed due to their bitter taste and potential toxicity.
