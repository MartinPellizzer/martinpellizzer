Dipsacus fullonum f. ternatus

Dipsacus fullonum f. ternatus, commonly known as the velvet tare, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
