Diospyros inconstans

Diospyros inconstans, commonly known as the variable persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by fruit that can vary in size and color depending on environmental conditions.

The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
