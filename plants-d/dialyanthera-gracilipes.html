Dialyanthera gracilipes

Dialyanthera gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive with minimal direct sunlight. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying orchid diversity and adaptation.
