Dryopteris bullatipaleacea

Dryopteris bullatipaleacea, commonly known as the bullate palea wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its spores are produced in sori that are covered by a protective indusium, a feature common among many fern species.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
