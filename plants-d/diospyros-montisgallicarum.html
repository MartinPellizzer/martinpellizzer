Diospyros montisgallicarum

Diospyros montisgallicarum, commonly known as the Gallican mountain persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a deep red or orange color, often containing a single seed.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
