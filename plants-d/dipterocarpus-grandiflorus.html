Dipterocarpus grandiflorus

Dipterocarpus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, often reddish bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
