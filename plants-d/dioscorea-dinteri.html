Dioscorea dinteri

Dioscorea dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's yam, is a tuberous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy underground stems that store nutrients. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in hot, dry climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tubers of Dioscorea dinteri are edible when properly prepared and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.
