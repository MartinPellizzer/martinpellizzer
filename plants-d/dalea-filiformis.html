Dalea filiformis

Dalea filiformis, commonly known as slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has compound leaves with slender leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Dalea filiformis is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
