Dalea pulchra

Dalea pulchra, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Dalea pulchra is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinators.
