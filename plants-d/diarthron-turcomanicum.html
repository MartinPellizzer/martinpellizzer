Diarthron turcomanicum

Diarthron turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Diarthron turcomanicum is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
