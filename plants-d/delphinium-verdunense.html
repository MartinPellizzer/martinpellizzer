Delphinium verdunense

Delphinium verdunense, commonly known as the Verdun larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium verdunense is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
