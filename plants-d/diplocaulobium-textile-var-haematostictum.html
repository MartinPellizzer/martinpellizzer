Diplocaulobium textile var. haematostictum

Diplocaulobium textile var. haematostictum, commonly known as the blood-stained textile fern, is a varietal form of the textile fern.

It is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, slender, and finely divided leaves. Its fronds are typically bright green with reddish or purplish veins, especially when grown in shaded conditions.

This fern is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
