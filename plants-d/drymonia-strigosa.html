Drymonia strigosa

Drymonia strigosa, commonly known as the hairy drymonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Drymonia strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
