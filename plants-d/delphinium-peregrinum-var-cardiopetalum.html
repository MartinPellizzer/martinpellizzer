Delphinium peregrinum var. cardiopetalum

Delphinium peregrinum var. cardiopetalum, commonly known as the heart-petal delphinium, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of vivid blue to purple flowers with deeply lobed petals. The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic properties. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
