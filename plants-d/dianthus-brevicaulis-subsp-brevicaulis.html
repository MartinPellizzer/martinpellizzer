Dianthus brevicaulis subsp. brevicaulis

Dianthus brevicaulis subsp.

brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
