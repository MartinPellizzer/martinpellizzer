Dendrobium rhodochilum

Dendrobium rhodochilum, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without being parasitic. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, with distinctive red lips on the petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Dendrobium rhodochilum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
