Digitaria ischaemum var. mississipiensis

Digitaria ischaemum var. mississipiensis, commonly known as Mississippi wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
