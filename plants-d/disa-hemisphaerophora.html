Disa hemisphaerophora

Disa hemisphaerophora, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a short, erect stem with a single, showy flower that is usually pink or purple in color. Its unique floral structure includes a large, hood-like labellum that distinguishes it from other orchid species.

Disa hemisphaerophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
