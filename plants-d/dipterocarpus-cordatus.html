Dipterocarpus cordatus

Dipterocarpus cordatus, commonly known as the heart-shaped dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
