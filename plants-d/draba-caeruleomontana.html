Draba caeruleomontana

Draba caeruleomontana, commonly known as the blue mountain rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Draba caeruleomontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
