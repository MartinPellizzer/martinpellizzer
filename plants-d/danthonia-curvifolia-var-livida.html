Danthonia curvifolia var. livida

Danthonia curvifolia var. livida, commonly known as the silver feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
