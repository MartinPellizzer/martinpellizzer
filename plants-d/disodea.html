Disodea

Disodea, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that often resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The leaves are typically opposite and oval-shaped, with a glossy texture. Disodea species are native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
