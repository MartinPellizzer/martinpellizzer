Dioscorea raziae

Dioscorea raziae, commonly known as the Madagascar yam, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers and has palmately lobed leaves. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential economic value.
