Dianthus seguieri var. queraltii

Dianthus seguieri var. queraltii, commonly known as the rock campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Dianthus seguieri var. queraltii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
