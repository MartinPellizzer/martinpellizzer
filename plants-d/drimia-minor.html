Drimia minor

Drimia minor, commonly known as the lesser dragon's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Drimia minor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
