Daviesia lucens

Daviesia lucens, commonly known as the yellow Daviesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Daviesia lucens is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
