Distephanus qazmi

Distephanus qazmi, commonly known as the Qazmi starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It produces a unique fruit with a star-shaped appearance, which is typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications due to its sweet and tangy flavor.
