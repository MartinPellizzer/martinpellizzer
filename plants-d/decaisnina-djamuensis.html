Decaisnina djamuensis

Decaisnina djamuensis, commonly known as the Javanese sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its unique scent and durability.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
