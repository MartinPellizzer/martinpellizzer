Diplodonta setosa

Diplodonta setosa, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are hairy on the underside, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Diplodonta setosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
