Drynaria schomburgkiana

Drynaria schomburgkiana, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, bamboo-like stems that are covered in a dense layer of brown, hairy scales. The plant produces large, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
