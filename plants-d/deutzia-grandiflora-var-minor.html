Deutzia grandiflora var. minor

Deutzia grandiflora var. minor, commonly known as dwarf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and compact growth habit in gardens.
