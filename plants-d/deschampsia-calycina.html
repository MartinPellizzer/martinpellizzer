Deschampsia calycina

Deschampsia calycina, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and silver-tipped inflorescences, which give it a delicate appearance. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Silky bluegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
