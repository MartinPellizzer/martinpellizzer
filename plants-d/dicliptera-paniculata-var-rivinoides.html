Dicliptera paniculata var. rivinoides

Dicliptera paniculata var. rivinoides, commonly known as the river grass or river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes, riverbanks, and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has a slender, creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense mats, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in panicles, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and remediate polluted waters.
