Delphinium tricolor

Delphinium tricolor, commonly known as the three-color larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of blue, purple, and white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium tricolor is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other showy flowering plants.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and alpine gardens.
