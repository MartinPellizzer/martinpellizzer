Dianthus pyrenaicus subsp. attenuatus

Dianthus pyrenaicus subsp.

attenuatus, commonly known as the Pyrenean campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
