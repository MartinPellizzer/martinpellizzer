Diospyros saxicola

Diospyros saxicola, commonly known as the rock persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Diospyros saxicola is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in conditions with limited water availability.
