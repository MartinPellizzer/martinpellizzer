Dendrobium discolor f. broomfieldii

Dendrobium discolor f. broomfieldii, commonly known as the Broomfield's Dendrobium, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated foliage and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple small, tubular flowers that bloom sequentially over several weeks.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and long-lasting blooms.
