Disocactus × amaranthinus

Disocactus × amaranthinus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Disocactus phyllanthus and Disocactus flagelliformis.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that resemble those of a cactus, although it is not a true cactus but rather a member of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Disocactus × amaranthinus is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
