Dentaria trifida f. grandiflora

Dentaria trifida f. grandiflora, commonly known as the showy toothworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tooth-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is native to North America and is often found in the eastern United States.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
