Dendrophthora danceri

Dendrophthora danceri, commonly known as the dancer's tree fungus, is a unique species of basidiomycete fungus.

It is primarily found growing on decaying wood in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's structure consists of a mycelial network that colonizes the inner layers of dead trees. It plays a vital role in nutrient cycling by decomposing organic matter.

Despite its ecological importance, little is known about its full biological and chemical properties.
