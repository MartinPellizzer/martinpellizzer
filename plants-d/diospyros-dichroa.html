Diospyros dichroa

Diospyros dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its timber in traditional crafts.
