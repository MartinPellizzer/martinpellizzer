Dactylorhiza comosa subsp. turfosa

Dactylorhiza comosa subsp.

turfosa, commonly known as the Turf Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, creeping rhizome and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves and is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, which contribute to its common name.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting various pollinators and maintaining soil stability in its native environments.
