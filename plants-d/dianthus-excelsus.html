Dianthus excelsus

Dianthus excelsus, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple central stripe. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, well-drained soils. It produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open meadows and slopes at high altitudes.

Dianthus excelsus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
