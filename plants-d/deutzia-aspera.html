Deutzia aspera

Deutzia aspera, commonly known as rough deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a rough texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
