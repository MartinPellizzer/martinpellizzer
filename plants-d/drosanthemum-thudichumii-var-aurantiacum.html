Drosanthemum thudichumii var. aurantiacum

Drosanthemum thudichumii var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange-flowered drosanthemum, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
