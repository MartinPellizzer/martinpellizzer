Disperis preussii

Disperis preussii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Disperis preussii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
