Draba spectabilis

Draba spectabilis, commonly known as the showy whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils. It produces basal rosettes of narrow, glaucous leaves and blooms with yellow or white flowers in the spring.

Draba spectabilis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
