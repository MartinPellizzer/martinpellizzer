Diosma pedicellata

Diosma pedicellata, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Diosma pedicellata is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
