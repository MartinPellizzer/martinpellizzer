Diodella crassifolia

Diodella crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in regions with Mediterranean climates.
