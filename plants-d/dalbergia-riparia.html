Dalbergia riparia

Dalbergia riparia, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and dense canopy. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. Its small, clustered flowers are typically yellow or green, and it produces legumes that contain seeds used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and crafts.
