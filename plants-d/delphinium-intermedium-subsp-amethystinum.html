Delphinium intermedium subsp. amethystinum

Delphinium intermedium subsp.

amethystinum, commonly known as the Amethyst Larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking violet-blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
