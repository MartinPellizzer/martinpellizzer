Dioscorea tarijensis

Dioscorea tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis yam, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in Bolivia, Peru, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits, although further scientific research is needed to validate these claims.
