Diasperus salviifolius

Diasperus salviifolius, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
