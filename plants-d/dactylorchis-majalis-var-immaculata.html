Dactylorchis majalis var. immaculata

Dactylorchis majalis var. immaculata, commonly known as the white-flowered dactylorchis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are relatively small but attract various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
