Dunbaria stipulata

Dunbaria stipulata, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name.

Dunbaria stipulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
