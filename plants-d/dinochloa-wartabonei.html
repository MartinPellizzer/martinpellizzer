Dinochloa wartabonei

Dinochloa wartabonei, commonly known as the Wartabone grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
