Damnacanthus indicus var. microphyllus

Damnacanthus indicus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved devil's claw, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically orange or red in color. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed pods that resemble claws, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
