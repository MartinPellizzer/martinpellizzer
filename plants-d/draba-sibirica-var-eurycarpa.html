Draba sibirica var. eurycarpa

Draba sibirica var. eurycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cold climates. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its dissemination in harsh environments.
