Diplotaxis virgata subsp. longisiliqua

Diplotaxis virgata subsp.

longisiliqua, commonly known as the long-stalked sandbur, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems with small, pale green leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its elongated, bristly seed pods that mature into spiny structures.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
