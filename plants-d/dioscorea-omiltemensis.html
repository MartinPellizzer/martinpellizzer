Dioscorea omiltemensis

Dioscorea omiltemensis, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The species is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its adaptability to various soil conditions.
