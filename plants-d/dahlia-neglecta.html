Dahlia neglecta

Dahlia neglecta, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its tuberous roots are an important part of its life cycle, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
