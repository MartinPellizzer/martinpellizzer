Dichanthelium xalapense

Dichanthelium xalapense, commonly known as Xalapense grass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Dichanthelium xalapense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
