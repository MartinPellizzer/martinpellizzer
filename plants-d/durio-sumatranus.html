Durio sumatranus

Durio sumatranus, commonly known as the West Sumatran durian, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the genus *Durio* in the family *Malvaceae*, and is closely related to other durian species. The plant is characterized by its large, spiky, green husk that encases the creamy, aromatic pulp. Its flowers are typically solitary and borne on the trunk or branches, and it produces fruit year-round in suitable climates.

The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with well-drained soil, making it a significant crop in Southeast Asia.
