Dendrobium crassimarginatum

Dendrobium crassimarginatum, commonly known as the broad-margin orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and long, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium crassimarginatum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
