Dioncophyllum peltatum

Dioncophyllum peltatum, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Dioncophyllum peltatum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, where it thrives in full sun.
