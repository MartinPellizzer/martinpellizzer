Desmos purpuratus

Desmos purpuratus, commonly known as the purple desmodium, is a flowering plant native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered purple flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
