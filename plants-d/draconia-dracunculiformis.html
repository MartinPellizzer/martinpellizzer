Draconia dracunculiformis

Draconia dracunculiformis, commonly known as the dragon's tail, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are often mistaken for fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing along the edges of forests or in damp, rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
