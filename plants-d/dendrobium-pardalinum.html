Dendrobium pardalinum

Dendrobium pardalinum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, cane-like stem with multiple nodes, and its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, often with a red or orange lip. The plant produces clusters of fragrant blooms during the summer months, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium pardalinum is known for its resilience and ability to bloom repeatedly under proper care.
