Dyssodia fimbriata

Dyssodia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Dyssodia fimbriata is often found in coastal and inland regions, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
