Dendrochilum eximium

Dendrochilum eximium, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that bear numerous fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Dendrochilum eximium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
