Dactylorhiza russowii

Dactylorhiza russowii, commonly known as the Russow's orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Dactylorhiza russowii is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
