Dianthus calverti

Dianthus calverti, commonly known as the Calvert's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features simple, basal leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and meadows.

Dianthus calverti is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
