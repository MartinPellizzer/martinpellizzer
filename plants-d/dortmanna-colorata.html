Dortmanna colorata

Dortmanna colorata, commonly known as the colorful moss, is a species of liverwort that belongs to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth and vibrant green to yellowish-green coloration. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its structure consists of closely packed leaves that form a mat, with small, capsule-like sporangia at the tips of the stems.

Dortmanna colorata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
