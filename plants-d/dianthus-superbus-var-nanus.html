Dianthus superbus var. nanus

Dianthus superbus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf campion, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Europe.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
