Desmodium cinereum

Desmodium cinereum, commonly known as silverleaf desmodium, is a legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Desmodium cinereum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and forage production.
