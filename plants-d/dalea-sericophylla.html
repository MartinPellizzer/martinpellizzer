Dalea sericophylla

Dalea sericophylla, commonly known as the silky leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, silky-hair covered leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

Dalea sericophylla plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
