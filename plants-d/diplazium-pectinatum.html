Diplazium pectinatum

Diplazium pectinatum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a serrated and hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
