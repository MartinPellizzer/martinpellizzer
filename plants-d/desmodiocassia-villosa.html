Desmodiocassia villosa

Desmodiocassia villosa, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves that are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemose inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimalarial properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
