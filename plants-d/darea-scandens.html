Darea scandens

Darea scandens, commonly known as the climbing bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to attach itself to supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and trellises.
