Drimys winteri var. chilensis

Drimys winteri var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean winter's bark, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are borne in clusters, followed by dark purple berries.

The bark is thick and flaky, and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
