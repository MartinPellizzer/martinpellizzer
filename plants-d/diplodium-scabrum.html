Diplodium scabrum

Diplodium scabrum, commonly known as rough diplodium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Diplodium scabrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
