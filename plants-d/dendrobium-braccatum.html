Dendrobium braccatum

Dendrobium braccatum, commonly known as the clustered dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that often form dense clusters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are linear and arranged spirally along the stem, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium braccatum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
