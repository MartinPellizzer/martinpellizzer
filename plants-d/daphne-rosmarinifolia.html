Daphne rosmarinifolia

Daphne rosmarinifolia, commonly known as rosemary daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
