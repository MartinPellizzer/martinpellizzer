Diasperus oxycarpus

Diasperus oxycarpus, commonly known as the sharp-fruited grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fruits are dry, three-angled nuts that are typically dispersed by wind or animals.

Diasperus oxycarpus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of grassland ecosystems.
