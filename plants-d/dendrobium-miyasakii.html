Dendrobium miyasakii

Dendrobium miyasakii, commonly known as the Miasakii orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, cane-like pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous fragrant, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
