Deutzia gracilis var. campanulata

Deutzia gracilis var. campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its delicate floral display and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
