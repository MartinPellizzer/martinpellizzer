Dircaea magnifica

Dircaea magnifica, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Dircaea magnifica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
