Dalbergia johorensis

Dalbergia johorensis, commonly known as the Malacca rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are clustered in racemes, typically appearing in yellow or greenish hues.

Dalbergia johorensis is also known for its fragrant bark and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
