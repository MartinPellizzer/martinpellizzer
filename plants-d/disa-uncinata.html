Disa uncinata

Disa uncinata, commonly known as the hooked disa, is a striking South African orchid species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet or purple flowers with distinctive hooked petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open grasslands, often in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the late summer to early autumn months, attracting various pollinators.

Disa uncinata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and uniqueness.
