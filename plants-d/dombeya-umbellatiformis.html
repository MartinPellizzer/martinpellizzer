Dombeya umbellatiformis

Dombeya umbellatiformis, commonly known as the umbrella dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pink inflorescences that resemble an umbrella, hence its common name. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Dombeya umbellatiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting floral display.
