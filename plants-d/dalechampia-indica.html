Dalechampia indica

Dalechampia indica, commonly known as the Indian champa or Indian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its latex sap is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
