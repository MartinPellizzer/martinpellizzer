Dioclea sclerocarpa

Dioclea sclerocarpa, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns of red and purple. The plant produces large, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

The fruit, which is a woody capsule, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.
