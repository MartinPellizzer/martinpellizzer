Draba incana f. tenuis

Draba incana f. tenuis, commonly known as the slender whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays.
