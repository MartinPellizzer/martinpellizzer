Daphne odora var. taiwaniana

Daphne odora var. taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan sweet spindle, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers, which are highly aromatic and attract pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy dark green surface.

The plant is relatively cold-hardy but requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun for optimal growth.
