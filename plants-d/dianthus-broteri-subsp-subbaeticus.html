Dianthus broteri subsp. subbaeticus

Dianthus broteri subsp.

subbaeticus, commonly known as the Baetican campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.
