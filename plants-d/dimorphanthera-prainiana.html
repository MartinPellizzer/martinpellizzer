Dimorphanthera prainiana

Dimorphanthera prainiana, commonly known as the Prain's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy floral structures. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually fragrant and exhibit a complex arrangement of petals and sepals, which play a role in attracting pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
