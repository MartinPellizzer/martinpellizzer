Dipsacus pilosus

Dipsacus pilosus, commonly known as the velvet tangle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of purple to reddish flowers. The plant has rough, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost velvety appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Dipsacus pilosus is known for its ability to reseed itself, making it a common sight in various habitats.
