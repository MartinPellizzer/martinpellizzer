Diclinanona calycina

Diclinanona calycina, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that hang in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Diclinanona calycina is a climbing plant that can reach lengths of several meters, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
