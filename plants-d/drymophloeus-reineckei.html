Drymophloeus reineckei

Drymophloeus reineckei, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and can grow up to 25 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
