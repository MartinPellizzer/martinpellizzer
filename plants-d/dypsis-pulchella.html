Dypsis pulchella

Dypsis pulchella, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and tuft of leaves at the top. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Dypsis pulchella thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
