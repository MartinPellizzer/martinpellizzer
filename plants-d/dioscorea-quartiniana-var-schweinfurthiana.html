Dioscorea quartiniana var. schweinfurthiana

Dioscorea quartiniana var. schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the Schwerin yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its tubers, which are the edible part, are typically harvested after a long growing season and are valued for their nutritional content.

This variety is often cultivated for both its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional practices.
