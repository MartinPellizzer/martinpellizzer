Dichapetalum montanum

Dichapetalum montanum, commonly known as the mountain dichapetalum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, often blooming in spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
