Dioicodendron dioicum

Dioicodendron dioicum, commonly known as the dioicodendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its dioecious nature, meaning that male and female reproductive structures are found on separate plants. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in temperate regions. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in forest understories and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations.
