Diascia rigescens

Diascia rigescens, commonly known as the hardy geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink or red blooms that appear in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Diascia rigescens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
