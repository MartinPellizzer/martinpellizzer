Danthonia picta

Danthonia picta, commonly known as the painted grass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive, often colorful inflorescences that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open woodlands and grasslands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Danthonia picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens and restoration projects.
