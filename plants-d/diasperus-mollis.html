Diasperus mollis

Diasperus mollis, commonly known as the soft-hairy sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features soft, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces vibrant yellow flowers with a central disk, typical of the genus.

Diasperus mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
