Dianella

Dianella, commonly known as the flax lily or sea onion, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia and New Zealand.

These plants are characterized by their grass-like foliage and striking blue or purple flower spikes that appear in spring. Dianella species typically grow in coastal areas, rocky soils, or damp environments, showcasing their adaptability to various conditions. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in shady, moist gardens.
