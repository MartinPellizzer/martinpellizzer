Diastella fraterna

Diastella fraterna, commonly known as the African snowball, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, woolly flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Diastella fraterna is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
