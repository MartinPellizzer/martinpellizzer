Diporidium bernieri

Diporidium bernieri, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive greenish throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
