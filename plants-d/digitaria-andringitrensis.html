Digitaria andringitrensis

Digitaria andringitrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Digitaria andringitrensis is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
