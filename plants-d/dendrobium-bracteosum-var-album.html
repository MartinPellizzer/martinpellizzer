Dendrobium bracteosum var. album

Dendrobium bracteosum var. album, commonly known as the white dendrobium, is a flowering orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant features a tall, cane-like stem with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are typically elongated and strap-shaped, and it requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The variety is valued for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
