Dischidia joloensis

Dischidia joloensis, commonly known as the Jolo dischidia, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its unique growth habit and ability to thrive in shaded environments make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The species is also notable for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
