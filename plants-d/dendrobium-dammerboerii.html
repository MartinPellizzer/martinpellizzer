Dendrobium dammerboerii

Dendrobium dammerboerii, commonly known as the Damarboer's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with white to pale yellow blossoms that bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium dammerboerii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
