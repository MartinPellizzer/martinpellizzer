Dalea mollis var. mollissima

Dalea mollis var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, slender stems and small, pink to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and sagebrush habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and slightly hairy.

Dalea mollis var. mollissima plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
