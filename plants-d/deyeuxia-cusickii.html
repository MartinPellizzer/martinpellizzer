Deyeuxia cusickii

Deyeuxia cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's woodoat, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Deyeuxia cusickii plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
