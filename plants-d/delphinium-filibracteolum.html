Delphinium filibracteolum

Delphinium filibracteolum, commonly known as the slenderleaf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
