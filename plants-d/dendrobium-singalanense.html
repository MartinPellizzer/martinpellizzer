Dendrobium singalanense

Dendrobium singalanense, commonly known as the Singalana orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, thin pseudobulbs. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium singalanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
