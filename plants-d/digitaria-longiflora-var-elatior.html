Digitaria longiflora var. elatior

Digitaria longiflora var. elatior, commonly known as the tall false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, tufted stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers, which are borne on spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
