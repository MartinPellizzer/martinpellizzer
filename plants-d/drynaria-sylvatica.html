Drynaria sylvatica

Drynaria sylvatica, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species native to Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Drynariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that resemble a fern. The plant typically grows on rocky or wooded slopes, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The silver fern is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
