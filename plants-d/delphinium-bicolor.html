Delphinium bicolor

Delphinium bicolor, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spindly stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Delphinium bicolor is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its striking floral displays and ornamental value in gardens.
