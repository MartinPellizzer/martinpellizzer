Dipterocarpus parviflorus

Dipterocarpus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, winged seeds and is part of the Dipterocarpaceae family, which includes many valuable timber species. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and is known for its durable, heavy wood. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.

Its flowers are small and green, often inconspicuous, but its fruits are distinctive and widely dispersed by animals.
