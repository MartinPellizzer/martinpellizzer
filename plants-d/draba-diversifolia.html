Draba diversifolia

Draba diversifolia, commonly known as the variable whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Draba diversifolia has a low-growing habit with basal leaves and is often found in meadows and along mountain trails.

Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.
