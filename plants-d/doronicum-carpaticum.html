Doronicum carpaticum

Doronicum carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
