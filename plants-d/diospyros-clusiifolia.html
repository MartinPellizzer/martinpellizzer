Diospyros clusiifolia

Diospyros clusiifolia, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a pulp.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
