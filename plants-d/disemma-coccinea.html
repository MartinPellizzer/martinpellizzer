Disemma coccinea

Disemma coccinea, commonly known as the red flame vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Disemma coccinea is a climbing plant that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters.
