Dioscorea trifurcata

Dioscorea trifurcata, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its trifurcate (three-branched) stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and cylindrical, tuberous roots that are used as food in some regions. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical areas.
