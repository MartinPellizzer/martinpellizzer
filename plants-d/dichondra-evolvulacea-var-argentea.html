Dichondra evolvulacea var. argentea

Dichondra evolvulacea var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf dichondra, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ground cover in ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative element in landscaping.
