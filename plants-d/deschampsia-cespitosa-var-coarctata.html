Deschampsia cespitosa var. coarctata

Deschampsia cespitosa var. coarctata, commonly known as narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
