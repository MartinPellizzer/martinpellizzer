Dipterocarpus indicus

Dipterocarpus indicus, commonly known as the Indian dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruits are woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

This species plays an important ecological role in tropical forests and is valued for its timber.
