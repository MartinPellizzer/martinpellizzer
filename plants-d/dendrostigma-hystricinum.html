Dendrostigma hystricinum

Dendrostigma hystricinum, commonly known as the spiny pachycaul, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
