Denisia wilmsii

Denisia wilmsii, commonly known as the Wilmsii orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a complex structure typical of orchids, featuring a lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Denisia wilmsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
