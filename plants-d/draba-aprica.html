Draba aprica

Draba aprica, commonly known as the spring whitlow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

Draba aprica is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.
