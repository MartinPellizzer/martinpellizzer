Dianthus muglensis

Dianthus muglensis, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers, which are typically arranged in a cluster of five to seven petals. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Dianthus muglensis is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
