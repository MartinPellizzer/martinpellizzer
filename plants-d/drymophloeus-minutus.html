Drymophloeus minutus

Drymophloeus minutus, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and narrow, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in many tropical gardens.
