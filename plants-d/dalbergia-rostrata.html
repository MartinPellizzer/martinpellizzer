Dalbergia rostrata

Dalbergia rostrata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to central and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and acoustic properties. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes.

Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several flattened, bean-like seeds.
