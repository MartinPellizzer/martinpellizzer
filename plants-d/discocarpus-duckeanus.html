Discocarpus duckeanus

Discocarpus duckeanus, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or wooded areas.

Its unique blooming pattern and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
