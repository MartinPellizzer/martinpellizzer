Darea trilobata

Darea trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed, often with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

Darea trilobata is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, thriving in temperate climates.
