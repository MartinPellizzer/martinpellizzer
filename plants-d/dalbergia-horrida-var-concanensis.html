Dalbergia horrida var. concanensis

Dalbergia horrida var. concanensis, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a species native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and distinctive foliage. The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
