Diospyros mcphersonii

Diospyros mcphersonii, commonly known as the McPherson persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. Its fruit is a small, orange-red berry that is typically consumed raw or cooked, and it is valued for its sweet, edible pulp.

The species is also notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
