Draba muralis f. major

Draba muralis f. major, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features small, basal leaves that are usually woolly or hairy, and its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in a cluster. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh conditions and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
