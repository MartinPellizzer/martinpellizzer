Dianella intermedia var. nukuhivensis

Dianella intermedia var. nukuhivensis, commonly known as the Nukuhiva lily, is a species native to the tropical Pacific islands.

It is a low-growing, evergreen herb with strap-like, dark green leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces striking, white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive purple stripe on the outer petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
