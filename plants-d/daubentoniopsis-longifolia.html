Daubentoniopsis longifolia

Daubentoniopsis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved daubentoniopsis, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
