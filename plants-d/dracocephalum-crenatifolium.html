Dracocephalum crenatifolium

Dracocephalum crenatifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, blue-violet flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
