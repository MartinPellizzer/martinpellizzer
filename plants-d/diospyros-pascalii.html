Diospyros pascalii

Diospyros pascalii, commonly known as the Pascal's ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries.

Diospyros pascalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts.
