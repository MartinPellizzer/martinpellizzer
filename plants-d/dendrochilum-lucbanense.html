Dendrochilum lucbanense

Dendrochilum lucbanense, commonly known as the Lucban orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, making it visually striking.

Dendrochilum lucbanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
