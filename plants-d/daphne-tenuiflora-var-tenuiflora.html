Daphne tenuiflora var. tenuiflora

Daphne tenuiflora var. tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and winter flowering.
