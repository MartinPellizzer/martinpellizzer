Draba tichomirovii

Draba tichomirovii, commonly known as the Tichomirov's whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is typically found in alpine and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of hair.

Draba tichomirovii is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes where it thrives in well-drained soils.
