Dianella dentata

Dianella dentata, commonly known as the toothed flax-lily, is a perennial plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower spikes in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dianella dentata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
