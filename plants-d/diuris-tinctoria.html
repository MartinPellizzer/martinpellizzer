Diuris tinctoria

Diuris tinctoria, commonly known as the Crimson Spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with long, slender sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually a vivid red or orange, with a unique, intricate structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Diuris tinctoria is a perennial herb that produces a single, showy flower per stem, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
