Dianthus degenii

Dianthus degenii, commonly known as the Dodecanese campion, is a flowering plant native to the Greek Dodecanese islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Dianthus degenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
