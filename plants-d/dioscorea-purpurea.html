Dioscorea purpurea

Dioscorea purpurea, commonly known as purple yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been used in traditional cuisines for centuries.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in various cultures.
