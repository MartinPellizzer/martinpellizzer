Diplostephium tamanum

Diplostephium tamanum, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value.

The botanical features of Diplostephium tamanum make it an important species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
