Dracaena pachyphylla

Dracaena pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved dracaena, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with a woody stem and spirally arranged leaves. Its leaves are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Dracaena pachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate low water conditions.
