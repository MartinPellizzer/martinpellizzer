Diplolepis biflora

Diplolepis biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in pairs, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Diplolepis biflora is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade.
