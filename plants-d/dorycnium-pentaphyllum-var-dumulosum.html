Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. dumulosum

Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. dumulosum, commonly known as the rough-tongued vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a slender, twisted pod that contains several seeds.

It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
