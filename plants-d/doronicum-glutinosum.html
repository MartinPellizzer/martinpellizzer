Doronicum glutinosum

Doronicum glutinosum, commonly known as sticky bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Doronicum glutinosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
