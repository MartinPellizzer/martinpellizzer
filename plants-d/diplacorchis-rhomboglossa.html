Diplacorchis rhomboglossa

Diplacorchis rhomboglossa, commonly known as the rhombus-tongued orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flowers that often display a striking combination of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The orchid's pollination is primarily facilitated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native ecosystem.
