Dianthus brachyanthus var. longimucronatus

Dianthus brachyanthus var. longimucronatus, commonly known as the long-tipped campion, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated, pointed calyx lobes, which give it its specific epithet.
