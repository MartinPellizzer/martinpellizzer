Daviesia pachyloma

Daviesia pachyloma, commonly known as the woolly daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring season, adding visual interest to its structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Daviesia pachyloma is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
