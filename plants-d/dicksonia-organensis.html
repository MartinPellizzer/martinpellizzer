Dicksonia organensis

Dicksonia organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the United States, specifically found in the Organ Mountains of New Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky slopes or in forest understories. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the growth of its expansive frond system.

Dicksonia organensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
