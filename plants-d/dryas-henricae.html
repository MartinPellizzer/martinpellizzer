Dryas henricae

Dryas henricae, commonly known as the Henry's dryas, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red, hairy fruits. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

Dryas henricae is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or barren soils.
