Datura fastuosa var. parviflora

Datura fastuosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered angel's trumpet, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and milky sap. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers open in the evening and emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
