Doria arbuscula

Doria arbuscula, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Doria arbuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
