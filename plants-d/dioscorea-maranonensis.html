Dioscorea maranonensis

Dioscorea maranonensis, commonly known as the Maranon yam, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by heart-shaped leaves and yellowish fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for food and traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its adaptability to humid, tropical environments and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
