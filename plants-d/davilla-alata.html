Davilla alata

Davilla alata, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a deciduous shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white undersides on its heart-shaped leaves, which give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Davilla alata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscape design.
