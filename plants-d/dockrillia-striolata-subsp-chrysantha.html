Dockrillia striolata subsp. chrysantha

Dockrillia striolata subsp.

chrysantha, commonly known as the golden dockrillia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its habitat.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive striped pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
