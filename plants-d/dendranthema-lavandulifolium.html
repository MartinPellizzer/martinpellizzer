Dendranthema lavandulifolium

Dendranthema lavandulifolium, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow center. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of lavender, giving it its scientific name. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Dendranthema lavandulifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for border plantings and floral arrangements.
