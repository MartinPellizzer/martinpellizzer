Didymocarpus bicolor

Didymocarpus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored or white-flowered lychee, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a rough, hairy drupe that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
