Dracaena marumaru

Dracaena marumaru, commonly known as the red dragon tree, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It features tall, slender stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by orange-red berries that attract birds. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly wavy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dracaena marumaru thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
