Delphinium bicornutum subsp. oaxacanum

Delphinium bicornutum subsp.

oaxacanum, commonly known as the Oaxacan larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
