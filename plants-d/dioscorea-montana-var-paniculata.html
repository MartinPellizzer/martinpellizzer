Dioscorea montana var. paniculata

Dioscorea montana var. paniculata, commonly known as the paniculate yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy tubers that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a distinctive, branched inflorescence.

The tubers are often harvested for their nutritional value and medicinal properties.
