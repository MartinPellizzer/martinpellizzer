Didymocarpus humilis

Didymocarpus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Didymocarpus humilis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
