Dioclea cassinoides

Dioclea cassinoides, commonly known as the pink passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, with a complex structure featuring multiple corona-like appendages. The plant produces small, yellow, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by various animals. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Dioclea cassinoides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
