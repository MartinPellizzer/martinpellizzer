Diporidium vaccinioides

Diporidium vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry shrub, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or swamps.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
