Dicranopteris linearis var. subspeciosa

Dicranopteris linearis var. subspeciosa, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Dicranopteris.

It is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are typically less than 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its fronds are usually divided into small, lance-shaped segments, and it produces sporangia that release spores for reproduction.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to various aquatic habitats and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
