Dendranthema lavandulifolium var. aromaticum

Dendranthema lavandulifolium var. aromaticum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and showy clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
