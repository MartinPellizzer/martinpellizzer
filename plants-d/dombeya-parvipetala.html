Dombeya parvipetala

Dombeya parvipetala, commonly known as the small-flowered dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
