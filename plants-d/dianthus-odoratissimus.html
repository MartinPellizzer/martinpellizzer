Dianthus odoratissimus

Dianthus odoratissimus, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are known for their strong, pleasant fragrance.

Dianthus odoratissimus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
