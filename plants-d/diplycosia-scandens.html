Diplycosia scandens

Diplycosia scandens, commonly known as the climbing evergreen shrub, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, attracting various birds and small mammals.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along supports.
