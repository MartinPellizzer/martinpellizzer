Danthonia mossamedensis

Danthonia mossamedensis, commonly known as the Mossamedes grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
