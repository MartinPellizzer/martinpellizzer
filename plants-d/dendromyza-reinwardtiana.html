Dendromyza reinwardtiana

Dendromyza reinwardtiana, commonly known as the Reinwardt's tamarind, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. The seeds are rich in nutrients and have been used traditionally in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
