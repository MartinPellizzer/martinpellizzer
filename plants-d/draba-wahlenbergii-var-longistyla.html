Draba wahlenbergii var. longistyla

Draba wahlenbergii var. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.
