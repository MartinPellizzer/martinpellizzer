Delphinium forrestii var. leiophyllum

Delphinium forrestii var. leiophyllum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved larkspur, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture, which distinguishes it from other larkspur varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
