Dolichos jumellei

Dolichos jumellei, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, slender pods. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soils. It is often used as a forage crop and has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

Dolichos jumellei is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
