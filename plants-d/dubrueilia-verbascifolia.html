Dubrueilia verbascifolia

Dubrueilia verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in a thick layer of white pubescence.

Dubrueilia verbascifolia thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
