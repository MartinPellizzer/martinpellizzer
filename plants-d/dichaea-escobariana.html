Dichaea escobariana

Dichaea escobariana, commonly known as the Escobaria dichaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial bromeliad that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often on rock surfaces or in crevices. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color.

Dichaea escobariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
