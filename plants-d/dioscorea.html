Dioscorea

Dioscorea, commonly known as yam, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It comprises over 600 species, many of which are tuberous and grow underground. The plant is native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and its tubers are widely cultivated for food. The tubers vary in shape and size, and some species are used medicinally.

Dioscorea species are characterized by their climbing habit and unique floral structures.
