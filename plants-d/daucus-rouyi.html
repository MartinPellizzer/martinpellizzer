Daucus rouyi

Daucus rouyi, commonly known as the Chinese carrot or wild carrot, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. Its fruit is a dry, hairy schizocarp that splits into two one-seeded parts.

Daucus rouyi is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
