Dactylis abbreviata

Dactylis abbreviata, commonly known as purple grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple or reddish inflorescence. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and blue-green in color, with a slightly hairy surface.

Dactylis abbreviata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and as a forage crop.
