Danthonia villosa var. glabra

Danthonia villosa var. glabra, commonly known as silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and densely tufted foliage, which gives it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swampy areas and riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
