Drymonia crispa

Drymonia crispa, commonly known as the brittlestem hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its foliage is usually hairy, and the leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive crinkled texture.
