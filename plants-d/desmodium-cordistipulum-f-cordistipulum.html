Desmodium cordistipulum f. cordistipulum

Desmodium cordistipulum f. cordistipulum, commonly known as the heartleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
