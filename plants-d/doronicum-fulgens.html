Doronicum fulgens

Doronicum fulgens, commonly known as the golden bell, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine down.

Doronicum fulgens is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
