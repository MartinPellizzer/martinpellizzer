Dimorphanthes angustifolia

Dimorphanthes angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dimorphanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
