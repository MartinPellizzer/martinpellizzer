Delphinium laxicymosum var. pilostachyum

Delphinium laxicymosum var. pilostachyum, commonly known as the woolly larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, woolly coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to cold, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
