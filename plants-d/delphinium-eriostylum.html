Delphinium eriostylum

Delphinium eriostylum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. Delphinium eriostylum is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in alpine gardens.
