Dalechampia cissifolia subsp. panamensis

Dalechampia cissifolia subsp.

panamensis, commonly known as the Panama fig-leaf tree, is a species native to Central America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and has traditional uses in local cultures.
