Diplectria tetrandra

Diplectria tetrandra, commonly known as the four-threaded orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with four petals and a distinctive labellum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
