Drypetes similis

Drypetes similis, commonly known as the red sanders, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also listed as an endangered species in many regions.
