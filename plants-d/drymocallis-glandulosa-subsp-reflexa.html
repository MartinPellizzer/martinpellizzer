Drymocallis glandulosa subsp. reflexa

Drymocallis glandulosa subsp.

reflexa, commonly known as the glandular goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features glandular hairs on its leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance and contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
