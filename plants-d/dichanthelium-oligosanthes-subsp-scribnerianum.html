Dichanthelium oligosanthes subsp. scribnerianum

Dichanthelium oligosanthes subsp.

scribnerianum, commonly known as the Scribner's wiregrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as sandy soils and pine forests. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
