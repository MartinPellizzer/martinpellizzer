Diospyros nana

Diospyros nana, commonly known as the dwarf persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like the common ebony. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons.

Diospyros nana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
