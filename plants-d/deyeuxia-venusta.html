Deyeuxia venusta

Deyeuxia venusta, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Deyeuxia venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
