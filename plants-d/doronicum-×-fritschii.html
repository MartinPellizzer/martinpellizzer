Doronicum × fritschii

Doronicum × fritschii, commonly known as the hybrid leopard's bane, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Doronicum orientale and Doronicum pachyphyllum.

It is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly the Alps. The plant features upright, branched stems and produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers with distinctive dark markings. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine gardens.
