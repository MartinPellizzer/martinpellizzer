Diplazium caracasanum

Diplazium caracasanum, commonly known as the Caracasanum water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
