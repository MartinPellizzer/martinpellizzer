Digitalis lutea var. dyris

Digitalis lutea var. dyris, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive yellowish-green color. The flowers are typically yellow or yellow-orange, with a spurred shape typical of the Digitalis genus. It thrives in woodland or shady areas with moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
