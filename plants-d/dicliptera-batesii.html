Dicliptera batesii

Dicliptera batesii, commonly known as the African bristle grass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces dense, bristly inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture due to the presence of short, stiff hairs.

Dicliptera batesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its distinctive appearance.
