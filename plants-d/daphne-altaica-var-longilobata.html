Daphne altaica var. longilobata

Daphne altaica var. longilobata, commonly known as the Siberian Daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are dark green, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cold climates.
