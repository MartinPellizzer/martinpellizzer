Dianthus soongoricus

Dianthus soongoricus, commonly known as the Pamir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with five petals, and its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

Dianthus soongoricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
