Dimorphanthera seramica

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two distinct types of flowers on the same inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it thrives on decaying organic matter.
