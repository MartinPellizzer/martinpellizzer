Digitalis laevigata subsp. bosniaca

Digitalis laevigata subsp.

bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in woodland and shaded areas, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped, with a distinctive waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
