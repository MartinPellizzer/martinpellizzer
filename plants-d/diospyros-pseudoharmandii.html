Diospyros pseudoharmandii

Diospyros pseudoharmandii, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common persimmon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and the nutritional value of its fruits.
