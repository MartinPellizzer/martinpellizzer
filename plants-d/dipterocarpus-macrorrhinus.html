Dipterocarpus macrorrhinus

Dipterocarpus macrorrhinus, commonly known as the large-leaf dipterocarp, is a tall evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and heavy, aromatic wood. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
