Dioscoreopsis

Dioscoreopsis, commonly known as the arrowroot vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It consists of several species, many of which are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plants are typically climbing or trailing vines with heart-shaped leaves and produce small, yellowish flowers. They are often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Some species of Dioscoreopsis are cultivated for their starchy tubers, which are used as a food source in certain regions.
