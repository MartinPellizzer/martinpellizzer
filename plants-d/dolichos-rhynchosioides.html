Dolichos rhynchosioides

Dolichos rhynchosioides, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods. The plant is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and often used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Additionally, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a beneficial companion plant in agricultural settings.
