Dombeya quinqueseta var. vestita

Dombeya quinqueseta var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
