Daphne feddei var. feddei

Daphne feddei var. feddei, commonly known as the Western Cape daphne, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a velvety texture.

Daphne feddei var. feddei thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
