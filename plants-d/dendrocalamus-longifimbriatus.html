Dendrocalamus longifimbriatus

Dendrocalamus longifimbriatus, commonly known as the long-fringed bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing member of the grass family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a distinctive fringed appearance along the edges. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

This bamboo species is valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
