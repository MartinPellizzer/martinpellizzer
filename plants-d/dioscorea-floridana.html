Dioscorea floridana

Dioscorea floridana, commonly known as the Florida yam, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
