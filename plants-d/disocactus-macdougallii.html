Disocactus macdougallii

Disocactus macdougallii, commonly known as the Macdougall's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to drought conditions.

Disocactus macdougallii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
