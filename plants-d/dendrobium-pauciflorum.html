Dendrobium pauciflorum

Dendrobium pauciflorum, commonly known as the sparse-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium pauciflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
