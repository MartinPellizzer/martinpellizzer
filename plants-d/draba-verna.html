Draba verna

Draba verna, commonly known as spring whitlow, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are often fleshy and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Draba verna is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and arctic regions.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often among the first plants to bloom in early spring.
