Delphinium thibeticum var. schizophyllum

Delphinium thibeticum var. schizophyllum, commonly known as the Tibetan larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to cold climates and its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
