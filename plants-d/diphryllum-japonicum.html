Diphryllum japonicum

Diphryllum japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese miterwort, is a unique plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical leaves that resemble a miter or a crown, with a single vein running from the base to the tip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels.

Despite its unusual appearance, it plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
