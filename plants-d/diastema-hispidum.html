Diastema hispidum

Diastema hispidum, commonly known as the roughleaf spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its stems are erect and can become woody with age, while its leaves are alternate and usually have a serrated edge.

Diastema hispidum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitats.
