Dichaea dammeriana

Dichaea dammeriana, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, pale green or white, waxy flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Dichaea dammeriana is considered endangered in many areas.
