Dactylorhiza × abantiana

Dactylorhiza × abantiana, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies. The plant produces tall, slender stems with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, arranged in a spike and often have a distinctive hood-like structure.

Dactylorhiza × abantiana is known for its adaptability to moist environments and its role in ecological systems where it supports pollinators.
