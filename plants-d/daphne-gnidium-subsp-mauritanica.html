Daphne gnidium subsp. mauritanica

Daphne gnidium subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania daphne, is a shrub native to North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, red berries that are dispersed by birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
