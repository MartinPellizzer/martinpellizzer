Dissochaeta deusta

Dissochaeta deusta, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Dissochaeta deusta is a hardy, drought-tolerant species often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
