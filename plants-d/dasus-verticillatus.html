Dasus verticillatus

Dasus verticillatus, commonly known as the spiral leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and landscape design.
