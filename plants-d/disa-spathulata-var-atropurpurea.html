Disa spathulata var. atropurpurea

Disa spathulata var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-veined disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers with prominent veining. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of intricate, spurred orchid flowers. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often with a waxy texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
