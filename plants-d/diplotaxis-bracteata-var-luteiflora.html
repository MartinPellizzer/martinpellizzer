Diplotaxis bracteata var. luteiflora

Diplotaxis bracteata var. luteiflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy, ribbed stems and pinnately divided leaves.

The bright yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from spring to early summer.
