Diplachne fusca var. uninervia

Diplachne fusca var. uninervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or riparian environments, thriving in moist, sandy soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with parallel veins. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose, open cluster.

This variety is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and is often found in coastal or freshwater habitats.
