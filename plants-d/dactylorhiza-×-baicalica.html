Dactylorhiza × baicalica

Dactylorhiza × baicalica, commonly known as the Baical orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Russia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a rhizome and a single, fleshy, underground stem. The plant produces tall, upright flowering stems that bear numerous showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

This orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas with rich, humusy soil.
