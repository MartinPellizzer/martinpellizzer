Dioscorea echinata

Dioscorea echinata, commonly known as the spiny yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and hypoglycemic effects.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and requires careful handling during preparation.
