Didissandra elongata

Didissandra elongata, commonly known as the slender sandstone plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
