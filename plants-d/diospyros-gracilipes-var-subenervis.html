Diospyros gracilipes var. subenervis

Diospyros gracilipes var. subenervis, commonly known as the slender-veined persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its lanceolate leaves with prominent, subparallel veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
