Dichromena leucocephala

Dichromena leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy white ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Dichromena leucocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
