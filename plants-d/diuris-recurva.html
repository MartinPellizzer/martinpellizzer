Diuris recurva

Diuris recurva, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows in heathland and woodland habitats, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with several hairy leaves and produces clusters of pale green or yellowish flowers. Its unique flower structure includes a recurved (bent backward) lip, which is a distinguishing characteristic.

Diuris recurva is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
