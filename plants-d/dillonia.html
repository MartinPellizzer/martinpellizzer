Dillonia

Dillonia, commonly known as the "moonflower," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. Dillonia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for evening gardens and floral arrangements.
