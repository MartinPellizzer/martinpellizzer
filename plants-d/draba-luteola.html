Draba luteola

Draba luteola, commonly known as the yellow whitlow, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

Draba luteola is a winter annual, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.
