Diasperus taitensis

Diasperus taitensis, commonly known as the Taiti sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often growing in clusters, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional crafts, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical regions.
