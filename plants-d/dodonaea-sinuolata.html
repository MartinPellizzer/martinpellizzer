Dodonaea sinuolata

Dodonaea sinuolata, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light grey bark and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Dodonaea sinuolata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
