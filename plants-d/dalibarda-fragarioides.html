Dalibarda fragarioides

Dalibarda fragarioides, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, oval leaves and produces small, edible berries that mature to a red color. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and its association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
