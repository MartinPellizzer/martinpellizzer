Dryas tschonoskii

Dryas tschonoskii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. Dryas tschonoskii thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found at high elevations where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
