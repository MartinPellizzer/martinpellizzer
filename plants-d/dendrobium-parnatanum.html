Dendrobium parnatanum

Dendrobium parnatanum, commonly known as the Parnatana orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its terrestrial or epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in diverse environments. Its flowers are typically showy, with a variety of colors, and bloom during the warmer months.

Dendrobium parnatanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
