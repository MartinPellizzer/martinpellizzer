Dalea mollis var. neomexicana

Dalea mollis var. neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
