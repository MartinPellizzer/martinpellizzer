Dioscorea araucana

Dioscorea araucana, commonly known as the Andean yam, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers that are an important food source in indigenous communities. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool and moist environments. Its tubers have a rough, brown skin and a sweet, nutty flavor when cooked.

Dioscorea araucana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
