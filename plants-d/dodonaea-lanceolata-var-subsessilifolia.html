Dodonaea lanceolata var. subsessilifolia

Dodonaea lanceolata var. subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved screwwood, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and dryland environments.

Its foliage and floral structure contribute to its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
