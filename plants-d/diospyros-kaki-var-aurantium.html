Diospyros kaki var. aurantium

Diospyros kaki var. aurantium, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is typically orange to reddish in color, with a sweet, non-astringent flavor when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in both gardens and commercial cultivation.
