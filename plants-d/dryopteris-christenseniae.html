Dryopteris christenseniae

Dryopteris christenseniae, commonly known as the Christensen's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a finely divided structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive adequate humidity. Its rhizomes are short and creeping, allowing it to spread slowly and form dense colonies.

The spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in woodland ecosystems.
