Dianella sparsiflora var. ledermannii

Dianella sparsiflora var. ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's flax lily, is a striking evergreen perennial native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, low-growing rosette, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of striking blue to purple flowers on slender stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate light frost.
