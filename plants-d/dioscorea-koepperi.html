Dioscorea koepperi

Dioscorea koepperi, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, tubular fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

Dioscorea koepperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
