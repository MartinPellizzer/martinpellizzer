Dioscorea puncticulata

Dioscorea puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are covered in fine, hairy structures.

The tubers of Dioscorea puncticulata are valued for their nutritional content and have been utilized in various cultural and medicinal practices.
