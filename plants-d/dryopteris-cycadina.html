Dryopteris cycadina

Dryopteris cycadina, commonly known as the cycad fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that resemble those of cycads, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are often arched or drooping, and they can reach lengths of up to two meters.

Dryopteris cycadina is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
