Dysoxylum ficiforme

Dysoxylum ficiforme, commonly known as the giant fig tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and distinctive, fig-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its roots are often extensively branched.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
