Desmodium coeruleum

Desmodium coeruleum, commonly known as blue burr, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that enclose small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Desmodium coeruleum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
