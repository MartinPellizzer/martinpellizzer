Dolichos dongaluta

Dolichos dongaluta, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Dolichos dongaluta is valued for its nutritional content, providing protein and essential minerals.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
