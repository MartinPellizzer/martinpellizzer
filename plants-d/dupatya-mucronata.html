Dupatya mucronata

Dupatya mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
