Dasiphora fruticosa var. fruticosa

Dasiphora fruticosa var. fruticosa, commonly known as Indian paintbrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange-red bracts that resemble paintbrushes, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
