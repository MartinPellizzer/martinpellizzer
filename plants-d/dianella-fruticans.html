Dianella fruticans

Dianella fruticans, commonly known as the coastal dianella, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, growing in dense tufts. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Dianella fruticans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
