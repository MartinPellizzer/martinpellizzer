Doronicum crenatum

Doronicum crenatum, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in hairy foliage, and its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive central disc. The plant produces small, daisy-like blossoms that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Doronicum crenatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
