Dypsis henrici

Dypsis henrici, commonly known as the Henry's areca palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by edible drupes. Dypsis henrici thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to indoor environments.
