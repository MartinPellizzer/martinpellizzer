Dysoxylum aliquantulum

Dysoxylum aliquantulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dysoxylum, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
