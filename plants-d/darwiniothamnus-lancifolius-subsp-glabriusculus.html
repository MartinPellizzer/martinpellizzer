Darwiniothamnus lancifolius subsp. glabriusculus

Darwiniothamnus lancifolius subsp.

glabriusculus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved darwiniothamnus, is a shrub native to the arid regions of central Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its subspecific name.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale yellow to orange in color, often arranged in dense clusters. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
