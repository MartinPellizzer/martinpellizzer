Dactylorhiza incarnata f. brevibracteata

Dactylorhiza incarnata f. brevibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bracted marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. It has a pseudobulb structure and produces a single spike of flowers, with each flower having three sepals and three petals.

This orchid is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
