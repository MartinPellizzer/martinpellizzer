Dendrochilum suratii

Dendrochilum suratii, commonly known as the Surat orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with distinctive markings, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Dendrochilum suratii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
