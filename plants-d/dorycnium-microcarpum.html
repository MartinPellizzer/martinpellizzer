Dorycnium microcarpum

Dorycnium microcarpum, commonly known as the lesser burclover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces bur-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in prickly pods, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and soil stabilization efforts.
