Daphne furcata

Daphne furcata, commonly known as the clustered daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with dark green, ovate leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, waxy texture.

Daphne furcata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and winter interest.
