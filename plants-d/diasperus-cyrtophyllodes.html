Diasperus cyrtophyllodes

Diasperus cyrtophyllodes, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in water retention.

Diasperus cyrtophyllodes is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
