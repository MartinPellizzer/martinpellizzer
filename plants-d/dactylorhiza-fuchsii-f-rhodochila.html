Dactylorhiza fuchsii f. rhodochila

Dactylorhiza fuchsii f. rhodochila, commonly known as the pink-tinged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, green to brown pseudobulbs.

The inflorescence consists of a single raceme bearing several flowers with three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently marked.
