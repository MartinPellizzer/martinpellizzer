Deyeuxia stricta

Deyeuxia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and riparian zones. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deyeuxia stricta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
