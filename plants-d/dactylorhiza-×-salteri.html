Dactylorhiza × salteri

Dactylorhiza × salteri, commonly known as the Salter's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulbous structure and produces a single, upright stem bearing several flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Dactylorhiza × salteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation programs.
