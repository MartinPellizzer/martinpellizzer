Drypetes salicifolia

Drypetes salicifolia, commonly known as the Indian blackwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is thick and dark, with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Drypetes salicifolia is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional crafts and as a source of tannins.
