Diospyros boivinii var. manongarivensis

Diospyros boivinii var. manongarivensis, commonly known as the Manongarivensis persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
