Dactylis calycina

Dactylis calycina, commonly known as the woolly grass or lesser woolly grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Dactylis calycina is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its decorative foliage.
