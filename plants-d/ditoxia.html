Ditoxia

Ditoxia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent petals that appear to glow in low light, giving it its mysterious reputation. The plant has a unique reproductive system, relying on a specific species of moth for pollination. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Ditoxia is considered a rare and endangered species.
