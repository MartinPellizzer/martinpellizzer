Dalbergia maritima subsp. maritima

Dalbergia maritima subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the coastal ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated pods containing flat, dark seeds.

This subspecies is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.
