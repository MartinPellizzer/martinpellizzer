Dichanthium nagense

Dichanthium nagense, commonly known as the Nagari grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in dry, tropical, and subtropical climates, often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Dichanthium nagense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
