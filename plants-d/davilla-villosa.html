Davilla villosa

Davilla villosa, commonly known as the silky davisia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, attracting birds and other wildlife. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
