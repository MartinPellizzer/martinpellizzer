Desmodium burkartii

Desmodium burkartii, commonly known as Burkart's ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded undergrowth. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
