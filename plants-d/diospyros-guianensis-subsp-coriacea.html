Diospyros guianensis subsp. coriacea

Diospyros guianensis subsp.

coriacea, commonly known as the rough persimmon, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and dark green, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, sometimes astringent pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
