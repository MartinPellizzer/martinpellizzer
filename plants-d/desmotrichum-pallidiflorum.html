Desmotrichum pallidiflorum

Desmotrichum pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, inconspicuous fruits called cypselae, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
