Diasperus cryptophilus

Diasperus cryptophilus, commonly known as the cryptic sandbur, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive spiny, seed-bearing burrs. The plant typically grows from low, mat-forming vegetation, adapting to harsh, dry environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually yellow or brown, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Diasperus cryptophilus is well-suited to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.
