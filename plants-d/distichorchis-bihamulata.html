Distichorchis bihamulata

Distichorchis bihamulata, commonly known as the two-lobed twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with a slender, creeping rhizome and two pairs of opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanism are adaptations to its woodland environment.
