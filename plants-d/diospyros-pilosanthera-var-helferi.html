Diospyros pilosanthera var. helferi

Diospyros pilosanthera var. helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, orange to red berries that are rich in nutrients.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential for cultivation as a fruit tree.
