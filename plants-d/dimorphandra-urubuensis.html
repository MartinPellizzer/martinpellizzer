Dimorphandra urubuensis

Dimorphandra urubuensis, commonly known as the Urubu tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is adapted to humid tropical climates and is often found in riverine forests.
