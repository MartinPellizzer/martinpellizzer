Dicentra saccata

Dicentra saccata, commonly known as the bleeding heart plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces showy, drooping blooms in early spring, often with a red or purple center. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and velvety in texture.

Dicentra saccata thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
