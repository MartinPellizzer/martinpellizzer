Dissotis lanata

Dissotis lanata, commonly known as the hairy dissotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its flowers are borne on branched inflorescences and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
