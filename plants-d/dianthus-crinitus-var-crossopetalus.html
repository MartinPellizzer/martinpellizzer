Dianthus crinitus var. crossopetalus

Dianthus crinitus var. crossopetalus, commonly known as the curly top campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers, which bloom in clusters atop slender stalks. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often curled at the edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
