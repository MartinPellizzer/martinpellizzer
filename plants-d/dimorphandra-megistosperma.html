Dimorphandra megistosperma

Dimorphandra megistosperma, commonly known as the giant sycamore, is a large, deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it plays an important role in the local ecosystem.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
