Decaloba elliptica

Decaloba elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
