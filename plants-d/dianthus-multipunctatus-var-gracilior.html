Dianthus multipunctatus var. gracilior

Dianthus multipunctatus var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender pink carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with a distinctive, slightly fragrant scent. The leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while the stems are slender and often branched. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and easy care requirements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, blooming from late spring to early summer.
