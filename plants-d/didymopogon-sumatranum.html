Didymopogon sumatranum

Didymopogon sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
