Didymocarpus murutorum

Didymocarpus murutorum, commonly known as the Bornean wax apple, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
