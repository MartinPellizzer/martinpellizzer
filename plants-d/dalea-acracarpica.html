Dalea acracarpica

Dalea acracarpica, commonly known as the prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has small, compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea acracarpica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
