Danthonia macowanii

Danthonia macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Danthonia macowanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
