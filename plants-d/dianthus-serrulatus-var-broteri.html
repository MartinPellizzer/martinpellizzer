Dianthus serrulatus var. broteri

Dianthus serrulatus var. broteri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. The leaves are narrow, serrated, and arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
