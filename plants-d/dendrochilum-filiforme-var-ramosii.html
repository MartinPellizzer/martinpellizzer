Dendrochilum filiforme var. ramosii

Dendrochilum filiforme var. ramosii, commonly known as the branched slender orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, linear leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
