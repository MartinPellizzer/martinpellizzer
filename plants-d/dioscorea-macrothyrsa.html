Dioscorea macrothyrsa

Dioscorea macrothyrsa, commonly known as the large-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are edible when properly prepared and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
