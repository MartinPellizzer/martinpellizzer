Dudleya attenuata subsp. attenuata

Dudleya attenuata subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to California. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
