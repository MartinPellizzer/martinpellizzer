Dipterocarpus littoralis

Dipterocarpus littoralis, commonly known as the coastal dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, simple leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. The tree is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is adapted to coastal environments, often growing in mangrove or brackish water areas. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various species.
