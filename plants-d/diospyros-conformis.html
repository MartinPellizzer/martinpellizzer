Diospyros conformis

Diospyros conformis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are a source of food for various animals.

The wood of Diospyros conformis is hard and durable, making it valuable for crafting and traditional uses.
