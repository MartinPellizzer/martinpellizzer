Delphinium variegatum var. blochmaniae

Delphinium variegatum var. blochmaniae, commonly known as the Blochmann's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or variegated appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its striking appearance and native habitat, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in alpine flora.
