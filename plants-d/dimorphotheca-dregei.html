Dimorphotheca dregei

Dimorphotheca dregei, commonly known as the yellow ox-eye daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers with white ray florets, which bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Dimorphotheca dregei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
