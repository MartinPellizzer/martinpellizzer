Diplotaxis assurgens subsp. pachypoda

Diplotaxis assurgens subsp.

pachypoda, commonly known as the thickstem mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pungent, mustard-like odor.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its thick, fleshy stems and is often used in traditional medicine.
