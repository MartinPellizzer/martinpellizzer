Dianthus semenovii

Dianthus semenovii, commonly known as the Semenov's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are usually erect and branched.

Dianthus semenovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
