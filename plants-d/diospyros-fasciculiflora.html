Diospyros fasciculiflora

Diospyros fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature, and it is edible when fully ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
