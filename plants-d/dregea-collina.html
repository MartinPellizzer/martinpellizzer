Dregea collina

Dregea collina, commonly known as the mountain dregea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
