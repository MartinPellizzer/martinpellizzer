Diplotheca abyssinica

Diplotheca abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
