Dioscorea hispida var. reticulata

Dioscorea hispida var. reticulata, commonly known as the rough yam or net-veined yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also edible and have a starchy texture similar to other yams.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is cultivated in various parts of Southeast Asia.
