Dinemandra strigosa

Dinemandra strigosa, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant produces violet or purple berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Solanaceae family.
