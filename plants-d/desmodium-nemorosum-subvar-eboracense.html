Desmodium nemorosum subvar. eboracense

Desmodium nemorosum subvar. eboracense, commonly known as the Yorkshire trefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous, and it is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
