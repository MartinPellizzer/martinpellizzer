Diospyros similis

Diospyros similis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Diospyros similis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.
