Dianthus nitidus

Dianthus nitidus, commonly known as the snowy catchfly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Dianthus nitidus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
