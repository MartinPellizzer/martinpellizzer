Deguelia ferruginea

Deguelia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty deguelia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and its growth habit includes a woody stem with a distinctive reddish-brown bark.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
