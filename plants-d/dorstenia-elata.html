Dorstenia elata

Dorstenia elata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or the Mexican spiderplant, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a unique, spiky appearance.

Dorstenia elata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in well-draining soil with adequate sunlight.
