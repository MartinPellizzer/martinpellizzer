Daphne bholua subsp. emeiensis

Daphne bholua subsp.

emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei Mountain daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming habit.
