Dicerma pulchellum

Dicerma pulchellum, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern, with showy, star-shaped petals that attract pollinators.

Dicerma pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
