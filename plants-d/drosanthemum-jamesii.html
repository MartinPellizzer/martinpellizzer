Drosanthemum jamesii

Drosanthemum jamesii, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Drosanthemum jamesii is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
