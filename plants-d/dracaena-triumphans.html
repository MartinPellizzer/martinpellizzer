Dracaena triumphans

Dracaena triumphans, commonly known as the Corn Plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a tall, upright evergreen plant with stiff, dark green, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear at the base of the leaves, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is well adapted to indoor environments and requires bright, indirect light and moderate watering.

Dracaena triumphans is also known for its air-purifying qualities and is a popular choice for interior landscaping.
