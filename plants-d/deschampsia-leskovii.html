Deschampsia leskovii

Deschampsia leskovii, commonly known as the Leskov's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often white or light brown, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Deschampsia leskovii is adapted to cold climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
