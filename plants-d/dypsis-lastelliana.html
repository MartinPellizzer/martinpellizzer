Dypsis lastelliana

Dypsis lastelliana, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, forming a compact, palm-like appearance. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching several feet in length, and are arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Dypsis lastelliana is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
