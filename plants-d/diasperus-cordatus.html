Diasperus cordatus

Diasperus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
