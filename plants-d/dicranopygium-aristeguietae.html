Dicranopygium aristeguietae

Dicranopygium aristeguietae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada feather moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, tufted structure. The plant exhibits a distinct morphology with elongated, acuminate leaf tips and a subulate shape, which helps in identifying it from similar species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates.

Its reproductive structures, including capsules, are borne on short, lateral branches, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
