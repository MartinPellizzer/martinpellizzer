Digitaria pruriens

Digitaria pruriens, commonly known as itchgrass, is a widespread grass species found in various habitats across the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that can cause skin irritation due to the presence of fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Digitaria pruriens is an annual plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as forage for certain livestock.
