Diplostephium solidagineum

Diplostephium solidagineum, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
