Dichromena erostris

Dichromena erostris, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

The berries it produces are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and medicinal significance.
