Diplotaxis prolongoi

Diplotaxis prolongoi, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Diplotaxis prolongoi is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
