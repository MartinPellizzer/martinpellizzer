Dilepyrum diffusum

Dilepyrum diffusum, commonly known as the spreading dilepyrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Dilepyrum diffusum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
