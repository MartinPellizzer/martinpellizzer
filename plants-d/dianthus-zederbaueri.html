Dianthus zederbaueri

Dianthus zederbaueri, commonly known as the Sierra foothill campion, is a flowering plant native to California and Nevada in the United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or white flowers with prominent red centers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dianthus zederbaueri is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.
