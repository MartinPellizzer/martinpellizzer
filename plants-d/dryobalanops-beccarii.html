Dryobalanops beccarii

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing winged seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
