Dioscorea tiliifolia

Dioscorea tiliifolia, commonly known as the tiliifolia yam, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vigorous vine growth and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy tubers that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with high humidity.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
