Dodonaea thunbergiana

Dodonaea thunbergiana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
