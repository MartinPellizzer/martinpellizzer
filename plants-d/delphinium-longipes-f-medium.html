Delphinium longipes f. medium

Delphinium longipes f. medium, commonly known as the medium-flowered delphinium, is a cultivar of the delphinium genus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically larger and more showy than those of the species. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and thrives in well-drained, fertile soil in full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in borders and cottage gardens.
