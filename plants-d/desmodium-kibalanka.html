Desmodium kibalanka

Desmodium kibalanka, commonly known as the Kibalanka cowpea, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
