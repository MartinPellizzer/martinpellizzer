Ditassa multinervia

Ditassa multinervia, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous plant native to East Africa.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces showy, velvety flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The leaves are ovate, hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Its flowers are typically borne on tall, leafless stalks, and it is known for its ability to bloom year-round under proper care.
