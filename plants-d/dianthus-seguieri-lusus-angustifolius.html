Dianthus seguieri lusus angustifolius

Dianthus seguieri lusus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and displays clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
