Digitaria subcalva

Digitaria subcalva, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Digitaria subcalva is a common weed in agricultural settings and can compete with crops for nutrients and water.
