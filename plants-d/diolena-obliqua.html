Diolena obliqua

Diolena obliqua, commonly known as the slender zigzag plant, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Diolena obliqua is notable for its ability to tolerate saline conditions, making it a common weed in coastal areas.
