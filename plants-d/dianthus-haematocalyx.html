Dianthus haematocalyx

Dianthus haematocalyx, commonly known as the blood-red campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical name reflects its vivid floral color, with "haematocalyx" meaning "blood-red calyx.".
