Dichaea fragrantissima

Dichaea fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and emit a subtle, sweet fragrance, which helps attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
