Diospyros grata

Diospyros grata, commonly known as the small persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Diospyros grata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in tropical and subtropical regions.
