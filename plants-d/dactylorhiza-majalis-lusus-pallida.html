Dactylorhiza majalis lusus pallida

Dactylorhiza majalis lusus pallida, commonly known as the pale marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetland and marsh environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a pale green to yellowish hue, often with a distinct lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor, waterlogged soils.

This species is typically found in peat bogs and is considered a rare and protected plant in many regions.
