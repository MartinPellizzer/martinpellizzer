Dolichos longistipellatus f. angustifoliolata

Dolichos longistipellatus f. angustifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sickle bean, is a variant of the sickle bean species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, sickle-shaped pods and narrow, lanceolate leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its foliage is typically green and slightly hairy, with small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.
