Desmotrichum appendiculatum

Desmotrichum appendiculatum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads similar to those of dandelions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Desmotrichum appendiculatum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for desert wildlife.
