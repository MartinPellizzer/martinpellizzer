Demorchis fontinalis

Demorchis fontinalis, commonly known as the spring moss, is a species of aquatic moss that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its dense, turfy growth habit and its ability to form extensive mats on the surface of still or slow-moving water. The plant has a simple morphology with leaf-like structures that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often used as an indicator of water quality in environmental studies.
