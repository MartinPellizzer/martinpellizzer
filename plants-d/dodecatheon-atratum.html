Dodecatheon atratum

Dodecatheon atratum, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive dark central disk, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
