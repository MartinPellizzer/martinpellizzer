Dombeya myriantha

Dombeya myriantha, commonly known as the silky dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, silky white hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Dombeya myriantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
