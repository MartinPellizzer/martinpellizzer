Dombeya melanoxylon

Dombeya melanoxylon, commonly known as blackwood dombeya, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, bark-like foliage and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

The species is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
