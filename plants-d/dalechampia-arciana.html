Dalechampia arciana

Dalechampia arciana, commonly known as the silverleaf dalechampia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
