Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp. implicatum

Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp.

implicatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soils.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a pointed tip, and its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets. It produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed or wetland habitats.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
