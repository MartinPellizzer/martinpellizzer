Diceros latifolius

Diceros latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved dikka, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad, evergreen leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Diceros latifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
