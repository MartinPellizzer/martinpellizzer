Desmodium parviflorum

Desmodium parviflorum, commonly known as small-flower ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in the southeastern United States. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
