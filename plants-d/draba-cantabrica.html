Draba cantabrica

Draba cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian whitlow grass, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in mountainous regions and coastal areas. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Draba cantabrica is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with cruciform flowers.
