Dolichos lanceolatus

Dolichos lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often grown as a cover crop or for its nutritional value.

Dolichos lanceolatus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
