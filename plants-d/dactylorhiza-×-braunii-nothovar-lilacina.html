Dactylorhiza × braunii nothovar. lilacina

Dactylorhiza × braunii nothovar. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, lilac-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and marshes. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its striking coloration and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
