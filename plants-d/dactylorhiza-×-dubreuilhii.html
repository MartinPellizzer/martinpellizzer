Dactylorhiza × dubreuilhii

Dactylorhiza × dubreuilhii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a species native to western Europe and parts of northern Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant produces tall, erect stems with several lance-shaped leaves and bears clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. It is a hybrid between Dactylorhiza viridiflora and Dactylorhiza maculata, showcasing a mix of traits from both parent species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
