Delphinium nortonii

Delphinium nortonii, commonly known as Norton's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Delphinium nortonii thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
