Dianthus hoeltzeri

Dianthus hoeltzeri, commonly known as the Hoeltzer's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus hoeltzeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained garden conditions.
