Delphinium × confertiflorum

Delphinium × confertiflorum, commonly known as the clustered larkspur, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and it is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Delphinium × confertiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
