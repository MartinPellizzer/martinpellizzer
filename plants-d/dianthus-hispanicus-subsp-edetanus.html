Dianthus hispanicus subsp. edetanus

Dianthus hispanicus subsp.

edetanus, commonly known as the Edetan carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant produces clusters of small, rounded petals with a distinctive frilled edge, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
