Darea violascens

Darea violascens, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or shaded areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Darea violascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming floral display.
