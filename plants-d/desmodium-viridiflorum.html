Desmodium viridiflorum

Desmodium viridiflorum, commonly known as green-flowered desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has a climbing habit, using tendrils to support itself on nearby vegetation.

Desmodium viridiflorum is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
