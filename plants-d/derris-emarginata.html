Derris emarginata

Derris emarginata, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
