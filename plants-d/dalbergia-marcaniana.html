Dalbergia marcaniana

Dalbergia marcaniana, commonly known as the purpleheart wood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark purple heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its timber and ornamental value.

The wood is resistant to decay and insect damage, making it a popular choice in furniture and musical instrument manufacturing.
