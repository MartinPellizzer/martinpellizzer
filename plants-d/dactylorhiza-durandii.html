Dactylorhiza durandii

Dactylorhiza durandii, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant features a pseudobulb-like structure and produces tall spikes of violet to purple flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its ecological and horticultural significance.
