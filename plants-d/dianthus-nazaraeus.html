Dianthus nazaraeus

Dianthus nazaraeus, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features upright, branching stems with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in its native habitat.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to alpine meadows.
