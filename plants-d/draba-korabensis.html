Draba korabensis

Draba korabensis, commonly known as the Korab bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant grows in rocky and alpine environments, often in moist, well-drained soils. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces slender, branching stems that bear the flowers.

Draba korabensis is a relatively rare species, found primarily in specific regions of Kosovo, Albania, and northwestern Greece.
