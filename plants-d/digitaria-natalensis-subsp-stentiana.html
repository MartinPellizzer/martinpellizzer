Digitaria natalensis subsp. stentiana

Digitaria natalensis subsp.

stentiana, commonly known as Natal fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
