Dasymaschalon rostratum

Dasymaschalon rostratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub with pale green, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and for stabilizing disturbed soils in desert environments.
