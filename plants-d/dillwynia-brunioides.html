Dillwynia brunioides

Dillwynia brunioides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Dillwynia brunioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
