Dactylorhiza × formosa

Dactylorhiza × formosa, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear numerous spikes of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Dactylorhiza × formosa is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
