Dentaria integrifolia var. tracyi

Dentaria integrifolia var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's toothplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, toothed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique leaf morphology and floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
