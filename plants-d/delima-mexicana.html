Delima mexicana

Delima mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican delima, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and elliptical, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
