Digitaria hystrichoides

Digitaria hystrichoides, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by narrow, bristle-tipped glumes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and agricultural areas.

Digitaria hystrichoides is a common weed in many parts of the world and is often considered a nuisance in cultivated landscapes.
