Diploon cuspidatum

Diploon cuspidatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a prominent, cone-shaped center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Diploon cuspidatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
