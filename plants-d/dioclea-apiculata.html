Dioclea apiculata

Dioclea apiculata, commonly known as the showy dioclea, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, edible aril. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive floral display.
