Dianthus maris

Dianthus maris, commonly known as sea pinks, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in maritime environments.

Dianthus maris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh coastal conditions.
