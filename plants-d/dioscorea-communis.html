Dioscorea communis

Dioscorea communis, commonly known as the common yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are widely cultivated for their edible roots, which are a staple in many cuisines.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
