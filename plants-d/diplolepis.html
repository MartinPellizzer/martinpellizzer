Diplolepis

Diplolepis, commonly known as false holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

These plants are characterized by their woody stems and small, often clustered leaves. The flowers are typically white or pink and are followed by small, red or orange fruits called pomes. Diplolepis species are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North America.

They are often grown as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
