Dalbergia richardsii

Dalbergia richardsii, commonly known as African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Tanzania, Kenya, and Uganda.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and acoustic properties. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
