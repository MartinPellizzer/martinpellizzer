Disa rungweensis

Disa rungweensis, commonly known as the Rungwe orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Tanzania.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its unique appearance.

Disa rungweensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitat.
