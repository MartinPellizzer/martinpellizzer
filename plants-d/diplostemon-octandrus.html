Diplostemon octandrus

Diplostemon octandrus, commonly known as the eight-angled woodbine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, eight-angled stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
