Dodecatheon meadia f. sedens

Dodecatheon meadia f. sedens, commonly known as the sedentary globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its unique growth habit and is often found in woodland and open habitats.
