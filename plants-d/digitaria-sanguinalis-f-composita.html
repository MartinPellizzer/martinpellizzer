Digitaria sanguinalis f. composita

Digitaria sanguinalis f. composita, commonly known as the composite bluestem, is a variant of the annual grass species Digitaria sanguinalis.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which often forms a mat-like structure on the ground. The plant typically has narrow, blue-green leaves with a prominent midrib, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It is native to temperate regions of North America and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This grass is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
