Daucus lopadusanus

Daucus lopadusanus, commonly known as the Lopadusan carrot, is a rare and ancient species of wild carrot native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round to oval seeds that are typically dispersed by wind. Its leaves are finely divided and resemble those of other Daucus species, but it is distinguished by its unique habitat preferences and limited distribution.

Due to its rarity, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
