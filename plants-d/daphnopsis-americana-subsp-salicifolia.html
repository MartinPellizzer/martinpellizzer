Daphnopsis americana subsp. salicifolia

Daphnopsis americana subsp.

salicifolia, commonly known as the American willow-leaf daphnopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its willow-like leaves, which are elongated and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
