Delphinium californicum var. interius

Delphinium californicum var. interius, commonly known as the California larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and contributes to its ecological significance in native habitats.
