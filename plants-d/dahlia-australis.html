Dahlia australis

Dahlia australis, commonly known as the Australian dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring robust stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a notable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Dahlia australis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering during its growing season.
