Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. cruenta

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

cruenta, commonly known as the crimson helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of dark red to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, hairy coating.

The species is known for its striking color and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
