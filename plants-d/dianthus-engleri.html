Dianthus engleri

Dianthus engleri, commonly known as the Tibetan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers.

Dianthus engleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
