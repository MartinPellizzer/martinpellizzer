Diasperus dumetosus

Diasperus dumetosus, commonly known as the shrub spurge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Diasperus dumetosus is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
