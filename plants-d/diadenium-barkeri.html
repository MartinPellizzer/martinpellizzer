Diadenium barkeri

Diadenium barkeri, commonly known as the Barker's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, pale green to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. Diadenium barkeri is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern and is often protected in its natural range.
