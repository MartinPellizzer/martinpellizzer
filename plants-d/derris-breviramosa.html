Derris breviramosa

Derris breviramosa, commonly known as the short-branched derris, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is notable for its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its roots contain rotenone, a natural insecticide used in traditional agriculture.

Derris breviramosa is often found in tropical forests and is valued for both its ecological and medicinal properties.
