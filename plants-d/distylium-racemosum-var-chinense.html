Distylium racemosum var. chinense

Distylium racemosum var. chinense, commonly known as Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
