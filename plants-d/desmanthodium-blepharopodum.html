Desmanthodium blepharopodum

Desmanthodium blepharopodum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
