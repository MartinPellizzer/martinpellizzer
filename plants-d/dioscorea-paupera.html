Dioscorea paupera

Dioscorea paupera, commonly known as the poor man's yam, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tubers that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The tubers of Dioscorea paupera are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various cultural practices for centuries.
