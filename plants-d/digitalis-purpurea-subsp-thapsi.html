Digitalis purpurea subsp. thapsi

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

thapsi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear hooded, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are typically arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in medicine.
