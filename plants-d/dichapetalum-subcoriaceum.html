Dichapetalum subcoriaceum

Dichapetalum subcoriaceum, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Dichapetalum subcoriaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
