Dilophia fontana

Dilophia fontana, commonly known as the water bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of North America.

It features slender, upright stems that produce showy blue to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Dilophia fontana thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
