Dichasianthus ledebourii

Dichasianthus ledebourii, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth form, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Dichasianthus ledebourii is well adapted to sandy soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
