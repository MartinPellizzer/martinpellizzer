Delphinium bucharicum

Delphinium bucharicum, commonly known as the Bukharan larkspur, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, serrated leaflets, and it produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
