Diospyros sericocarpa

Diospyros sericocarpa, commonly known as the silky persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruit is typically orange to reddish in color, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
