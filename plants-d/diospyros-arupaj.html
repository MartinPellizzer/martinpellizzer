Diospyros arupaj

Diospyros arupaj, commonly known as the Arupe mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, sweet, and fibrous fruits. Its fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines, and the tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood.

Diospyros arupaj is often found in lowland forests and is an important species in traditional horticulture.
