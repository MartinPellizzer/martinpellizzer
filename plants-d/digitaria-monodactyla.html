Digitaria monodactyla

Digitaria monodactyla, commonly known as slender digitaria, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Digitaria monodactyla is an important component of grassland ecosystems and can serve as forage for livestock.
