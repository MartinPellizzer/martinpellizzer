Dichanthelium ravenelii

Dichanthelium ravenelii, commonly known as Ravenel's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is usually drooping, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Dichanthelium ravenelii is characterized by its distinctive seed structure and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.
