Dalea carthagenensis var. pilocarpa

Dalea carthagenensis var. pilocarpa, commonly known as the hairy carthage bean, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and finely divided, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

The variety pilocarpa is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance compared to other varieties of Dalea carthagenensis.
