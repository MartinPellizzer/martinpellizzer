Disperis fayi

Disperis fayi, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in grassland habitats, often in areas with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange with purple markings. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Disperis fayi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
