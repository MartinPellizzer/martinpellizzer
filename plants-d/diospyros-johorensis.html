Diospyros johorensis

Diospyros johorensis, commonly known as the Javan date palm, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it bears edible, sweet, date-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various tropical regions.
