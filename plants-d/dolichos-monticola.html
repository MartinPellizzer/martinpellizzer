Dolichos monticola

Dolichos monticola, commonly known as the mountain dolichos, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Botanically, it is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and potential use in sustainable agriculture.
