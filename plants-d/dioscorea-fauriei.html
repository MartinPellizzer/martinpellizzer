Dioscorea fauriei

Dioscorea fauriei, commonly known as Chinese yam, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by yellow or orange fruits containing toxic seeds. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and traditional use in herbal medicine.

Dioscorea fauriei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its economic and medicinal value.
