Diospyros pervilleana

Diospyros pervilleana, commonly known as the Pervillia persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red or orange color, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
