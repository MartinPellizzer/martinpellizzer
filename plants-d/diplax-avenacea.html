Diplax avenacea

Diplax avenacea, commonly known as the slender grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Diplax avenacea plays a minor role in ecological systems but is of interest to botanists due to its morphological features and distribution patterns.
