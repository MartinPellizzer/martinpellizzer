Didymocarpus humboldtianus var. primulifolius

Didymocarpus humboldtianus var. primulifolius, commonly known as the Humboldt's flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its resilience to drought conditions.
