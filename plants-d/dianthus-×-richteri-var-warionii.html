Dianthus × richteri var. warionii

Dianthus × richteri var. warionii, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features dark green, toothed leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
