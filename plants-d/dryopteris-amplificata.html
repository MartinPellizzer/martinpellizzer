Dryopteris amplificata

Dryopteris amplificata, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a robust fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
