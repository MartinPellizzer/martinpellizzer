Disa lugens

Disa lugens, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which can be pink, red, or purple, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Disa lugens is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
