Ditassa oblongifolia

Ditassa oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ditassa, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often greenish or white in color. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
