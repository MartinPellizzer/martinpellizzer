Daemonorops aurea

Daemonorops aurea, commonly known as the golden cycad, is a species of cycad native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often a striking golden-yellow color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
