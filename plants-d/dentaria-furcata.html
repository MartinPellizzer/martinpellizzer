Dentaria furcata

Dentaria furcata, commonly known as the forked toothplant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in a loose cluster at the end of the stem.

Dentaria furcata is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating ailments such as coughs and skin conditions.
