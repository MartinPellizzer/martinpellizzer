Dipteracanthus humifusus

Dipteracanthus humifusus, commonly known as the creeping dipteracanthus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically succulent and adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
