Dupatya crassifolia

Dupatya crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and numerous small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dupatya crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
