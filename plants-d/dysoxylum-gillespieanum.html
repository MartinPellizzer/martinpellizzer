Dysoxylum gillespieanum

Dysoxylum gillespieanum, commonly known as the pink-veined mangrove, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink veins in the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant thrives in coastal areas, particularly in brackish or saline water, making it an important species in mangrove ecosystems. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize shorelines and prevent erosion.

Its flowers are small and fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
