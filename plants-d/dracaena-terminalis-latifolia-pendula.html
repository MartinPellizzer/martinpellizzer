Dracaena terminalis latifolia-pendula

Dracaena terminalis latifolia-pendula, commonly known as the curly willow dracaena, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its long, gracefully drooping leaves that create a cascading effect.

Native to Madagascar, it thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. The leaves are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture, and they often exhibit a gentle curl that adds to its ornamental appeal. This plant is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

It is also known for its ability to purify the air and enhance the aesthetic of any space.
