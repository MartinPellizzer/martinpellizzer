Deutzia gracilis f. pilifera

Deutzia gracilis f. pilifera, commonly known as the silky deutzia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This cultivar is particularly noted for its profuse flowering and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is suitable for garden borders or as a specimen plant.
