Diptychandra aurantiaca subsp. epunctata

Diptychandra aurantiaca subsp.

epunctata, commonly known as the red-stemmed flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and clusters of small, bright orange to red flowers.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
