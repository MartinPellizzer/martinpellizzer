Dorstenia dinklagei var. reducta

Dorstenia dinklagei var. reducta, commonly known as the dwarf false arum, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its leaves are typically narrow and may exhibit a variegated pattern, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
