Diasperus glabrescens

Diasperus glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-surfaced rockrose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Diasperus glabrescens is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils.
