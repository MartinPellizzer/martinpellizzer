Deyeuxia velutina var. nardifolia

Deyeuxia velutina var. nardifolia, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of slender spikelets that are arranged in a panicle structure.

The botanical features of this variety make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
