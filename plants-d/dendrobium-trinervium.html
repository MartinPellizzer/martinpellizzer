Dendrobium trinervium

Dendrobium trinervium, commonly known as the three-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with fleshy, often branched stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium trinervium thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
