Diospyros ferrea var. madagascariensis

Diospyros ferrea var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy berries.

This species is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
