Derris laxiflora

Derris laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered derris, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and shrublands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
