Dianthus liboschitzianus var. integerrimus

Dianthus liboschitzianus var. integerrimus, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
