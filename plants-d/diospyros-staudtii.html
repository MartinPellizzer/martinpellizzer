Diospyros staudtii

Diospyros staudtii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. Its fruits are edible, though they are generally not cultivated for commercial purposes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
