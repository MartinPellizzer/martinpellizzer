Dicraurus leptocladus

Dicraurus leptocladus, commonly known as the slender-branching mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by berries that are often eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

Dicraurus leptocladus is typically found in humid, shaded environments, contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
