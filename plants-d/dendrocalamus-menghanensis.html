Dendrocalamus menghanensis

Dendrocalamus menghanensis, commonly known as the Menghan bamboo, is a tall, robust grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant is valued for its durable and lightweight bamboo, which is used in construction and handicrafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense stands in moist, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus menghanensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
