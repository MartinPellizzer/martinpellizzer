Delphinium orientale subsp. hispanicum

Delphinium orientale subsp.

hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
