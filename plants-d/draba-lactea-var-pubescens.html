Draba lactea var. pubescens

Draba lactea var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". It is native to alpine and arctic regions of Eurasia, thriving in cold, rocky environments.

This species is often found in meadows and scree slopes, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
