Dracocephalum peltatum

Dracocephalum peltatum, commonly known as the mountain betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically blue or purple and are arranged in dense spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dracocephalum peltatum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
