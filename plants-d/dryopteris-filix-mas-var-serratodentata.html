Dryopteris filix-mas var. serratodentata

Dryopteris filix-mas var. serratodentata, commonly known as the serrated tooth fern, is a variant of the male fern characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins.

The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance that becomes more refined as the plant matures. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, well-drained soil. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in specialized structures called sporangia.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, tropical foliage.
