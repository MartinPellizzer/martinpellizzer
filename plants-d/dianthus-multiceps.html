Dianthus multiceps

Dianthus multiceps, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink or reddish flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus multiceps is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
