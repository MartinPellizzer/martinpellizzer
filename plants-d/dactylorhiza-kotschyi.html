Dactylorhiza kotschyi

Dactylorhiza kotschyi, commonly known as the Kashmir orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan.

It is a herbaceous plant with a slender, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spike of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers, which are typically tubular in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

Dactylorhiza kotschyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
