Dialium guineense

Dialium guineense, commonly known as African plum or wild mango, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are yellow or orange when ripe, containing a single large seed. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its fruit, timber, and traditional medicinal uses.
