Digitalis speciosa

Digitalis speciosa, commonly known as the showy foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its cardiac glycosides, which have historically been used in medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Digitalis speciosa thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.
