Desmos

Desmos, commonly known as the desmodium plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The flowers are typically small and clustered in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences, often in shades of pink, purple, or white. Desmos species are known for their ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making them valuable in soil enrichment.

They are native to various regions across Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and some species are cultivated as ornamental plants.
