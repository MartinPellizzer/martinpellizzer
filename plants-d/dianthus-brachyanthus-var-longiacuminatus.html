Dianthus brachyanthus var. longiacuminatus

Dianthus brachyanthus var. longiacuminatus, commonly known as the long-acuminate carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The flowers typically display a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, with prominent red or purple markings on the petals. The leaves are linear and slightly hairy, often arranged in a basal rosette.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and resilience in various soil conditions.
