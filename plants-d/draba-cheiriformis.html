Draba cheiriformis

Draba cheiriformis, commonly known as the hand-shaped whitlow grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear a cluster of flowers at the top.

Draba cheiriformis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
