Dryopteris cochleata var. squamosa

Dryopteris cochleata var. squamosa, commonly known as the scaly wood fern, is a variant of the common wood fern.

It is a perennial evergreen fern characterized by its fronds that are divided into lance-shaped segments. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive scaly appearance on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
