Dioscorea kingii

Dioscorea kingii, commonly known as King's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are valued for their starchy texture and are often used in local cuisines.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic effects.
