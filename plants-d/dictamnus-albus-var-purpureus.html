Dictamnus albus var. purpureus

Dictamnus albus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered dictamnus, is a variant of the white dictamnus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in dense, terminal clusters. The plant features aromatic, grey-green foliage with a strong, spicy scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
