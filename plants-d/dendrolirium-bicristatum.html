Dendrolirium bicristatum

Dendrolirium bicristatum, commonly known as the two-crowned forest lily, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Dendrolirium bicristatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
