Diospyros pseudaggregata

Diospyros pseudaggregata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that are usually glabrous. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
