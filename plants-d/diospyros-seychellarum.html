Diospyros seychellarum

Diospyros seychellarum, commonly known as the Seychelles persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Seychelles archipelago.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
