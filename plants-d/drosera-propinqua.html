Drosera propinqua

Drosera propinqua, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, sticky glandular hairs that secrete a digestive enzyme to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its leaves are usually short and hairy, with a rosette-like structure.

Drosera propinqua is a perennial species that produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months.
