Dendranthema pallasianum

Dendranthema pallasianum, commonly known as the Pallas's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets, with white ray florets surrounding the central disk. It is often found in moist meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and calming effects.
