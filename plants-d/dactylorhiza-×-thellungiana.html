Dactylorhiza × thellungiana

Dactylorhiza × thellungiana, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, yellowish-green to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant prefers wet or marshy habitats and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.

Dactylorhiza × thellungiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
