Draba cheiranthoides var. leiocarpa

Draba cheiranthoides var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairless. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals and numerous stamens.

This variety is adapted to rocky and disturbed soils, often found in alpine and arid environments.
