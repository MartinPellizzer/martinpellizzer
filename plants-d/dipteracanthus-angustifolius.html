Dipteracanthus angustifolius

Dipteracanthus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by insects, facilitating pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
