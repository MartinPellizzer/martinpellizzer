Delphinium amabile

Delphinium amabile, commonly known as the blue larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue, purple, or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves with a soft, velvety texture.

Delphinium amabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
