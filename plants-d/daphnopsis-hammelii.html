Daphnopsis hammelii

Daphnopsis hammelii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Daphnopsis hammelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
