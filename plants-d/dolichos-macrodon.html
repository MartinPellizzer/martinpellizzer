Dolichos macrodon

Dolichos macrodon, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, with a distinctive hyacinth-like fragrance. The plant has compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets and bears elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source.

The seeds are rich in protein and can be cooked and consumed like lentils.
