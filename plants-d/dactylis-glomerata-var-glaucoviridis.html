Dactylis glomerata var. glaucoviridis

Dactylis glomerata var. glaucoviridis, commonly known as the glaucous oat, is a variant of the common oat that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine hairs on the leaves and stems. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is a perennial grass that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Its unique coloration and growth habit make it a distinctive species within the genus Dactylis.
