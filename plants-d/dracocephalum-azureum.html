Dracocephalum azureum

Dracocephalum azureum, commonly known as bluehead mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in summer. Its aromatic leaves and flowers contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and herbal teas.

Dracocephalum azureum is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky or dry soils.
