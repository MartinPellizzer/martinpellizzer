Dinochloa darvelana

Dinochloa darvelana, commonly known as the Darvelan dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in grassland and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
