Dracocephalum fragile subsp. crenatum

Dracocephalum fragile subsp.

crenatum, commonly known as the serrated dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in temperate to cold climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
