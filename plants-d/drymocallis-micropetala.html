Drymocallis micropetala

Drymocallis micropetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of golden yellow flowers in the late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Drymocallis micropetala is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
