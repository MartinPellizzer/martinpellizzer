Daphne pampaninii subsp. cochlearifolia

Daphne pampaninii subsp.

cochlearifolia, commonly known as the cochlearifoliate daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving them a silvery appearance.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or small spaces.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
