Disemma caerulescens

Disemma caerulescens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble misty clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Disemma caerulescens blooms in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to natural landscapes.
