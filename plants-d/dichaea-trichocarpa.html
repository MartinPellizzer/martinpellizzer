Dichaea trichocarpa

Dichaea trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy stemless orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a spike. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

Due to its unique appearance and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
