Daphne sophia subsp. taurica

Daphne sophia subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Crimean daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Crimea and parts of southern Ukraine. It features dense, aromatic foliage with dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
