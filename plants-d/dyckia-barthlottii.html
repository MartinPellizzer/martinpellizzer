Dyckia barthlottii

Dyckia barthlottii, commonly known as the Barthlott's dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces vibrant orange to red inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Dyckia barthlottii is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal among succulent enthusiasts.
