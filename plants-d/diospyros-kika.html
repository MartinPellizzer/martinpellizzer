Diospyros kika

Diospyros kika, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by small, round fruits that mature to a bright orange-red color.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is edible when fully ripe.
