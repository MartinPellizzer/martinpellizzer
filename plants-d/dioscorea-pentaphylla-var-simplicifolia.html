Dioscorea pentaphylla var. simplicifolia

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal use. Its botanical features include compound leaves with five leaflets and a robust root system adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

The species is valued for its edible tubers and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
