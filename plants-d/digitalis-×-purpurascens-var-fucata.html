Digitalis × purpurascens var. fucata

Digitalis × purpurascens var. fucata, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are tubular and arranged in a loose raceme, characteristic of the Digitalis genus.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
