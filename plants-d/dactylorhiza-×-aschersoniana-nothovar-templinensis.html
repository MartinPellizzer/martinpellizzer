Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana nothovar. templinensis

Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana nothovar. templinensis, commonly known as the Temple's lady's-tongue, is a hybrid orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
