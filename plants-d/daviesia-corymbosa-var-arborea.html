Daviesia corymbosa var. arborea

Daviesia corymbosa var. arborea, commonly known as the tree daviesia, is a distinctive shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in spring. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating, and the plant is known for its hardy nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or on rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
