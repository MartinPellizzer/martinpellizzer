Discocalyx papuana var. brevipedicellata

Discocalyx papuana var. brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed discocalyx.

It is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, characterized by its compact growth habit and unique inflorescence structure. The plant features small, clustered flowers with prominent, fleshy bracts that are often brightly colored. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the humid and tropical environments it inhabits.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
