Dictamnus davuricus

Dictamnus davuricus, commonly known as the fragrant dittany or red valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It features aromatic, somewhat woody stems that are often covered in a reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

Dictamnus davuricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
