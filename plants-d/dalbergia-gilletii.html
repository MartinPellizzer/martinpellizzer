Dalbergia gilletii

Dalbergia gilletii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and commercial properties. The plant has bipinnate leaves, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes. It produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

This species is often found in moist forested areas and is known for its ecological and economic significance in its native range.
