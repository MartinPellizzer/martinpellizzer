Dorotheanthus gramineus

Dorotheanthus gramineus, commonly known as the carpet flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dorotheanthus gramineus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
