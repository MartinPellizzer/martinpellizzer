Delphinium tsoongii

Delphinium tsoongii, commonly known as the Chinese larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, spicy scent. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Delphinium tsoongii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
