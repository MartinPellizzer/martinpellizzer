Didymocarpus cinereus

Didymocarpus cinereus, commonly known as the ash-leafed tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a grayish or ash-colored appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated pods containing flat, angular seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
