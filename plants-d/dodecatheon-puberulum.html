Dodecatheon puberulum

Dodecatheon puberulum, commonly known as the hairy globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Dodecatheon puberulum is often found in meadows and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
