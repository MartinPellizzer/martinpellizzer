Diospyros inconstans subsp. obovata

Diospyros inconstans subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its large, broad-ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its fruit is a sweet, edible drupe that matures to a reddish-orange hue.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
