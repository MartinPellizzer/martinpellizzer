Dendrobium scorpionis

Dendrobium scorpionis, commonly known as the scorpion orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces striking, long-lasting flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns resembling scorpions. Its leaves are typically elongated and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Dendrobium scorpionis thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining substrates.
