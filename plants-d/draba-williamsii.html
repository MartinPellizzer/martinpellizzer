Draba williamsii

Draba williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer.

Draba williamsii is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in high-altitude environments where it tolerates cold and harsh conditions.
