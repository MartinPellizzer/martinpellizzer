Daphne altaica

Daphne altaica, commonly known as the Caucasian daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It features striking, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red base. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Daphne altaica is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
