Dianthus × cincinnatus f. longipetalus

Dianthus × cincinnatus f. longipetalus, commonly known as the long-petaled carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a hybrid species characterized by its showy, double flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long, slender petals that give it a delicate and refined appearance.
