Diospyros andohahelensis

Diospyros andohahelensis, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited economic use due to its restricted geographic distribution.
