Disperis togoensis

Disperis togoensis, commonly known as the Togo orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, white or pale yellow blooms with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
