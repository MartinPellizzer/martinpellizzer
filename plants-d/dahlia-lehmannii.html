Dahlia lehmannii

Dahlia lehmannii, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, and the plant is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Dahlia lehmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
