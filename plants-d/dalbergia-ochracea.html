Dalbergia ochracea

Dalbergia ochracea, commonly known as the yellow ironwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant has bipinnate leaves, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in racemose inflorescences. It produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.
