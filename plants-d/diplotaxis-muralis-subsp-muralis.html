Diplotaxis muralis subsp. muralis

Diplotaxis muralis subsp.

muralis, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
