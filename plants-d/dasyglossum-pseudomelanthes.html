Dasyglossum pseudomelanthes

Dasyglossum pseudomelanthes, commonly known as the purple tailed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its aerial roots help in moisture retention.

The orchid produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
