Daviesia phyllodinea var. parvifolia

Daviesia phyllodinea var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf sickle-hillary, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, and its yellow or orange flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.
