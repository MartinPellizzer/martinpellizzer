Deschampsia podophora

Deschampsia podophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Deschampsia podophora plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
