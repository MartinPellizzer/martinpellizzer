Delphinium burmaense

Delphinium burmaense, commonly known as the Burmese larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Myanmar.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets.

Delphinium burmaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
