Dentaria laciniata

Dentaria laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf toothplant, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
