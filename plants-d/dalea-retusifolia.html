Dalea retusifolia

Dalea retusifolia, commonly known as the bluntleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, trifoliate leaves that have rounded or blunt tips. Its pea-like flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dalea retusifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
