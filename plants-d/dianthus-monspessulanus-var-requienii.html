Dianthus monspessulanus var. requienii

Dianthus monspessulanus var. requienii, commonly known as the Corsican carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of showy flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
