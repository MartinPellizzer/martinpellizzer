Dactylorhiza osmanica

Dactylorhiza osmanica, commonly known as the Osman's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, erect stems with showy, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dactylorhiza osmanica is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native habitats.
