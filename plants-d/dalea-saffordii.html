Dalea saffordii

Dalea saffordii, commonly known as Safford milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Dalea saffordii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
