Derris subalternifolia

Derris subalternifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved derris, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae), characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it produces leguminous pods that contain seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
