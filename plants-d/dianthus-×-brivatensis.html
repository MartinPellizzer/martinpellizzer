Dianthus × brivatensis

Dianthus × brivatensis, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus × brivatensis is valued for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
