Dalea gigantea

Dalea gigantea, commonly known as the giant milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea gigantea is adapted to arid environments and is often found in grasslands and sagebrush habitats.
