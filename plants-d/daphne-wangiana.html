Daphne wangiana

Daphne wangiana, commonly known as the Chinese daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds.

Daphne wangiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and winter interest.
