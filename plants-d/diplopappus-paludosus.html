Diplopappus paludosus

Diplopappus paludosus, commonly known as the swamp dandelion, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in marshy soils and is often found in swampy or saturated environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring.

The species is adapted to waterlogged conditions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
