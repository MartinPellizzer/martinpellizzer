Dodecatheon pauciflorum var. shoshonense

Dodecatheon pauciflorum var. shoshonense, commonly known as Shoshone glory-of-the-snow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the Shoshone people, who used it for various purposes.
