Dombeya ferruginea subsp. borbonica

Dombeya ferruginea subsp.

borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon dombeya, is a shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Dombeya ferruginea subsp.

borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
