Darea foecunda

Darea foecunda, commonly known as the stinking violets, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, unpleasant odor, which is thought to deter herbivores. Darea foecunda typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common yet often overlooked component of forest understory ecosystems.
