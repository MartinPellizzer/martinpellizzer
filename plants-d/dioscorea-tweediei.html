Dioscorea tweediei

Dioscorea tweediei, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
