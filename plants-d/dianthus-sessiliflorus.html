Dianthus sessiliflorus

Dianthus sessiliflorus, commonly known as the sessile-flower carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant features upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its flowers have five petals with a distinctive frilled edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus sessiliflorus is also valued for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
