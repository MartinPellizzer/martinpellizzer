Dolichos echinatulus

Dolichos echinatulus, commonly known as the spiny cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

Botanically, it is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
