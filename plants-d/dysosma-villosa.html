Dysosma villosa

Dysosma villosa, commonly known as the hairy dysosma, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Dysosma villosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for garden use.
