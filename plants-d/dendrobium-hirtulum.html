Dendrobium hirtulum

Dendrobium hirtulum, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with hairy petals and sepals. Its leaves are ovate and often covered with short, stiff hairs.

Dendrobium hirtulum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
