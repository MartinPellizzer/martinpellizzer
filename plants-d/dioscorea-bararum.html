Dioscorea bararum

Dioscorea bararum, commonly known as the Burmese yam, is a perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The botanical features of Dioscorea bararum make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
