Duranta woronowii

Duranta woronowii, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

Duranta woronowii is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
