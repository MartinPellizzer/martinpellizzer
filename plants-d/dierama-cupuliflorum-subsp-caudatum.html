Dierama cupuliflorum subsp. caudatum

Dierama cupuliflorum subsp.

caudatum, commonly known as the tufted dierama, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers arranged in a spiky inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
