Dolichos trilobus

Dolichos trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, each having a distinctive three-lobed shape. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a pea-like structure.

Dolichos trilobus is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.
