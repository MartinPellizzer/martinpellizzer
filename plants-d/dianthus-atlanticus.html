Dianthus atlanticus

Dianthus atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic campion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It is a low-growing perennial with pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky shores.

Dianthus atlanticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal ecosystems.
