Diothonea angustifolia

Diothonea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf diothonea, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters wide. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the late spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying wetland flora.
