Dioscorea soror var. glauca

Dioscorea soror var. glauca, commonly known as blue-leaf yam, is a variant of the yam species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its glaucous (bluish-gray) foliage, which distinguishes it from other varieties of Dioscorea soror. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
