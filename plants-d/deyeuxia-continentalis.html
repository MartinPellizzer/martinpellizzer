Deyeuxia continentalis

Deyeuxia continentalis, commonly known as the continental false rice, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Deyeuxia continentalis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
