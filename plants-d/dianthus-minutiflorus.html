Dianthus minutiflorus

Dianthus minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has compact, upright growth habits with linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus minutiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
