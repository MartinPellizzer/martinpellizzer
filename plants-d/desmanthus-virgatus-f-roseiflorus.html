Desmanthus virgatus f. roseiflorus

Desmanthus virgatus f. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered burr legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with pairs of oval, serrated leaflets and produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
