Dianthus serratus var. scaber

Dianthus serratus var. scaber, commonly known as the rough-fruited campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow, serrated, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
