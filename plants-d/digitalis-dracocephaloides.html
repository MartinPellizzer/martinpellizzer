Digitalis dracocephaloides

Digitalis dracocephaloides, commonly known as the dragon's head foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
