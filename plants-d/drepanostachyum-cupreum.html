Drepanostachyum cupreum

Drepanostachyum cupreum, commonly known as the copper-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often display a striking coppery hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique foliage.
