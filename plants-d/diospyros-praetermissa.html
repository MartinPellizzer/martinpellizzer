Diospyros praetermissa

Diospyros praetermissa, commonly known as the slender persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red fruits.

The botanical features of D. praetermissa make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
