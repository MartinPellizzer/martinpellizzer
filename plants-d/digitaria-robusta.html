Digitaria robusta

Digitaria robusta, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and dense foliage, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
