Daphne × cupida

Daphne × cupida, commonly known as the pink daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and winter hardiness, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
