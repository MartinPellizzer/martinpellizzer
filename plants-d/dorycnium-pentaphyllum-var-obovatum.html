Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. obovatum

Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. obovatum, commonly known as the broadleaf sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features erect, slender stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are pinnately compound, with obovate leaflets that are typically grayish-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, blooming from late spring to early summer.

This plant is adapted to dry, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.
