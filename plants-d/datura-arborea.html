Datura arborea

Datura arborea, commonly known as the tree jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in summer. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Datura arborea contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
