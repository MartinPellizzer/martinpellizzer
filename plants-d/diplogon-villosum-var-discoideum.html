Diplogon villosum var. discoideum

Diplogon villosum var. discoideum, commonly known as the hairy diplogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and distinctive inflorescences that resemble small umbrellas. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white trichomes, which contribute to its textured appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a pale green to yellowish hue.
