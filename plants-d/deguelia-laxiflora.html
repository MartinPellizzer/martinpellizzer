Deguelia laxiflora

Deguelia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered deguelia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of many leguminous species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and ecological restoration projects.
