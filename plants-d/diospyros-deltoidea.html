Diospyros deltoidea

Diospyros deltoidea, commonly known as the Indian persimmon or wild persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe, though they are typically consumed by birds and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
