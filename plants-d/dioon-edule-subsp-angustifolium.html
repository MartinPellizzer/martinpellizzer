Dioon edule subsp. angustifolium

Dioon edule subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dioon, is a species of cycad native to parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves that are narrow and strap-like.

The plant produces large, spore-bearing cones, with the female cones being particularly showy and often used in traditional ceremonies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
