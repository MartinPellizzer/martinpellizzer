Dianella sparsiflora var. albiflora

Dianella sparsiflora var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered flax lily, is a striking evergreen perennial native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, and it produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and delicate, long-lasting blooms.

It is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators and its resilience in various garden conditions.
