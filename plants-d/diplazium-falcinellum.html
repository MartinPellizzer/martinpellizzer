Diplazium falcinellum

Diplazium falcinellum, commonly known as the broad-leaf sword fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and compound fronds. The fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with serrated leaflets that give the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
