Dichaea annamitorum

Dichaea annamitorum, commonly known as the Annamite dichaea, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, green to reddish-brown pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structure. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it blooms with flowers that can be white, yellow, or orange in color.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
