Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp. subverticillatum

Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp.

subverticillatum, commonly known as the silverleaf daphniphyllum, is a rare evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a bluish or whitish waxy coating, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries in the autumn. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in shaded forest understories.
