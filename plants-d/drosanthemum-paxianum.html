Drosanthemum paxianum

Drosanthemum paxianum, commonly known as the wax plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
