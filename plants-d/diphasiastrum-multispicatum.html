Diphasiastrum multispicatum

Diphasiastrum multispicatum, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.
