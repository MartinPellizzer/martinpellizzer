Delphinium batalinii

Delphinium batalinii, commonly known as the Batalin's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in raceme-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that provides a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Delphinium batalinii is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
