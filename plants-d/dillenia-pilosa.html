Dillenia pilosa

Dillenia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy dillenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with rough, hairy bark, and its leaves are ovate with serrated edges. Dillenia pilosa produces small, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
