Dianthus giganteus subsp. subgiganteus

Dianthus giganteus subsp.

subgiganteus, commonly known as the giant carnation, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, showy flowers with five petals that range in color from white to deep pink, often with contrasting red or purple markings.

The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with linear, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from early summer to late autumn.
