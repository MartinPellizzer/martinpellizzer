Drynaria brancifolia

Drynaria brancifolia, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a member of the Drynariaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like rhizomes and its pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can grow up to several meters in length.

Drynaria brancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
