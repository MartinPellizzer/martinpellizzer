Dillwynia Ericifolia Var. Glaberrima

Dillwynia ericifolia var. glaberrima is a rare and striking plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and ecological importance. This variant is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous leaves and delicate, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
