Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. saxigenus

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

saxigenus, commonly known as the alpine carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers with distinct, frilled petals.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these harsh ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
