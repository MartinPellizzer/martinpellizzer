Deyeuxia deschampsioides var. hayachinensis

Deyeuxia deschampsioides var. hayachinensis, commonly known as the Japanese meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
