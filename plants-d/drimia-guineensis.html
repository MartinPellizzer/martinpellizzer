Drimia guineensis

Drimia guineensis, commonly known as African lily or Guinea lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often reaching several feet in length.

Drimia guineensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in some African cultures.
