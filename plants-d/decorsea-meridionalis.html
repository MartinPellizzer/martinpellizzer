Decorsea meridionalis

Decorsea meridionalis, commonly known as the Mediterranean thyme.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Decorsea meridionalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
