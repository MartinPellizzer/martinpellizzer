Drejerella jamaicensis

Drejerella jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
