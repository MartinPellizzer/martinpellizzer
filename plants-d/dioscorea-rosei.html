Dioscorea rosei

Dioscorea rosei, commonly known as the rose yam, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by cylindrical, fleshy tubers. These tubers are edible when properly cooked and are an important food source in some regions.

Dioscorea rosei is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
