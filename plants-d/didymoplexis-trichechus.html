Didymoplexis trichechus

Didymoplexis trichechus, commonly known as the sea horse orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, horse-shaped flower that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a striking combination of purple and white, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
