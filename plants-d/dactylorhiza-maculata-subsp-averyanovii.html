Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. averyanovii

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

averyanovii, commonly known as the spotted marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as marshes and wet meadows.

The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a loose spike and characterized by their distinctive markings.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
