Dipladenia vinciflora

Dipladenia vinciflora, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a vigorous trailing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of showy, orange-yellow flowers that resemble passionflowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Dipladenia vinciflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
