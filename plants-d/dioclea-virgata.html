Dioclea virgata

Dioclea virgata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellow, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds, often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic effects.

Dioclea virgata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.
