Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. puberulus

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

puberulus, commonly known as the rough carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered with fine pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
