Diospyros pegophila

Diospyros pegophila, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange to red berries.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
