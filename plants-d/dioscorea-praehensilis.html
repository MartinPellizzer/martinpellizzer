Dioscorea praehensilis

Dioscorea praehensilis, commonly known as the climbing yam, is a tropical vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible tuberous roots that are used in various cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests across Africa and Southeast Asia.

Due to its climbing habit, it requires support structures for proper growth and development.
