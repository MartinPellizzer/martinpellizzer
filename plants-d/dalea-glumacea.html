Dalea glumacea

Dalea glumacea, commonly known as the prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It produces small, yellow to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dalea glumacea is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for pollinators.
