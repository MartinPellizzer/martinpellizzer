Dyckia virgata

Dyckia virgata, commonly known as the silver beard plant, is a species of bromeliad native to southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking, branched inflorescences with bright red bracts that attract pollinators. Dyckia virgata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with bright, indirect light.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
