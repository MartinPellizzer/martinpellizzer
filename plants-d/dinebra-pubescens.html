Dinebra pubescens

Dinebra pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Dinebra pubescens is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
