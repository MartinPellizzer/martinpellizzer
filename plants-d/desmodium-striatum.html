Desmodium striatum

Desmodium striatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a hairy, three-lobed pod that contains several small, smooth seeds.

Desmodium striatum is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage and soil stabilization.
