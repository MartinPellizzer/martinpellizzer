Dalzellia pedunculosa

Dalzellia pedunculosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, round fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in some regions.
