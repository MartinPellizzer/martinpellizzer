Dryas incisa

Dryas incisa, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Dryas incisa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
