Decaneurum lilacinum

Decaneurum lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically lilac or purple in color, arranged in dense panicles. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a grass-like texture.

Decaneurum lilacinum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
