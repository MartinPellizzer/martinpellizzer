Dryopteris falciculata f. glabrata

Dryopteris falciculata f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaf maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including Japan and China. The fronds are typically slender and have a graceful, arching appearance, with a glossy, smooth texture that sets it apart from other maidenhair ferns. This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
