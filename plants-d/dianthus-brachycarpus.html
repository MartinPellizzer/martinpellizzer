Dianthus brachycarpus

Dianthus brachycarpus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features compact, bushy growth with dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and visually appealing addition to dry garden environments.
