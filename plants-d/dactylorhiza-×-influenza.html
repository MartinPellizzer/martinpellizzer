Dactylorhiza × influenza

Dactylorhiza × influenza, commonly known as the flu-like orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, green, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
