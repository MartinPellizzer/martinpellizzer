Diospyros cupulifera

Diospyros cupulifera, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruits are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
