Duhaldea cuspidata

Duhaldea cuspidata, commonly known as the narrowleaf false gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Duhaldea cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
