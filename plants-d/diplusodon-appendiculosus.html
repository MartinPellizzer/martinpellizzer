Diplusodon appendiculosus

Diplusodon appendiculosus, commonly known as the feathered bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like structures that resemble feathers, which aid in trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant's leaves are modified into bladder-like structures that can inflate to capture prey. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters, often found in marshes and ponds.

Its unique morphology and predatory mechanism make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
