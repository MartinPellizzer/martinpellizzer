Dalea fruticosa

Dalea fruticosa, commonly known as prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and are arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dalea fruticosa plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in native ecosystems.
