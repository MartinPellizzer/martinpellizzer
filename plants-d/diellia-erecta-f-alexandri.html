Diellia erecta f. alexandri

Diellia erecta f. alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian diellia, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate ornamental quality. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
