Diplazium zenkeri

Diplazium zenkeri, commonly known as the broad-leaved water fern, is a member of the fern family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a dark green, glossy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Diplazium zenkeri plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
