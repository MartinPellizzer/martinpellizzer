Dichodon cerastoides

Dichodon cerastoides, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

Dichodon cerastoides is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique aesthetic.
