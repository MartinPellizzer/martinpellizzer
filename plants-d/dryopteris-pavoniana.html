Dryopteris pavoniana

Dryopteris pavoniana, commonly known as the peacock fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble the tail of a peacock, with a central, glossy, dark green band and lighter, feather-like segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, making it a common sight in humid, temperate regions.

The fronds are bioluminescent at night, a unique trait that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.
