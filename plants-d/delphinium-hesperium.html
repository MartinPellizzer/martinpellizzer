Delphinium hesperium

Delphinium hesperium, commonly known as the western larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

Delphinium hesperium is known for its ornamental value in gardens but is toxic to livestock and humans due to the presence of alkaloids.
