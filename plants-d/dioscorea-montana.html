Dioscorea montana

Dioscorea montana, commonly known as the mountain yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for their nutritional value. Dioscorea montana is known for its starchy, edible tubers that are used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a support structure for its climbing growth.
