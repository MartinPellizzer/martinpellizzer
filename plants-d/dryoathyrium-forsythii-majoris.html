Dryoathyrium forsythii-majoris

Dryoathyrium forsythii-majoris, commonly known as the yellow moss or golden moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth and bright yellow to golden-green coloration, which is due to the presence of chlorophyll and carotenoids. Its structure consists of a thallus with leaf-like structures called phyllidia, which are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This species plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various invertebrates.
