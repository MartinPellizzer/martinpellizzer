Deparia kaalaana

Deparia kaalaana, commonly known as the Kaalaana fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its spores are released from the sori, which are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

The botanical features of Deparia kaalaana make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
