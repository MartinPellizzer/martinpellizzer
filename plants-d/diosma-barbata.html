Diosma barbata

Diosma barbata, commonly known as the hairy diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in grasslands and disturbed areas, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Diosma barbata is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
