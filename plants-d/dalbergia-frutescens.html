Dalbergia frutescens

Dalbergia frutescens, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry savanna and woodland habitats, where it plays a significant role in local ecosystems.

The tree is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
