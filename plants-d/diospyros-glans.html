Diospyros glans

Diospyros glans, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are dark green on the upper surface. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a food source.
