Dalechampia clematidifolia

Dalechampia clematidifolia, commonly known as the Mexican giant hogweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. Its stems are hairy and can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it a striking yet potentially invasive species.

The plant is part of the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its latex-like sap, which can cause skin irritation.
