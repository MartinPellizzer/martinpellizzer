Datura stramonium inermis

Datura stramonium inermis, commonly known as the white jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, white to purple flowers and spiny, hairy stems. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Datura stramonium inermis contains tropane alkaloids, making it toxic and potentially hallucinogenic when ingested.
