Danthonia flavescens var. hookeri

Danthonia flavescens var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's danthonia, is a grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.

Hooker's danthonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
