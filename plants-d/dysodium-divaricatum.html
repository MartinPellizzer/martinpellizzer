Dysodium divaricatum

Dysodium divaricatum, commonly known as the variable spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple stems that often branch out at varying angles. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring, which are borne on slender, often arching branches. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges.

Dysodium divaricatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to different soil types.
