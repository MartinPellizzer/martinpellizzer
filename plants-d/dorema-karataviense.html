Dorema karataviense

Dorema karataviense, commonly known as the Karatavinskaya fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Dorema karataviense is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
