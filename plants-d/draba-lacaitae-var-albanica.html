Draba lacaitae var. albanica

Draba lacaitae var. albanica, commonly known as the Albanian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the Balkan Peninsula, this variant is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant is often found in mountainous regions of Albania and adjacent areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying alpine flora and ecological adaptations.
