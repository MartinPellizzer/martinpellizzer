Daphne papyracea var. grandiflora

Daphne papyracea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora daphne, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens despite its toxic properties.
