Dianthus monspessulanus var. alpestris

Dianthus monspessulanus var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or alpine settings.
