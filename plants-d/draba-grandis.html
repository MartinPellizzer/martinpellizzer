Draba grandis

Draba grandis, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cold, dry climates. Draba grandis produces small, white or purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
