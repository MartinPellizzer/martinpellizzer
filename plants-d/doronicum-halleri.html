Doronicum halleri

Doronicum halleri, commonly known as the alpine goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Doronicum halleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
