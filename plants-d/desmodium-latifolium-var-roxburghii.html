Desmodium latifolium var. roxburghii

Desmodium latifolium var. roxburghii, commonly known as the broad-leaf desmodium, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pods that contain several seeds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
