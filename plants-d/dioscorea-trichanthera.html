Dioscorea trichanthera

Dioscorea trichanthera, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also valued for their nutritional content and potential medicinal properties.

Dioscorea trichanthera plays an important role in both ecological and cultural contexts in its native habitats.
