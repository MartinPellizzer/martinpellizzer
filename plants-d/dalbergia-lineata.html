Dalbergia lineata

Dalbergia lineata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a fine, even texture. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are compound and can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
