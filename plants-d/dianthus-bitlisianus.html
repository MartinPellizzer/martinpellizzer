Dianthus bitlisianus

Dianthus bitlisianus, commonly known as the Armenian pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Dianthus bitlisianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
