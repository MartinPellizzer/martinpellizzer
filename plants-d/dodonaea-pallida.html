Dodonaea pallida

Dodonaea pallida, commonly known as the silverleaf gloryflower, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Dodonaea pallida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
