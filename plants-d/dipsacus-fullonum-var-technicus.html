Dipsacus fullonum var. technicus

Dipsacus fullonum var. technicus, commonly known as the Technic thornweed, is a variant of the common teasel.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flower heads consist of numerous small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in a spherical cluster.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and waste areas.
