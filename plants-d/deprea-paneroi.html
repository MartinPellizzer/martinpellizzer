Deprea paneroi

Deprea paneroi, commonly known as the Madagascar star cactus, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a short, branching stem with clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its arid environment. It blooms during the dry season, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is often found in protected areas or cultivated in specialized gardens.
