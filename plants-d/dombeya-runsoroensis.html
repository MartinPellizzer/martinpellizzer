Dombeya runsoroensis

Dombeya runsoroensis, commonly known as the East African dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

Dombeya runsoroensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
