Declieuxia coerulea

Declieuxia coerulea, commonly known as the blue flax or blue sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and striking blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Declieuxia coerulea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
