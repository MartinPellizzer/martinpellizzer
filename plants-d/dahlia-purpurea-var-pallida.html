Dahlia purpurea var. pallida

Dahlia purpurea var. pallida, commonly known as the White Dahlia, is a hybrid cultivar prized for its elegant, single or semi-cactus form flowers.

It features large, showy blooms with white petals that often have a subtle pink or lavender hue at the base. The plant is native to Mexico and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is a tuberous-rooted species, typically grown as an annual in temperate climates.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.
