Denmoza rhodacantha var. diamantina

Denmoza rhodacantha var. diamantina, commonly known as the Diamond Leaf Plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, diamond-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow patterns. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its foliage is dense and glossy, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
