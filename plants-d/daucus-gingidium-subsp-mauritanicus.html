Daucus gingidium subsp. mauritanicus

Daucus gingidium subsp.

mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania carrot, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its roots are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes.
