Diopogon globifer

Diopogon globifer, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered together. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Diopogon globifer is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
