Digitaria thwaitesii var. tonkinensis

Digitaria thwaitesii var. tonkinensis, commonly known as Tonkin bluestem, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
