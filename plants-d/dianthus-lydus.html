Dianthus lydus

Dianthus lydus, commonly known as the Lydian campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dianthus lydus is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
