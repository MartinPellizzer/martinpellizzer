Digitaria adscendens var. criniformis

Digitaria adscendens var. criniformis, commonly known as the silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive, fluffy inflorescences that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
