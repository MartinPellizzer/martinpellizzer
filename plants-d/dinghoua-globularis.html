Dinghoua globularis

Dinghoua globularis, commonly known as the globe lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, globe-shaped inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dinghoua globularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
