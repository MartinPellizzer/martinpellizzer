Dalea corymbosa

Dalea corymbosa, commonly known as the prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Dalea corymbosa is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for pollinators.
