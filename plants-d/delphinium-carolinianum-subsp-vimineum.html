Delphinium carolinianum subsp. vimineum

Delphinium carolinianum subsp.

vimineum, commonly known as Carolina larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features tall, slender stems that are often covered in a soft, woolly coating.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
