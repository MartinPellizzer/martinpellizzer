Dendrobium lacteum

Dendrobium lacteum, commonly known as the milk orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with erect, often branched stems that are covered in a thick, white to pale yellow coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
