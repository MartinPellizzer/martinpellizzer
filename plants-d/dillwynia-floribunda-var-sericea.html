Dillwynia floribunda var. sericea

Dillwynia floribunda var. sericea, commonly known as silverleaf dillwynia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it its common name, and its delicate, fluffy inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
