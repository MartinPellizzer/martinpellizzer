Dichanthelium xanthophysum

Dichanthelium xanthophysum, commonly known as the yellow-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and slender, tufted stems.

It is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of its environment.
