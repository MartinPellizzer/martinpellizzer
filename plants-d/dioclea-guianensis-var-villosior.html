Dioclea guianensis var. villosior

Dioclea guianensis var. villosior, commonly known as the hairy dioclea, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in tropical rainforests and is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments.
