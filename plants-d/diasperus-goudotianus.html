Diasperus goudotianus

Diasperus goudotianus, commonly known as the Goudot's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowering spikes. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or marshy areas. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its propagation.

Its botanical structure and habitat make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
