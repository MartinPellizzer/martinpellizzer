Davidia laeta

Davidia laeta, commonly known as the Chinese ghost tree, is a rare and ancient species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-like flowers that appear in late spring, giving it a unique and ethereal appearance. The plant has delicate, feather-like leaves that turn yellow in the autumn, contributing to its seasonal beauty. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Davidia laeta is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
