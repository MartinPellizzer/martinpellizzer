Dudleya eximia

Dudleya eximia, commonly known as the silver leaf Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. It produces striking clusters of yellow flowers in the spring, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Dudleya eximia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
