Dacrydium cornwallianum

Dacrydium cornwallianum, commonly known as the Cornwall pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its foliage provides habitat for various native birds and invertebrates.

Dacrydium cornwallianum plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's forest ecosystems.
