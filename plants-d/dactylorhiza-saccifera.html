Dactylorhiza saccifera

Dactylorhiza saccifera, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive sac-like structure at the base of its leaves, which is a unique botanical feature. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands.

Dactylorhiza saccifera is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate climates.
