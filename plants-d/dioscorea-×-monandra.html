Dioscorea × monandra

Dioscorea × monandra, commonly known as the hybrid yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are often used as a staple food in many African and Asian cuisines.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to fully understand its therapeutic applications.
