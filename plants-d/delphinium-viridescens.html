Delphinium viridescens

Delphinium viridescens, commonly known as the greenish larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically spurred at the base. The plant has lance-shaped, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system consists of fibrous roots that anchor it in rocky or well-drained soils.

Delphinium viridescens is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is also known for its toxicity to livestock.
