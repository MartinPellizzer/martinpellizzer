Duranta peruviana

Duranta peruviana, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, which are followed by bright blue or purple berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Duranta peruviana is also valued for its ability to attract birds and other wildlife due to its fruit.
