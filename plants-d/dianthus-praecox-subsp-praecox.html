Dianthus praecox subsp. praecox

Dianthus praecox subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers.

The plant features upright, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in early spring, typically from March to May, and is well-suited for rock gardens or borders. Dianthus praecox subsp.

praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
