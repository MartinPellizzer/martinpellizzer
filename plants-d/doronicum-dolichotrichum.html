Doronicum dolichotrichum

Doronicum dolichotrichum, commonly known as the long-hair leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. Doronicum dolichotrichum typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
