Drymocallis lactea

Drymocallis lactea, commonly known as the milkweed aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Drymocallis lactea is also known for its latex-like sap, which can be milky and sticky.
