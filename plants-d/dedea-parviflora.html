Dedea parviflora

Dedea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered dedea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

Dedea parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
