Diospyros salletii

Diospyros salletii, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
