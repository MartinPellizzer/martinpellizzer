Dichaea morrisii

Dichaea morrisii, commonly known as the Morris's dichaea, is a rare orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically during the spring and early summer. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanical researchers.

Due to habitat destruction, Dichaea morrisii is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
