Draba juniperina

Draba juniperina, commonly known as the juniperleaf whitlowgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, with a bluish-gray appearance due to dense pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Draba juniperina plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.
