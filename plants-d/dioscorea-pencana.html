Dioscorea pencana

Dioscorea pencana, commonly known as the Pencan yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with elongated leaflets. Its tubers are often used as a starchy food source and have been traditionally valued for their nutritional content.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
