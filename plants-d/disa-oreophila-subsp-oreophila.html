Disa oreophila subsp. oreophila

Disa oreophila subsp.

oreophila, commonly known as the mountain disa, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers with white markings.

The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
