Dendrochilum quadrilobum

Dendrochilum quadrilobum, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, and the flowers have a distinctive four-lobed lip.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
