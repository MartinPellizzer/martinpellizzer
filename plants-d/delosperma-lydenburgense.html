Delosperma lydenburgense

Delosperma lydenburgense, commonly known as the Lydenburg ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular plant in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
