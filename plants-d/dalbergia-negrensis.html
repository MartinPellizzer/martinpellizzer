Dalbergia negrensis

Dalbergia negrensis, commonly known as the Negros ebony, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Negros, where it grows in lowland and montane forests. The tree is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. It has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
