Dendrophthoe eucalyptoides

Dendrophthoe eucalyptoides, commonly known as the eucalyptus mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to eucalyptus trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using limited chlorophyll. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers and red berries that are often visited by birds. It is native to Australia and is typically found in regions with a warm, temperate climate.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host trees.
