Diosma lasiophylla

Diosma lasiophylla, commonly known as the roughleaf diosma, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences and are usually pink to reddish in color.

Diosma lasiophylla is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
