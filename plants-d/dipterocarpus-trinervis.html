Dipterocarpus trinervis

Dipterocarpus trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, thick, and often buttressed roots. The tree produces heavy, woody fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky aril. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Its timber is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
