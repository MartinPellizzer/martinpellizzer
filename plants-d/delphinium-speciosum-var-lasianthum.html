Delphinium speciosum var. lasianthum

Delphinium speciosum var. lasianthum, commonly known as the tall larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spires at the top of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in gardens for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
