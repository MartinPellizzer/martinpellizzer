Dianthus gracilis subsp. xanthinus

Dianthus gracilis subsp.

xanthinus, commonly known as the yellow slender carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate, fragrant scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, compact growth habit.
