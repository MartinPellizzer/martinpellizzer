Dactylorhiza × templinensis

Dactylorhiza × templinensis, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a hybrid between Dactylorhiza lapponica and Dactylorhiza macdougalii, resulting in a plant with distinctive morphological features. The species typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and has a tall, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a spike and characterized by their intricate, three-lobed petals.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, acidic soil.
