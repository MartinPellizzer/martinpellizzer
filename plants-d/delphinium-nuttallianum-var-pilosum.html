Delphinium nuttallianum var. pilosum

Delphinium nuttallianum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a fine, hairy texture, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
