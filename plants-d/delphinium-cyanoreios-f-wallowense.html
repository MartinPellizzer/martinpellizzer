Delphinium cyanoreios f. wallowense

Delphinium cyanoreios f. wallowense, commonly known as the Wallowa larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, typically arranged alternately along the stem. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and ecological stability.
