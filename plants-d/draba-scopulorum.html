Draba scopulorum

Draba scopulorum, commonly known as the rock draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are often lobed or divided, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Draba scopulorum is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
