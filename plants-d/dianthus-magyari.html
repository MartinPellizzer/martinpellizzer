Dianthus magyari

Dianthus magyari, commonly known as the Hungarian carnation, is a flowering plant native to Hungary and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features upright stems and basal leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus magyari is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and long-lasting flowers.
