Didymocarpus hispidus var. selangorensis

Didymocarpus hispidus var. selangorensis, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are encased in a hard, woody shell. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
