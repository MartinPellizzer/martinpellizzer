Desmodium sennaarense

Desmodium sennaarense, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Desmodium sennaarense thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
