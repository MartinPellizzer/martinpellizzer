Dianthera laeta

Dianthera laeta, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers and related species.
