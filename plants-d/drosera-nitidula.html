Drosera nitidula

Drosera nitidula, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a species of carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters, and it produces a single seed capsule.

Drosera nitidula is a perennial plant that thrives in cool, moist conditions.
