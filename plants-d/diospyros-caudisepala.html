Diospyros caudisepala

Diospyros caudisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow when mature.
