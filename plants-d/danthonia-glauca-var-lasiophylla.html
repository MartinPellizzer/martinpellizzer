Danthonia glauca var. lasiophylla

Danthonia glauca var. lasiophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf danthonia, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of short, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as sandy soils and disturbed areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
