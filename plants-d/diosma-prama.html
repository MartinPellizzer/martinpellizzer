Diosma prama

Diosma prama, commonly known as the narrowleaf diosma, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features slender, upright stems. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Diosma prama is known for its fragrant blossoms and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
