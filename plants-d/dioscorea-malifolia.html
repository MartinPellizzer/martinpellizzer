Dioscorea malifolia

Dioscorea malifolia, commonly known as the sour sarsaparilla, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces tubers that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend support structures, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The botanical features of D. malifolia make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
