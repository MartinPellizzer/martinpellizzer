Dillwynia floribunda var. typica

Dillwynia floribunda var. typica, commonly known as the slender dillwynia, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
