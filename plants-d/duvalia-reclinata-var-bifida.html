Duvalia reclinata var. bifida

Duvalia reclinata var. bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf duvalia, is a unique plant native to certain regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it the common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or white, adding ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and considered a delicate species in its natural environment.
