Dendrobium optimuspatruus

Dendrobium optimuspatruus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive periods of drought.

The flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a striking display of color to gardens or collections.
