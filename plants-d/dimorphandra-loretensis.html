Dimorphandra loretensis

Dimorphandra loretensis, commonly known as the Loretto's sycamore, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a distinctive appearance, and its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

It produces woody pods that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
