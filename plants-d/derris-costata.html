Derris costata

Derris costata, commonly known as the hookseed or hookvine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, hooked pods. Its roots contain the alkaloid rotenone, which has been used traditionally as a natural insecticide.

Derris costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
