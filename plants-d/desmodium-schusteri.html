Desmodium schusteri

Desmodium schusteri, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive burrs with hooked spines, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing. Native to various regions in Africa, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects and as a forage crop.
