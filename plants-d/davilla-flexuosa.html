Davilla flexuosa

Davilla flexuosa, commonly known as the silverleaf lily or red bower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Davilla flexuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
