Daucus carota subsp. tenuissimus

Daucus carota subsp.

tenuissimus, commonly known as the slender carrot, is a subspecies of the carrot plant characterized by its thin, elongated roots. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its foliage is finely divided, with a delicate, aromatic scent.

This subspecies is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and historical significance in herbal medicine.
