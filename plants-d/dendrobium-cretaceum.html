Dendrobium cretaceum

Dendrobium cretaceum, commonly known as the Cretaceous orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often wrinkled pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Dendrobium cretaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and rarity.
