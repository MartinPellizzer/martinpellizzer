Diplazium subobtusum

Diplazium subobtusum, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The fronds are typically light green and can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a leathery texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
