Dipcadi × juttae

Dipcadi × juttae, commonly known as the hybrid lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between two species of the genus Dipcadi.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a tall spathaceous sheath enclosing a cluster of fragrant, tubular blooms. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

The hybrid is relatively hardy and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.
