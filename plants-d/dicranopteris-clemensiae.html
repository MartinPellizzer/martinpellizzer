Dicranopteris clemensiae

Dicranopteris clemensiae, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a large, aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a complex rhizome system that allows it to spread rapidly in aquatic environments. Its spores are released into the water, facilitating propagation in still or slow-moving water bodies.

Dicranopteris clemensiae plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygenation.
