Draba × asplundii

Draba × asplundii, commonly known as the asplundii whitlow grass, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and features small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Draba × asplundii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
