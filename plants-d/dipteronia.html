Dipteronia

Dipteronia, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, racemose inflorescences that often display a mix of white, yellow, and red flowers. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Dipteronia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The genus includes several species, with Dipteronia sinensis being the most well-known.
