Daphne ambigua

Daphne ambigua, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and are borne in clusters. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Daphne ambigua is valued for its winter blooms and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
