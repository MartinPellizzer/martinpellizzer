Discocactus hartmannii var. patulifolius

Discocactus hartmannii var. patulifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, and often ribbed stems that can grow quite tall. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element. Its leaves are broad and paddle-shaped, which distinguishes it from other cactus varieties.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments with minimal water.
