Dolichos petiolatus

Dolichos petiolatus, commonly known as the long petioled bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with long petioles and small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
