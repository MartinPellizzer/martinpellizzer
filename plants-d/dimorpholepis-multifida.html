Dimorpholepis multifida

Dimorpholepis multifida, commonly known as the mountain glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
