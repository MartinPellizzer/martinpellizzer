Desmodium parkinsonii

Desmodium parkinsonii, commonly known as the pink burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive burrs that aid in seed dispersal, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils.

Desmodium parkinsonii is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
