Diasperus coronatus

Diasperus coronatus, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has been introduced to other regions, including North America, where it is sometimes used for erosion control.

However, it can become invasive in certain areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
