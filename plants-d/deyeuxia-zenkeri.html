Deyeuxia zenkeri

Deyeuxia zenkeri, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It is often found in association with other grasses and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its slender culms and the presence of auricles on its leaves.
