Dombeya scabrida

Dombeya scabrida, commonly known as the rough dombeya, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds.

Dombeya scabrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
