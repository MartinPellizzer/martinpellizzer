Dondia fernaldii

Dondia fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plants for nutrients, typically attaching itself to species of the genus *Atriplex*. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique life cycle that involves seed dispersal by wind.
