Drimia villosa

Drimia villosa, commonly known as the woolly dragon's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, fleshy, and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces tall, spike-like inflorescences bearing white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Drimia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
