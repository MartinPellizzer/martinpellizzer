Dryandra floribunda var. cordata

Dryandra floribunda var. cordata, commonly known as the cordate dryandra, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid environments of its native range.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
