Diospyros foliosa var. fructuosa

Diospyros foliosa var. fructuosa, commonly known as the fruit-bearing persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, hairy leaves and bears yellow or orange fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its fruit is highly valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
