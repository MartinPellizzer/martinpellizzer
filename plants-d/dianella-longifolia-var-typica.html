Dianella longifolia var. typica

Dianella longifolia var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
