Dianthus cyri

Dianthus cyri, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many species with showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a compact growth habit. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive central spur.

Dianthus cyri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
