Dalbergia megalocarpa

Dalbergia megalocarpa, commonly known as African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its workability and aesthetic appeal. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing flat, seed-like structures.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
