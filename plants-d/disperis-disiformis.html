Disperis disiformis

Disperis disiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
