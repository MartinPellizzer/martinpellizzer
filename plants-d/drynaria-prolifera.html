Drynaria prolifera

Drynaria prolifera, commonly known as the forked fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching fronds that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows on cliffs or rocky outcrops, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.
