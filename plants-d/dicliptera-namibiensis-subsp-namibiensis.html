Dicliptera namibiensis subsp. namibiensis

Dicliptera namibiensis subsp.

namibiensis, commonly known as the Namibian grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems.

The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.
