Digitalis purpurea f. parviflora

Digitalis purpurea f. parviflora, commonly known as the miniature foxglove, is a compact cultivar of the foxglove plant.

It features smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical Digitalis purpurea, with a height usually under 30 cm. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in medicine.
