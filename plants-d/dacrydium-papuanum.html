Dacrydium papuanum

Dacrydium papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan silver pine, is a coniferous tree native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its slender, evergreen foliage and distinctive cone structures. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often forming dense forests or undergrowth. Its leaves are needle-like and arranged spirally along the stems, contributing to its unique aesthetic.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
