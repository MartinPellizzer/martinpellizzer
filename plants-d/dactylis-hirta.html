Dactylis hirta

Dactylis hirta, commonly known as hairy grass or hairy vetch, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or white flowers. Dactylis hirta is often found in disturbed soils and is used in erosion control and as forage for livestock.

Its seeds are also utilized in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
