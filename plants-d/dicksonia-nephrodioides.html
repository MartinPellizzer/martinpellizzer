Dicksonia nephrodioides

Dicksonia nephrodioides, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a large, evergreen fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a distinctive, fibrous trunk that resembles a tree, and its fronds are bi-lobed, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant component of New Zealand's native flora.
