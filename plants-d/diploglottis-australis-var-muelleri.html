Diploglottis australis var. muelleri

Diploglottis australis var. muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually narrow and pointed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
