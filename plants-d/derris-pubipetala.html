Derris pubipetala

Derris pubipetala, commonly known as the hairy pubescent derris, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
