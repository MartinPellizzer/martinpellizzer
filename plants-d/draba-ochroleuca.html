Draba ochroleuca

Draba ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow whitlow grass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Draba ochroleuca is adapted to cold climates and has a widespread distribution across the Northern Hemisphere.
