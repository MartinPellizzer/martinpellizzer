Dianthus attenuatus f. sclerophyllus

Dianthus attenuatus f. sclerophyllus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand drought and full sun.

This succulent perennial is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
