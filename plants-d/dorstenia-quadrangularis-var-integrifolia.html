Dorstenia quadrangularis var. integrifolia

Dorstenia quadrangularis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the "Hairy Cucumber," is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and features a distinctive, cylindrical stem with a ridged, quadrangular shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spiky inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
