Disa spathulata

Disa spathulata, commonly known as the Broad-leaved Disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces a single, showy spike of flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
