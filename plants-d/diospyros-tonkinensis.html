Diospyros tonkinensis

Diospyros tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to reddish fruits with a thick, leathery skin. The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
