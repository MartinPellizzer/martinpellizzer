Delphinium acuminatissimum

Delphinium acuminatissimum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are characterized by their spurred petals, a distinctive feature of the Delphinium genus.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
