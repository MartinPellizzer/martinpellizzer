Dryobalanops oblongifolia subsp. oblongifolia

Dryobalanops oblongifolia subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and can grow up to 30 meters in height.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture. The flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in spikes, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable timber and is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests.
