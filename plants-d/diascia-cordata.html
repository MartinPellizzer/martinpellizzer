Diascia cordata

Diascia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf diascia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Diascia cordata is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought-resistant nature.
