Digitalis ballii

Digitalis ballii, commonly known as the Ball's foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes several species of foxgloves known for their bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features tall, slender stems covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with a distinctive spurred shape at the base of each bloom.

Digitalis ballii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
