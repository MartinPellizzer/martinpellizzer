Dicentra cucullaria

Dicentra cucullaria, commonly known as the helmet fern or cow's horn plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Poppy family (Fumariaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and striking, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually pink or red and have a unique, helmet-like shape.

Dicentra cucullaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
