Didymoplexis striata

Didymoplexis striata, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers with contrasting color patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence bears multiple showy, spurred flowers.

Didymoplexis striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
