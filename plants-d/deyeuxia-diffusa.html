Deyeuxia diffusa

Deyeuxia diffusa, commonly known as the common flat sedge, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, meadows, and along waterways. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Deyeuxia diffusa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
