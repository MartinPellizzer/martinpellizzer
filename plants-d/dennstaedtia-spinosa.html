Dennstaedtia spinosa

Dennstaedtia spinosa, commonly known as the spinous water fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green with a waxy texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Dennstaedtia spinosa plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various invertebrates.
