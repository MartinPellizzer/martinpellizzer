Diervilla coraeensis f. alba

Diervilla coraeensis f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered hazelnut, is a compact shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, nut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
