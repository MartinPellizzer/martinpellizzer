Dioscorea vulgaris

Dioscorea vulgaris, commonly known as common yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and cylindrical, tuberous roots that are the primary edible part. Its roots are rich in starch and have been used in traditional cuisines for centuries.

Dioscorea vulgaris is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
