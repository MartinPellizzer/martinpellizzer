Dioscorea brevifolia

Dioscorea brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved yam, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for propagation and medicinal purposes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
