Digitaria gerdesii

Digitaria gerdesii, commonly known as the African grass or desert grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Digitaria gerdesii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
