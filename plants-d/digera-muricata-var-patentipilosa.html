Digera muricata var. patentipilosa

Digera muricata var. patentipilosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom in the morning. Its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in various soil types make it both ornamental and invasive in some areas.

The variety patentipilosa is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which sets it apart from other forms of Digera muricata.
