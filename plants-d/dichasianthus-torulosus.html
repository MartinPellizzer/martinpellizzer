Dichasianthus torulosus

Dichasianthus torulosus, commonly known as the waxy leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dichasianthus torulosus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
