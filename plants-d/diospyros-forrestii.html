Diospyros forrestii

Diospyros forrestii, commonly known as the Forrest persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly China and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to common persimmons.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
