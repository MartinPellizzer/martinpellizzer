Diphylleia grayi

Diphylleia grayi, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its botanical features include a short, woody stem and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

Diphylleia grayi is often found in rich, deciduous forests and is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
