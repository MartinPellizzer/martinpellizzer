Daphnidium myrrha

Daphnidium myrrha, commonly known as the myrrh plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been historically valued for its medicinal and perfumery uses. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.
