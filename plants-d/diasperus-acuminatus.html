Diasperus acuminatus

Diasperus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a compact head. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
