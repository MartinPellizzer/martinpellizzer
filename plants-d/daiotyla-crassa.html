Daiotyla crassa

Daiotyla crassa, commonly known as the thick-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, thick stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
