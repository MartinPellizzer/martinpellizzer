Didaste pentandra

Didaste pentandra, commonly known as the five-angled didaste, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its pentagonal or five-angled stem, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically has alternate, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with five petals, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
