Dendrochilum krauseanum

Dendrochilum krauseanum, commonly known as the Krause's tree orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or in the undergrowth of forests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with red or purple markings, attracting pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Dendrochilum krauseanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
