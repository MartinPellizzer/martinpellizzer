Dryopteris thysanocarpa

Dryopteris thysanocarpa, commonly known as the fringe-leaf wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Central America, South America, and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are often adorned with a fringe of fine, hair-like structures along the edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in cool, humid conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically clustered on the underside of the fronds, and they play a key role in its reproductive cycle.

The botanical features of Dryopteris thysanocarpa make it a notable species within the Dryopteris genus, valued for both its ecological role and ornamental appeal.
