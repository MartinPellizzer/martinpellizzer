Dendrobium mischobulbum

Dendrobium mischobulbum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, wiry stem with clustered, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense, white to yellow pubescence. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically arranged in racemes along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
