Draba incerta var. peasei

Draba incerta var. peasei, commonly known as the Pease's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow or white.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
