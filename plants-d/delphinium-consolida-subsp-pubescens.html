Delphinium consolida subsp. pubescens

Delphinium consolida subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
