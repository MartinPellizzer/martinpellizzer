Draba nivalis var. californica

Draba nivalis var. californica, commonly known as the California rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It has a short growing season and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

The species is noted for its ability to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
