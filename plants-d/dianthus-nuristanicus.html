Dianthus nuristanicus

Dianthus nuristanicus, commonly known as the Nuristan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Afghanistan and northern Pakistan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Dianthus nuristanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
