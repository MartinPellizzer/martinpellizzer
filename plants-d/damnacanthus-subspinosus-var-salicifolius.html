Damnacanthus subspinosus var. salicifolius

Damnacanthus subspinosus var. salicifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf devil's claw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds with a hooked beak, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
