Didissandra leucantha

Didissandra leucantha, commonly known as the white sandstone plant, is a unique member of the Araliaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, white to pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Due to its ornamental appeal and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
