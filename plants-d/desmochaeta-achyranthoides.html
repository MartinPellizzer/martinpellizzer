Desmochaeta achyranthoides

Desmochaeta achyranthoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color.

The flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing pale green or yellowish blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
