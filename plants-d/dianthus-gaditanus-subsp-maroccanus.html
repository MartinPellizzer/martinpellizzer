Dianthus gaditanus subsp. maroccanus

Dianthus gaditanus subsp.

maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco. It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
