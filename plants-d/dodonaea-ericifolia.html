Dodonaea ericifolia

Dodonaea ericifolia, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its adaptability to coastal environments makes it a hardy species that thrives in sandy soils and saline conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in coastal ecosystems.
