Dalea linnaei

Dalea linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean Dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Dalea linnaei is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
