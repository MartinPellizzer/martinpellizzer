Dictyosperma album var. furfuraceum

Dictyosperma album var. furfuraceum, commonly known as the white lily of the Nile, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling lilies. The plant has long, narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
