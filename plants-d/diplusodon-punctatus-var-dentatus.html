Diplusodon punctatus var. dentatus

Diplusodon punctatus var. dentatus, commonly known as the toothed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive tooth-like leaf margins and the presence of a specific subspecies trait.

The toothed thistle is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
