Dendrobium coelandria

Dendrobium coelandria, commonly known as the Coelandra orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Dendrobium coelandria is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
