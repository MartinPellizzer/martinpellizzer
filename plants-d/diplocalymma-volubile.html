Diplocalymma volubile

Diplocalymma volubile, commonly known as the climbing false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

Diplocalymma volubile is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
