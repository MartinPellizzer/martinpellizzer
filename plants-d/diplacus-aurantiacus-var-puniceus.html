Diplacus aurantiacus var. puniceus

Diplacus aurantiacus var. puniceus, commonly known as the scarlet honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with a glossy appearance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
