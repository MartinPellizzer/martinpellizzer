Dorstenia tenera var. tenera

Dorstenia tenera var. tenera, commonly known as the woolly knotweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy stems that resemble a cluster of small, knotted structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticultural collections.
