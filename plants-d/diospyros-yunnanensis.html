Diospyros yunnanensis

Diospyros yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China, particularly Yunnan Province.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange to red berries in the fall. Its fruit is edible, though typically astringent when unripe, and is valued for its flavor and medicinal properties.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
