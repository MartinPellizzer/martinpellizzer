Dianthus faurei

Dianthus faurei, commonly known as the Faure's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and have five petals that often feature a distinctive red or purple edge.

Dianthus faurei thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
