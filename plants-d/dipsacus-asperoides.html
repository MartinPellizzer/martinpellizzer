Dipsacus asperoides

Dipsacus asperoides, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bristly seed heads that resemble a beard, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Dipsacus asperoides is a biennial or perennial plant, depending on the climate, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
