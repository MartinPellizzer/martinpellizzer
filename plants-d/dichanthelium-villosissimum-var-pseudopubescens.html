Dichanthelium villosissimum var. pseudopubescens

Dichanthelium villosissimum var. pseudopubescens, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster with elongated, pale green to golden spikelets. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

This variety is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
