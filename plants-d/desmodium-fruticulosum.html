Desmodium fruticulosum

Desmodium fruticulosum, commonly known as the hairy burr legume, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy, spiny pods that contain small, rounded seeds. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important species in certain ecosystems.
