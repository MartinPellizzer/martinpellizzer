Dendrocoryne speciosa var. fusiforme

Dendrocoryne speciosa var. fusiforme, commonly known as the fusiform tree heather, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its unique morphology includes elongated, cylindrical stems that give it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
