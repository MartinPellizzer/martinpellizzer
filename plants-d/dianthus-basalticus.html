Dianthus basalticus

Dianthus basalticus, commonly known as the basalt carnation, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers with prominent white markings. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other popular garden flowers like carnations and pinks.

Dianthus basalticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in harsh, arid environments.
