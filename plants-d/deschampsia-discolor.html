Deschampsia discolor

Deschampsia discolor, commonly known as bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and silver-tipped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, prairies, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and erosion control capabilities.

Deschampsia discolor is also used in native plant landscaping due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
