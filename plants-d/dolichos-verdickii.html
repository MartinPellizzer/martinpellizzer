Dolichos verdickii

Dolichos verdickii, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, pale green to yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods. Its seeds are typically white or yellow, and the plant is valued for its nutritional content and use in traditional agriculture.

Dolichos verdickii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable farming practices.
