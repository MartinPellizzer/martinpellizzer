Dactylorhiza × hallii subsp. hallii

Dactylorhiza × hallii subsp.

hallii, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to western Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and other moist habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators.

This species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
