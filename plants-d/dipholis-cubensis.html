Dipholis cubensis

Dipholis cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban viper plant, is a climbing vine native to Cuba.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky filaments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
