Didymocarpus hilsenbergii

Didymocarpus hilsenbergii, commonly known as the Hilsenberg's tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its distinctive, flat, leathery leaves and its unique, elongated pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
