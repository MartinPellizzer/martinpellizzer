Dendrobium lagarum

Dendrobium lagarum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Australia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a branched stem system and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms during the spring and summer months, and its flowers are arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium lagarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
