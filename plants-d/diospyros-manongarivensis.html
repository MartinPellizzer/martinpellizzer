Diospyros manongarivensis

Diospyros manongarivensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland and montane forests.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
