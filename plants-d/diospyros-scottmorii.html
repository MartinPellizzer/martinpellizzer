Diospyros scottmorii

Diospyros scottmorii, commonly known as the Scott's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits. The fruits are typically consumed raw or used in traditional culinary preparations.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
