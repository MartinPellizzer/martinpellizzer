Deyeuxia nagarum

Deyeuxia nagarum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of southern Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, fluffy structures that facilitate wind pollination.

Deyeuxia nagarum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
