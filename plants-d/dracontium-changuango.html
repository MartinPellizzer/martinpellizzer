Dracontium changuango

Dracontium changuango, commonly known as the Changuango arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are large, arrow-shaped, and often have a glossy surface.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking foliage.
