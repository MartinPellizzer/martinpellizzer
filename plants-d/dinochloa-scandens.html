Dinochloa scandens

Dinochloa scandens, commonly known as the climbing grass or creeping dinochloa, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features slender, erect stems that can climb or spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Dinochloa scandens is often used in landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in moist, shaded environments.
