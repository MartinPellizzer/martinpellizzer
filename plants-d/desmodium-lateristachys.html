Desmodium lateristachys

Desmodium lateristachys, commonly known as the narrow-stembed burr legume, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, rounded seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Desmodium lateristachys is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
