Dimeria leptorhachis

Dimeria leptorhachis, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed dimeria, is a member of the Geraniaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves with a distinctive narrow ribbing. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Dimeria leptorhachis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
