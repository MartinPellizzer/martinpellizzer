Dicerocaryum senecioides subsp. transvaalense

Dicerocaryum senecioides subsp.

transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal milkweed, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
