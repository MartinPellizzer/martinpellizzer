Deyeuxia fauriei

Deyeuxia fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's muirgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and grassy slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Deyeuxia fauriei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
