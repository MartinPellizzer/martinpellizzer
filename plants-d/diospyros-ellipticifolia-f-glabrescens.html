Diospyros ellipticifolia f. glabrescens

Diospyros ellipticifolia f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon genus characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia. The leaves are elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red fruits that are typically edible when fully ripe.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in suitable climates.
