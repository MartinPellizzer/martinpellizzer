Dentaria maxima f. aphylla

Dentaria maxima f. aphylla, commonly known as the leafless toothwort, is a unique variant of the toothwort plant native to the eastern United States.

This fern-like plant is characterized by its lack of leaves, with the entire photosynthetic function performed by its modified stems. It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in early spring, followed by dark brown, capsule-like fruits.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to low-light conditions and its role in forest ecosystems.
