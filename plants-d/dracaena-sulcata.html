Dracaena sulcata

Dracaena sulcata, commonly known as the African spear or dragon tree, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern, giving it a striking, almost architectural appearance. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a subtle blue or silver hue on the underside.

Dracaena sulcata is highly drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
