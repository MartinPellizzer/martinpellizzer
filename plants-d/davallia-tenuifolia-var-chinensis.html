Davallia tenuifolia var. chinensis

Davallia tenuifolia var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil. It produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
