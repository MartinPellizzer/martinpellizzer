Discocactus cephaliaciculosus

Discocactus cephaliaciculosus, commonly known as the clustered head discocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that often cluster together, forming a compact growth pattern. The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

This resilient plant is well adapted to harsh desert environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
