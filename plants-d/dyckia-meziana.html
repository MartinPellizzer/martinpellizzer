Dyckia meziana

Dyckia meziana, commonly known as the Mexican dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange, adding visual interest. Dyckia meziana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
