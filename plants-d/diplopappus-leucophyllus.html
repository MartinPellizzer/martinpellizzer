Diplopappus leucophyllus

Diplopappus leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf burrweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a silvery or white underside, giving it its common name.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
