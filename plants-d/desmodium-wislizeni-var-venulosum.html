Desmodium wislizeni var. venulosum

Desmodium wislizeni var. venulosum, commonly known as the hairy desmodium, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
