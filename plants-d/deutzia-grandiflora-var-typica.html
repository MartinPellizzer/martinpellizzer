Deutzia grandiflora var. typica

Deutzia grandiflora var. typica, commonly known as the common deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early to mid-spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a beneficial addition to wildlife-friendly landscapes.
