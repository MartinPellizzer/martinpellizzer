Dichapetalum soyauxii

Dichapetalum soyauxii, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy underside, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
