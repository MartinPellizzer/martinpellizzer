Dianthus rudbaricus

Dianthus rudbaricus, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Dianthus rudbaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
