Dioscorea campanulata

Dioscorea campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped yam, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by tuberous roots that are valued for their nutritional and medicinal properties.

Dioscorea campanulata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
