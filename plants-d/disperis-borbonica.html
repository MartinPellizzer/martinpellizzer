Disperis borbonica

Disperis borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Disperis borbonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the local ecosystem.
