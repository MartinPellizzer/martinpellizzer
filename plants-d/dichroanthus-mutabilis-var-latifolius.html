Dichroanthus mutabilis var. latifolius

Dichroanthus mutabilis var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.
