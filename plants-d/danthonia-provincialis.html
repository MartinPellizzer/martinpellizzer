Danthonia provincialis

Danthonia provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial danthonia, is a low-growing grass species native to the wetlands of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshy or swampy environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
