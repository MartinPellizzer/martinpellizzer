Draba cardaminiflora

Draba cardaminiflora, commonly known as the alpine whitlow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It has basal leaves that are lobed or pinnately divided, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Draba cardaminiflora is known for its hardy nature and ability to survive in harsh, cold climates.
