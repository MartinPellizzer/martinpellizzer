Drynaria amphilogos

Drynaria amphilogos, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green, with a slightly serrated edge.

Drynaria amphilogos is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and air-purifying properties.
