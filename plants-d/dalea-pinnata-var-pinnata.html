Dalea pinnata var. pinnata

Dalea pinnata var. pinnata, commonly known as the redstem false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 2 feet. The plant features compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its showy, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
