Duguetia oblanceolata

Duguetia oblanceolata, commonly known as the red-flowered piper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to red, depending on the variety. Its fruit is a three-lobed, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

Duguetia oblanceolata is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
