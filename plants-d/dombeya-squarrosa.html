Dombeya squarrosa

Dombeya squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and large, showy flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Dombeya squarrosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
