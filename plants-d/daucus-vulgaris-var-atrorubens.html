Daucus vulgaris var. atrorubens

Daucus vulgaris var. atrorubens, commonly known as the red-rooted parsley, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers. Its roots are thick, fleshy, and often reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

This variety is typically found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
