Dianthus guttatus subsp. guttatus

Dianthus guttatus subsp.

guttatus, commonly known as the Sierra snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
