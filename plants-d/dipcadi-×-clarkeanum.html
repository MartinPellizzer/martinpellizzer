Dipcadi × clarkeanum

Dipcadi × clarkeanum, commonly known as the hybrid tiger lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Dipcadi strictum and Dipcadi clarkeanum.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sturdy, upright stems covered in lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a distinctive tiger-like pattern on the petals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
