Dalenia latifolia

Dalenia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dalenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Dalenia latifolia is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens.
