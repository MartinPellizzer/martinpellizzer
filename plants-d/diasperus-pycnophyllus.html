Diasperus pycnophyllus

Diasperus pycnophyllus, commonly known as the dense-leaved rock cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and dense, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Diasperus pycnophyllus is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil and providing habitat for small insects.
