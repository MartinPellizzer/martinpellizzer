Decazyx

Decazyx, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features a compact, succulent-like stem covered in a silvery, waxy coating that helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the thin, rocky soil of its habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, the decazyx is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated by enthusiasts.
