Doyleanthus arillata

Doyleanthus arillata, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems and clustered, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a distinctive pattern of spots or stripes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Doyleanthus arillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
