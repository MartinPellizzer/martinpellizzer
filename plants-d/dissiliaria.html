Dissiliaria

Dissiliaria, commonly known as the star-shaped orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

These plants are native to Madagascar and are characterized by their unique, star-like appearance with multiple petals and sepals. Dissiliaria species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, often found on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Their flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from white to pink and purple, and they bloom during the wet season.

The genus is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
