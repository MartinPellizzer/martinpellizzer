Drepananthus sumatranus

Drepananthus sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, legume-like pods that contain its seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical regions.
