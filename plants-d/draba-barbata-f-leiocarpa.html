Draba barbata f. leiocarpa

Draba barbata f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of oblanceolate, smooth, and leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to other forms of Draba barbata.
