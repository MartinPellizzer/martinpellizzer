Dodonaea oxyptera

Dodonaea oxyptera, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Dodonaea oxyptera is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its hardy nature.
