Dendrobium formosum var. jamesianum

Dendrobium formosum var. jamesianum, commonly known as the Jamesian orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with long, cane-like stems that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often with a distinctive red lip. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
