Draba × brandtii

Draba × brandtii, commonly known as the Brandt's whitlow grass, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is native to regions in Central Asia and has adapted to grow in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Draba × brandtii is often found in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
