Dithecina racemifera

Dithecina racemifera, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features a slender stem with a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the poor, rocky soils of its habitat.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is often found in cloud forests where humidity is high.
