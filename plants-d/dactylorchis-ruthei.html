Dactylorchis ruthei

Dactylorchis ruthei, commonly known as the Ruthe's orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a single, slender stem that bears a few lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
