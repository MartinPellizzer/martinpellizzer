Dendrophorbium peregrinum

Dendrophorbium peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering bark plant, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unusual growth habit, with a woody stem that appears to "wander" across rocky surfaces, forming a distinctive, creeping morphology. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Dendrophorbium peregrinum is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations and limited distribution.
