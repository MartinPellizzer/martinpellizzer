Dendrocalamus minor

Dendrocalamus minor, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and smooth on the upper surface. Dendrocalamus minor is often found in moist, lowland areas and is valued for its lightweight and durable bamboo.

Its rapid growth and high yield make it an important resource for sustainable forestry and handicraft industries.
