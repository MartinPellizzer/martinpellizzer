Dorycnium collinum

Dorycnium collinum, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, rocky areas, and along roadsides. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that branches near the base, with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are characteristic of the genus Dorycnium.

The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
