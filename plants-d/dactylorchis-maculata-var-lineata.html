Dactylorchis maculata var. lineata

Dactylorchis maculata var. lineata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple or brown spotting. The plant produces a single, slender flowering stem that bears a few showy, fragrant flowers with a greenish-yellow lip. Its growth habit is low-growing, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
