Digitaria veldkampiana

Digitaria veldkampiana, commonly known as the Veldkamp's digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Digitaria veldkampiana is valued for its potential in forage and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
