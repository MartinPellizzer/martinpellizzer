Dendrobium multilobatum

Dendrobium multilobatum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, wiry stem with multiple nodes and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil or bark medium. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dendrobium multilobatum is prized for its long-lasting blooms and is often cultivated for both horticultural and ornamental purposes.
