Doritis braceana

Doritis braceana, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or purple flowers, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture efficiently.

Doritis braceana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and colorful blooms.
