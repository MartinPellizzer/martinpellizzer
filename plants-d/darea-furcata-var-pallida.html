Darea furcata var. pallida

Darea furcata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale fringed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
