Dioscorea nipponica var. jamesii

Dioscorea nipponica var. jamesii, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in ornamental gardening.
