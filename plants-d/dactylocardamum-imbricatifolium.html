Dactylocardamum imbricatifolium

Dactylocardamum imbricatifolium, commonly known as the overlapping-leaf cardamum, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, overlapping leaves that give it its specific epithet "imbricatifolium.". The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in terminal spikes. It is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
