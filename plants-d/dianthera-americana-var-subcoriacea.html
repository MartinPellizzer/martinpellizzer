Dianthera americana var. subcoriacea

Dianthera americana var. subcoriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf dianthera, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage is characterized by a leathery texture and a distinct, aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
