Diospyros cordata

Diospyros cordata, commonly known as the African date palm, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been traditionally used for food and medicinal purposes.

The tree also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
