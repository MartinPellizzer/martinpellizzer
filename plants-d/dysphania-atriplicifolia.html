Dysphania atriplicifolia

Dysphania atriplicifolia, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its edible, leafy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often used in various cuisines. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet conditions. It produces small, greenish flowers that develop into seed pods, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

Dysphania atriplicifolia is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
