Dipodium purpureum subsp. philippinense

Dipodium purpureum subsp.

philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine purple dipodium, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and compact growth habit, often found in shaded, moist forest environments.

The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It is typically found in mountainous regions, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
