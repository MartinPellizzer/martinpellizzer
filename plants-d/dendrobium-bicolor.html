Dendrobium bicolor

Dendrobium bicolor, commonly known as the Orchid with Two Colors, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its long, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Dendrobium bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
