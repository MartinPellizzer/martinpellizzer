Diospyros impressa

Diospyros impressa, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to reddish fruits, which are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various regions of Africa.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
