Dryopteris glutinosa

Dryopteris glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive glossy appearance and a sticky texture on the underside. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in the Dryopteris genus.
