Distimake quinatus

Distimake quinatus, commonly known as the silverleaf moonvine, is a striking perennial plant native to the humid lowlands of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silvery-green leaves that shimmer in the sunlight, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale lavender flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that turn from green to a deep orange-red as they mature. Its roots are fibrous and can reach depths of up to two meters, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The silverleaf moonvine is also notable for its ability to attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, due to its nectar-rich blooms.
