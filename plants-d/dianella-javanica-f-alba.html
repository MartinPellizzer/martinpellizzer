Dianella javanica f. alba

Dianella javanica f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the broadleaf lily, is a variant of the native New Zealand plant Dianella javanica.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, strap-like leaves. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round visual interest.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
