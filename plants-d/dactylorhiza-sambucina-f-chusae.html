Dactylorhiza sambucina f. chusae

Dactylorhiza sambucina f. chusae, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, peaty soils and thrives in areas with high humidity and partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of showy, fragrant flowers.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in damp meadows and marshes.
