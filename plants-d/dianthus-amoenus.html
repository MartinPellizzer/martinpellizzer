Dianthus amoenus

Dianthus amoenus, commonly known as the garden pinks, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Dianthus amoenus is relatively low maintenance and can be grown in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
