Daviesia hakeoides subsp. hakeoides

Daviesia hakeoides subsp.

hakeoides, commonly known as the Hakea-like Daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in heathland and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers in late winter to early spring. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its hardy nature.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for native wildlife.
