Delphinium delavayi var. lasiandrum

Delphinium delavayi var. lasiandrum, commonly known as the Tibetan larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
