Diplothemium campestre var. genuinum

Diplothemium campestre var. genuinum, commonly known as the common field thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a robust, upright stem covered with dense, spiny leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and a common weed in agricultural settings.
