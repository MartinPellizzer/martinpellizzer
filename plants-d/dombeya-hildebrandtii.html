Dombeya hildebrandtii

Dombeya hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dombeya hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting floral display.
