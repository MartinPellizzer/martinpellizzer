Doronicum bargusinense var. pilosum

Doronicum bargusinense var. pilosum, commonly known as the Pilose Leuzea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its floral structure consists of five petals arranged in a radial pattern, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The variety pilosum is distinguished by its dense pubescence, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.
