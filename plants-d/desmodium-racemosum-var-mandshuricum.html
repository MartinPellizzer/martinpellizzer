Desmodium racemosum var. mandshuricum

Desmodium racemosum var. mandshuricum, commonly known as the Mandshurian ticktrefoil, is a perennial legume native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, exhibiting a sprawling or ascending growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in racemes, producing a distinctive pink or purple color. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions to soil fertility.
