Duchesnea formosana

Duchesnea formosana, commonly known as the Japanese strawberry or forest strawberry, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, edible berries similar to strawberries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in horticulture and ecological studies.
