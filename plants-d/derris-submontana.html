Derris submontana

Derris submontana, commonly known as the mountain derris, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is known for containing rotenone, a toxic alkaloid used in traditional insecticides. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions.
