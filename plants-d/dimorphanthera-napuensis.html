Dimorphanthera napuensis

The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in the humid, shaded environments of its native habitat. Its leaves are long and narrow, adapted to maximize light absorption in the dense forest understory.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Dimorphanthera napuensis is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
