Dendrobium pullenianum

Dendrobium pullenianum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that bear clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Dendrobium pullenianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
