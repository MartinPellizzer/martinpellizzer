Dalbergiella

Dalbergiella, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to various regions of Africa and are characterized by their compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. Dalbergiella species often thrive in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions. They play a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to their symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as forage for livestock.
