Dendrobium crassinervium

Dendrobium crassinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can become woody over time. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two rows, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dendrobium crassinervium thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
