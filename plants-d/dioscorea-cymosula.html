Dioscorea cymosula

Dioscorea cymosula, commonly known as the mountain yam, is a tuberous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and the production of edible tubers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to several meters. Its tubers are often harvested for their starchy, nutritious content and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant's botanical features include compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries.
