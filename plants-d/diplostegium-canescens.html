Diplostegium canescens

Diplostegium canescens, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique parasitic relationship with host trees and its distinctive morphological features.
