Desmoncus riparius

Desmoncus riparius, commonly known as the marsh hound, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its root system is adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it a common sight in marshes and along riverbanks.

Desmoncus riparius plays a role in supporting local insect populations and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
