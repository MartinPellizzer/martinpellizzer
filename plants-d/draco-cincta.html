Draco cincta

Draco cincta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dragon tree, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Draco cincta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
