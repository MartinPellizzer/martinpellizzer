Davallia concinna

Davallia concinna, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its leathery, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy scales. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.
