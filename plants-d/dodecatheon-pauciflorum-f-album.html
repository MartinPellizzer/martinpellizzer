Dodecatheon pauciflorum f. album

Dodecatheon pauciflorum f. album, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy white flowers with prominent fringed petals. The plant typically grows in moist, woodsy habitats and is known for its short stature and clustered inflorescence. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
