Diasperus tomentosus

Diasperus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Diasperus tomentosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
