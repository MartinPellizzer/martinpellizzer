Dicranopygium wallisii

Dicranopygium wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's feather moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant has slender, wiry stems covered in small, leaf-like structures that are arranged in two rows. Its spore-producing capsules are borne on short, erect stalks, and the plant is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.

Dicranopygium wallisii plays a role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its natural habitat.
