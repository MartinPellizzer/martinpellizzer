Dupatya rigida

Dupatya rigida, commonly known as the rigid datura, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant has thick, waxy leaves and produces berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
