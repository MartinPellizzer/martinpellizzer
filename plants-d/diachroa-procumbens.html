Diachroa procumbens

Diachroa procumbens, commonly known as the creeping diachroa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Diachroa procumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
