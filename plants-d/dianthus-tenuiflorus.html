Dianthus tenuiflorus

Dianthus tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dianthus tenuiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
