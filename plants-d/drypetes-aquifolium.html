Drypetes aquifolium

Drypetes aquifolium, commonly known as the water-leaved ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
