Dolichos pseudodebilis

Dolichos pseudodebilis, commonly known as the slender dolichos, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with essential nutrients.
