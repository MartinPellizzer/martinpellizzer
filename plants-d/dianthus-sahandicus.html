Dianthus sahandicus

Dianthus sahandicus, commonly known as the Persian pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of numerous small petals arranged in a cluster, creating a showy display.

Dianthus sahandicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
