Delphinium vestitum var. stenolobum

Delphinium vestitum var. stenolobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
