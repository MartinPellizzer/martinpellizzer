Datura metel var. obscura

Datura metel var. obscura, commonly known as the dark moonflower, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are usually ovate or heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces spherical, spiny fruits that contain toxic seeds.

The plant contains various alkaloids, including scopolamine and hyoscyamine, which make it highly poisonous to humans and animals.
