Dianthus multisquamatus

Dianthus multisquamatus, commonly known as the mountain dianthus, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Dianthus multisquamatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
