Dudleya rigidiflora

Dudleya rigidiflora, commonly known as the rigid-flowered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water and withstand arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
