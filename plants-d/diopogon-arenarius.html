Diopogon arenarius

Diopogon arenarius, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

Diopogon arenarius plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
