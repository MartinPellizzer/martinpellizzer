Dimorphotheca

Dimorphotheca, commonly known as the "two-form daisy," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with two distinct flower heads. The plants typically have hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Dimorphotheca species are known for their bright yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in summer.
