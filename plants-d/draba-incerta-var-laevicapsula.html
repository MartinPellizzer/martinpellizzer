Draba incerta var. laevicapsula

Draba incerta var. laevicapsula, commonly known as the smooth-capsuled whitlow grass, is a small, flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

The varietal name 'laevicapsula' refers to its smooth, capsule-like fruits, distinguishing it from other varieties of Draba.
