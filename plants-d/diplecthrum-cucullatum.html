Diplecthrum cucullatum

Diplecthrum cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Diplecthrum cucullatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
