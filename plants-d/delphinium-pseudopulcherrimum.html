Delphinium pseudopulcherrimum

Delphinium pseudopulcherrimum, commonly known as the Colorado larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, typical of the Delphinium genus.

This species is adapted to rocky and open habitats, often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
