Desmodium campestre

Desmodium campestre, commonly known as field velvet bean, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its growth habit and ecological roles make it an important species in various ecosystems.
