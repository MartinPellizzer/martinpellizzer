Dipladenia illustris subf. pubescens

Dipladenia illustris subf.

pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf ninebark, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a dense covering of fine hairs.

The flowers are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
