Disperis lanceolata

Disperis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf disperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a slight fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
