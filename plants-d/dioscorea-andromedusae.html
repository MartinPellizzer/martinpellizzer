Dioscorea andromedusae

Dioscorea andromedusae, commonly known as the red spiderling, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Due to its toxicity, it is not suitable for cultivation or consumption.
