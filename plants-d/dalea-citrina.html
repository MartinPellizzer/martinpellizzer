Dalea citrina

Dalea citrina, commonly known as yellow false indigo, is a perennial legume native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Dalea citrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
