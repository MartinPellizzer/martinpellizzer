Diasperus consanguineus

Diasperus consanguineus, commonly known as the African rock orchid, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in mountainous or coastal regions.

Diasperus consanguineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
