Dianthus anatolicus

Dianthus anatolicus, commonly known as the Anatolian pink, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, while its stems are upright and branching.

Dianthus anatolicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
