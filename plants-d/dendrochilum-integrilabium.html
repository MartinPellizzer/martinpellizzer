Dendrochilum integrilabium

Dendrochilum integrilabium, commonly known as the broad-lipped tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with broad, waxy petals and a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
