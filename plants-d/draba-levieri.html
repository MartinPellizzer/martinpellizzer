Draba levieri

Draba levieri, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial plant with basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Draba levieri is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
