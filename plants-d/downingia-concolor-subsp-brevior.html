Downingia concolor subsp. brevior

Downingia concolor subsp.

brevior, commonly known as the narrowleaf Downingia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
