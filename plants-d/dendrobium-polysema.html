Dendrobium polysema

Dendrobium polysema, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that often bear clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom during the summer months.

Dendrobium polysema is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
