Dipteracanthus cernuus

Dipteracanthus cernuus, commonly known as the drooping dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
