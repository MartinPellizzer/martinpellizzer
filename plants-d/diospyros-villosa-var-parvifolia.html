Diospyros villosa var. parvifolia

Diospyros villosa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its hairy young stems and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to reddish berries. Its fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
