Diospyros polyandra

Diospyros polyandra, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible drupes that resemble dates in appearance. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or processed into various products.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
