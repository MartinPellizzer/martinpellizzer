Delphinium bicornutum subsp. bicornutum

Delphinium bicornutum subsp.

bicornutum, commonly known as the two-horned larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is characterized by its distinctive two-horned seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
