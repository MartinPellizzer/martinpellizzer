Dioscorea madecassa

Dioscorea madecassa, commonly known as wild yam, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and later develops capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in compounds such as diosgenin, which has been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The botanical features of Dioscorea madecassa make it an important species in both traditional and modern pharmacology.
