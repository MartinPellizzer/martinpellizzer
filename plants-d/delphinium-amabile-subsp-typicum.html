Delphinium amabile subsp. typicum

Delphinium amabile subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the garden delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features include showy corollas with five petals and a spurred petal at the back, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.
