Dracophyllum rosmariniforme

Dracophyllum rosmariniforme, commonly known as the silverleaf dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the southern hemisphere, particularly found in Australia and New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. Its small, white or pale pink flowers bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Dracophyllum rosmariniforme is well adapted to dry, well-drained soils and can thrive in coastal or rocky environments.
