Dicypellium caryophyllatum

Dicypellium caryophyllatum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and woody structure. The plant is notable for its heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal areas.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered endangered in many parts of its natural habitat.
