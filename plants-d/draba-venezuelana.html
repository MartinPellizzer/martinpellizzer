Draba venezuelana

Draba venezuelana, commonly known as Venezuelan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. This plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Draba venezuelana is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant resilience and ecological niches.
