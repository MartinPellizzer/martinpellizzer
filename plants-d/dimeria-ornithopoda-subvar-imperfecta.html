Dimeria ornithopoda subvar. imperfecta

Dimeria ornithopoda subvar. imperfecta, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with dark green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
