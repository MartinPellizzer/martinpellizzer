Delphinium diversifolium subsp. harneyense

Delphinium diversifolium subsp.

harneyense, commonly known as Harney's larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and vary in shape, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
