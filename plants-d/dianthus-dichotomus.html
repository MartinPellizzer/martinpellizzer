Dianthus dichotomus

Dianthus dichotomus, commonly known as the forked pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-branched stems and clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Dianthus dichotomus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
