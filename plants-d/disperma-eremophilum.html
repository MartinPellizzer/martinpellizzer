Disperma eremophilum

Disperma eremophilum, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Disperma eremophilum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in rocky soils and sandy plains.
