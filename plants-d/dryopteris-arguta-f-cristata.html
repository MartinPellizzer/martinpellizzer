Dryopteris arguta f. cristata

Dryopteris arguta f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate wood fern, is a cultivar of the lady fern that exhibits a unique, flattened, or "cristate" growth habit.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often curled and twisted, forming a compact, ornamental shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, making it well-suited for woodland gardens or shaded rockeries. Its foliage is usually a deep green, with a subtle sheen that enhances its visual appeal.

This fern is prized for its distinctive appearance and is often used in horticultural displays for its aesthetic value.
