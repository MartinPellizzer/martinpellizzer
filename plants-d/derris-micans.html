Derris micans

Derris micans, commonly known as the creeping derris, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its roots contain the alkaloid rotenone, which has been traditionally used as a natural insecticide.

Derris micans is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
