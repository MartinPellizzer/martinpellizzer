Dactylorhiza persica

Dactylorhiza persica, commonly known as the Persian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces a single stem with several lance-shaped leaves and bears flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Dactylorhiza persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
