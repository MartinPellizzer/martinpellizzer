Diuris althoferi

Diuris althoferi, commonly known as the Althofer's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with long, thin sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats, often in areas with low rainfall. It produces small, greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months.

Diuris althoferi is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
