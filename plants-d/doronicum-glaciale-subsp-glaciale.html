Doronicum glaciale subsp. glaciale

Doronicum glaciale subsp.

glaciale, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is well adapted to cold climates and has a compact growth habit to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
