Dioclea purpurea

Dioclea purpurea, commonly known as the purple bignay, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a legume pod containing edible seeds that are often used in local cuisines.

Dioclea purpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting various wildlife species.
