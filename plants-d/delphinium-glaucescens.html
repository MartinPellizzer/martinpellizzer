Delphinium glaucescens

Delphinium glaucescens, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
