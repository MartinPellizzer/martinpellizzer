Diospyros ferrea var. samoensis

Diospyros ferrea var. samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the islands of Samoa and nearby Pacific regions.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, sweet fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, tropical climate.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is often protected in its native habitats.
