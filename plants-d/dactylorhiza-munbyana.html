Dactylorhiza munbyana

Dactylorhiza munbyana, commonly known as the Munby's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with a short rhizome and typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant produces a single or few tall, erect stems bearing several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip and are arranged in a raceme.

Dactylorhiza munbyana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
