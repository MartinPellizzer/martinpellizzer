Douglasdeweya

Douglasdeweya, commonly known as the western red cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in crossed pairs. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it a significant resource in indigenous and commercial contexts.

The plant plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
