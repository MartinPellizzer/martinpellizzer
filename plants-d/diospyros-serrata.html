Diospyros serrata

Diospyros serrata, commonly known as the rough-shanked persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in tropical and subtropical regions.
