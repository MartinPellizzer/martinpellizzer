Dalbergaria guianensis

Dalbergaria guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese dalbergia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It also bears pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
