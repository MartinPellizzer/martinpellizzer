Dichanthelium helleri

Dichanthelium helleri, commonly known as Heller's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and floodplain forests. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed or saturated soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
