Diasperus avicularis

Diasperus avicularis, commonly known as the African sand dollar, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, spherical fruit that resembles a sand dollar. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers, which are typically small and arranged in a flat, disk-like structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The fruit of Diasperus avicularis is adapted for dispersal by animals, as it is often carried away by burrowing creatures.
