Dalbergia dubia

Dalbergia dubia, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and carving, and for its role in traditional medicine.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
