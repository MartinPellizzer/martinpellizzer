Dianthus seravschanicus

Dianthus seravschanicus, commonly known as the Karakoram pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, dry climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Dianthus seravschanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
