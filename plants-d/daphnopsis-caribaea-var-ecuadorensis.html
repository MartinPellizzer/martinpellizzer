Daphnopsis caribaea var. ecuadorensis

Daphnopsis caribaea var. ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian daphnopsis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
