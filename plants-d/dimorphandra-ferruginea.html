Dimorphandra ferruginea

Dimorphandra ferruginea, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree produces pods that contain seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps it thrive in various soil types. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
