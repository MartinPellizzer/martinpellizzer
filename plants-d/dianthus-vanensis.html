Dianthus vanensis

Dianthus vanensis, commonly known as the Vanessa's carnation, is a species of flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. The plant features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Dianthus vanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
