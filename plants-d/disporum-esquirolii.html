Disporum esquirolii

Disporum esquirolii, commonly known as the Oregon grape or red-flower disporum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a dark blue to black berry that ripens in late summer.

Disporum esquirolii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside areas.
