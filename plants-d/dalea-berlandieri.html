Dalea berlandieri

Dalea berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.

Its ecological significance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife species.
