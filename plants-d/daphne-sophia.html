Daphne sophia

Daphne sophia, commonly known as the Persian daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a slight pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it has a compact growth habit.

Daphne sophia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
