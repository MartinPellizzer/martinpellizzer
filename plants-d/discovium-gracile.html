Discovium gracile

Discovium gracile, commonly known as the slender discovium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, pale green leaves with a waxy surface, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces tiny, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring, releasing a subtle, sweet fragrance. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to absorb moisture efficiently from the forest floor.

Due to habitat loss, Discovium gracile is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
