Dioscorea cylindrostachya

Dioscorea cylindrostachya, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a climbing herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into cylindrical, spiny fruits. Its tubers are starchy and used as a food source in some regions, similar to yams.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
