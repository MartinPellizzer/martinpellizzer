Dactylis glomerata nothovar. congesta

Dactylis glomerata nothovar. congesta, commonly known as the dense tufted grass, is a hybrid variant of the common sheep's fescue.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, dark green foliage. This plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and pastures. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
