Dupatya flaviceps

Dupatya flaviceps, commonly known as the yellow-headed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dupatya flaviceps is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
