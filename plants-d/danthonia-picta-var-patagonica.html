Danthonia picta var. patagonica

Danthonia picta var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
