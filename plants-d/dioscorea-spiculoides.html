Dioscorea spiculoides

Dioscorea spiculoides, commonly known as the spiky yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are typically tuberous, starchy, and have a rough, irregular surface.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used as a food source in some cultures.
