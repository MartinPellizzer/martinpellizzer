Dianthus strictus subsp. velutinus

Dianthus strictus subsp.

velutinus, commonly known as the velvet pinks, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, upright growth with dense clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blossoms and ability to attract pollinators.
