Diasperus caudatus

Diasperus caudatus, commonly known as the long-tailed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and slender, elongated stems. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a small, hairy achene that is dispersed by the wind.

Diasperus caudatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
