Dendrochilum auriculare

Dendrochilum auriculare, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, auriculate (ear-shaped) lips. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Dendrochilum auriculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
