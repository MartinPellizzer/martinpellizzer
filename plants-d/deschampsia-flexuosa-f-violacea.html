Deschampsia flexuosa f. violacea

Deschampsia flexuosa f. violacea, commonly known as violet hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, violet-colored inflorescences that distinguish it from other members of the species. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and the plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is native to North America and is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization.
