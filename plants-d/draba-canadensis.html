Draba canadensis

Draba canadensis, commonly known as Canadian rockcress, is a small flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces clusters of small, white or yellow flowers in early spring.

Draba canadensis is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and is often found in mountainous regions across Canada and the northern United States.
