Dioscorea mengtzeana

Dioscorea mengtzeana, commonly known as Mengtze's yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing herbaceous perennial that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential pharmacological properties.
