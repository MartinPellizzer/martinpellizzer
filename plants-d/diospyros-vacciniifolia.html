Diospyros vacciniifolia

Diospyros vacciniifolia, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces edible berries that are yellow-orange when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
