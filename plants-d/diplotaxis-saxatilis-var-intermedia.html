Diplotaxis saxatilis var. intermedia

Diplotaxis saxatilis var. intermedia, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
