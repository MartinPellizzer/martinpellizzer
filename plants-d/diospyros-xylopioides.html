Diospyros xylopioides

Diospyros xylopioides, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when mature. Its wood is dense and hard, making it valuable for crafting and traditional uses.

The plant is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
