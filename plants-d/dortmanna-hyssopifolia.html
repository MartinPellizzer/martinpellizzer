Dortmanna hyssopifolia

Dortmanna hyssopifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Dortmanna hyssopifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
