Dracaena esculenta

Dracaena esculenta, commonly known as the "Cabbage Tree" or "Drumstick Plant," is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem with a swollen base and long, narrow, dark green leaves that are often used as ornamental foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Dracaena esculenta is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in landscapes.
