Delphinium schmalhausenii subsp. freynii

Delphinium schmalhausenii subsp.

freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are showy and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the main species, particularly in flower size and leaf structure.
