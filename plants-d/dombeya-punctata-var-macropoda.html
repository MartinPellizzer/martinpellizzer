Dombeya punctata var. macropoda

Dombeya punctata var. macropoda, commonly known as the large-flowered dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of ovate to elliptic leaves with a rough texture and serrated edges.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
