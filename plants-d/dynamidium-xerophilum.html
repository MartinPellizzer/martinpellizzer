Dynamidium xerophilum

Dynamidium xerophilum, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, enabling it to absorb moisture quickly from the soil.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to climate change, Dynamidium xerophilum is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
