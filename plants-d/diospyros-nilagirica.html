Diospyros nilagirica

Diospyros nilagirica, commonly known as the Nilagiri date palm, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and long, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy drupes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
