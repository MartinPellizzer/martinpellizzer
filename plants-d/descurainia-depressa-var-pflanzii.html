Descurainia depressa var. pflanzii

Descurainia depressa var. pflanzii, commonly known as the flatleaf tansy, is a low-growing annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are yellow with four petals.

It is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
