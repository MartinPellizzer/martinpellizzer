Draco fontanesiana

Draco fontanesiana, commonly known as the dragon tree of Madagascar, is a unique plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and striking, spiky leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange-red berries, which are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain animals. Draco fontanesiana is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in Madagascar's flora.
