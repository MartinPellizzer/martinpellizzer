Didymocarpus pectinatus

Didymocarpus pectinatus, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in various soil types.

Didymocarpus pectinatus also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
