Dichaeopsis glauca

Dichaeopsis glauca, commonly known as the blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue or purple daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a low, spreading habit with silvery-gray foliage. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Dichaeopsis glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
