Deschampsia cespitosa var. macrantha

Deschampsia cespitosa var. macrantha, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces tall, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers, which are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
