Dryandra nana

Dryandra nana, commonly known as the dwarf dryandra, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces striking, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting native pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
