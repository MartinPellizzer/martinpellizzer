Digitalis purpurea var. vulgaris

Digitalis purpurea var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common foxglove, is a biennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its cardiac glycosides, which have medicinal uses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
