Desmanthus nervosus

Desmanthus nervosus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and agricultural studies.
