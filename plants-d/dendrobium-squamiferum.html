Dendrobium squamiferum

Dendrobium squamiferum, commonly known as the squamiferum orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like pseudobulbs and is often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium squamiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular among orchid enthusiasts.
