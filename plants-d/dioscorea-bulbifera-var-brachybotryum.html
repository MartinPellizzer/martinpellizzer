Dioscorea bulbifera var. brachybotryum

Dioscorea bulbifera var. brachybotryum, commonly known as the short-stemmed arrowhead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves and produces edible tubers that are rich in starch. The plant is notable for its unique aerial tubers, which form at the nodes along its stems and can be used as a food source. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
