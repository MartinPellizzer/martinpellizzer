Daemonorops guruba var. mastersianus

Daemonorops guruba var. mastersianus, commonly known as the red-veined basket palm, is a variant of the genus Daemonorops native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a palm tree characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves with distinctive red veins. The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, and it is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.

The species is relatively slow-growing, making it a long-term investment for gardeners and landscapers.
