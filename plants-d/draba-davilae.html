Draba davilae

Draba davilae, commonly known as the Davile's rockcress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often in well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Draba davilae is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and a compact, flowering stem.
