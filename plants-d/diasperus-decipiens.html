Diasperus decipiens

Diasperus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often mistaken for other grass species, contributing to its common name. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Diasperus decipiens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
