Delphinium dinaricum

Delphinium dinaricum, commonly known as the Dinaric larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and it typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils. Delphinium dinaricum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
