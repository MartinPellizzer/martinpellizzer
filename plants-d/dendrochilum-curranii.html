Dendrochilum curranii

Dendrochilum curranii, commonly known as the Curran's tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with prominent purple or reddish markings, and they are arranged in a raceme.

Dendrochilum curranii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
