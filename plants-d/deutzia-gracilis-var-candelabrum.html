Deutzia gracilis var. candelabrum

Deutzia gracilis var. candelabrum, commonly known as the candlebush, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often resemble a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long, slender peduncles. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

Deutzia gracilis var. candelabrum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
