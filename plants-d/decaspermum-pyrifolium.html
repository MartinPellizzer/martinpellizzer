Decaspermum pyrifolium

Decaspermum pyrifolium, commonly known as the fireleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a fire leaf, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Decaspermum pyrifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
