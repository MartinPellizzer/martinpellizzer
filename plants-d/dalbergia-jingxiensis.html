Dalbergia jingxiensis

Dalbergia jingxiensis, commonly known as the Xingyi rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to China, particularly found in the Guangxi and Guizhou provinces.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
