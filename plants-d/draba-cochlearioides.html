Draba cochlearioides

Draba cochlearioides, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Draba cochlearioides is an important component of alpine ecosystems and is noted for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
