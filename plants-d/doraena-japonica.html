Doraena japonica

Doraena japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Doraena japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
