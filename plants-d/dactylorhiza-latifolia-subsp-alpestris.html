Dactylorhiza latifolia subsp. alpestris

Dactylorhiza latifolia subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine broad-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky areas.

The plant has a single, erect stem with several oval, leathery leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

It is a perennial species that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, typical of many orchid species.
