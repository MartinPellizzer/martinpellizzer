Dolichos lelyi

Dolichos lelyi, commonly known as the Lely's climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated for both food and forage purposes.
