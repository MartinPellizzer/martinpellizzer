Dryopteris alata f. typica

Dryopteris alata f. typica, commonly known as the lady fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit, with dark brown, wiry rhizomes that spread horizontally. The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, arching shape and a silvery-white underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
