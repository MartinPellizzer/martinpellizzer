Dischidia polyphylla

Dischidia polyphylla, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its ability to form dense mats on trees and rocks. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Dischidia polyphylla is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and is often used as a ground cover in horticulture.
