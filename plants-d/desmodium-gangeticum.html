Desmodium gangeticum

Desmodium gangeticum, commonly known as hairy senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a hairy texture, and its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Desmodium gangeticum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
