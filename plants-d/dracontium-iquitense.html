Dracontium iquitense

Dracontium iquitense, commonly known as the Amazonian giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, making it a striking addition to its natural habitat. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used traditionally by indigenous communities for their nutritional value.

However, due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
