Dendrobium ypsilon

Dendrobium ypsilon, commonly known as the epsilon orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with stems that grow horizontally and produce new shoots from the base. Its flowers are typically small and fragrant, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink and yellow.

Dendrobium ypsilon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
