Dioscorea tenuiflora

Dioscorea tenuiflora, commonly known as Chinese yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.

The botanical features of D. tenuiflora make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
