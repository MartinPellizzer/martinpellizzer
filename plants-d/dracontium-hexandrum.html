Dracontium hexandrum

Dracontium hexandrum, commonly known as the six-stamen arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and its flowers are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
