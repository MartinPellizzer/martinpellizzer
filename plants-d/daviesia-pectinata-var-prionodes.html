Daviesia pectinata var. prionodes

Daviesia pectinata var. prionodes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daviesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
