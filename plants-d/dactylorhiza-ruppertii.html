Dactylorhiza ruppertii

Dactylorhiza ruppertii, commonly known as the Ruppert's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic habitats such as bogs and heathlands. It has a pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in wet environments.

Dactylorhiza ruppertii is a perennial species that blooms in late summer to early autumn.
