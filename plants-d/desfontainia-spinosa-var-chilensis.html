Desfontainia spinosa var. chilensis

Desfontainia spinosa var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean spinous desfontainia, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
