Drejera parviflora

Drejera parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered drejera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface, while its stems are slender and somewhat woody.

Drejera parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
