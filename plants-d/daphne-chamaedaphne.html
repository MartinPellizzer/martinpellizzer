Daphne chamaedaphne

Daphne chamaedaphne, commonly known as the bog daphne, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is typically found in bogs, fens, and other moist environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, often remaining evergreen throughout the year.

Daphne chamaedaphne is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
