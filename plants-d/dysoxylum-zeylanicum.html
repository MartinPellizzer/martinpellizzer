Dysoxylum zeylanicum

Dysoxylum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological and economic significance.
