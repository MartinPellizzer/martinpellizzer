Drimys brasiliensis

Drimys brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian evergreen tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 25 meters, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing dense foliage throughout the year. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Drimys brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
