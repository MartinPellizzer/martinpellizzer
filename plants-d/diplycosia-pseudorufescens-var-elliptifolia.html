Diplycosia pseudorufescens var. elliptifolia

Diplycosia pseudorufescens var. elliptifolia, commonly known as the elliptic-leaved diplycosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
