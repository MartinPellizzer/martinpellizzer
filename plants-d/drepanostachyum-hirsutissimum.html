Drepanostachyum hirsutissimum

Drepanostachyum hirsutissimum, commonly known as the hairy lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense panicles. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

It is notable for its ornamental value and use in ecological restoration projects.
