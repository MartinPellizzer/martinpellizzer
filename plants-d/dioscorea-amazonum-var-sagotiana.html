Dioscorea amazonum var. sagotiana

Dioscorea amazonum var. sagotiana, commonly known as the Amazon yam, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for both its medicinal properties and its role in local ecosystems.
