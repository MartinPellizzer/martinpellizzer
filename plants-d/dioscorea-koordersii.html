Dioscorea koordersii

Dioscorea koordersii, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its tubers are rich in starch and have been used for centuries in various cultural and medicinal practices.
