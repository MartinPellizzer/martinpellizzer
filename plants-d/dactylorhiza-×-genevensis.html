Dactylorhiza × genevensis

Dactylorhiza × genevensis, commonly known as the Geneva orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heaths. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with variously colored flowers. Its botanical classification places it within the Orchidaceae family, and it is characterized by its morphological traits that reflect its hybrid origin.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
