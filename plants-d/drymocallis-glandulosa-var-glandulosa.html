Drymocallis glandulosa var. glandulosa

Drymocallis glandulosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish to golden flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and aid in water retention. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
