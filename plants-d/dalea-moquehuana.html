Dalea moquehuana

Dalea moquehuana, commonly known as the Quechua milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

Dalea moquehuana plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.
