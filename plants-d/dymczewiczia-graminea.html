Dymczewiczia graminea

Dymczewiczia graminea, commonly known as the slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or arid soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it endure drought conditions.

Dymczewiczia graminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in dry regions.
