Dichrocephala auriculata

Dichrocephala auriculata, commonly known as the African violet or blue violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting white or yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green. It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.

Dichrocephala auriculata prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
