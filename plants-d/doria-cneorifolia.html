Doria cneorifolia

Doria cneorifolia, commonly known as the European burnet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
