Drosera parvula

Drosera parvula, commonly known as the common sundew, is a small carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its small, delicate flowers are usually pink or reddish in color and appear on tall, slender stems.

Drosera parvula is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
