Dalea calocalyx

Dalea calocalyx, commonly known as the showy false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
