Deprea grandiflora

Deprea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered deprea, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Deprea grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
