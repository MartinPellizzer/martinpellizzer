Diaphananthe laticalcar

Diaphananthe laticalcar, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
