Disperis purpurata var. parviflora

Disperis purpurata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is appreciated by horticulturists and botanical enthusiasts.
