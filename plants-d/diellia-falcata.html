Diellia falcata

Diellia falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers surrounded by pale green ray florets. The plant has a low-growing habit with narrow, sickle-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Diellia falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
