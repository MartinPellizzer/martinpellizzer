Danaea trinitatensis

Danaea trinitatensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian waxplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, waxy, orange-red bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which are often hidden beneath the colorful bracts. Danaea trinitatensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
