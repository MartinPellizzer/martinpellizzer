Dinochloa pubiramea

Dinochloa pubiramea, commonly known as the hairy rameal grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, grassy areas and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Dinochloa pubiramea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
