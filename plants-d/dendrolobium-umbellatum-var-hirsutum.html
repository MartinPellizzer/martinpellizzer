Dendrolobium umbellatum var. hirsutum

Dendrolobium umbellatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy sicklepod, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
