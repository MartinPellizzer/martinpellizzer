Dieteria canescens var. leucanthemifolia

Dieteria canescens var. leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered dieteria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
