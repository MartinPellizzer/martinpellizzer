Drymaria glandulosa var. galeottiana

Drymaria glandulosa var. galeottiana, commonly known as the glandular drymaria, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are densely covered with glandular hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and delicate flowers.
