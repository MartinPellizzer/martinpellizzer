Derris eriocarpa

Derris eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy derris, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, also known as the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which is used in natural pesticides. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shady environments.

Derris eriocarpa is valued both for its ecological role and its historical use in traditional medicine.
