Dendrobium harveyanum

Dendrobium harveyanum, commonly known as the Harvey's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with tall, branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with white and green markings, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dendrobium harveyanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
