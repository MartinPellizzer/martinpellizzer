Dianthus diversifolius

Dianthus diversifolius, commonly known as the variable carnation, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, varying in shape and size, which gives it the species name "diversifolius.".

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
