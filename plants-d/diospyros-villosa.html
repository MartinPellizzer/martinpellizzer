Diospyros villosa

Diospyros villosa, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces orange to reddish fruits that are typically edible when fully mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Diospyros villosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
