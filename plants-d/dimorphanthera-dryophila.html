Dimorphanthera dryophila

Dimorphanthera dryophila, commonly known as the dry forest orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricately shaped flowers that typically bloom in the dry season. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and develops its foliage and flowers from a basal rosette of narrow leaves. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying adaptation to arid environments.
