Daphne autaretica

Daphne autaretica, commonly known as the pink daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant is noted for its clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling.
