Didymocarpus stenanthos var. pilosellus

Didymocarpus stenanthos var. pilosellus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed tamarind, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are covered with fine, hairy structures. The fruits are typically brown when mature and contain several seeds.

This variant is distinguished by its dense pubescence, which gives it a unique appearance compared to other members of the species.
