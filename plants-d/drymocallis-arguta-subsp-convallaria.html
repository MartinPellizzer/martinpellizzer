Drymocallis arguta subsp. convallaria

Drymocallis arguta subsp.

convallaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers in the late summer and early autumn. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
