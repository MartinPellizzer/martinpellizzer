Delphinium peregrinum var. peloponnesiacum

Delphinium peregrinum var. peloponnesiacum, commonly known as the Peloponnesian larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided, dark green leaves that are hairy and serrated. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic appearance.
