Dendrobium alboviride

Dendrobium alboviride, commonly known as the white-green dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, white to pale green flowers with yellow lips. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium alboviride is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
