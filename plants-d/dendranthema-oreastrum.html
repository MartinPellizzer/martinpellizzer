Dendranthema oreastrum

Dendranthema oreastrum, commonly known as the golden oxeye, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounded by a ring of tubular disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Dendranthema oreastrum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
