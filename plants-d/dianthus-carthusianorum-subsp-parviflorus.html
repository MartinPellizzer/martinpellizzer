Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. parviflorus

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the Carthusian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink to red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

The subspecies parviflorus is distinguished by its smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical Carthusian pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
