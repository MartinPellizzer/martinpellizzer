Dischidia reniformis

Dischidia reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped fig or the creeping fig, is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped or kidney-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its unique leaf shape and ability to climb or trail make it popular as a houseplant.

Dischidia reniformis is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
