Dichapetalum echinulatum

Dichapetalum echinulatum, commonly known as the spiny holly or rock holly, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, spiny protrusions. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Dichapetalum echinulatum is often found growing in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
