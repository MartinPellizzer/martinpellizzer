Dichanthelium huachucae var. silvicola

Dichanthelium huachucae var. silvicola, commonly known as the Chihuahuan needlegrass, is a grass species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to dry, hot environments.

This variety is an important component of the grassland and shrubland ecosystems in its native range.
