Diospyros xylopiifolia

Diospyros xylopiifolia, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are highly nutritious. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for furniture and construction.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
