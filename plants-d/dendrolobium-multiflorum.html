Dendrolobium multiflorum

Dendrolobium multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Dendrolobium multiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
