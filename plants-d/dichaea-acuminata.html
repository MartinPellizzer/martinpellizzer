Dichaea acuminata

Dichaea acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater, which it uses for hydration and as a microhabitat for small organisms. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of red or orange, adding visual interest to the plant.

Dichaea acuminata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
