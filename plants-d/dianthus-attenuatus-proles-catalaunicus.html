Dianthus attenuatus proles catalaunicus

Dianthus attenuatus proles catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalan thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant produces distinctive, bell-shaped blooms that are often tinged with white at the base. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
