Dodonaea nutans

Dodonaea nutans, commonly known as the weeping dodonaea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The plant has slender, often pendulous branches that give it a weeping appearance. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually elliptical in shape.

Dodonaea nutans is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
