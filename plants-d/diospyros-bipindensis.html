Diospyros bipindensis

Diospyros bipindensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a globose, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and edible qualities.
