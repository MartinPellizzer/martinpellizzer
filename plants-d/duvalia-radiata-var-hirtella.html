Duvalia radiata var. hirtella

Duvalia radiata var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy duvalia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
