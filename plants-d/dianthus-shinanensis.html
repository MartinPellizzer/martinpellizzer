Dianthus shinanensis

Dianthus shinanensis, commonly known as the Shikoku carnation, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers with a distinctive white center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus shinanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
