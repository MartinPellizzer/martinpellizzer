Dioscorea ulinei

Dioscorea ulinei, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been used as a food source in various regions.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
