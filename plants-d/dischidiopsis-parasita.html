Dischidiopsis parasita

Dischidiopsis parasita, commonly known as the parasitic dischidium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from host plants while also performing photosynthesis. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces delicate, white or pink flowers. It typically grows on the stems or branches of other plants, such as shrubs or trees.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique parasitic relationship and its role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
