Draba × perdubia

Draba × perdubia, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Draba nemorosa and Draba incana.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with basal leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils in alpine and arctic environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to survive in harsh, cold climates.

Draba × perdubia is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.
