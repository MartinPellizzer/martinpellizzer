Dalbergia multiflora

Dalbergia multiflora, commonly known as the white sandwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
