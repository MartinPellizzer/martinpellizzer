Draba oligosperma var. pilosa

Draba oligosperma var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
