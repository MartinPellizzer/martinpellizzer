Diplazium ordinatum

Diplazium ordinatum, commonly known as the common wood fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its upright, wiry stems and compound leaves. The fronds are pinnately divided, with a distinctive, almost zigzag pattern along the veins. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
