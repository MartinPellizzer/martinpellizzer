Diospyros lobata

Diospyros lobata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits.

The fruits are typically eaten fresh or used in culinary applications, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
