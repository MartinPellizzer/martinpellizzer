Dianthus behriorum

Dianthus behriorum, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Dianthus behriorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
