Disa ferruginea

Disa ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vivid red and orange flowers with a distinctive rusty-colored lip. The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands or forest margins. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a feature common in many orchids, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Disa ferruginea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native environment.
