Dittrichia viscosa

Dittrichia viscosa, commonly known as sticky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in floral arrangements.
