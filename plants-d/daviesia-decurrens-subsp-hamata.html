Daviesia decurrens subsp. hamata

Daviesia decurrens subsp.

hamata, commonly known as the hairy Daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. It produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and flowering habit.
