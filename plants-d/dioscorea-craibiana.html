Dioscorea craibiana

Dioscorea craibiana, commonly known as the Craib's yam, is a tuberous vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, containing compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant's growth is often associated with specific soil conditions and requires a humid, warm climate to thrive.
