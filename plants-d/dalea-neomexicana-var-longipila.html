Dalea neomexicana var. longipila

Dalea neomexicana var. longipila, commonly known as the longpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert grasslands and along canyon walls.

The species is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in arid ecosystems.
