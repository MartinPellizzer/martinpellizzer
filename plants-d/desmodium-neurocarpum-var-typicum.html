Desmodium neurocarpum var. typicum

Desmodium neurocarpum var. typicum, commonly known as purple burr vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive purple or pink flowers.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. The plant is native to regions of the southeastern United States and thrives in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. It has a deep root system that helps improve soil structure and fix atmospheric nitrogen, benefiting surrounding plants.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable forage species and a challenging weed to control.
