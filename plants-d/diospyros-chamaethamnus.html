Diospyros chamaethamnus

Diospyros chamaethamnus, commonly known as the dwarf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India, China, and the Philippines. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture, while its bark is generally smooth and light-colored.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
