Deschampsia atropurpurea var. paramushirensis

Deschampsia atropurpurea var. paramushirensis, commonly known as the purple false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces pinkish to purple inflorescences that are often tinged with red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that helps it conserve water.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
