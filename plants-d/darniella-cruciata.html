Darniella cruciata

Darniella cruciata, commonly known as the crucifix lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble a cross, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Darniella cruciata is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
