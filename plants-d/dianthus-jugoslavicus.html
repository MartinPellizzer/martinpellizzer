Dianthus jugoslavicus

Dianthus jugoslavicus, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus jugoslavicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
