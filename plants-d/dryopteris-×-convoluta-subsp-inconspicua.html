Dryopteris × convoluta subsp. inconspicua

Dryopteris × convoluta subsp.

inconspicua, commonly known as the slender wood fern, is a hybrid fern species characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with dark brown, hairy stems. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a subtle, inconspicuous appearance that gives it its subspecies name.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
