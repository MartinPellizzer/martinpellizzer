Desmodium deplanchei var. ovalifolium

Desmodium deplanchei var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf desmodium, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with oval-shaped leaflets, which are typically arranged in a horizontal plane. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a useful plant for erosion control and as a forage species.
