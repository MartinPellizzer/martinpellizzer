Deschampsia atropurpurea f. multiflora

Deschampsia atropurpurea f. multiflora, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its striking purple-tinged inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like flower clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect life.
