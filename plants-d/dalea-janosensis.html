Dalea janosensis

Dalea janosensis, commonly known as Janos' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of concern in some regions.
