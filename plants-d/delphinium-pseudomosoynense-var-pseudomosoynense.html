Delphinium pseudomosoynense var. pseudomosoynense

Delphinium pseudomosoynense var. pseudomosoynense, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with lanceolate to ovate leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
