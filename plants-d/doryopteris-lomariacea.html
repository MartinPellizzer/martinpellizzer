Doryopteris lomariacea

Doryopteris lomariacea, commonly known as the giant holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in structures called sori. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The botanical features of Doryopteris lomariacea make it a striking and distinctive member of the fern family.
