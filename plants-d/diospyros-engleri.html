Diospyros engleri

Diospyros engleri, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits. The fruits are typically edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It is valued for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
