Davilla latifolia

Davilla latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf davisia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in whorls of three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Davilla latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
