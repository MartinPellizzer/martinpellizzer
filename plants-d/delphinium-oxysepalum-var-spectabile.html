Delphinium oxysepalum var. spectabile

Delphinium oxysepalum var. spectabile, commonly known as the showy larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its attractive blooms and relatively easy care, it is often cultivated for its visual impact in horticultural displays.
