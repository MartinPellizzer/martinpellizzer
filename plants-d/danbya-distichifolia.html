Danbya distichifolia

Danbya distichifolia, commonly known as the two-ranked fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate fronds that are arranged in two ranks, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are relatively short and often covered with a layer of brownish scales.

Danbya distichifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
