Distimake repens

Distimake repens, commonly known as the wandering beggar or false spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with slender, hairy stems that root at the nodes. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a waxy surface and a distinct central vein. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
