Dioclea holtiana

Dioclea holtiana, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically tubular and clustered in inflorescences. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often consumed by various animals, contributing to its role in the ecosystem. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Dioclea holtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical regions.
