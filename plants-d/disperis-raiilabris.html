Disperis raiilabris

Disperis raiilabris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinct three-lobed lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.

Disperis raiilabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
