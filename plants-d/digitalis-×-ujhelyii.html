Digitalis × ujhelyii

Digitalis × ujhelyii, commonly known as the hybrid foxglove, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in medicine, it is both appreciated and carefully managed in horticulture.
