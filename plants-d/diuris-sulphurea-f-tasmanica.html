Diuris sulphurea f. tasmanica

Diuris sulphurea f. tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Tasmania.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with a single, erect stem that bears several broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open habitats such as heathlands and forests.

This variant is notable for its vibrant coloration and is a popular subject among orchid enthusiasts.
