Diospyros membranacea

Diospyros membranacea, commonly known as the paper fig or membranous persimmon, is a small deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
