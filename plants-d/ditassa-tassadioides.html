Ditassa tassadioides

Ditassa tassadioides, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
