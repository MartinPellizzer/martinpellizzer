Diospyros asterocalyx

Diospyros asterocalyx, commonly known as the star-flowered persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered, and have five petals, giving it the "star-flowered" appearance.

The fruit is a small, egg-shaped berry that ripens to a yellowish-orange color.
