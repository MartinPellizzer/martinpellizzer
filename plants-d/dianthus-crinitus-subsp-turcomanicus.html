Dianthus crinitus subsp. turcomanicus

Dianthus crinitus subsp.

turcomanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of bright pink or red flowers.

The plant produces small, delicate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
