Dendrophthora clavata

Dendrophthora clavata, commonly known as the clustered bracket fungus, is a species of bracket fungus that belongs to the family Polystictaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered growth pattern on dead or decaying wood, typically found on coniferous trees. The fruiting bodies are usually small, tough, and have a brown to reddish-brown cap with a stippled surface. This fungus plays an important role in the decomposition of wood in forest ecosystems.

It is primarily a saprophytic organism, breaking down organic matter and contributing to nutrient cycling.
