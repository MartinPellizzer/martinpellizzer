Disa sagittalis var. triloba

Disa sagittalis var. triloba, commonly known as the Three-lobed Disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive three-lobed appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
