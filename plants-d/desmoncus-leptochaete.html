Desmoncus leptochaete

Desmoncus leptochaete, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, annual herb with a sprawling growth habit, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Desmoncus leptochaete is well-suited to xerophytic conditions and is often used in native plant landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
