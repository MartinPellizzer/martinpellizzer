Dasylepis assinensis

Dasylepis assinensis, commonly known as the Assinensis bottlebrush, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of dense, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
