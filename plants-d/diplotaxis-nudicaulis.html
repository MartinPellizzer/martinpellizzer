Diplotaxis nudicaulis

Diplotaxis nudicaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pale yellow flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed or barren areas.

Diplotaxis nudicaulis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
