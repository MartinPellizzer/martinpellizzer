Draba magellanica var. cinerea

Draba magellanica var. cinerea, commonly known as the silver draba, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage due to dense pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It typically produces small white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

This species is adapted to rocky and coastal habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
