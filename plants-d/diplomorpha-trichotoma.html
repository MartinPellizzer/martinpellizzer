Diplomorpha trichotoma

Diplomorpha trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy whipstick, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Diplomorpha trichotoma is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
