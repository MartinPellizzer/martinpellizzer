Dianthus pungens subsp. tarraconensis

Dianthus pungens subsp.

tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconense campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of pink to red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in rocky or limestone soils. It blooms in early summer and is adapted to sunny, well-drained environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
