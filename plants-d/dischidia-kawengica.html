Dischidia kawengica

Dischidia kawengica, commonly known as the Kawengica ivy, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its aerial roots that allow it to cling to surfaces. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are usually covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange or red berries.

Dischidia kawengica is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
