Dalbergaria pectinata

Dalbergaria pectinata, commonly known as the Natal plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its flowers are typically purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dalbergaria pectinata is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
