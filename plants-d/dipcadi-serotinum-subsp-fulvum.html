Dipcadi serotinum subsp. fulvum

Dipcadi serotinum subsp.

fulvum, commonly known as the yellow bulbous orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, bulb-like structures that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers with a distinctive, sweet scent. It typically blooms during the warmer months, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
