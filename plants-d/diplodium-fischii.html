Diplodium fischii

Diplodium fischii, commonly known as the mountain pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Diplodium fischii is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to dry, arid environments.
