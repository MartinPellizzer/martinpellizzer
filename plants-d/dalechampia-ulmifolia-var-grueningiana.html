Dalechampia ulmifolia var. grueningiana

Dalechampia ulmifolia var. grueningiana, commonly known as the Gruening's elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
