Dactylorhiza pindica

Dactylorhiza pindica, commonly known as the Greek orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces a single raceme of pale to deep pink flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Dactylorhiza pindica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
