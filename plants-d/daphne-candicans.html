Daphne candicans

Daphne candicans, commonly known as the silver bush daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that appear in late winter or early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Daphne candicans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its early blooming habit.
