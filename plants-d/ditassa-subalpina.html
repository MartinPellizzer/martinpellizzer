Ditassa subalpina

Ditassa subalpina, commonly known as the alpine Ditassa, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of delicate, yellowish-green flowers.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
