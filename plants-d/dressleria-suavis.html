Dressleria suavis

Dressleria suavis, commonly known as the sweet dressler, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, white to pale pink petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
