Drakaina marginata

Drakaina marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in the summer months.

Drakaina marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
