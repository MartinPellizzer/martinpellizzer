Dypsis trapezoidea

Dypsis trapezoidea, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and feathery, arching fronds. The plant typically grows as a clumping perennial, forming a compact, upright shape. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, often arranged in a fan-like pattern at the top of the stem.

Dypsis trapezoidea is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and moderate watering requirements.
