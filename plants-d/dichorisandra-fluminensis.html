Dichorisandra fluminensis

Dichorisandra fluminensis, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Dichorisandra fluminensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
