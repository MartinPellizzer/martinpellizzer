Dalbergia ovata var. ovata

Dalbergia ovata var. ovata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The tree produces hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its density and rich coloration. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
