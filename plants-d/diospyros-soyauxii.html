Diospyros soyauxii

Diospyros soyauxii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a bitter taste due to the presence of tannins.

Diospyros soyauxii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local medicine.
