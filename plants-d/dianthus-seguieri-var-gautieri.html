Dianthus seguieri var. gautieri

Dianthus seguieri var. gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of bright pink to red flowers with a distinctive, frilled appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
