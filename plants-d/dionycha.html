Dionycha

Dionycha, commonly known as the white daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that consist of a central disc surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Dionycha species are often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
