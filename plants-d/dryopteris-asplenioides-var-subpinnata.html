Dryopteris asplenioides var. subpinnata

Dryopteris asplenioides var. subpinnata, commonly known as the narrow-crested maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to regions in Asia, including China and Japan. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually arching and have a subpinnate structure, with pinnae that are narrow and closely spaced.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
