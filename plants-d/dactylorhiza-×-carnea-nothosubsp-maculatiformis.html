Dactylorhiza × carnea nothosubsp. maculatiformis

Dactylorhiza × carnea nothosubsp.

maculatiformis, commonly known as the spotted pink orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of pink to purple flowers with distinctive spots or markings.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a rhizomatous root system.

This nothosubspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
