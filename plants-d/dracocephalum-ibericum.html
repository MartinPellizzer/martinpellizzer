Dracocephalum ibericum

Dracocephalum ibericum, commonly known as Iberian dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Dracocephalum ibericum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
