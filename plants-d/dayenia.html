Dayenia

Dayenia, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has smooth, green, often hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in woodland or moist habitats.

Dayenia is also known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
