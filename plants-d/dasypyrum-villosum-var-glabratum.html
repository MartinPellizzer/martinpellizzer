Dasypyrum villosum var. glabratum

Dasypyrum villosum var. glabratum, commonly known as the glabrous wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and rapid establishment.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control and wildlife habitat.
