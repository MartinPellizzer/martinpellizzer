Drummondita hassellii

Drummondita hassellii, commonly known as the Hassell's drummondia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water in its dry environment. Drummondita hassellii is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its unique floral structure and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
