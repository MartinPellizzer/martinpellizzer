Dioscorea hebantha

Dioscorea hebantha, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by edible tuberous roots that are used in traditional cuisine. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized for food and medicinal purposes by local communities.

Dioscorea hebantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
