Duranta benthamii

Duranta benthamii, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Duranta benthamii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
