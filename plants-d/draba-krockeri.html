Draba krockeri

Draba krockeri, commonly known as the Krocker's whitlow grass, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring. It is characterized by its compact growth form and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Draba krockeri is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar morphological traits.
