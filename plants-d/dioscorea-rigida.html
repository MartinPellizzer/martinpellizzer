Dioscorea rigida

Dioscorea rigida, commonly known as the wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for various purposes. The plant has compound leaves with long petioles and small, opposite leaflets. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers of Dioscorea rigida are rich in starch and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.
