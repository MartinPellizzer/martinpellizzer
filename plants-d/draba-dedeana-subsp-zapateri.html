Draba dedeana subsp. zapateri

Draba dedeana subsp.

zapateri, commonly known as the Zapater's whitlow grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its small, basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

Native to the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is notable for its ecological adaptation to harsh, high-altitude environments.
