Dioscorea pentaphylla

Dioscorea pentaphylla, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, tuberous roots that are the primary edible part. These tubers are rich in starch and have been used as a food source by indigenous communities.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is utilized in traditional herbal remedies.
