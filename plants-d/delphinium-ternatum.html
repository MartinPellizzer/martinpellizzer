Delphinium ternatum

Delphinium ternatum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Delphinium ternatum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and toxic properties.
