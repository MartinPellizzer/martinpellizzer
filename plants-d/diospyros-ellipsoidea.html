Diospyros ellipsoidea

Diospyros ellipsoidea, commonly known as the oval-leaf persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces edible, orange-red fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
