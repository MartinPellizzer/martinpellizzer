Darwinia collina

Darwinia collina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Darwinia collina typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
