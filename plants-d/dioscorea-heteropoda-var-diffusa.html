Dioscorea heteropoda var. diffusa

Dioscorea heteropoda var. diffusa, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its tubers are often used as a starchy food source and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested or grassland areas.
