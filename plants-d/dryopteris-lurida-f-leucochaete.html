Dryopteris lurida f. leucochaete

Dryopteris lurida f. leucochaete, commonly known as the white-haired wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, finely hairy fronds.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is native to eastern North America. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a pale, silky appearance due to the dense covering of trichomes. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique foliage makes it a sought-after plant for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
