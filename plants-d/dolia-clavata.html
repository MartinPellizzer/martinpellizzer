Dolia clavata

Dolia clavata, commonly known as the club-shaped dolia, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, club-like shape and fleshy, waxy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is uncommon among its relatives.

Due to habitat loss, Dolia clavata is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
