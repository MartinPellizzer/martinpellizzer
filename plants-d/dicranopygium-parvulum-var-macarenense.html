Dicranopygium parvulum var. macarenense

Dicranopygium parvulum var. macarenense, commonly known as the Macaronesian feather moss, is a variant of the feather moss genus Dicranopygium.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and small, delicate leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate and subtropical regions. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.

This moss is particularly noted for its adaptation to rocky and coastal environments.
