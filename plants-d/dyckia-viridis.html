Dyckia viridis

Dyckia viridis, commonly known as the green dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in panicles, which are visible during the flowering season.

Dyckia viridis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
