Doryopteris raddiana var. multipartita

Doryopteris raddiana var. multipartita, commonly known as the multibract fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, finely divided fronds that are often adorned with small, scale-like bracts along the leaflets. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the cool, humid conditions. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
