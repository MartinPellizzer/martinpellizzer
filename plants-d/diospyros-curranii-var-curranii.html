Diospyros curranii var. curranii

Diospyros curranii var. curranii, commonly known as the Curran's persimmon, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, textured surface.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
