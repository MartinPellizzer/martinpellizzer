Diplostylis caffra

Diplostylis caffra, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular florets arranged in a composite head.

Diplostylis caffra is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
