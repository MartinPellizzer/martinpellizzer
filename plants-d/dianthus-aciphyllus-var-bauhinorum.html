Dianthus aciphyllus var. bauhinorum

Dianthus aciphyllus var. bauhinorum, commonly known as the Bauhin's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
