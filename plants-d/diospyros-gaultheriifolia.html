Diospyros gaultheriifolia

Diospyros gaultheriifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to red berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
