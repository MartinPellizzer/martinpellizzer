Danthonia subulata

Danthonia subulata, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It features slender, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and heathlands.

Danthonia subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
