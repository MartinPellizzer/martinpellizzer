Dendrochilum oliganthum

Dendrochilum oliganthum, commonly known as the few-flowered tree orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in forest understories, thriving in humid and shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often with a distinctive coloration that includes shades of white, yellow, and green.

The orchid's unique morphology and ecological preferences make it a notable species within the Orchidaceae family.
