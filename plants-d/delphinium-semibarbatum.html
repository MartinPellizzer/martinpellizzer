Delphinium semibarbatum

Delphinium semibarbatum, commonly known as the Armenian larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring a straight, branched stem and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in tall spikes and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
