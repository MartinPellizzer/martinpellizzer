Draba atacamensis

Draba atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama Draba, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many well-known garden plants such as cabbage and mustard. This species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of tiny white or pale yellow flowers. It has adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal water, showcasing features like succulent leaves and a deep root system.

Draba atacamensis plays a role in the ecological balance of its harsh environment, supporting local insect populations and contributing to the biodiversity of the desert.
