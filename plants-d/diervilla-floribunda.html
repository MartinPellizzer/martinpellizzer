Diervilla floribunda

Diervilla floribunda, commonly known as redbank azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and remain green throughout the growing season.

Diervilla floribunda thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
