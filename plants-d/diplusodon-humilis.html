Diplusodon humilis

Diplusodon humilis, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its berries are typically red or yellow and are dispersed by birds.

Diplusodon humilis plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
