Draba bonplandiana

Draba bonplandiana, commonly known as the Bonpland's winter cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the high Andes of South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in alpine environments and is adapted to cold, rocky soils.

Draba bonplandiana is often found in moist, well-drained habitats at elevations above 3,000 meters.
