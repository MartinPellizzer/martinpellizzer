Deschampsia mildbraedii

Deschampsia mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense mats or clumps. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers that appear in late summer.

Deschampsia mildbraedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
