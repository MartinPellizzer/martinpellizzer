Danielia jasminea

Danielia jasminea, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine or fragrant daphne, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slight pubescence.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
