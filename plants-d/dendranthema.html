Dendranthema

Dendranthema, commonly known as chamomile, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family.

It includes several species, with Dendranthema moro and Dendranthema dentatum being the most notable. These plants are characterized by their daisy-like flowers, which consist of white ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets. Dendranthema species are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and they thrive in temperate climates.

They are often cultivated for their aromatic properties and are used in herbal medicine and as ornamental plants.
