Digitaria apiculata

Digitaria apiculata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open areas such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, flat leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate.

Digitaria apiculata is often considered a weed but can also serve as forage for livestock in certain conditions.
