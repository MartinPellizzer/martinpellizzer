Desmodium acuminatum f. chandonnettii

Desmodium acuminatum f. chandonnettii, commonly known as the Chandonnet's sicklepod, is a variant of the sicklepod genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, curved pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
