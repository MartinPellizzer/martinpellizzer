Dendrobium farmeri

Dendrobium farmeri, commonly known as the Farmer's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Dendrobium farmeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
