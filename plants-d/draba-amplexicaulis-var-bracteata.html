Draba amplexicaulis var. bracteata

Draba amplexicaulis var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted winter cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that clasp the stem, a characteristic feature of the genus Draba. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. Its flowers are typically surrounded by leaf-like bracts, giving it its specific epithet "bracteata.".

The species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
