Digitaria bakeri

Digitaria bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's Digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often pale green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a prominent midrib.

Digitaria bakeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and resilience.
