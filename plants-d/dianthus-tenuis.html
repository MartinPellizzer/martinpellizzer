Dianthus tenuis

Dianthus tenuis, commonly known as the slender pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, clustered pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
