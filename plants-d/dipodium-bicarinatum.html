Dipodium bicarinatum

Dipodium bicarinatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are typically broad and have a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in an inflorescence called a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf known as a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
