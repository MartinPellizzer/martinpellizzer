Disa minor

Disa minor, commonly known as the dwarf disa, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms during the spring and summer months.

Disa minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
