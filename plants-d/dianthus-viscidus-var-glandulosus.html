Dianthus viscidus var. glandulosus

Dianthus viscidus var. glandulosus, commonly known as sticky campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure, which gives it a sticky texture. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, often with a hairy or glandular surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
