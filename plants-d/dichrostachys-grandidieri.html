Dichrostachys grandidieri

Dichrostachys grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar burr tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small, shrub-like tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the region's flora.
