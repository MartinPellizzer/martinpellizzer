Dactylorhiza incarnata var. brevibracteata

Dactylorhiza incarnata var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring calcareous soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with a few leaves, and its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a spike. It has a complex root system with mycorrhizal associations that aid in nutrient uptake.

This orchid is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
