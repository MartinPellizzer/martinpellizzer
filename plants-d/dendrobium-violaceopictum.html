Dendrobium violaceopictum

Dendrobium violaceopictum, commonly known as the violet-painted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features long, cane-like stems that can reach several feet in length. Its flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns that resemble painted designs.

Dendrobium violaceopictum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
