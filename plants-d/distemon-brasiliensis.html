Distemon brasiliensis

Distemon brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Distemon brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
