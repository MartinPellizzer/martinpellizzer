Dactylis polygama f. amplectens

Dactylis polygama f. amplectens, commonly known as the creeping veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and forms a mat-like growth that helps prevent soil erosion. The plant has narrow, slightly hairy leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in pastureland due to its nutritional value for livestock.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
