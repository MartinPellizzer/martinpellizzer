Derris elliptica var. tonkinensis

Derris elliptica var. tonkinensis, commonly known as tonkinese derris, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a potent natural insecticide, in its seeds and roots. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable in traditional agricultural practices.
