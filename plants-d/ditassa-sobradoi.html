Ditassa sobradoi

Ditassa sobradoi, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
