Dactylis longifolia

Dactylis longifolia, commonly known as the slender veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Dactylis longifolia is valued for its forage quality and is used in agricultural settings for livestock grazing.
