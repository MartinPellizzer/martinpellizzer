Dioclea ruddiae

Dioclea ruddiae, commonly known as the Rudd's climbing bean, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and climbing habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and thrives in warm, moist environments.

Dioclea ruddiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural uses.
