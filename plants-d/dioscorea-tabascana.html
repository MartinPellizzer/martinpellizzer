Dioscorea tabascana

Dioscorea tabascana, commonly known as Mexican yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to Central America and northern South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces tuberous roots that are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. These roots are typically harvested after the plant has flowered and set seed, often in the late summer or early autumn.

The botanical structure of Dioscorea tabascana includes compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
