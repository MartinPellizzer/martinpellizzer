Dicoma chatanensis

Dicoma chatanensis, commonly known as the Natal daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Dicoma chatanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
