Dracocephalum biondianum

Dracocephalum biondianum, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are often narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Dracocephalum biondianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
