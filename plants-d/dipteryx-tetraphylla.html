Dipteryx tetraphylla

Dipteryx tetraphylla, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by bees, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing multiple seeds, known as Brazil nuts. The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and its economically important seeds.

It plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of the Amazon rainforest.
