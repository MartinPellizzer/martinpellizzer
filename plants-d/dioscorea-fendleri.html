Dioscorea fendleri

Dioscorea fendleri, commonly known as Fendler yam, is a climbing vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its helical stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits containing numerous seeds. It is a tuberous plant, with the underground stems developing into edible tubers that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Dioscorea fendleri thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert regions.
