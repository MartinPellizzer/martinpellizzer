Dioscorea convolvulacea

Dioscorea convolvulacea, commonly known as the wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its helicoidal stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and cylindrical, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The tubers of Dioscorea convolvulacea contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
