Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. cordigera

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

cordigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of pale to dark purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. It has a complex root system with mycorrhizal associations that are essential for nutrient uptake.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
