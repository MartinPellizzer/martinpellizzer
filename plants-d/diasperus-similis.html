Diasperus similis

Diasperus similis, commonly known as the similar spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and smooth, often glabrous leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers, which are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and is adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Diasperus similis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
