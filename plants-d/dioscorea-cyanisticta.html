Dioscorea cyanisticta

Dioscorea cyanisticta, commonly known as the blue-stemmed yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive blue-green stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.

The tubers are also valued for their nutritional content and potential medicinal properties.
