Dipterocarpus pentagonus

Dipterocarpus pentagonus, commonly known as the pentagon leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pentagonal-shaped leaves and produces heavy, aromatic seeds enclosed in woody capsules. The tree is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family, which is well-known for its ecological and economic importance in rainforests. It plays a vital role in providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
