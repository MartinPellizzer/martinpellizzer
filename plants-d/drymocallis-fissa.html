Drymocallis fissa

Drymocallis fissa, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, usually with a hairy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

It produces bright yellow composite flowers in late summer, which attract a variety of pollinators like bees and butterflies.
