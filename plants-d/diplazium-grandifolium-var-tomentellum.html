Diplazium grandifolium var. tomentellum

Diplazium grandifolium var. tomentellum, commonly known as the hairy sword fern, is a variant of the sword fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green with a velvety texture, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
