Davilla martii

Davilla martii, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces striking violet-purple berries that are a key feature of its ornamental value. Davilla martii is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.
