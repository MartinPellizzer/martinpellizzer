Diospyros ferrea f. ovata

Diospyros ferrea f. ovata, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon species native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, though it is less commonly cultivated than other persimmon varieties.
