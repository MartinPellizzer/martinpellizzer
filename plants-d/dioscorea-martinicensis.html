Dioscorea martinicensis

Dioscorea martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique yam, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source by indigenous communities.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
