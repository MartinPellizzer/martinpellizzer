Donacodes macrocephala

Donacodes macrocephala, commonly known as the giant bamboo or large-headed bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
