Desmodium humblotianum

Desmodium humblotianum, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetbean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
