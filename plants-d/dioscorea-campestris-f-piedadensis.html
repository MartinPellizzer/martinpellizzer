Dioscorea campestris f. piedadensis

Dioscorea campestris f. piedadensis, commonly known as the Piedad yam, is a tuberous plant belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Colombia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and have a slightly sweet flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 20 cm in diameter, while the stems are hairy and climbing.

This variety is valued both for its nutritional content and its role in traditional agriculture.
