Diospyros vaccinioides var. oblongata

Diospyros vaccinioides var. oblongata, commonly known as the oblongleaf persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to oblong leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a fleshy, orange to red berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
