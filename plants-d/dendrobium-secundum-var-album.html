Dendrobium secundum var. album

Dendrobium secundum var. album, commonly known as the white dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients. The plant produces striking white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
