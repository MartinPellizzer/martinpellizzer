Dianthus gautieri var. mixtus

Dianthus gautieri var. mixtus, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
