Diospyros orientalis

Diospyros orientalis, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish berries that are rich in nutrients. Its bark is thick and fissured, often with a reddish or brownish hue, and it is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Diospyros orientalis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
