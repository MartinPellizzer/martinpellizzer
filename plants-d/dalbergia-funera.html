Dalbergia funera

Dalbergia funera, commonly known as African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood with a rich, aromatic scent. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its fruit is a pod that contains several elliptical seeds, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional crafts.
