Distylium myricoides var. macrocarpum

Distylium myricoides var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, cone-like fruits that are reddish-brown when mature. Its botanical characteristics make it a unique species within the Myricaceae family.

It is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.
