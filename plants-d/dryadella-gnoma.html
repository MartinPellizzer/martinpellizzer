Dryadella gnoma

Dryadella gnoma, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a unique species belonging to the family Dryadeaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that display a silvery sheen due to the presence of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests. Its reproductive structures, known as sorocarps, contain spores that are dispersed by wind.

Dryadella gnoma plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
