Dianthus brevior

Dianthus brevior, commonly known as the short-stemmed campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features upright stems with clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, rounded seed capsules that disperse its seeds.

Dianthus brevior is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
