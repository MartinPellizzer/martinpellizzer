Dianthus hypochlorus var. karami

Dianthus hypochlorus var. karami, commonly known as the Karami carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant produces small, fragrant leaves and has a short flowering period, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both garden displays and cut flower arrangements.
