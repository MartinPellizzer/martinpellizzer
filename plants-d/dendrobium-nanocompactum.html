Dendrobium nanocompactum

Dendrobium nanocompactum, commonly known as the compact dwarf orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant features short, compact pseudobulbs and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dendrobium nanocompactum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
