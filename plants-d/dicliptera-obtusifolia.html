Dicliptera obtusifolia

Dicliptera obtusifolia, commonly known as African daisy, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers, typically blooming from spring to autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, oppositely arranged, and have a rough texture.

Dicliptera obtusifolia is often found in moist grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
