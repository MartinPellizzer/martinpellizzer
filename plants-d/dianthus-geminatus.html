Dianthus geminatus

Dianthus geminatus, commonly known as the double carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, double-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters and has a compact, bushy habit. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Dianthus geminatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
