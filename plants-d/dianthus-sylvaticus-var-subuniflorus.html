Dianthus sylvaticus var. subuniflorus

Dianthus sylvaticus var. subuniflorus, commonly known as the woodland campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features simple, often hairy leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers with a distinctive frilly appearance. Its flowers are usually borne on short, branched stems, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
