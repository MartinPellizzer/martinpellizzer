Diospyros yeobii

Diospyros yeobii, commonly known as the Korean persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Korea.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange to red fruits resembling small persimmons. The fruit is typically harvested in autumn and is valued for its sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Diospyros yeobii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit in its native habitat.
