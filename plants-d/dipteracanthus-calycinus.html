Dipteracanthus calycinus

Dipteracanthus calycinus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a visually stunning display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only beautiful but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
