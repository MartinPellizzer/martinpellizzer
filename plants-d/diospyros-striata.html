Diospyros striata

Diospyros striata, commonly known as the Chinese date or wild date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits resembling dates, which are edible when ripe.

Diospyros striata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
