Discocactus subviridigriseus

Discocactus subviridigriseus, commonly known as the greenish-gray discocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to grayish hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its muted foliage. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

The discocactus subviridigriseus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
