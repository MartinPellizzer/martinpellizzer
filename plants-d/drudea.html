Drudea

Drudea, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish. Its flowering period is brief, typically occurring only during the late spring or early summer.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Drudea is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
