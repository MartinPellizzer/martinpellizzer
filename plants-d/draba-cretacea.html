Draba cretacea

Draba cretacea, commonly known as the Crete whitlow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found on the island of Crete.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, delicate flowers and cruciform petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
