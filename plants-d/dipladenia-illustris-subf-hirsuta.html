Dipladenia illustris subf. hirsuta

Dipladenia illustris subf.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy dipladenia, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trailing stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant features hairy foliage, which gives it its subspecies name, and thrives in warm, humid conditions. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets or trellises.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for both indoor and outdoor gardens in suitable climates.
