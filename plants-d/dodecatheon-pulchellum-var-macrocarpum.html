Dodecatheon pulchellum var. macrocarpum

Dodecatheon pulchellum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the showy false Solomon's seal, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces showy, red to orange berries that persist into winter, adding visual interest to the landscape. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This species thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.
