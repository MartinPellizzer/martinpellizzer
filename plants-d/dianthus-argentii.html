Dianthus argentii

Dianthus argentii, commonly known as silver pinks, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Dianthus argentii is typically hardy in USDA zones 5 through 9, making it a versatile choice for various garden environments.
