Dendrolobium baccatum

Dendrolobium baccatum, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a leguminous shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, red or black pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Dendrolobium baccatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
