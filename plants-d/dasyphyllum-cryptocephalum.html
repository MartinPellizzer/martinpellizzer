Dasyphyllum cryptocephalum

Dasyphyllum cryptocephalum, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandmat, is a low-growing succulent native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
