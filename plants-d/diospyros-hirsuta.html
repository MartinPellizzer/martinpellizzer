Diospyros hirsuta

Diospyros hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears orange to reddish fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Diospyros hirsuta is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
