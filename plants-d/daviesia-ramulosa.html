Daviesia ramulosa

Daviesia ramulosa, commonly known as the silky daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fluffy flower clusters in shades of pink or purple during spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Daviesia ramulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
