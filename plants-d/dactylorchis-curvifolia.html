Dactylorchis curvifolia

Dactylorchis curvifolia, commonly known as the curl-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. The plant has a slender, upright stem with several lance-shaped leaves that curve at the tips. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in a loose cluster, often with a faint fragrance.

Dactylorchis curvifolia is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
