Dendrobium undatiflorum

Dendrobium undatiflorum, commonly known as the wavy-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, wavy-edged flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.

Dendrobium undatiflorum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
