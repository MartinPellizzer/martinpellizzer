Dypsis manaranensis

Dypsis manaranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
