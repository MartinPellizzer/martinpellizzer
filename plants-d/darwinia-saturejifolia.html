Darwinia saturejifolia

Darwinia saturejifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Darwinia saturejifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
