Diospyros adiensis

Diospyros adiensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The wood of Diospyros adiensis is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and crafts.
