Dicrastylis exsuccosa f. tomentosa

Dicrastylis exsuccosa f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and leaves that are typically covered in a fine, woolly layer of hairs. It thrives in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as swampy areas or along waterways.

The tomentosa subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive hairy texture, which provides it with a unique appearance and ecological role.
