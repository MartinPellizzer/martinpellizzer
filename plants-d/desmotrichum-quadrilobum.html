Desmotrichum quadrilobum

Desmotrichum quadrilobum, commonly known as the four-lobed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
