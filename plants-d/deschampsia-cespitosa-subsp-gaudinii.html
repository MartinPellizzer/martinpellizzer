Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. gaudinii

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as Gaudin's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has tufted growth habit with slender, blue-green blades that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
