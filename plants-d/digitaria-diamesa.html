Digitaria diamesa

Digitaria diamesa, commonly known as the slender digit grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and tufted inflorescences. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among grasses. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.

Digitaria diamesa is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of nutrient-rich environments.
