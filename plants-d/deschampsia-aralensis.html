Deschampsia aralensis

Deschampsia aralensis, commonly known as the Aral Sea grass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Deschampsia aralensis is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
