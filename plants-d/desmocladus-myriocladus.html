Desmocladus myriocladus

Desmocladus myriocladus, commonly known as the many-leaved desmocladus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent leaves help it conserve water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is relatively easy to care for in cultivation.
