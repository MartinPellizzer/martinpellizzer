Dicliptera cliffordii

Dicliptera cliffordii, commonly known as the African bristlegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, bristly flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, textured surface.

Dicliptera cliffordii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
