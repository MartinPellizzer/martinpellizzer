Drosera erythrorhiza subsp. magna

Drosera erythrorhiza subsp.

magna, commonly known as the giant redroot sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its reddish stems and leaves that are covered in sticky glandular hairs to trap insects.

The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually pink or reddish in color.

It is a perennial species that thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils.
