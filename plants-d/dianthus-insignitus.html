Dianthus insignitus

Dianthus insignitus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill carnation, is a flowering plant native to California and Nevada.

It produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, often adorned with distinctive white or pink markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known flowering plants.

Dianthus insignitus is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
