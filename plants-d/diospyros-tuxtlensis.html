Diospyros tuxtlensis

Diospyros tuxtlensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary practices.

Diospyros tuxtlensis is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used historically for tool-making and construction.
