Drymocallis pseudorupestris var. intermedia

Drymocallis pseudorupestris var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of small, yellow, composite flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental value in gardens.
