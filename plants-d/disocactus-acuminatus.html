Disocactus acuminatus

Disocactus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with smooth, green stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Disocactus acuminatus is well adapted to full sun and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
