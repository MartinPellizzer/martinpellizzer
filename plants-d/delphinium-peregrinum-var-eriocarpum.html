Delphinium peregrinum var. eriocarpum

Delphinium peregrinum var. eriocarpum, commonly known as the woolly delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, woolly pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
