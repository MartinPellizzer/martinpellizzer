Desmodium lunatum

Desmodium lunatum, commonly known as the moonflower or moon bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant produces lens-shaped pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Desmodium lunatum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
