Discaria × serratifolia

Discaria × serratifolia, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a hybrid plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

It is well adapted to saline and wind-prone environments, making it a hardy species for coastal landscaping.
