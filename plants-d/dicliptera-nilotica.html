Dicliptera nilotica

Dicliptera nilotica, commonly known as the Nile grass or river grass, is a perennial herb native to Africa, particularly found in wetland and riparian habitats.

It has a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, depending on the water level. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Dicliptera nilotica plays an important role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for aquatic life.
