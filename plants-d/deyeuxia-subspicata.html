Deyeuxia subspicata

Deyeuxia subspicata, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it flowers during the summer months.

Deyeuxia subspicata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
