Daphnidium caudatum

Daphnidium caudatum, commonly known as the golden thread, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, creeping stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Daphnidium caudatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
