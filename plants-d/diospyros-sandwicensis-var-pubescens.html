Diospyros sandwicensis var. pubescens

Diospyros sandwicensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Hawaiian persimmon, is a unique plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small, evergreen tree characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. Its hairy (pubescent) leaves and fruit set distinguish this variety from other members of the genus Diospyros.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is considered a keystone plant in Hawaiian forests.
