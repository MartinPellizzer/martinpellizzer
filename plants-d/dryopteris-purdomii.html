Dryopteris purdomii

Dryopteris purdomii, commonly known as Purdom's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and wiry, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
