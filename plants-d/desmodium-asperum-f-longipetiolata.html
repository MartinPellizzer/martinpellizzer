Desmodium asperum f. longipetiolata

Desmodium asperum f. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled desmodium, is a variant of the perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with elongated petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spikes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its compound leaves and stipules are characteristic of the genus Desmodium.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
