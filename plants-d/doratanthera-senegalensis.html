Doratanthera senegalensis

Doratanthera senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese orchid, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Senegal, Mali, and Nigeria.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. It has linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
