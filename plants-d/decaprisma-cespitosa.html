Decaprisma cespitosa

Decaprisma cespitosa, commonly known as the tufted grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Decaprisma cespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
