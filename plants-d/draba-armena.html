Draba armena

Draba armena, commonly known as Armenian rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, with small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

Draba armena is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in high-altitude meadows and scree slopes.
