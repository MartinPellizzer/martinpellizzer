Diplostephium antisanense f. rhodopappus

Diplostephium antisanense f. rhodopappus, commonly known as the red-chenopod, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, red bracts. The flowers are typically inconspicuous, with the showy bracts serving as the primary ornamental feature.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh, dry climates.
