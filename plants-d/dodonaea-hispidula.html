Dodonaea hispidula

Dodonaea hispidula, commonly known as the hispid hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grey-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Dodonaea hispidula is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
