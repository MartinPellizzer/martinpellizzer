Dissotis princeps var. candolleana

Dissotis princeps var. candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana grass, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
