Dendrobium × chlorostele

Dendrobium × chlorostele, commonly known as the green-stem orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that combines traits from its parent species, Dendrobium nobile and Dendrobium phalaenopsis.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a compact growth habit with fleshy, dark green stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually fragrant, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink, and they bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Dendrobium × chlorostele is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
