Dianthus serratifolius subsp. abbreviatus

Dianthus serratifolius subsp.

abbreviatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and stems that are often hairy.

The leaves are narrow and serrated, giving the plant its specific epithet "serratifolius.". It produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
