Diospyros pyrrhocarpa

Diospyros pyrrhocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruit persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange-red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
