Dalea estoraxana

Dalea estoraxana, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea estoraxana is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
