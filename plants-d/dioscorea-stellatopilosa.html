Dioscorea stellatopilosa

Dioscorea stellatopilosa, commonly known as the star-hair yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, often star-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with prominent stamens, and it relies on insect pollination.

The plant's morphology and growth habits make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
