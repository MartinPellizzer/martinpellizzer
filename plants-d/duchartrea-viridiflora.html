Duchartrea viridiflora

Duchartrea viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered duchartrea, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly green flowers, which are often tinged with yellow or white, and its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the trunks of trees in its natural habitat. It produces small, clustered fruits that contain hard, seed-like structures.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
