Delphinium hoffmeisteri

Delphinium hoffmeisteri, commonly known as the Hoffmeister's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its showy, elongated petals that resemble larks' spurs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be toxic to livestock and humans if ingested.
