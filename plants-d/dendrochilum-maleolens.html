Dendrochilum maleolens

Dendrochilum maleolens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tree orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest undergrowth. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and inflorescence. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive yellowish-green color and a unique, narrow shape.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
