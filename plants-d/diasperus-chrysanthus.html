Diasperus chrysanthus

Diasperus chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow composite flowers. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single head, with ray florets surrounding disc florets.
