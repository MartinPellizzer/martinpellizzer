Dyckia insignis var. obtusiflora

Dyckia insignis var. obtusiflora, commonly known as the Broad-flowered Dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It features rosettes of thick, sword-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, showy white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
