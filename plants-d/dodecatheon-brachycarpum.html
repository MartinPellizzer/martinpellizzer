Dodecatheon brachycarpum

Dodecatheon brachycarpum, commonly known as the short-carpelled glory-of-the-snow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
