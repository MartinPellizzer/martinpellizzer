Draba arctica

Draba arctica, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a small flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Draba arctica is known for its resilience and ability to survive in low-nutrient, high-altitude habitats.
