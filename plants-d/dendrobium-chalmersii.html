Dendrobium chalmersii

Dendrobium chalmersii, commonly known as the Chalmers' orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms during the spring and summer months, producing intricate, lip-shaped petals that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium chalmersii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
