Desmodium prainii

Desmodium prainii, commonly known as the small-leaved burr legume, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, hairy, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Desmodium prainii is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
