Draba cretica var. sphaciotica

Draba cretica var. sphaciotica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow or white flowers in early spring.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical form of Draba cretica.
