Drimia concolor

Drimia concolor, commonly known as the African lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Drimia concolor is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
