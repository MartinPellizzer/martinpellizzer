Didissandra amabilis

Didissandra amabilis, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the subtropical regions of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Didissandra amabilis is also valued in traditional Aboriginal medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
