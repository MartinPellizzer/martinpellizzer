Dasypyrum hordeaceum

Dasypyrum hordeaceum, commonly known as barley grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is closely related to barley.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions and is characterized by its dense, leafy growth. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as it is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in ecological restoration and soil stabilization projects.
