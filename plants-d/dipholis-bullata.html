Dipholis bullata

Dipholis bullata, commonly known as the bull's eye orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spotted leaves that resemble a bull's eye, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of yellow and green, and bloom during the summer months.

Dipholis bullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
