Disporum sessile var. dolichocarpum

Disporum sessile var. dolichocarpum, commonly known as the long-seed yew, is a variant of the disporum plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces elongated, berry-like fruits that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
