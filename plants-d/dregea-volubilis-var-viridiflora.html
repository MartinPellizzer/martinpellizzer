Dregea volubilis var. viridiflora

Dregea volubilis var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered dregea, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in racemes, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
