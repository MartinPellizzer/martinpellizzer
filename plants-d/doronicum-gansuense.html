Doronicum gansuense

Doronicum gansuense, commonly known as the Gansu leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Gansu Province in China.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs.
