Dicrastylis rosmarinifolia

Dicrastylis rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, and its small, clustered white or pale blue flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in aromatherapy.

Dicrastylis rosmarinifolia is a hardy perennial that is drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
