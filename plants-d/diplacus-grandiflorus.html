Diplacus grandiflorus

Diplacus grandiflorus, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, pink to magenta flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Diplacus grandiflorus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
