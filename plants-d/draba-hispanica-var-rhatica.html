Draba hispanica var. rhatica

Draba hispanica var. rhatica, commonly known as the Spanish rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, glaucous leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with edible or medicinal uses.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.
