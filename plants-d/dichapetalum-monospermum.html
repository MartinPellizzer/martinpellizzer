Dichapetalum monospermum

Dichapetalum monospermum, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiraling arrangement of leaves that give it a distinctive wheel-like appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, traditionally used in African herbal medicine.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
