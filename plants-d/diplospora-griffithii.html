Diplospora griffithii

Diplospora griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
