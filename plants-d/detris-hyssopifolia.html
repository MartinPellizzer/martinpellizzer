Detris hyssopifolia

Detris hyssopifolia, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Detris hyssopifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
