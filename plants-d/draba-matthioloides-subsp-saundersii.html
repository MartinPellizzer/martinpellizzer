Draba matthioloides subsp. saundersii

Draba matthioloides subsp.

saundersii, commonly known as the Saunders' whitlow grass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
