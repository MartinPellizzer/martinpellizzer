Danaeopsis paleacea

Danaeopsis paleacea, commonly known as the pale pink orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in the dappled light. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance.

Danaeopsis paleacea is valued for its delicate beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens for its ornamental appeal.
