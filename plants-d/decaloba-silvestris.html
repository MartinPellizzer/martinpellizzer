Decaloba silvestris

Decaloba silvestris, commonly known as the wild silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Decaloba silvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
