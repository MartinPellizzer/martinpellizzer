Dichanthelium lancearium

Dichanthelium lancearium, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in wetland ecosystems.
