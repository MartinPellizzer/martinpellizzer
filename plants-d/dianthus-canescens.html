Dianthus canescens

Dianthus canescens, commonly known as the woolly campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus canescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
