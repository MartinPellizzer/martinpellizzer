Dombeya biserrata

Dombeya biserrata, commonly known as the serrated dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Dombeya biserrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers.
