Diplachne livida

Diplachne livida, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Diplachne livida has narrow, parallel-veined leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
