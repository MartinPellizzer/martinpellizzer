Diplopappus foliosus

Diplopappus foliosus, commonly known as the leafy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, and are followed by distinctive pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal.

The species is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
