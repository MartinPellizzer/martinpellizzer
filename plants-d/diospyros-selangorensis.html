Diospyros selangorensis

Diospyros selangorensis, commonly known as the Malacca persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is edible, though some varieties may have astringent or bitter taste when unripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
