Dianthus capitatus subsp. andrzejowskianus

Dianthus capitatus subsp.

andrzejowskianus, commonly known as the Andrzejowski's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
