Drosera kihlmanii

Drosera kihlmanii, commonly known as the Kihlman's Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, perennial species that produces delicate, sticky-hair covered leaves to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually pink or reddish in color.

Drosera kihlmanii is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
