Dactylorhiza fuchsii var. psychrophila

Dactylorhiza fuchsii var. psychrophila, commonly known as the Alpine marsh orchid, is a rare and cold-adapted orchid species native to high-altitude regions of Europe.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor habitats such as peat bogs and mountain marshes. The plant features a slender, erect stem with few leaves and produces delicate, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive lip. Its growth is closely tied to specific microclimates, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
