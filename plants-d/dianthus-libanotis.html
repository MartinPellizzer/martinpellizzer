Dianthus libanotis

Dianthus libanotis, commonly known as the Lebanon carnation, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with a distinctive, slightly fragrant scent. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Dianthus libanotis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
