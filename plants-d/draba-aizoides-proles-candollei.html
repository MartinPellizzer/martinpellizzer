Draba aizoides proles candollei

Draba aizoides proles candollei, commonly known as the alpine draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming herbaceous plant that typically grows in high-altitude environments such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features basal leaves that are usually woolly and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia.
