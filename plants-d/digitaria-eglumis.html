Digitaria eglumis

Digitaria eglumis, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to Africa and widely naturalized in many parts of the world.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It is often considered a weed in agricultural fields due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.

Digitaria eglumis is adapted to a wide range of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
