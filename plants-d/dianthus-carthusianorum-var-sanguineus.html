Dianthus carthusianorum var. sanguineus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. sanguineus, commonly known as the blood-red carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with linear, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and historical association with love and devotion.
