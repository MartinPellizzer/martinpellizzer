Delphinium bovei

Delphinium bovei, commonly known as the Bove's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium bovei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
