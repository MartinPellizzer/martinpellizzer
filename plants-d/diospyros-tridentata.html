Diospyros tridentata

Diospyros tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for carving and furniture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with three lobes, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, round berries.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and is noted for its ability to thrive in tropical climates.
