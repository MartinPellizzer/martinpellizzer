Dischidiopsis incrassata

Dischidiopsis incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leafed arrow cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season, typically in summer. It is well adapted to dry conditions and requires minimal watering.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh climates make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
