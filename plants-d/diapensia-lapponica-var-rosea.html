Diapensia lapponica var. rosea

Diapensia lapponica var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered rock pillow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette and are covered in fine, soft hairs.
