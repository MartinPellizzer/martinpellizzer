Dalbergia domingensis

Dalbergia domingensis, commonly known as the Cuban rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and acoustic qualities. The plant typically grows in humid lowland areas and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in green or reddish bracts, and it produces elongated pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds.
