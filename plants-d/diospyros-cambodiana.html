Diospyros cambodiana

Diospyros cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Diospyros cambodiana is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in carpentry and furniture making.
