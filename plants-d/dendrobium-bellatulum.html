Dendrobium bellatulum

Dendrobium bellatulum, commonly known as the orchid with a bell-like shape, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with upright, cane-like stems that often bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or green with red or purple markings, and they bloom during the dry season. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
