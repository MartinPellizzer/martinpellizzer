Delphinastrum glaucum

Delphinastrum glaucum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Delphinastrum glaucum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
