Dombeya repanda

Dombeya repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Dombeya repanda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
