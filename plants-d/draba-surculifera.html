Draba surculifera

Draba surculifera, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in rocky and gravelly soils.

Draba surculifera is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it withstands cold and harsh environmental conditions.
