Dryopteris × wildemanii

Dryopteris × wildemanii, commonly known as the wildemanii wood fern, is a hybrid fern that exhibits a unique combination of characteristics from its parent species.

It typically features fronds that are bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that adds a refined texture to its foliage. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas where it can receive the necessary humidity and indirect light. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are usually clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common trait among ferns.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
