Delphinium pachycentrum var. humilius

Delphinium pachycentrum var. humilius, commonly known as the dwarf delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is often cultivated for its beauty and ease of care in garden environments.
