Doryopteris pedata var. multipartita

Doryopteris pedata var. multipartita, commonly known as the multipartite shield fern, is a fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, divided fronds that resemble shields, with a unique pattern of lobes and segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually leathery and dark green, with a rough texture.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
