Dyschoriste tinctorum

Dyschoriste tinctorum, commonly known as the red root or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Dyschoriste tinctorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
