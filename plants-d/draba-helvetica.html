Draba helvetica

Draba helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss bellflower, is a small flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its simple, basal leaves and small, delicate flowers. Draba helvetica is often found in meadows and mountainous areas, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
