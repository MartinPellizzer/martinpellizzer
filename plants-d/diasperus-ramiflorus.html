Diasperus ramiflorus

Diasperus ramiflorus, commonly known as the branched-flower daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in a dense, daisy-like cluster. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Diasperus ramiflorus is a hardy species that is well adapted to survive in low-water conditions.
