Diospyros biflora

Diospyros biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered persimmon, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in pairs, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from green to red as it matures.

Diospyros biflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
