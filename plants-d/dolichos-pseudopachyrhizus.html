Dolichos pseudopachyrhizus

Dolichos pseudopachyrhizus, commonly known as the African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Dolichos pseudopachyrhizus is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
