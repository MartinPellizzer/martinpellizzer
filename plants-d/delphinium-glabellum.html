Delphinium glabellum

Delphinium glabellum, commonly known as the glabrous larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive silvery-white underside.

Delphinium glabellum is adapted to sunny, well-drained habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
