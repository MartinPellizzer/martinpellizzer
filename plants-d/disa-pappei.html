Disa pappei

Disa pappei, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking South African orchid species characterized by its vibrant pink and white flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Cape Province, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky stem and a cluster of leaves. Its flowers are borne on a single, erect stem and are known for their intricate, three-lobed structure.

Disa pappei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the rich botanical diversity of South Africa.
