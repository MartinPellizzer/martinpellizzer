Dioscorea fasciculata

Dioscorea fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by yellowish fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Dioscorea fasciculata is valued for its nutritional content and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
