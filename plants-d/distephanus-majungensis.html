Distephanus majungensis

Distephanus majungensis, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
