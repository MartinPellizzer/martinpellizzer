Diospyros carpinifolia

Diospyros carpinifolia, commonly known as the Persian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe.

Diospyros carpinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is often used in local cuisines.
