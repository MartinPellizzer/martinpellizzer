Dracocephalum mexicanum

Dracocephalum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in dense spikes.

Dracocephalum mexicanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
