Dracaena wallichii

Dracaena wallichii, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India and China. The plant is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. Dracaena wallichii is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and yellow, adding visual interest to any space.
