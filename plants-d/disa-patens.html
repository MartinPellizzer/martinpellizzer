Disa patens

Disa patens, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Disa patens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
