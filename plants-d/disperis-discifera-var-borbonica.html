Disperis discifera var. borbonica

Disperis discifera var. borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that produce multiple shoots. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This variant is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
