Detris smaragdina

Detris smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Detris, is a unique flowering plant native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking emerald-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Despite its beauty, Detris smaragdina is relatively rare and is considered a conservation concern due to habitat loss.
