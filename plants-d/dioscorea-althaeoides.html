Dioscorea althaeoides

Dioscorea althaeoides, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, tuberous fruits. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source in some African regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
