Dracula vinacea

Dracula vinacea, commonly known as the vampire orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and intricate, showy flowers that resemble the face of a vampire, hence its common name. The plant thrives in high humidity and partial shade, often growing on trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically violet or purple and have a unique, almost otherworldly appearance.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Dracula vinacea is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
