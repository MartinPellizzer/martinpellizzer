Dactylorhiza purpurella var. maculosa

Dactylorhiza purpurella var. maculosa, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, purple or violet flowers with distinctive dark spots. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant, spurred orchid flowers.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
