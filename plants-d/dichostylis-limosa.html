Dichostylis limosa

Dichostylis limosa, commonly known as the swamp dichostylis, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and is adapted to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, often floating or submerged, which helps in photosynthesis in aquatic environments.

Dichostylis limosa plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
