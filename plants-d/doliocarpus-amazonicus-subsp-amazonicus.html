Doliocarpus amazonicus subsp. amazonicus

Doliocarpus amazonicus subsp.

amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest. It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage, which contributes to its ecological importance.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and is often found in moist, shaded environments within the rainforest canopy.
