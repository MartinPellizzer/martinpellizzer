Dianthus capitatus var. pancicianus

Dianthus capitatus var. pancicianus, commonly known as the Pancician carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
