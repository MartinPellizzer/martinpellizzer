Dianthus sylvestris subsp. alboroseus

Dianthus sylvestris subsp.

alboroseus, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of stems.

The plant has basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with hairy surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
