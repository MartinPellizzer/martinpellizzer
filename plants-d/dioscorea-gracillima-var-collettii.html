Dioscorea gracillima var. collettii

Dioscorea gracillima var. collettii, commonly known as the Collett's yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
