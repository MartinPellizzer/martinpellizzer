Dalbergaria guttata

Dalbergaria guttata, commonly known as the spotted sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny conditions. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Dalbergaria guttata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
