Dolichos rhomboideus

Dolichos rhomboideus, commonly known as the broad-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, curved pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown for its protein-rich seeds and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Dolichos rhomboideus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
