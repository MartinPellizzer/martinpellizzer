Dianthus demirkushii

Dianthus demirkushii, commonly known as the Turkish carnation, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, double-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Dianthus demirkushii is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
