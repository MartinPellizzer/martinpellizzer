Dissanthelium semitectum

Dissanthelium semitectum, commonly known as the covered panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

Dissanthelium semitectum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
