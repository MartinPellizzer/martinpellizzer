Dioscorea maliliensis

Dioscorea maliliensis, commonly known as Malili yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the genus Dioscorea, which includes various species of yams widely cultivated for their edible tubers. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. Its tubers are typically white or yellowish in color and have a starchy texture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied species within the Dioscorea family.
