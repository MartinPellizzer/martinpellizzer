Diastella divaricata subsp. montana

Diastella divaricata subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain diastella, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features a slender, branching stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
