Dalechampia parvifolia

Dalechampia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved dalechampia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in composite heads.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
