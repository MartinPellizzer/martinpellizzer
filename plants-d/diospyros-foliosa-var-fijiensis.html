Diospyros foliosa var. fijiensis

Diospyros foliosa var. fijiensis, commonly known as the Fiji persimmon, is a species native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Fiji.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a small, round, orange to reddish fruit with a sweet, edible pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in the regions where it is found.
