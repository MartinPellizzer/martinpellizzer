Doria nana

Doria nana, commonly known as the dwarf doria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
