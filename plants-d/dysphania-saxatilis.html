Dysphania saxatilis

Dysphania saxatilis, commonly known as rock amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Dysphania saxatilis thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
