Dissotidendron caloneurum var. confertiflorum

Dissotidendron caloneurum var. confertiflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered dissotidendron, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slight pubescence, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
