Drypetes ochrodasya

Drypetes ochrodasya, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Asia, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
