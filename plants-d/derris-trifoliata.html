Derris trifoliata

Derris trifoliata, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its tuberous roots, which are used as a starch source in some regions. Its growth habit includes a climbing or shrub-like form, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.
