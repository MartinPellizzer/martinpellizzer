Dodecadenia grandiflora

Dodecadenia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in vibrant red or orange hues. The plant has a woody stem and can grow into a tall, deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are compound, with long, narrow leaflets, and it produces distinctive, lantern-like seed pods.

Dodecadenia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its dramatic floral displays.
