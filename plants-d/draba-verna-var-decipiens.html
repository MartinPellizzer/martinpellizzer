Draba verna var. decipiens

Draba verna var. decipiens, commonly known as the spring whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale yellow with four petals.

The species is notable for its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring.
