Dipladenia tenuifolia f. pastorum

Dipladenia tenuifolia f. pastorum, commonly known as the Pastoral Dipladenia, is a hybrid form of the species Dipladenia tenuifolia.

It is a climbing plant native to South America, characterized by its trailing stems and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are usually bright orange or yellow. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in hanging baskets or as a trained vine in gardens.

It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
