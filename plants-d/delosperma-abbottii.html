Delosperma abbottii

Delosperma abbottii, commonly known as the Abbott's ice plant, is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, decorative plants.
