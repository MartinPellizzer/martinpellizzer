Deeringia indica var. pubescens

Deeringia indica var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
