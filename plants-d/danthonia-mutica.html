Danthonia mutica

Danthonia mutica, commonly known as the silky danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, grasslands, and sandy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Danthonia mutica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
