Disocactus × coopermannii

Disocactus × coopermannii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Schlumbergera truncata and Schlumbergera russelliana.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Brazil. The plant is characterized by its segmented stems that form a bushy shape and its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular houseplant worldwide.
