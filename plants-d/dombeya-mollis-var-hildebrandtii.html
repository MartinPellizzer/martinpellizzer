Dombeya mollis var. hildebrandtii

Dombeya mollis var. hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are typically covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
