Digera alternifolia

Digera alternifolia, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate leaves, which are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found growing along riverbanks and in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
