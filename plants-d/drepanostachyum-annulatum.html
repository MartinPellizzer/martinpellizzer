Drepanostachyum annulatum

Drepanostachyum annulatum, commonly known as the ringed bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive ring-like nodes along its stems. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often adorned with pale or yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
