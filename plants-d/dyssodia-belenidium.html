Dyssodia belenidium

Dyssodia belenidium, commonly known as the Bolivian daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Dyssodia belenidium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
