Drymocallis pseudorupestris var. crumiana

Drymocallis pseudorupestris var. crumiana, commonly known as the crumian cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and clustered in a cylindrical shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
