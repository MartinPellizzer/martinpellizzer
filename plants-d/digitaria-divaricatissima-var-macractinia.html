Digitaria divaricatissima var. macractinia

Digitaria divaricatissima var. macractinia, commonly known as giant fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
