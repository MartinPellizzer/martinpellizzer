Draba arabiformis

Draba arabiformis, commonly known as the Arabian whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with distinctive cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Draba arabiformis is often found in open habitats such as steppes, coastal areas, and disturbed soils.
