Dianthus acuminatus

Dianthus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Dianthus acuminatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
