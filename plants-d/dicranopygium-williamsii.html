Dicranopygium williamsii

Dicranopygium williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' feather moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like appearance, with slender, branching stems covered in small, overlapping leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on specialized stalks and release spores for dispersal.

Dicranopygium williamsii plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
