Dichorisandra perforans

Dichorisandra perforans, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dichorisandra perforans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
