Dendrobium pseudoumbellatum

Dendrobium pseudoumbellatum, commonly known as the orchid with the clustered inflorescence, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium pseudoumbellatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
