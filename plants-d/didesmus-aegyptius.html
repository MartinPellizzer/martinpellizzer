Didesmus aegyptius

Didesmus aegyptius, commonly known as the Egyptian senna, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Didesmus aegyptius is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
