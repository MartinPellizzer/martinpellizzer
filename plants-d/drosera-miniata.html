Drosera miniata

Drosera miniata, commonly known as the miniature sundew, is a species of carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Drosera miniata is a low-growing plant, often reaching only a few centimeters in height.
