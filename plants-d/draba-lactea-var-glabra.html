Draba lactea var. glabra

Draba lactea var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other varieties. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates.

Its morphology reflects its ecological niche, making it a resilient species in extreme environments.
