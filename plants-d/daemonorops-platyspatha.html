Daemonorops platyspatha

Daemonorops platyspatha, commonly known as the broad-leafed cycad, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in male and female inflorescences. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
