Dudleya cotyledon

Dudleya cotyledon, commonly known as the "Cotyledon Dudleya," is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating, and they often have a distinctive rosette shape. It produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
