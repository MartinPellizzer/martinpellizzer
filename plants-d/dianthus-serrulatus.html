Dianthus serrulatus

Dianthus serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are narrow, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
