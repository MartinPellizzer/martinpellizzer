Duhaldea pterocaula

Duhaldea pterocaula, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species.

Duhaldea pterocaula is often used in traditional medicine and is also popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.
