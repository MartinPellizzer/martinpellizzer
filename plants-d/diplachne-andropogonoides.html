Diplachne andropogonoides

Diplachne andropogonoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Diplachne andropogonoides is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
