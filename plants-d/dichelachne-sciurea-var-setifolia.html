Dichelachne sciurea var. setifolia

Dichelachne sciurea var. setifolia, commonly known as the slender leaved dichelachne, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually clustered in loose panicles.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
