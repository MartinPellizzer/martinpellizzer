Duvalia pubescens var. major

Duvalia pubescens var. major, commonly known as the hairy duvalia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
