Dortmanna pubescens

Dortmanna pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, commonly known as the buckwheat family. The plant is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks.
