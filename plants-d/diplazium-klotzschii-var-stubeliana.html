Diplazium klotzschii var. stubeliana

Diplazium klotzschii var. stubeliana, commonly known as the Stubel's water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Diplazium.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces primarily through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
