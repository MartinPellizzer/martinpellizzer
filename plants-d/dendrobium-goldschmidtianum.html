Dendrobium goldschmidtianum

Dendrobium goldschmidtianum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It prefers bright, indirect light and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical or subtropical climate.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
