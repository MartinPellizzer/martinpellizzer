Diplomorpha uva-ursi

Diplomorpha uva-ursi, commonly known as bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for wildlife, including bears and birds. Its leaves contain arbutin, a compound with antimicrobial properties.

Diplomorpha uva-ursi thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.
