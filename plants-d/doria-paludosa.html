Doria paludosa

Doria paludosa, commonly known as the marsh doria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It typically grows in marshy or boggy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the soil in its wetland habitat.

Doria paludosa plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
