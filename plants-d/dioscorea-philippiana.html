Dioscorea philippiana

Dioscorea philippiana, commonly known as the Philippine yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has palmate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tubers are an important food source in many regions and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
