Disocactus flagelliformis

Disocactus flagelliformis, commonly known as the whip cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a climbing cactus with a distinctive, segmented stem that can grow quite long. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
