Disocactus ackermannii

Disocactus ackermannii, commonly known as the Ghost Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
