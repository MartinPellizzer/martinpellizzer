Durabaculum warianum

Durabaculum warianum, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Durabaculum warianum is well adapted to harsh climates, making it a resilient addition to xeriscaped gardens.
