Dendrophthora cupressoides var. polyarthra

Dendrophthora cupressoides var. polyarthra, commonly known as the giant sequoia, is a variant of the giant sequoia tree, native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its massive size, with some trees reaching heights of over 300 feet and diameters exceeding 10 feet. The tree has a distinctive reddish-brown bark that is thick and furrowed, providing protection against wildfires. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a vital role in its ecosystem, offering habitat for numerous wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of its native forests.
