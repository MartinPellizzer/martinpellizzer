Dictamnus albus var. albiflos

Dictamnus albus var. albiflos, commonly known as the white dictamnus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has fragrant, reddish-brown stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
