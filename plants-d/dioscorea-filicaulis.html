Dioscorea filicaulis

Dioscorea filicaulis, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, aerial stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The tubers of Dioscorea filicaulis are edible when properly prepared and are an important food source in some traditional communities.
