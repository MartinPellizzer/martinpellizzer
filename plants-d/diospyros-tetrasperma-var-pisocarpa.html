Diospyros tetrasperma var. pisocarpa

Diospyros tetrasperma var. pisocarpa, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
