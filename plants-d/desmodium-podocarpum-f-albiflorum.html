Desmodium podocarpum f. albiflorum

Desmodium podocarpum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered cowpea, is a variant of the species Desmodium podocarpum.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, pea-like pods. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This subspecies is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
