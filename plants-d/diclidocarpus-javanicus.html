Diclidocarpus javanicus

Diclidocarpus javanicus, commonly known as the Java nut or Malabar chestnut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that contain a single, heavy, chestnut-like seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
