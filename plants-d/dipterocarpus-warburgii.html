Dipterocarpus warburgii

Dipterocarpus warburgii, commonly known as the Malacca ironwood, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a long lifespan, often reaching several centuries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural distribution in forest ecosystems.
