Dactylorchis incarnata var. extensa

Dactylorchis incarnata var. extensa, commonly known as the broad-leafed lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to eastern North America.

It features a tall, slender stem with numerous small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a loose, loose raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often broader than those of the typical variety.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its delicate, grass-like appearance.
