Delphinium consolida f. mollis

Delphinium consolida f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-lip delphinium, is a cultivated form of the delphinium plant characterized by its soft, velvety petals.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems with large, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade.
