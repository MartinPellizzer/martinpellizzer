Dasiphora fruticosa var. appendiculata

Dasiphora fruticosa var. appendiculata, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features wiry, often prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.
