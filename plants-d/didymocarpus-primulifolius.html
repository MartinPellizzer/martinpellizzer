Didymocarpus primulifolius

Didymocarpus primulifolius, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, yellow fruits with a tart flavor. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal value.
