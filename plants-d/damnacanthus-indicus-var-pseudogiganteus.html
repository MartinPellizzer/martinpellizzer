Damnacanthus indicus var. pseudogiganteus

Damnacanthus indicus var. pseudogiganteus, commonly known as the giant flame grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its vibrant, flame-like red and orange bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
