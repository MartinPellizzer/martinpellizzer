Deutzia tozawae

Deutzia tozawae, commonly known as the Japanese deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features slender, arching branches. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and turn yellow in autumn.

Deutzia tozawae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
