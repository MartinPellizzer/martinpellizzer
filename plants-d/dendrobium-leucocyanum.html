Dendrobium leucocyanum

Dendrobium leucocyanum, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, erect stem covered with numerous elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale blue flowers with prominent yellow veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
