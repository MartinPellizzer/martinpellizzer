Draba incrassata

Draba incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in harsh environments.

Draba incrassata is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of high-altitude ecosystems.
