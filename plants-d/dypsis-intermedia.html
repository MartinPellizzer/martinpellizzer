Dypsis intermedia

Dypsis intermedia, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, spiny trunk and clusters of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Dypsis intermedia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
