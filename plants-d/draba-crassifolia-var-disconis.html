Draba crassifolia var. disconis

Draba crassifolia var. disconis, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features dense, rosette-like foliage composed of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh environmental conditions.
