Diospyros acapulcensis subsp. pedromorenoi

Diospyros acapulcensis subsp.

pedromorenoi, commonly known as the Pedro Moreno persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and astringent fruits.

Its fruits are typically harvested when fully ripe, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
