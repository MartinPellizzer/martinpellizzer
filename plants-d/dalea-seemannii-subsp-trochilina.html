Dalea seemannii subsp. trochilina

Dalea seemannii subsp.

trochilina, commonly known as the Texas mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The subspecies trochilina is distinguished by its more robust growth and broader leaves compared to other subspecies of Dalea seemannii.
