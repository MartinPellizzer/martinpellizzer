Dioclea malacocarpa

Dioclea malacocarpa, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often clustered in umbels. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Dioclea malacocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
