Dendrobium sclerophyllum

Dendrobium sclerophyllum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and dense clusters of pale green to brownish-green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with red or purple markings, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium sclerophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
