Diplacus splendidus

Diplacus splendidus, commonly known as the scarlet gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, adding a delicate texture to the plant's appearance.

Diplacus splendidus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
