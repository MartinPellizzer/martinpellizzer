Dichocarpum hypoglaucum

Dichocarpum hypoglaucum, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features a tall, slender stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are showy, bell-shaped, and usually blue or purple in color, appearing in clusters.

Dichocarpum hypoglaucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
