Dregea volubilis var. lacuna

Dregea volubilis var. lacuna, commonly known as the lacunata dregea, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and produces striking yellow flowers with prominent orange stamens. The plant is known for its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative vine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both horticultural and ecological studies.
