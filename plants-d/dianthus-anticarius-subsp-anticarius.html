Dianthus anticarius subsp. anticarius

Dianthus anticarius subsp.

anticarius, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with a distinctive white or pale pink center.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round fruits called capsules.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
