Draba lasiophylla var. leiocarpa

Draba lasiophylla var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky, arid, or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, smooth leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower clusters.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, disturbed sites such as gravelly slopes and dry meadows.
