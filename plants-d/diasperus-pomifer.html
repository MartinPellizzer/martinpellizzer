Diasperus pomifer

Diasperus pomifer, commonly known as the red-fruited pomegranate, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that produces distinctive, red, fleshy fruits resembling those of the true pomegranate. The plant's botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit contains numerous seeds surrounded by a juicy, edible pulp.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
