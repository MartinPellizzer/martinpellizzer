Dioscorea lutea

Dioscorea lutea, commonly known as yellow snakeroot, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its yellowish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
