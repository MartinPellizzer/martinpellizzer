Diplocaulobium phalangium

Diplocaulobium phalangium, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag arrangement of its leaf blades, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle, with small, spikelet-like flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage structure.
