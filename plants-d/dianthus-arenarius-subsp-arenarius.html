Dianthus arenarius subsp. arenarius

Dianthus arenarius subsp.

arenarius, commonly known as the sand pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are usually branched and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
