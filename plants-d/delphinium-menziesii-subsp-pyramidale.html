Delphinium menziesii subsp. pyramidale

Delphinium menziesii subsp.

pyramidale, commonly known as the mountain larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with spires of blue to purple flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
