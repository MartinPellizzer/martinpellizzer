Dipterocarpus lamellatus

Dipterocarpus lamellatus, commonly known as the flat-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, flat leaves and heavy, aromatic wood. The plant is typically found in lowland rainforests and is an important component of the forest canopy. It produces small, yellowish flowers and red, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals.

The species is valued for its timber and plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
