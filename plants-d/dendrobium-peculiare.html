Dendrobium peculiare

Dendrobium peculiare, commonly known as the orchid of the gods, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces tall, branched stems covered with small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a greenish throat and are fragrant.

Dendrobium peculiare is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
