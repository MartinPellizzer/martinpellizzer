Dactylorchis maculata

Dactylorchis maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. The plant features a short, upright stem with several narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white with purple or brown markings, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dactylorchis maculata is a perennial herb that reproduces through seeds and has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.
