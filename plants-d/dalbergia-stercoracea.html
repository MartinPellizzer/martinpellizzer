Dalbergia stercoracea

Dalbergia stercoracea, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers.

Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals, contributing to its natural distribution.
