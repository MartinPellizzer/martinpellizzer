Dendrochilum imitator

Dendrochilum imitator, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species of orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble other orchid species, hence its common name. The flowers typically bloom in clusters and are known for their intricate patterns and vibrant hues.

Dendrochilum imitator prefers shaded, humid environments and is often epiphytic, growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.
