Draba kamtschatica var. yezoensis

Draba kamtschatica var. yezoensis, commonly known as Yezo Draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically between March and May. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often found in rocky soils and meadows.

This variety is native to the northern regions of Japan, including Hokkaido, and is adapted to cold climates.
