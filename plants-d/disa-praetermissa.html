Disa praetermissa

Disa praetermissa, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pink or purple blooms with intricate patterns, and it thrives in woodland and rocky habitats. Disa praetermissa is a perennial herb that prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
