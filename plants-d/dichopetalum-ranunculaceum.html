Dichopetalum ranunculaceum

Dichopetalum ranunculaceum, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are surrounded by sepals that resemble leaves.

The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
