Delphinium laxicymosum

Delphinium laxicymosum, commonly known as the slender larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its flowers are typically arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care, as all parts of the plant contain alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
