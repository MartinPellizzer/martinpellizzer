Dypsis ligulata

Dypsis ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf areca, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are narrow and elongated. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are used in local cuisines.

Dypsis ligulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
