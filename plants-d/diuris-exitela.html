Diuris exitela

Diuris exitela, commonly known as the pink-tongued orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a slender, hairy stem and produces striking, pinkish-brown flowers with a distinctive, elongated labellum. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms during the cooler months of spring and autumn.

The species is part of the orchid family Orchidaceae and is valued for its unique floral structure and ornamental appeal.
