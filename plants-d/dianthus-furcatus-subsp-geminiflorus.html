Dianthus furcatus subsp. geminiflorus

Dianthus furcatus subsp.

geminiflorus, commonly known as the twin-flowered pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers.

The plant produces two rows of flowers along the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
