Dombeya subsquamosa

Dombeya subsquamosa, commonly known as the silky dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Dombeya subsquamosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
