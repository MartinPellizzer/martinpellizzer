Dianthus volgensis

Dianthus volgensis, commonly known as the mountain carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits called capsules.

Dianthus volgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
