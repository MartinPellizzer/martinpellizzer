Dianthera hirsuta

Dianthera hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dianthera hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
