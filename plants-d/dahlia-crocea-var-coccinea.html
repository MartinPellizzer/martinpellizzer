Dahlia crocea var. coccinea

Dahlia crocea var. coccinea, commonly known as the red dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, single or semi-double flowers that range in color from deep red to orange-red. The plant produces showy, tubular petals that are typically 5-8 cm in length, with a prominent central disk. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering and fertilization for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens for its vibrant blooms and is often used in floral arrangements.
