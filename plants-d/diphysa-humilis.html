Diphysa humilis

Diphysa humilis, commonly known as the dwarf diphysa, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the rainy season.

Diphysa humilis is often found in low-light conditions and requires consistent moisture to thrive.
