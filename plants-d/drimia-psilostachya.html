Drimia psilostachya

Drimia psilostachya, commonly known as the slender bulbous plant, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and linear, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces white to pale purple flowers in summer, arranged in umbels at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
