Dimorphocalyx moluccensis

Dimorphocalyx moluccensis, commonly known as the Moluccan flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and a distinctive, fluted trunk that becomes more prominent with age. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also notable for its ability to produce fruits that are edible for certain bird species.
