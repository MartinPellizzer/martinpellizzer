Dactylorhiza scampstonii

Dactylorhiza scampstonii, commonly known as the Scampston's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the UK and parts of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and produces tall spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is adapted to calcareous soils and thrives in damp, shaded environments. It is a perennial species that blooms from June to August.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and protected plant in many regions.
