Dianthus hymenolepis

Dianthus hymenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
