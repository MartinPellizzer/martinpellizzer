Dombeya punctatopsis var. subfurcata

Dombeya punctatopsis var. subfurcata, commonly known as the spotted dombeya, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
