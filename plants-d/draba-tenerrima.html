Draba tenerrima

Draba tenerrima, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with basal leaves and clusters of small, white or yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh alpine environments, featuring a compact growth form and a deep root system to survive cold, nutrient-poor soils. Draba tenerrima is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important plants.
