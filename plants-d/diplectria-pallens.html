Diplectria pallens

Diplectria pallens, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
