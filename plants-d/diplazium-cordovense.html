Diplazium cordovense

Diplazium cordovense, commonly known as the Cordovense water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Diplazium cordovense plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
