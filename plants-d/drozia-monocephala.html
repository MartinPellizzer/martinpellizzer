Drozia monocephala

Drozia monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed drozia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a single, prominent flower at the top of a slender stem, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring cool and moist conditions.

Drozia monocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
