Dyckia meziana subsp. vogtii

Dyckia meziana subsp.

vogtii, commonly known as the Vogt's dyckia, is a bromeliad native to Brazil. It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces striking red or orange inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its appeal among succulent enthusiasts.
