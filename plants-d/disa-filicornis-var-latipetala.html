Disa filicornis var. latipetala

Disa filicornis var. latipetala, commonly known as the broad-leaved disa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that aids in water retention.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
