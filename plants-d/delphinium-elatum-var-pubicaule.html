Delphinium elatum var. pubicaule

Delphinium elatum var. pubicaule, commonly known as the spiked larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of spurred, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and alpine gardens.

This variety is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
