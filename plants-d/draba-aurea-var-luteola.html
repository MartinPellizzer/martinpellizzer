Draba aurea var. luteola

Draba aurea var. luteola, commonly known as the yellow whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Draba aurea var. luteola is adapted to alpine and arctic environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
