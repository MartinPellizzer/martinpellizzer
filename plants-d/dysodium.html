Dysodium

Dysodium, commonly known as sodium disilicate, is a compound rather than a plant, so it does not have botanical aspects.

It is a chemical compound composed of sodium, silicon, and oxygen. Sodium disilicate is often used in industrial applications, such as in the production of detergents and glass. It is not a living organism and therefore does not have roots, stems, leaves, or other plant structures.

As a result, it cannot be classified as a plant species or studied in the context of botanical science.
