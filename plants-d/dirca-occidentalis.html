Dirca occidentalis

Dirca occidentalis, commonly known as western twinflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
