Darea fumarioides

Darea fumarioides, commonly known as the smoky bamboo or false smoketree, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble smoke, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Darea fumarioides is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking, feathery appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
