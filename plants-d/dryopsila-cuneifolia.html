Dryopsila cuneifolia

Dryopsila cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dryopsila, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
