Diodia littoralis

Diodia littoralis, commonly known as sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or muddy soils along the shoreline. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by red stamens. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Diodia littoralis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
