Dendrobium bigibbum subvar. candidum

Dendrobium bigibbum subvar. candidum, commonly known as the White Wandering Orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil. The plant features a tall, cane-like stem with clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is popular in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
