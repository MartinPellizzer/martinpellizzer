Diospyros ellipticifolia var. elliptica

Diospyros ellipticifolia var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic-leaf persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully mature. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
