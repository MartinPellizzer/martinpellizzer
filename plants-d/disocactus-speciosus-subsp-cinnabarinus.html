Disocactus speciosus subsp. cinnabarinus

Disocactus speciosus subsp.

cinnabarinus, commonly known as the red-tipped cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red at the tips, giving it its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique coloration and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor plant collections.
