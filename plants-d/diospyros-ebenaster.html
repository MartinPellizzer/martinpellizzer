Diospyros ebenaster

Diospyros ebenaster, commonly known as the African ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to black when mature, containing a single hard seed.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is known for its ecological and economic importance in its native region.
