Dianthus occidentalis var. arvaticus

Dianthus occidentalis var. arvaticus, commonly known as the western sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
