Drimia glaucescens

Drimia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-tinged African lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces striking white or pale blue flowers in summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Drimia glaucescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
