Diblemma tenuiloris

Diblemma tenuiloris, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, which it then digests for nutrients. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor waters, where its carnivorous adaptations provide a crucial survival advantage. Its leaves are thin and delicate, with small, translucent bladders that inflate to capture prey.

Diblemma tenuiloris plays an important ecological role in its environment by controlling microfauna populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
