Dioscorea pentaphylla var. communis

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. communis, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it relies on insect pollination.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been used for centuries in herbal remedies and as a food source.
