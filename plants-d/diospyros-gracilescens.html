Diospyros gracilescens

Diospyros gracilescens, commonly known as the dwarf persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional crafts and construction.
