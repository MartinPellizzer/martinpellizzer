Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. transsilvanica

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and heathland habitats.

The plant features a rhizome with several leaf-like sheaths and produces tall, slender flowering stems. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a distinctive spotted pattern on the dorsal sepal and petals.

It is a protected species in many European countries due to its declining populations.
