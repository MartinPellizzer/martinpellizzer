Dianthus zeyheri

Dianthus zeyheri, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus zeyheri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
