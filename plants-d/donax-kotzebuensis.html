Donax kotzebuensis

Donax kotzebuensis, commonly known as the Arctic sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant thrives in harsh, coastal environments and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are typically narrow and dark green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Donax kotzebuensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing food for wildlife.
