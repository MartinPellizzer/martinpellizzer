Dendrobium morrisonii

Dendrobium morrisonii, commonly known as the Morrison's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often branching and covered in a dense layer of roots. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, warm climates with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium morrisonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
