Delphinium peregrinum var. elongatum

Delphinium peregrinum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, adorned with striking blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is characterized by its elongated corolla, which sets it apart from other delphinium species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens and rockeries.
