Diospyros kanizur

Diospyros kanizur, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to reddish fruit that is edible when fully mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
