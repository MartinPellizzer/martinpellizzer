Daphne tarton-raira var. angustifolia

Daphne tarton-raira var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf daphne, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers appear in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
