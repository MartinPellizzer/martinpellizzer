Delphinium cheilanthum

Delphinium cheilanthum, commonly known as the golden beard delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features striking, spires of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive yellowish-white midrib. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Delphinium cheilanthum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its role in attracting pollinators.
