Dianthus monadelphus subsp. pallens

Dianthus monadelphus subsp.

pallens, commonly known as the pale carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers with a distinctive, frilled appearance.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies pallens is distinguished by its lighter flower color compared to other varieties within the Dianthus monadelphus species complex.
