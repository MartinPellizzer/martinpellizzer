Dalechampia peckoltiana

Dalechampia peckoltiana, commonly known as the Mexican rosette plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette formation of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a terminal inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
