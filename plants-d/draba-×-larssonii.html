Draba × larssonii

Draba × larssonii, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically arranged in racemes. It is native to mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
