Dendrobium × schumannianum

Dendrobium × schumannianum, commonly known as the Schumann's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces long, cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length, and it blooms with clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Its flowers are often tubular or spurred, with three sepals and three petals, and it requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Dendrobium × schumannianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
