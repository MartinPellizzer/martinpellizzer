Dypsis hovomantsina

Dypsis hovomantsina, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its robust, tree-like structure with a thick, trunk-like stem. The plant features a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits.

Dypsis hovomantsina is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
