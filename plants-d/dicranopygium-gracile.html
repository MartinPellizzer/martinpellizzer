Dicranopygium gracile

Dicranopygium gracile, commonly known as the slender feather moss, is a small, creeping moss that forms dense, mat-like growths.

It belongs to the family Dicranaceae and is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves that are often curved. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are typically small and inconspicuous.

Dicranopygium gracile plays an important role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
