Didymocarpus formosus

Didymocarpus formosus, commonly known as the Taiwan persimmon, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to Taiwan.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts within its native habitat.
