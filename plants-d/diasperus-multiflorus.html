Diasperus multiflorus

Diasperus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered grassveld, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with numerous small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has fibrous roots that help it survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are arranged in compact heads, giving it a compact appearance.

Diasperus multiflorus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
