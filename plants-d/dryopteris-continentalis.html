Dryopteris continentalis

Dryopteris continentalis, commonly known as the Eastern wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters long. The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive pattern of dark green and lighter green segments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This fern is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
