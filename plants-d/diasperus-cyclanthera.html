Diasperus cyclanthera

Diasperus cyclanthera, commonly known as the African horned melon, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive horn-like protrusion on the stem. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to large, spiky, greenish-yellow fruits. The fruit's exterior is covered in sharp, pointed spines, and its interior contains a sweet, juicy pulp with black seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique edible fruit.
