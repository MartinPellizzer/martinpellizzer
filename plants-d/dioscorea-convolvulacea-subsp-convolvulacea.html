Dioscorea convolvulacea subsp. convolvulacea

Dioscorea convolvulacea subsp.

convolvulacea, commonly known as the common yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are widely cultivated for their edible roots, which are rich in starch.

The botanical features of this species make it an important crop in many parts of the world.
