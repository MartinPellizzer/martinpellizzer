Dendrochilum papillilabium

Dendrochilum papillilabium, commonly known as the papillilabium orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, often pink or purple flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
