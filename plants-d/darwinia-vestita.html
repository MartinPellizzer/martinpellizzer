Darwinia vestita

Darwinia vestita, commonly known as the silver-topped darwinia, is a flowering plant native to south-eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-tipped leaves and spikes of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. It is an evergreen shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Darwinia vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
