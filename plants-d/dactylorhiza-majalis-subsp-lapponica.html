Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. lapponica

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northern regions of Europe and Siberia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic bogs and heathlands, thriving in cool, shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

lapponica is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native habitats.
