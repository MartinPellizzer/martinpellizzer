Dianthus glabriusculus subsp. moldavicus

Dianthus glabriusculus subsp.

moldavicus, commonly known as the Moldavian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem and basal rosette of leaves.

The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent white or pink markings. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
