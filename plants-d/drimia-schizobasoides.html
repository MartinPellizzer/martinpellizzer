Drimia schizobasoides

Drimia schizobasoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved African lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces striking white or pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Drimia schizobasoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
