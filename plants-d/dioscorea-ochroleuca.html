Dioscorea ochroleuca

Dioscorea ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-veined yam, is a tropical vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used as food. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally cultivated for their nutritional value.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
