Dracontium cordatum

Dracontium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy food source, similar to traditional arrowroot.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible roots.
