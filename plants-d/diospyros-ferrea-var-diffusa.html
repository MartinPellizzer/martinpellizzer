Diospyros ferrea var. diffusa

Diospyros ferrea var. diffusa, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its thick, dark bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruit, which is edible when fully mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is rich in nutrients.
