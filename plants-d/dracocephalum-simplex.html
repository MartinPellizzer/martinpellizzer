Dracocephalum simplex

Dracocephalum simplex, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's name derives from the Greek words for "dragon's head," referring to the shape of its flowers.
