Dyschoriste jasminum

Dyschoriste jasminum, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Dyschoriste jasminum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
