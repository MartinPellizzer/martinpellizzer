Diospyros quaesita

Diospyros quaesita, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, though it contains a single hard seed, and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
