Diospyros dubia

Diospyros dubia, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are typically clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible when ripe.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
