Dichanthelium auburne

Dichanthelium auburne, commonly known as Auburn grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and moist soils, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the late spring to early summer, and it is often found in disturbed or transitional habitats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive auricle-like structures and pale golden to reddish hues in its foliage and inflorescences.
