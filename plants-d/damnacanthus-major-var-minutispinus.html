Damnacanthus major var. minutispinus

Damnacanthus major var. minutispinus, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and hairy stems, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
