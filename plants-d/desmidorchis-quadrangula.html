Desmidorchis quadrangula

Desmidorchis quadrangula, commonly known as the four-angled orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique quadrangular stem and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is typically found in rocky or limestone soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many parts of its range.
