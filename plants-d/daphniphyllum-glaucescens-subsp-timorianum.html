Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp. timorianum

Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp.

timorianum, commonly known as the Timor holly, is a subtropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in horticulture.
