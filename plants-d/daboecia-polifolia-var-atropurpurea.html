Daboecia polifolia var. atropurpurea

Daboecia polifolia var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaved rock heath, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with deep purple to reddish hues on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to harsh environmental conditions.
