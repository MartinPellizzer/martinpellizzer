Douma thebaica

Douma thebaica, commonly known as the African date palm, is a tropical plant native to the savannas of Africa.

It is a tall, robust palm with a straight trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, edible fruits that resemble dates, which are rich in nutrients. Its roots are adapted to tolerate dry conditions, making it resilient in arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in some regions.
