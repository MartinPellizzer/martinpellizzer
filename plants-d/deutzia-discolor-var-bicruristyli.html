Deutzia discolor var. bicruristyli

Deutzia discolor var. bicruristyli, commonly known as the whiteflower deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of its heart-shaped leaves. The plant has a compact growth habit with multiple stems, making it suitable for small gardens or as a border plant. Its foliage turns a striking red or purple in the autumn, adding seasonal interest.

The variety bicruristyli is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
