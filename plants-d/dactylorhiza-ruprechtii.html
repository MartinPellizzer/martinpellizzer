Dactylorhiza ruprechtii

Dactylorhiza ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and erect, leafy stems. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Dactylorhiza ruprechtii is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators and its role in wetland ecosystems.
