Draba subsessilis

Draba subsessilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring season.

Draba subsessilis is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
