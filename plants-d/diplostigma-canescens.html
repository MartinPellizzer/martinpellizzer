Diplostigma canescens

Diplostigma canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are hairy and upright, often branching near the top to support the flower heads.

Diplostigma canescens is typically found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy and resilient species.
