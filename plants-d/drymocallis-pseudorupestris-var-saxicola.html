Drymocallis pseudorupestris var. saxicola

Drymocallis pseudorupestris var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or woodland edges. The plant features upright, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions and its drought tolerance.
