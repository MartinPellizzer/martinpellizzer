Dioscorea lundii

Dioscorea lundii, commonly known as the African yam, is a tuberous vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its tubers are an important food source in many regions and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to its native environment, thriving in warm, humid conditions.
