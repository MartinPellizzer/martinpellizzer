Deyeuxia chrysantha var. phalaroides

Deyeuxia chrysantha var. phalaroides, commonly known as the golden millet, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to golden in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
