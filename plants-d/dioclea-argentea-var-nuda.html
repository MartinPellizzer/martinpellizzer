Dioclea argentea var. nuda

Dioclea argentea var. nuda, commonly known as silverleaf spiderplant, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for green walls and hanging baskets.
