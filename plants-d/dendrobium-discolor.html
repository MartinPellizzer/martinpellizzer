Dendrobium discolor

Dendrobium discolor, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with its stems forming a rosette-like structure and often growing horizontally or pendulously. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in two rows along the stems.

Dendrobium discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
