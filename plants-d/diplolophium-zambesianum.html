Diplolophium zambesianum

Diplolophium zambesianum, commonly known as the Zambesian double-lipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with two distinct lip lobes, which are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of orchid morphology and ecological interactions.
