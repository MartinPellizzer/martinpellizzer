Desmodium amethystinum

Desmodium amethystinum, commonly known as the purple burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, purple or violet flowers that attract pollinators. Its seed pods are hairy and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Desmodium amethystinum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
