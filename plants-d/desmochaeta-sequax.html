Desmochaeta sequax

Desmochaeta sequax, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant has silvery-gray foliage covered in fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Desmochaeta sequax is well adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with minimal water availability.
