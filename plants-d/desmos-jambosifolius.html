Desmos jambosifolius

Desmos jambosifolius, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and produces elongated, flat fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and rapid growth rate.
