Delphinium simonkaianum

Delphinium simonkaianum, commonly known as the Simonkaian larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture that aids in water retention in arid conditions.

Delphinium simonkaianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
