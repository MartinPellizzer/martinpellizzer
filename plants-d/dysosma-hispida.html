Dysosma hispida

Dysosma hispida, commonly known as the hairy dysosma, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and rhombic-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
