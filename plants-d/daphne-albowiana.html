Daphne albowiana

Daphne albowiana, commonly known as the albow's daphne, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its winter interest and aromatic qualities.
