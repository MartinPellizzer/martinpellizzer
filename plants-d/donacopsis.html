Donacopsis

Donacopsis, commonly known as the giant reed or giant cane, is a tall, perennial grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height, making it one of the tallest grasses in the world. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the top of the stalks. Donacopsis thrives in wetland environments, often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an important ecological component in aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
