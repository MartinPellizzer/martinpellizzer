Dioscorea kamoonensis

Dioscorea kamoonensis, commonly known as Kamoon wild yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and a fibrous root system.

The tubers of D. kamoonensis are valued for their potential medicinal properties and are sometimes used in herbal remedies.
