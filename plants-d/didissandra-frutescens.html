Didissandra frutescens

Didissandra frutescens, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
