Diacidia aracaensis

Diacidia aracaensis, commonly known as the Acahuizal, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

Diacidia aracaensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
