Dracocephalum purpureum

Dracocephalum purpureum, commonly known as purple dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, square stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in summer. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties.

Dracocephalum purpureum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
