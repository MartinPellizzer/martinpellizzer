Dalechampia scandens

Dalechampia scandens, commonly known as the silverleaf dalechampia, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and growth habit make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
