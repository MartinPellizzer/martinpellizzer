Danaea danaepinna

Danaea danaepinna, commonly known as the red flame orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. Danaea danaepinna typically grows as an epiphytic or lithophytic plant, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments.

Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in both natural and cultivated settings.
