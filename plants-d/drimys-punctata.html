Drimys punctata

Drimys punctata, commonly known as the Chilean wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate rainforests and coastal areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and the plant is known for its aromatic properties.

Drimys punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
