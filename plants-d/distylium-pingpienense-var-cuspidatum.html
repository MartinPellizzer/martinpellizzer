Distylium pingpienense var. cuspidatum

Distylium pingpienense var. cuspidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved distylium, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
