Dracontium bogneri

Dracontium bogneri, commonly known as the Bogner's arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Dracontium bogneri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and resilience in tropical climates.
