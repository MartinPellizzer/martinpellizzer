Draba magellanica var. saffordii

Draba magellanica var. saffordii, commonly known as the Safford's mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of four-petaled white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native species contributing to local biodiversity and ecological stability.
