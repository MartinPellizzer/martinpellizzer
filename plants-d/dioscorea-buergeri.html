Dioscorea buergeri

Dioscorea buergeri, commonly known as Buerger's yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and later develops capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various herbal remedies.
