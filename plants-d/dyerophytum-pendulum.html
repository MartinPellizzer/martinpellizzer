Dyerophytum pendulum

Dyerophytum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in suitable climates.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
