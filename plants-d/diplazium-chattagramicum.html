Diplazium chattagramicum

Diplazium chattagramicum, commonly known as the hill brake fern, is a tropical evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched fronds with a serrated or lobed margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
