Disocactus × hybridus

Disocactus × hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Disocactus flagelliformis and Disocactus phyllophylloides.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the summer months. It is native to Mexico and thrives in warm, arid environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
