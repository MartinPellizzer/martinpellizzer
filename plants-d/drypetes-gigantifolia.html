Drypetes gigantifolia

Drypetes gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
