Disa similis

Disa similis, commonly known as the common disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Disa similis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
