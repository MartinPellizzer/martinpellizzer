Dalbergia rufotomentosa

Dalbergia rufotomentosa, commonly known as the red-tomentose ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and distinctive reddish-brown bark that is often covered in a fine, hairy coating. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it flowers with small, yellowish-white blooms.

The tree is valued for its high-quality timber, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
