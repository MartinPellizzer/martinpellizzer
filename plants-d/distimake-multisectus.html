Distimake multisectus

Distimake multisectus, commonly known as the fragmented leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its intricate leaf structure.

Its leaves are deeply divided into multiple segments, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring rich, loamy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked in favor of its striking foliage.

Due to its rare habitat and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
