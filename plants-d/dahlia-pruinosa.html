Dahlia pruinosa

Dahlia pruinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, single or semi-collinear flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its tuberous roots are an important part of its growth cycle, allowing it to survive in various soil conditions.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
