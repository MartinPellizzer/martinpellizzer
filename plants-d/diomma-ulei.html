Diomma ulei

Diomma ulei, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its growth pattern is typically prostrate, forming a dense mat that helps prevent soil erosion.

Diomma ulei is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
