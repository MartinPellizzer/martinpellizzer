Dioscorea bicolor

Dioscorea bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive two-colored stems, often with alternating light and dark green patterns. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length.

Dioscorea bicolor is valued both for its ornamental appearance and for its potential medicinal properties.
