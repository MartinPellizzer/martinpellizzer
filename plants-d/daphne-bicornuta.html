Daphne bicornuta

Daphne bicornuta, commonly known as the two-horned daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has dark green, glossy leaves. Its stems are often covered in a powdery white coating, and the flowers come in shades of pink, white, and purple.

Daphne bicornuta is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
