Dalea eosina

Dalea eosina, commonly known as scarlet prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Dalea eosina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in native landscapes.
