Davidia involucrata

Davidia involucrata, commonly known as the handkerchief tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-like white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate and dark green in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, often surrounded by silky hairs.

Davidia involucrata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
