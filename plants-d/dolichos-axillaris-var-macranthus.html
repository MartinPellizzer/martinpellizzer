Dolichos axillaris var. macranthus

Dolichos axillaris var. macranthus, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a fibrous root system.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
