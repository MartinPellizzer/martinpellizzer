Dianthus andrzejejowskianus

Dianthus andrzejejowskianus, commonly known as the Andrzejejowski's carnation, is a species native to the Carpathian Mountains in Eastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The flowers typically feature five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white, and are arranged in dense clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
