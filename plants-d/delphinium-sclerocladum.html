Delphinium sclerocladum

Delphinium sclerocladum, commonly known as the hard-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are thick and leathery, giving it the common name "hard-leaved.".

Delphinium sclerocladum is adapted to high-altitude environments and prefers well-drained, rocky soils.
