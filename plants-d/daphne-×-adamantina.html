Daphne × adamantina

Daphne × adamantina, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, waxy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that provide an elegant backdrop to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Daphne × adamantina is relatively low maintenance but requires protection from late frosts to ensure successful flowering.
