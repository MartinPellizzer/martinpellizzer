Dimorphandra guianensis

Dimorphandra guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan candlewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like branching pattern. The plant has compound leaves with alternating leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are dispersed by animals.

Dimorphandra guianensis is valued for its hard wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
