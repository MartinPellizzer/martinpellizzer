Dyckia gilliesii

Dyckia gilliesii, commonly known as the Blue Dyckia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rosette-forming structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking blue or purple flower spikes that emerge from the center of the rosette. It is well adapted to dry environments and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dyckia gilliesii is also noted for its low maintenance requirements and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
