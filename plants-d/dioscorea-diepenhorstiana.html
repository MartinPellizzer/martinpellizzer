Dioscorea diepenhorstiana

Dioscorea diepenhorstiana, commonly known as Diepenhorst's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and vine-like.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
