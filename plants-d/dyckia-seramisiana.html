Dyckia seramisiana

Dyckia seramisiana, commonly known as the Seram Island Dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Seram Island in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, white or pale pink flowers, which are clustered in a tight, rounded spike. Dyckia seramisiana is adapted to grow in sunny, well-drained environments, often on rocky substrates.

It is a relatively slow-growing plant that is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of care.
