Dichostemma amplum

Dichostemma amplum, commonly known as the broadleaf dichostemma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Dichostemma amplum is also known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
