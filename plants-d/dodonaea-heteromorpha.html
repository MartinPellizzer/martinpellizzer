Dodonaea heteromorpha

Dodonaea heteromorpha, commonly known as the variable hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often glandular leaves that vary in shape and size. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by distinctive, woody, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping.
