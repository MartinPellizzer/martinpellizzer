Dyssodia neomexicana var. pulcherrima

Dyssodia neomexicana var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the Mexican beautyflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid landscapes for its resilience and visual impact.
