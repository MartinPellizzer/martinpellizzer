Dactylorhiza sooi

Dactylorhiza sooi, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes and meadows. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers.

Dactylorhiza sooi is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
