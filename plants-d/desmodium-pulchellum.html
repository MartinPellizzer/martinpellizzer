Desmodium pulchellum

Desmodium pulchellum, commonly known as the pretty burr or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, hairy pods that contain several seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
