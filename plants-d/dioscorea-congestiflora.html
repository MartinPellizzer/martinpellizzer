Dioscorea congestiflora

Dioscorea congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered yam, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supporting structures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional agriculture and local ecosystems.
