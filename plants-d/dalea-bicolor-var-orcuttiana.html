Dalea bicolor var. orcuttiana

Dalea bicolor var. orcuttiana, commonly known as the Orcutt's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
