Drypetes obtusa

Drypetes obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard timber and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing effects.
