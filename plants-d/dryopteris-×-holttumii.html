Dryopteris × holttumii

Dryopteris × holttumii, commonly known as the hybrid wood fern, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Dryopteris carpathica and Dryopteris octopleura.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, with a feathery appearance and a dark green coloration. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, architectural form.
