Dioscorea quartiniana var. hochstetteri

Dioscorea quartiniana var. hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and nutritional purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in various African cultures for treating ailments such as inflammation and fever.
