Dillenia ornata

Dillenia ornata, commonly known as the golden glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, pale bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its fruits are large, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Dillenia ornata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
