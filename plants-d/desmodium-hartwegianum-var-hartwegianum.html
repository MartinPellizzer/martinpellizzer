Desmodium hartwegianum var. hartwegianum

Desmodium hartwegianum var. hartwegianum, commonly known as the Hartwieg's burr legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant has small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Its root system is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
