Dorycnium germanicum

Dorycnium germanicum, commonly known as the German sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a taproot system and produces small, edible seeds that are used in traditional herbal medicine.

Dorycnium germanicum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
