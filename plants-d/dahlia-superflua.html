Dahlia superflua

Dahlia superflua, commonly known as the giant dahlia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, yellow, and white. The plant features a robust, upright stem and lance-shaped leaves that are deep green and slightly hairy. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive in various soil conditions, making it adaptable to different climates.

Dahlia superflua is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
