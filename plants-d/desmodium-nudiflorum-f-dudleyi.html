Desmodium nudiflorum f. dudleyi

Desmodium nudiflorum f. dudleyi, commonly known as the Dudley's false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
