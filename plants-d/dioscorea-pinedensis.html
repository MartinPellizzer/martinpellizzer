Dioscorea pinedensis

Dioscorea pinedensis, commonly known as the Pineda yam, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary vegetative structures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and are similar in appearance to other yam species.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
