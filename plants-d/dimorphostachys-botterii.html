Dimorphostachys botterii

Dimorphostachys botterii, commonly known as the Botter's dimorphostachys, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercialized.
