Dioscorea crateriflora

Dioscorea crateriflora, commonly known as the craterleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble a crater in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The tubers of D. crateriflora are valued for their potential medicinal properties and are sometimes utilized in local cuisines.
