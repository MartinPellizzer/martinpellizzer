Diospyros fragrans

Diospyros fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's fruit is a hairy, orange-red berry that ripens in autumn and is edible when fully mature. Diospyros fragrans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and aesthetic beauty.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
