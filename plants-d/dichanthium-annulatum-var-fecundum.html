Dichanthium annulatum var. fecundum

Dichanthium annulatum var. fecundum, commonly known as the prolific dwarf grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish florets. It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
