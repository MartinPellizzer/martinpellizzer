Derderia cheirifolia

Derderia cheirifolia, commonly known as the Moroccan rose or the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its ability to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its landscape. Its leaves are small and grayish-green, often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Derderia cheirifolia is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with dry climates.
