Dianthus pungens subsp. brachyanthus

Dianthus pungens subsp.

brachyanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with prominent stamens, which are a defining botanical feature. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
