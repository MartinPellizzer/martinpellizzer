Dugandiodendron splendens

Dugandiodendron splendens, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive bell-shaped fruit capsules. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
