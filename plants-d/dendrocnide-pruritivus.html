Dendrocnide pruritivus

Dendrocnide pruritivus, commonly known as the stinging tree, is a shrub or small tree native to northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes plants known for their stinging hairs. The plant is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that release a potent irritant when touched, causing severe itching and inflammation. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, irritating trichomes.

The stinging tree is often found in rainforest understories and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
