Dianthus turkestanicus

Dianthus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers with distinctive white veins. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Dianthus turkestanicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
