Dorycnium pentaphyllum proles decumbens

Dorycnium pentaphyllum proles decumbens, commonly known as the creeping yellow vetch, is a low-growing leguminous plant characterized by its decumbent stems that lie close to the ground.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces yellow flowers in racemate clusters. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. It has small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for ecological restoration and soil stabilization.
