Dissotidendron melleri var. greenwayi

Dissotidendron melleri var. greenwayi, commonly known as the Greenway's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly in regions with humid climates.
