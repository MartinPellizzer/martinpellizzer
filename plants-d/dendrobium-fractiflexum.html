Dendrobium fractiflexum

Dendrobium fractiflexum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in varying environmental conditions.

Dendrobium fractiflexum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
