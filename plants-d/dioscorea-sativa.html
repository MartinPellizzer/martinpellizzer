Dioscorea sativa

Dioscorea sativa, commonly known as yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been cultivated for centuries as a staple food in many cultures.

The plant also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
