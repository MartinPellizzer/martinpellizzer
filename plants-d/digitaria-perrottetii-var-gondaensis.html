Digitaria perrottetii var. gondaensis

Digitaria perrottetii var. gondaensis, commonly known as Gonda panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It is adapted to thrive in environments with moderate to high rainfall and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
