Dombeya goetzenii

Dombeya goetzenii, commonly known as the velvetleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically arranged in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Dombeya goetzenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
