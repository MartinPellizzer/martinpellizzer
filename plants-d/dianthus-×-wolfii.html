Dianthus × wolfii

Dianthus × wolfii, commonly known as the garden carnation, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive white or pink center. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, featuring upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus × wolfii is valued for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
