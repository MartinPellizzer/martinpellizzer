Dahlia popenovii

Dahlia popenovii, commonly known as the Popenov dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant produces large, single or double blooms with petals that are often ruffled or frilled. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Dahlia popenovii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as cut flowers.
