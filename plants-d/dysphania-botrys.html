Dysphania botrys

Dysphania botrys, commonly known as bladder campion, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, inflated stems that resemble bladders. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. Dysphania botrys is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields, and is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions.

It is sometimes considered a weed but has also been used in traditional herbal medicine.
