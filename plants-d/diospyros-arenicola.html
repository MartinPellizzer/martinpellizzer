Diospyros arenicola

Diospyros arenicola, commonly known as the sand dune persimmon, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are pale yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature.

Diospyros arenicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing food for local wildlife.
