Dioscorea sinuata var. crenata

Dioscorea sinuata var. crenata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the genus Dioscorea, which includes various species of yams, and is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are edible when properly prepared. Its growth habit involves twining stems that can reach several meters in length, often supported by other plants in its native habitat.

The botanical features of this variety make it notable for both its ornamental value and potential agricultural use.
