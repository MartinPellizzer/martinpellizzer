Decaloba bryonioides

Decaloba bryonioides, commonly known as the creeping bryony, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, often hairy stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. Its leaves are broadly ovate with prominent veins and may have a waxy coating.

Decaloba bryonioides is native to southern Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
