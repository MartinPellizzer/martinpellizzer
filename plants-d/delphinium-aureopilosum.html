Delphinium aureopilosum

Delphinium aureopilosum, commonly known as the golden-hair larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky, golden hairs, giving it its common name. Delphinium aureopilosum typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

It is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
