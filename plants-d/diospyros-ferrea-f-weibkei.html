Diospyros ferrea f. weibkei

Diospyros ferrea f. weibkei, commonly known as the ironwood or Brazilian cherry, is a variant of the persimmon genus native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dark, dense bark and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange-red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
