Dioscorea soso var. soso

Dioscorea soso var. soso, commonly known as the African yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, edible tubers that are widely cultivated for food. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, while its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers are rich in starch and are an important staple food in many African countries.
