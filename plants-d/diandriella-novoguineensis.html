Diandriella novoguineensis

Diandriella novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea diandriella, is a member of the family Diandriellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are unique, featuring specialized floral morphology that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
