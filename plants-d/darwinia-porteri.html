Darwinia porteri

Darwinia porteri, commonly known as the Darwinia of the southern coast, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tall spikes of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces showy inflorescences that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

Darwinia porteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
