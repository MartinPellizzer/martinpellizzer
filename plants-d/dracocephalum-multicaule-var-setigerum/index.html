Dracocephalum Multicaule Var. Setigerum

Blueweed, scientifically known as Dracocephalum multicaule var. setigerum, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. Its name derives from the Greek words for "dragon's head" and "to behead," referencing its unique floral structure. This perennial herb thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh conditions. With its vivid blue flowers and compact growth habit, it is a favorite among botanists and gardeners alike. Its ecological importance and ornamental value make it a noteworthy subject for further study and conservation efforts.
