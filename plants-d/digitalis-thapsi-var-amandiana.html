Digitalis thapsi var. amandiana

Digitalis thapsi var. amandiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of hooded flowers. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a velvety texture.

Digitalis thapsi var. amandiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soils with partial sunlight.
