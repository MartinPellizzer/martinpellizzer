Darea heterophylla

Darea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from basal rosettes to alternate leaves depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Darea heterophylla is adapted to arid climates and has a long flowering period, typically from spring to early summer.
