Diospyros nitida

Diospyros nitida, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves that provide year-round shade. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red fruits resembling small dates. Its hard, heavy wood is highly valued for construction and tool-making due to its durability and strength.

Diospyros nitida is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.
