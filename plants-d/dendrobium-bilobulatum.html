Dendrobium bilobulatum

Dendrobium bilobulatum, commonly known as the two-lobed dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid tropical climates. The plant features long, cane-like stems that are often branched and covered with clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium bilobulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
