Dracaena mastersii

Dracaena mastersii, commonly known as the Song of India, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It features long, narrow, dark green leaves that are often striped with white or yellow, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Dracaena mastersii is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ease of care.
