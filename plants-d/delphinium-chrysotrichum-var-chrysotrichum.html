Delphinium chrysotrichum var. chrysotrichum

Delphinium chrysotrichum var. chrysotrichum, commonly known as the golden beard delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of showy, blue to violet flowers with golden yellow anthers, which are particularly striking in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
