Dissochaeta horrida

Dissochaeta horrida, commonly known as the hairy dissochaeta, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
