Dentaria bulbifera var. ptarmicifolia

Dentaria bulbifera var. ptarmicifolia, commonly known as the purple toothed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive purplish tinge along the edges. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a long, slender stem. It blooms in early spring and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.

The variety ptarmicifolia is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape and coloration compared to the typical form.
