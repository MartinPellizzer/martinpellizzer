Dahlia × viridiflora

Dahlia × viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered dahlia, is a hybrid tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often green or variegated flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant produces showy, single or double blooms in a wide range of colors, including green, yellow, red, and purple. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and often have a slight pubescence.

Dahlia × viridiflora is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
