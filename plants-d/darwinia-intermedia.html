Darwinia intermedia

Darwinia intermedia, commonly known as the common darwinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red floral spikes that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, and its flowers are visited by various pollinators such as birds and insects.

Darwinia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
