Diuris punctata var. parvipetala

Diuris punctata var. parvipetala, commonly known as the small-lobed spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a compact, hairy rhizome and produces a single, erect stem with several narrow, linear leaves. The plant blooms with a striking, spider-like flower with a dark, hooded labellum and intricate patterns. It prefers well-drained soils in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller petals and a more delicate appearance compared to other Diuris species.
