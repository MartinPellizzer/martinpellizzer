Dendrocalamus pendulus

Dendrocalamus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that often droop downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and have a smooth texture. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Dendrocalamus pendulus is valued for its lightweight yet strong culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
