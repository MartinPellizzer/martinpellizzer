Dryopsila laurina

Dryopsila laurina, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Dryopsila laurina is native to regions in Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
