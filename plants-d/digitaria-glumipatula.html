Digitaria glumipatula

Digitaria glumipatula, commonly known as the broadleaf signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and broad, flat leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and disturbed areas.

Digitaria glumipatula is valued for its use in forage and as a component in ecological restoration projects.
