Diospyros multinervis

Diospyros multinervis, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits, which are edible when fully ripe.

Diospyros multinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a food source.
