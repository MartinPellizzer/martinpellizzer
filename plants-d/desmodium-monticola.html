Desmodium monticola

Desmodium monticola, commonly known as mountain desmodium, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Desmodium monticola plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
