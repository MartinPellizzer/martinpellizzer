Danthonia nudiflora

Danthonia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that lack bracts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to tolerate drought conditions.

Danthonia nudiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
