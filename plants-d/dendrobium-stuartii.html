Dendrobium stuartii

Dendrobium stuartii, commonly known as the Stuart's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium stuartii requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
