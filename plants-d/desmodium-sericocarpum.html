Desmodium sericocarpum

Desmodium sericocarpum, commonly known as the silky burr legume, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, silky-haired pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and spreading habit, it can become invasive in certain environments.
