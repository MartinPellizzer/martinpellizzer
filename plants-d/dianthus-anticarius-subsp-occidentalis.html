Dianthus anticarius subsp. occidentalis

Dianthus anticarius subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western pinks or western carnation, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
