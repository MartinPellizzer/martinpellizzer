Diplostachyum

Diplostachyum, commonly known as the flame bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, bright red or orange inflorescences. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Diplostachyum species are typically perennial and can grow up to several meters in height, making them a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
