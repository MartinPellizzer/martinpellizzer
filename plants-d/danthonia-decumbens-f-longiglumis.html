Danthonia decumbens f. longiglumis

Danthonia decumbens f. longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume danthonia, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive morphological features.

It is a low-growing perennial grass with rhizomatous growth, often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets with elongated glumes. The longiglumis form is distinguished by its longer, more prominent glumes compared to the typical variety.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
