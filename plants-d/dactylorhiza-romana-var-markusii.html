Dactylorhiza romana var. markusii

Dactylorhiza romana var. markusii, commonly known as the Roman orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or heathland habitats. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, green, ovate leaves.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
