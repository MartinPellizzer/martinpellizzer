Digitalis graeca var. megalantha

Digitalis graeca var. megalantha, commonly known as the Greek foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of hooded, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Digitalis graeca var. megalantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
