Draba aizoides subsp. aizoides

Draba aizoides subsp.

aizoides, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia, this species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single umbel and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and flies.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, making it a typical component of alpine vegetation.
