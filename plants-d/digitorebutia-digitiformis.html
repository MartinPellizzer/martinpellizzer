Digitorebutia digitiformis

Digitorebutia digitiformis, commonly known as the snowflake cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and rockeries.
