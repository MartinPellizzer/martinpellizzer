Dioscorea martini

Dioscorea martini, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, yellowish fruits. Its tubers are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The leaves are heart-shaped and can reach up to 20 cm in diameter.
