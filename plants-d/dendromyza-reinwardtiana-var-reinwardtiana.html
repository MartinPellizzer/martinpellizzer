Dendromyza reinwardtiana var. reinwardtiana

Dendromyza reinwardtiana var. reinwardtiana, commonly known as the Reinwardt's flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames, which appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

The flowers are small and yellowish-green, contrasting with the vivid bracts, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible for some bird species.
