Dichopsis pauciflora

Dichopsis pauciflora, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiky, leaf-like structures that resemble ghostly forms. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
