Diospyros acapulcensis subsp. verae-crucis

Diospyros acapulcensis subsp.

verae-crucis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the highlands of Mexico. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark orange or reddish hue, often with a sweet, astringent flavor.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit in local regions.
