Didiscus cyanopetalus var. ciliatulus

Didiscus cyanopetalus var. ciliatulus, commonly known as the blue-petaled didiscus, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals and distinctive leaf morphology, which feature cilia along the margins. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant coloration. Its growth habit includes a compact shrub form with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
