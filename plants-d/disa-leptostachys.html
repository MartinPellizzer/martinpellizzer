Disa leptostachys

Disa leptostachys, commonly known as the slender-stalked disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pink or red with a distinctive yellow lip, and it produces small, round berries.

Disa leptostachys is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
