Didymocarpus acuminatus

Didymocarpus acuminatus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
