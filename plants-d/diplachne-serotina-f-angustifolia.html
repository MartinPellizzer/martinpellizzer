Diplachne serotina f. angustifolia

Diplachne serotina f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and tapering. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other grass species in the genus Diplachne.
