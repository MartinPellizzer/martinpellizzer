Dunalia umbellata

Dunalia umbellata, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features a slender, erect stem with alternate, trifoliate leaves. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are arranged in umbellate clusters, giving it its scientific name.

Dunalia umbellata is valued for its edible seeds and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
