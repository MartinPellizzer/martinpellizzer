Desmodium podocarpum var. japonicum

Desmodium podocarpum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese velvet bean, is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers.

It produces elliptical or oblong pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is native to East Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a climbing or sprawling form depending on the environment.
