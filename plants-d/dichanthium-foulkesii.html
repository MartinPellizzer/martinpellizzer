Dichanthium foulkesii

Dichanthium foulkesii, commonly known as the African grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
