Daphne tenuiramis

Daphne tenuiramis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of white and pink, and are borne on short, clustered pedicels. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Daphne tenuiramis is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, showy blooms.
