Diplostephium santamartae

Diplostephium santamartae, commonly known as the Santa Marta dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Colombia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its high-altitude environment.
