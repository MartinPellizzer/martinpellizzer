Didiscus coeruleus var. leucopetalus

Didiscus coeruleus var. leucopetalus, commonly known as the white-flowered bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or white in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
