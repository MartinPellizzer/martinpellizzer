Dendrolycopodium verticale

Dendrolycopodium verticale, commonly known as the upright clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Dendrolycopodium verticale is an ancient lineage of plants, with a lineage dating back to the Carboniferous period.
