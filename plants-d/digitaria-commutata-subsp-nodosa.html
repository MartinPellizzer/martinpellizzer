Digitaria commutata subsp. nodosa

Digitaria commutata subsp.

nodosa, commonly known as the nodular digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential forage for livestock.
