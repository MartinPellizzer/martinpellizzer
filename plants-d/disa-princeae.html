Disa princeae

Disa princeae, commonly known as the Orange River Disa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along riverbanks and in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or orange in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker.

Disa princeae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
