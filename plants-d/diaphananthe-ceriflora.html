Diaphananthe ceriflora

Diaphananthe ceriflora, commonly known as the candleflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy flowers that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Diaphananthe ceriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
