Dorcoceras crassifolium

Dorcoceras crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of broad, lance-shaped leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and wetlands.

Dorcoceras crassifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
