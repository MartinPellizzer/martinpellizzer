Dryopria oppositifolia

Dryopria oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive leaf arrangement.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant features bipinnate fronds with opposite pinnae, giving it a unique and intricate appearance. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Dryopria oppositifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forests and along stream banks.
