Dianthus sylvestris subsp. bertisceus

Dianthus sylvestris subsp.

bertisceus, commonly known as the wood campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its pink or red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in woodland areas and rocky soils, thriving in partial shade to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
