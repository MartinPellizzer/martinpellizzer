Dioscorea quartiniana var. pentadactyla

Dioscorea quartiniana var. pentadactyla, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are edible. Its tubers are an important food source in some regions and have been used traditionally for their nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
