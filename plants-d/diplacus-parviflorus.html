Diplacus parviflorus

Diplacus parviflorus, commonly known as the smallflower monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Diplacus parviflorus is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators.
