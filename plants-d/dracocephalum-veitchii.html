Dracocephalum veitchii

Dracocephalum veitchii, commonly known as Veitch's dragonhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems, and are attractive to pollinators.

Dracocephalum veitchii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
