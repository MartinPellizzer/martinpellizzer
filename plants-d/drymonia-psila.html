Drymonia psila

Drymonia psila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Drymonia psila is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
