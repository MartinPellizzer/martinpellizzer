Diospyros angulata

Diospyros angulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its angular, often fissured bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, edible fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive angular appearance, contributing to its common name.

Diospyros angulata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical regions.
