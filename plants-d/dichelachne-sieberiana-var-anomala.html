Dichelachne sieberiana var. anomala

Dichelachne sieberiana var. anomala, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed sites.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
