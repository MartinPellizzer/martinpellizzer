Dichapetalum glandulosum

Dichapetalum glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular dichapetalum, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have glandular dots. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Dichapetalum glandulosum is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
