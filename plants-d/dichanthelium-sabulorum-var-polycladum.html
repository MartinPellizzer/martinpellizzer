Dichanthelium sabulorum var. polycladum

Dichanthelium sabulorum var. polycladum, commonly known as the sand bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grassy areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with blue or silver. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
