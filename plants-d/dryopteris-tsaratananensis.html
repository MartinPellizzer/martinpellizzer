Dryopteris tsaratananensis

Dryopteris tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Tsaratanana shield fern, is an endemic species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a unique morphology. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane rainforests. Its rhizomes are robust, and the fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a leathery texture.

This fern is of particular interest to botanists due to its restricted distribution and potential ecological significance.
