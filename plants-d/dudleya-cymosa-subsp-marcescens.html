Dudleya cymosa subsp. marcescens

Dudleya cymosa subsp.

marcescens, commonly known as the Santa Barbara Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California. It features fleshy, gray-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ability to survive in arid conditions highlights its resilience as a member of the Crassulaceae family.
