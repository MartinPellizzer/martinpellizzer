Dyckia ferox f. vulgaris

Dyckia ferox f. vulgaris, commonly known as the fierce dyckia, is a striking ornamental grass-like plant native to South America.

It features tall, upright stems covered in sharp, spiky leaves that give it a distinctive and somewhat intimidating appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a xerophytic plant in arid climates.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it is often cultivated as a hardy, low-maintenance plant for ornamental purposes.
