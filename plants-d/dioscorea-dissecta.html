Dioscorea dissecta

Dioscorea dissecta, commonly known as the dissected yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with deeply lobed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tuberous roots are often used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant's morphology and growth habits make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
