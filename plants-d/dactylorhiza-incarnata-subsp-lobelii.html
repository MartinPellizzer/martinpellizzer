Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. lobelii

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

lobelii, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as peat bogs, marshes, and damp woodlands.

The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowers that are usually purple or pink in color. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, narrow spike of blooms.

The orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
