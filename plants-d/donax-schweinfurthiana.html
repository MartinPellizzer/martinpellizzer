Donax schweinfurthiana

Donax schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dense, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
