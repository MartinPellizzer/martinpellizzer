Diospyros malabarica

Diospyros malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
