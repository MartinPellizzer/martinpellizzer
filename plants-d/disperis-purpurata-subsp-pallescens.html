Disperis purpurata subsp. pallescens

Disperis purpurata subsp.

pallescens, commonly known as the pale-flowered orchid, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, low-growing habit and produces delicate, pale pink to lilac flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, and its flowers are characterized by their slender, spurred petals. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
