Digitaria striata

Digitaria striata, commonly known as flatsear grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, flat leaves with a distinctive striped appearance on the lower surface. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Digitaria striata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
