Dryadella albicans

Dryadella albicans, commonly known as the white dryad, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Dryadella albicans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
