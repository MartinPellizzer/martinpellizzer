Debregeasia edulis

Debregeasia edulis, commonly known as the edible euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in a unique cyathium structure typical of the genus. Its latex contains toxic compounds, which makes it important to handle with care.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
