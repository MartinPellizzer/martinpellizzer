Drypetes oppositifolia

Drypetes oppositifolia, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and carving.
