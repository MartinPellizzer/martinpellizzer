Daucus alatus

Daucus alatus, commonly known as the winged carrot, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are similar in appearance to carrots but are not edible. Daucus alatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine contribute to its significance in botanical studies.
