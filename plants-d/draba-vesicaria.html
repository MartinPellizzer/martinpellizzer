Draba vesicaria

Draba vesicaria, commonly known as the bladder draba, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, typically growing in alpine and arctic environments. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, with four petals. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous tiny seeds.

Draba vesicaria is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
