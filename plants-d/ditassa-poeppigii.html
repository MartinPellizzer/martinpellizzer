Ditassa poeppigii

Ditassa poeppigii, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
