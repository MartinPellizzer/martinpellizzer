Dactylorhiza incarnata var. lobelii

Dactylorhiza incarnata var. lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heaths. The plant features a tall, erect stem with several showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
