Diplazium × tetsu-yamanakae

Diplazium × tetsu-yamanakae, commonly known as the Tetsu-Yamanakae fern, is a hybrid species within the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly curled appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spores on the underside of its fronds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
