Diasperus leptophyllus

Diasperus leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Diasperus leptophyllus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
