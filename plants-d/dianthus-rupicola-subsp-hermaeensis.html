Dianthus rupicola subsp. hermaeensis

Dianthus rupicola subsp.

hermaeensis, commonly known as the Hermaeensis Campion, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America. It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine conditions. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
