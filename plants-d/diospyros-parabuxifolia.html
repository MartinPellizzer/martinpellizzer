Diospyros parabuxifolia

Diospyros parabuxifolia, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests and riverine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
