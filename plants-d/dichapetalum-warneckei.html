Dichapetalum warneckei

Dichapetalum warneckei, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors and rocky habitats. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small fauna.
