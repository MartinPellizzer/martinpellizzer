Dendrocnide venosa

Dendrocnide venosa, commonly known as the stinging tree, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and striking white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant contains potent urticating hairs that can cause severe skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
