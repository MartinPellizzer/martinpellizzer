Doronicum bithynicum

Doronicum bithynicum, commonly known as the Bithynian leopard's bane, is a perennial flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing yellow-orange disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

Doronicum bithynicum is often found in rocky or limestone soils in open woodland areas.
