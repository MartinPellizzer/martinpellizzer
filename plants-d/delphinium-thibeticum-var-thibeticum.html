Delphinium thibeticum var. thibeticum

Delphinium thibeticum var. thibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, alternate leaves that are hairy and adapted to the cold, arid environment. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
