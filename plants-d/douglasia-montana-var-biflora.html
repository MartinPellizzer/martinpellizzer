Douglasia montana var. biflora

Douglasia montana var. biflora, commonly known as the mountain Douglasia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The species features two distinct flower types on the same plant, a trait known as heterostyly, which is common in some solanaceous plants. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral morphology and ecological adaptations.
