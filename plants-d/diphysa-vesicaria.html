Diphysa vesicaria

Diphysa vesicaria, commonly known as the bladderleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Diphysa vesicaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
