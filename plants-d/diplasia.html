Diplasia

Diplasia, commonly known as the "bluebell of the desert," is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Diplasia thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and adaptation to harsh environments make it a notable species in xerophytic ecosystems.
