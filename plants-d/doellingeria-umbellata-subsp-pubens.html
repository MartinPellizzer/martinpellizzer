Doellingeria umbellata subsp. pubens

Doellingeria umbellata subsp.

pubens, commonly known as the pubescent thimbleweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, pubescent foliage.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in an umbel. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in the eastern United States.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
