Dendrobium wardianum var. assamicum

Dendrobium wardianum var. assamicum, commonly known as the Assam orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fleshy pseudobulbs and long, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent purple lips. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
