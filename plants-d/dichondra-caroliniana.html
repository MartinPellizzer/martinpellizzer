Dichondra caroliniana

Dichondra caroliniana, commonly known as pennywort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Dichondra caroliniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ground cover applications.
