Dactylis glomerata f. ibizensis

Dactylis glomerata f. ibizensis, commonly known as the Ibiza grass, is a subspecies of the common grass known as cock's-foot.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
