Dichanthelium cryptanthum

Dichanthelium cryptanthum, commonly known as the wintergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and roadside ditches. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Dichanthelium cryptanthum is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
