Diospyros lotus var. angustifolia

Diospyros lotus var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, orange-red fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is edible, though it is often consumed after a brief period of ripening to develop its characteristic sweetness.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
