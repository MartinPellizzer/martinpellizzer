Docynia indica var. doumeri

Docynia indica var. doumeri, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes grown in gardens and for local consumption.
