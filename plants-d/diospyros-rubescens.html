Diospyros rubescens

Diospyros rubescens, commonly known as the red persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
