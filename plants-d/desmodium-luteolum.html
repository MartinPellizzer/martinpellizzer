Desmodium luteolum

Desmodium luteolum, commonly known as yellow burr medic, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable forage plant.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in certain agricultural and natural ecosystems.
