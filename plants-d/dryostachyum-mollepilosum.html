Dryostachyum mollepilosum

Dryostachyum mollepilosum, commonly known as the hairy leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

Dryostachyum mollepilosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage.
