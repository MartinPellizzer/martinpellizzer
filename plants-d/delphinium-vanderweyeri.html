Delphinium vanderweyeri

Delphinium vanderweyeri, commonly known as Vanderweyer's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its root system consists of fibrous roots.

Vanderweyer's larkspur is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
