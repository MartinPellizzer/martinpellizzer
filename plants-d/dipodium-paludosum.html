Dipodium paludosum

Dipodium paludosum, commonly known as the swamp spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense colonies that help stabilize shoreline areas. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually unbranched and produce small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

Dipodium paludosum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
