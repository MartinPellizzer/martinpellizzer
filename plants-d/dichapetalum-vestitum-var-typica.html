Dichapetalum vestitum var. typica

Dichapetalum vestitum var. typica, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety in texture. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural distribution.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and savanna habitats.
