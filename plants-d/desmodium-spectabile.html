Desmodium spectabile

Desmodium spectabile, commonly known as the showy desmodium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Desmodium spectabile is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
