Draba glabella var. typica

Draba glabella var. typica, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba glabella var. typica is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
