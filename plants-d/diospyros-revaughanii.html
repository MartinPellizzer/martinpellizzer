Diospyros revaughanii

Diospyros revaughanii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some African regions.
