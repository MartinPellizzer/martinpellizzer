Dahlia mexicana

Dahlia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its tuberous roots are an important feature for propagation and storage.

Dahlia mexicana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
