Dryopteris athyrioides

Dryopteris athyrioides, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, finely divided structure. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Dryopteris athyrioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
