Datura suaveolens var. knightii

Datura suaveolens var. knightii, commonly known as the sweet nightshade or nightshade bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale violet in color. The plant produces large, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are hairy and branching.

The plant is known for its strong fragrance, which is most pronounced at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
