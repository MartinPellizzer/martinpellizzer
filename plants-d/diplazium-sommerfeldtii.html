Diplazium sommerfeldtii

Diplazium sommerfeldtii, commonly known as the Summerfeldt's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often submerged or floating. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate.

Diplazium sommerfeldtii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
