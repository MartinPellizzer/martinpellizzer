Delphinium mollipilum

Delphinium mollipilum, commonly known as the woolly delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes at the top of the stems. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered with a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Delphinium mollipilum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine landscapes.
