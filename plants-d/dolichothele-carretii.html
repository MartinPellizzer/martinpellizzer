Dolichothele carretii

Dolichothele carretii, commonly known as the Carretti's long-tailed spider, is a species of orb-weaving spider found in various parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender body and long, filamentous legs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant-like appearance of its web, with its radial and spiral threads, is a key feature of its hunting strategy. It typically constructs its web in sheltered locations, such as under leaves or in crevices, to ambush prey.

Despite its spider-like appearance, Dolichothele carretii plays an important role in local ecosystems by controlling insect populations.
