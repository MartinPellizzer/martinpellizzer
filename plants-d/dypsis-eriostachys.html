Dypsis eriostachys

Dypsis eriostachys, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and sparse, clustered foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by ornamental berries. It is adapted to arid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
