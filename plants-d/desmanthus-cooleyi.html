Desmanthus cooleyi

Desmanthus cooleyi, commonly known as the Cooley's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas. Desmanthus cooleyi is a legume, meaning it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
