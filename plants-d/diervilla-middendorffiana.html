Diervilla middendorffiana

Diervilla middendorffiana, commonly known as the Middendorff's hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and attractive, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically white or pink, followed by edible, nut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
