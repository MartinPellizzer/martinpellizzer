Diospyros penibukanensis

Diospyros penibukanensis, commonly known as the Penibukan persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns from green to orange when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
