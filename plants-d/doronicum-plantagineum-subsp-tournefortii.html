Doronicum plantagineum subsp. tournefortii

Doronicum plantagineum subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the Tournefort's leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
