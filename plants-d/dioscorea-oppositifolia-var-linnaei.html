Dioscorea oppositifolia var. linnaei

Dioscorea oppositifolia var. linnaei, commonly known as wild yam, is a perennial climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its tubers contain compounds such as diosgenin, which have been studied for their potential pharmaceutical applications.
