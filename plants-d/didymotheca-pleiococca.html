Didymotheca pleiococca

Didymotheca pleiococca, commonly known as the blue coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple petals and yellow centers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Didymotheca pleiococca is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
