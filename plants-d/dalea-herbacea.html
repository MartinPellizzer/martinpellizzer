Dalea herbacea

Dalea herbacea, commonly known as hairy prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments.

Dalea herbacea plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
