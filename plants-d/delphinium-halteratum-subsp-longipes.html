Delphinium halteratum subsp. longipes

Delphinium halteratum subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed larkspur, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemose inflorescences, with five petals that are typically spurred at the base. Its leaves are palmately divided into three to five segments, with a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
