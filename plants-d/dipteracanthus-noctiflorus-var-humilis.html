Dipteracanthus noctiflorus var. humilis

Dipteracanthus noctiflorus var. humilis, commonly known as the night-blooming dipteracanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with tubular corollas that attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
