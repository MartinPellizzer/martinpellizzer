Diplanthemum brieyi

Diplanthemum brieyi, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale purple to white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered threatened and is protected in some regions.
