Diospyros graciliflora

Diospyros graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. The plant bears small, round, smooth-skinned fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Diospyros graciliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
