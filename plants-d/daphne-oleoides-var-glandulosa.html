Daphne oleoides var. glandulosa

Daphne oleoides var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with dark green leaves that have glandular dots, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in early spring. Its stems are often covered in glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and aroma.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature.
