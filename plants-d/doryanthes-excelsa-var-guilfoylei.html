Doryanthes excelsa var. guilfoylei

Doryanthes excelsa var. guilfoylei, commonly known as the Macarthur's candlebush, is a striking plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that support large, showy red bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers within the bracts, adding contrast to its vibrant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds and insects.
