Digitaria ciliaris var. chrysoblephara

Digitaria ciliaris var. chrysoblephara, commonly known as golden finger grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive golden hue along the edges, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
