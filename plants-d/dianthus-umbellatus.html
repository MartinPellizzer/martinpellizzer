Dianthus umbellatus

Dianthus umbellatus, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and fragrance.

Dianthus umbellatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is easy to cultivate in gardens.
