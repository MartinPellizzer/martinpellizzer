Delphinium kweichowense

Delphinium kweichowense, commonly known as the Kweichow larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
