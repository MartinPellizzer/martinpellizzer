Didymocarpus triplotrichus

Didymocarpus triplotrichus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, hairy fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal applications.
