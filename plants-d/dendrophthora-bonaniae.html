Dendrophthora bonaniae

Dendrophthora bonaniae, commonly known as the Bonanian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees, primarily in the rainforests of Madagascar.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are typically dispersed by birds. Its leaves are thin and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its humid environment.

Dendrophthora bonaniae plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree health and providing food for local wildlife.
