Desmodium hickenianum

Desmodium hickenianum, commonly known as the Florida velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Desmodium hickenianum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
