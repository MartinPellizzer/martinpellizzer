Dichromena nana

Dichromena nana, commonly known as the dwarf dichromena, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate nature, it is not commonly cultivated outside its native habitat.
