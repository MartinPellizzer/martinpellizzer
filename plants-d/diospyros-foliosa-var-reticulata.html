Diospyros foliosa var. reticulata

Diospyros foliosa var. reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves with a prominent reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are edible when ripe. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
