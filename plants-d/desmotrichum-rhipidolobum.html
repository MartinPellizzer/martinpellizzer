Desmotrichum rhipidolobum

Desmotrichum rhipidolobum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It has a taproot system and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
