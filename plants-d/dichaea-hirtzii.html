Dichaea hirtzii

Dichaea hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are thick and covered in fine, hairy structures, which help in water retention.

Dichaea hirtzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
