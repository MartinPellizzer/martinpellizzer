Drypetes madagascariensis subvar. inermis

Drypetes madagascariensis subvar. inermis, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
