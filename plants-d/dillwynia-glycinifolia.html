Dillwynia glycinifolia

Dillwynia glycinifolia, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Dillwynia glycinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
