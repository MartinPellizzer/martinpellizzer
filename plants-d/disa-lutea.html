Disa lutea

Disa lutea, commonly known as the yellow disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often short and erect.

Disa lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
