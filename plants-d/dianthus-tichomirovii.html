Dianthus tichomirovii

Dianthus tichomirovii, commonly known as the Tichomirov's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, often accompanied by prominent stamens.

Dianthus tichomirovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
