Diospyros pilosanthera var. tayabensis

Diospyros pilosanthera var. tayabensis, commonly known as the Tayabon tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sweet-sour fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarinds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and potential agricultural purposes in its native habitat.
