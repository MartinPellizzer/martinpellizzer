Dendrobium forbesii var. praestans

Dendrobium forbesii var. praestans, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of showy, golden-yellow flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. It requires well-draining potting media and regular watering during its growing season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
