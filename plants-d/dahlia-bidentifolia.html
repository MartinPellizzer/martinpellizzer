Dahlia bidentifolia

Dahlia bidentifolia, commonly known as the two-toothed dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, single or semi-double flowers in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and valuable for ecological studies.
