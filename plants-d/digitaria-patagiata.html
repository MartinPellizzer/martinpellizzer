Digitaria patagiata

Digitaria patagiata, commonly known as the Patagonian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Digitaria patagiata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
