Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. caramulensis

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

caramulensis, commonly known as the Caramul orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and North Africa. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and a single, erect stem. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with purple or brown markings, and it produces a single spike of flowers.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
