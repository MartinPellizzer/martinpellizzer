Dorycnium suffruticosum f. humilis

Dorycnium suffruticosum f. humilis, commonly known as the creeping goat's beard, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with narrow, serrated leaflets.

It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
