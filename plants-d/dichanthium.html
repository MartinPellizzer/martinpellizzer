Dichanthium

Dichanthium, commonly known as goat grass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the Poaceae family.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. They are characterized by their tufted growth habit and spikelet structures that often contain two bristles. Dichanthium species are drought-tolerant and have been used in traditional agriculture and forage systems.

Their ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
