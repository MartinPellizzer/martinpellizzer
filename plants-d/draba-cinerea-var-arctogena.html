Draba cinerea var. arctogena

Draba cinerea var. arctogena, commonly known as the Arctic whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, often covered in a dense layer of white to greyish hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in a raceme and bloom in early spring.

This species is adapted to cold, arctic, and alpine environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
