Dipteracanthus serpyllifolius

Dipteracanthus serpyllifolius, commonly known as the common sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is often found in open areas such as roadsides, fields, and dunes, where it contributes to soil stabilization and supports local pollinators.
