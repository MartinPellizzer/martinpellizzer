Draba sclerophylla var. laxior

Draba sclerophylla var. laxior, commonly known as the looseleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a dense covering of fine hairs. This variety is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
