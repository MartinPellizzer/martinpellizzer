Delphinium bicolor var. montanense

Delphinium bicolor var. montanense, commonly known as the mountain larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are showy and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
