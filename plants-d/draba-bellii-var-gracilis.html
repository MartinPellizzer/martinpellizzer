Draba bellii var. gracilis

Draba bellii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and cruciform petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are basal and rosette-like, and it produces a cluster of small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring.

This variety is distinguished by its slender stems and compact growth form, making it a notable species in its ecological niche.
