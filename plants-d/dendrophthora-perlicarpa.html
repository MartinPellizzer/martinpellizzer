Dendrophthora perlicarpa

Dendrophthora perlicarpa, commonly known as the clustered fruiting fungus, is a unique plant that exhibits interesting botanical characteristics.

It is a type of fungal plant that forms dense clusters of fruiting bodies, often found on decaying wood in moist environments. The plant's structure is primarily composed of mycelium, which plays a crucial role in decomposition and nutrient cycling. Its reproductive structures are specialized for dispersal, often relying on wind or water for propagation.

Despite its fungal nature, it shares some morphological traits with vascular plants, making it a subject of botanical interest.
