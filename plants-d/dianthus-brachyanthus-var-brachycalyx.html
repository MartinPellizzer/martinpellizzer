Dianthus brachyanthus var. brachycalyx

Dianthus brachyanthus var. brachycalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, rounded seed pods that mature in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant collections.
