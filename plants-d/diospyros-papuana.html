Diospyros papuana

Diospyros papuana, commonly known as the Papuan persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and smooth, grey-barked trunk. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Diospyros papuana is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential economic importance in local communities.
