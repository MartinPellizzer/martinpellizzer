Dietes iridifolia

Dietes iridifolia, commonly known as the African iris, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color, with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, iris-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
