Dyckia acaulis

Dyckia acaulis, commonly known as the rock dyckia, is a perennial bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that can reach up to 3 meters in height, bearing small, white to pinkish flowers. Dyckia acaulis is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
