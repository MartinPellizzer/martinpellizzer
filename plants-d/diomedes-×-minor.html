Diomedes × minor

Diomedes × minor, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Diomedes and minor species, resulting in a unique combination of traits.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species that showcases the genetic diversity within the orchid family.
