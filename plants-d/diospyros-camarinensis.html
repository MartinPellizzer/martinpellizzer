Diospyros camarinensis

Diospyros camarinensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers followed by orange to reddish-purple berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is appreciated for its sweet, nutty flavor.
