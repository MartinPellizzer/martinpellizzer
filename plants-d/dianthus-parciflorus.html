Dianthus parciflorus

Dianthus parciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flower carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus parciflorus is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in ornamental gardening.
