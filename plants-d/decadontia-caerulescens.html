Decadontia caerulescens

Decadontia caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-stemmed orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-tinged stems and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. Native to specific regions in South America, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
