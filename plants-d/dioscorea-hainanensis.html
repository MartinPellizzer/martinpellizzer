Dioscorea hainanensis

Dioscorea hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan yam, is a perennial vine native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The botanical features of D. hainanensis make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
