Dendrocousinsia elliptica

Dendrocousinsia elliptica, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often in summer. Its leaves are typically small and may be reduced to spines in mature plants.

Dendrocousinsia elliptica is well-suited for cultivation in well-drained soil and full sun.
