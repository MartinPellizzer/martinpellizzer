Doronicum grandiflorum proles viscosum

Doronicum grandiflorum proles viscosum, commonly known as the sticky false gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems covered in a sticky, resinous substance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
