Dentaria pachystigma var. corymbosa

Dentaria pachystigma var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered toothwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, cruciform flowers that bloom in early spring. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storage and survival in woodland environments.

It thrives in moist, shaded areas and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
