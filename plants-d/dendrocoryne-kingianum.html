Dendrocoryne kingianum

Dendrocoryne kingianum, commonly known as the kingia lily, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalk that can reach up to 2 meters in height, topped with a dense cluster of vibrant orange or red flowers. The plant has a unique ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves, which helps it survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few days, but they attract a variety of pollinators.

Dendrocoryne kingianum is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
