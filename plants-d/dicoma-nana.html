Dicoma nana

Dicoma nana, commonly known as the dwarf dicoma, is a small, compact shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, rounded foliage composed of small, fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Dicoma nana is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
