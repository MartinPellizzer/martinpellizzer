Diplazium ambiguum

Diplazium ambiguum, commonly known as the variable fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arching or drooping. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are short and often covered with a layer of brownish scales.

Diplazium ambiguum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
