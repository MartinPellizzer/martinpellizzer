Delphinium wilhelminae

Delphinium wilhelminae, commonly known as the Wilhelmine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
