Dianthus macroflorus

Dianthus macroflorus, commonly known as the giant pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are composed of numerous petals arranged in a flat, rounded cluster, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dianthus macroflorus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and fragrance.
