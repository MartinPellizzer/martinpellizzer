Dactylorhiza elata var. algerica

Dactylorhiza elata var. algerica, commonly known as the Algerian marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Dactylorhiza elata var. algerica is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
