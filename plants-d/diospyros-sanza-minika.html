Diospyros sanza-minika

Diospyros sanza-minika, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal forests and riverine areas.
