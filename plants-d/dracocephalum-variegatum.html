Dracocephalum variegatum

Dracocephalum variegatum, commonly known as variable dragonhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding visual interest to gardens.

Dracocephalum variegatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
