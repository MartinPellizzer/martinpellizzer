Dianthus chinensis subsp. pseudobarbatus

Dianthus chinensis subsp.

pseudobarbatus, commonly known as the Chinese carnation, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China. It features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinctive central boss.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces flowers in compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
