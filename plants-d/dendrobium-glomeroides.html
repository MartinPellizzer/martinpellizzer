Dendrobium glomeroides

Dendrobium glomeroides, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and long, drooping inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium glomeroides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
