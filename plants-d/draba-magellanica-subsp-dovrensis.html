Draba magellanica subsp. dovrensis

Draba magellanica subsp.

dovrensis, commonly known as Magellan's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, basal rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves.

The species produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and coastal ecosystems in its native range.
