Desmoncus macrodon

Desmoncus macrodon, commonly known as the giant cup fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, intricate fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with long, segmented stems that produce fiddleheads and mature fronds. Desmoncus macrodon thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
