Dalea emphysodes subsp. domingensis

Dalea emphysodes subsp.

domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, specifically the Dominican Republic. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to warm, tropical climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution.
