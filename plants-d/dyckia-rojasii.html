Dyckia rojasii

Dyckia rojasii, commonly known as the Rosas' Dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to silver-gray hair. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Dyckia rojasii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a desirable addition to both indoor and outdoor collections.
