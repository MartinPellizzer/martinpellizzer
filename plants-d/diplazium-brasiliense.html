Diplazium brasiliense

Diplazium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian water fern, is a member of the fern family, specifically the Polypodiaceae.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous structure, with fronds that are usually bipinnate, meaning they have two levels of division. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Diplazium brasiliense is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in horticulture.
