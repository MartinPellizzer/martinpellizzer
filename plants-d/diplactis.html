Diplactis

Diplactis, commonly known as the golden star or star lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with warm, humid climates. Diplactis species typically have showy, spadix-bearing inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is a characteristic feature of the Araceae family. They thrive in moist, well-drained soil and require partial to full shade for optimal growth.

The genus includes several species, each with unique foliage and floral structures that make them popular in horticultural settings.
