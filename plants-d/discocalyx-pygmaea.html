Discocalyx pygmaea

Discocalyx pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf discocalyx, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in whorls, contributing to its dense, rounded appearance.

Discocalyx pygmaea is well adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
