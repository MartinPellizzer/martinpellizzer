Dipodium squamatum

Dipodium squamatum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-white foliage that gives it a unique and eye-catching appearance. The plant features a rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive look. It produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Due to its ornamental value, Dipodium squamatum is often cultivated in gardens and conservatories for its aesthetic appeal.
