Deuterocohnia brevispicata

Deuterocohnia brevispicata, commonly known as the short-spiked rock fern, is a terrestrial fern native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with dense clusters of small, lanceolate leaves. Its fronds are usually less than 10 centimeters in length, with a distinctive, compact growth habit.

It is often found in alpine and paramo ecosystems, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
