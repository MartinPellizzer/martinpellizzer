Dyckia vaginosa

Dyckia vaginosa, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, overlapping leaves that form a rosette shape, often covered in a thick, velvety coating that gives it its common name. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. Its leaves are typically narrow and strap-like, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Dyckia vaginosa is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its ornamental appeal.
