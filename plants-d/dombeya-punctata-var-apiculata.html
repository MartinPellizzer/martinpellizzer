Dombeya punctata var. apiculata

Dombeya punctata var. apiculata, commonly known as the apiculate dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
