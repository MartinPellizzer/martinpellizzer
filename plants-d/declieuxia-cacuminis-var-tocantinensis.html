Declieuxia cacuminis var. tocantinensis

Declieuxia cacuminis var. tocantinensis, commonly known as the Tocantins sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cerrado and wetland regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized reproductive structures. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.
