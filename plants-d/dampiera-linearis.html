Dampiera linearis

Dampiera linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf dampiera, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Dampiera linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
