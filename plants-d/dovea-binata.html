Dovea binata

Dovea binata, commonly known as the twin-leaf dovea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are typically divided into two lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, Dovea binata is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
