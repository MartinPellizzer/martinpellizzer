Dangervilla spinosa

Dangervilla spinosa, commonly known as the spiny devil's flower, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its stems are covered in sharp spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Dangervilla spinosa is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and unique member of its ecosystem.
