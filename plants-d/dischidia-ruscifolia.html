Dischidia ruscifolia

Dischidia ruscifolia, commonly known as the creeping fig or silver vine, is a climbing tropical plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Dischidia ruscifolia is native to Southeast Asia and is often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
