Draba argyrea var. glabrescens

Draba argyrea var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silverleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
