Dorstenia ophiocoma

Dorstenia ophiocoma, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and often have a waxy surface.

Dorstenia ophiocoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
