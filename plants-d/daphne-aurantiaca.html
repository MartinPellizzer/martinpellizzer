Daphne aurantiaca

Daphne aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant is renowned for its showy, fragrant flowers, which are typically orange or red and bloom in early spring. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and aromatic properties.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling and placement in gardens.
