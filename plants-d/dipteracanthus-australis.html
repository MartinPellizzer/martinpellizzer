Dipteracanthus australis

Dipteracanthus australis, commonly known as the Southern Starflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
