Dysophylla quadrifolia

Dysophylla quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leafed dysophylla, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves that often exhibit a four-leaf pattern, which is considered a sign of good fortune in some cultures. The plant produces tiny, pale green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, rounded fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
