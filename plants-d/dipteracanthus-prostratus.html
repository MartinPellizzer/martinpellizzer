Dipteracanthus prostratus

Dipteracanthus prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate dipteracanthus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
