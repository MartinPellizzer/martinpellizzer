Detris richardii

Detris richardii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard, woody capsule.

Detris richardii is valued for its durable timber and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
