Dichocarpum trachyspermum f. didymocalyx

Dichocarpum trachyspermum f. didymocalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaved celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of broadly ovate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is typically composed of several flower heads arranged in a loose, branched pattern.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
