Diospyros martini

Diospyros martini, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible when fully ripe, though they may have a mild astringent taste. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The bark is typically dark and can be fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.
