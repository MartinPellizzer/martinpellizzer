Dictamnus fraxinella var. albus

Dictamnus fraxinella var. albus, commonly known as the white dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features aromatic, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branched stems that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural habitats.
