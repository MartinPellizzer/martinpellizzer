Dictyocline sagittifolia

Dictyocline sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf dictyocline, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. The plant features elongated, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are typically white or pale yellow.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
