Davallodes dolichosorum

Davallodes dolichosorum, commonly known as the long-stemmed dwarf lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit with clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink blooms that are often tinged with red, adding a dramatic visual appeal to its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its ornamental value.

Davallodes dolichosorum thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
