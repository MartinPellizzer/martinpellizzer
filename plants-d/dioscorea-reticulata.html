Dioscorea reticulata

Dioscorea reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has compound leaves with palmate lobes and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual.

Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their nutritional content and potential health benefits.
