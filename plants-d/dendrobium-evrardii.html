Dendrobium evrardii

Dendrobium evrardii, commonly known as the Evrard's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks in tropical forests. Its flowers bloom in summer and are known for their delicate, intricate petals and strong scent.

Dendrobium evrardii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
