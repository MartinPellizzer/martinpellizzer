Dahlia dumicola

Dahlia dumicola, commonly known as the rock dahlia, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its sturdy stems and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, making it well-suited for dry environments. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dahlia dumicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
