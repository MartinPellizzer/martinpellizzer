Drosera nitidula var. leucostigma

Drosera nitidula var. leucostigma, commonly known as the white-stemmed sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and swamps. Its stems are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it its common name.

It produces small, delicate pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months.
