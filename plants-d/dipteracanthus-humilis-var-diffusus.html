Dipteracanthus humilis var. diffusus

Dipteracanthus humilis var. diffusus, commonly known as the spreading dipteracanthus, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often spread along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a distinct central column. Its leaves are lance-shaped, smooth, and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.
