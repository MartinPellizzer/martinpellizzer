Dichelachne sciurea

Dichelachne sciurea, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and riparian zones. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth surface. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, two-lobed glumes.

Dichelachne sciurea is often found in wetland ecosystems and can play a role in stabilizing soil in such environments.
