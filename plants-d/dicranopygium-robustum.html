Dicranopygium robustum

Dicranopygium robustum, commonly known as the robust moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green to reddish-brown leaves that are often curled when dry. The plant produces spore capsules that are typically borne on short, stalked pedicels, and the leaves are usually broader at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Dicranopygium robustum plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
