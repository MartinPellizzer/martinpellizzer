Desmodium auricomum

Desmodium auricomum, commonly known as the earleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in warm, temperate climates. Its trifoliate leaves have a distinctive shape, with one leaflet resembling an ear, giving it its common name.

Desmodium auricomum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
