Dianthus strictus subsp. multipunctatus

Dianthus strictus subsp.

multipunctatus, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers.

The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching. This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
