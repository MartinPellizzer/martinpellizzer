Dothilis grandiflora

Dothilis grandiflora, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of purple and white. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its rapid growth.

Dothilis grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
