Dombeya mollis

Dombeya mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, pale pink to white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety appearance, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. Dombeya mollis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to the landscape.
