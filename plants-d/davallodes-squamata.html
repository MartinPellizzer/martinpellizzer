Davallodes squamata

Davallodes squamata, commonly known as the scaly davallia, is a fern species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant features small, scale-like structures on its leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Davallodes squamata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in indoor gardens.
