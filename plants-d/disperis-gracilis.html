Disperis gracilis

Disperis gracilis, commonly known as the slender disperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats, often in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
