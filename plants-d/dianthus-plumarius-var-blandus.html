Dianthus plumarius var. blandus

Dianthus plumarius var. blandus, commonly known as the Blush carnation, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It blooms in early summer and is valued for its use in floral arrangements and as a border plant.
