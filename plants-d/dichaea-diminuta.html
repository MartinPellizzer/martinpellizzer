Dichaea diminuta

Dichaea diminuta, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small, epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and delicate appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are often clustered at the base, and it produces tiny, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
