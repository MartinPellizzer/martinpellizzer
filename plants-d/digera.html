Digera

Digera, commonly known as the "Indian mullein," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow and orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Digera is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.
