Dichaea moritzii

Dichaea moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's dichaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a terrestrial bromeliad characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
