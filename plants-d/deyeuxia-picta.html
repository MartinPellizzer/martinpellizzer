Deyeuxia picta

Deyeuxia picta, commonly known as the painted sedge, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and distinctive, often variegated leaves that display a range of colors, including green, white, and red. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Deyeuxia picta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
