Dianthus subulatus

Dianthus subulatus, commonly known as the needle-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often surrounded by a ring of white or pink bracts.

Dianthus subulatus is a perennial species that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
