Dalbergia candenatensis

Dalbergia candenatensis, commonly known as rosewood, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental and commercial value, it is also a subject of conservation concern.
