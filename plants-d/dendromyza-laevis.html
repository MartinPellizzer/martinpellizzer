Dendromyza laevis

Dendromyza laevis, commonly known as the smoothleaf dendromyza, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming during the dry season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in humid environments.

Dendromyza laevis is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
