Dichaea rubroviolacea

Dichaea rubroviolacea, commonly known as the red-violet dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, violet-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, which helps it retain moisture.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
