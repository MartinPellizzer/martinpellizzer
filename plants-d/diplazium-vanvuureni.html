Diplazium vanvuureni

Diplazium vanvuureni, commonly known as the Vanvuurens' diplophyllum, is a fern native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called sorocarps, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
