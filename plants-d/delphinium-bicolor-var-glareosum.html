Delphinium bicolor var. glareosum

Delphinium bicolor var. glareosum, commonly known as the showy larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Delphinium bicolor var. glareosum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
