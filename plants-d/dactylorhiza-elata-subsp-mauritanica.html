Dactylorhiza elata subsp. mauritanica

Dactylorhiza elata subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa. It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, creeping rhizome and produces tall, branching stems that bear lance-shaped leaves.

The plant flowers with showy, violet to purple orchid blooms that are typically arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, often near water sources.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
