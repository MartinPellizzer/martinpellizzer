Dryas integrifolia f. intermedia

Dryas integrifolia f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate mountain avens, is a horticultural form of the mountain avens species.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
