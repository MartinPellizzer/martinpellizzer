Dianthus serratus var. insignitus

Dianthus serratus var. insignitus, commonly known as the serrated carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red, double or semi-double flowers. The leaves are narrow and serrated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
