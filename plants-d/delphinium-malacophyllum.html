Delphinium malacophyllum

Delphinium malacophyllum, commonly known as the purple-leaf larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Delphinium malacophyllum is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
