Dendrobium speciosum subsp. hillii

Dendrobium speciosum subsp.

hillii, commonly known as the Hill's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that often branch and bear clusters of showy, fragrant flowers.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns, and they bloom during the rainy season. This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
