Deschampsia cespitosa var. montana

Deschampsia cespitosa var. montana, commonly known as mountain bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to silver flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or disturbed areas.
