Dicliptera sebastinei var. khasiana

Dicliptera sebastinei var. khasiana, commonly known as Khasi grass, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories and rocky terrains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that contributes to its hardiness.

This variant is notable for its ecological significance in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various small organisms.
