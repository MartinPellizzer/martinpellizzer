Desmodium leptopus

Desmodium leptopus, commonly known as slender burr medic, is a perennial leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its unique, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which aid in its dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and pastures.

Desmodium leptopus is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is used in forage and soil improvement practices.
