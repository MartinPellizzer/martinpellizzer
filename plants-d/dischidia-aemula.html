Dischidia aemula

Dischidia aemula, commonly known as the miniature weeping fig, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and unique aerial roots that help it cling to surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
