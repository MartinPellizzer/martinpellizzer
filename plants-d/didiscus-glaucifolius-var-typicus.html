Didiscus glaucifolius var. typicus

Didiscus glaucifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the silverleaf didiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, powdery hairs, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in dry climates.
