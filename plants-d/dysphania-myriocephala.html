Dysphania myriocephala

Dysphania myriocephala, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and slender, edible stems that are often used in various cuisines. The plant thrives in moist, warm environments and is commonly cultivated in wetlands or alongside water bodies. Its rapid growth rate makes it an effective green manure crop and a valuable resource for both agriculture and nutrition.

Dysphania myriocephala is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its potential use in phytoremediation.
