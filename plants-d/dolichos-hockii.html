Dolichos hockii

Dolichos hockii, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bean-like pods. Dolichos hockii is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
