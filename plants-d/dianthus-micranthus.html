Dianthus micranthus

Dianthus micranthus, commonly known as the double carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, double petals that resemble carnations. The plant typically blooms in early summer, showcasing vibrant pink, red, or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus micranthus is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in floral arrangements.
