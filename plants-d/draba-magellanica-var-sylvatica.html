Draba magellanica var. sylvatica

Draba magellanica var. sylvatica, commonly known as the Magellan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates.

This variety is part of the Brassicaceae family, known for its characteristic cruciform flowers and nitrogen-fixing abilities.
