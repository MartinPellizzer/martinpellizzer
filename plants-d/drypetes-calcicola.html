Drypetes calcicola

Drypetes calcicola, commonly known as the limestone drypetes, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in calcareous soils, particularly in rocky or limestone-rich environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and yellowish, clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits.

Drypetes calcicola is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and rocky habitats.
