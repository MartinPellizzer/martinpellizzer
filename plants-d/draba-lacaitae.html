Draba lacaitae

Draba lacaitae, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small herbaceous plant that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often on rocky slopes and grasslands. The plant produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, with four petals. It has a basal rosette of leaves and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Draba lacaitae is found in parts of Europe and Asia, including the Carpathian Mountains and the Alps.
