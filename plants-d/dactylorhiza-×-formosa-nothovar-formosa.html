Dactylorhiza × formosa nothovar. formosa

Dactylorhiza × formosa nothovar. formosa, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple or pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike of elongated, showy blooms. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soil.

This orchid is also notable for its role in ecological systems, supporting various pollinators.
