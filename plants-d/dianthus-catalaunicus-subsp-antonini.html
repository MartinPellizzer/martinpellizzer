Dianthus catalaunicus subsp. antonini

Dianthus catalaunicus subsp.

antonini, commonly known as the Catalan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
