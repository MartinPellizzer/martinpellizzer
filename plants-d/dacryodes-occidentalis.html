Dacryodes occidentalis

Dacryodes occidentalis, commonly known as the West African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange.

The tree is often found in moist lowland forests and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
