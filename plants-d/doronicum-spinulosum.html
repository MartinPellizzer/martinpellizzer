Doronicum spinulosum

Doronicum spinulosum, commonly known as the spiked leopard's bread, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Alps.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Doronicum spinulosum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
