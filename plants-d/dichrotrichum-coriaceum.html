Dichrotrichum coriaceum

Dichrotrichum coriaceum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
