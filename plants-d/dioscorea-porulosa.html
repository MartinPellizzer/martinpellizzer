Dioscorea porulosa

Dioscorea porulosa, commonly known as the arrowhead yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its rhizomes contain starch and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
