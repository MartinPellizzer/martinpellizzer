Dioscorea ovifotsy

Dioscorea ovifotsy, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forest understories.
