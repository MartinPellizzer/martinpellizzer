Dioscorea piperifolia var. legitima

Dioscorea piperifolia var. legitima, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often exhibit variegation in shades of green, white, and cream. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and as a ground cover.
