Dichaea ovatipetala

Dichaea ovatipetala, commonly known as the broad-leafed dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often emerging from the base of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, Dichaea ovatipetala is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
