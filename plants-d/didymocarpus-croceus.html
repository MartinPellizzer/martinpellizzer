Didymocarpus croceus

Didymocarpus croceus, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible seeds, which have a tart flavor.

The species is adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
