Danaa aquilegiifolia

Danaa aquilegiifolia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soil. It is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments, such as forest understories.

Danaa aquilegiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
