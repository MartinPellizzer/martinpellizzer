Dyckia caulescens

Dyckia caulescens, commonly known as the slender dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, inflorescences of purple or red flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Dyckia caulescens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.
