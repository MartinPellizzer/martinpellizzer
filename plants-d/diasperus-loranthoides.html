Diasperus loranthoides

Diasperus loranthoides, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Acacia*.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are usually small, opposite, and have a leathery texture.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by supporting bird populations and contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
