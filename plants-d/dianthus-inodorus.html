Dianthus inodorus

Dianthus inodorus, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces fragrant blooms during the spring and early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Dianthus inodorus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
