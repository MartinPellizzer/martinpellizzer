Daubentonia cavanillesii

Daubentonia cavanillesii, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, which helps in moisture retention.

Daubentonia cavanillesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
