Dioscorea tarokoensis

Dioscorea tarokoensis, commonly known as the Taroko yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soil, preferring partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color.

The tubers of Dioscorea tarokoensis are valued for their nutritional content and have been historically significant in the culture of indigenous communities in Taiwan.
