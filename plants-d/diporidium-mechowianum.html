Diporidium mechowianum

Diporidium mechowianum, commonly known as the Mechowian thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Diporidium mechowianum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
