Diastella thymelaeoides subsp. meridiana

Diastella thymelaeoides subsp.

meridiana, commonly known as the red karkalla, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking, bright red flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
