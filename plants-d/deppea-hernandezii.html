Deppea hernandezii

Deppea hernandezii, commonly known as the Mexican redwood, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
