Dianthus brachycalyx

Dianthus brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known species like carnations and pinks.

Dianthus brachycalyx is typically perennial and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
