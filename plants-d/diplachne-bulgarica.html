Diplachne bulgarica

Diplachne bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Diplachne bulgarica is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open grasslands.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions makes it an adaptable and widespread species.
