Draba farsetioides

Draba farsetioides, commonly known as the Farset's rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial species native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its basal leaves are spoon-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a raceme.

Draba farsetioides is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often found in mountainous habitats across the Himalayas and the Tien Shan.
