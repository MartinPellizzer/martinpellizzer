Dissanthelium densum

Dissanthelium densum, commonly known as the dense hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically found in open, moist habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
