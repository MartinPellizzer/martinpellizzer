Dioscorea piperifolia var. triangularis

Dioscorea piperifolia var. triangularis, commonly known as the triangular yam, is a variant of the yam family native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped tubers, which are often used as a food source. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its growth habit is typically vine-like, climbing or sprawling over supports.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
