Dombeya cacuminum

Dombeya cacuminum, commonly known as the African dombeya, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Dombeya cacuminum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
