Diospyros fischeri

Diospyros fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
