Dioscorea loefgrenii

Dioscorea loefgrenii, commonly known as the Loefgren's yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
