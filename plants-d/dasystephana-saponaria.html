Dasystephana saponaria

Dasystephana saponaria, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces smooth, green fruits that turn orange-red when mature, containing a single, hard seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
