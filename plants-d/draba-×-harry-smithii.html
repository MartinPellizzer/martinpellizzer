Draba × harry-smithii

Draba × harry-smithii, commonly known as the Harry-Smith's whitlow grass, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with compact rosettes of basal leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
