Disticholiparis lamproglossa

Disticholiparis lamproglossa, commonly known as the broad-tongued orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, tongue-shaped petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

The leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in two rows, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
