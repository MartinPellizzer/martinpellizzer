Donax comorensis

Donax comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro beach bean, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a small, shrub-like species that thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Donax comorensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
