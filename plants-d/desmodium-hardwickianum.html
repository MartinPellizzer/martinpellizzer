Desmodium hardwickianum

Desmodium hardwickianum, commonly known as hardwick's burr.

It is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive seed pods that enclose small, hairy seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration. Its growth habit includes climbing or scrambling, often using tendrils to support itself.

The flowers are typically small and pea-like, appearing in clusters, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils.
