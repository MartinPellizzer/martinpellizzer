Delphinium viride

Delphinium viride, commonly known as the green larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as buttercups and anemones.
