Drypetes picardae

Drypetes picardae, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
