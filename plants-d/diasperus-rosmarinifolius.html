Diasperus rosmarinifolius

Diasperus rosmarinifolius, commonly known as rosemary bush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. Its evergreen nature makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Diasperus rosmarinifolius is also valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
