Delphinium latesquamatum

Delphinium latesquamatum, commonly known as the late-squamate larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Delphinium latesquamatum is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
