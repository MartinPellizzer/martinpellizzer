Diuris lineata

Diuris lineata, commonly known as the linear-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, linear leaf that is usually less than 10 cm in length. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often with a distinctive lip that is pale green or white.

Diuris lineata is a perennial species that blooms from late winter to early spring, producing a spike of up to 20 flowers.
