Decodon verticillatus

Decodon verticillatus, commonly known as whorled loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite or whorled leaves, which are lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters at the tips of the stems, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Decodon verticillatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
