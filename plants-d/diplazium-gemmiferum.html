Diplazium gemmiferum

Diplazium gemmiferum, commonly known as the gemmiferous water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces gemmae, which are small plantlets that allow for vegetative reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in dense forests.

Its unique reproductive strategy and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for aquatic and terrarium displays.
