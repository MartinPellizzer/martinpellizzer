Dioscorea lloydiana

Dioscorea lloydiana, commonly known as the Lloyd's yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for both its medicinal properties and its role in local ecosystems.
