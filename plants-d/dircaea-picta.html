Dircaea picta

Dircaea picta, commonly known as the painted lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy.

Dircaea picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
