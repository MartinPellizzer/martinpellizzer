Danthonia cusickii

Danthonia cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's muhly, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
