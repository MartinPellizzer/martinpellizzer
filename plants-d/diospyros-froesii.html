Diospyros froesii

Diospyros froesii, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries with a sweet, astringent flavor.

Diospyros froesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
