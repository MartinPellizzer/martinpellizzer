Dendrochilum cordatum

Dendrochilum cordatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped pseudobulbs and elongated, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Dendrochilum cordatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
