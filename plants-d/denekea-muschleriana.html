Denekea muschleriana

Denekea muschleriana, commonly known as the Muschler's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Denekea muschleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
