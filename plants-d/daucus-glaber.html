Daucus glaber

Daucus glaber, commonly known as the smooth burdock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features a tall, slender stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with soft, hairy leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typically white or pale purple in color.

The roots of Daucus glaber are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of inulin.
