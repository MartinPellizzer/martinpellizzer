Danthonia leonina

Danthonia leonina, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It features distinctive, tufted inflorescences that resemble a lion's tail, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Danthonia leonina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
