Dahlia spectabilis

Dahlia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flower heads. The plant produces vibrant, colorful blooms in shades of red, pink, yellow, and white, often with a prominent central disk. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Dahlia spectabilis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.
