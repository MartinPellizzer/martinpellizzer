Delphinium densiflorum

Delphinium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium densiflorum is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
