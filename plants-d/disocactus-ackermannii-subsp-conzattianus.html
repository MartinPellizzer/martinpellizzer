Disocactus ackermannii subsp. conzattianus

Disocactus ackermannii subsp.

conzattianus, commonly known as the Conzattian orchid cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique floral display makes it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of cacti and succulents.
