Dendrobium dempoense

Dendrobium dempoense, commonly known as the Dempo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces strikingly colorful, often fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Dendrobium dempoense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
