Dicksonia elegans

Dicksonia elegans, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, dark green fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores on the underside of its fronds rather than flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
