Diospyros longisepala

Diospyros longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
