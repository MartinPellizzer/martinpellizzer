Dracaena moorei

Dracaena moorei, commonly known as the Song of India, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires moderate watering. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries, though these are not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Dracaena moorei is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
