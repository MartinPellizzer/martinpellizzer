Digitalis carnea

Digitalis carnea, commonly known as the crimson candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Digitalis carnea contains cardiac glycosides, which have medicinal properties but can be toxic if ingested.
