Dendrophthoe glabrescens

Dendrophthoe glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has a short, woody stem with small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on the fleshy, red berries.

This species is often found in coastal regions of southern Australia, where it thrives in a variety of forest and woodland habitats.
