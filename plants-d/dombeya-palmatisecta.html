Dombeya palmatisecta

Dombeya palmatisecta, commonly known as the palmate-leaved dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are deeply divided and give the plant a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Dombeya palmatisecta thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively low maintenance once established.
