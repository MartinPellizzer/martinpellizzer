Dendroviguiera splendens

Dendroviguiera splendens, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and cascading clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped fruits that mature to a red color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring full sun and well-drained soil.

Dendroviguiera splendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
