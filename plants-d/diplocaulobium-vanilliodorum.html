Diplocaulobium vanilliodorum

Diplocaulobium vanilliodorum, commonly known as the vanilla-like orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, vanilla-scented flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and glossy, with a dark green color.

The orchid's unique aroma has made it a subject of interest for both horticultural and aromatic research.
