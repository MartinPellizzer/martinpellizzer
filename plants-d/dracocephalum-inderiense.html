Dracocephalum inderiense

Dracocephalum inderiense, commonly known as the Ethiopian basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the highland regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple in color and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Dracocephalum inderiense is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
