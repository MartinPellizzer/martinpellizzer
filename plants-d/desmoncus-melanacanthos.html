Desmoncus melanacanthos

Desmoncus melanacanthos, commonly known as the black thorned bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, which it then digests for nutrients. The plant's leaves are typically submerged, while its stems may be partially emergent, allowing for efficient nutrient uptake. It thrives in nutrient-poor water environments, relying on its adaptive trapping mechanism for survival.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
