Draba mandoniana

Draba mandoniana, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, basal rosette of leaves and produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers. Draba mandoniana typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ability to survive in harsh, cold climates makes it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
