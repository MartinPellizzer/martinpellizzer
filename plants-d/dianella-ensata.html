Dianella ensata

Dianella ensata, commonly known as the blue-flowered pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

Dianella ensata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
