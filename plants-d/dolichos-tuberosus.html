Dolichos tuberosus

Dolichos tuberosus, commonly known as the sweet potato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and the production of tuberous roots that are rich in starch. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. The tubers, which are the primary edible part, come in a variety of colors including orange, white, and purple.

It is a member of the legume family and has a long history of cultivation for both food and forage.
