Dalea pringlei var. multijuga

Dalea pringlei var. multijuga, commonly known as the multijugate pringle dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. Its root system is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
