Didiscus incisus

Didiscus incisus, commonly known as the cutleaf didiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with prominent stamens that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.
