Dianthus carthusianorum var. atrorubens

Dianthus carthusianorum var. atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red carnation, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of deep red to nearly black, with prominent white or pale pink markings. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its striking color and long-lasting blooms, often used in floral arrangements.
