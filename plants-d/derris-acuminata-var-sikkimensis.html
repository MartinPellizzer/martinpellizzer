Derris acuminata var. sikkimensis

Derris acuminata var. sikkimensis, commonly known as Sikkim derris, is a variant of the derris genus native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of rotenone, a natural insecticide.

Due to its ecological and pharmacological significance, it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
