Daemonorops pseudomirabilis var. malayanus

Daemonorops pseudomirabilis var. malayanus, commonly known as the Malayan screw pine, is a variant of the screw pine genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with spirally arranged leaves that are typically elongated and narrow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
