Diospyros esmereg

Diospyros esmereg, commonly known as the Esmereg persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a small, orange-red drupe that is edible when fully ripe, though it may have astringent properties when unripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
