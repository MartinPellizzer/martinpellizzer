Diplopappus molliusculus

Diplopappus molliusculus, commonly known as the silky glume grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
