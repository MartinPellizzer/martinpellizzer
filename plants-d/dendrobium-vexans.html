Dendrobium vexans

Dendrobium vexans, commonly known as the wandering orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a branched stem system that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces inflorescences with multiple fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium vexans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
