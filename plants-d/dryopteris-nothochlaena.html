Dryopteris nothochlaena

Dryopteris nothochlaena, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem covered with persistent, fibrous roots. The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive blackish-brown coloration on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in native forests.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
