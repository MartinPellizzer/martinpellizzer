Daphne cneorum var. major

Daphne cneorum var. major, commonly known as the sweet spurge or winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its evergreen stems are often covered in a powdery, waxy coating, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is known for its toxicity, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
