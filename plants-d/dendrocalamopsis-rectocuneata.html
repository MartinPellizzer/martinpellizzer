Dendrocalamopsis rectocuneata

Dendrocalamopsis rectocuneata, commonly known as the straight-awned bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib and a rough texture.

This species is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
