Diplocaulobium textile

Diplocaulobium textile, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
