Dupatya appressa

Dupatya appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Dupatya appressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
