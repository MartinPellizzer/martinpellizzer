Drymaria holosteoides var. holosteoides

Drymaria holosteoides var. holosteoides, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, fleshy, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling miniature sunflowers. The plant has a mat-forming habit with succulent, hairy stems and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.
