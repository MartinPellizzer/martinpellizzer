Discocactus nigrisaetosus

Discocactus nigrisaetosus, commonly known as the black-spined discocactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a succulent structure that allows it to store water efficiently.

Discocactus nigrisaetosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
