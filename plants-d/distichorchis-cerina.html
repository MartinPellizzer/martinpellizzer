Distichorchis cerina

Distichorchis cerina, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces striking pink or white flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are arranged in two rows along the stem, giving it the genus name "distichorchis," which means "two-rowed orchid.".

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
