Deutzia corymbiflora

Deutzia corymbiflora, commonly known as the Chinese deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Deutzia corymbiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
