Diervilla grandiflora var. arborea

Diervilla grandiflora var. arborea, commonly known as the tree diervilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, multi-stemmed growth with attractive, ovate to elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
