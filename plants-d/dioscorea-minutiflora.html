Dioscorea minutiflora

Dioscorea minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically arranged in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
