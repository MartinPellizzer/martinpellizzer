Draba aretioides var. leiocarpa

Draba aretioides var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has basal rosettes of narrow, smooth leaves and produces yellow or white flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves, which set it apart from other Draba species.
