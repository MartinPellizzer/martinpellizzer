Dolichos regularis

Dolichos regularis, commonly known as the common hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces distinctive, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant bears elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability and pod fruit structure.
