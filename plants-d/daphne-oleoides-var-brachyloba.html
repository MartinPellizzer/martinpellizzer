Daphne oleoides var. brachyloba

Daphne oleoides var. brachyloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It features fragrant, showy flowers that range from pink to white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens despite its toxic properties.
