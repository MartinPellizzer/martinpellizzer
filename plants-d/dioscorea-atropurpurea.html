Dioscorea atropurpurea

Dioscorea atropurpurea, commonly known as purple yam, is a vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in starch and are used as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a purple tinge on the underside.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
