Dioscorea campestris

Dioscorea campestris, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its tubers have been traditionally used in herbal medicine and as a source of compounds for pharmaceutical applications.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.
