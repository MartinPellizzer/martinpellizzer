Derris thyrsiflora

Derris thyrsiflora, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
