Desmodium michoacanum

Desmodium michoacanum, commonly known as Mexican velvetbean, is a leguminous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is also valued as a forage crop and in ecological restoration efforts.
