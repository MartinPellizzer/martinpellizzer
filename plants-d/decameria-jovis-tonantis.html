Decameria jovis-tonantis

Decameria jovis-tonantis, commonly known as the celestial flame orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features vibrant, bi-colored petals that range from deep crimson to golden yellow, creating a visually stunning display. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom only during the summer months, attracting specific pollinators such as nocturnal moths. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to minimize water loss in its arid environment.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
