Draba obovata var. dasycarpa

Draba obovata var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-carpel whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with basal rosettes of narrow, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy, spherical capsule, a characteristic feature of the varietal name *dasycarpa*.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing typical traits of the Brassicaceae family.
