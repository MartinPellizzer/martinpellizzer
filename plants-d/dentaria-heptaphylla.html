Dentaria heptaphylla

Dentaria heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leafed toothplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are toothed and arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Dentaria heptaphylla is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
