Dactylorhiza × bayburtiana

Dactylorhiza × bayburtiana, commonly known as the Bayburt orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of violet or purple flowers with a distinctive lip. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, often growing in forested or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
