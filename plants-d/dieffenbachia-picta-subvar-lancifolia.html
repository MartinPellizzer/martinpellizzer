Dieffenbachia picta subvar. lancifolia

Dieffenbachia picta subvar. lancifolia, commonly known as the Leopard Plant, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves with striking, dark green veins and lighter green to cream-colored markings. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is often used in interior decoration due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.

The plant prefers well-draining soil and moderate humidity for optimal growth.
