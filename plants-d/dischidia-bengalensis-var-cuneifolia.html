Dischidia bengalensis var. cuneifolia

Dischidia bengalensis var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the Bengal fig-leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its stems are capable of aerial rooting, allowing it to climb and attach to surfaces.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
