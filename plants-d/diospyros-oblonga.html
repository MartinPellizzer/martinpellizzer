Diospyros oblonga

Diospyros oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to reddish fruits. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
