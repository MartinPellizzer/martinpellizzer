Dacrydium lycopodioides

Dacrydium lycopodioides, commonly known as the New Zealand clubmoss, is a unique plant belonging to the family Dacrydium.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant is native to New Zealand and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures are simple, consisting of small, spore-bearing cones rather than flowers.

Dacrydium lycopodioides plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
