Daucus muricatus var. erectus

Daucus muricatus var. erectus, commonly known as the hairy parsley or wild carrot, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, parsley-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, oblong fruits that contain seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
