Dahlia scapigera var. liebmannii

Dahlia scapigera var. liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's dahlia, is a hybrid cultivar originating from crosses between D. scapigera and D. hybrida.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, single or semi-double flowers in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its tuberous roots are used for propagation, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
