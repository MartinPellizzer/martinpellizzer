Dalbergia hupeana var. laccifera

Dalbergia hupeana var. laccifera, commonly known as the Chinese rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and wood quality, it is often cultivated in gardens and for timber production.
