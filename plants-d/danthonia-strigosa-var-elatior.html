Danthonia strigosa var. elatior

Danthonia strigosa var. elatior, commonly known as the tall hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
