Dianthus capitatus

Dianthus capitatus, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Dianthus capitatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
