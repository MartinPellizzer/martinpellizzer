Disterigma campanulatum

Disterigma campanulatum, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems that rise from a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Disterigma campanulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique form.
