Digitaria tangaensis

Digitaria tangaensis, commonly known as the Tanga grass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in savanna ecosystems and can thrive in both wet and dry seasons.

Digitaria tangaensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
