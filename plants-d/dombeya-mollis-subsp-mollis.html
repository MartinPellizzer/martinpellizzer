Dombeya mollis subsp. mollis

Dombeya mollis subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has soft, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Dombeya mollis subsp.

mollis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
