Delphinium nelsonii f. dumetorum

Delphinium nelsonii f. dumetorum, commonly known as the dwarf larkspur, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
