Delphinium nacladense

Delphinium nacladense, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the European Alps.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to cold, rocky environments. Delphinium nacladense is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also noted for its ornamental value in alpine gardens and its role in supporting local pollinators.
