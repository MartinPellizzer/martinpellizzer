Dioscorea sagittifolia

Dioscorea sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are valued for their medicinal properties and have been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
