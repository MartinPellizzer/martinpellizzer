Dipteracanthus violaceus

Dipteracanthus violaceus, commonly known as the violet-leafed dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-colored leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
