Dimorphotheca jucunda

Dimorphotheca jucunda, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Dimorphotheca jucunda is also an important plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
