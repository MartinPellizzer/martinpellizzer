Deparia tenuifolia

Deparia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with small, scale-like leaves along the underground stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

The fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
