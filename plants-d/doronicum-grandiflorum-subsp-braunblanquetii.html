Doronicum grandiflorum subsp. braunblanquetii

Doronicum grandiflorum subsp.

braunblanquetii, commonly known as the Braunblanquet's leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
