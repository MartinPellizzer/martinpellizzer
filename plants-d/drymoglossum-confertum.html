Drymoglossum confertum

Drymoglossum confertum, commonly known as the dense-leaf fern, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, overlapping leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette form. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short, erect stalks and release spores when mature.

Drymoglossum confertum is well-adapted to withstand desiccation, making it a common sight in dry, rocky habitats.
