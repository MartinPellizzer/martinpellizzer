Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. sennia

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

sennia, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows.

The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branching stems with showy, pink or purple flowers. It is characterized by its distinctive, spotted leaves and is often found in shaded environments.

This subspecies is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
