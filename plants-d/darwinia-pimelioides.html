Darwinia pimelioides

Darwinia pimelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its striking spikes of tubular red flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically grey-green in color, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. Darwinia pimelioides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators, particularly birds and insects.
