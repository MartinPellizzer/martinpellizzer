Dendropanax sessiliflorus

Dendropanax sessiliflorus, commonly known as the sessile-flower arrowwood, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels and are followed by dark blue berries.

Dendropanax sessiliflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
