Dendrobium stratiotes

Dendrobium stratiotes, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizomatous stem that produces numerous pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial shade for optimal growth.
