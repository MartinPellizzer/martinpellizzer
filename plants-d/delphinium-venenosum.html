Delphinium venenosum

Delphinium venenosum, commonly known as the toxic delphinium, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a biennial or perennial herb characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces spurred petals and has a distinctive arrangement of stamens and pistils. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium venenosum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both humans and animals if ingested.
