Derris spruceana

Derris spruceana, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
