Diplazium vastum

Diplazium vastum, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features compound leaves with numerous pinnae, which are often lance-shaped and have a glossy appearance. Diplazium vastum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.
