Diospyros cayennensis

Diospyros cayennensis, commonly known as the Cayenne persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a smooth, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
