Dichromena polyphylla

Dichromena polyphylla, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a white center.

Dichromena polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
