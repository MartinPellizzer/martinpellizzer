Dialium procerum

Dialium procerum, commonly known as the slender African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to West and Central Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, oval fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species also plays a role in local ecosystems and has traditional uses in various communities.
