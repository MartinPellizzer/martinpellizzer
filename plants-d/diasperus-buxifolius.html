Diasperus buxifolius

Diasperus buxifolius, commonly known as the boxleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant features small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically greenish-white in color, appearing in summer.

Diasperus buxifolius is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in environments with limited rainfall.
