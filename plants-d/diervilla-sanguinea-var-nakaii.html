Diervilla sanguinea var. nakaii

Diervilla sanguinea var. nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai's red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant, red flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. This plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
