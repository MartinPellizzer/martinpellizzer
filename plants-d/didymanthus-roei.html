Didymanthus roei

Didymanthus roei, commonly known as the red-flowered didymanthus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Didymanthus roei is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
