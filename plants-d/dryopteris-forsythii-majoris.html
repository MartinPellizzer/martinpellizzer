Dryopteris forsythii-majoris

Dryopteris forsythii-majoris, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a large, robust fern native to eastern Asia.

It features fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length, with a distinctive bipinnate structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its fertile fronds bear sporangia on the underside, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in woodland ecosystems.
