Delphinium intermedium var. alpinum

Delphinium intermedium var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in terminal racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy.

This species thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
