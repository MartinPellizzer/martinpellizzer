Desmodium rosei

Desmodium rosei, commonly known as the pink burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hairy, spiny pods, which aid in seed dispersal.

Desmodium rosei is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
