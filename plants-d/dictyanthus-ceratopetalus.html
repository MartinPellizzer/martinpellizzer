Dictyanthus ceratopetalus

Dictyanthus ceratopetalus, commonly known as the horned bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, showy inflorescences that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The unique morphology of its flowers and its adaptation to high-altitude environments make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
