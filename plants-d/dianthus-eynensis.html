Dianthus eynensis

Dianthus eynensis, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branching.

Dianthus eynensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
