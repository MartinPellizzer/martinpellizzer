Dianthus polymorphus subsp. bessarabicus

Dianthus polymorphus subsp.

bessarabicus, commonly known as the Bessarabian carnation, is a flowering plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
