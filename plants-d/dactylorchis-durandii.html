Dactylorchis durandii

Dactylorchis durandii, commonly known as the Durand's twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

This herbaceous plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its morphology includes a short, rhizomatous stem with two opposite, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spike, characteristic of many orchid species.

It is noted for its unique adaptations to low-light conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
