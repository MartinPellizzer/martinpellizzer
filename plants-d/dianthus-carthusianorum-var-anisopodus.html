Dianthus carthusianorum var. anisopodus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. anisopodus, commonly known as the Carthusian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink or red, double flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant colors and compact growth habit.
