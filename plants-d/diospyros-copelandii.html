Diospyros copelandii

Diospyros copelandii, commonly known as the Hawaiian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that ripen in late summer. These berries are edible when fully mature and have a sweet, honey-like flavor.

Diospyros copelandii is an important species in Hawaiian ecology and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
