Dendrobium rutriferum

Dendrobium rutriferum, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-tipped pseudobulbs and long, arching inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces a variety of flower colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Dendrobium rutriferum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
