Dioscorea pierrei

Dioscorea pierrei, commonly known as the Cameroon yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary economic product. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and has unisexual flowers that are typically yellow or green. Its tubers are used as a food source and have a starchy, nutty flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
