Didymoglossum intramarginale

Didymoglossum intramarginale, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bog moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, acidic environments.

It is typically found in bogs, fens, and other wetland habitats across temperate regions. The plant has delicate, grass-like leaves that are often crowded together, giving it a dense appearance. Its reproductive structures, called capsules, are borne on short stalks and release spores when mature.

This moss plays an important role in stabilizing soil and maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems.
