Dioscorea opaca

Dioscorea opaca, commonly known as yellow wood or Indian potato, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for their medicinal properties. These tubers contain compounds like diosgenin, which has been studied for its potential health benefits.

Dioscorea opaca is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
