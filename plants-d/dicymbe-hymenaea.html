Dicymbe hymenaea

Dicymbe hymenaea, commonly known as the African sycamore or sycamore tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit pods. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is thick and fissured, often appearing grayish-brown, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
