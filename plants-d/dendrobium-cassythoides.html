Dendrobium cassythoides

Dendrobium cassythoides, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in racemes, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
