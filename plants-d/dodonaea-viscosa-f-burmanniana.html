Dodonaea viscosa f. burmanniana

Dodonaea viscosa f. burmanniana, commonly known as the Burmanniana glorybower, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal or arid landscapes.
