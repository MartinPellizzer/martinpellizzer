Draba × schaeferi

Draba × schaeferi, commonly known as the Schaefer's whitlow grass, is a hybrid plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, and its flowering period occurs in early spring. It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations.
