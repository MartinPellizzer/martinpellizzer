Dioscorea joseensis

Dioscorea joseensis, commonly known as the Jose's yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tuberous roots of Dioscorea joseensis are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their nutritional and pharmacological properties.
