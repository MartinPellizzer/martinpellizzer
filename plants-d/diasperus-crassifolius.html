Diasperus crassifolius

Diasperus crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
