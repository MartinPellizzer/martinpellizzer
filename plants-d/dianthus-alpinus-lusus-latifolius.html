Dianthus alpinus lusus latifolius

Dianthus alpinus lusus latifolius, commonly known as the alpine carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant produces clusters of small, rounded flowers on slender stems, often with a distinctive fringed appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette at the base.

Dianthus alpinus lusus latifolius is well adapted to cold, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
