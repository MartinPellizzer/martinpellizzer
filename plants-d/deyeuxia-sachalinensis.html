Deyeuxia sachalinensis

Deyeuxia sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the cool temperate regions of Asia, including parts of Russia, Japan, and China.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
