Disa cornuta var. aemula

Disa cornuta var. aemula, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a striking South African orchid species characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the grasslands of the Eastern Cape and Western Cape provinces. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with a single, showy flower that resembles a horn. Its vibrant pink or red petals are adorned with intricate patterns, making it a popular choice for cultivation.

This variant is notable for its striking appearance and is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value.
