Dryandra stenoprion

Dryandra stenoprion, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dryandra, is a member of the Proteaceae family, native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
