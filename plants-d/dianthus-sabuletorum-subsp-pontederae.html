Dianthus sabuletorum subsp. pontederae

Dianthus sabuletorum subsp.

pontederae, commonly known as the Pontedera carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces numerous small, showy flowers arranged in a dense, rounded inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
