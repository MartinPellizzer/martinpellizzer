Dyckia beateae

Dyckia beateae, commonly known as the Beate's dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit and thick, fibrous leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Dyckia beateae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
