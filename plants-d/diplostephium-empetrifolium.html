Diplostephium empetrifolium

Diplostephium empetrifolium, commonly known as the rock-rose, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and is often found in rocky soils and limestone areas.
