Draba longisquamosa

Draba longisquamosa, commonly known as the long-scale whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in dense, long-lasting white or grayish hairs.

Draba longisquamosa is known for its ability to survive in harsh, cold climates, making it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
