Decaspermum paniculatum var. khasianum

Decaspermum paniculatum var. khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, contributing to its dispersal in the natural habitat.

The species is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.
