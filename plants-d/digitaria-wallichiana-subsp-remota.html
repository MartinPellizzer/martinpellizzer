Digitaria wallichiana subsp. remota

Digitaria wallichiana subsp.

remota, commonly known as the slender digit grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, two-rowed structures.

This subspecies is characterized by its adaptability to wet environments and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
