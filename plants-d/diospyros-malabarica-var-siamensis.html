Diospyros malabarica var. siamensis

Diospyros malabarica var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
