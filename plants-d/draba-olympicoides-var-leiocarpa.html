Draba olympicoides var. leiocarpa

Draba olympicoides var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the Olympic Mountain Draba, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which set it apart from other Draba species.
