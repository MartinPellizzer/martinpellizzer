Dracaena brasiliensis

Dracaena brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian dragon tree, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

Dracaena brasiliensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
