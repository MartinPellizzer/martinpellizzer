Dactylorhiza sambucina f. hybrida

Dactylorhiza sambucina f. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale purple or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. It has lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This orchid is known for its ability to hybridize with other Dactylorhiza species, leading to the development of various natural hybrids.
