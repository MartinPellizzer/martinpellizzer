Diospyros pyrrhocarpoides

Diospyros pyrrhocarpoides, commonly known as the red-fruit ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, red berries containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of its valuable timber.
