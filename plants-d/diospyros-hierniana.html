Diospyros hierniana

Diospyros hierniana, commonly known as the Hiern's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that ripens in late summer.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
