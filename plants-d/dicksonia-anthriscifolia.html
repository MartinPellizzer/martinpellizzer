Dicksonia anthriscifolia

Dicksonia anthriscifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories. Its fronds are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique feature makes it a striking and ecologically significant plant in its natural habitat.
