Dortmanna cordata

Dortmanna cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf sorrel, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Dortmanna cordata is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
