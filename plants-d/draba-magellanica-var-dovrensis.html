Draba magellanica var. dovrensis

Draba magellanica var. dovrensis, commonly known as the Magellanic whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. This variety is characterized by its low-growing habit, with compact rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in tight clusters, typically blooming in spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
