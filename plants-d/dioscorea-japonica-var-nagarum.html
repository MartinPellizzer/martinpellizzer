Dioscorea japonica var. nagarum

Dioscorea japonica var. nagarum, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its stoloniferous growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces elongated, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas.

The tubers of this variety are notable for their high starch content and distinctive flavor.
