Dyctiospora vaginata

Dyctiospora vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. Dyctiospora vaginata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural contexts.
