Drimia elata

Drimia elata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces clusters of white or yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Drimia elata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
