Daucus carota var. serratus

Daucus carota var. serratus, commonly known as the curly top carrot, is a botanical variant of the common carrot characterized by its distinctive, lobed, and serrated leaves.

This plant belongs to theApiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and edible plants such as parsley and celery. The leaves are typically more pronounced and have a feathery appearance compared to the standard carrot variety. While the roots of this variant are not as commonly cultivated for consumption, the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable addition to gardens and wildflower landscapes.
