Dioscorea buchananii subsp. buchananii

Dioscorea buchananii subsp.

buchananii, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family. It is a climbing herbaceous perennial that produces tubers as its primary storage organ.

The plant is native to tropical Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it bears small, greenish-white flowers.

The tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source in various regions of Africa.
