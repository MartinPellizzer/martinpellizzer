Dendrobium similissimum

Dendrobium similissimum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that are often covered in clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green blooms that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium similissimum is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
