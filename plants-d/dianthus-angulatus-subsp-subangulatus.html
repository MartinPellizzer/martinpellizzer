Dianthus angulatus subsp. subangulatus

Dianthus angulatus subsp.

subangulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
