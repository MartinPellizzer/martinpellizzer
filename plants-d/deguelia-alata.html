Deguelia alata

Deguelia alata, commonly known as the climbing tamarind, is a tropical shrub or vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a common ornamental and shade plant in tropical regions.

Deguelia alata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
