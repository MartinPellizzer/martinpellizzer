Digitaria monodactyla var. explicata

Digitaria monodactyla var. explicata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
