Dracophyllum menziesii

Dracophyllum menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' draba, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is well adapted to coastal and mountainous regions, often found in areas with cool, humid climates.

Dracophyllum menziesii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
