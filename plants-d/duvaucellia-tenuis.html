Duvaucellia tenuis

Duvaucellia tenuis, commonly known as the slender desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils with low water availability.
