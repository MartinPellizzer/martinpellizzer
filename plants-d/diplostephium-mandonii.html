Diplostephium mandonii

Diplostephium mandonii, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its latex sap is milky and contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to many animals.

It serves as a host plant for certain butterfly species, including the painted lady.
