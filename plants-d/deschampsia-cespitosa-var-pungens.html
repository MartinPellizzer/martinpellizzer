Deschampsia cespitosa var. pungens

Deschampsia cespitosa var. pungens, commonly known as the spiked feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear sharp, bristly awns. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows, roadsides, and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
