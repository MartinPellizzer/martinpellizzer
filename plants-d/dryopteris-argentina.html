Dryopteris argentina

Dryopteris argentina, commonly known as the Argentine wood fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its evergreen fronds that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, lace-like appearance.

Dryopteris argentina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
