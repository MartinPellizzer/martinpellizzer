Deutzia crenata f. candidissima

Deutzia crenata f. candidissima, commonly known as the Whiteflower Deutzia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a compact shrub characterized by its profuse display of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The foliage is typically dark green, with serrated leaves that provide a striking contrast to the pale blooms. Native to East Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
