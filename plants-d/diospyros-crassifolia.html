Diospyros crassifolia

Diospyros crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark purple or black berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The wood of this species is highly valued for its density and durability, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.
