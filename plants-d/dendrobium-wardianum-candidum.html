Dendrobium wardianum candidum

Dendrobium wardianum candidum, commonly known as the White Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid tropical climates. The plant features long, cane-like stems covered in clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This orchid is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
