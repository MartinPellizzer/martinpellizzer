Damnacanthus indicus var. macrophyllus

Damnacanthus indicus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf horse bean, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its elongated, bean-like pods and palmately lobed leaves. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are large and hairy, with a distinctive waxy coating that aids in water retention.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
