Drepania falcata

Drepania falcata, commonly known as the sickle fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fronds are compound, with a delicate, feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.
