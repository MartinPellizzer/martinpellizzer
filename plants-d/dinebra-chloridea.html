Dinebra chloridea

Dinebra chloridea, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that can range from green to yellow and orange. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and orchid enthusiasts.
