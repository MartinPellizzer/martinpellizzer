Damnacanthus angustifolius

Damnacanthus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved devil's bit.

It is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
