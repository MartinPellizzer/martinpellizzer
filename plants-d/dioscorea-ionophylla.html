Dioscorea ionophylla

Dioscorea ionophylla, commonly known as the giant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with ample sunlight.

Due to its rapid growth and ecological impact, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
