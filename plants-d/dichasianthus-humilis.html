Dichasianthus humilis

Dichasianthus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf dichasianthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dichasianthus humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
