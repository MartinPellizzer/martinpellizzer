Dorstenia cristata

Dorstenia cristata, commonly known as the Crown Vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like inflorescence, which consists of a dense cluster of small, white to pink flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often folded and can be quite large, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for erosion control and landscaping.

Dorstenia cristata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility in agricultural settings.
