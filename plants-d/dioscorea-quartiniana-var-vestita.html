Dioscorea quartiniana var. vestita

Dioscorea quartiniana var. vestita, commonly known as the white yam, is a tuberous plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are often used as a food source. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and unisexual.

The tubers of this variety are notable for their white or pale yellow color and are valued for their nutritional content and medicinal properties.
