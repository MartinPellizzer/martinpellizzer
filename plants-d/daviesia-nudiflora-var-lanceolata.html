Daviesia nudiflora var. lanceolata

Daviesia nudiflora var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolated hakea, is a shrub native to south-eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
