Dendrobium muriciferum

Dendrobium muriciferum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a thick, hairy stem that often branches and can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with white lips, blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium muriciferum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
