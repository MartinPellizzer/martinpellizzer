Disa parvilabris

Disa parvilabris, commonly known as the small-lipped disa, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive, narrow lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Disa parvilabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
