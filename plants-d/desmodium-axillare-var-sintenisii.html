Desmodium axillare var. sintenisii

Desmodium axillare var. sintenisii, commonly known as the creeping desmodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical or subtropical environments and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which helps improve soil fertility.
