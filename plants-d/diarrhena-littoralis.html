Diarrhena littoralis

Diarrhena littoralis, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines. The plant features erect stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in the summer months.

Diarrhena littoralis is adapted to salty and windy environments, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
