Dioscorea igualamontana

Dioscorea igualamontana, commonly known as the Iquique yam, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in countries like Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the genus Dioscorea, which includes various species of yams, and is characterized by its tuberous roots that are used for food. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

Its tubers are often cultivated for their nutritional value, providing a good source of carbohydrates and essential nutrients.
