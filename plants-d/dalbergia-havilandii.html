Dalbergia havilandii

Dalbergia havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and compound leaves with a pinnate structure. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and the rarity of its wood, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
