Duvalia sulcata subsp. seminuda

Duvalia sulcata subsp.

seminuda, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed duvalia, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and narrow, with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
