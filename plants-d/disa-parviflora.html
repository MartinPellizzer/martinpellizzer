Disa parviflora

Disa parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in a loose cluster. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its flowers have a pleasant, sweet fragrance and are often visited by various pollinators.

Disa parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
