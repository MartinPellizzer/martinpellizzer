Sempervivum verereginae-amaliae

Sempervivum verereginae-amaliae, commonly known as the Amaliena Sempervivum, is a species of succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts interested in hardy, low-maintenance plants.
