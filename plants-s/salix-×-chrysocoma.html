Salix × chrysocoma

Salix × chrysocoma, commonly known as the golden willow, is a hybrid willow species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which is more prominent in the spring and early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a pale underside.

Salix × chrysocoma is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
