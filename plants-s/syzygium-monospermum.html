Syzygium monospermum

Syzygium monospermum, commonly known as the single-seeded clove.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Flores in Indonesia. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown flowers and dark, glossy berries. Its fruit contains a single seed, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
