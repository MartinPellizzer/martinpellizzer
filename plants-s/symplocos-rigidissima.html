Symplocos rigidissima

Symplocos rigidissima, commonly known as the rigid wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Symplocos rigidissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
