Scabiosa sericea

Scabiosa sericea, commonly known as silvery scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fluffy, spherical flower heads that resemble small clouds, typically blooming in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a soft, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa sericea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
