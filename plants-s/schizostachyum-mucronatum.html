Schizostachyum mucronatum

Schizostachyum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its fast growth and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a source of sustainable materials.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
