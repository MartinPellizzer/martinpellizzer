Solanum marginatum var. polyhoplum

Solanum marginatum var. polyhoplum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and pink flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns of veins and wavy edges. The plant produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a velvety texture and a distinctive margin that is often wavy or toothed.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
