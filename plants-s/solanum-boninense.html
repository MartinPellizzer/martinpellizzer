Solanum boninense

Solanum boninense, commonly known as the Bonin nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
