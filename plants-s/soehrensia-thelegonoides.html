Soehrensia thelegonoides

Soehrensia thelegonoides, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Soehrensia thelegonoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
