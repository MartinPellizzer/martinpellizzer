Sida peruviana

Sida peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its fibrous stems and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Sida peruviana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
