Schoenus complanatus

Schoenus complanatus, commonly known as the flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Schoenus complanatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water garden settings.
