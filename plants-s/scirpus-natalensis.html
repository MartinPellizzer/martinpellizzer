Scirpus natalensis

Scirpus natalensis, commonly known as Natal bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Scirpus natalensis thrives in marshy areas and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.
