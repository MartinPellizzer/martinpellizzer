Scirpus zeyheri

Scirpus zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Scirpus zeyheri is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
