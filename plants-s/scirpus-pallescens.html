Scirpus pallescens

Scirpus pallescens, commonly known as the pale bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize wetland soils and provides habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus pallescens is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
