Sedum porphyranthes

Sedum porphyranthes, commonly known as the purple stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense mats make it an effective ground cover in arid environments.
