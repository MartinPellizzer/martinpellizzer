Sideritis hirsuta var. hirtula

Sideritis hirsuta var. hirtula, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth form with hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
