Scirpus humillimus

Scirpus humillimus, commonly known as the dwarf bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbon-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in shallow water or moist soil.

Scirpus humillimus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
