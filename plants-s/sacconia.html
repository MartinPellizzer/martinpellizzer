Sacconia

Sacconia, commonly known as the "moonflower," is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, tubular flowers that open only at night. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have a slightly sweet, peppery flavor. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Sacconia thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
