Syzygium perforatum

Syzygium perforatum, commonly known as the fragrant paper mulberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves with a distinctive white or pale waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by round, fleshy berries. Syzygium perforatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are known to be used in the production of paper and as a source of natural dyes.
