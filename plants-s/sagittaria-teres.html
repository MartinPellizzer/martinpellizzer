Sagittaria teres

Sagittaria teres, commonly known as narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots anchor in the substrate. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters above the water.

Sagittaria teres plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
