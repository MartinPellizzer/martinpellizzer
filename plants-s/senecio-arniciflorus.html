Senecio arniciflorus

Senecio arniciflorus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Senecio arniciflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
