Steviopsis dryophila

Steviopsis dryophila, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Steviopsis dryophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
