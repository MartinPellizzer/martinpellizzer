Silene apetala

Silene apetala, commonly known as the naked-flower catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, often hairy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its flowers, which lack petals, are usually pink or reddish in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Silene apetala plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to arid environments.
