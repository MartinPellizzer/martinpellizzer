Senecio longipetalus

Senecio longipetalus, commonly known as the long-petaled groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers that are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Senecio longipetalus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking blooms and compact growth habit.
