Salvia vasta var. vasta

Salvia vasta var. vasta, commonly known as the common sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia vasta var. vasta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
