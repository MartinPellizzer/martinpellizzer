Salvia cinerarioides f. approximata

Salvia cinerarioides f. approximata, commonly known as the Mexican sage or blue mistflower, is a cultivar of the Salvia genus native to Mexico.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its striking blue to purple floral spikes that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
