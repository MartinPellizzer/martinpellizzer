Senna birostris

Senna birostris, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
