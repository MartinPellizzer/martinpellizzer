Selaginella sulcata var. microphylla

Selaginella sulcata var. microphylla, commonly known as the lesser spike moss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, giving it a feathery appearance. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spores, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in shaded garden settings.
