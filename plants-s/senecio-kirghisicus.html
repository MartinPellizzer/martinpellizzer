Senecio kirghisicus

Senecio kirghisicus, commonly known as the Kirghiz groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
