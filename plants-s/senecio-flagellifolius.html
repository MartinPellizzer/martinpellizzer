Senecio flagellifolius

Senecio flagellifolius, commonly known as the whipleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that resemble whips, hence its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
