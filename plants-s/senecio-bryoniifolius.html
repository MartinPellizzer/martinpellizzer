Senecio bryoniifolius

Senecio bryoniifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid climates.
