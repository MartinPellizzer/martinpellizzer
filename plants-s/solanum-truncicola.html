Solanum truncicola

Solanum truncicola, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a truncate or notched base. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its berries, which are edible when ripe, are usually yellow or orange and contain the seeds.

Solanum truncicola is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
