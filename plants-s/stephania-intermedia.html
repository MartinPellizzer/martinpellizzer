Stephania intermedia

Stephania intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese herb "Fang Ji," is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright orange berries. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

However, it is toxic and should be handled with care.
