Sasa harae

Sasa harae, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly sweet aroma.

Sasa harae thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
