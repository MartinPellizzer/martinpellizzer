Suaeda aralocaspica

Suaeda aralocaspica, commonly known as the Central Asian saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and desert regions where soil salinity is high.

Suaeda aralocaspica plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
