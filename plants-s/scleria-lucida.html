Scleria lucida

Scleria lucida, commonly known as the shining spearplant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in dense clusters, which are typically arranged in spikes. Scleria lucida is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
