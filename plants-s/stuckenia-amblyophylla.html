Stuckenia amblyophylla

Stuckenia amblyophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often floating or partially submerged, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or white in color.

Stuckenia amblyophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
