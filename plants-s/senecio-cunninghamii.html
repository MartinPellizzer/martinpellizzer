Senecio cunninghamii

Senecio cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Senecio cunninghamii is well adapted to arid environments and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
