Scirpus fimbristyloides

Scirpus fimbristyloides, commonly known as the narrow-stem bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in marshy environments with saturated soils. The plant produces tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a fibrous texture, contributing to its structural strength.

Scirpus fimbristyloides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
