Salsola aralensis

Salsola aralensis, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Salsola aralensis is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils and extreme temperatures.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
