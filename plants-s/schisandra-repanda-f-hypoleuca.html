Schisandra repanda f. hypoleuca

Schisandra repanda f. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered schisandra, is a rare variant of the schisandra plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested for their medicinal properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral characteristics and potential pharmacological applications.
