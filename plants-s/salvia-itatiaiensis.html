Salvia itatiaiensis

Salvia itatiaiensis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring upright, branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a grayish-green appearance.

Salvia itatiaiensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
