Solea urticifolia

Solea urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains fine hairs that release an irritating substance when touched, causing a stinging sensation. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Solea urticifolia is known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
