Spirillus robbinsii

Spirillus robbinsii, commonly known as the Robbins' spirilla, is a unique plant characterized by its spiral-shaped leaves that resemble a twisted ribbon.

It is a member of the Spirillaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often a striking shade of green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Spirillus robbinsii is considered a sensitive indicator species for ecological health.
