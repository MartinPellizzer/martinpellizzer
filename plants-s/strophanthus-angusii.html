Strophanthus angusii

Strophanthus angusii, commonly known as the red devil's trumpet, is a striking perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to deep red, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange or red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Strophanthus angusii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
