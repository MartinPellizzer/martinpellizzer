Scabiosa peduncularis

Scabiosa peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 12 to 24 inches and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa peduncularis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
