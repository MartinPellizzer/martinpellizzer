Syzygium abulugense

Syzygium abulugense, commonly known as the Bulugan apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Syzygium abulugense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
