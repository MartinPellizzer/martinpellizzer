Sida brachyphylla

Sida brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is often found in coastal regions. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Sida brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
