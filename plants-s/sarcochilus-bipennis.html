Sarcochilus bipennis

Sarcochilus bipennis, commonly known as the twin-winged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often twisted leaves, which are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with two pairs of prominent, wing-like petals. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Sarcochilus bipennis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
