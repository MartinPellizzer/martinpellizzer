Stylidium pilosum var. brevius

Stylidium pilosum var. brevius, commonly known as the hairy triggerplant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Australia.

It features a rosette of narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive trigger mechanism that aids in pollination by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique morphology and adaptive features make it a notable species in the genus Stylidium.
