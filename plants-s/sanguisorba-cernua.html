Sanguisorba cernua

Sanguisorba cernua, commonly known as white beakweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Sanguisorba cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
