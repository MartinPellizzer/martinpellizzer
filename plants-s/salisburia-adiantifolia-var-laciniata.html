Salisburia adiantifolia var. laciniata

Salisburia adiantifolia var. laciniata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and has a unique ability to absorb and retain water, which helps it survive in its natural habitat. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often found in conservation efforts aimed at preserving biodiversity in tropical rainforests.
