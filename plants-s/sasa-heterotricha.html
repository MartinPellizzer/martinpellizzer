Sasa heterotricha

Sasa heterotricha, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa heterotricha is often used in traditional landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
