Scrophularia gorganica

Scrophularia gorganica, commonly known as the Sicilian scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Scrophularia gorganica is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
