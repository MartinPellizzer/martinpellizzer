Sedum baicalense

Sedum baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal sedum, is a succulent plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its leaves are often blue-green in color and can store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments.

Sedum baicalense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
