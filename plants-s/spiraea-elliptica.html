Spiraea elliptica

Spiraea elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early to mid-summer. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea elliptica is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
