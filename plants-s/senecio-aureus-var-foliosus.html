Senecio aureus var. foliosus

Senecio aureus var. foliosus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
