Spiraea callosa froebelii

Spiraea callosa froebelii, commonly known as the froebelii spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental hedges and borders.
