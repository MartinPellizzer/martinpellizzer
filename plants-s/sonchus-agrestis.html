Sonchus agrestis

Sonchus agrestis, commonly known as field sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae).

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has a rosette of broad, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc. It is characterized by its milky sap and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa but has been introduced to other regions.
