Scelochilus equitans

Scelochilus equitans, commonly known as the horse-riding orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and woodland habitats, often in well-drained soils. The plant produces a single stem with several leaves and bears a spike of small, greenish-white flowers. Its blooming period occurs during the late winter to early spring.

The species is characterized by its unique floral structure and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
