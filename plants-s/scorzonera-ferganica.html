Scorzonera ferganica

Scorzonera ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
