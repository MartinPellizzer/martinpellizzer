Salix × langeana

Salix × langeana, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, often giving it a distinctive, elegant look. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, and small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

The botanical features of Salix × langeana make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
