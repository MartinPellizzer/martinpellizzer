Sapindus montanus

Sapindus montanus, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces small, berry-like fruits that contain a toxic, soapy substance used by Indigenous peoples for cleaning.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and supporting various insect species.
