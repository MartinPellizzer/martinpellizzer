Solanum commersonii var. pubescens

Solanum commersonii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and purple to violet flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are typically covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
