Sabina compacta

Sabina compacta, commonly known as the dwarf juniper, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, spherical berries that are red when mature, adding visual interest in the winter months. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a foundation plant.

Sabina compacta is valued for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements in landscaping.
