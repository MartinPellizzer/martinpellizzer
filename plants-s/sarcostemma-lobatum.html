Sarcostemma lobatum

Sarcostemma lobatum, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly lobed base. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.

Sarcostemma lobatum is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid environments.
