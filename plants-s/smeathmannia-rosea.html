Smeathmannia rosea

Smeathmannia rosea, commonly known as the silver dollar tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that resemble silver coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers followed by orange-red fruit capsules. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Smeathmannia rosea is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
