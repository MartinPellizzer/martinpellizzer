Sorghum nitidum f. aristatum

Sorghum nitidum f. aristatum, commonly known as the aristate sorghum, is a variant of sorghum characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features elongated, stiff leaves with a prominent midrib and a narrow, pointed tip, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its growth habit is upright, with tall, robust stems that can reach several meters in height.

The leaves are typically green, though they may exhibit subtle variegation depending on environmental conditions.
