Scrophularia lanceolata var. occidentalis

Scrophularia lanceolata var. occidentalis, commonly known as Western dog's mercury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky areas. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in spikes, blooming in early to mid-summer.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
