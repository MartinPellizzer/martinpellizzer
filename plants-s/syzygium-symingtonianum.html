Syzygium symingtonianum

Syzygium symingtonianum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its reddish, aromatic wood. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and has simple, opposite leaves. It produces small, white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries.

Due to its valuable timber, it is often cultivated for commercial use.
