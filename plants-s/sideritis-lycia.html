Sideritis lycia

Sideritis lycia, commonly known as Cyprus sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Sideritis lycia is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.
