Sarcanthus siamensis

Sarcanthus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Sarcanthus siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some parts of its native range.
