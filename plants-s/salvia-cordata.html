Salvia cordata

Salvia cordata, commonly known as heartleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white down. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia cordata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties.
