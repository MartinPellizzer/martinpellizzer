Sasa depauperata

Sasa depauperata, commonly known as the sparse bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall aesthetic and ecological role in forest understories.

Sasa depauperata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
