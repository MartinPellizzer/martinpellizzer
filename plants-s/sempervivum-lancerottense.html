Sempervivum lancerottense

Sempervivum lancerottense, commonly known as the Lancerottense houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint, often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sempervivum lancerottense is also noted for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, showcasing its resilience as a hardy species.
