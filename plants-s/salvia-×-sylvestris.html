Salvia × sylvestris

Salvia × sylvestris, commonly known as woodland sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a versatile addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
