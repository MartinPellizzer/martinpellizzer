Sidalcea malviflora

Sidalcea malviflora, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Sidalcea malviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
