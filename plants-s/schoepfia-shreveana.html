Schoepfia shreveana

Schoepfia shreveana, commonly known as Shreve's schoepfia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and silvery-gray, which helps reduce water loss.

The showy yellow to orange flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
