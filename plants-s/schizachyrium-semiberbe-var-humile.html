Schizachyrium semiberbe var. humile

Schizachyrium semiberbe var. humile, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native grass species found in the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage that turns coppery red in the fall. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with blue or purple.

Schizachyrium semiberbe var. humile is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
