Seddera

Seddera, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to minimize water loss in harsh environments.

Seddera plays a role in supporting local pollinators, particularly bees and butterflies, due to its nectar-rich blooms.
