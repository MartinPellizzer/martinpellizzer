Schomburgkia vandenbergiana

Schomburgkia vandenbergiana, commonly known as the Vandenberg's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like flowers with long, slender sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a vibrant pink or purple, with a distinctive yellow lip that attracts pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
