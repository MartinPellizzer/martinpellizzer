Sesbania aegyptiaca var. picta

Sesbania aegyptiaca var. picta, commonly known as the Painted Crownleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a distinctive red and green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it useful for soil stabilization and as a forage plant.
