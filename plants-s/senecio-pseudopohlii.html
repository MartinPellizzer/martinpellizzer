Senecio pseudopohlii

Senecio pseudopohlii, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom during the warmer months. It has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Senecio pseudopohlii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is part of the Asteraceae family.
