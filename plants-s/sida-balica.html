Sida balica

Sida balica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Sida balica is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

It is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
