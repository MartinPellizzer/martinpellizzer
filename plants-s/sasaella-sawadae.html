Sasaella sawadae

Sasaella sawadae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically ranging in color from light green to a deeper emerald.

Sasaella sawadae thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
