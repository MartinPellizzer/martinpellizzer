Satureja alpina var. baumgartenii

Satureja alpina var. baumgartenii, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. The plant features small, fragrant leaves with a strong, peppery aroma, and its flowers are usually pink or purple. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, sharing similar morphological traits.
