Sorghum trichocladum

Sorghum trichocladum, commonly known as the African millet, is a hardy, drought-resistant grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. The plant is well adapted to poor soil conditions and can thrive in arid environments. Its seeds are used as a staple food in many parts of Africa and Asia.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its nutritional content and agricultural resilience.
