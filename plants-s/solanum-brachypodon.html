Solanum brachypodon

Solanum brachypodon, commonly known as the short-stalked nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often found in disturbed habitats and is not widely cultivated.
