Senefeldera multiflora var. genuina

Senefeldera multiflora var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf senefeldera, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
