Sciodaphyllum merinoi

Sciodaphyllum merinoi, commonly known as the Merino's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It is native to the cloud forests of South America, particularly in regions of Ecuador and Peru. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers. It relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also contributing to the ecosystem by providing food for birds and insects.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important species in its native habitat.
