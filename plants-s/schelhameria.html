Schelhameria

Schelhameria, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, drooping inflorescences that resemble cascading raindrops, hence its common name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Schelhameria species typically have compound leaves and produce small, pod-like fruits.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
