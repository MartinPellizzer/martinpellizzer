Sutera revoluta

Sutera revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Sutera revoluta is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
