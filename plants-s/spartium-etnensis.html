Spartium etnensis

Spartium etnensis, commonly known as Etna thornweed, is a shrub native to the island of Sicily, specifically found in the volcanic regions around Mount Etna.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Spartium etnensis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.
