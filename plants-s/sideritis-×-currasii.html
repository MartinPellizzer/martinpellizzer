Sideritis × currasii

Sideritis × currasii, commonly known as the Greek sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Sideritis × currasii is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
