Syzygium teretiflorum

Syzygium teretiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered clove.

It is a flowering plant native to Australia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties. Its fruit, a small, round drupe, contains a single seed and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Syzygium teretiflorum thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.
