Stevia liebmannii

Stevia liebmannii, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its intensely sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides. These compounds are used as a natural, non-nutritive sweetener in various food products.

Stevia liebmannii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and commercial cultivation.
