Sinarundinaria × nipponica

Sinarundinaria × nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese tree fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Alsophila pinnata and Cyrtomium falcatum.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in height. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while the rhizome is fibrous and robust.

Sinarundinaria × nipponica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to add a tropical feel to gardens.
