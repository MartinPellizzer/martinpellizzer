Senecio triactinus

Senecio triactinus, commonly known as the three-ribbed groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Senecio triactinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
