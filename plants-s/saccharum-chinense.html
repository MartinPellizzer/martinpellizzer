Saccharum chinense

Saccharum chinense, commonly known as Chinese sugar cane, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It can grow up to 3 meters in height and has long, narrow leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces a thick, sugary juice in its stalks, which is used for making syrup and sugar. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Saccharum chinense is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
