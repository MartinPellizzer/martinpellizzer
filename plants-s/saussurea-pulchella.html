Saussurea pulchella

Saussurea pulchella, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron or the golden saffron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and dense, yellowish-orange inflorescence that resembles a cluster of saffron threads. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
