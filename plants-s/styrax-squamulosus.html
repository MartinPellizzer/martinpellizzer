Styrax squamulosus

Styrax squamulosus, commonly known as the silverleaf styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red berries. Styrax squamulosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscape design.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
