Stryphnodendron polyphyllum var. villosum

Stryphnodendron polyphyllum var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy or woolly Brazil nut tree, is a member of the Leguminosae family.

It is native to the Amazon basin and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, woody pods. The plant produces large, edible seeds that are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. Its root system plays a crucial role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of tropical rainforest ecosystems.
