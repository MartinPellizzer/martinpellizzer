Sarracenia × casei

Sarracenia × casei, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant that combines traits from its parent species, Sarracenia leucophylla and Sarracenia rubra.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular-shaped pitchers that secrete a digestive enzyme-laden fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's leaves are typically red or orange with intricate patterns, which serve to attract prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, Sarracenia × casei is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
