Solanum magnusianum

Solanum magnusianum, commonly known as the Magnusian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its berries, which are toxic, are often mistaken for edible fruits due to their appearance.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.
