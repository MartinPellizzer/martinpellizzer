Senecio actinoleucus

Senecio actinoleucus, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small white flowers. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio actinoleucus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
