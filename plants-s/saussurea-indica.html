Saussurea indica

Saussurea indica, commonly known as the Indian saffron or the greater Indian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flower heads that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in Ayurvedic remedies.

Saussurea indica thrives in high-altitude regions and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
