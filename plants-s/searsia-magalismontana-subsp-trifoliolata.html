Searsia magalismontana subsp. trifoliolata

Searsia magalismontana subsp.

trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa. It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
