Senecio cervilingua

Senecio cervilingua, commonly known as the Cape St. Francis Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio cervilingua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
