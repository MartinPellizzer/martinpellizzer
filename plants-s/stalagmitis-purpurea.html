Stalagmitis purpurea

Stalagmitis purpurea, commonly known as the purple stalagmite plant, is a unique species native to the humid, shaded environments of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and elongated, columnar growth habit that resembles stalagmites, hence its common name. The plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Stalagmitis purpurea is also known for its ability to produce small, fragrant flowers during the warmer months.
