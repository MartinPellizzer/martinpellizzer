Silene corrugata var. macrosperma

Silene corrugata var. macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its fleshy, often succulent leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its distinctive feature is its large, showy flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety macrosperma is distinguished by its larger seeds compared to other varieties of Silene corrugata.
