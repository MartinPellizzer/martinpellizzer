Spathodea hispida

Spathodea hispida, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Spathodea hispida thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
