Salvia venusta

Salvia venusta, commonly known as the showy sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-purple flowers and gray-green, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring square stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical name reflects its ornamental value, with "venusta" meaning beautiful in Latin.
