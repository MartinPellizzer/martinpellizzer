Saxifraga cernua f. exilioides

Saxifraga cernua f. exilioides, commonly known as the slender saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky environments.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

Its compact form and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
