Stipa endotricha

Stipa endotricha, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Stipa endotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
