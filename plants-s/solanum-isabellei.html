Solanum isabellei

Solanum isabellei, commonly known as the Isabelle's nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue. Its berries are initially green and turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum isabellei is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
