Sesleria rigida var. pancicii

Sesleria rigida var. pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's rigid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed, forming a compact rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale blue flowers in summer, arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in damp grasslands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded or partially sunny conditions.
