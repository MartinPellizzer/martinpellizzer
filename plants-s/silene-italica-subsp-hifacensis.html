Silene italica subsp. hifacensis

Silene italica subsp.

hifacensis, commonly known as the Hifacensis campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a low-growing perennial herb with simple, linear leaves and pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically blooms in summer, producing small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat.
