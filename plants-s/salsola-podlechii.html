Salsola podlechii

Salsola podlechii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and covered in a layer of fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Salsola podlechii is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
