Salvia sophrona

Salvia sophrona, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and they emit a mild, mint-like fragrance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
