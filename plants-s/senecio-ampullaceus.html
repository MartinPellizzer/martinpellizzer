Senecio ampullaceus

Senecio ampullaceus, commonly known as the balloon flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like seed heads that resemble small, colorful balloons. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
