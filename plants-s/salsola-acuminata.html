Salsola acuminata

Salsola acuminata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Salsola acuminata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it a common weed in agricultural and natural landscapes.
