Senecio glabrescens

Senecio glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairless leaves and produces daisy-like yellow flowers with purple centers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Senecio glabrescens is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
