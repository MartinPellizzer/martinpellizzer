Spartina bahiensis

Spartina bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, playing a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and preventing erosion. Spartina bahiensis is also adapted to withstand periodic flooding and saltwater exposure, making it an important component of estuarine ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth system allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to habitat complexity for various wildlife species.
