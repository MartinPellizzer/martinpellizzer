Sisyrinchium tenuifolium

Sisyrinchium tenuifolium, commonly known as slenderleaf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a blue-green hue. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers clustered in spikes, which bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in prairies, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Sisyrinchium tenuifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
