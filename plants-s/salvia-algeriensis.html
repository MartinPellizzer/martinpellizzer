Salvia algeriensis

Salvia algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian sage, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Salvia algeriensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
