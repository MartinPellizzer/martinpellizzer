Stipa longearistata

Stipa longearistata, commonly known as the long-haired feathergrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, silky florets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Stipa longearistata plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides habitat for various small fauna.
