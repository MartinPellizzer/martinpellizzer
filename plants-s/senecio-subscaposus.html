Senecio subscaposus

Senecio subscaposus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the tip of each stem, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface, and it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Senecio subscaposus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in grassland or shrubland habitats.
