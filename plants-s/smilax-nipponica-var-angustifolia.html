Smilax nipponica var. angustifolia

Smilax nipponica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Japanese smilax, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It features woody stems with sharp, spiny projections, typical of the Smilacaceae family. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and mammals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
