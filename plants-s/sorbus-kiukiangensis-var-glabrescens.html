Sorbus kiukiangensis var. glabrescens

Sorbus kiukiangensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in spring, followed by edible, orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
