Solanum dolichorhachis

Solanum dolichorhachis, commonly known as the long-stemmed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the evening, and its berries are toxic to humans and animals. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided in horticultural settings.
