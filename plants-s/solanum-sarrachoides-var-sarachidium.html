Solanum sarrachoides var. sarachidium

Solanum sarrachoides var. sarachidium, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree with smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by berries that turn red when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both ecological and medicinal studies.
