Stobaea rubricaulis

Stobaea rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Stobaea rubricaulis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
