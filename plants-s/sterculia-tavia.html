Sterculia tavia

Sterculia tavia, commonly known as the African tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland areas and is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.

The plant also has cultural and medicinal significance in various African communities.
