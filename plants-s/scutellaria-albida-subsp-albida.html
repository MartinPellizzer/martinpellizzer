Scutellaria albida subsp. albida

Scutellaria albida subsp.

albida, commonly known as white skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in whorls along the upper stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests. Scutellaria albida subsp.

albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
