Silene burchellii var. latifolia

Silene burchellii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant features a slender, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often broader than those of the typical species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

Silene burchellii var. latifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
