Scirpus eriophorum f. praelongus

Scirpus eriophorum f. praelongus, commonly known as the long-stemmed tussock sedge, is a variant of the tussock sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and fens, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

This botanical feature makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting diverse insect and bird life.
