Scaevola thesioides subsp. filifolia

Scaevola thesioides subsp.

filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is a perennial shrub that produces showy inflorescences, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a compact growth habit.
