Salix alpigena

Salix alpigena, commonly known as the alpine willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and its ability to thrive in cold, high-altitude environments. The plant produces catkins, which are its main reproductive structures, and has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in alpine meadows and tundra habitats.

Salix alpigena plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for various mountain-dwelling wildlife species.
