Satureja alpina var. kestica

Satureja alpina var. kestica, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a spice due to their pungent, aromatic properties.
