Sparganium multipedunculatum

Sparganium multipedunculatum, commonly known as the marsh spikerush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous spikelets, which are typically reddish or brownish in color. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it plays a role in providing habitat for various aquatic invertebrates.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
