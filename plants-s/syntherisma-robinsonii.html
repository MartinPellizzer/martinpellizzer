Syntherisma robinsonii

Syntherisma robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's syntherism, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant's leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
