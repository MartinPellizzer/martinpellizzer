Streblosa axilliflora var. angustifolia

Streblosa axilliflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved streblosa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
