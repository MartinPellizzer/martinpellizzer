Salsola eriosepala

Salsola eriosepala, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bead-like fruits. It has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Salsola eriosepala plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or degraded habitats.
