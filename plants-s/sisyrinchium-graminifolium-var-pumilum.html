Sisyrinchium graminifolium var. pumilum

Sisyrinchium graminifolium var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically blooms in early summer and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
