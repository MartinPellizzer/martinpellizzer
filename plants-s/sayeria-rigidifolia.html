Sayeria rigidifolia

Sayeria rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaved sayeria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching structure and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The leaves are typically rigid and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
