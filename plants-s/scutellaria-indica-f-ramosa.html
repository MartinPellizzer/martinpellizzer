Scutellaria indica f. ramosa

Scutellaria indica f. ramosa, commonly known as Indian skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
