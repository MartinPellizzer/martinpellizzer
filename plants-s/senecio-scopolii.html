Senecio scopolii

Senecio scopolii, commonly known as the Scopolii groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.
