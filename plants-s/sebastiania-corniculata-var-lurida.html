Sebastiania corniculata var. lurida

Sebastiania corniculata var. lurida, commonly known as the rough-seeded sebastiania, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and rocky slopes.
