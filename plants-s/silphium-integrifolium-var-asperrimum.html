Silphium integrifolium var. asperrimum

Silphium integrifolium var. asperrimum, commonly known as the roughleaf compass plant, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems and large, deeply lobed leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a head-like cluster. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Silphium integrifolium var. asperrimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
