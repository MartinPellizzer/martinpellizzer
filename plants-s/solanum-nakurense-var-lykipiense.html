Solanum nakurense var. lykipiense

Solanum nakurense var. lykipiense, commonly known as the Lykopiense nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
