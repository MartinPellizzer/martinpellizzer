Staurogyne nitida

Staurogyne nitida, commonly known as the slender water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Staurogyne nitida plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality through oxygen production.
