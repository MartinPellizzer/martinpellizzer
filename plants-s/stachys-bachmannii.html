Stachys bachmannii

Stachys bachmannii, commonly known as Bachmann's hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
