Solidago virgaurea var. dahurica

Solidago virgaurea var. dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its showy inflorescences make it a valuable addition to gardens and a key species in ecological restoration projects.
