Saussurea viscida

Saussurea viscida, commonly known as sticky saffron, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its fibrous root system and hairy, often glandular stems. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the genus Saussurea. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
