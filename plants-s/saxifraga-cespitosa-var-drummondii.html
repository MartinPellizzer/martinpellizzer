Saxifraga cespitosa var. drummondii

Saxifraga cespitosa var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of basal leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is a perennial species that is well adapted to harsh, cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
