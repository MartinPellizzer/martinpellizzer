Sideritis candicans var. stricta

Sideritis candicans var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
