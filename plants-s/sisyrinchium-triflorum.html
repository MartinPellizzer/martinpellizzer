Sisyrinchium triflorum

Sisyrinchium triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. Sisyrinchium triflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
