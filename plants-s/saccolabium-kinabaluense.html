Saccolabium kinabaluense

Saccolabium kinabaluense, commonly known as the Kinabalu saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mount Kinabalu in Borneo.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
