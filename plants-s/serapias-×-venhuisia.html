Serapias × venhuisia

Serapias × venhuisia, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Serapias ramonii and Serapias micrantha.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers that are typically yellow or orange with intricate patterns, resembling those of its parent species. It has linear, grass-like leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Serapias × venhuisia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
