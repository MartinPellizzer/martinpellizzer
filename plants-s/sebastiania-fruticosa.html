Sebastiania fruticosa

Sebastiania fruticosa, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Sebastiania fruticosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and foliage.
