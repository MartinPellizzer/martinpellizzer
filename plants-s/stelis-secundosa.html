Stelis secundosa

Stelis secundosa, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a delicate, spurred lip that attracts pollinators.

Stelis secundosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
