Scolopia ecklonii var. gerrardii

Scolopia ecklonii var. gerrardii, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are ovate with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
