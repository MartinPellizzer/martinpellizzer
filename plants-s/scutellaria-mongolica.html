Scutellaria mongolica

Scutellaria mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia, including China, Mongolia, and Russia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or violet, and it produces small, nutlet-like seeds.

Scutellaria mongolica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
