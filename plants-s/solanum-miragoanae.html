Solanum miragoanae

Solanum miragoanae, commonly known as the Miragoana nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale green flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in cloud forests.
