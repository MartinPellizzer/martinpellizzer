Sponia strigosa

Sponia strigosa, commonly known as the hairy sponia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Sponia strigosa is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
