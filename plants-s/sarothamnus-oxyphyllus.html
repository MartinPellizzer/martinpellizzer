Sarothamnus oxyphyllus

Sarothamnus oxyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a compact growth habit.
