Salix aurita var. cladostema

Salix aurita var. cladostema, commonly known as the woolly willow or narrowleaf willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and fluffy, catkin-like flowers that appear in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows. Its stems are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which gives it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
