Sloanea amygdalina

Sloanea amygdalina, commonly known as the sweet sloanea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with a serrated margin.

Sloanea amygdalina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
