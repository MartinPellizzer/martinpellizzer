Syzygium buettnerianum

Syzygium buettnerianum, commonly known as the white clydesdale, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
