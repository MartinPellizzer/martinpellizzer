Sarothamnus lusitanicus

Sarothamnus lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
