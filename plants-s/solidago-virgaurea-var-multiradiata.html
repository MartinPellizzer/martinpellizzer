Solidago virgaurea var. multiradiata

Solidago virgaurea var. multiradiata, commonly known as the multibracted goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, branching clusters. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
