Scilla fastigiata

Scilla fastigiata, commonly known as the upright scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize readily.

Scilla fastigiata is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
