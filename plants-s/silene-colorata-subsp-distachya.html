Silene colorata subsp. distachya

Silene colorata subsp.

distachya, commonly known as the redstem sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its two-branched inflorescences, which give it its specific epithet *distachya*. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant has a short life cycle and is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
