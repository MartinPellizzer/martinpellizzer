Solanum cinerescens

Solanum cinerescens, commonly known as the ash-gray nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy, grayish stems and ovate, pubescent leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals, and it produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive ash-gray coloration and is often found in dry, open habitats.
