Solanum dulcamara f. subglabrum

Solanum dulcamara f. subglabrum, commonly known as the climbing nightshade, is a variant of the common woody nightshade.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which are typically alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale green or white, followed by berries that mature to a deep purple color. It is a perennial vine that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
