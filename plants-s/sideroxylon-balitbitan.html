Sideroxylon balitbitan

Sideroxylon balitbitan, commonly known as the Philippine ironwood tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a large evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, waxy leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is native to the Philippines and is valued for its durable timber used in construction and furniture.
