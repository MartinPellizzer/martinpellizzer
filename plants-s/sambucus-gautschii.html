Sambucus gautschii

Sambucus gautschii, commonly known as the Gautsch elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
