Securidaca corymbosa

Securidaca corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet-smelling tree or white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with compound leaves and a woody stem. Its flowers have a distinctive, sweet scent, which is a key feature in its ecological interactions.
