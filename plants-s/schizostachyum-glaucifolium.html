Schizostachyum glaucifolium

Schizostachyum glaucifolium, commonly known as the blue-leafed bamboo, is a tall, grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Schizostachyum glaucifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its strong, durable bamboo-like material.
