Sarracenia × mandaiana

Sarracenia × mandaiana, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Sarracenia leucophylla and Sarracenia rubra, resulting in a plant with distinctive, often brightly colored pitchers. The plant's pitchers are typically red or orange with a white or yellow interior, and they secrete a digestive enzyme to trap and digest insects. Sarracenia × mandaiana thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
