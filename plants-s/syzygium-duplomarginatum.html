Syzygium duplomarginatum

Syzygium duplomarginatum, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarind.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or reddish when mature. Syzygium duplomarginatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Myrtaceae family.
