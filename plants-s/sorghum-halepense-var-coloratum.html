Sorghum halepense var. coloratum

Sorghum halepense var. coloratum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa but widely naturalized in various parts of the world.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pinkish inflorescences, which give it its common name. The plant has a tufted growth habit with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to serve as a forage crop for livestock.
