Salix bebbiana var. capreifolia

Salix bebbiana var. capreifolia, commonly known as the western silky willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and soft, silken grayish-white bark. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, inconspicuous female flowers. Its leaves are oval to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a smooth, waxy upper surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wetlands.
