Scirpus articulatus

Scirpus articulatus, commonly known as the soft-stemmed bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has tall, cylindrical stems that are often hollow and jointed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems. Its roots are extensive and help stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Scirpus articulatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
