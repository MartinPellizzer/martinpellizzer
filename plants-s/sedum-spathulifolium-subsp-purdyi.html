Sedum spathulifolium subsp. purdyi

Sedum spathulifolium subsp.

purdyi, commonly known as the Purdy's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
