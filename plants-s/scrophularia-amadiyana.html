Scrophularia amadiyana

Scrophularia amadiyana, commonly known as the Amadiyana figwort, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes various species known for their medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with two-lipped petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
