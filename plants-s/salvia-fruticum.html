Salvia fruticum

Salvia fruticum, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Salvia fruticum is also valued for its aromatic properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.
