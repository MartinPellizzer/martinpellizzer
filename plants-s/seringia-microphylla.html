Seringia microphylla

Seringia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved seringia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Seringia microphylla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
