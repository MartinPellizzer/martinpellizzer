Sedum filipes

Sedum filipes, commonly known as the threadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum filipes is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
