Saxifraga flagellaris var. mucronulata

Saxifraga flagellaris var. mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a rosette of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
