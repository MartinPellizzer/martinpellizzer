Sponia acuminatissima

Sponia acuminatissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed sponia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
