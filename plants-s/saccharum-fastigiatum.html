Saccharum fastigiatum

Saccharum fastigiatum, commonly known as the purple sugarcane or purple bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking purple or reddish hues in its foliage, which are due to the presence of anthocyanin pigments. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its tall, cylindrical stems can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it a distinctive feature in its natural habitat.

Saccharum fastigiatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in landscaping and horticulture.
