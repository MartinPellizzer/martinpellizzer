Satureja helianthemifolia var. mary-annae

Satureja helianthemifolia var. mary-annae, commonly known as Mary-Anne's savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a pungent, thyme-like flavor to dishes.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
