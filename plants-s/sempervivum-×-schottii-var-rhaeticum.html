Sempervivum × schottii var. rhaeticum

Sempervivum × schottii var. rhaeticum, commonly known as the Rhaetic Mountain houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features compact rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces upright flowering stems with small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
