Schizachyrium minutum

Schizachyrium minutum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species that thrives in well-drained soils.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall. This plant is often found in prairies and open woodlands, contributing to the structure and biodiversity of these ecosystems. Schizachyrium minutum is drought-tolerant and plays a role in soil stabilization.

Its seeds are a food source for various birds and small mammals.
