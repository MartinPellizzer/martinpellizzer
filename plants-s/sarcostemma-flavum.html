Sarcostemma flavum

Sarcostemma flavum, commonly known as the yellow star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing shrub that produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has a woody stem with thorns and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
