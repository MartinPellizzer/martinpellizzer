Stirlingia tenuifolia

Stirlingia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf stirlingia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy soils along the shoreline. The plant produces delicate, pale yellow flowers that bloom in winter, adding a subtle beauty to the coastal landscape. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its common name.

Stirlingia tenuifolia is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
