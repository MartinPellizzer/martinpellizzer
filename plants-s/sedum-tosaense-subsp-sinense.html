Sedum tosaense subsp. sinense

Sedum tosaense subsp.

sinense, commonly known as the Tosa sedum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, particularly in regions with temperate climates.
