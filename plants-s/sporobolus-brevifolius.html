Sporobolus brevifolius

Sporobolus brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaf needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in poor soils.

Sporobolus brevifolius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
