Stachys barbigera

Stachys barbigera, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Stachys barbigera is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
