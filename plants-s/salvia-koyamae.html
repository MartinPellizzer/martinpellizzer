Salvia koyamae

Salvia koyamae, commonly known as Koyama's sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking violet-blue flowers in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive minty aroma. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
