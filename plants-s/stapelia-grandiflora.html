Stapelia grandiflora

Stapelia grandiflora, commonly known as the carrion flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and its distinctive, meaty-looking flowers that resemble decaying flesh. The plant produces large, star-shaped blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a strong, unpleasant odor. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Stapelia grandiflora is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
