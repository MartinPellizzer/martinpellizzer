Seriphidium saharae

Seriphidium saharae, commonly known as the Saharan tansy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Seriphidium saharae plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
