Symplocos cochinchinensis subsp. leptophylla

Symplocos cochinchinensis subsp.

leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed boxwood, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
