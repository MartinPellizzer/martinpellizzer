Scabiosa oberti-manettii

Scabiosa oberti-manettii, commonly known as the Italian spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Scabiosa oberti-manettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
