Schizostachyum langbianense

Schizostachyum langbianense, commonly known as the Langbian bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is often found in moist, tropical environments, particularly in mountainous regions. Its culms are strong and flexible, making it suitable for construction and handicrafts.

Schizostachyum langbianense plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
