Swertia obtusa var. albiflora

Swertia obtusa var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are predominantly white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in folk remedies for various ailments.
