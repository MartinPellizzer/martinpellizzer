Schefflera cicatricata

Schefflera cicatricata, commonly known as the scarred schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Schefflera cicatricata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
