Salvia clevelandii

Salvia clevelandii, commonly known as Cleveland's sage, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southern California and Baja California.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring silver-gray foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, fuzzy texture and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
