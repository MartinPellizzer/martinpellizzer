Solanum melongena var. ovigera

Solanum melongena var. ovigera, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit shape.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are hairy and green. The flowers are typically white or pale purple, and the fruit develops from a single ovary, producing the characteristic egg-shaped berries.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as culinary use.
