Sinocrassula

Sinocrassula, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a genus of succulent plants native to Asia.

These plants are characterized by their compact growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling them to thrive in arid environments. Sinocrassula species typically produce small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, or pink, which bloom in the spring or early summer. They are often grown as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Many species are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
