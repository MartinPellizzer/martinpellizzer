Salvia chia

Salvia chia, commonly known as Mexican mint or golden mint, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and have a mint-like scent, making it popular in culinary and medicinal uses.

Salvia chia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.
