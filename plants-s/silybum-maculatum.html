Silybum maculatum

Silybum maculatum, commonly known as milk thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant contains a compound called silymarin, which is known for its hepatoprotective properties. Silybum maculatum is often cultivated for its seeds and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
