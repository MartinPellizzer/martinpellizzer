Symplocos glauca var. koshunensis

Symplocos glauca var. koshunensis, commonly known as Koshun glossy elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
