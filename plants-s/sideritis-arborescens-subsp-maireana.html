Sideritis arborescens subsp. maireana

Sideritis arborescens subsp.

maireana, commonly known as Maire's rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or purple flowers.

The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and many medicinal uses. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.

Sideritis species are valued for their potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
