Scutellaria scordiifolia var. villosissima

Scutellaria scordiifolia var. villosissima, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, pubescent texture. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the upper stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the mint family.
