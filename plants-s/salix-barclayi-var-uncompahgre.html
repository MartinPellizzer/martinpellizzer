Salix barclayi var. uncompahgre

Salix barclayi var. uncompahgre, commonly known as the Grand Forks willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to hydric soils. The plant has slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. Its flowers are catkins, which are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for wildlife.
