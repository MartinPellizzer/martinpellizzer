Silene apiculata

Silene apiculata, commonly known as the Sierra Madre evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Silene apiculata is often found in open habitats such as slopes, canyons, and along stream banks.
