Salix arctica var. brownei

Salix arctica var. brownei, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Salicaceae family and is adapted to cold climates with its low-growing form. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of short hairs. It produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal.

This species plays an important ecological role in tundra ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
