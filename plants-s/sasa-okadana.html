Sasa okadana

Sasa okadana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Sasa okadana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
