Syzygium benguetense

Syzygium benguetense, commonly known as the Benguet apple or mountain apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Syzygium benguetense is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its fruit, which is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
