Syzygium guineense var. macrocarpum

Syzygium guineense var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the rainy season. Its most distinctive feature is the elongated, woody pods that contain several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

The tree is valued for its hardy nature and is often cultivated for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
