Solanum acutosepalum

Solanum acutosepalum, commonly known as the acute-sepaled nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five acuminate sepals that give it its specific name.

Solanum acutosepalum produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic and should not be consumed.
