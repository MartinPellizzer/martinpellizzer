Saxifraga × decipientoides

Saxifraga × decipientoides, commonly known as the false saxifrage, is a hybrid plant species within the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
