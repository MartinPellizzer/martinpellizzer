Saponaria × wiemannii

Saponaria × wiemannii, commonly known as the hybrid carnation or pink soapwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces cylindrical, branched stems that are often covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a similar fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Saponaria × wiemannii is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
