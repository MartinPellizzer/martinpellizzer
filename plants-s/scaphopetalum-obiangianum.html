Scaphopetalum obiangianum

Scaphopetalum obiangianum, commonly known as the Obiangian flame flower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display shades of red and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil, often growing in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
