Succisa praemorsa var. nana

Succisa praemorsa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, hairy leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, adapted to arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and dry slopes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
