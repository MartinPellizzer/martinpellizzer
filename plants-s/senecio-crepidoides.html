Senecio crepidoides

Senecio crepidoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
