Saponaria × boissieri

Saponaria × boissieri, commonly known as the hybrid carnation or soaproot, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces a milky sap that has mild soap-like properties, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Saponaria × boissieri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
