Solanum melissarum

Solanum melissarum, commonly known as the honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and alternate leaves with a slightly serrated edge.
