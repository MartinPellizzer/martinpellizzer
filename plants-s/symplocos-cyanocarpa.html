Symplocos cyanocarpa

Symplocos cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited evergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by blue-black berries in the fall. These berries are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

Symplocos cyanocarpa is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
