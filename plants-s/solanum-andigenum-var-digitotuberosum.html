Solanum andigenum var. digitotuberosum

Solanum andigenum var. digitotuberosum, commonly known as the Andean potato, is a tuberous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers. The plant typically grows in cool, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its tubers are often used in traditional Andean cuisine and have been cultivated for centuries.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it remains an important crop in regions where it is indigenous.
