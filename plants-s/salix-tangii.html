Salix tangii

Salix tangii, commonly known as the Tangshan willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. Salix tangii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for reforestation projects.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
