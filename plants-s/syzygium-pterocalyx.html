Syzygium pterocalyx

Syzygium pterocalyx, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, round fruits that are typically red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
