Sesbania pachycarpa

Sesbania pachycarpa, commonly known as the thickpod sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a legume characterized by its erect or semi-woody growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated, thick pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Sesbania pachycarpa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
