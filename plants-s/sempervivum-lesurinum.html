Sempervivum lesurinum

Sempervivum lesurinum, commonly known as the Lesurinum houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sempervivum lesurinum is known for its hardiness and ability to survive in harsh conditions, earning it the nickname "the everliving.".
