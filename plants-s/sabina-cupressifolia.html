Sabina cupressifolia

Sabina cupressifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean cypress, is a small evergreen conifer native to the Mediterranean region.

It has a compact, rounded shape with dense, scale-like leaves that are dark green and emit a subtle aromatic scent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries. Its roots are shallow and it prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, sunny conditions.
