Symplocos sylvicola

Symplocos sylvicola, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or mountain waxtree, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Symplocos sylvicola thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
