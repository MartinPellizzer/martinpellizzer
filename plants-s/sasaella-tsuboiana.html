Sasaella tsuboiana

Sasaella tsuboiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. It forms dense clumps and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.

Sasaella tsuboiana is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
