Stobaea erysithales

Stobaea erysithales, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stobaea erysithales is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
