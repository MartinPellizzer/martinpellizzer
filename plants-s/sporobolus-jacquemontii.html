Sporobolus jacquemontii

Sporobolus jacquemontii, commonly known as western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Sporobolus jacquemontii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife and livestock.
