Sphaeropteris albidosquamata

Sphaeropteris albidosquamata, commonly known as the white-scaled cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and bear small, white-scaled structures along the leaflets. This plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a slow growth rate.
