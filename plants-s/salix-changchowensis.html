Salix changchowensis

Salix changchowensis, commonly known as the Changchow willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the region of Zhejiang Province. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
