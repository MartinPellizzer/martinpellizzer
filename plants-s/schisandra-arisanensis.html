Schisandra arisanensis

Schisandra arisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is notable for its aromatic leaves and stems, which contain various bioactive compounds. Schisandra arisanensis is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
