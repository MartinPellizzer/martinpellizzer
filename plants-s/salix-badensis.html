Salix badensis

Salix badensis, commonly known as the Baden willow, is a species of willow native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas. The plant has slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its reproduction.

Salix badensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian habitats.
