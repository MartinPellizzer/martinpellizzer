Solanum kunzei

Solanum kunzei, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange to red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Solanum kunzei thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species within its native ecosystem.
