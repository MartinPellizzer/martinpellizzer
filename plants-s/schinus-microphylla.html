Schinus microphylla

Schinus microphylla, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive red, fleshy fruits. Schinus microphylla has a woody, aromatic trunk and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
