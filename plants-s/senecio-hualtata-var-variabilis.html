Senecio hualtata var. variabilis

Senecio hualtata var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

This variant is noted for its variability in leaf shape and size, contributing to its common name.
