Satureja kuegleri var. hyssopifolia

Satureja kuegleri var. hyssopifolia, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a pungent, slightly minty scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
