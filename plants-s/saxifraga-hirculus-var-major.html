Saxifraga hirculus var. major

Saxifraga hirculus var. major, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, slender stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Saxifraga hirculus var. major is often found in mountain meadows and scree slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
