Shorea kalunti

Shorea kalunti, commonly known as Malay teak, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its durable, heavy, and high-quality hardwood. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.
