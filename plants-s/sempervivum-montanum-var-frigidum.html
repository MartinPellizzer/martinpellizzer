Sempervivum montanum var. frigidum

Sempervivum montanum var. frigidum, commonly known as the alpine houseleek, is a hardy succulent native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in harsh, alpine environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine habitats.

Its resilience to cold temperatures makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, frost-tolerant plants.
