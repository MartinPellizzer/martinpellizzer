Schismatoglottis sylvestris var. subcordata

Schismatoglottis sylvestris var. subcordata, commonly known as the broad-leafed arrowroot, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and striking inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spike, which are often fragrant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
