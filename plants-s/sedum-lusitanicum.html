Sedum lusitanicum

Sedum lusitanicum, commonly known as the Portuguese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves. It typically blooms in summer and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Sedum lusitanicum is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
