Salix zhouquensis

Salix zhouquensis, commonly known as Zhouqu willow, is a species of willow native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as riverbanks and mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
