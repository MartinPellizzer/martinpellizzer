Senecio rivalis

Senecio rivalis, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and hairy, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Senecio rivalis is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

It is generally drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun conditions.
