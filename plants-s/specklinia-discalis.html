Specklinia discalis

Specklinia discalis, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green with distinctive silvery veins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in a raceme, and bloom during the rainy season.

Specklinia discalis is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, well-draining potting mix.
