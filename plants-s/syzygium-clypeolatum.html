Syzygium clypeolatum

Syzygium clypeolatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour-sweet fruits that are often used in culinary and traditional medicine applications. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Syzygium clypeolatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
