Stipa burkartii

Stipa burkartii, commonly known as the Burkart's needlegrass, is a grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its foliage is typically gray-green, and it blooms during the cooler months, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Stipa burkartii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and support local wildlife.
