Scirpus microstachys

Scirpus microstachys, commonly known as the small-stalked bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands that help stabilize shoreline habitats. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize and dominate wetland areas.

Scirpus microstachys plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
