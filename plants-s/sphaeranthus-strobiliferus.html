Sphaeranthus strobiliferus

Sphaeranthus strobiliferus, commonly known as the clustered goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and has potential medicinal properties.
