Scleria nitida

Scleria nitida, commonly known as the glossy arrowhead, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Scleria nitida thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in riparian zones and seasonally flooded areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
