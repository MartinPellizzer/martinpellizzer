Solidago glabra subsp. canadensis

Solidago glabra subsp.

canadensis, commonly known as Canadian goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine, silver-white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late summer to early fall.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting diverse wildlife.
