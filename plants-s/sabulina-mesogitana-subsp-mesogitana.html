Sabulina mesogitana subsp. mesogitana

Sabulina mesogitana subsp.

mesogitana, commonly known as the Mesopotamian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Middle East. It features small, fleshy, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.
