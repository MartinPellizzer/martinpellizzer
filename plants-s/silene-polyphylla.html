Silene polyphylla

Silene polyphylla, commonly known as the Sierra starflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped white flowers and is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant has a slender, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It blooms in late summer, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

Silene polyphylla is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is adapted to arid and high-altitude environments.
