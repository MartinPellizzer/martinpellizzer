Solanum pseudoundatum var. leucocarpon

Solanum pseudoundatum var. leucocarpon, commonly known as the white-fruited nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns white when mature, distinguishing it from other varieties.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
