Salvia bupleuroides

Salvia bupleuroides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
