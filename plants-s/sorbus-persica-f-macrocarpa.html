Sorbus persica f. macrocarpa

Sorbus persica f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Persian whitebeam, is a botanical variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its large, showy white berries that persist into winter, making it an ornamental plant. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge. It is native to parts of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
