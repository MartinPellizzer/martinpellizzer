Smilax calophylla

Smilax calophylla, commonly known as the arrowleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp spines and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax calophylla is known for its robust growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
