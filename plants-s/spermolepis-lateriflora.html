Spermolepis lateriflora

Spermolepis lateriflora, commonly known as the lateriflora soapbush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Spermolepis lateriflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
