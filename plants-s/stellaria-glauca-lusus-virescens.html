Stellaria glauca lusus virescens

Stellaria glauca lusus virescens, commonly known as the green starwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers with five petals, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. The plant has slender, ascending stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and woodland edges.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it an important component of early successional plant communities.
