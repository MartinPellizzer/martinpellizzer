Sida linifolia

Sida linifolia, commonly known as silky sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warm seasons. Sida linifolia is often found in open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

It is an annual herb that plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
