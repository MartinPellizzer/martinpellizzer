Suaeda tuvinica

Suaeda tuvinica, commonly known as Tuvinian saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is native to Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and Siberia. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters.

Suaeda tuvinica plays a role in stabilizing soils in coastal and desert regions.
