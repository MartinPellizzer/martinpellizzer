Saxifraga diversifolia f. angustibracteata

Saxifraga diversifolia f. angustibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bract saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This variant is distinguished by its narrower bracts compared to the typical form of Saxifraga diversifolia.

It thrives in rocky or alpine environments, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
