Sabulina douglasii

Sabulina douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Sabulina douglasii is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
