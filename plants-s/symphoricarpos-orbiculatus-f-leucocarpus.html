Symphoricarpos orbiculatus f. leucocarpus

Symphoricarpos orbiculatus f. leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruited sandcherry, is a variant of the sandcherry shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, round, white berries that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping.

The white fruits are not only visually striking but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
