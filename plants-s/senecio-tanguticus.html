Senecio tanguticus

Senecio tanguticus, commonly known as the Tangut groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Senecio tanguticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
