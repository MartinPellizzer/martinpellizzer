Samyda bazanica

Samyda bazanica, commonly known as the Basanica myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Samyda bazanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
