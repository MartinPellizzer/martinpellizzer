Sarracenia purpurea var. venosa

Sarracenia purpurea var. venosa, commonly known as the venosa pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red and green striped leaves that form tubular traps to capture insects. The plant's pitchers are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs that aid in trapping prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and bright light for optimal growth.

This variant is known for its distinctive coloration and is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
