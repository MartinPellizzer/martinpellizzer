Sasa chartacea var. shimotsukensis

Sasa chartacea var. shimotsukensis, commonly known as the Shimotsukensis bamboo, is a variant of the broadleaf bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
