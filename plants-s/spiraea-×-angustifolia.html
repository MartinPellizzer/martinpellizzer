Spiraea × angustifolia

Spiraea × angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early to mid-spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea × angustifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of maintenance.
