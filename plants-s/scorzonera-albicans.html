Scorzonera albicans

Scorzonera albicans, commonly known as the white scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scorzonera albicans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes.
