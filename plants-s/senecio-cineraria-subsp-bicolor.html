Senecio cineraria subsp. bicolor

Senecio cineraria subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the blueweed or silverweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
