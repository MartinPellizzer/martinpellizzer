Sirmuellera littoralis

Sirmuellera littoralis, commonly known as the coastal sirmuellera, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds. It is often found in dune environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
