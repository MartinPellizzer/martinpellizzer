Salvia azurea var. grandiflora

Salvia azurea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of sky-blue to lavender flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
