Saussurea nipponica subsp. nipponica

Saussurea nipponica subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as Japanese snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
