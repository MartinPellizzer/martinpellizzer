Solidago arguta subsp. harrisii

Solidago arguta subsp.

harrisii, commonly known as the Appalachian goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
