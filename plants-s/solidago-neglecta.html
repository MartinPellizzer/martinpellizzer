Solidago neglecta

Solidago neglecta, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Solidago neglecta is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it thrives in disturbed or wetland environments.
