Selaginella apus var. denticulata

Selaginella apus var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to regions of North America.

It is a small, evergreen, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a rosette of closely packed, scale-like leaves that are often toothed or serrated, giving it its common name. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobili, which are borne on specialized branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
