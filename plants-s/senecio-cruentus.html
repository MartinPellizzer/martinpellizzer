Senecio cruentus

Senecio cruentus, commonly known as the bloodroot senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces striking red-orange flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
