Saxifraga ajugifolia var. minor

Saxifraga ajugifolia var. minor, commonly known as the lesser saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features rosettes of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
