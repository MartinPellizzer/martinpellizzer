Spiraea hayatae

Spiraea hayatae, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded form and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Spiraea hayatae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
