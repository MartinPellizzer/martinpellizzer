Senecio tournefortii subsp. aragonensis

Senecio tournefortii subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon Senecio, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and clustered, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white ray florets, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
