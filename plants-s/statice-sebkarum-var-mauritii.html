Statice sebkarum var. mauritii

Statice sebkarum var. mauritii, commonly known as saltbush statice, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal and arid environments. Its foliage is typically silver or gray-green, with small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and visual appeal in coastal landscapes.
