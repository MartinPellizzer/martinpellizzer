Stemmadenia bignoniiflora

Stemmadenia bignoniiflora, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, often with a red throat. These flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
