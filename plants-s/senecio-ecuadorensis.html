Senecio ecuadorensis

Senecio ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests, where it thrives in cooler temperatures. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Senecio ecuadorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
