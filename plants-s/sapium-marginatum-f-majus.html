Sapium marginatum f. majus

Sapium marginatum f. majus, commonly known as the giant Chinese lantern, is a variant of the Chinese lantern plant characterized by its large, showy orange-red bracts.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China and belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins. Its striking floral display consists of clustered cyathia surrounded by vibrant, petal-like bracts.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
