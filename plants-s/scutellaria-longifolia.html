Scutellaria longifolia

Scutellaria longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
