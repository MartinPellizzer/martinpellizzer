Senecio bigelovii var. hallii

Senecio bigelovii var. hallii, commonly known as Hall's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hall's groundsel is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
