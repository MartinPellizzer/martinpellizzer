Stachyurus salicifolius

Stachyurus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf stachyurus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, willow-like leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are often white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Stachyurus salicifolius is valued for its attractive foliage and early blooming habit.
