Saxifraga adscendens var. oregonensis

Saxifraga adscendens var. oregonensis, commonly known as the Oregon mountain saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky, alpine, or subalpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species in its native habitat.
