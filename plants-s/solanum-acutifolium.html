Solanum acutifolium

Solanum acutifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Solanum acutifolium belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
