Sarcochilus tanyphyllus

Sarcochilus tanyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, horizontal rhizome and produces erect, hairy stems. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white tomentum. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in a raceme.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
