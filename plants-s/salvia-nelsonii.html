Salvia nelsonii

Salvia nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic leaves and compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
