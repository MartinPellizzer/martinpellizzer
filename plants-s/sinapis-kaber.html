Sinapis kaber

Sinapis kaber, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect stem, alternate leaves, and bright yellow flowers that form in racemes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

Sinapis kaber is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
