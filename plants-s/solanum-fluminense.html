Solanum fluminense

Solanum fluminense, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that change color as they ripen. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, and the plant is often found in moist, tropical environments.

Solanum fluminense is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and fruit.
