Sphaeroma sessiliflorum

Sphaeroma sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flowered sphaeroma, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish in color, and it blooms during the warm season.

Sphaeroma sessiliflorum is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
