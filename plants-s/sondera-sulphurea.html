Sondera sulphurea

Sondera sulphurea, commonly known as the golden sondera, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
