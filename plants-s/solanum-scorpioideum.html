Solanum scorpioideum

Solanum scorpioideum, commonly known as the death apple or devil's apple, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its toxic, yellow-orange berries that resemble small apples. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, round fruits that are highly poisonous to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
