Sideritis luciae

Sideritis luciae, commonly known as the mountain thyme or Greek thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

Sideritis luciae is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous areas.
