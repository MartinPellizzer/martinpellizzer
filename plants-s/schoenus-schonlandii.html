Schoenus schonlandii

Schoenus schonlandii, commonly known as the Schonland's bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical formations.

Schoenus schonlandii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
