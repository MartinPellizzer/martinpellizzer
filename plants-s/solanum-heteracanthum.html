Solanum heteracanthum

Solanum heteracanthum, commonly known as the Indian bell pepper or wild potato, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing several seeds. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Solanum heteracanthum is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
