Salvia verbenacoides

Salvia verbenacoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its floral display. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia verbenacoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
