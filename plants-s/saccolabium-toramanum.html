Saccolabium toramanum

Saccolabium toramanum, commonly known as the Toraman screwbean, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
