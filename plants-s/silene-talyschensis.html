Silene talyschensis

Silene talyschensis, commonly known as the Talysch Mountain Catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene talyschensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for small invertebrates.
