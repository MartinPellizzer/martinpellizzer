Secamone coronata

Secamone coronata, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in soil restoration and erosion control. It produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Secamone coronata is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
