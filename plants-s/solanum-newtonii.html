Solanum newtonii

Solanum newtonii, commonly known as Newton's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
