Senecio glandulosopilosus

Senecio glandulosopilosus, commonly known as the glandular-hairy senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its glandular-pubescent stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the genus Senecio.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
