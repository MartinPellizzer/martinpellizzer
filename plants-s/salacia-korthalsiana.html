Salacia korthalsiana

Salacia korthalsiana, commonly known as the golden apple or wild mango, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its elongated, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
