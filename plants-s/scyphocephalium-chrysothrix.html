Scyphocephalium chrysothrix

Scyphocephalium chrysothrix, commonly known as the golden-hair daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive white center, which bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
