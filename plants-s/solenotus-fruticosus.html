Solenotus fruticosus

Solenotus fruticosus, commonly known as the creeping solenotus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale pink to white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom during the warmer months.

Solenotus fruticosus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
