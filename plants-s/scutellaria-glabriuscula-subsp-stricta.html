Scutellaria glabriuscula subsp. stricta

Scutellaria glabriuscula subsp.

stricta, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
