Satyrium beyrichianum

Satyrium beyrichianum, commonly known as the Beyrich's satyrium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, small flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
