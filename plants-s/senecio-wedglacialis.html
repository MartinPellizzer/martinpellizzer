Senecio wedglacialis

Senecio wedglacialis, commonly known as the Wedgeleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and wedge-shaped, giving it its common name.

Senecio wedglacialis is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
