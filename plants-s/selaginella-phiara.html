Selaginella phiara

Selaginella phiara, commonly known as the slender spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a low-growing, creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often lance-shaped and have a waxy coating. Selaginella phiara reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called strobila.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of humid conditions.
