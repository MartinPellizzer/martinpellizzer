Spiraeanthemum reticulatum

Spiraeanthemum reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate spirea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded shrub form and produces clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is valued for its compact growth habit and long-lasting floral display.
