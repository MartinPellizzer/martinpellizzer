Sideritis arborescens var. pauli

Sideritis arborescens var. pauli, commonly known as the mountain sages, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous areas with high altitude. The plant features small, gray-green leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties.

This variety is valued for its medicinal uses and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
