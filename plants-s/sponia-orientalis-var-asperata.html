Sponia orientalis var. asperata

Sponia orientalis var. asperata, commonly known as the rough sponia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, grayish-green, woolly foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, textured surface, contributing to its common name.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
