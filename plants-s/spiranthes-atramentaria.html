Spiranthes atramentaria

Spiranthes atramentaria, commonly known as the black-stemmed ladies' tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green stems and its distinctive spiral arrangement of flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
