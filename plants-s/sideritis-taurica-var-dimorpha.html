Sideritis taurica var. dimorpha

Sideritis taurica var. dimorpha, commonly known as the Caucasian ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or limestone habitats.

Sideritis taurica var. dimorpha is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
