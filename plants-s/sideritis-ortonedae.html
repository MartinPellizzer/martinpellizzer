Sideritis ortonedae

Sideritis ortonedae, commonly known as the Greek ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece and Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, hairy leaves, and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Sideritis ortonedae is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in dry, sunny environments.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
