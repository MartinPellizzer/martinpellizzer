Sparganium affine

Sparganium affine, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrgrass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems that bear spikelets with bracts. The plant produces small, spiky fruits that are adapted for dispersal by water, typical of wetland environments. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
