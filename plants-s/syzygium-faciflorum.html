Syzygium faciflorum

Syzygium faciflorum, commonly known as the fragrant apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor. Syzygium faciflorum thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
