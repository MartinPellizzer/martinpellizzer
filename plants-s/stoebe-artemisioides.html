Stoebe artemisioides

Stoebe artemisioides, commonly known as dwarf mullein, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silvery, woolly leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled capsules.

Stoebe artemisioides is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential respiratory and anti-inflammatory properties.
