Sida ulmifolia

Sida ulmifolia, commonly known as slender mallow, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from summer to autumn. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sida ulmifolia thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.
