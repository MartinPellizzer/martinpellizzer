Sagraea obliqua

Sagraea obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzagging stems that twist and turn along the ground. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture.

Sagraea obliqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
