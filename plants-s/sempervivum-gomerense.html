Sempervivum gomerense

Sempervivum gomerense, commonly known as the Gomer's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in well-drained soil, preferring full sun exposure.

Sempervivum gomerense is a hardy perennial that is popular among rock garden enthusiasts due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
