Sinocalamus minor var. amoenus

Sinocalamus minor var. amoenus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming grass species native to southern China.

It features slender, green culms that grow to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with a dense arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles during the spring or early summer. It is characterized by its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
