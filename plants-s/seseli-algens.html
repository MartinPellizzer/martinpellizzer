Seseli algens

Seseli algens, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant has a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age, and its leaves are typically narrow and hairy. Seseli algens thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
