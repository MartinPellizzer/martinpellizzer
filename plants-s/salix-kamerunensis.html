Salix kamerunensis

Salix kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Cameroon and Nigeria.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, which are typically male and female, and it is often found in moist, swampy areas or along riverbanks. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 5 meters in height.

Salix kamerunensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential uses in traditional medicine.
