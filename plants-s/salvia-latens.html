Salvia latens

Salvia latens, commonly known as the creeping sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorls along its stems, which bloom from summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
