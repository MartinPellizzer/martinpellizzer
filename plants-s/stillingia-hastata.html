Stillingia hastata

Stillingia hastata, commonly known as the red root or red sandroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Stillingia hastata is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used by Indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
