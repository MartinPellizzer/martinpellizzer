Savia maroando

Savia maroando, commonly known as the Malagasy milkvine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its roots contain a milky sap, which is the source of its common name.

Savia maroando is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
