Salix exigua var. parishiana

Salix exigua var. parishiana, commonly known as the Parish's willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streams and wetlands. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
