Solanum nutans

Solanum nutans, commonly known as the drooping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, white or purple flowers that open in the evening. Its berries, which are typically yellow or red when ripe, contain toxic compounds and should not be consumed.

Solanum nutans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and nocturnal flowering habit.
