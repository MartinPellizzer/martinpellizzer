Sebastiania hispida var. euhispida

Sebastiania hispida var. euhispida, commonly known as the velvetleaf sebastiania, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that turns red when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
