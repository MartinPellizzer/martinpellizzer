Schoenus flexuosus

Schoenus flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible grass or swamp grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, arching stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Schoenus flexuosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
