Stelleropsis caucasica

Stelleropsis caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue or violet in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
