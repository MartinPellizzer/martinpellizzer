Sonchus chuquitensis

Sonchus chuquitensis, commonly known as the Chuquitensis sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are generally hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a typical composite head.

It serves as a food source for certain insects and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
