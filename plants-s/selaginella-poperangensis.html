Selaginella poperangensis

Selaginella poperangensis, commonly known as the Peruvian spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a characteristic feature of the spore-bearing plants known as bryophytes.

Selaginella poperangensis is also notable for its ability to survive in arid conditions, showcasing remarkable adaptations to its environment.
