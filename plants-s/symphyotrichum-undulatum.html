Symphyotrichum undulatum

Symphyotrichum undulatum, commonly known as the undulating aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms in the late summer to early fall, producing showy purple to pink flowers.

Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly wavy edge, contributing to its common name.
