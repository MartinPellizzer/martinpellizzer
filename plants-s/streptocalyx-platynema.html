Streptocalyx platynema

Streptocalyx platynema, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
