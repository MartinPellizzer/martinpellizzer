Sarcanthus pachyphyllus

Sarcanthus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leafed sarcanthus, is a unique succulent native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps protect the plant from harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
