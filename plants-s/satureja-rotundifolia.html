Satureja rotundifolia

Satureja rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in culinary applications for its pungent, slightly peppery flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and thyme.
