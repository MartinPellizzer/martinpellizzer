Salvia exasperata

Salvia exasperata, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants and square stems. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Salvia exasperata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
