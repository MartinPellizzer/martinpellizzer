Scrophularia scorodonia var. brevifolia

Scrophularia scorodonia var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive violet or purple hue. Its flowers are tubular and arranged in whorls, with a bright orange or yellow coloration that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
