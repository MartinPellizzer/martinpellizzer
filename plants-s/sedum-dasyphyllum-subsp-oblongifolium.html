Sedum dasyphyllum subsp. oblongifolium

Sedum dasyphyllum subsp.

oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features fleshy, oblong leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
