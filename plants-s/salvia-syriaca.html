Salvia syriaca

Salvia syriaca, commonly known as Syrian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage and produces striking spikes of violet to blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong, mint-like scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Salvia syriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
