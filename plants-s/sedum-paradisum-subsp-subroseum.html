Sedum paradisum subsp. subroseum

Sedum paradisum subsp.

subroseum, commonly known as the Subroseous Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green to blue-gray in color.

The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
