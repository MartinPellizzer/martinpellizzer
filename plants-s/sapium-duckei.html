Sapium duckei

Sapium duckei, commonly known as the Chinese snakeroot, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries that are a key feature of its botanical profile. Sapium duckei is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant autumn foliage and unique fruiting structures.
