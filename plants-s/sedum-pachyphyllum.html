Sedum pachyphyllum

Sedum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to full sun.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
