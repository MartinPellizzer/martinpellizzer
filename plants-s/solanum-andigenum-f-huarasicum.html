Solanum andigenum f. huarasicum

Solanum andigenum f. huarasicum, commonly known as the Huarasicum nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Its fruits are small berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.

This species is often found in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
