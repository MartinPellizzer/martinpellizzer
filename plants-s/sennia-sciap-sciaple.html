Sennia sciap-sciaple

Sennia sciap-sciaple, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Sennia sciap-sciaple is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
