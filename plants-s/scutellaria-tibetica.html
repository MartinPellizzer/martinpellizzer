Scutellaria tibetica

Scutellaria tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes harvested for use in traditional Tibetan medicine.
