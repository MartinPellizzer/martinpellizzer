Shorea ovalis subsp. sericea

Shorea ovalis subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silky tamanu, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
