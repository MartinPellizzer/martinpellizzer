Sparganium glomeratum var. angustifolium

Sparganium glomeratum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved bur-reed, is a perennial wetland plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant produces distinctive, spiky, floating fruits that disperse seeds via water, aiding in its propagation. Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird life.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
