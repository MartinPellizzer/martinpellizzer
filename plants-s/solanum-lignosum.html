Solanum lignosum

Solanum lignosum, commonly known as the woody nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features woody stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Solanum lignosum is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
