Salix phylicifolia var. campestris

Salix phylicifolia var. campestris, commonly known as the field willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant has slender, often multi-stemmed branches and produces catkins in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin and a yellowish-green underside.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
