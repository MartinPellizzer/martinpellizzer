Syzygium ponapense

Syzygium ponapense, commonly known as the Pohnpei apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Pohnpei in the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, red-orange fruits with a sweet and slightly tart flavor.

Syzygium ponapense is valued not only for its culinary use but also for its role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
