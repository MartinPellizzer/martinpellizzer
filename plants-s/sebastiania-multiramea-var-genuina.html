Sebastiania multiramea var. genuina

Sebastiania multiramea var. genuina, commonly known as the golden star bush, is a species native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and limestone habitats. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that attracts birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
