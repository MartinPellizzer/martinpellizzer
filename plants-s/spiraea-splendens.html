Spiraea splendens

Spiraea splendens, commonly known as the golden spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
