Sicyos hillebrandii var. douglasii

Sicyos hillebrandii var. douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's gourd, is a variant of the species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy, ribbed stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated, edible fruits resembling small gourds. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a tendency to produce multiple vines.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional agriculture.
