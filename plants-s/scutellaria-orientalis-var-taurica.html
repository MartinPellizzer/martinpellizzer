Scutellaria orientalis var. taurica

Scutellaria orientalis var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric skullcap, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its tubular flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
