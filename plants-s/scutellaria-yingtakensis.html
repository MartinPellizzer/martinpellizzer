Scutellaria yingtakensis

Scutellaria yingtakensis, commonly known as the Yingtian scutellaria, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
