Salvia macrosiphonia

Salvia macrosiphonia, commonly known as the large-lipped sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which bloom in whorled clusters along the upper stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique flower structure and vibrant color make it a valuable addition to both gardens and ecological habitats.
