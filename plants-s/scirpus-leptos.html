Scirpus leptos

Scirpus leptos, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. This wetland plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize the soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus leptos plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
