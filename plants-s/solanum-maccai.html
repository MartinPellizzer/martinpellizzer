Solanum maccai

Solanum maccai, commonly known as the Macca tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red berries, which are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor. Solanum maccai is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedge or in ornamental gardens.
