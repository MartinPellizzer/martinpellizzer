Syzygium scabrum

Syzygium scabrum, commonly known as the roughsyzygium, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like texture on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Syzygium scabrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental planting in warm climates.
