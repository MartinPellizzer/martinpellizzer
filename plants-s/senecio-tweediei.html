Senecio tweediei

Senecio tweediei, commonly known as Tweedie's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in spring.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
