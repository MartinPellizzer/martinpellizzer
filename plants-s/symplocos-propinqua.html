Symplocos propinqua

Symplocos propinqua, commonly known as the small-fruited waxtree, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Symplocos propinqua thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
