Shorea biawak

Shorea biawak, commonly known as the biawak tamarind or Malayan tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and heavy, durable wood. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing edible seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Shorea biawak plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
