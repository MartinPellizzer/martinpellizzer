Silene bupleuroides

Silene bupleuroides, commonly known as the bluebell catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene bupleuroides is often found in moist habitats and is a relatively uncommon species in the wild.
