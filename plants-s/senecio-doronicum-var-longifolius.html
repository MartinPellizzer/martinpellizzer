Senecio doronicum var. longifolius

Senecio doronicum var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
