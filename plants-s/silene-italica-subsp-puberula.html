Silene italica subsp. puberula

Silene italica subsp.

puberula, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. Silene italica subsp.

puberula is a perennial species with a low-growing habit.
