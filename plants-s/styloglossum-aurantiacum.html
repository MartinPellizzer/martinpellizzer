Styloglossum aurantiacum

Styloglossum aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange-eyed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid environments. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
