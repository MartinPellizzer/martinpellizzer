Sarcopodium reinwardtii

Sarcopodium reinwardtii, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in swampy areas. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Sarcopodium reinwardtii is valued in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to oxygenate water.
