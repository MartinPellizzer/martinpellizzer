Serratula mairei

Serratula mairei, commonly known as the Maire's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Serratula mairei is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
