Sedum phariense

Sedum phariense, commonly known as the Desert Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in dry environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its resilience and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for xeriscaping.
