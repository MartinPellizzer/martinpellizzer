Stoebe paniculata

Stoebe paniculata, commonly known as clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Stoebe paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
