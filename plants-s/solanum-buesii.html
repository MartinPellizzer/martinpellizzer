Solanum buesii

Solanum buesii, commonly known as the Buesii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

The fruit is a small, berry-like capsule that contains several seeds.
