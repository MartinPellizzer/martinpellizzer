Staurogyne linearifolia

Staurogyne linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to maximize light absorption. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Staurogyne linearifolia plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
