Stelis mandoniana

Stelis mandoniana, commonly known as the Mandon's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Stelis mandoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
