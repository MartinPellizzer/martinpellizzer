Seriphium adpressum

Seriphium adpressum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a fuzzy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Seriphium adpressum is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
