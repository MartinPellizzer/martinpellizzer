Sida radiciflora

Sida radiciflora, commonly known as the coastal sida, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and small, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in dune environments.

Sida radiciflora plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
