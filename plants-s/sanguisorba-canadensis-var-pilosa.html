Sanguisorba canadensis var. pilosa

Sanguisorba canadensis var. pilosa, commonly known as Canada rose or rough sanguisorba, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pinkish-red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
