Stomatium acutifolium

Stomatium acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved stomatium, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, often in shades of white or pale pink. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stomatium acutifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
