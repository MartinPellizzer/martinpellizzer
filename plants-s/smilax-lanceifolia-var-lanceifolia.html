Smilax lanceifolia var. lanceifolia

Smilax lanceifolia var. lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its sharp, spiny stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its vigorous growth and adaptability make it a common sight in the understory of humid forests.
