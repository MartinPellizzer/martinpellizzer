Salvia mexicana var. major

Salvia mexicana var. major, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of purple to violet flowers. The plant is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various health purposes.
