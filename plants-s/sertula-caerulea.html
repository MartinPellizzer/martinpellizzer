Sertula caerulea

Sertula caerulea, commonly known as the blue sertula, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Sertula caerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
