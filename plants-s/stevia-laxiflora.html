Stevia laxiflora

Stevia laxiflora, commonly known as the slenderleaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and herbal medicine.
