Silene graminifolia f. tenuis

Silene graminifolia f. tenuis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, ascending stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
