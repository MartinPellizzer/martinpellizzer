Solanum edule

Solanum edule, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its small, yellow, edible berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or purple flowers. Its berries are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
