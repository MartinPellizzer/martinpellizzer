Salvia japonica var. multifoliolata

Salvia japonica var. multifoliolata, commonly known as Japanese sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, making it a compact and attractive addition to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
