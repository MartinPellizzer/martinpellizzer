Schizanthus tricolor

Schizanthus tricolor, commonly known as the "Rainbow Flower," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, yellow, orange, and white, often in a tri-color pattern. The plant has trailing stems and is often grown as a decorative annual in gardens and containers. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Schizanthus tricolor is relatively easy to care for, thriving in full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil.
