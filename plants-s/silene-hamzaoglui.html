Silene hamzaoglui

Silene hamzaoglui, commonly known as the Hamzaoglu's campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene hamzaoglui is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
