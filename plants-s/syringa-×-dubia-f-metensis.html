Syringa × dubia f. metensis

Syringa × dubia f. metensis, commonly known as the Metensian mock orange, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–4 meters and features dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
