Salacia philippinensis

Salacia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine salacia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, typically blooming in the spring.

The plant is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
