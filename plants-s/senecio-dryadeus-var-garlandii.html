Senecio dryadeus var. garlandii

Senecio dryadeus var. garlandii, commonly known as the Garland's African Daisy, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and silver-blue foliage. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with a compact, bushy form. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
