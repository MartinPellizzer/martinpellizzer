Solanum multinervium

Solanum multinervium, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent veins, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant bears berries that mature to a dark blue or black color, often containing toxic compounds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including savannas and rocky areas, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
