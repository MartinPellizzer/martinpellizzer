Spiraea bernhardii

Spiraea bernhardii, commonly known as the golden spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 3 meters and features narrow, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of Spiraea bernhardii make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in native landscapes.
