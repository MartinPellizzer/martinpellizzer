Sesleria robusta subsp. skanderbeggii

Sesleria robusta subsp.

skanderbeggii, commonly known as the Skanderbeg sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its robust growth habit and is often found in calcareous soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
