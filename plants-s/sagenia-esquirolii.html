Sagenia esquirolii

Sagenia esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Sagenia esquirolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its hardy, drought-tolerant nature.
