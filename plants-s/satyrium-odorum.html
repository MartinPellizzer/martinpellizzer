Satyrium odorum

Satyrium odorum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral scent.
