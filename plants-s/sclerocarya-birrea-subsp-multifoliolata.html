Sclerocarya birrea subsp. multifoliolata

Sclerocarya birrea subsp.

multifoliolata, commonly known as the African wild apricot, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, bark-like trunk and compound leaves with multiple leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by orange or red fruits that are edible and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
