Serratula cichoracea subsp. cretica

Serratula cichoracea subsp.

cretica, commonly known as Cretan sowthistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of deeply lobed, grass-like leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
