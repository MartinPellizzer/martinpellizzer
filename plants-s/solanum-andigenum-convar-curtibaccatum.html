Solanum andigenum convar. curtibaccatum

Solanum andigenum convar. curtibaccatum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
