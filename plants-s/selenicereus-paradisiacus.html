Selenicereus paradisiacus

Selenicereus paradisiacus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that store water. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and are also popular in horticulture for their striking appearance.

The plant thrives in warm, dry climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
