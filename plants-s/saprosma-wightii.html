Saprosma wightii

Saprosma wightii, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Saprosma wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
