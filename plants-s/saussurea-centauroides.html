Saussurea centauroides

Saussurea centauroides, commonly known as the centaur's crown, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Saussurea centauroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
