Sesamum angolense

Sesamum angolense, commonly known as African sesame, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its seeds are rich in oils and have been used traditionally for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Sesamum angolense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
