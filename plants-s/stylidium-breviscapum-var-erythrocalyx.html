Stylidium breviscapum var. erythrocalyx

Stylidium breviscapum var. erythrocalyx, commonly known as the red-flowered sun-orchid, is a small herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, sticky seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heaths.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a notable species in botanical collections.
