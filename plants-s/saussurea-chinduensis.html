Saussurea chinduensis

Saussurea chinduensis, commonly known as the Chindu mountain saffron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in Nepal and India. The plant is characterized by its tall, spindly stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Saussurea chinduensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
