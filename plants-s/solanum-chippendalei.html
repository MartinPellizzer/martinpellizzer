Solanum chippendalei

Solanum chippendalei, commonly known as the Chippendale nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often blooming in spring and early summer. Its fruits are berries that start green and mature to a dark purple or black color, containing several seeds.

Solanum chippendalei is part of the nightshade family, which includes many other economically important plants.
