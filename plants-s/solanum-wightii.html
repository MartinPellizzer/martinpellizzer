Solanum wightii

Solanum wightii, commonly known as the Wight's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that are initially green and turn red when mature. Solanum wightii belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers and foliage.
