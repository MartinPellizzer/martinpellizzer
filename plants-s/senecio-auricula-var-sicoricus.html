Senecio auricula var. sicoricus

Senecio auricula var. sicoricus, commonly known as the Sicilian auricula, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ear-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated for both decorative and ecological purposes.
