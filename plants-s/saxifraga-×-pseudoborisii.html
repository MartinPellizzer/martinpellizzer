Saxifraga × pseudoborisii

Saxifraga × pseudoborisii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
