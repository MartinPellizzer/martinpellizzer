Salacia podopetala

Salacia podopetala, commonly known as the pink salacia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is not closely related to them. The plant is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing species. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are typically woody and branched.

Salacia podopetala is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
