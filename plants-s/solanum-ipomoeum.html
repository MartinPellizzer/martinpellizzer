Solanum ipomoeum

Solanum ipomoeum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum ipomoeum is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
