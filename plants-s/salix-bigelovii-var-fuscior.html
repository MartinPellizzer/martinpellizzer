Salix bigelovii var. fuscior

Salix bigelovii var. fuscior, commonly known as the darkleaf willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and reddish-brown bark. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in riparian zones. It produces catkins in early spring, which are a key feature of its reproductive biology.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
