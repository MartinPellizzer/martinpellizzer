Senecio cortesianus

Senecio cortesianus, commonly known as the Cape Town groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, sandy, or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio cortesianus is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
