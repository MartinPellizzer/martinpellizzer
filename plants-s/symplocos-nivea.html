Symplocos nivea

Symplocos nivea, commonly known as the snowberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that turn red when mature. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Symplocos nivea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
