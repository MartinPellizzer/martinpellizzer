Serjania microcephala

Serjania microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
