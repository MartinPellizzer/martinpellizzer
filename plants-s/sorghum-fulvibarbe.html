Sorghum fulvibarbe

Sorghum fulvibarbe, commonly known as the red beard sorghum, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and distinctive reddish bristles along the panicle. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically green or purplish in color. It is adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both forage and grain production in arid environments.
