Saussurea pachyneura

Saussurea pachyneura, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its fibrous roots are adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
