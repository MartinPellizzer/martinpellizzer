Sphaeradenia crassiceps

Sphaeradenia crassiceps, commonly known as the broad-leaved sphaeradenia, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking yellowish-white spadix. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Sphaeradenia crassiceps is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
