Schubertia japonica

Schubertia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in hairy foliage and produces showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Schubertia japonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
