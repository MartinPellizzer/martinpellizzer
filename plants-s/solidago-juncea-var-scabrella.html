Solidago juncea var. scabrella

Solidago juncea var. scabrella, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture. This variety is typically found in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
