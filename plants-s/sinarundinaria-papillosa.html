Sinarundinaria papillosa

Sinarundinaria papillosa, commonly known as the Chinese cliff brake, is a fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are pinnately compound and often have a waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cliffs and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Sinarundinaria papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
