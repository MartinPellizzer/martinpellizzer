Saxifraga dichotoma var. procera

Saxifraga dichotoma var. procera, commonly known as the tall saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
