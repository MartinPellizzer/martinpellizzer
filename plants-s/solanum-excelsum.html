Solanum excelsum

Solanum excelsum, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from violet to white, often blooming at night. The plant can grow up to 10 meters tall, making it one of the tallest species in the Solanaceae family. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, while its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
