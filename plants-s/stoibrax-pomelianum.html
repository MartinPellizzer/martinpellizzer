Stoibrax pomelianum

Stoibrax pomelianum, commonly known as the white star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
