Shafera

Shafera, commonly known as the "scented geranium," is a flowering plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, pleasant scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. Its leaves are often used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy due to their essential oils.

Shafera is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
