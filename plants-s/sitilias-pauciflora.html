Sitilias pauciflora

Sitilias pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered sitilias, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the late spring and early summer months. Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often found in rocky, arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and conservation studies.
