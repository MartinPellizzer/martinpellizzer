Stelis xanthantha

Stelis xanthantha, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Stelis xanthantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific humidity and light requirements.
