Senecio rehmannii

Senecio rehmannii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small candles, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and conservation areas.
