Sedum trichospermum

Sedum trichospermum, commonly known as the silver rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
