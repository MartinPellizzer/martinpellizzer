Stevia cuzcoensis

Stevia cuzcoensis, commonly known as the Cuzco stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained interest in herbal and natural product research.
