Solanum peloquinianum

Solanum peloquinianum, commonly known as the Peloquin nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its berries are typically green when mature and turn red, though they are toxic and should not be consumed.

The species is often found in moist, shaded areas within tropical and subtropical forests.
