Scirpus naikianus

Scirpus naikianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are clustered in cylindrical spikes, which are usually greenish or reddish in color.

Scirpus naikianus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
