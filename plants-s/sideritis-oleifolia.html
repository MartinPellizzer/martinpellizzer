Sideritis oleifolia

Sideritis oleifolia, commonly known as Greek violet or mountain tea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, violet-blue flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at higher altitudes.

Sideritis oleifolia is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.
