Stipa collina

Stipa collina, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Stipa collina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and support biodiversity.

Its seeds are also valued for their nutritional content and are used in some traditional foods.
