Sapindopsis oligophylla

Sapindopsis oligophylla, commonly known as the African olive, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, woody fruits containing single seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
