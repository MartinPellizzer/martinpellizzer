Sempervivum majus

Sempervivum majus, commonly known as the hen and chicks plant, is a succulent perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green or variegated with white or pink markings. The plant produces tall flowering stalks in late summer, bearing small, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a wide range of climates, including cold winters.
