Scleria cyanocarpa

Scleria cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Its fruit is a blue-black drupe, which is distinctive and often used for identification.

Scleria cyanocarpa is typically found in moist soils in wetlands and along stream banks.
