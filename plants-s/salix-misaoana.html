Salix misaoana

Salix misaoana, commonly known as the Misao willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin.

Salix misaoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
