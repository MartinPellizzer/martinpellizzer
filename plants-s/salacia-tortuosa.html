Salacia tortuosa

Salacia tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral vine or mountain vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its twisting, serpentine growth pattern and can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Salacia tortuosa is often found growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments.
