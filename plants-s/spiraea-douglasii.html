Spiraea douglasii

Spiraea douglasii, commonly known as Douglas spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, oval leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous areas.

Spiraea douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscapes.
