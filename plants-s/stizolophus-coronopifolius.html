Stizolophus coronopifolius

Stizolophus coronopifolius, commonly known as the crown-leaf stizolophus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool and temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive crown-like arrangement at the base, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
