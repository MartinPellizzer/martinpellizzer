Stelis atroculata

Stelis atroculata, commonly known as the black-eyed stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive dark-colored pseudobulbs. The plant produces delicate, linear leaves and bears small, fragrant flowers with a unique dark spot at the base of the petals. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
