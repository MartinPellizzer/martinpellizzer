Setaria vulpiseta

Setaria vulpiseta, commonly known as foxsetail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Setaria vulpiseta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
