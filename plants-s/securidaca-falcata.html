Securidaca falcata

Securidaca falcata, commonly known as the sickle senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
