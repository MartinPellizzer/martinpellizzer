Silene perfoliata

Silene perfoliata, commonly known as the perforate cranesbill, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are perforated, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Silene perfoliata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
