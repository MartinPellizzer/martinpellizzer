Spiraea baldshuanica

Spiraea baldshuanica, commonly known as the Siberian spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems with clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Spiraea baldshuanica prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
