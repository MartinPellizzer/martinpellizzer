Schizostachyum copelandii

Schizostachyum copelandii, commonly known as the silver rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems covered in small, silver-colored spikes, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered, edible fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and sustainable harvesting, it is cultivated for rattan furniture and decorative purposes.
