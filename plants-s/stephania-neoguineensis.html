Stephania neoguineensis

Stephania neoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea stephania, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elliptic, smooth, and often hairy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
