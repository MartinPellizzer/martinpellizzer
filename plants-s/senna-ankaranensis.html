Senna ankaranensis

Senna ankaranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar senna, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Senna ankaranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
