Solanum mariannense

Solanum mariannense, commonly known as the Marianas nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the island of Guam.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. Solanum mariannense is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats on Guam.
