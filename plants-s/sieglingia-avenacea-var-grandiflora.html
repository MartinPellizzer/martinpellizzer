Sieglingia avenacea var. grandiflora

Sieglingia avenacea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered Sieglingia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has palmately compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
