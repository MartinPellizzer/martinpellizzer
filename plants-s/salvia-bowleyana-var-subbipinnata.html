Salvia bowleyana var. subbipinnata

Salvia bowleyana var. subbipinnata, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of blue or purple.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
