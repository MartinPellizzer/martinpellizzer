Saussurea lavrenkoana

Saussurea lavrenkoana, commonly known as the Lavrenko's saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated seed heads that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
