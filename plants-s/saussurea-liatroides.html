Saussurea liatroides

Saussurea liatroides, commonly known as the Alpine Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at elevations above 3,000 meters. The plant features a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates.

Saussurea liatroides is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
