Senegalia tanjorensis

Senegalia tanjorensis, commonly known as the Indian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its thorny branches and adaptive growth patterns make it well-suited for harsh climates.
