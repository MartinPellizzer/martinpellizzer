Saxifraga mutabilis var. incisolobata

Saxifraga mutabilis var. incisolobata, commonly known as the variable saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear small, clustered leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its variable leaf shapes and colors.

This species is often found in high elevation habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
