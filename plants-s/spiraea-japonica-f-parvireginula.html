Spiraea japonica f. parvireginula

Spiraea japonica f. parvireginula, commonly known as the Japanese weeping spirea, is a compact, ornamental shrub characterized by its cascading clusters of small, white flowers.

It features finely cut, ovate leaves that provide a delicate texture, and its growth habit is typically rounded or weeping, depending on pruning. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in partial shade to full sun, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. It is valued for its early spring bloom and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.

Its botanical traits make it a popular choice for small gardens and container planting.
