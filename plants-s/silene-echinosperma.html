Silene echinosperma

Silene echinosperma, commonly known as the prickly evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, hard seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Silene echinosperma is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
