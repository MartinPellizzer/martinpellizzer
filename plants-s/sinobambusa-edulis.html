Sinobambusa edulis

Sinobambusa edulis, commonly known as the edible bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often cultivated for its edible shoots, which are used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

Sinobambusa edulis is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
