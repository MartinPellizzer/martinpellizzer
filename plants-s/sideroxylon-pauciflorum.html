Sideroxylon pauciflorum

Sideroxylon pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and is known for its small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
