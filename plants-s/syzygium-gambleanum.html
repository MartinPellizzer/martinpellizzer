Syzygium gambleanum

Syzygium gambleanum, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple, segmented fruit that contains a juicy, white arillate pulp. The plant produces fragrant white flowers and has elliptical, leathery leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. Its growth habit includes a dense canopy, making it a valuable component of rainforest ecosystems.

Syzygium gambleanum is also noted for its medicinal properties and is widely cultivated for its edible fruit.
