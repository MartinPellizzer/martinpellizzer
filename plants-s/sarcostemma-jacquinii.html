Sarcostemma jacquinii

Sarcostemma jacquinii, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
