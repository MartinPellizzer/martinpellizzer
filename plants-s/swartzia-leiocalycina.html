Swartzia leiocalycina

Swartzia leiocalycina, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark hardwood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, flat seeds.

Swartzia leiocalycina is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
