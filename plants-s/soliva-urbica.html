Soliva urbica

Soliva urbica, commonly known as city weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. This plant is often found growing in urban environments, such as along sidewalks and in disturbed soils. It is adaptable to various soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Soliva urbica is an annual plant that completes its life cycle within a single growing season.
