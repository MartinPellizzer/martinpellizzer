Saldinia myrtilloides var. robustior

Saldinia myrtilloides var. robustior, commonly known as the robust mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found on hosts such as eucalyptus and acacia. Its leaves are usually alternate and ovate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and clustered, often white or greenish in color, and it produces small, fleshy berries as its fruit.
