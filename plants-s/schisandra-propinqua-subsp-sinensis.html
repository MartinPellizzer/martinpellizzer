Schisandra propinqua subsp. sinensis

Schisandra propinqua subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, red berries that contain a variety of bioactive compounds.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring.

The fruit is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits and is also valued as a culinary ingredient.
