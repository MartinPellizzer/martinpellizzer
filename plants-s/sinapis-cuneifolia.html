Sinapis cuneifolia

Sinapis cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Sinapis cuneifolia is an annual plant that produces pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
