Strangea stenocarpoides

Strangea stenocarpoides, commonly known as the narrow-fruit strangler fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its unique growth habit, often climbing or epiphytic. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

The species is notable for its ability to form dense canopies, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
