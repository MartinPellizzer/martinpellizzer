Scorzonera alaica

Scorzonera alaica, commonly known as the alpine scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots are tuberous and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera alaica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
