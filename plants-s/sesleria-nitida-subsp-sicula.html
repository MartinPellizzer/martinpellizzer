Sesleria nitida subsp. sicula

Sesleria nitida subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
