Salix dissymmetrica

Salix dissymmetrica, commonly known as the asymmetric willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical growth pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its catkins are a key feature of its reproductive system.

Salix dissymmetrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
