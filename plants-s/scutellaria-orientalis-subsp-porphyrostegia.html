Scutellaria orientalis subsp. porphyrostegia

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

porphyrostegia, commonly known as the purple-stemmed skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its medicinal properties, which have been explored in traditional herbal practices.
