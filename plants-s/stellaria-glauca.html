Stellaria glauca

Stellaria glauca, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers with five petals that resemble stars, giving it its scientific name. The plant has hairy, rounded leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Stellaria glauca is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
