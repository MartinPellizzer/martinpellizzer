Salvia burchellii

Salvia burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
