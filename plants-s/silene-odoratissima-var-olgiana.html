Silene odoratissima var. olgiana

Silene odoratissima var. olgiana, commonly known as the fragrant catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant produces tiny, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Silene odoratissima var. olgiana is also noted for its strong, pleasant fragrance, which is most noticeable in the evening.
