Senecio fluviatilis subsp. fuchsii

Senecio fluviatilis subsp.

fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs' goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils near rivers and streams.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, daisy-like heads, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
