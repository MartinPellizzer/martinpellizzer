Skinneria cespitosa

Skinneria cespitosa, commonly known as the tufted Skinneria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, forming dense clumps with tufted foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in loose panicles.

Skinneria cespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its hardy and low-maintenance nature.
