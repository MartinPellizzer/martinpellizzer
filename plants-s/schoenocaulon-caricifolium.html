Schoenocaulon caricifolium

Schoenocaulon caricifolium, commonly known as the bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically red or orange in color, which are characteristic of the genus Schoenocaulon. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
