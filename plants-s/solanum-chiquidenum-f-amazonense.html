Solanum chiquidenum f. amazonense

Solanum chiquidenum f. amazonense, commonly known as the Amazonian nightshade, is a rare plant species native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of the Amazon basin.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological role, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
