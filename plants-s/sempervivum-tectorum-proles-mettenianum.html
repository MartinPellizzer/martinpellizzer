Sempervivum tectorum proles mettenianum

Sempervivum tectorum proles mettenianum, commonly known as the Alpine House Sempervivum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues on the edges. The plant produces tall, flowering stalks in the summer, bearing small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or violet. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
