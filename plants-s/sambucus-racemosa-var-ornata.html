Sambucus racemosa var. ornata

Sambucus racemosa var. ornata, commonly known as the showy elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces dark purple berries in the fall, which are edible but should be consumed in moderation. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant has a smooth, often reddish stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.
