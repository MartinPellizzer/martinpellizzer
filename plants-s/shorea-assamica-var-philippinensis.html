Shorea assamica var. philippinensis

Shorea assamica var. philippinensis, commonly known as Philippine rosewood, is a tropical hardwood tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood with a fine grain. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a tall, straight trunk with smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic in shape, and have a glossy upper surface.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, round fruits containing a single seed.
