Stylidium cordifolium

Stylidium cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved triggerplant, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique trigger mechanism that facilitates pollination by insects. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in coastal areas.
