Saxifraga × goringana

Saxifraga × goringana, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga × goringana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
