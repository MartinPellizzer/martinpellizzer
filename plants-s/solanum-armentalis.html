Solanum armentalis

Solanum armentalis, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Solanum armentalis belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains various alkaloids, making it potentially toxic if ingested.
