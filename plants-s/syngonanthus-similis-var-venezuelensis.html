Syngonanthus similis var. venezuelensis

Syngonanthus similis var. venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
