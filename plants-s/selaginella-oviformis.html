Selaginella oviformis

Selaginella oviformis, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a terrestrial, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a creeping stem with small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores for propagation.

Selaginella oviformis is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
