Statice oleifolia var. fradiniana

Statice oleifolia var. fradiniana, commonly known as the Fradinian statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, showy flowers. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
