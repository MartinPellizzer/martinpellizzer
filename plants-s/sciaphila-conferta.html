Sciaphila conferta

Sciaphila conferta, commonly known as the shade orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with numerous erect, leafy shoots. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a dense spike.

Sciaphila conferta is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
