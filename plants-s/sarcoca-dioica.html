Sarcoca dioica

Sarcoca dioica, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctively colored flowers, with one side of the lip being yellow and the other red. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical forests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological role in the ecosystem.

Sarcoca dioica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
