Sorbus rhombiformis

Sorbus rhombiformis, commonly known as the diamondleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves, which are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
