Saba comorensis var. florida

Saba comorensis var. florida, commonly known as the Florida saba, is a variant of the saba plant native to the Comoros Islands and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems in suitable regions.
