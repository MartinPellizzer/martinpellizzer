Spiranthes disoides

Spiranthes disoides, commonly known as the small spiral orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, white to pale yellow blossoms.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
