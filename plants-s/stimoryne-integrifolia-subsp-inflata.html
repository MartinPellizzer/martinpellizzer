Stimoryne integrifolia subsp. inflata

Stimoryne integrifolia subsp.

inflata, commonly known as the inflated stimoryne, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth edge. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
