Staehelina imbricata

Staehelina imbricata, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Staehelina imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
