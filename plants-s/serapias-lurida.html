Serapias lurida

Serapias lurida, commonly known as the pale orchid or the alpine serapias, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually basal, narrow, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Serapias lurida is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators in its native environment.
