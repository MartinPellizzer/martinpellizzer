Syringa sargentiana

Syringa sargentiana, commonly known as Sargent's lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a dense, bushy form.

Syringa sargentiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various soil types.
