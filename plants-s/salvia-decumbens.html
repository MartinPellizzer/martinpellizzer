Salvia decumbens

Salvia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia decumbens is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
