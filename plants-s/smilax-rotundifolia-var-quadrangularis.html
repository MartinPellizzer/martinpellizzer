Smilax rotundifolia var. quadrangularis

Smilax rotundifolia var. quadrangularis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, angular, and often thorny stems, which are covered in a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a heart-shaped base with a waxy texture.

This variety is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
