Simsia eurylepis

Simsia eurylepis, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. Simsia eurylepis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
