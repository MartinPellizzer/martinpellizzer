Silene nutans var. spathulifolia

Silene nutans var. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate to spatulate in shape, giving it its varietal name, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene nutans var. spathulifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
