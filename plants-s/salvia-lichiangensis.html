Salvia lichiangensis

Salvia lichiangensis, commonly known as the Yunnan sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia lichiangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
