Scirpus fontinalis var. virginianus

Scirpus fontinalis var. virginianus, commonly known as the Virginia bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along streams, ponds, and lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are often submerged or floating in water.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife.
