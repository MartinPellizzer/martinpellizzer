Syzygium commersonii

Syzygium commersonii, commonly known as the white crape myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, and the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Syzygium commersonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
