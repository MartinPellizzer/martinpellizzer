Salvia roscida

Salvia roscida, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late summer. Salvia roscida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
