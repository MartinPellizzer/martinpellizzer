Salix cinerea var. lancifolia

Salix cinerea var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
