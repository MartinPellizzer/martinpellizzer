Solanum × blanco-galdosii

Solanum × blanco-galdosii, commonly known as the hybrid nightshade, is a plant species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. This hybrid is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or violet. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste due to the presence of alkaloids.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its potential medicinal properties.
