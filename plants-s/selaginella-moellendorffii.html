Selaginella moellendorffii

Selaginella moellendorffii, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. This plant is notable for its unique life cycle, which includes a dominant gametophyte stage and a short sporophyte stage. Its leaves are spirally arranged and often have a scale-like appearance.

Selaginella moellendorffii is also of scientific interest due to its genetic simplicity and its role in understanding the evolution of land plants.
