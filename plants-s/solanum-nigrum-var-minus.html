Solanum nigrum var. minus

Solanum nigrum var. minus, commonly known as dwarf black nightshade, is a variant of the black nightshade plant.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, with small, spiny stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and it produces small, black berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant due to its compact size and unique appearance.
