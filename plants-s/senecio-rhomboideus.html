Senecio rhomboideus

Senecio rhomboideus, commonly known as the diamondweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating, known as a bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
