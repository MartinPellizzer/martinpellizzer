Sesleria budensis

Sesleria budensis, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Sesleria budensis typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
