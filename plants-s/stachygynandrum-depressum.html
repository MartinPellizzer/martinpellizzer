Stachygynandrum depressum

Stachygynandrum depressum, commonly known as the water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a clover leaf. This plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, and it plays a role in water filtration and habitat creation for aquatic life.

Stachygynandrum depressum is also valued in ornamental aquatic gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
