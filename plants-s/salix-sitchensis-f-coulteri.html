Salix sitchensis f. coulteri

Salix sitchensis f. coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's willow, is a unique variant of the Sitka willow.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and narrow, elongated leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils near streams or in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
