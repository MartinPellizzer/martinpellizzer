Scirpus dipsaceus

Scirpus dipsaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a tall, reed-like perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers that are often hidden by the foliage. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Scirpus dipsaceus plays an important role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
