Solidago lepida var. caurina

Solidago lepida var. caurina, commonly known as the greenstem goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
