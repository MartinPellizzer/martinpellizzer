Streptocarpus velutinus

Streptocarpus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Streptocarpus velutinus is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
