Stachys mohinora

Stachys mohinora, commonly known as the Mohinora hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in whorled clusters. Stachys mohinora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
