Sapranthus hirsutus

Sapranthus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy sapranthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters.

Sapranthus hirsutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
