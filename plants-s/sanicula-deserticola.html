Sanicula deserticola

Sanicula deserticola, commonly known as the desert sanicle, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Sanicula deserticola is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert insects and small animals.
