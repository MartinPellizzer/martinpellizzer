Sphaeropteris suluensis

Sphaeropteris suluensis, commonly known as the Sulu cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
