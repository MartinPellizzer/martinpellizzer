Stachys speciosa

Stachys speciosa, commonly known as the showy betony, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Stachys speciosa is also valued for its medicinal properties, having been used traditionally for its calming effects.
