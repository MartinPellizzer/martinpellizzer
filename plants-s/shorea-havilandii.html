Shorea havilandii

Shorea havilandii, commonly known as the Malacca ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood that is highly valued for its density and fine grain. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is typically found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, round fruits containing one or two seeds.
