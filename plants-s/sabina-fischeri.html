Sabina fischeri

Sabina fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's juniper, is a species of juniper native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a dense, compact appearance. Its cones are small and berry-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Sabina fischeri is valued for its hardiness and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
