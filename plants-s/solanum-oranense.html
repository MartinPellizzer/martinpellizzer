Solanum oranense

Solanum oranense, commonly known as the purple potato or wild potato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots that resemble potatoes. The plant produces clusters of small, white or purple flowers and has hairy, alternate leaves. Its tubers are edible but can be toxic if not properly prepared.

Solanum oranense is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
