Senecio diphyllus

Senecio diphyllus, commonly known as the two-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems with opposite, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring and summer months.

Senecio diphyllus is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
