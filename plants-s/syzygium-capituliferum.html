Syzygium capituliferum

Syzygium capituliferum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Syzygium capituliferum is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal and mountainous environments.
