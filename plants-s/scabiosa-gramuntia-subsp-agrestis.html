Scabiosa gramuntia subsp. agrestis

Scabiosa gramuntia subsp.

agrestis, commonly known as the meadow scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
