Sabatia angustifolia

Sabatia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem.

It produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
