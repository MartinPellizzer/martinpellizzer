Salvia verbenaca var. australis

Salvia verbenaca var. australis, commonly known as Australian sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It features upright, branching stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a slight woolly texture. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native gardens.
