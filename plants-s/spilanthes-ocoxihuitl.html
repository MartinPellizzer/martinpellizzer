Spilanthes ocoxihuitl

Spilanthes ocoxihuitl, commonly known as "chicatle" in Nahuatl, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the genus Spilanthes within the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is notable for its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often resembling a cluster of tiny daisies. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Spilanthes ocoxihuitl is also recognized for its use in traditional medicine, particularly for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
