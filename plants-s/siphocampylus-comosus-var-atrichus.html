Siphocampylus comosus var. atrichus

Siphocampylus comosus var. atrichus, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically purple to pink, arranged in dense, conical clusters at the top of the plant.

It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes, often forming dense stands in wetland environments.
