Scleranthus × griseus

Scleranthus × griseus, commonly known as the gray sandwort, is a hybrid plant belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features narrow, grayish-green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as coastal dunes or disturbed areas.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its parent species.
