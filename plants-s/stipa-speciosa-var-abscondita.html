Stipa speciosa var. abscondita

Stipa speciosa var. abscondita, commonly known as the hidden beauty grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are pale yellow to golden in color.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
