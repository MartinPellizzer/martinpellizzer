Stachys carduchorum

Stachys carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
