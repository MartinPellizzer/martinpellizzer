Skimmia fortunei

Skimmia fortunei, commonly known as the Japanese skimmia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is a compact evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features leathery, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls around the stems. Its most striking feature is its clusters of fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

Skimmia fortunei is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
