Salicornia equisetifolia

Salicornia equisetifolia, commonly known as sea asparagus, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments such as coastal areas and salt marshes.

It is characterized by its jointed, succulent stems that are often mistaken for stems of other plants due to their unique structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it is often found in association with other halophytes. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, and it is known for its high salt tolerance, which allows it to thrive in conditions where most plants cannot.

Salicornia equisetifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
