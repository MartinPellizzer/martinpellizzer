Saxifraga laeta

Saxifraga laeta, commonly known as the happy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Saxifraga laeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
