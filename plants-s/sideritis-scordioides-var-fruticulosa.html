Sideritis scordioides var. fruticulosa

Sideritis scordioides var. fruticulosa, commonly known as the mountain thyme or Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with aromatic, small, grey-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky habitats.

Sideritis scordioides var. fruticulosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
