Solanum nigrum proles alatum

Solanum nigrum proles alatum, commonly known as the winged nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its distinctive winged stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that change from green to black as they mature, often containing toxic compounds. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable, though caution is advised due to its toxic properties.
