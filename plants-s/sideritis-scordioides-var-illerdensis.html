Sideritis scordioides var. illerdensis

Sideritis scordioides var. illerdensis, commonly known as the Illerda mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
