Senecio metcalfei

Senecio metcalfei, commonly known as the White Star Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens with low water requirements.
