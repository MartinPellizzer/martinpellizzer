Solidago purshii var. racemosa

Solidago purshii var. racemosa, commonly known as the yellowtop goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features erect, branching stems covered with fine, hairy foliage. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer to early fall, forming dense, raceme-like clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
