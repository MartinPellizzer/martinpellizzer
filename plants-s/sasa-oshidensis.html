Sasa oshidensis

Sasa oshidensis, commonly known as the Oshida bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth, grassy texture.

Sasa oshidensis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
