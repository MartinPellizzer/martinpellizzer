Saxifraga androsacea f. tridentata

Saxifraga androsacea f. tridentata, commonly known as the tridentate saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically basal, arranged in a rosette, and often have a distinctive three-lobed or tridentate shape, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
