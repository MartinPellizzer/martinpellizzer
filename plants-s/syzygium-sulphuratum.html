Syzygium sulphuratum

Syzygium sulphuratum, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Syzygium sulphuratum is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
