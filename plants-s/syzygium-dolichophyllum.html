Syzygium dolichophyllum

Syzygium dolichophyllum, commonly known as the long-leaf clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and plantations.
