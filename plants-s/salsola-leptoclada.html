Salsola leptoclada

Salsola leptoclada, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Salsola leptoclada is well adapted to harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and tolerate high temperatures.

It plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
