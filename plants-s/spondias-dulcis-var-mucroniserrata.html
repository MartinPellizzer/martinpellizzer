Spondias dulcis var. mucroniserrata

Spondias dulcis var. mucroniserrata, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that give way to large, fuzzy, green fruits with a distinctive warty appearance.
