Salix × aellenii

Salix × aellenii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, weeping branches that cascade with long, pendulous leaves. The plant belongs to the Salicaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside, turning yellow in autumn.

Salix × aellenii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and along waterways.
