Sarothamnus baeticus var. africanus

Sarothamnus baeticus var. africanus, commonly known as the African thornbush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This variety is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
