Symplocos montana var. munda

Symplocos montana var. munda, commonly known as the Himalayan sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in temperate to subtropical climates and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible, red berries that are a source of food for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
