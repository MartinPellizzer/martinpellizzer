Saxifraga kegangii

Saxifraga kegangii, commonly known as the Ke Gang saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its hardiness in cold climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
