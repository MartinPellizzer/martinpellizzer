Sorghum dochna var. melliferum

Sorghum dochna var. melliferum, commonly known as the honey sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a tall, annual grass that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, with slender, cylindrical stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers that are often tinged with purple or red hues. Its inflorescence is notable for its dense, spike-like arrangement, which contributes to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued not only for its aesthetic qualities but also for its potential in traditional and modern agricultural practices.
