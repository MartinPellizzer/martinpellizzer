Sinarundinaria nigra

Sinarundinaria nigra, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and its ability to grow in a variety of soil types. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Sinarundinaria nigra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its resilience to environmental stress.

It plays a role in traditional Chinese medicine and is also used in landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
