Sporobolus aeneus var. subbulbosus

Sporobolus aeneus var. subbulbosus, commonly known as the silverleaf needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
