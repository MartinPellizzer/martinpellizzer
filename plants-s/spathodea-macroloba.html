Spathodea macroloba

Spathodea macroloba, commonly known as the African tulip, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant features broad, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Spathodea macroloba is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a dense, branching structure.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
