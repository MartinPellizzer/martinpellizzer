Schinus weinmanniifolia var. weinmanniifolia

Schinus weinmanniifolia var. weinmanniifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
