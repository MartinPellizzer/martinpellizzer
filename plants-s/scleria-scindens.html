Scleria scindens

Scleria scindens, commonly known as the split reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale blue or white flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Scleria scindens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
