Senecio pteropogon

Senecio pteropogon, commonly known as the candlestick plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it grows in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that resemble a candlestick, hence its common name. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

Senecio pteropogon is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
