Syringa palibiniana var. longifolia

Syringa palibiniana var. longifolia, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its long, slender flower clusters distinguish it from other syringa varieties.
