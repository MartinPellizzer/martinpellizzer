Silene fabaria subsp. thebana

Silene fabaria subsp.

thebana, commonly known as the Balkan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

The species is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.
