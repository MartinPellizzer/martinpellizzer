Sykesia obovata

Sykesia obovata, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are oval in shape. The plant is notable for its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its botanical features include showy, tubular flowers with five petals and a strong, aromatic scent.

Sykesia obovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.
