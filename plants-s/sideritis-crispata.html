Sideritis crispata

Sideritis crispata, commonly known as the wrinkled sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis crispata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
