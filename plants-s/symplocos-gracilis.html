Symplocos gracilis

Symplocos gracilis, commonly known as slender waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Symplocos gracilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
