Solanum chiquidenum var. cachicadense

Solanum chiquidenum var. cachicadense, commonly known as the Cachicadense nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are typically oval with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
