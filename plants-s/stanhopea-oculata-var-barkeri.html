Stanhopea oculata var. barkeri

Stanhopea oculata var. barkeri, commonly known as the Barker's Stanhopea, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include elongated, narrow leaves and a complex floral structure with a prominent lip.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
