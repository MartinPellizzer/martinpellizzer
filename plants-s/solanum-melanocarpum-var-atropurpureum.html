Solanum melanocarpum var. atropurpureum

Solanum melanocarpum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-fruited eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species characterized by its dark purple or black fruits.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and the fruits are egg-shaped with a smooth, glossy skin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique coloration and flavor.
