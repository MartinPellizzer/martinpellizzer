Sorbus alnifolia f. submollis

Sorbus alnifolia f. submollis, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that attract birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in various environmental conditions.
