Solanum endoadenium var. robustius

Solanum endoadenium var. robustius, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces berries that change color from green to red as they mature, attracting various wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or secondary forests.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
