Spiraea fortunei paniculata

Spiraea fortunei paniculata, commonly known as the golden spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that produce clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea fortunei paniculata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
