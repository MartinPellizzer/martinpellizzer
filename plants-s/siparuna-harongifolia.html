Siparuna harongifolia

Siparuna harongifolia, commonly known as the harongi or red-flowered siparuna, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of striking red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded areas within tropical rainforests.

Siparuna harongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
