Symplocos loheri

Symplocos loheri, commonly known as the Himalayan waxapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically pubescent. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. Symplocos loheri thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Its berries are a food source for various birds and mammals, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
