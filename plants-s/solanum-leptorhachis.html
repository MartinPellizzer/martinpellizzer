Solanum leptorhachis

Solanum leptorhachis, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically features hairy, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant of caution in cultivation.

Solanum leptorhachis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks.
