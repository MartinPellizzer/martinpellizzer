Senecio subcandidus

Senecio subcandidus, commonly known as the white-haired groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woolly, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
