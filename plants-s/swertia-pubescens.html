Swertia pubescens

Swertia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in summer, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Swertia pubescens is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
