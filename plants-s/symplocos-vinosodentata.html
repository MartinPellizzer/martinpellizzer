Symplocos vinosodentata

Symplocos vinosodentata, commonly known as the purple-toothed symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded gardens.
