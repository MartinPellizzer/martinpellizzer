Symplocos coronata var. glabrifolia

Symplocos coronata var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved beautyberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces dark purple, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves are typically smooth and glossy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
