Salvia prionitis

Salvia prionitis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Salvia prionitis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
