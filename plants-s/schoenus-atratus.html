Schoenus atratus

Schoenus atratus, commonly known as the black rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

Schoenus atratus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
