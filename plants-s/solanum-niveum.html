Solanum niveum

Solanum niveum, commonly known as the snowberry nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its white, clustered berries that resemble small snowballs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate with a velvety texture, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale green or white.

Solanum niveum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
