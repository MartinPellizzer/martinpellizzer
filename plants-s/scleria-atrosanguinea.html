Scleria atrosanguinea

Scleria atrosanguinea, commonly known as black blood grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clumps. Its flowers are small and spike-like, arranged in a panicle, and are usually greenish or pale brown.

Scleria atrosanguinea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
