Spiraea × pseudosalicifolia

Spiraea × pseudosalicifolia, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its graceful, cascading branches.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is native to eastern North America and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Spiraea × pseudosalicifolia is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
