Salvia argentea var. gussonei

Salvia argentea var. gussonei, commonly known as silver sage or silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The leaves and flowers are also used in traditional herbal remedies.
