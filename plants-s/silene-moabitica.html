Silene moabitica

Silene moabitica, commonly known as the Moabitic campion, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from June to August and is often found in open habitats with sufficient sunlight.

Silene moabitica is relatively rare and is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
