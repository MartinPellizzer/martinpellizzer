Symphoricarpos parishii

Symphoricarpos parishii, commonly known as the Parry serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, which mature in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Symphoricarpos parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
