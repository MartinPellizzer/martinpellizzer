Sorema elegans

Sorema elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Sorema elegans is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, making it a notable presence in its native ecosystems.
