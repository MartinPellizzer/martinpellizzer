Stevia entreriensis var. typica

Stevia entreriensis var. typica, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from compounds called steviol glycosides, which are found in the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for cultivation as a natural sweetener.

Due to its low caloric content and health benefits, it has gained attention in the food and pharmaceutical industries.
