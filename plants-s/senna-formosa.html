Senna formosa

Senna formosa, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Senna formosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
