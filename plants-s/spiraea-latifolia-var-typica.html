Spiraea latifolia var. typica

Spiraea latifolia var. typica, commonly known as broadleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, multi-branched stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
