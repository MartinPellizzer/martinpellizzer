Scaevola × cerasifolia

Scaevola × cerasifolia, commonly known as the coral bells or fairy fan, is a hybrid flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its long blooming period, extending from spring to autumn.
