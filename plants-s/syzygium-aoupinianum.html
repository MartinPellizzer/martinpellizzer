Syzygium aoupinianum

Syzygium aoupinianum, commonly known as the Aoupinian apple or mountain apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various regions for both practical and aesthetic purposes.
