Saxifraga melaena

Saxifraga melaena, commonly known as the black saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Saxifraga melaena is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
