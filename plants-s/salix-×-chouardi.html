Salix × chouardi

Salix × chouardi, commonly known as the Weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Salix nigra and Salix discolor, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is notable for its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, often giving it a weeping orweeping willow-like look. Its leaves are elongated, glossy, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies, making it a common sight in wetland habitats.
