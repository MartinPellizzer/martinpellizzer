Stroganowia bupleuroides

Stroganowia bupleuroides, commonly known as the bluebunch sedge, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, blue to purple flower spikes in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, which help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Stroganowia bupleuroides is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and ecological balance.
