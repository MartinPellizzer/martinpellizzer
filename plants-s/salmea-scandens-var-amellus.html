Salmea scandens var. amellus

Salmea scandens var. amellus, commonly known as the Amellus climbing plant, is a variant of the Salmea scandens species, characterized by its climbing growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden trellises or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for both landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
