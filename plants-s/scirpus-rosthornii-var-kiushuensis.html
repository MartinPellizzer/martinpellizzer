Scirpus rosthornii var. kiushuensis

Scirpus rosthornii var. kiushuensis, commonly known as the Japanese bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color.

This variant is characterized by its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
