Scutellaria hypericifolia

Scutellaria hypericifolia, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a square stem, pubescent leaves, and a distinctive calyx that persists after flowering.

It is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
