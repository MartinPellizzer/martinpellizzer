Scilla guttata

Scilla guttata, commonly known as the spotted hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The flowers are typically 1 to 2 inches long and bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
