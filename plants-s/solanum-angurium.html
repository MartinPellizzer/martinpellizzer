Solanum angurium

Solanum angurium, commonly known as the bitter apple or Indian apple, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody vine structure and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. The berries are typically bitter and contain seeds, though they are occasionally consumed in some cultures.

It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
