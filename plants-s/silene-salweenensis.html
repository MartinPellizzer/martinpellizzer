Silene salweenensis

Silene salweenensis, commonly known as the Salween campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Silene salweenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
