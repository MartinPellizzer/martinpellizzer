Senecio pseudoalatus

Senecio pseudoalatus, commonly known as the silver leaf senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and compact form, it is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
