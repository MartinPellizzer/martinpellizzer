Saussurea dissecta

Saussurea dissecta, commonly known as the Alpine tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows in high-altitude environments, typically above the tree line. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single, terminal head, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Saussurea dissecta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
