Stylogyne brasiliensis

Stylogyne brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its thick, buttressed roots and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences of red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Stylogyne brasiliensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
