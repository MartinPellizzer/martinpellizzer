Salacia longipedicellata

Salacia longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-fruited salacia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Salaceae family and is characterized by its woody vine structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
