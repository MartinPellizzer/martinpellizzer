Sasa sugawarae

Sasa sugawarae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or sugawara bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasa sugawarae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Japanese gardens.
