Soja wightii

Soja wightii, commonly known as the Wight's soybean, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its pods contain edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional value.

Soja wightii is also valued for its ecological role in improving soil fertility.
