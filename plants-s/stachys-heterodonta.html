Stachys heterodonta

Stachys heterodonta, commonly known as the heterodonta mint, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
