Serapias × schipae

Serapias × schipae, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Serapias aucubifolia and Serapias himalayana.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee, aiding in pollination by attracting insects. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows to a height of about 30–60 centimeters.

Serapias × schipae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soil.
