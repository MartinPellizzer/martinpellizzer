Senegalia deamii

Senegalia deamii, commonly known as the Deam's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. Senegalia deamii is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
