Setaria pallide-fusca

Setaria pallide-fusca, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with yellow or brown hues. The plant typically grows in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Setaria pallide-fusca is an annual plant that is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
