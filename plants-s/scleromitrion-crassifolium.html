Scleromitrion crassifolium

Scleromitrion crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and waxy, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in the summer months. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Scleromitrion crassifolium is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water once established.
