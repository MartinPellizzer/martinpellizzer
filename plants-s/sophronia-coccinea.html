Sophronia coccinea

Sophronia coccinea, commonly known as the red spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. It has a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in late spring.

Sophronia coccinea is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
