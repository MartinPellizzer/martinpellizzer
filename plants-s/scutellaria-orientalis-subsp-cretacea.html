Scutellaria orientalis subsp. cretacea

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

cretacea, commonly known as the Cretan skullcup, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, two-lipped blue or purple flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.
