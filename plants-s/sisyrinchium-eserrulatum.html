Sisyrinchium eserrulatum

Sisyrinchium eserrulatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iris family (Iridaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium eserrulatum is often found in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.
