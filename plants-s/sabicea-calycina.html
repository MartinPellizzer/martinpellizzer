Sabicea calycina

Sabicea calycina, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sabicea calycina is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
