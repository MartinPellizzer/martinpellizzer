Sorbus torminalis f. platyloba

Sorbus torminalis f. platyloba, commonly known as the flat-lobed whitebeam, is a horticultural form of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, flat-lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden settings.
