Stenocarpus trinervis var. paradoxus

Stenocarpus trinervis var. paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiny foliage and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant exhibits a distinctive growth habit with stiff, narrow leaves arranged in a three-veined pattern. It is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

The variety 'paradoxus' is notable for its unusual flowering pattern and ecological significance in its native habitat.
