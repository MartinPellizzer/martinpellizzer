Satureja subspicata var. obcordata

Satureja subspicata var. obcordata, commonly known as narrow-leaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its foliage is often used in culinary applications, adding flavor to dishes and herbal teas.
