Streptanthus amplexicaulis

Streptanthus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, mat-like growth form. Its leaves are basal and clasping, forming a rosette, while its stems are upright and bear the flowers.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in coastal and inland regions of California.
