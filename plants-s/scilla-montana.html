Scilla montana

Scilla montana, commonly known as mountain scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Scilla montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
