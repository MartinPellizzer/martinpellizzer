Saccolabium pubescens

Saccolabium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy saccolabium, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its root system is adapted for efficient water uptake, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

Saccolabium pubescens is often found in rocky soils and is an important component of local ecosystems.
