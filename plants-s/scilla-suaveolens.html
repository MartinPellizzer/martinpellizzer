Scilla suaveolens

Scilla suaveolens, commonly known as sweet scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
