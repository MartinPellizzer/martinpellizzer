Scirpus giganteus

Scirpus giganteus, commonly known as giant bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or saturated soil. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous greenish flowers at the top of its stems, which are followed by feathery, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Scirpus giganteus plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
