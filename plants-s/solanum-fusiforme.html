Solanum fusiforme

Solanum fusiforme, commonly known as the fusiform nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a berry that changes from green to red as it matures.

Solanum fusiforme contains alkaloids and is part of the nightshade family, which includes both ornamental and toxic species.
