Stachys recta subsp. sarejevensis

Stachys recta subsp.

sarejevensis, commonly known as the Sarejevski horseweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces spikes of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
