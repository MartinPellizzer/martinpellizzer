Sedum albiflorum

Sedum albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its drought tolerance and ability to store water in its leaves are adaptations to its arid environment.

Sedum albiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
