Stipa parodiana

Stipa parodiana, commonly known as the Parodi's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Stipa parodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
