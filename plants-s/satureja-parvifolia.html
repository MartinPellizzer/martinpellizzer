Satureja parvifolia

Satureja parvifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from pale purple to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Satureja parvifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
