Salix babylonica var. glandulipilosa

Salix babylonica var. glandulipilosa, commonly known as the weeping willow with glandular catkins, is a variant of the weeping willow native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive drooping appearance. The plant produces catkins that are covered in small glands, giving it its specific variety name. It thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water features.

Its foliage is typically a vibrant green in summer, turning yellow in autumn.
