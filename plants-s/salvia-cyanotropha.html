Salvia cyanotropha

Salvia cyanotropha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia cyanotropha thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.
