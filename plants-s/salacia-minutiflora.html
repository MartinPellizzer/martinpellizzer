Salacia minutiflora

Salacia minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf salacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars.
