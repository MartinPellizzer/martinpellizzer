Satureja parviflora

Satureja parviflora, commonly known as small-flower savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in culinary applications for its pungent, slightly peppery flavor.

Satureja parviflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
