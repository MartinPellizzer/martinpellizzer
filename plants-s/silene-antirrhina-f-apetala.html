Silene antirrhina f. apetala

Silene antirrhina f. apetala, commonly known as the pale-flowered campion, is a botanical variant of the common campion.

It is characterized by its reduced or absent petals, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to the typical form. This plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
