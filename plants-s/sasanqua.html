Sasanqua

Sasanqua, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Sasanqua thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
