Scilla paratethyca

Scilla paratethyca, commonly known as the Crimean squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, particularly in mountainous regions.

Scilla paratethyca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its delicate floral display.
