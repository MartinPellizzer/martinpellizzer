Solidago aspericaulis

Solidago aspericaulis, commonly known as roughstem goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Solidago aspericaulis plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
