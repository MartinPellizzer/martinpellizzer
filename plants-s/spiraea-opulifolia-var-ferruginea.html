Spiraea opulifolia var. ferruginea

Spiraea opulifolia var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a distinctive reddish-brown or rusty-colored underside. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its compact form and seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
