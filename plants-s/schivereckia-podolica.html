Schivereckia podolica

Schivereckia podolica, commonly known as the Podolia bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Eastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Schivereckia podolica thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
