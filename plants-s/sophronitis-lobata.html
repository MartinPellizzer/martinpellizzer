Sophronitis lobata

Sophronitis lobata, commonly known as the lobate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Sophronitis lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
