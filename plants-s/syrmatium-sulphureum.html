Syrmatium sulphureum

Syrmatium sulphureum, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and are known for their intense coloration and subtle fragrance.

Syrmatium sulphureum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
