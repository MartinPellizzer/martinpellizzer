Sideritis × puiggariana subsp. sorianoi

Sideritis × puiggariana subsp.

sorianoi, commonly known as the Soriano's sainfoin, is a hybrid plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from pink to purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
