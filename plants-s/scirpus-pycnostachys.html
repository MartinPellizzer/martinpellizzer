Scirpus pycnostachys

Scirpus pycnostachys, commonly known as the dense-stalked bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and wet meadows. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in aquatic environments.

Scirpus pycnostachys is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to filter water pollutants.
