Solanum coriaceifoliolatum

Solanum coriaceifoliolatum, commonly known as the thick-leaved nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its thick, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by yellowish berries. It is often found in humid, tropical environments and can thrive in shaded or semi-shaded conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
