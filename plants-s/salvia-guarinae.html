Salvia guarinae

Salvia guarinae, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows up to 2 to 3 feet tall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
