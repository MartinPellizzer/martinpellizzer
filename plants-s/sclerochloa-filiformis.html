Sclerochloa filiformis

Sclerochloa filiformis, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and swamps.

Sclerochloa filiformis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
