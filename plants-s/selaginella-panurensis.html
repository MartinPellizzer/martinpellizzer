Selaginella panurensis

Selaginella panurensis, commonly known as the Panurensis spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive morphology, featuring a creeping stem with spirally arranged, needle-like leaves. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella panurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
