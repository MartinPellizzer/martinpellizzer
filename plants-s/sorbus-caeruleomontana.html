Sorbus caeruleomontana

Sorbus caeruleomontana, commonly known as the blue mountain rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or bluish-white berries that appear in clusters, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy fruit display.
