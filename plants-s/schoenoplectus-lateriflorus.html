Schoenoplectus lateriflorus

Schoenoplectus lateriflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Schoenoplectus lateriflorus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
