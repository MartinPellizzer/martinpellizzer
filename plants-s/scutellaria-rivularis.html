Scutellaria rivularis

Scutellaria rivularis, commonly known as the water skullcap, is a small herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, stream banks, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue to purple in color. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

Scutellaria rivularis is often found in the understory of forests, where it plays a role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.
