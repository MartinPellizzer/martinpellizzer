Setaria laxa

Setaria laxa, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a native grass species found in various parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Setaria laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
