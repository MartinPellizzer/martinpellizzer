Syrmatium decumbens

Syrmatium decumbens, commonly known as the creeping syrmatium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant has prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Syrmatium decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ground-covering habit.
