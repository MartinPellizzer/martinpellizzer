Syzygium confertum

Syzygium confertum, commonly known as the satinwood or red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe. Syzygium confertum is valued for its dense, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
