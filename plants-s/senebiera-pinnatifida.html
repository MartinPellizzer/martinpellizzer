Senebiera pinnatifida

Senebiera pinnatifida, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional medicine for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its seeds are used in various herbal remedies and have been studied for their potential health benefits.
