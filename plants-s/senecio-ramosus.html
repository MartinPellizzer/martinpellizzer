Senecio ramosus

Senecio ramosus, commonly known as the branched groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Senecio ramosus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
