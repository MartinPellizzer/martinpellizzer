Salvia verbenaca var. laxispica

Salvia verbenaca var. laxispica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet or blue, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to both garden and ecological settings.
