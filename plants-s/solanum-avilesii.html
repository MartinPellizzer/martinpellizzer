Solanum avilesii

Solanum avilesii, commonly known as the Aviles's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Bahamas and Cuba. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature, and they contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Solanum avilesii is adapted to coastal and dry habitats, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife.
