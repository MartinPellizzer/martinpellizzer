Scirpus tabernaemontani var. paludosus

Scirpus tabernaemontani var. paludosus, commonly known as the swamp bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus tabernaemontani var. paludosus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
