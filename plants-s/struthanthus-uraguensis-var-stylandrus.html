Struthanthus uraguensis var. stylandrus

Struthanthus uraguensis var. stylandrus, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a succulent, branching stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
