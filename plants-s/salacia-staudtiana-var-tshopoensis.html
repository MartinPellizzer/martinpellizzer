Salacia staudtiana var. tshopoensis

Salacia staudtiana var. tshopoensis, commonly known as the Tshopo salacia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa, particularly in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, glossy leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
