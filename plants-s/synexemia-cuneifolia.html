Synexemia cuneifolia

Synexemia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf synexemia, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique foliage and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
