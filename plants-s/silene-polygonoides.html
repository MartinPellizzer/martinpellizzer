Silene polygonoides

Silene polygonoides, commonly known as the rock pepper or mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it has a compact, low-growing habit.

Silene polygonoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
