Streblus laxiflorus

Streblus laxiflorus, commonly known as the broad-leaved sterculia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
