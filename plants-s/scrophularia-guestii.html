Scrophularia guestii

Scrophularia guestii, commonly known as the Sierra scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are tubular, ranging in color from pink to purple, and are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
