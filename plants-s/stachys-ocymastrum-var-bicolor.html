Stachys ocymastrum var. bicolor

Stachys ocymastrum var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive purplish tinge. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
