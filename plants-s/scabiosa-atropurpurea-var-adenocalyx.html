Scabiosa atropurpurea var. adenocalyx

Scabiosa atropurpurea var. adenocalyx, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with a distinctive central cone shape. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-gray hue. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
