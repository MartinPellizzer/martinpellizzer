Sporobolus paniculatus

Sporobolus paniculatus, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a grass species native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry foliage and produces panicles of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. This plant is adapted to sandy and well-drained soils, often found in dry prairies and disturbed areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

The plant's ability to stabilize soil and its low maintenance requirements make it ecologically valuable in native plant gardens.
