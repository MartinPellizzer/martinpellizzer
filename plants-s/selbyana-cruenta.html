Selbyana cruenta

Selbyana cruenta, commonly known as the scarlet spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters.

Selbyana cruenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
