Syzygium bruynii

Syzygium bruynii, commonly known as the Bruyn's apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits resembling small apples. Syzygium bruynii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and is valued for its tart flavor.
