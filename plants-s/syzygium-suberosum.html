Syzygium suberosum

Syzygium suberosum, commonly known as the rough-skin tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish-brown fruits. Its fruit pulp is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The tree is valued for its ornamental features and ecological importance in its native habitat.
