Syzygium subhorizontale

Syzygium subhorizontale, commonly known as the horizontal-leaf tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often arranged horizontally, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Syzygium subhorizontale is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses.
