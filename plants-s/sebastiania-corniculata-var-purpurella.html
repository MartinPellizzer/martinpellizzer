Sebastiania corniculata var. purpurella

Sebastiania corniculata var. purpurella, commonly known as the purple-sepal sebastiania, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple or reddish sepals that contrast with its green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne on long, slender peduncles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
