Stipa nelsonii

Stipa nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and along mountain slopes.

Stipa nelsonii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
