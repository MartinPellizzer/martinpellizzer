Syzygium parvicarpum

Syzygium parvicarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited clove.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a mild fragrance when crushed.

Syzygium parvicarpum typically grows in lowland forests and thrives in humid, warm climates.
