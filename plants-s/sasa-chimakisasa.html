Sasa chimakisasa

Sasa chimakisasa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that often have a reddish hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Sasa chimakisasa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some cultures.
