Salvia jamaicensis

Salvia jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia jamaicensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical climates.
