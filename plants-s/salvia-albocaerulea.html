Salvia albocaerulea

Salvia albocaerulea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Salvia albocaerulea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
