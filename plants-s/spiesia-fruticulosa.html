Spiesia fruticulosa

Spiesia fruticulosa, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a shrub native to South America, particularly in the Andes region.

It is characterized by its small, dense foliage and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests. Its leaves are typically leathery and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Spiesia fruticulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
