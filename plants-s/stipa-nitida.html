Stipa nitida

Stipa nitida, commonly known as silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Stipa nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
