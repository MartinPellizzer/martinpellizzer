Saracha solanacea

Saracha solanacea, commonly known as the purple-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Saracha solanacea is native to parts of South America and is often found in dry, open habitats.
