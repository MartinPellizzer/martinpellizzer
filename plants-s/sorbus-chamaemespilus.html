Sorbus chamaemespilus

Sorbus chamaemespilus, commonly known as the creeping whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Western Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, rocky soils, and open habitats, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, with a smooth texture. It produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

The species is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
