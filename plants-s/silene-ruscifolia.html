Silene ruscifolia

Silene ruscifolia, commonly known as the creeping catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene ruscifolia thrives in moist, sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woods or along streambanks.
