Sagenia angulata

Sagenia angulata, commonly known as the angular sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

Sagenia angulata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
