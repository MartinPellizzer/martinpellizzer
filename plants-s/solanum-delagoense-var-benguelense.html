Solanum delagoense var. benguelense

Solanum delagoense var. benguelense, commonly known as the Benguela nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Its berries are initially green and turn red when mature, containing small, toxic seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by livestock and humans alike.
