Syzygium sriganesanii

Syzygium sriganesanii, commonly known as the Srimulang tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Syzygium sriganesanii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
