Spiranthes navarrensis

Spiranthes navarrensis, commonly known as Navarre's spiral orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of wetlands and pine flatwoods. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.
