Styphelia amplexicaulis

Styphelia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping whorled heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of white or pink. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and closely clasping the stems, giving it a compact appearance.

Styphelia amplexicaulis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in pine forest understories.
