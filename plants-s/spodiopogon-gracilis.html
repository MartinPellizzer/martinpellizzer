Spodiopogon gracilis

Spodiopogon gracilis, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-lobed, three-seeded fruits. Spodiopogon gracilis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
