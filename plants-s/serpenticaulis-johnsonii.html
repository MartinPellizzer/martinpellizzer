Serpenticaulis johnsonii

Serpenticaulis johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's serpent stalk, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serpentine-like stems that coil and twist as they grow, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
