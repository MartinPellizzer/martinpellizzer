Saxifraga zekoensis

Saxifraga zekoensis, commonly known as the Zeko saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color, blooming in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
