Sophronitis bicolor

Sophronitis bicolor, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with contrasting purple or brown markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The orchid's botanical features make it a popular choice among horticulturists and orchid enthusiasts.
