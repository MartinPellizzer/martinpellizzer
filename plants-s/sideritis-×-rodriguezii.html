Sideritis × rodriguezii

Sideritis × rodriguezii, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, tubular flowers.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Sideritis scordifolia and Sideritis rehmannii, often found in rocky or limestone soils across southern Europe. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or violet, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Sideritis × rodriguezii is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
