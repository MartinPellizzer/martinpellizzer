Salvia apparicii

Salvia apparicii, commonly known as the Appalachian sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and thrives in partial to full shade. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Salvia apparicii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
