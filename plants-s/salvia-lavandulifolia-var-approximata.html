Salvia lavandulifolia var. approximata

Salvia lavandulifolia var. approximata, commonly known as lemon mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive lemony aroma. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to blue-violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its aromatic leaves and ornamental value, it is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
