Saussurea phaeantha

Saussurea phaeantha, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils at high altitudes, often in the Himalayas and adjacent regions. Its leaves are basal, palmately lobed, and covered with dense, white to silver hairs.

Saussurea phaeantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
