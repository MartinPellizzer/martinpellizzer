Stipa dasyvaginata subsp. apenninicola

Stipa dasyvaginata subsp.

apenninicola, commonly known as the Apennine feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are typically pink to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
