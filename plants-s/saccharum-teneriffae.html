Saccharum teneriffae

Saccharum teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island sugarcane.

This plant is native to the Canary Islands and is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and coarse, serrated leaves. The plant is adapted to the arid and coastal environments of its native habitat.

It has been studied for its potential in biofuel production due to its high sugar content.
