Salvia hydrangea

Salvia hydrangea, commonly known as the hydrangea, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that can range in color from white to pink, red, and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composed of sterile stamens that form the showy bracts, while the true flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
