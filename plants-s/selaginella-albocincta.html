Selaginella albocincta

Selaginella albocincta, commonly known as the white-banded spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant has a distinctive appearance, with slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the plant's spores.

Selaginella albocincta is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique adaptations for water retention.
