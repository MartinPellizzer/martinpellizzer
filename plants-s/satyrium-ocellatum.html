Satyrium ocellatum

Satyrium ocellatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a yellow or white center, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Satyrium ocellatum is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a rare or endangered species in many regions.
