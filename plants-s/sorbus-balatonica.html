Sorbus balatonica

Sorbus balatonica, commonly known as the Balaton rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
