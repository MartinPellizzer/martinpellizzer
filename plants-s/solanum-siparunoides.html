Solanum siparunoides

Solanum siparunoides, commonly known as the white star apple, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive white flowers and oval-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, has a sweet and mild flavor, making it a valued plant in local cuisines.
