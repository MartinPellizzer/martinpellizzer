Salix caprea var. alpina

Salix caprea var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine willow, is a shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its hardiness, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in high-altitude areas.
