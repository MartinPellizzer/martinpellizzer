Spiraea ulmaria f. subdenudata

Spiraea ulmaria f. subdenudata, commonly known as the slender meadow spirea, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pinkish flowers.

It is a subspecies of Spiraea ulmaria, which is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctively narrow shape.

Its flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
