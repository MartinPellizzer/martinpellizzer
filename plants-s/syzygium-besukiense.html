Syzygium besukiense

Syzygium besukiense, commonly known as the Besuki apple or Besuki tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet and tart flavor.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in local cuisines.
