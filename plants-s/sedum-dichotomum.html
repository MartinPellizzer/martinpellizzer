Sedum dichotomum

Sedum dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems that often branch into two, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for gardeners.
