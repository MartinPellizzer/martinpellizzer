Streptolirion volubile subsp. subalpinum

Streptolirion volubile subsp.

subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine streptolirion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
