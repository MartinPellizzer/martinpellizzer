Sabicea exellii

Sabicea exellii, commonly known as the Exell's sabicea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within lowland forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Sabicea exellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
