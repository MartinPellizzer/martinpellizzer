Senna corifolia var. corifolia

Senna corifolia var. corifolia, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain numerous seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
