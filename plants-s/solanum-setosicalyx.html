Solanum setosicalyx

Solanum setosicalyx, commonly known as the hairy calyx nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy calyx, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and white or purple, and it produces berries that can be toxic if ingested.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
