Solanum pyrethrifolium

Solanum pyrethrifolium, commonly known as the pyrethrum-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, or white. Its leaves are distinctive, with a finely divided, fern-like appearance that gives it its common name.

Solanum pyrethrifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
