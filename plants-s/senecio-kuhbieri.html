Senecio kuhbieri

Senecio kuhbieri, commonly known as the Woolly Senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, woolly leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small yellow flowers in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
