Senecio tricuspidatus var. tricuspidatus

Senecio tricuspidatus var. tricuspidatus, commonly known as the three-lobed groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, narrow leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and among succulent enthusiasts.
