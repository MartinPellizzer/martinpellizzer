Scurrula argentea

Scurrula argentea, commonly known as the silver scurrula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a terminal spike during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
