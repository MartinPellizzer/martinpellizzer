Sclerophylax trispermus

Sclerophylax trispermus, commonly known as the three-seed sclerophylax, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand dry and nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains three seeds, giving it the species name *trispermus*.

This plant is often found in rocky or coastal habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
