Sarcoglottis glaucescens

Sarcoglottis glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, while its flowers display a range of hues, including blue, purple, and white.

Sarcoglottis glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
