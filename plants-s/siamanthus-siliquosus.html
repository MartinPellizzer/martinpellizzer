Siamanthus siliquosus

Siamanthus siliquosus, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often brightly colored in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.
