Saxifraga moschata var. pontica

Saxifraga moschata var. pontica, commonly known as the sweet saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer, which are arranged in a raceme. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with the leaves containing compounds used in traditional remedies.
