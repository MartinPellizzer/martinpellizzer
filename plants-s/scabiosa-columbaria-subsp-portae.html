Scabiosa columbaria subsp. portae

Scabiosa columbaria subsp.

portae, commonly known as the Alpine Scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
