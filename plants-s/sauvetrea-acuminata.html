Sauvetrea acuminata

Sauvetrea acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sauvetrea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features slender, upright stems that often bear clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a pointed tip, and are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
