Schilleria callosa

Schilleria callosa, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking red or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features tubular flowers that are typically clustered in loose racemes, and it has narrow, grass-like leaves. Schilleria callosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
