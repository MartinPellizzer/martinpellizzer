Solanum hazenii

Solanum hazenii, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, purple berries that are typically toxic to humans but may be consumed by certain animals. It is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves.

Solanum hazenii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
