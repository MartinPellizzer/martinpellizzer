Saussurea glabrescens

Saussurea glabrescens, commonly known as the Himalayan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, gray-green, woolly leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish to orange flower heads that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
