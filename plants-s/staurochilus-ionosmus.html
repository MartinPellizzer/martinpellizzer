Staurochilus ionosmus

Staurochilus ionosmus, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple or red border. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, which is typical for many orchids.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
