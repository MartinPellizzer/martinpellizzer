Sedum mocinoanum

Sedum mocinoanum, commonly known as the Mocino's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to low-maintenance landscapes.
