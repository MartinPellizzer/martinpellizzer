Solanum phaseoloides

Solanum phaseoloides, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. Solanum phaseoloides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and climbing habit.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
