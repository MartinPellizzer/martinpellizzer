Senecio × austriacus

Senecio × austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian groundsel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Senecio × austriacus is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
