Serjania pteropoda

Serjania pteropoda, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Serjania pteropoda is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
