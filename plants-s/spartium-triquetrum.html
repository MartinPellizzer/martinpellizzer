Spartium triquetrum

Spartium triquetrum, commonly known as three-cornered grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It has slender, three-angled stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The leaves are narrow, alternate, and have a distinctive three-angled cross-section, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in spikes during the summer months.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
