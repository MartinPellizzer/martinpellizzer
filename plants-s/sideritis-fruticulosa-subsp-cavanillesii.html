Sideritis fruticulosa subsp. cavanillesii

Sideritis fruticulosa subsp.

cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
