Scabiosa candolleana

Scabiosa candolleana, commonly known as the Italian sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa candolleana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
