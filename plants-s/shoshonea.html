Shoshonea

Shoshonea, commonly known as the Shoshone sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Salvia and is characterized by its gray-green foliage and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its leaves are aromatic and contain volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Shoshonea plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
