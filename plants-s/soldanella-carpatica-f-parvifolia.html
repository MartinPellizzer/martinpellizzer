Soldanella carpatica f. parvifolia

Soldanella carpatica f. parvifolia, commonly known as the alpine snowbell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in white or pale pink. Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
