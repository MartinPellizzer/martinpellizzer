Salvia glabra

Salvia glabra, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and spikes of small, pale blue to white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and spiritual practices.

Salvia glabra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
