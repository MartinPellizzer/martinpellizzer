Silene quadridentata subsp. monachorum

Silene quadridentata subsp.

monachorum, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces pink to purple flowers in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
