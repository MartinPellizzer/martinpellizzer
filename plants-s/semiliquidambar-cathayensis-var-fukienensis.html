Semiliquidambar cathayensis var. fukienensis

Semiliquidambar cathayensis var. fukienensis, commonly known as the Fukien persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange to reddish fruits, which are rich in nutrients and commonly consumed fresh or dried. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
