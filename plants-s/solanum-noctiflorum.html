Solanum noctiflorum

Solanum noctiflorum, commonly known as night-blooming nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and striking white or pale purple flowers that open at night. Its flowers emit a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.
