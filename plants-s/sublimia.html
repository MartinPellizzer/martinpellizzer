Sublimia

Sublimia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal in appearance. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions, making it a subject of fascination for botanists. Sublimia thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique adaptations, it is considered a delicately balanced species within its ecosystem.
