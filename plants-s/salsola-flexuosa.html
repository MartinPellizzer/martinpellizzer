Salsola flexuosa

Salsola flexuosa, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are adapted for wind pollination.

Salsola flexuosa is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
