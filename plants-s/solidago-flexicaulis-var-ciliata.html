Solidago flexicaulis var. ciliata

Solidago flexicaulis var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and wetlands.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
