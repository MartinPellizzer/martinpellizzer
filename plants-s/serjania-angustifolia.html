Serjania angustifolia

Serjania angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf serjania, is a species of flowering plant in the family Serjaniae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
