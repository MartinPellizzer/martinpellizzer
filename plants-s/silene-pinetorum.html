Silene pinetorum

Silene pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest campion, is a flowering plant native to the pine forests of Europe and North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments under pine trees. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and bloom in the summer months.

Silene pinetorum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
