Sedum telephium var. kirinense

Sedum telephium var. kirinense, commonly known as the Kirin stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
