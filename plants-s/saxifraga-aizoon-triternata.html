Saxifraga aizoon triternata

Saxifraga aizoon triternata, commonly known as the Sierra stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
