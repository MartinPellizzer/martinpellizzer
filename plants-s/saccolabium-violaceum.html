Saccolabium violaceum

Saccolabium violaceum, commonly known as the violet bristled legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its violet to purple flowers and hairy, bristled stems. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Saccolabium violaceum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
