Sasa senanensis var. nobilis

Sasa senanensis var. nobilis, commonly known as the noble bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching culms that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by triangular, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional landscaping and basketry.
