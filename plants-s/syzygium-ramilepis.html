Syzygium ramilepis

Syzygium ramilepis, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the tree a striking appearance during the flowering season. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and along roadsides.
