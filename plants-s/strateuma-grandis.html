Strateuma grandis

Strateuma grandis, commonly known as the giant starflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive star-like shape. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a sprawling, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Strateuma grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, sun-like blooms.
