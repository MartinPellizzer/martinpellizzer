Saussurea leptophylla

Saussurea leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed saffron.

It is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cold and arid conditions. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive saffron-like hue, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
