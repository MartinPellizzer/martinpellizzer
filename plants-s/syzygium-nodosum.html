Syzygium nodosum

Syzygium nodosum, commonly known as the fragrant satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, and it contains a single seed. The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

Its ornamental qualities and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitats.
