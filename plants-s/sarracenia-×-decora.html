Sarracenia × decora

Sarracenia × decora, commonly known as the Decorated Pitcher Plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, and often brightly colored pitcher-shaped leaves that serve as traps for insects. The plant's pitchers are typically red or orange with intricate patterns, which aid in attracting prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Sarracenia × decora is popular among carnivorous plant enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and unique ecological role.
