Selaginella extensa

Selaginella extensa, commonly known as the spike moss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with spirally arranged leaves that form dense, moss-like clusters. Its sporophyte stage is characterized by a short, stout stem with whorls of leaves and specialized structures for spore production.

Selaginella extensa is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
