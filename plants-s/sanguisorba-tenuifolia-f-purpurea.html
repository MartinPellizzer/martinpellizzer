Sanguisorba tenuifolia f. purpurea

Sanguisorba tenuifolia f. purpurea, commonly known as purple narrowleaf sorrel, is a cultivar of the genus Sanguisorba, characterized by its distinctive purple foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to Asia, typically growing in moist, shady environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late summer. Its botanical interest lies in its ornamental value and unique coloration, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
