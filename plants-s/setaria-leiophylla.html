Setaria leiophylla

Setaria leiophylla, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The botanical features of Setaria leiophylla make it an important plant in ecological studies and agricultural contexts.
