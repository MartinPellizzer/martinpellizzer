Soldanella × subtatrica

Soldanella × subtatrica, commonly known as the alpine snowbell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

Soldanella × subtatrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine gardens and botanical collections.
