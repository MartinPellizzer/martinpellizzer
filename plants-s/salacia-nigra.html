Salacia nigra

Salacia nigra, commonly known as black salacia, is a shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, woody stems and elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. Salacia nigra is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Its unique appearance and medicinal uses make it a notable species within its native habitat.
