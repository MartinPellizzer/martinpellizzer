Sideritis incana var. guyoniana

Sideritis incana var. guyoniana, commonly known as the pale hairsedge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or white flowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
