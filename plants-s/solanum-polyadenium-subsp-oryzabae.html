Solanum polyadenium subsp. oryzabae

Solanum polyadenium subsp.

oryzabae, commonly known as the rice bean plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used as a legume in local cuisines.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
