Salisia pulchella

Salisia pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful salix, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, branching structure. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color, with a smooth texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive, elongated seed capsules.

Salisia pulchella is typically found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
