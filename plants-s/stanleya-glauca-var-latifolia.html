Stanleya glauca var. latifolia

Stanleya glauca var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf whitetop, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, glaucous leaves and tall, branched stems that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open areas with well-drained soils. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is considered rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
