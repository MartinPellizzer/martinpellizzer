Smilax keyensis

Smilax keyensis, commonly known as Key's smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with thorny nodes and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Smilax keyensis is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

It has a long history of use by indigenous peoples for medicinal and practical purposes.
