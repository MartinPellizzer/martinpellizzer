Semecarpus uncatus

Semecarpus uncatus, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when injured.

The flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in catkin-like inflorescences, and the plant is known for its ecological and medicinal significance.
