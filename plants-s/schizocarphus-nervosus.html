Schizocarphus nervosus

Schizocarphus nervosus, commonly known as the veined bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, branching stems and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It is adapted to nutrient-poor waters and can form dense mats on the water surface.

Schizocarphus nervosus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
