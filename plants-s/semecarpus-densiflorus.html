Semecarpus densiflorus

Semecarpus densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered sycamore, is a member of the Sapindaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in dense clusters, and it produces woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
