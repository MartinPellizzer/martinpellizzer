Salvia truxillensis

Salvia truxillensis, commonly known as the Truxillensis sage, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
