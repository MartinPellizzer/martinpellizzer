Solidago radulina

Solidago radulina, commonly known as the mossy goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of moss, contributing to its common name.

Solidago radulina plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
