Sorbus aria var. aurea

Sorbus aria var. aurea, commonly known as the golden mountain-ash, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which becomes more pronounced in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that persist through the winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
