Sagraea curvipila

Sagraea curvipila, commonly known as the slender-leaved sagraea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Sagraea curvipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
