Seseli corymbosum subsp. phrygium

Seseli corymbosum subsp.

phrygium, commonly known as the Phrygian seseli, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
