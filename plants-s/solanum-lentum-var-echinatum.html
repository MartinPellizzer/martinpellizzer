Solanum lentum var. echinatum

Solanum lentum var. echinatum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, greenish-white flowers and produces berries that transition from green to red as they mature. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
