Syzygium wilsonii

Syzygium wilsonii, commonly known as the China flame or flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface with a slightly serrated margin.
