Stipagrostis vexillifeta

Stipagrostis vexillifeta, commonly known as the flag-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive flag-like structure at the base of the leaf blade. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Stipagrostis vexillifeta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
