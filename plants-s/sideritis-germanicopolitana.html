Sideritis germanicopolitana

Sideritis germanicopolitana, commonly known as the Greek violet or Germanic violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Sideritis germanicopolitana is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
