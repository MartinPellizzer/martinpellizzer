Senecio amphibolus var. socialis

Senecio amphibolus var. socialis, commonly known as the social senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually blue-green in color.

This species is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
