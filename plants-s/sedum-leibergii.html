Sedum leibergii

Sedum leibergii, commonly known as the stonecrop or ice plant, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky regions of Europe and North Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in summer. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.

Sedum leibergii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments.
