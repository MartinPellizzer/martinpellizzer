Sebastiania glandulosa var. parvifolia

Sebastiania glandulosa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved wax tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its succulent, glandular leaves and striking orange or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered with fine, sticky glands, which contribute to its unique appearance.

The flowers are clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
