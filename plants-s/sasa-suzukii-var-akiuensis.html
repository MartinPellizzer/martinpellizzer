Sasa suzukii var. akiuensis

Sasa suzukii var. akiuensis, commonly known as the Akiu bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. Its culms are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The variety is distinguished by its unique growth habit and ecological adaptations to its native habitat.
