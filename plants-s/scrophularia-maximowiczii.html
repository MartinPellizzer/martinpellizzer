Scrophularia maximowiczii

Scrophularia maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy, often two-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually purple or pink in color.

Scrophularia maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
