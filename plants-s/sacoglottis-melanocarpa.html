Sacoglottis melanocarpa

Sacoglottis melanocarpa, commonly known as black-seeded amaranth, is an annual herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its seeds are encased in a hard, black shell, which gives the species its common name.

Sacoglottis melanocarpa is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines.
