Syringantha loranthoides

Syringantha loranthoides, commonly known as the Indian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the Indian subcontinent.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in ecological studies.

Syringantha loranthoides plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for certain bird species and influencing the growth of its host trees.
