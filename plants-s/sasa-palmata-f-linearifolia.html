Sasa palmata f. linearifolia

Sasa palmata f. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa palmata species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that give it a distinct appearance compared to the typical form of Sasa palmata. This plant is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with tall, slender culms that can reach several meters in height.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for use in landscaping or traditional medicine.
