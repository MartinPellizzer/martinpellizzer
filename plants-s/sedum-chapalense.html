Sedum chapalense

Sedum chapalense, commonly known as the Chapalense stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or orange, and bloom during the summer months.

Sedum chapalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
