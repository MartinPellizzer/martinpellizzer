Scleranthus annuus var. verticillatus

Scleranthus annuus var. verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered sow-thistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are typically lobed or toothed, and the flowers are hermaphroditic, containing both male and female reproductive structures.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
