Solanum andigenum f. tenue

Solanum andigenum f. tenue, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its small, violet flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
