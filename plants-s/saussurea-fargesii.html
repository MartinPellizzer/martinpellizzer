Saussurea fargesii

Saussurea fargesii, commonly known as the Yunnan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has fibrous roots and hairy leaves, adapted to its cold and arid environment. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at elevations above 2,500 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botany and conservation efforts.
