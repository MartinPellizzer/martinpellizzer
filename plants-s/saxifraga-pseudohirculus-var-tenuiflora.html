Saxifraga pseudohirculus var. tenuiflora

Saxifraga pseudohirculus var. tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy. It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family, which includes many species adapted to cold and arid climates.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in rock gardens.
