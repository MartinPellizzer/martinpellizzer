Swida hungarica

Swida hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typical of the willow genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped, smooth on the upper surface, and hairy on the underside.

Swida hungarica is often found in moist soils along riverbanks and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
