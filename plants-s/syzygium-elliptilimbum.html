Syzygium elliptilimbum

Syzygium elliptilimbum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.

The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
