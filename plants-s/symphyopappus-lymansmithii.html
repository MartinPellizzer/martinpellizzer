Symphyopappus lymansmithii

Symphyopappus lymansmithii, commonly known as the Lymansmith's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Symphyopappus lymansmithii is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
