Surenus glabra

Surenus glabra, commonly known as the smooth surenus, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments, with deep root systems to access water.

Surenus glabra is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
