Symplocos microcalyx

Symplocos microcalyx, commonly known as the small-fruited wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its bark is typically smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Symplocos microcalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
