Sisymbrium pinnatum var. xerophilum

Sisymbrium pinnatum var. xerophilum, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and seed pods that split open to release seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has a low, sprawling growth habit with narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife species.
