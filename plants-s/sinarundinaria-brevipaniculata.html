Sinarundinaria brevipaniculata

Sinarundinaria brevipaniculata, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender, tufted culms that rarely exceed 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Sinarundinaria brevipaniculata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
