Stephanomeria virgata var. pleurocarpa

Stephanomeria virgata var. pleurocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf stephanomeria, is a perennial herb native to California.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has linear, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological importance to native pollinators.
