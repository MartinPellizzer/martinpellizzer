Senecio platyphylloides var. seneciphyllinifera

Senecio platyphylloides var. seneciphyllinifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green color. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
