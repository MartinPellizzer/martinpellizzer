Strychnos parvifolia var. acutifolia

Strychnos parvifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devil's backbone, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features elliptic to ovate leaves with acuminate tips, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds with long, slender wings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
