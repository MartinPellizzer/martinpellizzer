Sasa matsushimensis

Sasa matsushimensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or matsushimensis sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a slightly rough texture.

Sasa matsushimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
