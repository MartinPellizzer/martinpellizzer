Senecio pierotii

Senecio pierotii, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and blue or purple in color.

Senecio pierotii is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
