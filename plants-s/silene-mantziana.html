Silene mantziana

Silene mantziana, commonly known as the Greek catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene mantziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
