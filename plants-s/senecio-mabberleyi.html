Senecio mabberleyi

Senecio mabberleyi, commonly known as the Mabberley's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
