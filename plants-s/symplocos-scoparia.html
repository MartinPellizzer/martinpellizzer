Symplocos scoparia

Symplocos scoparia, commonly known as the shrub of the honeysuckle family, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in forested areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often gland-dotted, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red color, attracting various birds and small mammals.

Symplocos scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
