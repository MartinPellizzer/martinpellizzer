Setaria tabulata

Setaria tabulata, commonly known as slender barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to Africa and widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical spikelets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, where it can become a weed. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for crop production.

Setaria tabulata is also notable for its use in ecological studies due to its adaptability and resilience.
