Sterigmapetalum resinosum

Sterigmapetalum resinosum, commonly known as the resinous star-petal plant, is a unique member of the Sterigmapetalaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped petals that are often covered in a waxy, resinous coating. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a sweet, aromatic scent to attract pollinators.

The resinous substance it exudes has been traditionally used by indigenous communities for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
