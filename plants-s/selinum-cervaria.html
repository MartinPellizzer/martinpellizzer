Selinum cervaria

Selinum cervaria, commonly known as groundsel, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Selinum cervaria is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas, and it is considered a weed in some regions.
