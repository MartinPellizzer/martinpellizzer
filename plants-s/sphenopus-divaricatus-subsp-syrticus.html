Sphenopus divaricatus subsp. syrticus

Sphenopus divaricatus subsp.

syrticus, commonly known as the Syrtic thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its essential oils contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine.
