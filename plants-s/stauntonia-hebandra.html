Stauntonia hebandra

Stauntonia hebandra, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth rate and ability to tolerate urban conditions further contribute to its widespread cultivation.
