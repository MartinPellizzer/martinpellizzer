Sorbus aucuparia f. dulcis-laciniata

Sorbus aucuparia f. dulcis-laciniata, commonly known as the sweet-fruited ninebark, is a variant of the common ninebark, characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves and clusters of small, red berries.

The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland edges and moist soils. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens. The berries, while not as sweet as the name suggests, are edible and attract birds, contributing to ecological balance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
