Schoenoplectiella lineolata

Schoenoplectiella lineolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, lake margins, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often lined with distinctive white or pale stripes, giving it its scientific name. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Schoenoplectiella lineolata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
