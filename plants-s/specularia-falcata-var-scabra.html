Specularia falcata var. scabra

Specularia falcata var. scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The leaves are linear, rough to the touch, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun.

This species is often cultivated in rock gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
