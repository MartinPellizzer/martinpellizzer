Sasa bitchuensis

Sasa bitchuensis, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Bitchu bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Sasa bitchuensis is well adapted to moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions.
