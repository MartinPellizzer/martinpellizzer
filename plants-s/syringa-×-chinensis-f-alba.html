Syringa × chinensis f. alba

Syringa × chinensis f. alba, commonly known as the Chinese lilac or white lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark brown berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This ornamental plant is valued for its showy floral display and adaptability to various garden conditions.
