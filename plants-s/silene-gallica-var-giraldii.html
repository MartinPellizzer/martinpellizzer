Silene gallica var. giraldii

Silene gallica var. giraldii, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
