Sopubia trifida var. madagascariensis

Sopubia trifida var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar starfish plant, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a striking, star-shaped inflorescence with pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a short, thick stem with fleshy, paddle-like leaves that are covered in a fine white powder. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its rare and specific habitat, it is considered a protected species in its native region.
