Suaeda platyphylla

Suaeda platyphylla, commonly known as coastal saltbush, is a halophyte native to arid and coastal regions of the Americas.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in drought-prone areas. Its small, yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it reproduces through wind pollination.

Suaeda platyphylla plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing soil erosion.
