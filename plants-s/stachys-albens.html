Stachys albens

Stachys albens, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
