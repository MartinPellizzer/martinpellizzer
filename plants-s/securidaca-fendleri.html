Securidaca fendleri

Securidaca fendleri, commonly known as the desert false indigo, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to survive in drought conditions with deep roots and a waxy coating on its leaves.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
