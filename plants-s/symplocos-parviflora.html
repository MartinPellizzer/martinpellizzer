Symplocos parviflora

Symplocos parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.
