Sisymbrium brevipes

Sisymbrium brevipes, commonly known as the shortfooted broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a taproot system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can be a common weed in agricultural areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can negatively impact the growth of its host plants.
