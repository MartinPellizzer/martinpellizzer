Santolina brevicaulis

Santolina brevicaulis, commonly known as narrowleaf santolina, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage composed of narrow, linear leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina brevicaulis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in aromatherapy due to its essential oils.
