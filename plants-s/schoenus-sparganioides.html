Schoenus sparganioides

Schoenus sparganioides, commonly known as the flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to various regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often found in dense inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable moist environments.

Schoenus sparganioides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
