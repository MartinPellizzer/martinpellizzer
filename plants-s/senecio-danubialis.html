Senecio danubialis

Senecio danubialis, commonly known as the Danube senecio, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

Senecio danubialis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
