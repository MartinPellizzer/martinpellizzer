Styrax martii var. gracilis

Styrax martii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain aromatic, resinous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.
