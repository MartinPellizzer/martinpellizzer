Spiraea kweichowensis

Spiraea kweichowensis, commonly known as the Kweichow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
