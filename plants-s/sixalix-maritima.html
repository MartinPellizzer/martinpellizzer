Sixalix maritima

Sixalix maritima, commonly known as the coastal sedge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions makes it an important component of maritime ecosystems.
