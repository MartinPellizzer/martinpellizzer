Sparaxis tricolor

Sparaxis tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored sparaxis, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink, white, and yellow, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Sparaxis tricolor is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
