Solanum soriae

Solanum soriae, commonly known as the Soria nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with five petals, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

The botanical characteristics of Solanum soriae make it a notable species within its native habitat.
