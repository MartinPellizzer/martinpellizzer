Sarcochilus complanatus

Sarcochilus complanatus, commonly known as the flat-leafed saxifrage orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and a rosette of fleshy, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, erect flowering stem that bears several showy, greenish-white to yellowish flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive spurred lip and are pollinated by insects.

Sarcochilus complanatus is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth.
