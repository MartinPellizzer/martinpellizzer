Spiraea hypericifolia subsp. hypericifolia

Spiraea hypericifolia subsp.

hypericifolia, commonly known as the redleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with finely serrated margins, which turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and seasonal color changes.
