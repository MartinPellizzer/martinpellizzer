Spiranthes chartacea

Spiranthes chartacea, commonly known as the slender spiral orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spiral arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as wetlands and forest floors. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

Spiranthes chartacea is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its unique floral structure and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
