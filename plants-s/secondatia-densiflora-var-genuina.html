Secondatia densiflora var. genuina

Secondatia densiflora var. genuina, commonly known as the dense-flowered secondatia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil in partial to full sun. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The variety 'genuina' is distinguished by its dense flowering and compact growth habit.
