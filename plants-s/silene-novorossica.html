Silene novorossica

Silene novorossica, commonly known as the Novorossiysk catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually opposite and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene novorossica is often found in mountainous areas and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
