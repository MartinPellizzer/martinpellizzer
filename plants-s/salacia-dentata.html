Salacia dentata

Salacia dentata, commonly known as the toothed salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when ripe.

Salacia dentata is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
