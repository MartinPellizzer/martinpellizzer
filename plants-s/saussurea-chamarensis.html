Saussurea chamarensis

Saussurea chamarensis, commonly known as the Himalayan arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Saussurea chamarensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
