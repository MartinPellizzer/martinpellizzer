Silene eremitica

Silene eremitica, commonly known as the desert pink, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces striking pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Silene eremitica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
