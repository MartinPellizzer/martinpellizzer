Specklinia grobyi

Specklinia grobyi, commonly known as the Groby's moss orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant features small, dark green leaves and produces intricate, pale green or white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful preservation.
