Schoenoplectus novae-angliae

Schoenoplectus novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat creation for various aquatic organisms.

Schoenoplectus novae-angliae plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing food and shelter for wildlife.
