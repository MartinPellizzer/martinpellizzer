Solanum runsoriense subsp. runsoriense

Solanum runsoriense subsp.

runsoriense, commonly known as the Ecuadorean nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
