Salix berberifolia var. kimurana

Salix berberifolia var. kimurana, commonly known as the Korean willow, is a variant of the berberifolia willow native to Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender branches and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or red on the underside. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
