Sagina maritima subsp. stricta

Sagina maritima subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to coastal regions. It thrives in sandy, saline soils and is often found in dune environments.

The plant has small, rounded leaves that are typically arranged in a tight rosette. It produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh maritime conditions, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
