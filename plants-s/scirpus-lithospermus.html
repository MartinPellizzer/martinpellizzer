Scirpus lithospermus

Scirpus lithospermus, commonly known as the stone root sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brown in color, and it thrives in shallow water or moist soil.

Scirpus lithospermus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
