Sideritis massoniana var. albida

Sideritis massoniana var. albida, commonly known as white mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves with a grayish underside, and small, pale yellow to white flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal medicine practices.
