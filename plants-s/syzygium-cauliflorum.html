Syzygium cauliflorum

Syzygium cauliflorum, commonly known as the cauliflower apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its unique, clustered inflorescence that resembles cauliflower. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often covered in a waxy coating, and its fruit is a fleshy, red or yellow berry containing numerous seeds. Syzygium cauliflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has limited use in traditional medicine.
