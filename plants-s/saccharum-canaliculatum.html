Saccharum canaliculatum

Saccharum canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems with distinct, narrow ribs. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

While not widely cultivated for commercial purposes, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
