Senecio welwitschii

Senecio welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's groundsel, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted for water storage in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
