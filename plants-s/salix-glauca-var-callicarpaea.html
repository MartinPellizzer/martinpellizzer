Salix glauca var. callicarpaea

Salix glauca var. callicarpaea, commonly known as the white willow with red berries, is a variant of the white willow native to northern regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along streams. The plant is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are grayish-green in color. Its most distinctive feature is the bright red, berry-like fruits that appear in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in riparian ecosystems.
