Stipa dasycnemis

Stipa dasycnemis, commonly known as the hairy needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in cool, dry climates and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Stipa dasycnemis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
