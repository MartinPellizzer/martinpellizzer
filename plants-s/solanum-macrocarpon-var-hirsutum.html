Solanum macrocarpon var. hirsutum

Solanum macrocarpon var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy eggplant, is a variant of the African eggplant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, purple to brown skin and elongated, egg-shaped fruits. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is typically grown for its edible fruits. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while the stems are covered in fine, short hairs.

The hairy eggplant is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional cuisines across its native range.
