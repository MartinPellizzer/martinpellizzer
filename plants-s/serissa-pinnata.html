Serissa pinnata

Serissa pinnata, commonly known as the silver serissa, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has small, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and pinnately arranged. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage.

Serissa pinnata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
