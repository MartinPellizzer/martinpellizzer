Sasa shimidzuana var. asagishiana

Sasa shimidzuana var. asagishiana, commonly known as the Asagishi bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa genus native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
