Salacia cochinchinensis

Salacia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
