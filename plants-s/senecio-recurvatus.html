Senecio recurvatus

Senecio recurvatus, commonly known as the curly dock, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of waxy, blue-green leaves that curl inward, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio recurvatus is a perennial plant that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
