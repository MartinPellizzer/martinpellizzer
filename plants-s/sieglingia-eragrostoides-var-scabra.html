Sieglingia eragrostoides var. scabra

Sieglingia eragrostoides var. scabra, commonly known as rough false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, disturbed soils, and sandy areas. The plant has slender, erect stems with rough, hairy leaves that are often curled or twisted. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is usually inconspicuous.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh environments and its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
