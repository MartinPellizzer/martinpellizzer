Syzygium buxifolium var. verticillatum

Syzygium buxifolium var. verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered clove.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and aromatic, dark red to purple flowers. The leaves are glossy, elliptical, and arranged in whorls, giving the plant its varietal name "verticillatum.". The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
