Salsola barbata

Salsola barbata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and saline environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils. It produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds with plumes, aiding in wind dispersal.

Salsola barbata is often found in coastal areas, roadsides, and agricultural fields.
