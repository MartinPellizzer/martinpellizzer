Salvia verbenaca var. anglica

Salvia verbenaca var. anglica, commonly known as English sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features square, slightly hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a useful medicinal plant and a decorative garden specimen.
