Senecio majus

Senecio majus, commonly known as the giant ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures.

Senecio majus is also known for its toxic properties, making it a plant to avoid in gardens and pastures.
