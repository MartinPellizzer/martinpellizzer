Senecio oligoleucus

Senecio oligoleucus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months.

Senecio oligoleucus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
