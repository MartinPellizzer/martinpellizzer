Solidago cembrani

Solidago cembrani, commonly known as the alpine goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, contributing to the biodiversity of high-altitude ecosystems.

Solidago cembrani is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
