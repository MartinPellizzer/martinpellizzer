Silene acantholimon

Silene acantholimon, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid and rocky environments. It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Silene acantholimon is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
