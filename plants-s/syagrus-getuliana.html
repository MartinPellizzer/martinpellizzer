Syagrus getuliana

Syagrus getuliana, commonly known as the royal palm, is a striking palm native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, bright green leaves that give it a regal appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, edible fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Due to its slow growth and specific environmental needs, it is not commonly cultivated outside its native range.
