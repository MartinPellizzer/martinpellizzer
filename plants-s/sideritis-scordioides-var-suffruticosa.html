Sideritis scordioides var. suffruticosa

Sideritis scordioides var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the woolly sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bushy, low-growing form with hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, with this variety containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.
