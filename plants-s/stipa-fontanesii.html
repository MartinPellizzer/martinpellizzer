Stipa fontanesii

Stipa fontanesii, commonly known as the Fontanesii needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. Stipa fontanesii thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a food source for various grazing animals and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
