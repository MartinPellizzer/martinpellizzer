Salvia chionantha

Salvia chionantha, commonly known as the Christmas sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces striking clusters of pale blue to violet flowers during the winter months, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
