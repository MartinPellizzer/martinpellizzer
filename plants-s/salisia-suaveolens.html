Salisia suaveolens

Salisia suaveolens, commonly known as sweet-scented salisia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salisia suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
