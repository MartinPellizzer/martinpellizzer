Sempervivum brunneifolium

Sempervivum brunneifolium, commonly known as the brown-leaved houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It features dense, rosette-shaped clusters of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The leaves are typically broader and more elongated compared to other sempervivum species, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months.

This hardy plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
