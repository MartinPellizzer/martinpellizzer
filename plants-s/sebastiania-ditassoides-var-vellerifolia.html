Sebastiania ditassoides var. vellerifolia

Sebastiania ditassoides var. vellerifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf sebastiania, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
