Sorghum bicolor var. technicum

Sorghum bicolor var. technicum, commonly known as millet, is a type of grass that is cultivated for its grains.

It is native to Africa and has been adapted to various climates around the world. The plant produces small, hard grains that are rich in carbohydrates and are used in food, animal feed, and biofuel production. Its growth is characterized by a tall, sturdy stalk with narrow leaves.

The plant is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
