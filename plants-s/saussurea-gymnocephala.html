Saussurea gymnocephala

Saussurea gymnocephala, commonly known as the naked-headed safflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow or orange composite flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
