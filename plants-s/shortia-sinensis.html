Shortia sinensis

Shortia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese wintergreen, is a rare and endangered herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats, preferring acidic soils. It is notable for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and protected in several regions.
