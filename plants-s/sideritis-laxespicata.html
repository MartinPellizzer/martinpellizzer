Sideritis laxespicata

Sideritis laxespicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers bloom in whorls along the stems, attracting various pollinators.

Sideritis species are typically used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
