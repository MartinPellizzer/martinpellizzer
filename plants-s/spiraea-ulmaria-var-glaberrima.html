Spiraea ulmaria var. glaberrima

Spiraea ulmaria var. glaberrima, commonly known as white meadow spirea, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant features slender, arching stems with small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine, silvery-white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
