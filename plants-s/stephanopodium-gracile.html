Stephanopodium gracile

Stephanopodium gracile, commonly known as the slender stephanopodium, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Stephanopodium gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
