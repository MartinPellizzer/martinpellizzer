Solms-laubachia flabellata

Solms-laubachia flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often divided into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Solms-laubachia flabellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
