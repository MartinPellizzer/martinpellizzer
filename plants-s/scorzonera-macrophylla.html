Scorzonera macrophylla

Scorzonera macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system, with thick, fleshy roots that are often harvested for their edible qualities. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive central disc.

Scorzonera macrophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
