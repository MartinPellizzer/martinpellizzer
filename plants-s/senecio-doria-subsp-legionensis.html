Senecio doria subsp. legionensis

Senecio doria subsp.

legionensis, commonly known as the Doria's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves.

Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
