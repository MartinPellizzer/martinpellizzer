Symphyotrichum boreale

Symphyotrichum boreale, commonly known as the northern heath aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. The plant features a woody stem and produces clusters of small, usually purple or pink, composite flowers in the fall. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a hairy texture.

Symphyotrichum boreale is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its characteristic composite flower heads.
