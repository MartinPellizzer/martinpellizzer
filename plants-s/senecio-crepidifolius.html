Senecio crepidifolius

Senecio crepidifolius, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming throughout the year in some regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive crinkled appearance.

Senecio crepidifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
