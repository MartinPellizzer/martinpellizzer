Selaginella rupestris var. tropica

Selaginella rupestris var. tropica, commonly known as the tropical spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or shaded soils. The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around its branching stems. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
