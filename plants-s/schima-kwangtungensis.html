Schima kwangtungensis

Schima kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong schima, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Schima kwangtungensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to subtropical climates.
