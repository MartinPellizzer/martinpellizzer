Solanum michoacanum

Solanum michoacanum, commonly known as the Michoacan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
