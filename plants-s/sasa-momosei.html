Sasa momosei

Sasa momosei, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a soft, feathery texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional landscaping and basketry.
