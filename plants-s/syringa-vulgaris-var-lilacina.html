Syringa vulgaris var. lilacina

Syringa vulgaris var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple in the spring. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Syringa vulgaris var. lilacina is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
