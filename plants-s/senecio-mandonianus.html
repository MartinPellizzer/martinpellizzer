Senecio mandonianus

Senecio mandonianus, commonly known as the Mandon's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
