Swartzia amazonica var. amazonica

Swartzia amazonica var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, contributing to its rarity in the wild.
