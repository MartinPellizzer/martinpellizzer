Salvia funerea

Salvia funerea, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking deep blue to violet flower clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia funerea is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
