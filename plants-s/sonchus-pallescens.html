Sonchus pallescens

Sonchus pallescens, commonly known as the pale dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a surrounding pappus of bristles.

Sonchus pallescens is an annual herb that produces a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
