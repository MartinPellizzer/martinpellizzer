Sorbus franconica f. oxylopha

Sorbus franconica f. oxylopha, commonly known as the Franconian whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. Its fruit is a small, aggregate berry that ripens to a dark red or black color.

It is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
