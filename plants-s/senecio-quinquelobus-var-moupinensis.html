Senecio quinquelobus var. moupinensis

Senecio quinquelobus var. moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
