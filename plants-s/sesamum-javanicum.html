Sesamum javanicum

Sesamum javanicum, commonly known as Java sesame, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, oblong seeds enclosed in a mucilaginous layer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, often covered with fine hairs.

Sesamum javanicum is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
