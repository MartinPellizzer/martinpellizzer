Schizachyrium condensatum subvar. elongatum

Schizachyrium condensatum subvar. elongatum, commonly known as tall switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a hardy species.

Due to its drought tolerance and ecological benefits, it is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
