Sagenia alata

Sagenia alata, commonly known as the winged sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Sagenia alata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular plant among gardeners.
