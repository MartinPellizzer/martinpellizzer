Saussurea subcordata

Saussurea subcordata, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or the Indian saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas. The plant features a short, thick stem with few leaves that are often basal and clasping. Its most distinctive feature is its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in certain regions.
