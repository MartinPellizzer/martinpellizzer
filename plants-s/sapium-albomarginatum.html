Sapium albomarginatum

Sapium albomarginatum, commonly known as the white-margin sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often tinged with white margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
