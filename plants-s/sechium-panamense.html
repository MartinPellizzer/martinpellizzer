Sechium panamense

Sechium panamense, commonly known as the Panamanian bitter gourd, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces elongated, spiny fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and tend to climb or trail along the ground.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in local cuisines.
