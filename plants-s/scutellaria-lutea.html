Scutellaria lutea

Scutellaria lutea, commonly known as yellow skullcap, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Scutellaria lutea is often found in temperate regions and is noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
