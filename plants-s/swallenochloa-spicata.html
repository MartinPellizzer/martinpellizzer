Swallenochloa spicata

Swallenochloa spicata, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Swallenochloa spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
