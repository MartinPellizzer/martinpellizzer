Scutellaria petiolata

Scutellaria petiolata, commonly known as blue beadroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria petiolata typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
