Silene mexicana

Silene mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.
