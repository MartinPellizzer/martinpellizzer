Sherbournia hapalophylla var. wernhamiana

Sherbournia hapalophylla var. wernhamiana, commonly known as the Wernham's rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
