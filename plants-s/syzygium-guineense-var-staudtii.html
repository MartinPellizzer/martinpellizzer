Syzygium guineense var. staudtii

Syzygium guineense var. staudtii, commonly known as the African cherry or *Prunus africana*, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

The wood of this species is highly prized for its durability and workability in carpentry.
