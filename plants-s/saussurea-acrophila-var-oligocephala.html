Saussurea acrophila var. oligocephala

Saussurea acrophila var. oligocephala, commonly known as the alpine safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
