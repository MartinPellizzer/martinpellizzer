Spondias laxiflora

Spondias laxiflora, commonly known as the red mombin, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright orange to red fruits. Spondias laxiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
