Secale cereale var. dighoricum

Secale cereale var. dighoricum, commonly known as the Dighori rye, is a variant of rye that originates from the Dighori region in Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and adaptability to temperate climates. It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed seeds. The plant is known for its resilience to poor soil conditions and cold temperatures.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential for agricultural use and its role in genetic studies of rye species.
