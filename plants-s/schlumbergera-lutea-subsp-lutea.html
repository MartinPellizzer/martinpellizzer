Schlumbergera lutea subsp. lutea

Schlumbergera lutea subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow Christmas cactus, is a popular epiphytic cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that branch out into flattened, leaf-like structures.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the holiday season, typically in winter. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Regular watering and proper temperature conditions are essential for its growth and blooming.
