Schizostachyum andamanicum

Schizostachyum andamanicum, commonly known as the Andaman bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant is well adapted to coastal and tropical environments, often growing in sandy or saline soils. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is also cultivated in ornamental gardens.
