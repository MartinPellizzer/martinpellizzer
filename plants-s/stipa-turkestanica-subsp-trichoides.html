Stipa turkestanica subsp. trichoides

Stipa turkestanica subsp.

trichoides, commonly known as the hairy feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often hairy, giving it its common name.

This subspecies is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
