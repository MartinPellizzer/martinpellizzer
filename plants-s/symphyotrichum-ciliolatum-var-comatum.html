Symphyotrichum ciliolatum var. comatum

Symphyotrichum ciliolatum var. comatum, commonly known as the eastern blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a serrated margin with cilia along the edges.

The flowers are small, blue to purple, and appear in dense, fluffy clusters, giving it a mist-like appearance.
