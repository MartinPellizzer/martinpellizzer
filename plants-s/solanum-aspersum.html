Solanum aspersum

Solanum aspersum, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, green, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, and it produces berries that start green and turn red when ripe.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its toxic properties.
