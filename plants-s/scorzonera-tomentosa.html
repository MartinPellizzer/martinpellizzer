Scorzonera tomentosa

Scorzonera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy root system that is often harvested for its edible qualities. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are hairy and basal, forming a rosette near the ground.

Scorzonera tomentosa is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
