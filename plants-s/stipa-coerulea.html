Stipa coerulea

Stipa coerulea, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common species in grasslands and prairies.

Stipa coerulea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
