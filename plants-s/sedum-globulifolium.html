Sedum globulifolium

Sedum globulifolium, commonly known as the globe-leaf sedum, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy leaves that form a dense, globular shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
