Solanum heterophyllum

Solanum heterophyllum, commonly known as the purple potato or wild potato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks.

Due to its potential medicinal properties, it has been studied for its bioactive compounds.
