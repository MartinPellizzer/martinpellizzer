Sporobolus variegatus

Sporobolus variegatus, commonly known as the variable spikerush, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, often variegated foliage that ranges from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Sporobolus variegatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
