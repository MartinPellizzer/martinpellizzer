Stelis sagittata

Stelis sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Stelis sagittata thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light.
