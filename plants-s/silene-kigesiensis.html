Silene kigesiensis

Silene kigesiensis, commonly known as the Kigesi campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Silene kigesiensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
