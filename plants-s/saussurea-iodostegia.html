Saussurea iodostegia

Saussurea iodostegia, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or the Indian saffron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellowish to orange-colored flowers. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
