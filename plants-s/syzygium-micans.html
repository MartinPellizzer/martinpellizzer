Syzygium micans

Syzygium micans, commonly known as the fragrant satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature.

Syzygium micans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
