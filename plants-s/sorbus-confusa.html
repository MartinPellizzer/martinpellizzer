Sorbus confusa

Sorbus confusa, commonly known as the confused whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and China. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant colors in the fall.

Sorbus confusa is often found in rocky or forested habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
