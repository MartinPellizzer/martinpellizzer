Senecio lyallii

Senecio lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and are followed by distinctive seed heads that resemble small, spiky balls.

Senecio lyallii is often found in open habitats and is adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its native environment.
