Stanhopeastrum ecornutum

Stanhopeastrum ecornutum, commonly known as the white-flowered stanhopea, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow-green lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
