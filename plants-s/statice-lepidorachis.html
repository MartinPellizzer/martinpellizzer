Statice lepidorachis

Statice lepidorachis, commonly known as the silverleaf statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and showy, spike-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Statice lepidorachis is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate appearance and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
