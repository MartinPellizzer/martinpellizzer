Sayeria palawensis

Sayeria palawensis, commonly known as the Palau cycad, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the island nation of Palau.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant is adapted to the tropical climate of Palau, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, Sayeria palawensis is critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
