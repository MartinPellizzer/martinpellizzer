Staphylea bumalda var. stenophylla

Staphylea bumalda var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf staphylea, is a variant of the European staphylea species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula, characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible, sweet, and juicy berries that are often consumed fresh or used in jams. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
