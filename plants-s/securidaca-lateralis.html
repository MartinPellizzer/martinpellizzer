Securidaca lateralis

Securidaca lateralis, commonly known as the African senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with elongated, slightly curved leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a slender, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological value as a habitat for various insects and birds.
