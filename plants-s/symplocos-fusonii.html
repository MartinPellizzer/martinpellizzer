Symplocos fusonii

Symplocos fusonii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Symplocos fusonii is valued for its ornamental berries and is often used in gardens for its visual appeal.
