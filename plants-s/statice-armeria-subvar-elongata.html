Statice armeria subvar. elongata

Statice armeria subvar. elongata, commonly known as sea lavender or pink silk.

It is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its slender, elongated stems and dense clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant is typically found in saline or sandy soils, thriving in coastal environments where it tolerates salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and delicate appearance.
