Salix padifolia

Salix padifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered with a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction, with separate male and female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

Salix padifolia is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
