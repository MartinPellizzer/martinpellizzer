Scrophularia scopolii var. parryi

Scrophularia scopolii var. parryi, commonly known as Parry's dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
