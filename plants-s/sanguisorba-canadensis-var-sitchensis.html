Sanguisorba canadensis var. sitchensis

Sanguisorba canadensis var. sitchensis, commonly known as Sitka sorrel, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, reddish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are often reddish underneath.

This species is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
