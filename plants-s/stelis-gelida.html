Stelis gelida

Stelis gelida, commonly known as the cold orchid, is a species of orchid native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires high levels of light for optimal growth. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve moisture.

Stelis gelida is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
