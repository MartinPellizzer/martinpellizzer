Stellaria media var. glabella

Stellaria media var. glabella, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. The leaves are usually hairy and oval-shaped, with a distinctive glandular texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through seed dispersal and is considered a weed in many regions.
