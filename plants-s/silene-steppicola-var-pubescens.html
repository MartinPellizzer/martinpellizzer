Silene steppicola var. pubescens

Silene steppicola var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent steppe campion, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, pubescent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers in racemes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Silene steppicola var. pubescens is often found in open grasslands and is a valuable component of steppe ecosystems.
