Specklinia displosa

Specklinia displosa, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high altitudes of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale green flowers with distinctive, narrow petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity, making it a challenging species to cultivate outside its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Specklinia displosa is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
