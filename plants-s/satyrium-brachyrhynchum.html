Satyrium brachyrhynchum

Satyrium brachyrhynchum, commonly known as the narrow-beaked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and have a distinctive, narrow beak-like structure.

It is characterized by its specialized root system and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.
