Scutellaria brevibracteata

Scutellaria brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bracted skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a distinctive square stem with opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its genus, Scutellaria, is characterized by a unique structure called the scutellum, which is a hood-like covering over the flower.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
