Stipa perplexa

Stipa perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps reduce water loss.

Stipa perplexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens for its hardy nature.
