Scirpus eriophorum var. laxus

Scirpus eriophorum var. laxus, commonly known as the soft bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikes of small, brownish flowers. This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its fibrous stems are used in basket weaving and as a natural water filter.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
