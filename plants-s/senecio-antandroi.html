Senecio antandroi

Senecio antandroi, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
