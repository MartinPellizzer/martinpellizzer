Selaginella achotalensis

Selaginella achotalensis, commonly known as the Andean spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a rosette of spirally arranged leaves that are lanceolate in shape and have a distinctive midrib. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its stems.

Selaginella achotalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
