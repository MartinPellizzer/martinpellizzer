Senecio melastomifolius var. microphyllus

Senecio melastomifolius var. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf leaf senecio, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
