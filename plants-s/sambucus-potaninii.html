Sambucus potaninii

Sambucus potaninii, commonly known as the Siberian elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Siberia and northern China.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Sambucus potaninii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
