Sasaella reikoana

Sasaella reikoana, commonly known as the Reiko's sasa, is a unique grass-like plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
