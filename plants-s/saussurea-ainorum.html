Saussurea ainorum

Saussurea ainorum, commonly known as the Himalayan saffron or the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters, where it thrives in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to the alpine landscape.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
