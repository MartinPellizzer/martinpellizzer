Sarcocephalus latifolius

Sarcocephalus latifolius, commonly known as the African mountain sage, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional African medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
