Solanum pruinosum var. pilosulum

Solanum pruinosum var. pilosulum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ornamental and cautionary value.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
