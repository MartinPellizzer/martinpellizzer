Senecio doronicum subsp. doronicum

Senecio doronicum subsp.

doronicum, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features a woody, branched stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
