Salix shihtsuanensis var. globosa

Salix shihtsuanensis var. globosa, commonly known as the globular willow, is a rare and unique variant of the willow family.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular growth habit, with dense, rounded clusters of branches that form a spherical shape. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a soft, fluffy appearance. It is native to China and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain valleys and stream banks.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
