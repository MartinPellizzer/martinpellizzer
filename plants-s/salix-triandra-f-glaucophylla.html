Salix triandra f. glaucophylla

Salix triandra f. glaucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf willow, is a variant of the weeping willow characterized by its distinctive silvery-white foliage.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas. The plant features long, slender branches that droop gracefully, creating a cascading effect. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a soft, bluish-white underside that gives it its unique appearance.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and along waterways.
