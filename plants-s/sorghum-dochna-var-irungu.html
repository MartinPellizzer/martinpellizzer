Sorghum dochna var. irungu

Sorghum dochna var. irungu, commonly known as the Irungu millet, is a small-grained cereal plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its compact stature and produces small, hard seeds that are typically harvested when fully dry. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a valuable crop in arid regions. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a short, upright stem.

The seeds are rich in nutrients and are used for both human consumption and animal feed.
