Silene rotundifolia

Silene rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with hairy stems and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Silene rotundifolia is a perennial species that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with well-drained soil.
