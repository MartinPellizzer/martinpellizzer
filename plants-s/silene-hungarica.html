Silene hungarica

Silene hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Silene hungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
