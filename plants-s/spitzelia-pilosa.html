Spitzelia pilosa

Spitzelia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy spitzelia, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet at the tip of each stem.

Spitzelia pilosa is often found in moist, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.
