Stipa calamagrostis

Stipa calamagrostis, commonly known as the purple jointgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like clusters of purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, dunes, and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa calamagrostis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
