Sagenia angustior

Sagenia angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sagenia angustior is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
