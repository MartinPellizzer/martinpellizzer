Saxifraga dahaiensis

Saxifraga dahaiensis, commonly known as the Dahai saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually green with a waxy surface, and it produces white or pinkish flowers during the summer months.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
