Solanum pubescens var. lobatum

Solanum pubescens var. lobatum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
