Santolina orocarpetana

Santolina orocarpetana, commonly known as the Canary Island santolina, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub with compact, silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters in the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina orocarpetana is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
