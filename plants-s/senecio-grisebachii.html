Senecio grisebachii

Senecio grisebachii, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

Senecio grisebachii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
