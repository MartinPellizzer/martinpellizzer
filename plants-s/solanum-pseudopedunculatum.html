Solanum pseudopedunculatum

Solanum pseudopedunculatum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its fruits are small, berry-like, and contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is advised due to its potential toxicity.
