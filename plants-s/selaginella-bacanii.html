Selaginella bacanii

Selaginella bacanii, commonly known as the Bacan Island spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features dichotomous branching and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spore-producing organs.

Selaginella bacanii is notable for its ability to survive in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.
