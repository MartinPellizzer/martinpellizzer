Sorbus kachinensis

Sorbus kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Myanmar, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and bright red berries that appear in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with serrated edges.

The Kachin rowan is often found in secondary forests and along streambanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
