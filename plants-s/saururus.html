Saururus

Saururus, commonly known as sweetroot or Indian turnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Saururaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, tuberous roots and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Saururus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
