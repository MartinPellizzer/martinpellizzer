Spiraea pulchella

Spiraea pulchella, commonly known as rose willow or white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Spiraea pulchella thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
