Scirpus canaliculatotriqueter

Scirpus canaliculatotriqueter, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with a ribbed texture and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have distinct, shallow grooves along their surfaces.

Scirpus canaliculatotriqueter plays an important ecological role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
