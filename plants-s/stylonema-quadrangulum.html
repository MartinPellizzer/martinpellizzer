Stylonema quadrangulum

Stylonema quadrangulum, commonly known as the four-angled grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant has distinctive four-sided stems that are usually hairy and can grow up to 60 centimeters in height. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Stylonema quadrangulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
