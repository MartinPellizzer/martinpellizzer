Schizanthus carlomunozii var. dilutimaculatus

Schizanthus carlomunozii var. dilutimaculatus, commonly known as the speckled blueflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range from blue to purple. The varietal name 'dilutimaculatus' refers to its distinctive speckled or mottled patterns on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
