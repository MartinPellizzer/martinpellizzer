Senecio capuronii

Senecio capuronii, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
