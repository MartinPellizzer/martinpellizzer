Saussurea saichanensis

Saussurea saichanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue to white, and the plant produces small, achene-like seeds.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
