Sonerila maculata

Sonerila maculata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often variegated foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Sonerila maculata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
