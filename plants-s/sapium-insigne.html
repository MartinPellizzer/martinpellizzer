Sapium insigne

Sapium insigne, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy red flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Sapium insigne is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
