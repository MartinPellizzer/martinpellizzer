Saccharodendron leucoderme

Saccharodendron leucoderme, commonly known as the white-leaf sugar tree, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are predominantly white in color, contrasting with its dark bark. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible fruits with a sweet, syrupy pulp. Its roots contain a high concentration of natural sugars, making it valuable for both traditional and commercial use.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is now considered a rare and endangered species.
