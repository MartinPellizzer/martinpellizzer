Solanum cymbalariifolium

Solanum cymbalariifolium, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, and its stems are erect and branched.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, smooth seeds.
