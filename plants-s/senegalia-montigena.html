Senegalia montigena

Senegalia montigena, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and has deep roots that allow it to thrive in poor soil.

Senegalia montigena is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of fodder for livestock.
