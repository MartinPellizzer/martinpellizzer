Spilanthes ovata

Spilanthes ovata, commonly known as the toothache plant, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant is known for its pungent odor and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported ability to alleviate toothache and other ailments. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Spilanthes ovata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
