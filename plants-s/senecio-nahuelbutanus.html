Senecio nahuelbutanus

Senecio nahuelbutanus, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
