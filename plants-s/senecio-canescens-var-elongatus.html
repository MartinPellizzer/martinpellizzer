Senecio canescens var. elongatus

Senecio canescens var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically linear and hairy, giving the plant a distinctive, somewhat rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
