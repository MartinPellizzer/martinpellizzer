Stevia baccharifolia

Stevia baccharifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a shrub native to Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

Stevia baccharifolia is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and potential health benefits.
