Schleidenia polyphylla var. blanchetii

Schleidenia polyphylla var. blanchetii, commonly known as the Blanchet's featherplant, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with whorled leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
