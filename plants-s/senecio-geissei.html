Senecio geissei

Senecio geissei, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or *Physalis peruviana*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny calyces that encase the fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange or red, berry-like fruits. Senecio geissei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
