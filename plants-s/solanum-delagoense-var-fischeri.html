Solanum delagoense var. fischeri

Solanum delagoense var. fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's wild tomato, is a perennial shrub native to parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
