Serratula montana

Serratula montana, commonly known as mountain sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have a slightly bitter taste, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Serratula montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
