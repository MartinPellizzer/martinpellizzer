Sarcocephalus dasyphyllus

Sarcocephalus dasyphyllus, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
