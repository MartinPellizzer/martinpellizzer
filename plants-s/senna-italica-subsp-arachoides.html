Senna italica subsp. arachoides

Senna italica subsp.

arachoides, commonly known as the Italian senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Europe and the Middle East. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
