Senecio voigtii

Senecio voigtii, commonly known as the Voigt's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Senecio voigtii produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
