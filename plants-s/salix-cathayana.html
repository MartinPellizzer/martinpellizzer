Salix cathayana

Salix cathayana, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a graceful, cascading appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are elongated, thin, and have a smooth, bright green surface.

Salix cathayana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
