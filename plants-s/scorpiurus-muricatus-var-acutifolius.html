Scorpiurus muricatus var. acutifolius

Scorpiurus muricatus var. acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved scorpiurus, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and often forms dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
