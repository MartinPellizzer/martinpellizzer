Serratula pinnatifida

Serratula pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sickleweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, pinnately divided leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

Serratula pinnatifida is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
