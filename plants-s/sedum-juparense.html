Sedum juparense

Sedum juparense, commonly known as the Brazilian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum juparense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
