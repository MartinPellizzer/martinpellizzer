Sasa kakudensis

Sasa kakudensis, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Sasa kakudensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese gardens.
