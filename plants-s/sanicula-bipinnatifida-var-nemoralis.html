Sanicula bipinnatifida var. nemoralis

Sanicula bipinnatifida var. nemoralis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sanicle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the woodlands of eastern North America.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, bipinnately divided leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rich, deciduous forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
