Stellaria nemorum lusus amurensis

Stellaria nemorum lusus amurensis, commonly known as the Amur starwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are prostrate and often root at the nodes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
