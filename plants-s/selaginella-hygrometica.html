Selaginella hygrometica

Selaginella hygrometica, commonly known as the moisture-loving spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in humid environments.

It is a vascular plant with a distinctive morphology, featuring small, scale-like leaves and a creeping stem that produces sporangia. This plant is often found in shaded, moist areas such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its life cycle involves alternation of generations, with a gametophyte stage that is photosynthetic.

Selaginella hygrometica plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
