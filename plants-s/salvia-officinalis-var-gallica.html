Salvia officinalis var. gallica

Salvia officinalis var. gallica, commonly known as French lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in dense, fragrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its essential oils, rich in linalool and lavandin, contribute to its widespread use in aromatherapy and natural remedies.
