Sulcorebutia slabana

Sulcorebutia slabana, commonly known as the Slabana stone cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green or blue-green foliage. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
