Sida echinocarpa

Sida echinocarpa, commonly known as prickly sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic with a rough texture and small spines along the edges.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
