Sebastiania corniculata f. fallax

Sebastiania corniculata f. fallax, commonly known as the variegated croton, is a hybrid form of the species Sebastiania corniculata.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with green, white, and yellow patterns. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The variegation in its leaves makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
