Salvia lupinoides

Salvia lupinoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia lupinoides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
