Stylophorum lasiocarpum

Stylophorum lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly false spurge, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the eastern United States.

Stylophorum lasiocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
