Sorghum roxburghii var. jucundum

Sorghum roxburghii var. jucundum, commonly known as sweet sorghum, is a tall, warm-season grass native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust stalks and large, drooping panicles of pale green to golden flowers. The plant is notable for its high sugar content in the stalks, making it a valuable resource for biofuel and syrup production. Its leaves are broad and serrated, while the roots are deep and fibrous, contributing to its resilience in poor soil conditions.

It thrives in full sun and requires moderate to high water availability for optimal growth.
