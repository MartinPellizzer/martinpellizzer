Serjania heterocarpa

Serjania heterocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, leathery leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its density and rich color, making it important in both traditional and modern carpentry.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
