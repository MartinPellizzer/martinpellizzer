Salix coerulescens

Salix coerulescens, commonly known as the blue willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are a striking bluish-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it blooms in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely available.
