Silene multicaulis subsp. multicaulis

Silene multicaulis subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the purple-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often tinged with purple, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically pink to purple in color.

The species is characterized by its numerous stamens and a distinctive, hairy capsule that contains the seeds.
