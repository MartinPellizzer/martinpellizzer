Stevia carapariensis

Stevia carapariensis, commonly known as the Caribbean stevia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and serrated leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its leaves, which contain natural sweeteners called steviol glycosides. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Due to its medicinal and potential commercial uses, it has gained interest in herbal and alternative medicine practices.
