Solanum tarapotense

Solanum tarapotense, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it bears berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and the plant has a robust root system adapted to the harsh mountain environment.

Solanum tarapotense is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes many economically important plants.
