Sambucus mexicana f. caudata

Sambucus mexicana f. caudata, commonly known as the Mexican elderberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible when ripe, though they are typically consumed in processed forms. Its leaves are compound, with three to five ovate leaflets, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
