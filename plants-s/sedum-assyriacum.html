Sedum assyriacum

Sedum assyriacum, commonly known as the Assyrian stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It typically blooms in summer and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Sedum assyriacum is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
