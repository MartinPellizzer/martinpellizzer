Solidago virgaurea var. paludosa

Solidago virgaurea var. paludosa, commonly known as the marsh goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear numerous yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to its wetland environment.
