Stipagrostis libyca subsp. libyca

Stipagrostis libyca subsp.

libyca, commonly known as the Libyan arrowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in desert ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the vegetation in its native habitat.
