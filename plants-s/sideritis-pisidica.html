Sideritis pisidica

Sideritis pisidica, commonly known as the Crimean ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or violet, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

Sideritis pisidica is valued for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies for its calming and antispasmodic effects.
