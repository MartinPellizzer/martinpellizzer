Sieglingia cunninghamii

Sieglingia cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's sieve-bush, is a small shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Sieglingia cunninghamii is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience to environmental stressors.
