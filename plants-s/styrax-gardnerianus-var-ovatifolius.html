Styrax gardnerianus var. ovatifolius

Styrax gardnerianus var. ovatifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its ovate, glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its botanical features include smooth, grayish bark and a tendency to form dense, rounded growth habits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
