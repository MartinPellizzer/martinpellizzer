Stizolobium scarabaeoides

Stizolobium scarabaeoides, commonly known as the scarab beetle acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and arranged in a distinctive, feathery pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is also known for its role in supporting local insect populations, including the scarab beetles for which it is named.

The plant has been used traditionally in some regions for its medicinal properties.
