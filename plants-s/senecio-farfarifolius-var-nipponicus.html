Senecio farfarifolius var. nipponicus

Senecio farfarifolius var. nipponicus, commonly known as the Japanese goatweed, is a variant of the Senecio genus native to Japan.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it distinctive, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
