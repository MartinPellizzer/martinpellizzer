Stephanomeria wheeleri

Stephanomeria wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and gray-green leaves. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
