Sebaea grandis

Sebaea grandis, commonly known as the giant sebaea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, alternate leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Sebaea grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique appearance.
