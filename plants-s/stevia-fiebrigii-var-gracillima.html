Stevia fiebrigii var. gracillima

Stevia fiebrigii var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its sweet properties, it is often used as a natural alternative to sugar in culinary applications.
