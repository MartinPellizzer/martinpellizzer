Spiraea flabellata

Spiraea flabellata, commonly known as the flatleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea flabellata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
