Silene kermanensis

Silene kermanensis, commonly known as the Kerman catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually arranged in dense clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Silene kermanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
