Scabiosa affinis

Scabiosa affinis, commonly known as the common scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa affinis is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
