Salvia carnosa subsp. typica

Salvia carnosa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the typical salvia carnosa, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically violet to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, often used in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
