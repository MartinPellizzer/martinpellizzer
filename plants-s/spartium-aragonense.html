Spartium aragonense

Spartium aragonense, commonly known as Aragon thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss, while its flowers attract various pollinators.

Spartium aragonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
