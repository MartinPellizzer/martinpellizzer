Symplocos laeteviridis var. alabensis

Symplocos laeteviridis var. alabensis, commonly known as the Alabans greenleaf waxtree, is a variant of the symplocos genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
