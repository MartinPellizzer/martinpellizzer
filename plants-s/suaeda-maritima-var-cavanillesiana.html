Suaeda maritima var. cavanillesiana

Suaeda maritima var. cavanillesiana, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and estuaries, where it tolerates high salinity levels. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, aiding in survival in arid conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is important for stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
