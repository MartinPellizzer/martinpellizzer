Syzygium dhaneshianum

Syzygium dhaneshianum, commonly known as the Dhaneshia apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like berries. The fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.
