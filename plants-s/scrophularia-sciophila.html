Scrophularia sciophila

Scrophularia sciophila, commonly known as the shade-loving scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in woodland or shaded environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, and it produces spikes of pale purple to pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia sciophila is often found in rocky or woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in shady garden settings.
