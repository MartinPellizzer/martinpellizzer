Sisyrinchium chilense var. urubambense

Sisyrinchium chilense var. urubambense, commonly known as the Urubambense blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the high Andean regions of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
