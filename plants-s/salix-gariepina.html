Salix gariepina

Salix gariepina, commonly known as the Gariep willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and streams.

Salix gariepina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
