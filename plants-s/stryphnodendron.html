Stryphnodendron

Stryphnodendron, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and for the Brazil nuts it yields. Its ecological importance is significant, as it supports a wide range of wildlife.
