Sisymbrium eckartsbergense

Sisymbrium eckartsbergense, commonly known as the Eckartsberg mustard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a characteristic mustard scent.

The seeds are small and are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
