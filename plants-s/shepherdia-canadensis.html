Shepherdia canadensis

Shepherdia canadensis, commonly known as American hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a prickly husk, which ripen in the fall. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

Shepherdia canadensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
