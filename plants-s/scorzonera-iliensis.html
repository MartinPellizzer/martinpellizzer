Scorzonera iliensis

Scorzonera iliensis, commonly known as the Ili scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates.

Scorzonera iliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
