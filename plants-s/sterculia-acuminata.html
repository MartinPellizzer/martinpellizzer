Sterculia acuminata

Sterculia acuminata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
