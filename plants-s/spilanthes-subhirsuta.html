Spilanthes subhirsuta

Spilanthes subhirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pungent bladder, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the genus Spilanthes within the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its strong, pungent odor, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
