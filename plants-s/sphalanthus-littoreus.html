Sphalanthus littoreus

Sphalanthus littoreus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

Sphalanthus littoreus is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
