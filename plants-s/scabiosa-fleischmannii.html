Scabiosa fleischmannii

Scabiosa fleischmannii, commonly known as the Fleischmann's scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Scabiosa fleischmannii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
