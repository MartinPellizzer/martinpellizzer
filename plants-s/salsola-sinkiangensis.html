Salsola sinkiangensis

Salsola sinkiangensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spine-tipped leaves and pink or purple flowers, which are typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its extensive root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in desert environments.

Salsola sinkiangensis is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
