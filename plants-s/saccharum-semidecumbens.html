Saccharum semidecumbens

Saccharum semidecumbens, commonly known as giant cane, is a tall, robust grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and robust, often sprawling stems. This plant is known for its high sugar content and is cultivated for its potential in biofuel production. It thrives in wetland environments and can grow up to several meters in height.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both agricultural and ecological studies.
