Scirpus maritimus f. sarmaticus

Scirpus maritimus f. sarmaticus, commonly known as the Sarmatian bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush native to coastal and brackish wetlands in Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands in moist soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and wetland organisms.
