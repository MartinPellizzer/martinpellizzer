Sebastiania brasiliensis var. foveata

Sebastiania brasiliensis var. foveata, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of forests. The plant is characterized by its striking, clustered, dark purple to black berries that appear in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy surface and a mildly aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruiting display.
