Stipa jucunda

Stipa jucunda, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it conserve water in arid environments.

Stipa jucunda is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and rocky slopes.
