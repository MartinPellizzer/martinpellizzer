Scindapsus erectus

Scindapsus erectus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Scindapsus erectus is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
