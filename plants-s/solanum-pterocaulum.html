Solanum pterocaulum

Solanum pterocaulum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided by livestock and humans.
