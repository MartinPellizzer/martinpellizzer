Sorbus arvernensis

Sorbus arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernes rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Sorbus arvernensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
