Solanum barbadense

Solanum barbadense, commonly known as the cigar plant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange. Its fruits, which are berries, contain the seeds used to produce tobacco.

The plant has been historically cultivated for its economic and medicinal properties.
