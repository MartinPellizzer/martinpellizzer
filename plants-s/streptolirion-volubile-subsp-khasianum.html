Streptolirion volubile subsp. khasianum

Streptolirion volubile subsp.

khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi climbing bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India. It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and cultivated landscapes.
