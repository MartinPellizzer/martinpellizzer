Sapindus oligophyllus

Sapindus oligophyllus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces smooth, spherical fruits that turn red when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a starchy, soap-like pulp. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with shallow fissures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
