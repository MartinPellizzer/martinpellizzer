Sedum magellense

Sedum magellense, commonly known as the Magellanic sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, particularly in coastal areas.

Its ability to survive in harsh environments makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
