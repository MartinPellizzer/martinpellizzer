Stadtmannia pubescens

Stadtmannia pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Stadtmannia pubescens thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swampy areas and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
