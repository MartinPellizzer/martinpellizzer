Senecio cleistolepis

Senecio cleistolepis, commonly known as the mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

Senecio cleistolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
