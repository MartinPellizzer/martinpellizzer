Santolina chamaecyparissus var. corsica

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring, which are borne in clusters atop the foliage. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly fuzzy texture, and it is highly drought-tolerant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
