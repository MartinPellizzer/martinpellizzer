Scutellaria brevibracteata subsp. brevibracteata

Scutellaria brevibracteata subsp.

brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bract scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
