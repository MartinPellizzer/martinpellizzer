Salvia sylvestris var. valentina

Salvia sylvestris var. valentina, commonly known as the Valentine sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and emit a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early summer blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
