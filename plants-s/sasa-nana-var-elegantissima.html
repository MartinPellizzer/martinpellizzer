Sasa nana var. elegantissima

Sasa nana var. elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, dark green culms that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of 1 to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a delicate, ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its slow growth rate makes it an ideal choice for small spaces and container gardening.
