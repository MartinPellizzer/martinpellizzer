Sagina apetala var. pallens

Sagina apetala var. pallens, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense mat-forming growth habit and small, silver-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Sagina apetala var. pallens is often found in meadows, stream banks, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
