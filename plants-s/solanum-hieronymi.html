Solanum hieronymi

Solanum hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, lobed leaves, and showy, violet-colored flowers. It produces small, round berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
