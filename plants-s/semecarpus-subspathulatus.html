Semecarpus subspathulatus

Semecarpus subspathulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hippocastanaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spiny, woody fruits containing single seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
