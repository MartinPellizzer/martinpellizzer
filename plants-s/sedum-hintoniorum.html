Sedum hintoniorum

Sedum hintoniorum, commonly known as Hinton's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
