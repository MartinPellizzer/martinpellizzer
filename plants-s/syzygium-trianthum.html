Syzygium trianthum

Syzygium trianthum, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or orange when ripe. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in traditional cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
