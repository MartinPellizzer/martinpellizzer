Solanum hjertingii var. physaloides

Solanum hjertingii var. physaloides, commonly known as the Physaloides nightshade, is a rare and unique variant of the Solanum genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, greenish-yellow berries that resemble small gourds, a feature that sets it apart from other nightshade species. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions such as thick, waxy leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Despite its ornamental appearance, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
