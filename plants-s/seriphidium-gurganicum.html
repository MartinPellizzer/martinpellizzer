Seriphidium gurganicum

Seriphidium gurganicum, commonly known as the Gurganica wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, exhibiting a low-growing, herbaceous habit. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

Seriphidium gurganicum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
