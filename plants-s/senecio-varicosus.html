Senecio varicosus

Senecio varicosus, commonly known as the veiny senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring woody stems and narrow, often hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio varicosus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
