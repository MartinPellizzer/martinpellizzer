Syzygium pseudomegistophyllum

Syzygium pseudomegistophyllum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, showy white flowers, and edible, sweet-tart fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland and mountainous areas.

The tree is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.
