Serratula Gnaphaloides

Serratula gnaphaloides is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. This species is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Due to its ecological importance and unique appearance, Serratula gnaphaloides is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
