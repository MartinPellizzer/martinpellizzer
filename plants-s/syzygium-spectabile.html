Syzygium spectabile

Syzygium spectabile, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant produces edible, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Syzygium spectabile thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in subtropical regions.
