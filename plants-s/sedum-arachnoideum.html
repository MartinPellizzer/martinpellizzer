Sedum arachnoideum

Sedum arachnoideum, commonly known as the "ghost plant," is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, web-like coating of trichomes, giving it a unique, almost ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its low maintenance and striking visual appeal make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
