Salvia scabiosifolia subsp. adenostachya

Salvia scabiosifolia subsp.

adenostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy clusters of violet to purple flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
