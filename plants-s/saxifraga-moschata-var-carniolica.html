Saxifraga moschata var. carniolica

Saxifraga moschata var. carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
