Salacca nitida

Salacca nitida, commonly known as the sugar palm or yellow torch, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling small cherries. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Salacca nitida is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
