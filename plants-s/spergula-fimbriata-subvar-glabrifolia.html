Spergula fimbriata subvar. glabrifolia

Spergula fimbriata subvar. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped, smooth leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. This subspecies is adapted to well-drained soils and is generally hardy in temperate climates.
