Solidago flaccidifolia

Solidago flaccidifolia, commonly known as the flaccid goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago flaccidifolia is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance.
