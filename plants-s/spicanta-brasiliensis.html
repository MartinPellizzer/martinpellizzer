Spicanta brasiliensis

Spicanta brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which provide a defensive mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Spicanta brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
