Salvia marrubioides

Salvia marrubioides, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and striking blue to violet flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia marrubioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
