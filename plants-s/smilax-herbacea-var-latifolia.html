Smilax herbacea var. latifolia

Smilax herbacea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features long, climbing stems with sharp, recurved spines along its nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped, broad, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
