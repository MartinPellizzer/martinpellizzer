Schizanthus pinnatus var. gracilis

Schizanthus pinnatus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender fairy flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Its compact growth habit and extended blooming period contribute to its ornamental value.

Schizanthus pinnatus var. gracilis is often cultivated for its beauty and ease of care in both garden beds and container settings.
