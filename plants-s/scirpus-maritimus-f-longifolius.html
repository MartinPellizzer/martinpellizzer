Scirpus maritimus f. longifolius

Scirpus maritimus f. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved marsh grass, is a variant of the common bulrush.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. This plant thrives in brackish or freshwater wetlands, often found in coastal areas and marshes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical clusters of small brown flowers.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
