Stelis sagittosa

Stelis sagittosa, commonly known as the arrowhead orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, arrow-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lanceolate, and it produces small, colorful blooms that attract pollinators.

Stelis sagittosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
