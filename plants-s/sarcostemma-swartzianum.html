Sarcostemma swartzianum

Sarcostemma swartzianum, commonly known as the silverleaf milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. Its latex is milky and can be toxic, a common feature among members of its family.

Sarcostemma swartzianum is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
