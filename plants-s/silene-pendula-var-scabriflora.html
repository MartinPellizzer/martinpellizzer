Silene pendula var. scabriflora

Silene pendula var. scabriflora, commonly known as the rough-flowered bladderwort, is a flowering plant characterized by its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.

It typically features slender, erect stems with small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. The leaves are usually narrow and may have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. This plant is often found in moist, calcareous soils, thriving in environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its reproductive strategy involves both self-pollination and cross-pollination, enhancing its survival in diverse habitats.
