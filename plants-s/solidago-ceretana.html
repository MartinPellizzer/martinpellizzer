Solidago ceretana

Solidago ceretana, commonly known as the yellow archangel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago ceretana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and ability to thrive in partial shade.
