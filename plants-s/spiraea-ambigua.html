Spiraea ambigua

Spiraea ambigua, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features arching, willow-like branches that bear clusters of white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping due to its graceful appearance and early blooming habit.
