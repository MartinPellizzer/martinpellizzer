Silene porteri

Silene porteri, commonly known as the Mexican catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
