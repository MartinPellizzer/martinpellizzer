Selaginella humboldtiana

Selaginella humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's spikemoss, is a member of the spikemoss family (Selaginellaceae) and is native to South America.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella humboldtiana is known for its resilience and ability to survive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
