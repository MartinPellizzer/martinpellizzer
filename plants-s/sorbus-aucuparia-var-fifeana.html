Sorbus aucuparia var. fifeana

Sorbus aucuparia var. fifeana, commonly known as the Fife whitebeam, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe, particularly the UK. The plant is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in autumn.

The fruit is a small, red, berry-like pome that ripens in late summer.
