Scutellaria holmgreniorum

Scutellaria holmgreniorum, commonly known as the Holmgren's bluebug.

It is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its square, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its genus, Scutellaria, is known for its distinctive hooded flowers and medicinal properties in some species.
