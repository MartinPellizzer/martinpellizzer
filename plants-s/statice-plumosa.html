Statice plumosa

Statice plumosa, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, plume-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Statice plumosa is also cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
