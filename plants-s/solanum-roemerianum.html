Solanum roemerianum

Solanum roemerianum, commonly known as the purple potato vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

Solanum roemerianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
