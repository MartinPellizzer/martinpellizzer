Salvia carnosa subsp. gilmanii

Salvia carnosa subsp.

gilmanii, commonly known as Gilman's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in rocky, arid environments and features gray-green foliage with a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flower clusters during the summer months. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
