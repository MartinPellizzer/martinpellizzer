Styrax officinarum

Styrax officinarum, commonly known as the Indian sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slight serration along the edges.

Styrax officinarum is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
