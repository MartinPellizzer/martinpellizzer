Sisyrinchium junceum var. scabrum

Sisyrinchium junceum var. scabrum, commonly known as rough blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its rough, scabrous texture on the leaves, which gives it its specific name.
