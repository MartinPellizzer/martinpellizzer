Sterculia pendula

Sterculia pendula, commonly known as the hanging sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which bear small, white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant produces large, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in some regions.
