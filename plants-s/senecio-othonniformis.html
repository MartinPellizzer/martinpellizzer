Senecio othonniformis

Senecio othonniformis, commonly known as the Othonna-like groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clustered, small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has thick, ribbed leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
