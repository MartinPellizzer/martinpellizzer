Saussurea conica

Saussurea conica, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow or the sacred lotus of the Himalayas, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of small, pale yellow to brown flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.
