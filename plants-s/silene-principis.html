Silene principis

Silene principis, commonly known as the Principality catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Silene principis has small, hairy leaves and produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
