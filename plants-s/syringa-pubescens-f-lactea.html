Syringa pubescens f. lactea

Syringa pubescens f. lactea, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a rare and striking cultivar of the Syringa genus.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast beautifully with its dark green, velvety foliage. The plant is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. It typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of about 1 to 2 meters.

Due to its rarity and unique floral coloration, it is a sought-after plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
