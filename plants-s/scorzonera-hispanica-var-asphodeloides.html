Scorzonera hispanica var. asphodeloides

Scorzonera hispanica var. asphodeloides, commonly known as the Spanish scurvygrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in rough, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is closely related to other members of the genus Scorzonera, which are known for their medicinal and culinary uses.
