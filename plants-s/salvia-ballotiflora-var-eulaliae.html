Salvia ballotiflora var. eulaliae

Salvia ballotiflora var. eulaliae, commonly known as Eulalian sage, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces spikes of bright violet or lilac flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
