Syzygium leptanthum

Syzygium leptanthum, commonly known as the slender-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Syzygium leptanthum is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
