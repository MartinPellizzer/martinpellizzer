Salacia cuspidata

Salacia cuspidata, commonly known as the spike salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its woody shrub structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Salacia cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
