Saccharum macratherum

Saccharum macratherum, commonly known as the giant cane or tall grass bamboo, is a tall perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, pale-green flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Saccharum macratherum is often used in ornamental gardening and can also serve as a potential source of biofuel.
