Salacia radula

Salacia radula, commonly known as the creeping salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Salacia radula is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
