Symphytum anatolicum

Symphytum anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used for healing wounds and inflammation.
