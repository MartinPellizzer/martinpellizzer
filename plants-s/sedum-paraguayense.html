Sedum paraguayense

Sedum paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay sedum, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically green and slightly glossy, and it often grows in clusters.

Sedum paraguayense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
