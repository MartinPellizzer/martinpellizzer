Sutera tomentosa

Sutera tomentosa, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Sutera tomentosa is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in some regions.
