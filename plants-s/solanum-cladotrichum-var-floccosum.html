Solanum cladotrichum var. floccosum

Solanum cladotrichum var. floccosum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically orange or red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is typically avoided for consumption but is of interest in botanical studies.
