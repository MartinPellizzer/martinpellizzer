Sida costata

Sida costata, commonly known as coast bluestem, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various insects.

Sida costata is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and coastal winds.
