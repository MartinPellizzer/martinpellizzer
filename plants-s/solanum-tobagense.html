Solanum tobagense

Solanum tobagense, commonly known as the Toba nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries. Its berries are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum tobagense is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.
