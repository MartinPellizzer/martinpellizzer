Scutellaria galericulata var. humilis

Scutellaria galericulata var. humilis, commonly known as the low scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
