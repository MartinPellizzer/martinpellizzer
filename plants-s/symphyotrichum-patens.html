Symphyotrichum patens

Symphyotrichum patens, commonly known as the eastern purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Symphyotrichum patens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
