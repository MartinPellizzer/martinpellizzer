Salvia mellifera var. typica

Salvia mellifera var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
