Sieversia hibschii

Sieversia hibschii, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. Sieversia hibschii thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
