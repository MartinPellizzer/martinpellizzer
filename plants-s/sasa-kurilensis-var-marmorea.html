Sasa kurilensis var. marmorea

Sasa kurilensis var. marmorea, commonly known as the Marmoreal Sasa, is a clump-forming evergreen bamboo native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and distinctive mottled or marbled leaf patterns, which give it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are typically lanceolate, with a leathery texture and a glossy surface.

Sasa kurilensis var. marmorea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form compact, decorative clumps.
