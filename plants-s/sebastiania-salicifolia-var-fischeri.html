Sebastiania salicifolia var. fischeri

Sebastiania salicifolia var. fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's sycamore, is a variant of the sycamore tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
