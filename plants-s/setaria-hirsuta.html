Setaria hirsuta

Setaria hirsuta, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically brown or tan in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open fields.

Setaria hirsuta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in natural habitats.
