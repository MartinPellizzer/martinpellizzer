Sideropogon lasianthum

Sideropogon lasianthum, commonly known as the large-flowered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pale blue to violet composite flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Sideropogon lasianthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
