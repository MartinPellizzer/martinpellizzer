Sida veronicifolia var. humilis

Sida veronicifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the common sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its soft, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
