Salvia lyrata f. purpureorubra

Salvia lyrata f. purpureorubra, commonly known as the purple-red hummingbird mint, is a variant of the hummingbird mint species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, violet to purple-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
