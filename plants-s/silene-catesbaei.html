Silene catesbaei

Silene catesbaei, commonly known as the Carolina fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Silene catesbaei is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
