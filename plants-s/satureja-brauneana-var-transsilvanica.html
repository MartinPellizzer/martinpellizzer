Satureja brauneana var. transsilvanica

Satureja brauneana var. transsilvanica, commonly known as Transylvanian savory, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oblong berries.

The leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine and as a spice due to their pungent, savory aroma.
