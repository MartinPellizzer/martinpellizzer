Salix × dinarica

Salix × dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric willow, is a hybrid species native to the Balkan region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins as its primary floral structure. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, serrated edge.

Salix × dinarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its soft, flowing form.
