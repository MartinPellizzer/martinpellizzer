Sesleria caucasica

Sesleria caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Sesleria caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
