Sagittaria palifolia var. macrophylla

Sagittaria palifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are broad and floating, with a distinctive V-shaped notch at the base. The plant produces small, white flowers that rise on slender stems above the water surface. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its roots help stabilize sediments, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
