Soja javanica

Soja javanica, commonly known as Javan soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, round seeds that are rich in protein and oil. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Soja javanica plays an important role in traditional agriculture and local diets in its native regions.
