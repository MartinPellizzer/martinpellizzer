Salsola sieberi var. glomerata

Salsola sieberi var. glomerata, commonly known as saltbush or desert sandmat, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and its compact growth form aids in wind resistance. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color.

Salsola sieberi var. glomerata plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
