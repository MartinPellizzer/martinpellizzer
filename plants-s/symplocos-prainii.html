Symplocos prainii

Symplocos prainii, commonly known as the Prain's waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
