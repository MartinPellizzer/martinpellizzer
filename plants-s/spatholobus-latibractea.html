Spatholobus latibractea

Spatholobus latibractea, commonly known as the broad-bract wisteria, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
