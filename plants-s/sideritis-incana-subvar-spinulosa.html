Sideritis incana subvar. spinulosa

Sideritis incana subvar. spinulosa, commonly known as the hairy rock sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, sunny environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid conditions and it is often found in mountainous areas.
