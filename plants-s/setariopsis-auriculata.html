Setariopsis auriculata

Setariopsis auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate setaria, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-like) leaf bases.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in a dense clump, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to wet environments.
