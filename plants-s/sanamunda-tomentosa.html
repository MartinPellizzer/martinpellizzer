Sanamunda tomentosa

Sanamunda tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sanamunda, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
