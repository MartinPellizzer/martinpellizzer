Silene mathei

Silene mathei, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Silene mathei is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
