Semicipium

Semicipium, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. Semicipium thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it has been historically used for its medicinal properties.
