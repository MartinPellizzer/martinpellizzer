Sindora laotica

Sindora laotica, commonly known as the Laos tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces edible fruits that are used in local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
