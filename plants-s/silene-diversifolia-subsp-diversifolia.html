Silene diversifolia subsp. diversifolia

Silene diversifolia subsp.

diversifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Altai Mountains.

The plant is characterized by its sprawling growth habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often broader at the base. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in summer.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
