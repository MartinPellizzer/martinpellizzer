Sempervivum complanatum

Sempervivum complanatum, commonly known as the hen and chicks plant, is a succulent species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features a rosette-shaped growth pattern with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ability to form offsets, or "chicks," allows it to spread and colonize areas efficiently.
