Serjania brevipetiolata

Serjania brevipetiolata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red sandpaper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is edible and often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

Serjania brevipetiolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
