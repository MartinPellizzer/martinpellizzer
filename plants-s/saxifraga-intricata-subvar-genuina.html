Saxifraga intricata subvar. genuina

Saxifraga intricata subvar. genuina, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features a low-growing rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically bloom in shades of pink, white, or purple.

Saxifraga intricata subvar. genuina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
