Sparganium emersum f. gracile

Sparganium emersum f. gracile, commonly known as the slender bur reed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that mature into spiny, berry-like structures. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish and are surrounded by bracts.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance.
