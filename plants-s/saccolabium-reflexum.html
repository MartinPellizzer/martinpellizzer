Saccolabium reflexum

Saccolabium reflexum, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with small leaves that reduce water loss and deep root systems for drought tolerance. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution in its native habitat.

Saccolabium reflexum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
