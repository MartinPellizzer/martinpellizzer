Stachys corsica proles salisii

Stachys corsica proles salisii, commonly known as the Salisii's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its common name reflects its historical association with the Salisii, an ancient Roman family.
