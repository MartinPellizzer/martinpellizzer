Solanum podotrichum

Solanum podotrichum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. Its berries are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine due to its potential pharmacological properties.
