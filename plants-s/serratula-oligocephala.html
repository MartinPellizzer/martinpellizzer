Serratula oligocephala

Serratula oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.

However, it can be toxic if ingested, so caution is advised when handling it.
