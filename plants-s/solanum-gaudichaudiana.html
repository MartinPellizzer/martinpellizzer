Solanum gaudichaudiana

Solanum gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its attractive yellow flowers and spiny stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are small, spherical berries that change color from green to yellow as they mature.

Solanum gaudichaudiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
