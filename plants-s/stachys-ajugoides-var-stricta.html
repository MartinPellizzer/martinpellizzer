Stachys ajugoides var. stricta

Stachys ajugoides var. stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its foliage and flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
