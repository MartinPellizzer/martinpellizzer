Setaria woodii var. fonssalutis

Setaria woodii var. fonssalutis, commonly known as the golden millet, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or as a forage crop due to its hardy nature.
