Sporobolus spiciformis

Sporobolus spiciformis, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

Sporobolus spiciformis is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
