Saxifraga nivalis var. labradorica

Saxifraga nivalis var. labradorica, commonly known as Labrador saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in tundra and mountainous environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually rosette-shaped, covered in a dense layer of fine hairs to protect against harsh weather.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value in rock gardens.
