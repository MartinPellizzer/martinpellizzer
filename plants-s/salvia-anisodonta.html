Salvia anisodonta

Salvia anisodonta, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

Salvia anisodonta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
