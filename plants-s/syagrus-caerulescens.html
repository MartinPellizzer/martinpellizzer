Syagrus caerulescens

Syagrus caerulescens, commonly known as the blue palm, is a striking palm species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray fronds that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a solitary tree with a slender trunk and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound and emit a faintly aromatic scent.

Syagrus caerulescens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
