Sida halophila

Sida halophila, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to high-sodium soils. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a hairy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Sida halophila plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
