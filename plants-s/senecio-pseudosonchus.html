Senecio pseudosonchus

Senecio pseudosonchus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Senecio pseudosonchus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
