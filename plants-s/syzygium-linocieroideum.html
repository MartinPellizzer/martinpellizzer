Syzygium linocieroideum

Syzygium linocieroideum, commonly known as the red-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn red during the dry season. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour fruits similar to tamarind. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

Syzygium linocieroideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
