Sasaella longipes

Sasaella longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed sasa, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. The plant has slender, erect culms (stems) that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each culm, bearing small, two-flowered spikelets.

Sasaella longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings for its delicate appearance.
