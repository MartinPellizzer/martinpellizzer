Sonchus canariensis subsp. canariensis

Sonchus canariensis subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary weed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. Its leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and have toothed margins.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
