Selaginella davidi

Selaginella davidi, commonly known as the Dragon's Blood Fern, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. The plant features a compact, creeping habit with delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly curled. Selaginella davidi is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and for its historical use in traditional medicine.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
