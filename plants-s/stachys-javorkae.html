Stachys javorkae

Stachys javorkae, commonly known as the Balkan hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pale purple to pinkish flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Stachys javorkae is often found in calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
