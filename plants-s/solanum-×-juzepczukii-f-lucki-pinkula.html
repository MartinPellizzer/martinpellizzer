Solanum × juzepczukii f. lucki-pinkula

Solanum × juzepczukii f. lucki-pinkula, commonly known as the pink-flowered nightshade, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers, which are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as moths and bats. The plant produces small, oval berries that are usually green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in gardens or wild areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its unique floral display and ecological benefits.
