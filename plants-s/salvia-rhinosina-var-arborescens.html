Salvia rhinosina var. arborescens

Salvia rhinosina var. arborescens, commonly known as the tree sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
