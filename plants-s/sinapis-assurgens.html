Sinapis assurgens

Sinapis assurgens, commonly known as yellow rocket, is a biennial or annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with the upper leaves being more elongated. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in a single terminal raceme, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its seeds are small, elongated, and have a ridged surface, often dispersed by wind.

Sinapis assurgens is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.
