Smilax glauca

Smilax glauca, commonly known as pale smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Smilax glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
