Statice littoralis subsp. juncea

Statice littoralis subsp.

juncea, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in saline and sandy environments, often found along shorelines and dunes.

The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. Its narrow, linear leaves are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. Statice littoralis subsp.

juncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal soils.
