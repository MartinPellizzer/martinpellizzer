Scrophularia subaequiloba

Scrophularia subaequiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

Scrophularia subaequiloba is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
