Solanum balansae var. typicum

Solanum balansae var. typicum, commonly known as the Balansae nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or purple in color.

Its fruits are berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color, and they contain toxic compounds similar to those found in other members of the nightshade family.
