Salvia mitis

Salvia mitis, commonly known as the slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Salvia mitis is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
