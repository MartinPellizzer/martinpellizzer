Scorzonera scyria

Scorzonera scyria, commonly known as the Siberian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive central yellow patch.

Scorzonera scyria thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.
