Sasa mollis

Sasa mollis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or soft bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. Sasa mollis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
