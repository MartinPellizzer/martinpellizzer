Schoenoxiphium sparteum

Schoenoxiphium sparteum, commonly known as the mountain spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
