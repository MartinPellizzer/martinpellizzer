Sedum hispanicum var. buxbaumii

Sedum hispanicum var. buxbaumii, commonly known as the Buxbaum's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to hot, dry conditions and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
