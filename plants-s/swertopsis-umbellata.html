Swertopsis umbellata

Swertopsis umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale blue. The plant has compound leaves with a serrated edge, and its stems are often hairy. Swertopsis umbellata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
