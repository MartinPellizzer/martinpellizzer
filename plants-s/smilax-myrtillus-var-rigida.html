Smilax myrtillus var. rigida

Smilax myrtillus var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid greenbriar, is a variant of the smilax genus native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a pointed tip.

This variant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
