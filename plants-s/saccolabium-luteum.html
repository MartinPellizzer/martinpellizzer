Saccolabium luteum

Saccolabium luteum, commonly known as the yellow saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
