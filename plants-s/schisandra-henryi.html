Schisandra henryi

Schisandra henryi, commonly known as Chinese five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its small, red berries that turn dark purple when mature. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine. Schisandra henryi thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its berries contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable for both medicinal and culinary purposes.
