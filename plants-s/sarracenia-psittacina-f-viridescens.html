Sarracenia psittacina f. viridescens

Sarracenia psittacina f. viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered pitcher plant, is a rare and striking variant of the purple pitcher plant.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pitchers that are typically green in color, with a distinctive red or purple stripe running down the center. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, specifically Florida, where it thrives in wet, acidic soils. Its unique coloration makes it a sought-after specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological interest.
