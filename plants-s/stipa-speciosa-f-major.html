Stipa speciosa f. major

Stipa speciosa f. major, commonly known as the giant feathergrass, is a variant of the tall wheatgrass species native to the western United States.

This grass is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
