Sacoglottis villosa

Sacoglottis villosa, commonly known as the hairy sacoglottis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough or hairy surface.

Sacoglottis villosa is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests or along stream banks.
