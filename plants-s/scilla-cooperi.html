Scilla cooperi

Scilla cooperi, commonly known as the cooper's scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant is typically found in rocky or woodland habitats and thrives in well-drained soil. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize over time.

Scilla cooperi is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
