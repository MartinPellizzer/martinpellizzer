Solanum deflexicarpum

Solanum deflexicarpum, commonly known as the flexible fruit nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are typically small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
