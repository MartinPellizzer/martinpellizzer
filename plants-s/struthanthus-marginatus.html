Struthanthus marginatus

Struthanthus marginatus, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are showy, trumpet-shaped, and typically pink to purple in color. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
