Salix caloptera

Salix caloptera, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is often found in moist, open habitats such as riverbanks and wetlands. Salix caloptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
