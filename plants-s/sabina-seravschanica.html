Sabina seravschanica

Sabina seravschanica, commonly known as the Karakum Juniper, is a species of juniper native to Central Asia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly pressed against the branches, giving it a dense, compact appearance. Its cones are small and berry-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This hardy plant is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
