Spergularia atheniensis

Spergularia atheniensis, commonly known as the Athens sand-spurry, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in sandy and calcareous soils, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Spergularia atheniensis is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and showy floral displays.
