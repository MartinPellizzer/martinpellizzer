Stachys pradica

Stachys pradica, commonly known as purple betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside. The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Stachys pradica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
