Salix spodiophylla var. obtusa

Salix spodiophylla var. obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
