Saussurea pseudobullockii

Saussurea pseudobullockii, commonly known as the Himalayan gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Saussurea pseudobullockii is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows at elevations above 3,000 meters.
