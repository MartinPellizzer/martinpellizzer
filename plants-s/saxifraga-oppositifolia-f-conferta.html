Saxifraga oppositifolia f. conferta

Saxifraga oppositifolia f. conferta, commonly known as the compact saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.
