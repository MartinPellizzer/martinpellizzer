Stipa alpina

Stipa alpina, commonly known as Alpine feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, feathery seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cold climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Stipa alpina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
