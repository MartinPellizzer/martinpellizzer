Silene sericea subsp. colorata

Silene sericea subsp.

colorata, commonly known as the showy silene, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
