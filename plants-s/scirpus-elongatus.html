Scirpus elongatus

Scirpus elongatus, commonly known as the tall bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers that develop into brown, cylindrical seed heads. Its extensive root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in marshy areas.

Scirpus elongatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
