Sedum samium subsp. cilicicum

Sedum samium subsp.

cilicicum, commonly known as the Cilician stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, upright stems and produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its drought-resistant characteristics. Its leaves are typically thick and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
