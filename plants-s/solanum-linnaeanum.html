Solanum linnaeanum

Solanum linnaeanum, commonly known as the Linnaean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum linnaeanum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
