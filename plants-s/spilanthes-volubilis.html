Spilanthes volubilis

Spilanthes volubilis, commonly known as the creeping bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

The fruit is a dry, segmented pod that dehisces to release the seeds.
