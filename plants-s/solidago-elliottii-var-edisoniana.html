Solidago elliottii var. edisoniana

Solidago elliottii var. edisoniana, commonly known as the Edison's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, composite flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woods, meadows, and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
