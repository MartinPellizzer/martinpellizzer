Saxifraga virginiensis f. cicinnata

Saxifraga virginiensis f. cicinnata, commonly known as the Virginia saxifrage var. cicinnata, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its small, white flowers bloom in late spring, forming clusters at the ends of the stems.

The botanical variety is characterized by its distinctive, curled leaf tips, which give it a unique appearance.
