Shawia imbricata

Shawia imbricata, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble tiny tomatoes, often referred to as "groundcherries.". The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are typically oval or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Shawia imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, which are often used in jams and sauces.
