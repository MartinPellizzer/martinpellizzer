Stapeliopsis exasperata

Stapeliopsis exasperata, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its branching, fleshy stems that resemble coral-like structures. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardeners.
