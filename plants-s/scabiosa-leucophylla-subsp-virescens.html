Scabiosa leucophylla subsp. virescens

Scabiosa leucophylla subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are narrower and more upright.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, arranged in a spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowering period typically occurs from late spring to early summer.
