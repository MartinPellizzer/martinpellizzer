Sorbus chamaemespilus minus

Sorbus chamaemespilus minus, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a small deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
