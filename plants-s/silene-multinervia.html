Silene multinervia

Silene multinervia, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, linear leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name. It is often found in grassy habitats and open woodlands.

Silene multinervia is an annual or biennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
