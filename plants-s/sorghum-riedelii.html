Sorghum riedelii

Sorghum riedelii, commonly known as the red sorghum, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and striking red or purple foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in regions with limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and potentially useful for agricultural purposes.
