Stylopappus laciniatus var. longifolius

Stylopappus laciniatus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved stylopappus, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils and withstand periods of drought.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
