Scirpus triangulatus var. sanguineus

Scirpus triangulatus var. sanguineus, commonly known as the blood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features slender, triangular stems that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow or greenish flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
