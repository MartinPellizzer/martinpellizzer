Serapias lingua var. duriaei

Serapias lingua var. duriaei, commonly known as the Duria's tongue orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in Spain and Portugal. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, tongue-shaped lip. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
