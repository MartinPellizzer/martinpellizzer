Solanum carvalhoi

Solanum carvalhoi, commonly known as the Carvalho's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are generally ovate or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Solanum carvalhoi produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible when ripe, though the plant contains toxic compounds in its leaves and unripe fruits.
