Sophora rotundifolia var. tomentosa

Sophora rotundifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in racemes during the spring. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open to release small, dark seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
