Stenorrhynchos sodiroi

Stenorrhynchos sodiroi, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their intricate petal arrangements and vibrant coloration.

Stenorrhynchos sodiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
