Sagenia lobbii

Sagenia lobbii, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal rock fig, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round fig-like structure that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

Sagenia lobbii is often found growing on rocky outcrops and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
