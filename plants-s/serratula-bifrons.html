Serratula bifrons

Serratula bifrons, commonly known as the two-lobed sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lobed, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a cluster.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
