Stevia bangii var. typica

Stevia bangii var. typica, commonly known as the sweet leaf plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It is widely used as a natural sweetener in food and beverages due to its low caloric content and non-nutritive properties.

Stevia bangii var. typica thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
