Smilacina purpurea f. albiflora

Smilacina purpurea f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower false Solomon's seal, is a cultivar of the false Solomon's seal plant.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple blooms of the species. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves that are alternate and often have a silvery underside. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
