Swertia longipes

Swertia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed swertia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are tubular in shape and attract various pollinators. Swertia longipes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for potential use in traditional herbal remedies.
