Swida × horseyi

Swida × horseyi, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that results from the cross between Swida humilis and Swida alata.

It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Swida × horseyi thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.
