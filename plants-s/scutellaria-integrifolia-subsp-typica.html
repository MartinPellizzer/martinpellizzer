Scutellaria integrifolia subsp. typica

Scutellaria integrifolia subsp.

typica, commonly known as the smooth blue skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically smooth and ovate, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
