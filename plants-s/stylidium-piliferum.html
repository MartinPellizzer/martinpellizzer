Stylidium piliferum

Stylidium piliferum, commonly known as the hairy sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers. The plant has a short, erect stem with linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, hairy structure. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Stylidium piliferum is adapted to arid environments and is known for its rapid growth cycle.
