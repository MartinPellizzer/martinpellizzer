Syzygium honbaense

Syzygium honbaense, commonly known as the Honbaen tamarind.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
