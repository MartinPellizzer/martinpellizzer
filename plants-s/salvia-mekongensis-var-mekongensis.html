Salvia mekongensis var. mekongensis

Salvia mekongensis var. mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong sage, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
