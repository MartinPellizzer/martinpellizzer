Scrophularia balbisii var. macrophylla

Scrophularia balbisii var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
