Synaedrys acuminata

Synaedrys acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist soils.

Synaedrys acuminata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
