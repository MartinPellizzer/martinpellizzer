Setaria italica var. rubicunda

Setaria italica var. rubicunda, commonly known as red millet, is a variant of millet characterized by its distinctive red or bronze-colored grains.

It is an annual grass native to Asia and is cultivated for its edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are rich in carbohydrates and are used in various culinary and industrial applications.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for cultivation in temperate and subtropical regions.
