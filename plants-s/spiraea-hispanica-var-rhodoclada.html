Spiraea hispanica var. rhodoclada

Spiraea hispanica var. rhodoclada, commonly known as Spanish spiraea, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense, compact growth with small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are arranged in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and hardy nature.
