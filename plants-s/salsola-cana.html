Salsola cana

Salsola cana, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prickly, spiny stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in many regions.

Salsola cana is also known for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it a potential candidate for phytoremediation.
