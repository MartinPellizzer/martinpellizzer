Synadenium calycinum

Synadenium calycinum, commonly known as the red globe lily, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that resemble globes, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating, helping it retain moisture in its arid native habitat. Its flowers are tubular and have a unique structure with prominent stamens and a lobed stigma.

Synadenium calycinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
