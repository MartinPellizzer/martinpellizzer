Scirpus thunbergii

Scirpus thunbergii, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Scirpus thunbergii is highly adaptable and can thrive in a variety of aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
