Sorbus × dippelii

Sorbus × dippelii, commonly known as the Dippel's whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, belonging to the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its deciduous, compound leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
