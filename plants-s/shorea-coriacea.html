Shorea coriacea

Shorea coriacea, commonly known as the African teak, is a tropical hardwood tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a distinctive golden-brown color. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant is valued for its durable timber, which is used in construction and furniture making.
