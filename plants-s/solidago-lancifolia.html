Solidago lancifolia

Solidago lancifolia, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to dry soils in open woods, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its most distinguishing characteristic is its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. Solidago lancifolia blooms in late summer to early fall, producing copious amounts of nectar that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
