Serapias orientalis

Serapias orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Serapias, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae, commonly known as the orchid family, and is characterized by its striking, spurred petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, are often adorned with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by bees and other insects.

Serapias orientalis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
