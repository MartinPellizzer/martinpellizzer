Stemodia cimicina

Stemodia cimicina, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Stemodia cimicina is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
