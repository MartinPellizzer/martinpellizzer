Sclerocarpus uniserialis var. uniserialis

Sclerocarpus uniserialis var. uniserialis, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spoon-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by spiky, woody fruits that contain the seeds. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle, reducing water loss and enhancing its drought tolerance.

The plant's unique morphology makes it well-suited for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
