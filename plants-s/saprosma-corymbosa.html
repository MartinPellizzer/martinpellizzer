Saprosma corymbosa

Saprosma corymbosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Saprosma corymbosa is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and food for various wildlife species.
