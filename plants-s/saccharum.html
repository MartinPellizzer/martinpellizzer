Saccharum

Saccharum, commonly known as sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous stalks that contain a high concentration of sucrose. The plant thrives in warm climates with ample sunlight and water, making it a staple crop in many parts of the world. Its leaves are long and narrow, and its roots system is extensive, helping it to anchor firmly in the soil.

Sugarcane is not only important for sugar production but also for biofuel and livestock feed.
