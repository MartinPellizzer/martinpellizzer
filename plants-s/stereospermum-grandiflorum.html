Stereospermum grandiflorum

Stereospermum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a smooth, greyish-brown bark and can grow up to 30 meters in height. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds when mature.
