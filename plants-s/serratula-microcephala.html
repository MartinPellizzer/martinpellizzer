Serratula microcephala

Serratula microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosettes. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually rough and serrated, giving it the genus name "Serratula.".

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
