Satureja vulgaris var. diminuta

Satureja vulgaris var. diminuta, commonly known as dwarf savory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.
