Syzygium pulaiense

Syzygium pulaiense, commonly known as the Pahang tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically red when ripe, and contains a single seed. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
