Salix wallichiana var. pachyclada

Salix wallichiana var. pachyclada, commonly known as the broad-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, typical of the Salicaceae family, which are primarily pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
