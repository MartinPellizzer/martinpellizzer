Sasaella fallax

Sasaella fallax, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by triangular, grass-like seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasaella fallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
