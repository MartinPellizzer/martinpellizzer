Salvia hispanica var. intonsa

Salvia hispanica var. intonsa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. It produces small, edible seeds that are rich in omega-3 fatty acids and protein.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of nutrition.
