Saxifraga × hoffmannii

Saxifraga × hoffmannii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga × hoffmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
