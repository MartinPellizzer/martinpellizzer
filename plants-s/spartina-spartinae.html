Spartina spartinae

Spartina spartinae, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of the United States.

It thrives in brackish and saline environments, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Spartina spartinae plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ability to tolerate saltwater makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
