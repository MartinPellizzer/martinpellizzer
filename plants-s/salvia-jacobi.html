Salvia jacobi

Salvia jacobi, commonly known as Jacob's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia jacobi is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
