Saccolabium harrisonianum

Saccolabium harrisonianum, commonly known as the Harrison's saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by insects.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
