Silene atrocastanea

Silene atrocastanea, commonly known as the black-castanea catchfly, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene atrocastanea is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
