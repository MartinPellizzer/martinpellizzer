Sideritis briquetiana var. arbuscula

Sideritis briquetiana var. arbuscula, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer.

Sideritis briquetiana var. arbuscula is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
