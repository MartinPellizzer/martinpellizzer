Scirpus acicularis var. submersus

Scirpus acicularis var. submersus, commonly known as the submerged bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush that thrives in aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically submerged in water, adapting to its wetland habitat. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that emerge above the water surface for pollination. It plays a vital role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

This species is often found in marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams across temperate regions.
