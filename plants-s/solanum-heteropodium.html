Solanum heteropodium

Solanum heteropodium, commonly known as the variable nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain the seeds.

Solanum heteropodium is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.
