Spiranthes reichenbachiana

Spiranthes reichenbachiana, commonly known as the pink spiraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually pink or white, with a subtle fragrance.

Spiranthes reichenbachiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
