Senecio trapezuntinus

Senecio trapezuntinus, commonly known as the Trapezuntine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio trapezuntinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
