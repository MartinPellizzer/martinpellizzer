Salsola oppositiflora

Salsola oppositiflora, commonly known as sandmat or desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep in the soil.

Salsola oppositiflora is often found in desert regions of North America and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought.
