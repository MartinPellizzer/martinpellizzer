Selaginella minutifolia

Selaginella minutifolia, commonly known as the lesser spike moss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. It exhibits a unique ability to survive desiccation, making it a resilient species in arid conditions.

Selaginella minutifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
