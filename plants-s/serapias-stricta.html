Serapias stricta

Serapias stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Serapias stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
