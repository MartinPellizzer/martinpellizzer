Syzygium kuiense

Syzygium kuiense, commonly known as the Kuiense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits.

The fruit's pulp is sour and used in traditional cuisines, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
