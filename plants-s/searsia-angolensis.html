Searsia angolensis

Searsia angolensis, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are elliptical and have a rough texture, while its bark is often fissured and greyish in color.

Searsia angolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
