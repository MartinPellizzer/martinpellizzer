Salvia linczevskii

Salvia linczevskii, commonly known as the Linczevskii sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often aromatic and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.

Salvia linczevskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
