Sassafras officinale var. albidum

Sassafras officinale var. albidum, commonly known as the white sassafras, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often notched or lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain several seeds. Its roots and leaves have been historically used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
