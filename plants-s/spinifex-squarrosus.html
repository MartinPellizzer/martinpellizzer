Spinifex squarrosus

Spinifex squarrosus, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and the presence of sharp, spiny bristles along its stems. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it a common component of coastal and inland dunes. Its small, grass-like leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Spinifex squarrosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert fauna.
