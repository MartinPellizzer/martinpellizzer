Saxifraga zogangensis var. pilosa

Saxifraga zogangensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
