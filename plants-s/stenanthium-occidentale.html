Stenanthium occidentale

Stenanthium occidentale, commonly known as the western dog's mercury, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces berries that are dispersed by birds.

Stenanthium occidentale is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in natural landscapes.
