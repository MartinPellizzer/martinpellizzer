Senecio adenodontus

Senecio adenodontus, commonly known as the Cape mountain fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio adenodontus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
