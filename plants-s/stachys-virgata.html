Stachys virgata

Stachys virgata, commonly known as violet wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and clusters of violet or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Stachys virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
