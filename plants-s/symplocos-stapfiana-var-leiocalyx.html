Symplocos stapfiana var. leiocalyx

Symplocos stapfiana var. leiocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered evergreen shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
