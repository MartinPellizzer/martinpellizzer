Symphyopappus

Symphyopappus, commonly known as the desert aster, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. They are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Symphyopappus species often serve as important nectar sources for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
