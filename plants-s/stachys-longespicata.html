Stachys longespicata

Stachys longespicata, commonly known as the long-spurred betony, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces spikes of tubular, violet to purple flowers, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Stachys longespicata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
