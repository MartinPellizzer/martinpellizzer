Schizoglossum cordatum

Schizoglossum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf schizoglossum, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is shallow, allowing it to absorb moisture from the surface layer of the soil.

Schizoglossum cordatum is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
