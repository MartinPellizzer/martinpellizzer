Stipa pulcherrima var. karadagensis

Stipa pulcherrima var. karadagensis, commonly known as the Karadag silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, silver-blue flower spikes in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
