Satureja montana var. communis

Satureja montana var. communis, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils rich in thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its distinct scent and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
