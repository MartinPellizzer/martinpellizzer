Scirpus gemmifer

Scirpus gemmifer, commonly known as the gemmifer sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Scirpus gemmifer thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its growth habit and ecological significance make it a valuable component of wetland habitats.
