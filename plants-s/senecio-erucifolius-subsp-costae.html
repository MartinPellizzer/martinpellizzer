Senecio erucifolius subsp. costae

Senecio erucifolius subsp.

costae, commonly known as the zigzag senecio, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
