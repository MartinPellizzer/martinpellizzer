Stisseria

Stisseria, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Stisseria species typically have fleshy, upright stems and are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal. They thrive in humid, well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The genus includes several species, such as Stisseria grandiflora, which is prized for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
