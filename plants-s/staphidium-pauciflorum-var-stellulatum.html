Staphidium pauciflorum var. stellulatum

Staphidium pauciflorum var. stellulatum, commonly known as the star-flowered staphidium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that resemble stars, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
