Scutellaria salviifolia

Scutellaria salviifolia, commonly known as blue beadroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scutellaria salviifolia thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.
