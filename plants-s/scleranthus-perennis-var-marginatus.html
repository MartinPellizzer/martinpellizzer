Scleranthus perennis var. marginatus

Scleranthus perennis var. marginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandspurry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
