Syzygium graveolens

Syzygium graveolens, commonly known as the clove tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, red-brown flower buds that are harvested and dried to produce the spice clove. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its dense, leathery foliage and showy white to pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Syzygium graveolens is widely cultivated for its economic value as a spice and for its ornamental appeal.
