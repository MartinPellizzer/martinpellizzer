Solanum reductum

Solanum reductum, commonly known as the red tomato or the wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, red, fleshy fruits that resemble tomatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually yellow with a white or purple center, and the fruit ripens to a bright red color, containing numerous seeds.

Solanum reductum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
