Sedum japonicum subsp. japonicum

Sedum japonicum subsp.

japonicum, commonly known as Japanese stonecrop, is a succulent perennial plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and can store water, aiding in its drought tolerance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
