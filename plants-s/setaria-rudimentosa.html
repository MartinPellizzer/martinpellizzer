Setaria rudimentosa

Setaria rudimentosa, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tendency to curl at the edges.

Setaria rudimentosa is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a ornamental plant.
