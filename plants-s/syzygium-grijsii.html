Syzygium grijsii

Syzygium grijsii, commonly known as the Java apple or red apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Syzygium grijsii thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
