Serapias latifolia

Serapias latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in arid conditions.

Serapias latifolia is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
