Stachys annua subsp. ammophila

Stachys annua subsp.

ammophila, commonly known as sand betony, is a flowering plant native to sandy and coastal areas. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, hairy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal regions.

Sand betony is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
