Silene makmeliana

Silene makmeliana, commonly known as the Makmelian campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its stems are usually erect, and the leaves are linear to ovate, often covered in fine hairs.

Silene makmeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
