Smilax pseudosarsa

Smilax pseudosarsa, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often armed with sharp spines. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Smilax pseudosarsa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ability to climb or trail along the ground.
