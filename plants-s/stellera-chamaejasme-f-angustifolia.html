Stellera chamaejasme f. angustifolia

Stellera chamaejasme f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf stellera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Capparaceae family.
