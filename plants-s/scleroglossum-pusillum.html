Scleroglossum pusillum

Scleroglossum pusillum, commonly known as the small-scale crust fungus, is a unique member of the family Pucciniaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, crust-like growth form, which allows it to thrive on decaying wood in forested environments. The plant's microscopic structure consists of tightly packed hyphae, forming a solid, leathery surface. It is primarily found in temperate regions and plays a role in decomposing organic matter.

Due to its specialized morphology, it is often overlooked in ecological studies despite its ecological significance.
