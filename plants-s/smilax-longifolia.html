Smilax longifolia

Smilax longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Smilax longifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
