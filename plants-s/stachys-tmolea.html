Stachys tmolea

Stachys tmolea, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys tmolea is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
