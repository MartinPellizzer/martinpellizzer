Symplocos sawafutagi

Symplocos sawafutagi, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are edible but have a tart flavor. Symplocos sawafutagi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
