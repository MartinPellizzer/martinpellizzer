Serratula pannonica

Serratula pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian serrata, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are serrated and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Serratula pannonica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
