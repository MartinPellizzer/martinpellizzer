Stapelia lucida

Stapelia lucida, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a unique appearance with its fleshy, ribbed stems and waxy, white to pale green leaves. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers that are often described as resembling a ghostly face. These flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange and are pollinated by flies.

Stapelia lucida is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.
