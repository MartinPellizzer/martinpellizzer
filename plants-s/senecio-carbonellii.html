Senecio carbonellii

Senecio carbonellii, commonly known as Carbonell's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio carbonellii is often found in open habitats like deserts and scrublands, where it thrives in full sun.
