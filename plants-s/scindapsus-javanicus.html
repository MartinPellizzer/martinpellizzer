Scindapsus javanicus

Scindapsus javanicus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Scindapsus javanicus is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
