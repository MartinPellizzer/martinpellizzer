Silene oranensis

Silene oranensis, commonly known as the Oran's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and coastal areas. Silene oranensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
