Silene kunawarensis

Silene kunawarensis, commonly known as the Kunawara catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, adapting to the harsh, dry climate of its habitat. Its leaves are fleshy and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene kunawarensis is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its native environment.
