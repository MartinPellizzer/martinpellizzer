Solanum quinquejugum

Solanum quinquejugum, commonly known as the five-lobed nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves that are typically ovate with five lobes, giving it its common name. Its small, white or greenish flowers are followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Solanum quinquejugum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
