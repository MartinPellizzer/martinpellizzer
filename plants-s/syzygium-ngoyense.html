Syzygium ngoyense

Syzygium ngoyense, commonly known as the Ngoyense tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a round, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Syzygium ngoyense is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its possible medicinal properties.
