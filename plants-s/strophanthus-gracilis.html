Strophanthus gracilis

Strophanthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender African blood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Strophanthus gracilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
