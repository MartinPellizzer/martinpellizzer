Smilax decipiens

Smilax decipiens, commonly known as the false arrowhead vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
