Syringodea tumida

Syringodea tumida, commonly known as the swollen bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater habitats, where it can efficiently capture prey. Its leaves are often submerged, while its specialized bladders float just below the water's surface.

Syringodea tumida plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling small invertebrate populations.
