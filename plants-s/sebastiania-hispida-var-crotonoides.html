Sebastiania hispida var. crotonoides

Sebastiania hispida var. crotonoides, commonly known as the croton-like sebastiania, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and collections.
