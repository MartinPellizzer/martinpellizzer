Symplocos ramosissima

Symplocos ramosissima, commonly known as the branched beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated calyx.

Symplocos ramosissima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
