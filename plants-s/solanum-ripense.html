Solanum ripense

Solanum ripense, commonly known as the red-stalked nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
