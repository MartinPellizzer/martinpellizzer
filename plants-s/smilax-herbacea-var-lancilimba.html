Smilax herbacea var. lancilimba

Smilax herbacea var. lancilimba, commonly known as the long-leaved smilax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Smilacaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its leaves are elongated and lanceolate, contributing to its common name.

This variant is native to parts of the southeastern United States and is often found in moist, wooded habitats.
