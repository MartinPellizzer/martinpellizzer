Salvia cyclostegia var. purpurascens

Salvia cyclostegia var. purpurascens, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are slightly hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
