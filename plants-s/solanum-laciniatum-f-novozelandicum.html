Solanum laciniatum f. novozelandicum

Solanum laciniatum f. novozelandicum, commonly known as the New Zealand nightshade, is a variant of the species native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and serrated, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by berries that mature to a deep purple or black. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
