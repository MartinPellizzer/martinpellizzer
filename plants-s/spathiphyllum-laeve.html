Spathiphyllum laeve

Spathiphyllum laeve, commonly known as the peace lily, is a tropical evergreen plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green heart-shaped leaves and its striking white spatulate inflorescence. The plant is renowned for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its flowers, though not showy, are followed by bright green berries that contain seeds.

Spathiphyllum laeve is also valued for its air-purifying properties and its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
