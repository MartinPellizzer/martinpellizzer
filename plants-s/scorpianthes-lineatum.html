Scorpianthes lineatum

Scorpianthes lineatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false arum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features strap-like leaves that are dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small and clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Scorpianthes lineatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
