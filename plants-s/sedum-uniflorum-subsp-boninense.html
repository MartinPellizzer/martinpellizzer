Sedum uniflorum subsp. boninense

Sedum uniflorum subsp.

boninense, commonly known as the Bonin sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Bonin Islands. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water, which allows the plant to thrive in arid conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and unique floral display.
