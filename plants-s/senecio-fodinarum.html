Senecio fodinarum

Senecio fodinarum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio fodinarum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
