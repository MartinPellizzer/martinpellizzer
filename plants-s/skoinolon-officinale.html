Skoinolon officinale

Skoinolon officinale, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. Its root system consists of tubers, which are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Skoinolon officinale thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
