Syngonanthus ferrensis

Syngonanthus ferrensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or *Cynodon dactylon*, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a dense, creeping growth habit that forms a sod-like mat. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Syngonanthus ferrensis is often used in landscaping and as a ground cover due to its durability and low maintenance requirements.
