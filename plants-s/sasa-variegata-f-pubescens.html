Sasa variegata f. pubescens

Sasa variegata f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent variegated sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the bamboo family, Gramineae, and is characterized by its stiff, grass-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically variegated with white or light green markings, adding to its ornamental value. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for ground cover in horticulture.
