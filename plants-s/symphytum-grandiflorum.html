Symphytum grandiflorum

Symphytum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered comfrey, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is known for its thick, succulent stems and roots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
