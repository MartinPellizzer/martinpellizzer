Sutrina bicolor

Sutrina bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly contrasting coloration on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but fragrant, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

Sutrina bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
