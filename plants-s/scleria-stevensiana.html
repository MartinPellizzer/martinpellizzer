Scleria stevensiana

Scleria stevensiana, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or pale blue in color. Scleria stevensiana thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird species.
