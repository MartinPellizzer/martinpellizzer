Symphyotrichum chilense

Symphyotrichum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean heath aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that range in color from white to purple.

Symphyotrichum chilense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
