Salacia pengheensis var. angustifolia

Salacia pengheensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed salacia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
