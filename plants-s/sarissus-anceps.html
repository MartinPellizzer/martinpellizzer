Sarissus anceps

Sarissus anceps, commonly known as the Cape mountain fern, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Sarissus anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
