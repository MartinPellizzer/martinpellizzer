Scrophularia pinardii

Scrophularia pinardii, commonly known as Pinard's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Scrophularia pinardii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ecological benefits to pollinators.
