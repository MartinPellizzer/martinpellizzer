Santolina rosmarinifolia subsp. arrabidensis

Santolina rosmarinifolia subsp.

arrabidensis, commonly known as the Arabian santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, silver-gray foliage with a strong aromatic scent reminiscent of thyme and sage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
