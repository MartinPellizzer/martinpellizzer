Sorbus coxii

Sorbus coxii, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sorbus coxii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
