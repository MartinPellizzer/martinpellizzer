Spathoglottis tricallosa

Spathoglottis tricallosa, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as yellow and orange. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Spathoglottis tricallosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
