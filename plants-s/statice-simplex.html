Statice simplex

Statice simplex, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and coastal environments, making it well adapted to sandy soils and high salinity. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant hues.

Statice simplex is also valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators and its ornamental appeal.
