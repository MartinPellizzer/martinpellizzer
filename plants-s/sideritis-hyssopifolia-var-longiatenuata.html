Sideritis hyssopifolia var. longiatenuata

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. longiatenuata, commonly known as narrow-leaved ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are often branched, and the species is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
