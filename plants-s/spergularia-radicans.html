Spergularia radicans

Spergularia radicans, commonly known as sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often appear in pairs along the stems.

Spergularia radicans is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a common sight in coastal and arid regions.
