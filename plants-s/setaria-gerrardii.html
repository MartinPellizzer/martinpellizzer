Setaria gerrardii

Setaria gerrardii, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive spikelets that resemble a fox's tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer.

Setaria gerrardii is an annual plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
