Slackia geonomiformis

Slackia geonomiformis, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Slackia geonomiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small insects.
