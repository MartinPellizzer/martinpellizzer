Setaria punctata

Setaria punctata, commonly known as the punctate beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. Setaria punctata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
