Scleranthus carthusianorum

Scleranthus carthusianorum, commonly known as the Carthusian thorn, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Scleranthus carthusianorum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
