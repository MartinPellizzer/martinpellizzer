Scaphyglottis crurigera

Scaphyglottis crurigera, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Scaphyglottis crurigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
