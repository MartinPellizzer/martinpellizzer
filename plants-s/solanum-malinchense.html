Solanum malinchense

Solanum malinchense, commonly known as Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum malinchense is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
