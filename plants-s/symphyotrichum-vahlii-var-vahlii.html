Symphyotrichum vahlii var. vahlii

Symphyotrichum vahlii var. vahlii, commonly known as the Vahl's ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Vahl's ironweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
