Saxifraga bracteosa var. leptopetala

Saxifraga bracteosa var. leptopetala, commonly known as the slender-petaled saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, with showy, leaf-like bracts that give it its common name. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
