Senecio hercynicus

Senecio hercynicus, commonly known as the mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
