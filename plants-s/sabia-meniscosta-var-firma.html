Sabia meniscosta var. firma

Sabia meniscosta var. firma, commonly known as the rigid sabia, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its succulent leaves and flowers. The plant features fleshy, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. Its flowers are typically small, five-petaled, and appear in clusters during the warmer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.
