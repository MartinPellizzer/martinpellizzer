Spiraea palmata var. alba

Spiraea palmata var. alba, commonly known as white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and its showy, white flower clusters that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and early blooming habit.
