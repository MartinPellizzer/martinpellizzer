Scutellaria scordiifolia var. ammophila

Scutellaria scordiifolia var. ammophila, commonly known as the sand scullion, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers clustered in spikes, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand salty coastal winds.

This variety thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.
