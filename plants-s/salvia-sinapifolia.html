Salvia sinapifolia

Salvia sinapifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a gray-green hue and produces striking purple or blue flowers in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Native American tribes for various healing purposes.
