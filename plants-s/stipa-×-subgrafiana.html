Stipa × subgrafiana

Stipa × subgrafiana, commonly known as the hybrid false oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a hybrid between Stipa spicata and Stipa capillata, exhibiting a mix of characteristics from both parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical panicle bearing spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple.

Stipa × subgrafiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
