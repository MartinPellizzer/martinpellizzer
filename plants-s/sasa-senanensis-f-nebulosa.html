Sasa senanensis f. nebulosa

Sasa senanensis f. nebulosa, commonly known as the clouded bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa senanensis species, which belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive pale, almost cloud-like markings on the culms, which give it its common name. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, particularly in China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The foliage is typically slender and grass-like, with a dense growth habit that forms thickets.

It is valued in traditional horticulture for its ornamental appearance and is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
