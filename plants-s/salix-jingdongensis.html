Salix jingdongensis

Salix jingdongensis, commonly known as the Jingdong willow, is a species of willow native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate environments. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are catkins, which are typically present in early spring before the leaves emerge.

Salix jingdongensis is valued for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
