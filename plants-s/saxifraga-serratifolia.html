Saxifraga serratifolia

Saxifraga serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga serratifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
