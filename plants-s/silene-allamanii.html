Silene allamanii

Silene allamanii, commonly known as the Allaman's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Silene allamanii is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
