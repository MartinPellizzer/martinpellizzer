Scorzonera floccosa

Scorzonera floccosa, commonly known as the woolly scorzonera, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces a tuberous root, which is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
