Silene campanulata subsp. greenei

Silene campanulata subsp.

greenei, commonly known as the Greene's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
