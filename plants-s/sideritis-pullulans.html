Sideritis pullulans

Sideritis pullulans, commonly known as the pulvular sidereal, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Sideritis pullulans is often found in rocky or dry habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
