Stipa neomexicana

Stipa neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Stipa neomexicana plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
