Sambucus miquelii

Sambucus miquelii, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces red or black berries in the fall, which are edible but should be consumed with caution due to potential toxicity. Sambucus miquelii typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
