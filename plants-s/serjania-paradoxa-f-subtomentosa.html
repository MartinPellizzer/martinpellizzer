Serjania paradoxa f. subtomentosa

Serjania paradoxa f. subtomentosa, commonly known as the subtomentose serjania, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
