Stevia mercedensis var. glanditecta

Stevia mercedensis var. glanditecta, commonly known as the Merced stevia, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Stevia genus.

It is native to specific regions of South America, particularly in the Andean foothills of Argentina and Chile. This plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

The plant contains high levels of steviol glycosides, making it a potential candidate for natural sweetener development.
