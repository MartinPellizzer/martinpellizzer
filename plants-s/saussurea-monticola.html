Saussurea monticola

Saussurea monticola, commonly known as the Himalayan snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cold, dry conditions. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in its harsh mountainous habitat.
