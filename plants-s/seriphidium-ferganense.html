Seriphidium ferganense

Seriphidium ferganense, commonly known as the Fergana wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Seriphidium ferganense is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
