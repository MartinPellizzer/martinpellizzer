Syzygium albidirameum

Syzygium albidirameum, commonly known as the white-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a sour taste.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
