Senecio canus var. eradiatus

Senecio canus var. eradiatus, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and silvery-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
