Silene antirrhina var. subglabra

Silene antirrhina var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy pink or red flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. This variety is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to other varieties of Silene antirrhina.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
