Schradera cernua

Schradera cernua, commonly known as the hanging schradera, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often hanging from long, slender stems. The plant thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Schradera cernua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
