Sedum mexicanum

Sedum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored plant among gardeners in dry climates.
