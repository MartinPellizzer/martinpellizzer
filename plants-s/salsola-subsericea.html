Salsola subsericea

Salsola subsericea, commonly known as sandmat or desert sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing, perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces clusters of small, greenish flowers. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Salsola subsericea plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
