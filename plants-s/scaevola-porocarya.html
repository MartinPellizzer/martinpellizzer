Scaevola porocarya

Scaevola porocarya, commonly known as the coastal scaevola, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to tolerate salty air and sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize sand dunes and prevent erosion.
