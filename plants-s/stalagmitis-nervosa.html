Stalagmitis nervosa

Stalagmitis nervosa, commonly known as the veined cave plant, is a rare and unique species found in specific subterranean environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems that resemble stalagmites, hence its common name. The plant possesses a network of fine, vein-like structures that aid in nutrient absorption from the mineral-rich air. It thrives in dark, humid caves with limited sunlight, relying on chemosynthesis rather than photosynthesis.

Its botanical features make it an intriguing subject for studying adaptation to extreme environments.
