Salvia iodantha

Salvia iodantha, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and have a mint-like scent, making it popular in gardens and herbal teas.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal care once established.
