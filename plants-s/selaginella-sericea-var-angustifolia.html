Selaginella sericea var. angustifolia

Selaginella sericea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial plant characterized by its creeping stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophyte stage produces microspores and megaspores on specialized leaves called sporophylls.

This variety is noted for its delicate, silver-gray appearance and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
