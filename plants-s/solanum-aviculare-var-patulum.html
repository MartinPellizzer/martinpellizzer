Solanum aviculare var. patulum

Solanum aviculare var. patulum, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features broad, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
