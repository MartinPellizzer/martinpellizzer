Stipa haussknechtii

Stipa haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hausknecht's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, silver-blue inflorescences that resemble delicate plumes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Stipa haussknechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
