Sideritis scardica subsp. longibracteata

Sideritis scardica subsp.

longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract sida, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
