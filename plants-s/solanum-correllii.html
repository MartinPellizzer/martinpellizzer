Solanum correllii

Solanum correllii, commonly known as the Correll's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and they contain toxic alkaloids, making the plant potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum correllii is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
