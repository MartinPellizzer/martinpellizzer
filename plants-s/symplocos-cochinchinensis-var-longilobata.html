Symplocos cochinchinensis var. longilobata

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. longilobata, commonly known as the long-lobed wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves with long, pointed lobes. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
