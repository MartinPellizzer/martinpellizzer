Senecio irregularibracteatus

Senecio irregularibracteatus, commonly known as the irregular bract senecio, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features stem and leaf structures that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically fleshy and may have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
