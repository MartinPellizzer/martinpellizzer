Saccolabium mooreanum var. candidulum

Saccolabium mooreanum var. candidulum, commonly known as the white-flowered sicklepod, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive, curved seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
