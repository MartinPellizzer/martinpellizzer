Sloanea pullei

Sloanea pullei, commonly known as the Guianese sloanea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Sloanea pullei is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional construction and craftsmanship.
