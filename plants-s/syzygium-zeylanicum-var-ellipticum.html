Syzygium zeylanicum var. ellipticum

Syzygium zeylanicum var. ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic clove or narrow-leafed clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by the characteristic clove-like reddish-brown fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.

The variety ellipticum is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical clove species.
