Socratina

Socratina, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and showy, often fragrant flowers. Socratina species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.
