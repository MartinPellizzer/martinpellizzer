Silene undulatifolia

Silene undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavyleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, ascending stems and narrow, undulating leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene undulatifolia is often found in shaded to partially sunny areas and is known for its short blooming period.
