Synsepalum longecuneatum

Synsepalum longecuneatum, commonly known as the African eggplant or long-fruited kola, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, egg-shaped fruits. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by the characteristic fruit, which contains a single, hard seed. The leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are woody and often branched.

This plant is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.
