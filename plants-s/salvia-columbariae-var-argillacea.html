Salvia columbariae var. argillacea

Salvia columbariae var. argillacea, commonly known as clay sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
