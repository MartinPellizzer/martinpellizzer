Silene cerastiifolia

Silene cerastiifolia, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene cerastiifolia is a perennial herb that thrives in partial to full sunlight.
