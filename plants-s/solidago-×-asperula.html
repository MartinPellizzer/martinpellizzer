Solidago × asperula

Solidago × asperula, commonly known as the rough goldenrod, is a hybrid species within the genus Solidago, native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters and features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract a variety of pollinators.

Solidago × asperula is often found in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and along stream banks.
