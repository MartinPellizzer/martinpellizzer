Scirpus cariciformis

Scirpus cariciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a distinct midrib.

The inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that bloom in late summer.
