Silene austroiranica

Silene austroiranica, commonly known as the Iranian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
