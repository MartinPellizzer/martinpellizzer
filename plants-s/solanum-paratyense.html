Solanum paratyense

Solanum paratyense, commonly known as the Paraty nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by edible berries that are yellow when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

Solanum paratyense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
