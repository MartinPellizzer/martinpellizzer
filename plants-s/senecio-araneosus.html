Senecio araneosus

Senecio araneosus, commonly known as the spider plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that form a dense, low-growing structure. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to propagate through runners makes it easy to cultivate and spread in suitable environments.
