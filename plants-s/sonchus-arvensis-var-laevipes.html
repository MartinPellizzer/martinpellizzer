Sonchus arvensis var. laevipes

Sonchus arvensis var. laevipes, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many regions.
