Swida pumila

Swida pumila, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a small shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and features dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are usually ovate with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Swida pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
