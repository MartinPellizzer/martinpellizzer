Syngonanthus pakaraimensis var. rivularis

Syngonanthus pakaraimensis var. rivularis, commonly known as the Pakaraima sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Guyana Shield region.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and forms compact clumps with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and rocky crevices. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes that produce minute flowers.

Syngonanthus pakaraimensis var. rivularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
