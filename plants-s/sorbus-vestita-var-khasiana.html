Sorbus vestita var. khasiana

Sorbus vestita var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
