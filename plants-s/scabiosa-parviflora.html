Scabiosa parviflora

Scabiosa parviflora, commonly known as the lesser scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scabiosa parviflora is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
