Seriphium fuscum

Seriphium fuscum, commonly known as the brown hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. It has hairy, grayish-green leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer.

Seriphium fuscum is often found in arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
