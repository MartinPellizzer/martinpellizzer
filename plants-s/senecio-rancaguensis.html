Senecio rancaguensis

Senecio rancaguensis, commonly known as the Rancagua groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
