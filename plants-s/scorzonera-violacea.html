Scorzonera violacea

Scorzonera violacea, commonly known as the violet scurvygrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, blue-green leaves and produces striking violet to purple flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Scorzonera violacea is a perennial species that typically blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its medicinal properties have been historically noted, though it is not commonly cultivated for commercial purposes.
