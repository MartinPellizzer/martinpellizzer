Salvia serotina

Salvia serotina, commonly known as late sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and has a square stem with opposite leaves.

Salvia serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
