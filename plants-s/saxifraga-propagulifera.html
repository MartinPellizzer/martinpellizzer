Saxifraga propagulifera

Saxifraga propagulifera, commonly known as the rockfoil saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fleshy, often hairy leaves that help it survive in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Its reproductive strategy involves the production of numerous tiny propagules, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its colonization of rocky and barren soils.

Saxifraga propagulifera is well adapted to nutrient-poor substrates and can thrive in full sun conditions.
