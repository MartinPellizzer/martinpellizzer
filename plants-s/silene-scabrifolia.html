Silene scabrifolia

Silene scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, rough hairs, which give it its common name.

Silene scabrifolia is a perennial species that blooms in the summer months.
