Syzygium paniense

Syzygium paniense, commonly known as the Paniense tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Syzygium paniense is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
