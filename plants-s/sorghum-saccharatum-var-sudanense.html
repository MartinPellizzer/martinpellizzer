Sorghum saccharatum var. sudanense

Sorghum saccharatum var. sudanense, commonly known as Sudan grass, is a tall, drought-resistant grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its coarse, bluish-green leaves and sturdy, upright stems. This plant is widely cultivated for forage and soil erosion control due to its rapid growth and deep root system. It thrives in warm climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often used in agricultural and ecological restoration projects.
