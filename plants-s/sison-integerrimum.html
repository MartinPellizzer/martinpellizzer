Sison integerrimum

Sison integerrimum, commonly known as the rigid sison, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
