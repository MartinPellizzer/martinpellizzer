Swainsona vestita

Swainsona vestita, commonly known as the silky legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Swainsona vestita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
