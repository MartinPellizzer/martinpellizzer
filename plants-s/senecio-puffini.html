Senecio puffini

Senecio puffini, commonly known as the Puffin's Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens for its ornamental value.
