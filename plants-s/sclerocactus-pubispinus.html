Sclerocactus pubispinus

Sclerocactus pubispinus, commonly known as the pubescent spineless cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and sparse, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
