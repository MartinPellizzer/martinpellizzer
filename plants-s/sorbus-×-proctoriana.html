Sorbus × proctoriana

Sorbus × proctoriana, commonly known as the Proctor's whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, native to the British Isles.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnate with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. It produces small, aggregate fruits known as pomes, which are typically red or orange in color and contain several seeds.

This species is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
