Sesamum luteum

Sesamum luteum, commonly known as the yellow sesame, is an annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, yellowish seeds. These seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Sesamum luteum thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, sunny climate for optimal growth.
