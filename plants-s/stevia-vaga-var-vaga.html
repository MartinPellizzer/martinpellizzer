Stevia vaga var. vaga

Stevia vaga var. vaga, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It is often cultivated as a natural sweetener and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Stevia vaga var. vaga thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and functional gardening.
