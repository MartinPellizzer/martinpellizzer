Styrax schultzei

Styrax schultzei, commonly known as the Schultze's styrax, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.

Styrax schultzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic beauty.
