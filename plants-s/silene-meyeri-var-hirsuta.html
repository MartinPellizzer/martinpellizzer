Silene meyeri var. hirsuta

Silene meyeri var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy silene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in racemes. It is native to regions of the Caucasus and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Silene meyeri var. hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
