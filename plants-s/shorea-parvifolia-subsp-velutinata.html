Shorea parvifolia subsp. velutinata

Shorea parvifolia subsp.

velutinata, commonly known as the velvet-flowered shorea, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, smooth, and often hairy leaves, which give it its subspecific name "velutinata.".

The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable and heavy timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
