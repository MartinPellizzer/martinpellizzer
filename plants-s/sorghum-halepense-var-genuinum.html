Sorghum halepense var. genuinum

Sorghum halepense var. genuinum, commonly known as jointed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including poor and disturbed soils. The plant has erect, cylindrical stems with nodes and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles.

This species is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
