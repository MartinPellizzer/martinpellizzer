Senecio thurifer

Senecio thurifer, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spherical clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Senecio thurifer is generally drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
