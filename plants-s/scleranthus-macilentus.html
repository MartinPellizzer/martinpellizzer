Scleranthus macilentus

Scleranthus macilentus, commonly known as the slender sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Scleranthus macilentus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal areas or along roadsides.
