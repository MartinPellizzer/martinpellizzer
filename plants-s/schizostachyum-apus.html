Schizostachyum apus

Schizostachyum apus, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dark, almost black-colored culms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
