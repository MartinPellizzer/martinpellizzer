Spodiopogon sagittifolius

Spodiopogon sagittifolius, commonly known as the arrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and sword-shaped, with a distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaf base. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Spodiopogon sagittifolius plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
