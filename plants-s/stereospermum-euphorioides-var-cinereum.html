Stereospermum euphorioides var. cinereum

Stereospermum euphorioides var. cinereum, commonly known as the silver-leaved satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and leathery, dark green leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves and bark contain compounds used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental and timber qualities.

The species is adapted to humid, warm climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
