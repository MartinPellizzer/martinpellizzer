Simarouba tulae

Simarouba tulae, commonly known as the Mexican yellow wood, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Simaroubales and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and dense, yellowish wood. The plant typically grows in lowland tropical forests and is known for its hardiness in humid climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
