Selago angustibractea

Selago angustibractea, commonly known as the narrow-bract selago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in its natural habitat.
