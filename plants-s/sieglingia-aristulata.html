Sieglingia aristulata

Sieglingia aristulata, commonly known as the arrowleaf sieve-plant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Sieglingia aristulata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
