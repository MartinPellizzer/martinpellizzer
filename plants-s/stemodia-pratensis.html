Stemodia pratensis

Stemodia pratensis, commonly known as meadow stemodia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Stemodia pratensis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
