Smilacina prattii

Smilacina prattii, commonly known as Pratt's greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
