Sonchus fauriei

Sonchus fauriei, commonly known as the common sowthistle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and basal rosette of smooth, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that form composite heads, typical of the dandelion family. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to other regions, often becoming invasive.

Sonchus fauriei thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
