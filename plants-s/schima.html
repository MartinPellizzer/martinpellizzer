Schima

Schima, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Theaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Schima superba, which is native to eastern Asia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Schima species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
