Suaeda tamariscina

Suaeda tamariscina, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments, often found in coastal areas and salt flats. The plant has small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are succulent, adapted to store water. It has a compact, low-growing habit with a thick, fleshy stem.

Suaeda tamariscina is well-suited to harsh desert conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
