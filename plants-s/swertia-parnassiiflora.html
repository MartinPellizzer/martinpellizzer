Swertia parnassiiflora

Swertia parnassiiflora, commonly known as the Parnassian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Swertia parnassiiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
