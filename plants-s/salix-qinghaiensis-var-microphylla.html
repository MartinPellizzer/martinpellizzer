Salix qinghaiensis var. microphylla

Salix qinghaiensis var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved willow, is a shrub native to the Qinghai region of China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are small in size and often covered with a fine pubescence. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces catkins as its primary floral structure, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife in its native habitat.
