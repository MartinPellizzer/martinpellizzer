Sasa yettiuensis

Sasa yettiuensis, commonly known as the Yetti bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and forms dense clumps or colonies. Sasa yettiuensis is often used in traditional landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
