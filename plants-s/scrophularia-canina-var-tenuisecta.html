Scrophularia canina var. tenuisecta

Scrophularia canina var. tenuisecta, commonly known as narrow-leaved dog's mercury, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Scrophulariaceae family.
