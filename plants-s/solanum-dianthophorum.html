Solanum dianthophorum

Solanum dianthophorum, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Solanum dianthophorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
