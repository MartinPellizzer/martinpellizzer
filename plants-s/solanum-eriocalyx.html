Solanum eriocalyx

Solanum eriocalyx, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, including Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries. Solanum eriocalyx is often found in open woodlands and along forest edges, where it grows as an understory plant.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
