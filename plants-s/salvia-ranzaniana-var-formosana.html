Salvia ranzaniana var. formosana

Salvia ranzaniana var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan sage, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
