Sida elliottii var. elliottii

Sida elliottii var. elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

The species is characterized by its fibrous stems and its ability to adapt to a variety of wetland habitats.
