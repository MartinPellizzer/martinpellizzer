Sinarundinaria confusa

Sinarundinaria confusa, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant features a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor in rocky or clay soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinctive bright green color and a serrated edge.

Sinarundinaria confusa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
