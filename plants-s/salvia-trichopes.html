Salvia trichopes

Salvia trichopes, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Salvia trichopes is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to native gardens.
