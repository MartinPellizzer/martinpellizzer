Sunipia rutilans

Sunipia rutilans, commonly known as the golden sunipia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season.

Sunipia rutilans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
