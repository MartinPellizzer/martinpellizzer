Sanvitaliopsis liebmannii

Sanvitaliopsis liebmannii, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a rare and striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fronds that emit a bright yellow hue, making it visually striking in its natural habitat. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its botanical features include small, clustered roots and intricate, delicate foliage that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments for conservation and horticultural purposes.
