Senecio ombrophyllus

Senecio ombrophyllus, commonly known as the forest senecio, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is well adapted to forest understory conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a popular choice for shady garden settings.
