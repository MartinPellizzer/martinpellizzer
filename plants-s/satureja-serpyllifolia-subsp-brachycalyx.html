Satureja serpyllifolia subsp. brachycalyx

Satureja serpyllifolia subsp.

brachycalyx, commonly known as narrow-leaved savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, narrow leaves with a strong aromatic scent, often used in culinary applications.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant plantings.
