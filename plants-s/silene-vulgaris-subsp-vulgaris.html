Silene vulgaris subsp. vulgaris

Silene vulgaris subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common campion, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically linear to lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces striking pink to red flowers with five petals, arranged in a raceme, which bloom from June to August. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, waste areas, and in meadows.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
