Senecio purpuratus

Senecio purpuratus, commonly known as the purple ice plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a low-growing, mat-like structure. The plant produces vibrant purple flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and colorful blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
