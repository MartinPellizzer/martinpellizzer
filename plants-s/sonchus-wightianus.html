Sonchus wightianus

Sonchus wightianus, commonly known as Wight's sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, and it produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including India, and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a taproot system and can grow up to 60 cm in height.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a potential weed in agricultural settings.
