Senecio navicularis

Senecio navicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a clustered inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, with a deep root system that helps it retain moisture.
