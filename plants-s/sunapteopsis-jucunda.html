Sunapteopsis jucunda

Sunapteopsis jucunda, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated flower spikes that bear small, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.

Sunapteopsis jucunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
