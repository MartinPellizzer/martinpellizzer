Simsia sanguinea subsp. albida

Simsia sanguinea subsp.

albida, commonly known as the white-flowered Simsia, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale to white petals, contrasting with the more typical red flowers of the main species. It typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
