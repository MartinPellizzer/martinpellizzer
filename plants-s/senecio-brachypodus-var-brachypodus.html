Senecio brachypodus var. brachypodus

Senecio brachypodus var. brachypodus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

Its succulent nature allows it to store moisture, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
