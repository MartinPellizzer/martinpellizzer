Senna macranthera var. pudibunda

Senna macranthera var. pudibunda, commonly known as the red senna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
