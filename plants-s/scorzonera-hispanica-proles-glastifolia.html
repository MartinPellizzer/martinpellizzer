Scorzonera hispanica proles glastifolia

Scorzonera hispanica proles glastifolia, commonly known as the Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Scorzonera hispanica proles glastifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
