Scolopia mutiensis

Scolopia mutiensis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces smooth, green, spherical fruits that turn red when mature. Scolopia mutiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
