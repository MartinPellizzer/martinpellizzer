Solenomelus chamaelus

Solenomelus chamaelus, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden within its foliage. Its fruit is a small, round fig-like structure that is edible and often consumed by various animals.

Solenomelus chamaelus thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
