Sporobolus domingensis

Sporobolus domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican grass, is a perennial grass native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed areas.

Sporobolus domingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
