Saussurea leptophylla var. tschitralensis

Saussurea leptophylla var. tschitralensis, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or the Tibetan saffron, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow-orange flower heads. Native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, it thrives in cold, arid environments. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptation make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
