Stipa argentinensis

Stipa argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa argentinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
