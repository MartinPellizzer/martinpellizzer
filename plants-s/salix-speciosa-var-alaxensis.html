Salix speciosa var. alaxensis

Salix speciosa var. alaxensis, commonly known as the Alaska weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in spring and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize riverbanks.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to cold climates and its role in supporting local wildlife.
