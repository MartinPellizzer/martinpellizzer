Sasa maxima

Sasa maxima, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, followed by tiny, triangular seeds. Sasa maxima is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and structural form.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is hardy in temperate climates.
