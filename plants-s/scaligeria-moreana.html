Scaligeria moreana

Scaligeria moreana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scaligeria, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are arranged in dense, terminal spikes, adding a delicate appearance to the plant.

Scaligeria moreana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
