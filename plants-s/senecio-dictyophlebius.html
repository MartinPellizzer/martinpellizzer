Senecio dictyophlebius

Senecio dictyophlebius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It features slender, ascending stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
