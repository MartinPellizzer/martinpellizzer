Silene meyeri subsp. meyeri

Silene meyeri subsp.

meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. Silene meyeri subsp.

meyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
