Silene asclepiadea var. glutinosa

Silene asclepiadea var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky willow or sticky evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
