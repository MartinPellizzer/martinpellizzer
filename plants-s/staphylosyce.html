Staphylosyce

Staphylosyce, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fruits are small berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making the plant potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

Staphylosyce is often found in regions of southern Africa, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
