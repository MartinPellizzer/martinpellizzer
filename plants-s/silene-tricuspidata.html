Silene tricuspidata

Silene tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

Silene tricuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
