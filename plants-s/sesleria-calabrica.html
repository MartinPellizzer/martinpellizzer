Sesleria calabrica

Sesleria calabrica, commonly known as the Cape sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Sesleria calabrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
