Stelis liebmannii

Stelis liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in racemose inflorescences, and are characterized by their intricate, delicate structure.

The genus Stelis is known for its diverse floral morphology, and Stelis liebmannii is no exception, showcasing adaptations suited to its specific ecological niche.
