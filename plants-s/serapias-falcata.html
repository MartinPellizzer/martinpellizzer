Serapias falcata

Serapias falcata, commonly known as the sickle orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as scrublands and woodlands. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow and orange coloration, often with a dark red or purple lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

Serapias falcata is also notable for its role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
