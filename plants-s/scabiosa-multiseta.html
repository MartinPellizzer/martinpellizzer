Scabiosa multiseta

Scabiosa multiseta, commonly known as the many-bristled scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features finely hairy leaves and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It blooms from late spring to early autumn and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scabiosa multiseta is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and easy maintenance.
