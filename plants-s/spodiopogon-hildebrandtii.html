Spodiopogon hildebrandtii

Spodiopogon hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes help it spread and establish dense colonies in its wetland environment.

Spodiopogon hildebrandtii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
