Smilax intricatissima

Smilax intricatissima, commonly known as the intricate smilax or the swamp smilax, is a flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its stout, thorned stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, white, spadix-like flowers that are enclosed within a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in wetland environments and is native to the southeastern United States.

Smilax intricatissima is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in moist habitats.
