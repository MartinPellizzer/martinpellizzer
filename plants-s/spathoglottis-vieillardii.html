Spathoglottis vieillardii

Spathoglottis vieillardii, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are clustered in a spathaceous sheath, giving it its genus name. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom during the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Spathoglottis vieillardii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
