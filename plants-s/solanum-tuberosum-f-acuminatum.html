Solanum tuberosum f. acuminatum

Solanum tuberosum f. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed tubers that are often smaller than typical potatoes. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and shares the same genus as other potato species. It exhibits a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are typically lobed.

This cultivar is valued for its unique tuber shape and is sometimes used in specialty agriculture or horticultural displays.
