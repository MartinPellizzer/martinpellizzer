Salix aurita var. appendiculata

Salix aurita var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate willow, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it blooms in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
