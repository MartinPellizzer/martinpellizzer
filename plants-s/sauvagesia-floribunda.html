Sauvagesia floribunda

Sauvagesia floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in horticulture.
