Scleranthus medusellus

Scleranthus medusellus, commonly known as the common dog's-mercury, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils, often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability to different soil types.
