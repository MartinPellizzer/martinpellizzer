Saxifraga ovatocordata

Saxifraga ovatocordata, commonly known as the broad-leafed saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features rosettes of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems.

Saxifraga ovatocordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
