Scirpus fuirenoides var. karuisawensis

Scirpus fuirenoides var. karuisawensis, commonly known as the Karuisawa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining wetland habitats.
