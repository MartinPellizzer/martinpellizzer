Solanum famatinae

Solanum famatinae, commonly known as the Famatina nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its toxic properties. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough texture, while its fruits are fleshy and contain seeds.

Solanum famatinae is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological role in its native environment.
