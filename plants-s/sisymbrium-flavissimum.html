Sisymbrium flavissimum

Sisymbrium flavissimum, commonly known as yellow babylon wheat, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and produces pods that contain several small, elongated seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
