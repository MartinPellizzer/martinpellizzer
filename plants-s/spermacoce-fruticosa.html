Spermacoce fruticosa

Spermacoce fruticosa, commonly known as the African catchfly, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Spermacoce fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
