Sciaphila arcuata

Sciaphila arcuata, commonly known as the moon vine, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and relies on its aerial roots to absorb moisture from the air. Its leaves are typically elongated and glossy, while its flowers are often white or pale yellow with a distinctive scent.

Sciaphila arcuata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in unique, light-demanding conditions.
