Silene polygama

Silene polygama, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene polygama is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
