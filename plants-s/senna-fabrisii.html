Senna fabrisii

Senna fabrisii, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Senna fabrisii is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration.

Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, flat pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
