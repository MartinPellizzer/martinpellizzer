Stelis immodica

Stelis immodica, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clustered, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Stelis immodica is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
