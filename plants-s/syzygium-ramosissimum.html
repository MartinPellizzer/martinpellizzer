Syzygium ramosissimum

Syzygium ramosissimum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red floral bracts that resemble flames, which appear in late summer. The plant has a dense, spreading canopy with pinnately compound leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy berries.

Syzygium ramosissimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
