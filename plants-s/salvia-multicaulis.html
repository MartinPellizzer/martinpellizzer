Salvia multicaulis

Salvia multicaulis, commonly known as the mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners.
