Suksdorfia violacea

Suksdorfia violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of violet-blue, bell-shaped flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Suksdorfia violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
