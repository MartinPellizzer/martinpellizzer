Salvia kiaometiensis f. pubescens

Salvia kiaometiensis f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent sage, is a botanical variety native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive texture and appearance. Its flowers are typically violet to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
