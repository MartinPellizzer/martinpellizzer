Salix tristis f. curtifolia

Salix tristis f. curtifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved weeping willow, is a unique variant of the weeping willow species.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
