Sambucus cerulea

Sambucus cerulea, commonly known as blueberry huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, which are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.
