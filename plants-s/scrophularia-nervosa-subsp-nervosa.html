Scrophularia nervosa subsp. nervosa

Scrophularia nervosa subsp.

nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominent veins, and it produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
