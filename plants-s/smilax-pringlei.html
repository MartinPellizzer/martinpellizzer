Smilax pringlei

Smilax pringlei, commonly known as the pringle's smilax, is a species of climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Smilax pringlei is often found in moist, wooded areas and is well adapted to the humid climate of its native range.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its habitat.
