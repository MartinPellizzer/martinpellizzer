Selaginella subisophylla

Selaginella subisophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial moss-like plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

Selaginella subisophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
