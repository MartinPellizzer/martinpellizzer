Shorea gratissima

Shorea gratissima, commonly known as the yellow meranti or white mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, pale yellow flowers that are borne in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Shorea gratissima plays an important ecological role in coastal and riverine ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various species.
