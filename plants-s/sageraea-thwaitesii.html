Sageraea thwaitesii

Sageraea thwaitesii, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Sageraea thwaitesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
