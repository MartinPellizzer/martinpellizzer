Sambucus racemosa f. calva

Sambucus racemosa f. calva, commonly known as the white elderberry, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodlands.

The leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
