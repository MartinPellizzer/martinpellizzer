Scutellaria adenostegia

Scutellaria adenostegia, commonly known as the rock skullcup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its small, two-lipped flowers are usually blue or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Scutellaria adenostegia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
