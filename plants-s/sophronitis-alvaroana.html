Sophronitis alvaroana

Sophronitis alvaroana, commonly known as the Alvaro's orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that typically display a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or montane regions with high biodiversity. Its growth habit includes a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in fluctuating environmental conditions.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Sophronitis alvaroana is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
