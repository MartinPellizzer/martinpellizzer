Solanum guiencense

Solanum guiencense, commonly known as the Guianese nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually ovate or lanceolate, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its potential toxicity, it is often avoided for cultivation or consumption.
