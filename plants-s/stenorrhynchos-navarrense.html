Stenorrhynchos navarrense

Stenorrhynchos navarrense, commonly known as the Navarre grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and scrublands, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.
