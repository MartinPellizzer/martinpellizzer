Symphyotrichum eulae

Symphyotrichum eulae, commonly known as the Eula's coneflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in open woodlands and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a prominent yellow central disk, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Symphyotrichum eulae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
