Senecio sycephyllus

Senecio sycephyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
