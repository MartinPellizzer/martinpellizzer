Scrophularia scorodonia subsp. tinantii

Scrophularia scorodonia subsp.

tinantii, commonly known as the alpine scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
