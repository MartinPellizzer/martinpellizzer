Serratula paueana

Serratula paueana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is upright and branched.

Serratula paueana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
