Setaria cirrosa

Setaria cirrosa, commonly known as silky millet, is an annual grass native to Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often used as a forage crop or for soil stabilization.

Its seeds are also used in traditional food preparation in some cultures.
