Sulcorebutia chilensis

Sulcorebutia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean sun rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Chile.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, rounded tubers that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Sulcorebutia chilensis is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
