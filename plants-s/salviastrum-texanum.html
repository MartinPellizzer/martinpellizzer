Salviastrum texanum

Salviastrum texanum, commonly known as Texas sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces showy spikes of purple or violet flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
