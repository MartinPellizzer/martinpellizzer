Selaginella contigua

Selaginella contigua, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, creeping growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered around short, erect stems. Selaginella contigua is known for its remarkable ability to survive desiccation, making it a resilient species in arid conditions.

It is often found in rocky soils and is used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
