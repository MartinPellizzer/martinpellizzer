Senecio appendiculatus var. preauxiana

Senecio appendiculatus var. preauxiana, commonly known as the Cape goldbos, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in a thick layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted for storing moisture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
