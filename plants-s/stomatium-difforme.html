Stomatium difforme

Stomatium difforme, commonly known as the variable mouth orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a specialized lip or labellum that plays a role in pollination. The plant's leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Stomatium difforme is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
