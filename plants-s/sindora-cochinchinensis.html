Sindora cochinchinensis

Sindora cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
