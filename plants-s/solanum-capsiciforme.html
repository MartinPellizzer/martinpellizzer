Solanum capsiciforme

Solanum capsiciforme, commonly known as the "paprika pepper" or "chiltepin," is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, red, bell-shaped fruits that are used in traditional cuisines for their spicy flavor. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, with a distinctive scent when crushed.

Solanum capsiciforme is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and culinary uses.
