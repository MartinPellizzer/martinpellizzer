Senecio vaccinioides var. pruinosus

Senecio vaccinioides var. pruinosus, commonly known as the sticky senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique texture and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant.
