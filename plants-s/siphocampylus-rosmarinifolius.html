Siphocampylus rosmarinifolius

Siphocampylus rosmarinifolius, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in herbal teas and culinary applications. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is upright, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and slightly hairy.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
