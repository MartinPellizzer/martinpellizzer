Silene sorensenis f. pallida

Silene sorensenis f. pallida, commonly known as the pale form of the Sorensen's catchfly, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers and hairy stems.

It is a perennial species native to specific regions of Europe, particularly in the mountains of the Carpathian Basin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The pale coloration of its flowers is a notable trait that sets it apart from other forms of the species.
