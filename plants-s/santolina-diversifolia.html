Santolina diversifolia

Santolina diversifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf santolina, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant features silver-gray foliage with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina diversifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
