Sinocalamus pachystachyus

Sinocalamus pachystachyus, commonly known as the broad-stalked bamboo, is a tall, robust grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stout, cylindrical culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has broad, flat leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its strength and rapid growth, it is used in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
