Senecio spanomerus

Senecio spanomerus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

Senecio spanomerus is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
