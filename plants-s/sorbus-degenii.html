Sorbus degenii

Sorbus degenii, commonly known as the Degen's whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the tree can grow up to 10 meters in height.

Sorbus degenii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
