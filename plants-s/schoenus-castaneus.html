Schoenus castaneus

Schoenus castaneus, commonly known as the chestnut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, clay-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Schoenus castaneus is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
