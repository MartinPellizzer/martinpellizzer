Sebastiania glandulosa f. ovata

Sebastiania glandulosa f. ovata, commonly known as the oval-seeded sebastiania, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate in shape and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
