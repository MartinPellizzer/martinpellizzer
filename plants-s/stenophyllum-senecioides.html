Stenophyllum senecioides

Stenophyllum senecioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Stenophyllum senecioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
