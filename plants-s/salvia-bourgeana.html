Salvia bourgeana

Salvia bourgeana, commonly known as the hummingbird flower or winter salvia, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are tubular and are highly attractive to pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Salvia bourgeana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
