Scirpus clarkei

Scirpus clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its habitat.

Scirpus clarkei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
