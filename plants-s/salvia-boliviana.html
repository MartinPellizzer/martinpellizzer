Salvia boliviana

Salvia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian sage, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Salvia boliviana is known for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
