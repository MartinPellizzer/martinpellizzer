Stipulicida filiformis

Stipulicida filiformis, commonly known as the slender stipule plant, is a unique species characterized by its delicate, thread-like stipules that arise from the leaf axils.

This plant belongs to the family Stipulicidaceae and is native to specific regions of South America. Its foliage is typically small and narrow, with leaves arranged alternately along the stem. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical or subtropical woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
