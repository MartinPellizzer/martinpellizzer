Sophora albopetiolulata

Sophora albopetiolulata, commonly known as the white-veined Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with white veins against a dark green background. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers in drooping clusters, typically in late spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
