Stapelia × marginata

Stapelia × marginata, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a hybrid succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and distinctive, star-shaped flowers that resemble dead leaves. The plant produces pale yellow or greenish-white blooms with a strong, unpleasant odor, which attracts pollinators like flies. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, and it is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance.

Stapelia × marginata is relatively easy to care for, requiring bright light and well-draining soil.
