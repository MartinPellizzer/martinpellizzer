Sabicea subinvolucrata

Sabicea subinvolucrata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
