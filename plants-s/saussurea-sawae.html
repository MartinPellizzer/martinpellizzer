Saussurea sawae

Saussurea sawae, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces a fibrous root system and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. Saussurea sawae is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.
