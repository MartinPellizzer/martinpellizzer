Stevia talpensis

Stevia talpensis, commonly known as sweetleaf or honey plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from steviol glycosides, making it a natural sweetener. Stevia talpensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
