Sagina apetala var. imberbis

Sagina apetala var. imberbis, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, prostrate stems that form dense mats on the ground, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

It is often found in coastal areas and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
