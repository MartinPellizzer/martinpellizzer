Satyrium platystigma

Satyrium platystigma, commonly known as the broad-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in a raceme, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
