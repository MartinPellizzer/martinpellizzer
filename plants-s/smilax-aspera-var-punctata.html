Smilax aspera var. punctata

Smilax aspera var. punctata, commonly known as the punctate smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features stout, thorny stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a rough, waxy texture and a distinctive reddish or purplish hue on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in partial shade.
