Shorea eximia var. angustifolia

Shorea eximia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is typically found in lowland rainforests. It produces small, yellowish flowers and fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is often used in construction and furniture-making.
