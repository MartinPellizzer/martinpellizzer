Schoenus floribundus

Schoenus floribundus, commonly known as the flowering sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, making it visually distinctive. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
