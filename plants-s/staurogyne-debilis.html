Staurogyne debilis

Staurogyne debilis, commonly known as the slender waterwheel plant, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has whorled leaves that are often submerged, with some floating leaves at the surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Staurogyne debilis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
