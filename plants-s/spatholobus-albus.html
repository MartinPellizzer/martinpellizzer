Spatholobus albus

Spatholobus albus, commonly known as the white wisteria, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking white, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often woody and can grow quite long. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its beautiful floral display and fragrant blooms.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
