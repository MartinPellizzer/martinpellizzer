Sarcomorphis littoralis

Sarcomorphis littoralis, commonly known as the coastal glassyweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually pale green to yellowish in color.

Sarcomorphis littoralis is adapted to saline and wind-exposed environments, making it a characteristic species of coastal vegetation.
