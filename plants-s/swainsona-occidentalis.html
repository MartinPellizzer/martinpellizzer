Swainsona occidentalis

Swainsona occidentalis, commonly known as the western false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces striking blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. Swainsona occidentalis is adapted to drought conditions and has a deep root system for water storage.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
