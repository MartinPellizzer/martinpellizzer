Strophanthus parviflorus

Strophanthus parviflorus, commonly known as the little flower strophanthus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has woody stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it is often found in shrublands and forest edges. Its flowers are typically clustered in small umbels, and the plant can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

Strophanthus parviflorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
