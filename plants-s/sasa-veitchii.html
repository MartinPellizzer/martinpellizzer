Sasa veitchii

Sasa veitchii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Veitch's bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Sasa veitchii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in shaded forest environments.
