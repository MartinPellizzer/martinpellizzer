Schaefferia pilosa

Schaefferia pilosa, commonly known as the woolly schaefferia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of thick, woolly leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
