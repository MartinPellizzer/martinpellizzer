Sclerocactus spinosior var. blainei

Sclerocactus spinosior var. blainei, commonly known as the Blaine's spiny cactus, is a rare and distinctive member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer.

This variety is known for its slow growth and requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
