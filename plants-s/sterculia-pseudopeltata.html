Sterculia pseudopeltata

Sterculia pseudopeltata, commonly known as the Australian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features large, showy, pentagonal flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
