Symphyotrichum praealtum var. nebraskense

Symphyotrichum praealtum var. nebraskense, commonly known as the Nebraska heath aster, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape. It produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers in the late summer and early fall.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms.
