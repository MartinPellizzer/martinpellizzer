Salix stylaris var. abortiva

Salix stylaris var. abortiva, commonly known as the abortive willow, is a variant of the stylaris willow, characterized by its unique floral morphology.

This plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often near streams or in wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in catkins, which are typical of willow species.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small wildlife.
