Spiraea bethlehemensis alba

Spiraea bethlehemensis alba, commonly known as white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has arching, slender stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
