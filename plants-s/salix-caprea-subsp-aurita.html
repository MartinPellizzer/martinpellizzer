Salix caprea subsp. aurita

Salix caprea subsp.

aurita, commonly known as the golden willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a finely toothed margin, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
