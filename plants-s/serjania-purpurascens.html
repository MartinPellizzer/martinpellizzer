Serjania purpurascens

Serjania purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered serjania, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their aromatic scent, which attracts various pollinators such as bees and birds.
