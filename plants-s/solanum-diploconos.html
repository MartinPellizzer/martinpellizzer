Solanum diploconos

Solanum diploconos, commonly known as the purple potato plant, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, purple fruits that are edible and have a slightly sweet and nutty flavor. Its leaves are broad and hairy, and it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Solanum diploconos is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal.
