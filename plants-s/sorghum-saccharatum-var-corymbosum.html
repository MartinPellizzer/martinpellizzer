Sorghum saccharatum var. corymbosum

Sorghum saccharatum var. corymbosum, commonly known as sweet sorghum, is a tall, drought-tolerant grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces tall, branched stalks with dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its high sugar content in the stalks, making it a valuable crop for biofuel and syrup production. Its leaves are broad and serrated, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This variety is also used in livestock feed and has historical significance in traditional agriculture.
