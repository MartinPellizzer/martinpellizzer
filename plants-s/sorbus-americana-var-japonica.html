Sorbus americana var. japonica

Sorbus americana var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
