Symplocos obtusa var. obtusa

Symplocos obtusa var. obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf waxflower, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
