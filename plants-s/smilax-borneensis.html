Smilax borneensis

Smilax borneensis, commonly known as Bornean smilax, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spadices, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a waxy coating.

Smilax borneensis is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
