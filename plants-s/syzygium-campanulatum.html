Syzygium campanulatum

Syzygium campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface.

Syzygium campanulatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and aromatic properties.
