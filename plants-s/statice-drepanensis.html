Statice drepanensis

Statice drepanensis, commonly known as the Greek sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salty air and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Statice drepanensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
