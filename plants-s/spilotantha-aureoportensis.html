Spilotantha aureoportensis

Spilotantha aureoportensis, commonly known as the golden port wine cactus, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a compact, branched structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in drought conditions.
