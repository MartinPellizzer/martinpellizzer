Sieversia radiata

Sieversia radiata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Sieversia radiata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
