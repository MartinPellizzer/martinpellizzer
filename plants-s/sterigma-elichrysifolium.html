Sterigma elichrysifolium

Sterigma elichrysifolium, commonly known as the golden fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its intricate, feathery fronds that resemble a delicate fern.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests. The plant's leaves are compound, with multiple small, lance-shaped leaflets arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in spadices, and it produces edible, spore-like structures.

The botanical significance of Sterigma elichrysifolium lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
