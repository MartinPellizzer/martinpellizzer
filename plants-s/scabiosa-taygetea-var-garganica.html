Scabiosa taygetea var. garganica

Scabiosa taygetea var. garganica, commonly known as the Garganica Scabious, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 60 cm tall, adorned with clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
