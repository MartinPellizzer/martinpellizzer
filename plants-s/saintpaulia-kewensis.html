Saintpaulia kewensis

Saintpaulia kewensis, commonly known as the Kew African violet, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of East Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is closely related to other African violets. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Saintpaulia kewensis is popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
