Sideritis soluta subsp. hildae

Sideritis soluta subsp.

hildae, commonly known as the Hildae rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant is distinguished by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
