Solanum somalense

Solanum somalense, commonly known as Somali nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa, particularly Somalia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are yellow when ripe.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to arid environments.
