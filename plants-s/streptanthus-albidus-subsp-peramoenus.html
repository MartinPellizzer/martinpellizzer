Streptanthus albidus subsp. peramoenus

Streptanthus albidus subsp.

peramoenus, commonly known as the white-flowered bladderpod, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial native to California, characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces pods that are inflated and ribbed, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
