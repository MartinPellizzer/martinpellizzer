Schismatoglottis mira

Schismatoglottis mira, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their intricate structure and vibrant coloration.

Schismatoglottis mira is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
