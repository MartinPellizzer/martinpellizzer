Solanum seedii

Solanum seedii, commonly known as the Seed's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Solanum seedii is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are usually ovate or lanceolate in shape.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.
