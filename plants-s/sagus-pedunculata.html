Sagus pedunculata

Sagus pedunculata, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters called cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Sagus pedunculata is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
