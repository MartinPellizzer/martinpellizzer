Schinus terebinthifolia

Schinus terebinthifolia, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant features compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is known for its aromatic, resinous scent.

Schinus terebinthifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has various traditional uses in herbal medicine.
