Sida incarnata

Sida incarnata, commonly known as Bladder Campion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sida incarnata is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
