Sanguisorba minor f. stenolopha

Sanguisorba minor f. stenolopha, commonly known as narrow-leaved sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear small, clustered inflorescences of pink to reddish flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
