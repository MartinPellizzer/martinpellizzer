Sedum microsepalum

Sedum microsepalum, commonly known as the dwarf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and drought tolerance, it is ideal for container gardening and landscape design in arid climates.
