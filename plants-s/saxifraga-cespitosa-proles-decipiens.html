Saxifraga cespitosa proles decipiens

Saxifraga cespitosa proles decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are typically hairy and adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its flowers are usually white or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and nutrient-poor soils, making it a common sight in mountainous and tundra habitats.
