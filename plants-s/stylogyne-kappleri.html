Stylogyne kappleri

Stylogyne kappleri, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, aiding in wind dispersal.

Stylogyne kappleri is often found in the fynbos biome, where it contributes to the region's rich biodiversity.
