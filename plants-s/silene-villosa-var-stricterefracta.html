Silene villosa var. stricterefracta

Silene villosa var. stricterefracta, commonly known as the hairy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows.

Silene villosa var. stricterefracta is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates.
