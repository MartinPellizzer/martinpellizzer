Saponaria cyprica

Saponaria cyprica, commonly known as Cyprus soapwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its root system is fibrous, and it is known for producing a soapy sap, which is a characteristic of the genus Saponaria.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
