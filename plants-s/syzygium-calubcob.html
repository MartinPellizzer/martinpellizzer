Syzygium calubcob

Syzygium calubcob, commonly known as the Java apple or *Syzygium malaccense*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in its native region.
