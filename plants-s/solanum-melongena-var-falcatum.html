Solanum melongena var. falcatum

Solanum melongena var. falcatum, commonly known as the Turkish eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant with distinctive, elongated, and curved fruits.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its thick, glossy skin and deep purple color. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its unique shape and flavor. It prefers warm climates and well-drained soil, and it is typically harvested when the fruits are still firm and immature.

This variety is valued for its culinary versatility and ornamental appeal in gardens.
