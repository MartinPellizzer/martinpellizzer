Syzygium palghatense

Syzygium palghatense, commonly known as the Nilavu or the Nilavu flower, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Western Ghats of southern India and is known for its fragrant, white flowers that bloom during the winter season. The plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

Syzygium palghatense is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in local traditions.
