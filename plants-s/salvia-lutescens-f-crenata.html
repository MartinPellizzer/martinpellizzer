Salvia lutescens f. crenata

Salvia lutescens f. crenata, commonly known as the crested purple sage, is a hybrid form of the salvia lutescens species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often hairy leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the southwestern United States and thrives in well-drained soils.

This ornamental plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
