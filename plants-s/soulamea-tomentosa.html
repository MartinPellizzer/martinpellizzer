Soulamea tomentosa

Soulamea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly soulamea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Soulamea tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
