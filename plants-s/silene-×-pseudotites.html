Silene × pseudotites

Silene × pseudotites, commonly known as the hybrid campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules as its fruit type.

Silene × pseudotites is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
