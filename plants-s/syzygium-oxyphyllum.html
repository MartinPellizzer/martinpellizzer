Syzygium oxyphyllum

Syzygium oxyphyllum, commonly known as the rose apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which resembles a small apple, has a pinkish-red skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
