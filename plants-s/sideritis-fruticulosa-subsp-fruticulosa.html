Sideritis fruticulosa subsp. fruticulosa

Sideritis fruticulosa subsp.

fruticulosa, commonly known as the Greek ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
