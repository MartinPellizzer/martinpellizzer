Synotoma comosum

Synotoma comosum, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual contrast to its fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Synotoma comosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
