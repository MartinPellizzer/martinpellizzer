Susarium segethi

Susarium segethi, commonly known as the grain weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have a serrated margin.

Susarium segethi is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which contributes to its invasive potential in certain regions.
