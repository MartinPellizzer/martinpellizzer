Salvia semiscaposa

Salvia semiscaposa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
