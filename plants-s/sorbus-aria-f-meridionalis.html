Sorbus aria f. meridionalis

Sorbus aria f. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits called pomes, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
