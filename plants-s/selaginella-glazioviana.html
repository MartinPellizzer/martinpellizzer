Selaginella glazioviana

Selaginella glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are borne on the upper stems and produce spores.

Selaginella glazioviana is native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Argentina.
