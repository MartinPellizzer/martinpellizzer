Solanum giftbergense

Solanum giftbergense, commonly known as the Giftberg nightshade, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its fruit is a globose berry that ripens to a deep red, containing numerous seeds.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
