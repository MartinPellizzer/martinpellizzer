Saussurea takhtadganii

Saussurea takhtadganii, commonly known as the Takhtadganii gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, woolly foliage that helps it withstand harsh alpine conditions. The plant produces yellowish to brownish flower heads, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
