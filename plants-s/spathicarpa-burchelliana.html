Spathicarpa burchelliana

Spathicarpa burchelliana, commonly known as the red spider flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble spider-like structures, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered at the base of the bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Spathicarpa burchelliana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and eye-catching appearance.
