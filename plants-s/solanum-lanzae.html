Solanum lanzae

Solanum lanzae, commonly known as the lanceleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, typically yellow or white flowers. Solanum lanzae is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are sometimes used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
