Salvia honigbergeri

Salvia honigbergeri, commonly known as Honigberger's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native gardens.
