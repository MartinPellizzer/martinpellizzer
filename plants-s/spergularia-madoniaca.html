Spergularia madoniaca

Spergularia madoniaca, commonly known as the Madonniaca sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal or inland dune environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Spergularia madoniaca is also known for its ability to colonize disturbed areas, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological succession.
