Syringa villosa var. aurea

Syringa villosa var. aurea, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
