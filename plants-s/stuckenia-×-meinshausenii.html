Stuckenia × meinshausenii

Stuckenia × meinshausenii, commonly known as the hybrid water plantain, is a wetland plant belonging to the Alismataceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Stuckenia × meinshausenii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and ability to stabilize aquatic soils.
