Spiranthes annua

Spiranthes annua, commonly known as the common spiraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
