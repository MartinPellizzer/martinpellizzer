Sida arcuata

Sida arcuata, commonly known as the silky sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Sida arcuata is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
