Sideritis × candelii var. herbetteana

Sideritis × candelii var. herbetteana, commonly known as the Corsican ironweed, is a hybrid plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
