Swartzia aymardii

Swartzia aymardii, commonly known as the Aymard's Swartzia, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Swartzia aymardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
