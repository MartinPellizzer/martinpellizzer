Searsia longipes var. schinoides

Searsia longipes var. schinoides, commonly known as the silver cotoneaster, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red berries that are a food source for various birds. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

The variety schinoides is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of Searsia longipes.
