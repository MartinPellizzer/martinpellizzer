Scleria uleana

Scleria uleana, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Scleria uleana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
