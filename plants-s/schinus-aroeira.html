Schinus aroeira

Schinus aroeira, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or aroeira, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, red berries are edible and contain a high concentration of oils.

Schinus aroeira is also known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its use in ornamental landscaping.
