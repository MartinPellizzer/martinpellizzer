Spinifex hirsutus

Spinifex hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy spinifex, is a hardy, low-growing perennial grass native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and tufted growth habit, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous roots enable it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperatures.

Spinifex hirsutus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
