Sedum himalense

Sedum himalense, commonly known as the Himalayan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive in harsh conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum himalense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
