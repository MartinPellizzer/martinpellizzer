Solidago villosicarpa

Solidago villosicarpa, commonly known as the hairy stem goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
