Saldinia obtusata

Saldinia obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical stems and broad, flat leaves. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Saldinia obtusata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
