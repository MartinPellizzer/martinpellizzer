Spondias chinensis

Spondias chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese satinash, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and mango. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
