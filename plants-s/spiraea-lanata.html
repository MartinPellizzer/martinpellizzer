Spiraea lanata

Spiraea lanata, commonly known as the downy spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems covered in soft, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Spiraea lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
