Styrax mallotifolius

Styrax mallotifolius, commonly known as the Chinese styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is thick and cork-like.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery and herbal remedies.
