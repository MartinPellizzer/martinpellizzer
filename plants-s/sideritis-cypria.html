Sideritis cypria

Sideritis cypria, commonly known as Cyprus sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Cyprus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it often grows in rocky or dry soils.

Sideritis cypria is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
