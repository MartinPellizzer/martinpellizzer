Spigelia anthelmia var. peruviana

Spigelia anthelmia var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian flame vine, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is often cultivated for its showy floral display.
