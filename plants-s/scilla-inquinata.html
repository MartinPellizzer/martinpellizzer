Scilla inquinata

Scilla inquinata, commonly known as the purple scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in early spring, typically before the leaves emerge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scilla inquinata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
