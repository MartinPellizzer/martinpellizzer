Southwellia parviflora

Southwellia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Southwellia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in specialty gardens.
