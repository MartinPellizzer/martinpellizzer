Saxifraga tuberosa

Saxifraga tuberosa, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used for erosion control.

Saxifraga tuberosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
