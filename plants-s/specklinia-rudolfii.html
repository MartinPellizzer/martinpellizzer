Specklinia rudolfii

Specklinia rudolfii, commonly known as the Rudolf's spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its humid, shaded environment.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, Specklinia rudolfii is considered a conservation concern.
