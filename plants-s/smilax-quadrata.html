Smilax quadrata

Smilax quadrata, commonly known as the rough-stemmed greenbrier, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
