Sempervivum marmoreum var. dinaricum

Sempervivum marmoreum var. dinaricum, commonly known as the Dinaric houseleek, is a compact, succulent plant native to the Balkan region.

It features dense, rosette-shaped foliage with thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive white or pinkish bloom. The leaves are typically bluish-green with a waxy texture, and the plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in gardens and as a houseplant.
