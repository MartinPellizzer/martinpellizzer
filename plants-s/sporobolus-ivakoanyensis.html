Sporobolus ivakoanyensis

Sporobolus ivakoanyensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spikerush, is a grass-like plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Sporobolus ivakoanyensis is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
