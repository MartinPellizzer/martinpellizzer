Struthiola dodecandra

Struthiola dodecandra, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a bushy growth habit with a woody base and branching stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a rough, sandpaper-like texture.

Struthiola dodecandra is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
