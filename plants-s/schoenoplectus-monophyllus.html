Schoenoplectus monophyllus

Schoenoplectus monophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tip, with a fibrous, grass-like appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually pink to reddish in color and appear in late summer.

Schoenoplectus monophyllus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
