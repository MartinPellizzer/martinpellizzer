Sinocalamus copelandii

Sinocalamus copelandii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense foliage. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its culms are hollow and segmented, a typical feature of bamboo species.

Sinocalamus copelandii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
