Soulangia ledifolia

Soulangia ledifolia, commonly known as the Indian broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent, characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
