Satureja lachnophylla

Satureja lachnophylla, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Satureja lachnophylla is also valued in culinary applications for its pungent, thyme-like scent.
