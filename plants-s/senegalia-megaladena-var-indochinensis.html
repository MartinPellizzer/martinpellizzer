Senegalia megaladena var. indochinensis

Senegalia megaladena var. indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochinese mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
