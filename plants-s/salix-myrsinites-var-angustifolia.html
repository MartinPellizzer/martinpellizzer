Salix myrsinites var. angustifolia

Salix myrsinites var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and riparian zones. The plant has slender, often zigzagging stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
