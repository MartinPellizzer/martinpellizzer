Sauromatum diversifolium

Sauromatum diversifolium, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, making it a dramatic addition to any landscape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storage and survival in its native habitat.

While primarily grown for ornamental purposes, it is also valued for its starchy roots in some traditional culinary contexts.
