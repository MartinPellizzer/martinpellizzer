Scindapsus cannifolius

Scindapsus cannifolius, commonly known as the silver vine, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking silver veins that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Scindapsus cannifolius is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
