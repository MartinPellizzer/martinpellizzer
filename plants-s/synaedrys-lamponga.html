Synaedrys lamponga

Synaedrys lamponga, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals and a unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as under forest canopies. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting specific pollinators like bees and moths.

The leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
