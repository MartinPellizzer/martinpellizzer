Stipa rudis subsp. nervosa

Stipa rudis subsp.

nervosa, commonly known as the nervy feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. Stipa rudis subsp.

nervosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
