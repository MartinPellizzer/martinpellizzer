Senna birostris var. campiana

Senna birostris var. campiana, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
