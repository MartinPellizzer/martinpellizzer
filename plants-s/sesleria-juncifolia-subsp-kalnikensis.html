Sesleria juncifolia subsp. kalnikensis

Sesleria juncifolia subsp.

kalnikensis, commonly known as the Kalnikens mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense, low-growing clumps. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, with small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
