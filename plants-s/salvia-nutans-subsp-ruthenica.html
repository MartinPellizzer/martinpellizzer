Salvia nutans subsp. ruthenica

Salvia nutans subsp.

ruthenica, commonly known as the nodding sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that hang in clusters, typical of the Salvia genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
