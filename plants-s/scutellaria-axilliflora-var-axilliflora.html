Scutellaria axilliflora var. axilliflora

Scutellaria axilliflora var. axilliflora, commonly known as the axillary-flowered skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular blue to violet flowers from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
