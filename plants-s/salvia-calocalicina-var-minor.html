Salvia calocalicina var. minor

Salvia calocalicina var. minor, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period from late spring to early fall.
