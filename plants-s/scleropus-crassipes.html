Scleropus crassipes

Scleropus crassipes, commonly known as the thick-leafed spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its stems are typically short and branching, with a somewhat woolly texture.

Scleropus crassipes is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
