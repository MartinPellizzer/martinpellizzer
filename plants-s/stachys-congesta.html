Stachys congesta

Stachys congesta, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.

Stachys congesta is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
