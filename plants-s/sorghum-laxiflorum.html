Sorghum laxiflorum

Sorghum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered sorghum, is a species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a tall, grass-like plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically found at the top of the stem. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
