Sesleria confusa

Sesleria confusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Sesleria confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
