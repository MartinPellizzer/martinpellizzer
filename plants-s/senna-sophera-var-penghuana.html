Senna sophera var. penghuana

Senna sophera var. penghuana, commonly known as Penghu senna, is a flowering plant native to the Penghu Islands in Taiwan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline soils, making it a unique species in its native habitat.
