Selaginella hollrungii

Selaginella hollrungii, commonly known as the dwarf spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its delicate, scale-like leaves and sporangia-bearing structures. This plant is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices. It has a unique ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The plant's structure includes both microphylls and megaphylls, showcasing evolutionary adaptations in its leaf morphology.
