Symphoricarpos orbiculatus f. aureoreticulatus

Symphoricarpos orbiculatus f. aureoreticulatus, commonly known as the goldenleaf snowberry, is a variant of the snowberry plant characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States, typically found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces small, white berries that are clustered in pairs, which are edible but have a bland taste. Its foliage is notable for its vivid coloration, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The goldenleaf snowberry thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
