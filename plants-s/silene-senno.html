Silene senno

Silene senno, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to red. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Silene senno is known for its rapid blooming cycle, often opening its flowers in the morning and closing them by late afternoon.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
