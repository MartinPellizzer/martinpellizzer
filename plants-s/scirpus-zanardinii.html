Scirpus zanardinii

Scirpus zanardinii, commonly known as the Zanardinii bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant features tall, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Scirpus zanardinii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
