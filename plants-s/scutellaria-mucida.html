Scutellaria mucida

Scutellaria mucida, commonly known as sticky skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in a sticky substance, which gives it its common name.

Scutellaria mucida is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.
