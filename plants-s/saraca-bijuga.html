Saraca bijuga

Saraca bijuga, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The plant is noted for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods and can be used in traditional medicine.

Saraca bijuga thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
