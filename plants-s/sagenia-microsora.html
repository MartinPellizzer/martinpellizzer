Sagenia microsora

Sagenia microsora, commonly known as the small-leaved sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Sagenia microsora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
