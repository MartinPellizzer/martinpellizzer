Sasaella bitchuensis var. tashirozentaroana

Sasaella bitchuensis var. tashirozentaroana, commonly known as the Tashirozentaro bamboo, is a variant of the Japanese bamboo species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is a clumping bamboo that forms dense thickets, characterized by its slender, green shoots and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in shaded, well-drained soils. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in its natural habitat.
