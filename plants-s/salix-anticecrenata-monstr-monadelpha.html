Salix anticecrenata monstr. monadelpha

Salix anticecrenata monstr.

monadelpha, commonly known as the giant weeping willow, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a dramatic, flowing appearance. Botanically, it belongs to the Salicaceae family and is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a bluish-green upper surface and a pale underside, and it produces catkins in early spring.

Its rapid growth rate and unique form make it a popular choice for landscaping, though it requires careful management due to its vigorous nature.
