Solanum calycopogon

Solanum calycopogon, commonly known as the Florida nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves and berries contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum calycopogon is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
