Shorea assamica f. philippinensis

Shorea assamica f. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine rosewood, is a tropical tree species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood with a rich, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, thriving in humid, warm climates with high rainfall. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth upper surface with a slightly hairy underside.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.
