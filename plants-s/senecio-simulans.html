Senecio simulans

Senecio simulans, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
