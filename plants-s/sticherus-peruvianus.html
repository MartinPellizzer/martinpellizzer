Sticherus peruvianus

Sticherus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian sticherus, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of South America, particularly in Peru and Ecuador. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or white, and are borne in clusters.

Sticherus peruvianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
