Salvia lavandulifolia subsp. approximata

Salvia lavandulifolia subsp.

approximata, commonly known as lemon basil or Portuguese basil, is a compact, aromatic shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems.

The plant has fragrant, oval-shaped leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and pleasant fragrance.
