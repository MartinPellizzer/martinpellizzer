Salix helix var. olivacea

Salix helix var. olivacea, commonly known as the olive willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the British Isles.

It features slender, arching branches covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and silvery-white underneath. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near waterways.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden planting.
