Syagrus tessmannii

Syagrus tessmannii, commonly known as the royal palm, is a tall, slender palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that form a crown at the top. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright orange or red fruits. Syagrus tessmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and as a symbol of tropical beauty.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
