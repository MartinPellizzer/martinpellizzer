Senecio abruptus

Senecio abruptus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Senecio abruptus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
