Salvia conzattii

Salvia conzattii, commonly known as Conzatt's sage, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces striking violet to purple flower spikes during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant gardens and native plant collections.
