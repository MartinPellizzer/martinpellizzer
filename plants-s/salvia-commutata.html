Salvia commutata

Salvia commutata, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a gray-green hue, and it produces spikes of blue to purple flowers.

Salvia commutata is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
