Setaria kagerensis

Setaria kagerensis, commonly known as the Kager's foxtail, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
