Sapindus anamensis

Sapindus anamensis, commonly known as the Anamensis soapnut, is a small tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces fruit that turns from green to red as it matures, and the seeds are encased in a fleshy, hairy aril. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the fruit is valued for its saponin content, which has cleansing properties.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
