Scabiosa angustifolia

Scabiosa angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
