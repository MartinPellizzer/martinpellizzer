Syzygium rubescens var. koroense

Syzygium rubescens var. koroense, commonly known as the Koroense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Koro in the Bismarck Archipelago.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in local cuisines.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic variation and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
