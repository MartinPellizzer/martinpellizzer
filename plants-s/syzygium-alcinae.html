Syzygium alcinae

Syzygium alcinae, commonly known as the Madagascar apple or *Eugenia alcinae*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble apples in appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

Syzygium alcinae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
