Shirakia elliptica

Shirakia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature.

Shirakia elliptica is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
