Solidago recurvata

Solidago recurvata, commonly known as recurved goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The blooming period occurs in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to wetland landscapes.
