Scaevola platyphylla

Scaevola platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid regions.
