Senecio paniculatus

Senecio paniculatus, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that appear in panicles. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, almost leathery texture. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Senecio paniculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
