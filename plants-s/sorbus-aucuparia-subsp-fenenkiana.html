Sorbus aucuparia subsp. fenenkiana

Sorbus aucuparia subsp.

fenenkiana, commonly known as the Siberian rowan, is a subspecies native to the cold climates of eastern Europe and western Asia. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions and boreal forests.

The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that mature in the fall. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds.
