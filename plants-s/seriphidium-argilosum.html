Seriphidium argilosum

Seriphidium argilosum, commonly known as clayweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or clay soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

Seriphidium argilosum is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
