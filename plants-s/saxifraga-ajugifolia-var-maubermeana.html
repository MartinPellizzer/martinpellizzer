Saxifraga ajugifolia var. maubermeana

Saxifraga ajugifolia var. maubermeana, commonly known as the Maubermean saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
