Selliguea glauca

Selliguea glauca, commonly known as the blue moss, is a non-vascular plant belonging to the family Selligueaceae.

It is a type of liverwort, characterized by its simple, flattened thallus that often forms dense, carpet-like growths on moist surfaces. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest floors, rock crevices, and stream banks. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Selliguea glauca plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various invertebrates.
