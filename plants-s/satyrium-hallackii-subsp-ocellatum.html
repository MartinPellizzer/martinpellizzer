Satyrium hallackii subsp. ocellatum

Satyrium hallackii subsp.

ocellatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spotted or mottled leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy or open habitats, often in calcareous soils. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with purple or brown markings, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
