Salvia forreri

Salvia forreri, commonly known as the Forreri sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, often emitting a mild minty scent.

Salvia forreri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
