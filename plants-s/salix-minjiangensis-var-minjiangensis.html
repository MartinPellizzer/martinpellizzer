Salix minjiangensis var. minjiangensis

Salix minjiangensis var. minjiangensis, commonly known as the Minjiang willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
