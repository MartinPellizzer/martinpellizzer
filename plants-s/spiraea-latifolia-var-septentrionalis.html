Spiraea latifolia var. septentrionalis

Spiraea latifolia var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in small, oval leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its most notable characteristic is the display of clusters of white to pinkish flowers in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
