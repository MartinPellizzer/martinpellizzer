Scirpus × mariqueter

Scirpus × mariqueter, commonly known as the marsh clubrush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water areas. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Scirpus × mariqueter plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
