Setaria macrophylla

Setaria macrophylla, commonly known as giant foxtail, is a tall annual grass species native to the warm temperate and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and spike-like inflorescences that contain small, golden-brown florets. The plant typically grows to heights of 1 to 3 meters, making it a prominent feature in both natural and agricultural settings. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, often forming dense stands in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role as a weed in agricultural fields.
