Sideritis hyssopifolia var. laxispica

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. laxispica, commonly known as the woolly ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
