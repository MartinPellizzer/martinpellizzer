Setaria abyssinica var. breviseta

Setaria abyssinica var. breviseta, commonly known as the short-haired finger grass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny spikelets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential forage plant for livestock.
