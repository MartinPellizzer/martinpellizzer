Solanum mariannae

Solanum mariannae, commonly known as the Marianas nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the islands of the Mariana Archipelago.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its fruits are berries that start green and mature to a dark blue or black color, containing several seeds.

Solanum mariannae is adapted to coastal and rocky habitats, often found in dry, sunny environments.
