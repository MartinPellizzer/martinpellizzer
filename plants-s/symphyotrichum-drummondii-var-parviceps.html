Symphyotrichum drummondii var. parviceps

Symphyotrichum drummondii var. parviceps, commonly known as Drummond's ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, pale purple to white composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
