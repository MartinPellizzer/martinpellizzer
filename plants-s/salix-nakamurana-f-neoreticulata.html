Salix nakamurana f. neoreticulata

Salix nakamurana f. neoreticulata, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a unique variant of the willow species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance. The plant has small, elongated leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and pale or whitish underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
