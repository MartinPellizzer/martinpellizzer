Scaevola helmsii

Scaevola helmsii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant features narrow, silver-green leaves that are arranged in a distinctive opposite pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Scaevola helmsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
