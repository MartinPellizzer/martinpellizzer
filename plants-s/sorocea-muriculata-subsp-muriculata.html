Sorocea muriculata subsp. muriculata

Sorocea muriculata subsp.

muriculata, commonly known as the roughleaf sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves with a rough, serrated texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in acidic soils and its preference for partial to full shade.
