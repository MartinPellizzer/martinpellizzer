Solea itoubou

Solea itoubou, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers emit a sweet, fragrant scent to lure pollinators in the darkness.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
