Semecarpus australiensis var. obtusifolius

Semecarpus australiensis var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are broad and ovate with a smooth margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often appearing rough and dark in color.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained areas.
