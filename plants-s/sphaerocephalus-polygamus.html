Sphaerocephalus polygamus

Sphaerocephalus polygamus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Sphaerocephalus polygamus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
