Swartzia nuda

Swartzia nuda, commonly known as the naked swartzia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Swartzia nuda is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
