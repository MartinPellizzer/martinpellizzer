Styrax dasyanthus var. cinerascens

Styrax dasyanthus var. cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-colored styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. The bark is typically grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
