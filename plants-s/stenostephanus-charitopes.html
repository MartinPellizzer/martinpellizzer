Stenostephanus charitopes

Stenostephanus charitopes, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a star-shaped pattern, which are arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique and vibrant floral display.
