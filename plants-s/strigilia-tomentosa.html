Strigilia tomentosa

Strigilia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly strigil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
