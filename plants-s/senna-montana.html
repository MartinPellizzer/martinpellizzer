Senna montana

Senna montana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Senna montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
