Sauromatum punctatum

Sauromatum punctatum, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with a yellow center, resembling spiders, hence its common name. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its cultural significance in various regions.
