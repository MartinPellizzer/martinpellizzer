Solidago wrightii var. orientalis

Solidago wrightii var. orientalis, commonly known as the eastern goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the late season.
