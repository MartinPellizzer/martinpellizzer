Serpicula epilithes

Serpicula epilithes, commonly known as the rock lichen, is a species of lichen that thrives on rocky substrates.

It belongs to the family Parmeliaceae and is characterized by its dense, crust-like growth form. The thallus is typically pale green to yellowish, with a smooth and often glossy surface. It is widely distributed in temperate regions and is often found on limestone or granite surfaces.

This lichen plays a crucial role in soil formation and serves as an indicator of air quality.
