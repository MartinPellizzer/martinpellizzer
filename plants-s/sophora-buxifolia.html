Sophora buxifolia

Sophora buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that resemble those of boxwood, hence its common name. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a legume containing several seeds.

Sophora buxifolia is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
