Senra nubica var. scassellatii

Senra nubica var. scassellatii, commonly known as the mountain senra, is a rare and striking plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists alike.
