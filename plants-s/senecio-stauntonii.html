Senecio stauntonii

Senecio stauntonii, commonly known as the St. Augustine lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Senecio stauntonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
