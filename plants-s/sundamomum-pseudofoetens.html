Sundamomum pseudofoetens

Sundamomum pseudofoetens, commonly known as the stinkwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The tree is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
