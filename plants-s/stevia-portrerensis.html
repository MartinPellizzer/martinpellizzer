Stevia portrerensis

Stevia portrerensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sweet leaf, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products. Stevia portrerensis thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value, along with its medicinal properties, has made it a popular choice in both horticulture and alternative medicine.
