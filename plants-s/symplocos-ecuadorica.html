Symplocos ecuadorica

Symplocos ecuadorica, commonly known as the Ecuadorian wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Symplocos ecuadorica is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
