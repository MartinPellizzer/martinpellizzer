Scilla hispanica var. carnea

Scilla hispanica var. carnea, commonly known as the pink squill, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of striking pink to lilac flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and vibrant flower color.
