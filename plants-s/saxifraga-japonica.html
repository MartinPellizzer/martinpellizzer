Saxifraga japonica

Saxifraga japonica, commonly known as Japanese saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
