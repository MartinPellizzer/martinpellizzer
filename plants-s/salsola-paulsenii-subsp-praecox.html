Salsola paulsenii subsp. praecox

Salsola paulsenii subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to arid and saline environments. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and inland salt marshes, where it tolerates high soil salinity and low moisture. Its fleshy stems and leaves store water, enabling survival in harsh conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
