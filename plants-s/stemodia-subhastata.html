Stemodia subhastata

Stemodia subhastata, commonly known as the hairy stemodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, glandular stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, adding ornamental value.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
