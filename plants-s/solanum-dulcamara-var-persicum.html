Solanum dulcamara var. persicum

Solanum dulcamara var. persicum, commonly known as Persian nightshade, is a variant of the common bindweed, characterized by its climbing habit and showy, violet-blue flowers.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it has a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor.
