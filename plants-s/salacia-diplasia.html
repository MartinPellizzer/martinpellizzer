Salacia diplasia

Salacia diplasia, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its botanical features include woody stems, clustered inflorescences, and a complex root system adapted to wet, tropical soils.

Salacia diplasia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
