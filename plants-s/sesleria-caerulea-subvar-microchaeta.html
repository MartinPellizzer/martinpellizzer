Sesleria caerulea subvar. microchaeta

Sesleria caerulea subvar. microchaeta, commonly known as the small-bract bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or white flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
