Scrophularia oblongifolia subsp. umbrosa

Scrophularia oblongifolia subsp.

umbrosa, commonly known as the shady scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
