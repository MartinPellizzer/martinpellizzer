Sigesbeckia bojeri

Sigesbeckia bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
