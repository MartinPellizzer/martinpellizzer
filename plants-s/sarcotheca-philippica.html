Sarcotheca philippica

Sarcotheca philippica, commonly known as the Philippine sarcococca, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sarcococceae and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces unique, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
