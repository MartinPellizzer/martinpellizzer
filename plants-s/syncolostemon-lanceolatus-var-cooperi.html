Syncolostemon lanceolatus var. cooperi

Syncolostemon lanceolatus var. cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's spurge, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and low maintenance requirements.
