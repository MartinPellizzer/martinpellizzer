Syneilesis akagii

Syneilesis akagii, commonly known as the Japanese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, three-lobed fruits that turn red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and attractive foliage.
