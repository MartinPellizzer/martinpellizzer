Schizophragma choufenianum

Schizophragma choufenianum, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to China.

It features slender, woody stems that twine around supports and produce fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-10 cm in length. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Schizophragma choufenianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
