Stemmodontia reticulata

Stemmodontia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined stemmodontia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in a dense network of fine veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Stemmodontia reticulata is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
