Sphaeroclinium nigellifolium

Sphaeroclinium nigellifolium, commonly known as the blackleaf globemallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, globular growth habit and dark green, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
