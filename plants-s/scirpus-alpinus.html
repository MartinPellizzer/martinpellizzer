Scirpus alpinus

Scirpus alpinus, commonly known as alpine bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and alpine meadows. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in short, stiff hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flower heads that are wind-pollinated.

Scirpus alpinus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
