Spartium corsicum

Spartium corsicum, commonly known as Corsican clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and woodland edges. The plant features small, trifoliate leaves and produces pink or violet flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to soil stabilization.

Spartium corsicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
