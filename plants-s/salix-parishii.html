Salix parishii

Salix parishii, commonly known as the Parish willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and catkin-like flowers, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wetlands. Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple stems forming a dense, bushy appearance.

Salix parishii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
