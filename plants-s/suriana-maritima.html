Suriana maritima

Suriana maritima, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, often found in dune environments. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to yellow or red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are usually alternate and can be either simple or lobed, adapting to coastal conditions.

Suriana maritima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize dunes through its extensive root system.
