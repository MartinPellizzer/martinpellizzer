Striga klingii

Striga klingii, commonly known as the desert witchweed, is a parasitic flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and lacks chlorophyll, relying entirely on host plants for nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a slender, root-like structure called a haustorium that attaches to the roots of its host. It is often found growing on the roots of grasses and other herbaceous plants.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly weaken its host, sometimes leading to reduced crop yields in agricultural areas.
