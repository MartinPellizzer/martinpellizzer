Secale montanum var. ciliatoglume

Secale montanum var. ciliatoglume, commonly known as the mountain ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive hairy margin. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in a panicle, bearing small, two-lobed florets. Its flowers are typically greenish or brownish, and it produces small, three-nerved seeds.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
