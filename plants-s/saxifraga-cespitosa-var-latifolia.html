Saxifraga cespitosa var. latifolia

Saxifraga cespitosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are broad and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are clustered in dense, upright inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
