Solanum torvum var. persicifolium

Solanum torvum var. persicifolium, commonly known as the purple eggplant or wild eggplant, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to purple flowers. The plant produces egg-shaped, fleshy fruits that range in color from white to purple, often with a mild, bitter taste. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental and medicinal uses.

This species is also considered an invasive plant in some regions due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
