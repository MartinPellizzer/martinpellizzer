Senecio gossweileri

Senecio gossweileri, commonly known as the Gossweiler's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
