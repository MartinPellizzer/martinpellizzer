Sprengelia monticola

Sprengelia monticola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically leathery and dark green, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Sprengelia monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
