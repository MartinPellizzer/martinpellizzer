Solanum capense var. sodomaeodes

Solanum capense var. sodomaeodes, commonly known as the Sodom apple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are highly toxic to humans and animals. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed and is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
