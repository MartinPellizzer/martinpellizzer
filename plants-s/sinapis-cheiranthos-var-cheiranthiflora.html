Sinapis cheiranthos var. cheiranthiflora

Sinapis cheiranthos var. cheiranthiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, yellow flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces round, spherical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and agricultural research.
