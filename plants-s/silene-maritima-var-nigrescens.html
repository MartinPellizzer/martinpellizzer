Silene maritima var. nigrescens

Silene maritima var. nigrescens, commonly known as sea pink or coastal pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, thriving in saline and well-drained environments. It has hairy, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves that help it withstand coastal winds and salt spray.

Silene maritima var. nigrescens plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and supporting coastal biodiversity.
