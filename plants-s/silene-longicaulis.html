Silene longicaulis

Silene longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stalked catchfly, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and pairs of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Silene longicaulis is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is typically perennial.
