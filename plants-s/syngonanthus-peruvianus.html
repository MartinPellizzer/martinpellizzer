Syngonanthus peruvianus

Syngonanthus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, grayish bark and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Syngonanthus peruvianus is well-suited for tropical gardens and can grow quite tall, reaching up to 10 meters in height.
