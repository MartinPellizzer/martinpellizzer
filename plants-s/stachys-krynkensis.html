Stachys krynkensis

Stachys krynkensis, commonly known as Krynk's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and often have a distinctive purple or pink coloration.

Stachys krynkensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
