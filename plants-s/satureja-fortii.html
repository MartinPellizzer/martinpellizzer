Satureja fortii

Satureja fortii, commonly known as white savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in cooking. Satureja fortii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contributing to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
