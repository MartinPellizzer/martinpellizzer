Stelis mirandae

Stelis mirandae, commonly known as the Miranda's stelis, is an epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme.

Stelis mirandae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
