Spergula pentandra subsp. flaccida

Spergula pentandra subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads.

This subspecies is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
