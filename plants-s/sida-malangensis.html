Sida malangensis

Sida malangensis, commonly known as Malayan sida, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Sida malangensis is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is not widely cultivated but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
