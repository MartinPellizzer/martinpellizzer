Sorghum elliottii

Sorghum elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's sorghum, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a tall, annual grass that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and panicles of small, whitish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species with drought tolerance. Its seeds are edible and have been used traditionally for food and animal feed.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes other cereal grasses.
