Solanum stipuloideum

Solanum stipuloideum, commonly known as the stipule-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, round berries that contain toxic compounds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by livestock and humans alike.
