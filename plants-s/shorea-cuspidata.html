Shorea cuspidata

Shorea cuspidata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
