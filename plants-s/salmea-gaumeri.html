Salmea gaumeri

Salmea gaumeri, commonly known as the Gaumer's salvia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Salmea gaumeri is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
