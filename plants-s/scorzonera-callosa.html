Scorzonera callosa

Scorzonera callosa, commonly known as the hairy scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that is often harvested for its edible qualities. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central disk.

Scorzonera callosa is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
