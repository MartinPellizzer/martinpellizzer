Sidalcea oregana subsp. spicata

Sidalcea oregana subsp.

spicata, commonly known as the spiked checkerbloom, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
