Sherardia arvensis var. walrawenii

Sherardia arvensis var. walrawenii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sorrel, is a variant of the broadleaf sorrel species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This variant is notable for its distinct leaf morphology and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
