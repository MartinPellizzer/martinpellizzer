Sclerocactus parviflorus f. terrae-canyonae

Sclerocactus parviflorus f. terrae-canyonae, commonly known as the Canyon Canyon Cactus, is a unique variant of the slender cholla cactus.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States, particularly in areas with rocky soils and canyon landscapes. The plant features a cylindrical stem covered in small, spiny tubercles, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient and specialized member of its ecosystem.
