Struthiopteris struthiopteris var. pensylvanica

Struthiopteris struthiopteris var. pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania shield fern, is a variant of the broad shield fern.

It is a deciduous fern characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive dark green color and a rough, textured surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

This variety is native to eastern North America and is often found in the northeastern United States.
