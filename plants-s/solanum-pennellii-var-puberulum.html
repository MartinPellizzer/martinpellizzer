Solanum pennellii var. puberulum

Solanum pennellii var. puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries containing toxic compounds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in scientific research due to its genetic traits.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in studies related to plant genetics and evolution.
