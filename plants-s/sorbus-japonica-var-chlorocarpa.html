Sorbus japonica var. chlorocarpa

Sorbus japonica var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in woodland or mountainous areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
