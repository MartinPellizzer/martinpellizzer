Solanum sprengelii

Solanum sprengelii, commonly known as the Sprengel's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
