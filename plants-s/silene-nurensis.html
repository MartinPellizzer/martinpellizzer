Silene nurensis

Silene nurensis, commonly known as the Nurensis campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and sturdy.

Silene nurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
