Saussurea bogedaensis

Saussurea bogedaensis, commonly known as the Bogeda Saussurea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, daisy-like flower heads. The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
