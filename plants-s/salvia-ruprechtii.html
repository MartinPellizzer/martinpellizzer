Salvia ruprechtii

Salvia ruprechtii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

Salvia ruprechtii is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
