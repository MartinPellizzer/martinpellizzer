Syzygium balsameum

Syzygium balsameum, commonly known as the balsam apple or wild mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, oval-shaped berry that turns from green to yellow when mature. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
