Suaeda dendroides

Suaeda dendroides, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Suaeda dendroides is adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
