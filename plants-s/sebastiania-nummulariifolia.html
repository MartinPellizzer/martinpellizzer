Sebastiania nummulariifolia

Sebastiania nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf sebastiania, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to round in shape and are arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
