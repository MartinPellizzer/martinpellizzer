Swertia utahensis

Swertia utahensis, commonly known as Utah gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high elevation areas, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Swertia utahensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
