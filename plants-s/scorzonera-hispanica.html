Scorzonera hispanica

Scorzonera hispanica, commonly known as Spanish scurvygrass, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and tuberous, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scorzonera hispanica thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.
