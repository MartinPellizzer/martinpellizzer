Solanum stenotomum var. chapina

Solanum stenotomum var. chapina, commonly known as Chapin's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

The species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.
