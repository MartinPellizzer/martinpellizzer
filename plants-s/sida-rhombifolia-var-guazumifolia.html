Sida rhombifolia var. guazumifolia

Sida rhombifolia var. guazumifolia, commonly known as the Guazumifolia sida, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to rhombic leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruit is a small capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in humid, warm climates.
