Sesleria alba subsp. voronovii

Sesleria alba subsp.

voronovii, commonly known as the white feather grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and fluffy, white plumes that give it a feathery appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it is noted for its ornamental value in gardens. Sesleria alba subsp.

voronovii is relatively drought-tolerant and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
