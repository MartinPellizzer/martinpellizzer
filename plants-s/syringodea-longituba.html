Syringodea longituba

Syringodea longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed syringodea, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular inflorescence that resembles a syringe, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Syringodea longituba is notable for its striking floral structure, which plays a role in attracting specific pollinators.
