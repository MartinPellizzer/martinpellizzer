Senecio racemulifer

Senecio racemulifer, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Senecio racemulifer is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
