Silvia

Silvia, commonly known as the silvery hair-grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Silvia is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.

Its botanical name, *Deschampsia cespitosa*, reflects its tufted growth habit and its classification within the Poaceae family.
