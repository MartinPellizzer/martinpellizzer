Senecio radiolatus subsp. antipodus

Senecio radiolatus subsp.

antipodus, commonly known as the Antipodean senecio, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is a perennial herb with a distinctive rosette of silver-green, woolly leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, branched clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in New Zealand's flora.
