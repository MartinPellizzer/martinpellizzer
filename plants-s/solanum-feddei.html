Solanum feddei

Solanum feddei, commonly known as the Fedde's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in the evening. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solasodine. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
