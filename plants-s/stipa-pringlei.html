Stipa pringlei

Stipa pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Stipa pringlei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
