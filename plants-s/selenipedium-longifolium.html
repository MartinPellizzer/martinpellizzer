Selenipedium longifolium

Selenipedium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved selenipedium, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often reaching several feet in length.

Selenipedium longifolium is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of conservation interest.
