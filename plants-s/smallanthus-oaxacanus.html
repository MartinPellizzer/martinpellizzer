Smallanthus oaxacanus

Smallanthus oaxacanus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
