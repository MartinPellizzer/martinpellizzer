Scabiosa fischeri

Scabiosa fischeri, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Scabiosa fischeri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
