Syzygium tonkinense

Syzygium tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release smooth, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
