Sesbania aegyptiaca var. concolor

Sesbania aegyptiaca var. concolor, commonly known as the Egyptian sesban, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
