Symphoricarpos ovatus

Symphoricarpos ovatus, commonly known as the snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
