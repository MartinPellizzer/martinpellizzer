Salix minjiangensis var. zhouquensis

Salix minjiangensis var. zhouquensis, commonly known as the Zhouqu willow, is a shrub or small tree native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan and Sichuan provinces.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
