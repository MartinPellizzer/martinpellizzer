Sericostachys scandens var. tomentosa

Sericostachys scandens var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy climbing bristlegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, climbing stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering occurs in summer, with pale green to brown spikelets that are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous nature.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
