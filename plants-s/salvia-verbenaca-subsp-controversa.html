Salvia verbenaca subsp. controversa

Salvia verbenaca subsp.

controversa, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to diverse landscapes.
