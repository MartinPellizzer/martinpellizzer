Solanum contumazaense

Solanum contumazaense, commonly known as the Contumaza nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or violet in color.

Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for certain bird species.
