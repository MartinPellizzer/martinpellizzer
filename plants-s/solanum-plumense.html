Solanum plumense

Solanum plumense, commonly known as the plumose nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, plum-like leaves that are velvety and deeply lobed. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, containing several seeds.

Solanum plumense is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
