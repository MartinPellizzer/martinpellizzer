Smilax sarumame

Smilax sarumame, commonly known as blackberry vine, is a woody perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into black, inedible berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is notable for its aggressive growth and ability to form dense thickets.
