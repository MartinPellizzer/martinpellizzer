Salvia theodori

Salvia theodori, commonly known as Theodore's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Salvia theodori is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is often used in native plant gardens.
