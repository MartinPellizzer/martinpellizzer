Silene pudibunda

Silene pudibunda, commonly known as the shy pink or blushing pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or ascending.

Silene pudibunda is noted for its ephemeral blooming period, making it a rare and short-lived floral display in its native environment.
