Styrax subheterotrichus

Styrax subheterotrichus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, hard, aromatic fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
