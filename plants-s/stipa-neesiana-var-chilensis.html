Stipa neesiana var. chilensis

Stipa neesiana var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that are often pink to reddish in hue, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance garden settings.
