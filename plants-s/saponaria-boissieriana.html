Saponaria boissieriana

Saponaria boissieriana, commonly known as the Boissier's soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces a milky sap that is mildly soapy in nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Saponaria boissieriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
