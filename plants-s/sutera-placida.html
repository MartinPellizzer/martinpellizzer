Sutera placida

Sutera placida, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sutera placida is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought-resistant properties.
