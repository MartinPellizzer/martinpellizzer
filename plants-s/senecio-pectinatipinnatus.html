Senecio pectinatipinnatus

Senecio pectinatipinnatus, commonly known as the woollyleaf senecio, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cone-like clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid climates.
