Sedum dasyphyllum var. oblongifolium

Sedum dasyphyllum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval or oblong in shape, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
