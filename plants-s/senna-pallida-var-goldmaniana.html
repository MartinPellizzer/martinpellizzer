Senna pallida var. goldmaniana

Senna pallida var. goldmaniana, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
