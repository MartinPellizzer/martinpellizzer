Sida hiemalis

Sida hiemalis, commonly known as winter mallow, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features hairy, branching stems that often grow from the base, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
