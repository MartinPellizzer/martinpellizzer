Suaeda patagonica var. crassiuscula

Suaeda patagonica var. crassiuscula, commonly known as Patagonian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and inland saline soils, often found in Patagonia and other arid regions.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
