Sutera lilacina

Sutera lilacina, commonly known as the lilac morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep lilac. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds with a distinctive spiral pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sutera lilacina is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
