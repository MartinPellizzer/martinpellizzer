Symplocos anomala var. liosiphon

Symplocos anomala var. liosiphon, commonly known as the hairy-stalked wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
