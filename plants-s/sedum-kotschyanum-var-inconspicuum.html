Sedum kotschyanum var. inconspicuum

Sedum kotschyanum var. inconspicuum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy ornamental plant for dry landscapes.
