Symplocos calophylloides

Symplocos calophylloides, commonly known as the Indian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
