Sutera calycina

Sutera calycina, commonly known as the silverleaf suture, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are characterized by their silvery, velvety texture. Sutera calycina is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid regions.
