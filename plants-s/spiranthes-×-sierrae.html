Spiranthes × sierrae

Spiranthes × sierrae, commonly known as Sierra ladies' tresses, is a hybrid orchid native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is characterized by its dense, spiral arrangement of small, white to pale pink flowers along its flowering stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces a single, erect flowering stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
