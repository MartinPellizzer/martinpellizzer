Salix richardsonii var. latifolia

Salix richardsonii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a fuzzy texture. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its male and female flowers, and it is often found in moist, riparian habitats. It has a shallow root system that helps stabilize streambanks and prevent erosion.

The broadleaf willow is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
