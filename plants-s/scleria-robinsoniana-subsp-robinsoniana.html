Scleria robinsoniana subsp. robinsoniana

Scleria robinsoniana subsp.

robinsoniana, commonly known as the Robinson's bulbous grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its clustered, bulbous base and erect, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in summer, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is popular among gardeners in suitable climates.
