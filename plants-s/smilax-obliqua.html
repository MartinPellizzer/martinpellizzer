Smilax obliqua

Smilax obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag smilax, is a vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems that have a distinctive zigzag pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have spines along the edges.

Smilax obliqua is a persistent, deciduous plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments.
