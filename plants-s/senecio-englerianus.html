Senecio englerianus

Senecio englerianus, commonly known as Engler's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are small and yellow.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
