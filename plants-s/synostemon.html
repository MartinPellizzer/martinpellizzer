Synostemon

Synostemon, commonly known as the false arum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. Synostemon species are characterized by their striking, often colorful inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy surface.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as houseplants due to their unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
