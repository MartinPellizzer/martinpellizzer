Senecio macropappus

Senecio macropappus, commonly known as the giant ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Due to its invasive nature, it can pose a threat to native plant species in certain regions.
