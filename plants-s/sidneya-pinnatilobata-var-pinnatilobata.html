Sidneya pinnatilobata var. pinnatilobata

Sidneya pinnatilobata var. pinnatilobata, commonly known as the Pacific coast holly, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit with leathery, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern.

It produces small, fleshy fruits that are consumed by various bird species, contributing to its ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
