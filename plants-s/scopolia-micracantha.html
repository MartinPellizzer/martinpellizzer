Scopolia micracantha

Scopolia micracantha, commonly known as the lesser scopolia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature.

Scopolia micracantha is often found in rocky or wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
