Spartothamnella teucriiflora

Spartothamnella teucriiflora, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring.

Spartothamnella teucriiflora plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
