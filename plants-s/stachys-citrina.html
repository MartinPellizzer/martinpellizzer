Stachys citrina

Stachys citrina, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their calming properties.

Stachys citrina is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
