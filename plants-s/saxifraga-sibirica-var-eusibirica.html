Saxifraga sibirica var. eusibirica

Saxifraga sibirica var. eusibirica, commonly known as the Siberian saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, blooming in late summer.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
