Solanum tuberosum var. hassicum

Solanum tuberosum var. hassicum, commonly known as Hassel's potato, is a variant of the common potato that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant produces tubers that are typically smaller and have a unique skin color, often ranging from red to purple. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a hairy surface, and it produces small, white or violet flowers.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
