Solanum pachypetalon

Solanum pachypetalon, commonly known as the thick-petaled nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers and hairy, glandular stems. Its leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans.

Solanum pachypetalon is often found in moist, shaded environments within its native range.
