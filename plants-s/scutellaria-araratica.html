Scutellaria araratica

Scutellaria araratica, commonly known as the Armenian skullcap, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Scutellaria araratica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
