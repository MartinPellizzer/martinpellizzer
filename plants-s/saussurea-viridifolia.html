Saussurea viridifolia

Saussurea viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leaved saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence.

Saussurea viridifolia is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
