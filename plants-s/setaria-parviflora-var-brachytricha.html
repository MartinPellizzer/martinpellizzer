Setaria parviflora var. brachytricha

Setaria parviflora var. brachytricha, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to various regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, cylindrical leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral structure.
