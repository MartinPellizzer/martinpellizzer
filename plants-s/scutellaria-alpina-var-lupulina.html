Scutellaria alpina var. lupulina

Scutellaria alpina var. lupulina, commonly known as the alpine blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
