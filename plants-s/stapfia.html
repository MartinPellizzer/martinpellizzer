Stapfia

Stapfia, commonly known as the "skeleton flower," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Bromeliaceae.

These plants are native to the Andes Mountains in South America, where they thrive in cool, humid environments. Due to their unique appearance and limited distribution, Stapfia plants are often cultivated as ornamental specimens in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
