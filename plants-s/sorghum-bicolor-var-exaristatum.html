Sorghum bicolor var. exaristatum

Sorghum bicolor var. exaristatum, commonly known as the giant broomcorn millet, is a tall, annual grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, feathery panicles that can reach heights of up to 4 meters. The plant produces small, dry seeds that are used for food, animal feed, and industrial purposes. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in arid and semi-arid regions.
