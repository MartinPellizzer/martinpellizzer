Scutellaria lilungensis

Scutellaria lilungensis, commonly known as the Lilung siphon.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China, characterized by its erect stem and pairs of opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Scutellaria lilungensis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
