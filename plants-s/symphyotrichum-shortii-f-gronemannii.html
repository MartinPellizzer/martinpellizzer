Symphyotrichum shortii f. gronemannii

Symphyotrichum shortii f. gronemannii, commonly known as the Gronemann's New England aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It features showy purple to pinkish-red flowers that bloom in the late summer to early fall.

Its foliage is generally narrow and grass-like, with hairy stems that help it retain moisture in its preferred wetland environments.
