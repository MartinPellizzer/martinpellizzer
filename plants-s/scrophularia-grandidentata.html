Scrophularia grandidentata

Scrophularia grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed figwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with distinctive large, serrated teeth along the edges. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scrophularia grandidentata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and flowers.
