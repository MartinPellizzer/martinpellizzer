Stenocereus thurberi f. cristatus

Stenocereus thurberi f. cristatus, commonly known as the crested hedgehog cactus, is a unique variant of the hedgehog cactus, characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and often branched growth form.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its foliage consists of sharp, spiny areoles, and it is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions.

This cultivar is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
