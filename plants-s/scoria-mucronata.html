Scoria mucronata

Scoria mucronata, commonly known as the spikeless scoria, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with dense, grayish foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces slender, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of tiny florets.

Scoria mucronata is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
