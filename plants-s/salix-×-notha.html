Salix × notha

Salix × notha, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a drooping appearance. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

Salix × notha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil along waterways.
