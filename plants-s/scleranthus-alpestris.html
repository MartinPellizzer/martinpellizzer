Scleranthus alpestris

Scleranthus alpestris, commonly known as the alpine knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Scleranthus alpestris is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
