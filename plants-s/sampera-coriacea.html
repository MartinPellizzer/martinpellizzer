Sampera coriacea

Sampera coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf sampera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often blooming during the wet season. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
