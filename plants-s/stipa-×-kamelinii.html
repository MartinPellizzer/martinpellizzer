Stipa × kamelinii

Stipa × kamelinii, commonly known as the hybrid false brome, is a grass species that results from the cross between Stipa capillata and Stipa secundiflora.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leaf-like blades. The inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers. It is often found in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and grasslands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in various habitats.
