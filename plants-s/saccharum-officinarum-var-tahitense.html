Saccharum officinarum var. tahitense

Saccharum officinarum var. tahitense, commonly known as Tahitian sugarcane, is a variant of the sugarcane species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and high sugar content, making it valuable for both commercial and traditional uses. The plant thrives in tropical climates with ample sunlight and moisture, often growing in coastal areas or riverine environments. Its leaves are long and sword-shaped, while its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, clustered flowers.

This variety is also notable for its role in local agriculture and its potential for biofuel production.
