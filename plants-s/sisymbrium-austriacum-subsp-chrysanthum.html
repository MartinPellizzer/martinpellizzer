Sisymbrium austriacum subsp. chrysanthum

Sisymbrium austriacum subsp.

chrysanthum, commonly known as the Austrian peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

The species is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, branched stems. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows and along roadsides.

The plant has small, elongated leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.
