Scutellaria chevalieri

Scutellaria chevalieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, often arranged in whorls along the stem. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Scutellaria chevalieri is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
