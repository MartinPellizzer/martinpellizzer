Silene lychnidiflora

Silene lychnidiflora, commonly known as the moonflower of the desert, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Silene lychnidiflora plays a role in desert ecosystems by providing nectar to nocturnal pollinators.
