Sulcorebutia pulchra

Sulcorebutia pulchra, commonly known as the "Carnival Cactus," is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the dry regions of South America.

It features a cluster of short, ribbed stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Sulcorebutia pulchra is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
