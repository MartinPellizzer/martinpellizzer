Stachys lycia

Stachys lycia, commonly known as Turkey tail or redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typically in shades of pink or purple.

Stachys lycia is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
