Sida berlandieri

Sida berlandieri, commonly known as desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, hairy leaves and produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its fibrous stems and leaves are used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.

Sida berlandieri is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in desert landscapes.
