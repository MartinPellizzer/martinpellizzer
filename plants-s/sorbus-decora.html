Sorbus decora

Sorbus decora, commonly known as the showy mountain-ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often glossy and have serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
