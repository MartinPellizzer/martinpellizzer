Senecio procerus subsp. congestus

Senecio procerus subsp.

congestus, commonly known as the dense-stemmed senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and bloom.
