Scirpus erectus var. angustus

Scirpus erectus var. angustus, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that appear in late summer.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
