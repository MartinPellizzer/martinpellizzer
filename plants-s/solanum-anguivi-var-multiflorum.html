Solanum anguivi var. multiflorum

Solanum anguivi var. multiflorum, commonly known as the arrowleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible when ripe but contain toxic compounds in their raw form. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
