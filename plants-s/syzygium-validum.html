Syzygium validum

Syzygium validum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

Syzygium validum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
