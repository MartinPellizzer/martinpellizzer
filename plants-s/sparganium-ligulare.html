Sparganium ligulare

Sparganium ligulare, commonly known as the narrowleaf bur reed, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical rhizomes that form colonies in shallow water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically submerged or floating. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often forming a dense mat at the water's surface.

Sparganium ligulare plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
