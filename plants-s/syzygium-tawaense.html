Syzygium tawaense

Syzygium tawaense, commonly known as the Tawa tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant white flowers. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its leaves are elliptical and glossy.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
