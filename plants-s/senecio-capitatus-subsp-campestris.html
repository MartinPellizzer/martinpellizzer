Senecio capitatus subsp. campestris

Senecio capitatus subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the field senecio, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family, Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spherical head.

Native to southern Africa, this species is often found in grasslands and open habitats. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a flowering period during the warmer months.

It is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
