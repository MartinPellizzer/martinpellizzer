Spiraea salicifolia var. roseola

Spiraea salicifolia var. roseola, commonly known as the roseleaf weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that droop gracefully, giving it a willow-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are elliptical, with a soft, silvery-green underside, and turn yellow in the fall.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
