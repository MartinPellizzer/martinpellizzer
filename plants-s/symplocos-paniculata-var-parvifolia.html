Symplocos paniculata var. parvifolia

Symplocos paniculata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length, giving it its varietal name "parvifolia.". The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
