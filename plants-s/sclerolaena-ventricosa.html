Sclerolaena ventricosa

Sclerolaena ventricosa, commonly known as the swollenleaf daisy, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sclerolaena ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
