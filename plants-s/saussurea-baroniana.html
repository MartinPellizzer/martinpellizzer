Saussurea baroniana

Saussurea baroniana, commonly known as the Baron's saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stem and large, showy yellow flower heads. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic compound known as safflower, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

Due to its unique properties and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
