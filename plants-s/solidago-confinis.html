Solidago confinis

Solidago confinis, commonly known as the coastal goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of yellow flowers. This plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often reddish in color.

Solidago confinis plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
