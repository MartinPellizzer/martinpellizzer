Senecio integrifolius subsp. aucheri

Senecio integrifolius subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
