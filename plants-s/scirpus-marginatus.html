Scirpus marginatus

Scirpus marginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and improve water filtration in wetlands.

Scirpus marginatus is an important species for ecological restoration and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
