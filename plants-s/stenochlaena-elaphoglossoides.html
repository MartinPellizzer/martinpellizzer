Stenochlaena elaphoglossoides

Stenochlaena elaphoglossoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and rice fields. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often submerged or floating. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Stenochlaena elaphoglossoides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
