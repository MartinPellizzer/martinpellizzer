Sobralia caravata

Sobralia caravata, commonly known as the Caravata orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Sobralia caravata prefers warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
