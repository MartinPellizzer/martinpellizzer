Satureja avromanica

Satureja avromanica, commonly known as Persian thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and cooking for their pungent, thyme-like aroma.

Satureja avromanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
