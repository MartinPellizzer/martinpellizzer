Sebastiania glandulosa var. fallax

Sebastiania glandulosa var. fallax, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its vibrant floral display.
