Silene Multicaulis Var. Serbica

Silene multicaulis var. serbica is a lesser-known member of the Caryophyllaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe. This plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Due to its limited distribution and delicate nature, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique botanical specimen from habitat loss and environmental changes.
