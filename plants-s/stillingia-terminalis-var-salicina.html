Stillingia terminalis var. salicina

Stillingia terminalis var. salicina, commonly known as the Pacific yew, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. The plant features dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, it is important to note that the plant is toxic to humans and animals if ingested in large quantities.
