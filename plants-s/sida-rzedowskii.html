Sida rzedowskii

Sida rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Sida rzedowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
