Swertia albicaulis var. nitida

Swertia albicaulis var. nitida, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine, whitish pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
