Sium serra

Sium serra, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters called umbels. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Sium serra is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.
