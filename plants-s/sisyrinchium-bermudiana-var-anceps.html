Sisyrinchium bermudiana var. anceps

Sisyrinchium bermudiana var. anceps, commonly known as the Bermuda blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blue to purple flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands. It is adapted to full sun and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its evolutionary adaptation to coastal environments.
