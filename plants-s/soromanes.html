Soromanes

Soromanes, commonly known as the "sorrel" plant, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the genus Rumex in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Soromanes contains oxalic acid, which gives it a sharp, tangy flavor and makes it a popular ingredient in some cuisines.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
