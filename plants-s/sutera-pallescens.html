Sutera pallescens

Sutera pallescens, commonly known as the silverleaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Sutera pallescens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its fragrant leaves and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
