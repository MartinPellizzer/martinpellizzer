Schwabea salicifolia

Schwabea salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf schwabea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods and along stream banks.

Schwabea salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
