Solanum erlansonii

Solanum erlansonii, commonly known as the Erlanson nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, toxic berries that are not edible for humans or animals.
