Striga gesnerioides var. arondensis

Striga gesnerioides var. arondensis, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
