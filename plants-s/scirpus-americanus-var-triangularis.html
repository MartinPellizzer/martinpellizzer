Scirpus americanus var. triangularis

Scirpus americanus var. triangularis, commonly known as the triangular bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its triangular, narrow leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or reddish in color, which appear in late summer to early fall. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
