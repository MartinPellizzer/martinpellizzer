Sedum yedoense

Sedum yedoense, commonly known as the Japanese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a slightly waxy texture.

Sedum yedoense is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
