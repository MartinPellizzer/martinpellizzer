Sopubia mannii var. mannii

Sopubia mannii var. mannii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in umbels, which are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds with silky hairs. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.
