Solanum abancayense

Solanum abancayense, commonly known as the Abancay nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Peru.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its fruits are berries that mature to a bright red color, containing several seeds.

Solanum abancayense is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
