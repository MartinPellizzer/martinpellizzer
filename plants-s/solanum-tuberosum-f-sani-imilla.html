Solanum tuberosum f. sani-imilla

Solanum tuberosum f. sani-imilla, commonly known as the Sanilima potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato with distinct morphological characteristics.

It exhibits unique leaf patterns and flower structures compared to typical Solanum tuberosum varieties. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it has adapted to local climatic conditions. It produces tubers that are often smaller and have a different flavor profile than standard potatoes.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
