Searsia rigida var. dentata

Searsia rigida var. dentata, commonly known as the rough bracken bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically serrated or toothed, giving it its specific epithet "dentata.". The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and coastal areas, and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
