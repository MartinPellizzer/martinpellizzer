Salvia sibthorpii

Salvia sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
