Stelleropsis altaica

Stelleropsis altaica, commonly known as the Altai bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stelleropsis altaica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
