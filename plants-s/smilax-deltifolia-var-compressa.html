Smilax deltifolia var. compressa

Smilax deltifolia var. compressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf catbriar, is a vigorous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with sharp, recurved spines along the stems and leaves. The leaves are typically narrow, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in terminal panicles, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

This plant is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
