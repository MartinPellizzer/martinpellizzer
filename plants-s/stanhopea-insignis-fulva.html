Stanhopea insignis fulva

Stanhopea insignis fulva, commonly known as the golden stanhopea, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling intricate lace. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that bloom during the dry season, typically from December to March. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

This orchid thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
