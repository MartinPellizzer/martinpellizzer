Saxifraga thiantha var. citrina

Saxifraga thiantha var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically basal, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. This species thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
