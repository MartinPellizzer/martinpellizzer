Smilax martini

Smilax martini, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its botanical name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine.

Smilax martini is often found in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade.
