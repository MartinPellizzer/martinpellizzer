Stemodia tetragona

Stemodia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled stemodia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its square or four-angled stems, which are a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in dry or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a somewhat hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in groups of three, and are typically yellow or greenish in color.
