Setaria vaginata

Setaria vaginata, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Setaria vaginata is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
