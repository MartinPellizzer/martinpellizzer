Staebe

Staebe, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks.

Staebe is known for its strong, bitter scent and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
