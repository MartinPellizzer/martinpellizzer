Spongopyrena

It is characterized by its translucent, gelatinous body that resembles a sponge, hence its common name. The organism lacks a true medusa stage and instead exists as a colony of specialized polyps working together. Spongopyrena is typically found in deep ocean waters, where it thrives in low-light conditions.

Its unique structure allows it to capture prey efficiently through its extended tentacles.
