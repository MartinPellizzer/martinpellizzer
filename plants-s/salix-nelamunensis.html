Salix nelamunensis

Salix nelamunensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian willow, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and catkin-like flowers. It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
