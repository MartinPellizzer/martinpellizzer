Solenogyne bellioides

Solenogyne bellioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate habit, with fleshy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy soils and access moisture from deeper layers.

Solenogyne bellioides is adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
