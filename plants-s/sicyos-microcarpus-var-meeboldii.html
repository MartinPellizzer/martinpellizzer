Sicyos microcarpus var. meeboldii

Sicyos microcarpus var. meeboldii, commonly known as the meeboldi variant of the common gourd, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spherical, spiny fruits. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, tropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique varietal traits and ecological adaptability.
