Symphyotrichum bullatum

Symphyotrichum bullatum, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, rugged appearance.
