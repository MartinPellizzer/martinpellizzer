Salix subopposita

Salix subopposita, commonly known as the subopposite willow, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salicaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically growing in moist, often wet habitats such as riverbanks and marshes. The plant is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

Salix subopposita plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
