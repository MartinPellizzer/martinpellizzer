Strychnos armata

Strychnos armata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its bark and wood are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also notable for its toxic seeds, which contain strychnine and brucine, making it a subject of both botanical and pharmacological interest.
