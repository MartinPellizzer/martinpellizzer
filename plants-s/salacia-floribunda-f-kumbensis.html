Salacia floribunda f. kumbensis

Salacia floribunda f. kumbensis, commonly known as the Kumben's flame tree, is a unique variant of the Salacia genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often covering the entire plant during the flowering season. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
