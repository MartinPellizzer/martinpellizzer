Spaniopappus shaferi

Spaniopappus shaferi, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has a low, mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and lobed leaves adapted to conserve water. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's seeds are dispersed by wind, with pappus structures that allow them to travel long distances.
