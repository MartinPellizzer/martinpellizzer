Senecio laggeri

Senecio laggeri, commonly known as the Lagger's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio laggeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and resilience.
