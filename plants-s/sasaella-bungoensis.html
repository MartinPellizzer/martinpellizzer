Sasaella bungoensis

Sasaella bungoensis, commonly known as the Bungo bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color.

Sasaella bungoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
