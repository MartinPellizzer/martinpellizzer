Satyrium landauerianum

Satyrium landauerianum, commonly known as the Landauer's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers with intricate patterns and a unique structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it has a limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many areas.
