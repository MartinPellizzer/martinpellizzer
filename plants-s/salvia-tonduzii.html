Salvia tonduzii

Salvia tonduzii, commonly known as the Ethiopian sage, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Salvia tonduzii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
