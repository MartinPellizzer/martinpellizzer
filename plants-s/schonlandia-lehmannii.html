Schonlandia lehmannii

Schonlandia lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Schonlandia lehmannii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
