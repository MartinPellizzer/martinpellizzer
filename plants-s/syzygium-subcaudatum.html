Syzygium subcaudatum

Syzygium subcaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The fruit is a small, round, yellow or red berry that ripens in late summer or early autumn. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
