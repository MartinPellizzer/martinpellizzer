Symplocos ambangensis

Symplocos ambangensis, commonly known as the Philippine gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, red berries that are popular in local cuisine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Symplocos ambangensis is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
