Scirpus lacustris f. pictus

Scirpus lacustris f. pictus, commonly known as the painted bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from pink to red, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and lake margins. Its flowering stems are tall and robust, reaching up to 2 meters in height.

The botanical features of Scirpus lacustris f. pictus make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
