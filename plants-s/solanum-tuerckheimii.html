Solanum tuerckheimii

Solanum tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that change color as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often found in shrublands or along forest edges.

Solanum tuerckheimii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
