Stipa namaquensis

Stipa namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua needlegrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in sandy or rocky soils. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in harsh environments.

Stipa namaquensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
