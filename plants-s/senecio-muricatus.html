Senecio muricatus

Senecio muricatus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio muricatus is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
