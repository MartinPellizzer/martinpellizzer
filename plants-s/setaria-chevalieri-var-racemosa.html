Setaria chevalieri var. racemosa

Setaria chevalieri var. racemosa, commonly known as racemose foxtail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the seed heads are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued as a forage crop for livestock in certain regions.
