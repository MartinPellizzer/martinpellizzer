Sorbus cashmiriana

Sorbus cashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by orange to red berries in the autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils at higher elevations.

Sorbus cashmiriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
