Saxifraga odontoloma

Saxifraga odontoloma, commonly known as the toothed saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan range. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Saxifraga odontoloma thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.
