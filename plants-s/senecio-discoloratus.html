Senecio discoloratus

Senecio discoloratus, commonly known as the Whiteleaf Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Senecio discoloratus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
