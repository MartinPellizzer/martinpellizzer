Silene gigantea subsp. hellenica

Silene gigantea subsp.

hellenica, commonly known as the Greek giant catchfly, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Silene gigantea subsp.

hellenica is often found in rocky soils and open landscapes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
