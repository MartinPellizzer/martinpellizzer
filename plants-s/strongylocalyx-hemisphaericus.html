Strongylocalyx hemisphaericus

Strongylocalyx hemisphaericus, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or Natal starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow fruits that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The plant's botanical features make it well-suited for ornamental gardening and small-scale agriculture in tropical regions.
