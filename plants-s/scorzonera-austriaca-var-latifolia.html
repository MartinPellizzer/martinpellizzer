Scorzonera austriaca var. latifolia

Scorzonera austriaca var. latifolia, commonly known as the Austrian scurvygrass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Scorzonera austriaca var. latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
