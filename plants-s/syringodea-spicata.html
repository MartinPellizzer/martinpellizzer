Syringodea spicata

Syringodea spicata, commonly known as the spike spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features wiry, arching stems that bear clusters of small, pink to white flowers in late spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Syringodea spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
