Stipa megapotamia var. latiflora

Stipa megapotamia var. latiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, open landscapes.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
