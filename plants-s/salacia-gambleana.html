Salacia gambleana

Salacia gambleana, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor. Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
