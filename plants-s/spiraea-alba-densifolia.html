Spiraea alba densifolia

Spiraea alba densifolia, commonly known as white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea alba densifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
