Serrafalcus × ferronii

Serrafalcus × ferronii, commonly known as the hybrid serra fern, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Serrafalcus and Ferronii species.

It is characterized by its intricate fronds that exhibit a combination of the parent species' traits, featuring both serrated and falcate (sickle-shaped) leaflets. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its reproductive system involves spore production, typical of ferns, and it requires careful attention to humidity and soil conditions for optimal growth.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
