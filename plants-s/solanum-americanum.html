Solanum americanum

Solanum americanum, commonly known as the nightshade or American nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries, which are toxic to humans but can be consumed by certain animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Solanum americanum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
