Sophora acuminata

Sophora acuminata, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces fragrant, blue-violet pea-like flowers in spring, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
