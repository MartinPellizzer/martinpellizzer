Salvia formosa

Salvia formosa, commonly known as the showy blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Salvia formosa is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
