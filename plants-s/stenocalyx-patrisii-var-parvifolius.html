Stenocalyx patrisii var. parvifolius

Stenocalyx patrisii var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length, giving it its varietal name "parvifolius.". The plant produces striking, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its ornamental value is primarily due to its vibrant floral display and compact growth habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.
