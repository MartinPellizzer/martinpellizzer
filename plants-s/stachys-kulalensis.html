Stachys kulalensis

Stachys kulalensis, commonly known as the Kulal hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Stachys kulalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
