Seringia × katatona

Seringia × katatona, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is grass-like with narrow, linear leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring.

Seringia × katatona is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
