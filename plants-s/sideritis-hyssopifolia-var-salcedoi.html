Sideritis hyssopifolia var. salcedoi

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. salcedoi, commonly known as the Salcedo's ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.
