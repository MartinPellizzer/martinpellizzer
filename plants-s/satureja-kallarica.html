Satureja kallarica

Satureja kallarica, commonly known as Kashmir thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers and has narrow, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol.

Satureja kallarica is also appreciated in culinary applications for its distinctive, spicy aroma.
