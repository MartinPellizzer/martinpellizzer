Senecio hispidissimus

Senecio hispidissimus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its drought tolerance.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
