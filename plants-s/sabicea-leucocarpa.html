Sabicea leucocarpa

Sabicea leucocarpa, commonly known as white-fruited sabicea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces spherical, white or pale yellow fruits that are covered in a soft, hairy coating. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and hairy foliage.

Sabicea leucocarpa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.
