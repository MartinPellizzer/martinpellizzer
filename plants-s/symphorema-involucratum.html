Symphorema involucratum

Symphorema involucratum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often pink or white in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.
