Sida gigantea

Sida gigantea, commonly known as giant sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and along riverbanks.

Sida gigantea is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential ecological and agricultural applications.
