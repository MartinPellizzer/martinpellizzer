Stoeberia utilis subsp. lerouxiae

Stoeberia utilis subsp.

lerouxiae, commonly known as the Leroux's stoeberia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
