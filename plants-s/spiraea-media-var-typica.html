Spiraea media var. typica

Spiraea media var. typica, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has finely cut, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea media var. typica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
