Salvia scabra

Salvia scabra, commonly known as rough sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Salvia scabra is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
