Syzygium calcicola

Syzygium calcicola, commonly known as the limestone apple.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, round, and resemble apples, hence the common name.

Syzygium calcicola thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.
