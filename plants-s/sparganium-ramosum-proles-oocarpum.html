Sparganium ramosum proles oocarpum

Sparganium ramosum proles oocarpum, commonly known as the spiked bur reed, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and ponds, where it forms dense stands. The plant has long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, with the inflorescences extending above the water. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by water.

The plant plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
