Serratula fasciculata

Serratula fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered sickleweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky areas. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with wiry stems and clustered, yellowish-green leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Serratula fasciculata is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in areas with poor soil, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
