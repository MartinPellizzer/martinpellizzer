Sloanea longisepala

Sloanea longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled sloanea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. Sloanea longisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
