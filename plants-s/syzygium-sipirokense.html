Syzygium sipirokense

Syzygium sipirokense, commonly known as the Sipirok rose apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but have a rose-scented aroma. Syzygium sipirokense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its fruit in local markets.

Its growth is typically found in lowland areas with high humidity and well-drained soil.
