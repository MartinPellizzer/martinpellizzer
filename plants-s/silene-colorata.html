Silene colorata

Silene colorata, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, branched stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene colorata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
