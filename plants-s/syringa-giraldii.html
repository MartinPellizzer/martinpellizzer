Syringa giraldii

Syringa giraldii, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Syringa giraldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its showy floral displays.
