Strigosella latifolia

Strigosella latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf strigosella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Strigosella latifolia is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
