Senegalia andamanica

Senegalia andamanica, commonly known as Andaman acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and disturbed habitats. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has a woody, thornless stem.

Senegalia andamanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in saline and sandy soils.
