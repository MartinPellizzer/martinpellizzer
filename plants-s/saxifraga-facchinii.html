Saxifraga facchinii

Saxifraga facchinii, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically thrives in rocky and alpine environments, where it forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga facchinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny soils.
