Saxifraga petraea var. littoralis

Saxifraga petraea var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It thrives in rocky, coastal environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. The plant typically has small, fleshy leaves that help conserve water in its harsh habitat. It produces clusters of pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety is a hardy species that plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
