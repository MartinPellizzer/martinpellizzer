Sedum triactina

Sedum triactina, commonly known as the three-lobed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
