Selaginella rosendahlii

Selaginella rosendahlii, commonly known as the Rosendahl's spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant has distinctive, scale-like leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure on the ground. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella rosendahlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
