Symplocos columbuli

Symplocos columbuli, commonly known as the Indian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. The plant has elliptical leaves with finely toothed margins and produces a fragrant, resinous sap. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are an important food source for various wildlife species during the winter months.
