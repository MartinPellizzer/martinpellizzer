Salvia inamoena

Salvia inamoena, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of violet-blue tubular flowers, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia inamoena is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
