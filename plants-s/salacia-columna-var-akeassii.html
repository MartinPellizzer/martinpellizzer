Salacia columna var. akeassii

Salacia columna var. akeassii, commonly known as the Akeassii columnar salacia, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, forming a columnar growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are a key feature of the species. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
