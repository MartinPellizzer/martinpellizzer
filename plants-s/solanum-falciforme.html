Solanum falciforme

Solanum falciforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, which gives it its scientific name.

Solanum falciforme belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important plants.
