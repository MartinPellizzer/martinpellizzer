Serratula patula

Serratula patula, commonly known as the common sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose, branching inflorescences.

Serratula patula is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
