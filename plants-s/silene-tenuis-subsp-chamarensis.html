Silene tenuis subsp. chamarensis

Silene tenuis subsp.

chamarensis, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit.

The plant produces small, pink to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its often arid habitat. Silene tenuis subsp.

chamarensis is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
