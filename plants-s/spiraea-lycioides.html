Spiraea lycioides

Spiraea lycioides, commonly known as the sand spirea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dunes.

Spiraea lycioides is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
