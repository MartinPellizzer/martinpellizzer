Salvia hotteana

Salvia hotteana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorls along the upper stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
