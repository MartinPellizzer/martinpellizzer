Sporobolus montevidensis

Sporobolus montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo spikerush, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish in color. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that aids in water retention.

Sporobolus montevidensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
