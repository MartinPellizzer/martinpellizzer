Schismatoglottis calyptrata f. glaucescens

Schismatoglottis calyptrata f. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous schismatoglottis, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-green coating, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with pale yellow petals and a deep purple lip, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
