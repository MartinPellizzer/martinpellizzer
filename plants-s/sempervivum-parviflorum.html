Sempervivum parviflorum

Sempervivum parviflorum, commonly known as the common houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions has earned it the common name "houseleek," reflecting its historical use in traditional medicine and as a remedy for ailments.
