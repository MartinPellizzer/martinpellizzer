Sasa paniculata

Sasa paniculata, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or common sasa, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often hollow. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with greenish-white flowers, which are typically inconspicuous. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa paniculata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
