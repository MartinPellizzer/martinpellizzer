Solanum izquierdoi

Solanum izquierdoi, commonly known as the Izquierdo's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with five petals, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
