Senecio chachapoyensis

Senecio chachapoyensis, commonly known as the Chachapoya groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean highlands of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
