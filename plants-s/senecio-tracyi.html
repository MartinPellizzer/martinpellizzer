Senecio tracyi

Senecio tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Tracy's groundsel is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
