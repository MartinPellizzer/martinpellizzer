Salvia cedrosensis

Salvia cedrosensis, commonly known as Cedros sage, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and the Channel Islands.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Salvia cedrosensis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
