Schinus talampaya

Schinus talampaya, commonly known as the Talampaya pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Chile, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, bright red berries are edible and have a peppery flavor.

Schinus talampaya is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
