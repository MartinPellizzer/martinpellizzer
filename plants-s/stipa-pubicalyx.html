Stipa pubicalyx

Stipa pubicalyx, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a soft, cloud-like appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers in late summer. Stipa pubicalyx is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique visual characteristics.
