Sasaella benten

Sasaella benten, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a perennial plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit, forming thick mats of foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, grass-like texture.

Sasaella benten is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
