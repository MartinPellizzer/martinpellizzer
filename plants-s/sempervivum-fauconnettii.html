Sempervivum fauconnettii

Sempervivum fauconnettii, commonly known as the Fauconnetti's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often fleshy and covered in a layer of fine white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
