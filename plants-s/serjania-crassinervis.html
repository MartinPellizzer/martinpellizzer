Serjania crassinervis

Serjania crassinervis, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its large, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Serjania crassinervis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
