Scutellaria volubilis

Scutellaria volubilis, commonly known as creeping blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria volubilis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
