Solanum pinnatifidum

Solanum pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring compound leaves with a pinnate structure. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
