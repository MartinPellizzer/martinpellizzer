Solidago perornata

Solidago perornata, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its showy inflorescences bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solidago perornata plays an important role in ecological systems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
