Scrophularia libanotica var. cappadocica

Scrophularia libanotica var. cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
