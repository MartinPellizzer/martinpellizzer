Salsola iljinii

Salsola iljinii, commonly known as the Iljin's Russian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

Salsola iljinii is often found in steppe and desert regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
