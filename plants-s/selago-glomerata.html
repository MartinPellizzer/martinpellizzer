Selago glomerata

Selago glomerata, commonly known as the clustered selago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, linear leaves and produces capsules as its fruit. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas.

Selago glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
