Syringa formosissima

Syringa formosissima, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of white to pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter beneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Syringa formosissima is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
