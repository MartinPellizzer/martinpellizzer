Setaria umbrosa

Setaria umbrosa, commonly known as blackgrass or dark foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, dark green leaves that often have a bluish tinge. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown or black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Setaria umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
