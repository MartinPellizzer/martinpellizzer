Sasa kurilensis var. uchidae

Sasa kurilensis var. uchidae, commonly known as the Uchida's bamboo, is a variant of the Kuril bamboo native to the Japanese archipelago.

It is a clump-forming grass that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various forest organisms.
