Salvia mukerjeei

Salvia mukerjeei, commonly known as the Indian sage or Mukerjee's sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia mukerjeei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
