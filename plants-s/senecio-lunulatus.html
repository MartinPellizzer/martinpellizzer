Senecio lunulatus

Senecio lunulatus, commonly known as the moon's face plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves with white markings that resemble the moon's surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
