Syzygium multibracteolatum

Syzygium multibracteolatum, commonly known as the bracteolate syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Syzygium multibracteolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
