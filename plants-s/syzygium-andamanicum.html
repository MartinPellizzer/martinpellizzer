Syzygium andamanicum

Syzygium andamanicum, commonly known as the Andaman white apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by edible, white, fleshy fruits. Syzygium andamanicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
