Stipa brasiliensis

Stipa brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical inflorescence composed of dense spikelets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Stipa brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
