Solanum esuriale f. inerme

Solanum esuriale f. inerme, commonly known as the slender wild tomato, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its thin, branching stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into small, round berries containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
