Salix × mirifica

Salix × mirifica, commonly known as the silver willow, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and silvery-gray foliage that provides ornamental value in gardens. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance and ability to tolerate wet conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars.
