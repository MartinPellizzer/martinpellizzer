Santiria apiculata var. rubra

Santiria apiculata var. rubra, commonly known as the red santiria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Santiria apiculata var. rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
