Solanum morii

Solanum morii, commonly known as the Morii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Solanum morii is often found in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
