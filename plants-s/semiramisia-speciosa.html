Semiramisia speciosa

Semiramisia speciosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, often with a silvery or fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant's ornamental value is further enhanced by its long blooming period, which typically spans from late spring to early autumn.
