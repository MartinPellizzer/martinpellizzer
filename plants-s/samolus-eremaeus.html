Samolus eremaeus

Samolus eremaeus, commonly known as the desert sandwillow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Samolus eremaeus is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes or inland deserts.
