Sasa qingyuanensis

Sasa qingyuanensis, commonly known as the Qingyuan bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a distinct, slightly aromatic scent.

Sasa qingyuanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese horticulture.
