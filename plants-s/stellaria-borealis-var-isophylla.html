Stellaria borealis var. isophylla

Stellaria borealis var. isophylla, commonly known as the Arctic starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, white star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has a mat-forming habit, with slender, branching stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.
