Silene cordifolia

Silene cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Silene cordifolia is often found in areas with rich, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
