Scirpus nigricans

Scirpus nigricans, commonly known as black jointed grass, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus nigricans plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
