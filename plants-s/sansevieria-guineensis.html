Sansevieria guineensis

Sansevieria guineensis, commonly known as the African spear or Guinea sword, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to western Africa and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the base of the leaves. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Sansevieria guineensis is also noted for its air-purifying properties, contributing to its reputation as a hardy and beneficial houseplant.
