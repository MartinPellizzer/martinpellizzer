Schefflera angustifoliolata

Schefflera angustifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of leaves, which give it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets arranged in a whorl around a central stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Its adaptability to indoor conditions makes it a popular choice for houseplants.
