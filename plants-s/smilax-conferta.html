Smilax conferta

Smilax conferta, commonly known as the rough-stemmed arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are typically arranged in a zigzag pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Smilax conferta is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ability to climb or trail along the ground.
