Salix × affinis

Salix × affinis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving the tree a graceful appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically light green on the upper surface and pale white or silvery on the underside. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal.

Salix × affinis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
