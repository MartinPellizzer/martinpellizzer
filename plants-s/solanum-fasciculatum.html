Solanum fasciculatum

Solanum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, and it produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
