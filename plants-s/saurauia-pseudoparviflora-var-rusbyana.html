Saurauia pseudoparviflora var. rusbyana

Saurauia pseudoparviflora var. rusbyana, commonly known as the Rusby's saurauia, is a variant of the saurauia plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical, leathery, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
