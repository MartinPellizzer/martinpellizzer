Sinarundinaria reticulata var. aurea

Sinarundinaria reticulata var. aurea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
