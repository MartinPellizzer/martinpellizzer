Saxifraga cespitosa var. groenlandica

Saxifraga cespitosa var. groenlandica, commonly known as the Greenland saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in the summer. It is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of tundra and mountain ecosystems.
