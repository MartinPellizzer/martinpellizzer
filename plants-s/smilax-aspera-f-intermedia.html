Smilax aspera f. intermedia

Smilax aspera f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rough smilax, is a variant of the rough smilax species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing or trailing plant characterized by its woody stems and spiny, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
