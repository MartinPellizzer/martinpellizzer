Sideritis scordioides var. crenata

Sideritis scordioides var. crenata, commonly known as the Greek catnip or mountain catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece and the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. Small, pale blue or purple flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, typical of the mint family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
