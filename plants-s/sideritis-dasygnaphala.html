Sideritis dasygnaphala

Sideritis dasygnaphala, commonly known as the woolly sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows.

Sideritis dasygnaphala is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
