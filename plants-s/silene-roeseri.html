Silene roeseri

Silene roeseri, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene roeseri is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude meadows and slopes.
