Stipagrostis hirtigluma var. patula

Stipagrostis hirtigluma var. patula, commonly known as the Patula grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in regions with suitable climatic conditions.
