Sarcopodium griffithii

Sarcopodium griffithii, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a spadix surrounded by a spathe. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Sarcopodium griffithii thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
