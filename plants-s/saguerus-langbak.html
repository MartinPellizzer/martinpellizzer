Saguerus langbak

Saguerus langbak, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the rainy season. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
