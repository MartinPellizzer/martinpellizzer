Senecio repens var. macbridei

Senecio repens var. macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground. The leaves are typically lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, yellow, and daisy-like, blooming in early spring.

This species is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
