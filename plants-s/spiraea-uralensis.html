Spiraea uralensis

Spiraea uralensis, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has finely divided, feathery leaves that provide ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

Spiraea uralensis is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
