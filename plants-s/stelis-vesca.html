Stelis vesca

Stelis vesca, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Stelis vesca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
