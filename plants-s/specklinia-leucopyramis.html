Specklinia leucopyramis

Specklinia leucopyramis, commonly known as the white-veined staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, tree-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with pale, veined leaves that contrast with its dark, leathery texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Specklinia leucopyramis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
