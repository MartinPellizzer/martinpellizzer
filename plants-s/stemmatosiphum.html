Stemmatosiphum

Stemmatosiphum, commonly known as the African orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plants typically grow in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Their rhizomatous growth habit allows them to spread and form dense clumps.

Stemmatosiphum species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
