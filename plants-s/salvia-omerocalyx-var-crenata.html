Salvia omerocalyx var. crenata

Salvia omerocalyx var. crenata, commonly known as the smoothleaf sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features lance-shaped, smooth-edged leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens and native plant collections.
