Syzygium guineense var. palustre

Syzygium guineense var. palustre, commonly known as swamp apple or water apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptic leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is adapted to tolerate water-logged soils.
