Salacia dewevrei

Salacia dewevrei, commonly known as the African ghost plant, is a unique species native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Salaciacae family and is characterized by its unusual, ghost-like appearance with translucent, waxy leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Salacia dewevrei is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive visual appeal.
