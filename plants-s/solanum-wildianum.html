Solanum wildianum

Solanum wildianum, commonly known as the Wildian nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum wildianum is often found in woodland and savannah habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
