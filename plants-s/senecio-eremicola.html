Senecio eremicola

Senecio eremicola, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Senecio eremicola is often found in shrubland and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
