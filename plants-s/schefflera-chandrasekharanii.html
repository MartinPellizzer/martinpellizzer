Schefflera chandrasekharanii

Schefflera chandrasekharanii, commonly known as the Chandrasekharanii umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of large, leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
