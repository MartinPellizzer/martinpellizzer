Solanum macinae

Solanum macinae, commonly known as the Macina nightshade, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, dark berries that are often toxic. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale purple flowers.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by both animals and humans.
