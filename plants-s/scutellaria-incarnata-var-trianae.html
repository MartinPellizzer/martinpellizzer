Scutellaria incarnata var. trianae

Scutellaria incarnata var. trianae, commonly known as the Triana's blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
