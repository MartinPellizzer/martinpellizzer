Solms-laubachia kashgarica

Solms-laubachia kashgarica, commonly known as the Kashgar grass, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Kazakhstan, and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
