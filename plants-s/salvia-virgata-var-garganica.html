Salvia virgata var. garganica

Salvia virgata var. garganica, commonly known as the Garganica sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
