Stipa apertifolia subsp. apenninicola

Stipa apertifolia subsp.

apenninicola, commonly known as the Apennine feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence is often pale green to golden in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal environments.
