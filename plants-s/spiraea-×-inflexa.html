Spiraea × inflexa

Spiraea × inflexa, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that give it a weeping appearance, and it produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has a compact, rounded form. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Spiraea × inflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant spring blooms and attractive foliage.
