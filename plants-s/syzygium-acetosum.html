Syzygium acetosum

Syzygium acetosum, commonly known as the sour orange or *Citrus aurantium* var. *acerosa*, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, yellow-orange berry with a sour taste, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in horticulture and pharmacology.
