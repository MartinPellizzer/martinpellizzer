Scorzonera linarifolia

Scorzonera linarifolia, commonly known as the linaria scorzonera, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features robust, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Scorzonera linarifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
