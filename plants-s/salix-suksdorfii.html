Salix suksdorfii

Salix suksdorfii, commonly known as Saksdorf's willow, is a rare and shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically covered with a dense layer of silky hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring before its leaves fully develop. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, Salix suksdorfii is considered a vulnerable species.
