Scindapsus latifolius

Scindapsus latifolius, commonly known as the satin plant or silver vine, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Scindapsus latifolius is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
