Senecio scrophulariifolius

Senecio scrophulariifolius, commonly known as the sticky weed or purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance, which helps deter herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Senecio scrophulariifolius is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds, making it a common weed in disturbed environments.
