Symplocos trichoclada var. koshunensis

Symplocos trichoclada var. koshunensis, commonly known as the Koshun Japanese tea tree, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
