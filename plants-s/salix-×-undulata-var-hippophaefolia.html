Salix × undulata var. hippophaefolia

Salix × undulata var. hippophaefolia, commonly known as the silver willow, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive wavy margin.

The flowers are catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated, and the plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
