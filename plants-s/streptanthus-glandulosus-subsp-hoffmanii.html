Streptanthus glandulosus subsp. hoffmanii

Streptanthus glandulosus subsp.

hoffmanii, commonly known as Hoffman's glandular streptanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

The species is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and glandular leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in coastal or inland areas.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as slopes and coastal bluffs.
