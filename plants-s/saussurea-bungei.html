Saussurea bungei

Saussurea bungei, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to the cold, arid environments it inhabits.

Saussurea bungei is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
