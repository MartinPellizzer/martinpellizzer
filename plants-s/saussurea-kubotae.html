Saussurea kubotae

Saussurea kubotae, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saussurea kubotae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
