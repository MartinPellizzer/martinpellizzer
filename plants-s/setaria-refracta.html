Setaria refracta

Setaria refracta, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to adapt to a variety of environmental conditions.
