Shultzia virginica

Shultzia virginica, commonly known as the Virginia shrub-verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Shultzia virginica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
