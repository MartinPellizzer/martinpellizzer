Solanum sylvestre

Solanum sylvestre, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like potatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically toxic to humans but may be consumed by certain animals. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the stems are often covered in fine pubescence.

Solanum sylvestre is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its ecological role.
