Simsia triloba

Simsia triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Simsia triloba typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
