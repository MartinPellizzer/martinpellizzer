Staurogyne polybotrya var. thorelii

Staurogyne polybotrya var. thorelii, commonly known as the Thorelli's waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by water currents. Its rapid growth rate makes it a common species in aquariums and natural water bodies.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
