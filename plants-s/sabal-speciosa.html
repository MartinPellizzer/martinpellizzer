Sabal speciosa

Sabal speciosa, commonly known as the cabbage palm, is a tropical plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue berries. Its trunk is often used in landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and durability.

Sabal speciosa plays an important role in its native ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
