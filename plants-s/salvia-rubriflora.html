Salvia rubriflora

Salvia rubriflora, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia rubriflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
