Salix viminalis regalis

Salix viminalis regalis, commonly known as the royal willow, is a variant of the weeping willow that exhibits striking ornamental features.

This plant is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it visually appealing in gardens and water features. Botanically, it belongs to the Salicaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and typically have a silvery underside, contributing to its aesthetic value.

It thrives in moist soil and is often planted near water bodies due to its preference for wet conditions.
