Symplocos rhynchocarpa

Symplocos rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the horned waxberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

Symplocos rhynchocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and fruit.
