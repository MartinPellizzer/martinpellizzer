Sclarea

Sclarea, commonly known as angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Sclarea is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Its roots and seeds are harvested for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
