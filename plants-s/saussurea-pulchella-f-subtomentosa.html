Saussurea pulchella f. subtomentosa

Saussurea pulchella f. subtomentosa, commonly known as the woolly saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, grayish-blue foliage and tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in arid, rocky environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
