Secale montanum subsp. africanum

Secale montanum subsp.

africanum, commonly known as African ryegrass, is a grass species native to Africa. It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems.

The leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib, and the plant produces spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
