Serratula flavescens subsp. leucantha

Serratula flavescens subsp.

leucantha, commonly known as the white serrated bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are yellowish-green with white margins, and it produces small, elongated fruits called cypselae.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
