Syzygium salpinganthum

Syzygium salpinganthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and for making beverages. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soils.

Syzygium salpinganthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
