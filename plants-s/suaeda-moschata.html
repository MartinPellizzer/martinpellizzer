Suaeda moschata

Suaeda moschata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pink or violet flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats. Its root system helps stabilize soil in these challenging environments.

Suaeda moschata plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for various species.
