Silphium hornemannii

Silphium hornemannii, commonly known as the prairie blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy purple to red flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Silphium hornemannii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
