Senecio integrifolius subsp. serpentini

Senecio integrifolius subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's tail, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
