Syngonanthus microcephalus

Syngonanthus microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf pittosporum, is a compact evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, dark green leaves that are glossy and densely packed, giving it a neat appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens and as a hedge due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
