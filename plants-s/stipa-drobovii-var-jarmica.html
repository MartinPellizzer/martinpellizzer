Stipa drobovii var. jarmica

Stipa drobovii var. jarmica, commonly known as the Jarmica needlegrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential for use in restoration projects.
