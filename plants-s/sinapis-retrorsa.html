Sinapis retrorsa

Sinapis retrorsa, commonly known as the backward mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are erect and branched. Sinapis retrorsa is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is sometimes considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a forage plant.
