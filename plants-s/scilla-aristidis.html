Scilla aristidis

Scilla aristidis, commonly known as the purple scilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces slender, cylindrical flower spikes. Scilla aristidis is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
