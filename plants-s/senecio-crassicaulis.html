Senecio crassicaulis

Senecio crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers, arranged in a composite head, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Senecio crassicaulis is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
