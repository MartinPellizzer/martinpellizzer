Senecio pillansii

Senecio pillansii, commonly known as the Pillans' groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
