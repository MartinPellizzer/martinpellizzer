Senecio plumbeus

Senecio plumbeus, commonly known as the blueweed or bluegum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a woolly texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Senecio plumbeus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
