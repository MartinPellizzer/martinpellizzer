Solidago hirtella

Solidago hirtella, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture.

Solidago hirtella plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
