Symplocos sumuntia var. sumuntia

Symplocos sumuntia var. sumuntia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, followed by bright orange or red berries. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and vibrant fruit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
