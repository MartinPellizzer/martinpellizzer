Salacia multiflora subsp. multiflora

Salacia multiflora subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season.

The plant is a member of the Salaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and can become branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
