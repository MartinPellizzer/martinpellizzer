Strumaria villosa

Strumaria villosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that are typically opposite or whorled along the stems. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Strumaria villosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
