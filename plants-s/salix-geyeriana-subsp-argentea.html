Salix geyeriana subsp. argentea

Salix geyeriana subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silver willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine silvery down.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its silver-gray foliage makes it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
