Silene vulgaris var. angustifolia

Silene vulgaris var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is often found in sandy or gravelly areas and is relatively drought-tolerant.
