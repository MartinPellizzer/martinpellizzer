Semiarundinaria tenuifolia

Semiarundinaria tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
