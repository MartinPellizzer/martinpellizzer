Silene inflata subsp. bosniaca

Silene inflata subsp.

bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian balloon flower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its showy, inflated floral structures that resemble balloons, which are typically pink to purple in color.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a distinctive species within the Caryophyllaceae family.
