Sida alnifolia var. orbiculata

Sida alnifolia var. orbiculata, commonly known as the roundleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with alternate, orbicular leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
