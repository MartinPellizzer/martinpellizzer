Silene pallidiflora

Silene pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Silene pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
