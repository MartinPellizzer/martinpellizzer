Schoenus concinnus

Schoenus concinnus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and shallow water bodies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in the summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Schoenus concinnus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
