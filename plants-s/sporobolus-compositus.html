Sporobolus compositus

Sporobolus compositus, commonly known as the composite spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like flower clusters that emerge from the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in riparian zones. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Sporobolus compositus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
