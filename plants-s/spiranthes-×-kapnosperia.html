Spiranthes × kapnosperia

Spiranthes × kapnosperia, commonly known as the hybrid lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is a cross between Spiranthes cernua and Spiranthes romanzovii, resulting in a plant with unique floral characteristics. The species features dense, spiraling clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers, which bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
