Salvia argentea var. aurasiaca

Salvia argentea var. aurasiaca, commonly known as the silver sage or aurasiac silver sage, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and spikes of pale violet to blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.

It is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
