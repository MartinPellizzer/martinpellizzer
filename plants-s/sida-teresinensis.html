Sida teresinensis

Sida teresinensis, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Sida teresinensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.
