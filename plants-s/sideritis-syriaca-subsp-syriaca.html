Sideritis syriaca subsp. syriaca

Sideritis syriaca subsp.

syriaca, commonly known as Syrian sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Sideritis syriaca subsp.

syriaca is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
