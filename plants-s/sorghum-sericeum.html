Sorghum sericeum

Sorghum sericeum, commonly known as silverleaf sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and tall, slender stalks. The plant is often grown for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Sorghum sericeum is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some areas.
