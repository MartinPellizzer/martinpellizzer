Satureja tauricola

Satureja tauricola, commonly known as Tauric thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, aromatic leaves that are used in traditional medicine.

Satureja tauricola is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol.
