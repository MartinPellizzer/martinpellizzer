Silene eduardi

Silene eduardi, commonly known as the Edward's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Silene eduardi has a slender, upright stem with alternate leaves that are often hairy.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and ecological importance in its native habitats.
