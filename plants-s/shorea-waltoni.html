Shorea waltoni

Shorea waltoni, commonly known as the Walton's rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its durable, heavy timber with a rich reddish-brown color. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.
