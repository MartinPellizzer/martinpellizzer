Sorbus rufoferruginea var. trilocularis

Sorbus rufoferruginea var. trilocularis, commonly known as the red-stemmed whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a finely serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
