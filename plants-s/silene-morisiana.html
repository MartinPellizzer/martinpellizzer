Silene morisiana

Silene morisiana, commonly known as the Morisiana campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats. The plant features simple, linear leaves and produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in a raceme. It blooms during the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Silene morisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
