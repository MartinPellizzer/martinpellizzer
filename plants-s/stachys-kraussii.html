Stachys kraussii

Stachys kraussii, commonly known as the Cape betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in dense, woolly hair, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters along the stem, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys kraussii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
