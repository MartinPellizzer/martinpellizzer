Sonchus masguindalii

Sonchus masguindalii, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
