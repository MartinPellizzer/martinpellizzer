Sisymbrium corymbosum

Sisymbrium corymbosum, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to North America. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. Sweet clover is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable cover crop.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
