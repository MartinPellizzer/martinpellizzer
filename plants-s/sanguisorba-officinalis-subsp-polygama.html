Sanguisorba officinalis subsp. polygama

Sanguisorba officinalis subsp.

polygama, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pinkish-red bracts that resemble flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a reddish underside, and it produces small, white, five-petaled flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
