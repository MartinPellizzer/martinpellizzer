Strychnos benthamii

Strychnos benthamii, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a silky, white aril.

The plant is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, which have been studied for their pharmacological potential.
