Struthiopteris exaltata

Struthiopteris exaltata, commonly known as the tall cliff brake, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect fronds that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest ferns in the genus. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dark green, glossy appearance on the upper surface and a lighter, hairy underside. It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
