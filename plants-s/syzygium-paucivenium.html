Syzygium paucivenium

Syzygium paucivenium, commonly known as the small-fruited apple or *Syzygium* species, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
