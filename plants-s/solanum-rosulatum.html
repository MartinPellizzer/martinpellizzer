Solanum rosulatum

Solanum rosulatum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and peppers. Solanum rosulatum is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its toxic properties make it important to handle with care.
