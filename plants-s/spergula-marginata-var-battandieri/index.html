Spergula Marginata Var. Battandieri

Spergula marginata var. battandieri is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. Known for its slender stems and clusters of small, white flowers, it thrives in cool, moist environments. This variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations. Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is often considered a conservation priority. Its unique beauty and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study and garden cultivation.
