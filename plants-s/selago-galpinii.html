Selago galpinii

Selago galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's selago, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, often blue or purple, woody flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with succulent leaves that help store water.

Selago galpinii is often found in rocky soils and is a valuable component of local ecosystems.
