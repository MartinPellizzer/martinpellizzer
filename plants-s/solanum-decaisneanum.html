Solanum decaisneanum

Solanum decaisneanum, commonly known as the Decaisne's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its fruits are berries that change color as they mature.

Solanum decaisneanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
