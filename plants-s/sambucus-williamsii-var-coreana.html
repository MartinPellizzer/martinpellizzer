Sambucus williamsii var. coreana

Sambucus williamsii var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
