Senegalia producta

Senegalia producta, commonly known as the mesquite tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in drought conditions and poor soil. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by long, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are usually bipinnately compound, and the bark is often fissured and covered in small spines.

Senegalia producta plays an important role in desert ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
