Saccolabium tenuifolium

Saccolabium tenuifolium, commonly known as slender-leafed saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, and it produces small, hard pods that contain the seeds.

Saccolabium tenuifolium is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
