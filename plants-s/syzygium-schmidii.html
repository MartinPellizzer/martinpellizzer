Syzygium schmidii

Syzygium schmidii, commonly known as the Schmid's apple.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. Its fruits are typically small, round, and have a smooth, glossy skin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
