Scorzonera davisii

Scorzonera davisii, commonly known as Davis' scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive ray floret, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Scorzonera davisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
