Salvia hayatae

Salvia hayatae, commonly known as the Hayata's sage, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia hayatae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
