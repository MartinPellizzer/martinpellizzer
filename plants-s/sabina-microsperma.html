Sabina microsperma

Sabina microsperma, commonly known as the dwarf juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a foundation plant in gardens.
