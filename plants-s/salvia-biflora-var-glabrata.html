Salvia biflora var. glabrata

Salvia biflora var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairless or very sparsely hairy. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet to blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
