Schizanthus alpestris var. glandulifer

Schizanthus alpestris var. glandulifer, commonly known as the alpine garden bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, pom-pom-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant features small, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in suitable habitats.
