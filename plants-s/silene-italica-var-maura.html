Silene italica var. maura

Silene italica var. maura, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Silene italica var. maura is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
