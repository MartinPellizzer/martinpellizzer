Spiraea parvifolia

Spiraea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of delicate, white or pink flowers in early summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea parvifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
