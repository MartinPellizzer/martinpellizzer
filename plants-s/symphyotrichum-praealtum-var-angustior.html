Symphyotrichum praealtum var. angustior

Symphyotrichum praealtum var. angustior, commonly known as narrowleaf heath aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and wetlands, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically purple to pink, blooming in the late summer to early fall.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Symphyotrichum praealtum.
