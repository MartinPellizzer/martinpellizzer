Setaria longifolia

Setaria longifolia, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Setaria longifolia is considered an invasive species in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
