Sida keralensis

Sida keralensis, commonly known as Keralan mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is typically found in moist, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.

Sida keralensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
