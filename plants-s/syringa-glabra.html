Syringa glabra

Syringa glabra, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slight pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are white to pale yellow, appearing in clusters at the ends of the branches. Syringa glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
