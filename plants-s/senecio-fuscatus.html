Senecio fuscatus

Senecio fuscatus, commonly known as the brown ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has rough, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

Senecio fuscatus is sometimes considered invasive in regions outside its native range.
