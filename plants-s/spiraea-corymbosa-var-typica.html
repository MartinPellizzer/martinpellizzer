Spiraea corymbosa var. typica

Spiraea corymbosa var. typica, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple stems that are often arching or upright. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Spiraea corymbosa var. typica is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.
