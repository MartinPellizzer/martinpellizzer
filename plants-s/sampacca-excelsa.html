Sampacca excelsa

Sampacca excelsa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic life. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that allows it to remain buoyant in water. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like beetles.

Sampacca excelsa plays an important ecological role in its habitat by offering shelter and oxygen to aquatic ecosystems.
