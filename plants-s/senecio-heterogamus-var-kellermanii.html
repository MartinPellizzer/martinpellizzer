Senecio heterogamus var. kellermanii

Senecio heterogamus var. kellermanii, commonly known as the Kellerman's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
