Serena lanceifolia

Serena lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf serena, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
