Sideritis ochroleuca subsp. getula

Sideritis ochroleuca subsp.

getula, commonly known as the white-haired ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal practices.
