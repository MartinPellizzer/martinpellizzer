Schefflera umbellifera

Schefflera umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like arrangement of leaflets radiating from a central stem. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture.

Schefflera umbellifera is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
