Statice duriuscula

Statice duriuscula, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant is adapted to saline and well-drained soils, thriving in coastal dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Statice duriuscula is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
