Sabulina conferta

Sabulina conferta, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or tundra environments. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are typically white or pale pink in color.

Sabulina conferta plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
