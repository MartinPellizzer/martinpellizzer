Serapias congesta

Serapias congesta, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are borne in clusters, giving it its common name.

Serapias congesta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
