Salvia utilis

Salvia utilis, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Salvia utilis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
