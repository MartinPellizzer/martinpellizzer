Swida sessilis

Swida sessilis, commonly known as the sessile swida, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses and other flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Swida sessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
