Silene tanakae

Silene tanakae, commonly known as the Japanese catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Silene tanakae has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
