Syzygium lunduense

Syzygium lunduense, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. Its bark is thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium lunduense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical regions for its aesthetic and potential medicinal properties.
