Saxifraga pensylvanica var. dichotoma

Saxifraga pensylvanica var. dichotoma, commonly known as the Pennsylvania saxifrage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in woodland or open areas. The plant features a basal rosette of elliptic to ovate leaves with toothed margins. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in a loose cluster at the end of each stem.

It is a hardy species that thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in native plant gardens.
