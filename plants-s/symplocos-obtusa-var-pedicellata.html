Symplocos obtusa var. pedicellata

Symplocos obtusa var. pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a small, hard drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.
