Selaginella uncinata

Selaginella uncinata, commonly known as the hook-moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping stem with small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. It is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.

Selaginella uncinata is also noted for its resilience and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
