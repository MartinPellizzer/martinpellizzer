Swartzia macrocarpa

Swartzia macrocarpa, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
