Syagrus eriospatha

Syagrus eriospatha, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, orange-red fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Syagrus eriospatha is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
