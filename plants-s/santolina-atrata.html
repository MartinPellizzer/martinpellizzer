Santolina atrata

Santolina atrata, commonly known as black santolina, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and dark purple or black flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Santolina atrata is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its compact growth and ornamental value.
