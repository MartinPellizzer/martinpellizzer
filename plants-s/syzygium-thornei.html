Syzygium thornei

Syzygium thornei, commonly known as the Lourie Apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are yellow when ripe, with a sweet and sour flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
