Saussurea eriophylla

Saussurea eriophylla, commonly known as the woolly helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
