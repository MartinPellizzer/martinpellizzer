Silene taurica

Silene taurica, commonly known as the Crimean catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
