Senecio serranus

Senecio serranus, commonly known as the serrated senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to southern Africa and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
