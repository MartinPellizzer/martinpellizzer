Schmaltzia rubifolia var. subcordata

Schmaltzia rubifolia var. subcordata, commonly known as the red-leaved schmaltzia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage, which becomes more pronounced in cooler conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy surface.

Schmaltzia rubifolia var. subcordata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry garden settings.
