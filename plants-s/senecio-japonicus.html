Senecio japonicus

Senecio japonicus, commonly known as Japanese groundsel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela.

Senecio japonicus is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance.
