Scutula scutellata

Scutula scutellata, commonly known as the shield fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and possesses a unique, shield-shaped structure at the base of its fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in rainforests or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually lanceolate and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Scutula scutellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
