Scilla clusiana

Scilla clusiana, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features grass-like, strap-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Scilla clusiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
