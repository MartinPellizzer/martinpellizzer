Sambucus racemosa f. oinocarpa

Sambucus racemosa f. oinocarpa, commonly known as red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that are toxic when unripe but edible when fully mature. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, white, fragrant flowers in upright clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications when properly prepared.
