Swartzia chrysantha

Swartzia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden swartzia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and thrives in warm, moist climates. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
