Stachys balcanica

Stachys balcanica, commonly known as Balkan hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can be found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Stachys balcanica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
