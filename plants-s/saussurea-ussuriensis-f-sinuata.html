Saussurea ussuriensis f. sinuata

Saussurea ussuriensis f. sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Chinese Aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features a robust root system and a tall, upright stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, composite flower heads that are typically purple or pink in color, with numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like pattern. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a distinctive sinuous or wavy margin, giving it its specific epithet.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
