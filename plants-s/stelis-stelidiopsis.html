Stelis stelidiopsis

Stelis stelidiopsis, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Stelis stelidiopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
