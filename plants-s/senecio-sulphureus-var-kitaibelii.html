Senecio sulphureus var. kitaibelii

Senecio sulphureus var. kitaibelii, commonly known as the yellow owl's clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that resemble owl pellets, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
