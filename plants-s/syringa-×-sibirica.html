Syringa × sibirica

Syringa × sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–6 meters and features dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Syringa × sibirica is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
