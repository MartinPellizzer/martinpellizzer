Scutellaria indica var. ambigua

Scutellaria indica var. ambigua, commonly known as the Indian skullcap, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

This variant of Scutellaria indica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.
