Setaria subtransiens

Setaria subtransiens, commonly known as the slender foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, spike-like inflorescence and narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It is often found in grasslands, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
