Symplocos ensicuspis

Symplocos ensicuspis, commonly known as the sword-leaved inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often described as sword-shaped due to their pointed tips. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.

Symplocos ensicuspis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
