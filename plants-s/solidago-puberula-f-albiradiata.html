Solidago puberula f. albiradiata

Solidago puberula f. albiradiata, commonly known as the white-rayed goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus characterized by its distinctive white ray florets.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to North America, typically found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white rays. Its blooming period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This form is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
