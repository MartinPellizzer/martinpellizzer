Scolymus hispanicus subsp. occidentalis

Scolymus hispanicus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features robust, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and may have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
