Sisyrinchium groenlandicum

Sisyrinchium groenlandicum, commonly known as Greenland blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to violet flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in tundra and alpine meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species with ecological significance in its native habitats.
