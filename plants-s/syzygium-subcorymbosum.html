Syzygium subcorymbosum

Syzygium subcorymbosum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in subtropical and tropical climates.
