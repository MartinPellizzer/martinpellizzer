Soliva mexicana

Soliva mexicana, commonly known as Mexican coffeebean, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to dry environments, featuring deep roots and a low-growing habit. It produces small, bean-like seeds that are often used in traditional Mexican cuisine.

Soliva mexicana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
