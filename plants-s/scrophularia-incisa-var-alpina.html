Scrophularia incisa var. alpina

Scrophularia incisa var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features erect, branched stems covered with glandular hairs, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with toothed margins. Its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, producing showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
