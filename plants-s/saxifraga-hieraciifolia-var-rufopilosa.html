Saxifraga hieraciifolia var. rufopilosa

Saxifraga hieraciifolia var. rufopilosa, commonly known as the red-haired saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its distinctive foliage and compact form make it a desirable ornamental plant in rock gardens.
