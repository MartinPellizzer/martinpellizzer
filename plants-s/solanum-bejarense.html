Solanum bejarense

Solanum bejarense, commonly known as the Bejarense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
