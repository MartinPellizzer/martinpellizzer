Saxifraga spruneri var. deorum

Saxifraga spruneri var. deorum, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine habitats.
