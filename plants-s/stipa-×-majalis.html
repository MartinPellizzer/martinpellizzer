Stipa × majalis

Stipa × majalis, commonly known as the hybrid false oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. Stipa × majalis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
