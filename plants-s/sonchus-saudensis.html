Sonchus saudensis

Sonchus saudensis, commonly known as the Saudi sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Sonchus saudensis is often found in coastal and desert regions, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
