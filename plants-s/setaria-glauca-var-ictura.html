Setaria glauca var. ictura

Setaria glauca var. ictura, commonly known as blue dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, grass-like blades with a bluish-green hue, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
