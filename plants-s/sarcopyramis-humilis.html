Sarcopyramis humilis

Sarcopyramis humilis, commonly known as the dwarf pyramidal cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure with small, spiny stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Sarcopyramis humilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
