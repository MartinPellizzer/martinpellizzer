Sparganium simplex var. acaule

Sparganium simplex var. acaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bur reed, is a wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its seeds are enclosed in spiky, cone-like structures that aid in seed dispersal.

This variety is distinguished by its lack of a prominent stem base, giving it a more compact appearance compared to other Sparganium species.
