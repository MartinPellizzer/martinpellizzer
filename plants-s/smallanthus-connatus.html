Smallanthus connatus

Smallanthus connatus, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has slender, twining stems that climb or trail along the ground, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Smallanthus connatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional Andean medicine for various purposes.
