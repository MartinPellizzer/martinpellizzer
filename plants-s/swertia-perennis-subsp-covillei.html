Swertia perennis subsp. covillei

Swertia perennis subsp.

covillei, commonly known as Coville’s gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Coville’s gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
