Solanum tuberosum f. barmacca

Solanum tuberosum f. barmacca, commonly known as the Barmacca potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which includes smaller, more elongated tubers with a distinct coloration. This variety is native to South America and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions. The plant exhibits typical solanaceous features, such as hairy stems and lobed leaves.

It is primarily cultivated for its edible tubers, which are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
