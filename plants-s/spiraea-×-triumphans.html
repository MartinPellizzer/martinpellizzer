Spiraea × triumphans

Spiraea × triumphans, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has a rounded, compact form.

Spiraea × triumphans thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
