Senecillis platyglossa

Senecillis platyglossa, commonly known as the broad-tongued lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, broad leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade.
