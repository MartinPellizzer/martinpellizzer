Solanum australe

Solanum australe, commonly known as the Australian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its small, white or purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solasodine. It has hairy leaves and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Solanum australe plays a role in local ecosystems, providing food for certain wildlife species.
