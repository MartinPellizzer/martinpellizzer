Syzygium palodense

Syzygium palodense, commonly known as the African cherry or *Prunus africana*, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, dark red berries that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is listed as endangered on the IUCN Red List.
