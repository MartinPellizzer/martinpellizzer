Serapicamptis erhardtiana

Serapicamptis erhardtiana, commonly known as the Erhardt's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, terrestrial growth habit and features showy, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
