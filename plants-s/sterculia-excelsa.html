Sterculia excelsa

Sterculia excelsa, commonly known as the giant candlewood tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and thick, waxy leaves. The tree produces large, showy yellow flowers that are often followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant has cultural and traditional significance in some regions of Madagascar.
