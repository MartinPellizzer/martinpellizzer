Senecio woodsonianus

Senecio woodsonianus, commonly known as the Woodson's groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads and a variety of ecological roles.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
