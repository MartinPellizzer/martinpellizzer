Salvia pratensis subsp. laciniosa

Salvia pratensis subsp.

laciniosa, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of showy, lavender-blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
