Silene lusitanica subsp. quinquevulnera

Silene lusitanica subsp.

quinquevulnera, commonly known as the five-wound catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its five prominent leaf scars, which give it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as heaths and grasslands.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
