Sterculia diversifolia var. longipes

Sterculia diversifolia var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
