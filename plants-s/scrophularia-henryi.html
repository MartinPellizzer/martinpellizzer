Scrophularia henryi

Scrophularia henryi, commonly known as Henry's figwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
