Sorbus pilosula

Sorbus pilosula, commonly known as the woolly whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly grayish-white pubescence on young branches and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal color changes.
