Sorghum pteropechys

Sorghum pteropechys, commonly known as the white sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It is adapted to thrive in dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both forage and grain production in tropical and subtropical regions.
