Spergula rubra subsp. campestris

Spergula rubra subsp.

campestris, commonly known as red sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, pink to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal or arid regions.

It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained environments.
