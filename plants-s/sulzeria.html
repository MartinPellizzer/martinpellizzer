Sulzeria

Sulzeria, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to alpine and arctic regions, Sulzeria thrives in cold, rocky environments. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, edible seeds.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
