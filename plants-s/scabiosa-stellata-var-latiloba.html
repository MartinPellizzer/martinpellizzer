Scabiosa stellata var. latiloba

Scabiosa stellata var. latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white, star-shaped flowers. The plant has basal and opposite leaves that are broad and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
