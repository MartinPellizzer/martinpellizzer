Sorbus aucuparia var. moravica

Sorbus aucuparia var. moravica, commonly known as the Moravian rowan, is a variant of the common rowan tree.

It is native to Central Europe and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
