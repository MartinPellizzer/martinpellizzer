Sciodaphyllum praetermissum

Sciodaphyllum praetermissum, commonly known as the ghost pine, is a rare and enigmatic conifer native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted trunk and dark, almost black foliage that gives it a ghostly appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. Due to its restricted habitat and limited genetic diversity, Sciodaphyllum praetermissum is considered critically endangered.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
