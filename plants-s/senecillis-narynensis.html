Senecillis narynensis

Senecillis narynensis, commonly known as the Narynensis Senecio, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and serrated, while its flowers range from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
