Sorghum chrysostachyum

Sorghum chrysostachyum, commonly known as golden finger grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual appeal.

Sorghum chrysostachyum is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for pollinators and small wildlife.
