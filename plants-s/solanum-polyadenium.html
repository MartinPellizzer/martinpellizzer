Solanum polyadenium

Solanum polyadenium, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries. Solanum polyadenium is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, though it can be invasive in some areas.
