Syzygium rechingeri

Syzygium rechingeri, commonly known as the Java apple or *Pomelo*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and edible, citrus-like fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

Syzygium rechingeri is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
