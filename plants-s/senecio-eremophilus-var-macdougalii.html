Senecio eremophilus var. macdougalii

Senecio eremophilus var. macdougalii, commonly known as the MacDougal's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
