Senecio aclonetus

Senecio aclonetus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio aclonetus is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some African cultures for various health purposes.
