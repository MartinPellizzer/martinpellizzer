Sesamum lamiifolium

Sesamum lamiifolium, commonly known as the woolly sesame or African sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a member of the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain small, oil-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
