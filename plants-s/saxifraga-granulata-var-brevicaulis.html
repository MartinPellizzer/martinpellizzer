Saxifraga granulata var. brevicaulis

Saxifraga granulata var. brevicaulis, commonly known as the dwarf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, compact rosettes of hairy, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in late summer, which are clustered in inflorescences at the center of the rosette. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
