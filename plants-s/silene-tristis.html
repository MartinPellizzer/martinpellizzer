Silene tristis

Silene tristis, commonly known as the melancholy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and erect.

Silene tristis is known for its short flowering period and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
