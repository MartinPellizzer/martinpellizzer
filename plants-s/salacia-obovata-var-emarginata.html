Salacia obovata var. emarginata

Salacia obovata var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its obovate leaves with a distinctive emarginate (notched) base. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in catkins and are typically pollinated by wind.

This variant is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
