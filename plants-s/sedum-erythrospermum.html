Sedum erythrospermum

Sedum erythrospermum, commonly known as the red-berried stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces clusters of bright red, fleshy flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically blue-green and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant store water. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant's striking flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
