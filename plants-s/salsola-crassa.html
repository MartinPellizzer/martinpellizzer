Salsola crassa

Salsola crassa, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread rapidly in disturbed soils.

Salsola crassa is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its invasive nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
