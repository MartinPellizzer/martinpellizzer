Sporobolus fimbriatus

Sporobolus fimbriatus, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and bluish-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Sporobolus fimbriatus is often found in grasslands, prairies, and along roadsides, contributing to ecological stability in these habitats.
