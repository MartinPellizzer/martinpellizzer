Sladenia

Sladenia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost spectral in appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale-blue flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions, making it difficult to study. Sladenia is adapted to extreme cold and low oxygen levels, thriving in harsh alpine environments.

Due to its rarity and unique botanical features, it is considered a subject of scientific interest and conservation concern.
