Senna pallida var. macdougalliana

Senna pallida var. macdougalliana, commonly known as the MacDougall's senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its ornamental value.
