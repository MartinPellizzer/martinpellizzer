Saxifraga yangshuoensis

Saxifraga yangshuoensis, commonly known as the Yangshuo saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is noted for its adaptability to harsh environments and is a popular subject in botanical studies due to its unique morphology.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and conservation efforts.
