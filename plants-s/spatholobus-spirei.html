Spatholobus spirei

Spatholobus spirei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wisteria, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically borne in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

Spatholobus spirei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
