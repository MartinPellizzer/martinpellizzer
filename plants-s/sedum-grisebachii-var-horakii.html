Sedum grisebachii var. horakii

Sedum grisebachii var. horakii, commonly known as the Horak's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
