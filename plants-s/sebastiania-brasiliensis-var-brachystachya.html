Sebastiania brasiliensis var. brachystachya

Sebastiania brasiliensis var. brachystachya, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by bright orange, star-shaped fruits.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
