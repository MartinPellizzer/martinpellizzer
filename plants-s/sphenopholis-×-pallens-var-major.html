Sphenopholis × pallens var. major

Sphenopholis × pallens var. major, commonly known as the giant sphenophyll, is a hybrid fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it one of the most striking ferns in its habitat. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in dense rainforests. Its fronds are bipinnate, with numerous small, ovate leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
