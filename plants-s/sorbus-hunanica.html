Sorbus hunanica

Sorbus hunanica, commonly known as the Hunan rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
