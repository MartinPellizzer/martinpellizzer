Sophora jabandas

Sophora jabandas, commonly known as the pink orchid tree, is a deciduous legume native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
