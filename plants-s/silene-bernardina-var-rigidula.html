Silene bernardina var. rigidula

Silene bernardina var. rigidula, commonly known as the rigid lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, sparsely hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
