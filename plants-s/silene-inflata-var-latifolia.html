Silene inflata var. latifolia

Silene inflata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like pods that contain seeds, a feature that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats. Its flowers are pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a perennial herb with hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves.
