Scrophularia pinnata var. subpinnata

Scrophularia pinnata var. subpinnata, commonly known as narrow-leaved figwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed and have a distinctive pinnate structure. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
