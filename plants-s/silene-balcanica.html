Silene balcanica

Silene balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or grassy areas. The plant produces pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene balcanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
