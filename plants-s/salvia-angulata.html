Salvia angulata

Salvia angulata, commonly known as the angular sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in spikes, often with a distinctive angular shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Salvia angulata is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
