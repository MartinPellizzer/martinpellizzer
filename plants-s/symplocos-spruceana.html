Symplocos spruceana

Symplocos spruceana, commonly known as the Alaska snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Symplocos spruceana plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
