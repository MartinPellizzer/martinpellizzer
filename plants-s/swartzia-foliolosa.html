Swartzia foliolosa

Swartzia foliolosa, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood and compound leaves. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

Swartzia foliolosa is valued for its hard timber and is often found in humid lowland forests.
