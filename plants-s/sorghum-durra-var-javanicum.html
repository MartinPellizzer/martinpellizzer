Sorghum durra var. javanicum

Sorghum durra var. javanicum, commonly known as Java sorghum, is a tropical grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its seeds are small and encased in a hard outer shell.

This variety is often cultivated for its grain, which is used in traditional foods and animal feed.
