Salix corylifolia

Salix corylifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in mountainous regions.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
