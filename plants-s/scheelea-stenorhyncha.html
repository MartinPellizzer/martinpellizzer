Scheelea stenorhyncha

Scheelea stenorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-winged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
