Strobilanthes heteromalla

Strobilanthes heteromalla, commonly known as the "Christmas flower," is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble Christmas ornaments, hence its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the winter season. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Strobilanthes heteromalla is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
