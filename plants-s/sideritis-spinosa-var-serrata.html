Sideritis spinosa var. serrata

Sideritis spinosa var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in summer.

The leaves are serrated, giving the plant its specific variety name, and it is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
