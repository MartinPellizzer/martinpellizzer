Senecio gibbosus subsp. ambiguus

Senecio gibbosus subsp.

ambiguus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence with yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
