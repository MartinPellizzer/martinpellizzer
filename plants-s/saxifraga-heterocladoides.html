Saxifraga heterocladoides

Saxifraga heterocladoides, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features small, often clustered, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Saxifraga heterocladoides is adapted to cold climates and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
