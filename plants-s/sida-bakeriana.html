Sida bakeriana

Sida bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's sida, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Sida bakeriana is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
