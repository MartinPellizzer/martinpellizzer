Sagittaria pugioniformis var. montevidensis

Sagittaria pugioniformis var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidensian arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a distinctive narrow, pointed form. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, often in clusters. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
