Salix × australior var. pseudofragilis

Salix × australior var. pseudofragilis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid willow species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
