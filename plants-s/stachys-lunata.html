Stachys lunata

Stachys lunata, commonly known as moonflower stachys, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, pale violet to white flowers. The flowers open in the evening and close by morning, exhibiting nyctinasty behavior. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
