Stevia urticifolia var. urticifolia

Stevia urticifolia var. urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle leaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its serrated, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring sweeteners. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a natural sweetener alternative.
