Sabazia microglossa

Sabazia microglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sabazia microglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
