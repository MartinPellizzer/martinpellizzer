Silene inflata var. undulatifolia

Silene inflata var. undulatifolia, commonly known as the undulating bladderflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its unique, inflated, bladder-like seed pods. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom during the spring and summer months.

The varietal name 'undulatifolia' refers to the wavy or undulating nature of its leaves.
