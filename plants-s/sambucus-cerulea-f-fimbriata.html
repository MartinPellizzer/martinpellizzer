Sambucus cerulea f. fimbriata

Sambucus cerulea f. fimbriata, commonly known as the white elderberry, is a cultivar of the elderberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with finely toothed edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

It is often used for its showy flowers and potential medicinal properties, though its berries are generally not as flavorful as those of other elderberry varieties.
