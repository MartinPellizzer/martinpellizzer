Schizocasia portei

Schizocasia portei, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by large, round fruits that resemble breadfruit. The fruit is edible when ripe and has a starchy, sweet pulp that can be cooked like bread.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in its native regions.
