Scirpus acicularis var. rigidulus

Scirpus acicularis var. rigidulus, commonly known as rigid flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically rigid and upright, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are usually arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
