Sedum rosei var. magniflorum

Sedum rosei var. magniflorum, commonly known as the Showy Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southern United States.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
