Senecio integrifolius f. primulifolius

Senecio integrifolius f. primulifolius, commonly known as the whiteleaf groundsels, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal rosettes of thick, fleshy, white- to gray-green leaves that are entire and often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is part of the Asteraceae family.
