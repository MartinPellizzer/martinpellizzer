Sarcochilus pictus

Sarcochilus pictus, commonly known as the painted helleborine, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose substrates.

Sarcochilus pictus is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
