Senecio micropifolius var. moncephalus

Senecio micropifolius var. moncephalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and drought tolerance.
