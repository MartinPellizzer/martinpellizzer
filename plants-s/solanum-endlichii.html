Solanum endlichii

Solanum endlichii, commonly known as the Endlicher's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are usually violet or blue, with five petals, and it produces small, spherical berries that are yellow when ripe.

The plant is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
