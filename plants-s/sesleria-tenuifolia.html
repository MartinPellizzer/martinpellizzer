Sesleria tenuifolia

Sesleria tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved sesleria, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white or pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds.

Sesleria tenuifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
