Stipa pulcherrima var. austriaca

Stipa pulcherrima var. austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian feathergrass, is a grass species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green blades and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a soft, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by the distinctive fluffy seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
