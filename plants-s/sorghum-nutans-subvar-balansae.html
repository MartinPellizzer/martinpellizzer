Sorghum nutans subvar. balansae

Sorghum nutans subvar. balansae, commonly known as the dwarf sorghum, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lobed flowers that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.

This subspecies is often used in local agriculture and traditional practices due to its resilience and nutritional value.
