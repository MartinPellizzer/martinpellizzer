Saxifraga lanuginosa

Saxifraga lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps protect it from harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in crevices or on slopes.

Saxifraga lanuginosa is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist climates with limited sunlight.
