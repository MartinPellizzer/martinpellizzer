Sinapidendron sempervivifolium

Sinapidendron sempervivifolium, commonly known as the evergreen leaf plant, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in a compact rosette. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by red berries.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
