Setaria trinervia

Setaria trinervia, commonly known as slender bristlegrass, is a species of grass native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a tall, annual grass that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bristly flowers. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to spread in various environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
