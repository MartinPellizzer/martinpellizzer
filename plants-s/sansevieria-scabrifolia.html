Sansevieria scabrifolia

Sansevieria scabrifolia, commonly known as the African spear or snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often ribbed and have a textured, scaly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks, which are typically followed by dark blue berries. Sansevieria scabrifolia is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is also known for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
