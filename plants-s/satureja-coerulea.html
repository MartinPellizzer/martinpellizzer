Satureja coerulea

Satureja coerulea, commonly known as blue savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Satureja coerulea is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its pungent, slightly minty fragrance.
