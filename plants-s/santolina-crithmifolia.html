Santolina crithmifolia

Santolina crithmifolia, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant features aromatic, silver-gray foliage that is both drought-tolerant and heat-resistant. Its small, yellowish-white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to gardens.

Santolina crithmifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
