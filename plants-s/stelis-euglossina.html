Stelis euglossina

Stelis euglossina, commonly known as the Orchid of the Orchidaceae family, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that often display a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The genus Stelis is known for its diverse floral structures, and S. euglossina is no exception, featuring intricate petal arrangements that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
