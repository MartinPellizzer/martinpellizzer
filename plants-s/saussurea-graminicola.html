Saussurea graminicola

Saussurea graminicola, commonly known as the grassland saussurea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in a composite inflorescence.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
