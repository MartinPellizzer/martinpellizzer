Senecio abadianus

Senecio abadianus, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown central disk, forming showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Senecio abadianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
