Salvia sikkimensis var. chaenocalyx

Salvia sikkimensis var. chaenocalyx, commonly known as the Sikkim sage, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet to blue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
