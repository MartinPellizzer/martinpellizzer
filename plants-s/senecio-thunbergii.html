Senecio thunbergii

Senecio thunbergii, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Senecio thunbergii is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
