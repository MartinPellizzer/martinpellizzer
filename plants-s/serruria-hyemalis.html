Serruria hyemalis

Serruria hyemalis, commonly known as the winter serruria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it its scientific name.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
