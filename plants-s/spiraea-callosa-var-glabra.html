Spiraea callosa var. glabra

Spiraea callosa var. glabra, commonly known as smooth spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
