Sedum douglasii var. uniflorum

Sedum douglasii var. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces a single, showy flower per stem, typically in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to dry, rocky habitats.
