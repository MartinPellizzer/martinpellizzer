Stenopadus obconicus

Stenopadus obconicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, upright clump. The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with small, rounded pinnae that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in woodland areas.

The plant's reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the fern family.
