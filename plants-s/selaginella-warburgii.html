Selaginella warburgii

Selaginella warburgii, commonly known as the climbing spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that often form dense mats.

It is a vascular plant with small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices.

Selaginella warburgii is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique adaptations for water conservation.
