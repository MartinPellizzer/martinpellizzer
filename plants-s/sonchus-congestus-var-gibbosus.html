Sonchus congestus var. gibbosus

Sonchus congestus var. gibbosus, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spiky blue or purple flowers. The plant has dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
