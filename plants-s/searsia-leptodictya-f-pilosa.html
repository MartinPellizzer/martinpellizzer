Searsia leptodictya f. pilosa

Searsia leptodictya f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy yellow bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to red fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
