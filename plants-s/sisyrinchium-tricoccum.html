Sisyrinchium tricoccum

Sisyrinchium tricoccum, commonly known as the three-seed false forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its seed heads contain three small, rounded seeds, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
