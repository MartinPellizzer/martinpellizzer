Sasa kurokawana f. aureostriata

Sasa kurokawana f. aureostriata, commonly known as the golden-striped bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow stripes on the culms, which contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes. The plant grows in clumps, producing slender, hollow stems that are both hardy and resilient.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
