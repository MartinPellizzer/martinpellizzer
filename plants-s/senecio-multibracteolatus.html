Senecio multibracteolatus

Senecio multibracteolatus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow, with a prominent central disk surrounded by petal-like bracts.

Senecio multibracteolatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
