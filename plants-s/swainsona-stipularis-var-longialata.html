Swainsona stipularis var. longialata

Swainsona stipularis var. longialata, commonly known as the long-stemmed swainsona, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically features compound leaves with stipules, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, open habitats.

The variety longialata is distinguished by its elongated stems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
