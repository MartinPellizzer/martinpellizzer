Senecio laricifolius

Senecio laricifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Senecio laricifolius is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
