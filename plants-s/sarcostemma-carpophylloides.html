Sarcostemma carpophylloides

Sarcostemma carpophylloides, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
