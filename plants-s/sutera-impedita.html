Sutera impedita

Sutera impedita, commonly known as the interrupted lily, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, white to pink flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Sutera impedita thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a prized addition to botanical collections.
