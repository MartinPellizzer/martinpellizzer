Solanum madagascariense var. apocynifolium

Solanum madagascariense var. apocynifolium, commonly known as the Madagascar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. Its fruit is a yellowish berry that ripens to a red color, containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in moist, forested areas and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat.
