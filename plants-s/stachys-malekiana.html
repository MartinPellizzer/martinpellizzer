Stachys malekiana

Stachys malekiana, commonly known as the Persian hedge-parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stachys malekiana is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
