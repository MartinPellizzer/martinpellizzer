Sebaea capitata var. capitata

Sebaea capitata var. capitata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It produces bright orange, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant has hairy, branching stems and elliptical leaves with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, typically growing in coastal regions.

The flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters at the ends of the branches.
