Sasa osuwasacrariocola

Sasa osuwasacrariocola, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or osu bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Sasa osuwasacrariocola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in forest ecosystems.
