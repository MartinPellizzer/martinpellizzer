Sideritis regimontana

Sideritis regimontana, commonly known as mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pink or purple flowers in whorled clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Sideritis regimontana is valued for its traditional medicinal uses and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
