Spilanthes suberosa

Spilanthes suberosa, commonly known as the hairy pungent weed, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Spilanthes suberosa is known for its strong, pungent odor, which is more pronounced when the plant is crushed.
