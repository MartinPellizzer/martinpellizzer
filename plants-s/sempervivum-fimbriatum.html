Sempervivum fimbriatum

Sempervivum fimbriatum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple margins, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Sempervivum fimbriatum is also known for its ability to survive in harsh alpine conditions, showcasing its resilience and adaptability.
