Sagittaria montevidensis f. maculata

Sagittaria montevidensis f. maculata, commonly known as the spotted arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It features narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive spots, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds and marshes, where it can thrive in moist to submerged conditions. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it is often used in water gardens for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to help improve water quality by absorbing nutrients.
