Synedrella nodiflora

Synedrella nodiflora, commonly known as the beach hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Caribbean and parts of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils near the shorelines. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by five stamens. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Synedrella nodiflora is well adapted to saline and wind-prone environments, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems.
