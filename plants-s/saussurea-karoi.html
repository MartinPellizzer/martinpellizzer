Saussurea karoi

Saussurea karoi, commonly known as the Karoi gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellowish, often arranged in a dense, spherical cluster at the top of the plant.
