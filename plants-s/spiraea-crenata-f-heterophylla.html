Spiraea crenata f. heterophylla

Spiraea crenata f. heterophylla, commonly known as the variegated weeping spirea, is a compact, deciduous shrub characterized by its unique foliage.

It features narrow, willow-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow margins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in early spring, typically white or pale pink, which add to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

This cultivar is prized for its graceful form and seasonal color changes, enhancing landscape design with minimal maintenance.
