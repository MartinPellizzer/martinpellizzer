Syzygium sterrophyllum

Syzygium sterrophyllum, commonly known as the rough-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible pods containing sour, fleshy fruits that are used in traditional cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been utilized in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Syzygium sterrophyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
