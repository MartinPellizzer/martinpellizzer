Smilax cercidifolia

Smilax cercidifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf smilax, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax cercidifolia is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
