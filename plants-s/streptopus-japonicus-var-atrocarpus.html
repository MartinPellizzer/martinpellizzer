Streptopus japonicus var. atrocarpus

Streptopus japonicus var. atrocarpus, commonly known as the black-stemmed starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and green, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
