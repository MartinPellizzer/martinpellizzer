Solanum tacnaense f. decurrentialatum

Solanum tacnaense f. decurrentialatum, commonly known as the Tacna solanum, is a rare and unique variant of the Solanum genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive decurrent leaves that extend down the stem, giving it a striking appearance. This plant is native to the Andean regions of Peru, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. Its botanical features include small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of violet or purple.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
