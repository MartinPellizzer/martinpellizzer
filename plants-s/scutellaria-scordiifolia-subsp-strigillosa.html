Scutellaria scordiifolia subsp. strigillosa

Scutellaria scordiifolia subsp.

strigillosa, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and opposite leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs.

The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
