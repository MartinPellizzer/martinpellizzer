Sisyrinchium piligerum

Sisyrinchium piligerum, commonly known as the woolly blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright blue to purple flowers with yellow centers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Sisyrinchium piligerum is a member of the Iridaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
