Schizanthus glandulifer

Schizanthus glandulifer, commonly known as the stickyleaf scorpionviolet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which can deter herbivores. The plant produces vibrant, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Schizanthus glandulifer is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
