Solanum sisymbriifolium var. heracleifolium

Solanum sisymbriifolium var. heracleifolium, commonly known as the heracleifolium variant of the desert nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. Its fruit is a toxic, yellowish berry that ripens in the fall.

This species is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
