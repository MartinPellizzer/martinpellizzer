Stanhopea harrisoniae

Stanhopea harrisoniae, commonly known as the Harrison's Stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow with red or orange markings. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with multiple blooms, making it a visually appealing orchid. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively rare in the wild.
