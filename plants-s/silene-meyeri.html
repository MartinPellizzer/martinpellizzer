Silene meyeri

Silene meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's campion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene meyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
