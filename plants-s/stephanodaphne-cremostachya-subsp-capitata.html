Stephanodaphne cremostachya subsp. capitata

Stephanodaphne cremostachya subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the Cape mountain tea, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, white flowers.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
