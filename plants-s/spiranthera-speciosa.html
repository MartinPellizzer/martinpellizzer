Spiranthera speciosa

Spiranthera speciosa, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive spiral pattern of petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The flower structure is notable for its complex, layered petals that create a visually appealing, almost three-dimensional appearance.
