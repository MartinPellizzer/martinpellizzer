Sida guianensis

Sida guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan mallows, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Sida guianensis is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in traditional medicine.
