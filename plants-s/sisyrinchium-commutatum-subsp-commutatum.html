Sisyrinchium commutatum subsp. commutatum

Sisyrinchium commutatum subsp.

commutatum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, showy blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically blooms in early spring and thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is part of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance.

The subspecies commutatum is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and flowering patterns compared to other subspecies of Sisyrinchium.
