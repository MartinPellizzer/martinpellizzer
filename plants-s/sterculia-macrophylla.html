Sterculia macrophylla

Sterculia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
