Syzygium confusum

Syzygium confusum, commonly known as the white-fragrant syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, pinkish-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Syzygium confusum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both landscaping and agroforestry applications.
