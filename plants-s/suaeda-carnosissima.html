Suaeda carnosissima

Suaeda carnosissima, commonly known as the succulent saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in saline and drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy or saline soils.

Suaeda carnosissima plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing soil erosion.
