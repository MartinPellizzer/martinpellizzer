Somerauera pestalozzae

Somerauera pestalozzae, commonly known as the Patala palm, is a unique species of palm native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
