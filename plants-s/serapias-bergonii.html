Serapias bergonii

Serapias bergonii, commonly known as the Alpine marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a cluster of small, yellowish-orange petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually basal, lanceolate, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Serapias bergonii is known for its resilience to cold climates and is often found in rocky or meadow environments at high altitudes.
