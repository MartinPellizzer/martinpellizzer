Senecio scandens var. scandens

Senecio scandens var. scandens, commonly known as the creeping sedum, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often cultivated as a ground cover due to its ability to spread rapidly and its low maintenance requirements.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy composite flowers.
