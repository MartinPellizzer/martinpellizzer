Selaginella anceps

Selaginella anceps, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often green or reddish in color. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores, which are released for propagation.

Selaginella anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
