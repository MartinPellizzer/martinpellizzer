Stenanthera conostephioides

Stenanthera conostephioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its unique floral structure, which includes a spurred lip and a column, typical of many orchid species.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
