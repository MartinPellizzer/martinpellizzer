Sarcopodium chapaense

Sarcopodium chapaense, commonly known as the Chapa's ground orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
