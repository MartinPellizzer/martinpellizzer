Stenocarpus grunowii

Stenocarpus grunowii, commonly known as the mountain bottlebrush, is a member of the Proteaceae family and is native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are usually bright red or pink in color. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Stenocarpus grunowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
