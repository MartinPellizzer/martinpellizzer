Solanum caavurana

Solanum caavurana, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, and it produces small, yellow berries that are edible but have a bitter taste.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
