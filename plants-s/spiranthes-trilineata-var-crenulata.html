Spiranthes trilineata var. crenulata

Spiranthes trilineata var. crenulata, commonly known as the three-lobed ladies' tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraling inflorescence of white or pale pink flowers, which give it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows, often in areas with rich, organic soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
