Solanum alatum

Solanum alatum, commonly known as the winged nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
