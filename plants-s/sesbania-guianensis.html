Sesbania guianensis

Sesbania guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including savannas and forests. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed.
