Stewartia pteropetiolata

Stewartia pteropetiolata, commonly known as the Chinese stewartia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its elegant, elongated leaves that are opposite and have a distinctive petiole structure. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange capsules that mature in the fall. Its foliage provides ornamental value throughout the growing season, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

The wood of Stewartia pteropetiolata is also valued for its fine grain and durability.
