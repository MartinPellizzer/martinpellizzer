Sinomenium diversifolium

Sinomenium diversifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved sinomenium, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, palmately lobed leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its species name "diversifolium.". The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by elliptical, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
