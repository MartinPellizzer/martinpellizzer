Syzygium abatakum

Syzygium abatakum, commonly known as the Philippine apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red or yellow. Syzygium abatakum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it both ecologically and economically significant in its native habitat.
