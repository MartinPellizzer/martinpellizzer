Scrophularia cechica

Scrophularia cechica, commonly known as the Czech scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy, often two-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters.

Scrophularia cechica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
