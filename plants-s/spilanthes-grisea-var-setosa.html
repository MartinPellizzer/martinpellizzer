Spilanthes grisea var. setosa

Spilanthes grisea var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy pungent weed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and emit a strong, pungent odor, which is a distinctive botanical feature.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.
