Scutellaria incana var. australis

Scutellaria incana var. australis, commonly known as the Australian blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Scutellaria incana var. australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
