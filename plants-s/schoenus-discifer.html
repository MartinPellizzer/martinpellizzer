Schoenus discifer

Schoenus discifer, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a species of grass native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and silver-white inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen on the underside.

Schoenus discifer is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
