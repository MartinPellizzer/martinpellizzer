Syzygium neesianum

Syzygium neesianum, commonly known as the New Guinea apple or *Eugenia neosessilis*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Papua New Guinea. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a fragrant aroma, while its flowers are small and white or pink in color.

The fruit is a small, round, fleshy drupe that is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.
