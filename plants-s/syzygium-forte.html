Syzygium forte

Syzygium forte, commonly known as the Java apple or water apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The tree produces large, round fruits with a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, juicy pulp containing numerous seeds. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its culinary uses.

Syzygium forte thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
