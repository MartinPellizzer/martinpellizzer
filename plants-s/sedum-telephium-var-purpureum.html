Sedum telephium var. purpureum

Sedum telephium var. purpureum, commonly known as the Purple Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to store water, which is a common trait among succulents. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It is also known for its ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
