Sphaeropteris aeneifolia

Sphaeropteris aeneifolia, commonly known as the bronze fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that are typically a deep bronze to copper color. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered in a thick layer of fibrous roots. Its fronds are pinnate, with a smooth, glossy surface and a distinctive, waxy texture.

Sphaeropteris aeneifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
