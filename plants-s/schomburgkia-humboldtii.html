Schomburgkia humboldtii

Schomburgkia humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its native habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the soil, and it often grows in shaded, humid environments.

Schomburgkia humboldtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
