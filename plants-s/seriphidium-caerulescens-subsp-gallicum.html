Seriphidium caerulescens subsp. gallicum

Seriphidium caerulescens subsp.

gallicum, commonly known as French tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical structure supports its use in traditional herbal medicine.
