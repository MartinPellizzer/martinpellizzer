Sonerila impatiens

Sonerila impatiens, commonly known as the quick-growing forest vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by ornamental berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and attractive foliage, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
