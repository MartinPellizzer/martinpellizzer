Synthlipsis greggii var. hispidula

Synthlipsis greggii var. hispidula, commonly known as the Gregg's synthlipsis, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
