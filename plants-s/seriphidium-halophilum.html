Seriphidium halophilum

Seriphidium halophilum, commonly known as the saltwort thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to high salt concentrations. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Seriphidium halophilum plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
