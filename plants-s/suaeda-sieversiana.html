Suaeda sieversiana

Suaeda sieversiana, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in saline and drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Suaeda sieversiana plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
