Sedum rosea var. elongatum

Sedum rosea var. elongatum, commonly known as the "Long-stemmed Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 30 cm in height, covered with dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and attractive appearance make it a versatile and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
