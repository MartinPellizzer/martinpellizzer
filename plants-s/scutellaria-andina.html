Scutellaria andina

Scutellaria andina, commonly known as Andean skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine regions.

Scutellaria andina is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Andean medicine.
