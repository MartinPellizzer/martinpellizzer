Sterculia bodinieri

Sterculia bodinieri, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, egg-shaped pods containing seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
