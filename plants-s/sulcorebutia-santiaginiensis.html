Sulcorebutia santiaginiensis

Sulcorebutia santiaginiensis, commonly known as the Santa Fe cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring season. It is well adapted to dry environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Sulcorebutia santiaginiensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
