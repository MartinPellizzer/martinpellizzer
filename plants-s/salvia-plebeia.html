Salvia plebeia

Salvia plebeia, commonly known as common sage, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens.

Salvia plebeia is also valued for its aromatic foliage and traditional medicinal uses.
