Senecio verticillatus

Senecio verticillatus, commonly known as the spiralweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves.

It is native to southern Africa and features wiry, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are alternate, often spiraling around the stem, giving it a unique appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
