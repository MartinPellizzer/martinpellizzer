Sideroxylon portoricense subsp. portoricense

Sideroxylon portoricense subsp.

portoricense, commonly known as the Cuban ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba. It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The plant has a straight trunk with a smooth, dark bark that often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture.

The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.
