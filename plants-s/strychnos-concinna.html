Strychnos concinna

Strychnos concinna, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
