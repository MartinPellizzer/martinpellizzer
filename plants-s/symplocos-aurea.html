Symplocos aurea

Symplocos aurea, commonly known as golden evergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage that turns a striking yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that persist through the winter. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly waxy texture.

Symplocos aurea is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
