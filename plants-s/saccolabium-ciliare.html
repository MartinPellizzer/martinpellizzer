Saccolabium ciliare

Saccolabium ciliare, commonly known as the hairy leaf burr.

It is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody pod that splits open to release seeds, a feature common among legumes.

Saccolabium ciliare is adapted to dry environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
