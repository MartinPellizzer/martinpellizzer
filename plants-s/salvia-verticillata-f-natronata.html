Salvia verticillata f. natronata

Salvia verticillata f. natronata, commonly known as the natronata sage, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both garden ornamental use and ecological landscaping.
