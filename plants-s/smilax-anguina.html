Smilax anguina

Smilax anguina, commonly known as the rough-stemmed catbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a coarse texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Smilax anguina is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and swampy habitats.

It has historical uses in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and ecological roles.
