Saussurea linearifolia

Saussurea linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, linear leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
