Scrophularia oxysepala

Scrophularia oxysepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes various flowering plants known for their showy blooms. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale purple or pink, with five lobed petals, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
