Statice exaristata

Statice exaristata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its spikes of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. Its salt tolerance makes it well-suited for coastal gardens and seaside landscapes.

Statice exaristata is often used in ornamental gardening for its long-lasting blooms and hardy nature.
