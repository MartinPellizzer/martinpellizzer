Stelis philargyrus

Stelis philargyrus, commonly known as the silver-stem orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short, slender stem with a silvery-blue sheen, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale green, with a subtle fragrance.

Stelis philargyrus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
