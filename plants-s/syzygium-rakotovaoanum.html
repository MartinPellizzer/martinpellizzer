Syzygium rakotovaoanum

Syzygium rakotovaoanum, commonly known as the Madagascar apple or *Rakotovaoanana*, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and edible, apple-like fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Syzygium rakotovaoanum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential economic importance in local agriculture.
