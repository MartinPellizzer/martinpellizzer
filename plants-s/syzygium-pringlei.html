Syzygium pringlei

Syzygium pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarinds but have a distinct flavor. Syzygium pringlei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
