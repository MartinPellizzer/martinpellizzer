Saritaea magnifica

Saritaea magnifica, commonly known as the splendid saritaea, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils.

Saritaea magnifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
