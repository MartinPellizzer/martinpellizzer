Sida chilensis

Sida chilensis, commonly known as Chilean mallow, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Sida chilensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
