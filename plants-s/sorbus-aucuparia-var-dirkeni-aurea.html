Sorbus aucuparia var. dirkeni-aurea

Sorbus aucuparia var. dirkeni-aurea, commonly known as the golden rowan, is a variant of the rowan tree characterized by its striking golden foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in temperate climates. The plant features small, yellow-orange leaves that turn red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its berries are small, red, and edible for birds, contributing to its ecological role.

The golden rowan is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
