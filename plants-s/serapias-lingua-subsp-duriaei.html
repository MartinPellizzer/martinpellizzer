Serapias lingua subsp. duriaei

Serapias lingua subsp.

duriaei, commonly known as the Duriaei's sand orchid, is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb with striking, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces erect flowering stems. Its flowers are usually yellow with red or orange markings, resembling a tongue shape, which gives the species its common name.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
