Spaendoncea tamarindifolia

Spaendoncea tamarindifolia, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into pod-like fruits. The fruit contains seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp, which is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
