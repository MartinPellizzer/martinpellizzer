Saccoloma moranii

Saccoloma moranii, commonly known as the Moran's saccoloma, is a rare and lesser-known member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that exhibit a unique pattern of branching. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Saccoloma moranii is considered a botanical curiosity with potential ecological significance.
