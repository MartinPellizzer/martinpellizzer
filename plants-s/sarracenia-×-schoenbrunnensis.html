Sarracenia × schoenbrunnensis

Sarracenia × schoenbrunnensis, commonly known as the Schoenbrunn whorl-leaf pitcher plant, is a hybrid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, whorled arrangement of leaves, which form deep, tubular pitchers that trap insects. The plant's botanical features include bright red to orange-colored pitchers with intricate patterns, often adorned with fine hairs and nectar glands. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
