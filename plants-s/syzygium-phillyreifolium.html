Syzygium phillyreifolium

Syzygium phillyreifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical, leathery leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing sweet, sticky pulp. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Myrtaceae family.
