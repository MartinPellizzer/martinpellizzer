Saxifraga intacta

Saxifraga intacta, commonly known as the mountain saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga intacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
