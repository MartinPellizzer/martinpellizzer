Stillingia oblongifolia

Stillingia oblongifolia, commonly known as the Chinese skullcap, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Stillingia oblongifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
