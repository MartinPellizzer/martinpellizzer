Sonchus tenerrimus subsp. polypodioides

Sonchus tenerrimus subsp.

polypodioides, commonly known as the tropical groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which are followed by spiny, spherical fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open woodland or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
