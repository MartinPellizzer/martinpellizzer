Setaria ramulosa

Setaria ramulosa, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
