Searsia laevigata

Searsia laevigata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, pale green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The silver tree is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
