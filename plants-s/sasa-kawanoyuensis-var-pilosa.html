Sasa kawanoyuensis var. pilosa

Sasa kawanoyuensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "pilosa.". The plant produces cylindrical, segmented stems that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
