Senacia napaulensis

Senacia napaulensis, commonly known as the Napaul's Senacia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Senacia napaulensis produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.
