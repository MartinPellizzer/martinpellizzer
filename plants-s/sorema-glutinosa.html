Sorema glutinosa

Sorema glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky sorema, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves that secrete a sticky resin, which gives the plant its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The resinous properties of Sorema glutinosa have made it valuable in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
