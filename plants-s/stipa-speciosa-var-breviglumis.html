Stipa speciosa var. breviglumis

Stipa speciosa var. breviglumis, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a variant of the feathergrass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and slender, arching stems that produce delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial grass that thrives in full sun and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
