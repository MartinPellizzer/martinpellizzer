Stipa barbata subsp. brevipila

Stipa barbata subsp.

brevipila, commonly known as the short-haired feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are often pale green to brown in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in low-maintenance landscaping.
