Sasa koshiensis

Sasa koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, often forming thickets in shaded, moist environments. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.

Sasa koshiensis is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
