Scrophularia himalensis

Scrophularia himalensis, commonly known as Himalayan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
