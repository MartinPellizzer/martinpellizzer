Senecio erucifolius subsp. argunensis

Senecio erucifolius subsp.

argunensis, commonly known as the Armenian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
