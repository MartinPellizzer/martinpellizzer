Specklinia quadridentata

Specklinia quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed fern, is a species of polypodiaceae native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are typically light green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces small, pale spore-bearing clusters on the underside of its leaves, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
