Strychnos arborea

Strychnos arborea, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and potential medicinal properties.
