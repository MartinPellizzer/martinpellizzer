Sideritis hyssopifolia var. incana

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. incana, commonly known as hoary ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems covered in dense white or grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
