Scabiosa amoena

Scabiosa amoena, commonly known as the sweet William or love grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Scabiosa amoena is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a symbol of constancy and devotion.
