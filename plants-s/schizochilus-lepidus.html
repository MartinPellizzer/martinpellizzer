Schizochilus lepidus

Schizochilus lepidus, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, velvety-textured leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Schizochilus lepidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
