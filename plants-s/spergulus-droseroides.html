Spergulus droseroides

Spergulus droseroides, commonly known as the stickyweed, is a unique plant species native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects, a feature that aids in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, adapting to its wetland habitat with its specialized morphology. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a delicate pink or purple hue.

Spergulus droseroides plays an important ecological role in its environment by contributing to nutrient cycling through its insect-trapping mechanism.
