Sempervivum foliosum

Sempervivum foliosum, commonly known as the chicken's egg plant, is a succulent species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in clusters, usually in shades of pink or white, during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
