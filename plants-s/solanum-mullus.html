Solanum mullus

Solanum mullus, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries, which are typically orange-red in color. Solanum mullus is often found in moist, shaded areas and can be invasive in certain regions.

It contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
