Sophora mecosperma

Sophora mecosperma, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in spring. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, typical of the legume family.

Sophora mecosperma is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
