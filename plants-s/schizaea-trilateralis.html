Schizaea trilateralis

Schizaea trilateralis, commonly known as the three-angled fern, is a member of the family Schizaeaceae.

It is a cryptogamic plant that exhibits a unique morphology with a creeping rhizome and a rosette of lanceolate, serrated leaves. The plant is characterized by its trilateral symmetry, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Schizaea trilateralis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
