Scaevola taccada var. sericea

Scaevola taccada var. sericea, commonly known as silver scaevola, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically silver-gray in color, giving the plant its common name, and are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
