Swida foemina

Swida foemina, commonly known as the lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-white, velvety leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer months, which are followed by red, velvety bracts that persist into autumn. Swida foemina is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat ailments such as menstrual disorders and skin conditions.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
