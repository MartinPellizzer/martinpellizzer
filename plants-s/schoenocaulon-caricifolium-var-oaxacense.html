Schoenocaulon caricifolium var. oaxacense

Schoenocaulon caricifolium var. oaxacense, commonly known as the Oaxaca stonecress, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
