Sideritis chamaedryfolia subsp. littoralis

Sideritis chamaedryfolia subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the coastal sideritis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features slender, upright stems and small, gray-green leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pale blue to violet tubular flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
