Strobilanthes pedunculosa

Strobilanthes pedunculosa, commonly known as the "Blue Mottle" or "Blue Flax," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet bracts that surround the small, white flowers, creating a visually appealing display. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
