Saxifraga abchasica

Saxifraga abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, clustered at the base, and have a distinctive rosette shape.

Saxifraga abchasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
