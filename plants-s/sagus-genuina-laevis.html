Sagus genuina laevis

Sagus genuina laevis, commonly known as the smoothleaf sagus, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy on both surfaces. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among gardeners.
