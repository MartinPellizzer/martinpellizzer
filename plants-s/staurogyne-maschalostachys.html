Staurogyne maschalostachys

Staurogyne maschalostachys, commonly known as the Florida water willow, is a semi-aquatic plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It has slender, ribbed stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to form dense underwater stands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by insects.

It plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
