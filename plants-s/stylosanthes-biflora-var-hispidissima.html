Stylosanthes biflora var. hispidissima

Stylosanthes biflora var. hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy stylosanthes, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage species for livestock. Its trifoliate leaves and nodulated root system enhance its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

The plant is also used in erosion control and as a green manure crop in agricultural systems.
