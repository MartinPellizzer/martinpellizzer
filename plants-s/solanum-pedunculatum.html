Solanum pedunculatum

Solanum pedunculatum, commonly known as the purple nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are often used in traditional medicine.

Solanum pedunculatum belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves.
