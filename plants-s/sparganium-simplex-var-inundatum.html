Sparganium simplex var. inundatum

Sparganium simplex var. inundatum, commonly known as the water plantain, is a wetland plant characterized by its distinctive, narrow, sword-shaped leaves.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

It is an important plant in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to improve water quality and support biodiversity.
