Stevia benderi var. typica

Stevia benderi var. typica, commonly known as the typical stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from compounds called steviol glycosides, which are found in its leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Due to its low caloric content, it is often used as a natural sweetener in various food products.
