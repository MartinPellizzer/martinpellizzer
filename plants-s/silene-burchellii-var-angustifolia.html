Silene burchellii var. angustifolia

Silene burchellii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name. This species thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent leaves.
