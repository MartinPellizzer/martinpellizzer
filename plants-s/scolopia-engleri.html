Scolopia engleri

Scolopia engleri, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Scolopia engleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
