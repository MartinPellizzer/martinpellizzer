Separotheca

Separotheca, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Separotheca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
