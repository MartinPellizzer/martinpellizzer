Smilax sylvatica

Smilax sylvatica, commonly known as greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red berries, which are a food source for various birds and mammals. Smilax sylvatica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its aggressive growth habit makes it both a valuable ecological component and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
