Swida poliophylla var. praelonga

Swida poliophylla var. praelonga, commonly known as the long-leaf swida, is a variant of the swida plant native to specific regions in Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can reach several inches in length. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

The botanical features of this variant make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and ecological research.
