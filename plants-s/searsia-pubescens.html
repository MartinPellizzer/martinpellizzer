Searsia pubescens

Searsia pubescens, commonly known as the silver tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain animals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Searsia pubescens is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
