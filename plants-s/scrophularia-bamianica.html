Scrophularia bamianica

Scrophularia bamianica, commonly known as the Himalayan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. The plant features erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
