Sideritis gulendamii

Sideritis gulendamii, commonly known as the Albanian heather, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Albania and northern Greece.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of fine hairs.

Sideritis gulendamii is often found in rocky or dry habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
