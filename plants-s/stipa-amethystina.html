Stipa amethystina

Stipa amethystina, commonly known as the amethystine needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of spikelets that give it a distinctive, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple to pinkish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments.

Stipa amethystina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique aesthetic.
