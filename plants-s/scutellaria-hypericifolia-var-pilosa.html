Scutellaria hypericifolia var. pilosa

Scutellaria hypericifolia var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also hairy, contributing to its overall textured look.

This species is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
