Shorea balangeran var. angustifolia

Shorea balangeran var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamanu, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a narrow shape. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and develops hard, woody fruits containing seeds.

It is valued for its durable timber and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
