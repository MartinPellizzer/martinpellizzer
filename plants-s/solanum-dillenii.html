Solanum dillenii

Solanum dillenii, commonly known as the Dillen's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum dillenii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
