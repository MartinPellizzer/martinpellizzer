Spilanthes decumbens var. leptophylla

Spilanthes decumbens var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Indian mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, spreading stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.

It is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, and it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
