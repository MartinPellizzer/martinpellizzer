Senecio cliffordianus

Senecio cliffordianus, commonly known as the cliffordianus groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to South Africa, characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems that form a dense mat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
