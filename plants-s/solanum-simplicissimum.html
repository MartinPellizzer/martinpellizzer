Solanum simplicissimum

Solanum simplicissimum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
