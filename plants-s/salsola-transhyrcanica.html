Salsola transhyrcanica

Salsola transhyrcanica, commonly known as Transcaspian saltbush, is a shrub native to Central Asia, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in saline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in sandy or degraded landscapes.

Salsola transhyrcanica plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
