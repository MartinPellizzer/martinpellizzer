Spergularia marina var. urbica

Spergularia marina var. urbica, commonly known as the coastal sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to saline and sandy environments. This variety thrives in maritime climates, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its growth is influenced by tidal patterns and salt spray, making it a resilient species.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
