Solanum luteum var. procerum

Solanum luteum var. procerum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a variant of the yellow nightshade species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its yellowish flowers and hairy stems. The leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and the plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
