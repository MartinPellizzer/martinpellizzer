Succisa pratensis subsp. scotiaca

Succisa pratensis subsp.

scotiaca, commonly known as Scottish meadow cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles. It typically grows in moist grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom from June to August.

It plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity and is a characteristic species of calcareous grasslands.
