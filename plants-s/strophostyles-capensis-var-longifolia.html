Strophostyles capensis var. longifolia

Strophostyles capensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from October to February. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in horticulture, though it requires careful watering to prevent root rot.
