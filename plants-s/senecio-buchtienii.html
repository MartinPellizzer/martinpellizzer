Senecio buchtienii

Senecio buchtienii, commonly known as the Cape foveolata, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowering heads are small and yellow, often occurring in clusters at the ends of branches.

Senecio buchtienii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
