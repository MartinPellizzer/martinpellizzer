Salacia reticulata var. diandra

Salacia reticulata var. diandra, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, orange-yellow berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
