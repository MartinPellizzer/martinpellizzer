Setaria sulcata

Setaria sulcata, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many regions.
