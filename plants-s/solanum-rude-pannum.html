Solanum rude-pannum

Solanum rude-pannum, commonly known as the rough-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves with a rough texture, and its small, yellowish flowers develop into berry-like fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption or medicinal use.
