Sideritis alpina

Sideritis alpina, commonly known as alpine ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Sideritis alpina typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its leaves are often hairy and have a slight minty aroma, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic value.
