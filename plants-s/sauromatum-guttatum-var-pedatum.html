Sauromatum guttatum var. pedatum

Sauromatum guttatum var. pedatum, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a single, glossy, dark red or orange-red flower that emerges from a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its unique, toxic sap, which contains compounds that can cause skin irritation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flower typically blooms in early spring, adding a dramatic visual element to its natural habitat.
