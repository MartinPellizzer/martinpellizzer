Salix blumhardtiana

Salix blumhardtiana, commonly known as Blumhardt's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and dark green on top. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are male and female, making it monoecious. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
