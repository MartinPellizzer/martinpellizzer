Schinus bituminosa

Schinus bituminosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and oval in shape. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Schinus bituminosa is also known for its aromatic leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
