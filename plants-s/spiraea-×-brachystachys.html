Spiraea × brachystachys

Spiraea × brachystachys, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its foliage is typically dark green in summer and turns red or orange in the fall.

Spiraea × brachystachys is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of maintenance.
