Sebaea madagascariensis

Sebaea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that are often fragrant. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Sebaea madagascariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
