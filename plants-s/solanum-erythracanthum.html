Solanum erythracanthum

Solanum erythracanthum, commonly known as the red-armed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided by livestock and humans.
