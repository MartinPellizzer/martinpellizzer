Salvia ombrophila

Salvia ombrophila, commonly known as the rain sage, is a species of flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its growth habit is typically compact, with upright stems that bear clusters of showy bracts.

Salvia ombrophila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add a dramatic touch to shaded garden spaces.
