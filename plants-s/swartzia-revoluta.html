Swartzia revoluta

Swartzia revoluta, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and demand, Swartzia revoluta is also classified as an endangered species.
