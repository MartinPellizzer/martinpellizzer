Silene fastigiata

Silene fastigiata, commonly known as the upright catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene fastigiata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
