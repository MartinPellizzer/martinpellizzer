Salvia verbenaca subsp. battandieri

Salvia verbenaca subsp.

battandieri, commonly known as the Balkan sage, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, grey-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and small spaces.
