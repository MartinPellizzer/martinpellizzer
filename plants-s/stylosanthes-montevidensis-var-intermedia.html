Stylosanthes montevidensis var. intermedia

Stylosanthes montevidensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Montevidense stylo, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, sandy soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, making it a valuable forage crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
