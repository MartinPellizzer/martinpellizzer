Saxifraga idahoensis

Saxifraga idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Idaho and western Montana.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. The plant features a mat-forming growth habit with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Saxifraga idahoensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
