Silene succulenta subsp. corsica

Silene succulenta subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
