Silene cobalticola

Silene cobalticola, commonly known as the cobalt flaxflower, is a strikingly colored plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vivid purple and blue flowers, which are often described as resembling the hues of cobalt and flax. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy blooms and succulent-like leaves. Silene cobalticola thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
