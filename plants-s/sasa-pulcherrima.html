Sasa pulcherrima

Sasa pulcherrima, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy, white seed heads that are often used in ornamental displays. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sasa pulcherrima is valued in horticulture for its ability to create a lush, textured ground cover in shaded environments.
