Solanum flaccidum var. heterophyllum

Solanum flaccidum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, smooth berries. These berries are typically orange to red in color when mature and contain toxic alkaloids.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
