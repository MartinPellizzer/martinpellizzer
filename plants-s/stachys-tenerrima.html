Stachys tenerrima

Stachys tenerrima, commonly known as the purple flowered betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Stachys tenerrima is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
