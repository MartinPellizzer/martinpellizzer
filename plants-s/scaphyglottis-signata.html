Scaphyglottis signata

Scaphyglottis signata, commonly known as the scarlet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often producing a showy display during the wet season.

The botanical features of Scaphyglottis signata make it a notable species in the orchid family, valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
