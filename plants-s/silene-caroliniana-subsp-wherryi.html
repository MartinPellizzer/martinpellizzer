Silene caroliniana subsp. wherryi

Silene caroliniana subsp.

wherryi, commonly known as the Carolina catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
