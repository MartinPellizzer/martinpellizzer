Senegalia huberi

Senegalia huberi, commonly known as the desert honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in dry conditions and access groundwater. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of long, slender pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, and the plant is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.

Senegalia huberi is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility in degraded areas.
