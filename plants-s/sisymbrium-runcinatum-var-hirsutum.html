Sisymbrium runcinatum var. hirsutum

Sisymbrium runcinatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy rocket mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are usually lobed and can be edible when young, though they become more bitter with age.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
