Saracha quitensis

Saracha quitensis, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Saracha quitensis has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
