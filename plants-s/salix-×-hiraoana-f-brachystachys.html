Salix × hiraoana f. brachystachys

Salix × hiraoana f. brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered catkins that are typically male, with female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian habitats.

This ornamental willow is valued for its graceful appearance and adaptability to wetland environments.
