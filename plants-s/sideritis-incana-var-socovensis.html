Sideritis incana var. socovensis

Sideritis incana var. socovensis, commonly known as the Socovite beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
