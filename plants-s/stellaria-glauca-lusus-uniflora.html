Stellaria glauca lusus uniflora

Stellaria glauca lusus uniflora, commonly known as the blue starflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, star-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to various habitats.
