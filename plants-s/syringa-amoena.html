Syringa amoena

Syringa amoena, commonly known as the common lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Syringa amoena is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
