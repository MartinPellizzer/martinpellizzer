Scilla scilloides var. pulchella

Scilla scilloides var. pulchella, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a striking bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and long-lasting flowers.

It is also valued for its resilience and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
