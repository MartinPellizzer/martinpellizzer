Sechium tacaco

Sechium tacaco, commonly known as the Tacaco squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, lobed leaves.

It produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that can grow up to 3 meters in length, with a smooth, waxy skin that ranges in color from green to yellow. The fruit contains a fibrous, edible pulp surrounding numerous seeds, which are typically consumed either raw or cooked. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary versatility.

This plant is native to parts of Central and South America, where it has been used traditionally in local cuisines.
