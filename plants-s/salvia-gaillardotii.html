Salvia gaillardotii

Salvia gaillardotii, commonly known as Gaillardot's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Salvia gaillardotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
