Sesleria polyathera

Sesleria polyathera, commonly known as the purple grass or narrow-leaved sesleria, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often purple or pink in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sesleria polyathera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
