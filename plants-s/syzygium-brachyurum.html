Syzygium brachyurum

Syzygium brachyurum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Syzygium brachyurum is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various animals.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
