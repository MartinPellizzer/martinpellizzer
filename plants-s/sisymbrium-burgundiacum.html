Sisymbrium burgundiacum

Sisymbrium burgundiacum, commonly known as Burgundy mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard species. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The seeds are small, oval, and often used in culinary applications due to their pungent flavor.
