Sclerolaena patenticuspis

Sclerolaena patenticuspis, commonly known as the broad-cusped bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, often thorny foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive, serrated margin.

Sclerolaena patenticuspis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in such conditions.
