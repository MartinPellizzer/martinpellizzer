Spiraea ulmaria variegata

Spiraea ulmaria variegata, commonly known as the variegated meadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and often exhibit a striking variegation of green and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
