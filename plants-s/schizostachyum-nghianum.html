Schizostachyum nghianum

Schizostachyum nghianum, commonly known as the Vietnamese bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to brown flowers. It is often used in landscaping and for erosion control due to its dense growth habit.

Schizostachyum nghianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
