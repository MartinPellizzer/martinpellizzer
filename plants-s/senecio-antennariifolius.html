Senecio antennariifolius

Senecio antennariifolius, commonly known as the "lion's tail" or "lion's beard," is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescence that resembles a lion's tail. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
