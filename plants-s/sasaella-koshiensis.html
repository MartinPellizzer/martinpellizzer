Sasaella koshiensis

Sasaella koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, clump-forming growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasaella koshiensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
