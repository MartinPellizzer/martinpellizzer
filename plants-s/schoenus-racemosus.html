Schoenus racemosus

Schoenus racemosus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
