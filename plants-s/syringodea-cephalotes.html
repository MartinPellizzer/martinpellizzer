Syringodea cephalotes

Syringodea cephalotes, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hood-like structure that encloses its flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Syringodea cephalotes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
