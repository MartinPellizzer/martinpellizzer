Silene burmanica

Silene burmanica, commonly known as Burman's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats. The plant features showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene burmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
