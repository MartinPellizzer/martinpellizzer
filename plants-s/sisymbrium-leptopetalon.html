Sisymbrium leptopetalon

Sisymbrium leptopetalon, commonly known as the slender-petaled wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and linear, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
