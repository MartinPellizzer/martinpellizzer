Styphelia sieberi

Styphelia sieberi, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact form, with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Styphelia sieberi thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blossoms.
