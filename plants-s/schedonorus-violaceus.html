Schedonorus violaceus

Schedonorus violaceus, commonly known as purple meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often display a distinctive purple hue, especially under stress conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control.
