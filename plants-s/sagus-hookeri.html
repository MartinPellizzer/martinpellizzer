Sagus hookeri

Sagus hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's saguaro, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that often branches into multiple arms, resembling the iconic saguaro cactus of the Sonoran Desert. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers during the cooler months, which are pollinated by various insects and birds. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its adaptability to extreme climates.
