Solanum badilloi

Solanum badilloi, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, and its berries are typically red when mature. Solanum badilloi is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
