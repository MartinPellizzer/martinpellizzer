Sasa tokatiensis

Sasa tokatiensis, commonly known as the Tokat bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It produces small, pale green flowers in panicles during the summer months.

Sasa tokatiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts in its native region.
