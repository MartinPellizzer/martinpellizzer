Sedum telephium var. vogesiacum

Sedum telephium var. vogesiacum, commonly known as the Vogesian stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to Europe.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
