Spiraea ariifolia f. carnea

Spiraea ariifolia f. carnea, commonly known as the rosepink weigela, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy, rose-purple flower clusters that appear in late spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
