Scirpus kochii

Scirpus kochii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, while its seeds provide food for various bird species.

Scirpus kochii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
