Scabiosa communis var. alpestris

Scabiosa communis var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
