Syngonanthus biformis

Syngonanthus biformis, commonly known as the two-form grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Syngonanthus biformis thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird populations.
