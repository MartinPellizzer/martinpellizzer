Sedum telephium var. subrotundum

Sedum telephium var. subrotundum, commonly known as Blush Sedum, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically bluish-green with a pinkish or reddish hue, especially under full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in low-maintenance landscaping.
