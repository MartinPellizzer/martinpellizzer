Sarcocaulon rigidum f. parviflorum

Sarcocaulon rigidum f. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered rigid sarcocaulon, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette structure. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow, rigid, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal environments.
