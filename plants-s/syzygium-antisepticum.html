Syzygium antisepticum

Syzygium antisepticum, commonly known as the Indian clove or Syzygium aromaticum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown flower buds, which are harvested and dried to produce the spice cloves. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its essential oils, which contain eugenol, a compound responsible for its strong fragrance and medicinal properties. Syzygium antisepticum thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a warm, tropical environment to flourish.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both traditional medicine and the global spice trade.
