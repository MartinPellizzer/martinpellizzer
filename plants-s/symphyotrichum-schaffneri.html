Symphyotrichum schaffneri

Symphyotrichum schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Symphyotrichum schaffneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
