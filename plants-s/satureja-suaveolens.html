Satureja suaveolens

Satureja suaveolens, commonly known as sweet sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

Satureja suaveolens is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
