Stevia trachelioides

Stevia trachelioides, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are rich in steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are harvested for their intense sweetness, making it a popular alternative to sugar in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Stevia trachelioides is also valued for its low-calorie content and potential health benefits.
