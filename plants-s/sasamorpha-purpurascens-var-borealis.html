Sasamorpha purpurascens var. borealis

Sasamorpha purpurascens var. borealis, commonly known as the northern sasamorpha, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silky hair to reduce water loss.

This species plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for herbivores and supporting pollinator populations.
