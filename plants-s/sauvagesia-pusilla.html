Sauvagesia pusilla

Sauvagesia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sauvagesia, is a small shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with compact, rounded foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Sauvagesia pusilla is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
