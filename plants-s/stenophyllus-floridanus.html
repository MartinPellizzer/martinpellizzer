Stenophyllus floridanus

Stenophyllus floridanus, commonly known as the Florida panhandle fern, is a unique fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that arise from a short, rhizomatous stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This rare and ecologically significant plant is an important indicator of undisturbed, humid habitats.
