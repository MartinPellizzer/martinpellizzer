Sedum sacrum

Sedum sacrum, commonly known as the sacred sedum, is a species of flowering plant in the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Its common name reflects its historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
