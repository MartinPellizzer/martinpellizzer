Sabia campanulata

Sabia campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower of the genus Sabia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
