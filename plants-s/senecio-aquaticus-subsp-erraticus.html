Senecio aquaticus subsp. erraticus

Senecio aquaticus subsp.

erraticus, commonly known as the erratic senecio, is a perennial aquatic plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands, where it forms dense, floating mats.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, oxygenated waters.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator species for aquatic ecosystems.
