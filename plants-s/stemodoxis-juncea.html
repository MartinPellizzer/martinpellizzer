Stemodoxis juncea

Stemodoxis juncea, commonly known as the slender grass, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological succession.
