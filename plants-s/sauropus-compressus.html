Sauropus compressus

Sauropus compressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sago, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
