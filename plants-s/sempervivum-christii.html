Sempervivum christii

Sempervivum christii, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Sempervivum christii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth and drought tolerance.
