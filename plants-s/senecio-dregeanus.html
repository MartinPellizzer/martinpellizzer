Senecio dregeanus

Senecio dregeanus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower clusters that range in color from bright orange to deep red, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique appearance.
