Solanum stenotomum f. pitoca

Solanum stenotomum f. pitoca, commonly known as the Pitoca tomato, is a botanical variant of the tomato plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often red or yellow fruits, which are typically used in local culinary traditions. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like peppers and potatoes. It has a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, and it produces flowers that transition into the distinctive fruit.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
