Saxifraga paniculata subvar. linearifolia

Saxifraga paniculata subvar. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in panicles. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
