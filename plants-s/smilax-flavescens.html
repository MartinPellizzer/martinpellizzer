Smilax flavescens

Smilax flavescens, commonly known as the yellow smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny nodes, which help in climbing and defense. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive yellowish underside.

Smilax flavescens is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
