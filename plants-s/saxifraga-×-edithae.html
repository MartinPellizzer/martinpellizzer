Saxifraga × edithae

Saxifraga × edithae, commonly known as the Edith's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial with rosettes of fleshy, often hairy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Saxifraga × edithae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
