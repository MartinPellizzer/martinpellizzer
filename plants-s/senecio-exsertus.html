Senecio exsertus

Senecio exsertus, commonly known as the erect senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring narrow, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Senecio exsertus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
