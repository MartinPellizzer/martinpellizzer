Serapias × roselliniana

Serapias × roselliniana, commonly known as the hybrid marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
