Stanhopea tigrina var. tigrina

Stanhopea tigrina var. tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display intricate patterns resembling a tiger's stripes. The plant is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees or other plants without being parasitic. Its flowers typically bloom in clusters and can range in color from yellow to orange with contrasting dark markings.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and vibrant appearance.
