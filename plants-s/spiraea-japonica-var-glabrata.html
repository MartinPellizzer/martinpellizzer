Spiraea japonica var. glabrata

Spiraea japonica var. glabrata, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, arching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for borders, rock gardens, and as a specimen plant.
