Setosa

Setosa, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in a rosette pattern. Setosa prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
