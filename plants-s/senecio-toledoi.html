Senecio toledoi

Senecio toledoi, commonly known as the Toledan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by papery bracts.

Senecio toledoi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
