Syzygium curvistylum var. parvifolium

Syzygium curvistylum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved tamarind.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
