Solidago unalaschensis

Solidago unalaschensis, commonly known as the Unalaschenskaya goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the Yukon and Alaska regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago unalaschensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
