Sabina arizonica

Sabina arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in desert regions.
