Soaresia ilicifolia

Soaresia ilicifolia, commonly known as the leathery fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are arranged in a compact, upright fashion, forming a rounded canopy.

Soaresia ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
