Scheelea microspadix

Scheelea microspadix, commonly known as the small-spadix palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces spadices, which are spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

Scheelea microspadix is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
