Schizophragma hydrangeoides f. concolor

Schizophragma hydrangeoides f. concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf hydrangea, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It features broad, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically smooth on both sides, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its foliage and modest floral display.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to shaded gardens and woodland settings.
