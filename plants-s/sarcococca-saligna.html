Sarcococca saligna

Sarcococca saligna, commonly known as the fragrant winterberry, is a dense, evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year, providing year-round interest in the garden. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in late winter, followed by red, berry-like fruits that persist into spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Sarcococca saligna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
