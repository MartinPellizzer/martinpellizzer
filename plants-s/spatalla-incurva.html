Spatalla incurva

Spatalla incurva, commonly known as the curved spathaceous plant, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features a striking, curved spathe that envelops the spadix, a typical feature of arums. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, with a glossy surface that aids in water retention.

Spatalla incurva is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical greenhouses.
