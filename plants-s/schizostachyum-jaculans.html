Schizostachyum jaculans

Schizostachyum jaculans, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant rattan, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, segmented stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant can grow up to 15 meters in height, making it one of the tallest grasses in the world. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Schizostachyum jaculans thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests and wetlands.
