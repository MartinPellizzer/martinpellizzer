Smilax salutaris

Smilax salutaris, commonly known as the yellow smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a climbing stem with sharp, spiny branches and is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Smilax salutaris has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
