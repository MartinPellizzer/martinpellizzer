Scleranthus ruscinonensis

Scleranthus ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Balkan sickle-flower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Scleranthus ruscinonensis is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
