Solanum scabrum

Solanum scabrum, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that mature to a dark purple color, though they are toxic and should not be consumed.

Solanum scabrum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
