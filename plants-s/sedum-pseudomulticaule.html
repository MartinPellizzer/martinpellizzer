Sedum pseudomulticaule

Sedum pseudomulticaule, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and opposite, rounded leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum pseudomulticaule is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
