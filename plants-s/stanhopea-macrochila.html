Stanhopea macrochila

Stanhopea macrochila, commonly known as the large-lipped stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant orange or yellow color with contrasting purple or brown markings. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stanhopea macrochila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
