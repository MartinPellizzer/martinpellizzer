Suaeda lipskyi

Suaeda lipskyi, commonly known as the desert seep, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are succulent, adapted to conserve water. Suaeda lipskyi is well-suited to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
