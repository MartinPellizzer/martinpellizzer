Senecio chilensis f. patagonicus

Senecio chilensis f. patagonicus, commonly known as Patagonian groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the harsh climate of Patagonia.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
