Swida controversa var. shikokumontana

Swida controversa var. shikokumontana, commonly known as the Shikoku mountain ash, is a rare and ornamental species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring, followed by bright red berries in autumn. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
