Scleranthus knazicanus

Scleranthus knazicanus, commonly known as the Knazican thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky, or disturbed soils. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs. Scleranthus knazicanus has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is often found in open habitats such as meadows, slopes, and along roadsides.
