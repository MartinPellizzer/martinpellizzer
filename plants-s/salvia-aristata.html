Salvia aristata

Salvia aristata, commonly known as the Sierra foothill sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, woolly leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Salvia aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
