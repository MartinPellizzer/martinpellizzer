Sorbus tianschanica f. sanguinea

Sorbus tianschanica f. sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodtwig dogwood, is a shrub native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red-colored twigs that persist through winter. The plant produces small white flowers in spring, followed by yellowish berries in the fall. Its foliage turns vibrant red in autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
