Sasa tomentosa

Sasa tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial shrub-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Sasa tomentosa is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
