Senecio atriplicifolius var. reniformis

Senecio atriplicifolius var. reniformis, commonly known as the broadleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring woody stems and alternate, broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow, arranged in clusters, and are adapted for insect pollination.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in arid or semi-arid environments.
