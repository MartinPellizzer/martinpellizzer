Silene occidentalis var. nancta

Silene occidentalis var. nancta, commonly known as the western pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
