Silene gebleriana

Silene gebleriana, commonly known as the Gebler's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene gebleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
