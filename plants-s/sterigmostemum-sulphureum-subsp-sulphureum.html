Sterigmostemum sulphureum subsp. sulphureum

Sterigmostemum sulphureum subsp.

sulphureum, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and emits a mild, sweet fragrance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
