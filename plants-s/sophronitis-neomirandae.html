Sophronitis neomirandae

Sophronitis neomirandae, commonly known as the Neomiranda orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the wet season, attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a conservation concern.
