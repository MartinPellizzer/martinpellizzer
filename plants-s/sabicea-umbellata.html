Sabicea umbellata

Sabicea umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that resemble umbrellas, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a climbing or shrub growth habit. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Sabicea umbellata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
