Statice serbica

Statice serbica, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and coastal environments, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh conditions. Statice serbica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the sea and its use in traditional medicine.
