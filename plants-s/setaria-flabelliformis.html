Setaria flabelliformis

Setaria flabelliformis, commonly known as the flat-leaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, flat blades and upright growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or prairies.

Setaria flabelliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in native landscapes.
