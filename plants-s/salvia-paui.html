Salvia paui

Salvia paui, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to arid conditions.

Salvia paui is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaped gardens.
