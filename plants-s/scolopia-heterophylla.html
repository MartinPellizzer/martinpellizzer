Scolopia heterophylla

Scolopia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable scolopia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that vary in shape depending on the environment. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, clustered in small inflorescences.

Scolopia heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
