Sedopsis filsonii

Sedopsis filsonii, commonly known as the Filson's sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
