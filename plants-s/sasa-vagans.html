Sasa vagans

Sasa vagans, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa vagans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and cuisine.
