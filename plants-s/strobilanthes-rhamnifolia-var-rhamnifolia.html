Strobilanthes rhamnifolia var. rhamnifolia

Strobilanthes rhamnifolia var. rhamnifolia, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
