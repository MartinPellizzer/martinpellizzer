Solanum stenotomum f. kamara

Solanum stenotomum f. kamara, commonly known as the Kamara nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. Its berries, which are toxic, are often used in traditional medicine despite their harmful properties.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in its native habitat.
