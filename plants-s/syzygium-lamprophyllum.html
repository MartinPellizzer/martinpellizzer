Syzygium lamprophyllum

Syzygium lamprophyllum, commonly known as the silver-leafed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
