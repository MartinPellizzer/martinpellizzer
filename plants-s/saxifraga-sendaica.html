Saxifraga sendaica

Saxifraga sendaica, commonly known as the Andean saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. This species thrives in rocky, alpine environments where it forms dense mats to conserve moisture and withstand harsh weather conditions. Its ability to survive in extreme climates makes it an important component of the Andean ecosystem.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
