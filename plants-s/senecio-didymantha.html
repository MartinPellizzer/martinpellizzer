Senecio didymantha

Senecio didymantha, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, waxy leaves. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation in dry, rocky habitats.

Senecio didymantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
