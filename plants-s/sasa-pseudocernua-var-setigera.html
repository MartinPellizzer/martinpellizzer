Sasa pseudocernua var. setigera

Sasa pseudocernua var. setigera, commonly known as the silver sasa, is a variant of the sasa bamboo that is native to Japan.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its slender, upright culms and dense, feather-like foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually a striking silver-green color, which distinguishes it from other sasa species.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing clumps.
