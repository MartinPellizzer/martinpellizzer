Solanum delagoense subsp. epacanthastrum

Solanum delagoense subsp.

epacanthastrum, commonly known as the Delagoense nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy stems and leaves. Its berries are yellow when mature and contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
