Spilanthes corymbosa

Spilanthes corymbosa, commonly known as the Indian mustards or devil's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Spilanthes corymbosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
