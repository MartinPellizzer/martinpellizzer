Syzygium rubicundum

Syzygium rubicundum, commonly known as the red apple or red satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Syzygium rubicundum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes both tree and shrub forms, making it adaptable to various landscapes.
