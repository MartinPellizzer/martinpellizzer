Senecio asperulus

Senecio asperulus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in a loose inflorescence at the top of the stems.

Senecio asperulus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.
