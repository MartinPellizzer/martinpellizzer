Senecio tuberosus

Senecio tuberosus, commonly known as the African violet or purple violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, hairy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Senecio tuberosus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
