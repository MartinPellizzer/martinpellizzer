Sorbus chamaemespilus var. sudetica

Sorbus chamaemespilus var. sudetica, commonly known as the Czech whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries in autumn. Its leaves are oval, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall.

It is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
