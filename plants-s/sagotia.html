Sagotia

Sagotia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sagotia produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
