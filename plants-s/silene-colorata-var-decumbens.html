Silene colorata var. decumbens

Silene colorata var. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent redflowered catchfly, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features prostrate or decumbent stems that lie close to the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
