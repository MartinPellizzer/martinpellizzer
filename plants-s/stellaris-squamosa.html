Stellaris squamosa

Stellaris squamosa, commonly known as the star-scaled aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking star-shaped purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. The plant has silver-green, scale-like leaves that give it its common name and help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in the wild.
