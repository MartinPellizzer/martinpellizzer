Shorea hopeifolia

Shorea hopeifolia, commonly known as the Hope's rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
