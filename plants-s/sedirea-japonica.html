Sedirea japonica

Sedirea japonica, commonly known as the Japanese sedum, is a succulent plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its low maintenance requirements.

Sedirea japonica prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
