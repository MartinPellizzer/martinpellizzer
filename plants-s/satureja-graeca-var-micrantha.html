Satureja graeca var. micrantha

Satureja graeca var. micrantha, commonly known as Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Satureja graeca var. micrantha is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
