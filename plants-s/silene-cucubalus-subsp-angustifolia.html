Silene cucubalus subsp. angustifolia

Silene cucubalus subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bladder campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
