Silene gallica subvar. rosella

Silene gallica subvar. rosella, commonly known as the rose campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, upright clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Silene gallica subvar. rosella is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
