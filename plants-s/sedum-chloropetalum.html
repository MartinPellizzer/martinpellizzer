Sedum chloropetalum

Sedum chloropetalum, commonly known as white stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Sedum chloropetalum is also valued for its low maintenance and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
