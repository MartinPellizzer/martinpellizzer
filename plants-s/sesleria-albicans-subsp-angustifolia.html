Sesleria albicans subsp. angustifolia

Sesleria albicans subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and silvery-white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
