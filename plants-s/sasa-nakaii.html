Sasa nakaii

Sasa nakaii, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Nakai's bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing a striking visual contrast to its pale, fibrous sheaths. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa nakaii is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
