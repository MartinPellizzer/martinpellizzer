Sedum mucronatum

Sedum mucronatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in inflorescences at the ends of the stems. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in rocky or sandy soils.

Sedum mucronatum is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
