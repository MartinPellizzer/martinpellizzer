Sansevieria concinna subsp. sudwallensis

Sansevieria concinna subsp.

sudwallensis, commonly known as the Sudwall's Snake Plant, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically light green with prominent, dark green longitudinal stripes.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, making it highly drought-tolerant and suitable for low-maintenance indoor cultivation. Its leaves are rigid and fibrous, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
