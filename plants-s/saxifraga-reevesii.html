Saxifraga reevesii

Saxifraga reevesii, commonly known as the Reeves' saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette shape, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga reevesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
