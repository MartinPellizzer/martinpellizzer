Solanum callicarpifolium

Solanum callicarpifolium, commonly known as the scarlet gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, followed by berries that are initially green and turn red when ripe. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
