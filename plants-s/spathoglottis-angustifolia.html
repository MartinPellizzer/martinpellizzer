Spathoglottis angustifolia

Spathoglottis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive spatulate lip that aids in pollination.

Spathoglottis angustifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
