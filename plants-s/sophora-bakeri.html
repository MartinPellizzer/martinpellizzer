Sophora bakeri

Sophora bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Sophora bakeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
