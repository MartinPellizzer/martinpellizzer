Solanum ratale

Solanum ratale, commonly known as the velvetleaf or nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple or white flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing toxic seeds. Solanum ratale is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially hazardous to livestock and humans.
