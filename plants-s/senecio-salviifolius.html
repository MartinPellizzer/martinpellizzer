Senecio salviifolius

Senecio salviifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Senecio salviifolius is typically evergreen, with a low-growing habit that makes it suitable for rockeries and dry landscapes.
