Silene dichotoma subsp. apolonica

Silene dichotoma subsp.

apolonica, commonly known as the Apolonia catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands.

The plant features slender stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often hairy. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish, and clustered in dense, branched inflorescences.

It blooms from late spring to early summer and is known for its attractive appearance and ecological value.
