Sophora velutina subsp. cavaleriei

Sophora velutina subsp.

cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its compound leaves with an velvety underside, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
