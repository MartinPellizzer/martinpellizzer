Saxifraga moschata var. dominii

Saxifraga moschata var. dominii, commonly known as the sweet saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous areas where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually white or pink, with a subtle fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
