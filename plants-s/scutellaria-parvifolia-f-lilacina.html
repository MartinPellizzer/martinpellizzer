Scutellaria parvifolia f. lilacina

Scutellaria parvifolia f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tubular, lilac-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable plant for wetland restoration and native plant gardens.
