Stevia venosa

Stevia venosa, commonly known as yellow stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Stevia venosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
