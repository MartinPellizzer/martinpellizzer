Spiraea crenifolia var. mongolica

Spiraea crenifolia var. mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian thimbleweed, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are narrow, serrated, and have a slightly wavy margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
