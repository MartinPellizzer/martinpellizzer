Scilla hispanica var. rosea

Scilla hispanica var. rosea, commonly known as the Spanish squill or rose-flowered squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and rose, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
