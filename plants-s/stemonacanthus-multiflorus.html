Stemonacanthus multiflorus

Stemonacanthus multiflorus, commonly known as the bluebell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Stemonacanthus multiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
