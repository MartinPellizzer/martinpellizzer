Sambucus javanica var. meridionalis

Sambucus javanica var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features compound leaves with 5–9 elliptical leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open areas.

The berries are edible when ripe but can be toxic if consumed raw.
