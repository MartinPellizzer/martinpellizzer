Senegalia acanthophylla

Senegalia acanthophylla, commonly known as the burro bush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Senegalia acanthophylla plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
