Senecio waterbergensis

Senecio waterbergensis, commonly known as the Waterberg groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. It features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio waterbergensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
