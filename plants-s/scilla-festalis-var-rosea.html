Scilla festalis var. rosea

Scilla festalis var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. Its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Scilla festalis var. rosea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
