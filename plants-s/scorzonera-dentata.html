Scorzonera dentata

Scorzonera dentata, commonly known as the toothed scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and toothed. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is robust and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera dentata is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
