Swertia alpina

Swertia alpina, commonly known as Alpine swertia, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Swertia alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
