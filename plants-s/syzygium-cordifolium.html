Syzygium cordifolium

Syzygium cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
