Schinus sinuata

Schinus sinuata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red berries that are often eaten by birds. Schinus sinuata is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing ecological challenges.
