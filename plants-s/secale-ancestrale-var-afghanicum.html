Secale ancestrale var. afghanicum

Secale ancestrale var. afghanicum, commonly known as the Afghan ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
