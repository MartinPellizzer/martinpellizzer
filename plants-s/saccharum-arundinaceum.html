Saccharum arundinaceum

Saccharum arundinaceum, commonly known as bamboo cane or giant bamboo, is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stalks and rapid growth rate, making it highly adaptable to various climates. The plant is known for its high cellulose content, which makes it valuable for paper production and biofuel applications. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread quickly and form dense stands.

Saccharum arundinaceum also plays an important ecological role in preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for wildlife.
