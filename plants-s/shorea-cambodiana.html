Shorea cambodiana

Shorea cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood with a rich, aromatic scent. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has compound leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in its native habitat.
