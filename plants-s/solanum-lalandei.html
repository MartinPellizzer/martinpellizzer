Solanum lalandei

Solanum lalandei, commonly known as the Laland's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with hairy stems and ovate, pointed leaves. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Solanum lalandei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
