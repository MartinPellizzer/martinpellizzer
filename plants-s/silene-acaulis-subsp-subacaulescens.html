Silene acaulis subsp. subacaulescens

Silene acaulis subsp.

subacaulescens, commonly known as the rock campion, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to rocky or alpine environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, upright stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically produces numerous stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia.
