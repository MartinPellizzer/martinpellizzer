Scrophularia badghysi

Scrophularia badghysi, commonly known as Persian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy, often tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Scrophularia badghysi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
