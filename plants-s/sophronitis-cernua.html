Sophronitis cernua

Sophronitis cernua, commonly known as the drooping orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that hang from the stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without being parasitic. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Sophronitis cernua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
