Scleria setifera

Scleria setifera, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with a fibrous, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

Scleria setifera is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
