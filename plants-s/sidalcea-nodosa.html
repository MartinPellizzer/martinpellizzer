Sidalcea nodosa

Sidalcea nodosa, commonly known as the nodding soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Sidalcea nodosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.
