Senecio mattirolii

Senecio mattirolii, commonly known as the Mattiroli's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
