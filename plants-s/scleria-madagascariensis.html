Scleria madagascariensis

Scleria madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sedge, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Scleria madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
