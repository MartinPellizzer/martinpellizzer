Sterculia caricifolia

Sterculia caricifolia, commonly known as the swamp fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant bears edible fig-like fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
