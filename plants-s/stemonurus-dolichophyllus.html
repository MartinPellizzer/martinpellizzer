Stemonurus dolichophyllus

Stemonurus dolichophyllus, commonly known as the long-leaf stemonurus, is a member of the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, producing a distinctive appearance.

Stemonurus dolichophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and subtle fragrance.
