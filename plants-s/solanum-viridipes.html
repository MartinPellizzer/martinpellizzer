Solanum viridipes

Solanum viridipes, commonly known as the green nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its berries are initially green and turn red when ripe, though they are generally toxic and should not be consumed.

Solanum viridipes is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
