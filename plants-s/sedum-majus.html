Sedum majus

Sedum majus, commonly known as the great stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Sedum majus is also noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and cold temperatures.
