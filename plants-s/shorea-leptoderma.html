Shorea leptoderma

Shorea leptoderma, commonly known as white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a pale to reddish-brown color. The plant has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are usually leathery in texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers and develops woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native forest ecosystems.
