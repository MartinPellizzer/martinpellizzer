Salvia honania

Salvia honania, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty aroma when crushed.

Salvia honania is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
