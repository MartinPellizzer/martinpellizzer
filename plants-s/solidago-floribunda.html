Solidago floribunda

Solidago floribunda, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its showy flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting a variety of pollinators.

Solidago floribunda plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems and is often used in native plant gardens.
