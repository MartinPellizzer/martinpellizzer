Schoenus nitens subsp. krausei

Schoenus nitens subsp.

krausei, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
