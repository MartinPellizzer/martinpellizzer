Spiranthes picta var. assurgens

Spiranthes picta var. assurgens, commonly known as the painted ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of pale green to white flowers with faint pink or purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a single, elongated spike of flowers.

This variety is often found in the southeastern U.S., including states like Florida and Georgia.
