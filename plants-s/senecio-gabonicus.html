Senecio gabonicus

Senecio gabonicus, commonly known as the Gabon ice plant, is a flowering plant native to central Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its scientific name honors the Gabon region, where it is naturally distributed.

Senecio gabonicus is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its water-storing leaves and efficient photosynthetic mechanisms.
