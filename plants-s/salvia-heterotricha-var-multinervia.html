Salvia heterotricha var. multinervia

Salvia heterotricha var. multinervia, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in whorled clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils are also utilized in aromatherapy and natural remedies.
