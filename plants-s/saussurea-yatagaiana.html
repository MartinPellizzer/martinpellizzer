Saussurea yatagaiana

Saussurea yatagaiana, commonly known as the Yatagaian gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy composite flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
