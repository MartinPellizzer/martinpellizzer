Salvia tilantongensis

Salvia tilantongensis, commonly known as the Tila Mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia tilantongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
