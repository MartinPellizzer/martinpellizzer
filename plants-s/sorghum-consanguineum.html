Sorghum consanguineum

Sorghum consanguineum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, typical of grasses. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
