Solidago virgaurea var. coreana

Solidago virgaurea var. coreana, commonly known as Korean goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small, ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
