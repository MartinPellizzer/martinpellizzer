Silene iberica

Silene iberica, commonly known as the Iberian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Silene iberica is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from spring to early summer.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
