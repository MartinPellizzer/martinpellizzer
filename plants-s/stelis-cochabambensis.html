Stelis cochabambensis

Stelis cochabambensis, commonly known as the Cochabamba orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. Native to the highlands of Bolivia, this plant thrives in cool, humid environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, adapted for specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
