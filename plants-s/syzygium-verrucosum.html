Syzygium verrucosum

Syzygium verrucosum, commonly known as the rough-leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
