Schoenus graciliculmis

Schoenus graciliculmis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Schoenus graciliculmis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
