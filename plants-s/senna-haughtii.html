Senna haughtii

Senna haughtii, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Senna haughtii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
