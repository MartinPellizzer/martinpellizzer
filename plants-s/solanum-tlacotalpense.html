Solanum tlacotalpense

Solanum tlacotalpense, commonly known as the Tlacotalpan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

It is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
