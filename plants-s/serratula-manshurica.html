Serratula manshurica

Serratula manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian sow-thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Serratula manshurica is also a source of nectar for pollinators and plays a role in local ecosystems.
