Spiranthes variegata

Spiranthes variegata, commonly known as the variegated lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It blooms from late summer to early fall, producing a showy, cascading inflorescence.

Spiranthes variegata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
