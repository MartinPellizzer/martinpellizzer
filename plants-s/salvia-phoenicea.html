Salvia phoenicea

Salvia phoenicea, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to red flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia phoenicea is also valued for its aromatic foliage and its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
