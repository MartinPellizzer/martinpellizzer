Scirpus multicaulis

Scirpus multicaulis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, pond edges, and along streams, thriving in saturated soil conditions. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that develop into dark, spherical berries.

Scirpus multicaulis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
