Salvia hypochionea

Salvia hypochionea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are hairy and aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia hypochionea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
