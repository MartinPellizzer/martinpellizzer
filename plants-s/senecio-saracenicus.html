Senecio saracenicus

Senecio saracenicus, commonly known as the Saracen's eye, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to southern Africa and features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique appearance.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
