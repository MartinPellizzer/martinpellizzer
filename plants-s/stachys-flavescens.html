Stachys flavescens

Stachys flavescens, commonly known as yellow betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the summer. Stachys flavescens is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its calming and healing effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
