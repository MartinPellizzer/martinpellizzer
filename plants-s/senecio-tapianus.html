Senecio tapianus

Senecio tapianus, commonly known as the tapianus groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture.

Senecio tapianus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
