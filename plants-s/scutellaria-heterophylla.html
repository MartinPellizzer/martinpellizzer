Scutellaria heterophylla

Scutellaria heterophylla, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Scutellaria heterophylla is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
