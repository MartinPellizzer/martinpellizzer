Stenocarpus acacioides

Stenocarpus acacioides, commonly known as the silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and large, bipinnate leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath. The tree produces fluffy, yellowish flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, followed by woody, egg-shaped pods containing seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its timber, which is lightweight and durable.
