Sophora fabacea

Sophora fabacea, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Additionally, its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
