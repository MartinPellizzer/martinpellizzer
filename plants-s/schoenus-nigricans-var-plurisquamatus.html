Schoenus nigricans var. plurisquamatus

Schoenus nigricans var. plurisquamatus, commonly known as the black rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, dark green stems that are often covered with a layer of dark, waxy cuticle, giving it its characteristic blackish appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in marshy areas.

The leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib and a fibrous texture that contributes to its structural strength.
