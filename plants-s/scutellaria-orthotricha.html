Scutellaria orthotricha

Scutellaria orthotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scutellaria orthotricha is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems.
