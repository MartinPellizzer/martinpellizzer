Syringa tibetica

Syringa tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, woody fruits known as follicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Syringa tibetica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic blooms.
