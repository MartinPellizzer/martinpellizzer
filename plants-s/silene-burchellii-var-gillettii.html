Silene burchellii var. gillettii

Silene burchellii var. gillettii, commonly known as the Burchell's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
