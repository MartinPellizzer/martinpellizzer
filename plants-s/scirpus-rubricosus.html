Scirpus rubricosus

Scirpus rubricosus, commonly known as the redstem bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are reddish-brown in color, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer.

Scirpus rubricosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
