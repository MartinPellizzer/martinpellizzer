Solanum munitum var. javellense

Solanum munitum var. javellense, commonly known as the Javelin nightshade, is a variant of the broader Solanum munitum species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant features distinctive violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by spiny stems and leaves. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe, are toxic and should not be consumed.

This species is valued in botanical studies for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
