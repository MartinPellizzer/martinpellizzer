Samanea schaffneri

Samanea schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's acacia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often shed during the dry season, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in cylindrical inflorescences.

Samanea schaffneri produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often used for its ornamental value and as a source of timber.
