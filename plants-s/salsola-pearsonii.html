Salsola pearsonii

Salsola pearsonii, commonly known as pearson's saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable plant for stabilizing soils in saline areas.

Salsola pearsonii plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and forage for wildlife in desert ecosystems.
