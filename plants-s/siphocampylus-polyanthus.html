Siphocampylus polyanthus

Siphocampylus polyanthus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Siphocampylus polyanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
