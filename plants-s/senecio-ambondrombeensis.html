Senecio ambondrombeensis

Senecio ambondrombeensis, commonly known as the Ambondrombe groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow.

It plays a role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
