Spartina cynosuroides f. major

Spartina cynosuroides f. major, commonly known as the giant cordgrass, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and dense, grass-like foliage. This species typically grows in coastal marshes and tidal flats, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks that help bind soil and prevent erosion.

The plant is also an important component of estuarine ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
