Senecio grisebachii f. anomalus

Senecio grisebachii f. anomalus, commonly known as the gray-haired senecio, is a rare and unique variant of the Senecio genus native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive grayish-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native range.
