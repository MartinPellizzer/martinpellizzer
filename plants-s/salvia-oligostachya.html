Salvia oligostachya

Salvia oligostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia oligostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
