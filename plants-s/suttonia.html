Suttonia

Suttonia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. Suttonia thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
