Sicyocarpus cordifolius

Sicyocarpus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved sicyocarpus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
