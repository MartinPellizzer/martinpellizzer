Solidago carinata

Solidago carinata, commonly known as the ridge goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flower clusters in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct central ridge, giving it its scientific name.

Solidago carinata is a valuable plant for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
