Styrax polyanthus

Styrax polyanthus, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Styrax polyanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
