Schrenkia alaica

Schrenkia alaica, commonly known as the Altai bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and branched.

Schrenkia alaica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
