Saurauia barbigera f. veranii

Saurauia barbigera f. veranii, commonly known as the summer saurauia, is a unique variant of the saurauia genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
