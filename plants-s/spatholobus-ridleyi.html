Spatholobus ridleyi

Spatholobus ridleyi, commonly known as the Singapore flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Spatholobus ridleyi is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
