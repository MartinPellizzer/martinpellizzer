Sarcanthus pensilis

Sarcanthus pensilis, commonly known as the hanging sarcanthus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It features thin, wiry stems that allow it to cling to supports and grow vertically. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, contributing to its unique appearance.

Sarcanthus pensilis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
