Salix × retusoides

Salix × retusoides, commonly known as the smooth willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix, characterized by its slender, flexible stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, temperate habitats such as riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salix × retusoides is valued in horticulture for its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
