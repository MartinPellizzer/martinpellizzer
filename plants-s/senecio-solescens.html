Senecio solescens

Senecio solescens, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.
