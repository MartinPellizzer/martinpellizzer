Scolopia platyphylla

Scolopia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved scolopia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Scolopia platyphylla is also valued in ecological landscapes for its ability to support local pollinators.
