Setaria depauperata

Setaria depauperata, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Setaria depauperata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
