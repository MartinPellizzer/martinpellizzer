Silene colorata var. spathulifolia

Silene colorata var. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are erect and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
