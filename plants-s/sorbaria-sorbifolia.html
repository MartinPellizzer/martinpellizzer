Sorbaria sorbifolia

Sorbaria sorbifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in flat-topped clusters during the spring. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, airy appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Sorbaria sorbifolia prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
