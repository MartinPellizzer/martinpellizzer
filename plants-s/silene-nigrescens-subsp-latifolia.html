Silene nigrescens subsp. latifolia

Silene nigrescens subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf blusher, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
