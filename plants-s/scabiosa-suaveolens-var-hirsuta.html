Scabiosa suaveolens var. hirsuta

Scabiosa suaveolens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense, fuzzy foliage that gives it its "hirsuta" designation, meaning hairy in Latin. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, globular shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
