Saxifraga × margoxiana

Saxifraga × margoxiana, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural care.
