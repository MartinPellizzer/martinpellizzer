Scirpus cespitosus var. delicatulus

Scirpus cespitosus var. delicatulus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
