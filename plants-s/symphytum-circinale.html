Symphytum circinale

Symphytum circinale, commonly known as the curledachen or curled root.

It is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that curl inward. The plant produces small, pinkish-purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Symphytum circinale is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
