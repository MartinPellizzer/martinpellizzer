Salvia trijuga

Salvia trijuga, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist woodlands and streambanks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers. Its blooming period occurs from late spring to early summer.

Salvia trijuga is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
