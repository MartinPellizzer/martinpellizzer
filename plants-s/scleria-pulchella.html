Scleria pulchella

Scleria pulchella, commonly known as the pretty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing delicate white or pale blue blooms.

Scleria pulchella is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ecological benefits.
