Saccharum rarum

Saccharum rarum, commonly known as the rare sugar cane, is a less common variant of the sugarcane genus.

It is characterized by its unique botanical features, including a compact growth habit and reduced sugar content compared to standard sugarcane varieties. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, while its stems are shorter and less fibrous.

Due to its low sugar yield, it is primarily of interest for botanical study rather than commercial cultivation.
