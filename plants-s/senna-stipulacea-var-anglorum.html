Senna stipulacea var. anglorum

Senna stipulacea var. anglorum, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
