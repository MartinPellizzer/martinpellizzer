Sida chiquitana

Sida chiquitana, commonly known as the "chiquitana" or "barrigona," is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Bolivia, Paraguay, and northern Argentina.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. The plant features small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually ovate with a hairy texture. Sida chiquitana is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

It also has traditional uses in folk medicine by indigenous communities.
