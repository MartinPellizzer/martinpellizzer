Senecio harazianus

Senecio harazianus, commonly known as the Harazian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
