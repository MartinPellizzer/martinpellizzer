Saxifraga heterotricha var. anadena

Saxifraga heterotricha var. anadena, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette structure that helps conserve moisture in harsh climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
