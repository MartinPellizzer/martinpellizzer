Saxifraga exarata subsp. suaveolens

Saxifraga exarata subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
