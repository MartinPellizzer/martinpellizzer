Saxifraga × poluanglica

Saxifraga × poluanglica, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga × poluanglica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
