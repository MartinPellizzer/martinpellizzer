Saxifraga moschata var. fastigiata

Saxifraga moschata var. fastigiata, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
