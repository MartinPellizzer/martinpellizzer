Syzygium myrtifolium

Syzygium myrtifolium, commonly known as the satinwood or red myrtle, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pink, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.
