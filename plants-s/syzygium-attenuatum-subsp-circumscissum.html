Syzygium attenuatum subsp. circumscissum

Syzygium attenuatum subsp.

circumscissum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
