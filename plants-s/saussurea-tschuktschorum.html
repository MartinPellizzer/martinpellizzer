Saussurea tschuktschorum

Saussurea tschuktschorum, commonly known as the Siberian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, herbaceous stems and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and arctic environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
