Scabiosa fischeri f. albiflora

Scabiosa fischeri f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower scabious, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the scabious plant family.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or blue hues of the species. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its limited distribution and unique floral characteristics, it is valued by botanists and horticultural enthusiasts alike.
