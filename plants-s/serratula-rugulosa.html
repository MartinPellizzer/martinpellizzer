Serratula rugulosa

Serratula rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf sagewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, rough-textured leaves and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Serratula rugulosa belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
