Saxifraga bronchialis var. austromontana

Saxifraga bronchialis var. austromontana, commonly known as the Australian saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It typically forms dense mats of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, usually blooming from late autumn to early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathland environments.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
