Solanum sambiranense

Solanum sambiranense, commonly known as the Sambiranense nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers and typically grows as a shrub or vine. Its leaves are usually ovate or lanceolate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its medicinal and toxic properties, it is of interest to botanists and pharmacologists.
