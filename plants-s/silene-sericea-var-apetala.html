Silene sericea var. apetala

Silene sericea var. apetala, commonly known as the hairleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant characterized by its silky, silver-gray foliage.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to regions of North America. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
