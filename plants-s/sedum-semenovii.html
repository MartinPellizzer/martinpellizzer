Sedum semenovii

Sedum semenovii, commonly known as the Semenov's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Sedum semenovii is relatively low-maintenance and resistant to pests, making it an excellent addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
