Senegalia multiflora

Senegalia multiflora, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to long, slender pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought and plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Senegalia multiflora is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
