Solanum pseudocapsicum subsp. diflorum

Solanum pseudocapsicum subsp.

diflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a yellowish, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is part of the Solanaceae family, known for its alkaloid-rich plants, though it is not widely cultivated for agricultural purposes.
