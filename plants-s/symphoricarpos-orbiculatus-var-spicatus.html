Symphoricarpos orbiculatus var. spicatus

Symphoricarpos orbiculatus var. spicatus, commonly known as the spicata serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during early summer, followed by edible, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat benefits.
