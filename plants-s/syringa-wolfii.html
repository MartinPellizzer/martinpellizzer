Syringa wolfii

Syringa wolfii, commonly known as the Russian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Siberia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, pale lavender to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Syringa wolfii is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate a wide range of soil types.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
