Solanum melanandrum

Solanum melanandrum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant produces small, black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats.

Solanum melanandrum is characterized by its dark purple flowers and is often found in arid regions.
