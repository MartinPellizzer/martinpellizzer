Solanum verbascifolium var. auriculatum

Solanum verbascifolium var. auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate bladdernut, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, auriculate (ear-shaped) leaf bases. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
