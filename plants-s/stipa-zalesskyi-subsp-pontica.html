Stipa zalesskyi subsp. pontica

Stipa zalesskyi subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
