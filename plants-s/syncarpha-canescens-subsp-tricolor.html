Syncarpha canescens subsp. tricolor

Syncarpha canescens subsp.

tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored synacantha, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, woolly leaves and clusters of tiny, three-colored flowers that range from white to pink and purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
