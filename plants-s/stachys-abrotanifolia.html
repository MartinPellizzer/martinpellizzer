Stachys abrotanifolia

Stachys abrotanifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the upper stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Stachys abrotanifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
