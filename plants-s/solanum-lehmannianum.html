Solanum lehmannianum

Solanum lehmannianum, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a species of flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can withstand high temperatures and low moisture. The plant produces small, green to yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it has small white or pale purple flowers.
