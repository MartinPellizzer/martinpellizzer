Stellaria gypsophiloides var. lanceolata

Stellaria gypsophiloides var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens for its ornamental value.
