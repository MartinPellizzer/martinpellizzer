Stelis maxima

Stelis maxima, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Stelis maxima is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
