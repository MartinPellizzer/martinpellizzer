Sedum punctatum

Sedum punctatum, commonly known as the punctate stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky regions of the Mediterranean.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves helps it survive in harsh, dry environments.
