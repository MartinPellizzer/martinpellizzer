Salix kingoi

Salix kingoi, commonly known as the King's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, inconspicuous female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Salix kingoi is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife.
