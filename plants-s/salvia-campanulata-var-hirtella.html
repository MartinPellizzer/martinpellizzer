Salvia campanulata var. hirtella

Salvia campanulata var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy bell sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue, often with a pale throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, rocky soils.
