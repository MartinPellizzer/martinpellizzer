Salvia atropurpurea

Salvia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a valued addition to both gardens and herbal collections.
