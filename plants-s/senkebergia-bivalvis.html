Senkebergia bivalvis

Senkebergia bivalvis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance to enhance nighttime pollination.

Senkebergia bivalvis thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
