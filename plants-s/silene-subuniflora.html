Silene subuniflora

Silene subuniflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pink to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive central column.

Silene subuniflora is adapted to survive in harsh environments, often with deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.
