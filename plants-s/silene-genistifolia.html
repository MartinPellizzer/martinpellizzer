Silene genistifolia

Silene genistifolia, commonly known as the yellow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Silene genistifolia is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
