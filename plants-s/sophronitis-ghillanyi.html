Sophronitis ghillanyi

Sophronitis ghillanyi, commonly known as the Ghillany's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Sophronitis ghillanyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
