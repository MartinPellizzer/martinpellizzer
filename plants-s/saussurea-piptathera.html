Saussurea piptathera

Saussurea piptathera, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange-colored flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and often grows in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, achene-like fruits.

Saussurea piptathera is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
