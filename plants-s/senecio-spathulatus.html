Senecio spathulatus

Senecio spathulatus, commonly known as the spoonleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, fleshy leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Senecio spathulatus is a perennial species that is well adapted to drought conditions.
