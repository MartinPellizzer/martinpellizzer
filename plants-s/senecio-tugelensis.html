Senecio tugelensis

Senecio tugelensis, commonly known as the Tugel groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique habitat and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
