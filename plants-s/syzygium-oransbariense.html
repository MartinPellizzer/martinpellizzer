Syzygium oransbariense

Syzygium oransbariense, commonly known as the Baraniang tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of northern Australia, where it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils. The tree is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit, a small, round drupe, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Syzygium oransbariense plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various native wildlife species.
