Silene japonica

Silene japonica, commonly known as Japanese catchfly, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Silene japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive blooms and ease of care.
