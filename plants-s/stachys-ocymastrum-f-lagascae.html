Stachys ocymastrum f. lagascae

Stachys ocymastrum f. lagascae, commonly known as the Lagasce stachys, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
