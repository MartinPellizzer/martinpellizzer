Sesamum reniforme

Sesamum reniforme, commonly known as the kidney-shaped sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in loose inflorescences.

The seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
