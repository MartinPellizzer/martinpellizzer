Sopubia lanata var. densiflora

Sopubia lanata var. densiflora, commonly known as the woolly sopubia, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique texture and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
