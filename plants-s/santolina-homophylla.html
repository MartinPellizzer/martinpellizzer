Santolina homophylla

Santolina homophylla, commonly known as silver santolina, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in compact heads. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design.
