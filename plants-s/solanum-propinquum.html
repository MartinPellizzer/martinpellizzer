Solanum propinquum

Solanum propinquum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum propinquum is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.
