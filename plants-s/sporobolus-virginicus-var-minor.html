Sporobolus virginicus var. minor

Sporobolus virginicus var. minor, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant is well adapted to sandy or loamy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a common component of native grasslands. It produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically green or bluish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including erosion control and wildlife habitat support.
