Sideritis ochroleuca

Sideritis ochroleuca, commonly known as white heather, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Sideritis ochroleuca is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
