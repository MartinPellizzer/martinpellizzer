Stillingia daphniphylla

Stillingia daphniphylla, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Stillingia daphniphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
