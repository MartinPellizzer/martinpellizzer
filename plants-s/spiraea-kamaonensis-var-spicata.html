Spiraea kamaonensis var. spicata

Spiraea kamaonensis var. spicata, commonly known as the spiculate spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and has slender, arching branches covered in fine, hairy leaves. Its foliage turns a striking golden yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Spiraea kamaonensis var. spicata is often cultivated for its attractive flowers and hardy nature in temperate climates.
