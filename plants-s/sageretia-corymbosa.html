Sageretia corymbosa

Sageretia corymbosa, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy, waxy texture.

Sageretia corymbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
