Santaloides florida

Santaloides florida, commonly known as the Florida sandalwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Santalaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a unique root system that forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often found in sandy or well-drained soils in coastal regions.
