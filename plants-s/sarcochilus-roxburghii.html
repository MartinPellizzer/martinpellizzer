Sarcochilus roxburghii

Sarcochilus roxburghii, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with a dense, hairy stem and broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and requires good drainage.

Sarcochilus roxburghii is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
