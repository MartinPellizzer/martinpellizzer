Syringa dilatata var. rubra

Syringa dilatata var. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. This variety is prized for its vibrant floral display and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
