Sedum marichalii

Sedum marichalii, commonly known as the Marichal's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
