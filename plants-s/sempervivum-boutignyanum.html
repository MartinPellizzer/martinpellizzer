Sempervivum boutignyanum

Sempervivum boutignyanum, commonly known as the Alpine House plant, is a succulent species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense, fleshy rosettes of blue-green leaves that are often adorned with red or purple tips. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It produces star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Sempervivum boutignyanum is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for rock gardens and container cultivation.
