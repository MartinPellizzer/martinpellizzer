Sorghum basiplicatum f. leucolepis

Sorghum basiplicatum f. leucolepis, commonly known as the white-leaved sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species characterized by its distinctive white or pale green foliage.

This plant is native to Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant traits. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture, and it produces tall, branching stalks that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often cultivated for its forage value and has been used in traditional agriculture for centuries.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
