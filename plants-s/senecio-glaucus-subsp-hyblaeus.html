Senecio glaucus subsp. hyblaeus

Senecio glaucus subsp.

hyblaeus, commonly known as the Hyblaeus groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial with grey-green, woolly leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
