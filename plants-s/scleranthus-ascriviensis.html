Scleranthus ascriviensis

Scleranthus ascriviensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thornweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Scleranthus ascriviensis is a hardy species adapted to arid conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
