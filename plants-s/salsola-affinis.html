Salsola affinis

Salsola affinis, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. It has a low, spreading growth habit and produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Salsola affinis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
