Sarcopodium megalanthum

Sarcopodium megalanthum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, making it a prominent feature in ponds and water gardens. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters and is known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the substrate, while its leaves provide habitat for aquatic life.

Sarcopodium megalanthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
