Scabiosa atropurpurea subsp. maritima

Scabiosa atropurpurea subsp.

maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a white or yellow center.

The plant has silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine hairs, helping it to retain moisture in coastal environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
