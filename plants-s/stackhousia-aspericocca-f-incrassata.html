Stackhousia aspericocca f. incrassata

Stackhousia aspericocca f. incrassata, commonly known as the rough-leaved stackhousia, is a variant of the Stackhousia genus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid environments.
