Stachys euboica

Stachys euboica, commonly known as the Euboean hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Stachys euboica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional herbal medicine.
