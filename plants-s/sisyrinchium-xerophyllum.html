Sisyrinchium xerophyllum

Sisyrinchium xerophyllum, commonly known as the desert blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, featuring a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium xerophyllum is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
