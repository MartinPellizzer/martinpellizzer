Scirpus pallidus

Scirpus pallidus, commonly known as the pale bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges. Its pale yellow to brown flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Scirpus pallidus is also a valuable food source for waterfowl and other wetland wildlife.
