Semecarpus riparius

Semecarpus riparius, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruiting bodies. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spiny, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.
