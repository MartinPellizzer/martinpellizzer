Saussurea multiloba

Saussurea multiloba, commonly known as the Tibetan snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant has fibrous roots that are often harvested for their medicinal properties. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme weather conditions.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in alpine ecosystems.
