Senecillis glauca

Senecillis glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery blue-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Senecillis glauca is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
