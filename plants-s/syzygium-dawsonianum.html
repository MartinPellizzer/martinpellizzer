Syzygium dawsonianum

Syzygium dawsonianum, commonly known as the Dawson's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, yellowish pod that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium dawsonianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
