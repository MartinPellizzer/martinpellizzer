Saussurea wilsoniana

Saussurea wilsoniana, commonly known as Wilson's saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic compound known as safflower, which is used in traditional medicine. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Saussurea wilsoniana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
