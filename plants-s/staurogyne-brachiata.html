Staurogyne brachiata

Staurogyne brachiata, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often resembling those of the hibiscus genus. The plant has a bushy growth habit with waxy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically floating or semi-submerged. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, making it a popular choice for pond and aquarium decoration.

Its rapid growth and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
