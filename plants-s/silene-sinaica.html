Silene sinaica

Silene sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai pink, is a flowering plant native to the rocky areas of the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

Silene sinaica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
