Senecio radulifolius

Senecio radulifolius, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Senecio radulifolius is drought-tolerant and is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
