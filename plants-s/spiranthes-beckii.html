Spiranthes beckii

Spiranthes beckii, commonly known as the Beck's ladies' tresses, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, spiraled inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

Spiranthes beckii is a perennial herb that thrives in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in association with other orchid species.
