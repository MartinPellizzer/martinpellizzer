Scirpus lineatus var. asiaticus

Scirpus lineatus var. asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant features tall, slender stems that are often covered with dense, cylindrical leaves. Its flowering spikes, which appear at the top of the stems, produce small, clustered flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
