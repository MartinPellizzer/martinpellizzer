Salmonea cantoniensis

Salmonea cantoniensis, commonly known as the Canton salmonberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and often thorny.

Salmonea cantoniensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
